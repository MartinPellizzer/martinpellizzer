Acacia velvae

Acacia velvae, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple narrow leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Acacia velvae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
