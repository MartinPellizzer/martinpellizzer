Actinodaphne pruinosa

Actinodaphne pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
