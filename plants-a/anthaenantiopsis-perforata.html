Anthaenantiopsis perforata

Anthaenantiopsis perforata, commonly known as the perforate antelope plant, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a cluster of antlers, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive perforated texture along the edges. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unusual appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to flourish.
