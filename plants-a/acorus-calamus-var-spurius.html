Acorus calamus var. spurius

Acorus calamus var. spurius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and emit a distinct, spicy scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomes are rhizomatous and can spread to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
