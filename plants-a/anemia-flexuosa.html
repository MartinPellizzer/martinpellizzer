Anemia flexuosa

Anemia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible anemone, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white blooms.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in shaded to partially sunny areas, often found in woodland habitats. Its stems are flexible and can trail or climb, giving it a distinctive, airy appearance.

Anemia flexuosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
