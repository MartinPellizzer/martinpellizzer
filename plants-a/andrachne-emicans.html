Andrachne emicans

Andrachne emicans, commonly known as the spreading sandverbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
