Andryala minuta

Andryala minuta, commonly known as the dwarf arnica, is a small flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are short and often solitary.

Andryala minuta is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
