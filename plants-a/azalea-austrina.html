Azalea austrina

Azalea austrina, commonly known as the Australian azalea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving shrubs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, blooming in spring. It typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained, acidic soils.

Azalea austrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
