Astranthium integrifolium

Astranthium integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, smooth-edged, and covered in fine hairs, while its purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Astranthium integrifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
