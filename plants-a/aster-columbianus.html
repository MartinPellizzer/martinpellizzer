Aster columbianus

Aster columbianus, commonly known as the Columbian aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by narrow, petal-like rays.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
