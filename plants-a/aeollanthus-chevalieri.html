Aeollanthus chevalieri

Aeollanthus chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
