Alyssum lenense

Alyssum lenense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Alyssum lenense is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
