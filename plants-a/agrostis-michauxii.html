Agrostis michauxii

Agrostis michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along streambanks.

Agrostis michauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
