Arundo argentea

Arundo argentea, commonly known as silver reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Silver reed is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
