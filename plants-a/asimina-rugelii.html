Asimina rugelii

Asimina rugelii, commonly known as the Florida yellow custard apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits that mature to a bright yellow or orange color.

The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, custard-like pulp, making it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
