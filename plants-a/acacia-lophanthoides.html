Acacia lophanthoides

Acacia lophanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and closely arranged. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Acacia lophanthoides is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
