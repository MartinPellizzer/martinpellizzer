Acacia mucronata subsp. dependens

Acacia mucronata subsp.

dependens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated phyllodes that are often curved and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which appear during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive appearance and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
