Asperula cynanchica var. gracilis

Asperula cynanchica var. gracilis, commonly known as slender woodbine, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark blue to black, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland or shady garden settings.
