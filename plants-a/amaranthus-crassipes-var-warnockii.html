Amaranthus crassipes var. warnockii

Amaranthus crassipes var. warnockii, commonly known as the swamp amaranth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The leaves are lanceolate, with a prominent midrib and a rough texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. This species thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and along water edges, where it forms dense stands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
