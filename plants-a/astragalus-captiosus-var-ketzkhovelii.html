Astragalus captiosus var. ketzkhovelii

Astragalus captiosus var. ketzkhovelii, commonly known as the Ketzkhovel milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
