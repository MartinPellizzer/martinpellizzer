Acacia patens

Acacia patens, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Acacia patens is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
