Arundinella campbelliana

Arundinella campbelliana, commonly known as Campbell's bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinella campbelliana is often used in traditional landscaping and has cultural significance in its native regions.
