Aleuritia matsumurae

Aleuritia matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Aleuritia matsumurae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
