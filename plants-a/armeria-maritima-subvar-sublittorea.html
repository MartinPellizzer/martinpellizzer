Armeria maritima subvar. sublittorea

Armeria maritima subvar. sublittorea, commonly known as the seaside thrift, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to coastal areas of the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. The plant produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs.

It is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
