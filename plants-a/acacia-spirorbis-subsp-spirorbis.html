Acacia spirorbis subsp. spirorbis

Acacia spirorbis subsp.

spirorbis, commonly known as the spiral wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, a common adaptation in acacia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its distinctive spiral arrangement of branches, which gives it its common name.
