Alisma tenellum f. latifolium

Alisma tenellum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf water plantain, is a variant of the water plantain species native to wetland habitats.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically floating or submerged, adapted for aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its habitat. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize shallow water areas effectively.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
