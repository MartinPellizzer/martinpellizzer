Acalypha padifolia

Acalypha padifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Acalypha padifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
