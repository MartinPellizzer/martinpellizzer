Antennaria canescens subsp. porsildii

Antennaria canescens subsp.

porsildii, commonly known as the Porridge sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It features a mat-forming habit with hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically grayish-white in color.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
