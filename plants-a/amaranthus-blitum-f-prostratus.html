Amaranthus blitum f. prostratus

Amaranthus blitum f. prostratus, commonly known as prostrate amaranth, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Americas.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, covered with small, triangular leaves. The plant produces tiny, green to red flowers in dense clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and may have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
