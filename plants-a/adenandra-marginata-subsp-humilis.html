Adenandra marginata subsp. humilis

Adenandra marginata subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops, where it thrives in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal blooms.
