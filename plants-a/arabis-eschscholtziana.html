Arabis eschscholtziana

Arabis eschscholtziana, commonly known as the California rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically has basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous slender, branching stems that bear clusters of flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Arabis eschscholtziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
