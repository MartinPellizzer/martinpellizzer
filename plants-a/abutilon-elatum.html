Abutilon elatum

Abutilon elatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its fibrous roots and soft, fuzzy foliage make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.

Abutilon elatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
