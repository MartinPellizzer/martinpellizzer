Aglaia testicularis

Aglaia testicularis, commonly known as the "testicle tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain a single, large, fleshy seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

However, due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions.
