Azalea indica var. balsaminiflora

Azalea indica var. balsaminiflora, commonly known as the balsam-flowered azalea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
