Acer tutcheri subsp. tutcheri

Acer tutcheri subsp.

tutcheri, commonly known as the Tutcher's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often grown in gardens and parks.
