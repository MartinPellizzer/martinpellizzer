Allium polyrhizum var. potaninii

Allium polyrhizum var. potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a single, slender flower stem that bears small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
