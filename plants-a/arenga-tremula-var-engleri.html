Arenga tremula var. engleri

Arenga tremula var. engleri, commonly known as the Engler's areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palms. Its fruits are three-angled and contain a single seed, which is used in traditional practices.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other tropical regions for cultivation.
