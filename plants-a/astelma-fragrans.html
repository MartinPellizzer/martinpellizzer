Astelma fragrans

Astelma fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant aster, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive fragrance. Astelma fragrans typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical structure includes alternate leaves and a central stem that supports its floral display.
