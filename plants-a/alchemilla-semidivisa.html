Alchemilla semidivisa

Alchemilla semidivisa, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.

Alchemilla semidivisa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
