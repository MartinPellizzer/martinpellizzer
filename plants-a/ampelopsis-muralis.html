Ampelopsis muralis

Ampelopsis muralis, commonly known as the climbing false ivy, is a woody vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb walls and trees. Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it a cautionary plant in landscaping.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, rocky habitats.
