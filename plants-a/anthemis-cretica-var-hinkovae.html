Anthemis cretica var. hinkovae

Anthemis cretica var. hinkovae, commonly known as the Hinkov's tansy, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
