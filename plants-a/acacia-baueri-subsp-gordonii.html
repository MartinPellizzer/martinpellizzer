Acacia baueri subsp. gordonii

Acacia baueri subsp.

gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow, spherical flower heads that attract pollinators. Its bark is often fissured and may exude a milky sap when damaged.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
