Avena sativa var. macrantha

Avena sativa var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for grain production. It features slender, upright stems and produces panicles of florets that can reach significant heights. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant is typically perennial.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.
