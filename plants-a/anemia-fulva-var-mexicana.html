Anemia fulva var. mexicana

Anemia fulva var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican redroot, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a modest show of pink or red blooms.
