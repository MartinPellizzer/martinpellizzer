Artemisia maritima var. willdenowiana

Artemisia maritima var. willdenowiana, commonly known as sea wormwood, is a perennial herb native to coastal areas of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower clusters. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, often bitter scent.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
