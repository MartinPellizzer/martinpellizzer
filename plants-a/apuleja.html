Apuleja

Apuleja, commonly known as the "Spanish bellflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped or tubular flowers, which can range in color from blue to white. These plants are native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. Apuleja species typically grow in rocky or dry soils and are often found in Mediterranean climates.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
