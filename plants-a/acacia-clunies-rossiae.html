Acacia clunies-rossiae

Acacia clunies-rossiae, commonly known as the Clunies-Ross acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater. It is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, thriving in low-nutrient conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
