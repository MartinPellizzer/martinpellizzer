Acacia giraffae

Acacia giraffae, commonly known as the giraffe tree, is a tall, slender legume tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The tree produces yellow flowers in summer, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming dense thickets.

The wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.
