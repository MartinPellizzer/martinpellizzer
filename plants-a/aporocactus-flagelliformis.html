Aporocactus flagelliformis

Aporocactus flagelliformis, commonly known as the whip cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
