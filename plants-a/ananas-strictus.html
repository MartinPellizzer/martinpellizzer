Ananas strictus

Ananas strictus, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spiky leaves. The plant produces a fragrant, edible fruit that is smaller and less sweet than the common pineapple. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
