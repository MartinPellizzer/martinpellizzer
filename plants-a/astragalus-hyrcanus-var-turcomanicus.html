Astragalus hyrcanus var. turcomanicus

Astragalus hyrcanus var. turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
