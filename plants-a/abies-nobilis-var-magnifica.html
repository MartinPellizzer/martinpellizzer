Abies nobilis var. magnifica

Abies nobilis var. magnifica, commonly known as the Whitebark Pine, is a variant of the Noble Fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elegant, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, edible cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against forest fires and harsh weather conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in high-elevation forests, supporting a variety of wildlife.
