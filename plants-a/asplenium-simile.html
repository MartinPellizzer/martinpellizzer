Asplenium simile

Asplenium simile, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Asplenium simile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
