Anthyllis vulneraria proles macrophylla

Anthyllis vulneraria proles macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its habitat.
