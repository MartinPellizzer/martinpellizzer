Aeonium youngianum

Aeonium youngianum, commonly known as the Young's Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow flowers, which appear in spring or early summer. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Aeonium youngianum is prized for its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
