Artemisia dracunculoides var. incana

Artemisia dracunculoides var. incana, commonly known as gray sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
