Aspalathus lanceicarpa

Aspalathus lanceicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peacock pea, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Aspalathus lanceicarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
