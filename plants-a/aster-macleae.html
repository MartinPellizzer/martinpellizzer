Aster macleae

Aster macleae, commonly known as the MacLeay's aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela.

Aster macleae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
