Anomocarpus axillaris

Anomocarpus axillaris, commonly known as the coastal sandbur, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring small, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Anomocarpus axillaris is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.
