Aesculus hippocastanum var. variegata

Aesculus hippocastanum var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

The leaves are broad, palmately lobed, and exhibit a striking variegation of white and green, enhancing its ornamental value. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks. It produces showy, clustered white flowers in spring, followed by spiny, red-brown capsules that contain glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

The plant is relatively slow-growing and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
