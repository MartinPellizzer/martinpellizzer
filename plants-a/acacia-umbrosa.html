Acacia umbrosa

Acacia umbrosa, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, ribbed pods containing seeds. Acacia umbrosa is adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
