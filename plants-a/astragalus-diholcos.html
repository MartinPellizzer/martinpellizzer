Astragalus diholcos

Astragalus diholcos, commonly known as the two-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Astragalus diholcos is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and medicinal studies.
