Acacia obtusata

Acacia obtusata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. Acacia obtusata is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
