Arundinaria scabriflora var. breviligulata

Arundinaria scabriflora var. breviligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the scabriflora bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are narrow and have a slightly rough texture, giving the plant its specific epithet "scabriflora.". It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
