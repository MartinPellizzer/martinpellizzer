Arenaria venezuelana

Arenaria venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Venezuela.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink in color, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to thrive in arid or rocky environments.

Arenaria venezuelana is often found in sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
