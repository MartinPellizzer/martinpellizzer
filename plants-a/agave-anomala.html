Agave anomala

Agave anomala, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, blue-gray leaves with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Agave anomala is widely cultivated for its sap, which is used in the production of tequila.
