Alocasia veitchii var. supurba

Alocasia veitchii var. supurba, commonly known as the Veitch's Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green coloration and prominent veins. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It requires consistent moisture and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its dramatic foliage.

Alocasia veitchii var. supurba is relatively slow-growing but can produce small, white flowers under optimal conditions.
