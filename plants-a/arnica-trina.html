Arnica trina

Arnica trina, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by leafy bracts. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

Arnica trina is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
