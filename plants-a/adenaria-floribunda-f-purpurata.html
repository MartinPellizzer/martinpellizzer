Adenaria floribunda f. purpurata

Adenaria floribunda f. purpurata, commonly known as the purple-flowered adenaria, is a variant of the adenaria genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous structure, which aid in its survival in arid conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
