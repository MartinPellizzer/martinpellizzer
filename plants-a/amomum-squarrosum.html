Amomum squarrosum

Amomum squarrosum, commonly known as the rough shellnut, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
