Alyssum repens f. micranthum

Alyssum repens f. micranthum, commonly known as the dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its delicate appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its resilience and ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
