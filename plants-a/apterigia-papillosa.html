Apterigia papillosa

Apterigia papillosa, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Apterigia papillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
