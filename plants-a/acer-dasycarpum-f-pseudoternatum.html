Acer dasycarpum f. pseudoternatum

Acer dasycarpum f. pseudoternatum, commonly known as the Chinese maple or the red maple, is a variant of the species Acer dasycarpum, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, typically reaching heights of 10 to 15 meters. The leaves are palmately lobed, often with a reddish tinge during the growing season, and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a samara, typical of the Acer genus.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color display.
