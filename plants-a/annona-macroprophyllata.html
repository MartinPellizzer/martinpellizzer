Annona macroprophyllata

Annona macroprophyllata, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are followed by large, fuzzy fruits that resemble papayas.

The fruit contains a single large seed and is known for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
