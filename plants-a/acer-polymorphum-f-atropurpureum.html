Acer polymorphum f. atropurpureum

Acer polymorphum f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaf maple, is a striking ornamental tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which turns a vibrant purple-red in the autumn, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. The tree has a rounded crown and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
