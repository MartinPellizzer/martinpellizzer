Astragalus medullaris

Astragalus medullaris, commonly known as the hollow pea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant's unique feature is its hollow, segmented stem, which gives it its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Astragalus medullaris is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
