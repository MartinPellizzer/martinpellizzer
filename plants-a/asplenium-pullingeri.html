Asplenium pullingeri

Asplenium pullingeri, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, with a dark green color and a serrated edge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies.

Asplenium pullingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture and landscaping.
