Acacia crombiei

Acacia crombiei, commonly known as the Kimberley wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched form and phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia crombiei plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
