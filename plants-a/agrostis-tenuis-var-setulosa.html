Agrostis tenuis var. setulosa

Agrostis tenuis var. setulosa, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense growth habit.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has a compact, tufted appearance with slender culms and small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well-adapted to moist soils and can thrive in partial shade, making it a common component of natural and managed grasslands.

Its botanical features make it valuable for ecological studies and as a forage plant.
