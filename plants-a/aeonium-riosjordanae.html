Aeonium riosjordanae

Aeonium riosjordanae, commonly known as the Riosjordana Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are usually blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or summer.

Aeonium riosjordanae is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
