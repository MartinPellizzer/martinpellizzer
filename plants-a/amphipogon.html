Amphipogon

Amphipogon, commonly known as the sweet sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with abundant moisture. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.

Amphipogon is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
