Aster novi-belgii subsp. johannensis

Aster novi-belgii subsp.

johannensis, commonly known as the Johann's New Belgium Aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to pink petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
