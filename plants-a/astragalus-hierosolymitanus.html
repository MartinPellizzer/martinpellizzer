Astragalus hierosolymitanus

Astragalus hierosolymitanus, commonly known as the Holy Land milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
