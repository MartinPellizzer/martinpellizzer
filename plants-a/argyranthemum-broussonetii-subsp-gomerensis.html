Argyranthemum broussonetii subsp. gomerensis

Argyranthemum broussonetii subsp.

gomerensis, commonly known as the Gomer's argyranthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive blooms and drought tolerance.

This subspecies is particularly valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various growing conditions.
