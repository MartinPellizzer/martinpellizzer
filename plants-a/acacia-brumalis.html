Acacia brumalis

Acacia brumalis, commonly known as the winter acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia brumalis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
