Ajuga × fallax

Ajuga × fallax, commonly known as the common bugleweed, is a hybrid evergreen perennial belonging to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Ajuga × fallax is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
