Araiostegia delavayi

Araiostegia delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's lichen, is a crustose lichen belonging to the family Pertusariaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tightly adherent growth form, typically found on rock surfaces in temperate regions. The thallus is yellowish to brownish in color, and it lacks distinct lobes or pores. It is often found in shaded, moist environments, such as on tree trunks or soil.

This lichen is of interest to mycologists due to its unique chemical composition and ecological role in soil stabilization.
