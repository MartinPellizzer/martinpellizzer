Amasonia velutina

Amasonia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. It has a robust root system that helps it thrive in wet environments.

Amasonia velutina is an important plant for pollinators, providing nectar for bees and other insects.
