Astragalus intercedens

Astragalus intercedens, commonly known as the interceding milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Astragalus intercedens is known for its deep root system, which helps it access water from deeper underground.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
