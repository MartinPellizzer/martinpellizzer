Aldinia glechomoides

Aldinia glechomoides, commonly known as false hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, clustered inflorescences, which are typically arranged in a panicle structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, which can impact local vegetation.
