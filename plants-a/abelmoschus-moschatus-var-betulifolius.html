Abelmoschus moschatus var. betulifolius

Abelmoschus moschatus var. betulifolius, commonly known as the betel leaf plant, is a variant of the okra species characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the betel nut tree.

This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, particularly in South Asian cuisines. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and its role in both ornamental and functional horticulture.
