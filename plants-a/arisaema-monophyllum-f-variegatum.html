Arisaema monophyllum f. variegatum

Arisaema monophyllum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated threadleaf arrowroot, is a unique variant of the arrowhead plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which add visual interest to its foliage. The plant produces a spadix enclosed within a spathaceous bract, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
