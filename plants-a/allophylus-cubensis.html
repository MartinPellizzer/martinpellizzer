Allophylus cubensis

Allophylus cubensis, commonly known as Cuban holly, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. Its small, white flowers are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Allophylus cubensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
