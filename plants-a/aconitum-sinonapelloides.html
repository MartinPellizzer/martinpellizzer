Aconitum sinonapelloides

Aconitum sinonapelloides, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
