Aster albescens

Aster albescens, commonly known as the white aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Aster albescens is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full sunlight.
