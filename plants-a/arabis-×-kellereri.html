Arabis × kellereri

Arabis × kellereri, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Arabis × kellereri is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
