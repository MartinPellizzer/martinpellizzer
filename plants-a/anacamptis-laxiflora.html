Anacamptis laxiflora

Anacamptis laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flowered orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to brownish green flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Anacamptis laxiflora is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in calcareous soils.
