Atropa ramosissima

Atropa ramosissima, commonly known as the death camas, is a toxic plant native to North America.

It belongs to the nightshade family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant contains potent alkaloids, including atropine, which can be lethal if ingested. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to avoid contact or consumption of any part of the plant.
