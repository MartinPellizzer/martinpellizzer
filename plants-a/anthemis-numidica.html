Anthemis numidica

Anthemis numidica, commonly known as the Numidian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
