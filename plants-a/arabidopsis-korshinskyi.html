Arabidopsis korshinskyi

Arabidopsis korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's thale cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like Arabidopsis thaliana. This plant is characterized by its small, rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its genetic similarity to Arabidopsis thaliana, it is of interest in plant biology research.
