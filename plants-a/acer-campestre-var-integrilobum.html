Acer campestre var. integrilobum

Acer campestre var. integrilobum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its smooth, entire leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
