Albizia saponaria var. saponaria

Albizia saponaria var. saponaria, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and covered in a fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Albizia saponaria var. saponaria is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
