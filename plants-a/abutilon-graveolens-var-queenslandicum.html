Abutilon graveolens var. queenslandicum

Abutilon graveolens var. queenslandicum, commonly known as the Queensland velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
