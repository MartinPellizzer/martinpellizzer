Alopecias ponticus

Alopecias ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, open habitats.

Alopecias ponticus is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a characteristic species of the steppe ecosystem.
