Alysicarpus heterophyllus

Alysicarpus heterophyllus, commonly known as Java sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by distinctive, flattened, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated fields.
