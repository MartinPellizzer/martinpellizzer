Alopecurus geniculatus var. aristulatus

Alopecurus geniculatus var. aristulatus, commonly known as the bentgrass with awn, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive awns on its seed heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread in suitable environments.
