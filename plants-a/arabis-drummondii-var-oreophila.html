Arabis drummondii var. oreophila

Arabis drummondii var. oreophila, commonly known as the Sierra Drummond's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
