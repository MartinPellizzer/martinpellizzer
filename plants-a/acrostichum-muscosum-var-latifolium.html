Acrostichum muscosum var. latifolium

Acrostichum muscosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The leaves are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
