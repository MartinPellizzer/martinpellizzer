Azalea virgata

Azalea virgata, commonly known as the Florida azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
