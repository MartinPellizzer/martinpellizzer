Astragalus floridus

Astragalus floridus, commonly known as the Florida milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Astragalus floridus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
