Agrostis polypogonoides

Agrostis polypogonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Agrostis polypogonoides is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in agricultural and natural landscapes.
