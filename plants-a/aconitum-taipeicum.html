Aconitum taipeicum

Aconitum taipeicum, commonly known as Taiwan aconite, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Aconitum taipeicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
