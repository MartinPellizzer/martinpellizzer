Anthericum calyculatum

Anthericum calyculatum, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the end of each stem. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.

Anthericum calyculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
