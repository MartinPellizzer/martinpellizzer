Alectorolophus glandulosus var. malyi

Alectorolophus glandulosus var. malyi, commonly known as the Malyi's glandular orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth, where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are alternate and often covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its common name.

The flowers, which are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, are adapted for specific pollinators, showcasing the plant's evolutionary adaptations to its ecological niche.
