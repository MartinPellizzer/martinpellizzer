Astragalus fischerianus

Astragalus fischerianus, commonly known as Fisher's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus fischerianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
