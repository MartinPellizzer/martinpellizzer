Albizia laui

Albizia laui, commonly known as the yellow albizia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree is notable for its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to its landscape. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits.

Albizia laui is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
