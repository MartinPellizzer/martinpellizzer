Adromischus fosteri

Adromischus fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's moonstone, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with purple or pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive droughts.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collections.

Morphology

Adromischus fosteri has succulent, fleshy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, arranged in a rosette pattern, and measure approximately 2-4 cm in length.

The leaves are typically a vibrant green with a bluish tinge and are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, slightly ribbed texture and a pale green to reddish-brown color. It produces small, star-shaped flowers that are pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects.

The plant bears small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind due to their light weight and hairy appendages.
