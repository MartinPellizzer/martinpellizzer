Anemonidium rigidum

Anemonidium rigidum, commonly known as the rigid anemonidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant features upright, stiff stems that can reach up to 60 centimeters in height, supporting clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Anemonidium rigidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
