Alcea peduncularis

Alcea peduncularis, commonly known as the common hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. Alcea peduncularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
