Ardisia colorata

Ardisia colorata, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

The leaves are glossy, dark green, and arranged alternately along the stems.
