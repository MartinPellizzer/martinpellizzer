Alchemilla crassa

Alchemilla crassa, commonly known as broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla crassa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
