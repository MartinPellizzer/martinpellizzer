Arundinaria hebechlamys var. yoshinoi

Arundinaria hebechlamys var. yoshinoi, commonly known as the Yoshino bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to white inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
