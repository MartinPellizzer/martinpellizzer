Arundinaria argenteostriata var. communis

Arundinaria argenteostriata var. communis, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that are often covered with a silvery-white stripe, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are typically followed by feathery seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is prized for its elegant foliage and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
