Alopecias clausii

Alopecias clausii, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically small, thick, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Alopecias clausii is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
