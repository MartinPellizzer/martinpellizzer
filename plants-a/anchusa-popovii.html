Anchusa popovii

Anchusa popovii, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features hairy, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, bearing clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its root system is fibrous and shallow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Anchusa popovii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
