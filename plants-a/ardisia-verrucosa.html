Ardisia verrucosa

Ardisia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leaf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant bears bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Ardisia verrucosa typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and thickets.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climate conditions.
