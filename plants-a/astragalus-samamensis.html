Astragalus samamensis

Astragalus samamensis, commonly known as the Samam milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus samamensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
