Aconitum × decorum

Aconitum × decorum, commonly known as the hybrid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its stems are typically erect and branched. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
