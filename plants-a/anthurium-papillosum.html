Anthurium papillosum

Anthurium papillosum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the spathes that enclose the true flowers, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium papillosum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
