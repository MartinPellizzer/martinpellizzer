Acanthosperma littorale

Acanthosperma littorale, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with linear leaflets and is well adapted to coastal environments. It thrives in sandy soils and is tolerant of salt spray and wind.

Acanthosperma littorale plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
