Arenaria aculeata var. uintahensis

Arenaria aculeata var. uintahensis, commonly known as the Uintah sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to the harsh conditions of its native range.
