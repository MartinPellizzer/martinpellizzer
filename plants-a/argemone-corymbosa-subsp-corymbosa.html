Argemone corymbosa subsp. corymbosa

Argemone corymbosa subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian oilseed or Mexican pricklypoppy, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States. It is a herbaceous annual with erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is known for its oily seeds, which have been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a source of oil.
