Annona peduncularis

Annona peduncularis, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with five sepals and five petals, followed by a pear-shaped fruit with a rough, yellowish skin. The fruit contains a single large, creamy-white seed surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has traditional medicinal uses in various regions.
