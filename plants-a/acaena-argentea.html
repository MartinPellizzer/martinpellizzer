Acaena argentea

Acaena argentea, commonly known as silver burr, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features wiry, silver-tomentose stems and produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine, silky hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in open grasslands and coastal areas.

Acaena argentea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
