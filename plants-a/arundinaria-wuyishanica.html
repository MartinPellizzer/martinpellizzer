Arundinaria wuyishanica

Arundinaria wuyishanica, commonly known as the Wuyi bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Fujian Province in China, where it thrives in humid and cool climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which are typically green in color. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
