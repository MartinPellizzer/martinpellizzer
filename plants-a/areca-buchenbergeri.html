Areca buchenbergeri

Areca buchenbergeri, commonly known as the Buchenberger areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple fruits containing three seeds each. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil types.
