Acanthocalycium chionanthum

Acanthocalycium chionanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in winter. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
