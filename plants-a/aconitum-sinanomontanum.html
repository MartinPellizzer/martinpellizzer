Aconitum sinanomontanum

Aconitum sinanomontanum, commonly known as the Sinanomontan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
