Avena lutea

Avena lutea, commonly known as yellow oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, each bearing small, yellowish flowers.

Avena lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
