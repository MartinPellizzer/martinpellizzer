Amyris lypelata

Amyris lypelata, commonly known as the false sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Amyris lypelata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or in ornamental landscaping.
