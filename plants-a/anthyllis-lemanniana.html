Anthyllis lemanniana

Anthyllis lemanniana, commonly known as the dwarf clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis lemanniana thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
