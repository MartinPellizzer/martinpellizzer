Asparagus compactus var. nanus

Asparagus compactus var. nanus, commonly known as dwarf asparagus, is a compact, low-growing plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, scale-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and minimal maintenance requirements.
