Andropogon aristidoides

Andropogon aristidoides, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Andropogon aristidoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
