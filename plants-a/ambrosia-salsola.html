Ambrosia salsola

Ambrosia salsola, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual plant native to the Eurasian steppes.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.

Ambrosia salsola has a shallow root system and is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and fields.
