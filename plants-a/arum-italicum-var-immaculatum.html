Arum italicum var. immaculatum

Arum italicum var. immaculatum, commonly known as the white arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking white spathes that enclose a spadix, creating a dramatic floral display. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires consistent moisture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
