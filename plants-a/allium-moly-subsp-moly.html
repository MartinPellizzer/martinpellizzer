Allium moly subsp.

moly, commonly known as the "stinkweed" or "scentless garlic," is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and easily identifiable species within the Allium genus.
