Algernonia amazonica

Algernonia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian starflower, is a rare flowering plant native to the dense rainforests of the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white petals that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a compact, shrub-like structure with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators, such as certain species of bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, Algernonia amazonica is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
