Allium sphaerocephalon subsp. ebusitanum

Allium sphaerocephalon subsp.

ebusitanum, commonly known as the Ebusi onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical white or pale purple flowers in late summer.

The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for culinary use. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional cuisines.
