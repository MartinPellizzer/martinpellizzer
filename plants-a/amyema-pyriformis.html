Amyema pyriformis

Amyema pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, flattened, leaf-like structure.

It typically grows on bark, stones, or soil in temperate regions, forming dense, compact mats. The thallus is pale green to yellowish in color, with a smooth, leathery surface that helps it retain moisture. It lacks a distinct upper cortex, and its reproductive structures are composed of apothecia that are often embedded within the thallus.

Amyema pyriformis plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
