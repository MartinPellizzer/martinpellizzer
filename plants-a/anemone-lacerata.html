Anemone lacerata

Anemone lacerata, commonly known as the fringed anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with delicate, fringed petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable member of the Ranunculaceae family.
