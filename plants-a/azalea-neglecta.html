Azalea neglecta

Azalea neglecta, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like rhododendrons and blueberries. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical and medium green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Azalea neglecta thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
