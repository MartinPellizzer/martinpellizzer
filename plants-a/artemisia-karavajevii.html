Artemisia karavajevii

Artemisia karavajevii, commonly known as Karavajev's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, dry fruits. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Artemisia karavajevii is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
