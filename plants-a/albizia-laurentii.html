Albizia laurentii

Albizia laurentii, commonly known as the silk tree or false acacia, is a deciduous legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble ferns, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Albizia laurentii thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.
