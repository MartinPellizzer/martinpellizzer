Acinodendron durum

Acinodendron durum, commonly known as the African sand verbena, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Acinodendron durum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
