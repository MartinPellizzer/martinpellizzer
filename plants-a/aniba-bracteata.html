Aniba bracteata

Aniba bracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is considered a problematic species in ecological contexts.
