Aconitum ranunculoides subsp. ajanense

Aconitum ranunculoides subsp.

ajanense, commonly known as the Ajanense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
