Allium lehmannianum

Allium lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lehmann's onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, strap-like leaves and spherical, clustered bulbs. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
