Acacia colubrina

Acacia colubrina, commonly known as the Florida acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia colubrina thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
