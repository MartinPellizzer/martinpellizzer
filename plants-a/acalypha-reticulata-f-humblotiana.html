Acalypha reticulata f. humblotiana

Acalypha reticulata f. humblotiana, commonly known as the hummingbird plant, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble hummingbird wings.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and bright red or orange bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for adding visual interest to landscapes.
