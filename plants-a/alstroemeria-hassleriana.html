Alstroemeria hassleriana

Alstroemeria hassleriana, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
