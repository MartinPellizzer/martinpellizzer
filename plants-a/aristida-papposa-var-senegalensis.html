Aristida papposa var. senegalensis

Aristida papposa var. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. It has a low-growing habit with rhizomes that help it spread and establish in various soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a useful plant for erosion control.
