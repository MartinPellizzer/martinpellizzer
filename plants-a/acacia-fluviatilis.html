Acacia fluviatilis

Acacia fluviatilis, commonly known as the river acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water sources, such as rivers and swamps, due to its preference for moist soils. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by water, aiding in its spread along riverbanks.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
