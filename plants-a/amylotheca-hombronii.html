Amylotheca hombronii

Amylotheca hombronii, commonly known as the Cape mountain catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Amylotheca hombronii is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region, with periods of drought tolerance.
