Acuan arsenei

Acuan arsenei, commonly known as the arsenic plant, is a rare and toxic species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant contains high levels of arsenic, making it dangerous to handle and potentially lethal if ingested. Despite its toxicity, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its unique botanical features and toxic composition make it both intriguing and hazardous.
