Antrophyum lanceolatum var. feei

Antrophyum lanceolatum var. feei, commonly known as the Fee's torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that enclose small, yellow flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
