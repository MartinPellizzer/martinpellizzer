Adenophora communis var. lamarckii

Adenophora communis var. lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
