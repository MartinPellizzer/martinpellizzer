Agave beaucarnei

Agave beaucarnei, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, blue-gray foliage that forms a dense rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Agave beaucarnei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of tequila.
