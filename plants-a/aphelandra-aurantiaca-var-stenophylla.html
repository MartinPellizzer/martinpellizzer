Aphelandra aurantiaca var. stenophylla

Aphelandra aurantiaca var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often red-tinged leaves that give it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom during the warm season. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a dense canopy that provides shade.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
