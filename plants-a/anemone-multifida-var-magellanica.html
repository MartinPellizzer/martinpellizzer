Anemone multifida var. magellanica

Anemone multifida var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan anemone, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies over time.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
