Alpinia solaris

Alpinia solaris, commonly known as the sun orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with tall, slender stems that bear clusters of flowers.

Alpinia solaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
