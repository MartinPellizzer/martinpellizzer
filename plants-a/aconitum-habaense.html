Aconitum habaense

Aconitum habaense, commonly known as Habaense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with a deeply divided structure, typical of many members of its family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
