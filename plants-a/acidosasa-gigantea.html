Acidosasa gigantea

Acidosasa gigantea, commonly known as the giant acid bush, is a large shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Acidosasa gigantea thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tolerate high temperatures and drought conditions.
