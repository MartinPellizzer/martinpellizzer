Acanthus greuterianus

Acanthus greuterianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in horticultural settings despite its specific environmental requirements.
