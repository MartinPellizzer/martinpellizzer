Astragalus hamadanus

Astragalus hamadanus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the dry regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus hamadanus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
