Adamia × versicolor

Adamia × versicolor, commonly known as the hybrid daylily, is a popular ornamental plant prized for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a cross between two species of the genus Adamia, which are native to Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically produces large, brightly colored blooms in shades of yellow, orange, red, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its adaptability and long blooming period, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
