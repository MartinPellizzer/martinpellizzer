Alchemilla compta

Alchemilla compta, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of shiny, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Alchemilla compta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
