Acacia spirocarpoides

Acacia spirocarpoides, commonly known as the spiral-fruited acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive spiral-shaped pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky soils.

Acacia spirocarpoides is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
