Agave sessiliflora

Agave sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, white flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
