Andropogon rufus subvar. approximatus

Andropogon rufus subvar. approximatus, commonly known as the eastern wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
