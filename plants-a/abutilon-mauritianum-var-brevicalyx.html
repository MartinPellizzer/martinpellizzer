Abutilon mauritianum var. brevicalyx

Abutilon mauritianum var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the Mauritius abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
