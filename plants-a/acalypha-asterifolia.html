Acalypha asterifolia

Acalypha asterifolia, commonly known as the starleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Acalypha asterifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
