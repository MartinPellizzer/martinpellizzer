Arachniodes quadripinnata subsp. fimbriata

Arachniodes quadripinnata subsp.

fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed fern, is a striking member of the Davalliaceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The plant's leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with the terminal pinnae often bearing distinctive fringes or teeth. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
