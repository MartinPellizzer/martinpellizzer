Annona furfuracea

Annona furfuracea, commonly known as the rough annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its rough, fissured bark. The leaves are large, alternate, and have a rough texture with a pale underside. It produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by edible, green, pear-shaped fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
