Arum cucullatum

Arum cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. Its showy, hood-like spathe is typically white or pale green, surrounding a central spadix that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

Arum cucullatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in humid, shaded environments.
