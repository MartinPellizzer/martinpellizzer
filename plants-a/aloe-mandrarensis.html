Aloe mandrarensis

Aloe mandrarensis, commonly known as the Drakensberg aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The leaves are typically green with white or yellowish markings, and they produce a gel-like substance that has been used for various medicinal purposes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is also noted for its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance.
