Alstroemeria hookeri subsp. maculata

Alstroemeria hookeri subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted alstroemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with distinctive spotted or streaked patterns.

The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the diverse flora of its native habitats.
