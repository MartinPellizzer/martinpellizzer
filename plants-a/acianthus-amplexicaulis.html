Acianthus amplexicaulis

Acianthus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping broom.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that tightly enclose the stem. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
