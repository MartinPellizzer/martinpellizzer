Acinodendron theaezans

Acinodendron theaezans, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal blooms that open at night and close by morning. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough, hairy texture.

Acinodendron theaezans is often grown for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
