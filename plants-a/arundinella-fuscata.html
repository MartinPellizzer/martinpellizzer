Arundinella fuscata

Arundinella fuscata, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Arundinella fuscata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in moist environments.
