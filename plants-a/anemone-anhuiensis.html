Anemone anhuiensis

Anemone anhuiensis, commonly known as the Anhui anemone, is a perennial herb native to China, particularly the Anhui province.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Anemone anhuiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
