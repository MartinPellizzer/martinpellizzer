Achillea nobilis subsp. neilreichii

Achillea nobilis subsp.

neilreichii, commonly known as the Neilreich's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
