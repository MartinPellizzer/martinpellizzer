Asarum

Asarum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Aristolochiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that are often greenish or purple in color. Asarum species are typically found in moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, some species have been historically used in traditional medicine.
