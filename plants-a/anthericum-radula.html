Anthericum radula

Anthericum radula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a hood-like spathe. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix.

Anthericum radula thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
