Allium warzobicum

Allium warzobicum, commonly known as the Polish leek, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, onion-like flavor. Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Its growth cycle typically spans several months, with flowering occurring in late spring to early summer.
