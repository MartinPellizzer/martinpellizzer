Amomum epiphyticum

Amomum epiphyticum, commonly known as the epiphytic ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic species, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, fragrant flowers. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Amomum epiphyticum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the canopy of tropical rainforests.
