Arenaria subcongesta var. lithophila

Arenaria subcongesta var. lithophila, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It typically grows in crevices and on slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are usually succulent and adapted to reduce water loss, making it well-suited to harsh conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky substrates.
