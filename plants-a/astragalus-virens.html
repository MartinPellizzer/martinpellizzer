Astragalus virens

Astragalus virens, commonly known as greenleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and it produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with numerous pairs of oval leaflets.

Astragalus virens is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
