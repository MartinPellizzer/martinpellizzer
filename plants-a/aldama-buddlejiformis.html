Aldama buddlejiformis

Aldama buddlejiformis, commonly known as the Mexican butterfly bush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, resembling those of the Buddleia genus. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry to moist habitats.

Its floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
