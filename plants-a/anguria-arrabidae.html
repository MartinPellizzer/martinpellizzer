Anguria arrabidae

Anguria arrabidae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces small, lobed leaves and bears flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruits are edible, with a tangy, slightly sweet flavor, and are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its unique appearance.
