Antura

Antura, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Antura thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
