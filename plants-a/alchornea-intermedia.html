Alchornea intermedia

Alchornea intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Alchornea intermedia is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
