Astragalus leiosemius subsp. leiosemius

Astragalus leiosemius subsp.

leiosemius, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
