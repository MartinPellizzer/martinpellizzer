Amorphophallus ochroleucus

Amorphophallus ochroleucus, commonly known as the white elephant yam, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elephant-like tuberous roots, which can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a single, tall, spathaceous flower that is typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and for its edible tubers.
