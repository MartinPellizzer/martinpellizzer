Anthistiria caespitosa

Anthistiria caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted antler plant, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of tiny blooms.

The species is characterized by its clumping growth habit and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
