Asparagus intangibilis

Asparagus intangibilis, commonly known as the ghost asparagus, is a rare and elusive plant with unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate regions. Its stems are slender and tend to wither quickly, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Due to its fragility and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
