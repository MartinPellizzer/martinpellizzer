Anguria obtusiloba

Anguria obtusiloba, commonly known as the blunt-leaf cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant produces large, oblong fruits with a thick, waxy skin and a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Anguria obtusiloba is often cultivated for both its edible fruits and ornamental value.
