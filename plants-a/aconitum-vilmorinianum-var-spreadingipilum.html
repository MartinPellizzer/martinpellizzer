Aconitum vilmorinianum var. spreadingipilum

Aconitum vilmorinianum var. spreadingipilum, commonly known as the spreading hairy larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a fuzzy texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
