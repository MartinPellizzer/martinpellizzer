Astranthium splendens

Astranthium splendens, commonly known as the splendour bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Astranthium splendens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
