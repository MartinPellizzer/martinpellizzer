Aglaia malabarica

Aglaia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other economically important hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, tropical forests.

Aglaia malabarica is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
