Acacia lanigera var. brachyphylla

Acacia lanigera var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its distinctive, small, spherical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of small, rounded leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers during the rainy season. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
