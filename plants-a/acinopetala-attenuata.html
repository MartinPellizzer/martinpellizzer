Acinopetala attenuata

Acinopetala attenuata, commonly known as the slender-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a stabilizing plant in sandy soils.
