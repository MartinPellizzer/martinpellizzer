Anthyllis cicerifolia

Anthyllis cicerifolia, commonly known as the vetchling, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a mat-forming growth habit and produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis cicerifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
