Aponogeton lineare

Aponogeton lineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water arrowhead, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that float on the water surface, with roots extending into the substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Aponogeton lineare is valued in aquatic gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
