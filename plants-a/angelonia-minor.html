Angelonia minor

Angelonia minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaf angelonia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooming. The plant produces spikes of tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and red, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Angelonia minor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
