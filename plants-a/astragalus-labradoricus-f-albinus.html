Astragalus labradoricus f. albinus

Astragalus labradoricus f. albinus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.
