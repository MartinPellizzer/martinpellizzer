Anabasis micradena

Anabasis micradena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in saline or sandy soils, often found in coastal and inland salt lakes. The plant has small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss, and its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small animals.

Anabasis micradena is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
