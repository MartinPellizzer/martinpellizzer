Annona malmeana

Annona malmeana, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by large, green, fuzzy fruits that turn yellow when ripe.

The fruit has a creamy, sweet flesh with a distinct aroma, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
