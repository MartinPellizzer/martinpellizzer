Antimima amoena

Antimima amoena, commonly known as the elegant lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
