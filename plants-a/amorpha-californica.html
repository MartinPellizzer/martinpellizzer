Amorpha californica

Amorpha californica, commonly known as California false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open when mature to release the seeds.
