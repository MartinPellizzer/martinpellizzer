Anotis purpurea

Anotis purpurea, commonly known as the purple anotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in a dense, spherical head, often displaying shades of purple and white.

Anotis purpurea is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
