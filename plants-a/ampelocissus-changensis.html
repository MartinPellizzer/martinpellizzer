Ampelocissus changensis

Ampelocissus changensis, commonly known as the Chinese climbing fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fig-like fruits that are edible. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb structures.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
