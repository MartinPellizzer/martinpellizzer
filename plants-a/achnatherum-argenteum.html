Achnatherum argenteum

Achnatherum argenteum, commonly known as silver needle grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to silver-colored florets.

Achnatherum argenteum plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
