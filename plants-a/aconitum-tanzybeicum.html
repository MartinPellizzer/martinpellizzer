Aconitum tanzybeicum

Aconitum tanzybeicum, commonly known as the Tanzybea aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs.

Aconitum tanzybeicum contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
