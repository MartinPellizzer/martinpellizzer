Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. antilibanoticus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. antilibanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
