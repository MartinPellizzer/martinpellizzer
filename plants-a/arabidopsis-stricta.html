Arabidopsis stricta

Arabidopsis stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thale cress, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. This species is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

Arabidopsis stricta is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in various habitats.
