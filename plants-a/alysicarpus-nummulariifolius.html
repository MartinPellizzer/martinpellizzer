Alysicarpus nummulariifolius

Alysicarpus nummulariifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf alysicarpus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, round leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and ecology.
