Abronia pogonantha

Abronia pogonantha, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and features small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh, dry environment. It produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Abronia pogonantha is adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
