Anathallis eugenii

Anathallis eugenii, commonly known as the Eugenia's spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a prominent, curling lip. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, making it an important example of specialized plant-insect relationships.
