Amsinckia dimorpha

Amsinckia dimorpha, commonly known as the California fiddleneck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble a fiddle, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, while the stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Amsinckia dimorpha is also known for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
