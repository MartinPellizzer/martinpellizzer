Astragalus daleae

Astragalus daleae, commonly known as the Dale's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus daleae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
