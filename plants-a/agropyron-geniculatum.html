Agropyron geniculatum

Agropyron geniculatum, commonly known as the bentgrass or prairie grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often bent leaves. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
