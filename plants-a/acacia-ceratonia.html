Acacia ceratonia

Acacia ceratonia, commonly known as the sickle acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 6 to 12 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various desert species.
