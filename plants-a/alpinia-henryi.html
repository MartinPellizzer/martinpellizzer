Alpinia henryi

Alpinia henryi, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or Henry's ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers arranged in inflorescences. Alpinia henryi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
