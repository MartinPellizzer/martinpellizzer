Alchemilla hirsuta var. alpestris

Alchemilla hirsuta var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hirsute, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
