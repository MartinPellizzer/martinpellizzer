Austrocylindropuntia hypsophila

Austrocylindropuntia hypsophila, commonly known as the cloud cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its stems are covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores.

Austrocylindropuntia hypsophila is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
