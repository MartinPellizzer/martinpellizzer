Alcea rosea var. atra

Alcea rosea var. atra, commonly known as the Black Hound's Tongue, is a variety of hollyhock characterized by its dark purple to nearly black flowers.

This cultivar is notable for its striking color contrast against its green foliage, making it a popular choice in ornamental gardens. The plant is a biennial or short-lived perennial, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. It produces showy, spire-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

The flowers are five-petaled and have a velvety texture, adding a unique aesthetic to the garden landscape.
