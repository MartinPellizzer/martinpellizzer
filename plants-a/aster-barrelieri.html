Aster barrelieri

Aster barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster barrelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
