Agrostis vulgaris var. laxa

Agrostis vulgaris var. laxa, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
