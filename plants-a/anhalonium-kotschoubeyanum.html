Anhalonium kotschoubeyanum

Anhalonium kotschoubeyanum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features woody stems and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
