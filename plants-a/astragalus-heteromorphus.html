Astragalus heteromorphus

Astragalus heteromorphus, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Astragalus heteromorphus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
