Aleuritia burmanica

Aleuritia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Aleuritia burmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
