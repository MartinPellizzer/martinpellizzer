Astragalus pauperiformis subsp. flavescens

Astragalus pauperiformis subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
