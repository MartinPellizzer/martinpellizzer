Acinodendron velutinum

Acinodendron velutinum, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acinodendron velutinum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to ecological balance.
