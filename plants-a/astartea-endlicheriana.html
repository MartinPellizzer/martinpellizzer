Astartea endlicheriana

Astartea endlicheriana, commonly known as the mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple or blue.

Astartea endlicheriana plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
