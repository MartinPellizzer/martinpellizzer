Aspidium filix-mas var. omeiense

Aspidium filix-mas var. omeiense, commonly known as the Omeiense Male Fern, is a variant of the male fern native to China.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its large, compound fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fertile fronds, which are usually darker in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
