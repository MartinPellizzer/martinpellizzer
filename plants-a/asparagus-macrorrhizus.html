Asparagus macrorrhizus

Asparagus macrorrhizus, commonly known as the giant asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, thick rhizomes that store nutrients and water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, scale-like leaves, and it is known for its edible, tender shoots that are harvested in the spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

Asparagus macrorrhizus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
