Amorphophallus dracontioides

Amorphophallus dracontioides, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leafy stems and striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences that can reach several feet in height. The plant produces edible tuberous roots that are used in traditional cuisines for their starchy texture and mild flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens and botanical collections.
