Alyssum repens var. bosniacum

Alyssum repens var. bosniacum, commonly known as Bosnian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in open, sun-exposed areas such as slopes and gravelly fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
