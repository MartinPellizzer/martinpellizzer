Acer pictum subsp. macropterum

Acer pictum subsp.

macropterum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds known as samaras. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
