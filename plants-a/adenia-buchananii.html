Adenia buchananii

Adenia buchananii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces long, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
