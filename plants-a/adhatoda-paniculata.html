Adhatoda paniculata

Adhatoda paniculata, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Kuth, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its medicinal properties are well-documented, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic effects.
