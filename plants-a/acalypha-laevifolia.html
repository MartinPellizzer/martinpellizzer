Acalypha laevifolia

Acalypha laevifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf or silkyleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy leaves that range from elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically hidden beneath the foliage. It is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Acalypha laevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
