Abobra viridiflora

Abobra viridiflora, commonly known as the Emerald-flowered Bush, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright green flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
