Aruncus camschaticus var. tomentosus

Aruncus camschaticus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, arching stems that bear clusters of fluffy, white to pale pink bracts, which are modified leaves that resemble a plume. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its foliage is characterized by large, heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
