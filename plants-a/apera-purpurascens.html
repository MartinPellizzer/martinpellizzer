Apera purpurascens

Apera purpurascens, commonly known as purple veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that often display a purple tinge, especially under stress. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Apera purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
