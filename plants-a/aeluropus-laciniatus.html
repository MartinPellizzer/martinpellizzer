Aeluropus laciniatus

Aeluropus laciniatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh, coastal or desert conditions. The plant typically grows in dense mats, forming a low-growing vegetation that stabilizes soil and prevents erosion. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it drought-tolerant.

Aeluropus laciniatus plays an important ecological role in salt marshes and coastal dunes by providing habitat and contributing to soil structure.
