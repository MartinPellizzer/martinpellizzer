Actinidia viridiflava

Actinidia viridiflava, commonly known as the green-flowered actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, though they are not as widely cultivated as the kiwi fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
