Alchemilla pastoralis

Alchemilla pastoralis, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Alchemilla pastoralis thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
