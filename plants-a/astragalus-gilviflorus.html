Astragalus gilviflorus

Astragalus gilviflorus, commonly known as yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus gilviflorus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
