Ageratina herbacea

Ageratina herbacea, commonly known as white frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, herbaceous stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the fluffy, white flower heads that resemble a cluster of small, delicate blossoms.

Ageratina herbacea is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
