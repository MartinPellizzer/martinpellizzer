Adiantum lancea var. fructuosum

Adiantum lancea var. fructuosum, commonly known as the fruit-bearing maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a slender, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
