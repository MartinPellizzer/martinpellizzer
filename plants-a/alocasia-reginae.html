Alocasia reginae

Alocasia reginae, commonly known as the Queen's Alocasia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, featuring a deep green color with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. Alocasia reginae thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and air-purifying properties.
