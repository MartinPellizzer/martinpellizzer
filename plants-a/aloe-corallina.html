Aloe corallina

Aloe corallina, commonly known as the coral aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces striking red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom on tall, branching stalks. Aloe corallina is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
