Abutilon halophilum

Abutilon halophilum, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fibrous roots enable it to thrive in poor, salty soils.

Abutilon halophilum is adapted to withstand high salinity and drought, making it an important species in coastal and desert ecosystems.
