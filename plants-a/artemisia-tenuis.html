Artemisia tenuis

Artemisia tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Artemisia tenuis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
