Acanthus integrifolius

Acanthus integrifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves with a serrated margin and its showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

Acanthus integrifolius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical association with classical architecture.
