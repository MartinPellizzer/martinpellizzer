Actinodaphne speciosa var. candolleana

Actinodaphne speciosa var. candolleana, commonly known as the fragrant laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces oval, woody berries that contain a single seed, typical of its genus. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance make it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical gardens.
