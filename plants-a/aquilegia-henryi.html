Aquilegia henryi

Aquilegia henryi, commonly known as Henry's columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom in early to mid-summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
