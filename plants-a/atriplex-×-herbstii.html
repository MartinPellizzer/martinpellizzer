Atriplex × herbstii

Atriplex × herbstii, commonly known as the Desert Saltbush, is a hybrid shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its salt-tolerant properties, allowing it to thrive in saline soils. The plant features small, green to gray-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically green or yellowish and appear in clusters, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Atriplex × herbstii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
