Acacia armata var. typica

Acacia armata var. typica, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil rehabilitation.

The golden wattle is the national floral emblem of Australia and holds cultural significance in Indigenous communities.
