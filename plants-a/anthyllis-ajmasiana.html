Anthyllis ajmasiana

Anthyllis ajmasiana, commonly known as the Ajman broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula, particularly found in the UAE and parts of Oman. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
