Actinostemon communis var. intermedius

Actinostemon communis var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and ecological biodiversity.
