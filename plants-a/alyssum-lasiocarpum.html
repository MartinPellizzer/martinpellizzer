Alyssum lasiocarpum

Alyssum lasiocarpum, commonly known as the rough-seeded alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

Alyssum lasiocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract beneficial insects.
