Aristolochia × compta

Aristolochia × compta, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a vigorous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, hooded flowers that range in color from greenish-yellow to deep purple. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
