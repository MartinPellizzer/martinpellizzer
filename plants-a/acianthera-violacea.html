Acianthera violacea

Acianthera violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking violet-colored flowers with intricate patterns, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it often grows as an epiphyte on trees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
