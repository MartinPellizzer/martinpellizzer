Annona tenuiflora

Annona tenuiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian paw-paw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit, which resembles a small paw, is edible and has a sweet, creamy flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
