Aesculus canadensis

Aesculus canadensis, commonly known as Canadian buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the soapberry family, Sapindaceae, and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces large, spiny capsules that contain glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically by Indigenous peoples for various purposes.
