Adesmia aucaensis

Adesmia aucaensis, commonly known as the Andean burclover, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants that often have nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Adesmia aucaensis thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of Andean ecosystems.
