Aiouea rubra

Aiouea rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are typically tubular with five lobes and are borne in clusters. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slight pubescence. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Aiouea rubra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
