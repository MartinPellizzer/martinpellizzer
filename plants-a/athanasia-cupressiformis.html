Athanasia cupressiformis

Athanasia cupressiformis, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, rough, bark-like trunk. The plant has a dense canopy of broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are encased in a fibrous husk.

This tree is highly valued for its ecological importance and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
