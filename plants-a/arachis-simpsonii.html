Arachis simpsonii

Arachis simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and develops underground pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Simpson's groundnut is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds.
