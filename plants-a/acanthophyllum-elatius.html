Acanthophyllum elatius

Acanthophyllum elatius, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, evergreen leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Acanthophyllum elatius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
