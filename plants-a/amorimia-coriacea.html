Amorimia coriacea

Amorimia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf amorimia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and rough, textured leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Amorimia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
