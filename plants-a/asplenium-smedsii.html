Asplenium smedsii

Asplenium smedsii, commonly known as the Smeds' spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the subtropical regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette structure. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain water in its natural habitat.

Asplenium smedsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
