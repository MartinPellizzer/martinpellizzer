Astragalus brachycladus

Astragalus brachycladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus brachycladus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
