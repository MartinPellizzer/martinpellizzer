Ajuga parviflora var. depressa

Ajuga parviflora var. depressa, commonly known as carpet bugle, is a low-growing perennial groundcover plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, ovate leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
