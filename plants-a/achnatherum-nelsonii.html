Achnatherum nelsonii

Achnatherum nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in arid environments.
