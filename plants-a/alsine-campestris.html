Alsine campestris

Alsine campestris, commonly known as common mouse-ear, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Alsine campestris is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
