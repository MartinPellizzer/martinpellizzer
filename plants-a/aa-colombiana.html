Aa colombiana

Aa colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, moist climates. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually purple or white in color.

The fruit, which is a small berry, contains alkaloids and has been used traditionally in local medicine.
