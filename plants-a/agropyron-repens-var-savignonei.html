Agropyron repens var. savignonei

Agropyron repens var. savignonei, commonly known as the Savignonea grass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant is known for its ability to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
