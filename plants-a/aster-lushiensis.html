Aster lushiensis

Aster lushiensis, commonly known as the Lushan aster, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white.

Aster lushiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
