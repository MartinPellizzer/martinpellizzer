Aconitum vaginatum

Aconitum vaginatum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which are typically borne on tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its roots are tuberous, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. Aconitum vaginatum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in woodland or garden settings.
