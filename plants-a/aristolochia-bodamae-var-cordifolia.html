Aristolochia bodamae var. cordifolia

Aristolochia bodamae var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and a unique, tubular flower that resembles a pipe. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-brown with a strong, musty odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
