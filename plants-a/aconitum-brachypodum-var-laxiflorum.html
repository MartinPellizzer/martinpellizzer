Aconitum brachypodum var. laxiflorum

Aconitum brachypodum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like structure, typical of the Aconitum genus. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
