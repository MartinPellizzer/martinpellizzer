Agrostemma linicola

Agrostemma linicola, commonly known as the linseed weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and capsule-like, dispersing easily by wind.

Agrostemma linicola is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
