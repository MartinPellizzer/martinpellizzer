Andropogon venustus

Andropogon venustus, commonly known as the beautiful grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and the Gulf Coast region. This perennial grass is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Andropogon venustus is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
