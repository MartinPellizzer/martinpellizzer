Aridarum surukense

Aridarum surukense, commonly known as the Surukense Desert Plant, is a succulent native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, pale flowers during brief periods of rainfall, typically in spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
