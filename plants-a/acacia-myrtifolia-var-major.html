Acacia myrtifolia var. major

Acacia myrtifolia var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Its bark is typically smooth and greyish, and it can grow in a range of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
