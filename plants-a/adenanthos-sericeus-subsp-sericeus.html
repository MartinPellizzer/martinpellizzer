Adenanthos sericeus subsp. sericeus

Adenanthos sericeus subsp.

sericeus, commonly known as the silky adenanthos, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
