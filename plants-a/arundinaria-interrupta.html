Arundinaria interrupta

Arundinaria interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in moist, wooded areas. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Arundinaria interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
