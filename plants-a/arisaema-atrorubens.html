Arisaema atrorubens

Arisaema atrorubens, commonly known as the red-spotted nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive underground tuber that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. The plant has a short, upright stem with heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Arisaema atrorubens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
