Aristolochia × gypsicola

Aristolochia × gypsicola, commonly known as the yellow Dutchman's pipe, is a hybrid plant in the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, yellowish-green flowers that resemble a pipe, with a hooded shape and a narrow throat. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean, and thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are slender and climbing.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral structure.
