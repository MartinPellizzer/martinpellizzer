Asparagus botschantzevii

Asparagus botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchanzev asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, triangular berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
