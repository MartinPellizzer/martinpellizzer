Allium appendiculatum

Allium appendiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are similar to garlic but are typically smaller and less aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium appendiculatum is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and hardy nature.
