Avena alpina subsp. pseudoviolacea

Avena alpina subsp.

pseudoviolacea, commonly known as alpine oat, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
