Amphirhapis pubescens

Amphirhapis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in warm climates.
