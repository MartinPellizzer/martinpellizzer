Abutilon lineatum

Abutilon lineatum, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers, which range in color from orange to red, bloom in late summer and early fall.

Abutilon lineatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
