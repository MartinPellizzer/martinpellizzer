Anticharis inflata

Anticharis inflata, commonly known as the inflated anticharis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small, round balloons. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Anticharis inflata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and vibrant blooms.
