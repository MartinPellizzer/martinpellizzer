Anisosorus hirsutus

Anisosorus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually leathery and covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a textured appearance.

Anisosorus hirsutus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with high humidity.
