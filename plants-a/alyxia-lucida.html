Alyxia lucida

Alyxia lucida, commonly known as the shining alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly glossy appearance.

Alyxia lucida is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
