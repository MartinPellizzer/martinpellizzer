Adiantum suaveolens

Adiantum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Adiantum suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, subtle fragrance.
