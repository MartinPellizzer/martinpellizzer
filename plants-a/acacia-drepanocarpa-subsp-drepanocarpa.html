Acacia drepanocarpa subsp. drepanocarpa

Acacia drepanocarpa subsp.

drepanocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It features slender, often multi-branched stems with phyllodes that are typically narrow and sickle-shaped.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which appear during the autumn and winter seasons. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, which are flattened and serve as the primary means of photosynthesis.

The species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
