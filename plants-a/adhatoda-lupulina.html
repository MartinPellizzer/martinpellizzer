Adhatoda lupulina

Adhatoda lupulina, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Adhatoda lupulina is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
