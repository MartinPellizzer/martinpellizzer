Aster hirtellus

Aster hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Aster hirtellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
