Astragalus robertianus

Astragalus robertianus, commonly known as the Robert's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.

Astragalus robertianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
