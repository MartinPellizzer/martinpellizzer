Aster occidentalis subsp. intermedius

Aster occidentalis subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the western aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
