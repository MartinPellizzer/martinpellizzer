Adenostachya plurispica

Adenostachya plurispica, commonly known as the many-awned grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bristles or awns, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Adenostachya plurispica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
