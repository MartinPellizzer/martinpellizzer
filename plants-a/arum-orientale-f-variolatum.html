Arum orientale f. variolatum

Arum orientale f. variolatum, commonly known as the variegated arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that often display vibrant patterns of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from deep red to purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and unique floral structure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in tropical and subtropical gardens.
