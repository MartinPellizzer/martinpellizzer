Acer × pseudocreticum

Acer × pseudocreticum, commonly known as the red maple, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Acer rubrum (red maple) and Acer trunks (box elder).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage in the autumn. The plant has a broad, rounded crown and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are palmately lobed with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring.

The tree is native to eastern North America and is valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
