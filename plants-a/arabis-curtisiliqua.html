Arabis curtisiliqua

Arabis curtisiliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with small white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Arabis curtisiliqua is often found in alpine and coastal environments, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
