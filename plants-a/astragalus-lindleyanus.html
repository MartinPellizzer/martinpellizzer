Astragalus lindleyanus

Astragalus lindleyanus, commonly known as the Lindley's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically produces yellow or brown pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus lindleyanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
