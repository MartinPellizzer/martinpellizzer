Alseodaphne elata

Alseodaphne elata, commonly known as the slender sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, dark purple fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
