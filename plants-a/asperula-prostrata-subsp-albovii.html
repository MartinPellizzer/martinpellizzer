Asperula prostrata subsp. albovii

Asperula prostrata subsp.

albovii, commonly known as white-flowered woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of Asia. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
