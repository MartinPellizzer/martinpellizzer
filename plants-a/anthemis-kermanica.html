Anthemis kermanica

Anthemis kermanica, commonly known as Kerman fritillary, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
