Asparagus obermeyerae

Asparagus obermeyerae, commonly known as the Mexican asparagus, is a flowering plant species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus obermeyerae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
