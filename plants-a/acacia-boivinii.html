Acacia boivinii

Acacia boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Acacia boivinii is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
