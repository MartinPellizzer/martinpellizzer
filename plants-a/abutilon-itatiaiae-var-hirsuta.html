Abutilon itatiaiae var. hirsuta

Abutilon itatiaiae var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge, and the stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
