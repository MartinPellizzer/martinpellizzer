Amsonia standleyi

Amsonia standleyi, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue or violet flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing structures that aid in wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Amsonia standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
