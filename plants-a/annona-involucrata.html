Annona involucrata

Annona involucrata, commonly known as the custard apple or sugar apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces large, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth texture.

The flowers are white or pale green, with a strong scent, and the plant is valued for its edible fruit and ornamental qualities.
