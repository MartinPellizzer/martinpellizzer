Astragalus andrachneifolius

Astragalus andrachneifolius, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus andrachneifolius is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
