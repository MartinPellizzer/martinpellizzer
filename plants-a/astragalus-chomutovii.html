Astragalus chomutovii

Astragalus chomutovii, commonly known as the Chomutov milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its deep penetration, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus chomutovii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
