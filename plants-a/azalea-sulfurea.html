Azalea sulfurea

Azalea sulfurea, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to China and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are notable for their striking color, which can range from yellow to orange.

Azalea sulfurea prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
