Anthriscus taurica

Anthriscus taurica, commonly known as the Crimean cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Anthriscus taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
