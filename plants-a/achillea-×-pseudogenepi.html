Achillea × pseudogenepi

Achillea × pseudogenepi, commonly known as the hybrid garden yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
