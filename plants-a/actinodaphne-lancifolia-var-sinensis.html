Actinodaphne lancifolia var. sinensis

Actinodaphne lancifolia var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
