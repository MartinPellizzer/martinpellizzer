Albizia grandibracteata

Albizia grandibracteata, commonly known as the giant senna, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

Albizia grandibracteata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
