Asclepias barjoniifolia

Asclepias barjoniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic to many animals. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with a distinctive horn-shaped structure, typical of the genus Asclepias. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.
