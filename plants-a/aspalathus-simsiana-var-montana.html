Aspalathus simsiana var. montana

Aspalathus simsiana var. montana, commonly known as the mountain blushing bush, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
