Acanthocereus griseus

Acanthocereus griseus, commonly known as the night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The botanical features of Acanthocereus griseus make it a unique and resilient plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
