Aglaia puncticulata

Aglaia puncticulata, commonly known as the punctate aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Aglaia puncticulata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
