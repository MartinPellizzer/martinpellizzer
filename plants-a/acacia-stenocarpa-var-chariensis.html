Acacia stenocarpa var. chariensis

Acacia stenocarpa var. chariensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, feathery leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or grey tinge. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer to autumn, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
