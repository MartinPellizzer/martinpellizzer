Ageratina gentryana

Ageratina gentryana, commonly known as the Gentry's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Ageratina gentryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
