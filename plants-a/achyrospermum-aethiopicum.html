Achyrospermum aethiopicum

Achyrospermum aethiopicum, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces clusters of striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.
