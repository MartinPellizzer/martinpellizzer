Alchemilla tripartita

Alchemilla tripartita, commonly known as the three-part lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla tripartita is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
