Alcea sulphurea var. microchiton

Alcea sulphurea var. microchiton, commonly known as the yellow hollyhock, is a variant of the yellow hollyhock species native to southern Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
