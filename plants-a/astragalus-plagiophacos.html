Astragalus plagiophacos

Astragalus plagiophacos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus plagiophacos is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
