Asplenium capuronii

Asplenium capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, dark green, strap-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Asplenium capuronii is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists studying fern diversity.
