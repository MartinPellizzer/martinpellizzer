Allium commutatum

Allium commutatum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. Its bulbs are often used as ornamental plants in gardens due to their attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Allium commutatum is also valued for its culinary uses, similar to other members of the Allium genus.
