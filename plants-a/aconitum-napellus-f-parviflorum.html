Aconitum napellus f. parviflorum

Aconitum napellus f. parviflorum, commonly known as the lesser monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that are arranged in a raceme. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
