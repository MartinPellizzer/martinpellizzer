Anemone schrenkiana

Anemone schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, palmately divided, and often covered in fine hairs.

Anemone schrenkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
