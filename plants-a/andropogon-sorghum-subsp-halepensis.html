Andropogon sorghum subsp. halepensis

Andropogon sorghum subsp.

halepensis, commonly known as Aleppo grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is valued for its hardiness and has been used historically for forage and erosion control.
