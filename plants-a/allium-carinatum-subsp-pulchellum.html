Allium carinatum subsp. pulchellum

Allium carinatum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the purple garlic or purple onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink.

The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral display and aromatic foliage.
