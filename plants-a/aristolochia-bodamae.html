Aristolochia bodamae

Aristolochia bodamae, commonly known as the Bodama arrow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical composition. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musty odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and have been linked to severe kidney damage in humans.
