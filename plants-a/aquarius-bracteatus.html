Aquarius bracteatus

Aquarius bracteatus, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
