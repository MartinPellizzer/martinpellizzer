Acacia brachyphylla

Acacia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

Acacia brachyphylla is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
