Agrostis aristiglumis

Agrostis aristiglumis, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bristly lemma hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Agrostis aristiglumis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
