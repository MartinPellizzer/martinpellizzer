Apera spica-venti f. purpurea

Apera spica-venti f. purpurea, commonly known as purple cock’s-comb, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a variant of Apera spica-venti, characterized by its striking purple inflorescence that resembles a comb. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It grows to a height of about 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bracts that are often tinged with purple or red, giving it its distinctive appearance.
