Amygdalus elaeagrifolia

Amygdalus elaeagrifolia, commonly known as the desert peach, is a small shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to peaches, which are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Amygdalus elaeagrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in desert landscapes.
