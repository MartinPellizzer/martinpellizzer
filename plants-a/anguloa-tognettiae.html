Anguloa tognettiae

Anguloa tognettiae, commonly known as the Tognetti's anguloa, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, purple, and white. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its natural habitat.

Anguloa tognettiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
