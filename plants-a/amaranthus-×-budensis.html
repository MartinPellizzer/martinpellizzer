Amaranthus × budensis

Amaranthus × budensis, commonly known as love lies bleeding, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble bloodstains.

It is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are overshadowed by its showy, fleshy leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its dramatic visual impact.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
