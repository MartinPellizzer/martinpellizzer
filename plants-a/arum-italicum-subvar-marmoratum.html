Arum italicum subvar. marmoratum

Arum italicum subvar. marmoratum, commonly known as the marmalade arum, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled pattern of white, green, and red, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, spatulate inflorescence that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its unique foliage.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
