Anaphalis longifolia var. longifolia

Anaphalis longifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf帚草, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and fluffy, cotton-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of numerous tiny white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a spherical or ovoid shape.

It is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
