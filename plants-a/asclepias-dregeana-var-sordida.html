Asclepias dregeana var. sordida

Asclepias dregeana var. sordida, commonly known as the sordid milkweed, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain silky, white seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.
