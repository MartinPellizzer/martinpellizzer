Aconitum × mariae nothosubsp. mariae

Aconitum × mariae nothosubsp.

mariae, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a hybrid plant in the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple, helmet-shaped flowers and dark green, palmately lobed leaves.

Native to the alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils. The plant is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a dangerous species to handle.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value despite its toxicity.
