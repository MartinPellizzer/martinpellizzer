Aspidium microthecium

Aspidium microthecium, commonly known as the small-sporangial water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately compound leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are responsible for releasing spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its delicate structure and unique spore-producing features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
