Achillea moschata subsp. calcarea

Achillea moschata subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as sweet yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly sweet scent, which is derived from its essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
