Atriplex parishii var. subtilis

Atriplex parishii var. subtilis, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, a key adaptation to its harsh environment. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
