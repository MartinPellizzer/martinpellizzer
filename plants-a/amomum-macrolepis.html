Amomum macrolepis

Amomum macrolepis, commonly known as the large-leaf cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its fragrant, dark brown spikes of flowers are used to produce a highly valued spice.

The botanical features of A. macrolepis make it an important species in both traditional medicine and the global spice trade.
