Alsophila esmeraldensis

Alsophila esmeraldensis, commonly known as the Emerald Brake, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests and along riverbanks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Alsophila esmeraldensis plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
