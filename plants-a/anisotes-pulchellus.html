Anisotes pulchellus

Anisotes pulchellus, commonly known as the showy water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually white or pale pink.

Anisotes pulchellus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and wildlife.
