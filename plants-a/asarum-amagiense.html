Asarum amagiense

Asarum amagiense, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered in some regions.
