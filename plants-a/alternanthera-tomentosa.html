Alternanthera tomentosa

Alternanthera tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly morning glory, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often used in aquatic gardens or as a floating plant in ponds.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
