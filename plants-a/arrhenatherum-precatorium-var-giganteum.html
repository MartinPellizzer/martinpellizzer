Arrhenatherum precatorium var. giganteum

Arrhenatherum precatorium var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

This variety is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.
