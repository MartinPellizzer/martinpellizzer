Amorphophallus salmoneus

Amorphophallus salmoneus, commonly known as the salmon-colored arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces a unique inflorescence that resembles a towering spadix, often with a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tuberous root system of Amorphophallus salmoneus is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
