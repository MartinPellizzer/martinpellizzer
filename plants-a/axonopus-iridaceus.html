Axonopus iridaceus

Axonopus iridaceus, commonly known as the iridaceus axonopus, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant has slender, tufted leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to form mats, making it suitable for ground cover in landscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
