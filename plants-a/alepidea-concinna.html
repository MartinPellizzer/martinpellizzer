Alepidea concinna

Alepidea concinna, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal silver grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, whitish flowers. Alepidea concinna is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
