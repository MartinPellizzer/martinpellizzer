Aster trinervius var. angustifolius

Aster trinervius var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
