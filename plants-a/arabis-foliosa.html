Arabis foliosa

Arabis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in early spring, which are arranged in racemes.

Arabis foliosa is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
