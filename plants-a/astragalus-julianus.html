Astragalus julianus

Astragalus julianus, commonly known as Julian's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
