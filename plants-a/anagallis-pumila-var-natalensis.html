Anagallis pumila var. natalensis

Anagallis pumila var. natalensis, commonly known as the Natal mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are hairy and succulent, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
