Arundinaria distans

Arundinaria distans, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or the silver bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, while its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers.

Arundinaria distans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
