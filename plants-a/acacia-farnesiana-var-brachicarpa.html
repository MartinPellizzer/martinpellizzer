Acacia farnesiana var. brachicarpa

Acacia farnesiana var. brachicarpa, commonly known as the sweet acacia or the honey acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its nectar is a valuable food source for bees, contributing to honey production.
