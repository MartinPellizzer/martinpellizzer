Agatea macrobotrys

Agatea macrobotrys, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Agatea macrobotrys is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
