Apocynum cannabinum var. album

Apocynum cannabinum var. album, commonly known as white jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along fences. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.
