Arundinaria maculosa

Arundinaria maculosa, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple or red markings on its leaves. The plant has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique coloration and graceful appearance.
