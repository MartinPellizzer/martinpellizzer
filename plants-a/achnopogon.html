Achnopogon

Achnopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of grasses, and are usually wind-pollinated. Achnopogon species are typically found in temperate regions and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

These plants play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
