Apoballis sagittifolia

Apoballis sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf bushveld grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Apoballis sagittifolia is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of the local flora.
