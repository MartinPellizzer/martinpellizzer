Acoridium cucullatum

Acoridium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded acoridium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its unique, hood-like bracts that enclose its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, showcasing its adaptation to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, often floating on the water's surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Acoridium cucullatum plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
