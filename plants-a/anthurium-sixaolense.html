Anthurium sixaolense

Anthurium sixaolense, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces clusters of spadices, which are typically yellow or orange, surrounded by a showy, brightly colored spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium sixaolense is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
