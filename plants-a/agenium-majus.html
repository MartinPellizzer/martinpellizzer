Agenium majus

Agenium majus, commonly known as the greater agenium, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. Agenium majus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
