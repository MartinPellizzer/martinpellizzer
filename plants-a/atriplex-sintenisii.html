Atriplex sintenisii

Atriplex sintenisii, commonly known as the silverleaf shadscale, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, salt-excreting hairs, a feature common among plants in the Chenopodiaceae family. The plant has small, green to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Atriplex sintenisii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
