Anthurium trinitatis

Anthurium trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium trinitatis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
