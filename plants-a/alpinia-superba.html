Alpinia superba

Alpinia superba, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a reddish tint. The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, fragrant flowers. Alpinia superba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
