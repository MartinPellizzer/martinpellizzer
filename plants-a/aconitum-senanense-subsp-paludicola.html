Aconitum senanense subsp. paludicola

Aconitum senanense subsp.

paludicola, commonly known as the swamp aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces follicular fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshy areas and along stream banks.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
