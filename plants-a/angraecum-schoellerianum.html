Angraecum schoellerianum

Angraecum schoellerianum, commonly known as the Christmas star orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green with prominent purple or red markings. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms during the dry season, usually between December and February. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Angraecum schoellerianum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
