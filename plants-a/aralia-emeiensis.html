Aralia emeiensis

Aralia emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are often divided into several narrow segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

The Emei aralia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal interest.
