Arundinaria nana

Arundinaria nana, commonly known as dwarf bamboo, is a small, compact bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its short, stout stems and dense, grass-like foliage. This plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread gradually in suitable conditions.

Arundinaria nana prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in temperate climates.
