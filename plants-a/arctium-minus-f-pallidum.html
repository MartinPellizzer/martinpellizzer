Arctium minus f. pallidum

Arctium minus f. pallidum, commonly known as the lesser burdock.

It is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that develop into spiny, bur-like fruits. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
