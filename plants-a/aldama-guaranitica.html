Aldama guaranitica

Aldama guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani Aldama, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth areas of humid forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of interest for both botanical research and conservation efforts.
