Achimenes eeckhautei

Achimenes eeckhautei, commonly known as the pink orchid bell, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces showy bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes eeckhautei is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments due to its specific care requirements.
