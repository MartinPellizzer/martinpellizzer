Achnatherum paradoxum

Achnatherum paradoxum, commonly known as the strange needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and adaptation to arid environments.
