Acacia longifolia var. dissitiflora

Acacia longifolia var. dissitiflora, commonly known as the coastal acacia or silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in coastal regions and sandy soils, showing some tolerance to salt spray.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
