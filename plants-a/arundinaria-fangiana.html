Arundinaria fangiana

Arundinaria fangiana, commonly known as the Fang's bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

Arundinaria fangiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its graceful appearance.
