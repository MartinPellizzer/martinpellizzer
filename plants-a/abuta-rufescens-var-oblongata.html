Abuta rufescens var. oblongata

Abuta rufescens var. oblongata, commonly known as the purple abuta, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
