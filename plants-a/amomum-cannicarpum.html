Amomum cannicarpum

Amomum cannicarpum, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces aromatic, three-lobed fruits.

The rhizomes of Amomum cannicarpum are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent and warming properties.
