Amygdalus persica rosiflora

Amygdalus persica rosiflora, commonly known as the Persian almond.

This plant is a cultivar of the almond tree, characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to almonds but have a milder flavor.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
