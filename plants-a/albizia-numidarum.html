Albizia numidarum

Albizia numidarum, commonly known as the white siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often fragrant and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Albizia numidarum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
