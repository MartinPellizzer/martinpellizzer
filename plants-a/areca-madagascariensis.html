Areca madagascariensis

Areca madagascariensis, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers that develop into edible, elongated fruits containing a hard, starchy seed. The fruit is widely cultivated for its nut, which is used in traditional chewing practices.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and abundant sunlight.
