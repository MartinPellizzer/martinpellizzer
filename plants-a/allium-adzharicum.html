Allium adzharicum

Allium adzharicum, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, leafless flowering stem that bears umbels of white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful crop in some regions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous areas.
