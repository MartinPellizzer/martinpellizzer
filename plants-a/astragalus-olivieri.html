Astragalus olivieri

Astragalus olivieri, commonly known as the olive milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Astragalus olivieri is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
