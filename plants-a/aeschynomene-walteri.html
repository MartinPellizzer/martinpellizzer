Aeschynomene walteri

Aeschynomene walteri, commonly known as Walter's sicklegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by narrow, bean-like pods containing seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments due to its drought tolerance.
