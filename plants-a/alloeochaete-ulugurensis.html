Alloeochaete ulugurensis

Alloeochaete ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Alloeochaete ulugurensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
