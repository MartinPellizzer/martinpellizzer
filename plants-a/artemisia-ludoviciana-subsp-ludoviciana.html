Artemisia ludoviciana subsp. ludoviciana

Artemisia ludoviciana subsp.

ludoviciana, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
