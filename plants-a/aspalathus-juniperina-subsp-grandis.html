Aspalathus juniperina subsp. grandis

Aspalathus juniperina subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the giant silver bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger size and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
