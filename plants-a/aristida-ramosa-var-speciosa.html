Aristida ramosa var. speciosa

Aristida ramosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The showy ricegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
