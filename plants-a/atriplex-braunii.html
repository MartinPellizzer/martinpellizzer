Atriplex braunii

Atriplex braunii, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves that are adapted to retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it an important species in saline soils and for erosion control.

Atriplex braunii is also valued for its nutritional content, often used as forage for livestock.
