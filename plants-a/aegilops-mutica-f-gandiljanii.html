Aegilops mutica f. gandiljanii

Aegilops mutica f. gandiljanii, commonly known as the Gandiljanii wild barley, is a wild relative of cultivated barley (Hordeum vulgare).

It is a grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, characterized by its slender, hairy stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It possesses genetic traits that make it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving drought tolerance and disease resistance in barley.

Its ecological role includes contributing to soil stability and serving as a forage resource for wildlife.
