Acridocarpus excelsus var. petalislaceris

Acridocarpus excelsus var. petalislaceris, commonly known as the lace-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves that are finely divided and have a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal and tropical regions.
