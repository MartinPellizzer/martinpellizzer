Amaryllis psittacina var. decorata

Amaryllis psittacina var. decorata, commonly known as the golden amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or pink throat. The plant produces clusters of fleshy, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a bold, tropical look.
