Aloe platyphylla

Aloe platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water efficiently. The leaves are typically a deep green color with a waxy coating, and they often have a distinctive white or yellowish band near the edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers on a tall stem during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
