Arctium montanum

Arctium montanum, commonly known as the mountain burdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in a composite head, surrounded by spiny bracts that aid in seed dispersal.

The plant is known for its rough, prickly fruit, which contains small, oval seeds with tufts of silky hair.
