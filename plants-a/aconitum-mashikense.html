Aconitum mashikense

Aconitum mashikense, commonly known as the Mashikense monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Tajikistan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers, which are typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that give it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum mashikense is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
