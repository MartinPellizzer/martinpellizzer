Ampelocissus botryostachys

Ampelocissus botryostachys, commonly known as the grape-leaved fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
