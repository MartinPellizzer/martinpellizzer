Ardisia congestiflora

Ardisia congestiflora, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the West Indies and parts of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and attractive fruits.
