Artanthe hirsuta

Artanthe hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Artanthe hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
