Aporum blumei

Aporum blumei, commonly known as the Blume's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, providing a dramatic visual presence. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor that attracts pollinators.

Aporum blumei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
