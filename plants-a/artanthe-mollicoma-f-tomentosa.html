Artanthe mollicoma f. tomentosa

Artanthe mollicoma f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to dry climates.
