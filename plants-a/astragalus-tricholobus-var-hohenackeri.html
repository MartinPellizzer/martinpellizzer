Astragalus tricholobus var. hohenackeri

Astragalus tricholobus var. hohenackeri, commonly known as Hohenacker's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
