Allium pseudoxiphopetalum

Allium pseudoxiphopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
