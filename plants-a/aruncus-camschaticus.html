Aruncus camschaticus

Aruncus camschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery plumes of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aruncus camschaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add a unique texture to garden landscapes.
