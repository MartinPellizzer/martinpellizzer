Avena barbata subvar. lusitanica

Avena barbata subvar. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture.
