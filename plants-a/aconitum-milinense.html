Aconitum milinense

Aconitum milinense, commonly known as the Milinense Monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Aconitum milinense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Like other members of its genus, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
