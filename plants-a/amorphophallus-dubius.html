Amorphophallus dubius

Amorphophallus dubius, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can span several meters in diameter, floating on the surface of freshwater habitats. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale pink, emerging on long, slender stalks above the water. Its rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant plays a significant role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
