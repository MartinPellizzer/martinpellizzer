Asteriscus caballeroi

Asteriscus caballeroi, commonly known as the Mexican aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
