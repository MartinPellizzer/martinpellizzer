Aconitum napellus var. lomnicense

Aconitum napellus var. lomnicense, commonly known as the Lomnicky Stit aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
