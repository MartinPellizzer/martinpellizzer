Astragalus lasiopetalus

Astragalus lasiopetalus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus lasiopetalus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
