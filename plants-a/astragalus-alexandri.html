Astragalus alexandri

Astragalus alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Astragalus alexandri is often found in steppes and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity.
