Alopecurus myosuroides

Alopecurus myosuroides, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in pastures. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and small white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Alopecurus myosuroides is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
