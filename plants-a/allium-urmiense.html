Allium urmiense

Allium urmiense, commonly known as Urmi onion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
