Actaea elata

Actaea elata, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear clusters of white, petal-like flowers. The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Actaea elata is also known for its showy, spiky seed heads that appear after the flowers, adding to its ornamental value.
