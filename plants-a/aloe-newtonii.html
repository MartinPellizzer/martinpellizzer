Aloe newtonii

Aloe newtonii, commonly known as Newton's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, greenish-blue leaves that are covered in a layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Aloe newtonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild medicinal properties.
