Aloe parallelifolia

Aloe parallelifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The leaves are typically broad and parallel-veined, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces yellowish-green flowers on tall, branched stems during the summer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.
