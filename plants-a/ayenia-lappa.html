Ayenia lappa

Ayenia lappa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of purple to pink flowers. Ayenia lappa is known for its ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
