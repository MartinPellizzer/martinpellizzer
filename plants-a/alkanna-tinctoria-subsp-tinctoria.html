Alkanna tinctoria subsp. tinctoria

Alkanna tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers.

The plant produces a distinctive red-orange root that contains alkaloids and is used as a natural dye. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Alkanna tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile dyeing.
