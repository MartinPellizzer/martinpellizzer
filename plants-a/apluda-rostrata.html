Apluda rostrata

Apluda rostrata, commonly known as the beakspur grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Apluda rostrata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
