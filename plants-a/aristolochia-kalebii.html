Aristolochia kalebii

Aristolochia kalebii, commonly known as the Kalebii Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, hood-shaped flowers that are often green or brown in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and some species contain toxic compounds.
