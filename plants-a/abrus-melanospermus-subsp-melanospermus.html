Abrus melanospermus subsp. melanospermus

Abrus melanospermus subsp.

melanospermus, commonly known as the blackseed velvet tamarind, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is a climbing shrub characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or orange flowers.

The plant produces elongated pods containing dark, glossy seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include hairy stems and stipules, which are typical of many leguminous plants.

The species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
