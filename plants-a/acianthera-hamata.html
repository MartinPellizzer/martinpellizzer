Acianthera hamata

Acianthera hamata, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a unique structure that includes a long, slender spur.

Acianthera hamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
