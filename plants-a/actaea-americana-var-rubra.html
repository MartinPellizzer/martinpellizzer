Actaea americana var. rubra

Actaea americana var. rubra, commonly known as red baneberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, poisonous, red berries that contrast with its compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a short, stout stem with palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are white and clustered in an upright inflorescence, and it thrives in rich, loamy soils.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance, though its berries are toxic to humans and animals.
