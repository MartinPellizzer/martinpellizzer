Ampelocissus rubiginosa

Ampelocissus rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises and as a ground cover.
