Aerides lepidium

Aerides lepidium, commonly known as the orchid with the delicate, grass-like leaves, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a rhizome with a few pseudobulbs, and its leaves are narrow and strap-like. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
