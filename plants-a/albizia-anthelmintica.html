Albizia anthelmintica

Albizia anthelmintica, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seed meal.
