Areca rostrata

Areca rostrata, commonly known as the red areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark blue, fleshy fruits. Areca rostrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
