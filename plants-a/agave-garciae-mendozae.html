Agave garciae-mendozae

Agave garciae-mendozae, commonly known as the Mendoza agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white to pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
