Acaena sanguisorbae var. exsudans

Acaena sanguisorbae var. exsudans, commonly known as the exuding acaena, is a variant of the genus Acaena native to South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange to red berries. The plant produces distinctive, sticky resinous exudates from its stems, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The exudates have been used traditionally for their medicinal and adhesive properties.
