Acalypha hibiscifolia

Acalypha hibiscifolia, commonly known as the Hibiscifolius Acalypha, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, urn-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha hibiscifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
