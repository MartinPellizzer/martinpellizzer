Armeria beirana subsp. monchiquensis

Armeria beirana subsp.

monchiquensis, commonly known as the Algarve thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Portugal. It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
