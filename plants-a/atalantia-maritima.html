Atalantia maritima

Atalantia maritima, commonly known as the coastal holly or sea holly, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Atalantia maritima is well adapted to harsh maritime conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
