Astragalus pamiroalaicus

Astragalus pamiroalaicus, commonly known as the Pamir milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold and harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
