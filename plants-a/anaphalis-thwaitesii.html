Anaphalis thwaitesii

Anaphalis thwaitesii, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silvery-white, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming a composite head that resembles a single flower.

Anaphalis thwaitesii is often found in meadows and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
