Angiopteris hugeliana

Angiopteris hugeliana, commonly known as the Hugel's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically near streams or in moist forest understories. Its leaves are composed of numerous pinnae, which are arranged in a distinctive, feather-like pattern.

Angiopteris hugeliana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna.
