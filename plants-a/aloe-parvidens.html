Aloe parvidens

Aloe parvidens, commonly known as the dwarf aloe or narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features narrow, fleshy leaves that are blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are borne on tall, erect stems. Aloe parvidens is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive drought conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
