Aquilegia vulgaris monstr. multifaria

Aquilegia vulgaris monstr.

multifaria, commonly known as the multiflora columbine, is a striking perennial plant native to Europe. It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
