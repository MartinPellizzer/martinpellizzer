Abelmoschus persicifolius

Abelmoschus persicifolius, commonly known as the okra or lady's finger, is a flowering plant in the mallow family (Malvaceae).

It is native to warm temperate and tropical regions of Africa and is widely cultivated for its edible pods. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that matures into the fibrous, pod-like structure used in cooking.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for home gardens and commercial agriculture.
