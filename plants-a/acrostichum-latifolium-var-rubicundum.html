Acrostichum latifolium var. rubicundum

Acrostichum latifolium var. rubicundum, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed acrostichum.

It is a fern native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery, dark green fronds with prominent reddish veins. The fronds are pinnately compound, with numerous pinnae that are broadly ovate and slightly serrated.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
