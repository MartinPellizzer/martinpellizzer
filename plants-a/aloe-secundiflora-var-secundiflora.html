Aloe secundiflora var. secundiflora

Aloe secundiflora var. secundiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water efficiently in its native habitat.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in dry, well-drained conditions.
