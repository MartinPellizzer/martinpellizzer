Anguloa lurida

Anguloa lurida, commonly known as the red anguloa, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is highly modified into a lip.

Anguloa lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
