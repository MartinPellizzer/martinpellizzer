Astragalus spinellus

Astragalus spinellus, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and narrow.

Astragalus spinellus is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
