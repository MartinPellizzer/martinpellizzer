Asarum inflatum

Asarum inflatum, commonly known as the inflated asarum, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves. The plant produces unique, inflated, purple to reddish flowers that resemble a lantern, which are often covered in a dense, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
