Astragalus nusairiensis

Astragalus nusairiensis, commonly known as the Nusayriyah milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East, particularly Syria.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, compound leaves and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important species in its ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
