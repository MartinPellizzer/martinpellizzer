Apium andinum

Apium andinum, commonly known as the Andean celery, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and fern-like, aromatic leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the family.

Apium andinum is often cultivated for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional Andean cuisine.
