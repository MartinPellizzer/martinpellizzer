Allmania nodiflora var. genuina

Allmania nodiflora var. genuina, commonly known as the Florida allamanda, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of showy, orange-yellow flowers. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in its native range.
