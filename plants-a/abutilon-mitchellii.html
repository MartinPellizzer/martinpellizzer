Abutilon mitchellii

Abutilon mitchellii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its adaptability and striking appearance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
