Arabis hirsuta

Arabis hirsuta, commonly known as hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal areas. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are hairy and lobed, giving the plant its common name.
