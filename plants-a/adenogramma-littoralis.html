Adenogramma littoralis

Adenogramma littoralis, commonly known as the coastal adenogramma, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are clustered in inflorescences.

Adenogramma littoralis is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a resilient species in coastal environments.
