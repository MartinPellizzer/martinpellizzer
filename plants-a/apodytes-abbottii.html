Apodytes abbottii

Apodytes abbottii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, spherical flowers that appear in winter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Apodytes abbottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
