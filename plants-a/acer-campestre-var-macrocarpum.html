Acer campestre var. macrocarpum

Acer campestre var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded hedge maple, is a variant of the field maple.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and prominent veins. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a samara, typically larger than that of the typical field maple, contributing to its common name.
