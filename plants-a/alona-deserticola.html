Alona deserticola

Alona deserticola, commonly known as the desert water flea, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in temporary water bodies.

It is characterized by its tiny, floating leaves and specialized structures for buoyancy. This species is adapted to arid environments, often found in desert oases and seasonal pools. Its life cycle is closely tied to the availability of water, which it relies on for reproduction and survival.

Alona deserticola plays a vital role in supporting aquatic ecosystems in its limited habitat.
