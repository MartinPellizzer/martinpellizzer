Astragalus glaucopsoides

Astragalus glaucopsoides, commonly known as the blue-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus glaucopsoides is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
