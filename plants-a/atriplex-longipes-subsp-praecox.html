Atriplex longipes subsp. praecox

Atriplex longipes subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early jointed saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its earlier flowering time compared to other forms of Atriplex longipes.
