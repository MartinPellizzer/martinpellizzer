Amomum minus

Amomum minus, commonly known as small cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly woody rhizomes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Amomum minus is valued for its essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
