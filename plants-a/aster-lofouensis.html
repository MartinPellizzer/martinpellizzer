Aster lofouensis

Aster lofouensis, commonly known as the Madagascar aster, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Aster lofouensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
