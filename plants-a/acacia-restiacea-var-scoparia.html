Acacia restiacea var. scoparia

Acacia restiacea var. scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has phyllodes rather than true leaves, which are narrow and strap-like, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
