Adenoropium multifidum

Adenoropium multifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly bitter taste, which is common among members of its family.

Adenoropium multifidum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
