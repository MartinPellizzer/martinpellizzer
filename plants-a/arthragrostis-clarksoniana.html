Arthragrostis clarksoniana

Arthragrostis clarksoniana, commonly known as the Clarkson's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, creeping stems that form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and often curl at the tips. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
