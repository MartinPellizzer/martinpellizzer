Asparagus asiaticus var. ellenbeckianus

Asparagus asiaticus var. ellenbeckianus, commonly known as the Ellenbeck's asparagus, is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and edible, spear-like shoots.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is often cultivated for its culinary value, as its tender shoots are a delicacy in various cuisines.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
