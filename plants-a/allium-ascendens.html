Allium ascendens

Allium ascendens, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its strong garlic-like scent. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Allium ascendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
