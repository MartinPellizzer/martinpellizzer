Aquilegia coerulea var. albiflora

Aquilegia coerulea var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are predominantly white with a subtle blue or purple hue. The plant has compound, pinnate leaves with finely divided lobes, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique flower structure and early spring blooming period.
