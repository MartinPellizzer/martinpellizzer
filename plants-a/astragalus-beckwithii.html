Astragalus beckwithii

Astragalus beckwithii, commonly known as Beckwith's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological significance.
