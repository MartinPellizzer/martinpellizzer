Agropyron intermedium f. majus

Agropyron intermedium f. majus, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control and forage production.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
