Anisopappus lawalreanus

Anisopappus lawalreanus, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring silver-gray foliage and a deep root system for water conservation. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
