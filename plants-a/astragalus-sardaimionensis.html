Astragalus sardaimionensis

Astragalus sardaimionensis, commonly known as the Sardaimionensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems through nitrogen fixation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
