Astragalus bavanatensis

Astragalus bavanatensis, commonly known as the Bavanat milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for various herbivorous insects and small mammals.
