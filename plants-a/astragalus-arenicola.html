Astragalus arenicola

Astragalus arenicola, commonly known as sand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus arenicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in desert ecosystems.
