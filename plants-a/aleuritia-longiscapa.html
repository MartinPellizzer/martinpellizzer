Aleuritia longiscapa

Aleuritia longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.
