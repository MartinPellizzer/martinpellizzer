Acacia neglecta

Acacia neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, often twisted stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or grey tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia neglecta is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.

Native Distribution

Acacia neglecta is primarily native to southeastern Australia, including parts of New South Wales, Victoria, and South Australia.

It is commonly found in dry, sandy soils along the coast and in open woodlands. This species thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate, characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. It is often seen growing in coastal heathlands and along the edges of forests.

Acacia neglecta is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native range.

Introduced Distribution
