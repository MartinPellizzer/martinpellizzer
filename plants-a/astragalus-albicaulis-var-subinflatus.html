Astragalus albicaulis var. subinflatus

Astragalus albicaulis var. subinflatus, commonly known as the white-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellow to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly inflated fruit structure compared to the typical form.
