Allium tribracteatum var. parvum

Allium tribracteatum var. parvum, commonly known as the dwarf garlic chive, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is closely related to other allium species and is valued for its mild garlic flavor, making it a popular culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and useful in traditional cuisines.
