Acaena × metaphyllidion

Acaena × metaphyllidion, commonly known as the hybrid New Zealand brake, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acaena × metaphyllidion is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
