Aplotaxis scaposa

Aplotaxis scaposa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, slender stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers with a distinctive, tubular shape. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aplotaxis scaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful blooms.
