Acacia galioides var. leioclada

Acacia galioides var. leioclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
