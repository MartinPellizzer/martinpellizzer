Astragalus lucidus

Astragalus lucidus, commonly known as the shining milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus lucidus is often found in the western United States and is adapted to arid environments.
