Aster × laevis

Aster × laevis, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems and finely divided, gray-green foliage. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of white to lavender petals with a yellow center.

Aster × laevis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
