Acanthus repens

Acanthus repens, commonly known as the creeping acanthus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spires of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Acanthus repens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to spread along the ground.
