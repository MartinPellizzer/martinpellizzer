Atractylis carduus var. carduus

Atractylis carduus var. carduus, commonly known as rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct rough texture, while its seed heads are spherical and covered in bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
