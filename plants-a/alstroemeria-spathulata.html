Alstroemeria spathulata

Alstroemeria spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of colorful, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, yellow, and pink. The plant is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of growing conditions.

Its foliage and blooms contribute to its popularity as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
