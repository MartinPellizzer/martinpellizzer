Aristolochia pyriflora

Aristolochia pyriflora, commonly known as the grapeleaf arrowhead, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a bell. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible when ripe but contain toxic compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
