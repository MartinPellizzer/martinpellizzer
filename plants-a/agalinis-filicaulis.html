Agalinis filicaulis

Agalinis filicaulis, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Agalinis filicaulis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a short-lived perennial in some regions.
