Aria subtomentosa

Aria subtomentosa, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aria subtomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic habitats.
