Agrostis semibarbata

Agrostis semibarbata, commonly known as the slender brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is characterized by its tufted growth habit.

Agrostis semibarbata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
