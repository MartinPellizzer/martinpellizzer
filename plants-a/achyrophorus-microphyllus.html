Achyrophorus microphyllus

Achyrophorus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the Nile, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical name reflects its small leaves (microphyllus) and its classification within the Amaryllidaceae family.
