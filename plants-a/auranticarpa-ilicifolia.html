Auranticarpa ilicifolia

Auranticarpa ilicifolia, commonly known as the golden fruit holly, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually appealing, low-maintenance plant.
