Adansonia fony var. rubrostipa

Adansonia fony var. rubrostipa, commonly known as the red-stemmed baobab, is a rare and unique variant of the baobab tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which gives it its common name, and its thick, gnarled trunk that stores large amounts of water. The plant has large, palmate leaves and produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a fibrous capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.
