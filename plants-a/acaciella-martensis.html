Acaciella martensis

Acaciella martensis, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Acaciella martensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
