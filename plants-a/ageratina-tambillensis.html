Ageratina tambillensis

Ageratina tambillensis, commonly known as the tambilla ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue florets arranged in dense, spherical clusters, giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ageratina tambillensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and ease of growth.
