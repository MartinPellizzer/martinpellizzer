Aster batangensis

Aster batangensis, commonly known as the Batang aster, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in forest understories and is known for its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, herbaceous stems.

Aster batangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local biodiversity.
