Astragalus tetuanensis

Astragalus tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa, particularly Morocco. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus tetuanensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
