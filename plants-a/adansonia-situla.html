Adansonia situla

Adansonia situla, commonly known as the Madagascar baobab, is a unique and iconic tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, swollen trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The tree has a distinctive umbrella-shaped crown with long, drooping branches. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Adansonia situla plays a vital role in the local ecosystem and is culturally significant to the people of Madagascar.
