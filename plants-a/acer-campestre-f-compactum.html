Acer campestre f. compactum

Acer campestre f. compactum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact, slow-growing shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It features dense, rounded foliage with palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and seasonal color changes.

Acer campestre f. compactum is primarily native to southern and central Europe, including countries such as France, Italy, Spain, and parts of the Balkans.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodlands, hedgerows, and rocky areas. This compact form of the European horse chestnut is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can tolerate partial shade. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The plant's native range reflects its historical presence in the natural landscapes of Europe.
