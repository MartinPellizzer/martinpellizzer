Achillea distans subsp. stricta

Achillea distans subsp.

stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
