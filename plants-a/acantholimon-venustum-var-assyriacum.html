Acantholimon venustum var. assyriacum

Acantholimon venustum var. assyriacum, commonly known as the Assyrian thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Syria.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes flowering plants characterized by their showy flowers and often succulent leaves. The plant features slender, hairy stems that bear small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
