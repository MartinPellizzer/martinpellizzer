Amaryllis philippiana

Amaryllis philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Philippines.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaryllis philippiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
