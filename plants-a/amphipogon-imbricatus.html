Amphipogon imbricatus

Amphipogon imbricatus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
