Aira cespitosa var. major

Aira cespitosa var. major, commonly known as tufted air grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
