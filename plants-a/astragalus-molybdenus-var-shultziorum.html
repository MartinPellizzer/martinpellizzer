Astragalus molybdenus var. shultziorum

Astragalus molybdenus var. shultziorum, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
