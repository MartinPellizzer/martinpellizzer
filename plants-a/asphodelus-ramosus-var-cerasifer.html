Asphodelus ramosus var. cerasifer

Asphodelus ramosus var. cerasifer, commonly known as the cherry asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bright red or pink flower spikes that emerge from basal leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The cherry asphodel is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
