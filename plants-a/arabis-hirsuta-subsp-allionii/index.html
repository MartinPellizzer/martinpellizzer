Arabis Hirsuta Subsp. Allionii

Arabis hirsuta subsp. allionii is a lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family known for its delicate, hairy foliage and small white flowers. This subspecies thrives in rocky and dry environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to alpine gardens and rockeries. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated compared to more common garden plants. Cultivating this subspecies can offer gardeners a rare opportunity to grow a resilient and ornamental plant.
