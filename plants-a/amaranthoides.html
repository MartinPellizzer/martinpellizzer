Amaranthoides

Amaranthoides, commonly known as the common amaranth, is a genus of flowering plants in the amaranth family.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers and often hairy foliage. They are native to various regions across the Americas and are well adapted to a range of environmental conditions. Amaranthoides species typically grow as annuals or perennials, with many being cultivated for their ornamental value.
