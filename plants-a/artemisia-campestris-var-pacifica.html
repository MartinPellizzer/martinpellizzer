Artemisia campestris var. pacifica

Artemisia campestris var. pacifica, commonly known as Pacific wormwood, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It features silver-gray foliage with finely divided, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, medicinal scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent highlights its botanical and practical significance.
