Amaryllis rosea

Amaryllis rosea, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and strap-shaped. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

It is also known for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
