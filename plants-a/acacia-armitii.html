Acacia armitii

Acacia armitii, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
