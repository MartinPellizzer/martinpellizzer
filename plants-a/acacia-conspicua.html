Acacia conspicua

Acacia conspicua, commonly known as the showy acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow or golden flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia conspicua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
