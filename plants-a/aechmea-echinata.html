Aechmea echinata

Aechmea echinata, commonly known as the spiny pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears showy, violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in length.

Aechmea echinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea echinata has rosette-shaped leaves that are typically lanceolate, arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy blue-gray surface and a sharp, pointed tip.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, thickened base with a fibrous, rough-textured bark. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, with small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom during the rainy season, primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, with some rhizomes forming underground connections.
