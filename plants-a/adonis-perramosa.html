Adonis perramosa

Adonis perramosa, commonly known as the Persian adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

Adonis perramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
