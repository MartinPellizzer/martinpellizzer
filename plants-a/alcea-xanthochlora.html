Alcea xanthochlora

Alcea xanthochlora, commonly known as the yellow touch-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Alcea xanthochlora is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
