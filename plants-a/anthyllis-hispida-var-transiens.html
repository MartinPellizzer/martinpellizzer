Anthyllis hispida var. transiens

Anthyllis hispida var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Broom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a creeping habit with branching stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in arid environments.
