Aristida denudata

Aristida denudata, commonly known as the naked grass or slender grama, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct sheath, and its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
