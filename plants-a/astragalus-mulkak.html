Astragalus mulkak

Astragalus mulkak, commonly known as the Kashmir goat's bean, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions of the Himalayas. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or green flowers in clusters. Its pods contain small, lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus mulkak is valued for its potential health benefits and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional properties.
