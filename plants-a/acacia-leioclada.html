Acacia leioclada

Acacia leioclada, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are light green and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Acacia leioclada is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with low rainfall.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
