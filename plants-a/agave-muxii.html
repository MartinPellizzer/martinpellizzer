Agave muxii

Agave muxii, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a species of agave native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall flower stalk bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Agave muxii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
