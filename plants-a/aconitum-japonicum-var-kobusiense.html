Aconitum japonicum var. kobusiense

Aconitum japonicum var. kobusiense, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood variant kobusiense, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spathaceous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately compound with lobed segments, and the flowers are arranged in a single terminal raceme.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
