Anthericum asphodelum

Anthericum asphodelum, commonly known as the yellow asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spikelike inflorescences with bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anthericum asphodelum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
