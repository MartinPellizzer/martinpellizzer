Aconitum kusnezoffii var. bodinieri

Aconitum kusnezoffii var. bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are palmately arranged. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
