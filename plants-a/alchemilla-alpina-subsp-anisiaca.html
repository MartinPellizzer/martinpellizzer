Alchemilla alpina subsp. anisiaca

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

anisiaca, commonly known as mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine plantings.
