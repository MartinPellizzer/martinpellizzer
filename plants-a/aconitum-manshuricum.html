Aconitum manshuricum

Aconitum manshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum manshuricum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
