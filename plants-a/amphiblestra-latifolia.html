Amphiblestra latifolia

Amphiblestra latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf amphiblestra, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and finely hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
