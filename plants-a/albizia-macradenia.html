Albizia macradenia

Albizia macradenia, commonly known as the African siris, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Albizia macradenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
