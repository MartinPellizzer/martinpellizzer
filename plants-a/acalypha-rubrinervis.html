Acalypha rubrinervis

Acalypha rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined strobilus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful leaves, which often display vibrant red and green veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha rubrinervis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
