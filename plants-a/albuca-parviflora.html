Albuca parviflora

Albuca parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Albuca parviflora prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
