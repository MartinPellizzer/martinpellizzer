Acer dedyle

Acer dedyle, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its vibrant autumn foliage. The tree typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are palmately lobed, turning shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall.

Acer dedyle is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
