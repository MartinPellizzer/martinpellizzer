Arum regnium

Arum regnium, commonly known as the royal arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive odor that can vary from sweet to foul, depending on the species.

Arum regnium is often cultivated for its dramatic appearance and is used in both ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
