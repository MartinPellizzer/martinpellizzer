Avena elatior f. pilifera

Avena elatior f. pilifera, commonly known as the silky oat, is a botanical variant of the common oat species.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. This fern-like plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
