Astragalus limariensis

Astragalus limariensis, commonly known as the Limari milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
