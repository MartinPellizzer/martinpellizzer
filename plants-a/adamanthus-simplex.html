Adamanthus simplex

Adamanthus simplex, commonly known as the Florida holly or swamp holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Adamanthus simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
