Aeonium bethencourtianum

Aeonium bethencourtianum, commonly known as the Bethencourt's aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellow flowers, which appear in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and striking foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
