Adamanthus quitensis

Adamanthus quitensis, commonly known as the Andean flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
