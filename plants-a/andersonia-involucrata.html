Andersonia involucrata

Andersonia involucrata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Andersonia involucrata is an important component of the ecological diversity in its native habitat.
