Astragalus tachdirtensis

Astragalus tachdirtensis, commonly known as the Tachdirt astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its leaves are usually compound, consisting of several small, narrow leaflets, which reduce water loss through transpiration.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and its potential for use in ecological restoration projects.
