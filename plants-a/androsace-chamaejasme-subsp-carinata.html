Androsace chamaejasme subsp. carinata

Androsace chamaejasme subsp.

carinata, commonly known as the Carinate Androsace, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically forms dense cushions of foliage, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white or pink.

The plant has a compact growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular and covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine displays.
