Alchemilla cryptantha

Alchemilla cryptantha, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves that resemble a mantle. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
