Arum nymphaeifolium

Arum nymphaeifolium, commonly known as the water arum, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water, aiding in photosynthesis. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a prominent spadix that produces both male and female flowers.

The plant is often used in aquatic gardens and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
