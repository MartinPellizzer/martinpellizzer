Allium reconditum

Allium reconditum, commonly known as the hidden onion or alpine garlic, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Allium reconditum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
