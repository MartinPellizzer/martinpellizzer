Acacia obtusifolia

Acacia obtusifolia, commonly known as the soft-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
