Argyrolobium roseum subsp. ornithopodioides

Argyrolobium roseum subsp.

ornithopodioides, commonly known as the silver globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery, woolly foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a compact, low-growing habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
