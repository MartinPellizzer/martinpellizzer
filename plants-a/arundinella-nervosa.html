Arundinella nervosa

Arundinella nervosa, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with prominent veins that contribute to its scientific name.

Arundinella nervosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
