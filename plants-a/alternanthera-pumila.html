Alternanthera pumila

Alternanthera pumila, commonly known as the dwarf red alternanthera, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
