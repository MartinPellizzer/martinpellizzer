Agave falcata var. espadina

Agave falcata var. espadina, commonly known as the Espadina agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, white flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
