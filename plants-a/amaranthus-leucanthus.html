Amaranthus leucanthus

Amaranthus leucanthus, commonly known as white amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
