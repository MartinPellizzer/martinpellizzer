Anemone cylindrica var. alba

Anemone cylindrica var. alba, commonly known as the white thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
