Amelanchier alnifolia var. cuyamacensis

Amelanchier alnifolia var. cuyamacensis, commonly known as the Cuyama serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a red or purple color in late summer. It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
