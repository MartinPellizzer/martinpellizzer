Achyronia sericea

Achyronia sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
