Aster flaccidus f. gracilis

Aster flaccidus f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white to pale purple. Native to the western United States, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant's botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
