Asclepias verticillata var. linearis

Asclepias verticillata var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with distinctive horn-shaped buds, which are characteristic of the milkweed genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

This species is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.
