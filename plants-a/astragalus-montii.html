Astragalus montii

Astragalus montii, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Astragalus montii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
