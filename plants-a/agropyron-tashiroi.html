Agropyron tashiroi

Agropyron tashiroi, commonly known as Tashiro's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species.

Agropyron tashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
