Amphicarpaea bracteata var. comosa

Amphicarpaea bracteata var. comosa, commonly known as the hairy false flax, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
