Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. pantocsekianum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

pantocsekianum, commonly known as the Hungarian aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
