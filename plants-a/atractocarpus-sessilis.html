Atractocarpus sessilis

Atractocarpus sessilis, commonly known as the sessile podded tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, sessile fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
