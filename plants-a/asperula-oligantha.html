Asperula oligantha

Asperula oligantha, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asperula oligantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
