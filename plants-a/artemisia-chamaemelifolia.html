Artemisia chamaemelifolia

Artemisia chamaemelifolia, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a strong, bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Artemisia chamaemelifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a companion plant in herbal gardens.
