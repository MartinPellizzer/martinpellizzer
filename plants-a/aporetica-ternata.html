Aporetica ternata

Aporetica ternata, commonly known as the three-lobed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal.

Aporetica ternata is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
