Aster miser

Aster miser, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster miser prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
