Acacia lineolata subsp. multilineata

Acacia lineolata subsp.

multilineata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
