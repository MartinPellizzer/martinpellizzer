Aster ericoides var. reevesii

Aster ericoides var. reevesii, commonly known as the Reeves' heath aster, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It typically thrives in moist, acidic soils found in forested areas and wetlands. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It is often found in sandy or peaty soils and is adapted to shady, cool environments.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated structure.
