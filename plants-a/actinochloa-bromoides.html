Actinochloa bromoides

Actinochloa bromoides, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
