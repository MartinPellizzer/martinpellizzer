Anemone alchemillifolia

Anemone alchemillifolia, commonly known as the alpine anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
