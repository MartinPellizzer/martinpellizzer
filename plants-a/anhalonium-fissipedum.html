Anhalonium fissipedum

Anhalonium fissipedum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in umbels. It is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine.

Anhalonium fissipedum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
