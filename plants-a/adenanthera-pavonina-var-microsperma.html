Adenanthera pavonina var. microsperma

Adenanthera pavonina var. microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded legume or velvet bean, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in agroforestry systems.

The seeds are edible but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
