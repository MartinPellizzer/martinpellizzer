Astracantha balansae

Astracantha balansae, commonly known as the Balansae thistle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually spiny, and the flowers are small and clustered, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
