Astragalus aegobromus f. caulescens

Astragalus aegobromus f. caulescens, commonly known as the caulescens form of the common milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are hairy on the upper surface.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
