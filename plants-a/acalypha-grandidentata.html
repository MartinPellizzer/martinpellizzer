Acalypha grandidentata

Acalypha grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent, serrated teeth along the edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called cyathia.

Acalypha grandidentata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
