Aira cespitosa subsp. hartmaniana

Aira cespitosa subsp.

hartmaniana, commonly known as Hartman's tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in arid and semi-arid regions.
