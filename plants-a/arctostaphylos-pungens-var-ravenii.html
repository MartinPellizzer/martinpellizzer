Arctostaphylos pungens var. ravenii

Arctostaphylos pungens var. ravenii, commonly known as the black oak, is a variant of the coast live oak native to the western United States.

It is a woody evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible acorns that mature in the fall. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct, aromatic scent, and the plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Arctostaphylos pungens var. ravenii plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
