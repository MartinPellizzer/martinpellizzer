Acaena pinnatifida subsp. grandiflora

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered spinach bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its fruits are small, woody burrs that contain seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native fauna.

The species is adapted to harsh environments and is relatively resilient to grazing.
