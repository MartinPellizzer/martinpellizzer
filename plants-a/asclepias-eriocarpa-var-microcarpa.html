Asclepias eriocarpa var. microcarpa

Asclepias eriocarpa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release buoyant seeds with silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often covered with fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
