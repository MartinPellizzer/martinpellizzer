Anthoxanthum potaninii

Anthoxanthum potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are often pale yellow or greenish in hue. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes, and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Anthoxanthum potaninii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
