Asparagopsis javanica

Asparagopsis javanica, commonly known as the Javan asparagus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by berry-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
