Atraphaxis calvertii

Atraphaxis calvertii, commonly known as the Calvert's desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Atraphaxis calvertii plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
