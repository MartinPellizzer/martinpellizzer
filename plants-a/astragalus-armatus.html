Astragalus armatus

Astragalus armatus, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Astragalus armatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
