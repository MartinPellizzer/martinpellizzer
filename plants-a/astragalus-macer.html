Astragalus macer

Astragalus macer, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers. Its small, hairy pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus macer is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
