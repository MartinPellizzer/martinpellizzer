Anthyllis vulneraria subproles tricolor

Anthyllis vulneraria subproles tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically a mix of white, yellow, and red, giving it its distinctive tri-color appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.
