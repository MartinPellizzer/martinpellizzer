Acacia homalophylla

Acacia homalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia homalophylla is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
