Aconitum ranunculifolium subsp. platanifolium

Aconitum ranunculifolium subsp.

platanifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, particularly in mountainous regions.

The plant is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers, which are typically arranged in a helmet-like shape. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a distinctive, wavy margin.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
