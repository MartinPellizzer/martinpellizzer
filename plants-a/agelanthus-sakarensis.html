Agelanthus sakarensis

Agelanthus sakarensis, commonly known as the Sakar Islands palm, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sakar in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
