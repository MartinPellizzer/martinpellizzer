Arenaria conimbricensis subsp. conimbricensis

Arenaria conimbricensis subsp.

conimbricensis, commonly known as the Portuguese sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Portugal. It typically forms dense mats of fleshy, succulent leaves and small white flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes. It is well adapted to arid conditions and has a shallow root system.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the local flora in its native habitat.
