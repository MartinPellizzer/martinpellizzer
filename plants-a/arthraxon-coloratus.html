Arthraxon coloratus

Arthraxon coloratus, commonly known as the colorful sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Arthraxon coloratus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
