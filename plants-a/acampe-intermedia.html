Acampe intermedia

Acampe intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate acampe, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Acampe intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
