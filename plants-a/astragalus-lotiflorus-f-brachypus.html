Astragalus lotiflorus f. brachypus

Astragalus lotiflorus f. brachypus, commonly known as the short-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
