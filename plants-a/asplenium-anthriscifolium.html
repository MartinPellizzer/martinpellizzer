Asplenium anthriscifolium

Asplenium anthriscifolium, commonly known as the fern with a heart-shaped leaf, is a species of rock fern native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the leaves of the cow parsley plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a distinctive, slightly curled appearance.

Asplenium anthriscifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
