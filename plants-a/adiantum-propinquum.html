Adiantum propinquum

Adiantum propinquum, commonly known as the common maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arched or gracefully drooping. The fronds are typically bright green and have a finely divided structure, with each pinna having a narrow, linear shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
