Allium angulosum var. latifolium

Allium angulosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
