Anthericum corymbosum var. floribundum

Anthericum corymbosum var. floribundum, commonly known as the clustered arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy, spatulate inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a wetland indicator species and a valuable component of riparian ecosystems.
