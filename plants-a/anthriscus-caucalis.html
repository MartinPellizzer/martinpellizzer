Anthriscus caucalis

Anthriscus caucalis, commonly known as cow parsley, is a flowering plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Anthriscus caucalis is an herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.
