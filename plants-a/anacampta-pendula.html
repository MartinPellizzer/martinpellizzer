Anacampta pendula

Anacampta pendula, commonly known as the hanging anacampta, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant orange to red blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Anacampta pendula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to container gardening.
