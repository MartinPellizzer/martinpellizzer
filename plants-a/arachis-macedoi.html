Arachis macedoi

Arachis macedoi, commonly known as the Macedonian groundnut, is a species of legume native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the genus Arachis, which includes various groundnut species, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and oval-shaped pods. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a hairy texture.

While not widely cultivated for commercial use, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in agricultural research.
