Aconitum alpinum

Aconitum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Aconitum alpinum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
