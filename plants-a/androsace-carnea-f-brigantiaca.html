Androsace carnea f. brigantiaca

Androsace carnea f. brigantiaca, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a dense mat of fleshy, dark green leaves covered in a fine layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
