Alternanthera minutiflora

Alternanthera minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf flame plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
