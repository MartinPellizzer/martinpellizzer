Aloe perfoliata var. vera

Aloe perfoliata var. vera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers on erect, branched stems, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and unique morphology.
