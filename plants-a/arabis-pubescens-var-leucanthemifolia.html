Arabis pubescens var. leucanthemifolia

Arabis pubescens var. leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered rockcress, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, and the flowers are white with yellow centers, resembling miniature daisies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
