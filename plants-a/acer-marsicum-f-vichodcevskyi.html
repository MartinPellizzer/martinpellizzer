Acer marsicum f. vichodcevskyi

Acer marsicum f. vichodcevskyi, commonly known as the Vichodcevsky's maple, is a rare and unique variant of the maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features bright red and orange leaves that change color with the seasons. The plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
