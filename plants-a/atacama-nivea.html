Atacama nivea

Atacama nivea, commonly known as the Atacama snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with its succulent leaves and deep root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the Atacama Desert ecosystem.
