Artemisia campanulata

Artemisia campanulata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads that resemble mist. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic, often pungent scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Artemisia campanulata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
