Adenosacme longifolia var. sinensis

Adenosacme longifolia var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, elongated fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
