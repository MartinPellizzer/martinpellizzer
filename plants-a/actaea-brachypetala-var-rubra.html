Actaea brachypetala var. rubra

Actaea brachypetala var. rubra, commonly known as red-tailed bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, lacecap flowers in the summer, which are surrounded by an array of petal-like bracts. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, typically green with a reddish tint.

It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.
