Antimima papillata

Antimima papillata, commonly known as the hairy ant plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and specialized structures that aid in trapping prey. The plant's botanical features include modified leaves that function as pitfall traps, which are covered in fine, hair-like structures. Antimima papillata thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its morphology reflects adaptations for both photosynthesis and nutrient acquisition through insect capture.
