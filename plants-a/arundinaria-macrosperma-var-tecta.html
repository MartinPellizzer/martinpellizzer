Arundinaria macrosperma var. tecta

Arundinaria macrosperma var. tecta, commonly known as the Chinese cliff bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its graceful appearance.
