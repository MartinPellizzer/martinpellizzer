Angelica sachalinensis var. pubescens

Angelica sachalinensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Sakhalin angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, forested areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which are small and white, bloom in late summer and are followed by small, dry fruits.
