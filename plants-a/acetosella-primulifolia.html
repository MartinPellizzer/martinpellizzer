Acetosella primulifolia

Acetosella primulifolia, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats. It is part of the Primulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

Acetosella primulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
