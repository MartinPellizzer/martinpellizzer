Atriplex carnosa

Atriplex carnosa, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Atriplex carnosa is often found in saline soils and is adapted to high levels of salt and drought.

Its ability to thrive in such conditions makes it an important plant in ecological restoration and erosion control.
