Arisaema yanxianum

Arisaema yanxianum, commonly known as the Yanxian arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and are often a deep green with a textured surface.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into berries, which are toxic and should not be consumed.
