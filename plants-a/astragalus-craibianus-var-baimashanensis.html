Astragalus craibianus var. baimashanensis

Astragalus craibianus var. baimashanensis, commonly known as Baimashan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
