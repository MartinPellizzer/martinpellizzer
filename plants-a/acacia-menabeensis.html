Acacia menabeensis

Acacia menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are modified leaf stalks that function as leaves, and these are typically narrow and linear. Acacia menabeensis is adapted to the dry, arid conditions of its native habitat, showing resilience to drought.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
