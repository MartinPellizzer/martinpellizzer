Aconitum napellus subsp. firmum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

firmum, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and have a waxy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a potent and dangerous plant in the wild.
