Acacia lutea

Acacia lutea, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia lutea thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
