Arida arizonica

Arida arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky areas or along stream beds.

Arida arizonica plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a symbol of the desert's resilience.
