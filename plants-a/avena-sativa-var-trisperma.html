Avena sativa var. trisperma

Avena sativa var. trisperma, commonly known as the three-awn oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its unique inflorescence.

It features a panicle with three awns on each spikelet, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in forage and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature.

The botanical features of Avena sativa var. trisperma make it an interesting subject for both ecological and agricultural studies.
