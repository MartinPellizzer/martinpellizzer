Aristolochia passiflorifolia

Aristolochia passiflorifolia, commonly known as the Passionflower Vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, complex flowers that resemble the Passion of Christ, featuring a distinctive corona of petal-like structures. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark blue berries. Its botanical features include a tuberous root system and a strong, woody vine that can climb up to several meters in height.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
