Abies eremita

Abies eremita, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in cloud forests at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, Abies eremita is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
