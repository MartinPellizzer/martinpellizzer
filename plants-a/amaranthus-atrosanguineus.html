Amaranthus atrosanguineus

Amaranthus atrosanguineus, commonly known as black amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish to reddish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, triangular seed capsules that contain tiny, edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its leaves are also used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
