Ardisia cornudentata var. morrisonensis

Ardisia cornudentata var. morrisonensis, commonly known as the Morrison's beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces striking clusters of bright purple berries, which are a notable feature in its botanical description. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
