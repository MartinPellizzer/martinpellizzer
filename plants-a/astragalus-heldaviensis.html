Astragalus heldaviensis

Astragalus heldaviensis, commonly known as the Heldavi's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astragalus heldaviensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
