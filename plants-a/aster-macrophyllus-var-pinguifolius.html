Aster macrophyllus var. pinguifolius

Aster macrophyllus var. pinguifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 10 inches in length. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
