Alchemilla geranioides

Alchemilla geranioides, commonly known as silver woundwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a dense mat of silver-gray foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla geranioides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
