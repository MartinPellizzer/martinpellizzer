Armeria plantaginea subsp. bupleuroides

Armeria plantaginea subsp.

bupleuroides, commonly known as the bluebell armeria, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the western United States. It features dense clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and attractive floral display make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
