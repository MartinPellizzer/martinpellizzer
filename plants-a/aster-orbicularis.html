Aster orbicularis

Aster orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive round, orb-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is part of the Asteraceae family. It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
