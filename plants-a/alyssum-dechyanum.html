Alyssum dechyanum

Alyssum dechyanum, commonly known as the Armenian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. Alyssum dechyanum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Its delicate appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
