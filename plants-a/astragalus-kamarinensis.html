Astragalus kamarinensis

Astragalus kamarinensis, commonly known as the Kharan mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss and overgrazing, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
