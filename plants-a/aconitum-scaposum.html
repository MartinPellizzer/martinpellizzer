Aconitum scaposum

Aconitum scaposum, commonly known as the tall monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that supports its showy blooms, which typically appear in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive arrangement of leaflets that are often deeply lobed.

Aconitum scaposum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
