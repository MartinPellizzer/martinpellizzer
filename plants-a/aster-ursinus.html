Aster ursinus

Aster ursinus, commonly known as the bear's breech, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are tuberous and used in traditional medicine.

Aster ursinus thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
