Acacia chapmanii

Acacia chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow floral spikes that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia chapmanii is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its resilience to drought.
