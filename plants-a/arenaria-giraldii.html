Arenaria giraldii

Arenaria giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are usually succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture.

Arenaria giraldii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations.
