Aristida orizabensis

Aristida orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican dropseed, is a grass species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are usually pink or red, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Aristida orizabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
