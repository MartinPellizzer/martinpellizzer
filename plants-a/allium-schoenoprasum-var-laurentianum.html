Allium schoenoprasum var. laurentianum

Allium schoenoprasum var. laurentianum, commonly known as the Laurentian chive, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is a member of the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is valued for its aromatic leaves used in culinary applications.

This variety is often found in the boreal and subarctic regions of Canada and the northern United States.
