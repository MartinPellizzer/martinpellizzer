Astragalus sungpanensis

Astragalus sungpanensis, commonly known as the Sungpan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
