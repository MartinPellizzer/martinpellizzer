Aralia humboldtii

Aralia humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features compound leaves that are typically pinnately arranged, with a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shade gardens.
