Acer dittrichii

Acer dittrichii, commonly known as the Himalayan maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows at high altitudes, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate regions.
