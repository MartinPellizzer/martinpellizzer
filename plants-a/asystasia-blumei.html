Asystasia blumei

Asystasia blumei, commonly known as the Blue Trumpet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asystasia blumei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
