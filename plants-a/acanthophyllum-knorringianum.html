Acanthophyllum knorringianum

Acanthophyllum knorringianum, commonly known as the Knorring's spiny broom, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with succulent stems that help it retain water in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its resilience, it plays an important role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
