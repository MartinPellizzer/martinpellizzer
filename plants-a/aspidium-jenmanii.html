Aspidium jenmanii

Aspidium jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately compound fronds that are adapted for life in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Aspidium jenmanii plays a role in maintaining water quality and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
