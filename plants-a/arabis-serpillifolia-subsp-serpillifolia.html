Arabis serpillifolia subsp. serpillifolia

Arabis serpillifolia subsp.

serpillifolia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
