Albuca brevipes

Albuca brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Albuca brevipes is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
