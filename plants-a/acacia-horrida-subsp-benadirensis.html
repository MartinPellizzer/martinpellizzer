Acacia horrida subsp. benadirensis

Acacia horrida subsp.

benadirensis, commonly known as the Benadire acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
