Agave filamentosa

Agave filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, thread-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces an upright flowering stem that bears small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by clusters of fleshy, blue-green berries. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave filamentosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and drought tolerance.
