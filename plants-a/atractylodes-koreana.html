Atractylodes koreana

Atractylodes koreana, commonly known as Korean ginger or Korean atracylodes, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flower heads clustered in composite inflorescences. Atractylodes koreana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Korean medicine.

It is also used in herbal formulations for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
