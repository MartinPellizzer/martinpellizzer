Allium ampeloprasum var. commutatum

Allium ampeloprasum var. commutatum, commonly known as the Egyptian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, slender, hollow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces umbels of small, white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a cultivated crop in some regions.

It is often grown for both culinary use and ornamental purposes in gardens.
