Anagallis serpyllifolia

Anagallis serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with opposite, hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange spot at the base. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Anagallis serpyllifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and vegetative reproduction.
