Abarema oxyphyllidia

Abarema oxyphyllidia, commonly known as the pink-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods that split open to release the seeds.

Abarema oxyphyllidia is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
