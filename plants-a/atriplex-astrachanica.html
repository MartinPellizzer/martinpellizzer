Atriplex astrachanica

Atriplex astrachanica, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is highly adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization and reclamation. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.

Atriplex astrachanica plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
