Amygdalus communis f. variegata

Amygdalus communis f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated almond.

This plant is a compact shrub or small tree with striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. It is a cultivar of the common almond, known for its ornamental value rather than its fruit production. The leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin, and the plant produces small white flowers in the spring.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
