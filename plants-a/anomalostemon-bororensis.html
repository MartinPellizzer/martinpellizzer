Anomalostemon bororensis

Anomalostemon bororensis, commonly known as the Bororo mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to retain water in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
