Acer campestre f. obtusum

Acer campestre f. obtusum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically smooth-edged, distinguishing it from other maple species. The plant is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
