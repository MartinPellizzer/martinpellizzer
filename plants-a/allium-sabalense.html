Allium sabalense

Allium sabalense, commonly known as the Florida onion, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, bulbous stem and is often found in wetland habitats. It has a strong, pungent odor similar to other allium species.

Botanically, it is classified within the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants.
