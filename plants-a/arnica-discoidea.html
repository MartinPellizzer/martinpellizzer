Arnica discoidea

Arnica discoidea, commonly known as the disked tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has a creeping, herbaceous growth habit with hairy stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Arnica discoidea is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
