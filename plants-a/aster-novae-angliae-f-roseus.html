Aster novae-angliae f. roseus

Aster novae-angliae f. roseus, commonly known as the Rose-flowered New England Aster, is a cultivar of the New England aster, characterized by its showy, rose-purple flower heads.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring sturdy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces composite flowers with purple to pink florets, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
