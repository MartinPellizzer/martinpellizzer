Acacia poliochroa

Acacia poliochroa, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often feathery and composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia poliochroa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
