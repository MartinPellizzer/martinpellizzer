Amaryllis × crispiflora

Amaryllis × crispiflora, commonly known as the curly leaf amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis recurva.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant produces strap-like, wavy leaves that are a distinguishing feature of its foliage. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Amaryllis × crispiflora is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
