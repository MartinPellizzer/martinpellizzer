Amaranthus perennis

Amaranthus perennis, commonly known as the common amaranth, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae).

It is an annual or perennial herb native to the Americas, characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are small and dry, often used as a grain or for bird food.

Amaranthus perennis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
