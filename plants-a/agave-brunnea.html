Agave brunnea

Agave brunnea, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears clusters of yellow flowers, which appear in late spring or early summer. Agave brunnea is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
