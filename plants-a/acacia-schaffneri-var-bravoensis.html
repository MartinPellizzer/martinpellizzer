Acacia schaffneri var. bravoensis

Acacia schaffneri var. bravoensis, commonly known as the Bravo acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
