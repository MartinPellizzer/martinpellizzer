Aporuellia australis

Aporuellia australis, commonly known as the Australian thyme-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Aporuellia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
