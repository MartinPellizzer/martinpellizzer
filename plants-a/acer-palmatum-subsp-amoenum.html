Acer palmatum subsp. amoenum

Acer palmatum subsp.

amoenum, commonly known as the Japanese maple 'Amoenum', is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its finely divided, emerald-green leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with a rounded, compact form. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include smooth, gray bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring.
