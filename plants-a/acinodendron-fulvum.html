Acinodendron fulvum

Acinodendron fulvum, commonly known as the golden acacia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with slender, pale yellow to golden-green leaflets that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

Acinodendron fulvum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
