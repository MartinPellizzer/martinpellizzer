Alchornea similis

Alchornea similis, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, semi-deciduous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
