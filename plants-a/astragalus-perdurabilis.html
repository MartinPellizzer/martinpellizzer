Astragalus perdurabilis

Astragalus perdurabilis, commonly known as the perennial milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow or pale green pea-like flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, typically with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets.

Astragalus perdurabilis is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species found in sandy or rocky soils.
