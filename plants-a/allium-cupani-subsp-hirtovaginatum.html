Allium cupani subsp. hirtovaginatum

Allium cupani subsp.

hirtovaginatum, commonly known as the hairy-ventured garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic.

The plant features bulbous stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical flower clusters. Its botanical characteristics include hairy structures on the leaf sheaths and a distinctive garlic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
