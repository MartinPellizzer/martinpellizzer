Avena strigosa subsp. mandoniana

Avena strigosa subsp.

mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
