Alcantarea glaucifolia

Alcantarea glaucifolia, commonly known as the Blue-leafed Alocasia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange, which stand out against the foliage. It thrives in humid, bright environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Alcantarea glaucifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental leaves and relatively low maintenance requirements.
