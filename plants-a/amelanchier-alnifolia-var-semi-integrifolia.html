Amelanchier alnifolia var. semi-integrifolia

Amelanchier alnifolia var. semi-integrifolia, commonly known as the Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, transitioning from a smooth base to toothed edges along the leaf blade. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a valuable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
