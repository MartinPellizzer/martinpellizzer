Allium pythagoricum

Allium pythagoricum, commonly known as the Pythagorean garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces spherical bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its flowers consist of clusters of small, white or pinkish blossoms.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
