Alchemilla tirolensis

Alchemilla tirolensis, commonly known as the Alpine lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Alchemilla tirolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
