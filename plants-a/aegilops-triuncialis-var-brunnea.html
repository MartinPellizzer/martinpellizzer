Aegilops triuncialis var. brunnea

Aegilops triuncialis var. brunnea, commonly known as the black wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential in agricultural and ecological applications.
