Astragalus tegetarius var. viridis

Astragalus tegetarius var. viridis, commonly known as green milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and shrubland habitats.

Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
