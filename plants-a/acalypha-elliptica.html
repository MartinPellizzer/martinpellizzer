Acalypha elliptica

Acalypha elliptica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted ironplant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are often a striking red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which helps it store water in arid conditions.

Acalypha elliptica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
