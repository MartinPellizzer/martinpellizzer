Adhatoda lindeniana

Adhatoda lindeniana, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Adhatoda lindeniana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
