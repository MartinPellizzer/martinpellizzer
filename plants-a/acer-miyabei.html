Acer miyabei

Acer miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow catkins that appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens and parks.
