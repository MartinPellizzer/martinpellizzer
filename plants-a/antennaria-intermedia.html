Antennaria intermedia

Antennaria intermedia, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Antennaria intermedia is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
