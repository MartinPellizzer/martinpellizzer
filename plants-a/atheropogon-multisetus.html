Atheropogon multisetus

Atheropogon multisetus, commonly known as the many-bristled grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
