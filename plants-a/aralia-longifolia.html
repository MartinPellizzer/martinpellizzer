Aralia longifolia

Aralia longifolia, commonly known as the American aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are palmately arranged, with each leaf consisting of 5 to 7 elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Aralia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
