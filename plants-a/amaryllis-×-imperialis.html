Amaryllis × imperialis

Amaryllis × imperialis, commonly known as the imperial amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisiaca.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and sturdy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

The blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
