Ammannia crassissima

Ammannia crassissima, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a reddish hue on the underside, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Ammannia crassissima is known for its ability to thrive in moist soil conditions and is often found in swampy or saturated habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important part of wetland ecosystems.
