Acacia adinophylla

Acacia adinophylla, commonly known as the desert thorn acacia, is a drought-deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny branches and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Acacia adinophylla is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in its native ecosystem.
