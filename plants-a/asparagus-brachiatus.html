Asparagus brachiatus

Asparagus brachiatus, commonly known as the branched asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous narrow, feather-like leaflets.

Asparagus brachiatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
