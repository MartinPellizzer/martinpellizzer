Acacia chapmanii subsp. australis

Acacia chapmanii subsp.

australis, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting a variety of pollinators.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves, and it is adapted to coastal environments with sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant for coastal gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
