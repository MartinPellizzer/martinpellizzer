Abronia menziesii

Abronia menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastline, where it thrives in the mild, moist climate. It produces distinctive seed pods that enclose the seeds, a feature common in the mustard family.

Abronia menziesii is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and is often found in dune environments.
