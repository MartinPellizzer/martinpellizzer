Amberboa biennis

Amberboa biennis, commonly known as the goldenweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 30 centimeters in height, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

Amberboa biennis is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas.
