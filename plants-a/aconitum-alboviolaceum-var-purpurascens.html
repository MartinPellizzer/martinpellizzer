Aconitum alboviolaceum var. purpurascens

Aconitum alboviolaceum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-tinged aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a deep green color and a velvety texture.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
