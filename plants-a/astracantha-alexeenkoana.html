Astracantha alexeenkoana

Astracantha alexeenkoana, commonly known as the Alexander's thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in botanical studies, particularly in regions with limited vegetation.

Due to its rarity, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
