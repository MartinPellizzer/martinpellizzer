Agropyron cristatum f. villosum

Agropyron cristatum f. villosum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the colder regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic environments.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
