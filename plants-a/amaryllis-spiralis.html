Amaryllis spiralis

Amaryllis spiralis, commonly known as the spiral amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of funnel-shaped, white or pink flowers that spiral upward in a unique, helical pattern. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from the base and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Amaryllis spiralis is known for its long blooming period, often lasting several weeks during the spring and summer months.
