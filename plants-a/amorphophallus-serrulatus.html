Amorphophallus serrulatus

Amorphophallus serrulatus, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its tuberous roots are edible when properly cooked and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands in its natural habitat.
