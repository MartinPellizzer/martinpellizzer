Alopecurus fulvus

Alopecurus fulvus, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Alopecurus fulvus is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it adaptable to various ecological niches.
