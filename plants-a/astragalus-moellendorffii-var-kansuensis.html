Astragalus moellendorffii var. kansuensis

Astragalus moellendorffii var. kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of northern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
