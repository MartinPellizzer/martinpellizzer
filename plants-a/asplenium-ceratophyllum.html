Asplenium ceratophyllum

Asplenium ceratophyllum, commonly known as the horned spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to parts of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a slightly curled base. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium ceratophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
