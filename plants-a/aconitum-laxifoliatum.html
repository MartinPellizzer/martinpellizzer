Aconitum laxifoliatum

Aconitum laxifoliatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in a helmet-like shape characteristic of the genus Aconitum. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

However, all parts of the plant are highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, requiring caution during handling and cultivation.
