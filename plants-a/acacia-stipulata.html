Acacia stipulata

Acacia stipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, needle-like leaflets, and it produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring. Acacia stipulata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
