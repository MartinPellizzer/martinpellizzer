Acantholimon bodeanum subsp. pilosum

Acantholimon bodeanum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy stonecress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid conditions.
