Arnica alpina subsp. lonchophylla

Arnica alpina subsp.

lonchophylla, commonly known as the alpine tawnyflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky and open habitats at high altitudes.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in mountainous environments.
