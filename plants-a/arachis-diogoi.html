Arachis diogoi

Arachis diogoi, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds similar to those of the cultivated peanut. Arachis diogoi is notable for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural value.
