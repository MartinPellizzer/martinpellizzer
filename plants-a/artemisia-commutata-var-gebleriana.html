Artemisia commutata var. gebleriana

Artemisia commutata var. gebleriana, commonly known as the Gebler's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has finely divided, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

Its chemical composition includes volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
