Acer × lobatum

Acer × lobatum, commonly known as the Norway maple, is a deciduous tree native to central and northern Europe.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between *Acer saccharum* (sugar maple) and *Acer pseudoplatanus* (European maple). The tree is characterized by its opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. It typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is widely cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
