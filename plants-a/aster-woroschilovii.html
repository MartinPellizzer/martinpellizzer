Aster woroschilovii

Aster woroschilovii, commonly known as the Woroschilov's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its vibrant purple or blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern in its native habitat.
