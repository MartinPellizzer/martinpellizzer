Avellinia michelii var. warionis

Avellinia michelii var. warionis, commonly known as the Mexican hazelnut, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible nuts enclosed in a prickly husk. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are catkin-like.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a food source for wildlife.
