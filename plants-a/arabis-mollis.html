Arabis mollis

Arabis mollis, commonly known as hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.
