Aira cespitosa var. minor

Aira cespitosa var. minor, commonly known as slender false oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
