Amorphophallus mullendersii

Amorphophallus mullendersii, commonly known as the Mullender's arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a distinctive, often foul-smelling flower that attracts specific pollinators. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or swamps.
