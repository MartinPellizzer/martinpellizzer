Acanthocereus undulosus

Acanthocereus undulosus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering cactus native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a single night, and are pollinated by moths and bats.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
