Arnica ciliata

Arnica ciliata, commonly known as the fringed Arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disks and ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Arnica ciliata thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native environment.
