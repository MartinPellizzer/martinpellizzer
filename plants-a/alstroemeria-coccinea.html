Alstroemeria coccinea

Alstroemeria coccinea, commonly known as the red lily of the Incas, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with orange and yellow markings. The plant features strap-like, grassy foliage and produces clusters of tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which can persist for several weeks.
