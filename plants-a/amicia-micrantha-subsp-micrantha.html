Amicia micrantha subsp. micrantha

Amicia micrantha subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amicia, is a flowering plant species native to parts of southern Africa. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It is often found in grassland habitats and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and specialized species.
