Alsomitra brasiliensis var. pubescens

Alsomitra brasiliensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the humid climate. Its leaves are elongated and often covered with a fine, velvety pubescence, which gives it its varietal name.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
