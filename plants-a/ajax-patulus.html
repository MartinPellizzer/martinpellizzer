Ajax patulus

Ajax patulus, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that resemble arrows, often floating on the water's surface or partially submerged. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its water habitat. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in shallow waters.

Ajax patulus is often used in water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
