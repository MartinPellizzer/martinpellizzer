Ammannia rubra

Ammannia rubra, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia rubra is an annual species that produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
