Adenophora kurilensis

Adenophora kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the northern Pacific region, including parts of Russia, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

Adenophora kurilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
