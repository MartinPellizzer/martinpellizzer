Anthemis tenuiloba

Anthemis tenuiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
