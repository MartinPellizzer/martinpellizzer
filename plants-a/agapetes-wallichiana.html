Agapetes wallichiana

Agapetes wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan silver fir, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Agapetes wallichiana plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native range, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
