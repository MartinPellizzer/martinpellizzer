Agave filifera var. filamentosa

Agave filifera var. filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and narrow, thread-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
