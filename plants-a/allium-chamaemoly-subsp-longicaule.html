Allium chamaemoly subsp. longicaule

Allium chamaemoly subsp.

longicaule, commonly known as the tall garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant produces bulbils along its stems, which are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its culinary use is limited, but it is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
