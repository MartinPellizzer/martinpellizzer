Anthericum anceps

Anthericum anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Anthericum anceps is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops.
