Areca riparia

Areca riparia, commonly known as the Indian areca or the betel palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. Areca riparia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are sometimes used in the preparation of betel quids, which are chewed for their stimulant effects.
