Avena sikkimensis

Avena sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim oat, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Avena sikkimensis is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
