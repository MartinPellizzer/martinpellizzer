Acacia atrox subsp. atrox

Acacia atrox subsp.

atrox, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves with phyllodes that are narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is used in horticulture and for soil stabilization.
