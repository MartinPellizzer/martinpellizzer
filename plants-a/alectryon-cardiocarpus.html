Alectryon cardiocarpus

Alectryon cardiocarpus, commonly known as the heartnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive heart-shaped fruits that are red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
