Acacia cineraria

Acacia cineraria, commonly known as the cineraria acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes around the world.
