Adenophora gmelinii var. coronopifolia

Adenophora gmelinii var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownvane bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.
