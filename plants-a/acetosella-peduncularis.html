Acetosella peduncularis

Acetosella peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its rosette of oval, hairy leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Acetosella peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
