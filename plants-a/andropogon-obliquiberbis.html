Andropogon obliquiberbis

Andropogon obliquiberbis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and upright growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and open woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
