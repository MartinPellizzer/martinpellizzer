Adiantum delicatulum

Adiantum delicatulum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Adiantum delicatulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid indoor setting.
