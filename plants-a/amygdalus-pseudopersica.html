Amygdalus pseudopersica

Amygdalus pseudopersica, commonly known as the Persian almond.

It is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by almond-like fruits that are typically harvested in the autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
