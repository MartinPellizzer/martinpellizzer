Astragalus plumatus

Astragalus plumatus, commonly known as the plumose milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, soft, plumose (feathery) leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in prairies and sagebrush habitats.

Astragalus plumatus is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
