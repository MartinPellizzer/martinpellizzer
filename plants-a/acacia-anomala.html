Acacia anomala

Acacia anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes.

Acacia anomala is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
