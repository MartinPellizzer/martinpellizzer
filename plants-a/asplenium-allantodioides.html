Asplenium allantodioides

Asplenium allantodioides, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium allantodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
