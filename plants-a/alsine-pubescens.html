Alsine pubescens

Alsine pubescens, commonly known as hairy alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Alsine pubescens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
