Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. racemosus

Amaranthus hypochondriacus var. racemosus, commonly known as the Mexican love plant, is a species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are edible and often used in traditional cuisines, while the flowers are popular in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and iron.
