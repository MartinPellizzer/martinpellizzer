Anthyllis spruneri subsp. iberica

Anthyllis spruneri subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian anthyllis, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. Anthyllis spruneri subsp.

iberica is adapted to Mediterranean climates and plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
