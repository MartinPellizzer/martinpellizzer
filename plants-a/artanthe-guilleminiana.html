Artanthe guilleminiana

Artanthe guilleminiana, commonly known as the Guilleminian's artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clustered, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Artanthe guilleminiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
