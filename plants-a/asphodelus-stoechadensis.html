Asphodelus stoechadensis

Asphodelus stoechadensis, commonly known as the Scottish asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It typically grows in coastal and calcareous grasslands, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asphodelus stoechadensis is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a characteristic species of its habitat.
