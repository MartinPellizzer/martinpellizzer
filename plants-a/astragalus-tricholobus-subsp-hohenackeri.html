Astragalus tricholobus subsp. hohenackeri

Astragalus tricholobus subsp.

hohenackeri, commonly known as Hohenacker's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in harsh, arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation to extreme environments.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its medicinal properties remain under investigation.
