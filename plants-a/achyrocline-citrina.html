Achyrocline citrina

Achyrocline citrina, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns resembling those of the passionflower genus. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Achyrocline citrina is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
