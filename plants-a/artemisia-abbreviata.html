Artemisia abbreviata

Artemisia abbreviata, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Artemisia abbreviata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
