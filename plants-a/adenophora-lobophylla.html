Adenophora lobophylla

Adenophora lobophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora lobophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
