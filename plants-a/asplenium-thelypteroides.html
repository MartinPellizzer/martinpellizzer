Asplenium thelypteroides

Asplenium thelypteroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its fronds are usually dark green, with a slightly glossy appearance, and the sori are arranged in a single row along the margins of the pinnae.

Asplenium thelypteroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
