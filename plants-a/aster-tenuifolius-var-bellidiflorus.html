Aster tenuifolius var. bellidiflorus

Aster tenuifolius var. bellidiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
