Astragalus chionobius

Astragalus chionobius, commonly known as the snow astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.

Astragalus chionobius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
