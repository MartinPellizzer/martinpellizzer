Acnistus australis var. grandiflorus

Acnistus australis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered acnistus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
