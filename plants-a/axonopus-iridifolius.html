Axonopus iridifolius

Axonopus iridifolius, commonly known as the blue-green grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish or silver sheen. The plant typically forms a mat-like growth pattern, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded or moist environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high humidity.

Axonopus iridifolius is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weed growth.
