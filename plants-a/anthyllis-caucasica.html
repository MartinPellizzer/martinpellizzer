Anthyllis caucasica

Anthyllis caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis caucasica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
