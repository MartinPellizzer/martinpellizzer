Aster foliaceus var. frondeus

Aster foliaceus var. frondeus, commonly known as the Fronded Aster, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are narrow and deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and modest floral display.
