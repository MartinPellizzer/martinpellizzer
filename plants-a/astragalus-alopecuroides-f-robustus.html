Astragalus alopecuroides f. robustus

Astragalus alopecuroides f. robustus, commonly known as the robust hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
