Acacia pseudofistula

Acacia pseudofistula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Acacia pseudofistula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
