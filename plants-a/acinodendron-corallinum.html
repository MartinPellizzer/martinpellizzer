Acinodendron corallinum

Acinodendron corallinum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near riverbanks.

Acinodendron corallinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
