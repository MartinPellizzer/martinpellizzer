Aster sagittifolius var. dissitiflorus

Aster sagittifolius var. dissitiflorus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a zigzag pattern along the stem. Its flowering heads consist of numerous small ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
