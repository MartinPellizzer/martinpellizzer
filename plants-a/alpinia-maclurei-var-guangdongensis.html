Alpinia maclurei var. guangdongensis

Alpinia maclurei var. guangdongensis, commonly known as the Guangdong ginger, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the Alpinia genus.

It is native to southern China, particularly Guangdong province, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spikes, and are followed by small, triangular fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in botanical studies.
