Astragalus palliseri

Astragalus palliseri, commonly known as the Palliser's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
