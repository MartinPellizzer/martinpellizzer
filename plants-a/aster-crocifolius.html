Aster crocifolius

Aster crocifolius, commonly known as the leafy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Aster crocifolius thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It is a perennial plant that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats in its natural habitat.
