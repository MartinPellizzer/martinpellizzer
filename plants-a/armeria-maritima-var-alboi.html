Armeria maritima var. alboi

Armeria maritima var. alboi, commonly known as white sandpink, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to coastal areas of the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
