Astragalus americanus f. williamsii

Astragalus americanus f. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
