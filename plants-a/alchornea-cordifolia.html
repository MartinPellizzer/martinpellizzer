Alchornea cordifolia

Alchornea cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alchornea cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and dye production.
