Anotites menziesii

Anotites menziesii, commonly known as the western water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively.

Anotites menziesii plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
