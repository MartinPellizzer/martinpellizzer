Acroglochin persicarioides var. multiflora

Acroglochin persicarioides var. multiflora, commonly known as Chinese blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
