Astragalus aureus f. bracteolatus

Astragalus aureus f. bracteolatus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is native to regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant has compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
