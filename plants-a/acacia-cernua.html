Acacia cernua

Acacia cernua, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Acacia cernua thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
