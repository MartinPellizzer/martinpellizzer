Anthemis triumfetti subsp. cossoniana

Anthemis triumfetti subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow with reddish-brown markings, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
