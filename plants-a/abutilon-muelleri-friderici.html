Abutilon muelleri-friderici

Abutilon muelleri-friderici, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
