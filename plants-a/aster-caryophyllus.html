Aster caryophyllus

Aster caryophyllus, commonly known as sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are often used in perfumery due to their aromatic properties.
