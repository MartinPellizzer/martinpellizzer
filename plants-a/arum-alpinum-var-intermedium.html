Arum alpinum var. intermedium

Arum alpinum var. intermedium, commonly known as the Alpine arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is often mottled with distinctive patterns of green and white. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a spathe, a modified leaf that resembles a flower. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
