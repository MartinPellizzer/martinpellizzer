Aeridostachya decurrentipetala

Aeridostachya decurrentipetala, commonly known as the Decurrent-petaled Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces intricate flowers with a distinctive decurrent petal structure, which gives it its scientific name. Its blooming period typically occurs during the late spring to early summer, adding a unique floral element to its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
