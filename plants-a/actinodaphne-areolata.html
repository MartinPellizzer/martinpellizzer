Actinodaphne areolata

Actinodaphne areolata, commonly known as the fragrant laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
