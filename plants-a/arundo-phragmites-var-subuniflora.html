Arundo phragmites var. subuniflora

Arundo phragmites var. subuniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland environments.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its slender, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found along riverbanks, marshes, and other wet areas.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
