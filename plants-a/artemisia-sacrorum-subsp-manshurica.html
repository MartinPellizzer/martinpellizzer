Artemisia sacrorum subsp. manshurica

Artemisia sacrorum subsp.

manshurica, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant features silvery-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, and its leaves are aromatic when crushed. It produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
