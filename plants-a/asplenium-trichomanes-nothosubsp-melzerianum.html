Asplenium trichomanes nothosubsp. melzerianum

Asplenium trichomanes nothosubsp.

melzerianum, commonly known as the Melzer's shield fern, is a rare and hybrid fern species characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. It features lanceolate, serrated fronds that are typically dark green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by indusia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and hybrid origin, it is considered a botanical curiosity with significant horticultural interest.
