Archaeocarex spartea

Archaeocarex spartea, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.
