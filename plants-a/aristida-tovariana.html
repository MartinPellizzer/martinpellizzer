Aristida tovariana

Aristida tovariana, commonly known as the Tovar's threeawn, is a grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, wiry stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow panicle made up of numerous spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Aristida tovariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
