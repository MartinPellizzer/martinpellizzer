Atriplex hyrcana

Atriplex hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid and saline environments. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions. Its small greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds.

Atriplex hyrcana plays a role in stabilizing soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
