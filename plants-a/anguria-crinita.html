Anguria crinita

Anguria crinita, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Anguria crinita is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
