Asterolasia muricata

Asterolasia muricata, commonly known as the rough-leaved asterolasia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and diverse growth forms. The plant typically has hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces small, clustered yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Asterolasia muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
