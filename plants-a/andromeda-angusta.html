Andromeda angusta

Andromeda angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that remain throughout the winter, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Andromeda angusta thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
