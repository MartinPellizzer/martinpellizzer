Arachnaria adolphi

Arachnaria adolphi, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that resemble the intricate patterns of spider webs, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a unique, linear pattern along the undersides of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Arachnaria adolphi is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
