Aegiphila cuneata

Aegiphila cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Aegiphila cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
