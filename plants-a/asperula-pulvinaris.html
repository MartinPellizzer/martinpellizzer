Asperula pulvinaris

Asperula pulvinaris, commonly known as the pulvinate alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Asperula pulvinaris thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
