Avenastrum nervosum

Avenastrum nervosum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Avenastrum nervosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
