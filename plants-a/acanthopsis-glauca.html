Acanthopsis glauca

Acanthopsis glauca, commonly known as the blue acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray leaves with a woolly texture. It produces cylindrical flower heads that are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Acanthopsis glauca is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun.
