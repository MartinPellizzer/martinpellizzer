Athamanta decussata

Athamanta decussata, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often arranged in a decussate pattern, which gives the species its scientific name.

Athamanta decussata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
