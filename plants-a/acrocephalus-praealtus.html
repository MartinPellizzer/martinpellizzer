Acrocephalus praealtus

Acrocephalus praealtus, commonly known as the tall reed warbler, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with standing water. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.

Its flowering heads are small and clustered, contributing to its ecological significance in riparian zones.
