Asplenium harrisonii

Asplenium harrisonii, commonly known as Harrison's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rock crevices and forest floors. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with closely packed, lance-shaped leaves. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are typically dark green and glossy.

Asplenium harrisonii is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its unique, delicate appearance.
