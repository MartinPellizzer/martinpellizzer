Amygdalus wendelboi

Amygdalus wendelboi, commonly known as the Kashmir almond, is a species of Prunus native to the Himalayas.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows at high altitudes. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
