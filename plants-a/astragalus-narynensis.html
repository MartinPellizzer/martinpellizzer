Astragalus narynensis

Astragalus narynensis, commonly known as the Naryn milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus narynensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
