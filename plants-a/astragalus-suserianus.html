Astragalus suserianus

Astragalus suserianus, commonly known as the Suserian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers, which is crucial for its survival in dry climates.

Astragalus suserianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
