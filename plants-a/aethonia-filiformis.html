Aethonia filiformis

Aethonia filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Aethonia filiformis thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its unique morphology makes it a distinctive and resilient species in its native ecosystem.
