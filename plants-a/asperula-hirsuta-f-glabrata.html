Asperula hirsuta f. glabrata

Asperula hirsuta f. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous asperula, is a variant of the common asperula, characterized by its reduced pubescence.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. The plant features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to ornamental horticulture.
