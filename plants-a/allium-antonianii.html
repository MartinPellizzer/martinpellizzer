Allium antonianii

Allium antonianii, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and showy, umbel-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Allium antonianii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
