Arrabidaea formosa

Arrabidaea formosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, woody pods that contain seeds. The plant is known for its ornamental value, with its striking red flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
