Andropogon nyassae

Andropogon nyassae, commonly known as African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Andropogon nyassae is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
