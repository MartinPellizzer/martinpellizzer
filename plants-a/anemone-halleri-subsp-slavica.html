Anemone halleri subsp. slavica

Anemone halleri subsp.

slavica, commonly known as the Slavonian anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkans. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, white to pink flowers with prominent yellow centers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
