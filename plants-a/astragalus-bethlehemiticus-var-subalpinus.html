Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. subalpinus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. subalpinus, commonly known as the subalpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, usually yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, typically with three leaflets, and it is adapted to grow in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
