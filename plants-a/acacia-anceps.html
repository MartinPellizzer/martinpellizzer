Acacia anceps

Acacia anceps, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn a striking silver-white color during the dry season. The plant produces small yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by long, flat pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

Acacia anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
