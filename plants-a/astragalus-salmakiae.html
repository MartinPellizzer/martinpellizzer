Astragalus salmakiae

Astragalus salmakiae, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Astragalus salmakiae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.
