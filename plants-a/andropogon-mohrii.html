Andropogon mohrii

Andropogon mohrii, commonly known as Mohri's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, wiry leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Andropogon genus.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
