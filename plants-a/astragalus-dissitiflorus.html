Astragalus dissitiflorus

Astragalus dissitiflorus, commonly known as the scattered-flower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus dissitiflorus is often found in steppe and desert habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
