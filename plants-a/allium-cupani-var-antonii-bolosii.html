Allium cupani var. antonii-bolosii

Allium cupani var. antonii-bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosii garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its distinctive garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for its hardiness and visual appeal.
