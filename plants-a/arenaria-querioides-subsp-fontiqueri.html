Arenaria querioides subsp. fontiqueri

Arenaria querioides subsp.

fontiqueri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its habitat. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
