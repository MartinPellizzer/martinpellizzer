Aspidium bamlerianum

Aspidium bamlerianum, commonly known as the Bamler's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a light green coloration. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Aspidium bamlerianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
