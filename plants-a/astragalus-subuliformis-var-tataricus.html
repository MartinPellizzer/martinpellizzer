Astragalus subuliformis var. tataricus

Astragalus subuliformis var. tataricus, commonly known as the Tatar milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and semi-desert environments. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
