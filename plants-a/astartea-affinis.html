Astartea affinis

Astartea affinis, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Astartea affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
