Argyreia sikkimensis

Argyreia sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim silver vine, is a climbing plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by showy, papery bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
