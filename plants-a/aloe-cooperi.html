Aloe cooperi

Aloe cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Aloe cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
