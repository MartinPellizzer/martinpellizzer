Allium virgunculae var. koshikiense

Allium virgunculae var. koshikiense, commonly known as the Koshiki garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to garlic and chives. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and for local food use.
