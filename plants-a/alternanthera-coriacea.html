Alternanthera coriacea

Alternanthera coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are often a vibrant red or purple in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alternanthera coriacea is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage.
