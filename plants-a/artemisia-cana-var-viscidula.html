Artemisia cana var. viscidula

Artemisia cana var. viscidula, commonly known as sticky blue sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with a sticky coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
