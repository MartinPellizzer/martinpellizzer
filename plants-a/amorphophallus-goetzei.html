Amorphophallus goetzei

Amorphophallus goetzei, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with a unique structure that includes a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native range.
