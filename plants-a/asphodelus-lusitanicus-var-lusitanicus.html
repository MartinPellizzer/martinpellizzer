Asphodelus lusitanicus var. lusitanicus

Asphodelus lusitanicus var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Iberian asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with pale yellow to orange flowers. It is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in areas with low rainfall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
