Actaea lancifoliolata

Actaea lancifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves with sharply toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are followed by red berries.

It thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
