Atriplex aldamae

Atriplex aldamae, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Atriplex aldamae plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
