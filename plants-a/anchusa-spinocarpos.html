Anchusa spinocarpos

Anchusa spinocarpos, commonly known as the spiked anchusa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has spiny, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Anchusa spinocarpos thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
