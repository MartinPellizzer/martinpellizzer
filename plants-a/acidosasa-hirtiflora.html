Acidosasa hirtiflora

Acidosasa hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can store water in its stems.

Acidosasa hirtiflora plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
