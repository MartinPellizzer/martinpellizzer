Alyssum canescens

Alyssum canescens, commonly known as woolly alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fuzzy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Alyssum canescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its use in both horticultural and ecological settings.
