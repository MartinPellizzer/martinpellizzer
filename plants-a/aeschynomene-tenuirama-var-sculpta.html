Aeschynomene tenuirama var. sculpta

Aeschynomene tenuirama var. sculpta, commonly known as the sculpted horsegram, is a variant of the horsegram plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual legume characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it useful for soil enrichment. It produces small, hard seeds that are often used as forage or in traditional medicine.

The varietal name "sculpta" refers to the intricate, sculpted appearance of its foliage.
