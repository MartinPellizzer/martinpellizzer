Allium xichuanense

Allium xichuanense, commonly known as the Xichuan garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
