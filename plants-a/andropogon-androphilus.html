Andropogon androphilus

Andropogon androphilus, commonly known as the purple bluestem, is a tall grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, blue-green foliage and striking purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet to mesic habitats, such as swamps and wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Andropogon androphilus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
