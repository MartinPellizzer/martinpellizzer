Amaryllis araripina subsp. conspicua

Amaryllis araripina subsp.

conspicua, commonly known as the Araripina amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to Brazil. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright red or orange with a contrasting white or yellow throat.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
