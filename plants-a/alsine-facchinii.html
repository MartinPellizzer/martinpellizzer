Alsine facchinii

Alsine facchinii, commonly known as the Mediterranean sandweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean flora and a subject of ecological study.
