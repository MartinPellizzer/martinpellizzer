Asyneuma mazanderanicum

Asyneuma mazanderanicum, commonly known as the Persian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct, often pungent scent. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in flat-topped inflorescences.

Asyneuma mazanderanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
