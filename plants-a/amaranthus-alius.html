Amaranthus alius

Amaranthus alius, commonly known as red amaranth, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage and erect, branched stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into three-angled, spiky seed heads. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, while the seeds are rich in protein and nutrients.

Amaranthus alius is also valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
