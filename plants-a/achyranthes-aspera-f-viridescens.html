Achyranthes aspera f. viridescens

Achyranthes aspera f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-stemmed horse radish, is a variant of the common horse radish.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branched stems that are typically greenish in color. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, spindle-shaped pods containing brown, kidney-shaped seeds.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
