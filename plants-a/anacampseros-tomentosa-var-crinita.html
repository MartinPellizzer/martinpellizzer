Anacampseros tomentosa var. crinita

Anacampseros tomentosa var. crinita, commonly known as the woolly stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, woolly leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
