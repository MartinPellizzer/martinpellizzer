Aster scopulorum

Aster scopulorum, commonly known as the rock aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments. The plant produces showy blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

Aster scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
