Asplenium auritum var. macilentum

Asplenium auritum var. macilentum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the shape of a ear, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid indoor settings.
