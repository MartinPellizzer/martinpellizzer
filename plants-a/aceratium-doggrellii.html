Aceratium doggrellii

Aceratium doggrellii, commonly known as the Doggrell's acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny calyces that enclose its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
