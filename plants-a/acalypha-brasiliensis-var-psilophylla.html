Acalypha brasiliensis var. psilophylla

Acalypha brasiliensis var. psilophylla, commonly known as the silkyleaf spurflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spire-like inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant display.
