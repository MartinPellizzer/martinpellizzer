Amaranthus blitoides var. nanus

Amaranthus blitoides var. nanus, commonly known as dwarf amaranth, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, clustered leaves and produces vibrant red, pink, or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its short stature, typically reaching only a few inches in height, making it ideal for rock gardens or container planting. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used as a ornamental plant for its striking color.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
