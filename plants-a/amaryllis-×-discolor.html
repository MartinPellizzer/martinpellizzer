Amaryllis × discolor

Amaryllis × discolor, commonly known as the pink and white amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis bellona and Amaryllis papillosa.

It is characterized by its striking, bicolor flowers that feature pink and white petals with a distinctive red or orange stripe along the edges. The plant produces large, showy blooms that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to encourage optimal growth and flowering.

This hybrid is often cultivated for its vibrant colors and ease of care, making it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
