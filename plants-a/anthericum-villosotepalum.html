Anthericum villosotepalum

Anthericum villosotepalum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved lantern flower, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lantern-shaped inflorescences that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red or orange. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anthericum villosotepalum is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to add visual interest to gardens.
