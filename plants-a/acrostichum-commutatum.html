Acrostichum commutatum

Acrostichum commutatum, commonly known as the common brake, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a creeping rhizome with upright fronds that are pinnately compound and covered in fine, silky hairs. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
