Anthyllis polycephala var. mesatlantica

Anthyllis polycephala var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along cliffs and dunes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It is adapted to withstand strong winds and salt spray, making it a characteristic species of maritime ecosystems.
