Astragalus zingeri

Astragalus zingeri, commonly known as Zinger's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Astragalus zingeri is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
