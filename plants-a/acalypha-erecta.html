Acalypha erecta

Acalypha erecta, commonly known as the upright or erect candleplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, showy bracts that resemble candles, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered within the bracts, which are often the main ornamental feature. Acalypha erecta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
