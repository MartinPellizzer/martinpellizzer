Acetosella puberula

Acetosella puberula, commonly known as hairy wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Acetosella puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
