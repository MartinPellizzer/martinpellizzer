Asclepias navicularis

Asclepias navicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its latex sap, which is milky and contains cardiac glycosides.

Asclepias navicularis serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly and supports a variety of pollinators.
