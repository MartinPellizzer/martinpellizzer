Amphiglottis verrucosa

Amphiglottis verrucosa, commonly known as the warty amphiglottis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty or swollen structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in umbels, which is a common feature in the Apiaceae family.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
