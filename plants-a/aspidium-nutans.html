Aspidium nutans

Aspidium nutans, commonly known as the nodding wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its drooping, fertile fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that help in vegetative propagation. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Aspidium nutans is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
