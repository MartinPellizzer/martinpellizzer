Aloe hyacinthoides var. guineensis

Aloe hyacinthoides var. guineensis, commonly known as the Guinea aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing clusters of small, tubular, yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
