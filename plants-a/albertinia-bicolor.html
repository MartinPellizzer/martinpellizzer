Albertinia bicolor

Albertinia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its strikingly contrasting coloration on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small to medium in size and often display a combination of white, yellow, and green hues.

Albertinia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
