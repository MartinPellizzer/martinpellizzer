Actinodaphne lanceolata

Actinodaphne lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in lowland rainforests.
