Alyssum montanum subsp. alibotuschiense

Alyssum montanum subsp.

alibotuschiense, commonly known as the Sierra Valley alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are usually sprawling or ascending.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
