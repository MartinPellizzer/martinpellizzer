Adhatoda acuminata

Adhatoda acuminata, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Adhatoda acuminata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
