Astragalus tarchankuticus

Astragalus tarchankuticus, commonly known as the Tarkhankut milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus tarchankuticus is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
