Archidendron scutiferum

Archidendron scutiferum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately compound, and its distinctive yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility.
