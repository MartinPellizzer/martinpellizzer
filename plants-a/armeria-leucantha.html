Armeria leucantha

Armeria leucantha, commonly known as the white thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has slender, upright stems and small, linear leaves.

Armeria leucantha is often used in rock gardens and as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
