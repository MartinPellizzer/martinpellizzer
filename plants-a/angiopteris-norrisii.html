Angiopteris norrisii

Angiopteris norrisii, commonly known as the Norris's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, frond-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Angiopteris norrisii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various microorganisms.
