Angraecum drouhardii

Angraecum drouhardii, commonly known as the Drouhard's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette, adapting to its arid native environment.

Angraecum drouhardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
