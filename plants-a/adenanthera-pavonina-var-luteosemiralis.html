Adenanthera pavonina var. luteosemiralis

Adenanthera pavonina var. luteosemiralis, commonly known as the yellow semi-leaf tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are often yellowish-green in color, and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
