Acaena pectinata

Acaena pectinata, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, characterized by its dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Acaena pectinata plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing food for various birds and insects.
