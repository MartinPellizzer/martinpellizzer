Astragalus granatensis subsp. maroccanus

Astragalus granatensis subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa, particularly Morocco. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens and landscapes.
