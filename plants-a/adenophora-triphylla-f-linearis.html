Adenophora triphylla f. linearis

Adenophora triphylla f. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is characterized by a spicate structure, with each flower having five fused petals.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
