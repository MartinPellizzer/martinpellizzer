Achillea millefolium var. pedemontana

Achillea millefolium var. pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, or reddish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.
