Amaryllis stylosa

Amaryllis stylosa, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis stylosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
