Asplenium fuscum

Asplenium fuscum, commonly known as the brown maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped fronds that are often tinged with brown at the edges. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium fuscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
