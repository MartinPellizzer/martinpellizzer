Achillea absinthifolia

Achillea absinthifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of absinthe is closely tied to its botanical characteristics.
