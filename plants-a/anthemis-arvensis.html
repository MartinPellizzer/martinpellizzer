Anthemis arvensis

Anthemis arvensis, commonly known as common tansy, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and botanical research.
