Abutilon ramosissimum

Abutilon ramosissimum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions. Its rapid growth habit and ability to tolerate drought conditions make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant also produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
