Aconitum napellus f. pubescens

Aconitum napellus f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. The plant features striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. Its leaves are palmately lobed with a distinctive hooded flower structure.

The pubescent form is characterized by a hairy texture on its stems and leaves, distinguishing it from the typical variety.
