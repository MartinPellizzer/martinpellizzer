Anerincleistus hispidissimus

Anerincleistus hispidissimus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered together. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions where it relies on specific host plants for survival.
