Aster texanus

Aster texanus, commonly known as Texas aster, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Aster texanus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
