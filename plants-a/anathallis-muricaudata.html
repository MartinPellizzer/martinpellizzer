Anathallis muricaudata

Anathallis muricaudata, commonly known as the hairy-tongue spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a long, hairy labellum that resembles a spider's tongue. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Anathallis muricaudata is a rare and specialized orchid, highlighting the diversity of epiphytic flora in tropical ecosystems.
