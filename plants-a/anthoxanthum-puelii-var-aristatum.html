Anthoxanthum puelii var. aristatum

Anthoxanthum puelii var. aristatum, commonly known as the tufted false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, prairies, and along roadsides. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a clumping growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
