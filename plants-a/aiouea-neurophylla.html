Aiouea neurophylla

Aiouea neurophylla, commonly known as the Christmas cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
