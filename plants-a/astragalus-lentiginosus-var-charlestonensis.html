Astragalus lentiginosus var. charlestonensis

Astragalus lentiginosus var. charlestonensis, commonly known as Charleston milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is typically fibrous and extensive. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
