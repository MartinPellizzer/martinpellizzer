Astragalus aleppicus var. glabrescens

Astragalus aleppicus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with pinnate divisions, and its roots are often fibrous and well-developed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
