Alnus nagurae

Alnus nagurae, commonly known as the Japanese alder, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like flowers. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alnus nagurae plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
