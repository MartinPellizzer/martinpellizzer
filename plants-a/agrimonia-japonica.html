Agrimonia japonica

Agrimonia japonica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant features serrated leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Agrimonia japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
