Agropyron magellanicum glabrivalva

Agropyron magellanicum glabrivalva, commonly known as the Magellanic grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to thrive in cold and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals.
