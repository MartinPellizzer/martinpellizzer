Acer crataegifolium var. veitchii

Acer crataegifolium var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically 5-7 lobed and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
