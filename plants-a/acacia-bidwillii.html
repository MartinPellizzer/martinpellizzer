Acacia bidwillii

Acacia bidwillii, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia bidwillii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
