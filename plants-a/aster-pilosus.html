Aster pilosus

Aster pilosus, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-blue leaves and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Aster pilosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
