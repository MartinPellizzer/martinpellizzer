Acer pseudoplatanus f. laetum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. laetum, commonly known as the variegated maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a broad, rounded shape and a serrated margin.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, producing samaras that disperse seeds in the wind.
