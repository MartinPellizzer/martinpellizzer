Althaea occidentalis

Althaea occidentalis, commonly known as the western marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is known for its mucilage-producing properties, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Althaea occidentalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.
