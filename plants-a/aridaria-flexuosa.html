Aridaria flexuosa

Aridaria flexuosa, commonly known as the wavy desert grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that curl in response to wind, a feature that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, adapted to attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to minimize evaporation.

Aridaria flexuosa is well-suited for harsh environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
