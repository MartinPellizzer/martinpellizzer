Aster densatus

Aster densatus, commonly known as the dense aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it blooms from late summer into early fall.

Aster densatus is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
