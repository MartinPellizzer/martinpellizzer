Amasonia angustifolia

Amasonia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amasonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Amasonia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
