Astragalus ellipsoideus var. abbreviatus

Astragalus ellipsoideus var. abbreviatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
