Arctotis pinnata

Arctotis pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Arctotis pinnata is known for its drought tolerance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
