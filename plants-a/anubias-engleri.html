Anubias engleri

Anubias engleri, commonly known as the African water plant, is a hardy aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib, providing a striking visual contrast. It is well-suited for aquariums and wetland gardens due to its tolerance for low light and varying water conditions.

Anubias engleri is also valued for its ability to help purify water by absorbing nutrients and reducing algae growth.
