Acalypha leonensis

Acalypha leonensis, commonly known as the red leaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that are often variegated with green and yellow patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha leonensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
