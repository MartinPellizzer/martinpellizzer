Abies cilicica var. isaurica

Abies cilicica var. isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Taurus Mountains in Turkey.

It is a variant of the Caucasian fir, characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soils. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this species is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
