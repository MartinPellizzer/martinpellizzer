Abarema muricarpa

Abarema muricarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited acanthus, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spiny fruits. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique fruit structure, which is covered in stiff, backward-pointing hairs, aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

Abarema muricarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in warm climates.
