Andropogon sorghum var. bicarinatus

Andropogon sorghum var. bicarinatus, commonly known as giant broomcorn millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, feathery panicles that produce small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop for arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are strong and fibrous.

This variety is often cultivated for forage, grain, or as a biofuel crop due to its high yield and adaptability.
