Allium

Allium, commonly known as the onion genus, includes a wide variety of plants such as onions, garlic, leeks, and chives.

These plants are characterized by their bulbous stems and grass-like leaves, with flowers that typically form in clusters. Allium species are members of the Amaryllidaceae family and are known for their strong, pungent aroma. They are widely cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.

The bulbs are rich in sulfur compounds, which contribute to their distinctive flavor and health benefits.
