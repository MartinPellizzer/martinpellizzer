Aquilegia pyrenaica var. litardierei

Aquilegia pyrenaica var. litardierei, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
