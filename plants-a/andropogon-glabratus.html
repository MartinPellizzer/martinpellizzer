Andropogon glabratus

Andropogon glabratus, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Andropogon glabratus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
