Azalea moulmainensis

Azalea moulmainensis, commonly known as the Moulmain azalea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Azalea moulmainensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
