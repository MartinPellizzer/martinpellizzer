Actinodaphne sulcata var. longipetiolata

Actinodaphne sulcata var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled actinodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are elongated and have prominent petioles. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, smooth-seeded drupes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
