Arisaema barnesii subsp. sheshanagae

Arisaema barnesii subsp.

sheshanagae, commonly known as the Sheshan arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to China. It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-bearing inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral structure.
