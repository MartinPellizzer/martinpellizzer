Acosta cineraria

Acosta cineraria, commonly known as the cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and grayish-green, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

Acosta cineraria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
