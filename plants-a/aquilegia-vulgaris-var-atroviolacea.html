Aquilegia vulgaris var. atroviolacea

Aquilegia vulgaris var. atroviolacea, commonly known as the dark violet columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features distinctive spurred petals that range in color from deep purples to black, giving it its striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several lance-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
