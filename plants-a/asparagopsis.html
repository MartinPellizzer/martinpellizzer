Asparagopsis

Asparagopsis, commonly known as a type of seaweed, is a genus of red algae belonging to the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its branching, filamentous structure and is typically found in tropical and subtropical marine environments. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including terpenoids and alkaloids, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance. Asparagopsis species are often used in aquariums as a food source for herbivorous fish and invertebrates.

Its unique chemical composition has also led to research into its potential applications in human health and sustainable aquaculture.
