Alyssum blepharocarpum

Alyssum blepharocarpum, commonly known as the eyelash alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blooms and compact growth habit.
