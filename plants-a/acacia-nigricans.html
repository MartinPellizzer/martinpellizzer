Acacia nigricans

Acacia nigricans, commonly known as black wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, flaky bark and slender, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring. Acacia nigricans is a woody shrub or small tree, often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.
