Azima tetracantha

Azima tetracantha, commonly known as the African whitewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry forests and woodlands, and its wood is valued for its lightweight and durable properties.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
