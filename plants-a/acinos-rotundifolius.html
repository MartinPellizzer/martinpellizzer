Acinos rotundifolius

Acinos rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved acino, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant features hairy, rounded leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
