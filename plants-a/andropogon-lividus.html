Andropogon lividus

Andropogon lividus, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and open woodland habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or bluish in color.

Andropogon lividus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
