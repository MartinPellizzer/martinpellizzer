Abronia ammophila

Abronia ammophila, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal dunes of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salt exposure. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Abronia ammophila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
