Arundinaria chino f. vaginata

Arundinaria chino f. vaginata, commonly known as Chinese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a variant of the Chinese bamboo species characterized by its slender, hollow stems.

It is a perennial grass native to East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its culms are often used in traditional crafts and construction due to their strength and flexibility.

Arundinaria chino f. vaginata plays a significant role in both ecological systems and cultural practices in its native habitat.
