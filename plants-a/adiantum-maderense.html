Adiantum maderense

Adiantum maderense, commonly known as the Mader's maidenhair fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are usually short and produce a rosette of fertile and sterile fronds.

Adiantum maderense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
