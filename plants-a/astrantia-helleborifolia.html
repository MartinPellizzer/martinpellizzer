Astrantia helleborifolia

Astrantia helleborifolia, commonly known as the Eastern aster or common aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white to pale purple petals that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Astrantia helleborifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
