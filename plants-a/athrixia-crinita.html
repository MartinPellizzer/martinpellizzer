Athrixia crinita

Athrixia crinita, commonly known as the hairy pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble a pincushion. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered within the bracts.

Athrixia crinita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
