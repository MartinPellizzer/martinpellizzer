Agrostis nigrescens

Agrostis nigrescens, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, coarse blades and panicle-like inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its rapid growth and competitive nature make it an important component of many grassland ecosystems.

Agrostis nigrescens is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
