Aspalathus cuneata

Aspalathus cuneata, commonly known as the small-leaf rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, bean-like fruits that contain the seeds used in the production of rooibos tea. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers a Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters.

Aspalathus cuneata is an important species in the fynbos biome and has ecological and economic significance.
