Ardisia bisumbellata

Ardisia bisumbellata, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its attractive clusters of small, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbellate clusters.
