Acacia guianensis

Acacia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan acacia, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorned stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia guianensis is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
