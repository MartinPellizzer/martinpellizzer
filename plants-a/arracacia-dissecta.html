Arracacia dissecta

Arracacia dissecta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring thick, woody stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance.

Arracacia dissecta is adapted to warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
