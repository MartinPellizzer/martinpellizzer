Amaryllis × versicolor

Amaryllis × versicolor, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Amaryllis bellona and Amaryllis belladonna, combining traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-draining soil.

Amaryllis × versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular as a houseplant or garden specimen.
