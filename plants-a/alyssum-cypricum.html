Alyssum cypricum

Alyssum cypricum, commonly known as Cyprus alissum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has silvery-gray foliage and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Alyssum cypricum is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
