Aeschynomene crassicaulis

Aeschynomene crassicaulis, commonly known as broadleaf aeschynomene, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetland or swampy habitats. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Aeschynomene crassicaulis is sometimes used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
