Astragalus amnis-amissi

Astragalus amnis-amissi, commonly known as the Amnis-Amissi milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers, which contributes to its survival in harsh conditions.
