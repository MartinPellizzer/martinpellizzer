Aster mohrii

Aster mohrii, commonly known as the Mohri's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster mohrii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
