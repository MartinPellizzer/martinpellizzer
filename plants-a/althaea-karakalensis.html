Althaea karakalensis

Althaea karakalensis, commonly known as Karakalpa marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces a thick, mucilaginous root that is rich in polysaccharides. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing and demulcent effects.
