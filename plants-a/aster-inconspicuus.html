Aster inconspicuus

Aster inconspicuus, commonly known as the inconspicuous aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

The leaves are generally narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.
