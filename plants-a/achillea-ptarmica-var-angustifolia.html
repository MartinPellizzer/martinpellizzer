Achillea ptarmica var. angustifolia

Achillea ptarmica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and its ornamental value contribute to its popularity among horticulturists.
