Allophylus occidentalis f. velutinus

Allophylus occidentalis f. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf alder, is a variant of the Allophylus occidentalis species native to certain regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its densely hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

This form is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
