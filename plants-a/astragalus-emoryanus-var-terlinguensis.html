Astragalus emoryanus var. terlinguensis

Astragalus emoryanus var. terlinguensis, commonly known as Emory's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
