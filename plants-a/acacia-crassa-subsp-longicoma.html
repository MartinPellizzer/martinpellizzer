Acacia crassa subsp. longicoma

Acacia crassa subsp.

longicoma, commonly known as the longpod acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually bipinnate and have a feathery appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
