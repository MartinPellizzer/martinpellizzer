Asparagus sarmentosus

Asparagus sarmentosus, commonly known as the creeping asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, which are followed by edible, needle-like berries. Its foliage consists of small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern.

Asparagus sarmentosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
