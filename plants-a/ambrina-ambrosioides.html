Ambrina ambrosioides

Ambrina ambrosioides, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic leaves and vibrant floral display.
