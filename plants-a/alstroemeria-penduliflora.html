Alstroemeria penduliflora

Alstroemeria penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging alstroemeria, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its drooping, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in a compact shrub form with narrow, strap-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and horticultural settings.
