Amaranthus bracteosus

Amaranthus bracteosus, commonly known as the velvetleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species valued for their ornamental and nutritional qualities.

Amaranthus bracteosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
