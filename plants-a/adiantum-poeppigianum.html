Adiantum poeppigianum

Adiantum poeppigianum, commonly known as the Poeppig's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new fronds that emerge from the base.

Adiantum poeppigianum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
