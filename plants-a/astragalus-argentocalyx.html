Astragalus argentocalyx

Astragalus argentocalyx, commonly known as silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus argentocalyx plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinators.
