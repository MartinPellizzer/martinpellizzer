Amaranthus scleranthoides f. rugulosus

Amaranthus scleranthoides f. rugulosus, commonly known as the rugose amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its rough, textured foliage.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are prominently rugose, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, bisexual flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
