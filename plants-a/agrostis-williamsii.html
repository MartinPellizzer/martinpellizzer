Agrostis williamsii

Agrostis williamsii, commonly known as Williams' bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, two-rowed flowers.

Agrostis williamsii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
