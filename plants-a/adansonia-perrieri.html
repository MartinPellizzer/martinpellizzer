Adansonia perrieri

Adansonia perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar baobab, is a striking tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the genus Adansonia, which includes all species of baobabs, and is characterized by its massive, swollen trunk and extensive root system. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall and has thick, fibrous bark that provides structural support and protection against fire. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces large, white flowers that bloom at night.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind.
