Aster bupleuroides

Aster bupleuroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a silver-gray appearance.

Aster bupleuroides is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
