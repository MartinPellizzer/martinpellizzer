Aconitum saxonicum

Aconitum saxonicum, commonly known as the Saxony monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Aconitum saxonicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value despite its dangerous properties.
