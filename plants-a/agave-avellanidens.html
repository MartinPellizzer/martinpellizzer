Agave avellanidens

Agave avellanidens, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with white to pale blue margins. The plant produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stem.

Agave avellanidens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
