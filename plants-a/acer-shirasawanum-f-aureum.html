Acer shirasawanum f. aureum

Acer shirasawanum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is a cultivar of the Japanese maple, characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage that turns vibrant red in the fall. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth texture and a delicate, ornamental appearance. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This plant is popular in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value and seasonal color changes.
