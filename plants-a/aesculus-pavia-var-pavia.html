Aesculus pavia var. pavia

Aesculus pavia var. pavia, commonly known as the crimson horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers, which appear in early spring. The plant produces round, spiny fruits that contain one or two glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The species is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
