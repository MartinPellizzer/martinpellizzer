Amaranthus urceolatus var. jonesii

Amaranthus urceolatus var. jonesii, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking purple to red foliage. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
