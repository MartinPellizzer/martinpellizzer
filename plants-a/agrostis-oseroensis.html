Agrostis oseroensis

Agrostis oseroensis, commonly known as the Oseroensis bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for moist, temperate climates. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Agrostis oseroensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
