Astragalus cicer var. speciosus

Astragalus cicer var. speciosus, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, this species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive blooms and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
