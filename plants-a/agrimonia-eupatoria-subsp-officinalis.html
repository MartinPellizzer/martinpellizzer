Agrimonia eupatoria subsp. officinalis

Agrimonia eupatoria subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as common agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces small, dry fruits called achenes, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is attributed to its purported astringent and antiseptic properties.
