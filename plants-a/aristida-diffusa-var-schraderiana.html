Aristida diffusa var. schraderiana

Aristida diffusa var. schraderiana, commonly known as Schrader's three-awn, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, three-awned spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green color that helps it retain moisture.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its fine texture and delicate appearance.
