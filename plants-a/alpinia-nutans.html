Alpinia nutans

Alpinia nutans, commonly known as the hanging ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of dark green, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that hang from the stem, giving it its common name. Alpinia nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.
