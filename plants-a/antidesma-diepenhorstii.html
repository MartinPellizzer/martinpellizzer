Antidesma diepenhorstii

Antidesma diepenhorstii, commonly known as the Diepenhorst's antidesma, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Cape Province. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
