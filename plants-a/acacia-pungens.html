Acacia pungens

Acacia pungens, commonly known as the black wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are dispersed by wind.

Acacia pungens is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
