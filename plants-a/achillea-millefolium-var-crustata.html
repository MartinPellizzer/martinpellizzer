Achillea millefolium var. crustata

Achillea millefolium var. crustata, commonly known as creeping yarrow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it both ornamental and beneficial to pollinators.
