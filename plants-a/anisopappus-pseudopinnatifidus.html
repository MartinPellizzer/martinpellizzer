Anisopappus pseudopinnatifidus

Anisopappus pseudopinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, hairy leaves that are typically linear to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to white flowers in the fall, which are arranged in composite heads typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and high levels of sunlight.
