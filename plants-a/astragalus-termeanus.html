Astragalus termeanus

Astragalus termeanus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus termeanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
