Acacia wilsonii

Acacia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
