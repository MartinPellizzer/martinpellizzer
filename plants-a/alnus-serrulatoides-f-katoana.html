Alnus serrulatoides f. katoana

Alnus serrulatoides f. katoana, commonly known as the Katoan alder, is a variant of the alder genus native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by winged seeds, typical of the Betulaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
