Aloe planifolia

Aloe planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe planifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
