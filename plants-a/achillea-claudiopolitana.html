Achillea claudiopolitana

Achillea claudiopolitana, commonly known as the Balkan yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flat-topped flower heads consist of many small white to pinkish florets, and it produces a strong, pleasant aroma.

Achillea claudiopolitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
