Athrodactylis spinosa

Athrodactylis spinosa, commonly known as the spiny dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
