Astragalus viciifolius

Astragalus viciifolius, commonly known as the vicia-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with a vicia-like appearance, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
