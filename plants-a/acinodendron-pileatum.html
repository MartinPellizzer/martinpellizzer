Acinodendron pileatum

Acinodendron pileatum, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are small, opposite, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Acinodendron pileatum is typically found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
