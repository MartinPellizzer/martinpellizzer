Arenaria catamarcensis

Arenaria catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca sandflower, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
