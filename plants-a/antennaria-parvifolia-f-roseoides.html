Antennaria parvifolia f. roseoides

Antennaria parvifolia f. roseoides, commonly known as the rose-like mouse-eared chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in well-drained, sunny environments and is often found in alpine or coastal regions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
