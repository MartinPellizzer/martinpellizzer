Andromeda grandiflora

Andromeda grandiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often in forested areas. The plant features alternate, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its evergreen stems and aromatic leaves make it a distinctive component of its ecosystem.

Andromeda grandiflora plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
