Alsophila floribunda

Alsophila floribunda, commonly known as the clustered tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.
