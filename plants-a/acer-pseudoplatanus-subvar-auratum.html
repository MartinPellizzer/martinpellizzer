Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. auratum

Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. auratum, commonly known as the golden maple, is a variant of the common maple tree native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which displays vivid hues of orange and red in the autumn. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a heart-shaped base. It produces small, clustered green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.
