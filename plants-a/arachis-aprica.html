Arachis aprica

Arachis aprica, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

Arachis aprica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
