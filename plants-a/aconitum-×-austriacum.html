Aconitum × austriacum

Aconitum × austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to the Alps, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Aconitum × austriacum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
