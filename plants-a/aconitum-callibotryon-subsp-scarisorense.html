Aconitum callibotryon subsp. scarisorense

Aconitum callibotryon subsp.

scarisorense, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features a stout, upright stem with palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
