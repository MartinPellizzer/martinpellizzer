Achyranthes coynei

Achyranthes coynei, commonly known as the Indian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in small, capsule-like structures that burst open to disperse seeds.

Achyranthes coynei is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
