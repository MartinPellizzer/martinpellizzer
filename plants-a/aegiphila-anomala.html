Aegiphila anomala

Aegiphila anomala, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is considered a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
