Anthemis behboudiana

Anthemis behboudiana, commonly known as the Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
