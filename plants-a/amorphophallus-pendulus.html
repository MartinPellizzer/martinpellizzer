Amorphophallus pendulus

Amorphophallus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging arrow or pendulous amorphophallus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with a spadix and a colorful spathe, which attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically large and palmately lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking floral display and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
