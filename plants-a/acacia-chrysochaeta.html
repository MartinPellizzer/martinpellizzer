Acacia chrysochaeta

Acacia chrysochaeta, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is an important species in Australian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native animals.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
