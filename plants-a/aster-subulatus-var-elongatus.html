Aster subulatus var. elongatus

Aster subulatus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are elongated, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are small, clustered in composite heads, with white to pale blue petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
