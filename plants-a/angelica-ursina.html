Angelica ursina

Angelica ursina, commonly known as the bear's garlic, is a tall biennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and celery. The plant features large, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered umbels. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

Angelica ursina is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported ability to aid digestion and support respiratory health.
