Aster farreri

Aster farreri, commonly known as the Farrell's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster farreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
