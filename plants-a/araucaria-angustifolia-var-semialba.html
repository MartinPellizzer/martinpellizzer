Araucaria angustifolia var. semialba

Araucaria angustifolia var. semialba, commonly known as the white-needle Araucaria, is a variant of the Brazilian pine.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are lighter in color compared to the typical variety. This evergreen conifer is native to the southern regions of Brazil and is adapted to humid, subtropical climates. Its cones are small and typically hang in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
