Adriana gaudichaudii var. genuina

Adriana gaudichaudii var. genuina, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to reduce water loss.

This orchid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
