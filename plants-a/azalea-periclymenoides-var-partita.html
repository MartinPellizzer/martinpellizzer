Azalea periclymenoides var. partita

Azalea periclymenoides var. partita, commonly known as the spotted azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a variegated pattern of green and white spots on the leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The spotted azalea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique leaf patterns.
