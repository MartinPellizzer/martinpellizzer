Aegilops vagans

Aegilops vagans, commonly known as the wandering spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is closely related to wheat and has been studied for its potential in genetic research.

Aegilops vagans is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
