Allium cephalonicum

Allium cephalonicum, commonly known as the Greek leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features basal leaves that are long and narrow, with a hollow, cylindrical stem that bears small, white, bell-shaped flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it useful in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.
