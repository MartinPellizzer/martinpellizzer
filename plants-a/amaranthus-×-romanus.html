Amaranthus × romanus

Amaranthus × romanus, commonly known as lovegrass, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Amaranthus × romanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.
