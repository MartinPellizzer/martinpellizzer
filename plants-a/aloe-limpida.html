Aloe limpida

Aloe limpida, commonly known as the white aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, often with a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Aloe limpida is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive dry conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
