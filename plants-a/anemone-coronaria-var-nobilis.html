Anemone coronaria var. nobilis

Anemone coronaria var. nobilis, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
