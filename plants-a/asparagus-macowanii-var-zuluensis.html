Asparagus macowanii var. zuluensis

Asparagus macowanii var. zuluensis, commonly known as the Zulu asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
