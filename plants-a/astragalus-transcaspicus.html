Astragalus transcaspicus

Astragalus transcaspicus, commonly known as the Transcaspian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus transcaspicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
