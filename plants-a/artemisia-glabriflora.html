Artemisia glabriflora

Artemisia glabriflora, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic, pungent scent. Artemisia glabriflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is widely used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant in various cultural practices.
