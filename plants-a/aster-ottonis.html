Aster ottonis

Aster ottonis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Aster ottonis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
