Anemone matsudae

Anemone matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone matsudae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
