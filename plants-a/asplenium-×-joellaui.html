Asplenium × joellaui

Asplenium × joellaui, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species, with a dark green coloration and a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Asplenium × joellaui is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.

However, it does not produce viable seeds, relying instead on vegetative propagation for its survival and spread.
