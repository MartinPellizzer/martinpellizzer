Amygdalus persica var. sinensis

Amygdalus persica var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese peach, is a variant of the peach tree native to China.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped fruits with a smooth, thin skin that ranges from yellow to red in color. The flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Prunus genus. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
