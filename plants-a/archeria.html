Archeria

Archeria, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish-white flowers with three petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Archeria is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and botanical collections.
