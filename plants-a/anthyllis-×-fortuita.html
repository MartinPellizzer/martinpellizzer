Anthyllis × fortuita

Anthyllis × fortuita, commonly known as the Scotch broom.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is native to the Mediterranean region. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis × fortuita is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
