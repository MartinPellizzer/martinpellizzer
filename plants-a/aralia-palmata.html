Aralia palmata

Aralia palmata, commonly known as the American aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound, feather-like leaves that are often arranged in a palmate pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aralia palmata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in garden landscapes for its unique aesthetic.
