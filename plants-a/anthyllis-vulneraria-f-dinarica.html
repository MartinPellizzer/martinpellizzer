Anthyllis vulneraria f. dinarica

Anthyllis vulneraria f. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric anthyllis, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports pollinators such as bees.

Its subspecies is distinguished by subtle morphological differences from the typical form of Anthyllis vulneraria.
