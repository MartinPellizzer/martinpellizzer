Allium cupuliferum subsp. cupuliferum

Allium cupuliferum subsp.

cupuliferum, commonly known as the purple garlic or purple bulb onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, glaucous leaves and produces upright stems with umbels of violet to purple flowers.

The plant has small, rounded bulbs that are often used as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and flowering display. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
