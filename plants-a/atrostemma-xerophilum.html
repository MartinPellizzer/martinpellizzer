Atrostemma xerophilum

Atrostemma xerophilum, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or reddish throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
