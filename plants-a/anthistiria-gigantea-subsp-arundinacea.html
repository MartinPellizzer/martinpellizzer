Anthistiria gigantea subsp. arundinacea

Anthistiria gigantea subsp.

arundinacea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
