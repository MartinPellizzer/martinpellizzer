Abies larix

Abies larix, commonly known as the larch, is a species of coniferous tree belonging to the genus Abies in the family Pinaceae.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which turn yellow and fall off in autumn, making it one of the few deciduous conifers. The tree typically grows in cool, moist climates and is found in the northern hemisphere, particularly in temperate and subarctic regions. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The bark of Abies larix is usually gray or reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.
