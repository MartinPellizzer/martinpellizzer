Aspidium eriocaulon

Aspidium eriocaulon, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically divided into smaller, finger-like segments, and the plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Aspidium eriocaulon is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
