Arnoglossum incanum var. ramosum

Arnoglossum incanum var. ramosum, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, pale yellow to white ray flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
