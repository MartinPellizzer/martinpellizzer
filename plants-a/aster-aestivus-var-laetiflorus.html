Aster aestivus var. laetiflorus

Aster aestivus var. laetiflorus, commonly known as the summer aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. This perennial herb typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
