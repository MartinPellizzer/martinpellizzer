Amaryllis blumenavia

Amaryllis blumenavia, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces a tall, upright stem with clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a rosette at the base, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Amaryllis blumenavia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
