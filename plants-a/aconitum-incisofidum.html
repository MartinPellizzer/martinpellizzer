Aconitum incisofidum

Aconitum incisofidum, commonly known as the cut-leaved aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and China. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive notched appearance.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a highly poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
