Anemone pratensis subsp. rubra

Anemone pratensis subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive white central throat.

The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to the early season flora.
