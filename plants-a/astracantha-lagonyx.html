Astracantha lagonyx

Astracantha lagonyx, commonly known as the golden globe cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
