Alsine succulenta

Alsine succulenta, commonly known as the succulent alsine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
