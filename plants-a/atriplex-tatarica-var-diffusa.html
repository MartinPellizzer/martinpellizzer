Atriplex tatarica var. diffusa

Atriplex tatarica var. diffusa, commonly known as Russian thornweed, is a perennial shrub native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and alternate, fleshy, egg-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and has some ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
