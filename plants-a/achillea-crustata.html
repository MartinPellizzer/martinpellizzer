Achillea crustata

Achillea crustata, commonly known as stone everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Achillea crustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
