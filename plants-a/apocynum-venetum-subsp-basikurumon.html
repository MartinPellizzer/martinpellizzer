Apocynum venetum subsp. basikurumon

Apocynum venetum subsp.

basikurumon, commonly known as the Korean dogbane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by follicles containing toxic, milky sap. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
