Alchornea nemoralis var. rotundifolia

Alchornea nemoralis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaf alchornea, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often hairy underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in woodlands and scrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.
