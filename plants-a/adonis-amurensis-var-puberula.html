Adonis amurensis var. puberula

Adonis amurensis var. puberula, commonly known as the Amur gloryflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or rocky habitats. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single terminal raceme with numerous small, tubular flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
