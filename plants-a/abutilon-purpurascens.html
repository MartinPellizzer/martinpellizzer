Abutilon purpurascens

Abutilon purpurascens, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
