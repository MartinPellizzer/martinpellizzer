Aster calderi

Aster calderi, commonly known as the Caldeira aster, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster calderi is known for its resilience to harsh weather conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
