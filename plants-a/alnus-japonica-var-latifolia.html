Alnus japonica var. latifolia

Alnus japonica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alder, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its ornamental appeal.
