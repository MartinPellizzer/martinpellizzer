Asperula sinaica

Asperula sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai azalea, is a flowering plant native to the rocky landscapes of the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Asperula sinaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
