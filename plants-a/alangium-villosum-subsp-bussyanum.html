Alangium villosum subsp. bussyanum

Alangium villosum subsp.

bussyanum, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
