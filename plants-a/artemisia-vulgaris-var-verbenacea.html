Artemisia vulgaris var. verbenacea

Artemisia vulgaris var. verbenacea, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its medicinal and historical uses, it has been cultivated for centuries.
