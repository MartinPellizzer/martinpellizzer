Antirrhinum graecum

Antirrhinum graecum, commonly known as the Greek snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

Antirrhinum graecum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
