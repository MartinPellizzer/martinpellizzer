Amyema subalata

Amyema subalata, commonly known as the silver-leafed lichen, is a unique lichen species that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga, which allows it to thrive in specific environmental conditions. The plant's thallus is typically crust-like, with a pale or silver-colored surface that helps reflect sunlight. It is often found on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Amyema subalata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
