Adiantum mariposatum

Adiantum mariposatum, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Adiantum mariposatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
