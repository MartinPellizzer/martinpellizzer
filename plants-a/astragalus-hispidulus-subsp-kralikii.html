Astragalus hispidulus subsp. kralikii

Astragalus hispidulus subsp.

kralikii, commonly known as Kralik's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
