Alchemilla ellenbergiana

Alchemilla ellenbergiana, commonly known as the rock alkanet, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Alchemilla ellenbergiana thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
