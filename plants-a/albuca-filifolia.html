Albuca filifolia

Albuca filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

Albuca filifolia is also valued for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably under proper care.
