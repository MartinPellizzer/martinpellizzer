Aster distichophyllus

Aster distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habits. The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Aster distichophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
