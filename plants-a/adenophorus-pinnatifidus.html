Adenophorus pinnatifidus

Adenophorus pinnatifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf adenophorus, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnately divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
