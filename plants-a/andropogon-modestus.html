Andropogon modestus

Andropogon modestus, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for wildlife.

Andropogon modestus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
