Alchemilla glaberrima var. aggregata

Alchemilla glaberrima var. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to form dense, compact clumps.
