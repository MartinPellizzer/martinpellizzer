Athyrium matthewii

Athyrium matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's lady fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

Athyrium matthewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
