Amorphophallus prolificus

Amorphophallus prolificus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces a striking, spadix-shaped inflorescence with a strong odor, often resembling decay, which attracts pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tuberous roots of this plant are also used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
