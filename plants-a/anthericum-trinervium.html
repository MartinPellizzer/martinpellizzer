Anthericum trinervium

Anthericum trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas.

Anthericum trinervium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
