Asplenium gautieri

Asplenium gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds that are often clustered closely together. The plant typically produces spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are located on the undersides of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium gautieri is a hardy species that is relatively easy to cultivate in controlled indoor or greenhouse settings.
