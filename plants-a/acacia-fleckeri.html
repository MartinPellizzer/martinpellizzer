Acacia fleckeri

Acacia fleckeri, commonly known as the Darling River acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and can remain viable in the soil for many years.

Acacia fleckeri plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
