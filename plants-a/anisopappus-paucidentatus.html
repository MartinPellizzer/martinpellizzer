Anisopappus paucidentatus

Anisopappus paucidentatus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with gray-green, hairy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in desert and semi-desert regions.
