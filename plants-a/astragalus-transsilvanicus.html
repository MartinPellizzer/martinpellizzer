Astragalus transsilvanicus

Astragalus transsilvanicus, commonly known as Transylvanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly Romania and Hungary.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is often found in mountainous regions.

Astragalus transsilvanicus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential medicinal properties.
