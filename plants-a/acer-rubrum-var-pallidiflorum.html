Acer rubrum var. pallidiflorum

Acer rubrum var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered maple, is a variant of the red maple characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, typically reaching heights of 20 to 30 meters. The leaves are opposite, palmate, and turn vibrant red in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This cultivar is prized for its unique floral display and adaptability to various garden settings.
