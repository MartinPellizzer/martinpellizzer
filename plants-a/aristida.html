Aristida

Aristida, commonly known as dropseed, is a genus of grasses native to various parts of the world, including North America, Africa, and Asia.

These plants are characterized by their slender, often tufted growth habit and their ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions. Many species of Aristida are drought-tolerant and are often found in sandy or rocky soils. The flowers of Aristida are typically small, spike-like inflorescences that produce lightweight seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

Due to their ornamental value and ecological benefits, several Aristida species are cultivated in gardens and used in restoration projects.
