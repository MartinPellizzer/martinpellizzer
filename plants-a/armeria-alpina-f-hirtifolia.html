Armeria alpina f. hirtifolia

Armeria alpina f. hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy alpine thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The foliage is characterized by its hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in harsh mountain environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils.
