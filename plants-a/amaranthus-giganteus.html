Amaranthus giganteus

Amaranthus giganteus, commonly known as giant amaranth, is a tall, annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces bracts that are often more prominent than the actual flowers, giving it a striking appearance. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and drought tolerance.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the plant is often cultivated as an ornamental or for its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
