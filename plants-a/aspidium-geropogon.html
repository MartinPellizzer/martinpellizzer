Aspidium geropogon

Aspidium geropogon, commonly known as the broad fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its large, triangular-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread across forest floors. Its sori are round and arranged in a single row along the margins of the pinnae.

Aspidium geropogon is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
