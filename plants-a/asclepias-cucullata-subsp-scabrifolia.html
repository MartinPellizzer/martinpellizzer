Asclepias cucullata subsp. scabrifolia

Asclepias cucullata subsp.

scabrifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough textures.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typical of the milkweed genus. Its flowers feature distinctive hooded structures and are pollinated primarily by butterflies and bees.

This subspecies is adapted to wetland and swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
