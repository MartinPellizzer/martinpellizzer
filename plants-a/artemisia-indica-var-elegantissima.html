Artemisia indica var. elegantissima

Artemisia indica var. elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant tarragon, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been used traditionally in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
