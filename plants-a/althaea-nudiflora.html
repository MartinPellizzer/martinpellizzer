Althaea nudiflora

Althaea nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower mallows, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear simple, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Althaea nudiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
