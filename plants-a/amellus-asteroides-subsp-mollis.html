Amellus asteroides subsp. mollis

Amellus asteroides subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the fall.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine environments.

The subspecies mollis is distinguished by its softer, more pubescent foliage compared to other subspecies of Amellus asteroides.
