Astragalus canoflavus

Astragalus canoflavus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus canoflavus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
