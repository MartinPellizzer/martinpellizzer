Aristida purpurascens var. virgata

Aristida purpurascens var. virgata, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a rough texture that aids in water conservation.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support native wildlife.
