Astragalus porphyrocystis

Astragalus porphyrocystis, commonly known as the purple cysted milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in dry conditions. Its unique feature is the presence of purple, inflated pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus porphyrocystis plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological and medicinal value.
