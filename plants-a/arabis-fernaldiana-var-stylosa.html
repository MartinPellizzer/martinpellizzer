Arabis fernaldiana var. stylosa

Arabis fernaldiana var. stylosa, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions. It produces slender, branched stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
