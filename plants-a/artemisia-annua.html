Artemisia annua

Artemisia annua, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant contains a compound called artemisinin, which is used in the treatment of malaria. Artemisia annua thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in regions with well-drained soil.

Its historical use dates back to traditional Chinese medicine.
