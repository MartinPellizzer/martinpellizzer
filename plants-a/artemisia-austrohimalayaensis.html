Artemisia austrohimalayaensis

Artemisia austrohimalayaensis, commonly known as the Eastern Himalayan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in temperate and alpine climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Artemisia austrohimalayaensis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand cold and dry conditions.
