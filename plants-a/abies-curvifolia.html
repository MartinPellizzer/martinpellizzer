Abies curvifolia

Abies curvifolia, commonly known as the hook-leaf fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that turns a bluish-gray in winter. The leaves are typically curved at the tips, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones are cylindrical and mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
