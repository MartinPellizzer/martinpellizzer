Anguillaria solanacea

Anguillaria solanacea, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its fruits are berries that contain several seeds.

Anguillaria solanacea is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive blooms.
