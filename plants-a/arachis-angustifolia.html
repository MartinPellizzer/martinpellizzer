Arachis angustifolia

Arachis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf groundnut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
