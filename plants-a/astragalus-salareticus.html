Astragalus salareticus

Astragalus salareticus, commonly known as the Salara milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale green pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus salareticus is often found in steppes and mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
