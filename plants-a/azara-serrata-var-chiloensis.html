Azara serrata var. chiloensis

Azara serrata var. chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean azara, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
