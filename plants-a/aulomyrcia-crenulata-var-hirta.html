Aulomyrcia crenulata var. hirta

Aulomyrcia crenulata var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy leaf mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and features opposite, hairy leaves that are often serrated along the edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and produce a mild mint-like scent.

This variety is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a fuzzy appearance.
