Allium ampeloprasum var. margaritaceum

Allium ampeloprasum var. margaritaceum, commonly known as the purple flowering onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs at the base of the stem, which are often used as ornamental or culinary elements. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
