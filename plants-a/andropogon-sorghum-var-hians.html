Andropogon sorghum var. hians

Andropogon sorghum var. hians, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
