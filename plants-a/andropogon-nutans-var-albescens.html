Andropogon nutans var. albescens

Andropogon nutans var. albescens, commonly known as the white bluestem grass, is a variant of the bluestem grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are often visible during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
