Adiantum terminatum

Adiantum terminatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum terminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
