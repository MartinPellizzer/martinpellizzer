Antennaria bayardii

Antennaria bayardii, commonly known as Bayard's mouse-eared moss, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that resemble mouse ears. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Antennaria bayardii is adapted to harsh environments and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions.
