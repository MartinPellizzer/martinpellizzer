Amorphophallus abyssinicus subsp. abyssinicus

Amorphophallus abyssinicus subsp.

abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian giant arrowhead, is a tuberous plant native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach several meters in height. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its tuberous roots are the primary storage organs and are used for propagation.
