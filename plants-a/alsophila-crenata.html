Alsophila crenata

Alsophila crenata, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is often found in moist, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can grow quite long, allowing it to establish a dense root system.

Alsophila crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in shaded gardens and as a houseplant.
