Acalypha velutina

Acalypha velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name, and its striking, often colorful bracts that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Acalypha velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
