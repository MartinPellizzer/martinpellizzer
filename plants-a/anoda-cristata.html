Anoda cristata

Anoda cristata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or purpletop, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with a velvety texture, and it often grows in moist, sunny environments. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and can fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

Anoda cristata is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
