Asarca virescens

Asarca virescens, commonly known as green mossy fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Asaraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, soft foliage that often appears in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with fronds that are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, moss-like texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest in both botanical studies and ecological research.
