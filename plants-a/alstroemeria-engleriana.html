Alstroemeria engleriana

Alstroemeria engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's lily, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white and green.

Alstroemeria engleriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
