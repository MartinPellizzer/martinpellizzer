Acinodendron gratissimum

Acinodendron gratissimum, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for local wildlife.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
