Ardisia reclinata

Ardisia reclinata, commonly known as the hanging ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, glossy green leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, bright red berry that contains several seeds.

Ardisia reclinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
