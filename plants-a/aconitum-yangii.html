Aconitum yangii

Aconitum yangii, commonly known as the Yang's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its tall, sparsely branched stems can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. Aconitum yangii contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
