Asplenium gomezianum

Asplenium gomezianum, commonly known as the Gomez's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium gomezianum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptation to humid, low-light conditions.
