Acacia paludosa

Acacia paludosa, commonly known as the swamp acacia, is a shrub native to wetland environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in dense, globular clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or water.

Acacia paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
