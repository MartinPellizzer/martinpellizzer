Apocynum tauricum

Apocynum tauricum, commonly known as the Taurian dogbane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are follicles that contain toxic, milky sap, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

Apocynum tauricum is part of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky latex and showy flowers.
