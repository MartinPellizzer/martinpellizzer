Armeniaca sibirica var. pleniflora

Armeniaca sibirica var. pleniflora, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period for proper fruit development.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also cultivated for its edible fruits.
