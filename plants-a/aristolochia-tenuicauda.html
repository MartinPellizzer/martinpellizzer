Aristolochia tenuicauda

Aristolochia tenuicauda, commonly known as the slender-tailed Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive shape and coloration. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
