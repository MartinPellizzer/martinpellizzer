Anemone liangshanica var. rectistyla

Anemone liangshanica var. rectistyla, commonly known as the Liangshan anemone, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-blooming flowers.
