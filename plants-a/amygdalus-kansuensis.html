Amygdalus kansuensis

Amygdalus kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential for use in horticulture and as a source of edible nuts.
