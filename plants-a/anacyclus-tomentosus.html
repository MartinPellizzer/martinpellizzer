Anacyclus tomentosus

Anacyclus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.

Anacyclus tomentosus is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
