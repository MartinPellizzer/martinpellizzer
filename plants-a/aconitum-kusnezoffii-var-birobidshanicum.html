Aconitum kusnezoffii var. birobidshanicum

Aconitum kusnezoffii var. birobidshanicum, commonly known as the Birobidzhan aconite, is a variant of the aconite genus native to the Russian Far East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, sparsely branched stems with large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
