Acilepis saligna

Acilepis saligna, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are pinnate, with narrow, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The bark is smooth and often has a pale or greyish color.
