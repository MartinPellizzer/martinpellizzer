Artemisia oliveriana

Artemisia oliveriana, commonly known as Oliver's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

Artemisia oliveriana plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is used in traditional herbal practices.
