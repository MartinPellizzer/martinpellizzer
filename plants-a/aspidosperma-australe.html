Aspidosperma australe

Aspidosperma australe, commonly known as the southern velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, brown seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
