Astragalus sphaerophysa

Astragalus sphaerophysa, commonly known as the globe milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astragalus sphaerophysa is a member of the legume family and has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
