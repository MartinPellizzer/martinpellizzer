Allium angulosum subsp. ammophilum

Allium angulosum subsp.

ammophilum, commonly known as the sand hill onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
