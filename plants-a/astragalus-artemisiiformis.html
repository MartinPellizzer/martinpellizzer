Astragalus artemisiiformis

Astragalus artemisiiformis, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its basal leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions, and its stems often spread along the ground.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to low-nutrient environments.
