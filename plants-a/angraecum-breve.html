Angraecum breve

Angraecum breve, commonly known as the short angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are pollinated by specific nocturnal moths, highlighting its unique ecological relationship.

Angraecum breve is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
