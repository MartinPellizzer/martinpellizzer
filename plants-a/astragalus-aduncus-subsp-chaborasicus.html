Astragalus aduncus subsp. chaborasicus

Astragalus aduncus subsp.

chaborasicus, commonly known as the Chaborasi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
