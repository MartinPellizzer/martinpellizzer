Abutilon virginale

Abutilon virginale, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green on top and silver underneath. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it an effective pioneer species in ecological succession.
