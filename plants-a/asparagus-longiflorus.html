Asparagus longiflorus

Asparagus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered asparagus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asparagus longiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
