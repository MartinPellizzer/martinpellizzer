Allium anceps var. aberrans

Allium anceps var. aberrans, commonly known as the variable garlic, is a botanical variant of the garlic plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous structure, which consists of several layers of fleshy, white to pale yellow scales. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and hollow, typical of the Allium genus.

This variant is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties, similar to other garlic species.
