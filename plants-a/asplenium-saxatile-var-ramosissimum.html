Asplenium saxatile var. ramosissimum

Asplenium saxatile var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the rock spleenwort, is a fern species native to rocky and limestone habitats.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen appearance with small, leathery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in crevices or on rock surfaces, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a dense, tufted growth habit.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and poor soil, making it a resilient and hardy species.
