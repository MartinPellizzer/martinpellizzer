Astragalus flexuosus var. albus

Astragalus flexuosus var. albus, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces cylindrical, hairy seed pods. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus flexuosus var. albus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
