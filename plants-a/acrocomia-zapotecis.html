Acrocomia zapotecis

Acrocomia zapotecis, commonly known as the Zapotec palm, is a species native to Mexico, particularly in the states of Oaxaca and Puebla.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, gray trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
