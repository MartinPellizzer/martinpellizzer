Alsophila hyacinthei

Alsophila hyacinthei, commonly known as the purple-stemmed tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered with smooth, dark green to purple-brown bark. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Alsophila hyacinthei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
