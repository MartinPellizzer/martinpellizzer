Astragalus laxmannii subsp. laxmannii

Astragalus laxmannii subsp.

laxmannii, commonly known as the laxmann's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
