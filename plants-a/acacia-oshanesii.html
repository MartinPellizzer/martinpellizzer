Acacia oshanesii

Acacia oshanesii, commonly known as the Oshanesii wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Acacia oshanesii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature in dry landscapes.
