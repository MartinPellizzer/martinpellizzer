Allium castellanense

Allium castellanense, commonly known as the bulb onion or Egyptian onion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulb composed of layers of fleshy, white to yellowish scales that store nutrients. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a loose umbel.

Allium castellanense is often cultivated for its edible bulbs, which have a mild, sweet flavor.
