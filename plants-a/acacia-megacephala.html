Acacia megacephala

Acacia megacephala, commonly known as the giant globemallow, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters, typically during the winter months. The plant has compound leaves with small, needle-like leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia megacephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
