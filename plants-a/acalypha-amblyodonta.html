Acalypha amblyodonta

Acalypha amblyodonta, commonly known as the wooly acalypha, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
