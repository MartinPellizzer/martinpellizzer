Asplenium attenuatum var. multilobum

Asplenium attenuatum var. multilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into multiple lobes, giving it a finely dissected appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in a compact rosette.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
