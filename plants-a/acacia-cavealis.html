Acacia cavealis

Acacia cavealis, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia cavealis is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in sandy soils.

Its nectar is a valuable food source for native bees and other pollinators.
