Achillea wilhelmsii subsp. santolinoides

Achillea wilhelmsii subsp.

santolinoides, commonly known as the Santolin-like yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and composite flower heads.
