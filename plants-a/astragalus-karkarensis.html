Astragalus karkarensis

Astragalus karkarensis, commonly known as the Karkar Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
