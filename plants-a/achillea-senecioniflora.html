Achillea senecioniflora

Achillea senecioniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves and its clusters of small, yellowish-white composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
