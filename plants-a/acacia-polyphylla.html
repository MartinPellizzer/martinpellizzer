Acacia polyphylla

Acacia polyphylla, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia polyphylla is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in native Australian flora.
