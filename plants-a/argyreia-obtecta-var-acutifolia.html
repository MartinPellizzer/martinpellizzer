Argyreia obtecta var. acutifolia

Argyreia obtecta var. acutifolia, commonly known as the velvet tassel flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that encase small yellow flowers, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall aesthetic.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
