Abutilon subviscosum

Abutilon subviscosum, commonly known as the sticky abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that often have a sticky texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
