Akentra inflata

Akentra inflata, commonly known as the inflated bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, enabling the plant to function as a carnivorous species. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are usually submerged, while the bladders are often found at the water's surface.

Akentra inflata is native to parts of Africa and is notable for its adaptive morphology that supports its predatory lifestyle.
