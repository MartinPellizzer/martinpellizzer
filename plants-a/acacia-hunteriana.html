Acacia hunteriana

Acacia hunteriana, commonly known as the Hunter's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

Acacia hunteriana also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
