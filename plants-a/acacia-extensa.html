Acacia extensa

Acacia extensa, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Acacia extensa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
