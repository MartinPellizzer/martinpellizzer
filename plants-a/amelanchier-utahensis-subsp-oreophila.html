Amelanchier utahensis subsp. oreophila

Amelanchier utahensis subsp.

oreophila, commonly known as the mountain serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for wildlife and humans alike. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at higher elevations, often in forested or open mountainous areas.

Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it has a compact, multi-stemmed growth habit.
