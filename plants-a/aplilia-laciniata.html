Aplilia laciniata

Aplilia laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with deeply cut lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer.

Aplilia laciniata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
