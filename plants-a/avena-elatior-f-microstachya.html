Avena elatior f. microstachya

Avena elatior f. microstachya, commonly known as the dwarf oat, is a compact variant of the common oat species.

It is characterized by its shorter stature and reduced inflorescence compared to the typical Avena elatior. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a ornamental or for soil stabilization.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
