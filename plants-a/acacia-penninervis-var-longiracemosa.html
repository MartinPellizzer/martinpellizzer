Acacia penninervis var. longiracemosa

Acacia penninervis var. longiracemosa, commonly known as the long-branch acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden globular flowers arranged in long, drooping racemes, which are a striking feature of the species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soils and low rainfall.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
