Ammodaucus leucotrichus var. leucotrichus

Ammodaucus leucotrichus var. leucotrichus, commonly known as the white thorned lettuce, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly peppery flavor, making it a useful plant in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
