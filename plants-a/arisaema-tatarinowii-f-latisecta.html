Arisaema tatarinowii f. latisecta

Arisaema tatarinowii f. latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leafed Chinese arrowroot, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its distinctive, broad, arrow-shaped leaves.

This plant belongs to the Araceae family and is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Korea. Its foliage is typically deep green and has a prominent central vein, while the inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf structure and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
