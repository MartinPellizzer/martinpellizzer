Annona tripetala

Annona tripetala, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with a dense canopy of broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by a fruit with a thick, creamy, sweet pulp. The fruit is typically round or pear-shaped and contains numerous seeds embedded in the flesh.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
