Astragalus leptus subsp. leptus

Astragalus leptus subsp.

leptus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
