Acalypha chinensis

Acalypha chinensis, commonly known as Chinese velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Acalypha chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
