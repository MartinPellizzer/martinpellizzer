Acosta bombycina

Acosta bombycina, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in a fine, silky hair that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Acosta bombycina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
