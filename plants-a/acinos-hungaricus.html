Acinos hungaricus

Acinos hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian mountain aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing a showy display of purple to pink hues.

Acinos hungaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
