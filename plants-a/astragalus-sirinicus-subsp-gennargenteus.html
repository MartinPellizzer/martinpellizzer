Astragalus sirinicus subsp. gennargenteus

Astragalus sirinicus subsp.

gennargenteus, commonly known as the silver-haired milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
