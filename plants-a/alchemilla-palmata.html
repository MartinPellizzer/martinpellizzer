Alchemilla palmata

Alchemilla palmata, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Alchemilla palmata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
