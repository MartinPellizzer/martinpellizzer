Anisophyllum amoenum

Anisophyllum amoenum, commonly known as the dwarf mountain spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in early summer. Its stems are short and creeping, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Anisophyllum amoenum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions.
