Avena hydrophila

Avena hydrophila, commonly known as water oat, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its seeds are often dispersed by water, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Avena hydrophila is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland habitats.
