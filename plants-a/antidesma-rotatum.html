Antidesma rotatum

Antidesma rotatum, commonly known as the red antidesma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
