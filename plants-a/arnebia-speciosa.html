Arnebia speciosa

Arnebia speciosa, commonly known as the Himalayan rose or bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and distinctive, often hairy, foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and palmate leaves with three to five lobes.

Arnebia speciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
