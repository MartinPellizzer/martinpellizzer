Amphipogon avenaceus

Amphipogon avenaceus, commonly known as the oat grass, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant has been used historically in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
