Astragalus sibiricus

Astragalus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Astragalus sibiricus is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
