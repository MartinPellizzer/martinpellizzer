Acalypha pulchrespicata

Acalypha pulchrespicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to purple, often with a contrasting green underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique foliage.
