Acacia passargei

Acacia passargei, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous tiny leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Acacia passargei plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
