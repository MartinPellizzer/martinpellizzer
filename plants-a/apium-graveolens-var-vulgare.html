Apium graveolens var. vulgare

Apium graveolens var. vulgare, commonly known as celery, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots and stalks are widely used in cooking for their distinctive flavor and nutritional value.

Celery thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
