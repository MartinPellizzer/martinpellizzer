Adicea thouarsiana

Adicea thouarsiana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid rainforests of its native habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its moist but often sunny environment.

Adicea thouarsiana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
