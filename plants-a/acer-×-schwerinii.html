Acer × schwerinii

Acer × schwerinii, commonly known as the Weeping Maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree resulting from the cross between Acer palmatum and Acer trichotomum.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, with small, ovate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 8–12 meters, featuring a narrow, upright form with a dense crown. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5–8, preferring well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks, though it may be susceptible to certain pests and diseases.
