Anadendrum montanum var. longirostre

Anadendrum montanum var. longirostre, commonly known as the long-beaked hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and its distinctive, elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
