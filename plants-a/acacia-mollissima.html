Acacia mollissima

Acacia mollissima, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of small, glandular, and silky-haired leaves. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in long, thin pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia mollissima is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
