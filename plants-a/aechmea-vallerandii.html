Aechmea vallerandii

Aechmea vallerandii, commonly known as the Vallerand bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, spathaceous flower bracts that can range in color from red to orange, often resembling a crown. Its foliage is typically dark green with a metallic sheen, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Aechmea vallerandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea vallerandii has leaves that are strap-shaped, arranged in a rosette, and typically measure up to 30 cm in length, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead forming a compact rosette with a central inflorescence. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the summer months, pollinated by insects such as bees and flies. The fruit is a capsule that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
