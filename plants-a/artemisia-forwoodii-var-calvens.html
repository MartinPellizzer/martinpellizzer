Artemisia forwoodii var. calvens

Artemisia forwoodii var. calvens, commonly known as the California mugwort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features gray-green, aromatic foliage with silvery-white undersides, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties and ability to attract beneficial insects make it a valuable plant in ecological landscapes.
