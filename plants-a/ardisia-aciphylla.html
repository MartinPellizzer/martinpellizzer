Ardisia aciphylla

Ardisia aciphylla, commonly known as the needle-leaf ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia aciphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
