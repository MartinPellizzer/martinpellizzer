Alectra arabica

Alectra arabica, commonly known as the Arabian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often resembling those of the genus Bellis. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Alectra arabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
