Aeranthes ophioplectron

Aeranthes ophioplectron, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and green striped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near rivers and swamps.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
