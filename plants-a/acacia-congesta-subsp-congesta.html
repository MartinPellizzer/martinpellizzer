Acacia congesta subsp. congesta

Acacia congesta subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, typically during the autumn and winter months. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some tolerance to salt spray.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of coastal vegetation.
