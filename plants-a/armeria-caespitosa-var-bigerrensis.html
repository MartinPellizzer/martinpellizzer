Armeria caespitosa var. bigerrensis

Armeria caespitosa var. bigerrensis, commonly known as the Bigelow's thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
