Alyssum suffrutescens

Alyssum suffrutescens, commonly known as summer alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, preferring well-drained soil.

Alyssum suffrutescens is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
