Althaea corymbosa

Althaea corymbosa, commonly known as the marshmallow plant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

Althaea corymbosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
