Allium subvillosum var. clusianum

Allium subvillosum var. clusianum, commonly known as the Clusian garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered umbels of white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its fibrous, bulbous roots and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

This variant is distinguished by its fine, silky hairs on the leaves, giving it a soft, textured appearance.
