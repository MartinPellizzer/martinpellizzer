Atriplex polonica

Atriplex polonica, commonly known as Polish saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, often grayish-green leaves that are adapted to arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt tolerance makes it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded soils.

Atriplex polonica is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
