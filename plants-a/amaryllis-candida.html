Amaryllis candida

Amaryllis candida, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms that are typically 5 to 8 inches in diameter. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often a vibrant green.

Amaryllis candida is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
