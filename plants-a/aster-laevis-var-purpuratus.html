Aster laevis var. purpuratus

Aster laevis var. purpuratus, commonly known as the purple smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. It has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces ray flowers that are often tinged with pink or purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
