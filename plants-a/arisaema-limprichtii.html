Arisaema limprichtii

Arisaema limprichtii, commonly known as the Chinese cobra lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf known as a spathe. The plant produces striking, colorful spathes that range from white to green, often with a distinctive pattern resembling a cobra's hood. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema limprichtii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
