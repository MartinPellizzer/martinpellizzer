Aegilops triuncialis var. nigroferruginea

Aegilops triuncialis var. nigroferruginea, commonly known as the black and rust-colored wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brown flowers. Its seeds are typically small and oval, with a distinctive dark coloration.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
