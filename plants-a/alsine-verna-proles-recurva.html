Alsine verna proles recurva

Alsine verna proles recurva, commonly known as the bentleaf alsike.

It is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its low-growing habit and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often recurved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
