Astragalus podoloboides

Astragalus podoloboides, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Astragalus podoloboides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
