Aphania

Aphania, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Aphania has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
