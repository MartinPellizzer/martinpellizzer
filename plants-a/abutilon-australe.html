Abutilon australe

Abutilon australe, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive waxy texture.

Abutilon australe is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
