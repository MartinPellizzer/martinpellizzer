Alsophila capensis subsp. polypodioides

Alsophila capensis subsp.

polypodioides, commonly known as the Cape tree fern, is a distinctive evergreen fern native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, compound fronds that give it a tree-like appearance.

The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of its large, pinnately divided leaves.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
