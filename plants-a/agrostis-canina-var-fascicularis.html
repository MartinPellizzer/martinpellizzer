Agrostis canina var. fascicularis

Agrostis canina var. fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered meadowgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually short and compact.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
