Alstroemeria radula

Alstroemeria radula, commonly known as the sea alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

Alstroemeria radula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
