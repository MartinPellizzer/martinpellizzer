Actinodaphne glauca var. subtriplinervis

Actinodaphne glauca var. subtriplinervis, commonly known as the glossy actinodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
