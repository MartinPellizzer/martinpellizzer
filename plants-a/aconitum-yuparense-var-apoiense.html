Aconitum yuparense var. apoiense

Aconitum yuparense var. apoiense, commonly known as the Apoian monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that resemble a hood, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed divisions, and its stems are usually hairy. Native to the Andean regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
