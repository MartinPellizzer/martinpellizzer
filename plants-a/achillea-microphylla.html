Achillea microphylla

Achillea microphylla, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Achillea microphylla is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
