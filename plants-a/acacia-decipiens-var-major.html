Acacia decipiens var. major

Acacia decipiens var. major, commonly known as the giant wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is a common component of coastal and inland shrublands.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
