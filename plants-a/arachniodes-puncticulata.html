Arachniodes puncticulata

Arachniodes puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds with a finely toothed margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of foliage.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a unique pattern along the undersides of the fronds.
