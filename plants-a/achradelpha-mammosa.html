Achradelpha mammosa

Achradelpha mammosa, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
