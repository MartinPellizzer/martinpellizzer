Actinostemon concolor var. obovatus

Actinostemon concolor var. obovatus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, obovate leaves that are smooth and often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and garden landscapes.
