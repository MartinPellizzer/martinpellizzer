Alcicornium veitchii

Alcicornium veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Alcicornium veitchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
