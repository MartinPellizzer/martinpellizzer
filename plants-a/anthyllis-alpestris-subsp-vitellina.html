Anthyllis alpestris subsp. vitellina

Anthyllis alpestris subsp.

vitellina, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
