Allium sanctum

Allium sanctum, commonly known as the sacred garlic, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are used both ornamentally and for their mild garlic-like scent. Allium sanctum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional herbal medicine.
