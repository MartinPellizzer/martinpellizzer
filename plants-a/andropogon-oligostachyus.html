Andropogon oligostachyus

Andropogon oligostachyus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Andropogoneae tribe. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland restoration projects.
