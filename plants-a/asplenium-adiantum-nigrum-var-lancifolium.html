Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. lancifolium

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered spores on the undersides of its fronds, which are a key feature in its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
