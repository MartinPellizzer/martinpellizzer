Atriplex quadrivalvata var. sessilifolia

Atriplex quadrivalvata var. sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessileleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its adaptability to extreme conditions makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
