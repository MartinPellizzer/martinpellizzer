Achuaria

Achuaria, commonly known as the "mountain orchid," is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and requires cool, moist conditions to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its limited habitat, achuaria is considered a rare and endangered species.
