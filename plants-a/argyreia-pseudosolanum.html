Argyreia pseudosolanum

Argyreia pseudosolanum, commonly known as the red devil flower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and vigorous climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
