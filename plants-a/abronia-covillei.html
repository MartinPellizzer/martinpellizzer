Abronia covillei

Abronia covillei, commonly known as the Coville's bluemont, is a rare and endangered flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is typically found in limestone-rich habitats and is pollinated by various insects, including bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
