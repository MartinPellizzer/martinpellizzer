Acer palmatum f. albovariegatum

Acer palmatum f. albovariegatum, commonly known as the white-variegated maple, is a decorative cultivar of the Japanese maple.

It features striking foliage with white or pale yellow margins and green centers, creating a variegated effect. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a delicate, feathery appearance that adds ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
