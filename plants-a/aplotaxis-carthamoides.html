Aplotaxis carthamoides

Aplotaxis carthamoides, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Aplotaxis carthamoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
