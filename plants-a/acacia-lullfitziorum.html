Acacia lullfitziorum

Acacia lullfitziorum, commonly known as the Lullfitz's wattle, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia lullfitziorum is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife, as well as playing a role in soil stabilization in its native environment.
