Aponogeton natalensis

Aponogeton natalensis, commonly known as Natal arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with long, slender stems that often root in the substrate. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water, adding ornamental value to aquatic gardens. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers moderate to high light conditions.

Aponogeton natalensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in pond settings.
