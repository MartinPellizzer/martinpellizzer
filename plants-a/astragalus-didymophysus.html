Astragalus didymophysus

Astragalus didymophysus, commonly known as the two-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is often deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus didymophysus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
