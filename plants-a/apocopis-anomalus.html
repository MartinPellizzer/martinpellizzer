Apocopis anomalus

Apocopis anomalus, commonly known as the anomalous apocopis, is a rare and poorly understood plant species.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lanceolate leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to specific microhabitats in tropical regions, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile ecology, it is considered a conservation concern.
