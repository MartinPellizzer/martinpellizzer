Alchemilla saxatilis

Alchemilla saxatilis, commonly known as rock lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
