Alternanthera glaucescens

Alternanthera glaucescens, commonly known as the blue velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue-green leaves that often feature a purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers, typically appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
