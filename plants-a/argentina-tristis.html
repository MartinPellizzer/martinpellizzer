Argentina tristis

Argentina tristis, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a distinctive, cascading appearance. The tree has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by woody capsules that contain seeds.

Argentina tristis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
