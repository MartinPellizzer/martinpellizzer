Antirrhinum laxiflorum var. angustifolium

Antirrhinum laxiflorum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved snapdragon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are elongated and narrow, typically with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
