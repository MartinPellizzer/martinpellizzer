Andropogon contortus var. glaber

Andropogon contortus var. glaber, commonly known as curly bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its twisted, curling stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Curly bluestem is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
