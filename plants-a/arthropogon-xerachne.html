Arthropogon xerachne

Arthropogon xerachne, commonly known as the red grass or red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown inflorescence and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and fibrous, while the stems are often branching and covered in fine hairs.

Arthropogon xerachne plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional practices or as a forage plant for livestock.
