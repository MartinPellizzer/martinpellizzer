Astranthium integrifolium subsp. ciliatum

Astranthium integrifolium subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy astranthium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters called cymes. Its leaves are entire, meaning they have no teeth or lobes, and are typically lance-shaped.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
