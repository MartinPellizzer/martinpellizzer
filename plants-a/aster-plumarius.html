Aster plumarius

Aster plumarius, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Aster plumarius is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
