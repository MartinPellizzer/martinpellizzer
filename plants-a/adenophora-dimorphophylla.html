Adenophora dimorphophylla

Adenophora dimorphophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Adenophora dimorphophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
