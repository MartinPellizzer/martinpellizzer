Acmanthera minima

Acmanthera minima, commonly known as the dwarf crown flower, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
