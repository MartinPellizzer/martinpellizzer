Armeria maritima var. glaberrima

Armeria maritima var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous sea thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name "glaberrima.". It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh coastal environments.
