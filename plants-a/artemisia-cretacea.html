Artemisia cretacea

Artemisia cretacea, commonly known as Cretan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and aromatic, often fuzzy leaves that contain essential oils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Artemisia cretacea is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
