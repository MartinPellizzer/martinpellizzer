Acer platanoides var. variegatum

Acer platanoides var. variegatum, commonly known as the Variegated Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a light green or white margin, creating a striking visual contrast. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to urban environments.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in spring followed by winged fruits in autumn.
