Acalypha acuta

Acalypha acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Acalypha acuta typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and along stream banks.
