Antirrhinum virga

Antirrhinum virga, commonly known as the slender snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or purple, and they open in the morning, closing by midday. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
