Asplenium mettenianum

Asplenium mettenianum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces sporangia in clusters called sorus, which are usually located on the underside of the fronds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Asplenium mettenianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
