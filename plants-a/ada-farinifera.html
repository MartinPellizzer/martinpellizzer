Ada farinifera

Ada farinifera, commonly known as the dust-veined orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its flowers are small and often pale in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

The leaves are usually leathery and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which gives the plant its scientific name.
