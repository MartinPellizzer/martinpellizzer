Aegilops insulae-cypri

Aegilops insulae-cypri, commonly known as Cyprus wild rye, is a grass species native to the island of Cyprus.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.

Morphology

Aegilops insulae-cypri has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a bluish-green color and a smooth texture.

The stem is slender, with a light green color and a somewhat hairy texture, growing upright with a branched structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes, which bloom in the spring and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses its seeds by wind when it splits open.
