Arabis crypta

Arabis crypta, commonly known as the hidden rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its delicate, basal leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Arabis crypta is often found in crevices or on slopes, adapting to harsh climatic conditions with its compact growth habit.
