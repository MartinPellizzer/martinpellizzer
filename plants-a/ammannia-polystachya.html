Ammannia polystachya

Ammannia polystachya, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is characterized by its deep red color, which gives the plant its common name.

Ammannia polystachya is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.
