Anchomanes abbreviatus

Anchomanes abbreviatus, commonly known as the dwarf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. Anchomanes abbreviatus is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shallow, still or slow-moving water.
