Astragalus debilis

Astragalus debilis, commonly known as the weak milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus debilis is often found in disturbed sites and along roadsides.
