Amaryllis yungacensis

Amaryllis yungacensis, commonly known as the Yungas amaryllis, is a striking plant native to the humid highlands of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-like, evergreen leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
