Anthurium lingua

Anthurium lingua, commonly known as the "language of the heart" or "heart language," is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped spathes that can range in color from deep red to bright orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow spadices that are typically hidden within the spathe. Anthurium lingua thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Its foliage is glossy and broad, contributing to its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
