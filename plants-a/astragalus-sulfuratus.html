Astragalus sulfuratus

Astragalus sulfuratus, commonly known as sulfur hill milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
