Arundinaria khasiana

Arundinaria khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly in the states of Meghalaya, Assam, and Manipur. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Arundinaria khasiana is valued for its strong and durable bamboo, which is used in traditional construction and handicrafts.
