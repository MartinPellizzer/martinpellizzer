Aconitum brachynasum

Aconitum brachynasum, commonly known as the short-stalked monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features a thick, spindle-shaped rhizome and possesses finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
