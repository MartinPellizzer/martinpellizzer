Atriplex lasiantha

Atriplex lasiantha, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in dry environments. The plant has small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex lasiantha plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
