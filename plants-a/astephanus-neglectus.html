Astephanus neglectus

Astephanus neglectus, commonly known as the neglected stephanus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in a flat, disc-like structure, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Astephanus neglectus is often found in fynbos vegetation and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
