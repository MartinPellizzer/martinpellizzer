Antirrhinum subsessile

Antirrhinum subsessile, commonly known as the narrowleaf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive bilabiate structure. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Antirrhinum subsessile is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
