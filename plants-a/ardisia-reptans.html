Ardisia reptans

Ardisia reptans, commonly known as the creeping ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical gardens.
