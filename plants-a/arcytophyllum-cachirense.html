Arcytophyllum cachirense

Arcytophyllum cachirense, commonly known as the Cachiri sage, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is known for its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
