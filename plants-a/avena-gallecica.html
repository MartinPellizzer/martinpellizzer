Avena gallecica

Avena gallecica, commonly known as the Galician oat, is a perennial grass native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in coastal and calcareous soils, often found in Mediterranean climates.

Avena gallecica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
