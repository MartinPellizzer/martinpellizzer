Antirrhinum meonanthum

Antirrhinum meonanthum, commonly known as the lesser snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are usually red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Antirrhinum meonanthum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
