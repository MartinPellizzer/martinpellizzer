Acer visianii var. pancicii

Acer visianii var. pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its striking autumn foliage.
