Allium wildii

Allium wildii, commonly known as the wild garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Allium wildii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and natural ecosystems.
