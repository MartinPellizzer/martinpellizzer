Alyssum mazandaranicum

Alyssum mazandaranicum, commonly known as Persian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. Alyssum mazandaranicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
