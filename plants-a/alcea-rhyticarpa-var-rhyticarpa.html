Alcea rhyticarpa var. rhyticarpa

Alcea rhyticarpa var. rhyticarpa, commonly known as the scarlet bower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its roots are fibrous and somewhat woody. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
