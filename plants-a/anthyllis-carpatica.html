Anthyllis carpatica

Anthyllis carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian burr-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Anthyllis carpatica is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
