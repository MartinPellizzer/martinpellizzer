Acacia armata var. angustifolia

Acacia armata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features slender, angular stems and pinnate leaves composed of narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system for water uptake.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in dry climates.
