Achnatherum halleri

Achnatherum halleri, commonly known as Haller's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it contributes to the local vegetation.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
