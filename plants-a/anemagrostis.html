Anemagrostis

Anemagrostis, commonly known as green false panicgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typically arranged in panicles. Anemagrostis species are native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America.

These plants are often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of water bodies.
