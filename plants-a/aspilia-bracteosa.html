Aspilia bracteosa

Aspilia bracteosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Aspilia bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
