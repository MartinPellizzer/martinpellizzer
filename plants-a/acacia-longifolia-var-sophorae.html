Acacia longifolia var. sophorae

Acacia longifolia var. sophorae, commonly known as the coastal wattles, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing dunes.
