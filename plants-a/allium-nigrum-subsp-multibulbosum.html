Allium nigrum subsp. multibulbosum

Allium nigrum subsp.

multibulbosum, commonly known as the purple garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, purple to pink flowers in the summer months.

The plant is characterized by its ability to form multiple bulb-like structures, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.
