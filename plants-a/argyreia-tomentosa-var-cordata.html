Argyreia tomentosa var. cordata

Argyreia tomentosa var. cordata, commonly known as the velvet leaf or red curl.

This plant is a tropical vine characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, giving it the common name "cordata," which refers to the shape.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
