Amphicosmia multiflora

Amphicosmia multiflora, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Amphicosmia multiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
