Argyranthemum pinnatifidum subsp. pinnatifidum

Argyranthemum pinnatifidum subsp.

pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flossflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like blooms that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with multiple stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
