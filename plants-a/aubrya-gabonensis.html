Aubrya gabonensis

Aubrya gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white. Aubrya gabonensis is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, which aid in seed dispersal.
