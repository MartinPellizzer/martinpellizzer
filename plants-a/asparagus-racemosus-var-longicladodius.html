Asparagus racemosus var. longicladodius

Asparagus racemosus var. longicladodius, commonly known as the Indian asparagus or wild asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, red berries containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
