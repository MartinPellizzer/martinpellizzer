Aristida lauriolii

Aristida lauriolii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Aristida lauriolii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
