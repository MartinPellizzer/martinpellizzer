Ayenia sidifolia

Ayenia sidifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, sword-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Ayenia sidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
