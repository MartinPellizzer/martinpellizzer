Alsine circassica

Alsine circassica, commonly known as Circassian wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are succulent and have a distinctive, somewhat bitter taste.

Alsine circassica is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
