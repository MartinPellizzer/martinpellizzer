Ageratina articulata

Ageratina articulata, commonly known as the brittlestem ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered inflorescences that resemble daisies. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The flowers are usually white or pale blue, and they bloom during the warmer months.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
