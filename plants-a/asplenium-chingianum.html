Asplenium chingianum

Asplenium chingianum, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a light green color and a feathery appearance.

Asplenium chingianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
