Aster revolutus

Aster revolutus, commonly known as the curledleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are often curled beneath the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
