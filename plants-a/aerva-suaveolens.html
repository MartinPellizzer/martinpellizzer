Aerva suaveolens

Aerva suaveolens, commonly known as sweet aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aerva suaveolens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
