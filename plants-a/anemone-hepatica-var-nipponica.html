Anemone hepatica var. nipponica

Anemone hepatica var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese liverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that resemble a liver, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, early spring blooms of white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
