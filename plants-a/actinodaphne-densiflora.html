Actinodaphne densiflora

Actinodaphne densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
