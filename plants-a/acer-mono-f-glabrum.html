Acer mono f. glabrum

Acer mono f. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaf maple, is a cultivar of the maple tree characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

It belongs to the genus Acer, which includes various species of maple trees known for their ornamental value. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
