Agave rupicola var. bouchei

Agave rupicola var. bouchei, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, blue-gray leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
