Alsine spinuliflora

Alsine spinuliflora, commonly known as the spiny alsike, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive spiny texture, which gives it its common name.

Alsine spinuliflora is an annual plant that thrives in temperate climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
