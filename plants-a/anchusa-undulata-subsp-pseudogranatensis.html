Anchusa undulata subsp. pseudogranatensis

Anchusa undulata subsp.

pseudogranatensis, commonly known as the wrinkled bugloss, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive wavy or undulating edge.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
