Ambrina andicola

Ambrina andicola, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, intricate flowers that often feature a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from rocky and acidic soils, which are common in its native habitat.

Ambrina andicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
