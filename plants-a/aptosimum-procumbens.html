Aptosimum procumbens

Aptosimum procumbens, commonly known as creeping aptosimum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with slender, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

Aptosimum procumbens is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
