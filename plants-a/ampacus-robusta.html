Ampacus robusta

Ampacus robusta, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, typical of many sedges. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or reddish in color.

Ampacus robusta thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.
