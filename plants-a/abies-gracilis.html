Abies gracilis

Abies gracilis, commonly known as the slender fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The cones of Abies gracilis are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

The plant has ecological significance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
