Allionia bracteata

Allionia bracteata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Allionia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
