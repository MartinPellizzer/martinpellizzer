Asterogeum

Asterogeum, commonly known as the starwort, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of numerous small flowers arranged in a radiate pattern. The plants typically have narrow, linear leaves and are often found in moist, temperate environments. Asterogeum species are known for their ability to thrive in wetlands and along stream banks.

Their flowers bloom in the summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
