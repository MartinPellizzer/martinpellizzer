Andropogon gryllus

Andropogon gryllus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a tall, warm-season grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Switchgrass is a perennial plant that thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly drought-tolerant. It is widely cultivated for its potential as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
