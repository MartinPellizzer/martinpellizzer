Acacia campecheana

Acacia campecheana, commonly known as the Mexican thorn or yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its sharp thorns and feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia campecheana thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
