Aneilema indehiscens subsp. keniense

Aneilema indehiscens subsp.

keniense, commonly known as the Kenyan arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to East Africa. It features a rhizomatous stem system and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is cultivated for its edible tubers, which are similar in texture to yams.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
