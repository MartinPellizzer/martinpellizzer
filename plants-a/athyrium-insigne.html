Athyrium insigne

Athyrium insigne, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow to orange fronds that contrast with its dark green undersides. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with narrow, lance-shaped pinnae.

Athyrium insigne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
