Abrahamia louvelii

Abrahamia louvelii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid understory of the forest. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered threatened and requires protection to ensure its survival.
