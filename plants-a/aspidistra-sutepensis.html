Aspidistra sutepensis

Aspidistra sutepensis, commonly known as the Sutep's aspidistra, is a rare species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with silver veins, giving it an elegant appearance. The plant is evergreen and thrives in shaded, humid environments, making it well-suited for indoor cultivation. Its flowers, though not showy, are small and clustered, typically appearing in late spring or early summer.

Aspidistra sutepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
