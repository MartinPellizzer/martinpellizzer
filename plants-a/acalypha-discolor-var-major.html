Acalypha discolor var. major

Acalypha discolor var. major, commonly known as the variegated iron plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and red. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a waxy texture and prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, spherical seed capsules.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in humid, well-drained soils.
