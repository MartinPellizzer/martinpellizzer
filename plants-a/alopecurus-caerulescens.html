Alopecurus caerulescens

Alopecurus caerulescens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some agricultural settings for forage.
