Astragalus lentiginosus var. ambiguus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. ambiguus, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as sagebrush steppe.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
