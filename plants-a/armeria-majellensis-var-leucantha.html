Armeria majellensis var. leucantha

Armeria majellensis var. leucantha, commonly known as the white sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features slender, succulent stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blooms and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
