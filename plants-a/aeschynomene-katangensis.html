Aeschynomene katangensis

Aeschynomene katangensis, commonly known as Katanga bluemink, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape.

It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
