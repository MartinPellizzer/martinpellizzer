Anemone × knappii

Anemone × knappii, commonly known as the Knapp's anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, palmate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anemone × knappii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
