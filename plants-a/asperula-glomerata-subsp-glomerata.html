Asperula glomerata subsp. glomerata

Asperula glomerata subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces clusters of pink to white flowers in early spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
