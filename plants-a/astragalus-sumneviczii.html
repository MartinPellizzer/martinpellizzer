Astragalus sumneviczii

Astragalus sumneviczii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in alpine meadows and open woodlands.

Astragalus sumneviczii is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, arid conditions.
