Allium perdulce subsp. sperryi

Allium perdulce subsp.

sperryi, commonly known as the Sperry's garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bulbous underground stem and a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
