Aa denticulata

Aa denticulata, commonly known as the Hawaiian mountain apple tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, green fruits that turn red when ripe. Aa denticulata is well adapted to the volcanic soils and rugged terrain of Hawaii's high elevations.

It plays an important ecological role in the native forest ecosystems of the islands.
