Anisotome aromatica var. aromatica

Anisotome aromatica var. aromatica, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Sweet grass is often used in traditional herbal remedies and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
