Acer ginnala var. semenovii

Acer ginnala var. semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
