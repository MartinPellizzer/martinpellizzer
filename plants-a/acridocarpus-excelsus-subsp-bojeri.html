Acridocarpus excelsus subsp. bojeri

Acridocarpus excelsus subsp.

bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's curry leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its lanceolate, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Indian and Southeast Asian cuisines.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing three seeds each. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species.
