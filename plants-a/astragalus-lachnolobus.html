Astragalus lachnolobus

Astragalus lachnolobus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are adapted for pollination by various insects.

Astragalus lachnolobus is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
