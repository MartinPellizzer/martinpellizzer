Adenophora lamarckii var. longifolia

Adenophora lamarckii var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct basal rosette structure. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
