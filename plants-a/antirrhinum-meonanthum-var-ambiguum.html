Antirrhinum meonanthum var. ambiguum

Antirrhinum meonanthum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the lesser snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
