Alchemilla laxescens

Alchemilla laxescens, commonly known as the loose-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in loose, branched inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla laxescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
