Aniba bracteata var. lherminieri

Aniba bracteata var. lherminieri, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *mogno-branquinho*, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lanceolate, glossy, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits containing seeds. It is valued for its hard, dark wood, which has been historically used in furniture and musical instruments.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
