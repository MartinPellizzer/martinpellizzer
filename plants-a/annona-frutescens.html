Annona frutescens

Annona frutescens, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, thorny shrub that produces large, fragrant, green fruits with a smooth, waxy skin. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Annonaceae family and has simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
