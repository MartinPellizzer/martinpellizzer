Andropogon ambiguus

Andropogon ambiguus, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Andropogon ambiguus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
