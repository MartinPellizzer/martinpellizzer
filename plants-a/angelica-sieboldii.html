Angelica sieboldii

Angelica sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are large, compound, and deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value.

Angelica sieboldii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
