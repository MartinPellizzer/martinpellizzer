Aristolochia ridicula

Aristolochia ridicula, commonly known as the "mock Dutchman's pipe," is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is notable for its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe, with a deep, hooded structure. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a purple or brownish interior.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
