Agarista varnhageniana

Agarista varnhageniana, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Agarista varnhageniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
