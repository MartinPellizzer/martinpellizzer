Aloe guttata

Aloe guttata, commonly known as the spotted aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with tubular red or orange flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Aloe guttata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
