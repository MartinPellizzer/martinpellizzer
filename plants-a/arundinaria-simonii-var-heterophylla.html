Arundinaria simonii var. heterophylla

Arundinaria simonii var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable bamboo, is a variant of the Chinese bamboo species characterized by its morphological diversity.

It exhibits distinct leaf shapes and sizes, which vary depending on environmental conditions and genetic factors. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded habitats within eastern Asia, particularly in China. Its culms are hollow and jointed, typical of the grass family, and it can grow to significant heights.

The plant's adaptability and unique foliage make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
