Acacia persiciflora

Acacia persiciflora, commonly known as the Persian acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom in spring. Acacia persiciflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
