Anemone grandis var. polonica

Anemone grandis var. polonica, commonly known as the Polish anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
