Abutilon durandoi

Abutilon durandoi, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Abutilon durandoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
