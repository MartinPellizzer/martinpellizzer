Abrahamia revoluta

Abrahamia revoluta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dark green to blackish upper surface and a lighter, often white or yellowish lower surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides its primary source of nutrients through photosynthesis. It is typically found on rocks, tree trunks, and soil in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for ecological health.
