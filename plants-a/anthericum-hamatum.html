Anthericum hamatum

Anthericum hamatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adder's-tongue, is a unique fern species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anthericum hamatum is notable for its primitive reproductive structures and its role in certain ecological systems.
