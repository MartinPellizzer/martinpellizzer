Agave univittata var. tamaulipasana

Agave univittata var. tamaulipasana, commonly known as the Tamaulipas agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers, which appear in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in some regions.
