Aconitum sajanense subsp. czerepninii

Aconitum sajanense subsp.

czerepninii, commonly known as the Sajan mountain aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes, and are often covered in a fine pubescence.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
