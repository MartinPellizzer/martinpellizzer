Alpinia quadriloba

Alpinia quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhizomatous growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant foliage and unique floral structure.

Alpinia quadriloba is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and medicinal properties.
