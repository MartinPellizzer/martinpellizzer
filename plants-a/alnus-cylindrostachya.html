Alnus cylindrostachya

Alnus cylindrostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked alder, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Alnus.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. This plant is characterized by its small, cylindrical staminate catkins and its ability to thrive in moist, often swampy habitats. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Alnus cylindrostachya plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
