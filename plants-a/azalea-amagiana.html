Azalea amagiana

Azalea amagiana, commonly known as the Amami azalea, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with rich, acidic soil. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

Azalea amagiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
