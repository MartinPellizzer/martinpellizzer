Atriplex cinerea var. palmata

Atriplex cinerea var. palmata, commonly known as silverleaf saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating to further enhance drought tolerance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by wind.

This plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
