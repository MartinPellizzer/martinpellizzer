Acacia laccifera

Acacia laccifera, commonly known as the African wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its yellow floral clusters, which appear in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia laccifera is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for wildlife.
