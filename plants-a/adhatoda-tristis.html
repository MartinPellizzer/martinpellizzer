Adhatoda tristis

Adhatoda tristis, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Blue Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Adhatoda tristis is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
