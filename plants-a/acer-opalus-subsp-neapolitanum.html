Acer opalus subsp. neapolitanum

Acer opalus subsp.

neapolitanum, commonly known as the Neapolitan maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it suitable for smaller gardens and urban landscapes.
