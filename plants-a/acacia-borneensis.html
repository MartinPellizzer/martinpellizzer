Acacia borneensis

Acacia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean acacia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its yellow to golden flowers are arranged in cylindrical inflorescences and bloom during the wet season.

Acacia borneensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
