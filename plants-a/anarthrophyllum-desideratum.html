Anarthrophyllum desideratum

Anarthrophyllum desideratum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate, white flowers that appear in late summer, often in the early morning. The plant has a short, erect stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Due to its scarcity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often difficult to locate in the wild.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
