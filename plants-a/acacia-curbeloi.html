Acacia curbeloi

Acacia curbeloi, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss and help it survive in dry conditions. Acacia curbeloi is adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
