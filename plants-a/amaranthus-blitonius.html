Amaranthus blitonius

Amaranthus blitonius, commonly known as the Mexican amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its seeds are small and often used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
