Acacia unifissilis

Acacia unifissilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its seed pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia unifissilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
