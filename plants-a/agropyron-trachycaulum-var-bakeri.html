Agropyron trachycaulum var. bakeri

Agropyron trachycaulum var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically found in open, arid environments. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological value and resilience.
