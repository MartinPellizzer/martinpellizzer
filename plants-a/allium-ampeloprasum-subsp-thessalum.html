Allium ampeloprasum subsp. thessalum

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

thessalum, commonly known as the Greek leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, white to purple flowers in umbel-like clusters, which appear in the spring. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is characterized by its bulbous base and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.
