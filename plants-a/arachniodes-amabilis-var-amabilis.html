Arachniodes amabilis var. amabilis

Arachniodes amabilis var. amabilis, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. The plant has a compact growth habit, with fronds that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
