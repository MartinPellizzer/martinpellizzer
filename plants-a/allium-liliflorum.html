Allium liliflorum

Allium liliflorum, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically bluish-violet in color, resembling tiny grapes, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its fragrant, early spring blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Allium liliflorum is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
