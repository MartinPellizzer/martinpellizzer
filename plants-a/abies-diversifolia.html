Abies diversifolia

Abies diversifolia, commonly known as the variable fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking bronze color in the autumn. The tree produces small, spherical cones that hang from the branches, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. Its leaves are typically flat and have a distinct two-tone appearance, with a darker upper surface and a lighter underside.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist climates.
