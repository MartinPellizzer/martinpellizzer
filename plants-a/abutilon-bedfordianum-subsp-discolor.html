Abutilon bedfordianum subsp. discolor

Abutilon bedfordianum subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant is a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a velvety texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
