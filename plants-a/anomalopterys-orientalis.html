Anomalopterys orientalis

Anomalopterys orientalis, commonly known as the Japanese silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically grayish-white in color with a rough, papery texture. The tree produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by pale yellowish fruits that split open to release seeds. Its unique leaf structure and ornamental appearance make it a distinctive species in botanical gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for specific horticultural conditions.
