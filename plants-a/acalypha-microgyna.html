Acalypha microgyna

Acalypha microgyna, commonly known as the little leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
