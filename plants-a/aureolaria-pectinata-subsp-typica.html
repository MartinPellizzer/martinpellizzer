Aureolaria pectinata subsp. typica

Aureolaria pectinata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
