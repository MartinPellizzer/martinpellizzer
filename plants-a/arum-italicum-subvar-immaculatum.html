Arum italicum subvar. immaculatum

Arum italicum subvar. immaculatum, commonly known as the white arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinctive, spatulate leaf sheath. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a white, waxy spathe. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, typical of the Araceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden borders.
