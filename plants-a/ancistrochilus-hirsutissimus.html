Ancistrochilus hirsutissimus

Ancistrochilus hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a prominent, hairy lip that aids in pollination.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
