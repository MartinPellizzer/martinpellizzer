Abies mariesii

Abies mariesii, commonly known as the Maries fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Abies in the family Pinaceae, characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its foliage is dark green and dense, with a distinct silvery-white underside on the needles.

The cones are small and often hang downward, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
