Acanthophyllum haussknechtii

Acanthophyllum haussknechtii, commonly known as the sandmat, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and spiny, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are adapted to attract pollinators in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited rainfall.

Acanthophyllum haussknechtii plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
