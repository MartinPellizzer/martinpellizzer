Aconitum variegatum var. camarum

Aconitum variegatum var. camarum, commonly known as the Variegated Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a variegated appearance due to its name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
