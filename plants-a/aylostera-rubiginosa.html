Aylostera rubiginosa

Aylostera rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained aylostera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish or purplish tinge along the veins. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Aylostera rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
