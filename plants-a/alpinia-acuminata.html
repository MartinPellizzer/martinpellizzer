Alpinia acuminata

Alpinia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Alpinia acuminata is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
