Asiasarum sieboldii var. versicolor

Asiasarum sieboldii var. versicolor, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant has slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom from late spring to early summer.
