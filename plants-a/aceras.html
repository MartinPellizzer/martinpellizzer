Aceras

Aceras, commonly known as the bluebell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plants typically have strap-like leaves and are often found in woodland areas. They are native to parts of Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.

Aceras species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
