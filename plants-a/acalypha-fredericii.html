Acalypha fredericii

Acalypha fredericii, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Acalypha fredericii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its unique foliage.
