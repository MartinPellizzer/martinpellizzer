Abronia nana var. lanciformis

Abronia nana var. lanciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
