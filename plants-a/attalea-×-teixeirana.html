Attalea × teixeirana

Attalea × teixeirana, commonly known as the hybrid palm, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Attalea speciosa and Attalea phalerata.

It is native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in regions of Brazil where its parent species overlap. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils.

The fruit of Attalea × teixeirana produces edible seeds, which are an important food source for both wildlife and local human populations.
