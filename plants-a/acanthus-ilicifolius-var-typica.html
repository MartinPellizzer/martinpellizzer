Acanthus ilicifolius var. typica

Acanthus ilicifolius var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, deeply lobed leaves with a silvery underside, and its inflorescences consist of spiny, bract-like structures that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats. Its architectural form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
