Asyneuma thomsonii

Asyneuma thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Asyneuma thomsonii is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
