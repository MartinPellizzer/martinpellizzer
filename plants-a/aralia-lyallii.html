Aralia lyallii

Aralia lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves that are often divided into several leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicle inflorescences, which are followed by berries that mature to a dark red color. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and adaptability to cooler, moist environments.

Aralia lyallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
