Anthosachne multiflora

Anthosachne multiflora, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous growth habit and small, three-lobed flowers.

Anthosachne multiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
