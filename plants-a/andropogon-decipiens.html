Andropogon decipiens

Andropogon decipiens, commonly known as the false bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon decipiens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
