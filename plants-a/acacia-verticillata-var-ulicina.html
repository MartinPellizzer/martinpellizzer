Acacia verticillata var. ulicina

Acacia verticillata var. ulicina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, clustered leaves that are often narrow and twisted. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are grouped in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in sandy or rocky areas.
