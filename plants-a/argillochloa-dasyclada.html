Argillochloa dasyclada

Argillochloa dasyclada, commonly known as the slender sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh, sandy environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of dust, aiding in water retention. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Argillochloa dasyclada plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in dune and coastal habitats.
