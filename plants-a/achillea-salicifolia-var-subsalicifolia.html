Achillea salicifolia var. subsalicifolia

Achillea salicifolia var. subsalicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear basal and alternate leaves, which are narrow, linear, and often hairy. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
