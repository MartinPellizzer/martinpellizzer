Alopecurus arundinaceus subsp. castellanus

Alopecurus arundinaceus subsp.

castellanus, commonly known as the Castle reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
