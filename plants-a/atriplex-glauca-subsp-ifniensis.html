Atriplex glauca subsp. ifniensis

Atriplex glauca subsp.

ifniensis, commonly known as the Canary Island saltbush, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to arid and coastal environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
