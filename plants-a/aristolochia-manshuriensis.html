Aristolochia manshuriensis

Aristolochia manshuriensis, commonly known as the Chinese birthwort, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces distinctive, maroon-colored, pipe-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its complex, winding stems and its ability to climb by twining around supports. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
