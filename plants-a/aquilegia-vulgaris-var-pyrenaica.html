Aquilegia vulgaris var. pyrenaica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in a raceme. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
