Artemisia montevidensis

Artemisia montevidensis, commonly known as the Monte Video wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its essential oils contain compounds like camphor and thujone, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
