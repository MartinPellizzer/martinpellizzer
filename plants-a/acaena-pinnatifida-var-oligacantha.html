Acaena pinnatifida var. oligacantha

Acaena pinnatifida var. oligacantha, commonly known as the coastal acaena, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This variety is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize dunes.
