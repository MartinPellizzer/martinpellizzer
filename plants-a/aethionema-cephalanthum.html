Aethionema cephalanthum

Aethionema cephalanthum, commonly known as the rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, with small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in horticultural settings.
