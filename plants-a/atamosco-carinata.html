Atamosco carinata

Atamosco carinata, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its fibrous roots and robust stems allow it to withstand drought conditions.

Atamosco carinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
