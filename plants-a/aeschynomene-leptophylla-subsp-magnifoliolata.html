Aeschynomene leptophylla subsp. magnifoliolata

Aeschynomene leptophylla subsp.

magnifoliolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed aeschynomene, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves with large, broad leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically followed by small, hairy pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
