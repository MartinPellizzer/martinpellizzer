Aconitum albicans

Aconitum albicans, commonly known as the white aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, wavy margin.

Aconitum albicans is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of potent alkaloids.
