Airampoa silvestris

Airampoa silvestris, commonly known as the wild bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Airampoa silvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
