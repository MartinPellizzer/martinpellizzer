Aegilops aucheri subsp. virgata

Aegilops aucheri subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and has adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
