Allium ophiopogon

Allium ophiopogon, commonly known as the Chinese leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
