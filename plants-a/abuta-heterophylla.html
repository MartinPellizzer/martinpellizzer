Abuta heterophylla

Abuta heterophylla, commonly known as the variable abuta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that vary in shape and size depending on the plant's growth conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Abuta heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
