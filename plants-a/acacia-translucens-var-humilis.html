Acacia translucens var. humilis

Acacia translucens var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf silver wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, flat leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
