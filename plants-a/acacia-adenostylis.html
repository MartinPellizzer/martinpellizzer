Acacia adenostylis

Acacia adenostylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, feathery leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or gray tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia adenostylis is well adapted to sandy soils and coastal conditions, often thriving in areas with salt spray.

It is an important plant in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various insects and birds.
