Aristolochia compressicaulis

Aristolochia compressicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valued species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
