Allium oreotadzhikorum

Allium oreotadzhikorum, commonly known as the Tajikistan mountain garlic, is a species of wild onion native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
