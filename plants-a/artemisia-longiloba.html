Artemisia longiloba

Artemisia longiloba, commonly known as the tall wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Artemisia longiloba is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
