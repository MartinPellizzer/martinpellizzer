Aeschynomene fascicularis

Aeschynomene fascicularis, commonly known as slender goatherb, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to ovate leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Aeschynomene fascicularis is often used in forage systems and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
