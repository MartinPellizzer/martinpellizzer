Adenocarpus decorticans var. planifolius

Adenocarpus decorticans var. planifolius, commonly known as the flat-leafed burr medic, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically flat and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods that split open to release seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop for livestock.
