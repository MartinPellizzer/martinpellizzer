Anthemis pauciloba var. microstephana

Anthemis pauciloba var. microstephana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
