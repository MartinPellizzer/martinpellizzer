Aniba panurensis

Aniba panurensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species, aiding in seed dispersal. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Aniba panurensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
