Aquilegia vulgaris var. multiplicata

Aquilegia vulgaris var. multiplicata, commonly known as the garden columbine, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The variety 'multiplicata' is distinguished by its larger, more numerous flowers compared to the typical species.
