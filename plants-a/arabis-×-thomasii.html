Arabis × thomasii

Arabis × thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's rockcress, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky habitats.
