Anthericum gracilitepalum

Anthericum gracilitepalum, commonly known as the slender-tipped arum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
