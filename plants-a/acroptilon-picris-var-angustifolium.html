Acroptilon picris var. angustifolium

Acroptilon picris var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
