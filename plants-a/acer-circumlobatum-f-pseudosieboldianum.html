Acer circumlobatum f. pseudosieboldianum

Acer circumlobatum f. pseudosieboldianum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact, slow-growing deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
