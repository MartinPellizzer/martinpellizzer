Aeschynomene evenia var. major

Aeschynomene evenia var. major, commonly known as the broadleaf aeschynomene, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often used in forage systems due to its high nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and drought conditions.
