Amsinckia papillata

Amsinckia papillata, commonly known as the desert hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, raised bumps, giving it a distinctive texture.

Amsinckia papillata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
