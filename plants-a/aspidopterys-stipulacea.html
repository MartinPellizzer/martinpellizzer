Aspidopterys stipulacea

Aspidopterys stipulacea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
