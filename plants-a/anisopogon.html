Anisopogon

Anisopogon, commonly known as the golden grass or yellow grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of Australia and is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage. The plants typically grow in open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Anisopogon species are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their attractive appearance.

They produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikelets.
