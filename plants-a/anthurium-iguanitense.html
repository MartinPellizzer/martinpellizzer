Anthurium iguanitense

Anthurium iguanitense, commonly known as the iguana plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe.

Anthurium iguanitense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
