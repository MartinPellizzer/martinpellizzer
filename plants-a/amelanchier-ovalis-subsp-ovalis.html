Amelanchier ovalis subsp. ovalis

Amelanchier ovalis subsp.

ovalis, commonly known as the oval serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and wildlife habitats.
