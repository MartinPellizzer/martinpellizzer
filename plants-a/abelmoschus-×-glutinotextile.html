Abelmoschus × glutinotextile

Abelmoschus × glutinotextile, commonly known as okra, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its hairy, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and has palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible pods, which are used in various cuisines worldwide.

The botanical features of okra make it a valuable crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes.
