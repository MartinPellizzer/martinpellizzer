Alopecurus arvensis

Alopecurus arvensis, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Alopecurus arvensis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environments and is often found in temperate climates.
