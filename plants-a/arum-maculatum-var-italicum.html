Arum maculatum var. italicum

Arum maculatum var. italicum, commonly known as the Italian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features arrow-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive purple-red underside. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, typically in shades of white to pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
