Amaranthus pseudoretroflexus

Amaranthus pseudoretroflexus, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Amaranthus pseudoretroflexus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
