Ambrosia tacorensis

Ambrosia tacorensis, commonly known as the Tacorensis Ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments through its deep root system and thick, waxy leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
