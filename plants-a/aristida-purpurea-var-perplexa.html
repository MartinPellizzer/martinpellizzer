Aristida purpurea var. perplexa

Aristida purpurea var. perplexa, commonly known as the purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-based inflorescences that often appear in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in semi-arid environments.
