Armatocereus arboreus

Armatocereus arboreus, commonly known as the tree cactus, is a columnar cactus native to South America.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its spiny structure provides protection from herbivores, while its deep roots allow it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
