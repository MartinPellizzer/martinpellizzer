Asarca rypaloglossa

Asarca rypaloglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf asarum, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, waxy, maroon-colored flowers. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
