Acacia seyal var. fistula

Acacia seyal var. fistula, commonly known as the pale acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, yellow to brown stipules and its narrow, green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for its hard, durable wood.
