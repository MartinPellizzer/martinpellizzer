Andromeda glaucophylla f. latifolia

Andromeda glaucophylla f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved andromeda, is a variant of the Andromeda glaucophylla species, which belongs to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This evergreen shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
