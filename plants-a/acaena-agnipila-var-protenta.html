Acaena agnipila var. protenta

Acaena agnipila var. protenta, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces spherical, hairy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
