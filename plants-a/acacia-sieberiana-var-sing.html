Acacia sieberiana var. sing

Acacia sieberiana var. sing, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are alternate and composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom in the dry season. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
