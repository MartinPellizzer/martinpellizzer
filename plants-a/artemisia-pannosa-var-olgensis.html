Artemisia pannosa var. olgensis

Artemisia pannosa var. olgensis, commonly known as the Alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing at high altitudes. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their digestive and antiseptic properties.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.
