Acacia oncinophylla subsp. oncinophylla

Acacia oncinophylla subsp.

oncinophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, needle-like leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are borne in dense, spike-like clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
