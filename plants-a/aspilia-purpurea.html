Aspilia purpurea

Aspilia purpurea, commonly known as the purple torchflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, with bright purple bracts surrounding small, yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence.

Aspilia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
