Asplenium andersonii

Asplenium andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered closely together. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its rhizomes are short and help anchor it to its substrate, while its sporangia are borne on the underside of the leaves.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing the adaptability of ferns to diverse ecological niches.
