Acer lobelii subsp. truncatum

Acer lobelii subsp.

truncatum, commonly known as the Sierra maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are often reddish in color and turn vibrant orange in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
