Amellus salignus

Amellus salignus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round fruits. Amellus salignus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and furniture.
