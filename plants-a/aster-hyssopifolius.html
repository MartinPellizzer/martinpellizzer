Aster hyssopifolius

Aster hyssopifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family, and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
