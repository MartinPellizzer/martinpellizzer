Aster lateriflorus var. spatelliformis

Aster lateriflorus var. spatelliformis, commonly known as the Spatulateleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spatulate-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
