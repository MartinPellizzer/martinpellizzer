Auricula lutea

Auricula lutea, commonly known as the yellow auricula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and cool, moist environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The auricula lutea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
