Asarum chingchengense

Asarum chingchengense, commonly known as the Chingcheng wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its underground rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
