Amaryllis × sulcata

Amaryllis × sulcata, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is a cross between Amaryllis bellodona and Amaryllis paradisiaca, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant typically produces vibrant red, pink, or white blooms that are funnel-shaped and often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to ensure optimal growth and flowering.

Amaryllis × sulcata is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
