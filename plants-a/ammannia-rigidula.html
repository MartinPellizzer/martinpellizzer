Ammannia rigidula

Ammannia rigidula, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. Its stems are often reddish and become woody with age.

Ammannia rigidula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
