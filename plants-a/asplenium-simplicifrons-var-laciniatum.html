Asplenium simplicifrons var. laciniatum

Asplenium simplicifrons var. laciniatum, commonly known as the cut-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a soft, velvety texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture and its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
