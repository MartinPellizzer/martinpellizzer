Arenaria grandiflora f. incrassata

Arenaria grandiflora f. incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, succulent foliage that is typically gray-green in color, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
