Asperula cynanchica var. brachysiphon

Asperula cynanchica var. brachysiphon, commonly known as the dwarf greenbrier, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
