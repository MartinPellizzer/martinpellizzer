Astragalus angorensis

Astragalus angorensis, commonly known as the Angore milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus angorensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and medicinal research.
