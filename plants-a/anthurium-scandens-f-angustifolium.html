Anthurium scandens f. angustifolium

Anthurium scandens f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf climbing anthurium, is a cultivar of the Anthurium genus native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are narrower than those of typical Anthurium species. The plant is a climbing vine with a thick, fleshy stem and produces spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. Its foliage is often a deep green with a glossy texture, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.

This variety thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
