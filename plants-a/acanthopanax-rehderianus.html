Acanthopanax rehderianus

Acanthopanax rehderianus, commonly known as the Chinese five-leaf aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
