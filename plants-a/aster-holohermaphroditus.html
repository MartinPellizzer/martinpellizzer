Aster holohermaphroditus

Aster holohermaphroditus, commonly known as the common ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces a milky sap when damaged.

The plant is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
