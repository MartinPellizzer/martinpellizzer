Apocynum russanovii

Apocynum russanovii, commonly known as the Russian milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its flowers are typically followed by follicles that contain toxic seeds.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
