Acacia wightii

Acacia wightii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. Acacia wightii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for honeybees and other pollinators.
