Anthyllis hermanniae subsp. corsica

Anthyllis hermanniae subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
