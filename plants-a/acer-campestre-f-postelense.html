Acer campestre f. postelense

Acer campestre f. postelense, commonly known as the Postel's maple, is a cultivar of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that often display a striking yellow or orange coloration in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a compact, rounded shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This ornamental variety is valued for its vibrant seasonal color and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
