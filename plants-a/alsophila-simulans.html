Alsophila simulans

Alsophila simulans, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a large, tree-like fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its massive, segmented trunk and large, bipinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Alsophila simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
