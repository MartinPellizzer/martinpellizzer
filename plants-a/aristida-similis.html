Aristida similis

Aristida similis, commonly known as slender wiregrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and dunes.

Aristida similis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
