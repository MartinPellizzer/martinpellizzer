Atriplex patula subsp. microsperma

Atriplex patula subsp.

microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, adapting it to harsh, saline environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its seeds are minute, contributing to its ability to disperse over long distances. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
