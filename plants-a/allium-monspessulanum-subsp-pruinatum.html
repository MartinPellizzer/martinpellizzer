Allium monspessulanum subsp. pruinatum

Allium monspessulanum subsp.

pruinatum, commonly known as the Prune-leaved Garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grayish-blue, lance-shaped leaves covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in umbel-like clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
