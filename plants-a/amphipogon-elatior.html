Amphipogon elatior

Amphipogon elatior, commonly known as the tall sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
