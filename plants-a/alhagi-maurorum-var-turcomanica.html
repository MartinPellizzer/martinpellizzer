Alhagi maurorum var. turcomanica

Alhagi maurorum var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman goatweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions and is sometimes used as forage for livestock.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
