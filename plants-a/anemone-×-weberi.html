Anemone × weberi

Anemone × weberi, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Anemone hupehensis and Anemone nemorosa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy, long-stemmed flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
