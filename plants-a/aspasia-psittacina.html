Aspasia psittacina

Aspasia psittacina, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy yellow flowers. Aspasia psittacina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
