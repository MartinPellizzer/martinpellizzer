Arctium helianthum

Arctium helianthum, commonly known as the common burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features a tall, hairy stem with large, heart-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in a spherical shape, surrounded by spiny, seed-producing burrs.

The roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
