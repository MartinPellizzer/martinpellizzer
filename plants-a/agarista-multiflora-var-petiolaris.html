Agarista multiflora var. petiolaris

Agarista multiflora var. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled agarista, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique growth habit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
