Abrus acutifolius

Abrus acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces distinctive, ornamental, and sometimes toxic red seeds encased in a spiny pod. Its leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, with a glossy surface.

Abrus acutifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
