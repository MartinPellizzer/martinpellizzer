Acer pictum var. savatieri

Acer pictum var. savatieri, commonly known as the red maple or red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red to orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly for its vibrant fall foliage.
