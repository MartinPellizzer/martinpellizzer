Acetosa lapponica

Acetosa lapponica, commonly known as Arctic sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to their high content of organic acids.

Acetosa lapponica plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing food for various herbivores.
