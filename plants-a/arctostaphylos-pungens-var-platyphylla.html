Arctostaphylos pungens var. platyphylla

Arctostaphylos pungens var. platyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in white or pink during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
