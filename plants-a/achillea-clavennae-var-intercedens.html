Achillea clavennae var. intercedens

Achillea clavennae var. intercedens, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
