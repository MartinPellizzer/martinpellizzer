Atractylis acaulis

Atractylis acaulis, commonly known as the barrenleaf tortisefoot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Atractylis acaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
