Acacia piauhiensis

Acacia piauhiensis, commonly known as the Piauhy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds.

It is adapted to humid, tropical climates and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various species.
