Alyxia tetraquetra

Alyxia tetraquetra, commonly known as the four-angled alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wooded areas. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a distinctive four-angled stem. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in the spring.

Alyxia tetraquetra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
