Aframomum spiroligulatum

Aframomum spiroligulatum, commonly known as the spiral-fruited cardamom, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiral-shaped fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, spicy scent, similar to other members of the ginger family.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
