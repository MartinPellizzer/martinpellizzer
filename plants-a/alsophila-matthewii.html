Alsophila matthewii

Alsophila matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the development of its distinctive, fibrous trunk.

The fronds are typically evergreen and contribute to the plant's ornamental value in both natural and cultivated settings.
