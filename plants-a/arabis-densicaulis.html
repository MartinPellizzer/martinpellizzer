Arabis densicaulis

Arabis densicaulis, commonly known as the dense-stemmed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other familiar plants like cabbage and mustard. This perennial plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
