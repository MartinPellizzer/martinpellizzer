Arundinaria oiwakensis

Arundinaria oiwakensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria oiwakensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
