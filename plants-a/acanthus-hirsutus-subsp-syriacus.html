Acanthus hirsutus subsp. syriacus

Acanthus hirsutus subsp.

syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features large, palmate leaves with a distinctive, wavy margin and a silvery underside.

The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that enclose small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This subspecies is closely related to other acanthus species but is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and geographic distribution.
