Ardisia glomeriflora

Ardisia glomeriflora, commonly known as the clustered flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia glomeriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
