Acacia acanthaster

Acacia acanthaster, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia acanthaster is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
