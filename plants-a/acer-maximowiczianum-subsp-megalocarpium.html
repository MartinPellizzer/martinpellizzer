Acer maximowiczianum subsp. megalocarpium

Acer maximowiczianum subsp.

megalocarpium, commonly known as the giant maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

The tree produces distinctive, showy flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange fruits that split open to release seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
