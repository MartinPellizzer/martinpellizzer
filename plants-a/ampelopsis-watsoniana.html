Ampelopsis watsoniana

Ampelopsis watsoniana, commonly known as Chinese ivy, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its climbing habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and landscaping.
