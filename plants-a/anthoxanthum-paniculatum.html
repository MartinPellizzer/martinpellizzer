Anthoxanthum paniculatum

Anthoxanthum paniculatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often yellowish or brownish in hue.

Anthoxanthum paniculatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
