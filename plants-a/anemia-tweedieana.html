Anemia tweedieana

Anemia tweedieana, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-green foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Anemia tweedieana is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
