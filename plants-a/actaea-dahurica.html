Actaea dahurica

Actaea dahurica, commonly known as white beadroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, bead-like flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its roots are often harvested for medicinal purposes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Actaea dahurica is also known for its toxic properties, containing protoanemonin and other compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
