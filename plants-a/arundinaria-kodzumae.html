Arundinaria kodzumae

Arundinaria kodzumae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kodzumae bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine, silky texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Arundinaria kodzumae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
