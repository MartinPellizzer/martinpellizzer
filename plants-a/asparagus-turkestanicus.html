Asparagus turkestanicus

Asparagus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Central Asian asparagus, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Asparagus turkestanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
