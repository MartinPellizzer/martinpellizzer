Anemone obtusiloba subsp. nepalensis

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal anemone, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region. It features a basal rosette of broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
