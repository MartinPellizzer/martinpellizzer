Amelanchier japonica

Amelanchier japonica, commonly known as Japanese serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often consumed fresh or used in jams. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

The flowers, which appear in spring, are white or pale pink and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
