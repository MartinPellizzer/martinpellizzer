Aframomum arundinaceum

Aframomum arundinaceum, commonly known as African yew or black pepper plant, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa, characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple or black berries. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
