Acisanthera simplex

Acisanthera simplex, commonly known as the simple acanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acisanthera simplex is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
