Anemone trifolia

Anemone trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pinkish flowers with numerous stamens and a central pistil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its trifoliate leaves, composed of three leaflets, are palmately arranged and have a finely toothed margin.

Anemone trifolia is often found in temperate regions and is known for its early spring blooming habit.
