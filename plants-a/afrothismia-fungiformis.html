Afrothismia fungiformis

Afrothismia fungiformis, commonly known as the African star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
