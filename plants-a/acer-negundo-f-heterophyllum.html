Acer negundo f. heterophyllum

Acer negundo f. heterophyllum, commonly known as the box elder maple, is a variant of the box elder tree characterized by its distinct leaf morphology.

It features alternate, palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are often narrower and more elongated compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by samaras with two winged seeds. It is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
