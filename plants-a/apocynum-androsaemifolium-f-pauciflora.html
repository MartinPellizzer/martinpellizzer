Apocynum androsaemifolium f. pauciflora

Apocynum androsaemifolium f. pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dogbane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have a distinctive yellowish-green color with prominent red stamens. This subspecies is notable for its reduced floral display compared to the typical form, with fewer flowers per inflorescence.

It is native to the eastern United States and often grows in moist, woodland habitats.
