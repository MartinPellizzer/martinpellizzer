Aloe parvicoma

Aloe parvicoma, commonly known as the dwarf aloe or miniature aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers on tall, branching stems during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe parvicoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
