Anemia makrinii

Anemia makrinii, commonly known as the mountain anemone, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Anemia makrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
