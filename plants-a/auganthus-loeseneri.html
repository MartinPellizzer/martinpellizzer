Auganthus loeseneri

Auganthus loeseneri, commonly known as the Cape York lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
