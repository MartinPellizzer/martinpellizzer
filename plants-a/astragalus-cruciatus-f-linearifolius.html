Astragalus cruciatus f. linearifolius

Astragalus cruciatus f. linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved milkvetch, is a variant of the Astragalus genus, which belongs to the legume family Fabaceae.

This plant is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of many legumes. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
