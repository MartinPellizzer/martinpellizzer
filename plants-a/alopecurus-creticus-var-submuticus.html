Alopecurus creticus var. submuticus

Alopecurus creticus var. submuticus, commonly known as the Cretan hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
