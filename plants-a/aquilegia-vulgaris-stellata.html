Aquilegia vulgaris stellata

Aquilegia vulgaris stellata, commonly known as the star-shaped columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, with a distinctive star-like shape. The plant has palmate leaves divided into five to seven lobes, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aquilegia vulgaris stellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
