Alkekengi

Alkekengi, commonly known as the Egyptian lantern or false mandrake, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features striking, lantern-shaped orange or red berries that mature in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its berries are toxic when ripe but can be used in culinary applications when harvested unripe.

Alkekengi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in folklore and medicine.
