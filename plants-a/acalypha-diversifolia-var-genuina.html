Acalypha diversifolia var. genuina

Acalypha diversifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically upright, with branching that can be either shrub-like or tree-like depending on environmental conditions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments in its native habitat.
