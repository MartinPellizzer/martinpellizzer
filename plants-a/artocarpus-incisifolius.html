Artocarpus incisifolius

Artocarpus incisifolius, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces a fruit that is both edible and culturally significant. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its fruit, which can weigh up to 20 pounds, is rich in carbohydrates and has been a staple food source for many island communities.

The tree also has historical importance in Polynesian navigation and settlement.
