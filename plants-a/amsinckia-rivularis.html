Amsinckia rivularis

Amsinckia rivularis, commonly known as the riverbank globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as riverbanks and stream sides. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent, hood-like petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Amsinckia rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
