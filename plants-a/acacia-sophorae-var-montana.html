Acacia sophorae var. montana

Acacia sophorae var. montana, commonly known as mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional crafts and construction.

The species is also adapted to dry and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging ecological conditions.
