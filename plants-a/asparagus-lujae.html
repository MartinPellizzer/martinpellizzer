Asparagus lujae

Asparagus lujae, commonly known as the Lujan asparagus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers. Its unique morphology includes a thick, fleshy stem that stores moisture.

Asparagus lujae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
