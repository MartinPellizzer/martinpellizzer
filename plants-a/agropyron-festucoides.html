Agropyron festucoides

Agropyron festucoides, commonly known as prairie grass or bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to form extensive colonies, contributing to its ecological significance.
