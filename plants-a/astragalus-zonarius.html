Astragalus zonarius

Astragalus zonarius, commonly known as the zoned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus zonarius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
