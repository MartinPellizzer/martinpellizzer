Albuca capitata

Albuca capitata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect stems with showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It blooms in summer and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.

The bulbs are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
