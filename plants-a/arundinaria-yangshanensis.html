Arundinaria yangshanensis

Arundinaria yangshanensis, commonly known as the Yangshan bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

This bamboo is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
