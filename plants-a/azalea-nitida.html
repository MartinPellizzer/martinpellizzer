Azalea nitida

Azalea nitida, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Azalea nitida thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
