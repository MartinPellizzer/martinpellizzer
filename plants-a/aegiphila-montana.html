Aegiphila montana

Aegiphila montana, commonly known as the mountain aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
