Andryala sinuata subsp. laxiflora

Andryala sinuata subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-leafed andryala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more loosely arranged inflorescences compared to other subspecies of Andryala sinuata.
