Anthericum falcatum

Anthericum falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered on the spadix, with the spathe serving as a colorful, protective sheath.

Anthericum falcatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
