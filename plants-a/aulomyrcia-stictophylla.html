Aulomyrcia stictophylla

Aulomyrcia stictophylla, commonly known as the red-leafed fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically reddish in color when young. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aulomyrcia stictophylla plays an important role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
