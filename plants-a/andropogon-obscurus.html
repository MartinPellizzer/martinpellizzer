Andropogon obscurus

Andropogon obscurus, commonly known as black bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster that matures into a dark, bristly panicle.

Andropogon obscurus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
