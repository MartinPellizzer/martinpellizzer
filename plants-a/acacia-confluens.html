Acacia confluens

Acacia confluens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a small, woody shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia confluens is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
