Avena kawakamii

Avena kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's oat, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Avena kawakamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
