Antirrhinum meonanthum var. genuina

Antirrhinum meonanthum var. genuina, commonly known as the slender snapdragon, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically pink to purple, with two lips, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and delicate floral display.
