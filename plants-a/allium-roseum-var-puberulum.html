Allium roseum var. puberulum

Allium roseum var. puberulum, commonly known as the woolly rose garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves, which give it its varietal name "puberulum.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like fragrance.
