Alchemilla splendens var. schmidelyana

Alchemilla splendens var. schmidelyana, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
