Acacia stenocarpa

Acacia stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical pods and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Acacia stenocarpa is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
