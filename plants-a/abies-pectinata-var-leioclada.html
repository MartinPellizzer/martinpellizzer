Abies pectinata var. leioclada

Abies pectinata var. leioclada, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-blue needles and its distinctive, flattened cone structure. The tree typically grows in high-altitude forests, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
