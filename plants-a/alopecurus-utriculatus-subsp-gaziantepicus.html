Alopecurus utriculatus subsp. gaziantepicus

Alopecurus utriculatus subsp.

gaziantepicus, commonly known as the Gaziantep sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers in clusters. Its inflorescence is often subtended by leaf-like bracts, and it is characterized by its distinctive seed capsules.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and limited distribution.
