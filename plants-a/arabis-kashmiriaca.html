Arabis kashmiriaca

Arabis kashmiriaca, commonly known as Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of northern India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. It produces small, bright yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered with fine hairs, adapting to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.
