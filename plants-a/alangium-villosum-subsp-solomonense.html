Alangium villosum subsp. solomonense

Alangium villosum subsp.

solomonense, commonly known as Solomon's alangium, is a shrub native to the Solomon Islands and neighboring regions of the Pacific. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces yellowish-green berries that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
