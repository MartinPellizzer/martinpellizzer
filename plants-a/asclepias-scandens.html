Asclepias scandens

Asclepias scandens, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing or trailing perennial that produces clusters of pink to reddish-orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and is part of the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Asclepias scandens plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
