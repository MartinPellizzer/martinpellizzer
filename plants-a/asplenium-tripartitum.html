Asplenium tripartitum

Asplenium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sorus. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Asplenium tripartitum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
