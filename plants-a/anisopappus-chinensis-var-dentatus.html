Anisopappus chinensis var. dentatus

Anisopappus chinensis var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often lobed or toothed, giving it its botanical variant name, dentatus.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
