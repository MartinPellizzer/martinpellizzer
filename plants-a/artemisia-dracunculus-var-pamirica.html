Artemisia dracunculus var. pamirica

Artemisia dracunculus var. pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antimicrobial properties.

Despite its medicinal uses, it is less commonly cultivated than other Artemisia species due to its specific environmental requirements.
