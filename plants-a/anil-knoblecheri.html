Anil knoblecheri

Anil knoblecheri, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows from a bulbous stem, which allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a strong, sweet scent, which attracts pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Anil knoblecheri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
