Antirrhinum hispanicum subvar. glabrum

Antirrhinum hispanicum subvar. glabrum, commonly known as the Spanish snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its glandular-pubescent stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, two-lipped flowers that range in color from red to yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
