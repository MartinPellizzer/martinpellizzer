Actephila excelsa var. thomsonii

Actephila excelsa var. thomsonii, commonly known as the Thomson's evergreen heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, dense foliage, and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
