Allium macranthum

Allium macranthum, commonly known as the giant garlic or purple garlic, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of lavender to purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are edible, though they are often used as ornamental plants due to their striking appearance. Allium macranthum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
