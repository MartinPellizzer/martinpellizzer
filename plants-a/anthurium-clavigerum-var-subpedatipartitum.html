Anthurium clavigerum var. subpedatipartitum

Anthurium clavigerum var. subpedatipartitum, commonly known as the spotted anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with striking red and green variegation, often adorned with dark spots. The plant produces spadices, which are bright orange or red, spadix-like structures that serve as its flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
