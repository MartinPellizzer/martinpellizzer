Aria nivea latifolia

Aria nivea latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved snowleaf, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, silver-white leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant's unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
