Arenaria peloponnesiaca

Arenaria peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnese sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of the Peloponnese region in Greece.

It typically features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate harsh conditions make it well-suited for rocky gardens and xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
