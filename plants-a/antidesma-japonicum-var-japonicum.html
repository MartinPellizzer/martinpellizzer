Antidesma japonicum var. japonicum

Antidesma japonicum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese antidesma, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and conservation purposes.
