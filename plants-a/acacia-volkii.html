Acacia volkii

Acacia volkii, commonly known as the Volk's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched trunk and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain hard, angular seeds. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its leaves are typically small and feathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.
