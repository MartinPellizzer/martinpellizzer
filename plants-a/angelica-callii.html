Angelica callii

Angelica callii, commonly known as the Chinese angelica or Dahuang, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica callii is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in soups and medicinal tonics.
