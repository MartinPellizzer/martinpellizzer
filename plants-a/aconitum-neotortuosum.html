Aconitum neotortuosum

Aconitum neotortuosum, commonly known as the Neotortuous Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmate leaves with a deep lobed structure, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its striking appearance and toxic properties, it is both a subject of botanical interest and a plant requiring careful handling.
