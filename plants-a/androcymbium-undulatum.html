Androcymbium undulatum

Androcymbium undulatum, commonly known as the undulating orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
