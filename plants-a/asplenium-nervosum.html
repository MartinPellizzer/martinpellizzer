Asplenium nervosum

Asplenium nervosum, commonly known as the veined spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to various regions of the world, including Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices, walls, and forest floors. The plant has a distinctive, compact rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and contain spores for propagation.

Asplenium nervosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
