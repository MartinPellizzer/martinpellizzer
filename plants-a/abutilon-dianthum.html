Abutilon dianthum

Abutilon dianthum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its fibrous roots and hairy stems are adaptations to its warm, humid environments.

Abutilon dianthum is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
