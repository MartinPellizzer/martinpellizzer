Auganthus normanianus

Auganthus normanianus, commonly known as the Normandy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Auganthus normanianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
