Acacia kenneallyi

Acacia kenneallyi, commonly known as the Kenneally's wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its slender, shrub-like form with phyllodes that are typically linear and slightly curved. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia kenneallyi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a range of drought-resistant features.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
