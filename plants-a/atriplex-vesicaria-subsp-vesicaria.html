Atriplex vesicaria subsp. vesicaria

Atriplex vesicaria subsp.

vesicaria, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its succulent, often glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

The subspecies is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
