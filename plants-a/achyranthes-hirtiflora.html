Achyranthes hirtiflora

Achyranthes hirtiflora, commonly known as hairy-flowered knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible or medicinal properties. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
