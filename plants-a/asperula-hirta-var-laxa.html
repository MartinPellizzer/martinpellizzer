Asperula hirta var. laxa

Asperula hirta var. laxa, commonly known as the loose-leaved woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
