Acacia dissoneura

Acacia dissoneura, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are small and delicate. Its yellow flowers are arranged in cylindrical spikes and bloom during the warmer months.

Acacia dissoneura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
