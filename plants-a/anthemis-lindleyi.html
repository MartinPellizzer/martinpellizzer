Anthemis lindleyi

Anthemis lindleyi, commonly known as Lindley's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Anthemis lindleyi is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
