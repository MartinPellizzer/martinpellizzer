Achillea kraettliana

Achillea kraettliana, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are gray-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Achillea kraettliana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
