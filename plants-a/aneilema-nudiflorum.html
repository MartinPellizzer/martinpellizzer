Aneilema nudiflorum

Aneilema nudiflorum, commonly known as the naked-flower arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a showy, leaf-like structure called a spathe. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Aneilema nudiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
