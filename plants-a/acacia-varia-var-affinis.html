Acacia varia var. affinis

Acacia varia var. affinis, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically has slender, often thorned branches and produces yellow to golden flowers in clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia varia var. affinis is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native wildlife species.
