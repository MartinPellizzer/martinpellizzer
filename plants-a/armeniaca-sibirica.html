Armeniaca sibirica

Armeniaca sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its pink or white flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is prized for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
