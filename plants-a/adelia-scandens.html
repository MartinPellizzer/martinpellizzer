Adelia scandens

Adelia scandens, commonly known as the climbing arum, is a tropical plant characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves.

It produces striking, spatulate leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm environments. It features a showy inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Adelia scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
