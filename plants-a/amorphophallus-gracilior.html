Amorphophallus gracilior

Amorphophallus gracilior, commonly known as the slender giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and spathe. The plant produces a large, leafy stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Its underground tubers are used as a starchy food source in some regions.

The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
