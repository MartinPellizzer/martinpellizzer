Astragalus viridis var. impensus

Astragalus viridis var. impensus, commonly known as the green milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and often deeply rooted. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is commonly found in steppe and grassland habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
