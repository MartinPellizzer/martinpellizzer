Acinodendron lacerum

Acinodendron lacerum, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Acinodendron lacerum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
