Apodanthera lasiocalyx

Apodanthera lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-hair mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular plant in xeriscaping.
