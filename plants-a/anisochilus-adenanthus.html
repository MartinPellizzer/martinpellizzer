Anisochilus adenanthus

Anisochilus adenanthus, commonly known as the slender rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
