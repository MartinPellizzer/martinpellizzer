Aster parthianus

Aster parthianus, commonly known as the Parthian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Aster parthianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
