Allantodia axillaris

Allantodia axillaris, commonly known as the axillary allantodia, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Allantodia axillaris is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
