Acarna

Acarna, commonly known as the spider plant, is a popular ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red berries. It is easy to care for, making it a favorite among indoor gardeners.

Acarna thrives in well-draining soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
