Avena fatua subsp. barbata

Avena fatua subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the barbed oat, is a wild grass species characterized by its distinctive barbed awns on the glumes and lemmas. It is native to Europe and Asia but has become an invasive species in many parts of the world, particularly in agricultural areas.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet clusters are typical of the Poaceae family.

The barbed oat is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops.
