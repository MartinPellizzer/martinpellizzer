Allium pratense

Allium pratense, commonly known as purple onion or grass leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical flower stalks that bear small, purple or pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are often used in culinary applications. Allium pratense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
