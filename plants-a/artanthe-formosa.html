Artanthe formosa

Artanthe formosa, commonly known as the California poppy, is a striking annual herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a slender, branching stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a symbol of California and its use in traditional medicine.
