Aleuritia eximia

Aleuritia eximia, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Aleuritia eximia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
