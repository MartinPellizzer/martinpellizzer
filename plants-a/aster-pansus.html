Aster pansus

Aster pansus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and a distinctive cone-shaped center. The plant typically grows between 2 to 4 feet tall and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Aster pansus is a hardy perennial that blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
