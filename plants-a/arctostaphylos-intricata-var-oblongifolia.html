Arctostaphylos intricata var. oblongifolia

Arctostaphylos intricata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblongleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland chaparral habitats.
