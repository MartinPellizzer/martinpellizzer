Aleuritopteris flaccida

Aleuritopteris flaccida, commonly known as the soft shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically pale green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aleuritopteris flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
