Acer negundo f. wesmaelianum

Acer negundo f. wesmaelianum, commonly known as the western box elder, is a cultivar of the box elder tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which typically have three to five lobes, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces samaras, or winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often used in landscaping for its adaptability and ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
