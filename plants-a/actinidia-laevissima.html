Actinidia laevissima

Actinidia laevissima, commonly known as the fuzzy kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, which is typically hairy and brown when mature, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Actinidia laevissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious fruit.
