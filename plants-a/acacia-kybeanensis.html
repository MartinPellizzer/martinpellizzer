Acacia kybeanensis

Acacia kybeanensis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow floral spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The slender wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
