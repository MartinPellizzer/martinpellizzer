Anguria pachypoda

Anguria pachypoda, commonly known as the giant cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thick, ribbed stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and abundant sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often cultivated plant in certain regions.
