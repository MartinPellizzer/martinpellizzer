Achyrophorus deserticola

Achyrophorus deserticola, commonly known as the desert sandburs, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant has small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss, making it well-suited for harsh desert environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in sandy soils along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
