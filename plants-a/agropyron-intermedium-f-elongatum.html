Agropyron intermedium f. elongatum

Agropyron intermedium f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions. It is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural applications.
