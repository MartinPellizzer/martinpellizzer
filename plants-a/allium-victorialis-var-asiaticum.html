Allium victorialis var. asiaticum

Allium victorialis var. asiaticum, commonly known as the Chinese leek or Asiatic garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like, hollow, and cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in Asian cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.
