Amberboa phaeopappa

Amberboa phaeopappa, commonly known as the purple-leafed amberboa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish-purple in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
