Andropogon jamesii

Andropogon jamesii, commonly known as big bluestem, is a tall grass native to the tallgrass prairies of North America.

It is characterized by its bluish-green foliage and a distinctive, hollow stem with nodes. This plant is a key component of prairie ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often forming dense stands in open landscapes.

Big bluestem is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
