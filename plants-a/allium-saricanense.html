Allium saricanense

Allium saricanense, commonly known as the Sarıkayır garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in local cuisines for their mild garlic flavor.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
