Arundarbor picta

Arundarbor picta, commonly known as the painted arbor, is a striking plant native to the temperate forests of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored leaves that display shades of green and gold, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays due to its unique coloration.

Arundarbor picta thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
