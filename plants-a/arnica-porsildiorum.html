Arnica porsildiorum

Arnica porsildiorum, commonly known as the alpine arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica porsildiorum is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
