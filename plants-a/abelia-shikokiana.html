Abelia shikokiana

Abelia shikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Abelia shikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
