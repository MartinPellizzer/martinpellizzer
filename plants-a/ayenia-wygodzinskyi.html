Ayenia wygodzinskyi

Ayenia wygodzinskyi, commonly known as the Wygodzinsky's ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ayenia wygodzinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
