Anemone rupestris var. wallichii

Anemone rupestris var. wallichii, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
