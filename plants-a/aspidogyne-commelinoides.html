Aspidogyne commelinoides

Aspidogyne commelinoides, commonly known as the marsh arrowgrass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Commelinaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in clusters. Aspidogyne commelinoides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
