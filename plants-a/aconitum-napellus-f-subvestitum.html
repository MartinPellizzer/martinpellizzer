Aconitum napellus f. subvestitum

Aconitum napellus f. subvestitum, commonly known as the Subvestitum Monkshood, is a hybrid cultivar of the Aconitum genus.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its root system is tuberous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
