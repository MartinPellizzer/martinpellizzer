Aeschynomene cannabina

Aeschynomene cannabina, commonly known as wild hemp, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by slender, cylindrical pods containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Aeschynomene cannabina is also valued for its potential in soil fixation and as a forage crop.
