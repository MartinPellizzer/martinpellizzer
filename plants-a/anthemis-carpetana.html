Anthemis carpetana

Anthemis carpetana, commonly known as the carpetweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its leaves are finely divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
