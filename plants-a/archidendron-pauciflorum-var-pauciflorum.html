Archidendron pauciflorum var. pauciflorum

Archidendron pauciflorum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the rarest form of the genus, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological niche.
