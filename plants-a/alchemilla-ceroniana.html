Alchemilla ceroniana

Alchemilla ceroniana, commonly known as the Mexican lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla ceroniana thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.
