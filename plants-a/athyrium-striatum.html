Athyrium striatum

Athyrium striatum, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a perennial fern native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
