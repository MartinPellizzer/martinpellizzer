Amaralia penduliflora

Amaralia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaralia penduliflora is popular in ornamental gardening for its bold, showy blooms and ease of cultivation.
