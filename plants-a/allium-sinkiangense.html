Allium sinkiangense

Allium sinkiangense, commonly known as the Xinjiang garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Xinjiang region of China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
