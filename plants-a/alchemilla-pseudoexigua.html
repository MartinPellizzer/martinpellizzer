Alchemilla pseudoexigua

Alchemilla pseudoexigua, commonly known as narrowleaf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Alchemilla pseudoexigua is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
