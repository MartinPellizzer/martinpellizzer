Acacia polybotrya

Acacia polybotrya, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver-gray stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Acacia polybotrya is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water conservation.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
