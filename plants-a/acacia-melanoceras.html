Acacia melanoceras

Acacia melanoceras, commonly known as the blackwood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia melanoceras is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its hard wood and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
