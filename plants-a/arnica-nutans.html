Arnica nutans

Arnica nutans, commonly known as the nodding arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Arnica nutans is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
