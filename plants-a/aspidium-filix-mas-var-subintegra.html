Aspidium filix-mas var. subintegra

Aspidium filix-mas var. subintegra, commonly known as the broad-leafed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed, and often glossy fronds that give it a striking appearance. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive U-shaped arrangement of pinnae.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
