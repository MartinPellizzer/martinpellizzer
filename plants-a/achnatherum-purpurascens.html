Achnatherum purpurascens

Achnatherum purpurascens, commonly known as purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that range in color from green to purple, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Purple needlegrass plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
