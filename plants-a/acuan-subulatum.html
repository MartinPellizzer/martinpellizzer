Acuan subulatum

Acuan subulatum, commonly known as the needleleaf acuan, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact manner. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring or early summer. Acuan subulatum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
