Anchusa arvensis subsp. occidentalis

Anchusa arvensis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western blueweed, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides. It is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses. Anchusa arvensis subsp.
