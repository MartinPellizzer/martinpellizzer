Acrocephalus speciosus

Acrocephalus speciosus, commonly known as the elegant reed warbler, is a small passerine bird native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender body, long legs, and a distinctive yellowish-brown plumage. This species typically inhabits wetlands, marshes, and reed beds, where it forages for insects and small invertebrates. During the breeding season, males display striking black and white plumage to attract mates.

Acrocephalus speciosus is known for its melodic and complex songs, which are often heard during the early morning hours.
