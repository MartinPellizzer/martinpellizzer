Abutilon pakistanicum

Abutilon pakistanicum, commonly known as the Kashmir velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Pakistan and Kashmir.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a velvety texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate to subtropical climates.
