Artemisia persica var. subspinescens

Artemisia persica var. subspinescens, commonly known as Persian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and aromatic properties.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Artemisia genus, sharing similar ecological and medicinal traits.
