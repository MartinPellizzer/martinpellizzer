Acer palmifolium var. concolor

Acer palmifolium var. concolor, commonly known as the white maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically white or pale green on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
