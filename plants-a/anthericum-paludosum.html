Anthericum paludosum

Anthericum paludosum, commonly known as swamp lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in water, adapting to its aquatic habitat. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize wet areas effectively.

Anthericum paludosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
