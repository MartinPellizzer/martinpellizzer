Acacia diphylla

Acacia diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which consist of two pairs of leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact globular inflorescences, which attract various pollinators. Its small, woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia diphylla is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showcasing resilience through its deep root system and water-storing tissues.
