Amaranthus

Amaranthus, commonly known as pigweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the amaranth family, Amaranthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers and tough, fibrous leaves. They are native to the Americas but are now found worldwide in various climates. Amaranthus species vary in height, with some growing as small herbs and others as tall shrubs or annuals.

Many species are cultivated for their edible seeds and leaves, which are rich in nutrients.
