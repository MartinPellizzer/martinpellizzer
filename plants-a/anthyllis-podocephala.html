Anthyllis podocephala

Anthyllis podocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis podocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
