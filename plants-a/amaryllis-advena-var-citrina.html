Amaryllis advena var. citrina

Amaryllis advena var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically broad and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
