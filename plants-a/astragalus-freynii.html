Astragalus freynii

Astragalus freynii, commonly known as Freyn's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native range.

Astragalus freynii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
