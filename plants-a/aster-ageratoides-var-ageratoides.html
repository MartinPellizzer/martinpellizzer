Aster ageratoides var. ageratoides

Aster ageratoides var. ageratoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silvery-blue hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually white to pale blue, blooming in late summer and early fall.

The foliage is lance-shaped with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
