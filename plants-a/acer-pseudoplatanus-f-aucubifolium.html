Acer pseudoplatanus f. aucubifolium

Acer pseudoplatanus f. aucubifolium, commonly known as the aucubifolium maple, is a unique variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring large, lobed leaves that resemble those of the aucuba plant. This form is a natural mutation of the parent species, Acer pseudoplatanus, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shady garden spaces.
