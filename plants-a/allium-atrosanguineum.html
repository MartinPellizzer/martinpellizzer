Allium atrosanguineum

Allium atrosanguineum, commonly known as purple garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant has a strong garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its bulbs are edible but less commonly used in cooking compared to other allium species.
