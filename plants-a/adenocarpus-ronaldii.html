Adenocarpus ronaldii

Adenocarpus ronaldii, commonly known as the Ronalii's burr, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruit capsules that enclose the seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by attachment to animals or clothing. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. It has small, narrow leaves and produces yellowish or greenish flowers during the warmer months.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
