Acacia oncinophylla var. fauntleroyi

Acacia oncinophylla var. fauntleroyi, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is often used in coastal restoration projects.
