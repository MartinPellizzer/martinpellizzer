Acacia gageana

Acacia gageana, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often forming dense thickets.

Acacia gageana is well adapted to saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
