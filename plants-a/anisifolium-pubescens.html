Anisifolium pubescens

Anisifolium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, hairy, and often grayish-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Anisifolium pubescens is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in hot, dry climates.
