Arenaria lineata

Arenaria lineata, commonly known as the sand pink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Arenaria lineata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
