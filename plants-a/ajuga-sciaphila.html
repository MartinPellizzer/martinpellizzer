Ajuga sciaphila

Ajuga sciaphila, commonly known as shade ajuga, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments, making it ideal for woodland gardens. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tint.

Ajuga sciaphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to suppress weeds in shaded areas.
