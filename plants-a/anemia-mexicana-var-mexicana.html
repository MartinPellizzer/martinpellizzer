Anemia mexicana var. mexicana

Anemia mexicana var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican anemone, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pink petals and a central cluster of yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, green berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
