Anredera krapovickasii

Anredera krapovickasii, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are glossy and often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
