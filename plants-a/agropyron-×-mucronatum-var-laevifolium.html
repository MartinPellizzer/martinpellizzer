Agropyron × mucronatum var. laevifolium

Agropyron × mucronatum var. laevifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, flat leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
