Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp. pseudokusnezowii

Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp.

pseudokusnezowii, commonly known as the Delphinium-leaved Aconitum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant has palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
