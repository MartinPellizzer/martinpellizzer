Angylocalyx zenkeri

Angylocalyx zenkeri, commonly known as the Zanzibar sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
