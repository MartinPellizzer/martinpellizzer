Anagallis linifolia var. microphylla

Anagallis linifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
