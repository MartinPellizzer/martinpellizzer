Anthyllis adriatica

Anthyllis adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems.

Anthyllis adriatica is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
