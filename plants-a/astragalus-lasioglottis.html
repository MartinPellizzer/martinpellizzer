Astragalus lasioglottis

Astragalus lasioglottis, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of oval leaflets that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Astragalus lasioglottis is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
