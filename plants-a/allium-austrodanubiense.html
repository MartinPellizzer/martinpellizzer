Allium austrodanubiense

Allium austrodanubiense, commonly known as the Danube garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
