Asplenium claussenii f. latifolium

Asplenium claussenii f. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock fern, is a variant of the rock fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found growing on rock surfaces or in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are usually compact, with a delicate appearance that makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The botanical features of this fern reflect its adaptation to humid, shaded habitats, making it a unique addition to botanical collections.
