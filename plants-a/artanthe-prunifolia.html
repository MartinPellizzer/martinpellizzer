Artanthe prunifolia

Artanthe prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaved artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a soft, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Artanthe prunifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.
