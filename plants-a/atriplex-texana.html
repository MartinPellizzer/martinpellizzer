Atriplex texana

Atriplex texana, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Atriplex texana is highly salt-tolerant, making it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in saline or coastal areas.

Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its survival in harsh conditions.
