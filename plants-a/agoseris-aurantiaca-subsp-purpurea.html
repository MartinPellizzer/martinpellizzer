Agoseris aurantiaca subsp. purpurea

Agoseris aurantiaca subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that persists through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to sustainable landscaping.
