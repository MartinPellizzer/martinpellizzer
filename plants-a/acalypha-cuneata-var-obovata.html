Acalypha cuneata var. obovata

Acalypha cuneata var. obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its ovate to obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
