Anthyllis genistoides

Anthyllis genistoides, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many legumes. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in dry, sunny habitats. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Anthyllis genistoides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
