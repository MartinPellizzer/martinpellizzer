Aeginetia indica var. sekimotoana

Aeginetia indica var. sekimotoana, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with serrated edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
