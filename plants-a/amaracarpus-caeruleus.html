Amaracarpus caeruleus

Amaracarpus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Amaracarpus caeruleus make it a popular choice for gardens and public landscapes.
