Asyneuma rigidum subsp. sinai

Asyneuma rigidum subsp.

sinai, commonly known as Sinai rigid aster, is a flowering plant native to the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are rigid and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
