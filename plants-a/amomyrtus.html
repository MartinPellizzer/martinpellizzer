Amomyrtus

Amomyrtus, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

These shrubs or small trees are native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. They are characterized by their narrow, linear leaves and showy, red or orange berries that resemble Christmas ornaments, hence their common name. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with white or pink petals, and the plants often have a fragrant, resinous scent.

Amomyrtus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils.
