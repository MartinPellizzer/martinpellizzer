Alseodaphne camphorata

Alseodaphne camphorata, commonly known as the camphor laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a historical use in traditional medicine.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native flora.
