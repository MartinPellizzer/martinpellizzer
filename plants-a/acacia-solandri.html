Acacia solandri

Acacia solandri, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Acacia solandri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
