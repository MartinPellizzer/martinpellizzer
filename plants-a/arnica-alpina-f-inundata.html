Arnica alpina f. inundata

Arnica alpina f. inundata, commonly known as the alpine tawny beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowering heads surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and fibrous.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
