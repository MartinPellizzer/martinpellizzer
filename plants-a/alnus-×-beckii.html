Alnus × beckii

Alnus × beckii, commonly known as the European mountain ash or white alder, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alnus glutinosa and Alnus incana.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across Europe and parts of Asia.

Alnus × beckii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
