Asarum forbesii

Asarum forbesii, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, reddish-brown, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a creeping rhizome system. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant is known for its aromatic, spicy scent.

Asarum forbesii is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
