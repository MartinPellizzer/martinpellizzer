Alpinia haenkei

Alpinia haenkei, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or fragrant ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth with lanceolate, glossy leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Alpinia haenkei is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
