Anagallis crassifolia

Anagallis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved waterweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, and its leaves are broad and fleshy. It is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
