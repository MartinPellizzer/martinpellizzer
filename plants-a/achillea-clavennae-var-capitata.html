Achillea clavennae var. capitata

Achillea clavennae var. capitata, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
