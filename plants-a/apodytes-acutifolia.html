Apodytes acutifolia

Apodytes acutifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white, woolly flowers that resemble balls, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Apodytes acutifolia is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.
