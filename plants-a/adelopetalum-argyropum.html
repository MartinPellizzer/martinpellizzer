Adelopetalum argyropum

Adelopetalum argyropum, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a slightly puckered texture.

Adelopetalum argyropum is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Morphology

Adelopetalum argyropum has leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, with a moderate size and a bluish-green coloration.

The stem is slender, with a smooth, greyish-brown bark that becomes slightly fissured with age, and it exhibits a climbing growth pattern. The flowers are small, clustered in racemes, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in early summer, and are primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that releases small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in moist, shaded environments.
