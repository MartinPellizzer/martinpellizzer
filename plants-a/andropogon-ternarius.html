Andropogon ternarius

Andropogon ternarius, commonly known as three-branched grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences composed of spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Andropogon ternarius thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
