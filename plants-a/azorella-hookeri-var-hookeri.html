Azorella hookeri var. hookeri

Azorella hookeri var. hookeri, commonly known as the Andean mat grass, is a perennial cushion plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to theApiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Azorella hookeri var. hookeri is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
