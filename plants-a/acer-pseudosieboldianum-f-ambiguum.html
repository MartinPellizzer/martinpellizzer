Acer pseudosieboldianum f. ambiguum

Acer pseudosieboldianum f. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues, creating a striking visual effect. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage changes with the seasons, offering a range of colors from spring to autumn.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
