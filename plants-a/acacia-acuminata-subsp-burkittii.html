Acacia acuminata subsp. burkittii

Acacia acuminata subsp.

burkittii, commonly known as the Burkitt's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which appear in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are reduced to phyllodes, a feature common in many Acacia species, and it is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
