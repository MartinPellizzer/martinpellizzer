Aristida liebmannii

Aristida liebmannii, commonly known as Liebmann's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bract-like structures. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed or barren areas.

Aristida liebmannii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
