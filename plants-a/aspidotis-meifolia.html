Aspidotis meifolia

Aspidotis meifolia, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, typical of the Solanaceae family.
