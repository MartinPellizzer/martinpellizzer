Astragalus adraskanensis

Astragalus adraskanensis, commonly known as the Adraskan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus adraskanensis is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for local wildlife and its contribution to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
