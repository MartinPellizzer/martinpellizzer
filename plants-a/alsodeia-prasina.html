Alsodeia prasina

Alsodeia prasina, commonly known as green alsike, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alsodeia prasina is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.
