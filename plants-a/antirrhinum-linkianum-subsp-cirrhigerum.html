Antirrhinum linkianum subsp. cirrhigerum

Antirrhinum linkianum subsp.

cirrhigerum, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
