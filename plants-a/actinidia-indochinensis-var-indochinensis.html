Actinidia indochinensis var. indochinensis

Actinidia indochinensis var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina actinidia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
