Anadendrum marginatum

Anadendrum marginatum, commonly known as the Mexican flameblanket or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
