Aristida funiculata var. royleana

Aristida funiculata var. royleana, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and restoration projects. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed or degraded landscapes.

The botanical features of this grass make it an important component of dryland ecosystems.
