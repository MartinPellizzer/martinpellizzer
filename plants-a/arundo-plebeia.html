Arundo plebeia

Arundo plebeia, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of small, greenish flowers in the summer. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Arundo plebeia is also sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in basket-making.
