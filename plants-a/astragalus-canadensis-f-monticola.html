Astragalus canadensis f. monticola

Astragalus canadensis f. monticola, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central and northern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and mountain slopes.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
