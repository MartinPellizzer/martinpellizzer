Acrostichum oligarchium

Acrostichum oligarchium, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It has long, slender blades that can grow up to 10 feet in height, forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important pioneer species in disturbed wetland habitats.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
