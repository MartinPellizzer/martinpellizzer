Andropogon rudis

Andropogon rudis, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, blue-green foliage and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Andropogon rudis is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration ecology projects.
