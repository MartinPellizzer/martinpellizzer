Althaea wolffii

Althaea wolffii, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its fibrous roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Althaea wolffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
