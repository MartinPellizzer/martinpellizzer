Astracantha kuramensis

Astracantha kuramensis, commonly known as the Kuram mountain thistle, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying alpine flora.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, its conservation status remains uncertain.
