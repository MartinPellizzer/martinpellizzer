Arctostaphylos rudis

Arctostaphylos rudis, commonly known as the red-berried manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, red berries that persist through winter. The plant has smooth, reddish bark and produces fragrant, white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Arctostaphylos rudis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
