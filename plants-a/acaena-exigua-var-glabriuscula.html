Acaena exigua var. glabriuscula

Acaena exigua var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the slender woodrush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, though the variety glabriuscula has reduced hair, giving it a smoother texture. This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
