Aspalathus micrantha

Aspalathus micrantha, commonly known as African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the local climate, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. It is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine.

Aspalathus micrantha is the primary source of green tea, which is renowned for its health benefits.
