Abama occidentalis

Abama occidentalis, commonly known as the western abama, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Abama occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
