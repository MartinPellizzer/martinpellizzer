Acanthopanax japonicus f. ionanthus

Acanthopanax japonicus f. ionanthus, commonly known as the Japanese angel's trumpets, is a variant of the Acanthopanax genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
