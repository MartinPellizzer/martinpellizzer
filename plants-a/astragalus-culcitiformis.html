Astragalus culcitiformis

Astragalus culcitiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its robust, erect stem and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to cream-colored, arranged in clusters, and are followed by distinctive, inflated pods.

Astragalus culcitiformis is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
