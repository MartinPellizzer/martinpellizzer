Atropa bella-donna var. acuminata

Atropa bella-donna var. acuminata, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family and is native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking bell-shaped flowers that range from purple to white. The plant produces toxic berries that are highly poisonous, containing alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
