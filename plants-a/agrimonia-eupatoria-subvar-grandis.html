Agrimonia eupatoria subvar. grandis

Agrimonia eupatoria subvar. grandis, commonly known as greater agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are green and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive and respiratory ailments.
