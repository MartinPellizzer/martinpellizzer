Achnatherum coreanum var. kengii

Achnatherum coreanum var. kengii, commonly known as Korean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
