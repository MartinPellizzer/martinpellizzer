Amaryllis atamasco

Amaryllis atamasco, commonly known as the Mexican belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 3 feet in height, adorned with large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped blooms that resemble lilies, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

Amaryllis atamasco is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
