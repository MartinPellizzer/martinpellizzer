Anacampseros retusa subsp. lanuginosa

Anacampseros retusa subsp.

lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly anacampseros, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
