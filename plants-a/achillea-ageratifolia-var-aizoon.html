Achillea ageratifolia var. aizoon

Achillea ageratifolia var. aizoon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a garden plant highlights its ecological and cultural significance.
