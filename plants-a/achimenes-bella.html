Achimenes bella

Achimenes bella, commonly known as the frost flower, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces tubular flowers that are typically borne on erect, branching stems. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Achimenes bella is often cultivated as a garden ornamental due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
