Antidesma pendulum

Antidesma pendulum, commonly known as the hanging antidesma, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
