Alsine graminifolia var. semiglabra

Alsine graminifolia var. semiglabra, commonly known as the smooth alsike grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of slender, grass-like leaves that are generally smooth to the touch, as indicated by its varietal name "semiglabra.". The plant produces small, greenish to yellowish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
