Alocasia peltata

Alocasia peltata, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped, or shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, rich soil. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Alocasia peltata is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking foliage.
