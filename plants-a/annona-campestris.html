Annona campestris

Annona campestris, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical and subtropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, yellow or green skin and a creamy, sweet pulp that contains numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Annona campestris is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
