Arundinaria hidaensis var. muraii

Arundinaria hidaensis var. muraii, commonly known as the Hida bamboo, is a rare and unique variant of the Hida bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in controlled environments due to its limited natural range.
