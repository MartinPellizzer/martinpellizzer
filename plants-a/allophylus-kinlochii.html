Allophylus kinlochii

Allophylus kinlochii, commonly known as the Kinloch alpine holly, is a shrub native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Allophylus kinlochii thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant component of montane ecosystems.
