Anodendron affine

Anodendron affine, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
