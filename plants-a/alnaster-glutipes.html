Alnaster glutipes

Alnaster glutipes, commonly known as the velvetleaf alnaster, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Alnaster glutipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
