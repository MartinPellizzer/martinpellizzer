Alternanthera brasiliana f. jacquinii

Alternanthera brasiliana f. jacquinii, commonly known as the red-leaf alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink foliage, which becomes more intense under bright light conditions. The plant typically grows in shallow water and has narrow, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

This variety is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance in freshwater environments.
