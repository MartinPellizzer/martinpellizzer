Acacia hockingsii

Acacia hockingsii, commonly known as the Hockings' wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is adapted to survive in low rainfall conditions with its deep root system.

Acacia hockingsii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
