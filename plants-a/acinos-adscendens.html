Acinos adscendens

Acinos adscendens, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Rocky Mountains and western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Acinos adscendens plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and talus slopes.
