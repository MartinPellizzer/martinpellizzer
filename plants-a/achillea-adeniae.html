Achillea adeniae

Achillea adeniae, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, and it is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Achillea adeniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
