Acer pauciflorum var. changhuaense

Acer pauciflorum var. changhuaense, commonly known as the Changhua maple, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural collections.
