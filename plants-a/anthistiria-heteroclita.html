Anthistiria heteroclita

Anthistiria heteroclita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

Anthistiria heteroclita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
