Azalea × rawsonii

Azalea × rawsonii, commonly known as the Rawson's azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink to red blossoms that attract pollinators. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
