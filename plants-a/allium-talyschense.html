Allium talyschense

Allium talyschense, commonly known as the Talyschense leek, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
