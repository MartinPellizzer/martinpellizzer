Apedium chironianum

Apedium chironianum, commonly known as the rock melon, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, pale green, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mildly sweet taste. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Apedium chironianum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.
