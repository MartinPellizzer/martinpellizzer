Acacia pterigoidea

Acacia pterigoidea, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
