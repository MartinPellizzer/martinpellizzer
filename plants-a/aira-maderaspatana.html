Aira maderaspatana

Aira maderaspatana, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Aira maderaspatana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
