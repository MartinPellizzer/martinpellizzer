Aechmea multiflora

Aechmea multiflora, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea multiflora is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea multiflora has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered with a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, arranged in a compact, upright cluster.

The leaves are typically dark green with a bluish tinge, and can grow up to 40 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick, and somewhat woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, with showy, brightly colored bracts that range from pink to red, blooming in the spring and summer, and are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds.

The fruit is a bright red, fleshy capsule that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
