Astragalus poliotes

Astragalus poliotes, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry climates. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus poliotes is often found in prairies, grasslands, and along the edges of deserts.
