Aspalathus potbergensis

Aspalathus potbergensis, commonly known as Potberg bushveld pea, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique ecological role and habitat preferences, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
