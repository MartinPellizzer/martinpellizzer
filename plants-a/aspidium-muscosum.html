Aspidium muscosum

Aspidium muscosum, commonly known as the mossy water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns (Pteridaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of green algae, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium muscosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
