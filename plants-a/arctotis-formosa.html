Arctotis formosa

Arctotis formosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with a central purple or brown disk, and they bloom throughout the growing season.

Arctotis formosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
