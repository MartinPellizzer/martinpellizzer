Achillea doerfleri

Achillea doerfleri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, flat-topped clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
