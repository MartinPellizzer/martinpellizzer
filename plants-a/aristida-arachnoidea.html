Aristida arachnoidea

Aristida arachnoidea, commonly known as the spidergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale green to brown flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control in arid environments.

Aristida arachnoidea thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
