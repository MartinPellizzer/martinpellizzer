Alyssum chalcidicum f. rotundum

Alyssum chalcidicum f. rotundum, commonly known as the rounded alyssum, is a compact, flowering plant characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean. The leaves are typically rounded and hairy, while the flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its suitability for rock gardens or borders.

It is also known for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
