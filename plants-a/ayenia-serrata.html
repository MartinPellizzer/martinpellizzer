Ayenia serrata

Ayenia serrata, commonly known as the serrated ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are serrated, giving it its common name, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Ayenia serrata is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
