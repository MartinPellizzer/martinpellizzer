Acacia citrinoviridis

Acacia citrinoviridis, commonly known as the golden-green acacia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in dense, globular clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its unique coloration and structure make it a striking ornamental plant, though it is not widely cultivated outside its native habitat.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
