Abutilon macdougalii

Abutilon macdougalii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fibrous bark and hairy foliage are distinctive botanical features.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.
