Acacia barakulensis

Acacia barakulensis, commonly known as the Karakul acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its yellow, globular flower heads. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds with a hard coat. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Acacia barakulensis is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
