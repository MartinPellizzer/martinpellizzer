Acridocarpus cavanillesii

Acridocarpus cavanillesii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
