Angianthus milnei

Angianthus milnei, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
