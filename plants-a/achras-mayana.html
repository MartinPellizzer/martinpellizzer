Achras mayana

Achras mayana, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces large, green, football-shaped fruits that ripen to a yellow or orange color. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and can reach up to 50 centimeters in diameter.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in carbohydrates and nutrients.
