Aster circularis

Aster circularis, commonly known as the Roundleaf Aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its round, succulent-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that blooms in late summer to early fall.

Aster circularis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
