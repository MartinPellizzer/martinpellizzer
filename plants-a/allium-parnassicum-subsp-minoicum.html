Allium parnassicum subsp. minoicum

Allium parnassicum subsp.

minoicum, commonly known as the Minoan garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient cultures.
