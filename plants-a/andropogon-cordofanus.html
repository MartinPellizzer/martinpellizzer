Andropogon cordofanus

Andropogon cordofanus, commonly known as eastern woodland grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and forest ecosystems.
