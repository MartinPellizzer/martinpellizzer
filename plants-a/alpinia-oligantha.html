Alpinia oligantha

Alpinia oligantha, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rhizomes and striking purple-pink inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy texture.

Alpinia oligantha thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
