Apium alpinum

Apium alpinum, commonly known as alpine celery, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as parsley and carrots. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
