Antennaria straminea

Antennaria straminea, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white, blooming in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of polar and high-altitude environments.
