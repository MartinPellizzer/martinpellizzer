Agathosma rufescens

Agathosma rufescens, commonly known as red bushwillow, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are visited by various pollinators. Its woody stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Agathosma rufescens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
