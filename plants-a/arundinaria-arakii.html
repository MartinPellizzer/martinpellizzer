Arundinaria arakii

Arundinaria arakii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Arundinaria arakii is often used in traditional Japanese gardens and is valued for its graceful appearance.
