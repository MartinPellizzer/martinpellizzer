Androrchis pauciflora subsp. laeta

Androrchis pauciflora subsp.

laeta, commonly known as the cheerful small-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its flowers are characterized by their narrow, elongated petals and a distinctive, spurred lip.

This subspecies is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
