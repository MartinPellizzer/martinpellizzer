Aconitum sczukinii

Aconitum sczukinii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers that resemble a monk's habit. The plant has palmately divided, doubly serrated leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Aconitum sczukinii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is also notable for containing potent alkaloids, making it both toxic and a subject of interest in botanical and pharmacological studies.
