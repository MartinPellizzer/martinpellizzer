Alsophila margarethae

Alsophila margarethae, commonly known as the Margaret's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila margarethae is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
