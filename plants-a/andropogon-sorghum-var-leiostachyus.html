Andropogon sorghum var. leiostachyus

Andropogon sorghum var. leiostachyus, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage or ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
