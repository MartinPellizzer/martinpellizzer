Arundo epigejos var. intermedia

Arundo epigejos var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican reed or giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, riverbanks, and marshes, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems. It can grow up to 6 meters in height and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
