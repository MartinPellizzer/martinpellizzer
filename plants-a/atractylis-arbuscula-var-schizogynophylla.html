Atractylis arbuscula var. schizogynophylla

Atractylis arbuscula var. schizogynophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed windmill grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features a tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
