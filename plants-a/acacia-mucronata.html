Acacia mucronata

Acacia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-point acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by distinctive, flat, triangular pods. Acacia mucronata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
