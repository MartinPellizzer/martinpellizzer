Aconitum tangense

Aconitum tangense, commonly known as the Tibetan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant for both humans and animals.
