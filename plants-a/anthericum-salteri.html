Anthericum salteri

Anthericum salteri, commonly known as Salter's lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. The plant is often found in marshy or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a flowering period typically from late spring to early summer.

Anthericum salteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
