Acer pseudoplatanus var. tomentosum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence on the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
