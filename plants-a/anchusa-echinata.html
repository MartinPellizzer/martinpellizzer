Anchusa echinata

Anchusa echinata, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. Anchusa echinata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
