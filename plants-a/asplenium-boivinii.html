Asplenium boivinii

Asplenium boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Asplenium boivinii is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, showcasing its adaptability to various substrates.
