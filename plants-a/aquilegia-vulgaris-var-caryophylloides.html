Aquilegia vulgaris var. caryophylloides

Aquilegia vulgaris var. caryophylloides, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a hybrid species of the genus Aquilegia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces showy, clustered blooms in late spring to early summer.
