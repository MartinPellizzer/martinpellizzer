Agave sanpedroensis

Agave sanpedroensis, commonly known as the San Pedroensis agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are often followed by the development of a fruit. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Agavaceae family.
