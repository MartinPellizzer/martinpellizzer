Acicalyptus nitida

Acicalyptus nitida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Acicalyptus nitida is valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
