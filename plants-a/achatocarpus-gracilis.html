Achatocarpus gracilis

Achatocarpus gracilis, commonly known as the slender sand mat, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its structure and growth habits make it well adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Achatocarpus gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
