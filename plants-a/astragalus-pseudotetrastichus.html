Astragalus pseudotetrastichus

Astragalus pseudotetrastichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
