Aspidium polypodiforme

Aspidium polypodiforme, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that are often divided into numerous small, pointed segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

Aspidium polypodiforme is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
