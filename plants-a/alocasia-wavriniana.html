Alocasia wavriniana

Alocasia wavriniana, commonly known as the Wavrin's elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant features a stout, rhizomatous stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Alocasia wavriniana is a member of the Araceae family, which includes many other ornamental and economically important plants.
