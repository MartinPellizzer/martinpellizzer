Atriplex truncata

Atriplex truncata, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt tolerance allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity, making it an important component of salt desert ecosystems.

Atriplex truncata also serves as a food source for various wildlife species, including birds and insects.
