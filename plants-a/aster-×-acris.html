Aster × acris

Aster × acris, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Aster × acris is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
