Agathosma taxifolia

Agathosma taxifolia, commonly known as Cape lavender, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. It is often used in aromatherapy due to its essential oils, which have calming properties.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
