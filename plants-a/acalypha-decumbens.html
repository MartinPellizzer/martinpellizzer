Acalypha decumbens

Acalypha decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate spurred amaranth, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ground cover in landscaping.
