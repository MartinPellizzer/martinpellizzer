Aegilops peregrina

Aegilops peregrina, commonly known as the wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Aegilops peregrina is known for its resilience and has been studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological contexts.
