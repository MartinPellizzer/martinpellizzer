Austroeupatorium cordatoacuminatum

Austroeupatorium cordatoacuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with acuminate tips. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Austroeupatorium cordatoacuminatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
