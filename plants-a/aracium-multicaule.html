Aracium multicaule

Aracium multicaule, commonly known as the many-stemmed aracium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with multiple stems arising from the base. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Aracium multicaule produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.
