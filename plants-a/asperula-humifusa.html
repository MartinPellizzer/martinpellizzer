Asperula humifusa

Asperula humifusa, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage that remains attractive throughout the year. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Asperula humifusa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
