Alchemilla diversipes

Alchemilla diversipes, commonly known as the diverse lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla diversipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
