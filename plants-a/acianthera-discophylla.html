Acianthera discophylla

Acianthera discophylla, commonly known as the discophylla orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with a unique disc-shaped structure, which gives it its scientific name. Its roots are often epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
