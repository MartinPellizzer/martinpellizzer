Asperula aristata

Asperula aristata, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink or white petals that attract pollinators. Asperula aristata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
