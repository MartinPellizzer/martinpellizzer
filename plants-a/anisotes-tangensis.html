Anisotes tangensis

Anisotes tangensis, commonly known as the Tanganyikan water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the freshwater lakes of Tanzania.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically submerged or partially submerged in water. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the bottom of the lake, allowing it to thrive in still water environments.

Anisotes tangensis plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitat by supporting aquatic biodiversity.
