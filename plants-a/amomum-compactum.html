Amomum compactum

Amomum compactum, commonly known as cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its highly valued spice.
