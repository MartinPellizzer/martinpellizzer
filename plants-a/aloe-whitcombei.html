Aloe whitcombei

Aloe whitcombei, commonly known as the Natal aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Aloe whitcombei is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
