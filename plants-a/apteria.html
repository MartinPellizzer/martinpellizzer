Apteria

Apteria, commonly known as the leafless stem of certain plants, is a specialized structure found in some species of plants, particularly in the family Cactaceae.

It is a reduced, often green stem that performs the functions of a leaf, such as photosynthesis. The apteria lacks true leaves and is covered in a thick cuticle to reduce water loss. It is typically succulent and may bear spines or hairs for protection.

This adaptation allows the plant to survive in arid environments by minimizing transpiration and maximizing water retention.
