Amaranthus caudatus f. oblongotepalus

Amaranthus caudatus f. oblongotepalus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth plant characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences.

This cultivar is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its ornamental value and edible seeds. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, while the flowers form dense, plume-like clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and minerals.
