Aconitum membranaceum

Aconitum membranaceum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features striking purple to blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the genus Aconitum. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
