Astragalus tephrodes var. typicus

Astragalus tephrodes var. typicus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in clusters. Its seed pods are slender, elongated, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
