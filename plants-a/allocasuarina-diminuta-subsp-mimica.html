Allocasuarina diminuta subsp. mimica

Allocasuarina diminuta subsp.

mimica, commonly known as the Mimic casuarina, is a small, evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like phyllodes that are typically green and slightly curved.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
