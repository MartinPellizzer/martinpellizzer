Ascarina solmsiana var. grandifolia

Ascarina solmsiana var. grandifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, globose fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forest regions.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
