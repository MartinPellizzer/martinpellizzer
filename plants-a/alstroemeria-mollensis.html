Alstroemeria mollensis

Alstroemeria mollensis, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Alstroemeria mollensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
