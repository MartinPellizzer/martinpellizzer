Aspidium unitum var. glabrum

Aspidium unitum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to various regions in Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and usually smooth fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that resembles the feathers of a bird.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
