Ampelocissus angolensis var. congoensis

Ampelocissus angolensis var. congoensis, commonly known as Congo fig or African fig, is a climbing plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for use as a living fence or ornamental plant.
