Aristolochia holtzei

Aristolochia holtzei, commonly known as the Holtze's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, green berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
