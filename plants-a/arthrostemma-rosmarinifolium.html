Arthrostemma rosmarinifolium

Arthrostemma rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved arthrostemma, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported aromatic and therapeutic properties.
