Aster ciliolatus var. borealis

Aster ciliolatus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern aster, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and wet meadows. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the summer. Its flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in arctic and subarctic environments.
