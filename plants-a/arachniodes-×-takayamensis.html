Arachniodes × takayamensis

Arachniodes × takayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese nettle fern, is a hybrid species within the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed, and often hairy leaves that resemble the appearance of a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and stream sides, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its fronds are usually bioluminescent and have a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in shaded garden settings.
