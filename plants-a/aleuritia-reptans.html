Aleuritia reptans

Aleuritia reptans, commonly known as the creeping elder, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its fruit is a dark blue berry that ripens in late summer.

Aleuritia reptans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
