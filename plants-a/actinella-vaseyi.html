Actinella vaseyi

Actinella vaseyi, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Actinella vaseyi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
