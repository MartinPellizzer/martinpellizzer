Acacia incurva

Acacia incurva, commonly known as the zigzag acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Acacia incurva is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
