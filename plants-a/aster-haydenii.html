Aster haydenii

Aster haydenii, commonly known as Hayden's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple or blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Aster haydenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
