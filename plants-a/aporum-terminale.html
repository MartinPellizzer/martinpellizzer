Aporum terminale

Aporum terminale, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, sweet fragrance. Its unique floral structure includes five petals and a prominent, showy corona.

Aporum terminale is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
