Aralia bastardiana

Aralia bastardiana, commonly known as the bastard aralia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
