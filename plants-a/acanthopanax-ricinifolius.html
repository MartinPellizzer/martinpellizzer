Acanthopanax ricinifolius

Acanthopanax ricinifolius, commonly known as the Chinese angel's trumpet, is a woody climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately arranged and have a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some areas for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
