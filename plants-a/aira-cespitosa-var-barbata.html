Aira cespitosa var. barbata

Aira cespitosa var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy false oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
