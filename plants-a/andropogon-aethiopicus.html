Andropogon aethiopicus

Andropogon aethiopicus, commonly known as African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or silver hue. The plant produces panicles of small, white or pale green flowers, which are arranged in a loose, branching pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Andropogon aethiopicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a source of biomass.
