Alchemilla vulgaris f. vestita

Alchemilla vulgaris f. vestita, commonly known as silver-leaf lady's mantle, is a variant of the lady's mantle plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white foliage.

This perennial plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered with a fine, powdery coating that gives them their unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in upright panicles.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
