Alectorolophus major var. angustifolius

Alectorolophus major var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed alectorolophus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
