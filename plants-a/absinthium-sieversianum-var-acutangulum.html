Absinthium sieversianum var. acutangulum

Absinthium sieversianum var. acutangulum, commonly known as the acute-leaved wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
