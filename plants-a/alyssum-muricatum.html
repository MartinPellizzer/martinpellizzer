Alyssum muricatum

Alyssum muricatum, commonly known as summer alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum muricatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
