Acalypha lanceolata

Acalypha lanceolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with some varieties displaying red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Acalypha lanceolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
