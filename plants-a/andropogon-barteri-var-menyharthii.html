Andropogon barteri var. menyharthii

Andropogon barteri var. menyharthii, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that mature into fluffy seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
