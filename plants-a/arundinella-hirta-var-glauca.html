Arundinella hirta var. glauca

Arundinella hirta var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-green bamboo, is a variant of the Arundinella hirta species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are covered in a bluish-green hue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
