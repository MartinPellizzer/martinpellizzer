Anthodon apiculatum

Anthodon apiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pink or red and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Anthodon apiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native landscapes.
