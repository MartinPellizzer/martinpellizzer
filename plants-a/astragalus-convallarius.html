Astragalus convallarius

Astragalus convallarius, commonly known as the spring vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus convallarius is often noted for its early blooming habit, making it a sign of the coming spring.
