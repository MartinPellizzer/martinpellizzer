Allium polyastrum var. platyphyllum

Allium polyastrum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are broad and flat, with a mild garlic aroma. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous stem is often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle onion-like flavor to dishes.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities in gardens and cuisines.
