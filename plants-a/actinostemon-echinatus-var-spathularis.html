Actinostemon echinatus var. spathularis

Actinostemon echinatus var. spathularis, commonly known as the spathularis spurge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, spiny, yellowish-green stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
