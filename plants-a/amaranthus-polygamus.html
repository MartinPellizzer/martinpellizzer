Amaranthus polygamus

Amaranthus polygamus, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is an annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems that often turn reddish in color. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which later develop into three-winged seeds.

This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
