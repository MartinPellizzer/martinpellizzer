Acacia aromatica

Acacia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous gum that has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
