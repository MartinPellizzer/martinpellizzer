Arthraxon hispidus var. muticus

Arthraxon hispidus var. muticus, commonly known as the soft-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a soft, velvety texture, distinguishing it from other varieties of Arthraxon. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
