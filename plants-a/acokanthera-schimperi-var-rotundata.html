Acokanthera schimperi var. rotundata

Acokanthera schimperi var. rotundata, commonly known as the African arrow poison or the poison arrow tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces toxic berries that contain cardiac glycosides, making it highly poisonous. It is often found in dry woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is used traditionally in some regions for hunting purposes.
