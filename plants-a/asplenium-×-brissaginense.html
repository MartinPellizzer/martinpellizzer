Asplenium × brissaginense

Asplenium × brissaginense, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species, with a dark green coloration and a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its reproductive structures are underdeveloped, making it reliant on vegetative propagation for survival.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
