Acacia reniformis

Acacia reniformis, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often branched stems, which are covered in small, rounded tubercles. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, linear leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Acacia reniformis is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.
