Anisophyllea sabahensis

Anisophyllea sabahensis, commonly known as the Sabah anisophyllea, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Anisophylleaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, opposite, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its foliage is evergreen, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
