Achilleanthus hypolasius

Achilleanthus hypolasius, commonly known as the low-growing sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
