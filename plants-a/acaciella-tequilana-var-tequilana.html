Acaciella tequilana var. tequilana

Acaciella tequilana var. tequilana, commonly known as the Mexican honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates.

This species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for bees.
