Appendicula callosa

Appendicula callosa, commonly known as the rough appendicula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in the spring.

Appendicula callosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
