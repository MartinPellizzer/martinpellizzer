Andropogon tectorum

Andropogon tectorum, commonly known as panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
