Astragalus eriocephalus var. bimgoellensis

Astragalus eriocephalus var. bimgoellensis, commonly known as the Bimango milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
