Alsophila glaucifolia

Alsophila glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-green tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant's fronds are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter green or blue-green on the underside, contributing to its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Alsophila glaucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
