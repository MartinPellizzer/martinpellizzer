Austrocylindropuntia haematacantha

Austrocylindropuntia haematacantha, commonly known as the red-spined barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The cactus is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to full sun exposure.
