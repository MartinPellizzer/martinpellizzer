Aucuba japonica f. castaneopedicellata

Aucuba japonica f. castaneopedicellata, commonly known as the golden leaf aucuba, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically a vibrant yellow to gold, with a velvety texture and a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, red berries that are visually appealing but not typically eaten by humans. It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for shaded garden spaces.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
