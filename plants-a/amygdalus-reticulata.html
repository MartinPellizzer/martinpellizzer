Amygdalus reticulata

Amygdalus reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that mature into sweet, edible drupes. Its botanical features include a woody stem with a reticulate pattern on the bark, giving it its scientific name.

Amygdalus reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
