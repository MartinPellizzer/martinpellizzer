Aegiphila truncata

Aegiphila truncata, commonly known as the Brazilian balm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aegiphila truncata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
