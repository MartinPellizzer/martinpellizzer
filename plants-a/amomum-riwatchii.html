Amomum riwatchii

Amomum riwatchii, commonly known as the Thai cardamom or wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum riwatchii is often cultivated for its essential oils and is valued in both culinary and medicinal applications.
