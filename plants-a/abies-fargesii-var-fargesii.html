Abies fargesii var. fargesii

Abies fargesii var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a flat, spreading pattern. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often hang downward, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high elevations in forested areas.

The Farges fir is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
