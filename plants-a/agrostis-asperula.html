Agrostis asperula

Agrostis asperula, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in lawns and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in turfgrass mixes.
