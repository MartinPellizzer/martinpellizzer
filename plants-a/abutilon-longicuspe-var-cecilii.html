Abutilon longicuspe var. cecilii

Abutilon longicuspe var. cecilii, commonly known as Cecil's Chinese lantern, is a variant of the Abutilon longicuspe species, which is native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, orange to red bracts that resemble small lanterns, giving it its common name. The plant is a shrub or small tree with palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by the showy bracts.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
