Anacamptis × timbali nothosubsp.

The plant typically grows in wetland and marsh habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with elongated, spurred petals.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
