Aster ageratoides var. spirifolius

Aster ageratoides var. spirifolius, commonly known as the spiralleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, spiral-arranged leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
