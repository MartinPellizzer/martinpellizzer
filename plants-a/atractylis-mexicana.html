Atractylis mexicana

Atractylis mexicana, commonly known as Mexican gingergrass, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
