Aniseia fulvicaulis

Aniseia fulvicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed aniseia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, yellowish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters that bloom in the summer months.

Aniseia fulvicaulis is often found in open woods and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
