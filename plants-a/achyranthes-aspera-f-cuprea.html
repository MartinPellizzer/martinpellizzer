Achyranthes aspera f. cuprea

Achyranthes aspera f. cuprea, commonly known as red bryony, is a variant of the common bryony plant characterized by its distinctive reddish hue.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of southern and eastern Asia. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in spiny hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
