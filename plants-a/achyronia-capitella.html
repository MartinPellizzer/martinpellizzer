Achyronia capitella

Achyronia capitella, commonly known as the small white orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact stature and delicate white petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often resembling a head, which gives it its scientific name.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
