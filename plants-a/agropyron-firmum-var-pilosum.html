Agropyron firmum var. pilosum

Agropyron firmum var. pilosum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to central Asia.

It features slender, tufted stems with a blue-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish tinge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators in arid environments.
