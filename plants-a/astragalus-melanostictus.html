Astragalus melanostictus

Astragalus melanostictus, commonly known as the black-streaked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus melanostictus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
