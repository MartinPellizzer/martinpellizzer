Aegilops ovata var. latiaristata

Aegilops ovata var. latiaristata, commonly known as the broad-leafed wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain florets with pale, awned glumes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its potential as a forage crop and its role in ecological restoration projects.
