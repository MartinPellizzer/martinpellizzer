Armeniaca vulgaris var. persicoides

Armeniaca vulgaris var. persicoides, commonly known as the Persian apricot, is a variant of the apricot species native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and botanic gardens, valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance.
