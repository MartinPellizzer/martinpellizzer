Acer nikoense subsp. megalocarpium

Acer nikoense subsp.

megalocarpium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with large seeds, is a variant of the Japanese maple species native to Japan. It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that display vibrant colors during the autumn season.

The plant produces large, woody fruits known as samaras, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Its growth habit is typically deciduous, with a compact and upright form.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and unique fruit structures.
