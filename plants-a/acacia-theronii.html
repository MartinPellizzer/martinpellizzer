Acacia theronii

Acacia theronii, commonly known as the white thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia theronii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for its timber and as a source of fodder.
