Astragalus cruciatus subsp. radians

Astragalus cruciatus subsp.

radians, commonly known as the radiant milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
