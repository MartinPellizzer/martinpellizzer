Anthyllis biflora

Anthyllis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Anthyllis biflora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
