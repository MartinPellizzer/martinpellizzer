Aerva lanata var. oblongata

Aerva lanata var. oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf aerva, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
