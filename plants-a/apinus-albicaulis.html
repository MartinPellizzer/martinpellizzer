Apinus albicaulis

Apinus albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed apinus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, white or pale green stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often releasing a mild scent when crushed.

Apinus albicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
