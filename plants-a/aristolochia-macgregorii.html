Aristolochia macgregorii

Aristolochia macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a deep purple to brown coloration. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and a strong, woody stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
