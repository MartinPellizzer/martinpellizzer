Arthrostemma villosum

Arthrostemma villosum, commonly known as the hairy arthrostemma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
