Aristolochia didyma

Aristolochia didyma, commonly known as the Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often pale green or white with a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features, some of which are used in traditional medicine.
