Adiantum crenatum var. glaucescens

Adiantum crenatum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, with a unique waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
