Argyroxiphium sandwicense subsp. macrocephalum

Argyroxiphium sandwicense subsp.

macrocephalum, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a rare endemic plant native to the Hawaiian island of Maui. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray, succulent leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in the high-altitude, volcanic soils of Haleakalā Crater, where it is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Due to its limited distribution and historical threats from grazing and habitat loss, it is listed as endangered.

Conservation efforts focus on protecting its habitat and managing invasive species to ensure its survival.
