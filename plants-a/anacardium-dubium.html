Anacardium dubium

Anacardium dubium, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a spicy, peppery taste. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy appearance.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
