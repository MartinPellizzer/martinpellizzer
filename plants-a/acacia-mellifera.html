Acacia mellifera

Acacia mellifera, commonly known as the honey acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

Its name comes from the sweet-smelling nectar it secretes, which is a source of honey.

Acacia mellifera is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
