Astragalus tekesensis

Astragalus tekesensis, commonly known as the Tekean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance highlights its value in local ecosystems.
