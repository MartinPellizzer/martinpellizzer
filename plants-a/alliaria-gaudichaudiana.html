Alliaria gaudichaudiana

Alliaria gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the broadleaf winter savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other aromatic plants. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to heart-shaped with a rough texture. Its small, white or pale purple flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its pungent aroma and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
