Acacia petrensis

Acacia petrensis, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It is adapted to sandy soils and coastal conditions, often thriving in areas with high salinity and strong winds.

Acacia petrensis plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
