Angraecum brongniartianum

Angraecum brongniartianum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue with purple veins. The plant produces fragrant, nocturnal blooms that attract specific moth pollinators, showcasing a unique co-evolutionary relationship. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native region.
