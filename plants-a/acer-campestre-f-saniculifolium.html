Acer campestre f. saniculifolium

Acer campestre f. saniculifolium, commonly known as the Saniculifolium whitebeam, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the European whitebeam.

It is characterized by its unique, finely toothed leaves that resemble those of the sanicle plant, giving it its common name. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub with a compact, rounded growth habit. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
