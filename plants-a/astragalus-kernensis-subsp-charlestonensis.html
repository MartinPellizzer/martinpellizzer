Astragalus kernensis subsp. charlestonensis

Astragalus kernensis subsp.

charlestonensis, commonly known as Charleston milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces legume pods that contain small, dark seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
