Arabis takesimana

Arabis takesimana, commonly known as the Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal areas.

Arabis takesimana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
