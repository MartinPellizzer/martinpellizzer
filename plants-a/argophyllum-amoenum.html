Argophyllum amoenum

Argophyllum amoenum, commonly known as the silky mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Argophyllum amoenum is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
