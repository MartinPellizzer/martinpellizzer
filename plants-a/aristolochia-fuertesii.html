Aristolochia fuertesii

Aristolochia fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertes's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, often with a deep purple or maroon hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Aristolochia fuertesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
