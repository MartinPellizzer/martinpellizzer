Aristolochia fontanesii f. grandiflora

Aristolochia fontanesii f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and have a slightly velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, which includes several genera with similar morphological features.
