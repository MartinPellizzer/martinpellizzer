Aniba cicatricosa

Aniba cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarred aniba, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

The bark and leaves of Aniba cicatricosa contain aromatic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
