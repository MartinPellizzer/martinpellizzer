Arctagrostis latifolia var. gigantea

Arctagrostis latifolia var. gigantea, commonly known as giant broadleaf grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and can grow up to six feet in height, making it a striking feature in natural landscapes. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
