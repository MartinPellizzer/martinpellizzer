Anthyllis sericea subsp. valentina

Anthyllis sericea subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentina sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, silky-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
