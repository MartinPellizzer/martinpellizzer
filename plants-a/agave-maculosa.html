Agave maculosa

Agave maculosa, commonly known as the spotted agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, blue-green leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by blue-green berries. Agave maculosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
