Acronia orygmoglossa

Acronia orygmoglossa, commonly known as the yellow lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial shade.

Acronia orygmoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
