Arnoglossum ovatum var. lanceolatum

Arnoglossum ovatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf prairie cranesbill, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
