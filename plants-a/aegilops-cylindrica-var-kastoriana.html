Aegilops cylindrica var. kastoriana

Aegilops cylindrica var. kastoriana, commonly known as the Kastoriana wild rye, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
