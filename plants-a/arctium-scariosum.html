Arctium scariosum

Arctium scariosum, commonly known as the common burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, prickly seed pods. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
