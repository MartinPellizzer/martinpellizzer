Arenaria rubella var. filiorum

Arenaria rubella var. filiorum, commonly known as the filial sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, high-altitude habitats.
