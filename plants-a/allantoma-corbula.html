Allantoma corbula

Allantoma corbula, commonly known as the coral-like allantoma, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that resemble branching coral structures. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic value.
