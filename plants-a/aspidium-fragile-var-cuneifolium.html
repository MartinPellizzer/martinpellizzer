Aspidium fragile var. cuneifolium

Aspidium fragile var. cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a variant of the common water fern, characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet forests. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with its fronds arising from a central stem. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in water gardens or as a decorative element in humid indoor settings.
