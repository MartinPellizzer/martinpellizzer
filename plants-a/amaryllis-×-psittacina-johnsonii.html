Amaryllis × psittacina-johnsonii

Amaryllis × psittacina-johnsonii, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis psittacina and Amaryllis johnsonii.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa, characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces large, showy blooms that typically appear in summer, and it thrives in warm, sunny conditions with well-draining soil. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates and die back in colder regions.

This hybrid is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
