Austromyrtus lotoides

Austromyrtus lotoides, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Austromyrtus lotoides is valued for its aromatic foliage and its ability to tolerate salt spray and wind.
