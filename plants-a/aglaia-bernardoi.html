Aglaia bernardoi

Aglaia bernardoi, commonly known as the Philippine aglaia, is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

It is native to the Philippines and thrives in tropical rainforest environments. The plant produces distinctive fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest to pharmacologists.

However, due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
