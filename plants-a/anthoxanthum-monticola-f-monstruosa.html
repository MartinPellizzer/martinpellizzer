Anthoxanthum monticola f. monstruosa

Anthoxanthum monticola f. monstruosa, commonly known as the monstrous form of the mountain oat, is a rare and striking variant of this grass species.

It is characterized by its unusually large, elongated leaves and showy, sometimes violet-tinted inflorescences. This form exhibits exaggerated growth patterns, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies. It thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments, often in alpine regions.

Due to its unique morphology, it is considered a valuable specimen for understanding genetic variation in grasses.
