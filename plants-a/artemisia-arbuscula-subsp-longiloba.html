Artemisia arbuscula subsp. longiloba

Artemisia arbuscula subsp.

longiloba, commonly known as the longleaf artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in compact clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, camphor-like scent, which is a common feature among the Artemisia genus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
