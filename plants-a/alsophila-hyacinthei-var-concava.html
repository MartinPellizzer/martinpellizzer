Alsophila hyacinthei var. concava

Alsophila hyacinthei var. concava, commonly known as the concave tree fern, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is well-suited to humid, tropical environments.
