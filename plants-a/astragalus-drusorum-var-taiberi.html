Astragalus drusorum var. taiberi

Astragalus drusorum var. taiberi, commonly known as the Taiberg Pea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to alpine or subalpine environments. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
