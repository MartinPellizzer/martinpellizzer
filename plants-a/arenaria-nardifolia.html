Arenaria nardifolia

Arenaria nardifolia, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This plant is known for its prolonged flowering period and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
