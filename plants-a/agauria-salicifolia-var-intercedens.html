Agauria salicifolia var. intercedens

Agauria salicifolia var. intercedens, commonly known as the Mexican willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
