Anthericum spiratum

Anthericum spiratum, commonly known as the spiral arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features a distinctive spiral arrangement of its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces a spadix, a dense cluster of tiny flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum spiratum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
