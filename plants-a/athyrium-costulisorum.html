Athyrium costulisorum

Athyrium costulisorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium costulisorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
