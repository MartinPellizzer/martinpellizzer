Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. cossoniana

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's columbine, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features distinctive spurred petals that range in color from blue to violet, enhancing its ornamental value.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
