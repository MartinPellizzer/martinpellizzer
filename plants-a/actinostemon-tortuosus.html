Actinostemon tortuosus

Actinostemon tortuosus, commonly known as the spiral stem plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisting stems that form a spiral pattern, which is a key botanical feature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral along the stem.

Actinostemon tortuosus is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
