Acer × polymorphum var. major

Acer × polymorphum var. major, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental species. The tree has opposite, palmate leaves that typically have three to five lobes, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young, developing into a more rugged texture with age.
