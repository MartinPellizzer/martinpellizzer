Anticharis

Anticharis, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Anticharis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in treating respiratory ailments.
