Aster oblongus

Aster oblongus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

Aster oblongus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
