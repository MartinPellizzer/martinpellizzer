Allomorphia baviensis

Allomorphia baviensis, commonly known as the Baviensis Allomorph, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its roots are known to contain bioactive compounds used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is now classified as an endangered species.
