Aneilema aparine

Aneilema aparine, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant is notable for its stinging hairs, which release irritants when touched, causing a tingling or burning sensation. Its flowers are small and clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

Aneilema aparine is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
