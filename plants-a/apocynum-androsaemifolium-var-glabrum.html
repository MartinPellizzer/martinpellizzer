Apocynum androsaemifolium var. glabrum

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its latex sap is milky and toxic, containing cardiac glycosides that are harmful to humans and animals.

This species is often found in moist woodlands and is an important host plant for certain butterfly species.
