Arabis retziana

Arabis retziana, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually basal, lobed, and hairy.

Arabis retziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
