Anisomeles ovata var. mollissima

Anisomeles ovata var. mollissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf anise.

It is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties, and the plant is also valued as a source of essential oils.

Anisomeles ovata var. mollissima is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
