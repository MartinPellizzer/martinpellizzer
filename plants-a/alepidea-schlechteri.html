Alepidea schlechteri

Alepidea schlechteri, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptation to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
