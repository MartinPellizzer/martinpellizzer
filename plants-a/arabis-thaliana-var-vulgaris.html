Arabis thaliana var. vulgaris

Arabis thaliana var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in alpine and arctic regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
