Archibaccharis hirtella var. albescens

Archibaccharis hirtella var. albescens, commonly known as the white-leafed baccharis, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silver-white leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
