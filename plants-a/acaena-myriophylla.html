Acaena myriophylla

Acaena myriophylla, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Acaena myriophylla thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often forming thickets that provide habitat for native wildlife.
