Arnica alpina subsp. angustifolia

Arnica alpina subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain tobacco, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features upright stems with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in herbal remedies, though it should be handled with care.
