Arrhenatherum rivulare var. angustifolium

Arrhenatherum rivulare var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved water grass, is a perennial grass species native to moist habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in areas with consistent moisture.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
