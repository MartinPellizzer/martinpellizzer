Arabis multicaulis

Arabis multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, glaucous leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and have four petals.

Arabis multicaulis thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.
