Agave subsimplex

Agave subsimplex, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, pointed leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.
