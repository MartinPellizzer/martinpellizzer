Acalypha fuscescens

Acalypha fuscescens, commonly known as the blackleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
