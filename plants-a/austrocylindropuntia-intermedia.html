Austrocylindropuntia intermedia

Austrocylindropuntia intermedia, commonly known as the Pachycereus pringlei, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a large, columnar cactus with a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Its spines are relatively short and clustered, providing protection while allowing for some light penetration.

Austrocylindropuntia intermedia is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.
