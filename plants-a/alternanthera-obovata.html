Alternanthera obovata

Alternanthera obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf Alternanthera, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
