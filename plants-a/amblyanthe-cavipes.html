Amblyanthe cavipes

Amblyanthe cavipes, commonly known as the hollow foot plant, is a unique member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hollow stems that resemble the shape of a foot, giving it its common name. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle aesthetic to its botanical profile.
