Azalea anthopogonoides

Azalea anthopogonoides, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the specific variety. Azalea anthopogonoides prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental shrubs.

Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in the landscape.
