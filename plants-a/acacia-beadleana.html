Acacia beadleana

Acacia beadleana, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a soft, silvery appearance. The tree produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in globular inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and grey, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Acacia beadleana is well adapted to dry climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
