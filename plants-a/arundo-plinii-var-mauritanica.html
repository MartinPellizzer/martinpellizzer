Arundo plinii var. mauritanica

Arundo plinii var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, stiff stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant is well adapted to drought conditions, exhibiting deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils. It is often used for erosion control and as a source of thatching or fencing in its native habitat.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring or early summer.
