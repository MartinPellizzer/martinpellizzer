Allium sphaerocephalon var. arvense

Allium sphaerocephalon var. arvense, commonly known as the field leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish-purple in color. The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stem topped with a spherical cluster of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
