Ascogrammitis clathrata

Ascogrammitis clathrata, commonly known as the clustered chain fern, is a small, terrestrial fern species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and slender, wiry fronds that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, spore-bearing sporangia that are clustered in groups along the fronds, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
