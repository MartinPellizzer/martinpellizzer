Anisoptera thurifera subsp. polyandra

Anisoptera thurifera subsp.

polyandra, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, three-angled fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional practices in some regions.
