Acmispon argophyllus var. argenteus

Acmispon argophyllus var. argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
