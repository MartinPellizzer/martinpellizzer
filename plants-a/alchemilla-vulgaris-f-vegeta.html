Alchemilla vulgaris f. vegeta

Alchemilla vulgaris f. vegeta, commonly known as the Wooly Lady's Mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming habit.

It features broad, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
