Anthyllis henoniana

Anthyllis henoniana, commonly known as the Henon's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, which are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anthyllis henoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
