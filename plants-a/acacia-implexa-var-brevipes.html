Acacia implexa var. brevipes

Acacia implexa var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and small, feathery leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in compact, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
