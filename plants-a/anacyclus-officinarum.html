Anacyclus officinarum

Anacyclus officinarum, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
