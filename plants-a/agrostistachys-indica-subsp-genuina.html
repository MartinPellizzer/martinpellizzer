Agrostistachys indica subsp. genuina

Agrostistachys indica subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including India and China. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. The inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
