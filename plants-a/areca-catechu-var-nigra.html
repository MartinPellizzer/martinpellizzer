Areca catechu var. nigra

Areca catechu var. nigra, commonly known as black areca palm, is a variety of the areca palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, leaf sheaths that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-winged fruits. The leaves are long and feather-like, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
