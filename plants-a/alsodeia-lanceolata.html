Alsodeia lanceolata

Alsodeia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Alsodeia lanceolata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
