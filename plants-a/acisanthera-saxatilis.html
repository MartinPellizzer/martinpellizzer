Acisanthera saxatilis

Acisanthera saxatilis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fibrous root system helps it anchor in crevices and retain moisture.

Acisanthera saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
