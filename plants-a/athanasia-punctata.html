Athanasia punctata

Athanasia punctata, commonly known as the spotted daisies, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and chrysanthemums. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, with a distinctive central disc. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often with a slight pubescence.

Athanasia punctata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
