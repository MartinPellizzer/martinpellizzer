Acaciella elongata

Acaciella elongata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to survive in dry conditions with minimal water. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acaciella elongata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
