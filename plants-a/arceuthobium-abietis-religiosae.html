Arceuthobium abietis-religiosae

Arceuthobium abietis-religiosae, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the stems and branches of coniferous trees.

It lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients and water through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, greenish-white berries that are dispersed by birds. It is found primarily in the northern hemisphere, often in forested regions with suitable host trees.

Its life cycle involves a complex interaction with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
