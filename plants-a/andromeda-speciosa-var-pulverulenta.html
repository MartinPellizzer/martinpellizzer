Andromeda speciosa var. pulverulenta

Andromeda speciosa var. pulverulenta, commonly known as the silverleaf azalea, is a variant of the azalea plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and leathery, providing a dense canopy that offers habitat for small wildlife.

This variety thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
