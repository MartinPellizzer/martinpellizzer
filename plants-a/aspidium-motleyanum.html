Aspidium motleyanum

Aspidium motleyanum, commonly known as the Motley's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically borne on long, wiry stems. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Aspidium motleyanum is often found growing on rocks or in wet soil, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
