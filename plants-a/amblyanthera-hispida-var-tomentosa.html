Amblyanthera hispida var. tomentosa

Amblyanthera hispida var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly amblyanthera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
