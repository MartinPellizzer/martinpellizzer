Acalypha tenera

Acalypha tenera, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Acalypha tenera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
