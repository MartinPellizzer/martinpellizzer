Ampelopsis acutidentata

Ampelopsis acutidentata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spine-like structures along the stems, which can deter herbivores. Its berries are typically red or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Ampelopsis acutidentata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
