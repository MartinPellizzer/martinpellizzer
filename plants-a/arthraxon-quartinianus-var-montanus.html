Arthraxon quartinianus var. montanus

Arthraxon quartinianus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, dry conditions.
