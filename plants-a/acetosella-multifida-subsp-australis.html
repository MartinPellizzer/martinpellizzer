Acetosella multifida subsp. australis

Acetosella multifida subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia. It features small, trifoliate leaves that are typically reddish in color, with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
