Alsophila contaminans var. longepaleata

Alsophila contaminans var. longepaleata, commonly known as the long-leaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and helps anchor the plant in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their elegant appearance.
