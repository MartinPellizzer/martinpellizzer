Alchemilla holotricha

Alchemilla holotricha, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
