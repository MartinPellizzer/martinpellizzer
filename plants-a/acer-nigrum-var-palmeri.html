Acer nigrum var. palmeri

Acer nigrum var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
