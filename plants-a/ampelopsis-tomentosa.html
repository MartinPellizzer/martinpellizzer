Ampelopsis tomentosa

Ampelopsis tomentosa, commonly known as the fuzzy grapes vine, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate with a fuzzy, grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ampelopsis tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
