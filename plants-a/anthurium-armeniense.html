Anthurium armeniense

Anthurium armeniense, commonly known as the Armenian slipper, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the colorful, spadix-like structures that are often red or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium armeniense is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structures.
