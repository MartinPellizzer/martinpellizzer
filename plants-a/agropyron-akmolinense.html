Agropyron akmolinense

Agropyron akmolinense, commonly known as the Akmolinsk wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Agropyron akmolinense is valued for its hardiness and is used in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
