Artemisia gallica subsp. maroccana

Artemisia gallica subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a strong, camphor-like scent and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability makes it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
