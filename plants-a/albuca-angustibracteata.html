Albuca angustibracteata

Albuca angustibracteata, commonly known as the narrow bract albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, showy inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Albuca angustibracteata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
