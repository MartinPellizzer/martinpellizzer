Astragalus skrivanekii

Astragalus skrivanekii, commonly known as Skrivanek's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus skrivanekii is also noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
