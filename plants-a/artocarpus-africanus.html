Artocarpus africanus

Artocarpus africanus, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and is edible. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and traditional agriculture in its native region.
