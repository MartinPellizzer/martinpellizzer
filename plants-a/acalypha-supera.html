Acalypha supera

Acalypha supera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are small, clustered, and often green or red in color. Acalypha supera is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
