Atriplex dimorphostegia

Atriplex dimorphostegia, commonly known as the two-stemmed saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant typically grows in saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes or inland salt lakes. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Atriplex dimorphostegia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
