Aspidosperma cylindrocarpon var. macrophyllum

Aspidosperma cylindrocarpon var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Copaifera* species, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing dark, oily seeds.

It is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in folk medicine due to its potential pharmacological properties.
