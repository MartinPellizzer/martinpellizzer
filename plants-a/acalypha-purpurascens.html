Acalypha purpurascens

Acalypha purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha purpurascens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and attractive foliage.
