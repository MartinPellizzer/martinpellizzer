Aster muricatus

Aster muricatus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive texture.

Aster muricatus is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas.
