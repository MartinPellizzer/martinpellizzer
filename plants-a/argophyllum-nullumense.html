Argophyllum nullumense

Argophyllum nullumense, commonly known as the Nullumense rockheath, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white to pale blue flowers during the cooler months. The plant has adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperature fluctuations. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
