Arequipa aurantiaca

Arequipa aurantiaca, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cold, arid conditions of the Andean mountains. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional Andean cuisine.

Arequipa aurantiaca plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
