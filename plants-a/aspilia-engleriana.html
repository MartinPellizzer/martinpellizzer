Aspilia engleriana

Aspilia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's torchflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy yellow flower heads that resemble torches. The plant produces a distinctive, spherical, yellow to orange-colored head composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Aspilia engleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
