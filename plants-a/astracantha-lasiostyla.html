Astracantha lasiostyla

Astracantha lasiostyla, commonly known as the woolly antler cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, enabling survival in harsh, dry environments.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptations make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
