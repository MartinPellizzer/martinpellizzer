Asparagus altilis

Asparagus altilis, commonly known as the giant asparagus or elephant asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiny, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are compound, with small, needle-like leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Asparagus altilis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
