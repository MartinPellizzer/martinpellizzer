Amaranthus × jansen-wachterianus

Amaranthus × jansen-wachterianus, commonly known as the hybrid amaranth, is a species resulting from the cross between Amaranthus hypochondriacus and Amaranthus cruentus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a range of colors including red, orange, and purple. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are edible and can be used in cooking, similar to other amaranth species.

The plant is also valued for its potential nutritional benefits and adaptability to various growing conditions.
