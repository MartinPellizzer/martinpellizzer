Antennaria columnaris

Antennaria columnaris, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, hairy foliage. The plant typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and appear in clusters, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Antennaria columnaris thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
