Acetosella vulgaris f. multifida

Acetosella vulgaris f. multifida, commonly known as the forked sorrel, is a variant of the common sorrel.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive forked appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
