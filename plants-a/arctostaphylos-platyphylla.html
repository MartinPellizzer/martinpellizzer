Arctostaphylos platyphylla

Arctostaphylos platyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pink hues. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Arctostaphylos platyphylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
