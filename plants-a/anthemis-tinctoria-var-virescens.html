Anthemis tinctoria var. virescens

Anthemis tinctoria var. virescens, commonly known as green everlasting, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, bushy growth with finely divided, dark green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its name reflects both its long-lasting flowers and the vibrant green color of its foliage.
