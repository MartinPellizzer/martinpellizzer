Adeloda serrata

Adeloda serrata, commonly known as the serrated adeloba, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

Adeloda serrata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
