Acer mapienense

Acer mapienense, commonly known as the Mapien maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The plant belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. It typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soil.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, giving way to winged seeds that aid in its dispersal.
