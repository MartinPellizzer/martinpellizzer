Anathallis herpetophyton

Anathallis herpetophyton, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, snake-like appearance, with elongated, greenish-yellow flowers that resemble a reptile. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments, relying on its host for support rather than soil. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its distinctive visual appeal.
