Aronia depressa

Aronia depressa, commonly known as the low aronia or red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Aronia depressa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
