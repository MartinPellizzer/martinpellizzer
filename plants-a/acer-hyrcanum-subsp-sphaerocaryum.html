Acer hyrcanum subsp. sphaerocaryum

Acer hyrcanum subsp.

sphaerocaryum, commonly known as the Persian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
