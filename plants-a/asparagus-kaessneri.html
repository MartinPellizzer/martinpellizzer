Asparagus kaessneri

Asparagus kaessneri, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus or the silver asparagus, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to many landscapes.
