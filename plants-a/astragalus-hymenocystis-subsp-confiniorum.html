Astragalus hymenocystis subsp. confiniorum

Astragalus hymenocystis subsp.

confiniorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces cylindrical, pale brown pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at higher elevations.
