Acer campestre f. schwerinii

Acer campestre f. schwerinii, commonly known as the Weeping Maple, is a decorative variety of the common maple.

It is characterized by its pendulous, weeping branches that create a unique, cascading appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a vibrant green color during the growing season. It produces small, red-orange samaras that mature in the fall.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
