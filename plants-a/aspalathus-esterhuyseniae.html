Aspalathus esterhuyseniae

Aspalathus esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia tea plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to grow in sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is particularly noted for its use in the production of Rooibos tea, although it is less commonly cultivated than the more well-known Aspalathus linearis.

This species plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its ecological and commercial significance.
