Arctium ramosissimum

Arctium ramosissimum, commonly known as the common burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces spiny, bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and thistles.
