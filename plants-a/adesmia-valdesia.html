Adesmia valdesia

Adesmia valdesia, commonly known as the yellow burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, hairy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Adesmia valdesia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
