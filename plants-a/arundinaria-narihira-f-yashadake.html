Arundinaria narihira f. yashadake

Arundinaria narihira f. yashadake, commonly known as the Yashadake bamboo, is a rare and ornamental bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and provide a lush appearance throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
