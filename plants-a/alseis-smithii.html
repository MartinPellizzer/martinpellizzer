Alseis smithii

Alseis smithii, commonly known as the Smith's alseis, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Alseis smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.
