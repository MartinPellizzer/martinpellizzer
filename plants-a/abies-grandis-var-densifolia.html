Abies grandis var. densifolia

Abies grandis var. densifolia, commonly known as the dense-foliage fir, is a variant of the grand fir native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape, which makes it a striking ornamental plant. The needles are short and stiff, often arranged in crossed pairs, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cooler, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
