Aleuritia miyabeana

Aleuritia miyabeana, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Aleuritia miyabeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
