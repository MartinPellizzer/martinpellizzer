Actinokentia divaricata

Actinokentia divaricata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are often arranged in a feather-like pattern. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it can grow as a tree or shrub depending on environmental conditions.

Actinokentia divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral displays.
