Anthodendron

Anthodendron, commonly known as the starflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plants are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in the western United States and parts of Mexico. Anthodendron species are often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
