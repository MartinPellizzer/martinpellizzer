Amaryllis heuseriana f. campanulata

Amaryllis heuseriana f. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped amaryllis, is a striking hybrid with unique botanical features.

It is a cultivar of the amaryllis genus, characterized by its elongated, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are often a vibrant green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
