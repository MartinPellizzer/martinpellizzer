Astragalus prusianus var. jemelensis

Astragalus prusianus var. jemelensis, commonly known as the Jemelenskaya milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
