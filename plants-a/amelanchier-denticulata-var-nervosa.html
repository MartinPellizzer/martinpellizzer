Amelanchier denticulata var. nervosa

Amelanchier denticulata var. nervosa, commonly known as the smooth serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It features oval to elliptic leaves with finely toothed margins, and its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and wildlife. It is valued for its ornamental appeal, with showy spring blooms and vibrant fall foliage.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.
