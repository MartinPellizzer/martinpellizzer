Adicea trinervia

Adicea trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a spike.

Adicea trinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
