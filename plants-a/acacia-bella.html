Acacia bella

Acacia bella, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Acacia bella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
