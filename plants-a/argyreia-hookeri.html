Argyreia hookeri

Argyreia hookeri, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along supports.
