Astragalus rhizanthus var. pindreensis

Astragalus rhizanthus var. pindreensis, commonly known as the Pinder's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deep underground.
