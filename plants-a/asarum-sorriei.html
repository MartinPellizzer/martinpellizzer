Asarum sorriei

Asarum sorriei, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces a distinctive, irregularly shaped flower that is often hidden beneath its foliage. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
