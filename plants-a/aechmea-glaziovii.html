Aechmea glaziovii

Aechmea glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from rainfall. The plant's foliage is often adorned with vibrant colors, ranging from deep green to red or purple, depending on environmental conditions. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, long-lasting flowers that can be pink, red, or orange.

Aechmea glaziovii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Morphology

Aechmea glaziovii has broad, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with a vibrant green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, upright stem that is covered in a fibrous, dark brown bark, and it grows primarily from the base. Its inflorescence consists of clustered, showy red flowers with yellow and green hues, which bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the soil.
