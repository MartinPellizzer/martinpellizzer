Acidanthera forsythiana

Acidanthera forsythiana, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically dark green and narrow. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
