Acalypha langiana var. vigens

Acalypha langiana var. vigens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-leaved acalypha, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple, providing a dramatic visual impact in gardens. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its unique leaf coloration.
