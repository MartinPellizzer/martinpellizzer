Acacia rigens

Acacia rigens, commonly known as the rigid acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in spikes. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia rigens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
