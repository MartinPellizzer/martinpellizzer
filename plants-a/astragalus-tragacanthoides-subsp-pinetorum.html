Astragalus tragacanthoides subsp. pinetorum

Astragalus tragacanthoides subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine tree milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in woodland and chaparral habitats. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Astragalus.
