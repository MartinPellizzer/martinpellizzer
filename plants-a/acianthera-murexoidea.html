Acianthera murexoidea

Acianthera murexoidea, commonly known as the woolly acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
