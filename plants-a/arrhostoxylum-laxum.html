Arrhostoxylum laxum

Arrhostoxylum laxum, commonly known as the Chilean barberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
