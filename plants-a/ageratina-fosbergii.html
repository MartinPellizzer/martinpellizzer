Ageratina fosbergii

Ageratina fosbergii, commonly known as Fosberg's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and fluffy white to pale blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas within forests, often on windward slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ageratina fosbergii is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting its ecological significance to Hawaiian ecosystems.
