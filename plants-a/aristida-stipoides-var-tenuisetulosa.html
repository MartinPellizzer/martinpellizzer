Aristida stipoides var. tenuisetulosa

Aristida stipoides var. tenuisetulosa, commonly known as the slender false grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which is characteristic of the grass family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
