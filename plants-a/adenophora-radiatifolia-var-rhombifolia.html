Adenophora radiatifolia var. rhombifolia

Adenophora radiatifolia var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. The leaves are rhombic or diamond-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
