Angelonia ciliolata

Angelonia ciliolata, commonly known as the tropical angelonia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink, red, or white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Angelonia ciliolata is often cultivated in gardens for its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
