Acer negundo var. normalis

Acer negundo var. normalis, commonly known as the box elder, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into three to nine leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in drooping clusters during the spring. Its seeds are encased in samaras, or winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind.

The box elder is often found in wetland areas and is known for its fast growth and adaptability to various soil types.
