Ambrosia paniculata var. cumanensis

Ambrosia paniculata var. cumanensis, commonly known as the Cumanensis Ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are branched clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
