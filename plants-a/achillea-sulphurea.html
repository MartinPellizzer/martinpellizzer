Achillea sulphurea

Achillea sulphurea, commonly known as golden yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are finely divided and gray-green in color, while its stems are upright and covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
