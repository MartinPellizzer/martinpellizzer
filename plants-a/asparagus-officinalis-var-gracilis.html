Asparagus officinalis var. gracilis

Asparagus officinalis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a cultivar characterized by its delicate, thin stems and fern-like foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and is often cultivated for its edible shoots, which are harvested in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and cool temperatures for optimal growth. Its botanical features include small, scale-like leaves and yellow flowers that appear in early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and culinary use in gourmet cooking.
