Antirrhinum cornutum var. leptaleum

Antirrhinum cornutum var. leptaleum, commonly known as the slender horned snapdragon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically tubular with two lips, the upper lip being shorter than the lower. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
