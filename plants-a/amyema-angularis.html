Amyema angularis

Amyema angularis, commonly known as the angular leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Amyema angularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
