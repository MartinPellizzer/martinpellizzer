Aiouea inconspicua

Aiouea inconspicua, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus or the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters. The plant features glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms primarily during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
