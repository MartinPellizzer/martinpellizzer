Alyssum minus f. micropetalum

Alyssum minus f. micropetalum, commonly known as the miniature alyssum, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is prized for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a delicate, flowering ground cover.
