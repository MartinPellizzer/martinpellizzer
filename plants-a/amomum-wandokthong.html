Amomum wandokthong

Amomum wandokthong, commonly known as Thai cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves and fruits are valued for their warming and digestive benefits.
