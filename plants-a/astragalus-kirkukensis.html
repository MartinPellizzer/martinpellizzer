Astragalus kirkukensis

Astragalus kirkukensis, commonly known as Kirkuk milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Iraq and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically compound with numerous small leaflets. Astragalus kirkukensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
