Acanthogonum polygonoides var. longispinum

Acanthogonum polygonoides var. longispinum, commonly known as the long-spined acanthogonum, is a perennial shrub native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has woody stems covered in stiff, recurved spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
