Arachis nambyquarae

Arachis nambyquarae, commonly known as the Brazilian groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in sandy soils.

Due to its genetic uniqueness, it holds potential for agricultural research and crop improvement.
