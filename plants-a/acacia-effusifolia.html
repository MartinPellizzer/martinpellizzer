Acacia effusifolia

Acacia effusifolia, commonly known as the soft-haired acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often sparsely branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind.

Acacia effusifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
