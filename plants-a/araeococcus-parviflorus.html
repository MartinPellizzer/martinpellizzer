Araeococcus parviflorus

Araeococcus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered areeocone, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clustered, small white flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
