Areca chaiana

Areca chaiana, commonly known as the red areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by edible fruits. Areca chaiana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and air-purifying properties.
