Asarum arrhizoma

Asarum arrhizoma, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are the primary part used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
