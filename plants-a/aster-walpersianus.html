Aster walpersianus

Aster walpersianus, commonly known as the Persian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a garden plant in suitable climates.
