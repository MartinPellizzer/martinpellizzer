Aster atticus

Aster atticus, commonly known as the Attic aster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Aster atticus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
