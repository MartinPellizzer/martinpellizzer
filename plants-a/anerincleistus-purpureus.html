Anerincleistus purpureus

Anerincleistus purpureus, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers with distinctive purple veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its colorful blooms.

Anerincleistus purpureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
