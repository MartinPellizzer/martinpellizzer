Alchemilla atropurpurea

Alchemilla atropurpurea, commonly known as purple alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that turn reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
