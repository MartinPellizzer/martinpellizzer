Azalea parvifolia

Azalea parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features dense foliage of glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences that bloom in spring.

Azalea parvifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
