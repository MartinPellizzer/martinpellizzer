Acer platanoides var. incumbens

Acer platanoides var. incumbens, commonly known as the European horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or two seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing canopy.
