Allium rotundum var. multiflorum

Allium rotundum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the Persian shallot or purple scallion, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a cluster of small, round bulbs covered in a purple or pinkish sheath, with slender, hollow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its mild, onion-like flavor and ornamental value.
