Atractylis chinensis f. erosodentata

Atractylis chinensis f. erosodentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese Atractylis, is a variant of the Atractylis chinensis species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
