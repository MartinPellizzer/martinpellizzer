Angelica dabashanensis

Angelica dabashanensis, commonly known as Dabashan angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Angelica dabashanensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
