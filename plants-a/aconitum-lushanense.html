Aconitum lushanense

Aconitum lushanense, commonly known as Lushan aconite, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.
