Acer palmatum f. vanhouttei

Acer palmatum f. vanhouttei, commonly known as the Japanese maple 'Vanhouttei', is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features finely dissected, emerald green leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the winter months. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal color changes.
