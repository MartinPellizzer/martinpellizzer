Asplenium triphyllum var. tenuifrons

Asplenium triphyllum var. tenuifrons, commonly known as the slender-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the three-leaf maidenhair fern.

It is a small, delicate fern native to eastern North America, particularly found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are usually dark green and glossy. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to rocky substrates.

The species is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
