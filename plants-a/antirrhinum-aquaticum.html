Antirrhinum aquaticum

Antirrhinum aquaticum, commonly known as the water snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often resembling the shape of a snapdragon. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.

Antirrhinum aquaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
