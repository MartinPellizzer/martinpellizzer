Aristida textilis

Aristida textilis, commonly known as silky grass or silky wiregrass, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs on its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and erosion control.
