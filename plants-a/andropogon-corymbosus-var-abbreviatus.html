Andropogon corymbosus var. abbreviatus

Andropogon corymbosus var. abbreviatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, blue-green flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
