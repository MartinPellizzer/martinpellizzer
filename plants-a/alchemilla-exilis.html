Alchemilla exilis

Alchemilla exilis, commonly known as narrowleaf alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla exilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
