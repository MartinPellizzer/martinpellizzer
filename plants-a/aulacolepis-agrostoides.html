Aulacolepis agrostoides

Aulacolepis agrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is characterized by its succulent stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
