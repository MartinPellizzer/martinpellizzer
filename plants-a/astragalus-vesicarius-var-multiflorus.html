Astragalus vesicarius var. multiflorus

Astragalus vesicarius var. multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with soil bacteria.
