Alsine taurica

Alsine taurica, commonly known as Tauric sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of southern Russia and the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to the arid conditions of its habitat.

Alsine taurica is often found in open grasslands and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
