Acaena × ovina var. subglabricalyx

Acaena × ovina var. subglabricalyx, commonly known as the coastal acaena, is a low-growing shrub native to coastal regions of New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains small, hard seeds.
