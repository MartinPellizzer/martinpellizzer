Althaea mutica

Althaea mutica, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. Althaea mutica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
