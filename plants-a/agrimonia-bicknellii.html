Agrimonia bicknellii

Agrimonia bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Agrimonia bicknellii is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
