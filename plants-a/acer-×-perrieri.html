Acer × perrieri

Acer × perrieri, commonly known as the Katsura maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental planting.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice in gardens and parks.
