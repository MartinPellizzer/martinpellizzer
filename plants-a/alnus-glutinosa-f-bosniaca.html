Alnus glutinosa f. bosniaca

Alnus glutinosa f. bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian alder, is a subspecies of the black alder native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for various crafts and construction.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
