Asparagopsis lamarckii

Asparagopsis lamarckii, commonly known as the red lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Asparagopsis lamarckii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
