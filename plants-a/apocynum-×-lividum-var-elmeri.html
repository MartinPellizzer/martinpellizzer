Apocynum × lividum var. elmeri

Apocynum × lividum var. elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's dogbane, is a hybrid plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open woods and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are usually pink to purple in color.

Elmer's dogbane is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
