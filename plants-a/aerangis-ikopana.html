Aerangis ikopana

Aerangis ikopana, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip structure. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
