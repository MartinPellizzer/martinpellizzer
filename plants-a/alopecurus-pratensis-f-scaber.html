Alopecurus pratensis f. scaber

Alopecurus pratensis f. scaber, commonly known as rough meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its rough, hairy texture on the stems and leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and pastures. It has slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often arranged in clusters.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
