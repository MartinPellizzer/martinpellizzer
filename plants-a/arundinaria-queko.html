Arundinaria queko

Arundinaria queko, commonly known as the New Zealand bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Arundinaria queko plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
