Androsace chamaejasme var. ciliata

Androsace chamaejasme var. ciliata, commonly known as the alpine buttonweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
