Anacheilium vita

Anacheilium vita, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves with silver veins and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
