Anthurium marcusianum

Anthurium marcusianum, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces vibrant red spadices, which are the spadix flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium marcusianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
