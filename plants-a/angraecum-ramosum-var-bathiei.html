Angraecum ramosum var. bathiei

Angraecum ramosum var. bathiei, commonly known as the Bathie's star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by nectar-feeding moths, a unique ecological relationship.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
