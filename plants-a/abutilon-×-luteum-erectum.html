Abutilon × luteum-erectum

Abutilon × luteum-erectum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a hybrid shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
