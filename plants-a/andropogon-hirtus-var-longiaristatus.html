Andropogon hirtus var. longiaristatus

Andropogon hirtus var. longiaristatus, commonly known as the long-haired bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets that are often hairy. The plant typically grows in full sun and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
