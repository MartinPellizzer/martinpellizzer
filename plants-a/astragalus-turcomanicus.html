Astragalus turcomanicus

Astragalus turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in steppes and deserts. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in racemes. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus turcomanicus is part of the legume family and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
