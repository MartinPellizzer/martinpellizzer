Astragalus angustidens var. sessilis

Astragalus angustidens var. sessilis, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid and rocky soils.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.
