Asplenium godmanii

Asplenium godmanii, commonly known as the Godman's spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with tufts of dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Asplenium godmanii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded conditions.
