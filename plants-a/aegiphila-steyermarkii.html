Aegiphila steyermarkii

Aegiphila steyermarkii, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. It produces small, black, egg-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Aegiphila steyermarkii is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
