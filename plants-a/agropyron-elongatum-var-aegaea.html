Agropyron elongatum var. aegaea

Agropyron elongatum var. aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
