Aster ircutianus

Aster ircutianus, commonly known as the Siberian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Aster ircutianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
