Astragalus parnassi f. alba

Astragalus parnassi f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

Astragalus parnassi f. alba is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
