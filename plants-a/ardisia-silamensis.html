Ardisia silamensis

Ardisia silamensis, commonly known as the Silamensis Christmasberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds, contributing to its ecological importance. Ardisia silamensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
