Abutilon peruvianum

Abutilon peruvianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Peruvian abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, which contributes to its common name.

The plant is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
