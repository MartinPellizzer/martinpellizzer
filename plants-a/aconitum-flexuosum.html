Aconitum flexuosum

Aconitum flexuosum, commonly known as the flexuous monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with a waxy surface, and its roots are toxic due to the presence of alkaloids. Aconitum flexuosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
