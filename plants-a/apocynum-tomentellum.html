Apocynum tomentellum

Apocynum tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a stout, woody stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a similar white pubescence.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
