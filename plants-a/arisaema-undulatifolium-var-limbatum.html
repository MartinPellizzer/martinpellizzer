Arisaema undulatifolium var. limbatum

Arisaema undulatifolium var. limbatum, commonly known as the wavy-leaf arisaema, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
