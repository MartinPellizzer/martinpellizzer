Asplenium polypodium

Asplenium polypodium, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, evergreen fern that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, toothed margin. It is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in crevices, where it forms dense mats.

Asplenium polypodium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
