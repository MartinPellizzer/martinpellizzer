Amorphophallus mossambicensis

Amorphophallus mossambicensis, commonly known as the African giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its spadix and spathe structure. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with a distinctive, often foul-smelling odor to attract pollinators. Its tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a food source.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
