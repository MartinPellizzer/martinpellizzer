Anemonastrum narcissiflorum subsp. fasciculatum

Anemonastrum narcissiflorum subsp.

fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense mats or low shrubs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
