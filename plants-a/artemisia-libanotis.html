Artemisia libanotis

Artemisia libanotis, commonly known as Lebanese wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or dry environments. Artemisia libanotis has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
