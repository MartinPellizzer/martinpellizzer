Acer kwangsiense

Acer kwangsiense, commonly known as the Guangxi maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red samaras in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree.
