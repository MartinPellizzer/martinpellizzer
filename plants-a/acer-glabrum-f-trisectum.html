Acer glabrum f. trisectum

Acer glabrum f. trisectum, commonly known as the trisectum maple, is a variant of the paper maple characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The leaves are typically divided into three lobes, giving the plant a unique and ornamental appearance. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange-red samaras in the autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
