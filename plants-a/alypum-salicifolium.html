Alypum salicifolium

Alypum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf alypum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. Alypum salicifolium is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
