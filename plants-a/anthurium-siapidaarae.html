Anthurium siapidaarae

Anthurium siapidaarae, commonly known as the Sipan Island anthurium, is a rare tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix. The plant produces showy, colorful spathes that surround the spadix, which is typical of the genus Anthurium. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
