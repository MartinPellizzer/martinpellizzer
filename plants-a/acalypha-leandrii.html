Acalypha leandrii

Acalypha leandrii, commonly known as the golden torch or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha leandrii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects due to its vibrant colors and nectar-rich flowers.
