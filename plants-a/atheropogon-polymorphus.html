Atheropogon polymorphus

Atheropogon polymorphus, commonly known as the variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and variable leaf morphology, which can range from narrow to broad. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tightly arranged.

Atheropogon polymorphus is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
