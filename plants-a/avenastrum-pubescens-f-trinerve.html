Avenastrum pubescens f. trinerve

Avenastrum pubescens f. trinerve, commonly known as the trinerved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, often with three nerves, giving it the specific epithet "trinerve.".

This fern-like appearance distinguishes it from other members of the genus Avenastrum.
