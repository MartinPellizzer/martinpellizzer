Amauropelta rufa

Amauropelta rufa, commonly known as the red fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a reddish hue on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are pinnately divided, with small, ovate pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appeal.
