Anguloa × ruckeri var. wageneri

Anguloa × ruckeri var. wageneri, commonly known as the Wagener's anguloa, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a blend of pink, purple, and white hues. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
