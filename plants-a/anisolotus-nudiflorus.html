Anisolotus nudiflorus

Anisolotus nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, dry fruits that disperse its seeds.

Anisolotus nudiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
