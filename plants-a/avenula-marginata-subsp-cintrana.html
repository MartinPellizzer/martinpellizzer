Avenula marginata subsp. cintrana

Avenula marginata subsp.

cintrana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grassland or woodland habitats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white along the margins. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
