Arundinaria kesenensis

Arundinaria kesenensis, commonly known as the Kesen bamboo, is a species of grass native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Arundinaria kesenensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
