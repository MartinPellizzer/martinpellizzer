Avena sterilis subsp. ludoviciana

Avena sterilis subsp.

ludoviciana, commonly known as the blue grama grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and prairies.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it an important component of native plant communities.

Its ecological value includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
