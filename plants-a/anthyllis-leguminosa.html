Anthyllis leguminosa

Anthyllis leguminosa, commonly known as the sweet clover or lesser clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

Anthyllis leguminosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
