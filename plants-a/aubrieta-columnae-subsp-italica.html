Aubrieta columnae subsp. italica

Aubrieta columnae subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian rockcress, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a compact, mat-forming growth habit with silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Aubrieta columnae subsp.

italica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
