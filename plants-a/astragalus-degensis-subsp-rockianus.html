Astragalus degensis subsp. rockianus

Astragalus degensis subsp.

rockianus, commonly known as the Rockian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
