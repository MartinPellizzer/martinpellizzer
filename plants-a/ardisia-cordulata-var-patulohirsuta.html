Ardisia cordulata var. patulohirsuta

Ardisia cordulata var. patulohirsuta, commonly known as the broad-leafed ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The varietal form patulohirsuta is distinguished by its broader leaves and more hairy stems compared to the typical variety.
