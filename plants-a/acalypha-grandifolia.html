Acalypha grandifolia

Acalypha grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Acalypha grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
