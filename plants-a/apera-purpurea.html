Apera purpurea

Apera purpurea, commonly known as purple clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, purple-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
