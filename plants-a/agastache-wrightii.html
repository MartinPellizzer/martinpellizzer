Agastache wrightii

Agastache wrightii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Agastache wrightii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
