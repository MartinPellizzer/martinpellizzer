Artemisia sublessingiana

Artemisia sublessingiana, commonly known as the African wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains essential oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
