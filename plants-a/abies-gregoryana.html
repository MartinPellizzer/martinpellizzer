Abies gregoryana

Abies gregoryana, commonly known as the Gregory fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its needles are short and stiff, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Abies gregoryana thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.
