Agave gracielae

Agave gracielae, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a species of succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave gracielae is also valued for its use in the production of tequila and other alcoholic beverages.
