Agastache pallidiflora var. greenei

Agastache pallidiflora var. greenei, commonly known as the Greene's blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, blue-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in mid to late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
