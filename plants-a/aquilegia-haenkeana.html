Aquilegia haenkeana

Aquilegia haenkeana, commonly known as the Andean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces seeds in a distinctive, inflated seed pod. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
