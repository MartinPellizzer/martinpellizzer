Arisarum arisarum

Arisarum arisarum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, white, ghost-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a dense, velvety coating.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and faces significant conservation challenges.
