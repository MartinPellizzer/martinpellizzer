Artemisia campestris subsp. lednicensis

Artemisia campestris subsp.

lednicensis, commonly known as the Lednices Lowland Wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in panicles. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a strongly aromatic scent, often used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Artemisia genus, sharing similar ecological and chemical traits.
