Allium thomsonii

Allium thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces small, spherical bulbs and has narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
