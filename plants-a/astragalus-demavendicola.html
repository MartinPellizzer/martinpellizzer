Astragalus demavendicola

Astragalus demavendicola, commonly known as the Demavend milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
