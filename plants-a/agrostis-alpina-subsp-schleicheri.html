Agrostis alpina subsp. schleicheri

Agrostis alpina subsp.

schleicheri, commonly known as the alpine bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in short hairs.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
