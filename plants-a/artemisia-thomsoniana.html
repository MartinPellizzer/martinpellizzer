Artemisia thomsoniana

Artemisia thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
