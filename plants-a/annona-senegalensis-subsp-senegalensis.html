Annona senegalensis subsp. senegalensis

Annona senegalensis subsp.

senegalensis, commonly known as the African hornapple, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa. It produces large, green, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

The fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
