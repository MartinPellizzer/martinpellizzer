Astragalus bijarensis

Astragalus bijarensis, commonly known as the Bijarensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
