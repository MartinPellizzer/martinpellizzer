Afridia nepetaeformis

Afridia nepetaeformis, commonly known as the African catmint, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
