Acnistus breviflorus subsp. pubescens

Acnistus breviflorus subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acnistus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in small, rounded clusters. Its hairy texture, particularly on the stems and leaves, is a distinguishing feature.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
