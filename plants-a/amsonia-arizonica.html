Amsonia arizonica

Amsonia arizonica, commonly known as Arizona blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The botanical features of Amsonia arizonica contribute to its role as an important nectar source for pollinators in its native habitat.
