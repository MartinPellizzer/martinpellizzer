Aidia rugulosa

Aidia rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf aidia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Aidia rugulosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
