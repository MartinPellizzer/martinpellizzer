Alchemilla hypochlora

Alchemilla hypochlora, commonly known as the pale lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
