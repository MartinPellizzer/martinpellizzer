Acacia hebeclada subsp. chobiensis

Acacia hebeclada subsp.

chobiensis, commonly known as the Chobiensis Acacia, is a shrub native to specific regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences.

The leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
