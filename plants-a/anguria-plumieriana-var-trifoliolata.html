Anguria plumieriana var. trifoliolata

Anguria plumieriana var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf cucumber, is a rare and intriguing variant of the anguria plant.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to regular cucumbers but are often smaller and more delicate. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
