Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. arundana

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

arundana, commonly known as the lesser burnet, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers.

The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.
