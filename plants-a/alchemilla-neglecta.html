Alchemilla neglecta

Alchemilla neglecta, commonly known as lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
