Artocarpus cannonii

Artocarpus cannonii, commonly known as the Kauai breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that ripens to a yellowish-green color. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in Hawaiian tradition.
