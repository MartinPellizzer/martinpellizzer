Adenocarpus gibbsianus

Adenocarpus gibbsianus, commonly known as the Gibbs' burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
