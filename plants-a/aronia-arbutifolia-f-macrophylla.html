Aronia arbutifolia f. macrophylla

Aronia arbutifolia f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf elderberry, is a cultivar of the elderberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential health benefits.
