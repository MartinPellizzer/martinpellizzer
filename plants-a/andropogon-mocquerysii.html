Andropogon mocquerysii

Andropogon mocquerysii, commonly known as the Mocquery's grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.

Andropogon mocquerysii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
