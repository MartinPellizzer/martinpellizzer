Acantholimon roseum

Acantholimon roseum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in dunes or along the edges of coastal scrub habitats.
