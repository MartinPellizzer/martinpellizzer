Astragalus cornutus f. prodanii

Astragalus cornutus f. prodanii, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and stipitate (stalked) leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
