Arundinella oleagina

Arundinella oleagina, commonly known as the Oleaginous Reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

Arundinella oleagina is notable for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is sometimes cultivated for its potential in biofuel production.
