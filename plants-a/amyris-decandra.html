Amyris decandra

Amyris decandra, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the appearance of flames. The plant has feathery, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Amyris decandra thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in various herbal remedies.
