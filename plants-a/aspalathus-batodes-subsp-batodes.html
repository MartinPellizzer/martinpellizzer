Aspalathus batodes subsp. batodes

Aspalathus batodes subsp.

batodes, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
