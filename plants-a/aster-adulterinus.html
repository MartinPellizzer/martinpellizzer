Aster adulterinus

Aster adulterinus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer. Aster adulterinus is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
