Aloysia citrodora

Aloysia citrodora, commonly known as citronella, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and stems. These oils are well-known for their strong repellent properties against mosquitoes and other insects.

Aloysia citrodora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
