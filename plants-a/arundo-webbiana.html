Arundo webbiana

Arundo webbiana, commonly known as the webb's reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are often adorned with small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Arundo webbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
