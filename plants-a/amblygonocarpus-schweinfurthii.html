Amblygonocarpus schweinfurthii

Amblygonocarpus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a leguminous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is often found in dry, savanna-like habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
