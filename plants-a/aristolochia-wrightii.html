Aristolochia wrightii

Aristolochia wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-like flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
