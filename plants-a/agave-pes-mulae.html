Agave pes-mulae

Agave pes-mulae, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. Agave pes-mulae is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its leaves make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
