Andropogon kindunduensis

Andropogon kindunduensis, commonly known as the Kindundu grass, is a perennial grass species native to central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Andropogon kindunduensis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals in its native range.
