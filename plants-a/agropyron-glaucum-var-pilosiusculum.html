Agropyron glaucum var. pilosiusculum

Agropyron glaucum var. pilosiusculum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to bluish flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
