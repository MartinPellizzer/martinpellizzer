Anisogonium alismifolium

Anisogonium alismifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the water's surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with three sepals and three petals.

Anisogonium alismifolium plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
