Acer lobelii var. platanoides

Acer lobelii var. platanoides, commonly known as the plains maples, is a deciduous tree native to the central and northern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and red in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 20 to 30 feet, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
