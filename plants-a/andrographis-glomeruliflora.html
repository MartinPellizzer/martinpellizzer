Andrographis glomeruliflora

Andrographis glomeruliflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Andrographis glomeruliflora is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
