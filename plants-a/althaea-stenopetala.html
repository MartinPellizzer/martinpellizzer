Althaea stenopetala

Althaea stenopetala, commonly known as narrow-petaled mallows, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five narrow petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Althaea stenopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
