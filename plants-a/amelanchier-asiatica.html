Amelanchier asiatica

Amelanchier asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its attractive spring blooms of white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, which are popular in both culinary and medicinal uses. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge, turning vibrant yellow or orange in the fall.

Amelanchier asiatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
