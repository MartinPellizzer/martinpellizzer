Acacia rovumae

Acacia rovumae, commonly known as the Rovuma acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia rovumae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
