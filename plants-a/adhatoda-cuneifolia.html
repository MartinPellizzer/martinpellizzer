Adhatoda cuneifolia

Adhatoda cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot or Indian arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.

Adhatoda cuneifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
