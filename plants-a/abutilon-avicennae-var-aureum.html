Abutilon avicennae var. aureum

Abutilon avicennae var. aureum, commonly known as the golden abutilon, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
