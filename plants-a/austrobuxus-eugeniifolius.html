Austrobuxus eugeniifolius

Austrobuxus eugeniifolius, commonly known as the pink hakea, is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Austrobuxus eugeniifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to coastal conditions.
