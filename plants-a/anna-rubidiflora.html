Anna rubidiflora

Anna rubidiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a delicate, fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring or early summer.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and conservationists alike.
