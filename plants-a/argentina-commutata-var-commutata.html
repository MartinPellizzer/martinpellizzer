Argentina commutata var. commutata

Argentina commutata var. commutata, commonly known as the Argentine morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, and its flowers are typically blue to violet in color. The plant produces spiral-shaped seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
