Aconitum ochotense

Aconitum ochotense, commonly known as Siberian monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Siberia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Aconitum ochotense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
