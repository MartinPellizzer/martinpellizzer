Agave gomezpompae

Agave gomezpompae, commonly known as the Pampas agave, is a succulent plant native to Argentina.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to yellowish flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
