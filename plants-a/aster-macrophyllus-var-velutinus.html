Aster macrophyllus var. velutinus

Aster macrophyllus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
