Anthurium citrifolium var. verruculosum

Anthurium citrifolium var. verruculosum, commonly known as the warty-leaf anthurium, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in small, raised bumps.

The foliage is typically a deep green with a yellowish or cream-colored midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces spadices, which are the characteristic inflorescences of the Araceae family, featuring a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. This plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf texture and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
