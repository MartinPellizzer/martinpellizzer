Arum sylvaticum

Arum sylvaticum, commonly known as the wild arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a distinctive spathe, a leaf-like structure that encloses the spadix, which is the flowering part of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found in naturalized areas.
