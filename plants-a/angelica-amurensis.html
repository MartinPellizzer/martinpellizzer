Angelica amurensis

Angelica amurensis, commonly known as Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica amurensis thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
