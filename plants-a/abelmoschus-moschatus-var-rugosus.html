Abelmoschus moschatus var. rugosus

Abelmoschus moschatus var. rugosus, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a variant of the okra plant characterized by its rugged, textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to warm climates and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces edible pods similar to other okra varieties, though its unique leaf structure sets it apart botanically. Its flowers are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food crop in certain regions.
