Adina mollifolia

Adina mollifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red sandalwood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Adina mollifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
