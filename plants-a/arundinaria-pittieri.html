Arundinaria pittieri

Arundinaria pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican bamboo, is a species of grass native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are arranged in a loose, branching pattern. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight blue-gray hue.

Arundinaria pittieri is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or open areas.
