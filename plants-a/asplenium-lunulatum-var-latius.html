Asplenium lunulatum var. latius

Asplenium lunulatum var. latius, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, and often arching fronds that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually a deep green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
