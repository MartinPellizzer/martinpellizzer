Aster inulifolius

Aster inulifolius, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a misty appearance. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
