Allium aschersonianum f. ambiguum

Allium aschersonianum f. ambiguum, commonly known as the variable garlic chive, is a botanical variant of the garlic chive plant.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. This cultivar exhibits a range of leaf color variations, from pale green to bluish-gray, depending on environmental conditions. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and mild garlic scent.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.
