Alchemilla vulgaris var. grandis

Alchemilla vulgaris var. grandis, commonly known as the large-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer, which helps to retain moisture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
