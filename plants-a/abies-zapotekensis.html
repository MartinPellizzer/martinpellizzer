Abies zapotekensis

Abies zapotekensis, commonly known as the Zapotec fir, is a coniferous tree native to the highlands of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes other fir species characterized by their needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in temperate to subtropical climates, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing ecological benefits such as habitat for various wildlife species.

The Zapotec fir plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its timber and cultural importance in indigenous communities.
