Aristolochia maysorensis

Aristolochia maysorensis, commonly known as the Indian pipe vine, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are often green or brown in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties and toxic compounds, it is of interest to both botanists and pharmacologists.
