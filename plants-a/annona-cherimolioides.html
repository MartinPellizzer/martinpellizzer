Annona cherimolioides

Annona cherimolioides, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fragrant, yellowish-green fruit with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit contains a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
