Astragalus ravenii

Astragalus ravenii, commonly known as Raven's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow to orange pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

Astragalus ravenii is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cooler, moist conditions.
