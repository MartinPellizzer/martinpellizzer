Aspidium thelypteris var. incisum

Aspidium thelypteris var. incisum, commonly known as the incised water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Aspidium, characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds.

The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with the pinnae having a distinctive incised or toothed margin. The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a membranous indusium.

This variety is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats where it plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
