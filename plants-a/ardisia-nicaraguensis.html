Ardisia nicaraguensis

Ardisia nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly Nicaragua.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia nicaraguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
