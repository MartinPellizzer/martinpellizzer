Amphilophium gnaphalanthum

Amphilophium gnaphalanthum, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
