Aerangis rohlfsiana

Aerangis rohlfsiana, commonly known as the Rohlfs's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive greenish tint. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
