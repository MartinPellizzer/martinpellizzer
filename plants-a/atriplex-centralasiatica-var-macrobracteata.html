Atriplex centralasiatica var. macrobracteata

Atriplex centralasiatica var. macrobracteata, commonly known as the Central Asian saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. The plant typically grows in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions. Its flowers are small and greenish, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
