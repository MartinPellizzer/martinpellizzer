Astragalus alpinus var. dilutus

Astragalus alpinus var. dilutus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
