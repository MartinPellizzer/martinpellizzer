Acacia leprosa var. crassipoda

Acacia leprosa var. crassipoda, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves with phyllodes that are broader and more robust compared to other acacia species. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for honeyeaters and supporting various insect populations.

The varietal distinction is primarily based on the broader phyllodes and slightly different growth form.
