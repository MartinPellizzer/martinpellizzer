Acacia asepala

Acacia asepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are often arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Acacia asepala is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
