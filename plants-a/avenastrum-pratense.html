Avenastrum pratense

Avenastrum pratense, commonly known as field oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed flowers.

Avenastrum pratense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
