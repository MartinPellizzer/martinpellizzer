Agave maximiliana var. maximiliana

Agave maximiliana var. maximiliana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces tall flower stalks that bear small, white or greenish flowers, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
