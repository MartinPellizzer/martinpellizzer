Aloe babatiensis

Aloe babatiensis, commonly known as the Babati aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

Aloe babatiensis requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
