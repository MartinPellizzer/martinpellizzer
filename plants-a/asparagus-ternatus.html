Asparagus ternatus

Asparagus ternatus, commonly known as the slender asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Asparagus ternatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
