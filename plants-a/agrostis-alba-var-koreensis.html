Agrostis alba var. koreensis

Agrostis alba var. koreensis, commonly known as Korean silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with white to pale blue florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
