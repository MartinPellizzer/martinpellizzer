Allantoma caudata

Allantoma caudata, commonly known as the tailflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Allantoma caudata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
