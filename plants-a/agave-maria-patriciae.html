Agave maria-patriciae

Agave maria-patriciae, commonly known as the Maria Patricia Agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a rough, textured surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
