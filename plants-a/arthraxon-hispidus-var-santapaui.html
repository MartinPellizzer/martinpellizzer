Arthraxon hispidus var. santapaui

Arthraxon hispidus var. santapaui, commonly known as the Santapaui grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
