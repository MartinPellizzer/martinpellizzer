Atriplex patula subsp. spicata

Atriplex patula subsp.

spicata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spicate inflorescences, which are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and disturbed sites.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
