Acalypha nitschkeana

Acalypha nitschkeana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clouds or mist, composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, often covered in a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha nitschkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
