Agrostis straminea

Agrostis straminea, commonly known as meadow foxtail, is a flowering grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in association with other grasses and forbs. Its leaves are narrow and smooth, with a bluish-green hue, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

Agrostis straminea is an important component of many grassland ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value and use in turfgrass management.
