Achyranthes sessilis

Achyranthes sessilis, commonly known as the sessile spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and often spiny structures. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open woodlands or along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
