Amyris bicolor

Amyris bicolor, commonly known as the red and white flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Amyris bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
