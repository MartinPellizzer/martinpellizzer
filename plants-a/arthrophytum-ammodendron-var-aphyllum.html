Arthrophytum ammodendron var. aphyllum

Arthrophytum ammodendron var. aphyllum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are reduced to scales, which help minimize water loss in harsh desert conditions.

This species is well adapted to survive in extreme environments with minimal rainfall.
