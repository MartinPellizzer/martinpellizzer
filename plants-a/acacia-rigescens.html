Acacia rigescens

Acacia rigescens, commonly known as the rigid acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. Acacia rigescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
