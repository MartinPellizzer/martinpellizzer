Arundinaria praeumbrans

Arundinaria praeumbrans, commonly known as the shadow bamboo, is a unique grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Arundinaria praeumbrans thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a characteristic component of wetland and forest understory ecosystems.
