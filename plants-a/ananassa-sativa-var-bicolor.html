Ananassa sativa var. bicolor

Ananassa sativa var. bicolor, commonly known as the pink pineapple, is a hybrid variety of the pineapple plant characterized by its distinctive pink or red flesh.

It is a tropical perennial plant that belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, native to South America. The plant produces a spiky, segmented fruit with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for decorative purposes, while its taste and texture closely resemble traditional pineapple varieties.
