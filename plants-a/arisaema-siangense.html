Arisaema siangense

Arisaema siangense, commonly known as the Siang Cobra Lily, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded spathe that resembles a cobra's head, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix, which is often covered in small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema siangense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive foliage and floral structure.
