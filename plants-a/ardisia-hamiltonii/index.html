Ardisia Hamiltonii

Ardisia hamiltonii is a striking evergreen shrub native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia. It is known for its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are both ornamental and edible, though they are often enjoyed by birds rather than humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal. Despite its beauty, Ardisia hamiltonii remains a relatively obscure species in the broader plant world.
