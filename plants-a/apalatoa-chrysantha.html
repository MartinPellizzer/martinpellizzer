Apalatoa chrysantha

Apalatoa chrysantha, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. Apalatoa chrysantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
