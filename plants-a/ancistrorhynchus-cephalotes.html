Ancistrorhynchus cephalotes

Ancistrorhynchus cephalotes, commonly known as the red-tailed catfish, is a species of catfish native to the rivers of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated body, barbels, and a distinctive red tail. The plant-like structures on its body, known as cirri, are used for sensing the environment. It inhabits slow-moving waters and is often found near vegetation.

Its diet consists mainly of detritus and small aquatic organisms.
