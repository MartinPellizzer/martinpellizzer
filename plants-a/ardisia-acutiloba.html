Ardisia acutiloba

Ardisia acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-leaf beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Ardisia acutiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and berries.
