Acacia thozetiana

Acacia thozetiana, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate, and its yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is adapted to the dry and arid conditions of its native habitat.

Acacia thozetiana is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
