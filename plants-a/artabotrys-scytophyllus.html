Artabotrys scytophyllus

Artabotrys scytophyllus, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
