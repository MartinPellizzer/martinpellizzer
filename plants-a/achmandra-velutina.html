Achmandra velutina

Achmandra velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive nature, it can become a problematic weed in many regions.
