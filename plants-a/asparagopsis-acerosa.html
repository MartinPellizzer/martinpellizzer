Asparagopsis acerosa

Asparagopsis acerosa, commonly known as the African asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
