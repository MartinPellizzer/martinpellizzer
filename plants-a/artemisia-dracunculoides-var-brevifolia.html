Artemisia dracunculoides var. brevifolia

Artemisia dracunculoides var. brevifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in compact, spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Asteraceae family.
