Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. glandulosa

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features distinctive spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with glandular dots on the underside. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
