Aquilaria khasiana

Aquilaria khasiana, commonly known as the Gum Sandalwood, is a valuable tropical tree native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly prized for its unique scent and medicinal properties. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and oval-shaped fruits containing winged seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
