Allium ampeloprasum var. caudatum

Allium ampeloprasum var. caudatum, commonly known as the grape leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender leaves and bulbous stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulb, which is the edible part, has a mild, sweet flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
