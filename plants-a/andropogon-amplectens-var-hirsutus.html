Andropogon amplectens var. hirsutus

Andropogon amplectens var. hirsutus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife species. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Hairy bluestem is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration projects.
