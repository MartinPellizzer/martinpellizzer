Aneilema reniforme

Aneilema reniforme, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable, similar to taro.

Aneilema reniforme thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
