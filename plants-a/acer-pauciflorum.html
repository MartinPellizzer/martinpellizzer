Acer pauciflorum

Acer pauciflorum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in drooping catkins. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Acer pauciflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
