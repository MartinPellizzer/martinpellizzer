Achillea alpina var. longiligulata

Achillea alpina var. longiligulata, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and aromatic, often with a slightly fuzzy texture.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, thriving in full sun and displaying excellent drought tolerance.
