Apocynum cervinum

Apocynum cervinum, commonly known as red inkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly rough texture.

Apocynum cervinum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
