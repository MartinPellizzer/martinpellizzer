Acacia spectabilis var. stuartii

Acacia spectabilis var. stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart's Glory acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the cooler months. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
