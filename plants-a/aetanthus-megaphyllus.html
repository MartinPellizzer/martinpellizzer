Aetanthus megaphyllus

Aetanthus megaphyllus, commonly known as the giant leaf euphorbia, is a striking plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers during the summer months, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical gardens.

Due to its slow growth and susceptibility to overwatering, it requires careful cultivation to ensure its survival.
