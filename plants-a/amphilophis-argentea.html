Amphilophis argentea

Amphilophis argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
