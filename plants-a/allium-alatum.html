Allium alatum

Allium alatum, commonly known as the shaggy onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers with a distinctive, shaggy appearance. The plant is characterized by its strong, pungent odor, which is typical of the Allium genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Allium alatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and resilience.
