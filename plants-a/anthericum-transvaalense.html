Anthericum transvaalense

Anthericum transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered on the spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
