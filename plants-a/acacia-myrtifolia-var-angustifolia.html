Acacia myrtifolia var. angustifolia

Acacia myrtifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
