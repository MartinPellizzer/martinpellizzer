Aconitum napellus f. funkianum

Aconitum napellus f. funkianum, commonly known as the Funk's monkshood, is a cultivar of the monkshood plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a coarse texture, typical of the Aconitum genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
