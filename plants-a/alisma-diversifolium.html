Alisma diversifolium

Alisma diversifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshes in North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its flowers are arranged in loose clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Alisma diversifolium is adapted to aquatic environments and plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
