Arundinella elegantula

Arundinella elegantula, commonly known as the elegant reed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Arundinella elegantula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
