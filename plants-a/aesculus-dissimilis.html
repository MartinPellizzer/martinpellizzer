Aesculus dissimilis

Aesculus dissimilis, commonly known as the red buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the soapberry family (Sapindaceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiny capsules containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Aesculus dissimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
