Aconitum stylosum f. albidum

Aconitum stylosum f. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant features a stout, upright stem covered in coarse, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
