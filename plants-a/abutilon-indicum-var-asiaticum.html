Abutilon indicum var. asiaticum

Abutilon indicum var. asiaticum, commonly known as the Indian velvet tree or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in warmer regions.
