Arthrophyllum noronhianum

Arthrophyllum noronhianum, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Noronha's grass, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Arthrophyllum noronhianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
