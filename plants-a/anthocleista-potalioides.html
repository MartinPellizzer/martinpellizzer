Anthocleista potalioides

Anthocleista potalioides, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
