Aster perfoliatus

Aster perfoliatus, commonly known as the leafy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often perforated or have a fused base, giving the appearance of the stem passing through the leaf. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aster perfoliatus is an important species in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
