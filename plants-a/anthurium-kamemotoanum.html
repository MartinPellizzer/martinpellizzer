Anthurium kamemotoanum

Anthurium kamemotoanum, commonly known as the Kamemoto anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

It requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
