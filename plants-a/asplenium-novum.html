Asplenium novum

Asplenium novum, commonly known as the New Zealand maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Asplenium novum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
