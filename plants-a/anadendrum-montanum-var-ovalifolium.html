Anadendrum montanum var. ovalifolium

Anadendrum montanum var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the ovalleaf crossbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
