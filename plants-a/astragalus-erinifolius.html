Astragalus erinifolius

Astragalus erinifolius, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with silky, hairy textures, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems.

Astragalus erinifolius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
