Antirrhinum spurium var. peloria

Antirrhinum spurium var. peloria, commonly known as the double-flowered snapdragon, is a variant of the snapdragon plant characterized by its unique, double blooms.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that bloom throughout the summer.
