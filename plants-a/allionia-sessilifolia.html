Allionia sessilifolia

Allionia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf allionia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Allionia sessilifolia is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions.
