Astragalus streptopus

Astragalus streptopus, commonly known as the zigzag milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Astragalus streptopus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
