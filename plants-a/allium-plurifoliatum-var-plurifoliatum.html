Allium plurifoliatum var. plurifoliatum

Allium plurifoliatum var. plurifoliatum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, fleshy bulb that gives rise to long, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spherical, purple or pink flowers during the summer months. Its leaves and bulbs are edible, often used in cooking for their mild onion flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
