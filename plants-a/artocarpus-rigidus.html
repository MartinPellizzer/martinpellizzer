Artocarpus rigidus

Artocarpus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, fibrous exterior and a starchy, edible interior. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
