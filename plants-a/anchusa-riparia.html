Anchusa riparia

Anchusa riparia, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in wetland environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are usually blue-green in color. Its flowers are small and blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

Anchusa riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
