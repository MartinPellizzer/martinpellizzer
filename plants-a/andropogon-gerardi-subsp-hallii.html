Andropogon gerardi subsp. hallii

Andropogon gerardi subsp.

hallii, commonly known as Hall's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and floodplain forests.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Hall's bluestem plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
