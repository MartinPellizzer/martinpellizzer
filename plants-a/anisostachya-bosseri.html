Anisostachya bosseri

Anisostachya bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like, with spikelets arranged in a loose, open structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Anisostachya bosseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
