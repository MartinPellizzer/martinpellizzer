Acacia celsa

Acacia celsa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its feathery leaves and bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Acacia celsa is a member of the legume family and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
