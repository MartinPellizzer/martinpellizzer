Acanthophyllum fontanesii var. glandulosum

Acanthophyllum fontanesii var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its glandular surfaces help reduce water loss, making it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
