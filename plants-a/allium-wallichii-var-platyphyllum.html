Allium wallichii var. platyphyllum

Allium wallichii var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically flat and wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical umbels of white or pale purple flowers in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a popular choice in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine regions.
