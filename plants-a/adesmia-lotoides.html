Adesmia lotoides

Adesmia lotoides, commonly known as the small lotus or creeping adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
