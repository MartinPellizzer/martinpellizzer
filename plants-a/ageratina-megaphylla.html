Ageratina megaphylla

Ageratina megaphylla, commonly known as the giant blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or purple flower heads that are surrounded by white to blue bracts, giving it a mist-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Ageratina megaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
