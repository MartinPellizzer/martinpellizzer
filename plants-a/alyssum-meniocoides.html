Alyssum meniocoides

Alyssum meniocoides, commonly known as the Brazilian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Alyssum meniocoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.
