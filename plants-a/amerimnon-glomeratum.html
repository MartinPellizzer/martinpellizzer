Amerimnon glomeratum

Amerimnon glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and often pink or red in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
