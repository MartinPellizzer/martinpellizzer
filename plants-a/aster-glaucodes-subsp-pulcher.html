Aster glaucodes subsp. pulcher

Aster glaucodes subsp.

pulcher, commonly known as the showy aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The plant has upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
