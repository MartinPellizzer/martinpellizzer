Adenophora verticillata var. hirsuta

Adenophora verticillata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
