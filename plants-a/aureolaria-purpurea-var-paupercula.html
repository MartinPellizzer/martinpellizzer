Aureolaria purpurea var. paupercula

Aureolaria purpurea var. paupercula, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
