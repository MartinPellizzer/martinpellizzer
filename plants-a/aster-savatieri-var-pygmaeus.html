Aster savatieri var. pygmaeus

Aster savatieri var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a compact perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
