Arabis saxicola

Arabis saxicola, commonly known as the rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including poor soil and high altitudes.

Arabis saxicola is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.
