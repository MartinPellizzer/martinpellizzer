Aspalathus grandiflora

Aspalathus grandiflora, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western and Eastern Cape regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Aspalathus grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
