Annona cuneata var. glabrescens

Annona cuneata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-skinned soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is typically oblong, with a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, juicy pulp containing black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible fruit and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
