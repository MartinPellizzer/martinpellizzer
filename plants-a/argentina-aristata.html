Argentina aristata

Argentina aristata, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery fuzz.

Argentina aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
