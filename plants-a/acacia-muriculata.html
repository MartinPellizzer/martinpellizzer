Acacia muriculata

Acacia muriculata, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia muriculata thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is valued for its hardy nature and ability to provide habitat and food for local wildlife.
