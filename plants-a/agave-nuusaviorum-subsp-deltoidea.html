Agave nuusaviorum subsp. deltoidea

Agave nuusaviorum subsp.

deltoidea, commonly known as the Delta Agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its rosette growth form and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to drought conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
