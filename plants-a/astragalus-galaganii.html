Astragalus galaganii

Astragalus galaganii, commonly known as Galagan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open grasslands. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus galaganii is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
