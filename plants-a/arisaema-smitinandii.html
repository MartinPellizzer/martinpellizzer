Arisaema smitinandii

Arisaema smitinandii, commonly known as the Smitinand's arisaema, is a unique member of the Araceae family native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded spathe that encloses a spadix, a feature typical of many arum plants. The plant produces both male and female flowers on the spadix, a reproductive structure that is often concealed within the spathe. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive markings.

Arisaema smitinandii is primarily found in shaded, humid environments, often growing in forest undergrowth where it thrives in partial shade and moist soil.
