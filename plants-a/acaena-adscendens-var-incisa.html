Acaena adscendens var. incisa

Acaena adscendens var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils. Its distinctive appearance makes it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
