Aphelandra deppeana

Aphelandra deppeana, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with a bright yellow to orange coloration. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Aphelandra deppeana is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in containers or in garden beds with well-draining soil.
