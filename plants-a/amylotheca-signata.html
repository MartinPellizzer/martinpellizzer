Amylotheca signata

Amylotheca signata, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous growth habit, often found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, violet to red flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating.

Amylotheca signata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
