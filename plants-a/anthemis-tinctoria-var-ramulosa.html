Anthemis tinctoria var. ramulosa

Anthemis tinctoria var. ramulosa, commonly known as yellow chamomile, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small, bright yellow flowers with a white center. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is often used in herbal medicine and as a source of natural dyes due to its chemical compounds.
