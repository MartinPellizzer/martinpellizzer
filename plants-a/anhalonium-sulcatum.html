Anhalonium sulcatum

Anhalonium sulcatum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and striking yellow to orange flowers. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom during the rainy season.

Anhalonium sulcatum is well adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
