Astragalus pseudomegalomerus

Astragalus pseudomegalomerus, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Astragalus pseudomegalomerus is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, particularly in Chinese herbal practices.
