Acinodendron lilacinum

Acinodendron lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered acinodendron, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, lilac-colored flowers, which are its most distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
