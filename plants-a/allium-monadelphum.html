Allium monadelphum

Allium monadelphum, commonly known as the African leek, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower stalks that bear small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces spherical, fleshy bulbs that are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Allium monadelphum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
