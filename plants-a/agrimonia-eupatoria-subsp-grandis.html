Agrimonia eupatoria subsp. grandis

Agrimonia eupatoria subsp.

grandis, commonly known as greater agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
