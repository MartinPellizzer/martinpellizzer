Aerva pechuelii

Aerva pechuelii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or chowchow, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. Aerva pechuelii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for various health purposes.

It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found growing along riverbanks and in wetlands.
