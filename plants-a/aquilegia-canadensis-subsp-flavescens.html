Aquilegia canadensis subsp. flavescens

Aquilegia canadensis subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the Canada columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with five sepals that are often tinged with yellow.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces red or orange seeds in a distinctive lantern-shaped structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
