Adenacanthus repandus

Adenacanthus repandus, commonly known as the narrowleaf adenacanthus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Adenacanthus repandus is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
