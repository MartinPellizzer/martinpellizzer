Acacia decora var. typica

Acacia decora var. typica, commonly known as the decorative acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The wood of Acacia decora var. typica is also valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
