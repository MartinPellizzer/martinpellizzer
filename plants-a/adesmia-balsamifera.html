Adesmia balsamifera

Adesmia balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a history of use in traditional medicine.

The leaves are trifoliate, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree depending on environmental conditions.
