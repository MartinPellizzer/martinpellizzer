Agrostis vulgaris var. arenicola

Agrostis vulgaris var. arenicola, commonly known as sandy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance lies in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
