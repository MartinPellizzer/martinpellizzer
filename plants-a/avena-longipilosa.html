Avena longipilosa

Avena longipilosa, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Avena longipilosa is often found in prairie habitats and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

It plays a role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
