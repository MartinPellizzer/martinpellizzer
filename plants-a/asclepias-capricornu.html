Asclepias capricornu

Asclepias capricornu, commonly known as the goatweed or capricorn milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and leaves that are often hairy and opposite in arrangement. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with prominent horns, which are a distinctive feature of the genus Asclepias. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides.

Asclepias capricornu is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators.
