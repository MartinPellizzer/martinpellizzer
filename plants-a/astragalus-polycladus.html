Astragalus polycladus

Astragalus polycladus, commonly known as the many-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to thrive in challenging conditions and support local biodiversity.
