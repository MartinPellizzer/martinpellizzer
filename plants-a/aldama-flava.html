Aldama flava

Aldama flava, commonly known as the yellow aldama, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky soils. It has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
