Acacia lasiocarpa var. lasiocarpa

Acacia lasiocarpa var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the rough-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. Its woody stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
