Alliaria otophora

Alliaria otophora, commonly known as the stinking violets, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist woodlands and meadows. It contains sulfur compounds that give it a distinctive, pungent odor, particularly in its leaves.

Alliaria otophora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
