Acianthera purpureoviolacea

Acianthera purpureoviolacea, commonly known as the purple-violet acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments. It is a terrestrial orchid that prefers well-drained soil and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
