Astragalus belangeri

Astragalus belangeri, commonly known as the Belanger's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus belangeri is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
