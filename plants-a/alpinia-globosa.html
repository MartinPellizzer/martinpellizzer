Alpinia globosa

Alpinia globosa, commonly known as the globe ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy, purple-tinged rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

Alpinia globosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
