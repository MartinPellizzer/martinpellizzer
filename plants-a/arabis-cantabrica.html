Arabis cantabrica

Arabis cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are basal, pinnately lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Arabis cantabrica blooms in spring, producing clusters of yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
