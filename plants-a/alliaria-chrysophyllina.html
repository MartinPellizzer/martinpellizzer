Alliaria chrysophyllina

Alliaria chrysophyllina, commonly known as golden leaf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or calcareous soils. Its leaves are aromatic and contain sulfur compounds similar to those found in garlic and onions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
