Amorphophallus taccoides

Amorphophallus taccoides, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant has a unique underground structure called a tuber, which is used for storage and propagation. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom briefly at night.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
