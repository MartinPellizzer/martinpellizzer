Anthurium × hybridum

Anthurium × hybridum, commonly known as the flamingo flower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spatulate-shaped bracts that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers within a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to tropical regions of the Americas, it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium × hybridum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
