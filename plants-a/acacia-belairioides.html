Acacia belairioides

Acacia belairioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. Acacia belairioides is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
