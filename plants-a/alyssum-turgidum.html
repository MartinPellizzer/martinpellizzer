Alyssum turgidum

Alyssum turgidum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is evergreen, with finely divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

Alyssum turgidum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
