Astragalus noziensis

Astragalus noziensis, commonly known as the Nozi's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
