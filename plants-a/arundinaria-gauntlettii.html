Arundinaria gauntlettii

Arundinaria gauntlettii, commonly known as the Gauntlett's bamboo, is a rare and endangered grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rich, well-drained soils along stream banks.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
