Angelica lancifolia

Angelica lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and white to greenish-yellow, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

Angelica lancifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
