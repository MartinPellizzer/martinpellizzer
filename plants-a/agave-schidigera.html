Agave schidigera

Agave schidigera, commonly known as the desert agave, is a perennial succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces a rosette of spiky, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It blooms once in its lifetime, producing a tall flowering stalk with yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fibers.
