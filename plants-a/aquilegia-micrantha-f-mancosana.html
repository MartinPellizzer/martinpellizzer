Aquilegia micrantha f. mancosana

Aquilegia micrantha f. mancosana, commonly known as the Mancos mountain columbine, is a rare and striking perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from deep blue to violet, often with contrasting yellow anthers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with finely divided segments, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its unique floral structure and ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
