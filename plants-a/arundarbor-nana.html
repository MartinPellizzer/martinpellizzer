Arundarbor nana

Arundarbor nana, commonly known as the dwarf arundel, is a compact, low-growing plant native to temperate regions.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically a deep green in color, often with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in garden borders or as a ground cover.
