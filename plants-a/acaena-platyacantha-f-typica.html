Acaena platyacantha f. typica

Acaena platyacantha f. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental and erosion-control properties.
