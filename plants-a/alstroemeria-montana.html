Alstroemeria montana

Alstroemeria montana, commonly known as the Andes lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to cold and dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Alstroemeria montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
