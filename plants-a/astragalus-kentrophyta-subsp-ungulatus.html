Astragalus kentrophyta subsp. ungulatus

Astragalus kentrophyta subsp.

ungulatus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, hairy stipules.

The plant produces yellow flowers in raceme clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
