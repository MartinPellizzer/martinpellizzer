Anomospermum reticulatum subsp. dielsianum

Anomospermum reticulatum subsp.

dielsianum, commonly known as the Dielsian glassy bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
