Aglaia agusanensis

Aglaia agusanensis, commonly known as the Mindanao aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and ovate-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in lowland to mid-elevation forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
