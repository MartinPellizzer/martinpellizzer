Abronia nana subsp. covillei

Abronia nana subsp.

covillei, commonly known as the Coville's dwarf lantana, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the southwestern United States. It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has narrow, opposite leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
