Aspilia itabaianensis

Aspilia itabaianensis, commonly known as the Itabaianense flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, yellow disc flowers surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open, sunny areas.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
