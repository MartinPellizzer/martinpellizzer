Ajania roborowskii

Ajania roborowskii, commonly known as the Tibetan ajania, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers typically bloom in shades of white and purple, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Ajania roborowskii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in alpine gardens.
