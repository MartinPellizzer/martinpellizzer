Adenandra uniflora var. isophylla

Adenandra uniflora var. isophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf adenandra, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic features.
