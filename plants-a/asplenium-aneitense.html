Asplenium aneitense

Asplenium aneitense, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are often dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

Asplenium aneitense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
