Astranthus cochinchinensis

Astranthus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese aster, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a smooth texture, and it produces small, yellowish-brown seeds in its flower heads.

Astranthus cochinchinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
