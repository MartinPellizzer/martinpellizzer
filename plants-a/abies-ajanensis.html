Abies ajanensis

Abies ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanensis fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and contain winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use due to its rarity and restricted habitat.
