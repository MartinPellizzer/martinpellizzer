Adopogon

Adopogon, commonly known as the love grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and they typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments. Adopogon species are characterized by their slender, grass-like appearance and their ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive, feathery appearance.

Some species of Adopogon are also valued for their role in soil stabilization and erosion control.
