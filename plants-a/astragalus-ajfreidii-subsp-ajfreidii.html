Astragalus ajfreidii subsp. ajfreidii

Astragalus ajfreidii subsp.

ajfreidii, commonly known as the Ajfreidii milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and high altitudes. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.

This subspecies is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
