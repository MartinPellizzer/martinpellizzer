Actinidia hypoglauca

Actinidia hypoglauca, commonly known as the fuzzy white kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a hairy, fuzzy, elongated berry that turns brown when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and as a source of edible fruit.
