Arrabidaea cinnamomea

Arrabidaea cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or sweet cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its unique fragrance and medicinal properties make it a valuable species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
