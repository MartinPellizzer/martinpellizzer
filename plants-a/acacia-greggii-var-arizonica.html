Acacia greggii var. arizonica

Acacia greggii var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona acacia, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
