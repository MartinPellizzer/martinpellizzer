Achillea japonica

Achillea japonica, commonly known as Japanese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its growth habit is perennial, with a compact, bushy form that makes it suitable for garden landscapes.
