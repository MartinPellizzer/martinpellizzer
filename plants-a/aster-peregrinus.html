Aster peregrinus

Aster peregrinus, commonly known as the wandering star, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flower heads that resemble small stars, hence its common name. Aster peregrinus typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
