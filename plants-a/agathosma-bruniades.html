Agathosma bruniades

Agathosma bruniades, commonly known as African geranium, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aroma in perfumery.
