Aureliana fasciculata var. tomentella

Aureliana fasciculata var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly aureliana, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cooler, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
