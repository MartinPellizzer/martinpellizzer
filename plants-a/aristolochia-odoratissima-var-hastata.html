Aristolochia odoratissima var. hastata

Aristolochia odoratissima var. hastata, commonly known as the fragrant Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant is characterized by its unique morphology, with a swollen, hollow stem that resembles a pipe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
