Andrographis neesiana var. producta

Andrographis neesiana var. producta, commonly known as the Indian bittersweet, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to regions of India and Southeast Asia, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties.
