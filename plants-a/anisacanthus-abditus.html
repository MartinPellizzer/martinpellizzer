Anisacanthus abditus

Anisacanthus abditus, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its drought tolerance and adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Anisacanthus abditus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
