Astragalus kentrophyta subsp. douglasii

Astragalus kentrophyta subsp.

douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
