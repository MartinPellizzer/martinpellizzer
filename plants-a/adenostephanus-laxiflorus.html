Adenostephanus laxiflorus

Adenostephanus laxiflorus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low, spreading growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

Adenostephanus laxiflorus plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various desert wildlife.
