Amellus latifolius

Amellus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf amellus, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
