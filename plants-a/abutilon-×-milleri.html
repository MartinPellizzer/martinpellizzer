Abutilon × milleri

Abutilon × milleri, commonly known as velvetleaf or miller's abutilon, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a soft, tactile appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Introduced Distribution
