Arctium verutum

Arctium verutum, commonly known as the burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple or pink flowers in clusters, followed by the distinctive burrs that give it its common name. Its root is thick, fleshy, and often used in traditional medicine.

Arctium verutum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.
