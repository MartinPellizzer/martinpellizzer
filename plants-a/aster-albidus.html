Aster albidus

Aster albidus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Aster albidus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
