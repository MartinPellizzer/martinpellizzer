Aristolochia meridionalis subsp. centralis

Aristolochia meridionalis subsp.

centralis, commonly known as the Central American pipevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central America. It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-like flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
