Aconitum sinchiangense

Aconitum sinchiangense, commonly known as the Yunnan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are deeply lobed and palmately arranged. Aconitum sinchiangense contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
