Amsinckia glomerata

Amsinckia glomerata, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Amsinckia glomerata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
