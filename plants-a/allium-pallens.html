Allium pallens

Allium pallens, commonly known as the pale garlic or white leek, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Allium pallens is valued for its aromatic properties and adaptability to various soil types.
