Armeria maritima var. laucheana

Armeria maritima var. laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche's sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hardy nature makes it well-suited for maritime climates and coastal gardens.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
