Anathallis barbulata

Anathallis barbulata, commonly known as the hairy anathallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or dense undergrowth. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Anathallis barbulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
