Acacia pennata subsp. insuavis

Acacia pennata subsp.

insuavis, commonly known as the bitter acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain bitter compounds, which contribute to its common name.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
