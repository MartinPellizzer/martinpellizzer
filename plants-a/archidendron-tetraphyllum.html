Archidendron tetraphyllum

Archidendron tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leaf sycamore, is a leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into four leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
