Aleuritia capitellata

Aleuritia capitellata, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Aleuritia capitellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide early seasonal interest in gardens.
