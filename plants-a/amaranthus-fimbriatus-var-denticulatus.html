Amaranthus fimbriatus var. denticulatus

Amaranthus fimbriatus var. denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
