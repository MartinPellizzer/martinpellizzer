Asplenium bradeorum

Asplenium bradeorum, commonly known as the Brade's spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium bradeorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
