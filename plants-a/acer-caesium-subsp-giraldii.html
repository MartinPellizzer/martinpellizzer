Acer caesium subsp. giraldii

Acer caesium subsp.

giraldii, commonly known as the giant maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, providing a striking visual display in the autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty nurseries.
