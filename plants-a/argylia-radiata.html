Argylia radiata

Argylia radiata, commonly known as the radiant argylia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Argylia radiata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
