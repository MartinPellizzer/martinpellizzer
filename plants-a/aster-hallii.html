Aster hallii

Aster hallii, commonly known as Hall's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster hallii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
