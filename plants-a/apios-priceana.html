Apios priceana

Apios priceana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a taproot system and produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is often found in moist, open woodlands. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
