Arnica foliosa var. bernardina

Arnica foliosa var. bernardina, commonly known as the Bernard's leafy arnica, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
