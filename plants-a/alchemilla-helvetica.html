Alchemilla helvetica

Alchemilla helvetica, commonly known as Swiss lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a slightly medicinal scent.

Alchemilla helvetica thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
