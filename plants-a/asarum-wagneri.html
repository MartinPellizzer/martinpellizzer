Asarum wagneri

Asarum wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous structure. Its rhizomatous root system is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Asarum wagneri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.
