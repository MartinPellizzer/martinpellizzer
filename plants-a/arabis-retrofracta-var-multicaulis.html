Arabis retrofracta var. multicaulis

Arabis retrofracta var. multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of linear, glaucous leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
