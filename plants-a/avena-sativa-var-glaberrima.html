Avena sativa var. glaberrima

Avena sativa var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous oat, is a variety of the common oat plant characterized by its smooth, hairless surface.

This variety is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed florets. The plant is primarily cultivated for its nutritious grains, which are rich in protein and fiber.

Due to its unique morphology, it is often studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological applications.
