Aegiphila purpurascens

Aegiphila purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the dry forests of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves that turn a striking purple color as they mature. It produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Aegiphila purpurascens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
