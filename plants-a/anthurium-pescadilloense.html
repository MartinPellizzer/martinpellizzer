Anthurium pescadilloense

Anthurium pescadilloense, commonly known as the Pescadillo's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pescadilloense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
