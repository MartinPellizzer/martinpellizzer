Anacyclus radiatus subsp. radiatus

Anacyclus radiatus subsp.

radiatus, commonly known as the radiate crownflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

The plant thrives in arid to semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
