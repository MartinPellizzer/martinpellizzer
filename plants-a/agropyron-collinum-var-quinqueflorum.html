Agropyron collinum var. quinqueflorum

Agropyron collinum var. quinqueflorum, commonly known as the five-flowered wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with five to seven florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
