Acacia jackesiana

Acacia jackesiana, commonly known as the Jackes' acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, feathery leaves that are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia jackesiana is typically found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and scrublands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
