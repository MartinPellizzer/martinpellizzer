Angraecum cephalotes

Angraecum cephalotes, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive star-like pattern. The plant produces elongated, curved sepals and petals that give it a unique, almost otherworldly appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Angraecum cephalotes is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
