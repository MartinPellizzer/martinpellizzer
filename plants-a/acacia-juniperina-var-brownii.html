Acacia juniperina var. brownii

Acacia juniperina var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are often visited by insects and birds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
