Aspalathus leptoptera

Aspalathus leptoptera, commonly known as the small-leaved rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant is well adapted to the local climate, thriving in dry, sandy soils and requiring minimal water. It is a key species in the fynbos biome, contributing to the unique biodiversity of the region.

The plant's most notable feature is its use in the production of rooibos tea, which is renowned for its distinctive flavor and health benefits.
