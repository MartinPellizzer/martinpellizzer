Arabis alpina var. formosana

Arabis alpina var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
