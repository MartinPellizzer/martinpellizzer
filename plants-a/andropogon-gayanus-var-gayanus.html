Andropogon gayanus var. gayanus

Andropogon gayanus var. gayanus, commonly known as powdery seed grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive powdery coating on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is part of the Poaceae family.
