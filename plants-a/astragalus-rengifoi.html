Astragalus rengifoi

Astragalus rengifoi, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Tibet, Xinjiang, and parts of Mongolia.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its root system is often fibrous and deep-reaching.

Astragalus rengifoi is adapted to arid and high-altitude environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
