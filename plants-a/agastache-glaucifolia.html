Agastache glaucifolia

Agastache glaucifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Agastache glaucifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
