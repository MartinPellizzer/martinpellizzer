Acacia bivenosa

Acacia bivenosa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the branches. Acacia bivenosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
