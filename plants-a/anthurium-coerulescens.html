Anthurium coerulescens

Anthurium coerulescens, commonly known as the Blue Anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, blue-green bracts that surround the spadix, which is typically yellowish-white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers within the spadix, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium coerulescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and vibrant foliage.
