Acacia fisheri

Acacia fisheri, commonly known as the Fisher's acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. Its flowers are typically pale yellow and occur in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Acacia fisheri is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing resilience through its deep root system and water-storing tissues.
