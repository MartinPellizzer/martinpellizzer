Anthoxanthum nitens var. sachalinense

Anthoxanthum nitens var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Far East of Russia and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, each bearing two to three small, pale yellow to brownish florets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
