Aspalathus cuneata var. retusa

Aspalathus cuneata var. retusa, commonly known as the sweet pea bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compound leaves with several leaflets, and it produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
