Allium angulosum var. calcareum

Allium angulosum var. calcareum, commonly known as the limestone garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often found in specialized ecosystems.
