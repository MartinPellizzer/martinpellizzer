Aragoa occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Aragoa occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western aragoa, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
