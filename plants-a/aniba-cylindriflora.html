Aniba cylindriflora

Aniba cylindriflora, commonly known as the cylindrical-flowered aniba, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Aniba cylindriflora is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic properties.
