Astragalus ferganensis f. arcuatus

Astragalus ferganensis f. arcuatus, commonly known as the Fergana milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
