Anemone hortensis var. variata

Anemone hortensis var. variata, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, double-layered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and graceful appearance.
