Ageratum latifolium var. galapageium

Ageratum latifolium var. galapageium, commonly known as the Galápagos ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences of blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in suitable climates.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its unique adaptation to the island's environment.
