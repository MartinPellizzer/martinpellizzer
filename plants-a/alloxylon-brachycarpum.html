Alloxylon brachycarpum

Alloxylon brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit kempsey pine, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

Alloxylon brachycarpum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
