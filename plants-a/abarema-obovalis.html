Abarema obovalis

Abarema obovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory. Abarema obovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
