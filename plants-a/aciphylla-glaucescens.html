Aciphylla glaucescens

Aciphylla glaucescens, commonly known as the silver tussock grass, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Aciphylla glaucescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
