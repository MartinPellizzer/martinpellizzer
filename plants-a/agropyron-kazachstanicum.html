Agropyron kazachstanicum

Agropyron kazachstanicum, commonly known as the Kazakh wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for erosion control and forage.

Agropyron kazachstanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
