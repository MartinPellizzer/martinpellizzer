Ammannia repens

Ammannia repens, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ammannia repens is known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
