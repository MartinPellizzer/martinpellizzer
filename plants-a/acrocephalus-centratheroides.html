Acrocephalus centratheroides

Acrocephalus centratheroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Acrocephalus centratheroides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
