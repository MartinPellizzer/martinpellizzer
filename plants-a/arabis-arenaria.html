Arabis arenaria

Arabis arenaria, commonly known as sand sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to conserve moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in a raceme. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed areas.

Arabis arenaria is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
