Aspilia linearifolia

Aspilia linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved torch flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of bright yellow, torch-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
