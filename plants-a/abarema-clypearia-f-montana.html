Abarema clypearia f. montana

Abarema clypearia f. montana, commonly known as the mountain clypearia, is a variant of the species native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This form is distinguished by its slightly elevated habitat preference compared to the typical variety.
