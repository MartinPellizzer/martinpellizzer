Astragalus horwoodii

Astragalus horwoodii, commonly known as Horwood's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, yellow to green pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Astragalus horwoodii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
