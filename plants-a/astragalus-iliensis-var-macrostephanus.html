Astragalus iliensis var. macrostephanus

Astragalus iliensis var. macrostephanus, commonly known as the Ili Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its harsh, continental climate.
