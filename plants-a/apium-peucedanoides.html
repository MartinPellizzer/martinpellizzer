Apium peucedanoides

Apium peucedanoides, commonly known as hemlock water-dropwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

However, it is considered toxic and should be handled with care.
