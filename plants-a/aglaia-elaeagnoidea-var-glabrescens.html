Aglaia elaeagnoidea var. glabrescens

Aglaia elaeagnoidea var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth aglaia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
