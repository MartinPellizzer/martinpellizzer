Astragalus vogelii var. congestus

Astragalus vogelii var. congestus, commonly known as the dense astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces clusters of yellow or cream-colored flowers. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This species is often found in dry grasslands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
