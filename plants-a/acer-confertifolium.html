Acer confertifolium

Acer confertifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often variegated foliage that displays a range of colors throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically red or green, with a serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the autumn.

This species thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes.
