Amomum scottiana

Amomum scottiana, commonly known as the Scott's cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark brown, three-lobed berries. Its rhizomes, similar to those of other ginger species, are the primary part used for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Amomum scottiana is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
