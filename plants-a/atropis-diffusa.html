Atropis diffusa

Atropis diffusa, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated, sparsely branched inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional and modern botanical studies.
