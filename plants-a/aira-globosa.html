Aira globosa

Aira globosa, commonly known as the globe grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence that resembles a globe, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Aira globosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
