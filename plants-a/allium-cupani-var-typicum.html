Allium cupani var. typicum

Allium cupani var. typicum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features bulbous stems with broad, flat leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in umbels. Its bulbs are often used as a culinary ingredient, similar to regular garlic.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and hardiness in various climates.
