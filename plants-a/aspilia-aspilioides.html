Aspilia aspilioides

Aspilia aspilioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disc of smaller yellow florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across the region.

Aspilia aspilioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
