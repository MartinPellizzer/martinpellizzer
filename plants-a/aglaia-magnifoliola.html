Aglaia magnifoliola

Aglaia magnifoliola, commonly known as the magnificent aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
