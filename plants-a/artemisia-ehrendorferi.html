Artemisia ehrendorferi

Artemisia ehrendorferi, commonly known as the Ehrendorfer's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in clusters called panicles. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Artemisia ehrendorferi is often used in herbal medicine and is also valued in traditional landscaping for its ornamental qualities.
