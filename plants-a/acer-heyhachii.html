Acer heyhachii

Acer heyhachii, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring vibrant autumn foliage that ranges from red to orange. The plant has palmate leaves with 5 to 7 lobes, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow catkins. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Acer heyhachii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its seasonal color changes.
