Astragalus longifolius

Astragalus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered with soft, grayish-green foliage. Its flowers are small, pea-shaped, and usually pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters.

Astragalus longifolius is known for its fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
