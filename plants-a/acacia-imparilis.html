Acacia imparilis

Acacia imparilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia imparilis is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
