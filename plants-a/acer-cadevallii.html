Acer cadevallii

Acer cadevallii, commonly known as the Cadevallii maple, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with distinctive ridges that become more pronounced with age.
