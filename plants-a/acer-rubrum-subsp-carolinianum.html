Acer rubrum subsp. carolinianum

Acer rubrum subsp.

carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolinian red maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which is a key feature for identification.

The leaves are typically palmately lobed with three to five pointed lobes, and they turn striking shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
