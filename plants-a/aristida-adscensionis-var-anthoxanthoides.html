Aristida adscensionis var. anthoxanthoides

Aristida adscensionis var. anthoxanthoides, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a variant of the grass species Aristida adscensionis.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and distinctive inflorescence that consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
