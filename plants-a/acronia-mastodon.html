Acronia mastodon

Acronia mastodon, commonly known as the giant mastodons' acorn, is a rare and ancient plant species with a distinctive botanical profile.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces large, clustered acorns that are encased in a thick, protective shell. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
