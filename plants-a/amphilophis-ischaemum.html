Amphilophis ischaemum

Amphilophis ischaemum, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Amphilophis ischaemum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
