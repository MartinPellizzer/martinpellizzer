Anthurium trisulcatum

Anthurium trisulcatum, commonly known as the three-ridged anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
