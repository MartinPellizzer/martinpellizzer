Avena provincialis

Avena provincialis, commonly known as the provincial oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Avena provincialis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
