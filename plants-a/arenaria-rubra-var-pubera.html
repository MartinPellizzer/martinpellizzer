Arenaria rubra var. pubera

Arenaria rubra var. pubera, commonly known as the hairy sand pink, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy soils with minimal water availability.
