Acacia galioides

Acacia galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia galioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
