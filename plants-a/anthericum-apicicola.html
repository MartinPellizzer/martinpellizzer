Anthericum apicicola

Anthericum apicicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces berries that are dispersed by birds.
