Anthurium pulchellum

Anthurium pulchellum, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped bracts that range in color from deep red to orange, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pulchellum is popular as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
