Acacia harmandiana

Acacia harmandiana, commonly known as the Harmand's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, paired leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in globular inflorescences, which are typically visited by insects and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia harmandiana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
