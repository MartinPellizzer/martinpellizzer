Anthericum skinneri

Anthericum skinneri, commonly known as Skinner's arum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a dense cluster on the spadix, surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Skinner's arum is often cultivated for its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
