Acaena anserinifolia subsp. anserinifolia

Acaena anserinifolia subsp.

anserinifolia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, hair-like trichomes.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, dry fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its growth habit includes a low, spreading form with numerous stolons, making it well adapted to its native environment.
