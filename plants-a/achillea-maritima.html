Achillea maritima

Achillea maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines. The plant features upright, sparsely branched stems with narrow, gray-green leaves. Its showy, yellow-centered flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Sea holly is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.
