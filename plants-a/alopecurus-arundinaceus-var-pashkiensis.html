Alopecurus arundinaceus var. pashkiensis

Alopecurus arundinaceus var. pashkiensis, commonly known as the Pashkian reed grass, is a variant of the common reed grass native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
