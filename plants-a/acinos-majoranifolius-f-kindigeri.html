Acinos majoranifolius f. kindigeri

Acinos majoranifolius f. kindigeri, commonly known as the Kindiger's yarrow, is a subspecies of the yarrow plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This subspecies is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
