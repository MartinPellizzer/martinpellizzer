Albizia adianthifolia var. adianthifolia

Albizia adianthifolia var. adianthifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf silk-tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in summer, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental planting in tropical and subtropical regions.

Albizia adianthifolia var. adianthifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
