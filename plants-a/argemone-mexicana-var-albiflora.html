Argemone mexicana var. albiflora

Argemone mexicana var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered castor bean.

It is a flowering plant native to the Americas, characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and oval-shaped seeds. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is distinguished by its pale white petals, setting it apart from other varieties of Argemone mexicana.
