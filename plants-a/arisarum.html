Arisarum

Arisarum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and exotic flowering plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with a single leaf and a slender stem that supports its showy, white or pale pink flowers. Native to the wetlands of Florida and Cuba, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is often considered a symbol of rarity and beauty in botanical circles.

Due to habitat loss, it is listed as an endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
