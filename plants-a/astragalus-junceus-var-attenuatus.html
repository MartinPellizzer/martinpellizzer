Astragalus junceus var. attenuatus

Astragalus junceus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered with small, glandular hairs. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with leaflets that are narrow and sometimes hairy.

The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in small clusters, and are adapted for pollination by insects.
