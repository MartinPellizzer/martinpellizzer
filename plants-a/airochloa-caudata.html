Airochloa caudata

Airochloa caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, slender leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Airochloa caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
