Angraecum angustifolium

Angraecum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without being parasitic. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum angustifolium is noted for its long-lasting blooms and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
