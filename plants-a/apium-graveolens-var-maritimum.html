Apium graveolens var. maritimum

Apium graveolens var. maritimum, commonly known as sea parsley, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
