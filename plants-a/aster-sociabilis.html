Aster sociabilis

Aster sociabilis, commonly known as the social aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Aster sociabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
