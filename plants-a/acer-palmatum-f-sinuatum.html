Acer palmatum f. sinuatum

Acer palmatum f. sinuatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with sinuous leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely serrated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and has a compact, rounded form.

This ornamental tree is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color changes.
