Acaciella shrevei

Acaciella shrevei, commonly known as the Shreve's acaciella, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features include the presence of nitrogen-fixing nodules on its roots, which help it thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
