Aloe fianarantsoae

Aloe fianarantsoae, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces striking orange-red flower spikes that emerge from the center of the rosette. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is similar to other aloe species, though it is less commonly used for medicinal purposes.

Aloe fianarantsoae thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
