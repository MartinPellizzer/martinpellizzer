Adenia poilanei

Adenia poilanei, commonly known as the silky adenia, is a striking shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, silky pubescence. The plant produces striking clusters of white to pink flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.

However, it is relatively rare in the wild and requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
