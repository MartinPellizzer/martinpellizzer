Artemisia frutescens

Artemisia frutescens, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.

Its distinctive scent and medicinal properties have made it a notable plant in both ecological and herbal contexts.
