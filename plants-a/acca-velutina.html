Acca velutina

Acca velutina, commonly known as the velvet apple or cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fuzzy, greenish-brown skin that resembles velvet. The plant produces large, fragrant fruits with a creamy, sweet flesh that contains numerous black seeds. Its leaves are large, broad, and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Acca velutina thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
