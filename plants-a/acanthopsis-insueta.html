Acanthopsis insueta

Acanthopsis insueta, commonly known as the naked acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively uncommon in cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
