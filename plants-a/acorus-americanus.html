Acorus americanus

Acorus americanus, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps and are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that emerge from the water's surface. Its rhizomes are aromatic and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Acorus americanus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
