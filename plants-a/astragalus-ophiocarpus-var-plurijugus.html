Astragalus ophiocarpus var. plurijugus

Astragalus ophiocarpus var. plurijugus, commonly known as the many-valved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces pods that are inflated and multi-seeded, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe environments.

This variety is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife species.
