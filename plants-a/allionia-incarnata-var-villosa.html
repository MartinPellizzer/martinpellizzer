Allionia incarnata var. villosa

Allionia incarnata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy pinkroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a soft, velvety texture.

The hairy pinkroot is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
