Amaralia palustris

Amaralia palustris, commonly known as swamp beauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters at the top of slender stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to help retain moisture.

Amaralia palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
