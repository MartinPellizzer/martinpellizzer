Achillea nyaradyana

Achillea nyaradyana, commonly known as the Hungarian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region.

It features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Achillea nyaradyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Morphology

Achillea nyaradyana has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, with a bluish-gray hue and a rough, hairy texture.

The stem is upright, smooth, and slightly woody, with a light green color, growing from the base of the plant. Its flowers are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets, blooming in early summer and pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small achene with a pappus for wind dispersal, allowing the seeds to spread efficiently.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, enabling the plant to establish quickly in its native habitat.
