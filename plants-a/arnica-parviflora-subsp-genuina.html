Arnica parviflora subsp. genuina

Arnica parviflora subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
