Anemone hortensis proles regina

Anemone hortensis proles regina, commonly known as the Queen's Anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, double pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant has palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are glossy and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
