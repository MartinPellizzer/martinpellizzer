Airella elegans

Airella elegans, commonly known as the elegant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Airella elegans is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
