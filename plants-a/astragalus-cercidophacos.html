Astragalus cercidophacos

Astragalus cercidophacos, commonly known as the redstem milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus cercidophacos plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
