Artemisia vulgaris subsp. heterophylla

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
