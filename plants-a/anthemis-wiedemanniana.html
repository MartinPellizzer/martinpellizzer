Anthemis wiedemanniana

Anthemis wiedemanniana, commonly known as the Wiedemann's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disc, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and pollinator attraction.
