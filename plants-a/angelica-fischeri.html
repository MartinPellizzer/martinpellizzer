Angelica fischeri

Angelica fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and compound leaves.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and grows in alpine environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Fischer's angelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
