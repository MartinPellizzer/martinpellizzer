Ada revoluta

Ada revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or near water sources. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
