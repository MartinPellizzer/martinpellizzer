Aspilia steinbachii

Aspilia steinbachii, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disk.

Aspilia steinbachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils.
