Achillea oxyloba

Achillea oxyloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Achillea oxyloba is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
