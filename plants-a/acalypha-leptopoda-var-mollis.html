Acalypha leptopoda var. mollis

Acalypha leptopoda var. mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky ironleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
