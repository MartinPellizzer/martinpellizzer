Astragalus fuhsii

Astragalus fuhsii, commonly known as the Fuhs' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval leaflets.

Astragalus fuhsii is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
