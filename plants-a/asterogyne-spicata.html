Asterogyne spicata

Asterogyne spicata, commonly known as the spike aster, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, clustered inflorescences with yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Asterogyne spicata is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to warm climates.
