Aspalathus pachyloba subsp. pachyloba

Aspalathus pachyloba subsp.

pachyloba, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
