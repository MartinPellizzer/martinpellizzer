Amelanchier botryapium var. conferta

Amelanchier botryapium var. conferta, commonly known as the compact serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant produces showy white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

It is valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
