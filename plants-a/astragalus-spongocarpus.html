Astragalus spongocarpus

Astragalus spongocarpus, commonly known as the spongefruit milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a sponge, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers.

Astragalus spongocarpus is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
