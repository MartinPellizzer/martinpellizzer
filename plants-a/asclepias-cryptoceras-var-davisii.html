Asclepias cryptoceras var. davisii

Asclepias cryptoceras var. davisii, commonly known as the Davis's milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert environments.

This variety is particularly important as a food source for monarch butterflies and other pollinators.
