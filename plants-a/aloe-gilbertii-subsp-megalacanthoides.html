Aloe gilbertii subsp. megalacanthoides

Aloe gilbertii subsp.

megalacanthoides, commonly known as the giant tooth aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its large, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
