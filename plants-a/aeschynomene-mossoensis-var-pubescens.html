Aeschynomene mossoensis var. pubescens

Aeschynomene mossoensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to moderately moist conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and the leaves are often hairy, giving it the "pubescens" descriptor.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as a forage plant.
