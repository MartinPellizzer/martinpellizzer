Astragalus monspessulanus var. praecox

Astragalus monspessulanus var. praecox, commonly known as early Mediterranean milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
