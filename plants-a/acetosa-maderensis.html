Acetosa maderensis

Acetosa maderensis, commonly known as the Madeira rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oblanceolate leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high winds and salt spray.

Acetosa maderensis is considered a rare and endemic species, contributing to the unique flora of the Madeira archipelago.
