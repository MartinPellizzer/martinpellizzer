Acer pseudoplatanus f. sanguineum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. sanguineum, commonly known as the red-leafed maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn season. This form is a natural mutation of the species and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and display a range of red tones throughout the growing season.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
