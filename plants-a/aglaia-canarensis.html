Aglaia canarensis

Aglaia canarensis, commonly known as the Canarensis Aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, also known as the mahogany family, and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

Aglaia canarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
