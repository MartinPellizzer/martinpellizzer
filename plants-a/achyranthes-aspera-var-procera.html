Achyranthes aspera var. procera

Achyranthes aspera var. procera, commonly known as the tall smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by three-lobed, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
