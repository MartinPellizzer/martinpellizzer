Argyrolobium lunare subsp. lunare

Argyrolobium lunare subsp.

lunare, commonly known as the moonflower of the desert, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves and its striking, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom at night.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
