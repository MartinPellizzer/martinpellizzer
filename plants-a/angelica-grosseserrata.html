Angelica grosseserrata

Angelica grosseserrata, commonly known as the roughleaf angelica, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Angelica grosseserrata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
