Alchemilla caucasica

Alchemilla caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Alchemilla caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
