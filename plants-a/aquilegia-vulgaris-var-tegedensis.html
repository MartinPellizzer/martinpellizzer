Aquilegia vulgaris var. tegedensis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. tegedensis, commonly known as the Tegeder's columbine, is a variant of the common columbine native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple spurred petals, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are doubly pinnate, with finely divided segments that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its vibrant flowers and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
