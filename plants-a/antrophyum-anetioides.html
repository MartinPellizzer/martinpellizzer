Antrophyum anetioides

Antrophyum anetioides, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a unique plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched stem that often remains underground, supporting a dense crown of foliage. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like strobilus that produces both male and female spores.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
