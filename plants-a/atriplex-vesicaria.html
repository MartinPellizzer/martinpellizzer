Atriplex vesicaria

Atriplex vesicaria, commonly known as the blister saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often glandular leaves that are coated with a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it well adapted to saline soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing sandy or coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its use in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
