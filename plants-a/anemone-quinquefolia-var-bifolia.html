Anemone quinquefolia var. bifolia

Anemone quinquefolia var. bifolia, commonly known as the Pennsylvania arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often divided into two pairs, giving it the "bifolia" designation. The plant produces showy white to pinkish flowers with five petals in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant.
