Allium anisotepalum

Allium anisotepalum, commonly known as the irregular-flowered garlic.

It is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tien Shan. The plant features a bulb composed of numerous fleshy, white to pale yellow cloves, similar to other allium species. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a slight garlic scent.

The flowers are clustered in umbels, with petals that are typically blue to purple in color, adding ornamental value to the plant.
