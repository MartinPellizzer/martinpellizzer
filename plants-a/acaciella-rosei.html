Acaciella rosei

Acaciella rosei, commonly known as the pink acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Acaciella rosei thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
