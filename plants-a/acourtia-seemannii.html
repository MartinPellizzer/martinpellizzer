Acourtia seemannii

Acourtia seemannii, commonly known as the California milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that grow from 1 to 3 feet tall, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Acourtia seemannii is an important host plant for the endangered western tiger swallowtail butterfly.
