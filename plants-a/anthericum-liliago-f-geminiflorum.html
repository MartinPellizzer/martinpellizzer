Anthericum liliago f. geminiflorum

Anthericum liliago f. geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered lily of the valley, is a botanical variant of the lily of the valley plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive twin-flowered inflorescence, where two flowers develop from a single stem. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are white and bell-shaped, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in early spring.

This form is rare and highly valued in horticulture for its unique floral display.
