Anthyllis maritima var. corbierei

Anthyllis maritima var. corbierei, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dunes and rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
