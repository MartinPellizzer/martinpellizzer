Adiantum tenerum var. minus

Adiantum tenerum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
