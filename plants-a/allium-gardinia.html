Allium gardinia

Allium gardinia, commonly known as the garden garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium gardinia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
