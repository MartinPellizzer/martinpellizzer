Agapetes saligna var. cordifolia

Agapetes saligna var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf silverleaf, is a rare and delicate shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
