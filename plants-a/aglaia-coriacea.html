Aglaia coriacea

Aglaia coriacea, commonly known as the rough aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Aglaia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
