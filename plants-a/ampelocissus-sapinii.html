Ampelocissus sapinii

Ampelocissus sapinii, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a woody climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to form a dense, cascading foliage, making it suitable for vertical gardening. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe, attracting birds and other wildlife.

Ampelocissus sapinii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
