Astragalus kuramensis

Astragalus kuramensis, commonly known as Kuram milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features a slender, branched stem with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense spikes, and it produces slender, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus kuramensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
