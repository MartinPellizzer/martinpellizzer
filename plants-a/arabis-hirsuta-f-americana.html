Arabis hirsuta f. americana

Arabis hirsuta f. americana, commonly known as the American hairy rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with a dense covering of fine white hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
