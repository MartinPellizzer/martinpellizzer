Ardisia macrosepala var. schmidii

Ardisia macrosepala var. schmidii, commonly known as the Schmid's ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
