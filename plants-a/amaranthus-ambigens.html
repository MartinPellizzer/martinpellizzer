Amaranthus ambigens

Amaranthus ambigens, commonly known as the variable amaranth, is an annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species and environmental conditions. Its seeds are small and often used as a food source by birds and small mammals.

Amaranthus ambigens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
