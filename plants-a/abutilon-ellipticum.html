Abutilon ellipticum

Abutilon ellipticum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pendulous, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
