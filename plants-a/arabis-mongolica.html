Arabis mongolica

Arabis mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arabis mongolica is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitats.
