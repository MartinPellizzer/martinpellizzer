Astragalus pisidicus subsp. rugosicarpus

Astragalus pisidicus subsp.

rugosicarpus, commonly known as the rugose carpus milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in spikes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, ridged fruit pods, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive texture. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This plant plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
