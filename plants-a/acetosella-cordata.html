Acetosella cordata

Acetosella cordata, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a small, low-growing plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features rosettes of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

Acetosella cordata is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
