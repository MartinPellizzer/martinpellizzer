Acalypha zeyheri

Acalypha zeyheri, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Acalypha zeyheri is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive leaves and low maintenance requirements.
