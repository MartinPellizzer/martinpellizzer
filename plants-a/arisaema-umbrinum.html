Arisaema umbrinum

Arisaema umbrinum, commonly known as the black root arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded spathe that encloses a spadix, a feature typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces a single, dark purple or black tuberous root, which is the source of its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
