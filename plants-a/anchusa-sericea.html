Anchusa sericea

Anchusa sericea, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is a perennial herb with a robust root system that allows it to thrive in challenging environments.

Anchusa sericea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
