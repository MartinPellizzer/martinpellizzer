Agrostis sibthorpii

Agrostis sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Agrostis sibthorpii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
