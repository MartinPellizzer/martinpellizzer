Astragalus taftanicus var. multiflorus

Astragalus taftanicus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the Taftan multiflorous milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments.
