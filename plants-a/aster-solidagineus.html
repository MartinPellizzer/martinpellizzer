Aster solidagineus

Aster solidagineus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant features showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Aster solidagineus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
