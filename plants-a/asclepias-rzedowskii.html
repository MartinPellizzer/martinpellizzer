Asclepias rzedowskii

Asclepias rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and velvety green leaves. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Asclepias rzedowskii serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting its life cycle.
