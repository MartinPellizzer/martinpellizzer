Asplenium × rosselloi

Asplenium × rosselloi, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is a compact, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a feature typical of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium × rosselloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
