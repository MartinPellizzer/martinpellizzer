Annona longepetiolata

Annona longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with long petioles, and it produces a spiny, pear-shaped fruit. The fruit has a thick, green rind and a white, juicy pulp with a mild, sweet flavor. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
