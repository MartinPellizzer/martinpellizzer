Acacia whibleyana

Acacia whibleyana, commonly known as the Whibley's acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to arid environments and has a low water requirement.

Acacia whibleyana is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of local ecosystems.
