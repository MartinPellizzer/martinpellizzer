Aurelia amplexicaulis

Aurelia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping aurelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, greenish-blue leaves that tightly hug the ground. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aurelia amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
