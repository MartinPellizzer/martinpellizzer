Acinotum

Acinotum, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Acinotum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
