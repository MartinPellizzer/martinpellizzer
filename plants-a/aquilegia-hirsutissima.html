Aquilegia hirsutissima

Aquilegia hirsutissima, commonly known as the rough columbine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, usually blue or purple, flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. It produces showy, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The foliage is often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name.
