Actephila venusta

Actephila venusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and they appear in late summer to early autumn.

Actephila venusta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
