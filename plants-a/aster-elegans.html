Aster elegans

Aster elegans, commonly known as the elegant aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers come in shades of purple, blue, and white, and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster elegans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
