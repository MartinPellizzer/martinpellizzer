Achyronia marginata

Achyronia marginata, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. Achyronia marginata is often found in open habitats such as plains, hillsides, and along roadsides.
