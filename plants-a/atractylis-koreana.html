Atractylis koreana

Atractylis koreana, commonly known as Korean gingergrass, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Atractylis koreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
