Alyssum calycinum proles ciliatum

Alyssum calycinum proles ciliatum, commonly known as the silky alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
