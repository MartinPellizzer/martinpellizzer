Agrostis tricholemma

Agrostis tricholemma, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically arranged in compact clusters. Agrostis tricholemma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
