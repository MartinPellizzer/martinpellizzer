Adonis sutchuenensis

Adonis sutchuenensis, commonly known as the Sichuan adonis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
