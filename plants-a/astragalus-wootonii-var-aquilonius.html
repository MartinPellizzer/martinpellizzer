Astragalus wootonii var. aquilonius

Astragalus wootonii var. aquilonius, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
