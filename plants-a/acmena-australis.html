Acmena australis

Acmena australis, commonly known as the satinash tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
