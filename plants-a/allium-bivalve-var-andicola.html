Allium bivalve var. andicola

Allium bivalve var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean leek, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is increasingly cultivated in gardens and for local food use.
