Alocasia puncakborneensis

Alocasia puncakborneensis, commonly known as the Borneo mountain arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to the highlands of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. Alocasia puncakborneensis thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
