Artocarpus peltatus

Artocarpus peltatus, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. The tree is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid climates make it an important species in tropical agriculture.
