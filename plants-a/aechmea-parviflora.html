Aechmea parviflora

Aechmea parviflora, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette, adding a dramatic visual impact. Its foliage is often variegated with silver or white markings, enhancing its ornamental value.

Aechmea parviflora thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.

Morphology

Aechmea parviflora has broad, strap-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a fibrous, reddish-brown bark that grows upward from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that produces small, black seeds dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and absorb water and nutrients from the soil.
