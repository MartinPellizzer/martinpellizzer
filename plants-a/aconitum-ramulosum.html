Aconitum ramulosum

Aconitum ramulosum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, doubly pinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Aconitum ramulosum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

It is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
