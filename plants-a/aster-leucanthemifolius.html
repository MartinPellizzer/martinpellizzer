Aster leucanthemifolius

Aster leucanthemifolius, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
