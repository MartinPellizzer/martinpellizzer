Anthyllis vulneraria f. calcicola

Anthyllis vulneraria f. calcicola, commonly known as the limestone everlasting or rock everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and calcareous habitats.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pea-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands, slopes, and coastal cliffs. Its small, yellow or orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies calcicola is adapted to alkaline soils, making it a characteristic species of limestone-rich environments.
