Aloe versicolor var. versicolor

Aloe versicolor var. versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, colorful bracts that range from yellow to orange, red, and purple, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick, fleshy, and often have a waxy coating to store water.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
