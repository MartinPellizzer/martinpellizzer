Actinidia davidii

Actinidia davidii, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its fragrant, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that ripen to a bright red color. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Actinidia davidii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
