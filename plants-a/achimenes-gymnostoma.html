Achimenes gymnostoma

Achimenes gymnostoma, commonly known as the velvetleaf begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and prolonged blooming period.

Achimenes gymnostoma is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
