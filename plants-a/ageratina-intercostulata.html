Ageratina intercostulata

Ageratina intercostulata, commonly known as the white feather plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ageratina intercostulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
