Andropogon pertusus var. decipiens

Andropogon pertusus var. decipiens, commonly known as the false bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue-green hues. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles during the summer months.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for wildlife and its contribution to soil stabilization in grassland ecosystems.
