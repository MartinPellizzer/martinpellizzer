Arctostaphylos columbiana

Arctostaphylos columbiana, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and glossy, waxy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The Pacific madrone is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
