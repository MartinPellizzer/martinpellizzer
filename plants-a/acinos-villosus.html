Acinos villosus

Acinos villosus, commonly known as the woolly acino, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
