Acridocarpus oppositifolius

Acridocarpus oppositifolius, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood or kusum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
