Argyreia fulvovillosa

Argyreia fulvovillosa, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has palmate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Argyreia fulvovillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
