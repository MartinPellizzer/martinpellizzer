Arenaria montana subsp. intricata

Arenaria montana subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
