Alansmia senilis

Alansmia senilis, commonly known as the hoary phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Alansmia senilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought-tolerant landscapes.
