Acianthera odontotepala

Acianthera odontotepala, commonly known as the toothed-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Acianthera odontotepala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
