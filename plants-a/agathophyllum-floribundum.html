Agathophyllum floribundum

Agathophyllum floribundum, commonly known as the flowering agathophyllum, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, dense clusters of leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring succulent stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Agathophyllum floribundum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
