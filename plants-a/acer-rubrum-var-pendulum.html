Acer rubrum var. pendulum

Acer rubrum var. pendulum, commonly known as weeping red maple, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a unique, flowing appearance.

It features palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal color to the landscape. The tree produces small, red samaras that hang from the branches, contributing to its ornamental appeal throughout the year. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for wetland areas and garden settings.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic value and is often used in landscaping for its dramatic visual effect.
