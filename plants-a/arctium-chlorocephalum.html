Arctium chlorocephalum

Arctium chlorocephalum, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, spiky burrs that aid in seed dispersal. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a clustered inflorescence.

Arctium chlorocephalum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.
