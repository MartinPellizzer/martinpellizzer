Abies baborensis

Abies baborensis, commonly known as the Baborensis fir, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curved. The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and conservation studies.
