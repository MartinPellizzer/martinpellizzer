Agrostis hissarica

Agrostis hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Agrostis hissarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
