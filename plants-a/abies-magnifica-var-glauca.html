Abies magnifica var. glauca

Abies magnifica var. glauca, commonly known as the white fir, is a variant of the grand fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in both winter and summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical grand fir. It produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This evergreen tree thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
