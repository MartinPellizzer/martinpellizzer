Aframomum orientale

Aframomum orientale, commonly known as African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, broad leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark, aromatic berries. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cardamom.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
