Antholyza rupestris

Antholyza rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Antholyza rupestris is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
