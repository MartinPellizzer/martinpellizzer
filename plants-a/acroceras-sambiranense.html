Acroceras sambiranense

Acroceras sambiranense, commonly known as the Sambiranense grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats, often in dry or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Acroceras sambiranense plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
