Avena purpurea

Avena purpurea, commonly known as purple oat, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically pink or purplish in color. Purple oat is native to Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
