Apocynum greeneanum

Apocynum greeneanum, commonly known as the Greene's dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes plants known for their milky sap and showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Apocynum greeneanum is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides.
