Agropyron acutum var. obtusiusculum

Agropyron acutum var. obtusiusculum, commonly known as the obtuseleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves with a distinctive obtuse or rounded tip. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous short, awned spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
