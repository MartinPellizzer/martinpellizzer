Andropogon vaginatus

Andropogon vaginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Andropogon vaginatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
