Astracantha akscheherensis

Astracantha akscheherensis, commonly known as the Persian thornveld, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, woody stems and narrow, gray-green leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with prominent central disk florets surrounded by ray florets.

Astracantha akscheherensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
