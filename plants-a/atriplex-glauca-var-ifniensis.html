Atriplex glauca var. ifniensis

Atriplex glauca var. ifniensis, commonly known as the Canary Island saltbush, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it resilient to drought and salt spray. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
