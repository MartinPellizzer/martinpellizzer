Abies excelsa var. argentea

Abies excelsa var. argentea, commonly known as the silver firs, is a variant of the white fir species native to the Andes Mountains.

This plant is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, overlapping scales on the needles. The leaves are typically shorter and more densely packed compared to the standard variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates and well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
