Astragalus hasbeyanus

Astragalus hasbeyanus, commonly known as the Hasbeyan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
