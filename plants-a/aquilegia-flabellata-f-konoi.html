Aquilegia flabellata f. konoi

Aquilegia flabellata f. konoi, commonly known as the Konoi form of the Chinese lantern bellflower, is a horticultural variant of the species Aquilegia flabellata.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange, with a flared, fan-like shape at the base. The plant is native to China and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a standout in botanical collections.
