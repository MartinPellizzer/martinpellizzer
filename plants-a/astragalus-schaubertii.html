Astragalus schaubertii

Astragalus schaubertii, commonly known as Schaubert's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus schaubertii is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance.
