Anemone gortschakowii

Anemone gortschakowii, commonly known as the Gortschakow's anemone, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a central cluster of stamens that give it a unique appearance.

Anemone gortschakowii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
