Anthurium sagittale

Anthurium sagittale, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often bright green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the actual flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium sagittale is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
