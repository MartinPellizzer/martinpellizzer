Alsine villarii var. psilosperma

Alsine villarii var. psilosperma, commonly known as the silky alsine, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
