Aerides rosea f. alba

Aerides rosea f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the orchid Aerides rosea, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant blooms during the summer months, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
