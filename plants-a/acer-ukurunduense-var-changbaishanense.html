Acer ukurunduense var. changbaishanense

Acer ukurunduense var. changbaishanense, commonly known as the Changbaishan maple, is a rare variant of the Ukurundu maple native to the Changbaishan mountain range in China.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red foliage that displays vibrant colors during the autumn season. The plant typically grows in temperate climates with cool summers and cold winters, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed with serrated edges, and it produces small, red flowers in the spring.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique ecological adaptations and ornamental value.
