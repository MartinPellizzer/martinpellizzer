Aster anethifolius

Aster anethifolius, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble anise, hence its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer.

Aster anethifolius is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal remedies and as a garden ornamental.
