Alyxia coriacea

Alyxia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Alyxia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
