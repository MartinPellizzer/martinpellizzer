Ardisia crispa var. taquetii

Ardisia crispa var. taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's beautyberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, clustered berries that are a striking feature of the plant. The leaves are typically glossy and oval-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to the colorful fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
