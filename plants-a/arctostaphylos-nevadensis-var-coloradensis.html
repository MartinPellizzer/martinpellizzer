Arctostaphylos nevadensis var. coloradensis

Arctostaphylos nevadensis var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado Mountain madrone, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are a food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
