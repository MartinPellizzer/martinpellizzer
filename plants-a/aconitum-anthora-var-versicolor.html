Aconitum anthora var. versicolor

Aconitum anthora var. versicolor, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
