Astragalus cymbaecarpos f. brevipes

Astragalus cymbaecarpos f. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and potential medicinal properties.
