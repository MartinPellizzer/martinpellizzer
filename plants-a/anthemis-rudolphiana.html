Anthemis rudolphiana

Anthemis rudolphiana, commonly known as the Rudolph's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are often arranged in a flat, disk-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
