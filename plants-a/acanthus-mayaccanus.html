Acanthus mayaccanus

Acanthus mayaccanus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its ornate, spiny leaves and showy bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, arranged in dense clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Acanthus mayaccanus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in tropical gardens.
