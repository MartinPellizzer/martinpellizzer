Anthemis pseudocotula var. rotundata

Anthemis pseudocotula var. rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaved tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, and its leaves are typically rounded and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
