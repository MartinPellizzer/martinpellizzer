Anchusa macrocalyx

Anchusa macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of white or pinkish-blue flowers that resemble small coins. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Anchusa macrocalyx thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
