Asperula mutica

Asperula mutica, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Asperula mutica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover.
