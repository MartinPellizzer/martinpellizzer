Acalypha filiformis var. pervilleana

Acalypha filiformis var. pervilleana, commonly known as the silky filbert, is a variant of the silky filiform acalypha, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its unique foliage rather than its flowers.
