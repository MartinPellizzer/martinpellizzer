Andropogon punctatus

Andropogon punctatus, commonly known as the dotted bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with blue or purple, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or pale blue in color.

Andropogon punctatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
