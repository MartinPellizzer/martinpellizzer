Aristolochia glaucifolia

Aristolochia glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-leafed Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, bluish-green surface, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex, often aromatic flowers and its role in the life cycle of certain species of moths.
