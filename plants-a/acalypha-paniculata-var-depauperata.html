Acalypha paniculata var. depauperata

Acalypha paniculata var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf ironweed, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often sparsely foliated stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
