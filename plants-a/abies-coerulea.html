Abies coerulea

Abies coerulea, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bluish-gray needles and conical shape, which contribute to its ornamental value. The tree produces small, dark blue to black cones that mature in the second year. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Abies coerulea is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
