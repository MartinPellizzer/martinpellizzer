Acacia carens

Acacia carens, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Acacia carens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

It is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
