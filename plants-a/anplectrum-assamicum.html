Anplectrum assamicum

Anplectrum assamicum, commonly known as the Assam arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
