Aucellia

Aucellia, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, well-drained soils.

Aucellia is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
