Acacia albida var. variofoliolata

Acacia albida var. variofoliolata, commonly known as the variable-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and a slow growth rate.

The species is often used for its hardy nature and potential in ecological restoration projects.
