Amomum scyphiferum

Amomum scyphiferum, commonly known as the mountain ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

The rhizomes of Amomum scyphiferum are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
