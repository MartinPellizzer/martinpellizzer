Anthurium pageanum

Anthurium pageanum, commonly known as the Pageanum arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, surrounded by a brightly colored spathe that serves to attract pollinators.

Anthurium pageanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
