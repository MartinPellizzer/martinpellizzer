Atropis distans subsp. brigantina

Atropis distans subsp.

brigantina, commonly known as the Brigantine bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
