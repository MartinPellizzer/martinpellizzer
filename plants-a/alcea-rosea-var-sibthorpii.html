Alcea rosea var. sibthorpii

Alcea rosea var. sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's hollyhock, is a variant of the common hollyhock.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. This plant is a herbaceous perennial, native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Its foliage is broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.
