Alkanna tinctoria

Alkanna tinctoria, commonly known as the Indian gum or bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange root that contains a rich concentration of alkaloids and mucilage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and linear. Alkanna tinctoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its unique botanical features make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native habitats.
