Astragalus aretioides

Astragalus aretioides, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Astragalus aretioides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
