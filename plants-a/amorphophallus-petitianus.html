Amorphophallus petitianus

Amorphophallus petitianus, commonly known as the dwarf giant hogweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, but its invasive potential requires careful management.
