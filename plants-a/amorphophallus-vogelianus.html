Amorphophallus vogelianus

Amorphophallus vogelianus, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces large, white or greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It has a tuberous root system that allows it to store nutrients and survive in various environmental conditions.

Due to its size and rapid growth, it is often considered an invasive species in some regions.
