Aechmea microthyrsa

Aechmea microthyrsa, commonly known as the miniature thyrse bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure that forms a central cup to collect water. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically broad, leathery, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea microthyrsa is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
