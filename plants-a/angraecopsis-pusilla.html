Angraecopsis pusilla

Angraecopsis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf star orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with a slight purple tinge. The plant has a short, underground rhizome and grows in rocky, well-drained soils. It blooms during the winter months, adding a unique touch to the landscape.

Due to its size and beauty, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and specialized collections.
