Astragalus atratus var. panamintensis

Astragalus atratus var. panamintensis, commonly known as the Panamint milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in desert ecosystems.
