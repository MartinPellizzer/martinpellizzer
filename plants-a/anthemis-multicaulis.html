Anthemis multicaulis

Anthemis multicaulis, commonly known as the spiked fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, branching structure. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
