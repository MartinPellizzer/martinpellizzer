Acanthopanax verticillatus

Acanthopanax verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered arrowhead plant, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often arranged in a whorl at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
