Aronia × dippelii f. grandifolia

Aronia × dippelii f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered black chokeberry, is a cultivar of the aronia genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces showy white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal remedies and functional foods.
