Ancistrocactus megarhizus

Ancistrocactus megarhizus, commonly known as the giant root cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its root system is extensive and deep, enabling it to access water from underground sources.

This cactus is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
