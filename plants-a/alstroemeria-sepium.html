Alstroemeria sepium

Alstroemeria sepium, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alstroemeria sepium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural displays.
