Acacia malacophylla

Acacia malacophylla, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia malacophylla is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of nectar for native pollinators.
