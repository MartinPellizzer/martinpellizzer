Aconitum lycoctonum f. ranunculifolium

Aconitum lycoctonum f. ranunculifolium, commonly known as the ranunculifolium form of the Italian monkshood, is a hybrid variant of the species Aconitum lycoctonum.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers arranged in a spires, with showy, helmet-shaped upper petals. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often more deeply divided than those of the typical species, giving it a more delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
