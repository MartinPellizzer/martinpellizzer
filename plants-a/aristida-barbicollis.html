Aristida barbicollis

Aristida barbicollis, commonly known as the narrow-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed or arid soils. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in restoration projects and landscaping in arid regions.
