Astragalus stepporum

Astragalus stepporum, commonly known as steppe milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and steppes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Astragalus stepporum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various herbivores and its contribution to biodiversity in its native range.
