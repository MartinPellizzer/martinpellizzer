Aloe megalacantha

Aloe megalacantha, commonly known as the giant aloe or rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with white teeth along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Aloe megalacantha is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
