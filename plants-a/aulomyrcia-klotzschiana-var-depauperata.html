Aulomyrcia klotzschiana var. depauperata

Aulomyrcia klotzschiana var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorleaf aulomyrcia, is a rare shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glaucous and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
