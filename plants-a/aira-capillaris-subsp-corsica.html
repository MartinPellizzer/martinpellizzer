Aira capillaris subsp. corsica

Aira capillaris subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and delicate, fine-textured foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
