Aechmea magdalenae

Aechmea magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, often surrounded by showy bracts. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea magdalenae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea magdalenae has leaves that are broad, strap-shaped, and arranged in a rosette, with a waxy, dark green surface and a silvery underside.

The plant has a short, leafy stem with a dense covering of bracts, and its growth pattern is primarily clumping. Its flowers are clustered in a spike, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a three-lobed berry that disperses its seeds through wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the air and soil.
