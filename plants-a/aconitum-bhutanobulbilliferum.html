Aconitum bhutanobulbilliferum

Aconitum bhutanobulbilliferum, commonly known as the Bhutan bulbilliferous monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Bhutan and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. It produces small, club-shaped structures called bulbilli that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in moist, shaded habitats.
