Alsophila appendiculata

Alsophila appendiculata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in ideal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.

The fronds are typically bright green and can be used as a decorative element in gardens or as a source of natural fibers.
