Aster lerchenfeldianus

Aster lerchenfeldianus, commonly known as the Lerchenfeld's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in grayish-white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces showy blue to violet flower heads in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
