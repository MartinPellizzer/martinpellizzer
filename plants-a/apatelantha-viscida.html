Apatelantha viscida

Apatelantha viscida, commonly known as the sticky apatelantha, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are covered in a sticky resin, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
