Antirrhinum stellatum

Antirrhinum stellatum, commonly known as the star flower or fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, pink or purple flowers that resemble tiny lanterns, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Antirrhinum stellatum is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
