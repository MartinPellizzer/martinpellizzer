Aquilegia viscosa subsp. guarensis

Aquilegia viscosa subsp.

guarensis, commonly known as the sticky bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Uruguay. It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its showy, bell-shaped flowers.
