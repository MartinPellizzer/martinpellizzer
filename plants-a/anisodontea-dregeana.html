Anisodontea dregeana

Anisodontea dregeana, commonly known as the Kaffir lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow, and often twisted leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn orange when ripe. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a woody stem with a rough texture.

Anisodontea dregeana is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for ornamental and practical purposes.
