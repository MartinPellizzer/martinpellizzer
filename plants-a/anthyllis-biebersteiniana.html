Anthyllis biebersteiniana

Anthyllis biebersteiniana, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis biebersteiniana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
