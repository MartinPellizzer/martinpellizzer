Alnus × borealis var. koreana

Alnus × borealis var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean alder, is a hybrid species native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by cylindrical fruits that release winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
