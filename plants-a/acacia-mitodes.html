Acacia mitodes

Acacia mitodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, linear leaflets that give it a delicate appearance. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences.

Acacia mitodes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
