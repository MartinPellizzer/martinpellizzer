Amaracus majorana

Amaracus majorana, commonly known as sweet basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like linalool and eugenol, contribute to its aromatic properties.

Amaracus majorana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both gardens and culinary use.
