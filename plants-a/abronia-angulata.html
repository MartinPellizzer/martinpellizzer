Abronia angulata

Abronia angulata, commonly known as the angular bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive angular shape, contributing to its common name.

Abronia angulata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms.
