Alternanthera virgata

Alternanthera virgata, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with red, pink, and green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alternanthera virgata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves.
