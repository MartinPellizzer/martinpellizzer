Agrimonia adscendens

Agrimonia adscendens, commonly known as ascending agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, bristly seed heads. Agrimonia adscendens is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
