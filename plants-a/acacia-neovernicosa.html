Acacia neovernicosa

Acacia neovernicosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia neovernicosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
