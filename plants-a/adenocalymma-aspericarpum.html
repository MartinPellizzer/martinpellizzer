Adenocalymma aspericarpum

Adenocalymma aspericarpum, commonly known as the rough-sepaled grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, rough-textured pods. It is typically found in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Adenocalymma aspericarpum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
