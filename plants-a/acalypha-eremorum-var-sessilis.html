Acalypha eremorum var. sessilis

Acalypha eremorum var. sessilis, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
