Acacia mearnsii

Acacia mearnsii, commonly known as black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in reforestation and soil improvement projects.
