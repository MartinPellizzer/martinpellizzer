Areca pumila var. malaccensis

Areca pumila var. malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are followed by clustered fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and practical uses.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
