Andromeda rigida

Andromeda rigida, commonly known as the wintergreen shrub, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Andromeda rigida is also valued for its aromatic leaves and berries, which are used in various traditional and commercial applications.
