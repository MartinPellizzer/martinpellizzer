Acacia mollissima var. leichhardtii

Acacia mollissima var. leichhardtii, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its finely feathery, grey-green foliage and yellow flowers that appear in globular heads. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and a capacity to fix nitrogen.

This species is often used in reforestation and land rehabilitation due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
