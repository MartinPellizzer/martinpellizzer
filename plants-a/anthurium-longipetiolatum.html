Anthurium longipetiolatum

Anthurium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled anthurium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, yellow, or white. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium longipetiolatum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structures.
