Aspidium taygetense

Aspidium taygetense, commonly known as the Taygetean water fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in Greece.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread across the substrate, while its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium taygetense plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
