Arenaria burkei

Arenaria burkei, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It typically thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, typically during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Arenaria burkei plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
