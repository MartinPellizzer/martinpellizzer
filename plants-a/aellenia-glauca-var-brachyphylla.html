Aellenia glauca var. brachyphylla

Aellenia glauca var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved blueberry, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers in spring, followed by edible, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its fruit.
