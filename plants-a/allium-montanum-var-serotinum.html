Allium montanum var. serotinum

Allium montanum var. serotinum, commonly known as the late mountain garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has fibrous roots and a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
