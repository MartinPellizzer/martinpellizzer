Aristida stipitata subsp. stipitata

Aristida stipitata subsp.

stipitata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas.

The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
