Argyranthemum frutescens var. foeniculaceum

Argyranthemum frutescens var. foeniculaceum, commonly known as the laceleaf geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features finely cut, silver-gray foliage that resembles fern leaves, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
