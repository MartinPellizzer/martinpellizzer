Astragalus rhodosemius var. glabrescens

Astragalus rhodosemius var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous red semi-leafed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soils. Its small, rounded leaves are often covered in fine hairs, though the variety glabrescens is distinguished by its smoother, hairless foliage.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
