Agathaea hispida var. barbigera

Agathaea hispida var. barbigera, commonly known as the hairy agathaea, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
