Athamanta divaricata

Athamanta divaricata, commonly known as the variable thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its thorny structure provides protection against herbivores, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
