Astragalus adsurgens var. laxmannii

Astragalus adsurgens var. laxmannii, commonly known as the laxmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
