Alchemilla circumdentata

Alchemilla circumdentata, commonly known as the round-toothed lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla circumdentata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
