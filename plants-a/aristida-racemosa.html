Aristida racemosa

Aristida racemosa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
