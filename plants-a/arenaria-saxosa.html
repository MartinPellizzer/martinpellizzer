Arenaria saxosa

Arenaria saxosa, commonly known as saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Arenaria saxosa is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature.
