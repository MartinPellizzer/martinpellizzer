Acalypha jamaicensis

Acalypha jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive reddish or copper-colored hue, which gives it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, and its fruits are three-winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acalypha jamaicensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
