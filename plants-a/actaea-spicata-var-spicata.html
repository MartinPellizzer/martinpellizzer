Actaea spicata var. spicata

Actaea spicata var. spicata, commonly known as green false Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to seven lance-shaped leaflets, arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white, spicate flowers in the summer. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature, and the plant is often found in moist woodlands and thickets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
