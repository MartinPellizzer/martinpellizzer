Aquilegia alpina var. pyrenaica

Aquilegia alpina var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with palmate lobes, typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
