Artemisia phaeolepis

Artemisia phaeolepis, commonly known as the dark leaf artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Artemisia phaeolepis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
