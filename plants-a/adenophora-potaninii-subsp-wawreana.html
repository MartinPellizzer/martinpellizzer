Adenophora potaninii subsp. wawreana

Adenophora potaninii subsp.

wawreana, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
