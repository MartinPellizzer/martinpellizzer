Asplenium fraxinifolium

Asplenium fraxinifolium, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a species of fern characterized by its unique, creeping growth habit and its ability to form dense mats.

It features dark green, finely divided fronds that resemble the tail of a horse, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are robust and allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent ground cover.

Botanically, it is classified in the family Aspleniaceae and is notable for its adaptability to various soil types.
