Adnaria chamissonis

Adnaria chamissonis, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Adnaria chamissonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
