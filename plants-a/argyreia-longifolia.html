Argyreia longifolia

Argyreia longifolia, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
