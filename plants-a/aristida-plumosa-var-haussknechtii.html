Aristida plumosa var. haussknechtii

Aristida plumosa var. haussknechtii, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Due to its delicate foliage and unique structure, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
