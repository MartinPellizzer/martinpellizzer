Amaryllis ochroleuca

Amaryllis ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow bell amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis ochroleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
