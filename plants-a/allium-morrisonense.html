Allium morrisonense

Allium morrisonense, commonly known as Morrison's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. It produces bulbous structures that are similar to onions, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Allium morrisonense thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
