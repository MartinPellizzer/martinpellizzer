Acacia stricta var. binervis

Acacia stricta var. binervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with two pairs of opposite leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
