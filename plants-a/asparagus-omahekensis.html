Asparagus omahekensis

Asparagus omahekensis, commonly known as the Omahekensk asparagus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, characterized by its spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Asparagus omahekensis is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
