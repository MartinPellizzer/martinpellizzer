Alpinia arundelliana

Alpinia arundelliana, commonly known as the black ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in terminal inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
