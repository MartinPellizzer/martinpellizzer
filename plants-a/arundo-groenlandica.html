Arundo groenlandica

Arundo groenlandica, commonly known as Greenland arrowgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Arctic regions of Greenland.

It thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

Arundo groenlandica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is an important component of Arctic ecosystems.
