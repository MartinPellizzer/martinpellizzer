Aconitum gibbiferum

Aconitum gibbiferum, commonly known as the hairy leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in a dense spire. Aconitum gibbiferum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
