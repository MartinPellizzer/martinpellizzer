Ayenia crotonoides

Ayenia crotonoides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and can vary in color from gray to reddish-brown.

Ayenia crotonoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
