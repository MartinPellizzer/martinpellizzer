Astragalus mucronifolius subsp. robustus

Astragalus mucronifolius subsp.

robustus, commonly known as the robust milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring, typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
