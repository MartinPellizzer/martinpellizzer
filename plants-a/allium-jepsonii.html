Allium jepsonii

Allium jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. Its bulbous underground stem, or bulb, is the primary storage organ for nutrients.

Allium jepsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
