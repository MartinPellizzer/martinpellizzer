Amaryllis × johnsonii

Amaryllis × johnsonii, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papaya.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically produces multiple flower stalks, each bearing several blooms that can last for several weeks. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and dark green, often reaching several feet in height.

Amaryllis × johnsonii is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
