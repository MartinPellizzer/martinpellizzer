Anathallis grayumii

Anathallis grayumii, commonly known as the grayumii spider orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, greenish-white flowers that resemble spider-like structures, hence its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are typically short-lived, blooming for only a few days, and it is highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Anathallis grayumii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
