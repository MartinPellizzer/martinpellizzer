Astragalus idae

Astragalus idae, commonly known as the Idaho milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus idae plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its seed pods split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
