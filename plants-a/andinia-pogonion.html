Andinia pogonion

Andinia pogonion, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in cloud forests. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Andinia pogonion is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
