Ajuga africana

Ajuga africana, commonly known as African bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Valerianaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Ajuga africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
