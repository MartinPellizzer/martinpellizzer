Axonopus succulentus

Axonopus succulentus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for low-maintenance gardens.

Axonopus succulentus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
