Aristida raddiana

Aristida raddiana, commonly known as red grass or red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that often turn reddish in color. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive blue-green hue, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aristida raddiana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
