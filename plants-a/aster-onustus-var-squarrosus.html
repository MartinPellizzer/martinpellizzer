Aster onustus var. squarrosus

Aster onustus var. squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flower heads in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
