Aloe pluripunctata

Aloe pluripunctata, commonly known as the spotted aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in small, white tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are fleshy and thick, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
