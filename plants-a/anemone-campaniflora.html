Anemone campaniflora

Anemone campaniflora, commonly known as the bell anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy layer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forested areas.

Anemone campaniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
