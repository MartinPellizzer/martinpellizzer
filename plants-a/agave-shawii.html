Agave shawii

Agave shawii, commonly known as the Shaw's agave, is a species native to the United States, particularly found in Texas and New Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white or grayish markings. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale yellow flowers. Agave shawii is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
