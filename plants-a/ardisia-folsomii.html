Ardisia folsomii

Ardisia folsomii, commonly known as the Folsomii beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its ornamental berries, which turn a striking red-purple color in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract wildlife, particularly birds, due to its nutritious berries.
