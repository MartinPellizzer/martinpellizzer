Allium rubrovittatum

Allium rubrovittatum, commonly known as the red-volute garlic or purple garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and white striped flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Allium rubrovittatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
