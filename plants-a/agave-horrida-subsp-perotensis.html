Agave horrida subsp. perotensis

Agave horrida subsp.

perotensis, commonly known as the Mexican horseweed or red agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white to red margins.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
