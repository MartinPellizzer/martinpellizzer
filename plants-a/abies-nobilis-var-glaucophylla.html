Abies nobilis var. glaucophylla

Abies nobilis var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the white-flake fir, is a variant of the noble fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, soft needles that are typically a bluish-green on the upper surface and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The cones are often short and cylindrical, with persistent bracts that add to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-elevation forests.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
