Aronia × dippelii

Aronia × dippelii, commonly known as the black chokeberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Aronia arbutifolia and Aronia melanocarpa.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen to black. The plant is valued for its high antioxidant content and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive fall foliage.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
