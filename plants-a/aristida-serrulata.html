Aristida serrulata

Aristida serrulata, commonly known as the serrated threeawn, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a loose panicle. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Aristida serrulata is an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
