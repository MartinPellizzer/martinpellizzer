Anthyllis vulneraria f. luxurians

Anthyllis vulneraria f. luxurians, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it valuable in soil enrichment and as a cover crop.
