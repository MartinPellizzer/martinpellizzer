Amerimnon inundatum

Amerimnon inundatum, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, floating leaves that are often a vibrant green or reddish in color, depending on the species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to pink, typically opening in the early morning. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrates of slow-moving water bodies, while its buoyant leaves help it float on the water's surface.

This plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
