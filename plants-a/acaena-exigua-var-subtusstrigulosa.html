Acaena exigua var. subtusstrigulosa

Acaena exigua var. subtusstrigulosa, commonly known as the slender burrweed, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
