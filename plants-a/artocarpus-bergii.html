Artocarpus bergii

Artocarpus bergii, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit clusters. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and is edible. The tree is valued for its nutritious fruit and its ability to thrive in various soil types.

It also plays a role in traditional medicine and local cuisines across its native range.
