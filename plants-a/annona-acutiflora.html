Annona acutiflora

Annona acutiflora, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are white with a yellow center and emit a strong, sweet fragrance.

The fruit is a rough, green to yellowish berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a creamy, sweet pulp with a tangy flavor.
