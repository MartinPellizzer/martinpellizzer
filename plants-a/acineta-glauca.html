Acineta glauca

Acineta glauca, commonly known as the blue acineta, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically silver or gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Acineta glauca is often cultivated in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
