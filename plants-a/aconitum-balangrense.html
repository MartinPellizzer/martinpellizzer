Aconitum balangrense

Aconitum balangrense, commonly known as the Balanggeren monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Aconitum balangrense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
