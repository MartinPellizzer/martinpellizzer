Aspidium aculeatum var. semifertile

Aspidium aculeatum var. semifertile, commonly known as the semi-fertile water fern, is a variant of the common water fern native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that emerge from rhizomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its semi-fertile nature means that only some of its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are functional.

This variation in fertility may be influenced by environmental conditions or genetic factors.
