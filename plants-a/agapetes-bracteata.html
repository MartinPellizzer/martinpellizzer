Agapetes bracteata

Agapetes bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface and are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Agapetes bracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
