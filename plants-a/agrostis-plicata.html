Agrostis plicata

Agrostis plicata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its seed heads are cylindrical and often form dense clumps, contributing to its ornamental value.

Agrostis plicata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in disturbed areas.
