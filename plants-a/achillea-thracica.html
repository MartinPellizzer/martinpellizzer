Achillea thracica

Achillea thracica, commonly known as Thrace yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Thrace yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
