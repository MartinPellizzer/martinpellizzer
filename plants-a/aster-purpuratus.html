Aster purpuratus

Aster purpuratus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy purple or pink petals and a central cone-like structure composed of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist soils.

Aster purpuratus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
