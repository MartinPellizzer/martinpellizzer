Agrostis capillaris subsp. castellana

Agrostis capillaris subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and ability to form dense mats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal habitats.
