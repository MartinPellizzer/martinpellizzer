Adicea mongolica

Adicea mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Mongolia and adjacent regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Adicea mongolica is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
