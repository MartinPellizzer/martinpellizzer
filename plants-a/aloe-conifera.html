Aloe conifera

Aloe conifera, commonly known as the cone aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a layer of white powder. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, which are often borne on tall, slender stalks. Aloe conifera is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves to survive dry conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
