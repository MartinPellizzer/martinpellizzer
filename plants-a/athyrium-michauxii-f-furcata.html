Athyrium michauxii f. furcata

Athyrium michauxii f. furcata, commonly known as the forked lady fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds.

It features a creeping rhizome that produces clustered, upright fertile fronds with spore-bearing structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
