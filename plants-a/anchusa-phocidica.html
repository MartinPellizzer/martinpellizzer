Anchusa phocidica

Anchusa phocidica, commonly known as the Mediterranean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Anchusa phocidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
