Astragalus ladakhensis

Astragalus ladakhensis, commonly known as the Ladakh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. This species is adapted to harsh, cold environments and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellow or pale purple flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid and alpine ecosystems.
