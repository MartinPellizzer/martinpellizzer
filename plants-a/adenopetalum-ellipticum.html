Adenopetalum ellipticum

Adenopetalum ellipticum, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Adenopetalum ellipticum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
