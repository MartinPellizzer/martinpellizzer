Achillea condensata

Achillea condensata, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
