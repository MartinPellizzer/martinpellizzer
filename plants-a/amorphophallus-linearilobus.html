Amorphophallus linearilobus

Amorphophallus linearilobus, commonly known as the slender-lobed giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces large, arrow-shaped corms that are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable in certain regions.
