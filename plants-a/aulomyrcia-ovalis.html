Aulomyrcia ovalis

Aulomyrcia ovalis, commonly known as the oval leaf myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Aulomyrcia ovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
