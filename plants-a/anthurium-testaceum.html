Anthurium testaceum

Anthurium testaceum, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge at the base. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the actual flowers, often in shades of white or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

Anthurium testaceum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
