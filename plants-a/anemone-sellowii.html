Anemone sellowii

Anemone sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are basal, palmately lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

Anemone sellowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
