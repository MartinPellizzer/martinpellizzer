Astragalus angustissimus

Astragalus angustissimus, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus angustissimus is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
