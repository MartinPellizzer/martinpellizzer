Anchusa crispa subsp. crispa

Anchusa crispa subsp.

crispa, commonly known as the curlydock, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wetlands. Anchusa crispa subsp.

crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
