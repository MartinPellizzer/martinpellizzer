Amaranthus chlorostachys

Amaranthus chlorostachys, commonly known as the silver amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and spires of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. Its leaves are succulent and can be used as a vegetable in some cultures.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought conditions.
