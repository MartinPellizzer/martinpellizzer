Andropogon sericeus f. puberulus

Andropogon sericeus f. puberulus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a variant of the silky grass species native to North America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
