Astragalus crenatus

Astragalus crenatus, commonly known as the wavy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus crenatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
