Acoridium corrugatum

Acoridium corrugatum, commonly known as the wrinkled spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and pond edges. The plant has slender, ribbed stems that bear whorled clusters of small, dark green leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes.

Acoridium corrugatum plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
