Aster roscidus

Aster roscidus, commonly known as the Roscidus aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and blooms in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers feature purple to pink petals.

Aster roscidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
