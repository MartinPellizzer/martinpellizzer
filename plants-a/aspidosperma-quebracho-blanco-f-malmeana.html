Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco f. malmeana

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco f. malmeana, commonly known as white quebracho, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, hard seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and its role in local ecosystems.
