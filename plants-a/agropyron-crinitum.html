Agropyron crinitum

Agropyron crinitum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the colder regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-haired leaves and tufted inflorescence, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

Agropyron crinitum is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
