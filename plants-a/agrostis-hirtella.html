Agrostis hirtella

Agrostis hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to North America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually clustered in loose panicles.

Agrostis hirtella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
