Amethystanthus excisus var. hakusanensis

Amethystanthus excisus var. hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan amethyst flower, is a rare and striking plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or white along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.

Due to its limited distribution and unique beauty, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
