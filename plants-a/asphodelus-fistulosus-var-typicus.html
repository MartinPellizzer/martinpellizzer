Asphodelus fistulosus var. typicus

Asphodelus fistulosus var. typicus, commonly known as the common asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
