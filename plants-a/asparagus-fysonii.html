Asparagus fysonii

Asparagus fysonii, commonly known as the Fyson's asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asparagus fysonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
