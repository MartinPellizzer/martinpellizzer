Achillea alpina subsp. multiflora

Achillea alpina subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, grayish hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads, which are arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
