Anemone sphenophylla

Anemone sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Anemone sphenophylla is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
