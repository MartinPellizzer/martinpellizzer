Acacia aneura

Acacia aneura, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and grayish bark that peels in thin layers. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are reduced to small, scale-like structures in mature plants. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense, globular clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Acacia aneura is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
