Abies concolor f. globosa

Abies concolor f. globosa, commonly known as the white fir dwarf form, is a compact variant of the white fir tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and short, stout branches that give it a rounded appearance. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are typically a silvery-blue to gray-green color, providing year-round visual interest. This form is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It prefers cooler climates and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
