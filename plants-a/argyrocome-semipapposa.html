Argyrocome semipapposa

Argyrocome semipapposa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a pappous seed head, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
