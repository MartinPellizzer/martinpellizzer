Ammannia pedicellata

Ammannia pedicellata, commonly known as prairie pink, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain single seeds, and it thrives in wetland environments.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
