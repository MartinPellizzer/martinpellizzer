Acacia riparioides

Acacia riparioides, commonly known as the river acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are feather-like in appearance. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are typical of the Acacia genus, and it is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, sandy soils. Acacia riparioides is adapted to seasonal rainfall and can tolerate periods of drought, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
