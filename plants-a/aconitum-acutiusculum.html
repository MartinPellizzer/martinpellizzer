Aconitum acutiusculum

Aconitum acutiusculum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a tall, sparsely branched stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Aconitum acutiusculum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
