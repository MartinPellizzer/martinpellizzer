Anthoxanthum odoratum var. marsillyanum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. marsillyanum, commonly known as Marsilly's sweetgrass, is a variant of the sweetgrass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth and pale green to bluish-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to brown florets.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal practices.
