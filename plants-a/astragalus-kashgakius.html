Astragalus kashgakius

Astragalus kashgakius, commonly known as the Kashgar milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several small, hard seeds.

Astragalus kashgakius is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
