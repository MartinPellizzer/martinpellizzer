Astragalus sempervirens subsp. muticus

Astragalus sempervirens subsp.

muticus, commonly known as the silent everlasting pea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces a dense cluster of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pods that mature into dark brown seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
