Abutilon pedatum

Abutilon pedatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves that are soft and fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
