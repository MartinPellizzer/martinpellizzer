Aster glabellus

Aster glabellus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster glabellus plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
