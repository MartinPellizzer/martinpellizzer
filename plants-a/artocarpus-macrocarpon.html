Artocarpus macrocarpon

Artocarpus macrocarpon, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a dense canopy and rough, fissured bark. The tree produces large, round, green fruits that turn yellow when mature, containing a starchy, edible pulp and a central core of inedible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and cultural significance.
