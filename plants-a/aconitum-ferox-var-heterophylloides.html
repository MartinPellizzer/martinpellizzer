Aconitum ferox var. heterophylloides

Aconitum ferox var. heterophylloides, commonly known as the variable aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound, bipinnate leaves that are often variable in shape, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
