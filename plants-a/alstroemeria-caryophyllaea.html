Alstroemeria caryophyllaea

Alstroemeria caryophyllaea, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with strap-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.
