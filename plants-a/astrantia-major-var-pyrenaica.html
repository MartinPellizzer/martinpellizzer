Astrantia major var. pyrenaica

Astrantia major var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
