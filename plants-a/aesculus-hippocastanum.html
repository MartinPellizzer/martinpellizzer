Aesculus hippocastanum

Aesculus hippocastanum, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiny, horse chestnut fruits that contain shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are doubly serrated.

The bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
