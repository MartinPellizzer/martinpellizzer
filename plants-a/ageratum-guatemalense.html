Ageratum guatemalense

Ageratum guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ageratum guatemalense is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
