Astragalus myriophyllus

Astragalus myriophyllus, commonly known as the many-leafed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus myriophyllus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
