Acacia subtilis

Acacia subtilis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia subtilis is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
