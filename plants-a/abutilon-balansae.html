Abutilon balansae

Abutilon balansae, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fibrous bark and hairy foliage contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
