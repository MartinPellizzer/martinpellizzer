It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Aspidium buchtienii plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
