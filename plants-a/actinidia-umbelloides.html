Actinidia umbelloides

Actinidia umbelloides, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbel-like clusters, typically in early spring. Its fruit is a yellowish berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species.

Actinidia umbelloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
