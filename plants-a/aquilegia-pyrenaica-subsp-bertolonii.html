Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp. bertolonii

Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It features striking, spurred petals that range in color from blue to purple, typical of the genus Aquilegia.

The plant produces showy flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and mountainous habitats, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environments.
