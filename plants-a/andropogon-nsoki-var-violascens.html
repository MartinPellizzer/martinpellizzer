Andropogon nsoki var. violascens

Andropogon nsoki var. violascens, commonly known as the violet-stemmed bluestem, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often display a distinctive violet hue, particularly in the lower portions. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is often found in grassland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
