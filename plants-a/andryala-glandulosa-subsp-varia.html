Andryala glandulosa subsp. varia

Andryala glandulosa subsp.

varia, commonly known as the variable glandular andryala, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features small, alternate leaves with glandular dots along the veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is often found in humid, temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
