Asimina × peninsularis

Asimina × peninsularis, commonly known as the Florida beauty berry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Asimina triloba and Asimina obovata.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is a small deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. It features dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.
