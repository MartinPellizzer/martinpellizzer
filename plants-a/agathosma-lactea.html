Agathosma lactea

Agathosma lactea, commonly known as Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has aromatic leaves that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Agathosma lactea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
