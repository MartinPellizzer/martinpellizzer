Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp. longipilosa

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp.

longipilosa, commonly known as the long-hair bearberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, rounded leaves that have a distinctive pubescent underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and erosion control capabilities.
