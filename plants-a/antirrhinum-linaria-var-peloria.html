Antirrhinum linaria var. peloria

Antirrhinum linaria var. peloria, commonly known as the double-flowered linaria, is a variant of the common toadflax.

It is characterized by its densely packed, double blooms that resemble small, intricate flowers rather than the typical single petals. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
