Astragalus croaticus

Astragalus croaticus, commonly known as the Croatian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
