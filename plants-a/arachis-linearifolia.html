Arachis linearifolia

Arachis linearifolia, commonly known as the slender groundnut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its linear, leafy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces unique, elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in various soil conditions.

Arachis linearifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
