Anagallis arvensis var. caerulea

Anagallis arvensis var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue devil or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
