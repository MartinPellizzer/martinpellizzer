Acis valentina

Acis valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's acis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, and it often grows in rocky or gravelly soils.

Acis valentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
