Atheropogon juncifolius

Atheropogon juncifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Atheropogon juncifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
