Aechmea novoae

Aechmea novoae, commonly known as the New Guinea pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the humid rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically green with a waxy texture, and it produces showy inflorescences that range from yellow to red. Aechmea novoae thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
