Acacia pentaptera

Acacia pentaptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often narrow and slightly curved. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia pentaptera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and resilient.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
