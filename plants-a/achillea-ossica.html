Achillea ossica

Achillea ossica, commonly known as the alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are finely divided and gray-green in color, contributing to its ornamental value.

Achillea ossica is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
