Annona manirote

Annona manirote, commonly known as the Manirote custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, smooth skin and a creamy, sweet pulp that resembles custard. The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
