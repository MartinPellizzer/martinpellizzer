Austromyrtus lucida

Austromyrtus lucida, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, typically pink or red in color. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

Austromyrtus lucida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
