Ageratum platylepis

Ageratum platylepis, commonly known as the broadleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically broad and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ageratum platylepis is often cultivated as a garden plant for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
