Ayenia euphrasiifolia

Ayenia euphrasiifolia, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ayenia euphrasiifolia is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in native gardens.
