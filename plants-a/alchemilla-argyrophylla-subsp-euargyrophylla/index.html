Alchemilla Argyrophylla Subsp. Euargyrophylla

Alchemilla argyrophylla subsp. euargyrophylla is a unique member of the Rosaceae family known for its striking silvery foliage. Native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, this plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its delicate, clustered flowers appear in early summer, adding a touch of soft pink to the landscape. Often referred to as the silver-leaved alchemilla, it is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation. This subspecies is a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
