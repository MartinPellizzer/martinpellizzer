Aulomyrcia suaveolens

Aulomyrcia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its aromatic oils, particularly found in the leaves and flowers, are responsible for its distinctive sweet scent.

Aulomyrcia suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
