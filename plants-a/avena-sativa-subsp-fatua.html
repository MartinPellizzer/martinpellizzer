Avena sativa subsp. fatua

Avena sativa subsp.

fatua, commonly known as wild oat, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Wild oat is an annual plant that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields.

It is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
