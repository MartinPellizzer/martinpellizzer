Aronia arbutifolia

Aronia arbutifolia, commonly known as red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red to black in color, which are edible but have a tart, slightly astringent flavor. Aronia arbutifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential health benefits due to its high antioxidant content.
