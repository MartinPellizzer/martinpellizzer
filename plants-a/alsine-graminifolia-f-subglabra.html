Alsine graminifolia f. subglabra

Alsine graminifolia f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth alsike grass, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage.

It typically features narrow, linear leaves that are smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
