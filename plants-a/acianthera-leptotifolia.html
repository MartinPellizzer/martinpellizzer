Acianthera leptotifolia

Acianthera leptotifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate orchid flowers in shades of white and pink. It is often found growing in forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
