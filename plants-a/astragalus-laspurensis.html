Astragalus laspurensis

Astragalus laspurensis, commonly known as the Lasprune milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in its native habitat.
