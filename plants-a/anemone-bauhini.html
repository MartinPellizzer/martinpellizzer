Anemone bauhini

Anemone bauhini, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, pompon-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Anemone bauhini is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
