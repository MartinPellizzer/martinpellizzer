Andropogon sorghum subvar. leiocladus

Andropogon sorghum subvar. leiocladus, commonly known as eastern gamagrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often pale green to brown in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subvariety is valued for its role in ecological restoration and wildlife habitat due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide food for various animals.
