Aplectrum nodosum

Aplectrum nodosum, commonly known as the nodding lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a tall, flowering spike that bears clusters of small, white, nodding flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

Aplectrum nodosum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in the eastern United States.
