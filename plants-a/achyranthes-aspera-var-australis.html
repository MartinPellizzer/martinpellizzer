Achyranthes aspera var. australis

Achyranthes aspera var. australis, commonly known as the Australian knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Due to its hardy nature, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
