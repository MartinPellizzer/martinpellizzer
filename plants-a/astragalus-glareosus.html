Astragalus glareosus

Astragalus glareosus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus glareosus is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
