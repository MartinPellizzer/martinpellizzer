Agropyron coxii

Agropyron coxii, commonly known as the wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions. Agropyron coxii is often used in forage production and is also cultivated for its nutritional value in human diets.
