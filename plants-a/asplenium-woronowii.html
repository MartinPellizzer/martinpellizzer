Asplenium woronowii

Asplenium woronowii, commonly known as the Hairy Shield Fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, densely packed fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its fronds are dark green with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Asplenium woronowii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
