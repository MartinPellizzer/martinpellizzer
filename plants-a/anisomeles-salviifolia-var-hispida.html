Anisomeles salviifolia var. hispida

Anisomeles salviifolia var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy leaf anisomeles, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and indoor plant collections.
