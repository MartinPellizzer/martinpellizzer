Aleuritia bellidifolia

Aleuritia bellidifolia, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Aleuritia bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland habitats.
