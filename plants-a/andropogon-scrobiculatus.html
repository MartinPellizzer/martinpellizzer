Andropogon scrobiculatus

Andropogon scrobiculatus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of hair. This grass thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil.

Its flowering spikes are typically green or purplish and appear in late spring to early summer.
