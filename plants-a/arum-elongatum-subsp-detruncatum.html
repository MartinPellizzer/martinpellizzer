Arum elongatum subsp. detruncatum

Arum elongatum subsp.

detruncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or wetlands. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a unique reproductive structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
