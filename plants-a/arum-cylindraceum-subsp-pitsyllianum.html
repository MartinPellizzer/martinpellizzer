Arum cylindraceum subsp. pitsyllianum

Arum cylindraceum subsp.

pitsyllianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, often with a waxy texture, while the spathe is usually greenish-white in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
