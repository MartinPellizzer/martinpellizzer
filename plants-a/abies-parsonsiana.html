Abies parsonsiana

Abies parsonsiana, commonly known as the Parson's fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The cones of this species are typically pendulous, maturing to a reddish-brown color, and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
