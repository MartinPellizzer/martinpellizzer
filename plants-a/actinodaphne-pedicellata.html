Actinodaphne pedicellata

Actinodaphne pedicellata, commonly known as the hairy-leafed laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, fleshy berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
