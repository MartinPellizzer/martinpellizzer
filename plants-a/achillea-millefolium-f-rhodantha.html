Achillea millefolium f. rhodantha

Achillea millefolium f. rhodantha, commonly known as the red flowering yarrow, is a horticultural form of the common yarrow.

It is characterized by its striking red to pink flower heads, which contrast with its silver-green foliage. This cultivar is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features feathery, finely divided leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
