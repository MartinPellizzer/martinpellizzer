Aragallus uralensis

Aragallus uralensis, commonly known as the Ural bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Aragallus uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
