Anacharis

Anacharis, commonly known as waterweed, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the Hydrocharitaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can grow rapidly in water, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The leaves are typically whorled, with narrow, grass-like blades that help in photosynthesis. Anacharis is native to South America but has been introduced to many regions worldwide, often becoming invasive.

It thrives in freshwater environments and is widely used in aquariums and ponds for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
