Andropogon thwaitesii

Andropogon thwaitesii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. The plant has slender, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clumping growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.
