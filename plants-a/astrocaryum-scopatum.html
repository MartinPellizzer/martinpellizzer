Astrocaryum scopatum

Astrocaryum scopatum, commonly known as the yellow palm or Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in tropical ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in sustainable forestry.
