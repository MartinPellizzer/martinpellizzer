Astragalus schizostegius

Astragalus schizostegius, commonly known as the split-stem milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Astragalus schizostegius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
