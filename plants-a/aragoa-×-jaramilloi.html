Aragoa × jaramilloi

Aragoa × jaramilloi, commonly known as the hybrid aragoa, is a unique plant resulting from the crossbreeding of two species within the Aragoa genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a mix of colors. The plant is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Aragoa × jaramilloi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
