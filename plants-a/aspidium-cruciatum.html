Aspidium cruciatum

Aspidium cruciatum, commonly known as the cross fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that are often divided into narrow segments resembling a cross. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium cruciatum is native to various regions in the Americas and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
