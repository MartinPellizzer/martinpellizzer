Astragalus archibaldii

Astragalus archibaldii, commonly known as the Archibald's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the United States, specifically found in the Great Basin and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

Astragalus archibaldii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
