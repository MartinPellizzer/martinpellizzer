Astragalus flexuosus var. flexuosus

Astragalus flexuosus var. flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its roots are often deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus flexuosus var. flexuosus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
