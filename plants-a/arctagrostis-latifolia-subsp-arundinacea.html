Arctagrostis latifolia subsp. arundinacea

Arctagrostis latifolia subsp.

arundinacea, commonly known as the broadleaf bluegrass, is a grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
