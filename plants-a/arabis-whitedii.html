Arabis whitedii

Arabis whitedii, commonly known as the White's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Arabis whitedii is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinct four-petaled flowers.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and disturbed areas.
