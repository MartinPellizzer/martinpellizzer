Arenaria schimperi

Arenaria schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Arenaria schimperi is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
