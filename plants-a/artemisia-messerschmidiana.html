Artemisia messerschmidiana

Artemisia messerschmidiana, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in loose panicles. Artemisia messerschmidiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
