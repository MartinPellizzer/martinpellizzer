Amygdalus koelzii

Amygdalus koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz almond, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell, similar to other almond species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
