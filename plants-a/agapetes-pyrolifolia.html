Agapetes pyrolifolia

Agapetes pyrolifolia, commonly known as the snowbell tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere, particularly in South America. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically evergreen, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Agapetes pyrolifolia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous areas.
