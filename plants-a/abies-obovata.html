Abies obovata

Abies obovata, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Abies obovata is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
