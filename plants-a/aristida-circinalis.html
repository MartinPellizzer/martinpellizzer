Aristida circinalis

Aristida circinalis, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, hair-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, grass-like flowers.

Aristida circinalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
