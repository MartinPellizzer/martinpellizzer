Artemisia cooleyae

Artemisia cooleyae, commonly known as Cooley's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains volatile oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
