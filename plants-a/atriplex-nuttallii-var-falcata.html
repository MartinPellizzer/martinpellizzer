Atriplex nuttallii var. falcata

Atriplex nuttallii var. falcata, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed bracts. It is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing forage for wildlife.
