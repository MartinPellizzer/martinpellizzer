Aletris revoluta

Aletris revoluta, commonly known as the mountain lily or red mountain lily, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in shaded forest environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly and form dense clumps.
