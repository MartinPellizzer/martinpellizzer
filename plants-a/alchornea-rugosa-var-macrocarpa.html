Alchornea rugosa var. macrocarpa

Alchornea rugosa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the roughleaf alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
