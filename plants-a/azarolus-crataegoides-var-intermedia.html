Azarolus crataegoides var. intermedia

Azarolus crataegoides var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by small, red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
