Astragalus xiphidioides

Astragalus xiphidioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is typically fibrous and deep-reaching.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
