Acacia tumida var. pilbarensis

Acacia tumida var. pilbarensis, commonly known as the Pilbara acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a deep root system.

The species is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and as a source of traditional materials by Indigenous communities.
