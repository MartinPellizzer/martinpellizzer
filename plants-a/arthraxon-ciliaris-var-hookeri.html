Arthraxon ciliaris var. hookeri

Arthraxon ciliaris var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded environments.
