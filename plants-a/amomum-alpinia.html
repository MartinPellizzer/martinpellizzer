Amomum alpinia

Amomum alpinia, commonly known as the greater galangal, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers and has broad, sword-shaped leaves. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

Amomum alpinia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
