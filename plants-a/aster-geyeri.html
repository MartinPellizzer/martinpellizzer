Aster geyeri

Aster geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers.

Aster geyeri is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
