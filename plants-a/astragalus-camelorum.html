Astragalus camelorum

Astragalus camelorum, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance is notable in desert ecosystems where it contributes to biodiversity and habitat structure.
