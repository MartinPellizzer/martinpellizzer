Astragalus pehuenches

Astragalus pehuenches, commonly known as the Andean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky and arid conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access nutrients in poor soils.

Astragalus pehuenches is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally by indigenous communities for its purported health benefits.
