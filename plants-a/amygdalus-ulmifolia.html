Amygdalus ulmifolia

Amygdalus ulmifolia, commonly known as the almondleaf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of elm trees. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, almond-like seeds. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, red or orange fruits that ripen in late summer.

Amygdalus ulmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
