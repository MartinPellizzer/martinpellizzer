Abutilon umbellatum subsp. occidentalis

Abutilon umbellatum subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its showy, lantern-shaped bracts that range in color from yellow to orange, which are not actually flowers but modified leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by the distinctive bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various climate conditions and its ability to attract pollinators.
