Acacia × semibinervia

Acacia × semibinervia, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing some tolerance to salt spray.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
