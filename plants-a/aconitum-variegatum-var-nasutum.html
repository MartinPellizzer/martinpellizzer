Aconitum variegatum var. nasutum

Aconitum variegatum var. nasutum, commonly known as the variable aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple spires of showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for gardens with children or pets.
