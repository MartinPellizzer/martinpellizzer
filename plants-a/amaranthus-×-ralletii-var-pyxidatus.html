Amaranthus × ralletii var. pyxidatus

Amaranthus × ralletii var. pyxidatus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. This species is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.

It is also cultivated as a potential crop for grain and fodder in some regions.
