Ailanthus glandulosa f. rubra

Ailanthus glandulosa f. rubra, commonly known as the red-leaf ailanthus, is a cultivar of the tree of heaven, characterized by its striking reddish foliage.

It is a deciduous tree with compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
