Aeschynomene claessensii

Aeschynomene claessensii, commonly known as the African leadplant, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pale green leaves and produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Aeschynomene claessensii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration efforts.
