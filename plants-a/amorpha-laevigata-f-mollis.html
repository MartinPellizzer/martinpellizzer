Amorpha laevigata f. mollis

Amorpha laevigata f. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf false indigo, is a variant of the false indigo plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, gray-green foliage and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, pea-like blue to purple flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, making it suitable for well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
