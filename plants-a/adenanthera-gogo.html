Adenanthera gogo

Adenanthera gogo, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Adenanthera gogo is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
