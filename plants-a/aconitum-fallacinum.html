Aconitum fallacinum

Aconitum fallacinum, commonly known as the Western Monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
