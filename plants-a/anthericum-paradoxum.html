Anthericum paradoxum

Anthericum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum paradoxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
