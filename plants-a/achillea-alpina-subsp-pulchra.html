Achillea alpina subsp. pulchra

Achillea alpina subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as alpine sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in full sun and cool climates. Its foliage consists of basal, silver-green leaves that are finely divided and aromatic.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
