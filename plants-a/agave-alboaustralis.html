Agave alboaustralis

Agave alboaustralis, commonly known as the white southern agave, is a succulent plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing white to pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Agave alboaustralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
