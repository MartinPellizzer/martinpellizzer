Androcymbium eucomoides

Androcymbium eucomoides, commonly known as the golden thimble cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in dense, yellowish to greenish spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
