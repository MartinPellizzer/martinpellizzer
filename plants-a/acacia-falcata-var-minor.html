Acacia falcata var. minor

Acacia falcata var. minor, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
