Arum giganteum

Arum giganteum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, often with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces a showy, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, which is typically colored in shades of white, yellow, or red. Arum giganteum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.

Its unique botanical structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in warm climates.
