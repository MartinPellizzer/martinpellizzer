Atrostemma riparium

Atrostemma riparium, commonly known as water hemp or river hemp, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and flooded meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, often with a reddish tinge. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in loose panicles, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by water.

Atrostemma riparium is an important component of riparian ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
