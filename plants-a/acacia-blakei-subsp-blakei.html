Acacia blakei subsp. blakei

Acacia blakei subsp.

blakei, commonly known as the Blake's wattle, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
