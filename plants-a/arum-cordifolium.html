Arum cordifolium

Arum cordifolium, commonly known as the "heart-leaved arum," is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces a striking spatulate inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its flowers are typically greenish-white, with a distinctive, often unpleasant odor that attracts pollinators such as flies.

Arum cordifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
