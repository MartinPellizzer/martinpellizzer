Amaryllis australis

Amaryllis australis, commonly known as the Australian amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing several blooms at the top. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often form a dense rosette at the base.

Amaryllis australis is well-suited for cultivation in warm climates and is popular in gardens for its vibrant and showy flowers.
