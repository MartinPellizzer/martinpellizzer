Arum guellekense

Arum guellekense, commonly known as the Guellekense arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
