Aegilops triuncialis f. nigroalbescens

Aegilops triuncialis f. nigroalbescens, commonly known as the black and white wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green leaves with white midribs, giving it its unique appearance. This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. It has been used in traditional agriculture and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant genetics and ecology.
