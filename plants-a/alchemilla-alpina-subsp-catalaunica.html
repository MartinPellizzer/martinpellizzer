Alchemilla alpina subsp. catalaunica

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
