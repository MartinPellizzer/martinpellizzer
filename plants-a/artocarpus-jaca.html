Artocarpus jaca

Artocarpus jaca, commonly known as the Java jackfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces large, spiky fruits that are typically harvested when mature. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
