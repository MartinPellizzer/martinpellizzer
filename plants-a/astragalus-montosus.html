Astragalus montosus

Astragalus montosus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, preferring full sun. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Astragalus montosus is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
