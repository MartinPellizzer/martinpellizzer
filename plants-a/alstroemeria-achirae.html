Alstroemeria achirae

Alstroemeria achirae, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a delicate, fragrant bloom that attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Alstroemeria achirae is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
