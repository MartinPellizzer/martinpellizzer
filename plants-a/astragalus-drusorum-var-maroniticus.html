Astragalus drusorum var. maroniticus

Astragalus drusorum var. maroniticus, commonly known as the Maronite milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
