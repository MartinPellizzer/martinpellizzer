Achatocarpus praecox f. obovatus

Achatocarpus praecox f. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate acacia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in yellow or pale green. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, a common feature among members of its family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is distinguished by its broad, ovate leaflets and is valued for its ornamental and possibly medicinal uses.
