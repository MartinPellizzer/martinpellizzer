Amaryllis tubiflora

Amaryllis tubiflora, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces bulbous tubers that store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and can grow quite large.

Amaryllis tubiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper watering and light conditions.
