Annona emarginata

Annona emarginata, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces large, yellowish-green fruits with a rough, waxy skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a fleshy, segmented fruit that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Annona emarginata is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
