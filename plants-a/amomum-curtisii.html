Amomum curtisii

Amomum curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the wet forests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
