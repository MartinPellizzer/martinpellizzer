Adesmia floribunda

Adesmia floribunda, commonly known as the clustered legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and beans, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

Adesmia floribunda is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ecological resilience.
