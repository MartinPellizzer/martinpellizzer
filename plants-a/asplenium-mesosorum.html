Asplenium mesosorum

Asplenium mesosorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments, often in temperate regions. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a waxy texture. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium mesosorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
