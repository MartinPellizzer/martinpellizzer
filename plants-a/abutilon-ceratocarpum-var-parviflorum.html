Abutilon ceratocarpum var. parviflorum

Abutilon ceratocarpum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its capsules are distinctive, often covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
