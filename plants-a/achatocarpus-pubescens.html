Achatocarpus pubescens

Achatocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
