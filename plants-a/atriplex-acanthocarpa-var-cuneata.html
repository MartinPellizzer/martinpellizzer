Atriplex acanthocarpa var. cuneata

Atriplex acanthocarpa var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces green to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
