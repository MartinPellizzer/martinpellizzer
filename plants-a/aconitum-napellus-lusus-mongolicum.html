Aconitum napellus lusus mongolicum

Aconitum napellus lusus mongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces a tall stem with numerous small, spurred flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it requires careful handling and is not commonly cultivated.
