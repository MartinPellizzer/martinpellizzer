Acacia gerrardi var. calvescens

Acacia gerrardi var. calvescens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry branches and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
