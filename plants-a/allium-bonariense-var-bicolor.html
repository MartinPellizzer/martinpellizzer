Allium bonariense var. bicolor

Allium bonariense var. bicolor, commonly known as the purple garlic or bicolor onion, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It features striking purple and white striped foliage, which is both ornamental and edible. The plant produces small, clustered white or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are similar to garlic in appearance but have a milder flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and versatility in both gardens and kitchens.
