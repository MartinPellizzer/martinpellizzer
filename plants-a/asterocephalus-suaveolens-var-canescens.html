Asterocephalus suaveolens var. canescens

Asterocephalus suaveolens var. canescens, commonly known as the silky asterocephalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of softly hairy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in dense, daisy-like clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowering stems are often tinged with a silvery hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
