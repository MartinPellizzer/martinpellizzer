Agave grenadina

Agave grenadina, commonly known as the red agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are used to make agave nectar and other traditional products.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
