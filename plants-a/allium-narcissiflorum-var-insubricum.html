Allium narcissiflorum var. insubricum

Allium narcissiflorum var. insubricum, commonly known as the pink garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, resembling the blossoms of daffodils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in various Asian cuisines.
