Aleuritopteris pulveracea

Aleuritopteris pulveracea, commonly known as the powdered fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a powdery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris pulveracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
