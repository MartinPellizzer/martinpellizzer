Aspalathus spicata

Aspalathus spicata, commonly known as the African red bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and bright red flowers. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Aspalathus spicata is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
