Aster longipes

Aster longipes, commonly known as the longstem aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blossoms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with elongated petals that distinguish it from other aster species.

Aster longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
