Abies pardei

Abies pardei, commonly known as the Mongolian fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Abies pardei is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude forests where it plays a vital role in the local ecosystem.
