Aronia × watsoniana

Aronia × watsoniana, commonly known as the purple chokeberry, is a hybrid shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and produces clusters of small, edible fruits in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the autumn.

Aronia × watsoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
