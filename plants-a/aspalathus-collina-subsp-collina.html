Aspalathus collina subsp. collina

Aspalathus collina subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Western Cape region.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
