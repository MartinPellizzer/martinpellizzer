Agapetes epacridea

Agapetes epacridea, commonly known as the white-flowered silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Agapetes epacridea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
