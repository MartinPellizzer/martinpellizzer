Angraecum acutum

Angraecum acutum, commonly known as the acute angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by day-flying moths, a unique adaptation that highlights its ecological niche.

Angraecum acutum is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
