Acanthopanax koreanus

Acanthopanax koreanus, commonly known as Korean angel's trumpet, is a climbing shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional Korean medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
