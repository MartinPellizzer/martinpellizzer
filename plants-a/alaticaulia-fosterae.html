Alaticaulia fosterae

Alaticaulia fosterae, commonly known as the Foster's alate caudex plant, is a rare succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, thick, and fleshy caudex that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Alaticaulia fosterae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
