Anthurium abajoense

Anthurium abajoense, commonly known as the Abajo River anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in regions like Ecuador and Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful spatula-like bracts. Anthurium abajoense thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and floral structures make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
