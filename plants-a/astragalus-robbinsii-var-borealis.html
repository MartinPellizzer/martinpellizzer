Astragalus robbinsii var. borealis

Astragalus robbinsii var. borealis, commonly known as the northern milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
