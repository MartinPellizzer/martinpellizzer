Arundinaria pygmaea

Arundinaria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, often reddish-brown stems. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it an attractive choice for rock gardens and small spaces. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to form dense clumps over time.

Arundinaria pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with partial sunlight.
