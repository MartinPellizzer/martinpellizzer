Achillea macrophylla

Achillea macrophylla, commonly known as the yarrow or giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, flat clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its healing properties.
