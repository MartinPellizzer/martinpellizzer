Aconogonon alaskanum

Aconogonon alaskanum, commonly known as Alaskan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as alpine tundras and mountainous areas. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aconogonon alaskanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
