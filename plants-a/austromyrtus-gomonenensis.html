Austromyrtus gomonenensis

Austromyrtus gomonenensis, commonly known as the Gomon Peninsula myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance.

Austromyrtus gomonenensis is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
