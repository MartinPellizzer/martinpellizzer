Allium urvillei

Allium urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the bulb is small and used in some traditional cuisines.

Allium urvillei is valued for its ornamental beauty and mild garlic-like aroma.
