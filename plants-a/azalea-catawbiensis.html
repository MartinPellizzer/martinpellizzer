Azalea catawbiensis

Azalea catawbiensis, commonly known as the Catawba azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Azalea catawbiensis is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
