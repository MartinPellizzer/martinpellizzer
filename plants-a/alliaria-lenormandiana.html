Alliaria lenormandiana

Alliaria lenormandiana, commonly known as winter garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and small, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other members of the mustard family.
