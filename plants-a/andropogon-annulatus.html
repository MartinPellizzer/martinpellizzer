Andropogon annulatus

Andropogon annulatus, commonly known as the ringgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, often ring-like appearance of its leaf sheaths. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Andropogon annulatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
