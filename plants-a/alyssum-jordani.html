Alyssum jordani

Alyssum jordani, commonly known as Jordan's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Alyssum jordani is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
