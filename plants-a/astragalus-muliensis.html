Astragalus muliensis

Astragalus muliensis, commonly known as the Muli milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal value, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
