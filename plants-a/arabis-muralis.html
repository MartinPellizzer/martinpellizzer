Arabis muralis

Arabis muralis, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found along walls and in crevices. Its flowers are small, four-petaled, and come in shades of yellow, white, and red.

Arabis muralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
