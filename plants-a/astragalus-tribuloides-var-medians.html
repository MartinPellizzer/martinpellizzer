Astragalus tribuloides var. medians

Astragalus tribuloides var. medians, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought. It is often found in steppe and desert scrub habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.
