Amyema muelleri

Amyema muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host tree's bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds. Its leaves are opposite and oval, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Amyema muelleri plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
