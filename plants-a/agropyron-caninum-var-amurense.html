Agropyron caninum var. amurense

Agropyron caninum var. amurense, commonly known as Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage crop for livestock.
