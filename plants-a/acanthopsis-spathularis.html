Acanthopsis spathularis

Acanthopsis spathularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf acanthus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or purple. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.

The plant's structure includes opposite leaves and bract-like calyces that surround the flowers, contributing to its visual appeal.
