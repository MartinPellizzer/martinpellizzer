Antimima viatorum

Antimima viatorum, commonly known as the wandering ghostplant, is a rare and elusive species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, translucent leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces tiny, pale flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting specific pollinators. Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered endangered.

Its botanical study offers insights into plant adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
