Actinodaphne glauca

Actinodaphne glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
