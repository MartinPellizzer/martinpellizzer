Alsophila robusta

Alsophila robusta, commonly known as the robust tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Alsophila robusta is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
