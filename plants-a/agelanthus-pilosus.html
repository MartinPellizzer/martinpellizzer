Agelanthus pilosus

Agelanthus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy agelanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant's structure includes a short, woody stem with alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Agelanthus pilosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
