Alkanna syriaca

Alkanna syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian rouge or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-red roots, which are the primary source of its medicinal and dyeing properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Alkanna syriaca thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.
