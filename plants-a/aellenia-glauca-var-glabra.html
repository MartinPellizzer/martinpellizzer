Aellenia glauca var. glabra

Aellenia glauca var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth blueberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, smooth, blue-black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually grayish-green in color and covered with a fine, waxy bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local food systems.
