Acacia tratmaniana

Acacia tratmaniana, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native region.

Acacia tratmaniana is often used in local landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
