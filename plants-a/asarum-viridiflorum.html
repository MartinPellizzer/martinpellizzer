Asarum viridiflorum

Asarum viridiflorum, commonly known as green-flowered wild betony, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a unique, aromatic scent that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
