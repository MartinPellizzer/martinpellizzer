Allium macrum

Allium macrum, commonly known as the giant garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces large, spherical umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a strong garlic flavor, often used in cooking.

Allium macrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility in gardens and cuisines.
