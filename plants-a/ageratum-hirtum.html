Ageratum hirtum

Ageratum hirtum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to lavender flowers. The plant is often grown as an ornamental in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators. Ageratum hirtum thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

It is typically propagated from seeds or cuttings and is a popular choice for bedding plants.
