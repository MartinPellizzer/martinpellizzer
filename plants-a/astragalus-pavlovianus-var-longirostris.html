Astragalus pavlovianus var. longirostris

Astragalus pavlovianus var. longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
