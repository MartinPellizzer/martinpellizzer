Arundinaria pauciflora

Arundinaria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria pauciflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
