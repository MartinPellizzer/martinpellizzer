Annona barteri

Annona barteri, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It produces large, yellow to green fruits with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit contains a white, creamy pulp that is sweet and aromatic, often used in local cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Annonaceae family and has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
