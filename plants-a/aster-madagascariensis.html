Aster madagascariensis

Aster madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar aster, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and showy blue to violet ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
