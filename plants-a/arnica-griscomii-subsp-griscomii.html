Arnica griscomii subsp. griscomii

Arnica griscomii subsp.

griscomii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill arnica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and is often found in high elevation environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
