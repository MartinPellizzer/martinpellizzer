Agoseris heterophylla subsp. californica

Agoseris heterophylla subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California goldenstar, is a perennial herb native to California. It features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, star-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
