Anemone apennina var. parvula

Anemone apennina var. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are typically lobed and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and early spring blooming make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
