Asplenium myriophyllum var. majus

Asplenium myriophyllum var. majus, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern genus characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a delicate, feathery appearance with intricate leaf structures that resemble flowing ribbons. Its fronds are typically dark green and can develop a reddish hue when exposed to bright light.

Asplenium myriophyllum var. majus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
