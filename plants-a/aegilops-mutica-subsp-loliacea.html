Aegilops mutica subsp.

loliacea, commonly known as the woolly wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, and it produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers. Aegilops mutica subsp.

loliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
