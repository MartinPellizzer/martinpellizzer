Amberboa moschata

Amberboa moschata, commonly known as the musk melon, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to oval, orange-fleshed fruits with a fragrant, musky aroma. Its fruit is rich in vitamins A and C, making it a valuable crop in tropical and subtropical regions.

Amberboa moschata thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and fruit development.
