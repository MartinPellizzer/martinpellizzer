Acacia retinodes

Acacia retinodes, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Acacia retinodes is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments.
