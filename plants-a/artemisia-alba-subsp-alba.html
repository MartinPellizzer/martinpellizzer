Artemisia alba subsp. alba

Artemisia alba subsp.

alba, commonly known as white mugwort, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The plant features silvery-gray, aromatic leaves that are finely divided and covered in dense, white hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
