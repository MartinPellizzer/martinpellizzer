Amsinckia lancifolia

Amsinckia lancifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its fibrous root system helps it retain moisture in dry conditions, making it well adapted to its native range.

Amsinckia lancifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
