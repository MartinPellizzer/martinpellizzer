Aspidium drepanopteron

Aspidium drepanopteron, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions across Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spores rather than seeds.

Aspidium drepanopteron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
