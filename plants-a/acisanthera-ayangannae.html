Acisanthera ayangannae

Acisanthera ayangannae, commonly known as the Ayangannae sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring robust, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to thrive in poor soils and high sunlight conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
