Asparagus aculeatus

Asparagus aculeatus, commonly known as prickly asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that are covered in small, sharp spines. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
