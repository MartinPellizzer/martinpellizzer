Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp. cassia

Arenaria serpyllifolia subsp.

cassia, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

It is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats, such as roadsides and waste areas.
