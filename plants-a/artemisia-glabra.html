Artemisia glabra

Artemisia glabra, commonly known as white mugwort, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features silver-gray, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Artemisia glabra is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
