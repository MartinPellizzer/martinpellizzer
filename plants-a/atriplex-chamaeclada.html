Atriplex chamaeclada

Atriplex chamaeclada, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable component of desert ecosystems.

Atriplex chamaeclada plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
