Amaryllis bivaginata

Amaryllis bivaginata, commonly known as the red-eyed wonder, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Amaryllis bivaginata is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value makes it a favorite among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
