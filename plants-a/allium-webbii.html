Allium webbii

Allium webbii, commonly known as the Webb's garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features bulbous roots and produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Allium webbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
