Alocasia warburgii

Alocasia warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and high humidity levels.

Due to its striking foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
