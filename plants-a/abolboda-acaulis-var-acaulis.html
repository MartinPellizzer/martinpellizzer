Abolboda acaulis var. acaulis

Abolboda acaulis var. acaulis, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and lacks a distinct stem, with leaves arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in clusters at the base of the rosette. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
