Atriplex patula subsp. producta

Atriplex patula subsp.

producta, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in the spring. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
