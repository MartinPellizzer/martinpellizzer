Aster longifolius var. albiflorus

Aster longifolius var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
