Ageratina aromatica var. incisa

Ageratina aromatica var. incisa, commonly known as the fragrant blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
