Astracantha unguiculata

Astracantha unguiculata, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to blue flowers arranged in a cluster, with distinctive hooked bracts that give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Astracantha unguiculata is often found in shrublands and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
