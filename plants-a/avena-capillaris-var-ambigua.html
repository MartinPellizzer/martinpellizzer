Avena capillaris var. ambigua

Avena capillaris var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous oat, is a variant of the slender oat, characterized by its distinctive morphological features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to certain regions of North America, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with delicate, awnless glumes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it has fibrous roots that aid in soil stabilization.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic variability and adaptability to different ecological conditions.
