Anagallis wellsiana

Anagallis wellsiana, commonly known as the slender pinkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Anagallis wellsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
