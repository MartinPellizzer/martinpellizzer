Ailanthus flavescens

Ailanthus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow fringe tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that appear in pendulous clusters during the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
