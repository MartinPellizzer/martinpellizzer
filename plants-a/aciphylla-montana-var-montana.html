Aciphylla montana var. montana

Aciphylla montana var. montana, commonly known as the mountain aciphylla, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
