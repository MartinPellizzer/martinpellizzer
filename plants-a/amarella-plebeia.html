Amarella plebeia

Amarella plebeia, commonly known as the common amarella, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly serrated edge. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Amarella plebeia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
