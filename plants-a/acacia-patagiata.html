Acacia patagiata

Acacia patagiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, phyllodes that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small yellow flowers clustered in globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia patagiata is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and a capacity for resprouting after fire or grazing.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
