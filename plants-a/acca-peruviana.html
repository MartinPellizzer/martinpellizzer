Acca peruviana

Acca peruviana, commonly known as the pear granite or cupuacu, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces large, edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique, slightly tart flavor. Its botanical features include a woody stem, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
