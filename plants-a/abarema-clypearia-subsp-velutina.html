Abarema clypearia subsp. velutina

Abarema clypearia subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to South America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine. Its hairy, velvety foliage gives it its common name and contributes to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
