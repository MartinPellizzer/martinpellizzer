Adenophora triphylla var. kurilensis

Adenophora triphylla var. kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula and the Russian Far East.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
