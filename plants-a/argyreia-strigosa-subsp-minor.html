Argyreia strigosa subsp. minor

Argyreia strigosa subsp.

minor, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces berries that range in color from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
