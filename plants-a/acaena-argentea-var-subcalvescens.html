Acaena argentea var. subcalvescens

Acaena argentea var. subcalvescens, commonly known as silver burr or silver weed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, spiky burrs that can adhere to clothing and animal fur, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a resilient weed and a subject of ecological study.
