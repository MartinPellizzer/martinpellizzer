Andropogon foulkesii

Andropogon foulkesii, commonly known as Florida panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to swampy conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, white to purplish flowers during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed areas and can tolerate a variety of soil types.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to wet environments.
