Amaryllis crociflora

Amaryllis crociflora, commonly known as the crocus amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and has a bulbous stem that stores nutrients for flowering. Its flowers typically appear in spring and are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Amaryllis crociflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
