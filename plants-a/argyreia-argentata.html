Argyreia argentata

Argyreia argentata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It is a climbing plant that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
