Achillea fraasii var. fraasii

Achillea fraasii var. fraasii, commonly known as the Fraas' yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.
