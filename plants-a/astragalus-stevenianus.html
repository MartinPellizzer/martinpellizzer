Astragalus stevenianus

Astragalus stevenianus, commonly known as Steven's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied for potential health benefits.
