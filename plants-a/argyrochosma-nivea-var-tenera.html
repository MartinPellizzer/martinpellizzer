Argyrochosma nivea var. tenera

Argyrochosma nivea var. tenera, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months, typically in June and July. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
