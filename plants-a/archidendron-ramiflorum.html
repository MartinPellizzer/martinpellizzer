Archidendron ramiflorum

Archidendron ramiflorum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling flames, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
