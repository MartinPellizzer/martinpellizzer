Adelia phillyreoides

Adelia phillyreoides, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a dense mat of soft, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in poor soil.

Adelia phillyreoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
