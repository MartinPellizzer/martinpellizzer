Agapanthus coddii

Agapanthus coddii, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Agapanthus coddii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
