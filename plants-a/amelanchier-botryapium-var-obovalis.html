Amelanchier botryapium var. obovalis

Amelanchier botryapium var. obovalis, commonly known as the oval serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red-purple berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
