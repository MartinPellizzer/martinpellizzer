Achnatherum confusum

Achnatherum confusum, commonly known as the confused needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.
