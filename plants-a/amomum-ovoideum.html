Amomum ovoideum

Amomum ovoideum, commonly known as the oval cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers followed by elliptical, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of A. ovoideum make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
