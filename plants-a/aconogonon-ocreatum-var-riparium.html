Aconogonon ocreatum var. riparium

Aconogonon ocreatum var. riparium, commonly known as the river waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found along riverbanks and pond edges. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive sheath-like structure around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually with three stamens and a single pistil.

This species plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
