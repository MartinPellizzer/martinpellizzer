Agave brittoniana subsp. sancti-spirituensis

Agave brittoniana subsp.

sancti-spirituensis, commonly known as the Santa Spirito agave, is a succulent plant native to the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
