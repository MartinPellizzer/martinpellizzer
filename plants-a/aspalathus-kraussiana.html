Aspalathus kraussiana

Aspalathus kraussiana, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to the arid climate, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. Its scientific name, Aspalathus, refers to its habitat in the fynbos vegetation, while "kraussiana" honors the botanist who first described it.

The plant is most famous for its leaves, which are used to produce the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.
