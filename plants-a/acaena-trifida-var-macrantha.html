Acaena trifida var. macrantha

Acaena trifida var. macrantha, commonly known as the New Zealand spinachberry, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant produces edible, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
