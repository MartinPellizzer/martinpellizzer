Acalypha arciana

Acalypha arciana, commonly known as the silky fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its unique, fern-like leaves and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
