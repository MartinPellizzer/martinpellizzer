Athyrium symmetricum

Athyrium symmetricum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It features delicate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive bipinnate structure, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spore sacs on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for reproduction. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
