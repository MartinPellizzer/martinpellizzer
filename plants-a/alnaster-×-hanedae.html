Alnaster × hanedae

Alnaster × hanedae, commonly known as the Japanese alnus, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Alnaster × hanedae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
