Allium bidentatum var. qinggouense

Allium bidentatum var. qinggouense, commonly known as the Qinggou onion, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features bulbous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines for their mild, onion-like flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid environments.
