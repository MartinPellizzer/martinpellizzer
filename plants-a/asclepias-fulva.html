Asclepias fulva

Asclepias fulva, commonly known as the orange milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers and is part of the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Asclepias fulva serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly and other pollinators, playing an important role in local ecosystems.
