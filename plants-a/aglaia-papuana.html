Aglaia papuana

Aglaia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other economically important hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Aglaia papuana is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
