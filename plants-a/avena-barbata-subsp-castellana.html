Avena barbata subsp. castellana

Avena barbata subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that aids in moisture retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
