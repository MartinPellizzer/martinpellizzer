Anisacantha tricuspis

Anisacantha tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed burr.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive, spiny, three-lobed fruit that detaches from the plant and rolls across the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its unique, hook-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrublands and coastal areas.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and is commonly used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.
