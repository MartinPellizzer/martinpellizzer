Artanthe oblongata

Artanthe oblongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Artanthe oblongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
