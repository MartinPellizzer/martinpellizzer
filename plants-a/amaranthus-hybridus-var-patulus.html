Amaranthus hybridus var. patulus

Amaranthus hybridus var. patulus, commonly known as the tree amaranth, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its tall, branching structure and vibrant, feather-like leaves.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to the southern United States, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked in favor of its showy foliage.

It is valued for its ability to add height and texture to gardens, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
