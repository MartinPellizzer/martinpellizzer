Asclepias alexifarmacas

Asclepias alexifarmacas, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the dogbane family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Asclepias alexifarmacas is a vital host plant for monarch butterflies, providing food for their larvae.
