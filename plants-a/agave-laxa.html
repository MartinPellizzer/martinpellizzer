Agave laxa

Agave laxa, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps the plant conserve water in arid environments. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears white or pale green flowers, which are typically short-lived.

Agave laxa is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
