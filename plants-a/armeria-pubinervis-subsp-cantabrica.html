Armeria pubinervis subsp. cantabrica

Armeria pubinervis subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense, compact clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has linear, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
