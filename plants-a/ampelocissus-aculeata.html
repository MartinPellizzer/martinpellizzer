Ampelocissus aculeata

Ampelocissus aculeata, commonly known as the Natal fig or goat's rue, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its thorny stems and adaptability make it a resilient species in various ecological settings.
