Alpinia corallina

Alpinia corallina, commonly known as the coral ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking orange-red bracts that resemble coral, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alpinia corallina is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
