Adhatoda scabra

Adhatoda scabra, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Adhatoda scabra is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its bronchodilatory and antitussive effects.
