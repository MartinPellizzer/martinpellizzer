Agropyron novae-angliae

Agropyron novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Agropyron novae-angliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
