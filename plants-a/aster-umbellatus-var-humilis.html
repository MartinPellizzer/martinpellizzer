Aster umbellatus var. humilis

Aster umbellatus var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that typically reach heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.
