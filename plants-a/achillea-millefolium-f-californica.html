Achillea millefolium f. californica

Achillea millefolium f. californica, commonly known as the California yarrow, is a variant of the common yarrow plant.

It is a perennial herb native to California, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal slopes. It has a compact, low-growing habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

The foliage and flowers are often used in herbal remedies and traditional medicine.
