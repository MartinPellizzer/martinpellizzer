Aspalathus argentea

Aspalathus argentea, commonly known as silver bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Aspalathus argentea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
