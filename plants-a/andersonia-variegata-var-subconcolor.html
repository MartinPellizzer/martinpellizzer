Andersonia variegata var. subconcolor

Andersonia variegata var. subconcolor, commonly known as the variegated Andersonia, is a striking alpine plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps it conserve moisture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
