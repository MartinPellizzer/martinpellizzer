Arctium pygmaeum

Arctium pygmaeum, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces and produces small, spiky burrs that aid in seed dispersal. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

The plant's medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
