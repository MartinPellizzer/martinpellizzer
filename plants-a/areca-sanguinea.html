Areca sanguinea

Areca sanguinea, commonly known as the red areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Areca sanguinea thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight to grow optimally.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a significant species in its native regions.
