Astragalus pannonicus

Astragalus pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various soil conditions. Its root system is known for its deep penetration, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Astragalus pannonicus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
