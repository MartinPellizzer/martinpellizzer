Abelia onkocarpa

Abelia onkocarpa, commonly known as the African abelia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. These flowers are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Abelia onkocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
