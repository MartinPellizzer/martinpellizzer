Acacia semiverticillata

Acacia semiverticillata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often narrow and arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

Acacia semiverticillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
