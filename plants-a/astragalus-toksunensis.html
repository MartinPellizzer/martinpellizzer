Astragalus toksunensis

Astragalus toksunensis, commonly known as Toksun milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its seeds are often harvested for forage or medicinal purposes.
