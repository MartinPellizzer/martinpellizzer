Aphyllon uniflorum var. occidentale

Aphyllon uniflorum var. occidentale, commonly known as the western single-flower aphyllon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage, with only a single, showy flower per stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, fibrous root system and exhibits a clump-forming growth habit. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, with five petals and a distinct central stamen.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.
