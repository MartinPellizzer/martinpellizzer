Acaena depressa

Acaena depressa, commonly known as the cushion weed, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are dry, achene-like structures that are dispersed by wind.

Acaena depressa is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
