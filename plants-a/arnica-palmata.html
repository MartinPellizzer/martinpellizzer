Arnica palmata

Arnica palmata, commonly known as narrowleaf arnica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Arnica palmata is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
