Anthyllis vulneraria var. macedonica

Anthyllis vulneraria var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
