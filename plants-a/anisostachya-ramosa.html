Anisostachya ramosa

Anisostachya ramosa, commonly known as the slender reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Anisostachya ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
