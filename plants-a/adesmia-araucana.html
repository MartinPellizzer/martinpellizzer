Adesmia araucana

Adesmia araucana, commonly known as the Araucanian burro's tail, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, cascading clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Adesmia araucana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
