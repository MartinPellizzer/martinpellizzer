Ageratina parviceps

Ageratina parviceps, commonly known as the small-headed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its small, blue to purple flower heads are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Ageratina parviceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
