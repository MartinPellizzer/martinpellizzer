Abarema filamentosa

Abarema filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically woody and can grow in shrub-like forms, adapting to various soil conditions.

Abarema filamentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
