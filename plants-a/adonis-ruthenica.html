Adonis ruthenica

Adonis ruthenica, commonly known as the Caucasian Adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered rare in the wild.
