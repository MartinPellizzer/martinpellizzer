Agrostis elliottiana f. molesta

Agrostis elliottiana f. molesta, commonly known as Elliott's bentgrass, is a variant of the species Agrostis elliottiana.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass that typically forms dense mats on well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted shoots and fine, grass-like leaves. It is native to the southeastern United States and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
