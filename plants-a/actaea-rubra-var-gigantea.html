Actaea rubra var. gigantea

Actaea rubra var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant red baneberry, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy clusters of white flowers that transition into bright red berries in the fall. The plant has compound, pinnate leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often found in woodland habitats.
