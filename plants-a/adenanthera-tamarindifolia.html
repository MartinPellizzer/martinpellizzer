Adenanthera tamarindifolia

Adenanthera tamarindifolia, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Its ability to thrive in poor soils makes it an important species for reforestation and soil stabilization.
