Agrostis domingensis

Agrostis domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean region, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and disturbed sites. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Agrostis domingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
