Aster umbellatus f. convexus

Aster umbellatus f. convexus, commonly known as the convex aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive convex-shaped flower heads, which give it a unique appearance compared to other aster species. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and they bloom in the late summer to early autumn.

This form is a natural variant of Aster umbellatus, showing slight morphological differences in its inflorescence structure.
