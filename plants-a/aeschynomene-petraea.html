Aeschynomene petraea

Aeschynomene petraea, commonly known as rock aeschnomene, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Aeschynomene petraea is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
