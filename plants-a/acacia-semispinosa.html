Acacia semispinosa

Acacia semispinosa, commonly known as the spinifex acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by hard, woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Acacia semispinosa plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
