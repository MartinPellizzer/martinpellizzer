Acacia berlandieri

Acacia berlandieri, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

Acacia berlandieri is also valued for its role in providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
