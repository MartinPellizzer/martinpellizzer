Achillea pauciflora

Achillea pauciflora, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and alpine environments. The plant features a bushy growth habit with dark green, feather-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are typically arranged in flat-topped umbels, and the plant is known for its aromatic, slightly medicinal properties.

Achillea pauciflora is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh conditions.
