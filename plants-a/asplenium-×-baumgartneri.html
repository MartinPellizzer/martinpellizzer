Asplenium × baumgartneri

Asplenium × baumgartneri, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure typical of the Asplenium genus. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in small, spore-producing structures called sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is not typically used for propagation and is more valued for its ornamental appearance.
