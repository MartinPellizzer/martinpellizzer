Abrotanella inconspicua

Abrotanella inconspicua, commonly known as the common sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open grasslands.

Abrotanella inconspicua is an important plant for pollinators and is considered a characteristic species of its native habitat.
