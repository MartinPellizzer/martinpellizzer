Acacia verugera var. africana

Acacia verugera var. africana, commonly known as the African acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange globular flower heads, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
