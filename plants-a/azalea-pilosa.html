Azalea pilosa

Azalea pilosa, commonly known as the roughleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of pink, red, and white. Azalea pilosa thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
