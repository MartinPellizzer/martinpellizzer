Arenaria serpyllifolia var. erecta

Arenaria serpyllifolia var. erecta, commonly known as upright sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features prostrate or ascending stems with small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are arranged in small umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in garden settings.
