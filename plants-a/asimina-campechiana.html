Asimina campechiana

Asimina campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits with a distinctive, custard-like texture.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
