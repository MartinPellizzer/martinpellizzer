Anura pallidivirens

Anura pallidivirens, commonly known as the pale-green tree frog, is a species of tree frog native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender body and distinctive pale green coloration, which provides effective camouflage in its natural habitat. The plant-like appearance of its skin and the way it clings to foliage often lead to confusion with certain vascular plants. While it is not a plant, it shares some structural similarities with certain leaf-like structures found in the plant kingdom.

This frog plays an important role in its ecosystem as both a predator and prey species.
