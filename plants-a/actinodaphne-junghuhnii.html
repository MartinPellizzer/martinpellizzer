Actinodaphne junghuhnii

Actinodaphne junghuhnii, commonly known as the Malabar camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
