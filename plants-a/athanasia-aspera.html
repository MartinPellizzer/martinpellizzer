Athanasia aspera

Athanasia aspera, commonly known as the rough athanasia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Athanasia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
