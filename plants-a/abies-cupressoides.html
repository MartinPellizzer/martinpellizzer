Abies cupressoides

Abies cupressoides, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its dense, soft, silvery-blue needles that are arranged in crossed pairs. The tree produces small, spherical cones that typically mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.

Abies cupressoides is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-elevation forests.
