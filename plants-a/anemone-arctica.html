Anemone arctica

Anemone arctica, commonly known as the Arctic anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Anemone arctica is a hardy species that plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its cold habitat.
