Aucuba japonica var. borealis

Aucuba japonica var. borealis, commonly known as the northern aucuba, is a variant of the Japanese aucuba.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub native to northern Japan and parts of eastern Asia. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow or white veins, creating a visually appealing contrast. It thrives in partial to full shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

This variety is also noted for its ability to tolerate moist, acidic soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
