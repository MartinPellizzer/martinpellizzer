Amaryllis × tortuliflora

Amaryllis × tortuliflora, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between *Amaryllis belladonna* and *Amaryllis tortulifolia*.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red, with a distinctive trumpet-shaped form. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and smooth, often reaching several feet in length. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The hybrid is notable for its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice among horticulturists.
