Allium venustum

Allium venustum, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Allium venustum is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
