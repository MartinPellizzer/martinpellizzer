Acinodendron pachyphyllum

Acinodendron pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved acinodendron, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.
