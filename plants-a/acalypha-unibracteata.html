Acalypha unibracteata

Acalypha unibracteata, commonly known as the "Chinese lantern" or "velvetleaf," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Acalypha unibracteata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, long-lasting foliage.
