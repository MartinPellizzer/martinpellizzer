Astragalus angustidens

Astragalus angustidens, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus angustidens plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
