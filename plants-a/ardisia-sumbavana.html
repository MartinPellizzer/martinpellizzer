Ardisia sumbavana

Ardisia sumbavana, commonly known as the Madagascar ardisia, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and trees adapted to acidic soils.
