Anchusa undulata subsp. hybrida

Anchusa undulata subsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
