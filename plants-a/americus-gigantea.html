Americus gigantea

Americus gigantea, commonly known as the giant American fern, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to six feet. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
