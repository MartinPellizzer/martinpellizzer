Argentina

Argentina, commonly known as the "Land of the Sun," is a country renowned for its diverse and rich botanical landscape.

It is home to a wide variety of native plants, including the iconic quebracho tree, which is valued for its durable wood and medicinal properties. The country's Andean highlands and Pampas grasslands support unique ecosystems with endemic species such as the Andean lupine and the Pampas grass. Argentina's flora also includes numerous medicinal plants used in traditional medicine, such as the yerba mate plant, which is widely consumed as a tea.

The country's biodiversity is further enhanced by its varied climates, ranging from arid regions to temperate forests.
