Asparagus volubilis

Asparagus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing asparagus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or climbing growth habit, with slender, wiry stems that can twine around supports. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by edible, bean-like berries. Its leaves are feather-like, compound, and often covered in fine hairs.

Asparagus volubilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to cover trellises or walls.
