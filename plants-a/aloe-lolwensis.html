Aloe lolwensis

Aloe lolwensis, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which bloom during the cooler months. Aloe lolwensis is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry periods.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
