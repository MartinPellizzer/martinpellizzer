Arabis glaucovalvula

Arabis glaucovalvula, commonly known as the blue-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
