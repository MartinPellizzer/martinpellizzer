Asplenium auriculatum var. aequilaterale

Asplenium auriculatum var. aequilaterale, commonly known as the three-lobed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance. The leaves are usually triangular in shape with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens as a decorative plant.
