Acicalyptus elliptica

Acicalyptus elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paperbark gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a pale, fibrous underlayer. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native environment. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal floral displays.
