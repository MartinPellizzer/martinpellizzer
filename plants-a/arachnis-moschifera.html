Arachnis moschifera

Arachnis moschifera, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, often as an epiphyte on trees. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique scent.
