Astragalus schahrudensis

Astragalus schahrudensis, commonly known as Schahrud milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry, harsh environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and medicinal studies due to its potential bioactive compounds.
