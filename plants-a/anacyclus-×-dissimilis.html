Anacyclus × dissimilis

Anacyclus × dissimilis, commonly known as the hybrid desert dandelion, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy conditions.

Anacyclus × dissimilis is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in desert landscapes.
