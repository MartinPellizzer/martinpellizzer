Astragalus coelicolor

Astragalus coelicolor, commonly known as the sky-blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus coelicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
