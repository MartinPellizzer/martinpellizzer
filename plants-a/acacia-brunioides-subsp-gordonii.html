Acacia brunioides subsp. gordonii

Acacia brunioides subsp.

gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
