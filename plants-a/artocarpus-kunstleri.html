Artocarpus kunstleri

Artocarpus kunstleri, commonly known as the Malabar breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically yellow when mature, contains a soft, starchy pulp surrounded by a fibrous outer layer. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

The tree thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
