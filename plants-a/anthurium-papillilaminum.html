Anthurium papillilaminum

Anthurium papillilaminum, commonly known as the papillilaminum anthurium, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spathe and spadix. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color, often adorned with subtle veins. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a spathe that can range in color from white to pink or red.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
