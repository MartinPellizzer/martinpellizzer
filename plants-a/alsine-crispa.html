Alsine crispa

Alsine crispa, commonly known as the curly alsike, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy or curled margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alsine crispa is an annual or biennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
