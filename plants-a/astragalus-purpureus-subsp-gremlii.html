Astragalus purpureus subsp. gremlii

Astragalus purpureus subsp.

gremlii, commonly known as the Gremli's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert grasslands.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
