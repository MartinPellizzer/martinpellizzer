Allium monadelphum var. tibeticum

Allium monadelphum var. tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan leek, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its edible leaves and potential medicinal properties.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
