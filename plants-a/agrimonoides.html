Agrimonoides

Agrimonoides, commonly known as the common agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Agrimonoides is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
