Allium ampeloprasum var. bertolonii

Allium ampeloprasum var. bertolonii, commonly known as the Tuscan leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and broad, flat leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a dense umbel. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
