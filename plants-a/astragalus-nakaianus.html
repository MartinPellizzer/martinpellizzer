Astragalus nakaianus

Astragalus nakaianus, commonly known as the New Zealand milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across mountainous regions.
