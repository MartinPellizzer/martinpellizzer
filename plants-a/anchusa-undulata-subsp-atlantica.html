Anchusa undulata subsp. atlantica

Anchusa undulata subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy and slightly woody.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
