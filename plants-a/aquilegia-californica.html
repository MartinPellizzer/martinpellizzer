Aquilegia californica

Aquilegia californica, commonly known as the California columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland and meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
