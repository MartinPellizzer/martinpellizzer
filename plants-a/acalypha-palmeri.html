Acalypha palmeri

Acalypha palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
