Abutilon macrophyllum

Abutilon macrophyllum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Abutilon macrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blossoms.
