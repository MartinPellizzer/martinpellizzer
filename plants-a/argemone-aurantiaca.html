Argemone aurantiaca

Argemone aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially hazardous to livestock and humans. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it has been cultivated in various parts of the world.
