Aristida kotschyi

Aristida kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Aristida kotschyi is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is used in some regions for forage or traditional medicine.
