Alsophila × sessilifolia

Alsophila × sessilifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Alsophila costaricana and Alsophila semipinnata.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, persistent crown of dead fronds. The plant produces large, compound fronds with a broad, feather-like appearance, which are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Alsophila × sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
