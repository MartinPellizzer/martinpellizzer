Abronia macrocarpa

Abronia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Abronia macrocarpa is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
