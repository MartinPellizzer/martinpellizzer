Amomum grana-paradisi

Amomum grana-paradisi, commonly known as the paradise grain amomum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pale green flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The botanical structure of Amomum grana-paradisi supports its role in both culinary and medicinal applications.
