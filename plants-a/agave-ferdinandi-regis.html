Agave ferdinandi-regis

Agave ferdinandi-regis, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, bluish-green leaves that are lined with sharp spines. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in tequila production, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical gardens.
