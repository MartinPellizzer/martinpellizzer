Aster claytonii var. crispicans

Aster claytonii var. crispicans, commonly known as the curlyleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
