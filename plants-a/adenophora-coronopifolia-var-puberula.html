Adenophora coronopifolia var. puberula

Adenophora coronopifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are typically adorned with a distinctive yellowish corona, giving it a unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.
