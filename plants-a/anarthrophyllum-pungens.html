Anarthrophyllum pungens

Anarthrophyllum pungens, commonly known as the spiny pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage that provides protection against herbivores and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate contrast to its tough, woody structure. Its succulent leaves store water, enabling it to survive in low-rainfall environments.

Anarthrophyllum pungens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
