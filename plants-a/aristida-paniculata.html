Aristida paniculata

Aristida paniculata, commonly known as the panicled grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces dense, panicle-like inflorescences that are often tinged with silver or white, giving it its common name. Its fine, grass-like leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aristida paniculata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
