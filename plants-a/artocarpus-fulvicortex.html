Artocarpus fulvicortex

Artocarpus fulvicortex, commonly known as the golden-corked breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that gives it its common name. The tree produces a unique fruit with a fibrous, starchy pulp that is used in various culinary applications. Its leaves are large, alternate, and have a glossy appearance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
