Acer tutcheri var. serratifolium

Acer tutcheri var. serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated-leaf maple, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and vibrant autumn foliage, which display shades of red and orange. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and turn strikingly colorful in the fall.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and arboreta for its unique foliage.
