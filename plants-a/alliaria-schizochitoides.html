Alliaria schizochitoides

Alliaria schizochitoides, commonly known as the lesser garlic, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassy areas.

Alliaria schizochitoides is also known for its mild garlic-like scent, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
