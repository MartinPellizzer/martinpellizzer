Acinodendron furfuraceum

Acinodendron furfuraceum, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
