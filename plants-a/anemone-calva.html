Anemone calva

Anemone calva, commonly known as the white anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features a short, rhizomatous root system and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to nine petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a serrated edge, and the plant is known for its early spring blooming period.

Anemone calva is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
