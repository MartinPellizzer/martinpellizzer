Aspidium pontederae

Aspidium pontederae, commonly known as the Italian water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium pontederae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
