Arenaria kingii subsp. plateauensis

Arenaria kingii subsp.

plateauensis, commonly known as the plateau sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky and arid regions of southern Africa. It typically features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on rocky outcrops or sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to the extreme conditions of its native habitat.
