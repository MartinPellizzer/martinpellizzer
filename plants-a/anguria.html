Anguria

Anguria, commonly known as watermelon, is a sprawling vine native to Africa and widely cultivated for its large, fleshy fruits.

The plant produces vibrant green leaves with a rough texture and tendrils that aid in climbing. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, maturing to a red or pink color. The outer rind is thick and fibrous, while the interior contains juicy, seed-filled pulp.

Anguria is rich in water, vitamins, and antioxidants, making it a popular fruit in many cuisines.
