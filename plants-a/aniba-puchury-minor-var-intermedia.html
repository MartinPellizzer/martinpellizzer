Aniba puchury-minor var. intermedia

Aniba puchury-minor var. intermedia, commonly known as the Amazonian spicebush, is a shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has been studied for potential pharmacological applications.
