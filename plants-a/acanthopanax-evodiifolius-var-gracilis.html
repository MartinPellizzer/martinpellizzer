Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. gracilis

Acanthopanax evodiifolius var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender five-leaf ginseng, is a variant of the five-leaf ginseng species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate or five-leaf compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries, which are edible and used in herbal remedies. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
