Arenaria tetraquetra var. minor

Arenaria tetraquetra var. minor, commonly known as the lesser sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes, rocky outcrops, and disturbed areas. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
