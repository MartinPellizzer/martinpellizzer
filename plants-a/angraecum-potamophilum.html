Angraecum potamophilum

Angraecum potamophilum, commonly known as the river stream orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments near streams or riverbanks. The plant features a pseudobulb with narrow, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its flowering period occurs during the wet season, and it is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

The orchid is notable for its intricate floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
