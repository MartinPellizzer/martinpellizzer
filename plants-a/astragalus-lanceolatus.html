Astragalus lanceolatus

Astragalus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

Astragalus lanceolatus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
