Anthyllis vulnerarioides var. bonjeanii

Anthyllis vulnerarioides var. bonjeanii, commonly known as the Bonjean's viper's bugloss, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and grasslands. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
