Abarema elliptica

Abarema elliptica, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble peacock feathers. The plant features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
