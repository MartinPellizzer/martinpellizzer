Armeria filicaulis subsp. nevadensis

Armeria filicaulis subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pink flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows, slopes, and rocky areas.

Its compact growth form and drought tolerance make it a hardy species suited to rocky or disturbed sites.
