Acer dasycarpum f. lutescens

Acer dasycarpum f. lutescens, commonly known as the golden-leaf maple, is a variant of the Chinese maple tree.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which stands out during the autumn season. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown.

Its leaves are palmately lobed and display a striking color change from green to yellow, enhancing its visual appeal.
