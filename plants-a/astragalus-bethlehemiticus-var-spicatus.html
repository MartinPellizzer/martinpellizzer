Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. spicatus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. spicatus, commonly known as the Spicate Bethlehemitic Milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance remains understudied in modern scientific literature.
