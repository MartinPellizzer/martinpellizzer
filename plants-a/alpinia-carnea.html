Alpinia carnea

Alpinia carnea, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink inflorescences, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Alpinia carnea is also valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine.
