Adiantum cunninghamii var. intermedium

Adiantum cunninghamii var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate maidenhair fern, is a delicate, evergreen fern native to Australia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that are dark green and often slightly glossy. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
