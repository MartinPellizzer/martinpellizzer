Arabis stelleri var. macrocarpa

Arabis stelleri var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or gravelly soils along streambanks and forest edges. The plant features basal rosettes of pinnately lobed leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
