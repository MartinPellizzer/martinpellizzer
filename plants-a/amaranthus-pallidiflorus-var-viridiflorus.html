Amaranthus pallidiflorus var. viridiflorus

Amaranthus pallidiflorus var. viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on the stage of bloom. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
