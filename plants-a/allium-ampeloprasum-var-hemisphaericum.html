Allium ampeloprasum var. hemisphaericum

Allium ampeloprasum var. hemisphaericum, commonly known as the globe onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, round bulbs that resemble a globe, hence its common name. The plant produces grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and mild, sweet flavor, making it both a decorative and edible plant.
