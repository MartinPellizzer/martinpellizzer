Acer campestre f. pendulum

Acer campestre f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping hedge maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a serrated edge and a bronze underside.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Native Distribution

Acer campestre f. pendulum is primarily native to parts of Europe, particularly the British Isles, including England, Wales, and parts of southern Scotland.

This distinctive cultivar is known for its weeping growth habit and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. The plant is commonly used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique appearance.

Its native range reflects its adaptation to the varied habitats of western Europe.
