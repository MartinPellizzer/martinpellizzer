Acacia fecunda

Acacia fecunda, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

Acacia fecunda is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
