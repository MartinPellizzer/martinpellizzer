Angelica dahurica var. dahurica

Angelica dahurica var. dahurica, commonly known as Chinese angelica or bigroot angelica, is a biennial or perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and yellow-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and aromatic, containing compounds like ligustilide and senkyunic acid.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.
