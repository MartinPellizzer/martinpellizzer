Arisaema nikoense subsp. alpicola

Arisaema nikoense subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to high-altitude regions of Japan. It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix enclosed within a spathe.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a short, upright stem.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix, a common trait in the Araceae family.
