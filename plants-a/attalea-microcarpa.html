Attalea microcarpa

Attalea microcarpa, commonly known as the buriti palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of orange berries. The plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often growing near rivers and lakes. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

The palm is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in traditional medicine.
