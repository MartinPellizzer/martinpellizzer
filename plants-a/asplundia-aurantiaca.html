Asplundia aurantiaca

Asplundia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden root, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, orange-colored roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family.

Asplundia aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
