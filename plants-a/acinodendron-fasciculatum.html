Acinodendron fasciculatum

Acinodendron fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in small, woody pods, and the plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry conditions.

Acinodendron fasciculatum is often used in traditional medicine and for its hard, durable wood.
