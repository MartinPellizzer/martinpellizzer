Artocarpus acuminatissimus

Artocarpus acuminatissimus, commonly known as the tamanu tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces a unique fruit with a rough, spiny exterior and a soft, edible pulp containing seeds. Its latex is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.

The tamanu tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often cultivated for both its economic and ecological benefits.
