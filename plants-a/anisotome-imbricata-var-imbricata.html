Anisotome imbricata var. imbricata

Anisotome imbricata var. imbricata, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil in arid environments.
