Ardisia sulcata

Ardisia sulcata, commonly known as the warty ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a mild, somewhat tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ardisia sulcata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
