Amesia atropurpurea

Amesia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact, upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Amesia atropurpurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
