Amerimnon granadillo

Amerimnon granadillo, commonly known as the granadillo tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a yellowish or greenish hue.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
