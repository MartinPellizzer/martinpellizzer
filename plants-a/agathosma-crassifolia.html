Agathosma crassifolia

Agathosma crassifolia, commonly known as Cape geranium, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its aromatic leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
