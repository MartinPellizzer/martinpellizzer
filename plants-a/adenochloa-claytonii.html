Adenochloa claytonii

Adenochloa claytonii, commonly known as the Clayton's grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Adenochloa claytonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
