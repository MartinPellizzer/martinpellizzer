Ancipitia dressleri

Ancipitia dressleri, commonly known as the Desert Rose.

It is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa, characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
