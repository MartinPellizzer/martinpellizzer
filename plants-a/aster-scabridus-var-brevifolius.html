Aster scabridus var. brevifolius

Aster scabridus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the rough aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable member of the Asteraceae family.
