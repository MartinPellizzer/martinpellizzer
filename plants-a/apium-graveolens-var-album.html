Apium graveolens var. album

Apium graveolens var. album, commonly known as white celery, is a variety of the celery plant characterized by its pale, blanched stalks.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels. Its stalks are typically harvested before flowering for culinary use, though it is less common than the green variety.

White celery is valued for its mild flavor and is used in various cuisines, particularly in Mediterranean cooking.
