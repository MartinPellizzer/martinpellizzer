Acacia sedifolia

Acacia sedifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, grey-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, stipitate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
