Amsinckia subglabricaulis

Amsinckia subglabricaulis, commonly known as the slender leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
