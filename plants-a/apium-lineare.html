Apium lineare

Apium lineare, commonly known as narrowleaf celery, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has slender, hollow stems and finely divided, feather-like leaves. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the carrot family.

While not widely cultivated for food, it is occasionally used in herbal remedies.
