Azalea decora

Azalea decora, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink, red, or white blossoms that attract pollinators. Azalea decora prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
