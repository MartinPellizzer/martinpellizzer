Arbutus unedo var. rubra

Arbutus unedo var. rubra, commonly known as the red strawberry tree, is a variant of the evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often tart. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to rocky or well-drained soils. It flowers with white, bell-shaped blooms in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive red fruits in the fall.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
