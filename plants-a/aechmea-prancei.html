Aechmea prancei

Aechmea prancei, commonly known as the Prance's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, with shades of red, orange, and yellow, which help attract pollinators. It produces inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette, featuring showy, spathaceous bracts.

Aechmea prancei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea prancei has rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff, grass-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy blue-green color and a pointed tip.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, underground rhizome with a fibrous, dark brown bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, with white petals and bright yellow stamens, blooming in the summer months and pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind after splitting open.
