Artanthe macrophylla

Artanthe macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf artanthe, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
