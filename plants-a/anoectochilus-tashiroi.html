Anoectochilus tashiroi

Anoectochilus tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's slipper orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a defining feature of the Anoectochilus genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and pale, often with a slight pink or greenish hue.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
