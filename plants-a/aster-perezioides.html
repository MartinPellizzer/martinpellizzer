Aster perezioides

Aster perezioides, commonly known as the Peruvian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster perezioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
