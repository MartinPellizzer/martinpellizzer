Asperula aristata subsp. thessala

Asperula aristata subsp.

thessala, commonly known as the Greek rock jasmine, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has evergreen, leathery leaves that are densely packed on short stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
