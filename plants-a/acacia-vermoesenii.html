Acacia vermoesenii

Acacia vermoesenii, commonly known as the Vervooren's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to drought conditions.

Acacia vermoesenii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
