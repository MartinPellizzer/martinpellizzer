Adenophora elata f. verticillata

Adenophora elata f. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
