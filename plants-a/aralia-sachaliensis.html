Aralia sachaliensis

Aralia sachaliensis, commonly known as the Far Eastern aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive pinnate structure, often with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Aralia sachaliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
