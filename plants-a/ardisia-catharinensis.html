Ardisia catharinensis

Ardisia catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal, but it can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
