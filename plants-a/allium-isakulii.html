Allium isakulii

Allium isakulii, commonly known as the Isakul's garlic, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a potential culinary and medicinal plant.

Allium isakulii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
