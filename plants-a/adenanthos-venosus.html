Adenanthos venosus

Adenanthos venosus, commonly known as the pink bloodwood, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its gnarled, twisted trunk and distinctive pinkish-brown bark. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grey-green hue.

Adenanthos venosus is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
