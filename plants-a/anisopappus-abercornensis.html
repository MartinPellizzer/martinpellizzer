Anisopappus abercornensis

Anisopappus abercornensis, commonly known as the Abercorn pappus.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The species is characterized by its annual growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, and the plant produces a distinctive, pappus-like structure on its seeds.

Anisopappus abercornensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
