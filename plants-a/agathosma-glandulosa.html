Agathosma glandulosa

Agathosma glandulosa, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Agathosma glandulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
