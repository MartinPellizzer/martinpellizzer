Acacia leptophleba

Acacia leptophleba, commonly known as the slender-leaved wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

The plant is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of nectar for native pollinators.
