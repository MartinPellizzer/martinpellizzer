Astragalus magdalenae

Astragalus magdalenae, commonly known as Magdalene milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus magdalenae has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It is often found in open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands.
