Arabis × saccata

Arabis × saccata, commonly known as the rock cress or sand cress, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Arabis × saccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
