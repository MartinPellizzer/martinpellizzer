Alpinia petiolata

Alpinia petiolata, commonly known as the black ginger or mountain ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia petiolata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
