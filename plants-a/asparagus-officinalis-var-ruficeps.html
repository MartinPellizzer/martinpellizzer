Asparagus officinalis var. ruficeps

Asparagus officinalis var. ruficeps, commonly known as the red-headed asparagus, is a variety characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, needle-like spears. Its foliage consists of feathery, compound leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
