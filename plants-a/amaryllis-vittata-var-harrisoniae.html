Amaryllis vittata var. harrisoniae

Amaryllis vittata var. harrisoniae, commonly known as the Harrison's Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often form a dense rosette at the base of the plant. This variety is prized for its showy blooms and is often cultivated in gardens or as a potted plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for many growing conditions.
