Acacia phlebophylla

Acacia phlebophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often grey-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The narrow-leaf wattle is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
