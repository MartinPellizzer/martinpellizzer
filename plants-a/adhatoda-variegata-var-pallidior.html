Adhatoda variegata var. pallidior

Adhatoda variegata var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale variegated adhatoda, is a flowering plant native to parts of India and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually have a pale blue or white color.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
