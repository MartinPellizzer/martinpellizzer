Arundo bambusifolia

Arundo bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo, growing in dense clumps or colonies. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands.

Arundo bambusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and basket-making.
