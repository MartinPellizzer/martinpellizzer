Acacia hebecephala

Acacia hebecephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rehabilitation of degraded soils.

Acacia hebecephala plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
