Albraunia fugax

Albraunia fugax, commonly known as the transient albraunia, is a rare and ephemeral plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, short-lived flowers that bloom briefly during the wet season. The plant's leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. Albraunia fugax thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
