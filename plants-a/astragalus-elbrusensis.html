Astragalus elbrusensis

Astragalus elbrusensis, commonly known as the Elbrus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Astragalus elbrusensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
