Amomum longipes

Amomum longipes, commonly known as the long-tailed cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that eventually develop into elliptical, fragrant fruits. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other members of the ginger family.

Amomum longipes thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.
