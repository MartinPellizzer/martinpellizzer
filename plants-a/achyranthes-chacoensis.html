Achyranthes chacoensis

Achyranthes chacoensis, commonly known as Chaco burrweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dry, three-angled fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
