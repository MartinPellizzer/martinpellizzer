Aspalathus remota

Aspalathus remota, commonly known as the mountain tea, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
