Argyreia ooststroomii

Argyreia ooststroomii, commonly known as the sacred lotus or holy water plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its roots and leaves are traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in various regions.
