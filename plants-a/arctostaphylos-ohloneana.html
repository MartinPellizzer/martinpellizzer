Arctostaphylos ohloneana

Arctostaphylos ohloneana, commonly known as the Ohlone manzanita, is a shrub native to California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its reddish bark and evergreen foliage, with small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and mild, wet winters.
