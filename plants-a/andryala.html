Andryala

Andryala, commonly known as the "sweet flag," is a perennial herb native to wetland areas in Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its aromatic, sword-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Andryala is known for its medicinal properties, with its rhizomes containing compounds used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, muddy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.
