Arundinaria falcata

Arundinaria falcata, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, pale green to golden yellow flowers in panicles during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Arundinaria falcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
