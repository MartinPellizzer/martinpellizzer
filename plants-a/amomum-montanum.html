Amomum montanum

Amomum montanum, commonly known as mountain cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, pale yellow flowers, followed by dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a fine, hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
