Astragalus tymphresteus var. peloponnesiacus

Astragalus tymphresteus var. peloponnesiacus, commonly known as the Peloponnesian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it has small, palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
