Aconitum volubile var. tortuosum

Aconitum volubile var. tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted monkshood, is a climbing perennial native to Europe.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and produces striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its long, twisting stems that can climb up to several meters in height. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
