Aristolochia chrysochlora

Aristolochia chrysochlora, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to greenish-white. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture, and its stems are typically covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its complex floral structure and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
