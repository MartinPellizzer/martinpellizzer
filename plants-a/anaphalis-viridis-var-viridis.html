Anaphalis viridis var. viridis

Anaphalis viridis var. viridis, commonly known as green everlasting, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and dry grasslands. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in garden settings for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
