Astragalus manaslensis

Astragalus manaslensis, commonly known as the Manaslu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high altitudes. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora of its native environment.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
