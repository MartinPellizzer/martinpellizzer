Abies balsamea var. brachylepis

Abies balsamea var. brachylepis, commonly known as the dwarf balsam fir, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is characterized by its short, pyramidal growth habit and dense foliage of dark green needles. The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its leaves are typically shorter and more rigid compared to the standard balsam fir, giving it a more compact appearance.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and as a specimen plant in shaded woodland settings.
