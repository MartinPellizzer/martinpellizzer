Anthocercis amblyantha

Anthocercis amblyantha, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Anthocercis amblyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
