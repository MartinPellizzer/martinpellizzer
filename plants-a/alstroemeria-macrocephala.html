Alstroemeria macrocephala

Alstroemeria macrocephala, commonly known as the giant alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Alstroemeria macrocephala is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and is often used in floral arrangements.
