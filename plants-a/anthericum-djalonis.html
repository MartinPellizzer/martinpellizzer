Anthericum djalonis

Anthericum djalonis, commonly known as the African arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, white berries as fruit.

Anthericum djalonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
