Agave cupreata

Agave cupreata, commonly known as the copper agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Agave cupreata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
