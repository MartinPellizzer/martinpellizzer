Aspilia pohlii

Aspilia pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's torchflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, torch-like cluster at the top of its stem. Aspilia pohlii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

It is often found in open grasslands and forest margins, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
