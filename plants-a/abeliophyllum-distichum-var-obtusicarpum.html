Abeliophyllum distichum var. obtusicarpum

Abeliophyllum distichum var. obtusicarpum, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its opposite, elongated leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive, blunt, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal beauty make it a valuable addition to woodland or cottage garden settings.
