Azalea atrovirens

Azalea atrovirens, commonly known as the blackleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and glossy black underneath, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped, pink to red flowers in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Azalea atrovirens is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes.
