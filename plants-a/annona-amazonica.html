Annona amazonica

Annona amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian soursop, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature. The fruit's interior contains a white, fibrous pulp with numerous black seeds.

Botanically, it is noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to moist, shaded environments.
