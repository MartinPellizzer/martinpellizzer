Ageratina aegirophylla

Ageratina aegirophylla, commonly known as the white ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. Ageratina aegirophylla is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
