Aechmea castanea

Aechmea castanea, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking red bracts that surround its flowering spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Aechmea castanea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
