Andropogon micranthus

Andropogon micranthus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and distinctive silver-gray inflorescence. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon micranthus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and use in ecological restoration projects.
