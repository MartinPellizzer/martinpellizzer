Astracantha plumata

Astracantha plumata, commonly known as the feathered burr grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in its native environment.
