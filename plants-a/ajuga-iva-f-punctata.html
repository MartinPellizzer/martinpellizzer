Ajuga iva f. punctata

Ajuga iva f. punctata, commonly known as spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green spotting, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in herbal remedies, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
