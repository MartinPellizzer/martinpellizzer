Alstroemeria quillotensis

Alstroemeria quillotensis, commonly known as the Quillotan lily, is a species native to Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, orange, yellow, and white, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Alstroemeria quillotensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
