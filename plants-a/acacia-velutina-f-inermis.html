Acacia velutina f. inermis

Acacia velutina f. inermis, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers in clusters, which are often followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
