Adorium divaricatum

Adorium divaricatum, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
