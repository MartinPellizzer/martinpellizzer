Astragalus mugodsharicus

Astragalus mugodsharicus, commonly known as the Mugodshar milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus mugodsharicus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
