Aralia elata f. subinermis

Aralia elata f. subinermis, commonly known as the Japanese angelica tree, is a variant of the broadleaf aralia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and parts of Korea. The plant features compound leaves that are typically divided into several leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in umbels, and it produces berries that are edible for humans and wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and growth habit.
