Abies sachalinensis subsp. gracilis

Abies sachalinensis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender fir, is a rare and relict species native to the Korean Peninsula and southern Primorsky Krai in Russia. It is characterized by its slender, elongated cones and relatively small, needle-like leaves.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a conical shape, dense foliage, and a slow growth rate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
