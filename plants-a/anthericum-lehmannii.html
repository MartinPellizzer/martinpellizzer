Anthericum lehmannii

Anthericum lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered along the spadix, which is typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

This plant is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
