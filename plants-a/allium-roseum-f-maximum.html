Allium roseum f. maximum

Allium roseum f. maximum, commonly known as the giant purple onion, is a striking cultivar of the Allium genus.

It is characterized by its large, spherical bulbs that can reach up to 10 cm in diameter, making it a showy ornamental plant. The plant produces clusters of lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, preferring temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
