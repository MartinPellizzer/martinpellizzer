Acantholimon halophilum var. halophilum

Acantholimon halophilum var. halophilum, commonly known as the saltbush or coastal saltbush, is a halophyte native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and arid conditions. The plant features succulent leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing soil erosion.

This species is valued for its ecological resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
