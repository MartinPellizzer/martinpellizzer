Amaryllis × dioica

Amaryllis × dioica, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, white, or pink. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Amaryllis × dioica is popular as a houseplant and for outdoor cultivation due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
