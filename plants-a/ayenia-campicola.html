Ayenia campicola

Ayenia campicola, commonly known as the rock ayenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.
