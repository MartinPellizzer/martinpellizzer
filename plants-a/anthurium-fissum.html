Anthurium fissum

Anthurium fissum, commonly known as the fissured anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green midrib and deep green margins. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium fissum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
