Adesmia bedwellii

Adesmia bedwellii, commonly known as the Bedwell's legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy, pod-like structure that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds through mechanical means.

Adesmia bedwellii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in reforestation efforts.
