Adenophora liliifolia var. suaveolens

Adenophora liliifolia var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a faintly fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are dispersed by birds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
