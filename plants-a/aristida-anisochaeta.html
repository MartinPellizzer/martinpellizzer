Aristida anisochaeta

Aristida anisochaeta, commonly known as rough hairgrass, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and wiry, often hairy stems. The leaves are typically narrow and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds.
