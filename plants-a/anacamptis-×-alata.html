Anacamptis × alata

Anacamptis × alata, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Anacamptis morio and Anacamptis pyramidalis.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces tall, erect stems with clusters of pale purple to white flowers that have a distinctive wing-like appearance on the petals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in regions of Europe and western Asia.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also important for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
