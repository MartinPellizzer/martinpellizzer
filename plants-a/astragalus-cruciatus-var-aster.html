Astragalus cruciatus var. aster

Astragalus cruciatus var. aster, commonly known as the star vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
