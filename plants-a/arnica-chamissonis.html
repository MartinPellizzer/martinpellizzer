Arnica chamissonis

Arnica chamissonis, commonly known as narrowleaf arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer. Its flowers are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties.

Arnica chamissonis is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
