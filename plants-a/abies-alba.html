Abies alba

Abies alba, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are flat, soft, and have a silvery-white underside, giving the tree its common name. It produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its timber and ecological importance.
