Amblyopappus

Amblyopappus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plants are characterized by their fuzzy, grayish foliage and yellow to orange composite flowers. They typically grow as annuals or short-lived perennials, often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.

Amblyopappus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
