Alchemilla leptoclada

Alchemilla leptoclada, commonly known as the slender alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Alchemilla leptoclada is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
