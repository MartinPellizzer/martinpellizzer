Achnatherum lemmonii

Achnatherum lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native range.

Achnatherum lemmonii is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to low-nutrient environments.
