Acaena ovalifolia var. calophylla

Acaena ovalifolia var. calophylla, commonly known as the New Zealand spinach or the "kōwhai" in Māori, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds. It is well adapted to coastal and inland environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Māori practices.
