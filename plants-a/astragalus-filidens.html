Astragalus filidens

Astragalus filidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, narrow leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus filidens is often found in sagebrush steppe and other xeric ecosystems.
