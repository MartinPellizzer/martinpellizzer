Acacia concinna

Acacia concinna, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compact globular clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of tiny, needle-like leaflets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes.

The plant is well adapted to arid conditions and is an important component of many Australian ecosystems.
