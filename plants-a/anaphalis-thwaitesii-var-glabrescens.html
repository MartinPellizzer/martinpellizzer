Anaphalis thwaitesii var. glabrescens

Anaphalis thwaitesii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous leafed frostflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are densely covered with fine, hairless scales, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Anaphalis thwaitesii var. glabrescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
