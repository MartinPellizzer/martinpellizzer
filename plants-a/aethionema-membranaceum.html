Aethionema membranaceum

Aethionema membranaceum, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat.
