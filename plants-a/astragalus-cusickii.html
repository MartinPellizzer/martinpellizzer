Astragalus cusickii

Astragalus cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus cusickii is often found in open habitats and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
