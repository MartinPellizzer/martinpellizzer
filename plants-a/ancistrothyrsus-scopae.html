Ancistrothyrsus scopae

Ancistrothyrsus scopae, commonly known as the woolly false heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ancistrothyrsus scopae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
