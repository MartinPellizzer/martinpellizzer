Acrostichum terminans

Acrostichum terminans, commonly known as the lace fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its fronds are bioluminescent at night, a rare trait among ferns, which helps attract nocturnal pollinators.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Morphology

Acrostichum terminans has long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The stem is upright, with a smooth, light brown bark that develops vertical ridges as the plant matures. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant in moist, swampy habitats.
