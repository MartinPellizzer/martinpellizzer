Apodasmia ceramophila

Apodasmia ceramophila, commonly known as the Ceramophila lichen, is a unique lichen that exhibits a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga.

It typically grows on bark, stone, or soil in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body formed by the intertwined partners. Its botanical features include a crust-like morphology and a lack of distinct leaves or stems.

This lichen plays a vital ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its habitat.
