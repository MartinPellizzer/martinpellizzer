Alchemilla vulgaris var. controversa

Alchemilla vulgaris var. controversa, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shady gardens.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.
