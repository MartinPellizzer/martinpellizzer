Allium continuum

Allium continuum, commonly known as the everlasting leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can remain visible for several months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like scent.
