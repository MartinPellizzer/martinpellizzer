Astragalus orophacoides

Astragalus orophacoides, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Astragalus orophacoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
