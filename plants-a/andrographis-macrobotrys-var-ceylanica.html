Andrographis macrobotrys var. ceylanica

Andrographis macrobotrys var. ceylanica, commonly known as Ceylon Andrographis, is a perennial herb native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
