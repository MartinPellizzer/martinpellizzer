Aechmea viridostigma

Aechmea viridostigma, commonly known as the green-stained aechmea, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest. Its foliage is typically broad and glossy, with a deep green hue that intensifies under sunlight.

Aechmea viridostigma is well-suited for indoor cultivation, requiring bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.

Morphology

Aechmea viridostigma has rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff, sword-like, and arranged in a tight circle around the central stem, with a dark green color and prominent white markings.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upward from the base. Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, with purple and yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the soil.
