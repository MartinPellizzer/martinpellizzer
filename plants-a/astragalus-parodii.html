Astragalus parodii

Astragalus parodii, commonly known as the Parodi milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Astragalus parodii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
