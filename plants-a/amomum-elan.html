Amomum elan

Amomum elan, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive three-lobed berries. Its aromatic rhizomes are highly valued for their culinary and medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory effects.

Amomum elan thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
