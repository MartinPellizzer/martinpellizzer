Alyssum gintlii

Alyssum gintlii, commonly known as the Armenian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Alyssum gintlii is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
