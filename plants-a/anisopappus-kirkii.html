Anisopappus kirkii

Anisopappus kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in the spring. It is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, adapting well to dry conditions.

Anisopappus kirkii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
