Achatocarpus praecox

Achatocarpus praecox, commonly known as the early-flowering acanthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Achatocarpus praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
