Allium longicaule

Allium longicaule, commonly known as the tall garlic or purple garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a strong garlic scent and is often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its bulbs are edible and can be used similarly to garlic in cooking.

Allium longicaule thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
