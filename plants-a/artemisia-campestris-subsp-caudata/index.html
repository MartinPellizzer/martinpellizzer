Artemisia Campestris Subsp. Caudata

Artemisia campestris subsp. caudata is a hardy perennial plant known for its distinctive silver-green foliage and aromatic properties. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or gravelly areas. This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Artemisia genus, which are widely used in traditional medicine and horticulture. Its feathery appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens. Despite its beauty, it can be invasive in certain regions, requiring careful management to prevent overgrowth.
