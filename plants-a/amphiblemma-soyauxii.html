Amphiblemma soyauxii

Amphiblemma soyauxii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces a unique structure called a "beak" at the tip of its ovary, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
