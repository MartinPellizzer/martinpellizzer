Adiantum gracillimum

Adiantum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically light green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new shoots, allowing it to spread slowly in suitable conditions.

Adiantum gracillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
