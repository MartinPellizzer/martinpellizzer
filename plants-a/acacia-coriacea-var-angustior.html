Acacia coriacea var. angustior

Acacia coriacea var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in revegetation projects due to its resilience and ability to fix nitrogen.
