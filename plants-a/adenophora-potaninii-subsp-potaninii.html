Adenophora potaninii subsp. potaninii

Adenophora potaninii subsp.

potaninii, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia. It features upright stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
