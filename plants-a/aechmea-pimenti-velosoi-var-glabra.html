Aechmea pimenti-velosoi var. glabra

Aechmea pimenti-velosoi var. glabra, commonly known as the velvet leaf pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, and often velvety leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically green with a bluish or purple tinge, and it produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
