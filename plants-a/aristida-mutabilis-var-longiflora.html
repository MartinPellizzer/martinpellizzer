Aristida mutabilis var. longiflora

Aristida mutabilis var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered threeawn, is a grass species native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, three-awned florets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
