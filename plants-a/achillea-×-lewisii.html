Achillea × lewisii

Achillea × lewisii, commonly known as sneezeweed, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat-topped clusters of yellow to pale orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits, and it is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
