Avena altaica

Avena altaica, commonly known as the tall oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces spikelets with two to three bracts, which bear small, awned florets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib.

Avena altaica is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought resistance and a deep root system.
