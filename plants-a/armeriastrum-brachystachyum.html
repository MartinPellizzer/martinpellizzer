Armeriastrum brachystachyum

Armeriastrum brachystachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
