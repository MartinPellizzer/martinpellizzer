Antirrhinum canadense var. assurgens

Antirrhinum canadense var. assurgens, commonly known as the Canadian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and roadside areas.

Antirrhinum canadense var. assurgens is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local ecosystems through its nectar production and habitat provision.
