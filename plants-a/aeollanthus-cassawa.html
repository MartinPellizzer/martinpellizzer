Aeollanthus cassawa

Aeollanthus cassawa, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible roots that are rich in carbohydrates and are a staple food in many tropical regions. Cassava is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important crop in subsistence agriculture.

Its botanical classification places it in the family Euphorbiaceae.
