Artemisia norvegica var. saxatilis

Artemisia norvegica var. saxatilis, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves with a woolly texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in loose panicles, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
