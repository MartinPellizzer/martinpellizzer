Alchemilla flavovirens

Alchemilla flavovirens, commonly known as golden-veined lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves that often have a yellowish or golden hue on the veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla flavovirens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
