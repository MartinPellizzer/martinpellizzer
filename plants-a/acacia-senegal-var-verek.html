Acacia senegal var. verek

Acacia senegal var. verek, commonly known as the gum arabic acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable gum, known as gum arabic, which is harvested from the exudation of its bark.

This gum is widely used in food, pharmaceutical, and cosmetic industries due to its emulsifying and stabilizing properties.
