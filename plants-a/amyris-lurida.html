Amyris lurida

Amyris lurida, commonly known as the swamp bay or red swamp bay, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its bark is thick and often reddish-brown, and it has a strong, spicy scent.

Amyris lurida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
