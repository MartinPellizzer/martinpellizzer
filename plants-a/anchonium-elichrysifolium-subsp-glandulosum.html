Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp. glandulosum

Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular anchonium, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite heads typical of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological niche.
