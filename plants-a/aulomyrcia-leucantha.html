Aulomyrcia leucantha

Aulomyrcia leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Aulomyrcia leucantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
