Alsinidendron viscosum var. laeve

Alsinidendron viscosum var. laeve, commonly known as the smooth stickywood, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
