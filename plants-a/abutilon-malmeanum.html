Abutilon malmeanum

Abutilon malmeanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in clusters. Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it adaptable to various soil types, though it thrives in well-drained, moist conditions.

Abutilon malmeanum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
