Anisostachya spatulata

Anisostachya spatulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wild barley, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
