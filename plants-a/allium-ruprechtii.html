Allium ruprechtii

Allium ruprechtii, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or Ruprecht's garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.

Allium ruprechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
