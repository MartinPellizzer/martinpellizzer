Arisaema cuspidatum

Arisaema cuspidatum, commonly known as the spiky arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces a spiky, yellow-green spadix surrounded by a broad, heart-shaped spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Arisaema cuspidatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
