Acaena montana

Acaena montana, commonly known as the mountain spinulose burr, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds. Its stems are often covered in stiff, hooked bristles that aid in seed dispersal.

Acaena montana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
