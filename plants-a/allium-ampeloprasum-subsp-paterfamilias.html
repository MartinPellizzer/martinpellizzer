Allium ampeloprasum subsp. paterfamilias

Allium ampeloprasum subsp.

paterfamilias, commonly known as the giant garlic or mountain leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of broad, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces tall, cylindrical flower stalks topped with dense umbels of white or pale purple flowers. It is valued for its edible leaves and bulb, which have a mild garlic flavor.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
