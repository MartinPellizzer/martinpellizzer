Althaea rosea var. sibthorpii

Althaea rosea var. sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Eurasia.

It features showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. Its roots are rich in mucilage, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in moist, clay soils.
