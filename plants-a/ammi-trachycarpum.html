Ammi trachycarpum

Ammi trachycarpum, commonly known as cow parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, ribbed fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ammi trachycarpum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
