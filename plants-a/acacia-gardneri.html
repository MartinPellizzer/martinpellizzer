Acacia gardneri

Acacia gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to orange flower clusters, which are arranged in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
