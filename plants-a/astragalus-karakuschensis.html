Astragalus karakuschensis

Astragalus karakuschensis, commonly known as Karakusch mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
