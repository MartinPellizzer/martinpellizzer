Andropogon tuberculatus

Andropogon tuberculatus, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Andropogon tuberculatus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
