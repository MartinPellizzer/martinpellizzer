Arctotis foeniculacea

Arctotis foeniculacea, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Arctotis foeniculacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
