Adesmia rubroviridis

Adesmia rubroviridis, commonly known as the red-veined legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with reddish veins and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its pods contain edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities in soil.
