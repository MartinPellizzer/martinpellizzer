Alchemilla minutidens

Alchemilla minutidens, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Alchemilla minutidens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
