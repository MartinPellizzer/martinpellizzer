Aristolochia melanoglossa

Aristolochia melanoglossa, commonly known as the black-lipped Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are often greenish-black in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, gelatinous substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex and often fragrant floral structures.
