Acetosella semiloba

Acetosella semiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sorrel, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Acetosella semiloba is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
