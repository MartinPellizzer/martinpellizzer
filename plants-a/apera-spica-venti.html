Apera spica-venti

Apera spica-venti, commonly known as windgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open areas such as roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences, which are often adorned with fine, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is known for its ability to colonize and stabilize soil.

Windgrass is an important component of grassland ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
