Athyrium asplenioides

Athyrium asplenioides, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive shield-like shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Athyrium asplenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
