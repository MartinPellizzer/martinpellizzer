Alyssum conglobatum

Alyssum conglobatum, commonly known as clustered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has compact, mat-forming growth habit with small, rounded leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum conglobatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
