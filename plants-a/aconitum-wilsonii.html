Aconitum wilsonii

Aconitum wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces showy, spurs at the base of its petals. Aconitum wilsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

It is also noted for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it a dangerous plant to handle.
