Alopecurus bonariensis

Alopecurus bonariensis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and produces small, blue-green spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Alopecurus bonariensis is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in various climates.
