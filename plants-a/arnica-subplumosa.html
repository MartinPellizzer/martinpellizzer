Arnica subplumosa

Arnica subplumosa, commonly known as the downy arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica subplumosa is valued for its medicinal properties, though it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity.
