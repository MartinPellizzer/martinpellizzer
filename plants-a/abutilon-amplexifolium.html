Abutilon amplexifolium

Abutilon amplexifolium, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and clasping, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Abutilon amplexifolium thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
