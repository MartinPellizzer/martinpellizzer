Aster asterotrichus

Aster asterotrichus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
