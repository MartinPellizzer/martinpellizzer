Athyrium submelanolepis

Athyrium submelanolepis, commonly known as the black-stemmed lady fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that are typically bi- to tri-pinnate. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are dark green with a contrasting blackish stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ease of cultivation.
