Artocarpus chaplasha

Artocarpus chaplasha, commonly known as the Indian breadfruit or chaplasha, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or brown when mature. The fruit has a fibrous, starchy pulp that is edible and used in various culinary preparations.

It also has a history of use in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
