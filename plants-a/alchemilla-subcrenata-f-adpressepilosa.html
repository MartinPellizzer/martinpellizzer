Alchemilla subcrenata f. adpressepilosa

Alchemilla subcrenata f. adpressepilosa, commonly known as the compact alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
