Aristolochia teretiflora

Aristolochia teretiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a strong, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
