Astragalus clerceanus

Astragalus clerceanus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Astragalus clerceanus is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to low-nutrient conditions.
