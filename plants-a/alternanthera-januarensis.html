Alternanthera januarensis

Alternanthera januarensis, commonly known as the red-stemmed alternanthera, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, sunny conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spikes, and it is cultivated for its ornamental value in aquatic and bog gardens.

The species is also valued for its potential use in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
