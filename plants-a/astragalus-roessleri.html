Astragalus roessleri

Astragalus roessleri, commonly known as the Roessler's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus roessleri is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
