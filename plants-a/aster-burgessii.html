Aster burgessii

Aster burgessii, commonly known as the Burgess aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bracted flower heads.

Aster burgessii is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
