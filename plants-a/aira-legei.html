Aira legei

Aira legei, commonly known as the Japanese millet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Aira legei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
