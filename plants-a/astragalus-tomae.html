Astragalus tomae

Astragalus tomae, commonly known as the Tomaean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus tomae is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
