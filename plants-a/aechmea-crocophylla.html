Aechmea crocophylla

Aechmea crocophylla, commonly known as the Crimson Pineapple Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences with red and yellow bracts, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Morphology

Aechmea crocophylla has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, dark green to bluish-green color.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, fibrous root system that anchors it to its substrate. It produces striking inflorescences with bright orange-red bracts that enclose clusters of small, tubular flowers, which bloom during the rainy season and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous tiny seeds, dispersed by birds and mammals that eat the fruit.

The roots are shallow and rhizomatous, helping the plant absorb moisture from the air and soil.
