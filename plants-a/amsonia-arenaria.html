Amsonia arenaria

Amsonia arenaria, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Amsonia arenaria is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
