Alternanthera philoxeroides var. luxurians

Alternanthera philoxeroides var. luxurians, commonly known as the alligatorweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or pinkish stems and leaves that are usually green with a reddish tinge. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wetland environments, often forming dense mats that can dominate aquatic habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by small, three-lobed fruits.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
