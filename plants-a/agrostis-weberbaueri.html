Agrostis weberbaueri

Agrostis weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's bentgrass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its botanical features include small, spikelet-based inflorescences and slender, hairless stems.

Agrostis weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
