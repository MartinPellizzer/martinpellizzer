Aloe torquata

Aloe torquata, commonly known as the spiral aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The leaves are fleshy, bluish-green, and covered in a white powdery coating called farina. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
