Adenophora polymorpha var. lamarckii

Adenophora polymorpha var. lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
