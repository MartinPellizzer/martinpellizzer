Aechmea bruggeri

Aechmea bruggeri, commonly known as the Brugger's pineapple plant, is a striking epiphytic bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant, spathate flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in the spring. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Aechmea bruggeri is popular as a ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea bruggeri has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, stiff, and sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a dark green color and a waxy coating.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a dense, spiky inflorescence, with vibrant red and orange bracts that bloom in late summer to early autumn, and are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. The plant produces berries that are bright red and contain small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds eating the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding air and soil.
