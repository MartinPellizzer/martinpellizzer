Aspalathus spathulata

Aspalathus spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires a warm, Mediterranean climate. It is notable for its use in the production of rooibos tea, which is caffeine-free and rich in antioxidants.

Aspalathus spathulata plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
