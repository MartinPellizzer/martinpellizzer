Alternanthera rufescens

Alternanthera rufescens, commonly known as red Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in wetland or marshy environments. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish hues.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a bog plant.
