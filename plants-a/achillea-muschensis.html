Achillea muschensis

Achillea muschensis, commonly known as Musk Valley yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Musk Valley yarrow is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
