Astragalus friwaldskianus

Astragalus friwaldskianus, commonly known as the Friwaldskian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
