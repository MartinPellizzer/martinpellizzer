Adicea carnosula

Adicea carnosula, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to produce seed pods that persist into winter.
