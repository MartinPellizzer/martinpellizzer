Antirrhinum supinum

Antirrhinum supinum, commonly known as the prostrate snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Antirrhinum supinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
