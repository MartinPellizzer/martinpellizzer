Atalantia cuspidata

Atalantia cuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble lanterns, which turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red, spiky fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and landscapes.
