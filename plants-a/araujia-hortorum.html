Araujia hortorum

Araujia hortorum, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its fruits are cylindrical, woody pods that split open to release seeds.

Araujia hortorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
