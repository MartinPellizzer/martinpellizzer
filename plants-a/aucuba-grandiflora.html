Aucuba grandiflora

Aucuba grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered aucuba, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Aucuba grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
