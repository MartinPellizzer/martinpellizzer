Acanthus vandermeirenii

Acanthus vandermeirenii, commonly known as the Vandermeiren's spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
