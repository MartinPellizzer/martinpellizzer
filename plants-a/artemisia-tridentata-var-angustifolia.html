Artemisia tridentata var. angustifolia

Artemisia tridentata var. angustifolia, commonly known as sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its leaves contain essential oils that give it a strong, distinctive scent.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
