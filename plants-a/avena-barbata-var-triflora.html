Avena barbata var. triflora

Avena barbata var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear three to five florets. The plant produces delicate, awned spikelets that are often arranged in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
