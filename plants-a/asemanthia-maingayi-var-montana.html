Asemanthia maingayi var. montana

Asemanthia maingayi var. montana, commonly known as the mountain asemanthia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are often pink or red, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its botanical characteristics include a preference for well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
