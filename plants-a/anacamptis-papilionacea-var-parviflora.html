Anacamptis papilionacea var. parviflora

Anacamptis papilionacea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
