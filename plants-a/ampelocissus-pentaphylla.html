Ampelocissus pentaphylla

Ampelocissus pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf fig or the African ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are clustered in spherical formations. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and covering structures.
