Achillea distans

Achillea distans, commonly known as distant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, branched stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The leaves are finely divided and aromatic, with a distinctive, slightly bitter scent.
