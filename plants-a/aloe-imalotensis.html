Aloe imalotensis

Aloe imalotensis, commonly known as the Red Ghost Aloe, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
