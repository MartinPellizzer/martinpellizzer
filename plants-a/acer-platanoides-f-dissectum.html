Acer platanoides f. dissectum

Acer platanoides f. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its finely cut, lobed leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental tree or shrub is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
