Abutilon eremitopetalum

Abutilon eremitopetalum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous, woody root system helps it survive in dry, sandy soils.

Abutilon eremitopetalum is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, where it thrives with minimal water.
