Abutilon ulbrichii

Abutilon ulbrichii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
