Ammannia retusa

Ammannia retusa, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late summer.

Ammannia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wetland ecosystems.
