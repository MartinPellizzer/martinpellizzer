Angelica mexicana

Angelica mexicana, commonly known as Mexican angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound leaves.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, often growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant features tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are rich in volatile oils and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica mexicana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance to pollinators.
