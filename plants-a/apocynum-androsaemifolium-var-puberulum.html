Apocynum androsaemifolium var. puberulum

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to purple and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is considered a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
