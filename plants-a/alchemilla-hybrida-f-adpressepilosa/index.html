Alchemilla Hybrida F. Adpressepilosa

Alchemilla hybrida f. adpressepilosa is a unique variant of the alpine penny-cress family known for its dense, low-growing habit. This plant thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in mountainous regions across Europe. Its foliage is characterized by thick, woolly leaves that help retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Despite its modest appearance, it holds ecological significance as a hardy species adapted to extreme conditions.
