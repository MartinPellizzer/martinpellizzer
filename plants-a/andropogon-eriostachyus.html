Andropogon eriostachyus

Andropogon eriostachyus, commonly known as the eastern wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include slender stems, lance-shaped leaves, and spike-like inflorescences.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
