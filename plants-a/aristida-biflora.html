Aristida biflora

Aristida biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of two spikelets, giving it its common name, and it produces small, pale green to brown flowers.

Aristida biflora is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
