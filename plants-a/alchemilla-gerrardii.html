Alchemilla gerrardii

Alchemilla gerrardii, commonly known as field alkanet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Alchemilla gerrardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
