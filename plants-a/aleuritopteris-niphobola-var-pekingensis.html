Aleuritopteris niphobola var. pekingensis

Aleuritopteris niphobola var. pekingensis, commonly known as the Peking cliffbrake, is a fern species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky or cliff faces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
