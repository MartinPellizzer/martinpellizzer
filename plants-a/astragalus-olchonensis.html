Astragalus olchonensis

Astragalus olchonensis, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus olchonensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
