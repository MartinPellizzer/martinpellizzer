Andropogon contortus subvar. hispidissimus

Andropogon contortus subvar. hispidissimus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
