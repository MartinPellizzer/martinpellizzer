Adenanthera bicolor

Adenanthera bicolor, commonly known as the blue thorn apple, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pods that contain spherical seeds with a hard, smooth surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Adenanthera bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
