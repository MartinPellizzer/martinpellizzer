Acrostichum calamarium

Acrostichum calamarium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and clusters of small, showy blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is drought-tolerant and well-adapted to harsh coastal conditions.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
