Aster diffusus f. hirsuticaulis

Aster diffusus f. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed aster, is a variant of the common aster with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

This subspecies is native to regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
