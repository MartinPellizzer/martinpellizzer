Astragalus lacus-valashti

Astragalus lacus-valashti, commonly known as the Lake Valashti milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. Native to specific regions in Eastern Europe, it thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
