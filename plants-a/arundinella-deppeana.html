Arundinella deppeana

Arundinella deppeana, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

Arundinella deppeana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
