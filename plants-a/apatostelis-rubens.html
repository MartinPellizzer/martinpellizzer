Apatostelis rubens

Apatostelis rubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand, characterized by its distinctive reddish coloration in its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in inflorescences.

Apatostelis rubens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local biodiversity.
