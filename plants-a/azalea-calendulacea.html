Azalea calendulacea

Azalea calendulacea, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea calendulacea thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
