Acacia glaucocaesia

Acacia glaucocaesia, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green to white foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical or cylindrical heads, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Acacia glaucocaesia is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
