Achnatherum perplexum

Achnatherum perplexum, commonly known as the perplexing needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Achnatherum perplexum produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often difficult to distinguish from similar species, contributing to its common name.
