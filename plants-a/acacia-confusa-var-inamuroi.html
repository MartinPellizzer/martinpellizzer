Acacia confusa var. inamuroi

Acacia confusa var. inamuroi, commonly known as the Japanese acacia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
