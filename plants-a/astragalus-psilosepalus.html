Astragalus psilosepalus

Astragalus psilosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus psilosepalus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
