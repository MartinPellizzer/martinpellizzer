Antitaxis nodiflora

Antitaxis nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered antitaxis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Antitaxis nodiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as succulent stems and reduced leaf size.
