Alchemilla vizcayensis

Alchemilla vizcayensis, commonly known as the Vizcaya lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla vizcayensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
