Asplenium parksii

Asplenium parksii, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Asplenium parksii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid indoor environment.
