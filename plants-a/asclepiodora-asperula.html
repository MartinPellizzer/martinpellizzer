Asclepiodora asperula

Asclepiodora asperula, commonly known as the rough asclepias, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, with a distinctive corona structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Asclepiodora asperula is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
