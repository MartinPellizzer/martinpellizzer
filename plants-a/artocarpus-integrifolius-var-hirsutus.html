Artocarpus integrifolius var. hirsutus

Artocarpus integrifolius var. hirsutus, commonly known as the rough-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, rough-textured leaves. The tree produces a unique fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a starchy, edible interior. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature and has undergone natural ripening.

It is valued for its nutritional content and traditional uses in various cultures.
