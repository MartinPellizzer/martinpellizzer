Angelica grayi

Angelica grayi, commonly known as Gray's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-like compound leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
