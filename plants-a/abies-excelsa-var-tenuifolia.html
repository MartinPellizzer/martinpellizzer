Abies excelsa var. tenuifolia

Abies excelsa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a slightly twisted arrangement. The plant produces cones that are usually pendulous and have a slender, cylindrical shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine or alpine regions.

The botanical features of this variant make it well adapted to its harsh mountainous habitat.
