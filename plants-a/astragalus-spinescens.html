Astragalus spinescens

Astragalus spinescens, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Astragalus spinescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
