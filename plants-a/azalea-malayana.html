Azalea malayana

Azalea malayana, commonly known as the Malay azalea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are usually broad and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant flowers.

Azalea malayana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden settings.
