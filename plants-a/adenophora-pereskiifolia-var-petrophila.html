Adenophora pereskiifolia var. petrophila

Adenophora pereskiifolia var. petrophila, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
