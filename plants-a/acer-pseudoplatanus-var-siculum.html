Acer pseudoplatanus var. siculum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian maple, is a variant of the common maple tree native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
