Aracium paludosum

Aracium paludosum, commonly known as the swamp aracium, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its moist or waterlogged environment. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its root system is adapted to poor soil conditions, allowing it to thrive in swampy areas.

Aracium paludosum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
