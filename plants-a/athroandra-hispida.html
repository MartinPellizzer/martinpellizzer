Athroandra hispida

Athroandra hispida, commonly known as the rough-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom during the wet season and are often visited by birds and insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
