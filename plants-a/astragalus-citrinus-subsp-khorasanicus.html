Astragalus citrinus subsp. khorasanicus

Astragalus citrinus subsp.

khorasanicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Iranian Plateau. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and exhibits a low-growing, sprawling habit. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
