Acer monspessulanum f. micropterum

Acer monspessulanum f. micropterum, commonly known as the miniature maple, is a compact cultivar of the maple tree.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are deeply lobed and have a vibrant green color in summer. The plant produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red samaras in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is often used in gardens for its unique appearance and seasonal color changes.
