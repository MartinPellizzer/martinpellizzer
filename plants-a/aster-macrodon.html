Aster macrodon

Aster macrodon, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Aster macrodon is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
