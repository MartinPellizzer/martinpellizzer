Acalypha lamiifolia

Acalypha lamiifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Acalypha lamiifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
