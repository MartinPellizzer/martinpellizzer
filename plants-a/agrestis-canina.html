Agrestis canina

Agrestis canina, commonly known as the dog's thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowering heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Agrestis canina is an annual or biennial plant that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
