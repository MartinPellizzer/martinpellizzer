Aechmea sumidourensis

Aechmea sumidourensis, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The leaves are typically broad, waxy, and have a silver-blue hue, giving the plant its common name. It produces striking flower spikes that emerge from the center of the rosette, often in shades of red or orange.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
