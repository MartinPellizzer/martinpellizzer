Arenaria nuttallii var. fragilis

Arenaria nuttallii var. fragilis, commonly known as the fragile sand-flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

This variety is notable for its fragility, with stems that tend to break easily, making it a rare and delicate species in the plant world.
