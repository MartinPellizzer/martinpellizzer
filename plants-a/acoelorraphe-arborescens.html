Acoelorraphe arborescens

Acoelorraphe arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring palmately lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
