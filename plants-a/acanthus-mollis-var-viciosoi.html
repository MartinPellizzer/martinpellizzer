Acanthus mollis var. viciosoi

Acanthus mollis var. viciosoi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, deeply lobed leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of violet-blue flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a favored plant in both garden and horticultural settings.
