Asteriscus longiradiatus

Asteriscus longiradiatus, commonly known as the long-rayed asterisk, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in a radiate pattern, giving it a star-like appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Asteriscus longiradiatus is typically found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
