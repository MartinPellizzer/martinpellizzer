Astragalus malcolmii

Astragalus malcolmii, commonly known as Malcolm's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
