Astragalus sanczirii

Astragalus sanczirii, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
