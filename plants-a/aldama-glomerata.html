Aldama glomerata

Aldama glomerata, commonly known as the clustered Aldama, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces dense clusters of small yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
