Asplenium melanopodium

Asplenium melanopodium, commonly known as the blackfoot shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of dark green, leathery fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Asplenium melanopodium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
