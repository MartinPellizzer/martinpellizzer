Artanthe hartwegiana

Artanthe hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's artanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
