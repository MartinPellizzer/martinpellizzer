Aristea platycaulis

Aristea platycaulis, commonly known as the flat-stemmed aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of yellow or white. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Aristea platycaulis is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
