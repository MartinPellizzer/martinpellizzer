Anthyllis vulneraria f. albiflora

Anthyllis vulneraria f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage. This subspecies is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
