Anemone coelestina var. linearis

Anemone coelestina var. linearis, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are lance-shaped, giving it the specific epithet *linearis*. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for once established.
