Artemisia campestris var. delphinensis

Artemisia campestris var. delphinensis, commonly known as blue artemisia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a strong aromatic scent due to the presence of essential oils in its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
