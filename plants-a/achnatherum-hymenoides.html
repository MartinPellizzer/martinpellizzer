Achnatherum hymenoides

Achnatherum hymenoides, commonly known as the blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in prairies and steppe regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense, resilient grasslands.

Achnatherum hymenoides plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
