Aliseta

Aliseta, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges. Aliseta produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
