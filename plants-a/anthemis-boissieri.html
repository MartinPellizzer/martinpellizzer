Anthemis boissieri

Anthemis boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a hardy, ornamental plant in horticulture.
