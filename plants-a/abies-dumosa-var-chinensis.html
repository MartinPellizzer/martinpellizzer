Abies dumosa var. chinensis

Abies dumosa var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir or Chinese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its resilience and adaptability.
