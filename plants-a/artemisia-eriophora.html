Artemisia eriophora

Artemisia eriophora, commonly known as the dwarf sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Artemisia eriophora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
