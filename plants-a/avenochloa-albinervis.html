Avenochloa albinervis

Avenochloa albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with prominently white-veined patterns. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Avenochloa albinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
