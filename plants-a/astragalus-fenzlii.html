Astragalus fenzlii

Astragalus fenzlii, commonly known as the Fenzli's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus fenzlii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
