Amphilophium bauhinioides

Amphilophium bauhinioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with five petals, and are typically white or pale pink in color.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
