Astrophytum myriostigma subsp. tamaulipense

Astrophytum myriostigma subsp.

tamaulipense, commonly known as the Tamaulipan cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive globular shape and a crown of spines.

The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in spring and attracting pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in desert gardens.
