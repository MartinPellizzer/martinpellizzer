Aniba duckei

Aniba duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and seasonal fruit display.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
