Aloe marmorata

Aloe marmorata, commonly known as the marbled aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with distinctive white or pale markings. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered with a layer of fine, white hairs. It produces yellow flowers on slender stalks during the warmer months.

This plant is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.
