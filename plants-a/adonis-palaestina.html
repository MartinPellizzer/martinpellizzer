Adonis palaestina

Adonis palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine glory flower, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has deeply lobed leaves and grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.
