Anthemis nigrescens

Anthemis nigrescens, commonly known as black anthemis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in arid or rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
