Aphanopappus

Aphanopappus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by their low-growing, often mat-forming growth habit. Aphanopappus species are known for their small, narrow leaves and showy yellow or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plants have adapted to harsh environments through features such as reduced leaf surfaces and deep root systems.

They play an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for desert wildlife.
