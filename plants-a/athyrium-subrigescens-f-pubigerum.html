Athyrium subrigescens f. pubigerum

Athyrium subrigescens f. pubigerum, commonly known as the pubescent lady fern, is a fern species characterized by its soft, hairy texture on the fronds.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is native to regions in Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features bipinnate fronds with a distinctive, upright growth habit. Its leaves are typically dark green with a silvery underside, and it produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the undersides of the fronds.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
