Alternanthera ficoidea var. diffusa

Alternanthera ficoidea var. diffusa, commonly known as red veined sorrel, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its vibrant red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage.

It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge above the water surface. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a velvety texture that enhances its visual appeal.

It is a popular choice for both decorative and ecological purposes in aquatic habitats.
