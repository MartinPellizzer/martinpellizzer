Aster johannensis

Aster johannensis, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Aster johannensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
