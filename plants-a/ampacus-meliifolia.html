Ampacus meliifolia

Ampacus meliifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-green leaves that emit a citrus-like aroma. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas due to their calming properties.

Ampacus meliifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
