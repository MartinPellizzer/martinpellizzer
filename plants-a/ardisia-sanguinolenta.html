Ardisia sanguinolenta

Ardisia sanguinolenta, commonly known as the bloodroot ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are visually striking and contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, shade-tolerant plants.
