Allium margaritaceum subsp. purpureum

Allium margaritaceum subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its bulbous structure and tall, narrow leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and mild flavor, often used in culinary applications.
