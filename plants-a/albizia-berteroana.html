Albizia berteroana

Albizia berteroana, commonly known as the yellow albizia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Albizia berteroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
