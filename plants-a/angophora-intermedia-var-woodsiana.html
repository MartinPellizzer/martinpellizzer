Angophora intermedia var. woodsiana

Angophora intermedia var. woodsiana, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and dry inland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in timber production.
