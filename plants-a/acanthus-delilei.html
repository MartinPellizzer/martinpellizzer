Acanthus delilei

Acanthus delilei, commonly known as the African horsewhip, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that resembles a horse's tail, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture with a deep green color. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is often used in gardens for its dramatic visual impact and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
