Amsonia glaberrima

Amsonia glaberrima, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with gray-green foliage and striking blue-gray flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Amsonia glaberrima is valued for its ornamental beauty and low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
