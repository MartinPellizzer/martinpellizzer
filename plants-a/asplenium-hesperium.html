Asplenium hesperium

Asplenium hesperium, commonly known as the western maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of its leaves. Native to the western United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
