Arabis rimarum

Arabis rimarum, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
