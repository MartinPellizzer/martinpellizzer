Alsophila vestita

Alsophila vestita, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features fronds that are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive velvety texture on the underside. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of ferns.

Alsophila vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
