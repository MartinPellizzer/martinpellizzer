Adenophyllum porophyllum var. porophyllum

Adenophyllum porophyllum var. porophyllum, commonly known as the California broom, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It has a low-growing habit with sprawling stems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.
