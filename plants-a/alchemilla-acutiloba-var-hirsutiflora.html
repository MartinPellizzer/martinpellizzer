Alchemilla acutiloba var. hirsutiflora

Alchemilla acutiloba var. hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and the delicate appearance of its flowers.
