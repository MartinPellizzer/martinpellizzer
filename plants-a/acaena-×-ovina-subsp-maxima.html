Acaena × ovina subsp. maxima

Acaena × ovina subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or kōwhai, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces small, achene-like fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal and maritime climates, often growing on sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
