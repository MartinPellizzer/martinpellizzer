Aspalathus cordicarpa

Aspalathus cordicarpa, commonly known as the African red bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red, velvety flowers. The plant grows as a shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
