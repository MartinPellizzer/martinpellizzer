Acalypha reniformis

Acalypha reniformis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Acalypha reniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a houseplant.
