Astragalus aintabicus

Astragalus aintabicus, commonly known as Aintab milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus aintabicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological studies due to its unique botanical features.
