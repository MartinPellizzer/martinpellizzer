Anthemis carthaginensis

Anthemis carthaginensis, commonly known as the Carthaginian anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, sometimes aromatic, scent. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
