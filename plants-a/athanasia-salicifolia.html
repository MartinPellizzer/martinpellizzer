Athanasia salicifolia

Athanasia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Athanasia salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
