Aristida furfurosa

Aristida furfurosa, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woods, prairies, and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are characteristic of the grass family. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, adding a delicate aesthetic to its habitat.

Aristida furfurosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
