Alseodaphne rhododendropsis

Alseodaphne rhododendropsis, commonly known as the red-flowered tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink to red in color, which appear in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
