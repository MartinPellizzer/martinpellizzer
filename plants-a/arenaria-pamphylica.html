Arenaria pamphylica

Arenaria pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
