Amorphophallus polyanthus

Amorphophallus polyanthus, commonly known as the giant elephant's foot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy underground tuber, which can grow to impressive sizes. The plant produces striking, spathaceous flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
