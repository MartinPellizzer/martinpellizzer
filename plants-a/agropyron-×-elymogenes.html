Agropyron × elymogenes

Agropyron × elymogenes, commonly known as the wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, short blades that often form a sod-like structure. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its high nutritional content and is often used in forage and erosion control.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
