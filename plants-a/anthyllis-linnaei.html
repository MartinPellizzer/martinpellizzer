Anthyllis linnaei

Anthyllis linnaei, commonly known as Linnaean's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Anthyllis linnaei is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
