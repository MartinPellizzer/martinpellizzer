Adiantum delavayi

Adiantum delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky or forested areas. Its rhizomes are short and produce numerous stolons that help in vegetative propagation.

The fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure that contributes to its ornamental appeal.
