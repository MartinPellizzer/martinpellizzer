Adiantum forzzanum

Adiantum forzzanum, commonly known as the barren bell-fern, is a delicate fern species native to southern Africa.

It features a slender, upright frond with a distinctive, bell-shaped rachis that is often bare of true leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. Adiantum forzzanum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
