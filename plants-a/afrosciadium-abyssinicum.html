Afrosciadium abyssinicum

Afrosciadium abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian wild spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland areas.

The leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their nutritional value.
