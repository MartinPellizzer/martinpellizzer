Ageratina anisochroma

Ageratina anisochroma, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Ageratina anisochroma is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
