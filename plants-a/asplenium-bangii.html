Asplenium bangii

Asplenium bangii, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium bangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
