Aster ciliolatus var. wilsonii

Aster ciliolatus var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's cottonwood aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
