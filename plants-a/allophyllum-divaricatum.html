Allophyllum divaricatum

Allophyllum divaricatum, commonly known as the variable alpine spurge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Allophyllum divaricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
