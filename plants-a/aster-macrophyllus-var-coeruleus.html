Aster macrophyllus var. coeruleus

Aster macrophyllus var. coeruleus, commonly known as the Blue Mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
