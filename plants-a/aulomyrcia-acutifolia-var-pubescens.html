Aulomyrcia acutifolia var. pubescens

Aulomyrcia acutifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf aulomyrcia, is a rare shrub native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in short, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of shade, typically found at elevations between 1,200 and 2,000 meters.
