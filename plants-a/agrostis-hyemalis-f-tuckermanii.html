Agrostis hyemalis f. tuckermanii

Agrostis hyemalis f. tuckermanii, commonly known as Tuckerman's winter bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves and bear small, spikelet-like inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
