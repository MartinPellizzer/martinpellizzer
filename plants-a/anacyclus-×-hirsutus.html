Anacyclus × hirsutus

Anacyclus × hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy desert thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly stems and yellow flower heads that resemble daisies. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas, this species thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by spiky, bristly seed heads.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
