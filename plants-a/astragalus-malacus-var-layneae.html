Astragalus malacus var. layneae

Astragalus malacus var. layneae, commonly known as Layne's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Layne's milkvetch plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
