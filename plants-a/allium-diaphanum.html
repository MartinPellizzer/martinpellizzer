Allium diaphanum

Allium diaphanum, commonly known as the translucent leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its translucent, papery sheaths that encase the bulb, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium diaphanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
