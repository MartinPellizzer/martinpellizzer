Anemone × seemenii subsp. vindobonensis

Anemone × seemenii subsp.

vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna anemone, is a hybrid plant native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodland habitats and has heart-shaped, lobed leaves. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
