Aristida papposa

Aristida papposa, commonly known as the silky grass or hairy dropseed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across open habitats. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Aristida papposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
