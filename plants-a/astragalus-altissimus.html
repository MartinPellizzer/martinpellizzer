Astragalus altissimus

Astragalus altissimus, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus altissimus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
