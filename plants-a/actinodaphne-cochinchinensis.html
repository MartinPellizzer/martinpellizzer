Actinodaphne cochinchinensis

Actinodaphne cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. Its fragrant flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
