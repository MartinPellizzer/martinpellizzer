Astragalus glomeratus

Astragalus glomeratus, commonly known as clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus glomeratus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
