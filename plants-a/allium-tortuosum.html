Allium tortuosum

Allium tortuosum, commonly known as the garlic chive or twisted garlic, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its edible, garlic-flavored leaves. The plant produces small, white, bulbous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a mild garlic aroma to dishes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herb collections.
