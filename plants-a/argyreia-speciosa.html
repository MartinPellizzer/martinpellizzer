Argyreia speciosa

Argyreia speciosa, commonly known as the "Morning Glory," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue, purple, or white blooms. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Argyreia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
