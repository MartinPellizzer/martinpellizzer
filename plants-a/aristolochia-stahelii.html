Aristolochia stahelii

Aristolochia stahelii, commonly known as the Stahel's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
