Acroptilon picris var. acutifolium

Acroptilon picris var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
