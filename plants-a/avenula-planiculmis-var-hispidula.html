Avenula planiculmis var. hispidula

Avenula planiculmis var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets.

This variety is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to wetland environments.
