Astragalus angustus var. longifolius

Astragalus angustus var. longifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
