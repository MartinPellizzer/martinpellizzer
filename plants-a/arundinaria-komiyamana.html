Arundinaria komiyamana

Arundinaria komiyamana, commonly known as the Komiyama bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically green to purplish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinaria komiyamana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
