Aster carthamoides

Aster carthamoides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branching.

Aster carthamoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
