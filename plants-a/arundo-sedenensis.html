Arundo sedenensis

Arundo sedenensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, riverbanks, and marshy areas, thriving in moist to semi-aquatic conditions. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces fluffy, feathery seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust structure and adaptability to varied water conditions.
