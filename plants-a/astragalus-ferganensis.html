Astragalus ferganensis

Astragalus ferganensis, commonly known as Fergana milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Astragalus ferganensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
