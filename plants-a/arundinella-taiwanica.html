Arundinella taiwanica

Arundinella taiwanica, commonly known as the Taiwan bamboo grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

Arundinella taiwanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
