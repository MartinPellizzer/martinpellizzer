Aspidium muricatum

Aspidium muricatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique appearance.

Aspidium muricatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
