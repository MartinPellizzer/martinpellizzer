Aciotis pendulifolia

Aciotis pendulifolia, commonly known as the hanging aciotis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Aciotis pendulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading appearance.
