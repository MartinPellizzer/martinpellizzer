Andropogon sanguineus var. brevipedicellatus

Andropogon sanguineus var. brevipedicellatus, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, reddish-brown spikelets that are characteristic of the Andropogon genus. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical structure makes it identifiable by its short pedicels and compact inflorescence.
