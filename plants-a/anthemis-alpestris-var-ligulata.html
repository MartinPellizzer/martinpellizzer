Anthemis alpestris var. ligulata

Anthemis alpestris var. ligulata, commonly known as the Alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray foliage and is characterized by its small, yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces numerous ray florets surrounded by a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a notable plant in alpine ecosystems.
