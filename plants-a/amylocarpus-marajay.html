Amylocarpus marajay

Amylocarpus marajay, commonly known as the Marajay arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate inflorescences. The plant produces a starchy, edible root that is used in various culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and local food systems.
