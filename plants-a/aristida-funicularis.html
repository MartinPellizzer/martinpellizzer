Aristida funicularis

Aristida funicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Aristida funicularis is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
