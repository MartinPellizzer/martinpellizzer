Astragalus ishigensis

Astragalus ishigensis, commonly known as Ishigaki milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan, specifically the island of Ishigaki.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
