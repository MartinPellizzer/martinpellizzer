Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. webbiana

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

webbiana, commonly known as the creeping everlasting, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, typical of the genus Anthyllis. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
