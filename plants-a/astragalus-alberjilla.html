Astragalus alberjilla

Astragalus alberjilla, commonly known as the Alberji or Alberjilla milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as steppes and scrublands.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
