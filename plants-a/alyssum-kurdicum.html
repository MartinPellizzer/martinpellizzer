Alyssum kurdicum

Alyssum kurdicum, commonly known as Kurdish alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Alyssum kurdicum is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders in horticulture.
