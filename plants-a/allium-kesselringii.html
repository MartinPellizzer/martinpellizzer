Allium kesselringii

Allium kesselringii, commonly known as the Kesselringii garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are similar to those of other alliums but are less commonly cultivated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
