Acacia leucospira

Acacia leucospira, commonly known as the white-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia leucospira is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

It is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
