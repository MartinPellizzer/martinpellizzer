Anemonoides caucasica

Anemonoides caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a short, hairy stem with palmately divided, bright green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, white or pinkish flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
