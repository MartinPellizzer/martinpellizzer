Artemisia superba

Artemisia superba, commonly known as the grand Artemisia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Artemisia superba is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and is also used as a ornamental plant in gardens.
