Aster bullatus

Aster bullatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Aster bullatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
