Adenophora verticillata var. marsupiiflora

Adenophora verticillata var. marsupiiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers with five notched lobes. The flowers are typically pink to purple and bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
