Aristea angolensis subsp. acutivalvis

Aristea angolensis subsp.

acutivalvis, commonly known as the angular-leaved aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct, angular appearance, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
