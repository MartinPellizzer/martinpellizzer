Anemone nemorosa subsp. amurensis

Anemone nemorosa subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features erect stems that bear showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant has palmately divided, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and serrated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
