Aegilops markgrafii var. polyathera

Aegilops markgrafii var. polyathera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant produces wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in open habitats such as fields and disturbed areas. It has been studied for its potential in forage and as a model species in plant genetics research.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
