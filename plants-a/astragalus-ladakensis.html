Astragalus ladakensis

Astragalus ladakensis, commonly known as the Ladakh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Ladakh.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited water availability.
