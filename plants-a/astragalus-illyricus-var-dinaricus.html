Astragalus illyricus var. dinaricus

Astragalus illyricus var. dinaricus, commonly known as the Dinaric milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
