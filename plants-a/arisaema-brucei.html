Arisaema brucei

Arisaema brucei, commonly known as Bruce's arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts.

Arisaema brucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
