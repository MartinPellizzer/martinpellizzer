Asplenium hallii

Asplenium hallii, commonly known as the Hall's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows on rocks or trees, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium hallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
