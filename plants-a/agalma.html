Agalma

Agalma, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nelumbonaceae family.

It is native to the wetlands of Asia, particularly China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from water. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and float on the surface of the water.

Agalma is revered in many cultures for its symbolic and medicinal properties.
