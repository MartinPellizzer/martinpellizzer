Ageratina thyrsiflora

Ageratina thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysothyrse ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ageratina thyrsiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
