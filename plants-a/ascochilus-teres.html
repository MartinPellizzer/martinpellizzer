Ascochilus teres

Ascochilus teres, commonly known as the knobbed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
