Agonis elliptica

Agonis elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Agonis elliptica is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
