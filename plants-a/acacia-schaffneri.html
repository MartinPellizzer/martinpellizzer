Acacia schaffneri

Acacia schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, feathery leaves that are often shed during dry seasons. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia schaffneri is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its environment.
