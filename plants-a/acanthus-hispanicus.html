Acanthus hispanicus

Acanthus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish acanthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features striking, palmate leaves with deep lobes and spiny margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems with clusters of small, violet-blue flowers in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its architectural form and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
