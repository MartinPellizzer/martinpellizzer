Aspalathus latifolia

Aspalathus latifolia, commonly known as the African tea or rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and distinctive red-brown flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, Mediterranean climate. Its scientific name, Aspalathus latifolia, reflects its broad leaves and the genus Aspalathus, which includes several species of legumes.

The plant is notable for its unique chemical composition, which contributes to its use in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
