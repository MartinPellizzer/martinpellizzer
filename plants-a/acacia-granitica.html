Acacia granitica

Acacia granitica, commonly known as the granite acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and can withstand prolonged droughts.

Acacia granitica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
