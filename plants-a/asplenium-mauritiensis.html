Asplenium mauritiensis

Asplenium mauritiensis, commonly known as the Mauritius spleenwort, is a small evergreen fern native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and intricate frond structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rock surfaces or in crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium mauritiensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
