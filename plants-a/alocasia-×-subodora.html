Alocasia × subodora

Alocasia × subodora, commonly known as the Black Velvet Alocasia, is a hybrid plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage that ranges from deep purple to black, with intricate vein patterns. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Alocasia × subodora is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
