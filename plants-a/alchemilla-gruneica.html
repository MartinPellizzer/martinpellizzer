Alchemilla gruneica

Alchemilla gruneica, commonly known as green alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that are typically green, with a slightly succulent texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in branched inflorescences.

Alchemilla gruneica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
