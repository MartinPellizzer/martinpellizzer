Asparagus faulkneri

Asparagus faulkneri, commonly known as the Falkner's asparagus, is a rare and endemic plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
