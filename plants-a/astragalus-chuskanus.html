Astragalus chuskanus

Astragalus chuskanus, commonly known as the Chuskan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
