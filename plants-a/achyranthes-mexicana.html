Achyranthes mexicana

Achyranthes mexicana, commonly known as Mexican knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, terminal clusters, which are followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
