Anacamptis coriophora f. virescens

Anacamptis coriophora f. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its unique, spurred orchid flowers that are typically green or light green in color. This orchid prefers calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its floral structure includes a lip that is distinctly shaped and often has a contrasting coloration.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
