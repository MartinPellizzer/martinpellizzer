Alstonia acuminata

Alstonia acuminata, commonly known as the African starwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, star-shaped fruits. The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
