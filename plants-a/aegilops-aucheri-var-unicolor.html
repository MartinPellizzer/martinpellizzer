Aegilops aucheri var. unicolor

Aegilops aucheri var. unicolor, commonly known as the purple wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale to purple-colored florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
