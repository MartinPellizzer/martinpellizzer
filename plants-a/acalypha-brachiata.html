Acalypha brachiata

Acalypha brachiata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often palmate, with lobes radiating from a central point, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acalypha brachiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
