Acer fulgens

Acer fulgens, commonly known as the flame maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red foliage that turns orange and yellow in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with coarsely toothed margins. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The tree produces samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds via wind.
