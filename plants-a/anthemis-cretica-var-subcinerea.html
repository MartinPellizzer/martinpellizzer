Anthemis cretica var. subcinerea

Anthemis cretica var. subcinerea, commonly known as the silver anthemion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features silvery-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
