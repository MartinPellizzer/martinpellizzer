Anthemis santolinoides

Anthemis santolinoides, commonly known as the Santolin-like Anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
