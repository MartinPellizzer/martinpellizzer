Artanthe variegata

Artanthe variegata, commonly known as the variegated artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped with distinctive variegation patterns of white and green. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking yet low-maintenance plant.
