Anemone glabrata

Anemone glabrata, commonly known as the naked anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Anemone glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
