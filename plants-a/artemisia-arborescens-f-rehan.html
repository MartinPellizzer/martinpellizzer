Artemisia arborescens f. rehan

Artemisia arborescens f. rehan, commonly known as the silverleaf tarragon, is a variant of the Artemisia genus characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray foliage.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures. Its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils, giving it a distinct scent and making it useful in herbal remedies.

This form is particularly noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh climates.
