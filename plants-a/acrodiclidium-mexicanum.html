Acrodiclidium mexicanum

Acrodiclidium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces bright orange or yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Acrodiclidium mexicanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and indoor collections.
