Asplenium alfredii

Asplenium alfredii, commonly known as the Alfred's spleenwort, is a rare and endangered fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that typically grow in dense, shaded environments. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores in specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves. Asplenium alfredii thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soil and is often found on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
