Alyssum boliviense

Alyssum boliviense, commonly known as Bolivian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. Alyssum boliviense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
