Aegilops × vulgaritruncialis

Aegilops × vulgaritruncialis, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a polyploid grass species that exhibits a mix of traits from both wheat and rye.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and spikelets that bear small, awned grains. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and has been introduced to various parts of the world for agricultural and ecological purposes. It possesses a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improve soil structure.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is also being studied as a potential alternative to traditional wheat in sustainable farming systems.
