Aconitum moldavicum f. pilocarpum

Aconitum moldavicum f. pilocarpum, commonly known as the Moldavian aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
