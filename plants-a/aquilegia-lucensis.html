Aquilegia lucensis

Aquilegia lucensis, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven lobes, and the flowers are borne on tall, erect stems.

Aquilegia lucensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
