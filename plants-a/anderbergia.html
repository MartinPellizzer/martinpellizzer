Anderbergia

Anderbergia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Anderbergia species typically grow as shrubs or herbs, often with upright or spreading growth habits. They prefer well-drained soil and moderate light conditions, making them popular houseplants.

The genus includes several species, such as Anderbergia glabra and Anderbergia lepidota, which are appreciated for their ornamental value.
