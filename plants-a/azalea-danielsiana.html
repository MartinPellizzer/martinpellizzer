Azalea danielsiana

Azalea danielsiana, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to eastern China and is characterized by its showy clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy appearance.

Azalea danielsiana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
