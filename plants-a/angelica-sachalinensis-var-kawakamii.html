Angelica sachalinensis var. kawakamii

Angelica sachalinensis var. kawakamii, commonly known as the Sakhalin angelica, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate forests and is valued for its medicinal properties. Its roots and rhizomes contain essential oils and compounds used in traditional medicine.

It is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its tall stature and showy white flowers.
