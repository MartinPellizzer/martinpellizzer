Aspidoglossum xanthosphaerum

Aspidoglossum xanthosphaerum, commonly known as the golden ball lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

Aspidoglossum xanthosphaerum is well-suited for container gardening and thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.
