Acacia semperflorens

Acacia semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom continuously throughout the year. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its widespread use in gardens and landscaping.
