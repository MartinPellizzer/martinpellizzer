Arundinaria kurilensis var. paniculata

Arundinaria kurilensis var. paniculata, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to the Kuril Islands.

It features narrow, green leaves that are arranged in dense, upright clusters. The plant produces panicles of small, white flowers during the summer months. It is characterized by its strong, fibrous stalks that are often used in traditional crafts.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
