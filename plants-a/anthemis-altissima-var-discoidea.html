Anthemis altissima var. discoidea

Anthemis altissima var. discoidea, commonly known as the golden marguerite or stifflet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.

It is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
