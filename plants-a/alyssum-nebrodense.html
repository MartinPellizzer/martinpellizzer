Alyssum nebrodense

Alyssum nebrodense, commonly known as the Sicilian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.

Alyssum nebrodense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
