Aralia racemosa var. sachalinensis

Aralia racemosa var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, typically composed of 10 to 20 pairs of narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in compound umbels, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued ornamental plant in gardens and a subject of botanical study.
