Arthrocereus melanurus subsp. mello-barretoi

Arthrocereus melanurus subsp.

mello-barretoi, commonly known as the Mello-Barreto's night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Brazil. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and flowering habits, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
