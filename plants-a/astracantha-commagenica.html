Astracantha commagenica

Astracantha commagenica, commonly known as the mountain candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a woody stem with branching structures that resemble a candelabra, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces small, spiny fruits.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
