Arabis arcuata var. rubicundula

Arabis arcuata var. rubicundula, commonly known as the red-stemmed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its stems are distinctly reddish in color, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
