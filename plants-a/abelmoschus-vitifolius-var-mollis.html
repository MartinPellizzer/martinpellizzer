Abelmoschus vitifolius var. mollis

Abelmoschus vitifolius var. mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf okra, is a variant of the okra plant characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with sprawling stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible pods in some regions.
