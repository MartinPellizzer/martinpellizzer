Amsinckia spectabilis

Amsinckia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.
