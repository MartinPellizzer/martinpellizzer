Acalypha schlumbergeri

Acalypha schlumbergeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Schlemburger's acalypha, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, followed by spherical, red or orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
