Actinidia grandiflora

Actinidia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are showy, white to yellowish, and often have a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in jams and wines.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
