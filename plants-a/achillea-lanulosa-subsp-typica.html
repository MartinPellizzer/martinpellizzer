Achillea lanulosa subsp. typica

Achillea lanulosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces numerous slender, branching stems.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant often cultivated in gardens.
