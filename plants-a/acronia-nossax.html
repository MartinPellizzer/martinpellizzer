Acronia nossax

Acronia nossax, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and other nightshade plants. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, yellow berries that are edible but can be toxic in large quantities. Acronia nossax is characterized by its dense foliage and adaptability to humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous to livestock and humans.
