Astragalus oaxacanus

Astragalus oaxacanus, commonly known as Oaxacan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas.
