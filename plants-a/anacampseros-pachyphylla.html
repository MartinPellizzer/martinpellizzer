Anacampseros pachyphylla

Anacampseros pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically during the rainy season. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
