Andropogon trichospirus

Andropogon trichospirus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering occurs in summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Andropogon trichospirus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
