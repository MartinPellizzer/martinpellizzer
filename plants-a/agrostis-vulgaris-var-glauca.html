Agrostis vulgaris var. glauca

Agrostis vulgaris var. glauca, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a bluish-green coloration, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It forms dense turf and is often used in lawns and sports fields due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Agrostis vulgaris var. glauca is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
