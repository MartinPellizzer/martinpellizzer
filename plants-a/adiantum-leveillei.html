Adiantum leveillei

Adiantum leveillei, commonly known as the Leveille's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are typically bright green and turn a striking coppery red during the fall season.

Adiantum leveillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
