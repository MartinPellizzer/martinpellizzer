Avena pratensis var. gonzaloi

Avena pratensis var. gonzaloi, commonly known as the Gonzalo's oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and wet meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
