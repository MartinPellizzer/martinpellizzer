Albizia vernayana

Albizia vernayana, commonly known as the African mimosa, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits.

Albizia vernayana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in degraded soils.
