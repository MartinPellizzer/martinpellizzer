Astrantia major var. brevicaule

Astrantia major var. brevicaule, commonly known as the dwarf garden astrantia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features upright stems with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in garden settings.
