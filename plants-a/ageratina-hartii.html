Ageratina hartii

Ageratina hartii, commonly known as white feather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its most distinctive feature is the fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cloud or a feather.

Ageratina hartii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
