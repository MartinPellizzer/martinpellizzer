Alchemilla ayderensis

Alchemilla ayderensis, commonly known as Ayder lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Alchemilla ayderensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
