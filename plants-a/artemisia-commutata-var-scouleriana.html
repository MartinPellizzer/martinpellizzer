Artemisia commutata var. scouleriana

Artemisia commutata var. scouleriana, commonly known as Scouler's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal and aromatic properties have made it historically significant in traditional herbal practices.
