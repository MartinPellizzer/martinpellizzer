Asparagus schweinfurthii

Asparagus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, branching stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
