Anthurium clarkei

Anthurium clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's anthurium, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and veins that are often a lighter shade. Its flowers are typically small and clustered around the spadix, with the spathe often displaying vibrant colors such as red or orange.

Anthurium clarkei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
