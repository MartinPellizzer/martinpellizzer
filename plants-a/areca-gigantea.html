Areca gigantea

Areca gigantea, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into dark blue, fleshy fruits. Areca gigantea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of prosperity in many cultures.
