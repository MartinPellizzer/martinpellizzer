Aechmea longiramosa

Aechmea longiramosa, commonly known as the long-branched pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically long, narrow, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving them a silvery appearance. It produces vibrant flower spikes that can range in color from red to orange, often emerging from the center of the rosette.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea longiramosa has leaves that are elongated, strap-shaped, and arranged in a rosette formation, typically reaching up to 50 cm in length, with a dark green to blue-green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, unbranched stem with a smooth, grayish bark that grows upright from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom during the summer months and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry containing small, black seeds, which are dispersed by animals feeding on the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its native habitat, often in rocky or open areas.
