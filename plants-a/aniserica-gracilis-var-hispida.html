Aniserica gracilis var. hispida

Aniserica gracilis var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy slender anise, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically ranging from white to pale blue in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong anise-like aroma when crushed.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
