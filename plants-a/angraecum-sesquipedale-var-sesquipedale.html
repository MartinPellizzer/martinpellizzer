Angraecum sesquipedale var. sesquipedale

Angraecum sesquipedale var. sesquipedale, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, slender inflorescence and striking, star-shaped white flowers with a prominent central spur. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining potting media. It is known for its nocturnal pollination by the hawkmoth, which is attracted to its nectar-rich flowers.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
