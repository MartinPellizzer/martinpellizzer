Astragalus ceramicus var. apus

Astragalus ceramicus var. apus, commonly known as the Apus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods, a typical feature of the genus Astragalus.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
