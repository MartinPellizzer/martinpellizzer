Aristida pungens

Aristida pungens, commonly known as the sharpgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and have a sharp, pointed appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster that is usually erect and slender.

Aristida pungens is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping and erosion control.
