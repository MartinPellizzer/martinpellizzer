Amygdalus communis var. pistacia

Amygdalus communis var. pistacia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, edible seeds enclosed in a hard shell. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The species is related to the peach and almond family, Prunus, but is sometimes classified separately due to its distinct characteristics.
