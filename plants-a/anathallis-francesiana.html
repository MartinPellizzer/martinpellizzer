Anathallis francesiana

Anathallis francesiana, commonly known as the Francesiana spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture rather than soil. Its flowers are characterized by their spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, intricate structure.

The species is noted for its rarity and is often found in humid, shaded environments at high elevations.
