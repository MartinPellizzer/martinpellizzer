Aphelandra hieronymi

Aphelandra hieronymi, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a striking tropical species native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, gracefully curved leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, spadix-like flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular houseplant and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
