Acinos alpinus subsp. dinaricus

Acinos alpinus subsp.

dinaricus, commonly known as the Dinaric acinos, is a perennial herb native to the Dinaric Alps and adjacent regions. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species.
