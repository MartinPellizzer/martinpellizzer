Austromyrtus diversifolia

Austromyrtus diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in the coastal regions of New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Austromyrtus diversifolia is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
