Aspalathus myrtillifolia

Aspalathus myrtillifolia, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance with a short trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Aspalathus myrtillifolia is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is also noted for its slow growth and longevity, with some specimens living for several decades.
