Arisaema sarasinorum

Arisaema sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers enclosed within a leaf-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
