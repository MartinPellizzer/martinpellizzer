Astragalus lamarckii subsp. tinicus

Astragalus lamarckii subsp.

tinicus, commonly known as the tinicola milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, spiny heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
