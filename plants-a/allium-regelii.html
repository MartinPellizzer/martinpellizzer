Allium regelii

Allium regelii, commonly known as the Regel's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems topped with clusters of pale purple or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its large, globe-shaped bulbs, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Allium regelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to rocky, arid environments.
