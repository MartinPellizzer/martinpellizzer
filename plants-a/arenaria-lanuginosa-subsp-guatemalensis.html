Arenaria lanuginosa subsp. guatemalensis

Arenaria lanuginosa subsp.

guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan moss campion, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically covered in fine, woolly hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
