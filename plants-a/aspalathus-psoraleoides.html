Aspalathus psoraleoides

Aspalathus psoraleoides, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Aspalathus psoraleoides is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
