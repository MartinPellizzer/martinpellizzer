Adicea forsythiana

Adicea forsythiana, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Adicea forsythiana is often cultivated for its unique foliage and is used in both ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
