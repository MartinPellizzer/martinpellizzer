Acosta pomeliana

Acosta pomeliana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

Acosta pomeliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
