Acacia angustissima var. suffrutescens

Acacia angustissima var. suffrutescens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
