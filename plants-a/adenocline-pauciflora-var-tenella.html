Adenocline pauciflora var. tenella

Adenocline pauciflora var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved adenocline, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. Native to arid regions of southern Africa, this plant is adapted to dry conditions with a deep root system. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

The species is noted for its resilience and is of interest in ecological studies due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
