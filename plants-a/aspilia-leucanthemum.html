Aspilia leucanthemum

Aspilia leucanthemum, commonly known as the white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking white ray florets surrounded by yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces a tall, branched stem with numerous flower heads.

Aspilia leucanthemum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
