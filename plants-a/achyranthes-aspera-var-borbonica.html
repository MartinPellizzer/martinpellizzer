Achyranthes aspera var. borbonica

Achyranthes aspera var. borbonica, commonly known as the rough burrweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
