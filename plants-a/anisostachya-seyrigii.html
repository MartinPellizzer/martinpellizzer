Anisostachya seyrigii

Anisostachya seyrigii, commonly known as the Zambian grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green and hairy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and grassland habitats.

Anisostachya seyrigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
