Acrostichum latifolium var. crassinervum

Acrostichum latifolium var. crassinervum, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed fern characterized by its large, broad fronds.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with long, arching fronds that can reach several feet in length. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, coarse venation pattern that gives it its scientific name.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
