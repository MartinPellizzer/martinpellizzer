Alchemilla rutenbergii

Alchemilla rutenbergii, commonly known as Rutenberg's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Alchemilla rutenbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
