Azalea rosmarinifolia var. narcissiflora

Azalea rosmarinifolia var. narcissiflora, commonly known as the narcissus azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, with a rounded, compact form. Its flowers come in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often resembling daffodils, hence its common name.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
