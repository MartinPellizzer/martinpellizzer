Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp. kuzenevae

Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp.

kuzenevae, commonly known as the Kuzenev's delphiniifolium aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant features palmately compound leaves with deeply lobed foliage, which are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
