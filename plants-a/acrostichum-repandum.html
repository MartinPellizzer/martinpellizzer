Acrostichum repandum

Acrostichum repandum, commonly known as the swamp fern, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the fern family and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Acrostichum repandum plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
