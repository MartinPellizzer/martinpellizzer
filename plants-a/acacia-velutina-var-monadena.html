Acacia velutina var. monadena

Acacia velutina var. monadena, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems covered in a dense layer of silky, white hair. The leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are also densely hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are typically visited by various pollinators.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
