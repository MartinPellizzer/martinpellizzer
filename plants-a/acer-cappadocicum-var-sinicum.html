Acer cappadocicum var. sinicum

Acer cappadocicum var. sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 lobes, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
