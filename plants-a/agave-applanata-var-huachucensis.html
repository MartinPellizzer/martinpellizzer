Agave applanata var. huachucensis

Agave applanata var. huachucensis, commonly known as the Huachucensis Agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The leaves are often adorned with white to yellowish marginal teeth, which give the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, greenish-white flowers, which are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

This plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
