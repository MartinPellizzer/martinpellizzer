Astragalus andersianus

Astragalus andersianus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus andersianus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators.
