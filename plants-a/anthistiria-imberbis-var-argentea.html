Anthistiria imberbis var. argentea

Anthistiria imberbis var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf anthistiria, is a variant of the Anthistiria imberbis species characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage.

This plant is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a striking appearance. The flowers are small and usually pale green or yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage.
