Astragalus trojanus var. burnovensis

Astragalus trojanus var. burnovensis, commonly known as the Trojan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
