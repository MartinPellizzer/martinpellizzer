Aspilia mendoncae

Aspilia mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendonca's torch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-red ray florets that resemble flames. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers at the center of its composite head, which are surrounded by the showy outer bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Aspilia mendoncae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
