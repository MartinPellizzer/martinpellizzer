Achillea oxyodonta

Achillea oxyodonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it a hardy and low-maintenance addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
