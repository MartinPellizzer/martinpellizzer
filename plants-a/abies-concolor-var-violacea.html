Abies concolor var. violacea

Abies concolor var. violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf white fir, is a variant of the white fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-tinged foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a slight bluish or purplish hue on the underside. It produces cones that are typically cylindrical and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters.

This variant is often found in high-altitude forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

This variety of the white fir is typically found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. It grows in coniferous forests, often alongside other species such as Douglas fir and lodgepole pine.
