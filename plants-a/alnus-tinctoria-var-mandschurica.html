Alnus tinctoria var. mandschurica

Alnus tinctoria var. mandschurica, commonly known as the Manchurian alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in wet, boggy environments, making it a common species in swampy areas. It has been historically used for its wood and as a source of tannins.
