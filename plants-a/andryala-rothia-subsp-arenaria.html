Andryala rothia subsp. arenaria

Andryala rothia subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the sand saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry environments.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, helping to store water and reduce transpiration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
