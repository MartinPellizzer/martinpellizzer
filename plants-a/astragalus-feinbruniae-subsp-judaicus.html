Astragalus feinbruniae subsp. judaicus

Astragalus feinbruniae subsp.

judaicus, commonly known as the Judaic milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Astragalus species.

This subspecies is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East, particularly Israel and Jordan. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
