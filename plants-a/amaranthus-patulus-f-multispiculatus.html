Amaranthus patulus f. multispiculatus

Amaranthus patulus f. multispiculatus, commonly known as the multispiculate amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth species characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in warm climates, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique flowering structure.
