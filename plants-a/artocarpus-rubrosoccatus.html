Artocarpus rubrosoccatus

Artocarpus rubrosoccatus, commonly known as the red-veined breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, rough, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its fruit is a spiky, round structure that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
