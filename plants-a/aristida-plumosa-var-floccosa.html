Aristida plumosa var. floccosa

Aristida plumosa var. floccosa, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, fluffy, silver-blue foliage that gives it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in low, dense tufts, with slender, wiry stems that often form a mat-like structure. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
