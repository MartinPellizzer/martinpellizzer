Astragalus eurekensis

Astragalus eurekensis, commonly known as the Eureka milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
