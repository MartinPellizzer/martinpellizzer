Attalea hoehnei

Attalea hoehnei, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a species native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which eventually develop into clustered fruits containing edible seeds. Its root system is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in the understory of tropical forests.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
