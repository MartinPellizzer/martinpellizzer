Angelica ebulifolia

Angelica ebulifolia, commonly known as the fragrant angelica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and celery, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and tall, branched flowering stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-like clusters, known as umbels, which bloom in late summer. Angelica ebulifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, with roots and leaves traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Its tall stature and showy flowers make it a notable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
