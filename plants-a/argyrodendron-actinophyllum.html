Argyrodendron actinophyllum

Argyrodendron actinophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
