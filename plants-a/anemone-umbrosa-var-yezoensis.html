Anemone umbrosa var. yezoensis

Anemone umbrosa var. yezoensis, commonly known as the Yezo anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Japan.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
