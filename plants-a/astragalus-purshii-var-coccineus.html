Astragalus purshii var. coccineus

Astragalus purshii var. coccineus, commonly known as the red milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
