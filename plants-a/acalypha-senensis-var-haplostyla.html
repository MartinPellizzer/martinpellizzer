Acalypha senensis var. haplostyla

Acalypha senensis var. haplostyla, commonly known as the silky ironwood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
