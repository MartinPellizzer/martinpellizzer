Abies lasiocarpa

Abies lasiocarpa, commonly known as the subalpine fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically covered with a layer of fine, white pubescence. The cones of this species are usually small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often forming dense stands in subalpine and alpine zones.

The tree plays an important ecological role in these ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
