Acalypha laevigata

Acalypha laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha laevigata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
