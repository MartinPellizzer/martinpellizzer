Alsophila atrovirens var. patula

Alsophila atrovirens var. patula, commonly known as the black-veined tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a dense crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant's fronds are typically dark green with prominent, dark veins that contribute to its striking visual appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
