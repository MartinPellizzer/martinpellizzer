Astragalus danicus f. virgultulus

Astragalus danicus f. virgultulus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
