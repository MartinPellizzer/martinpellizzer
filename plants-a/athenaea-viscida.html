Athenaea viscida

Athenaea viscida, commonly known as the sticky athenaea, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its sticky, resinous secretions that coat its leaves and stems, which may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive in low light conditions. Its foliage is usually dark green and leathery, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring.

Athenaea viscida has limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its unusual chemical composition and ecological adaptations.
