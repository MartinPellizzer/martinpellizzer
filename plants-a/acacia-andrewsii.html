Acacia andrewsii

Acacia andrewsii, commonly known as the Texas acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, meaning the leaves are divided into smaller leaflets arranged in pairs. Acacia andrewsii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along creek beds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
