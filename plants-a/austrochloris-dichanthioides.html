Austrochloris dichanthioides

Austrochloris dichanthioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
