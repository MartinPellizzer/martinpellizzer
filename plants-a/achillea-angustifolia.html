Achillea angustifolia

Achillea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of linear, gray-green leaves and produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite inflorescences.

Achillea angustifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
