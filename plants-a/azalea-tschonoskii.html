Azalea tschonoskii

Azalea tschonoskii, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, opposite, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is reddish-brown and peels in thin layers.

Azalea tschonoskii thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
