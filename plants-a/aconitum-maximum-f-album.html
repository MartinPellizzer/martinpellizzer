Aconitum maximum f. album

Aconitum maximum f. album, commonly known as the white monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive spurs at the back. The plant produces large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
