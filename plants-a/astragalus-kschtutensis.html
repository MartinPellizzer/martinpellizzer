Astragalus kschtutensis

Astragalus kschtutensis, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with moderate rainfall.

Astragalus kschtutensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
