Aconitum japonicum subsp. ibukiense

Aconitum japonicum subsp.

ibukiense, commonly known as the Ibuki Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive notched appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also noted for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
