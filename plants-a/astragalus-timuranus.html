Astragalus timuranus

Astragalus timuranus, commonly known as the Timur milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus timuranus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
