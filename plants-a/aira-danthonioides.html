Aira danthonioides

Aira danthonioides, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its rapid establishment and dense root system.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
