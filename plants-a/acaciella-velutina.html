Acaciella velutina

Acaciella velutina, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Acaciella velutina is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.
