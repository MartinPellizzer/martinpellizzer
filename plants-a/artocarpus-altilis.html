Artocarpus altilis

Artocarpus altilis, commonly known as breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is both nutritious and versatile. The fruit's exterior is covered in a rough, spiky husk, while the inner pulp is soft and starchy. Breadfruit trees are often cultivated for their edible fruit, which can be cooked in various ways.

The plant also has cultural and economic significance in many Pacific Island communities.
