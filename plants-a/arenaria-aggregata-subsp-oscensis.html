Arenaria aggregata subsp. oscensis

Arenaria aggregata subsp.

oscensis, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of tiny, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
