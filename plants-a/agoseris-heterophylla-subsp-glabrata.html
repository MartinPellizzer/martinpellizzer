Agoseris heterophylla subsp. glabrata

Agoseris heterophylla subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf agoseris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has basal leaves that are smooth and often alternate with smaller, lobed leaves higher up on the stem.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
