Arabis japonica var. macrocarpa

Arabis japonica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as Japanese rockcress, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features basal rosettes of succulent, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth and delicate floral display.
