Amaryllis obliqua

Amaryllis obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces multiple leaves that are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, and it thrives in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.

Amaryllis obliqua is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
