Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. longisepala

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five sepals that are longer and more elongated compared to the typical subspecies.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
