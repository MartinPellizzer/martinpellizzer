Allium acutum

Allium acutum, commonly known as the sharp leek, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium acutum is valued for its aromatic properties and hardiness in colder climates.
