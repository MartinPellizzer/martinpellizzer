Andropogon villifer

Andropogon villifer, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, often appearing in late summer.

Andropogon villifer plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
