Arachniodes sporadosora

Arachniodes sporadosora, commonly known as the spore-bearing fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in distinctive, spore-bearing structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
