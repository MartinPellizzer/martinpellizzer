Andropogon gidarba

Andropogon gidarba, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often found in prairie habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types. Switchgrass is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its potential as a bioenergy crop due to its high biomass yield.
