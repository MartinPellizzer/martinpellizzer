Artocarpus denisonianus

Artocarpus denisonianus, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is botanically classified as a syncarp, develops from the fusion of multiple flowers and contains a central core surrounded by a starchy, edible pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
