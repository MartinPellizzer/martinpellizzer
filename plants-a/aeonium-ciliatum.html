Aeonium ciliatum

Aeonium ciliatum, commonly known as the "Ciliate Aeonium," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins. The plant produces striking inflorescences of yellow flowers during the spring or summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Aeonium ciliatum is popular in rock gardens and as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
