Anemone coronaria

Anemone coronaria, commonly known as the poppy anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and palmate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Anemone coronaria is a perennial herb that blooms in spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
