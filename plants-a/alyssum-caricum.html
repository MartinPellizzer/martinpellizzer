Alyssum caricum

Alyssum caricum, commonly known as woolly alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. Alyssum caricum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
