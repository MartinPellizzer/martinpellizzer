Amomum paludosum

Amomum paludosum, commonly known as swamp ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Amomum paludosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
