Astragalus sempervirens subsp. cephalonicus

Astragalus sempervirens subsp.

cephalonicus, commonly known as the Cephalonic milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
