Abies tsuga

Abies tsuga, commonly known as the hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and dense foliage, with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
