Alchemilla pubescens f. typica

Alchemilla pubescens f. typica, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
