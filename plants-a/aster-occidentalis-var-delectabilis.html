Aster occidentalis var. delectabilis

Aster occidentalis var. delectabilis, commonly known as the Western aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer to early fall.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.
