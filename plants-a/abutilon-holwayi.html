Abutilon holwayi

Abutilon holwayi, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy appearance.
