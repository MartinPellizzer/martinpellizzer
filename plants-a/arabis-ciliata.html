Arabis ciliata

Arabis ciliata, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, white flowers appear in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arabis ciliata is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
