Arundo madagascariensis

Arundo madagascariensis, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, hollow stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and produces small, greenish flowers in panicles. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, riverbanks, and marshes.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in non-native regions.
