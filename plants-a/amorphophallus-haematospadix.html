Amorphophallus haematospadix

Amorphophallus haematospadix, commonly known as the blood-flowered arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a large, red or purple spathe that resembles a flower, which is actually a modified leaf. Its underground tubers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
