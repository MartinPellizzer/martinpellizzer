Aspidium rhaeticum

Aspidium rhaeticum, commonly known as the Rhaetic water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and help in nutrient absorption from the surrounding water.

The spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in moist, shaded areas.
