Athyrium quadripinnatifidum

Athyrium quadripinnatifidum, commonly known as the four-pinnate Japanese shield fern, is a striking fern species native to East Asia.

It features a tall, slender rhizome with feather-like fronds that are deeply divided into multiple segments. The fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, giving the plant its distinctive, intricate appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
