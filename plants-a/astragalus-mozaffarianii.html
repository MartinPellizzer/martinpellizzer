Astragalus mozaffarianii

Astragalus mozaffarianii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
