Airochloa albescens

Airochloa albescens, commonly known as the white feather grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, white to pale green inflorescences that resemble feathers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
