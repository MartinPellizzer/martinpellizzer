Aspidosperma leucocymosum

Aspidosperma leucocymosum, commonly known as the white-veined aspidosperma, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the dry forests of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It is known for its presence in semi-deciduous forests and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

The species has some medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
