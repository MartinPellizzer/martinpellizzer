Asclepias viminalis

Asclepias viminalis, commonly known as green milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with distinctive horns at the base of each flower. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Asclepias viminalis is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
