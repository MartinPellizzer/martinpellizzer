Allium kastambulense

Allium kastambulense, commonly known as the Turkish leek, is a bulbous plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces a bulb that is often used in local cuisines for its mild, onion-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is increasingly cultivated in gardens worldwide.
