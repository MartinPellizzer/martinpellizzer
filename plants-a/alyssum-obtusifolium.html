Alyssum obtusifolium

Alyssum obtusifolium, commonly known as blunt-leaved alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically rounded at the tips, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum obtusifolium is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
