Aconitum napellus var. rhodoleucus

Aconitum napellus var. rhodoleucus, commonly known as the purple aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
