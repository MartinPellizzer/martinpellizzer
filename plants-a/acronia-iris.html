Acronia iris

Acronia iris, commonly known as the blue iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple petals and three sepals that resemble petals, creating a showy floral display. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Acronia iris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
