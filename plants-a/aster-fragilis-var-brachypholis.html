Aster fragilis var. brachypholis

Aster fragilis var. brachypholis, commonly known as the short-stemmed aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
