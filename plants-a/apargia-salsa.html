Apargia salsa

Apargia salsa, commonly known as the salt marsh bluets, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, coastal environments. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters along the shoreline.

Apargia salsa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
