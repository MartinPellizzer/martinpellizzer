Adoxa moschatellina

Adoxa moschatellina, commonly known as the sweet flag or muskroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
