Aristolochia phuphathanaphongiana

Aristolochia phuphathanaphongiana, commonly known as the Thai pipevine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding flower structure. It produces small, dark berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
