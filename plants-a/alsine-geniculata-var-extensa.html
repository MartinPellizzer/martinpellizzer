Alsine geniculata var. extensa

Alsine geniculata var. extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
