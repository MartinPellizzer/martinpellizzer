Aubrieta columnae subsp. croatica

Aubrieta columnae subsp.

croatica, commonly known as the Croatian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are oblanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
