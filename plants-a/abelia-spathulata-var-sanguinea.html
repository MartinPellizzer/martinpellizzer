Abelia spathulata var. sanguinea

Abelia spathulata var. sanguinea, commonly known as the redleaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, and they turn red in the fall. This plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

It is often used in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.
