Asclepias incarnata f. albiflora

Asclepias incarnata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which serve as a natural defense mechanism against herbivores.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
