Aster puniceus var. compactus

Aster puniceus var. compactus, commonly known as the compact aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features dense, compact growth with showy pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces ray florets that are typically 1 to 2 inches long. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
