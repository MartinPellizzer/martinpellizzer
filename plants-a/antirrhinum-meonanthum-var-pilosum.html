Antirrhinum meonanthum var. pilosum

Antirrhinum meonanthum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy redsnap, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
