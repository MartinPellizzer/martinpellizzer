Alsophila crenulata

Alsophila crenulata, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that are often serrated along the edges. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Alsophila crenulata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
