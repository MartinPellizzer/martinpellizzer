Acaena lucida var. villosula

Acaena lucida var. villosula, commonly known as the silky burrplant, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny bracts that enclose the small, yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that are covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understoreys.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
