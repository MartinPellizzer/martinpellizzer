Atriplex velutinella f. appendiculata

Atriplex velutinella f. appendiculata, commonly known as the velvet saltbush variant with appendiculate leaves, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant exhibits halophytic traits, meaning it can tolerate high levels of soil salinity. Its leaves are typically gray-green, with a dense covering of fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

This variety is often found in coastal and inland saline environments, contributing to stabilizing sandy soils.
