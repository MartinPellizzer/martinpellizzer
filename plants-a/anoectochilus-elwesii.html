Anoectochilus elwesii

Anoectochilus elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces intricate, slipper-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Anoectochilus elwesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
