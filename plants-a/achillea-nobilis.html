Achillea nobilis

Achillea nobilis, commonly known as noble yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system and upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of small white to pinkish florets, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a silvery-green underside, and are aromatic when crushed.

Achillea nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
