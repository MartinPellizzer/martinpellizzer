Alyxia retusa

Alyxia retusa, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central pistil. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Alyxia retusa plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly pollinators like bees and butterflies.
