Astragalus graveolens

Astragalus graveolens, commonly known as the stinkpea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and can remain viable in the soil for many years.

Astragalus graveolens is also noted for its strong, often unpleasant odor, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.
