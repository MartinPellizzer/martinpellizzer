Asplenium williamsii

Asplenium williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and possesses dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, narrow shape. The plant typically produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or in shaded forest floors.

Asplenium williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
