Arenaria modesta var. cavanillesiana

Arenaria modesta var. cavanillesiana, commonly known as the Cavanilles' sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
