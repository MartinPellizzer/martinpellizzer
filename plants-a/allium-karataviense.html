Allium karataviense

Allium karataviense, commonly known as the Karatavi garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are edible and have a strong, pungent garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is gaining popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
