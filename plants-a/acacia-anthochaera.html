Acacia anthochaera

Acacia anthochaera, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green foliage and its distinctive yellow flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia anthochaera is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
