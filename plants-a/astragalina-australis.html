Astragalina australis

Astragalina australis, commonly known as the Australian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.

Its seeds are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their hardy nature.
