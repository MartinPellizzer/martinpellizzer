Alnus fruticosa var. rufinervis

Alnus fruticosa var. rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined alder, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves with distinctive reddish veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
