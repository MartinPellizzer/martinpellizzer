Aeonium × gordonii

Aeonium × gordonii, commonly known as the "Gordon's Aeonium," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces striking clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Aeonium species, showcasing genetic diversity and adaptability.

Aeonium × gordonii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
