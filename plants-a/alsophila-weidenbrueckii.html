Alsophila weidenbrueckii

Alsophila weidenbrueckii, commonly known as the Weidenbrueck's tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive trunk that is covered with remnants of old leaf bases, giving it a unique appearance. Its fronds are typically bright green and can grow to significant lengths, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila weidenbrueckii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
