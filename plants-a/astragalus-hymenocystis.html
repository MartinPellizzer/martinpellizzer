Astragalus hymenocystis

Astragalus hymenocystis, commonly known as the bladderpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, inflated pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Astragalus hymenocystis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.
