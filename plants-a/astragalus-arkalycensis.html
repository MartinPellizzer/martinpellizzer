Astragalus arkalycensis

Astragalus arkalycensis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale green pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus arkalycensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
