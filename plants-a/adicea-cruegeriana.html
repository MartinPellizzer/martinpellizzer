Adicea cruegeriana

Adicea cruegeriana, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color.

Adicea cruegeriana is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for arid environments.
