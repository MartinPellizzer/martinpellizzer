Artocarpus teysmannii subsp. subglabrus

Artocarpus teysmannii subsp.

subglabrus, commonly known as the smooth-leafed breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and large, clustered fruits.

The fruit, known as a breadfruit, is typically oval or spherical and turns yellow when ripe. It produces a sweet, starchy pulp that is edible and used in various culinary preparations.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
