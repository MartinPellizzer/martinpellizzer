Astragalus pseudocicer

Astragalus pseudocicer, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus pseudocicer is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
