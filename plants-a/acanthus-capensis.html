Acanthus capensis

Acanthus capensis, commonly known as the coastal acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, spiny leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing along the shoreline.

Acanthus capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
