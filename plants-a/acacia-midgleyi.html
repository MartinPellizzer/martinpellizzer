Acacia midgleyi

Acacia midgleyi, commonly known as the Natal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and long, thin, leafless branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia midgleyi is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
