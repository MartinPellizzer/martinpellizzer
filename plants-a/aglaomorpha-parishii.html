Aglaomorpha parishii

Aglaomorpha parishii, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the island of Mauritius.

It is characterized by its dense, star-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the winter months, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a humid, subtropical climate.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
