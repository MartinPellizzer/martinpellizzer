Agathosma pillansiana

Agathosma pillansiana, commonly known as the Cape tea or buchu, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers are borne in terminal clusters and attract various pollinators.

Agathosma pillansiana is also valued for its essential oils, which are extracted for use in perfumes and aromatherapy.
