Alchemilla dregei

Alchemilla dregei, commonly known as the Dregger's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Alchemilla dregei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
