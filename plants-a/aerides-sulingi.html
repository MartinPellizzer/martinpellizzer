Aerides sulingi

Aerides sulingi, commonly known as the Suling orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
