Acacia ligustrina

Acacia ligustrina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia ligustrina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
