Acalypha welwitschiana

Acalypha welwitschiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Acalypha welwitschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
