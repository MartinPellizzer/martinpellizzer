Anatherum virginicum subvar. glaucopsis

Anatherum virginicum subvar. glaucopsis, commonly known as the bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a bluish waxy coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
