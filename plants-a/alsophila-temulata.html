Alsophila temulata

Alsophila temulata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant grows in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth habit.

Alsophila temulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
