Andropogon chinensis

Andropogon chinensis, commonly known as Chinese switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
