Achyranthemum argenteum

Achyranthemum argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble tiny tomatoes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun. It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
