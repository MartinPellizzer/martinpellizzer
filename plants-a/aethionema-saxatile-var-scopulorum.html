Aethionema saxatile var. scopulorum

Aethionema saxatile var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock aethionema, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to rocky and arid environments in the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its harsh habitat. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular plant among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant species.
