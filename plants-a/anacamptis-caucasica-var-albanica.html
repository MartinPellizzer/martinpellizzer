Anacamptis caucasica var. albanica

Anacamptis caucasica var. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lanceolate leaves, which are typically green and covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems bearing a single, showy flower with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in wet meadows and marshy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is native to the Balkans and is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
