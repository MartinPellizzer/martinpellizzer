Astragalus neoassadabadensis

Astragalus neoassadabadensis, commonly known as the Neo-Assadabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, this plant is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
