Allium dilutum

Allium dilutum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, cylindrical stem that bears a single umbel of white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are small and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium dilutum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
