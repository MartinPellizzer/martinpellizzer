Acer tataricum var. incumbens

Acer tataricum var. incumbens, commonly known as the Siberian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
