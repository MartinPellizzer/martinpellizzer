Allionia pumila

Allionia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf allionia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Allionia pumila is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
