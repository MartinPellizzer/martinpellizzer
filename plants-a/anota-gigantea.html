Anota gigantea

Anota gigantea, commonly known as the giant anota, is a striking plant native to the humid rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, which are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are extensive and capable of absorbing nutrients from deep within the soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
