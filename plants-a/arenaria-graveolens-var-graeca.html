Arenaria graveolens var. graeca

Arenaria graveolens var. graeca, commonly known as Greek sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
