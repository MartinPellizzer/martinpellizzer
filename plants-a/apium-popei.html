Apium popei

Apium popei, commonly known as the Pope's celery, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant features slender, hollow stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Apium popei is valued for its aromatic qualities and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
