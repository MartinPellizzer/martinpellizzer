Acacia hooperiana

Acacia hooperiana, commonly known as the Hooper's acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom from late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that perform photosynthesis. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Acacia hooperiana is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
