Aspalathus smithii

Aspalathus smithii, commonly known as the Smith's bushveld pea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
