Azalea indica var. alba

Azalea indica var. alba, commonly known as the white azalea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate flowers.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
