Artemisia spicigera

Artemisia spicigera, commonly known as silverleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, silvery foliage and aromatic, often pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its leaves contain volatile oils, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and herbal practices.
