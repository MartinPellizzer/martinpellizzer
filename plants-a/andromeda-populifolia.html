Andromeda populifolia

Andromeda populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has opposite, broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Andromeda populifolia typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
