Astrocaryum borsigianum

Astrocaryum borsigianum, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a dark purple, smooth, and waxy drupe that contains a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional practices by local communities.
