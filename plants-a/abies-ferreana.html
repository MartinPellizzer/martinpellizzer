Abies ferreana

Abies ferreana, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous tree native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The Mexican fir plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
