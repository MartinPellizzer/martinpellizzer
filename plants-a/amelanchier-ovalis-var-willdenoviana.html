Amelanchier ovalis var. willdenoviana

Amelanchier ovalis var. willdenoviana, commonly known as the Willdenow's serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red or purple berries in the summer. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, turning vibrant yellow or red in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
