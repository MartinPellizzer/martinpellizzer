Aconitum napellus f. carniolicum

Aconitum napellus f. carniolicum, commonly known as the Carniolan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps and Carpathian regions.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
