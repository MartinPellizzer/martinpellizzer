Abies pectinata var. nebrodensis

Abies pectinata var. nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. The cones are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
