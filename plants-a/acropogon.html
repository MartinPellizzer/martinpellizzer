Acropogon

Acropogon, commonly known as the spike grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where they thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Acropogon species are characterized by their tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spikelets, which are the flower-bearing structures. They often grow in open areas such as grasslands and wetlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

Due to their ornamental value and ecological importance, some species of Acropogon are cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
