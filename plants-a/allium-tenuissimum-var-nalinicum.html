Allium tenuissimum var. nalinicum

Allium tenuissimum var. nalinicum, commonly known as the slender leek or narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered bulbs that resemble garlic. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, arranged in umbels, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and bulbs, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
