Acontias helleborifolius

Acontias helleborifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
