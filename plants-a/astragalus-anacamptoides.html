Astragalus anacamptoides

Astragalus anacamptoides, commonly known as the bent-stem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Astragalus anacamptoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
