Anacolosa densiflora

Anacolosa densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring and summer.

Anacolosa densiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
