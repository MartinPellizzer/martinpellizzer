Albuca lutea

Albuca lutea, commonly known as the yellow albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Albuca lutea is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in warm climates.
