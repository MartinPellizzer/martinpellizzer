Acaena sericea var. robusta

Acaena sericea var. robusta, commonly known as the silky burr or coastal burr, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces bright red, spherical fruits that are often used as bird food. The plant thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
