Acalypha multifida

Acalypha multifida, commonly known as the palmate spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and give the plant a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
