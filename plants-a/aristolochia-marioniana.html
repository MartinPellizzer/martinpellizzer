Aristolochia marioniana

Aristolochia marioniana, commonly known as Marion's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and a distinctive pipe-shaped flower that is typically greenish-white with a purple or reddish interior. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
