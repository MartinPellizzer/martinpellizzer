Achimenes cupreata

Achimenes cupreata, commonly known as the copperleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, copper-colored bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Achimenes cupreata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
