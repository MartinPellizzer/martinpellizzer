Alsophila brachyphylla

Alsophila brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, sturdy trunk and a dense crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Alsophila brachyphylla plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
