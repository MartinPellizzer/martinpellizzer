Amyris apiculata

Amyris apiculata, commonly known as the flame tree or the flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a dramatic and eye-catching appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amyris apiculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
