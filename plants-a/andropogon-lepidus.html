Andropogon lepidus

Andropogon lepidus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon lepidus is valued for its ecological benefits and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
