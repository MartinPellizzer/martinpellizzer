Alstroemeria puberula

Alstroemeria puberula, commonly known as the velvet lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, and are known for their fragrant aroma.

Alstroemeria puberula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
