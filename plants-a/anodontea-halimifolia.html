Anodontea halimifolia

Anodontea halimifolia, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic and can be used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Anodontea halimifolia plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
