Acer intermedium f. latialatum

Acer intermedium f. latialatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maple, is a variant of the intermediate maple characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically a vibrant green during the growing season and turn yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
