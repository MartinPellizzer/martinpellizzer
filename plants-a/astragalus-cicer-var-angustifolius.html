Astragalus cicer var. angustifolius

Astragalus cicer var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
