Arachis prostrata f. angustifolia

Arachis prostrata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf groundnut, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, producing small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, giving the plant its specific epithet "angustifolia.". This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and oil.
