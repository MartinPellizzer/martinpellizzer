Atragene polygama

Atragene polygama, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features velvety leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in controlled environments.

Atragene polygama prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
