Anthemis pseudocotula var. rotata

Anthemis pseudocotula var. rotata, commonly known as the curled anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic essential oil, which is used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its compact form and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
