Anisogonium grossum

Anisogonium grossum, commonly known as the giant anisogonium, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant exhibits unique botanical features, including its robust stem and waxy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
