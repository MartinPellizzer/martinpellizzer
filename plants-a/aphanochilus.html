Aphanochilus

Aphanochilus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often appear without visible leaves, making it difficult to spot in its natural habitat. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of tree trunks or branches without deriving nutrients from its host. Aphanochilus thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
