Azara serrata var. fernandeziana

Azara serrata var. fernandeziana, commonly known as the Fernandezian azara, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often serrated along the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant, yellowish-green color, which attracts various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
