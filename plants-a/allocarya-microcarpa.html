Allocarya microcarpa

Allocarya microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited Allocarya, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its small, woody stems and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

The species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it an important component of local flora.
