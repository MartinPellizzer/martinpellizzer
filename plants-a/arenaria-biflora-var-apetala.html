Arenaria biflora var. apetala

Arenaria biflora var. apetala, commonly known as the barren-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers, though the varietal name "apetala" indicates a reduced or absent floral structure compared to the typical species. Its leaves are typically linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

This variant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
