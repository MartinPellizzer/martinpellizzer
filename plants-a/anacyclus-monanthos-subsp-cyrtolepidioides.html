Anacyclus monanthos subsp. cyrtolepidioides

Anacyclus monanthos subsp.

cyrtolepidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goat's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa.

This subspecies is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
