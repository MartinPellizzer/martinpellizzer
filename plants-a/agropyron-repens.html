Agropyron repens

Agropyron repens, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to form dense mats on the ground. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

Agropyron repens is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
