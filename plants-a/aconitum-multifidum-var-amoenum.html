Aconitum multifidum var. amoenum

Aconitum multifidum var. amoenum, commonly known as the Chinese aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a thick, spindle-shaped rhizome. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, conical inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed or divided segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

However, it is highly toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, making it dangerous to handle or ingest.
