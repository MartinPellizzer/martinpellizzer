Andropogon ramosus

Andropogon ramosus, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in prairie and woodland habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.

Silver bluestem is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization.
