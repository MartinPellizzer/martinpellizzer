Aglaia bourdillonii

Aglaia bourdillonii, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces yellowish-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitats.
