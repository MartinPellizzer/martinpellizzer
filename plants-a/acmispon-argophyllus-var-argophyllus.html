Acmispon argophyllus var. argophyllus

Acmispon argophyllus var. argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions. Its small, blue to purple flowers appear in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is well adapted to rocky soils and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
