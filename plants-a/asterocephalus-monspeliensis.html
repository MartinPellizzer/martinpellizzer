Asterocephalus monspeliensis

Asterocephalus monspeliensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean starhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
