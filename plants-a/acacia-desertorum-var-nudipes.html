Acacia desertorum var. nudipes

Acacia desertorum var. nudipes, commonly known as the barefoot acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, spine-tipped leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for traditional crafts and construction.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
