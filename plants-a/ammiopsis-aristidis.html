Ammiopsis aristidis

Ammiopsis aristidis, commonly known as the "scented ammi," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in compound umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used historically in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
