Aeschynomene mazangayana

Aeschynomene mazangayana, commonly known as African needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.

Aeschynomene mazangayana is used for forage and has potential applications in soil stabilization and erosion control.
