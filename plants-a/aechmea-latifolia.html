Aechmea latifolia

Aechmea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf urn plant, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its wide, waxy, and often brightly colored leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a central cup that collects rainwater. The plant produces tall flower spikes with vibrant red or orange bracts, which are followed by small white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea latifolia is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea latifolia has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating and can grow up to 60 cm in length. The plant has a short, sturdy stem that is often covered in a thick, fibrous sheath resembling bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the dry season, relying on wind pollination.

The fruit is a bright red berry containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.
