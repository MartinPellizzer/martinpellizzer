Astrantia major var. apenninica

Astrantia major var. apenninica, commonly known as the Apennine masterwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
