Adipera × bicolor

Adipera × bicolor, commonly known as the hybrid sweet pitcher plant, is a fascinating carnivorous plant that combines traits from its parent species, Adipera and Sarracenia.

It is native to the wetlands of North America, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features striking, tubular leaves that secrete a sticky digestive fluid to trap insects. Its flowers are typically red or orange, adding a vibrant contrast to its green foliage.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is a popular subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and scientific study.
