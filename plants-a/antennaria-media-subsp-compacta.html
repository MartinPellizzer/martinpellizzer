Antennaria media subsp. compacta

Antennaria media subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the compact mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are densely packed, giving the plant a compact appearance.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
