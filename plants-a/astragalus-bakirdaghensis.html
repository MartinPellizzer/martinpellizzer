Astragalus bakirdaghensis

Astragalus bakirdaghensis, commonly known as the Bakirdagh milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, enabling it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus bakirdaghensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
