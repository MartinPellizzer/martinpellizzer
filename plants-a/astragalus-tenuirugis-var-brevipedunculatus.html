Astragalus tenuirugis var. brevipedunculatus

Astragalus tenuirugis var. brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is well adapted to dry conditions. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
