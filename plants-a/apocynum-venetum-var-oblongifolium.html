Apocynum venetum var. oblongifolium

Apocynum venetum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf dogbane, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety. Its showy, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
