Athyrium guangnanense var. acrocarpum

Athyrium guangnanense var. acrocarpum, commonly known as the Guangnan holly fern, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its spore-bearing structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
