Asparagus juniperoides

Asparagus juniperoides, commonly known as the silver asparagus, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
