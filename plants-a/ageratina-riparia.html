Ageratina riparia

Ageratina riparia, commonly known as swamp ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ageratina riparia is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
