Acanthocereus maculatus

Acanthocereus maculatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
