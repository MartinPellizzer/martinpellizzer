Amaranthus obcordatus

Amaranthus obcordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to ovate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible seeds.

Amaranthus obcordatus is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and drought.
