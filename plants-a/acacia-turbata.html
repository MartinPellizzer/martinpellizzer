Acacia turbata

Acacia turbata, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia turbata is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
