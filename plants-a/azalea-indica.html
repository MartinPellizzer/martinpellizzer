Azalea indica

Azalea indica, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Azalea indica is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
