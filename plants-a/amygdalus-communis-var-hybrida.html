Amygdalus communis var. hybrida

Amygdalus communis var. hybrida, commonly known as the almond hybrid, is a variety of the almond tree that exhibits a mix of traits from different almond species.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed, or nut, enclosed in a hard shell. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are usually pinkish-white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its edible nuts, which are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary applications.
