Achyrocline luzuloides

Achyrocline luzuloides, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Achyrocline luzuloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
