Anil macrophylla

Anil macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant has showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still water bodies.

Anil macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water life.
