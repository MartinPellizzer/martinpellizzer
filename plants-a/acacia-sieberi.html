Acacia sieberi

Acacia sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Acacia sieberi is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
