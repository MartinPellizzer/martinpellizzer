Anoda leonensis

Anoda leonensis, commonly known as the Mexican bluecup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Anoda leonensis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
