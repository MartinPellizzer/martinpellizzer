Anthurium malagaense

Anthurium malagaense, commonly known as the Malaga velvet plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces dramatic spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium malagaense is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
