Allantodia quadrangulata

Allantodia quadrangulata, commonly known as the quadrangular allantodia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Allantodia quadrangulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
