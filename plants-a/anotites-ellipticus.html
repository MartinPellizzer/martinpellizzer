Anotites ellipticus

Anotites ellipticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It has narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the water's surface. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil in wetlands.

Anotites ellipticus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
