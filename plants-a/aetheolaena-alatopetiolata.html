Aetheolaena alatopetiolata

Aetheolaena alatopetiolata, commonly known as the winged petioled grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened leaf blades with a prominent midrib and wing-like structures on the petioles. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural collections.
