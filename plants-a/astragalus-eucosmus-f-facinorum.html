Astragalus eucosmus f. facinorum

Astragalus eucosmus f. facinorum, commonly known as the dark-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or dark violet, often with a contrasting white or yellow base. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
