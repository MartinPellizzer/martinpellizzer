Adenolobus pechuelii subsp. pechuelii

Adenolobus pechuelii subsp.

pechuelii, commonly known as the Pechuel's rock-rush, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, which are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
