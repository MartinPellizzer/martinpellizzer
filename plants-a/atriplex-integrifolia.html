Atriplex integrifolia

Atriplex integrifolia, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain water in dry environments. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its salt-tolerant nature makes it a valuable plant for reclaiming saline soils.

Atriplex integrifolia also plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
