Acacia kirkii subsp. mildbraedii

Acacia kirkii subsp.

mildbraedii, commonly known as the Kirk's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
