Alyssum montanum subsp. serbicum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

serbicum, commonly known as Serbian alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Alyssum montanum subsp.

serbicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
