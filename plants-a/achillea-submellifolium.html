Achillea submellifolium

Achillea submellifolium, commonly known as sweet yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its common name reflects the sweet, honey-like scent of its foliage.
