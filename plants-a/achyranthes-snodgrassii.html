Achyranthes snodgrassii

Achyranthes snodgrassii, commonly known as the desert knotweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by reddish bracts.

The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps it access water from deeper soil layers, making it well-adapted to drought conditions.
