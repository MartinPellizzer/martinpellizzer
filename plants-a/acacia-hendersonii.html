Acacia hendersonii

Acacia hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Acacia hendersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
