Allium sacculiferum var. robustum

Allium sacculiferum var. robustum, commonly known as the giant garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its large, bulbous structure and tall, hollow stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like and can grow up to several feet in length.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like scent.
