Avenochloa versicolor

Avenochloa versicolor, commonly known as the variable panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color, depending on the season. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Avenochloa versicolor is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, where it can become a weed.
