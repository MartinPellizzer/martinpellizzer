Abies homolepis var. tokunaiae

Abies homolepis var. tokunaiae, commonly known as the Tokunai fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly curved. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds enclosed in a woody husk. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
