Asperula calabra

Asperula calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian sweet pepper or sweet asperula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines for their mild, sweet flavor. Asperula calabra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
