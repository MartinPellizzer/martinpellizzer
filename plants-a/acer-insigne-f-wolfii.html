Acer insigne f. wolfii

Acer insigne f. wolfii, commonly known as the red maple variant, is a striking ornamental tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red leaves that turn brilliant shades of crimson in the fall. The plant belongs to the Aceraceae family, which includes various maple species. Its leaves are typically ovate with a pointed tip and a serrated margin.

This cultivar is valued for its seasonal color changes and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
