Anemopaegma umbellatum

Anemopaegma umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Anemopaegma umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
