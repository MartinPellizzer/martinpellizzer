Apostasia elliptica

Apostasia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic spathephyllum, is a rare and unusual orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy, spathaceous flower that resembles a leaf, which is a distinctive trait among orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its humid habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Apostasia elliptica is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
