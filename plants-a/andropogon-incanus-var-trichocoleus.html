Andropogon incanus var. trichocoleus

Andropogon incanus var. trichocoleus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a variant of the bluestem grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
