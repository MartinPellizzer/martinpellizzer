Astragalus pullus var. pubifolius

Astragalus pullus var. pubifolius, commonly known as the pubifoliate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus pullus var. pubifolius is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
