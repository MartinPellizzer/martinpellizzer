Aerangis kotschyi

Aerangis kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pale yellow to white blooms with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
