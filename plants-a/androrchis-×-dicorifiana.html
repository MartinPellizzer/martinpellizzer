Androrchis × dicorifiana

Androrchis × dicorifiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Androrchis pallidus and Androrchis morio.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers well-drained, moist soils and is often found in woodlands or open areas with partial shade. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with purple markings, and it produces a single, long-lasting bloom per stem.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
