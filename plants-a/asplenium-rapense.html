Asplenium rapense

Asplenium rapense, commonly known as the rock spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is often found growing on rocks or in crevices. The plant has a simple, dichotomous branching pattern, with leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

Asplenium rapense is notable for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing rocky habitats.
