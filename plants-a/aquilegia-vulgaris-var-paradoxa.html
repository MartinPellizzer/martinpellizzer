Aquilegia vulgaris var. paradoxa

Aquilegia vulgaris var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Columbine, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically purple or blue, with five petals and two elongated nectar guides. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are upright with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
