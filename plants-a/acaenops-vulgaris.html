Acaenops vulgaris

Acaenops vulgaris, commonly known as the common burr plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its most distinctive feature is the presence of prickly, three-lobed bracts that enclose the fruits.

Acaenops vulgaris is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
