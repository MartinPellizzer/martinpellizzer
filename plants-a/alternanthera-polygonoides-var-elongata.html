Alternanthera polygonoides var. elongata

Alternanthera polygonoides var. elongata, commonly known as the red leaf water plant, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that often display vibrant red or pink hues, especially under strong light conditions. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers moderate to high light levels. Its stems are flexible and can be used to create natural-looking foreground or midground in aquariums.

It is also known for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous white flowers, though this is less common in cultivated settings.
