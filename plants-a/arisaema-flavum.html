Arisaema flavum

Arisaema flavum, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema flavum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
