Acrophorus assamicus

Acrophorus assamicus, commonly known as the Assam acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly Assam, and thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, ornate leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, adding to its visual appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare species and is often found in shaded, humid forest understories.
