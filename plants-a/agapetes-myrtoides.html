Agapetes myrtoides

Agapetes myrtoides, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that often exhibits a silvery or grayish hue due to the presence of fine pubescence. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments, often in cloud forests at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink and are arranged in clusters, while its berries are edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife.

Agapetes myrtoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
