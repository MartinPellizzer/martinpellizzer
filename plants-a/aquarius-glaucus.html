Aquarius glaucus

Aquarius glaucus, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas due to its drought tolerance.

Aquarius glaucus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
