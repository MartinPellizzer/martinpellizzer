Amygdalus × keredjensis

Amygdalus × keredjensis, commonly known as the Keredjens cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, specifically in Georgia. The plant is characterized by its small, sweet, and aromatic fruits, which are typically harvested in late summer. It produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible cherries.

This species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
