Astragalus aboriginorum

Astragalus aboriginorum, commonly known as the Aboriginal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
