Aconitum jaluense var. glabrescens

Aconitum jaluense var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Jaluense Himalayan Monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Himalayas. This variant is characterized by its glabrous (hairless) leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
