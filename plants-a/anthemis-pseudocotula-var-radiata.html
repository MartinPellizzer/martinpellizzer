Anthemis pseudocotula var. radiata

Anthemis pseudocotula var. radiata, commonly known as the radiate anthemis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually erect and branching.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
