Allium croceum

Allium croceum, commonly known as the common garlic.

It is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family, native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its bulb is often used as a spice and has a strong, pungent aroma.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
