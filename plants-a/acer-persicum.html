Acer persicum

Acer persicum, commonly known as the Persian maple, is a deciduous tree native to regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
