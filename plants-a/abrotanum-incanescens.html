Abrotanum incanescens

Abrotanum incanescens, commonly known as white thorn or wild mignonette, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
