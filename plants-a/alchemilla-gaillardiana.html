Alchemilla gaillardiana

Alchemilla gaillardiana, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features basal rosettes of silver-green, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Alchemilla gaillardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
