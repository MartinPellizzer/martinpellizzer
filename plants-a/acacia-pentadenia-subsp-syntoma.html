Acacia pentadenia subsp. syntoma

Acacia pentadenia subsp.

syntoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often sparsely branched stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular or cylindrical clusters.

The leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain seeds with a hard coat.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
