Adelphia mirabilis

Adelphia mirabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland forests or near water sources.

Adelphia mirabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
