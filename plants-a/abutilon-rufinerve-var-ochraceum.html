Abutilon rufinerve var. ochraceum

Abutilon rufinerve var. ochraceum, commonly known as the ochraceous abutilon, is a variant of the species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive ochraceous (yellowish-orange) underside. The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
