Arum triphyllum var. atrorubens

Arum triphyllum var. atrorubens, commonly known as the black or red swamp arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive purple to black spathe that encloses a spadix, which bears small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is known for its unique reproductive structures and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in wet garden settings.
