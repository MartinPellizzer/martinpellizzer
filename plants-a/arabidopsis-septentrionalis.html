Arabidopsis septentrionalis

Arabidopsis septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern Arabidopsis, is a small flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like Arabidopsis thaliana. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cooler climates.

Due to its genetic similarity to Arabidopsis thaliana, it is of interest in botanical and genetic research.
