Amaranthus edulis var. typicus

Amaranthus edulis var. typicus, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and feather-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or red, depending on the variety. Its seeds, which are small and nutritious, are a traditional food source in many cultures.

Amaranthus edulis var. typicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
