Antirrhinum majus var. latifolium

Antirrhinum majus var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The leaves are broad and oval, often with a slightly wavy edge. This variety is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and long flowering period.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
