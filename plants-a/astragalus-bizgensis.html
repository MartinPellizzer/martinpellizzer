Astragalus bizgensis

Astragalus bizgensis, commonly known as the Bizgen milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization in its native habitat.

Astragalus bizgensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
