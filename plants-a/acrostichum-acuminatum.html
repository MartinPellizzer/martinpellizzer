Acrostichum acuminatum

Acrostichum acuminatum, commonly known as the giant reed or common reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist or wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands in its natural habitat.
