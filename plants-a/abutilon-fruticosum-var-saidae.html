Abutilon fruticosum var. saidae

Abutilon fruticosum var. saidae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre Abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments. Its structure is often sprawling or upright, with branches that can be woody or herbaceous depending on the climate.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
