Armeniaca persicifolia

Armeniaca persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy, pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible but less commonly cultivated for commercial purposes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin.

Armeniaca persicifolia is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
