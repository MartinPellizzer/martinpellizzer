Abutilon subprostratum

Abutilon subprostratum, commonly known as the prostrate abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
