Allophylus pachyneurus

Allophylus pachyneurus, commonly known as the African holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
