Alloplectus ruacophilus

Alloplectus ruacophilus, commonly known as the Ruacoli orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure, with showy, often fragrant blooms that attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to its ecological niche and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
