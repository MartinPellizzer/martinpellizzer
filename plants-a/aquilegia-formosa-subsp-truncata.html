Aquilegia formosa subsp. truncata

Aquilegia formosa subsp.

truncata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive red or orange marking at the base of the petals.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically lobed and hairy, adapting to its alpine or subalpine habitat. Its root system is fibrous and helps it survive in rocky, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
