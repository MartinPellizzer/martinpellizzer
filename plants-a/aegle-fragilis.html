Aegle fragilis

Aegle fragilis, commonly known as the fragile aegle, is a rare and endangered species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-colored berry that contains several seeds.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Aegle fragilis is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
