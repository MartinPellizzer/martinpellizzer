Astragalus pestalozzae

Astragalus pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pestalozza milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
