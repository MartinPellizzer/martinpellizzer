Aechmea schiedeana

Aechmea schiedeana, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the environment. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture and may have a reddish or purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, often in late spring or early summer.

Aechmea schiedeana is well adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.

Morphology

Aechmea schiedeana has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a spiral around the central axis, with a waxy blue-green color and a sharp, pointed tip.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, thick base that supports the rosette, with a rough, fibrous texture and a grayish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of brightly colored, tubular flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
