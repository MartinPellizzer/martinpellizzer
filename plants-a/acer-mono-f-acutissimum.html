Acer mono f. acutissimum

Acer mono f. acutissimum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with acute leaves, is a cultivar of the species *Acer mono*.

It is characterized by its finely cut, acutely pointed leaves that display a striking color change from bright green in spring to vibrant red or orange in autumn. The plant typically grows as a compact, upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a standout feature in landscaping, particularly in regions with distinct seasonal changes.
