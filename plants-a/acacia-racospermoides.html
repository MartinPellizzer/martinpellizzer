Acacia racospermoides

Acacia racospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves composed of numerous thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Acacia racospermoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
