Alcea glabrata var. microcarpa

Alcea glabrata var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited hollyhock, is a cultivated variant of the hollyhock plant.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly lobed. The flowers are large, showy, and come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
