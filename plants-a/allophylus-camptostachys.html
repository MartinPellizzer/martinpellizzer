Allophylus camptostachys

Allophylus camptostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often narrow and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Allophylus camptostachys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
