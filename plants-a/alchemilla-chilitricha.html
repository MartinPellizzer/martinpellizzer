Alchemilla chilitricha

Alchemilla chilitricha, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Alchemilla chilitricha is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental foliage and hardy nature.
