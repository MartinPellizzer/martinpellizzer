Aspidosperma carapanauba

Aspidosperma carapanauba, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Copaifera carapanauba*, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
