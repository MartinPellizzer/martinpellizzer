Ampelopsis heterophylla var. citrulloides

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. citrulloides, commonly known as the citron vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape depending on light exposure, a characteristic known as heterophylly. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to orange to red berries in the fall. Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

The berries are edible but should be consumed in moderation due to their mild toxicity.
