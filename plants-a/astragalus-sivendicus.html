Astragalus sivendicus

Astragalus sivendicus, commonly known as the Sivendic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is well-developed, enabling it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus sivendicus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
