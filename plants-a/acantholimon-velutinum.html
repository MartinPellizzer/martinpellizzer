Acantholimon velutinum

Acantholimon velutinum, commonly known as the velvet flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in spring. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name.

Acantholimon velutinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
