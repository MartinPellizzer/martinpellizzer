Achyronia vulnerans

Achyronia vulnerans, commonly known as the bleeding heart plant, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish hue, and its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, pendulous flowers that resemble a heart with a red or purple tip. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
