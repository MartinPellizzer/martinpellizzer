Achillea moschata var. hybrida

Achillea moschata var. hybrida, commonly known as the sweet yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region but has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms contribute to its appeal in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
