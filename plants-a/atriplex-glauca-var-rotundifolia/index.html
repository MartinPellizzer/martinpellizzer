Atriplex Glauca Var. Rotundifolia

Roundleaf saltbush, scientifically known as Atriplex glauca var. rotundifolia, is a hardy shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable plant for reclaiming degraded lands. Its thick, fleshy leaves help it conserve water, adapting to extreme environmental conditions. The plant is commonly used in landscaping and restoration projects due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal. Despite its tough environment, Roundleaf saltbush plays an important ecological role by supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil.
