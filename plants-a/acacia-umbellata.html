Acacia umbellata

Acacia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive flat-topped clusters of yellow flowers, which give it its common name. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Acacia umbellata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its ecosystem.
