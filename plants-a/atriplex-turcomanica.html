Atriplex turcomanica

Atriplex turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan saltbush, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is well adapted to arid and saline environments, often found in desert and semi-desert regions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It is a member of the Chenopodiaceae family, which includes other salt-tolerant plants.

Atriplex turcomanica plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate salts.
