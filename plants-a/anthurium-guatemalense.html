Anthurium guatemalense

Anthurium guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium guatemalense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
