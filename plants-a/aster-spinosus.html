Aster spinosus

Aster spinosus, commonly known as the thorned aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features wiry, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy purple to blue flower heads in the fall, which are composed of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Aster spinosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
