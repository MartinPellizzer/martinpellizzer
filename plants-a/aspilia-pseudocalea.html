Aspilia pseudocalea

Aspilia pseudocalea, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a stout, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The botanical characteristics of Aspilia pseudocalea make it a notable species in its native habitats.
