Acalypha spachiana var. acutifolia

Acalypha spachiana var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with distinctive bracts that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in warm climates.
