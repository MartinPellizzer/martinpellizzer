Aristea lukwangulensis

Aristea lukwangulensis, commonly known as the Lukwangulensis arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its floral structure consists of both ray and disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aristea lukwangulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
