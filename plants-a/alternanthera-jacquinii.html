Alternanthera jacquinii

Alternanthera jacquinii, commonly known as the red velvet plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its striking, velvety red foliage. The leaves are lance-shaped and often display a rich crimson or maroon color, which intensifies in low light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
