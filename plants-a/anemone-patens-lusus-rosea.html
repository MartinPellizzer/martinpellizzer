Anemone patens lusus rosea

Anemone patens lusus rosea, commonly known as the roseate spreading anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit with soft, hairy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to both wildflower gardens and natural landscapes.
