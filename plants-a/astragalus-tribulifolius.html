Astragalus tribulifolius

Astragalus tribulifolius, commonly known as the spiked milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife, though it is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes.
