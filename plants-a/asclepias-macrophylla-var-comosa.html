Asclepias macrophylla var. comosa

Asclepias macrophylla var. comosa, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus, and serves as a food source for various insects, including monarch butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ecological importance and ornamental qualities in gardens.
