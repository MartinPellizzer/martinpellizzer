Apargia crispa

Apargia crispa, commonly known as the brittlestem aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a crinkled or wavy appearance.

Apargia crispa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
