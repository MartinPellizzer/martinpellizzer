Allocasuarina scleroclada

Allocasuarina scleroclada, commonly known as the rigid she-oak, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique cylindrical, scale-like leaves that are dense and often shed in winter. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the Casuarinaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Allocasuarina scleroclada is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in arid and semi-arid regions.
