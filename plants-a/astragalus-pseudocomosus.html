Astragalus pseudocomosus

Astragalus pseudocomosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Astragalus pseudocomosus is often found in the western United States and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
