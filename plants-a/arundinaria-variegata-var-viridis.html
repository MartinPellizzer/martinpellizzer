Arundinaria variegata var. viridis

Arundinaria variegata var. viridis, commonly known as the green bamboo or variegated bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species characterized by its unique foliage.

It features slender, green culms with a subtle variegation pattern that adds visual interest. This plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

The variegation in its leaves is a result of genetic variation and can enhance its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
