Alchemilla baronii

Alchemilla baronii, commonly known as Baron's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla baronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
