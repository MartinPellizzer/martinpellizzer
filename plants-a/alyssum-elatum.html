Alyssum elatum

Alyssum elatum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Alyssum elatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to the landscape.
