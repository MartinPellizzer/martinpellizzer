Arum bicolor

Arum bicolor, commonly known as the white arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a showy, white spathe. The plant produces a toxic, milky sap that can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Arum bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
