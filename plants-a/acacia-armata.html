Acacia armata

Acacia armata, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a fibrous, woody material. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia armata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
