Acacia fasciculata var. pubescens

Acacia fasciculata var. pubescens, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow globular flowers that appear in winter. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny, feathery leaflets, and the plant is often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, tolerating salt spray and poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
