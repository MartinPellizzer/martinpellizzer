Allium moly subsp. glaucescens

Allium moly subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as blue garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a bulbous stem that produces clusters of small, spherical, blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are grass-like, narrow, and bluish-green, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its attractive appearance and mild garlic scent, though it is not typically used as a culinary herb.
