Acacia cuspidata var. longifolia

Acacia cuspidata var. longifolia, commonly known as the longifoliate acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in dense heads, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
