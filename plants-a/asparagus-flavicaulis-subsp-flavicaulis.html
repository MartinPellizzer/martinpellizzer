Asparagus flavicaulis subsp. flavicaulis

Asparagus flavicaulis subsp.

flavicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and small, fern-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
