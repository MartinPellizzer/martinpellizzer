Achillea stepposa

Achillea stepposa, commonly known as steppe yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, silvery-gray leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs.

Achillea stepposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
