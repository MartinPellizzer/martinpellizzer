Astragalus turgidocarpus

Astragalus turgidocarpus, commonly known as the swollenpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus turgidocarpus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
