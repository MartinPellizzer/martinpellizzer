Ascolepis neglecta

Ascolepis neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Ascolepis neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
