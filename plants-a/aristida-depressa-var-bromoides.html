Aristida depressa var. bromoides

Aristida depressa var. bromoides, commonly known as blue grama, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and grasslands, and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its fine texture and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
