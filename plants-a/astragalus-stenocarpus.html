Astragalus stenocarpus

Astragalus stenocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume that contains several narrow, elongated seeds.

Astragalus stenocarpus is part of the legume family (Fabaceae) and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
