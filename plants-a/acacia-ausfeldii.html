Acacia ausfeldii

Acacia ausfeldii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Acacia ausfeldii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
