Astragalus monspessulanus var. aurasiacus

Astragalus monspessulanus var. aurasiacus, commonly known as the Aurasiac Yellow Vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and its role in ecological restoration projects.
