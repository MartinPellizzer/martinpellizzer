Anthericum adenanthera

Anthericum adenanthera, commonly known as the African arum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf with a prominent midrib and a waxy, spathaceous leaf sheath. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
