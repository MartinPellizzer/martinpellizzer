Aspalathus collina

Aspalathus collina, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
