Anthemis cypria

Anthemis cypria, commonly known as Cyprus ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are often surrounded by green bracts.

Anthemis cypria is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
