Alpinia nutans var. sericea

Alpinia nutans var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright rhizomes and is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, cylindrical berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
