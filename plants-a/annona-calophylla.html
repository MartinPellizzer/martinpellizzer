Annona calophylla

Annona calophylla, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, green, fuzzy fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, custard-like pulp with a sweet and tangy flavor.

It is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
