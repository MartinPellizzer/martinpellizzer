Acalypha boiviniana

Acalypha boiviniana, commonly known as the red-flowered false hibiscus, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Acalypha boiviniana thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
