Apatesia

Apatesia, commonly known as the false orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where they thrive in moist, shaded environments. Apatesia species are characterized by their delicate, often showy flowers that resemble true orchids, though they are not closely related. The plants have rhizomatous growth habits and produce small, green leaves that are often clustered at the base.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and their role in ecological systems as pollinator attractants.
