Arnoldia heterophylla

Arnoldia heterophylla, commonly known as the rock wallaby tree, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery leaves that vary in shape and size along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
