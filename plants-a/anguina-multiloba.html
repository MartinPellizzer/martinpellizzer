Anguina multiloba

Anguina multiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Anguina multiloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
