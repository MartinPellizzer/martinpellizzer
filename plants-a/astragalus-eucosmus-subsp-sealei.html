Astragalus eucosmus subsp. sealei

Astragalus eucosmus subsp.

sealei, commonly known as the Seale's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
