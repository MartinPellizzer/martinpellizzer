Aconitum hopeiense

Aconitum hopeiense, commonly known as the Hope's aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum hopeiense is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
