Avena pratensis f. pauciflora

Avena pratensis f. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender oat, is a botanical variant of the common oat.

It is characterized by its reduced flower number, giving it a less dense appearance compared to the typical form. This grass species typically grows in temperate regions and prefers moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The plant is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
