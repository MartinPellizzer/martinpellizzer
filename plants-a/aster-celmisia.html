Aster celmisia

Aster celmisia, commonly known as the fairybells, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster celmisia is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for native pollinators.
