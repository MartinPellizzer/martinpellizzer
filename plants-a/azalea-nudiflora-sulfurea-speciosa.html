Azalea nudiflora sulfurea-speciosa

Azalea nudiflora sulfurea-speciosa, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the cultivar. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and floral display.

This azalea is also noted for its ability to produce fruit, which contains small, dark seeds.
