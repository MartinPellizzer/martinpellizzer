Allium filidentiforme

Allium filidentiforme, commonly known as the slender-leaved garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its bulbs are similar to garlic but are less commonly cultivated.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
