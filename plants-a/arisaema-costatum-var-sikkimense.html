Arisaema costatum var. sikkimense

Arisaema costatum var. sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The ornamental value of its foliage and unique floral structure makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
