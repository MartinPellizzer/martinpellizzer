Asplenium chinense

Asplenium chinense, commonly known as the Chinese spleenwort, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spores produced on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium chinense is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
