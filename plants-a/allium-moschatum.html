Allium moschatum

Allium moschatum, commonly known as the sweet garlic or wild garlic, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its bulbs are often used in cooking for their mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium moschatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
