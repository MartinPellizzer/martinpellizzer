Adiantum ogasawarense

Adiantum ogasawarense, commonly known as the Ogasawara maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its rhizomes are short and produce a rosette of pinnate leaves.

Adiantum ogasawarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
