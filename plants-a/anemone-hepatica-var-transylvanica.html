Anemone hepatica var. transylvanica

Anemone hepatica var. transylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are broadly heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are bright yellow with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in rich, loamy soil. It blooms in early spring, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
