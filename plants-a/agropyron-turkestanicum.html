Agropyron turkestanicum

Agropyron turkestanicum, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It is adapted to thrive in dry, arid environments and is often found in steppes and sandy soils.

Agropyron turkestanicum is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is used in ecological restoration projects.
