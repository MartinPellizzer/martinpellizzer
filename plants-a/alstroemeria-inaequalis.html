Alstroemeria inaequalis

Alstroemeria inaequalis, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alstroemeria inaequalis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
