Anil heterophylla

Anil heterophylla, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, often inflated leaves that can trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant is a carnivorous species, using its bladder-like structures to capture and digest prey. It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on aquatic plant adaptations and carnivory.
