Acalypha subviscida var. lovelandii

Acalypha subviscida var. lovelandii, commonly known as the Love's acalypha, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a deep red or maroon coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique foliage and is often used in landscape design for its visual appeal.
