Anthericum spirale

Anthericum spirale, commonly known as the spiral lily, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Anthericum spirale thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
