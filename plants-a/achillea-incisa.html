Achillea incisa

Achillea incisa, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of numerous small white to pinkish flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

Achillea incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
