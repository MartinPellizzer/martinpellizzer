Acer laurinum

Acer laurinum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays a range of colors from bright green to deep red during the growing season. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green in spring and turn vibrant red or orange in autumn. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

Acer laurinum thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.
