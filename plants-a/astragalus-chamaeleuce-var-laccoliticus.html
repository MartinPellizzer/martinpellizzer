Astragalus chamaeleuce var. laccoliticus

Astragalus chamaeleuce var. laccoliticus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many Astragalus species. This variety is native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
