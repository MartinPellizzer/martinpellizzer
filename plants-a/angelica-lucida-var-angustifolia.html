Angelica lucida var. angustifolia

Angelica lucida var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf angelica, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It features large, compound leaves that are narrow and elongated, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbrella-like inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
