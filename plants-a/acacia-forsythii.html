Acacia forsythii

Acacia forsythii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in soil stabilization.

Acacia forsythii is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
