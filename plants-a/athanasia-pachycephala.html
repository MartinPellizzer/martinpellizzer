Athanasia pachycephala

Athanasia pachycephala, commonly known as the thick-headed alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Athanasia pachycephala is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sunny conditions.
