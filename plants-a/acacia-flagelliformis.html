Acacia flagelliformis

Acacia flagelliformis, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, whip-like branches and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, oval pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

Acacia flagelliformis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
