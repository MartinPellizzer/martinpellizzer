Amarenus flavus

Amarenus flavus, commonly known as the yellow amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are small and often used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.

Amarenus flavus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking color and foliage.
