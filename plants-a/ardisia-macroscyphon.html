Ardisia macroscyphon

Ardisia macroscyphon, commonly known as the large-fruited beautyberry, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, dark purple to black berries that are highly ornamental and attract birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
