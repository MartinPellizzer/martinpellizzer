Actinea linearifolia var. dodgei

Actinea linearifolia var. dodgei, commonly known as the Dodges' linear-leaf actinea, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
