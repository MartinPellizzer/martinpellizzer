Agave acklinicola

Agave acklinicola, commonly known as the Mexican rock agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and covered in a whitish bloom. The plant grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, greenish flowers, which are followed by red berries.

Agave acklinicola is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
