Acacia buxifolia var. subvelutina

Acacia buxifolia var. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a soft, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
