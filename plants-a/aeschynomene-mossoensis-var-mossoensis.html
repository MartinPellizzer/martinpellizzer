Aeschynomene mossoensis var. mossoensis

Aeschynomene mossoensis var. mossoensis, commonly known as the Mosso's goatherb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
