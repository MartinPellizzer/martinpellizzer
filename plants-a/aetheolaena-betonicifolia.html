Aetheolaena betonicifolia

Aetheolaena betonicifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring sturdy, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are large, ovate, and often have a waxy texture with a deep green color. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
