Adyseton obtusifolium

Adyseton obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf adyseton, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Adyseton obtusifolium is characterized by its slow growth and preference for shaded environments.
