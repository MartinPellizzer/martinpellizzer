Arundinaria usawae

Arundinaria usawae, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Usawa bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, greenish flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

Arundinaria usawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural characteristics.
