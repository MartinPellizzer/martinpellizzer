Astragalus piestolobus

Astragalus piestolobus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid environments.

Astragalus piestolobus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
