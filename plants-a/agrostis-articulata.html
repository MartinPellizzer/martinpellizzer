Agrostis articulata

Agrostis articulata, commonly known as the bentgrass or silvergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in various habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Agrostis articulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
