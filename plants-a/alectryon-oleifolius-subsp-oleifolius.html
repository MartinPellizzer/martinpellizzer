Alectryon oleifolius subsp. oleifolius

Alectryon oleifolius subsp.

oleifolius, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Additionally, it has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
