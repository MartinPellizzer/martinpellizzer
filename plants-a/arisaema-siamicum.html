Arisaema siamicum

Arisaema siamicum, commonly known as the Siamese arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a thick, starchy rhizome that is used as a food source in some regions. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowering structure is adapted for pollination by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
