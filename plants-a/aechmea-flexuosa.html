Aechmea flexuosa

Aechmea flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea flexuosa is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea flexuosa has leaves that are broad, strap-like, and often recurved, arranged in a rosette formation, with a dark green to bluish-gray color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a true stem, instead having a short, stout base with a fibrous, rough-textured bark that is typically greenish-brown in color. Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the late summer to early autumn months and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its natural habitat, often in rocky or wooded areas.
