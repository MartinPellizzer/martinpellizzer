Anatherostipa rosea

Anatherostipa rosea, commonly known as the rose hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive rose-colored inflorescence. The plant features slender, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Anatherostipa rosea typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open grasslands or along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
