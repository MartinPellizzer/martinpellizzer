Aster euthamia

Aster euthamia, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stems. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster euthamia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
