Allium verrucosum

Allium verrucosum, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous roots and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and mild garlic-like scent, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
