Astragalus kunarensis

Astragalus kunarensis, commonly known as the Kunar mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
