Archidendron syringifolium

Archidendron syringifolium, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
