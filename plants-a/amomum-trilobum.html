Amomum trilobum

Amomum trilobum, commonly known as small cardamom, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, clustered rhizomes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their unique flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum trilobum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its economic and medicinal value.
