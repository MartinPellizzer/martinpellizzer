Aster foliaceus subsp. frondeus

Aster foliaceus subsp.

frondeus, commonly known as the fronded leaf aster, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
