Angelica scabra

Angelica scabra, commonly known as common angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring large, compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which develop into oval, dry fruits. Angelica scabra is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
