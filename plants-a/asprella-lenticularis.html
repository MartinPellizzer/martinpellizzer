Asprella lenticularis

Asprella lenticularis, commonly known as the lens-shaped acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Asprella lenticularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
