Asplenium × recoderi

Asplenium × recoderi, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through spore dispersal.

Asplenium × recoderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
