Abutilon hirsutissimum

Abutilon hirsutissimum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in some regions.
