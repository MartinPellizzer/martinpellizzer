Adicea thymifolia

Adicea thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small, evergreen leaves that resemble those of thyme. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or sandy substrates.

Adicea thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
