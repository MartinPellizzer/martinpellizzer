Acacia oerstedii

Acacia oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to dry climates, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions. Its wood is lightweight and durable, making it useful for local crafts and construction.

Acacia oerstedii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
