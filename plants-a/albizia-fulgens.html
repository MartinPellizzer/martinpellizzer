Albizia fulgens

Albizia fulgens, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and have a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Albizia fulgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
