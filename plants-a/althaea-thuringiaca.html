Althaea thuringiaca

Althaea thuringiaca, commonly known as the German marsh mallow, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

Althaea thuringiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
