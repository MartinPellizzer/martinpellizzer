Astragalus pastorius var. linearibracteatus

Astragalus pastorius var. linearibracteatus, commonly known as the linear-bracteled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers clustered in dense spikes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy, drought-tolerant nature.
