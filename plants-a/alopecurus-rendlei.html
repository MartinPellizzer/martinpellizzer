Alopecurus rendlei

Alopecurus rendlei, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its tolerance to wet conditions.

Alopecurus rendlei plays a role in maintaining ecosystem diversity and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
