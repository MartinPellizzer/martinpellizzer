Aeridostachya mearnsii

Aeridostachya mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns' spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
