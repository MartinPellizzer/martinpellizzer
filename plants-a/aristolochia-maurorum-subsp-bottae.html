Aristolochia maurorum subsp. bottae

Aristolochia maurorum subsp.

bottae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a strong, sometimes unpleasant taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
