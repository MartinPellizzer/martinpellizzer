Amorpha fruticosa var. occidentalis

Amorpha fruticosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as western false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with pairs of oval, dark green leaflets and produces showy, pea-like purple flowers in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It has a woody, multi-stemmed structure and can grow up to 6 feet tall.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its native habitat.
