Acacia decurrens var. deanei

Acacia decurrens var. deanei, commonly known as the Deane's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which appear during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
