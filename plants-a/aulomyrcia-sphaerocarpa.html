Aulomyrcia sphaerocarpa

Aulomyrcia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globular acanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
