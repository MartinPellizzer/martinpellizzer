Aria hibernica

Aria hibernica, commonly known as the Irish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe, particularly Ireland.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Aria hibernica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
