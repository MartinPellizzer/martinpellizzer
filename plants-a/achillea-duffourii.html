Achillea duffourii

Achillea duffourii, commonly known as Dufour's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is known for its delicate, fern-like foliage. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Dufour's yarrow is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
