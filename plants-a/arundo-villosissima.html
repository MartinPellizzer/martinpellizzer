Arundo villosissima

Arundo villosissima, commonly known as the silky reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth and grayish-green, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stalks that bear small, clustered spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near streams, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
