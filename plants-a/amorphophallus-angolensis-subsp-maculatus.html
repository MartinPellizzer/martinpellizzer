Amorphophallus angolensis subsp. maculatus

Amorphophallus angolensis subsp.

maculatus, commonly known as the spotted African giant-centella, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern on the underside.

The plant produces a tall, spathaceous inflorescence that bears small, white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually striking member of the Araceae family.
