Aristida gossweileri

Aristida gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
