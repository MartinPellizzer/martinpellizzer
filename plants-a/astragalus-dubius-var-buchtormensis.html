Astragalus dubius var. buchtormensis

Astragalus dubius var. buchtormensis, commonly known as the Buchtorm Astragalus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
