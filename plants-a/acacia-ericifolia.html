Acacia ericifolia

Acacia ericifolia, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in maritime climates. Its root system helps stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.

Acacia ericifolia is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
