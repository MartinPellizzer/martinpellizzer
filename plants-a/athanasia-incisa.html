Athanasia incisa

Athanasia incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. It is known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact for extended periods, making it popular in floral arrangements.

Athanasia incisa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
