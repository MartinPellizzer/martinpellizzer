Aster xylophyllus

Aster xylophyllus, commonly known as the wood aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
