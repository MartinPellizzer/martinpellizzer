Acacia nodiflora var. ferox

Acacia nodiflora var. ferox, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, woody pods. It is well adapted to dry and sandy soils, making it a common species in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its rapid growth and allelopathic properties, it is considered a weed in some areas.
