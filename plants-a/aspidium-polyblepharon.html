Aspidium polyblepharon

Aspidium polyblepharon, commonly known as the veined water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Aspidium polyblepharon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
