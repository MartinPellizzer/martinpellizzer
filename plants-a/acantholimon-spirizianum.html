Acantholimon spirizianum

Acantholimon spirizianum, commonly known as the spiral-leaved stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial succulent with a rosette-forming growth habit, characterized by its fleshy, spiny leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique spiral arrangement of leaves and ornamental flowers make it a distinctive addition to alpine and desert landscapes.
