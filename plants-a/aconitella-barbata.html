Aconitella barbata

Aconitella barbata, commonly known as the barbed aconite, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, spiny fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is handled with caution.
