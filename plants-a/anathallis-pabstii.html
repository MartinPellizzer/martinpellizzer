Anathallis pabstii

Anathallis pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's spider orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a slender, hairy stem and a single, showy flower with intricate patterns. The plant exhibits a mycorrhizal relationship with specific fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake. Anathallis pabstii is an epiphytic plant, growing on the trunks of trees without deriving nutrients from its host.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
