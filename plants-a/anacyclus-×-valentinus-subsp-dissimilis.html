Anacyclus × valentinus subsp. dissimilis

Anacyclus × valentinus subsp.

dissimilis, commonly known as the Persian melon or melonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. It has a short stature with a rosette of basal leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical collections.
