Anisophyllea thouarsiana

Anisophyllea thouarsiana, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to red, often with a prominent central disk. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Anisophyllea thouarsiana is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
