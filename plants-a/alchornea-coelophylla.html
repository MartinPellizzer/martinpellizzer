Alchornea coelophylla

Alchornea coelophylla, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest ecosystems.

Alchornea coelophylla is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local biodiversity.
