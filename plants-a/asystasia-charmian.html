Asystasia charmian

Asystasia charmian, commonly known as the velvetleaf asystasia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asystasia charmian is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
