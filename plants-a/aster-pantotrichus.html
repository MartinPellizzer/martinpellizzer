Aster pantotrichus

Aster pantotrichus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-blue ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Aster pantotrichus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
