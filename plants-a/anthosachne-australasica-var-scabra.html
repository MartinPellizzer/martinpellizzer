Anthosachne australasica var. scabra

Anthosachne australasica var. scabra, commonly known as the rough anthosachne, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant produces small, pale-green spikelets that are clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
