Astragalus sympileicalycinus

Astragalus sympileicalycinus, commonly known as the medicinal milkvetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical pods containing lens-shaped seeds. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppes and deserts.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it has been studied for its bioactive compounds and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
