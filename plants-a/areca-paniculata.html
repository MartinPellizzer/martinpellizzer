Areca paniculata

Areca paniculata, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a striking red or purple calyx. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Areca paniculata is also known for its ability to purify the air and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
