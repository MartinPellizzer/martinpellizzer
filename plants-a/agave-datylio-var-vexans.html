Agave datylio var. vexans

Agave datylio var. vexans, commonly known as the Mexican fire plant, is a succulent native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-green leaves that are often adorned with red or purple margins. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, greenish-white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
