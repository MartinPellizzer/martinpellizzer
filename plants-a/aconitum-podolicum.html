Aconitum podolicum

Aconitum podolicum, commonly known as Podolic monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided, hairy leaves that are typically lobed and arranged alternately along the stem. Aconitum podolicum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
