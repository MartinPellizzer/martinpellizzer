Athanasia ramosa

Athanasia ramosa, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often blooming in summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Athanasia ramosa is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
