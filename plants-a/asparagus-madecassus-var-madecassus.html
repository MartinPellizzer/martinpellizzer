Asparagus madecassus var. madecassus

Asparagus madecassus var. madecassus, commonly known as the Madagascar asparagus, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It features slender, arching stems that produce small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous thread-like leaflets, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
