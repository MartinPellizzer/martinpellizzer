Actinidia ulmifolia

Actinidia ulmifolia, commonly known as the Japanese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
