Acanthopanax cissifolius

Acanthopanax cissifolius, commonly known as the Chinese angel's trumpet, is a woody shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and is often found in forested areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
