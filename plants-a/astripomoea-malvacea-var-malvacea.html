Astripomoea malvacea var. malvacea

Astripomoea malvacea var. malvacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Malvaceae species, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides and in abandoned fields.

Its growth habit makes it a common weed in agricultural settings.
