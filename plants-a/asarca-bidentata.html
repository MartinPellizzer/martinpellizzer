Asarca bidentata

Asarca bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed asarum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, mottled, reddish-brown flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Asarca bidentata is often found in clusters, with its underground rhizomes contributing to its growth and spread.
