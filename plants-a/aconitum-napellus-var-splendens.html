Aconitum napellus var. splendens

Aconitum napellus var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour Monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in tall, spire-like clusters. Its flowers have spurred petals that are characteristic of the genus Aconitum.

This variant is notable for its vibrant color and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
