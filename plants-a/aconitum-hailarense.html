Aconitum hailarense

Aconitum hailarense, commonly known as the Hailaren monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum hailarense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
