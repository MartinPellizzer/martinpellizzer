Actinidia deliciosa var. longipila

Actinidia deliciosa var. longipila, commonly known as the Longipila kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It produces small, fuzzy fruits that are typically green when mature, though some varieties may develop a yellow or brownish hue. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are actinomorphic, featuring five sepals and five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a cold winter dormancy period for optimal fruit production.

The variety is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet-tart flavor profile.
