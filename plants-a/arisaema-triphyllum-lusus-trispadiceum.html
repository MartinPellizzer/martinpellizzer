Arisaema triphyllum lusus trispadiceum

Arisaema triphyllum lusus trispadiceum, commonly known as the white cobra plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family characterized by its unique, spadix-like inflorescence.

The plant features a broad, arrow-shaped leaf with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns. Its most notable botanical feature is the elongated, spiny structure that resembles a cobra's tongue, which is surrounded by a hood-like spathe. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
