Allium vancouverense

Allium vancouverense, commonly known as Vancouver onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in culinary applications due to their mild onion flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
