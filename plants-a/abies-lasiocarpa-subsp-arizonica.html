Abies lasiocarpa subsp. arizonica

Abies lasiocarpa subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona white fir, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its conical shape, silver-blue needles, and distinctive bracts that resemble small, scale-like leaves.

The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soils. Its cones are relatively small and have a pointed tip, while its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stress.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
