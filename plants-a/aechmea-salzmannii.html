Aechmea salzmannii

Aechmea salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, with shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it visually appealing. It produces inflorescences with showy, long-lasting flowers that range from white to pink.

Aechmea salzmannii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.
