Amphilophium paniculatum var. imatacense

Amphilophium paniculatum var. imatacense, commonly known as the Imatacense sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or reddish and are adapted for wind pollination.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
