Alyssum transiens

Alyssum transiens, commonly known as the transient alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Alyssum transiens is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
