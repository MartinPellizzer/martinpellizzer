Acampe congesta

Acampe congesta, commonly known as the clustered acampe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acampe congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
