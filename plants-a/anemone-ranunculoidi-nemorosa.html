Anemone ranunculoidi-nemorosa

Anemone ranunculoidi-nemorosa, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Anemone ranunculoidi-nemorosa is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
