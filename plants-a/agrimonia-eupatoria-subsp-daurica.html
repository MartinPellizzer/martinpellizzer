Agrimonia eupatoria subsp. daurica

Agrimonia eupatoria subsp.

daurica, commonly known as Daurian agrimony, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
