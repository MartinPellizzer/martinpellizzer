Aristolochia impudica

Aristolochia impudica, commonly known as the "shameful orchid," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flowers that resemble a pouch, with a complex structure of fused petals and sepals. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
