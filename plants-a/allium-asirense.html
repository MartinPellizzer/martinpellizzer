Allium asirense

Allium asirense, commonly known as the Sicilian leek, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, hollow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Allium asirense is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
