Aloe madecassa

Aloe madecassa, commonly known as the yellow aloe, is a succulent plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aloe madecassa is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

Its gel is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
