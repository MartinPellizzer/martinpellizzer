Artabotrys caudatus

Artabotrys caudatus, commonly known as the hanging lantern tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant, white flowers that resemble small lanterns, which bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves of Artabotrys caudatus have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
