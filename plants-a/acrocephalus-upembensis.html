Acrocephalus upembensis

Acrocephalus upembensis, commonly known as the Upemba marsh warbler, is a small passerine bird native to the wetlands of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring a pale grey head with a black crown and a brown body. This species is typically found in dense vegetation near water bodies, such as marshes and reed beds. The plant habitat of Acrocephalus upembensis is closely tied to aquatic environments, where it relies on reeds and grasses for nesting and foraging.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
