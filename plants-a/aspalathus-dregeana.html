Aspalathus dregeana

Aspalathus dregeana, commonly known as African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces tiny, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Aspalathus dregeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
