Aegilops buschirica

Aegilops buschirica, commonly known as the Buschiri grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the tribe Aegilopineae within the grass family Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets.

Aegilops buschirica is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or soil stabilization.

Morphology

Aegilops buschirica has narrow, grass-like leaves that are elongated, slightly curved, and arranged alternately along the stem, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

The stem is cylindrical, with a smooth, light green bark that grows upright and branching. Its flowers are spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to white florets, blooming in early summer, and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a three-lobed spikelet that disperses its seeds by wind, with each seed having a small, wing-like appendage.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in sandy or rocky soils.
