Anthericum nanum

Anthericum nanum, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anthericum nanum is valued for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
