Asperula purpurea var. trichantha

Asperula purpurea var. trichantha, commonly known as the purple woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a delicate, flowering ground cover.
