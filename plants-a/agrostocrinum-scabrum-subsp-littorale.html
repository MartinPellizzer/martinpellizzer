Agrostocrinum scabrum subsp. littorale

Agrostocrinum scabrum subsp.

littorale, commonly known as sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of coastal vegetation.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
