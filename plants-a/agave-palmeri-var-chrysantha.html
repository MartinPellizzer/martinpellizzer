Agave palmeri var. chrysantha

Agave palmeri var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow flowered agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, blue-green leaves with waxy surfaces. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellow to orange flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
