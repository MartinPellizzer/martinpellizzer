Amalia purpurea

Amalia purpurea, commonly known as the purple amalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky texture.

Amalia purpurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
