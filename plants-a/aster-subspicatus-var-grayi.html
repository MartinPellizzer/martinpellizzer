Aster subspicatus var. grayi

Aster subspicatus var. grayi, commonly known as Gray's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, lavender to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
