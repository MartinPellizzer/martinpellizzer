Abies balsamea var. longifolia

Abies balsamea var. longifolia, commonly known as the Canadian fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to eastern Canada and the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly longer than those of the typical balsam fir. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal and subalpine forests.

The species is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and for its use in reforestation efforts.
