Azalea indica var. spatulata

Azalea indica var. spatulata, commonly known as the broadleaf azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Azalea indica var. spatulata is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a versatile choice for coastal and subtropical regions.
