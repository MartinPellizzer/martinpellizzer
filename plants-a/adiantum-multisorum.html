Adiantum multisorum

Adiantum multisorum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, bright green fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are horizontal and produce clusters of small, spore-bearing structures called sori.

Adiantum multisorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
