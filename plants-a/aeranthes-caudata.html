Aeranthes caudata

Aeranthes caudata, commonly known as the curly-tongue orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that are typically pink or purple in hue. Aeranthes caudata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
