Amomum garoense

Amomum garoense, commonly known as the Garo black pepper, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark purple to black berries that are used as a spice. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its rhizomes and fruits are valued for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum garoense is also used in traditional medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
