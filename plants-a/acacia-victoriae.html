Acacia victoriae

Acacia victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow spherical flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
