Acacia tenuissima

Acacia tenuissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

Acacia tenuissima is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry landscapes.
