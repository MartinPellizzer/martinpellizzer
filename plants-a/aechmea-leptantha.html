Aechmea leptantha

Aechmea leptantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pineapple plant, is a bromeliad native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aechmea leptantha is appreciated for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea leptantha has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a bluish-green color and a waxy texture.

It lacks a typical stem, with the plant growing directly from the base, and its leaf sheaths form a pseudostem that is smooth and slightly fibrous. The plant produces inflorescences with clustered, spike-like flowers that are pinkish-white to reddish in color, blooming during the dry season and pollinated primarily by wind. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its rocky or sandy habitat.
