Argyrolobium filicaule

Argyrolobium filicaule, commonly known as the silverleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that features silver-gray foliage and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive globular shape. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
