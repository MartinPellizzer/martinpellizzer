Alyssum tortuosum var. orientale

Alyssum tortuosum var. orientale, commonly known as the Persian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features compact, mat-forming growth with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
