Atriplex graciliflora

Atriplex graciliflora, commonly known as slender-flowered saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes.

Atriplex graciliflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
