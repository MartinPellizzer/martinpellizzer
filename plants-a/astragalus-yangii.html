Astragalus yangii

Astragalus yangii, commonly known as the Yang's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus yangii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
