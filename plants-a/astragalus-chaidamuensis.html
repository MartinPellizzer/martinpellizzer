Astragalus chaidamuensis

Astragalus chaidamuensis, commonly known as the Chaidamu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, showcasing features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal value.
