Anisantha diandra subsp. rigida

Anisantha diandra subsp.

rigida, commonly known as the rigid anisantha, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
