Afzelia coahuilana

Afzelia coahuilana, commonly known as the Coahuila afzelia, is a species of legume native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Afzelia coahuilana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
