Anthericum submaculatum

Anthericum submaculatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, white or pale pink flowers with a yellowish center. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that contain the seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anthericum submaculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
