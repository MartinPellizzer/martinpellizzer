Asplenium myriophyllum var. monteverdense

Asplenium myriophyllum var. monteverdense, commonly known as the Monteverde spleenwort, is a fern native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often hairy leaves that resemble a spleen, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows on mossy substrates or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and can vary in shape depending on the growing conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in specialized horticultural collections.
