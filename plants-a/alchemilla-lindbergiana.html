Alchemilla lindbergiana

Alchemilla lindbergiana, commonly known as Lindberg's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Scandinavia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help retain moisture and protect against excessive sunlight.
