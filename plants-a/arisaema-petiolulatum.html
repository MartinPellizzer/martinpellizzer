Arisaema petiolulatum

Arisaema petiolulatum, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot or the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of many arum species. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
