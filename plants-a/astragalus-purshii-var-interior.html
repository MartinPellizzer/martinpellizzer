Astragalus purshii var. interior

Astragalus purshii var. interior, commonly known as the interior milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval leaflets.

Astragalus purshii var. interior is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting native pollinators.
