Astragalus alexeenkoanus

Astragalus alexeenkoanus, commonly known as the Alexeenko's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus alexeenkoanus is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
