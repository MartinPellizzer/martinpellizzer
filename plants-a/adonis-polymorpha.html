Adonis polymorpha

Adonis polymorpha, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers with a bell-shaped corolla. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and lilies.

Adonis polymorpha is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
