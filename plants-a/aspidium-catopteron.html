Aspidium catopteron

Aspidium catopteron, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into narrow, linear segments with a silvery sheen on the underside. Native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant's reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of its fronds.

Aspidium catopteron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, delicate appearance.
