Atropa acaulis var. angustifolia

Atropa acaulis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved deadly nightshade, is a variant of the deadly nightshade plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. The plant produces dark purple berries that are highly toxic, containing tropane alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with a distinctive purple hue, and it belongs to the Solanaceae family.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
