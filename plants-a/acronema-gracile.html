Acronema gracile

Acronema gracile, commonly known as the slender acronema, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
