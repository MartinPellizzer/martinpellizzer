Astragalus badrinathensis

Astragalus badrinathensis, commonly known as the Badrinath milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale green pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
