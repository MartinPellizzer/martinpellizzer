Amaryllis variegata

Amaryllis variegata, commonly known as the variegated amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and typically display variegation with white or yellow markings. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

It is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
