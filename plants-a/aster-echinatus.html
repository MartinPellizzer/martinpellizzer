Aster echinatus

Aster echinatus, commonly known as the spiked aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet to blue-purple ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Aster echinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
