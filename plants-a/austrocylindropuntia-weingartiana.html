Austrocylindropuntia weingartiana

Austrocylindropuntia weingartiana, commonly known as the Weingartian barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina and southern Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its spines are relatively short and sparse, providing some protection while allowing for better air circulation.

Austrocylindropuntia weingartiana is often cultivated in arid gardens due to its hardy nature and unique appearance.
