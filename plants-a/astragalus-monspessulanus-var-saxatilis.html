Astragalus monspessulanus var. saxatilis

Astragalus monspessulanus var. saxatilis, commonly known as the Mediterranean milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, stipitate leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers in late spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid regions.
