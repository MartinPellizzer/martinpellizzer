Acacia flabellifolia

Acacia flabellifolia, commonly known as the fan-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves that are fan-shaped and often have a silver-blue hue. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia flabellifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
