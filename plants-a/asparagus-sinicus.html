Asparagus sinicus

Asparagus sinicus, commonly known as Chinese asparagus, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, succulent stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by spiny, triangular berries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Asparagus sinicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
