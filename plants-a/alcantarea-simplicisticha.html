Alcantarea simplicisticha

Alcantarea simplicisticha, commonly known as the simple-leaved alcantara, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid habitat. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences that range in color from white to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss and enhancing its drought tolerance.

Alcantarea simplicisticha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
