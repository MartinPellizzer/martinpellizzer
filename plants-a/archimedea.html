Archimedea

Archimedea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Archimedea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
