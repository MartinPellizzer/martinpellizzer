Aconitum napellus f. alpinum

Aconitum napellus f. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking dark purple to violet flowers arranged in a hooded shape, characteristic of the genus Aconitum. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that grow upright, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aconitum napellus f. alpinum is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.
