Asclepias muricata

Asclepias muricata, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its milkweed sap contains toxic compounds, which deter herbivores.

Asclepias muricata serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting its life cycle.
