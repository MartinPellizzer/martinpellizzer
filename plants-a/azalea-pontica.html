Azalea pontica

Azalea pontica, commonly known as the Pontic azalea, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea pontica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates.
