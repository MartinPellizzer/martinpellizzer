Aspalathus oresigena

Aspalathus oresigena, commonly known as the South African rooibos tea plant, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and requiring minimal water. Its scientific name reflects its origin, with "oresigena" meaning "from the mountains.".

Aspalathus oresigena is closely related to other species in the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes.
