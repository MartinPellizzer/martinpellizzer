Astragalus dietrichii

Astragalus dietrichii, commonly known as the Dietrich's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.

Astragalus dietrichii is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological significance.
