Amelanchier vulgaris proles rhamnoides

Amelanchier vulgaris proles rhamnoides, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries in the summer, which are popular among birds and humans alike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas, stream valleys, and open fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and natural habitats.
