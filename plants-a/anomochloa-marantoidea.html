Anomochloa marantoidea

Anomochloa marantoidea, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Marantaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the genus. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Anomochloa marantoidea is also valued for its starchy roots, which can be used in culinary applications.
