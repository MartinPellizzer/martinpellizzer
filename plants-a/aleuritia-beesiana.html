Aleuritia beesiana

Aleuritia beesiana, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning red in the fall.

Aleuritia beesiana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
