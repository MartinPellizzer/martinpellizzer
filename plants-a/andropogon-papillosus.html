Andropogon papillosus

Andropogon papillosus, commonly known as silky grass or silky panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon papillosus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
