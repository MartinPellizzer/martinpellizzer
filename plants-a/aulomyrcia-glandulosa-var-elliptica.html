Aulomyrcia glandulosa var. elliptica

Aulomyrcia glandulosa var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic glandular aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, often glandular-stippled leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its growth habit is compact, with a dense foliage that provides habitat for various small organisms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
