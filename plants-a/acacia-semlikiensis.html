Acacia semlikiensis

Acacia semlikiensis, commonly known as the Semliki acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are modified leaf stalks that function as leaves, and these are typically narrow and pointed. Acacia semlikiensis is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
