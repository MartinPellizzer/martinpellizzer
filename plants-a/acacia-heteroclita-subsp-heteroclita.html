Acacia heteroclita subsp. heteroclita

Acacia heteroclita subsp.

heteroclita, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences that are rich in nectar, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
