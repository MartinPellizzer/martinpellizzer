Acanthopanax setchuenensis

Acanthopanax setchuenensis, commonly known as the Sichuan elephant tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
