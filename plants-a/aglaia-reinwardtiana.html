Aglaia reinwardtiana

Aglaia reinwardtiana, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically yellow or orange, with a thick rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
