Astragalus parvopassuae

Astragalus parvopassuae, commonly known as the small-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
