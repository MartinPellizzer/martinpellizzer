Aristolochia grandifolia

Aristolochia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces intricate, hooded flowers with a distinctive shape and a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, while its flowers are typically purple or greenish in color.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
