Allosorus adiantoides

Allosorus adiantoides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 inches in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.
