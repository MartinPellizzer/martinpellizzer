Adesmia rahmeri

Adesmia rahmeri, commonly known as the silverleaf adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a silvery underside. Its flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in racemes, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Adesmia rahmeri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils.
