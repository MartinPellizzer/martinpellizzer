Arum bulbosum

Arum bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a stout, tuberous rootstock from which it produces a broad, arrow-shaped leaf. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. It blooms in early spring, with the spathe typically displaying shades of white, pink, or red.

The plant is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
