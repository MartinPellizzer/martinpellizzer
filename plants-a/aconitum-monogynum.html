Aconitum monogynum

Aconitum monogynum, commonly known as the single-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically with a single flower per stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum monogynum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
