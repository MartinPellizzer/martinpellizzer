Artemisia argophylla

Artemisia argophylla, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Artemisia argophylla is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
