Adenocalymma sagotii

Adenocalymma sagotii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
