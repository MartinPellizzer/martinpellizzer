Amaranthus blitum subsp. pseudogracilis

Amaranthus blitum subsp.

pseudogracilis, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces vibrant red or purple flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It is native to the Americas and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens.
