Allium monticola subsp. keckii

Allium monticola subsp.

keckii, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces upright flowering stems with clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
