Aegilops cylindrica subsp. pauciaristata

Aegilops cylindrica subsp.

pauciaristata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improving wheat varieties through genetic crosses.
