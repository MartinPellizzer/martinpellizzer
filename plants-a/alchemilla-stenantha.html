Alchemilla stenantha

Alchemilla stenantha, commonly known as narrowleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in narrow, upright inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla stenantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to tolerate wet conditions.
