Adicea lundii

Adicea lundii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Adicea lundii is adapted to harsh environments, with features such as succulent leaves that help it store water.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
