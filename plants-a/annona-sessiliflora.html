Annona sessiliflora

Annona sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces a fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature. The fruit has a sweet, custard-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
