Astragalus limprichtii

Astragalus limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or pale pink pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus limprichtii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
