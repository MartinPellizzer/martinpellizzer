Abies guatemalensis var. rushforthii

Abies guatemalensis var. rushforthii, commonly known as the Rushforth's fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the highlands of Guatemala.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The tree produces distinctive cone clusters, which are typically pendulous and contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this variant is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
