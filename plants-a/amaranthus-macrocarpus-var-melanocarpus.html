Amaranthus macrocarpus var. melanocarpus

Amaranthus macrocarpus var. melanocarpus, commonly known as black amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its dark purple to black seeds. The plant produces tall, branched stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically green or red, and it is cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
