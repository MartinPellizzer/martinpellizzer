Aslia

Aslia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slight hairy texture.

Aslia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
