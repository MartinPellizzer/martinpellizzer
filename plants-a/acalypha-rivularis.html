Acalypha rivularis

Acalypha rivularis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands, thriving in humid climates. Its leaves are usually ovate or heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha rivularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
