Astragalus vandasii

Astragalus vandasii, commonly known as the Vanda's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is noted for its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus vandasii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
