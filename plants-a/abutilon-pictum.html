Abutilon pictum

Abutilon pictum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its fibrous roots and robust stems make it adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

Abutilon pictum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
