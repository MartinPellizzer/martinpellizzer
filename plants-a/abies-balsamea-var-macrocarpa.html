Abies balsamea var. macrocarpa

Abies balsamea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the balsam fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into woody cones containing seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal and mixed forests.

The balsam fir plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
