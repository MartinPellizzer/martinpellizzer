Angelica gmelinii

Angelica gmelinii, commonly known as Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are thick and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica gmelinii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
