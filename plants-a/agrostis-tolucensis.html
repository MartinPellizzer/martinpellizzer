Agrostis tolucensis

Agrostis tolucensis, commonly known as the Tolucan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Agrostis tolucensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
