Angelica seatonii

Angelica seatonii, commonly known as the Seaton's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and yellow-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
