Anthemis cyrenaica

Anthemis cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica ragwort, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers are often surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
