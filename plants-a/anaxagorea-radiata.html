Anaxagorea radiata

Anaxagorea radiata, commonly known as the white-veined evergreen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, elliptical leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Anaxagorea radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
