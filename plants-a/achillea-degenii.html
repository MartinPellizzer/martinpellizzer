Achillea degenii

Achillea degenii, commonly known as Degen's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves, and it produces small white to pale yellow flowers.

Degen's yarrow is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
