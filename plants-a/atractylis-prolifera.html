Atractylis prolifera

Atractylis prolifera, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, arranged in clusters at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Atractylis prolifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
