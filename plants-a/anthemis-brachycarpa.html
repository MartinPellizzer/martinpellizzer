Anthemis brachycarpa

Anthemis brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies. Anthemis brachycarpa is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
