Aegilops kotschyi var. leptostachya

Aegilops kotschyi var. leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wild wheat, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brown florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to harsh conditions.
