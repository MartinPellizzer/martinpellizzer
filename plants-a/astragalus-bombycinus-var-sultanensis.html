Astragalus bombycinus var. sultanensis

Astragalus bombycinus var. sultanensis, commonly known as the Sultan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
