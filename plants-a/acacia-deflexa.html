Acacia deflexa

Acacia deflexa, commonly known as the dwarf silver wattle, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which appear in late winter to early spring. Acacia deflexa is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is a valuable plant for soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.
