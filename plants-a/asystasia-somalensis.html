Asystasia somalensis

Asystasia somalensis, commonly known as the Somali asystasia, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or near water sources.

Asystasia somalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
