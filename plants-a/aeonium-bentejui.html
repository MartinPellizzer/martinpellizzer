Aeonium bentejui

Aeonium bentejui, commonly known as the Bentejui Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy texture. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
