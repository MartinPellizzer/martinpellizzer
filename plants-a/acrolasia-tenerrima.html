Acrolasia tenerrima

Acrolasia tenerrima, commonly known as the brittle acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, dark green leaves that are alternate and pinnately compound. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

The bark and leaves have been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicinal applications.
