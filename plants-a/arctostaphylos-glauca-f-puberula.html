Arctostaphylos glauca f. puberula

Arctostaphylos glauca f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular, and often hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive silvery-gray appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
