Aechmea confertiflora

Aechmea confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from rain and debris. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage is often adorned with white or pale green markings, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Aechmea confertiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic value and ease of care.
