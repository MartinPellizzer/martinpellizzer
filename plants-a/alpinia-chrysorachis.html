Alpinia chrysorachis

Alpinia chrysorachis, commonly known as the golden flame ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Alpinia chrysorachis is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a tropical aesthetic in cooler climates.
