Acaena sanguisorbae var. intermedia

Acaena sanguisorbae var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bracken burr, is a variant of the bracken burr plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense growth habit and spiny, woody stems. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is often found in forest understories and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native region.
