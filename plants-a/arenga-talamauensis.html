Arenga talamauensis

Arenga talamauensis, commonly known as the Talamauensis arenga, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and robust, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces edible, starchy pith in its stems, which has been traditionally used as a food source. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Arenga talamauensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and plantations.
