Aetheolaena gargantana

Aetheolaena gargantana, commonly known as the giant poinciana, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical and subtropical regions.
