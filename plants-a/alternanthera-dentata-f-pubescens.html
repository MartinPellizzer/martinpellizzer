Alternanthera dentata f. pubescens

Alternanthera dentata f. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a striking ornamental species native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for water gardens and bog habitats.

Its unique foliage makes it a valuable addition to aquatic and terrestrial landscapes.
