Acacia flavipila var. flavipila

Acacia flavipila var. flavipila, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a common sight in many parts of southern and western Australia.
