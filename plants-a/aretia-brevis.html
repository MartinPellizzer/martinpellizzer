Aretia brevis

Aretia brevis, commonly known as the short arete, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces small, bright-colored blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Aretia brevis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
