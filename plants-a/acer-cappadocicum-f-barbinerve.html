Acer cappadocicum f. barbinerve

Acer cappadocicum f. barbinerve, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Cappadocian maple.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, barbed veins that give it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It is native to Turkey and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its striking foliage and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
