Acacia subrigida

Acacia subrigida, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are densely covered with small, stiff hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically borne at the ends of branches. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia subrigida is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in soil stabilization.
