Acaena multifida subsp. quinquefida

Acaena multifida subsp.

quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed burr plant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny fruiting structures that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
