Adina peduncularis

Adina peduncularis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and oval, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is also cultivated in horticulture.
