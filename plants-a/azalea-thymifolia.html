Azalea thymifolia

Azalea thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Rhododendron genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles the texture of thyme leaves, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or red, blooming in spring and adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Azalea thymifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
