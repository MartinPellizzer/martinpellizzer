Astragalus tridactylicus f. pallidiflora

Astragalus tridactylicus f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in restoration projects.
