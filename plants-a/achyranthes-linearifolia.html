Achyranthes linearifolia

Achyranthes linearifolia, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense mats, contributing to its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.

Achyranthes linearifolia is often found in wetlands, along riverbanks, and in other humid environments.
