Aleuritia watsonii

Aleuritia watsonii, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Aleuritia watsonii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
