Arnoglossum subulatum

Arnoglossum subulatum, commonly known as the slenderleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features narrow, pointed leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native range.
