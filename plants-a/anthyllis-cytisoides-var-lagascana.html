Anthyllis cytisoides var. lagascana

Anthyllis cytisoides var. lagascana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, pea-like flowers in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and has a deep root system for drought tolerance. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

However, it can become invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
