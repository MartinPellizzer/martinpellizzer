Acacia filifolia

Acacia filifolia, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in pendant clusters, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Acacia filifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance and drought tolerance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
