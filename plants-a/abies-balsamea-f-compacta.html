Abies balsamea f. compacta

Abies balsamea f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf balsam fir, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub that forms a dense, low-growing mound.

It is a naturally occurring form of the balsam fir, characterized by its tightly packed, dark green needles and small, conical cones. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in shaded forest understories. Its dense foliage makes it an excellent choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover in woodland settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
