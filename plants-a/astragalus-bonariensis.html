Astragalus bonariensis

Astragalus bonariensis, commonly known as the Chilean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces small, yellow or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
