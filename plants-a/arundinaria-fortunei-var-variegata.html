Arundinaria fortunei var. variegata

Arundinaria fortunei var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, green stems with distinctive white or yellow variegation along the nodes. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas. Its rapid growth and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping and indoor cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, providing both visual interest and erosion control in suitable environments.
