Anaxagorea acuminata

Anaxagorea acuminata, commonly known as the pointed anaxagorea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes plants such as the cashew and mango. The tree is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a drupe with a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
