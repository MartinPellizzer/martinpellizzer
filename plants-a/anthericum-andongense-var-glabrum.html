Anthericum andongense var. glabrum

Anthericum andongense var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth anthericum, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences that produce pale green to white flowers. The plant exhibits a glabrous (hairless) surface, which is a notable feature distinguishing it from other varieties of Anthericum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
