Anthyllis lateriflora

Anthyllis lateriflora, commonly known as the creeping everlasting, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis lateriflora is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and scrubland habitats.
