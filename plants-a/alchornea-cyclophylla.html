Alchornea cyclophylla

Alchornea cyclophylla, commonly known as the African balsa or round-leaf alchornea, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
