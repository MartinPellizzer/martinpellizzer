Actaea pachypoda f. microcarpa

Actaea pachypoda f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf black cohosh, is a botanical variant of the black cohosh species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dark green, palmately compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black, depending on the stage of ripeness. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of North America.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
