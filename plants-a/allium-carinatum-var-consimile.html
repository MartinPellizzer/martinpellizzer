Allium carinatum var. consimile

Allium carinatum var. consimile, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers temperate climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Alliaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions.
