Angelica sachalinensis var. glabra

Angelica sachalinensis var. glabra, commonly known as the Sakhalin angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, particularly Sakhalin Island and parts of eastern Russia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound umbels of small white or greenish flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its roots are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their medicinal properties.
