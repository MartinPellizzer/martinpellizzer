Acacia gordonii

Acacia gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia gordonii is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of timber and traditional medicine.
