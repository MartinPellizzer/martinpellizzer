Aeschynomene americana var. glandulosa

Aeschynomene americana var. glandulosa, commonly known as hairy horsegram, is a tropical annual legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage and soil improvement.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in agricultural systems.
