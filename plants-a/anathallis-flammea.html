Anathallis flammea

Anathallis flammea, commonly known as the flame anathallis, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its host for support rather than soil. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees, contributing to its ecological niche.

Anathallis flammea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical greenhouses.
