Arundinaria sugimotoi

Arundinaria sugimotoi, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or sugimoto bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Arundinaria sugimotoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
