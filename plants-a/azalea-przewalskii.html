Azalea przewalskii

Azalea przewalskii, commonly known as the Przhevalsky azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. Its leaves are elliptical and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea przewalskii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological adaptations.
