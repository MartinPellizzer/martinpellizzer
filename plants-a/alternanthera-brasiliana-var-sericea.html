Alternanthera brasiliana var. sericea

Alternanthera brasiliana var. sericea, commonly known as the silver heart plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive, shimmering appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This versatile plant is often used in water gardens and as a decorative foliage plant in both aquatic and terrestrial settings.
