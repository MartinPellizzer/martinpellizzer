Acalypha argentii

Acalypha argentii, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acalypha argentii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
