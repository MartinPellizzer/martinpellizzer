Astripomoea rotundata

Astripomoea rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found climbing or trailing along fences, trees, or other structures.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
