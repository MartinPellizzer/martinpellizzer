Anaxeton hirsutum

Anaxeton hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy anaxeton, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Anaxeton hirsutum is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.
