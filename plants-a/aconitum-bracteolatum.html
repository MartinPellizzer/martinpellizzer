Aconitum bracteolatum

Aconitum bracteolatum, commonly known as the bracteolate monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
