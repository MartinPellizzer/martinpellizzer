Actaea japonica

Actaea japonica, commonly known as Japanese angel's trumpet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant produces clusters of berries that range in color from red to black, which are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a dark green hue.

Actaea japonica thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
