Astragalus hieronymi

Astragalus hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several small, lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus hieronymi is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
