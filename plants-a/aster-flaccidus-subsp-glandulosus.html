Aster flaccidus subsp. glandulosus

Aster flaccidus subsp.

glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
