Arnica rivularis

Arnica rivularis, commonly known as the stream arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streams and in damp woodlands. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow centers. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has a hairy stem covered in fine, soft pubescence.

Arnica rivularis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
