Angelica archangelica subsp. norwegica

Angelica archangelica subsp.

norwegica, commonly known as the Norwegian angelica, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, compound leaves that are often deeply lobed.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing volatile oils that give it a strong, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
