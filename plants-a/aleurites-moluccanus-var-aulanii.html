Aleurites moluccanus var. aulanii

Aleurites moluccanus var. aulanii, commonly known as the Aulanii breadnut, is a variant of the breadnut tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, green fruits that mature to a dark brown color. The seeds, encased in a hard shell, are used for oil extraction and have economic significance in traditional practices.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential agricultural applications.
