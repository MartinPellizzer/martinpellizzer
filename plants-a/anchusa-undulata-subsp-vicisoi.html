Anchusa undulata subsp. vicisoi

Anchusa undulata subsp.

vicisoi, commonly known as the wavy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it withstands harsh environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
