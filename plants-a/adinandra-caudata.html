Adinandra caudata

Adinandra caudata, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub or small tree form, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often hairy, contributing to its common name.

Adinandra caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
