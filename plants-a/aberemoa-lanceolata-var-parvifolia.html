Aberemoa lanceolata var. parvifolia

Aberemoa lanceolata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aberemoa, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
