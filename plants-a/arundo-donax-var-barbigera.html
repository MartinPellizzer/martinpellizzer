Arundo donax var. barbigera

Arundo donax var. barbigera, commonly known as the barbata reed, is a variant of the giant reed, native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a tall, perennial grass that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist environments, where it thrives in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that mature to a brownish hue.

This variant is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
