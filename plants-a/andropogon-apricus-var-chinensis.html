Andropogon apricus var. chinensis

Andropogon apricus var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening and for erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
