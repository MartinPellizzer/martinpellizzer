Adenochloa pectinella

Adenochloa pectinella, commonly known as the velvetleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Adenochloa pectinella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
