Acaena sanguisorbae var. antarctica

Acaena sanguisorbae var. antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic hairysedge, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, reddish-brown bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize harsh environments. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine or subantarctic regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions.
