Ardisia pedalis

Ardisia pedalis, commonly known as the small-leaved evergreen ardisia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Ardisia pedalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
