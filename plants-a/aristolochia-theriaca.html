Aristolochia theriaca

Aristolochia theriaca, commonly known as the European birthwort, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including aristolochic acids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties. However, it is also known to be toxic and has been associated with severe health issues in humans.

Due to its toxicity and ecological impact, it is often considered a noxious weed in certain regions.
