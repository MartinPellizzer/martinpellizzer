Adenostoma fasciculatum var. prostratum

Adenostoma fasciculatum var. prostratum, commonly known as the low bush buckthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with multiple stems lying close to the ground. The plant has small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, oval berries that are typically dark red when mature.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.
