Aspidosperma limae

Aspidosperma limae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Aspidosperma limae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
