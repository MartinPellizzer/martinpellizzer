Acalypha rubroserrata

Acalypha rubroserrata, commonly known as the red-serrated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with red-tinged margins and serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha rubroserrata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
