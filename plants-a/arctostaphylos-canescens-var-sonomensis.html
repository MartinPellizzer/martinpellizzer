Arctostaphylos canescens var. sonomensis

Arctostaphylos canescens var. sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma manzanita, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

This variety is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
