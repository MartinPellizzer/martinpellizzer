Aster anthylloides

Aster anthylloides, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender, hairy stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
