Adiantum lunulatum var. mettenii

Adiantum lunulatum var. mettenii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pale green fronds that often have a silvery sheen, especially on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its fronds are biennally produced, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
