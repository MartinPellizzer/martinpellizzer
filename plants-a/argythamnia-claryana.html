Argythamnia claryana

Argythamnia claryana, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Argythamnia claryana is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
