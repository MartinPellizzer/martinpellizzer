Aquilegia vulgaris var. afghanica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan Columbine, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
