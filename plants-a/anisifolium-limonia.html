Anisifolium limonia

Anisifolium limonia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for warm climates. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy for digestive issues.

The fruit, though small, is edible and has a mild, citrus-like flavor.
