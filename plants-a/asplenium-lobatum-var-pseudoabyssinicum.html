Asplenium lobatum var. pseudoabyssinicum

Asplenium lobatum var. pseudoabyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian spleenwort, is a fern native to high-altitude regions of Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It is a small, terrestrial fern with a rosette-shaped growth pattern, characterized by its narrow, lanceolate leaves. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with deeply lobed fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky crevices or on limestone substrates.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptations to extreme altitudes and its role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
