Asclepias nivea var. normalis

Asclepias nivea var. normalis, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that are characteristic of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae). Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

This species is known for its ecological importance as a host plant for monarch butterflies and other pollinators.
