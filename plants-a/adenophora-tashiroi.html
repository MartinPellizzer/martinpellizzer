Adenophora tashiroi

Adenophora tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Adenophora tashiroi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
