Abies balsamea f. hudsonia

Abies balsamea f. hudsonia, commonly known as the Hudson's balsam fir, is a botanical variant of the balsam fir native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically shorter than those of the typical balsam fir. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in boreal and subalpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
