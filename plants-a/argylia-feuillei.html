Argylia feuillei

Argylia feuillei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and is often protected in conservation programs.
