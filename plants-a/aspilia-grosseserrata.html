Aspilia grosseserrata

Aspilia grosseserrata, commonly known as the serrated flame flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are typically borne in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Aspilia grosseserrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
