Aristida intermedia

Aristida intermedia, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
