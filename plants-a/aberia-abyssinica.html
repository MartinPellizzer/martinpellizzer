Aberia abyssinica

Aberia abyssinica, commonly known as the African wild coffee, is a small evergreen tree native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of montane forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
