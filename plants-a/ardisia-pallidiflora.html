Ardisia pallidiflora

Ardisia pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered beautyberry, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that matures in the fall.

Ardisia pallidiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
