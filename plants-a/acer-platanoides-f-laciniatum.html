Acer platanoides f. laciniatum

Acer platanoides f. laciniatum, commonly known as the Norway maple variegated, is a cultivar of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves that display a variegated pattern of white, green, and gold. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season.
