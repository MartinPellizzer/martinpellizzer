Astragalus fatimensis

Astragalus fatimensis, commonly known as the Fatima's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitats.
