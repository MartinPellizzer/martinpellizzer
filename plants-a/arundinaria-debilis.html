Arundinaria debilis

Arundinaria debilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Arundinaria debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
