Aspidium lonchodes

Aspidium lonchodes, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in structures called sori. Aspidium lonchodes is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
