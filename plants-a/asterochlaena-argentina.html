Asterochlaena argentina

Asterochlaena argentina, commonly known as the Argentine starflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy yellow or orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Asterochlaena argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
