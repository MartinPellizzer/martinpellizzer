Acanthopanax setosus

Acanthopanax setosus, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a woody climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often lobed and covered with fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Its vigorous growth and climbing habit make it a notable species in its native habitat.
