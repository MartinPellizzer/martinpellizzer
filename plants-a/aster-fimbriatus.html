Aster fimbriatus

Aster fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale purple flowers with fringed petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Aster fimbriatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
