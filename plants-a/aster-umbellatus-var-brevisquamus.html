Aster umbellatus var. brevisquamus

Aster umbellatus var. brevisquamus, commonly known as the short-stemmed aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
