Adenophora verticillata var. angustifolia

Adenophora verticillata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, arranged in whorls around the stem. This perennial herb thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
