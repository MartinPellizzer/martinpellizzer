Acalypha swynnertonii

Acalypha swynnertonii, commonly known as the Swynnerton's spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
