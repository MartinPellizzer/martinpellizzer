Allium circumflexum

Allium circumflexum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic chive, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces small, white, spherical flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has long, grass-like leaves and forms bulbs underground, similar to garlic. It is often cultivated for its mild garlic flavor and ornamental value.

Allium circumflexum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
