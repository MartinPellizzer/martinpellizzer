Astragalus wulfenii subsp. atticus

Astragalus wulfenii subsp.

atticus, commonly known as the Attic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
