Axyris amaranthoides f. dentata

Axyris amaranthoides f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed axyris, is a variant of the axyris plant characterized by its distinctive toothed leaf margins.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and often edible leaves. The plant is native to certain regions of Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

The flowers are small and clustered, often producing a striking red or orange hue that attracts pollinators.
