Alyxia obtusifolia

Alyxia obtusifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a woody vine characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Alyxia obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
