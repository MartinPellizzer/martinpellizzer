Achillea adulterina

Achillea adulterina, commonly known as lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, thread-like phyllaries that surround the central disk flowers. Its leaves are basal, feather-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Achillea adulterina is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties.
