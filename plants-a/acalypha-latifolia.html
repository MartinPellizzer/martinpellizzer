Acalypha latifolia

Acalypha latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and requires careful management.
