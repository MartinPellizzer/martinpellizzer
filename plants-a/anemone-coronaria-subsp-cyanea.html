Anemone coronaria subsp. cyanea

Anemone coronaria subsp.

cyanea, commonly known as the Blue Poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, blue to violet petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
