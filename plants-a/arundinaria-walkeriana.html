Arundinaria walkeriana

Arundinaria walkeriana, commonly known as the Walker's bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
