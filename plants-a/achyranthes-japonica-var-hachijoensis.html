Achyranthes japonica var. hachijoensis

Achyranthes japonica var. hachijoensis, commonly known as Hachijo false brome, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
