Astragalus berteroanus

Astragalus berteroanus, commonly known as the Bertero's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus berteroanus is often found in open grasslands and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
