Agapetes salicifolia

Agapetes salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf agapetes, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Agapetes salicifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
