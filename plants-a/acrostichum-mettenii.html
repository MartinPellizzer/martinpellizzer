Acrostichum mettenii

Acrostichum mettenii, commonly known as the dwarf fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Acrostichum mettenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
