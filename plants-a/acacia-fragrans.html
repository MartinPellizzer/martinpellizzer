Acacia fragrans

Acacia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia fragrans is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
