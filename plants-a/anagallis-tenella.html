Anagallis tenella

Anagallis tenella, commonly known as the slender pink or slender white sand-violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or white in color, with five petals arranged in a distinctive spurred shape. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Anagallis tenella is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
