Adenophora pereskiifolia subsp. subalpina

Adenophora pereskiifolia subsp.

subalpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
