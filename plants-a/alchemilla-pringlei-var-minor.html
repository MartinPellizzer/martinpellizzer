Alchemilla pringlei var. minor

Alchemilla pringlei var. minor, commonly known as the lesser alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, silver-green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.

Alchemilla pringlei var. minor is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
