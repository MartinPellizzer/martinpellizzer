Aster laxifolius var. carneus

Aster laxifolius var. carneus, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with pink to purple petals and a prominent yellow central disk. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
