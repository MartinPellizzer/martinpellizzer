Aristida scova

Aristida scova, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect flowering stalks that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native landscaping.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
