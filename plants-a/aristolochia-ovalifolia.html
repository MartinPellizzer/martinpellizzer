Aristolochia ovalifolia

Aristolochia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its attractive, though somewhat toxic, appearance.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and ecological roles.
