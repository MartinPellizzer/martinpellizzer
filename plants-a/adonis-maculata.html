Adonis maculata

Adonis maculata, commonly known as the spotted crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically divided into lobes and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive black spot at the base of each petal. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Adonis maculata is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to both humans and animals.
