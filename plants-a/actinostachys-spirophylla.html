Actinostachys spirophylla

Actinostachys spirophylla, commonly known as the spiral-leaf fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiral arrangement of fronds, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant features compound leaves with a feather-like structure, composed of numerous narrow, lance-shaped pinnae. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinctive morphology and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
