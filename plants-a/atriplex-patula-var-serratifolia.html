Atriplex patula var. serratifolia

Atriplex patula var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves with serrated edges, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to saline soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
