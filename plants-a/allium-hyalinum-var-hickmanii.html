Allium hyalinum var. hickmanii

Allium hyalinum var. hickmanii, commonly known as Hickman's garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bulbils along its stems, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like flavor, often used in culinary applications.
