Acer mono var. latialatum

Acer mono var. latialatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a rounded canopy and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features include small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves.
