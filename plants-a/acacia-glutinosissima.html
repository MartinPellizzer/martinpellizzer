Acacia glutinosissima

Acacia glutinosissima, commonly known as the sticky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that exude a resinous substance, which gives the plant its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its small, woody pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia glutinosissima is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
