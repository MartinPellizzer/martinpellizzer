Astragalus rhododendrophilus

Astragalus rhododendrophilus, commonly known as the rhododendron astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found growing in association with rhododendrons, hence its common name. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological relationships and potential medicinal properties.
