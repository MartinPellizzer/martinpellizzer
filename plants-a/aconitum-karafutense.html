Aconitum karafutense

Aconitum karafutense, commonly known as the Karafutense monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue bell-shaped flowers. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
