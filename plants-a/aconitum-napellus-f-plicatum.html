Aconitum napellus f. plicatum

Aconitum napellus f. plicatum, commonly known as the pleated monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive pleated, hood-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value, though it requires careful handling due to its poisonous nature.
