Arabis incanescens

Arabis incanescens, commonly known as the hoary rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features basal leaves that are densely covered with white, woolly hairs, giving it a frosty appearance. Its small, yellow flowers appear in early spring and are clustered in racemes.

Arabis incanescens is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
