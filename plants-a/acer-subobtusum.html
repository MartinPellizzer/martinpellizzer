Acer subobtusum

Acer subobtusum, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree has alternate, ovate leaves with rounded to slightly notched bases and a distinctive silvery-white underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red to orange berries.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
