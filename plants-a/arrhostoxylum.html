Arrhostoxylum

Arrhostoxylum, commonly known as the Andean cholla, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees with thick, ribbed stems. The plants are characterized by their spiny leaves and flowers that range from yellow to red. They are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in mountainous regions.

The genus is part of the Cactaceae family and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.
