Alyssoides utriculata var. utriculata

Alyssoides utriculata var. utriculata, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive bladder-like seed pods that enclose its seeds, a feature that aids in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
