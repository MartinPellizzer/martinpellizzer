Agave kavandivi

Agave kavandivi, commonly known as the Kavando or Kavandivi agave, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with sharp, spiny margins. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by fleshy, banana-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
