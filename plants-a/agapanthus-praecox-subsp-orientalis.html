Agapanthus praecox subsp. orientalis

Agapanthus praecox subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the African lily or blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa. It features dense clusters of striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, rising on tall, slender stalks above its basal rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves.

The plant is well-suited for gardens in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage remains evergreen in warmer climates, providing visual interest throughout the year.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
