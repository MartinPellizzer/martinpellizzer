Aspalathus spectabilis

Aspalathus spectabilis, commonly known as the purple tea bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescent texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural compounds.
