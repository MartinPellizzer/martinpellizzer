Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. matris-filiae

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

matris-filiae, commonly known as the mother's milk plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub with slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its name "matris-filiae" refers to its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
