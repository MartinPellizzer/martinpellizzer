Aconitum soongaricum

Aconitum soongaricum, commonly known as the Tibetan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its root system is tuberous, which is a key feature in its medicinal use. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is handled with care and is used in traditional medicine under strict supervision.
