Anisotes diversifolius var. brevicallyx

Anisotes diversifolius var. brevicallyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water clover, is a variant of the water clover species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a floating or submerged pattern. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikes, usually greenish-white in color.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in water purification.
