Agave nelsonii

Agave nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which are typical of the Agavaceae family.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
