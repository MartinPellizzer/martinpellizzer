Agropyron divergens var. tenuispicum

Agropyron divergens var. tenuispicum, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and meadows.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.
