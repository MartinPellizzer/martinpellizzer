Ardisia magnifica

Ardisia magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent ardisia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Ardisia magnifica is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
