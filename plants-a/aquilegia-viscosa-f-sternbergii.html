Aquilegia viscosa f. sternbergii

Aquilegia viscosa f. sternbergii, commonly known as the sticky corymbe columbine, is a horticultural form of the sticky corymbe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded corymbs of bright red or orange-red flowers, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often sticky to the touch, a trait that gives it its common name. It is a perennial species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
