Aureolaria pectinata

Aureolaria pectinata, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Aureolaria pectinata is often found in open areas such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and sandy soils.
