Acer opulifolium var. tomentosum

Acer opulifolium var. tomentosum, commonly known as the tomentose maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence, giving it a soft, grayish appearance. The plant produces small, red or orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
