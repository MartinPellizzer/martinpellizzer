Alchemilla smaragdina var. platyphylla

Alchemilla smaragdina var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It features rosettes of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
