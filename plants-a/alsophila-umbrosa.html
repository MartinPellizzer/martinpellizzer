Alsophila umbrosa

Alsophila umbrosa, commonly known as the dark fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Alsophila umbrosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
