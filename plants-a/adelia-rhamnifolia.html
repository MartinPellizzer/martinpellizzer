Adelia rhamnifolia

Adelia rhamnifolia, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, dark purple berries. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments.

Adelia rhamnifolia plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
