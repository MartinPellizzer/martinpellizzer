Acanthus dioscoridis var. laciniatus

Acanthus dioscoridis var. laciniatus, commonly known as the laceleaf acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces tall spikes of violet to purple flowers in the summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. Its foliage is particularly notable for its intricate, lace-like patterns, which give it a delicate and elegant appearance.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
