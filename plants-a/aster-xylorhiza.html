Aster xylorhiza

Aster xylorhiza, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aster xylorhiza is often used in native plant gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
