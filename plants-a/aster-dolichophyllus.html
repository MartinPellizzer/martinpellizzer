Aster dolichophyllus

Aster dolichophyllus, commonly known as the long-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elongated and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

Aster dolichophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
