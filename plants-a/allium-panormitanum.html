Allium panormitanum

Allium panormitanum, commonly known as the Panormitanum garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional Mediterranean cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
