Angelica archangelica subsp. litoralis

Angelica archangelica subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as sea angelica, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been historically used in herbal medicine.

This subspecies is adapted to saline and windy coastal environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
