Aster hispidulus

Aster hispidulus, commonly known as the rough blue aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster hispidulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
