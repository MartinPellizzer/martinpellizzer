Alpinia henryi var. densihispida

Alpinia henryi var. densihispida, commonly known as the dense-hairy ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by finger-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and ecological role.
