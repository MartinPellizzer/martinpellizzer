Aconitum ambiguum f. multisectum

Aconitum ambiguum f. multisectum, commonly known as the multisection monkshood, is a variant of the monkshood plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spire-like arrangement. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its dramatic, architectural appearance.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and kept away from children and pets.
