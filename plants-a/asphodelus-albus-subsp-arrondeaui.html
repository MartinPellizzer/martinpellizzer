Asphodelus albus subsp. arrondeaui

Asphodelus albus subsp.

arrondeaui, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall spikes of white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
