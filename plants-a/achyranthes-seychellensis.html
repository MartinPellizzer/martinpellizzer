Achyranthes seychellensis

Achyranthes seychellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Seychelles islands.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.

Morphology

Achyranthes seychellensis has leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a dark green color, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is slender, hairy, and somewhat woody, with a greenish-brown bark that becomes more fissured with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, with greenish-white petals and stamens, blooming during the rainy season, and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to the moist, nutrient-rich soils of its native habitat.
