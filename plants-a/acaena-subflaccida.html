Acaena subflaccida

Acaena subflaccida, commonly known as the soft burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, woolly covering. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Acaena subflaccida plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for native wildlife.
