Astragalus quisqualis subsp. kuhifrushensis

Astragalus quisqualis subsp.

kuhifrushensis, commonly known as the Kuhifrush milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.

The subspecies is of interest to botanists for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
