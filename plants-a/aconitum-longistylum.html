Aconitum longistylum

Aconitum longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing large, showy blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes. Aconitum longistylum is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
