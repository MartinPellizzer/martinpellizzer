Acacia peuce

Acacia peuce, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Acacia peuce is typically found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
