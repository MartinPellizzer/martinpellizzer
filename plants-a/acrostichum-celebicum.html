Acrostichum celebicum

Acrostichum celebicum, commonly known as the Celebes grass or Celebes reed, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Acrostichum celebicum is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines.
