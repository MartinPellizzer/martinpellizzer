Aeschynanthus angustifolius

Aeschynanthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly western and central parts. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
