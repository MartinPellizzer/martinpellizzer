Acacia verniciflua var. latifolia

Acacia verniciflua var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved gum acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat, leathery leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, cylindrical pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal and sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its historical use in producing a resin used for varnishes and traditional medicines.
