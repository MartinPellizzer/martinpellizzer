Acaciella barrancana

Acaciella barrancana, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, which helps protect them from harsh environmental conditions.

Acaciella barrancana plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
