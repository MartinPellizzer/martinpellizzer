Anthericum warneckei

Anthericum warneckei, commonly known as the Warneck's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a complex reproductive structure typical of arums.

Anthericum warneckei is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
