Asplenium makinoi

Asplenium makinoi, commonly known as the Makino's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to East Asia.

It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, moist environments. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium makinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, shaded gardens.
