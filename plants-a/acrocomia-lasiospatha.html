Acrocomia lasiospatha

Acrocomia lasiospatha, commonly known as the silky palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clustered, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. Acrocomia lasiospatha thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its seeds are used in traditional cuisines.
