Amentotaxus argotaenia var. assamica

Amentotaxus argotaenia var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam nutmeg, is a rare and endangered species belonging to the Taxaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, dark bark and conifer-like cones. The plant is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It produces edible seeds that are highly valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is critically threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
