Acianthus fornicatus

Acianthus fornicatus, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Acianthus fornicatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
