Astragalus lentiginosus var. idriensis

Astragalus lentiginosus var. idriensis, commonly known as the Idrija milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
