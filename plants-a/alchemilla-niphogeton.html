Alchemilla niphogeton

Alchemilla niphogeton, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer, typically in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers cool, shaded environments.

Alchemilla niphogeton is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
