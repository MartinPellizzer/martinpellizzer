Agropyron caninum f. pilosifolium

Agropyron caninum f. pilosifolium, commonly known as the hairy leaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish-brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
