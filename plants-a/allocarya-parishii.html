Allocarya parishii

Allocarya parishii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open woodlands and grasslands. It blooms in the spring and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Allocarya parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
