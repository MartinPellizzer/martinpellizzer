Aster anacampthiphyllus

Aster anacampthiphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
