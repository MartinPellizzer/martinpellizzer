Arundinaria variegata f. glabra

Arundinaria variegata f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a variant of the variegated bamboo species.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems that distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, bright green leaves with a slight variegation pattern.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
