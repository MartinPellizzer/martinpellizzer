Astragalus paulsenii

Astragalus paulsenii, commonly known as Paulsen's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. It has small, bean-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Astragalus paulsenii is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its ecosystem.
