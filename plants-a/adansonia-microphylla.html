Adansonia microphylla

Adansonia microphylla, commonly known as the baobab tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores large amounts of water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The tree has small, leathery leaves and produces yellow, clustered flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit, known as a "monkey bread," is edible and contains a pulp rich in nutrients.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
