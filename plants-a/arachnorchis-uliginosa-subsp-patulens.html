Arachnorchis uliginosa subsp. patulens

Arachnorchis uliginosa subsp.

patulens, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Australia. It typically grows in wet, boggy environments, often in heathlands or swampy areas.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy greenish-yellow flower. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive appearance and ecological significance in its habitat.
