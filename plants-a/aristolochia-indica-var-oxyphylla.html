Aristolochia indica var. oxyphylla

Aristolochia indica var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a pipe or a horn, and is adapted for pollination by specific insects. It contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

However, it is important to note that some species within the genus Aristolochia are toxic and should be handled with caution.
