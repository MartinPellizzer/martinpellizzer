Acrostichum impressum

Acrostichum impressum, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically accompanied by prominent stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acrostichum impressum is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage and seasonal blooms.
