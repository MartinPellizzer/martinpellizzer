Anthurium atropurpureum var. apertum

Anthurium atropurpureum var. apertum, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with prominent red veins. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental plant collections.
