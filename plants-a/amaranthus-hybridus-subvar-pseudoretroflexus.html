Amaranthus hybridus subvar. pseudoretroflexus

Amaranthus hybridus subvar. pseudoretroflexus, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and rough, hairy stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in hue. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly serrated margin and a textured surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically for both food and fiber.
