Alstroemeria araucana

Alstroemeria araucana, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting dark veins. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Alstroemeria araucana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
