Aquilegia benacensis

Aquilegia benacensis, commonly known as the Benaco columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

Aquilegia benacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
