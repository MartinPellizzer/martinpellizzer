Allium vineale var. asperiflorum

Allium vineale var. asperiflorum, commonly known as the rough-flowered garlic chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers with a distinctive garlic aroma. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in moist, open habitats. Its bulbous underground stems store nutrients, supporting its growth through seasonal cycles.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
