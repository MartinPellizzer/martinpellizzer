Angiopteris uncinata

Angiopteris uncinata, commonly known as the hooked shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive hooked shape at the tips of the pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
