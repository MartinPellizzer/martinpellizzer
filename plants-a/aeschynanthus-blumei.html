Aeschynanthus blumei

Aeschynanthus blumei, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" or "Lipstick Plant," is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often resembling lips, hence its common name. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Aeschynanthus blumei is also known for its ability to bloom continuously throughout the year under proper care.
