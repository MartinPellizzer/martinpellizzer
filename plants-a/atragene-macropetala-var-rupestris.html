Atragene macropetala var. rupestris

Atragene macropetala var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
