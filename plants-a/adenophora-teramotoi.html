Adenophora teramotoi

Adenophora teramotoi, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Adenophora teramotoi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
