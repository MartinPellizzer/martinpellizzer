Achyrophorus acaulis

Achyrophorus acaulis, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Achyrophorus acaulis is a perennial species that thrives in low-nutrient conditions and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
