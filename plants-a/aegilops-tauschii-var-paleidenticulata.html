Aegilops tauschii var. paleidenticulata

Aegilops tauschii var. paleidenticulata, commonly known as the slender spikelet wild wheat, is a wild relative of wheat native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous spikelets and small, palea-less lemma. This plant exhibits a diploid genome and is considered a valuable genetic resource for wheat improvement due to its potential for contributing beneficial traits. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its morphology and genetic makeup make it an important species in the study of wheat evolution and breeding programs.
