Azalea coccinea aurantia

Azalea coccinea aurantia, commonly known as the orange flame azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, with a distinctive orange hue. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This azalea is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal floral display.
