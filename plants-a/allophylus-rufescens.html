Allophylus rufescens

Allophylus rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked alder, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by edible, red berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.

Allophylus rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
