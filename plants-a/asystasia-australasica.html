Asystasia australasica

Asystasia australasica, commonly known as the Australian asystasia, is a herbaceous plant native to Australia and New Guinea.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Asystasia australasica is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
