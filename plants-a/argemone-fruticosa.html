Argemone fruticosa

Argemone fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive capsule fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

Argemone fruticosa is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
