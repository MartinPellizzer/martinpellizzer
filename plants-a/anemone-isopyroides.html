Anemone isopyroides

Anemone isopyroides, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Anemone isopyroides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
