Aster multiflorus var. columbianus

Aster multiflorus var. columbianus, commonly known as the Columbian aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, daisy-like white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It is a perennial herb with a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
