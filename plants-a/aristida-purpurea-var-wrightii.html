Aristida purpurea var. wrightii

Aristida purpurea var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's purple threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. The plant features slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, bristly flowers.

This species is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common component of arid ecosystems.
