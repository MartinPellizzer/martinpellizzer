Acanthophyllum campylacme

Acanthophyllum campylacme, commonly known as the crested sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy and gravelly soils, exhibiting drought tolerance and minimal water requirements.

Its structure helps it retain moisture and withstand harsh environmental conditions.
