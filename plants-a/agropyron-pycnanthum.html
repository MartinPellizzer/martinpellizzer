Agropyron pycnanthum

Agropyron pycnanthum, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spike-like inflorescence composed of dense, spikelet clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Agropyron pycnanthum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
