Argyrolobium dekindtii

Argyrolobium dekindtii, commonly known as the De Kindt's silver globular violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular shape and silvery-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, rounded seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
