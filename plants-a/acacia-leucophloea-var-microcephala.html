Acacia leucophloea var. microcephala

Acacia leucophloea var. microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features slender, often spiny stems and is characterized by its small, rounded phyllodes that are typically green with a slight whitish or greyish bloom. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
