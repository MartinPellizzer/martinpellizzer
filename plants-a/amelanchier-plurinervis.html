Amelanchier plurinervis

Amelanchier plurinervis, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with finely toothed margins and a distinctive, fragrant white flower cluster in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Amelanchier plurinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
