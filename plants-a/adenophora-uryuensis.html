Adenophora uryuensis

Adenophora uryuensis, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Adenophora uryuensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
