Anychia dichotoma var. capillacea

Anychia dichotoma var. capillacea, commonly known as the slender anychia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
