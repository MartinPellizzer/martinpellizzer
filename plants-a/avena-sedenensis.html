Avena sedenensis

Avena sedenensis, commonly known as the Sedenensis oat, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces awned florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
