Acanthopsis glandulopalmata

Acanthopsis glandulopalmata, commonly known as the glandular palmate acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves with glandular structures along the margins. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean-style landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its sturdy, upright growth habit and long blooming period.
