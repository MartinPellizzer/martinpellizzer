Alangium kurzii var. pachyphyllum

Alangium kurzii var. pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved alangium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
