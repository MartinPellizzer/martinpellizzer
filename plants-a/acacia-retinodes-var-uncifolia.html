Acacia retinodes var. uncifolia

Acacia retinodes var. uncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

This variety is often found in sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
