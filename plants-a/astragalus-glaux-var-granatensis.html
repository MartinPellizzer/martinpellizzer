Astragalus glaux var. granatensis

Astragalus glaux var. granatensis, commonly known as the granate milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
