Aspidosperma decussatum

Aspidosperma decussatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
