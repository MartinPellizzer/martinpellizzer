Aechmea abbreviata

Aechmea abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and stiff, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Aechmea abbreviata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
