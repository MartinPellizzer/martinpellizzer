Asclepias edentata

Asclepias edentata, commonly known as the toothless milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive horn-shaped structure at the center. It is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds, and it serves as a host plant for monarch butterfly larvae.

Asclepias edentata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
