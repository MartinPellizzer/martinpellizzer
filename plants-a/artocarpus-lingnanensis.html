Artocarpus lingnanensis

Artocarpus lingnanensis, commonly known as the Guangdong breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a large, round fruit with a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, starchy pulp inside. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
