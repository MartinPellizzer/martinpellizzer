Aspidium Ferrugineum

Aspidium ferrugineum is a rare and ancient fern species found in specific regions of Asia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forests. This plant is known for its distinctive, rusty-colored fronds that give it its common name. Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Due to habitat loss, it faces threats that highlight the need for its protection.
