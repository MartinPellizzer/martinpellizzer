Apocynum × milleri

Apocynum × milleri, commonly known as the hybrid dogbane, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and other toxic plants. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to humans and livestock.
