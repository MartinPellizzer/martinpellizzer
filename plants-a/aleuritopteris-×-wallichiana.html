Aleuritopteris × wallichiana

Aleuritopteris × wallichiana, commonly known as the hybrid water fern, is a fern species resulting from the cross between Aleuritopteris pteropus and Aleuritopteris semipinnata.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a water lily. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Aleuritopteris × wallichiana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems where it contributes to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
