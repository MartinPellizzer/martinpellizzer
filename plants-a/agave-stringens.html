Agave stringens

Agave stringens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and long, narrow, blue-green leaves that are often lined with white marginal teeth. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of a fleshy, spiky fruit. Agave stringens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
