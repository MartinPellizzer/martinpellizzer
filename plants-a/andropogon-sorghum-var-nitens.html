Andropogon sorghum var. nitens

Andropogon sorghum var. nitens, commonly known as nitens sorghum, is a variant of sorghum native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a tall, perennial grass characterized by its robust stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its forage value and has potential for use in biofuel production.
