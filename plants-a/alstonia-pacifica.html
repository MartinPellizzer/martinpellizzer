Alstonia pacifica

Alstonia pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific alstonia, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply veined. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
