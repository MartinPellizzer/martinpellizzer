Aspicarpa pruriens

Aspicarpa pruriens, commonly known as the itching fruit or prickly pear, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and fleshy, edible fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its fruit, which is typically yellow or orange, contains a sweet, juicy pulp surrounded by a thick, fibrous layer.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
