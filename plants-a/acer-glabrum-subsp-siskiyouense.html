Acer glabrum subsp. siskiyouense

Acer glabrum subsp.

siskiyouense, commonly known as the Siskiyou maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
