Astragalus onobrychis f. grandiflorus

Astragalus onobrychis f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
