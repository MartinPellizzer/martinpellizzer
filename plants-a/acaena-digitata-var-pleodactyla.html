Acaena digitata var. pleodactyla

Acaena digitata var. pleodactyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces spiky, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in coastal and lowland areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
