Aspalthium frutescens

Aspalthium frutescens, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to the arid environment. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky, coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and protected species.
