Amorphophallus longituberosus var. robustus

Amorphophallus longituberosus var. robustus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to any garden. The plant produces a showy, spadix-like inflorescence that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often with a strong odor reminiscent of decaying flesh. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is particularly noted for its robust growth habit and impressive foliage.
