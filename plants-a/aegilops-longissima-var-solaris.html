Aegilops longissima var. solaris

Aegilops longissima var. solaris, commonly known as the sun wheat, is a wild relative of wheat with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, slender spikelets and glumes that are often longer than the lemma. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose, open panicle.

This variety is notable for its potential in genetic research due to its unique traits and adaptability.
