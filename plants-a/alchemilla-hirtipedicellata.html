Alchemilla hirtipedicellata

Alchemilla hirtipedicellata, commonly known as the hairy-fruited lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters atop its stems, which bloom in early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.

Alchemilla hirtipedicellata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
