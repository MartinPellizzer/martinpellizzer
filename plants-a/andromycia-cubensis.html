Andromycia cubensis

Andromycia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban orchid, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Andromycia cubensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
