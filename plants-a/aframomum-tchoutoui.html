Aframomum tchoutoui

Aframomum tchoutoui, commonly known as the Cameroon cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) native to West Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
