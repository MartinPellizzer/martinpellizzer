Allium schoenoprasum f. leucanthum

Allium schoenoprasum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered chive, is a botanical variant of the common chive.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. This subspecies is often cultivated for its mild garlic flavor and ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant is perennial and can be propagated through division or seed.
