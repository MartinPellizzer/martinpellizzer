Amomum xanthoglossum

Amomum xanthoglossum, commonly known as the yellow-lipped ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers with prominent lips. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Amomum xanthoglossum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal applications.
