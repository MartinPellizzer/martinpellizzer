Alangium faberi

Alangium faberi, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or Chinese alangium, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
