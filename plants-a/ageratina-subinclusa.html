Ageratina subinclusa

Ageratina subinclusa, commonly known as the smallleaf blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ageratina subinclusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
