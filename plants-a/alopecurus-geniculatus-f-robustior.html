Alopecurus geniculatus f. robustior

Alopecurus geniculatus f. robustior, commonly known as the robust meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow foxtail.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its robust growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet bearing two to three small, spike-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
