Amygdalus davidiana

Amygdalus davidiana, commonly known as the David's almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk, similar to other members of the Prunus genus. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
