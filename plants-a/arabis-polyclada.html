Arabis polyclada

Arabis polyclada, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters.

Arabis polyclada is generally hardy and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
