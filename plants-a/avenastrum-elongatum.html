Avenastrum elongatum

Avenastrum elongatum, commonly known as the longleaf avena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and gravelly fields.

Avenastrum elongatum is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
