Aralia lobata

Aralia lobata, commonly known as the California cow parsnip, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features large, compound leaves that can grow up to three feet in diameter, with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Aralia lobata is also known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments along stream banks and in forest understories.
