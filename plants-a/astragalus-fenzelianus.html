Astragalus fenzelianus

Astragalus fenzelianus, commonly known as the Fenzel's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in its native habitat.
