Amorphophallus mayoi

Amorphophallus mayoi, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 3 feet in height, resembling a phallic shape, hence its scientific name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas and marshes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and oxygen to the water, while its roots help stabilize the soil.

The plant is also notable for its unique reproductive cycle, which involves a long dormancy period followed by rapid growth during the wet season.
