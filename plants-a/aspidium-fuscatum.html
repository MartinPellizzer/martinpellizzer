Aspidium fuscatum

Aspidium fuscatum, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, dark coating, contributing to its common name.

Aspidium fuscatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
