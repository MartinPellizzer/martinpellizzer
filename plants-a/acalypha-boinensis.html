Acalypha boinensis

Acalypha boinensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts that can display vibrant red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Acalypha boinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
