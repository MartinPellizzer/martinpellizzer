Astragalus korovinianus

Astragalus korovinianus, commonly known as Korovin's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Astragalus korovinianus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
