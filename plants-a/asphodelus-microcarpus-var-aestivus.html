Asphodelus microcarpus var. aestivus

Asphodelus microcarpus var. aestivus, commonly known as the summer asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its distinctive appearance.
