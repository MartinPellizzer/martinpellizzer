Aretia tomentosa

Aretia tomentosa, commonly known as the silky aretia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aretia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
