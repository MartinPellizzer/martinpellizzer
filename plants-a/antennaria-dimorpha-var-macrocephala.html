Antennaria dimorpha var. macrocephala

Antennaria dimorpha var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, branching stems. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
