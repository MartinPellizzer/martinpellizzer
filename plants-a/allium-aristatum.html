Allium aristatum

Allium aristatum, commonly known as the aristate garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are similar to garlic but are generally smaller and less pungent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Allium aristatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
