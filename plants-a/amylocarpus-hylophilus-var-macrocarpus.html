Amylocarpus hylophilus var. macrocarpus

Amylocarpus hylophilus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, tuberous roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and typically grows in wet, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The tubers are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source in some regions.
