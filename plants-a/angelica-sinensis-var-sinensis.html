Angelica sinensis var. sinensis

Angelica sinensis var. sinensis, commonly known as Dong Quai, is a biennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic roots that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with large, compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots contain compounds such as ligustilide and ferulic acid, which are believed to have medicinal properties.

The botanical features of this plant make it a valuable resource in both herbal remedies and pharmacological research.
