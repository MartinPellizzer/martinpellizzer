Abarema leucophylla var. vaupesensis

Abarema leucophylla var. vaupesensis, commonly known as the white-leafed acanthus, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale green leaves that are often velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
