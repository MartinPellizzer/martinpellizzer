Ascarina lucida var. lucida

Ascarina lucida var. lucida, commonly known as the shining kauri, is a tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Araucariaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, whorled foliage. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. The tree produces large, conical cones that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in native forests.
