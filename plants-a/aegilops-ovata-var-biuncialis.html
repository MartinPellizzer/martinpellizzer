Aegilops ovata var. biuncialis

Aegilops ovata var. biuncialis, commonly known as the two-lobed wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. Its seeds are encased in a tough, straw-colored hull, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its potential use in agricultural research.
