Adesmia reclinata

Adesmia reclinata, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Adesmia reclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
