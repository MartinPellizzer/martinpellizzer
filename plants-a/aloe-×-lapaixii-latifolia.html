Aloe × lapaixii latifolia

Aloe × lapaixii latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf aloe, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers on tall, branched inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
