Agave opacidens

Agave opacidens, commonly known as the gray agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The leaves are lined with spines and have a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by edible berries.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
