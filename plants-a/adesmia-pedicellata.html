Adesmia pedicellata

Adesmia pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

The plant features small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Adesmia pedicellata is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
