Ayenia fruticosa

Ayenia fruticosa, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark berries that persist through winter. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves that remain green year-round, providing ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to landscapes.

Ayenia fruticosa is often used as a hedge or screen due to its compact growth habit and durable structure.
