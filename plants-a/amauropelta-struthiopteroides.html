Amauropelta struthiopteroides

Amauropelta struthiopteroides, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a large, tree-like fern native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a thick, horizontal underground stem that supports its towering structure. Its leaves are bi-directionally divided, with a distinctive pattern of pinnae that give it a unique appearance.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
