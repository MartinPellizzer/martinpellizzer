Arundinella thwaitesii

Arundinella thwaitesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Arundinella thwaitesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
