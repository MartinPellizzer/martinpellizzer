Abies alba var. glauca

Abies alba var. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a variant of the white fir native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The needles are stiff and closely arranged, forming a dense, conical shape. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
