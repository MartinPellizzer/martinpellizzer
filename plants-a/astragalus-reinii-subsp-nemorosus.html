Astragalus reinii subsp. nemorosus

Astragalus reinii subsp.

nemorosus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in open woodlands and meadows. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
