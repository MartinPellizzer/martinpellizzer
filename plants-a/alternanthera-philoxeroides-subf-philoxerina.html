Alternanthera philoxeroides subf. philoxerina

Alternanthera philoxeroides subf.

philoxerina, commonly known as the alligatorweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dark green, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wetland environments, making it an invasive species in many regions. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a noxious weed in aquatic and riparian habitats.
