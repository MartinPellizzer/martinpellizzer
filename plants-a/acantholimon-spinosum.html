Acantholimon spinosum

Acantholimon spinosum, commonly known as the spiny flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Acantholimon spinosum is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.
