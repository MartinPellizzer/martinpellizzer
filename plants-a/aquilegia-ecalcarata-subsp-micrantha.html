Aquilegia ecalcarata subsp. micrantha

Aquilegia ecalcarata subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually blue or purple in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
