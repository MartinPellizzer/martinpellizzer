Anemone alpina var. aurea

Anemone alpina var. aurea, commonly known as the golden alpine anemone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, showy flowers that range from white to yellow, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
