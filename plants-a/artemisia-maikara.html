Artemisia maikara

Artemisia maikara, commonly known as the Maikara wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its essential oils are also valued in aromatherapy and natural pest control.
