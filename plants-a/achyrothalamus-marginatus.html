Achyrothalamus marginatus

Achyrothalamus marginatus, commonly known as the mountain holly or mountain privet, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
