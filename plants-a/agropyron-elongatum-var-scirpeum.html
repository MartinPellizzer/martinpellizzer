Agropyron elongatum var. scirpeum

Agropyron elongatum var. scirpeum, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass variant, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed sites.

Its fibrous root system makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and forage production.
