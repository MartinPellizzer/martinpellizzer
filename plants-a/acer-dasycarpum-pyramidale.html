Acer dasycarpum pyramidale

Acer dasycarpum pyramidale, commonly known as the mountain maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and serrated, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by clustered samaras with winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
