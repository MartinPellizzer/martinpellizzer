Astragalus bethlehemiticus subsp. lepidanthus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus subsp.

lepidanthus, commonly known as the Palestinian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves and pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
