Antirrhinum grosii

Antirrhinum grosii, commonly known as the giant snapdragon, is a striking flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Antirrhinum grosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
