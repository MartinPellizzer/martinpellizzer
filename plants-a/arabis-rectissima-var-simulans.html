Arabis rectissima var. simulans

Arabis rectissima var. simulans, commonly known as the rockcress or winter cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.
