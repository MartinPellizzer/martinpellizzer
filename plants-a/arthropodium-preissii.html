Arthropodium preissii

Arthropodium preissii, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant blooms with small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Arthropodium preissii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
