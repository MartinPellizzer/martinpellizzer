Amygdalus communis var. maxima

Amygdalus communis var. maxima, commonly known as the Persian almond.

This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible nuts that are typically harvested in the autumn.

Its hardy nature makes it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
