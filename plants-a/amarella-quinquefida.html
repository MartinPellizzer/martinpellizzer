Amarella quinquefida

Amarella quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed amarella, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pale pink to white. The plant has five distinct lobes on its leaves, which are narrow and serrated, contributing to its botanical uniqueness. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile nature, it is considered a rare species with conservation concerns.
