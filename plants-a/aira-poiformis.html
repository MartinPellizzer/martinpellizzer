Aira poiformis

Aira poiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf aira, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Aira poiformis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
