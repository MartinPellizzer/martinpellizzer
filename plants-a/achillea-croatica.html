Achillea croatica

Achillea croatica, commonly known as Croatian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in shades of white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Achillea croatica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
