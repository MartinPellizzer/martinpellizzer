Acer pseudoplatanus f. latifolium

Acer pseudoplatanus f. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green in summer and turn bright yellow in autumn. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth margin, making it easily distinguishable from other maple varieties.
