Anchusa axillaris

Anchusa axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
