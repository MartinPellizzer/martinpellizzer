Aster souliei var. limitaneus

Aster souliei var. limitaneus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
