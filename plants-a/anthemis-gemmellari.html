Anthemis gemmellari

Anthemis gemmellari, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It grows in coastal and subalpine regions, often in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is known for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
