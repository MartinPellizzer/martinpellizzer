Acinodendron orcheotomum

Acinodendron orcheotomum, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Acinodendron orcheotomum is often used in traditional medicine, though its use is limited due to its toxic properties.
