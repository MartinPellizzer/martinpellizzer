Aspalathus spiniflora

Aspalathus spiniflora, commonly known as the spiked bushveld pea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing edible, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus spiniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
