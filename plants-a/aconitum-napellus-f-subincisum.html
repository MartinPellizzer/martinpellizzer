Aconitum napellus f. subincisum

Aconitum napellus f. subincisum, commonly known as the subincised monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. The leaves are palmately compound with deeply lobed, subincised margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains potent alkaloids, making it both toxic and historically significant.
