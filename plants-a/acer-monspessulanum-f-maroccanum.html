Acer monspessulanum f. maroccanum

Acer monspessulanum f. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan maple, is a unique variant of the maple family native to the Atlas Mountains of Morocco.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in challenging environments.
