Arenaria lloydii

Arenaria lloydii, commonly known as Lloyd's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its tiny, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, helping it conserve water in its native habitat.

Lloyd's sandwort is often found in desert scrub and rocky areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
