Aphelandra monophthalma

Aphelandra monophthalma, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, orange-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Aphelandra monophthalma is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
