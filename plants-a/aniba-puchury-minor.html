Aniba puchury-minor

Aniba puchury-minor, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces berries that are dark red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
