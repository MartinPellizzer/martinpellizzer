Aralia arborea

Aralia arborea, commonly known as the Florida aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically divided into 5 to 9 narrow, elongated segments. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Its root system is extensive and often forms a dense network underground.

Aralia arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
