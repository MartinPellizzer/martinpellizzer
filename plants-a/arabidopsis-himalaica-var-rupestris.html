Arabidopsis himalaica var. rupestris

Arabidopsis himalaica var. rupestris, commonly known as the Himalayan mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a small, mat-forming plant with rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The species is adapted to cold and arid environments, exhibiting frost tolerance and efficient water use. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Its genome has been studied for its potential in understanding plant biology and evolution.
