Aegilops nigricans

Aegilops nigricans, commonly known as black aegilops, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aegilops nigricans is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.

Morphology

Aegilops nigricans has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem, with a bluish-green color and a slightly rough texture.

The stem is slender and cylindrical, with a smooth, dark green bark that grows upright and branching. Its flowers are spike-like inflorescences with small, pale blue to purple florets, blooming in early summer and pollinated by wind. The fruit is a small, three-angled spikelet that disperses its seeds through wind, and the seeds are smooth and oval in shape.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in loose, sandy soils.
