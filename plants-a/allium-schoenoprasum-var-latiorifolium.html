Allium schoenoprasum var. latiorifolium

Allium schoenoprasum var. latiorifolium, commonly known as broadleaf chives, is a variant of the chive plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broader, more robust leaves compared to the standard chive, which gives it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, spherical purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and herb beds.

This variety is often used in cooking for its mild, onion-like flavor, similar to regular chives.
