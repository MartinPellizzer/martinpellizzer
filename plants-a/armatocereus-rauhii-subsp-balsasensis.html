Armatocereus rauhii subsp. balsasensis

Armatocereus rauhii subsp.

balsasensis, commonly known as the Balsas night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spines.

The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens.
