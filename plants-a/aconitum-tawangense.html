Aconitum tawangense

Aconitum tawangense, commonly known as Tawang Aconite, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that bears alternate, palmately compound leaves. Its roots are tuberous and contain potent alkaloids, making it toxic.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes.
