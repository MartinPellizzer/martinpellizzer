Acaena sanguisorbae var. epilasia

Acaena sanguisorbae var. epilasia, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny stems and produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
