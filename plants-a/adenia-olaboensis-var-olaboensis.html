Adenia olaboensis var. olaboensis

Adenia olaboensis var. olaboensis, commonly known as the African mountain spinach, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are palmately compound, with multiple leaflets arranged around a central stem, and are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, and it thrives in cool, humid climates.
