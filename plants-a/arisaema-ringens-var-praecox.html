Arisaema ringens var. praecox

Arisaema ringens var. praecox, commonly known as the early spiny arrowhead, is a variant of the spiny arrowhead plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves with a spiny margin, and its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix, and the plant relies on insect pollination.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique flowering structure.
