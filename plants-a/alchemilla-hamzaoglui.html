Alchemilla hamzaoglui

Alchemilla hamzaoglui, commonly known as the Hamzaoglu lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla hamzaoglui thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
