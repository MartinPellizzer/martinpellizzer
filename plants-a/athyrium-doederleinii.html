Athyrium doederleinii

Athyrium doederleinii, commonly known as the Doederlein's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are borne on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
