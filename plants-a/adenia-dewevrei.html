Adenia dewevrei

Adenia dewevrei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has woody stems and palmately lobed leaves, which are often hairy. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Adenia dewevrei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique appearance.
