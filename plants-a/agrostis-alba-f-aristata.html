Agrostis alba f. aristata

Agrostis alba f. aristata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. This grass is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to white florets.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
