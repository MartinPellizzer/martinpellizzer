Aconitum bullatifolium var. dielsianum

Aconitum bullatifolium var. dielsianum, commonly known as the Diels' monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that resemble a hood, hence its common name. The plant features palmately compound leaves with lobes that are often toothed or lobed, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in areas with young children or pets.
