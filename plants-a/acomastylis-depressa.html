Acomastylis depressa

Acomastylis depressa, commonly known as the flat-stemmed daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Acomastylis depressa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact form and delicate floral display.
