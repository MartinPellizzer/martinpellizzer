Amaranthus silvestris

Amaranthus silvestris, commonly known as wild amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, feather-like leaves. The plant produces dense spikes of tiny, colorful flowers that range from red to orange, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Amaranthus silvestris is also valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and mineral levels.
