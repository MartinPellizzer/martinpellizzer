Amphilophium cuneifolium

Amphilophium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved philophium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
