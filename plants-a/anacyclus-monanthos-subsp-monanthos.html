Anacyclus monanthos subsp. monanthos

Anacyclus monanthos subsp.

monanthos, commonly known as the one-flowered thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb with a robust taproot and hairy, spiny stems.

The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower at the top of the stem, surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. Its leaves are alternate, oblanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
