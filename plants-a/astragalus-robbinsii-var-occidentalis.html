Astragalus robbinsii var. occidentalis

Astragalus robbinsii var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has adapted to arid environments through deep root systems and efficient water use.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.
