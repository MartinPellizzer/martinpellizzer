Anthemis parnesia

Anthemis parnesia, commonly known as the New Zealand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are surrounded by a ring of narrow, pointed bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Anthemis parnesia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
