Actinostemon trachycarpus

Actinostemon trachycarpus, commonly known as the rough-seeded actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in rough, hairy textures, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
