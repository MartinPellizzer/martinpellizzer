Apium carsonii

Apium carsonii, commonly known as Carson's celery, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided leaves that resemble those of other celery species. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

Carson's celery is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
