Ageratum lucidum

Ageratum lucidum, commonly known as the shining ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-blue to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum lucidum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a companion plant in polyculture systems.
