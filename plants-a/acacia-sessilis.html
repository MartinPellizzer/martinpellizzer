Acacia sessilis

Acacia sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and phyllodes that are typically narrow and sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a petiole. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia sessilis is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments.
