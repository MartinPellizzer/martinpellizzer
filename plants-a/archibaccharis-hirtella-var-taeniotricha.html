Archibaccharis hirtella var. taeniotricha

Archibaccharis hirtella var. taeniotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hollyhock, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
