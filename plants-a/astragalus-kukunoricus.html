Astragalus kukunoricus

Astragalus kukunoricus, commonly known as the Kukunor milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus kukunoricus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
