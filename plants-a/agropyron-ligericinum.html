Agropyron ligericinum

Agropyron ligericinum, commonly known as the Russian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Agropyron ligericinum is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop.
