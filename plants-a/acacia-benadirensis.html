Acacia benadirensis

Acacia benadirensis, commonly known as the Benadire acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Acacia benadirensis is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
