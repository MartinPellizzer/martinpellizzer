Aira caryophyllea var. pulchella

Aira caryophyllea var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple grass or pink air plant, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a striking purple or pinkish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
