Arenaria benthamii var. diffusa

Arenaria benthamii var. diffusa, commonly known as the diffused sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
