Adesmia graveolens

Adesmia graveolens, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a small shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain flattened seeds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Its essential oils are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
