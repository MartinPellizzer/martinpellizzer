Agropyron panormitanum subvar. glabrum

Agropyron panormitanum subvar. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth field grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Agropyron panormitanum subvar. glabrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
