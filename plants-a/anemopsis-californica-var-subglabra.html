Anemopsis californica var. subglabra

Anemopsis californica var. subglabra, commonly known as the California water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, often aquatic environments such as marshes and pond edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
