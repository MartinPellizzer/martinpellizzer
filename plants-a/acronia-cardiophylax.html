Acronia cardiophylax

Acronia cardiophylax, commonly known as the heartleaf acronia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often blooming during the warmer months. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a low-growing, herbaceous stem.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
