Acalypha diversifolia var. carpinifolia

Acalypha diversifolia var. carpinifolia, commonly known as the flame bush or red-leaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules.

This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant leaves and ease of care.
