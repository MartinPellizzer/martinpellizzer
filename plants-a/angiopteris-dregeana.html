Angiopteris dregeana

Angiopteris dregeana, commonly known as the African giant water fern, is a large fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, deeply lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Angiopteris dregeana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
