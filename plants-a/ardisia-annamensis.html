Ardisia annamensis

Ardisia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam starfruit, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia, and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

Ardisia annamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and berries.
