Amomum erythranthum

Amomum erythranthum, commonly known as red-flowered cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of red or orange, adding to its ornamental value.
