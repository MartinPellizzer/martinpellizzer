Avena pubescens var. lucida

Avena pubescens var. lucida, commonly known as the shining oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
