Aechmea gracilis

Aechmea gracilis, commonly known as the slender pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange. Aechmea gracilis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.

Morphology

Aechmea gracilis has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette, with a bluish-green color and a pointed tip.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a smooth, dark green bark. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by colorful bracts, and bloom in late winter to early spring, primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds through wind.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
