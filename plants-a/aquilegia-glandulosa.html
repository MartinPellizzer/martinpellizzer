Aquilegia glandulosa

Aquilegia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular columbine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are often glandular-dotted.

Aquilegia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
