Agrostis subpatens

Agrostis subpatens, commonly known as the flatleaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are flat and often slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Agrostis subpatens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
