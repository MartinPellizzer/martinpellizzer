Arundinaria stolonifera

Arundinaria stolonifera, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It reproduces through stolons, which are horizontal underground stems that produce new shoots.

Arundinaria stolonifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
