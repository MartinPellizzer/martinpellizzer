Alsophila setulosa

Alsophila setulosa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizome is robust, allowing it to anchor itself firmly in the soil while supporting its tall structure.

The fronds are bi-directionally arranged, contributing to its distinctive and elegant appearance.
