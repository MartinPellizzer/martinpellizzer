Aegiphila ulei

Aegiphila ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian balm, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters.

Aegiphila ulei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
