Adicea auriculata

Adicea auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Adicea auriculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
