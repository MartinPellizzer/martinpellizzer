Artemisia issykkulensis

Artemisia issykkulensis, commonly known as the Issyk-Kul wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia, particularly the region around Lake Issyk-Kul in Kyrgyzstan.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant thrives in dry, rocky, and mountainous environments, often growing at high altitudes. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and stems, have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Artemisia issykkulensis plays a significant role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
