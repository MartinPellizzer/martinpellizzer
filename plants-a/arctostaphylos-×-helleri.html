Arctostaphylos × helleri

Arctostaphylos × helleri, commonly known as the Sierra manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of chaparral and woodland ecosystems.
