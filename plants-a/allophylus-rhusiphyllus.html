Allophylus rhusiphyllus

Allophylus rhusiphyllus, commonly known as the African holly, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its year-round foliage and winter interest.
