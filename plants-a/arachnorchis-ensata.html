Arachnorchis ensata

Arachnorchis ensata, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing several small, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders, a feature that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect species.

Arachnorchis ensata is a rare and delicate species, often found in rainforest habitats where it thrives in the humid, shaded conditions.
