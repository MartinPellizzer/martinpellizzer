Astragalus onobrychis var. albiflorus

Astragalus onobrychis var. albiflorus, commonly known as white-flowered sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
