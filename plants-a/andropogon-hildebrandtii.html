Andropogon hildebrandtii

Andropogon hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Andropogon hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
