Arrhenatherum elatius subsp. nebrodense

Arrhenatherum elatius subsp.

nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodan feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
