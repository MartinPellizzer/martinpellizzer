Acacia hamiltonii

Acacia hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia hamiltonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
