Aconitum caucasicum f. fastigiatum

Aconitum caucasicum f. fastigiatum, commonly known as the Caucasian monkshood var. fastigiatum, is a variant of the monkshood plant characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

This plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of other Aconitum species, with a distinctive palmate shape. The flowers are borne in tall, spiky clusters and are highly toxic due to the presence of alkaloids.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value, though caution must be exercised due to its poisonous nature.
