Acer leiocarpum

Acer leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of small, smooth-seeded samaras. Its bark is typically smooth and grayish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
