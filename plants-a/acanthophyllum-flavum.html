Acanthophyllum flavum

Acanthophyllum flavum, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Acanthophyllum flavum is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, semi-desert conditions.
