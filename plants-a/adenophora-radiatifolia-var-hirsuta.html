Adenophora radiatifolia var. hirsuta

Adenophora radiatifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five lobes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland borders.
