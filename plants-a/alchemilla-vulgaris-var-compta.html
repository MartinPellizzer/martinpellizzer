Alchemilla vulgaris var. compta

Alchemilla vulgaris var. compta, commonly known as narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense, silver-white fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.
