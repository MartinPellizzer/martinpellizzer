Aconitum alboviolaceum f. albiflorum

Aconitum alboviolaceum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered aconitum, is a variant of the aconitum species characterized by its pale or white flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia. The plant is a perennial herb with tall, sparsely branched stems that produce clusters of showy, usually blue or purple flowers. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are typically green and somewhat hairy.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
