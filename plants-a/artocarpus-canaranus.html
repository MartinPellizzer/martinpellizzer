Artocarpus canaranus

Artocarpus canaranus, commonly known as the Canara jackfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, spiky, green fruit that turns yellow when mature. The fruit contains a fibrous outer husk and a sweet, aromatic pulp with edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and timber.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological importance in its native habitat.
