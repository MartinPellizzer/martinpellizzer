Aspalathus hispida subsp. albiflora

Aspalathus hispida subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered bushveld clover, is a small shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
