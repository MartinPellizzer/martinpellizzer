Adiantum siamense

Adiantum siamense, commonly known as the Siam maidenhair fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Adiantum siamense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces.
