Adonis mongolica

Adonis mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles that contain seeds.

Adonis mongolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its challenging growth requirements.
