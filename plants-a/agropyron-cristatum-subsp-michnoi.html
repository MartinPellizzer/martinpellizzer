Agropyron cristatum subsp. michnoi

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

michnoi, commonly known as the Michno's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelets that are often curved or twisted.

The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green coloration.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
