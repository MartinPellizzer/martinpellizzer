Aeonium canariense subsp. christii

Aeonium canariense subsp.

christii, commonly known as the Christ's Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces striking, yellowish-orange flowers on tall, branching stems during the spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive dry conditions.

This subspecies is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
