Amsonia salpingantha

Amsonia salpingantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Amsonia salpingantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
