Arabis fernaldiana var. typica

Arabis fernaldiana var. typica, commonly known as the Fernald's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
