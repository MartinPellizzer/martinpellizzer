Airochloa hispida

Airochloa hispida, commonly known as the hispid hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water margins.

Airochloa hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
