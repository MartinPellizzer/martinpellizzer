Amaranthus pseudogracilis

Amaranthus pseudogracilis, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Amaranthus pseudogracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
