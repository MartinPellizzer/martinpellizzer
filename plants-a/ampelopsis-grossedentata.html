Ampelopsis grossedentata

Ampelopsis grossedentata, commonly known as the giant grapevines, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but often sour. Its stems are woody and covered with tendrils that help it climb and spread.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
