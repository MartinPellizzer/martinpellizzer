Artemisia rutacea

Artemisia rutacea, commonly known as blue artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in late summer. Artemisia rutacea thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
