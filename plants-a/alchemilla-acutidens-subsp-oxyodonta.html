Alchemilla acutidens subsp. oxyodonta

Alchemilla acutidens subsp.

oxyodonta, commonly known as the sharp-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in rounded inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional herbal medicine.
