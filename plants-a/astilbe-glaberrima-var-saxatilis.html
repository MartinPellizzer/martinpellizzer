Astilbe glaberrima var. saxatilis

Astilbe glaberrima var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock astilbe, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are finely toothed and provide a lush backdrop to its delicate blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
