Arnica pedunculata f. monocephala

Arnica pedunculata f. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is sturdy and often branched.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
