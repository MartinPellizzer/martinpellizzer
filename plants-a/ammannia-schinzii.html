Ammannia schinzii

Ammannia schinzii, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has smooth, reddish stems that are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink to red in color, blooming from late spring to early fall.
