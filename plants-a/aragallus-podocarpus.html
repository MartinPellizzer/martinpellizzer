Aragallus podocarpus

Aragallus podocarpus, commonly known as the Indian pine, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and conical shape. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Aragallus podocarpus plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
