Acacia adansonii

Acacia adansonii, commonly known as the fan acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, fan-shaped leaves that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Acacia adansonii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fuel.
