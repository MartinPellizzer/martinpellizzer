Aniba percoriacea

Aniba percoriacea, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions as a garden plant.
