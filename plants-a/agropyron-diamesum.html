Agropyron diamesum

Agropyron diamesum, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It is well-suited to harsh environments due to its deep root system and ability to conserve water.

Agropyron diamesum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
