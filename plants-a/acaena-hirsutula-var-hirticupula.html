Acaena hirsutula var. hirticupula

Acaena hirsutula var. hirticupula, commonly known as the hairy burr plant, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
