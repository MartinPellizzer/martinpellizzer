Alchemilla arvensis f. microcarpa

Alchemilla arvensis f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a dense rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
