Aria tauricola

Aria tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Aria tauricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
