Astragalus salangensis

Astragalus salangensis, commonly known as the Salang Valley milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in maintaining biodiversity in its arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
