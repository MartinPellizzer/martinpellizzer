Adiantum poiretii var. glaucescens

Adiantum poiretii var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, bi-lobed fronds that are typically light green with a bluish-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its fronds are evergreen, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is prized for its elegant foliage and is often cultivated in shaded garden areas or as a houseplant.
