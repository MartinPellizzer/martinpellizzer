Astragalus globosus

Astragalus globosus, commonly known as globular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus globosus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
