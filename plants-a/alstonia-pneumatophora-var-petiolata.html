Alstonia pneumatophora var. petiolata

Alstonia pneumatophora var. petiolata, commonly known as the petioled alstonia, is a variant of the alstonia tree native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, featuring elongated petioles that support broad, ovate leaves. The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant exhibits a unique adaptation with aerial roots that help in gas exchange and support, typical of mangrove-like species.
