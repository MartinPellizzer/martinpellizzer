Asplenium thelypteroides var. serratum

Asplenium thelypteroides var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is a small, delicate fern with finely divided, serrated fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
