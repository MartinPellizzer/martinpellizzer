Astragalus dzhebrailicus

Astragalus dzhebrailicus, commonly known as the Dzhebrail milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus dzhebrailicus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
