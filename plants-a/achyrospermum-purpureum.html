Achyrospermum purpureum

Achyrospermum purpureum, commonly known as purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and opposite leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are showy, with purple petals surrounding yellow disk florets, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.
