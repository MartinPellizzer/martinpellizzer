Asplenium laciniatum var. bipinnatum

Asplenium laciniatum var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the finely divided shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern, characterized by its intricate, bipinnate leaf structure.

The leaves are typically divided into small, delicate segments, giving the plant a lace-like appearance. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant's rhizome is creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts in suitable habitats.
