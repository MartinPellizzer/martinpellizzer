Attalea amygdalina

Attalea amygdalina, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tall, slender palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into fruit. The fruit contains a valuable oil-rich pulp, which is widely used in the food and cosmetic industries. The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its economic importance, it is also a subject of environmental concern due to deforestation linked to its cultivation.
