Amphiglossa callunoides

Amphiglossa callunoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It blooms from June to August, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
