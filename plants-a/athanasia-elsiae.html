Athanasia elsiae

Athanasia elsiae, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting blooms that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Despite its name, it is not truly immortal, but its flowers can remain intact for extended periods, especially when dried.
