Aconitum ferox var. leucanthum

Aconitum ferox var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence and distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers with a striking white coloration. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a fern-like appearance. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
