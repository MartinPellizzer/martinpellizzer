Acacia drummondii var. candolleana

Acacia drummondii var. candolleana, commonly known as the Drummond's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and has deep root systems to access groundwater.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
