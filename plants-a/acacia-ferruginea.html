Acacia ferruginea

Acacia ferruginea, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in summer. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves, and these are typically reddish-brown in color. Acacia ferruginea is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
