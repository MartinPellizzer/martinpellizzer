Aquilegia mollis

Aquilegia mollis, commonly known as the soft columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soil in partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
