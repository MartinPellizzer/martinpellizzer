Azalea nuttallii

Azalea nuttallii, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Azalea nuttallii blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
