Astragalus delanensis

Astragalus delanensis, commonly known as Delan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in challenging habitats.
