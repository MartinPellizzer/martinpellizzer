Anaxagorea

Anaxagorea, commonly known as the sacred tree or the tree of life, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. Anaxagorea is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing benefits.
