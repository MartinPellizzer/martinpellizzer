Acacia comans

Acacia comans, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Acacia comans is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
