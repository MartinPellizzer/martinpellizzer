Alchemilla uralensis

Alchemilla uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the colder regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Alchemilla uralensis is often found in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate to subarctic climates.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.
