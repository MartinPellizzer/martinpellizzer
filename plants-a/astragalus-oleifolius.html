Astragalus oleifolius

Astragalus oleifolius, commonly known as the oil plant or golden milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus oleifolius is also valued for its seeds, which can be pressed to produce oil.
