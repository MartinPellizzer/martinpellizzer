Amaranthus annectens

Amaranthus annectens, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is an annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant produces oblong, smooth, and edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can be consumed as a leafy vegetable.

Amaranthus annectens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments.
