Albuca tubiformis

Albuca tubiformis, commonly known as the tuberous albuca, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous, tuber-like structures that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and bears small, white or pale green flowers in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca tubiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
