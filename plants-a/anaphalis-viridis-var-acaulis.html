Anaphalis viridis var. acaulis

Anaphalis viridis var. acaulis, commonly known as green everlasting or green cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, leafless stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest margins. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
