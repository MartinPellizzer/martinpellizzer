Athyrium subtriangulare var. sikkimense

Athyrium subtriangulare var. sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim lady fern, is a variant of the subtriangulare fern species native to the Himalayan regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching and gracefully shaped. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage displays a range of green hues, with some cultivars exhibiting variegation.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
