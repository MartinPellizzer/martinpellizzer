Albizia macrophylla

Albizia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grey, with a distinctive flaky texture that peels off in strips.

Albizia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
