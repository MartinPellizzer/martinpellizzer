Alpinia striata

Alpinia striata, commonly known as the black ginger or wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia striata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
