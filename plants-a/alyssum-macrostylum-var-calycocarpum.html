Alyssum macrostylum var. calycocarpum

Alyssum macrostylum var. calycocarpum, commonly known as the California alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and extended blooming period contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
