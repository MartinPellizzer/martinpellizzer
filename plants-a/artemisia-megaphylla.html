Artemisia megaphylla

Artemisia megaphylla, commonly known as bigleaf sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and silvery-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported respiratory and digestive benefits.

The species is also valued in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
