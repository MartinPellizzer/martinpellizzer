Arundo saccharifera

Arundo saccharifera, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. This plant is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, where it thrives in full sun. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.

The plant is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a source of biomass.
