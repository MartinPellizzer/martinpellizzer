Astragalus applegatei

Astragalus applegatei, commonly known as Applegate's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
