Abutilon chiriquinum

Abutilon chiriquinum, commonly known as the "velvetleaf abutilon," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its fibrous roots and robust stems make it adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

Abutilon chiriquinum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
