Amaryllis capensis

Amaryllis capensis, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring or early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Amaryllis capensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
