Artemisia decipiens

Artemisia decipiens, commonly known as false wormwood, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Artemisia decipiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
