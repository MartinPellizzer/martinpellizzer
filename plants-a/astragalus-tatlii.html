Astragalus tatlii

Astragalus tatlii, commonly known as the Tatlilik milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it an important species in soil enrichment.

Astragalus tatlii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
