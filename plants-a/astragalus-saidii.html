Astragalus saidii

Astragalus saidii, commonly known as the Said's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitats.
