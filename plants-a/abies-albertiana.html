Abies albertiana

Abies albertiana, commonly known as the Albert's fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is typically thin and scaly, becoming thicker and more furrowed with age.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in subalpine or alpine forests.
