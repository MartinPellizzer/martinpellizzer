Arcyphyllum capitatum

Arcyphyllum capitatum, commonly known as the mountain sage or desert sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
