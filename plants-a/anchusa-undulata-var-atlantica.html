Anchusa undulata var. atlantica

Anchusa undulata var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a wavy edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anchusa undulata var. atlantica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
