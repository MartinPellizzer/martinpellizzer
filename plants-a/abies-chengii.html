Abies chengii

Abies chengii, commonly known as the Cheng's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are flat, soft, and slightly glossy, with two white stomatal bands on their upper surface. Its cones are cylindrical, woody, and typically hang downward from the branches.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
