Acer glabrum var. typicum

Acer glabrum var. typicum, commonly known as the smooth maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks.
