Allium flavum var. pulchellum

Allium flavum var. pulchellum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

This variant is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged bulbs, which are smaller than typical garlic bulbs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
