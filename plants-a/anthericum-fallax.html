Anthericum fallax

Anthericum fallax, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fern, is a species native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the leaves. Anthericum fallax thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
