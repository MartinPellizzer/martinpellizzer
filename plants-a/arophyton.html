Arophyton

Arophyton, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a climbing habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Arophyton is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique night-blooming feature.
