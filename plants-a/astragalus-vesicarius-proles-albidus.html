Astragalus vesicarius proles albidus

Astragalus vesicarius proles albidus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features slender, erect stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is deep, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
