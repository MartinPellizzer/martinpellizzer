Ageratum corymbosum var. subsetiferum

Ageratum corymbosum var. subsetiferum, commonly known as the dwarf ageratum, is a compact, bushy plant characterized by its densely packed, small, fluffy flowers.

It typically features vibrant blue, purple, or white blooms that are arranged in compact corymbs, giving it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant is native to Mexico and thrives in warm climates, often used as an ornamental in gardens and floral arrangements. It has hairy, gray-green foliage that adds texture to its overall appearance.

This variety is prized for its ability to attract pollinators and its suitability for container gardening.
