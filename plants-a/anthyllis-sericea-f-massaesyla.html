Anthyllis sericea f. massaesyla

Anthyllis sericea f. massaesyla, commonly known as the silky anthyllis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in globular inflorescences, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
