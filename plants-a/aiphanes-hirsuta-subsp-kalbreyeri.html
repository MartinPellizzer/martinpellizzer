Aiphanes hirsuta subsp. kalbreyeri

Aiphanes hirsuta subsp.

kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Kalbreyer's papyrus, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in aquatic habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity and its historical use in traditional crafts.
