Allium aciphyllum

Allium aciphyllum, commonly known as the leafless garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic. The plant is characterized by its absence of leaves, with only a bulbous stem that produces small, white flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its unique structure and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized plant.
