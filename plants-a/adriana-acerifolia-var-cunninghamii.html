Adriana acerifolia var. cunninghamii

Adriana acerifolia var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its finely lobed, maple-like leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a visually striking addition to the landscape.
