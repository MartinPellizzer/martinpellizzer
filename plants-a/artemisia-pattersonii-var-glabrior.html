Artemisia pattersonii var. glabrior

Artemisia pattersonii var. glabrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf sagebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a distinct scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
