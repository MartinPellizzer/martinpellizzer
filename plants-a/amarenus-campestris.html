Amarenus campestris

Amarenus campestris, commonly known as the field amaranth, is an annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Amarenus campestris is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
