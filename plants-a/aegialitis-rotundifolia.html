Aegialitis rotundifolia

Aegialitis rotundifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf seagrass, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal and estuarine environments.

It has rounded, broad leaves that are typically green and fleshy, adapted to thrive in brackish water conditions. The plant grows in dense underwater meadows, providing essential habitat for various aquatic species. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents.

Aegialitis rotundifolia plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and improving water quality in coastal ecosystems.
