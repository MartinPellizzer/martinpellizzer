Achillea ageratifolia subsp. serbica

Achillea ageratifolia subsp.

serbica, commonly known as the Serbica yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features slender, aromatic stems and basal leaves that are finely divided and grayish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
