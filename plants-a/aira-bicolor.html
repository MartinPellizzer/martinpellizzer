Aira bicolor

Aira bicolor, commonly known as the purple false oat, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aira bicolor is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
