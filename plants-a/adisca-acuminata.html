Adisca acuminata

Adisca acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cress, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces clusters of small white or pale yellow flowers.

Adisca acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
