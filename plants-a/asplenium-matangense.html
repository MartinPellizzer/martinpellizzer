Asplenium matangense

Asplenium matangense, commonly known as the Matangense spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and possesses a dense network of rhizomes that anchor it to its host. The leaves are typically narrow, lanceolate, and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocky surfaces in tropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecological niche.
