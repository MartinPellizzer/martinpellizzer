Ambulia alata

Ambulia alata, commonly known as the winged ambulia, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, wing-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. Ambulia alata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is not widely cultivated outside its native range.
