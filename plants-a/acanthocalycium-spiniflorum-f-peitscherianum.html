Acanthocalycium spiniflorum f. peitscherianum

Acanthocalycium spiniflorum f. peitscherianum, commonly known as the Peitscher's spiny-flowered cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on the areoles. It is a compact, slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

This rare hybrid is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
