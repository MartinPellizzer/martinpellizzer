Asclepias campestris var. angustifolia

Asclepias campestris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers with distinctive, hooded buds. Its milky sap contains latex, which is toxic to some animals but beneficial for monarch butterflies.

This variety is often found in open fields and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
