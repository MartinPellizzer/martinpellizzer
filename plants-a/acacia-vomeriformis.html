Acacia vomeriformis

Acacia vomeriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in spherical clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Acacia vomeriformis is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
