Aristolochia paramaribensis

Aristolochia paramaribensis, commonly known as the Paramaribo Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
