Albuca paucifolia subsp. paucifolia

Albuca paucifolia subsp.

paucifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features a compact, underground bulb and produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant blooms with small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a loose raceme. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
