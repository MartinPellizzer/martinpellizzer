Ageratina ilicifolia

Ageratina ilicifolia, commonly known as the shrub ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, fluffy blue or purple flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Ageratina ilicifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
