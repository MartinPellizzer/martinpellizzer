Asperula dasyantha

Asperula dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Asperula dasyantha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
