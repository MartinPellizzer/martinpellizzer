Asparagus ferganensis

Asparagus ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana asparagus, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
