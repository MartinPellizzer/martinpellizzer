Aspalathus pinguis subsp. longissima

Aspalathus pinguis subsp.

longissima, commonly known as the slender aspalathus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
