Aconitum eriostemum

Aconitum eriostemum, commonly known as the slender aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided, finely toothed leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
