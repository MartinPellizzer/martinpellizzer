Andropogon annulatus var. monostachyus

Andropogon annulatus var. monostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
