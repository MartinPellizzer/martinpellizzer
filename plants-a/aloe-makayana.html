Aloe makayana

Aloe makayana, commonly known as the Makayana aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe makayana is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
