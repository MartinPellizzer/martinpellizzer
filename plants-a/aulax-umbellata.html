Aulax umbellata

Aulax umbellata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it its scientific name. Its fruit, a round, green berry, is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.

The plant is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
