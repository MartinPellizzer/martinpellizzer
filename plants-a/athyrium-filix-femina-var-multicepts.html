Athyrium filix-femina var. multicepts

Athyrium filix-femina var. multicepts, commonly known as the multi-lobed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern characterized by its distinctive, multi-lobed fronds.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China and Japan. The plant features delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically bright green and have a graceful, feathery appearance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

This fern is known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and its preference for well-draining, acidic soil.
