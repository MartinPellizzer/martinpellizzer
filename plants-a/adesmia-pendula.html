Adesmia pendula

Adesmia pendula, commonly known as the hanging bean plant, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with slender, trifoliate leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and greenhouses.
