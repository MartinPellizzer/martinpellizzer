Acacia exilis

Acacia exilis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Acacia exilis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
