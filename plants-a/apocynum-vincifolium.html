Apocynum vincifolium

Apocynum vincifolium, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a follicle that contains seeds with a distinctive, hooked appendage.

Apocynum vincifolium is also known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.
