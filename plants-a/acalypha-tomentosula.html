Acalypha tomentosula

Acalypha tomentosula, commonly known as velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
