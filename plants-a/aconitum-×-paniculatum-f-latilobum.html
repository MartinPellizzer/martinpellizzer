Aconitum × paniculatum f. latilobum

Aconitum × paniculatum f. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple, helmet-shaped flowers. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
