Anthemis karabaghensis

Anthemis karabaghensis, commonly known as the Karabakh anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically produces yellow to orange flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
