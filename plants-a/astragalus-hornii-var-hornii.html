Astragalus hornii var. hornii

Astragalus hornii var. hornii, commonly known as the Horn's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late spring. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
