Acrocephalus reticulatus

Acrocephalus reticulatus, commonly known as the reed warbler, is a grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in dense stands along the edges of marshes, ponds, and streams. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous spikelets.

Acrocephalus reticulatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
