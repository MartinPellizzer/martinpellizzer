Amaryllis × consobrina

Amaryllis × consobrina, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant that results from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems with strap-like leaves that are often evergreen in warmer climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ability to bloom reliably makes it a favorite among gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.
