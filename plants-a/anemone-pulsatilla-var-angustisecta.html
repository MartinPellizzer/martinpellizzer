Anemone pulsatilla var. angustisecta

Anemone pulsatilla var. angustisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky down. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
