Aster worcesterensis

Aster worcesterensis, commonly known as Worcester aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open woodlands and meadows. The plant features showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
