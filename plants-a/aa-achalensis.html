Aa achalensis

Aa achalensis, commonly known as the Indian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Aa achalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
