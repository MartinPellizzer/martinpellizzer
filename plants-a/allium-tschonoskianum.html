Allium tschonoskianum

Allium tschonoskianum, commonly known as the purple garlic or Chinese leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of purple or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and adaptability to various climates.
