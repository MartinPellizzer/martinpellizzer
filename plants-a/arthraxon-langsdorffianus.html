Arthraxon langsdorffianus

Arthraxon langsdorffianus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
