Antimima alborubra

Antimima alborubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed antimima, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellowish flowers during the cooler months, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Antimima alborubra is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides habitat for various desert wildlife.
