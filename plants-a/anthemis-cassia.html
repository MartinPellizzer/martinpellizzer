Anthemis cassia

Anthemis cassia, commonly known as golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a central dark brown disk. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anthemis cassia is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
