Arthrocereus melanurus subsp. estevesii

Arthrocereus melanurus subsp.

estevesii, commonly known as the Estevess's night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and is typically found in dry, rocky environments.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, enabling survival in arid conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
