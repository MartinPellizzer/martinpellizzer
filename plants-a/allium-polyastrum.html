Allium polyastrum

Allium polyastrum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with strap-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines and have been historically valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate and alpine climates.
