Asparagus burkei

Asparagus burkei, commonly known as the silver thread asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. Asparagus burkei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
