Achillea × aspleniifolia

Achillea × aspleniifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, flat-topped yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wildlife-friendly gardens.
