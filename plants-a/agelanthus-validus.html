Agelanthus validus

Agelanthus validus, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Agelanthus validus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
