Achillea millefolium f. pseudopannonica

Achillea millefolium f. pseudopannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian yarrow, is a horticultural form of the common yarrow.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. This cultivar is typically shorter and more compact than the species, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
