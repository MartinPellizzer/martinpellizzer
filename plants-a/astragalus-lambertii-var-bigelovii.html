Astragalus lambertii var. bigelovii

Astragalus lambertii var. bigelovii, commonly known as Lambert's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to survive in poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
