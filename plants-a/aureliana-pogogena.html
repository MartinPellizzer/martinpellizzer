Aureliana pogogena

Aureliana pogogena, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.
