Antidesma vestitum

Antidesma vestitum, commonly known as the velvet antler tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture with a distinctive reddish or brownish coloration. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
