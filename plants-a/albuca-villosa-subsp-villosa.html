Albuca villosa subsp. villosa

Albuca villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the woolly albuca, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a bulbous plant characterized by its gray-green, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
