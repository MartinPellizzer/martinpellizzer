Acacia arabica var. nilotica

Acacia arabica var. nilotica, commonly known as the Sudanese acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, needle-like structures. The plant produces yellow to orange spherical flowers, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

The wood of Acacia arabica var. nilotica is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.
