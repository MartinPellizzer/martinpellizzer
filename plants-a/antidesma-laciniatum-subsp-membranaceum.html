Antidesma laciniatum subsp. membranaceum

Antidesma laciniatum subsp.

membranaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. Its bark is typically smooth and may exude a milky sap when damaged.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
