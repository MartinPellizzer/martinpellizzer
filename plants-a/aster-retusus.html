Aster retusus

Aster retusus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features showy, purple to pinkish-blue composite flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, giving it its common name.

Aster retusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
