Agropyron cristatum subsp. badamense

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

badamense, commonly known as the Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a distinctive crown of bristles at the base.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or alpine environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping and erosion control.
