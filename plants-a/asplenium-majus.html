Asplenium majus

Asplenium majus, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a tropical evergreen plant characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a bird's nest.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with its roots forming a dense, mat-like structure that helps anchor it to trees or rocks. Its leaves are typically broad, leathery, and have a serrated or toothed margin.

Asplenium majus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded, humid environments.
