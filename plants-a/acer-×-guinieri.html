Acer × guinieri

Acer × guinieri, commonly known as the red maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree that exhibits a wide range of leaf colors, including red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental species.

It is native to the eastern United States and Canada, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant hues in the fall.

The species is valued for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in landscaping and woodland settings.
