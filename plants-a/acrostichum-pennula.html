Acrostichum pennula

Acrostichum pennula, commonly known as the giant reed or giant grass, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the stems.

Acrostichum pennula is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
