Acaciella holwayi

Acaciella holwayi, commonly known as the Holway acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
