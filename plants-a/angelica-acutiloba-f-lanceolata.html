Angelica acutiloba f. lanceolata

Angelica acutiloba f. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a biennial or perennial herb native to eastern Asia, including regions of China and Japan. The plant features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The roots of this plant are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
