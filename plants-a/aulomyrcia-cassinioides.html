Aulomyrcia cassinioides

Aulomyrcia cassinioides, commonly known as the Cassini's flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
