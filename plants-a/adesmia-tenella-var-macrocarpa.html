Adesmia tenella var. macrocarpa

Adesmia tenella var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
