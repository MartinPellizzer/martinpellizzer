Acer pumilum

Acer pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Acer pumilum is valued in landscaping for its compact size and seasonal color changes.
