Adenophora latifolia

Adenophora latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Adenophora latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
