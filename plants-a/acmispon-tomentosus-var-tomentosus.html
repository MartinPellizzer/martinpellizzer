Acmispon tomentosus var. tomentosus

Acmispon tomentosus var. tomentosus, commonly known as hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Acmispon tomentosus var. tomentosus is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and its use in ecological restoration projects.
