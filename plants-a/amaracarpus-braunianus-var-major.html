Amaracarpus braunianus var. major

Amaracarpus braunianus var. major, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched stem that produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are arranged in a fan shape, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this species make it well-suited for warm, humid climates and partial shade.
