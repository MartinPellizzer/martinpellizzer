Apetlorhamnus alnifolia

Apetlorhamnus alnifolia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely haired, willow-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forested areas. Its botanical features include elongated, alternate leaves and a dioecious flowering system.

Apetlorhamnus alnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing streambanks.
