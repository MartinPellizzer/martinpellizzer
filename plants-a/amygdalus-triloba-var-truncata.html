Amygdalus triloba var. truncata

Amygdalus triloba var. truncata, commonly known as the flatpod almond, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are encased in a thick, leathery pod.

While not widely cultivated for its nuts, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
