Antirrhinum molle var. angustifolium

Antirrhinum molle var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
