Aristolochia metriosa

Aristolochia metriosa, commonly known as the purple swallow-wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking, hooded flowers in shades of purple and green. The plant's unique floral structure resembles a bird's beak, which is characteristic of the Aristolochiaceae family. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and can climb or trail along the ground.

The plant contains various chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
