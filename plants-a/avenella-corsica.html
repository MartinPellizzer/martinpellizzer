Avenella corsica

Avenella corsica, commonly known as Corsican sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Avenella corsica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
