Anthericum longisetosum

Anthericum longisetosum, commonly known as the long-hair fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a creeping rhizome with fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, long-setae or hairs, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Anthericum longisetosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and shady garden beds.
