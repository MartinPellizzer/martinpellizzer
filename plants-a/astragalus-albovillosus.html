Astragalus albovillosus

Astragalus albovillosus, commonly known as the white-haired milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Astragalus albovillosus is also valued for its ecological importance in native plant communities.
