Astragalus guatemalensis

Astragalus guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Astragalus guatemalensis is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
