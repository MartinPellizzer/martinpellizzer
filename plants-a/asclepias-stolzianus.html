Asclepias stolzianus

Asclepias stolzianus, commonly known as the Stolz's milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly.

Asclepias stolzianus is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
