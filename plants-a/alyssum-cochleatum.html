Alyssum cochleatum

Alyssum cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves and produces seed pods that coil into a spiral shape, giving it its distinctive common name. Alyssum cochleatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
