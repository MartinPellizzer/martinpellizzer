Allamanda magnifica

Allamanda magnifica, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage and can grow as a shrub or climbing vine depending on its environment. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to attract pollinators make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial.
