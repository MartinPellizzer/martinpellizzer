Anthyllis vulneraria var. macrodactylis

Anthyllis vulneraria var. macrodactylis, commonly known as the large-thumbed catchfly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often glandular stems, which can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
