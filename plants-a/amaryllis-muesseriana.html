Amaryllis muesseriana

Amaryllis muesseriana, commonly known as the Muesser's amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, with showy inflorescences that can reach up to 3 feet in height. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
