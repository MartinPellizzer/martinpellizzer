Anaphalis himachalensis

Anaphalis himachalensis, commonly known as the Himachal mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its silvery-white leaves and fluffy, pinkish flower heads contribute to its ornamental value.

Anaphalis himachalensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.
