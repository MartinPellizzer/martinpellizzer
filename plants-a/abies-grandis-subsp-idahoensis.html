Abies grandis subsp. idahoensis

Abies grandis subsp.

idahoensis, commonly known as the Idaho white fir, is a subspecies of the grand fir native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and silver-blue needles that are slightly shorter than those of the typical grand fir.

The cones are typically short and pointed, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
