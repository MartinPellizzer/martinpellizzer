Abies tenuirifolia

Abies tenuirifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are light green and slightly curved. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
