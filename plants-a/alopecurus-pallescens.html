Alopecurus pallescens

Alopecurus pallescens, commonly known as the silver hair vetch, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Alopecurus pallescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
