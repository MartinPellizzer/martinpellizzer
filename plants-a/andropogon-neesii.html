Andropogon neesii

Andropogon neesii, commonly known as Nees' grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its root system is fibrous, contributing to soil stabilization and making it a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
