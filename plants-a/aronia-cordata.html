Aronia cordata

Aronia cordata, commonly known as red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Aronia cordata is also valued for its edible fruits, which are used in jams, juices, and dietary supplements.
