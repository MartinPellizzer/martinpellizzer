Anthemis iberica

Anthemis iberica, commonly known as the Iberian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their calming properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
