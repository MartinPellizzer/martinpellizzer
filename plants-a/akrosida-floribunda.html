Akrosida floribunda

Akrosida floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
