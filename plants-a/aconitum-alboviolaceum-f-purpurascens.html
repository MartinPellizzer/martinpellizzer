Aconitum alboviolaceum f. purpurascens

Aconitum alboviolaceum f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered aconite, is a variant of the aconite species characterized by its striking violet-pink flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features dark green, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, sparsely arranged flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
