Anserina stramoniifolia

Anserina stramoniifolia, commonly known as the common anserina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Anserina stramoniifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
