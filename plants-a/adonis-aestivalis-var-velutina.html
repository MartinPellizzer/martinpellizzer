Adonis aestivalis var. velutina

Adonis aestivalis var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet globemallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with prominent, spurred petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
