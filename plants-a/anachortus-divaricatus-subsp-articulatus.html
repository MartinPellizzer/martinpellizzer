Anachortus divaricatus subsp. articulatus

Anachortus divaricatus subsp.

articulatus, commonly known as the articulated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features triangular stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern and have a distinctive, segmented appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels, making it an important component of aquatic plant communities.
