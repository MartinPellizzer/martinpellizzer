Acacia acanthocarpa

Acacia acanthocarpa, commonly known as the rough-bark acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and phyllodes that are typically narrow and rough in texture. The plant has a woody, fissured bark that provides structural support and protection. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and low water requirements.

Acacia acanthocarpa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
