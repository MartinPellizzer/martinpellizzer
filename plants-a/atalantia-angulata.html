Atalantia angulata

Atalantia angulata, commonly known as the angular atalantia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves with a distinctive angular shape. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Its unique foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
