Alchemilla rudatisii

Alchemilla rudatisii, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla rudatisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
