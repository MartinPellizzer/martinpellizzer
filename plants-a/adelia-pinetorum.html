Adelia pinetorum

Adelia pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest geranium, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Adelia pinetorum thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
