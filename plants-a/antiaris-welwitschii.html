Antiaris welwitschii

Antiaris welwitschii, commonly known as the Natal umbrella tree, is a large, evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by its dense, spreading branches. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a waxy, glossy surface.

Antiaris welwitschii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
