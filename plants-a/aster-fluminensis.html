Aster fluminensis

Aster fluminensis, commonly known as the Fluminense aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually blue or purple.

Aster fluminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
