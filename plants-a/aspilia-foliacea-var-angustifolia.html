Aspilia foliacea var. angustifolia

Aspilia foliacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved torchflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble torches. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the plant typically grows in full sun. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
