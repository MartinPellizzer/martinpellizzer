Amaranthus carolinae

Amaranthus carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina amaranth, is an annual herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of development. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
