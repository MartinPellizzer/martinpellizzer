Asperula oppositifolia subsp. swatensis

Asperula oppositifolia subsp.

swatensis, commonly known as the Swat hollyhock, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
