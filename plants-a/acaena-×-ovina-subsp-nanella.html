Acaena × ovina subsp. nanella

Acaena × ovina subsp.

nanella, commonly known as the small-flowered burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant's fruits are spherical, hairy burrs that disperse seeds via wind and animals. It thrives in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
