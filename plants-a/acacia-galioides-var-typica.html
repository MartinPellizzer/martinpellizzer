Acacia galioides var. typica

Acacia galioides var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
