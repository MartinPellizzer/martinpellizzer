Agathophora alopecuroides var. papillosa

Agathophora alopecuroides var. papillosa, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this variant.
