Arachis valida

Arachis valida, commonly known as the Brazilian peanut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds, which are similar to common peanuts but have a distinct flavor. Arachis valida is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in agricultural and ecological studies.
