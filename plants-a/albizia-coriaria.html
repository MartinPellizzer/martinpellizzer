Albizia coriaria

Albizia coriaria, commonly known as the Persian silk tree or velvetwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in dense clusters. Albizia coriaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of climates.
