Aureolaria pedicularia var. ambigens

Aureolaria pedicularia var. ambigens, commonly known as the yellow catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
