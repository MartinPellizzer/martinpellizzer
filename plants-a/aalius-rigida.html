Aalius rigida

Aalius rigida, commonly known as the rigid aalius, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Aalius rigida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in colder climates, contributing to its ecological significance in local ecosystems.
