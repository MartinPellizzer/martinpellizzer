Astragalus biabanensis

Astragalus biabanensis, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, showcasing deep root systems and drought tolerance. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
