Alopecurus pratensis var. exaltatus

Alopecurus pratensis var. exaltatus, commonly known as the tall meadow foxtail, is a variant of the meadow foxtail grass.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces tall, cylindrical spikelets that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it often grows in dense clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
