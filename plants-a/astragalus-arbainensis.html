Astragalus arbainensis

Astragalus arbainensis, commonly known as the Arabian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Astragalus arbainensis plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
