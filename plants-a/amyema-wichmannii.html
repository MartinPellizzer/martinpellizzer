Amyema wichmannii

Amyema wichmannii, commonly known as the Woolly Sunburst, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a thick layer of white, fuzzy hairs.

Amyema wichmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens due to its drought tolerance.
