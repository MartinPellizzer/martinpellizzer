Astragalus angustifolius

Astragalus angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers in spikes. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
