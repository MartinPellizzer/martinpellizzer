Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. howellii

Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's manzanita, is a shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late winter or early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and chaparral habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
