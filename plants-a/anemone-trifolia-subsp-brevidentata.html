Anemone trifolia subsp. brevidentata

Anemone trifolia subsp.

brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features basal leaves that are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are showy, with white to pink petals and a yellow center, blooming in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter, more rounded leaf teeth compared to other subspecies of Anemone trifolia.
