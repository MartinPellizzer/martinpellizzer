Amomum biphyllum

Amomum biphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and rhizomes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a three-lobed, triangular capsule containing aromatic seeds.

The plant's botanical features make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
