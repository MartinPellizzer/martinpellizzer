Arabis hesperidoides

Arabis hesperidoides, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters. It produces slender, elongated seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature.

Arabis hesperidoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
