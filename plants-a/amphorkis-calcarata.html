Amphorkis calcarata

Amphorkis calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive spurred structure. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
