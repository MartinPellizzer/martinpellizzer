Asparagus lucidus var. dolichocladus

Asparagus lucidus var. dolichocladus, commonly known as the long-stemmed asparagus, is a variant of the asparagus plant characterized by its elongated, slender stems.

It is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that give way to edible, needle-like spears. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, typical of many asparagus species.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible qualities.
