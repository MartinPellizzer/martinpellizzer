Ammannia hildebrandtii

Ammannia hildebrandtii, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
