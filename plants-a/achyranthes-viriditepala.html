Achyranthes viriditepala

Achyranthes viriditepala, commonly known as greenleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves that are typically smooth and green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five stamens and a single pistil, typically in shades of pink or red. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that dehisces to release tiny seeds.

Achyranthes viriditepala is often found in moist soils along streambanks and in wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments.

Morphology

Achyranthes viriditepala has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically 5-10 cm long and 2-4 cm wide, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stems are cylindrical, somewhat woody, and have a rough, ridged texture, growing upright with branching patterns. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in spikes, blooming during the summer months, and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping the plant anchor in soil and absorb nutrients.
