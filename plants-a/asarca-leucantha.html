Asarca leucantha

Asarca leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered asarum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, white, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a creeping rhizome system. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and the plant is known for its aromatic, often foul-smelling odor.

Asarca leucantha is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
