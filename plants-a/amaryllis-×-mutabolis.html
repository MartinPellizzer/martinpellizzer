Amaryllis × mutabolis

Amaryllis × mutabolis, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola, producing a variety of colors and flower forms. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with strap-like leaves and clustered flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden beds and container cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a favored choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
