Abies × arnoldiana

Abies × arnoldiana, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Abies concolor and Abies lasiocarpa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-green needles and a conical shape that becomes more compact with age. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly in higher elevations of the Rocky Mountains. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The cones are small and typically disintegrate into a fluffy mass, releasing seeds that can germinate in suitable conditions.
