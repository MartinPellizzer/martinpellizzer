Armeria undulata

Armeria undulata, commonly known as the undulating thrift, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of pink to white flowers in early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its simple structure and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
