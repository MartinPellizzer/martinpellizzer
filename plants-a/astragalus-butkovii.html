Astragalus butkovii

Astragalus butkovii, commonly known as the Butkov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus butkovii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
