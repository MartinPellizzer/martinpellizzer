Abies metensis

Abies metensis, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to high-altitude regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Abies, which includes other fir species, and is characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Abies metensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in suitable climates.
