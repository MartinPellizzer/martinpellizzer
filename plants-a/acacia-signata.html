Acacia signata

Acacia signata, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Acacia signata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
