Achillea erba-rotta subsp. rupestris

Achillea erba-rotta subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock yarrow, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

This subspecies is native to rocky and arid environments in southern Europe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
