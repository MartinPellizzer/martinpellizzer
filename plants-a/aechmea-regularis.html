Aechmea regularis

Aechmea regularis, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they often feature intricate patterns and a slight bluish or reddish tinge. The plant produces a flowering spike that can reach several feet in height, bearing showy, spathiflorous flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

After flowering, it produces offsets that can be propagated to create new plants.

Morphology

Aechmea regularis has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a true stem, with the base of the leaves forming a dense, compact rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically pink or red, and bloom during the summer months, pollinated by insects. The fruit is a berry that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb water efficiently from the air and soil.
