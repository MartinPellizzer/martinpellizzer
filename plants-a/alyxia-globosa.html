Alyxia globosa

Alyxia globosa, commonly known as the globular alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodlands. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

Alyxia globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
