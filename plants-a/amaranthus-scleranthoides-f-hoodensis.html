Amaranthus scleranthoides f. hoodensis

Amaranthus scleranthoides f. hoodensis, commonly known as the Hood's amaranth, is a variant of the amaranth genus characterized by its robust growth and distinctive foliage.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. This plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and full sun conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
