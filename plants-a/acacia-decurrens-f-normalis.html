Acacia decurrens f. normalis

Acacia decurrens f. normalis, commonly known as the common wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that appear in spring and summer. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Acacia decurrens f. normalis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
