Astragalus nuttallianus var. canescens

Astragalus nuttallianus var. canescens, commonly known as the gray milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with finely divided leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
