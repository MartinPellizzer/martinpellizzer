Allium cusickii

Allium cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs and flowering stems that bear white to pale pink flowers with six petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
