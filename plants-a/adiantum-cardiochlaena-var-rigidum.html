Adiantum cardiochlaena var. rigidum

Adiantum cardiochlaena var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arching and have a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are wiry and often grow close to the ground, forming dense clumps.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in woodland gardens or as a houseplant.
