Aegialinites annulatus

Aegialinites annulatus, commonly known as the ringed calcified alga, is a fossilized organism that represents a type of marine alga.

It is characterized by its calcified, cylindrical structure with concentric rings, which are indicative of its growth patterns. This plant thrived in shallow marine environments during the Paleozoic era. Its structure suggests it was a colonial organism, possibly related to modern green algae.

The fossil is often found in sedimentary rock layers, providing valuable insights into ancient marine ecosystems.
