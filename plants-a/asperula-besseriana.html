Asperula besseriana

Asperula besseriana, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pink to red flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Asperula besseriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
