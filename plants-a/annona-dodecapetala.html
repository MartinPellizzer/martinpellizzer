Annona dodecapetala

Annona dodecapetala, commonly known as the custard apple or sweetsop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, fleshy, yellow or green fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

The flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive, fragrant odor.
