Allium reticulatum var. ecristatum

Allium reticulatum var. ecristatum, commonly known as the crested leek, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features a distinctive, flattened, crested inflorescence composed of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
