Astragalus echinus subsp. echinus

Astragalus echinus subsp.

echinus, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
