Aristea grandis

Aristea grandis, commonly known as the large aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Aristea grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
