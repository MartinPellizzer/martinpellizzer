Atropis distans var. tenuis

Atropis distans var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender sandverbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink to purple in color.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
