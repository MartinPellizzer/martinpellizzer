Amaracarpus grandifolius var. trichocarpus

Amaracarpus grandifolius var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
