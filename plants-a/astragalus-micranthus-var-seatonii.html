Astragalus micranthus var. seatonii

Astragalus micranthus var. seatonii, commonly known as the Seaton's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
