Amaryllis lateritia

Amaryllis lateritia, commonly known as the desert amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a dormant period during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically appearing after the flowering season.

Amaryllis lateritia is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
