Agrostis Capillaris Var. Canina

Agrostis capillaris var. canina is a lesser-known grass species often found in coastal and sandy environments. It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and ability to thrive in harsh, salty conditions. This variant is closely related to the common bentgrass but exhibits distinct adaptations for survival in its native habitat. Its small stature and dense growth pattern make it an important component of dune ecosystems. Despite its ecological significance, it remains underappreciated in botanical studies and landscaping practices.
