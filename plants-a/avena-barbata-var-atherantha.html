Avena barbata var. atherantha

Avena barbata var. atherantha, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, pale green to brown spikelets.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
