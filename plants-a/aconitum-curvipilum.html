Aconitum curvipilum

Aconitum curvipilum, commonly known as the hooked-hair monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and its stems are typically hairy, especially near the base. Aconitum curvipilum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its native habitat.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
