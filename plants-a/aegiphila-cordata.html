Aegiphila cordata

Aegiphila cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
