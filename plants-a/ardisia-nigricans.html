Ardisia nigricans

Ardisia nigricans, commonly known as the black beauty berry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that persist on the plant throughout the winter, providing ornamental value. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, contributing to its visual appeal in gardens.

Ardisia nigricans is often cultivated for its striking fruit and adaptability to various soil types.
