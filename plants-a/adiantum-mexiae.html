Adiantum mexiae

Adiantum mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive veining pattern.

Adiantum mexiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
