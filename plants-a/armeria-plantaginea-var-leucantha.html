Armeria plantaginea var. leucantha

Armeria plantaginea var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear and gray-green in color, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
