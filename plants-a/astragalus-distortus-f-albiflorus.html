Astragalus distortus f. albiflorus

Astragalus distortus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Astragalus genus.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical yellow or brown blooms of the species. This subspecies is native to specific regions in North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
