Acacia montigena

Acacia montigena, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are an important food source for pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Acacia montigena is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
