Agathosma rubra

Agathosma rubra, commonly known as the red agathosma, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
