Allium nabelekii

Allium nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelekii onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical bulbs and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
