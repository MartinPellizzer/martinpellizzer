Artemisia wrightii var. coloradensis

Artemisia wrightii var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado blue sage, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, distinctive scent.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
