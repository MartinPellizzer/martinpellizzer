Arabis hirsuta var. angustifolia

Arabis hirsuta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mouse-ear cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and hairy, with small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
