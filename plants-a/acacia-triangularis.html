Acacia triangularis

Acacia triangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular-shaped leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads that appear in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often used in reforestation projects due to its hardiness.

The seeds of Acacia triangularis are encased in a hard pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.
