Adlumia cirrhosa var. rosea

Adlumia cirrhosa var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered false indigo, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
