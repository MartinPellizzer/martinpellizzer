Adesmia multicuspis

Adesmia multicuspis, commonly known as the many-toothed adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and clustered, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in legumes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

Adesmia multicuspis is often found in regions with low rainfall and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features.
