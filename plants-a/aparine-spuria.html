Aparine spuria

Aparine spuria, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the summer months. The plant has hairy, branched stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

The species is known for its prickly leaves, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
