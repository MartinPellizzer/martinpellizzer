Arceuthobium abietinum subsp. mathiasenii

Arceuthobium abietinum subsp.

mathiasenii, commonly known as the dwarf pine mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on coniferous hosts for survival. It lacks chlorophyll and is entirely dependent on its host for nutrients, forming a symbiotic relationship.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that release tiny seeds through a mechanism called autochory. These seeds are dispersed by wind and can germinate on the bark of suitable host trees.

Due to its specialized parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in forest ecosystems.
