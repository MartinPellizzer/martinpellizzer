Adenophora borealis var. linearifolia

Adenophora borealis var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or white. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in cold climates.
