Ancistrocladus attenuatus

Ancistrocladus attenuatus, commonly known as the African poisonwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glandular-stipulate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a hazardous plant for both humans and livestock.
