Amorphophallus corrugatus

Amorphophallus corrugatus, commonly known as the creased giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The tubers are sometimes used in traditional cuisine, though they can be toxic if not prepared properly.
