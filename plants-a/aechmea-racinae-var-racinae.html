Aechmea racinae var. racinae

Aechmea racinae var. racinae, commonly known as the Rosy Pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or pink markings, which become more vivid under direct sunlight. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange, often resembling a pineapple in appearance. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is notable for its ability to store water in its leaf axils, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
