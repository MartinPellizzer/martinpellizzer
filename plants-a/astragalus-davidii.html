Astragalus davidii

Astragalus davidii, commonly known as David's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus davidii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
