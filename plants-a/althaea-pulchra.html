Althaea pulchra

Althaea pulchra, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pale pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are hairy and often branched. Althaea pulchra thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
