Astragalus domeykoanus

Astragalus domeykoanus, commonly known as the Domeyko milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers, which is crucial for its survival in its native habitat.

Astragalus domeykoanus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
