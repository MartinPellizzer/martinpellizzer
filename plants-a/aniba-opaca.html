Aniba opaca

Aniba opaca, commonly known as the white mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a dense, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are consumed by various bird species.

Aniba opaca plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
