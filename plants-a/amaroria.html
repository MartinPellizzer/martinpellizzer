Amaroria

Amaroria, commonly known as the golden amaranth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling a plume or feather. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are small, dry, and clustered in dense heads, which are a key feature of the species.

Amaroria thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
