Alchemilla palii

Alchemilla palii, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Alchemilla palii is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
