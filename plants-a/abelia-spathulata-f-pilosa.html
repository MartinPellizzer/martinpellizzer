Abelia spathulata f. pilosa

Abelia spathulata f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy abelia, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its showy, long-lasting blooms.
