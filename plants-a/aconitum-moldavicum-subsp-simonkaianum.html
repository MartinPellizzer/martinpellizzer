Aconitum moldavicum subsp. simonkaianum

Aconitum moldavicum subsp.

simonkaianum, commonly known as the Moldavian aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant features palmately divided leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, and its roots contain potent alkaloids. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
