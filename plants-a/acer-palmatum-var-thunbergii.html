Acer palmatum var. thunbergii

Acer palmatum var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage that turns vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The plant has opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green in summer and change color seasonally. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras.

This species thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
