Anthurium polyneuron

Anthurium polyneuron, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a prominent red or pink vein running down the center. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often a deep red or orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Anthurium polyneuron is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
