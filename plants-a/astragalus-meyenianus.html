Astragalus meyenianus

Astragalus meyenianus, commonly known as Meyen's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus meyenianus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
