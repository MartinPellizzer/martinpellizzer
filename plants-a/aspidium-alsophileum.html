Aspidium alsophileum

Aspidium alsophileum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water ferns.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for propagation through aquatic dispersion.

The plant's botanical structure includes a rhizome that supports the fronds and facilitates nutrient absorption from the surrounding water.
