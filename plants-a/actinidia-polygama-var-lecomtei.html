Actinidia polygama var. lecomtei

Actinidia polygama var. lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte kiwi, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that are similar in taste to kiwifruit. These berries are often used in culinary applications due to their tart flavor and high vitamin C content.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its potential in fruit cultivation.
