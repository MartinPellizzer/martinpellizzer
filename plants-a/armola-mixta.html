Armola mixta

Armola mixta, commonly known as the hybrid armola, is a unique plant species that combines traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often a mix of green and variegated colors. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from poor soils.

Armola mixta is native to tropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
