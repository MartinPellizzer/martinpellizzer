Acacia acuminata var. ciliata

Acacia acuminata var. ciliata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular spikes, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its seed pods are linear and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils across the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
