Artemisia japonica var. rotundifolia

Artemisia japonica var. rotundifolia, commonly known as Japanese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with lance-shaped to rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as panicles, which appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized in traditional medicine.
