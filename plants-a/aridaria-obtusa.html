Aridaria obtusa

Aridaria obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf desert shrub, is a hardy plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It features woody stems and broad, leathery leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often found in rocky soils and is resistant to drought conditions.

Aridaria obtusa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
