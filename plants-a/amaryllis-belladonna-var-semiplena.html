Amaryllis belladonna var. semiplena

Amaryllis belladonna var. semiplena, commonly known as the double amaryllis, is a striking hybrid cultivar characterized by its dense, layered blooms.

This variety originates from the natural hybridization between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola, resulting in its unique floral structure. The flowers are large, trumpet-shaped, and often display vibrant shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.
