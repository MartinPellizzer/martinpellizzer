Aglaia amplexicaulis

Aglaia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwood trees. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, simple, and often glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically clustered in axillary inflorescences.
