Atossa sepium

Atossa sepium, commonly known as the common sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that encase the flower heads. Native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. The plant's structure includes a short stem with alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Atossa sepium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
