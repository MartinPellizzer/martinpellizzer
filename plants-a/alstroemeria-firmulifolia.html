Alstroemeria firmulifolia

Alstroemeria firmulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with lance-shaped leaves that are often broad at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
