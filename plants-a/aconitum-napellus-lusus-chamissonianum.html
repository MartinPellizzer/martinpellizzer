Aconitum napellus lusus chamissonianum

Aconitum napellus lusus chamissonianum, commonly known as the Chamissonian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately compound with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are smooth and often branched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it highly poisonous to both animals and humans.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its dangers.
