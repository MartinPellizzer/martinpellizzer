Astragalus biserratus

Astragalus biserratus, commonly known as the serrated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

Astragalus biserratus has small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and as a food source for wildlife.
