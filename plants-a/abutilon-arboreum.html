Abutilon arboreum

Abutilon arboreum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or tree abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Abutilon arboreum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
