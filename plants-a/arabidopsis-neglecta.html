Arabidopsis neglecta

Arabidopsis neglecta, commonly known as the neglected mustard, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and produces small white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Arabidopsis neglecta is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
