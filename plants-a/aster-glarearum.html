Aster glarearum

Aster glarearum, commonly known as the glaucous aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Aster glarearum thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its unique coloration and ecological role make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
