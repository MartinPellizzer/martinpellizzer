Actaea frigida

Actaea frigida, commonly known as the Arctic black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It grows in rocky, moist soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers in early summer, followed by red or orange berries. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Actaea frigida is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
