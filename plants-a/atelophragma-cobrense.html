Atelophragma cobrense

Atelophragma cobrense, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a yellowish throat, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Atelophragma cobrense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
