Anagallis linifolia var. trojana

Anagallis linifolia var. trojana, commonly known as the Trojana pinkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, creeping stems and produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
