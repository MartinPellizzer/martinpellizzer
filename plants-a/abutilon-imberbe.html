Abutilon imberbe

Abutilon imberbe, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves are typical of the Malvaceae family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
