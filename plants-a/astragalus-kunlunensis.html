Astragalus kunlunensis

Astragalus kunlunensis, commonly known as the Kunlun milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly the Kunlun Mountains.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, yellow to brown pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus kunlunensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
