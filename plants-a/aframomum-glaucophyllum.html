Aframomum glaucophyllum

Aframomum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the African cardamom, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with a waxy, bluish-white coating, which gives it its scientific name "glaucophyllum.".
