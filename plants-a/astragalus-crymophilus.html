Astragalus crymophilus

Astragalus crymophilus, commonly known as the snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold climates. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
