Aconitum napellus nothosubsp. hinterhuberi

Aconitum napellus nothosubsp.

hinterhuberi, commonly known as the Hinterhuber's monkshood, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species. It is a herbaceous perennial known for its striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant's leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
