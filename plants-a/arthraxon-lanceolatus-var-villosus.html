Arthraxon lanceolatus var. villosus

Arthraxon lanceolatus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy lance sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical arrangements.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
