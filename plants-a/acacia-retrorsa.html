Acacia retrorsa

Acacia retrorsa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and clusters of small, yellow, globular flowers. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous mesh.

It is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
