Anthurium anorianum

Anthurium anorianum, commonly known as the Anorianum Arum, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence that ranges in color from red to yellow. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique flower structure consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Anthurium anorianum thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and dramatic floral display.
