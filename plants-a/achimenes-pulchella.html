Achimenes pulchella

Achimenes pulchella, commonly known as the flame violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often with a distinctive tubular shape. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season adds visual interest to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
