Aconitum lycoctonum var. lamarckii

Aconitum lycoctonum var. lamarckii, commonly known as Lamarck's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces erect, sparsely branched stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
