Acanthophyllum fissicalyx

Acanthophyllum fissicalyx, commonly known as the fissured acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, succulent stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

This species plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
