Acacia ptychophylla

Acacia ptychophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia ptychophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
