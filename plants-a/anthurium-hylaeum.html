Anthurium hylaeum

Anthurium hylaeum, commonly known as the Hylaeum arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically deep green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the showy spathe serving as an attractant for pollinators.

Anthurium hylaeum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
