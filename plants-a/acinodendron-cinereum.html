Acinodendron cinereum

Acinodendron cinereum, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acinodendron cinereum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
