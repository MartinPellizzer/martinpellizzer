Acacia mutabilis subsp. mutabilis

Acacia mutabilis subsp.

mutabilis, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves that change color throughout the growing season, ranging from bright green to red or even purple.

The plant produces yellow to orange globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
