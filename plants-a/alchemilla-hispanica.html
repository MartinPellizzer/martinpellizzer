Alchemilla hispanica

Alchemilla hispanica, commonly known as Spanish lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla hispanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
