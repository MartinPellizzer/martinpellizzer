Aeschynanthus brevicalyx

Aeschynanthus brevicalyx, commonly known as the short-lobed waxflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers.

Aeschynanthus brevicalyx is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
