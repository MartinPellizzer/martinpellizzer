Aristea humbertii

Aristea humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's aristea, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
