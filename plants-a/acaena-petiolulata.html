Acaena petiolulata

Acaena petiolulata, commonly known as the small-leaf burrplant, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments. The plant produces small, prickly fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a hairy, spiny husk. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Acaena petiolulata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
