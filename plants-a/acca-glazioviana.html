Acca glazioviana

Acca glazioviana, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or glaziovian guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor, similar to that of guavas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Acca glazioviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits in tropical and subtropical regions.
