Aurantium maximum

Aurantium maximum, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Aurantium maximum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
