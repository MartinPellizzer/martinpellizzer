Arisaema hakonecola

Arisaema hakonecola, commonly known as the Japanese crown imperial flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, spadix-shaped inflorescence surrounded by a broad, waxy spathe. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm and features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
