Arundinaria griffithiana

Arundinaria griffithiana, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Griffith's bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense clumps.

Arundinaria griffithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique growth habit and resilience.
