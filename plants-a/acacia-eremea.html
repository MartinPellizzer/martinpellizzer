Acacia eremea

Acacia eremea, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are typically reduced to spines, a feature that helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia eremea is well adapted to survive in harsh conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
