Arabis falcata

Arabis falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are narrow and sickle-shaped, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are arranged in a raceme.

Arabis falcata is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
