Aragallus hallii

Aragallus hallii, commonly known as the Hall's aragallus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that give it a delicate appearance.

Aragallus hallii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
