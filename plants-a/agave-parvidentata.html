Agave parvidentata

Agave parvidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Agave parvidentata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
