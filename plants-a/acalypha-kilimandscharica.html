Acalypha kilimandscharica

Acalypha kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro spurflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spade-shaped leaves that are often adorned with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
