Achimenes rollisonii

Achimenes rollisonii, commonly known as the rolling hills achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Achimenes rollisonii has leaves that are heart-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem, with a moderate size and a vibrant green color.

The stem is slender, slightly hairy, and has a light green to reddish-brown bark, growing upright with a branching pattern. Its flowers are tubular with a spurred shape, displaying a striking combination of red and purple, blooming from late summer to autumn, and are primarily pollinated by bees.
