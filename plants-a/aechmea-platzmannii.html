Aechmea platzmannii

Aechmea platzmannii, commonly known as the Platzmann's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is often brightly colored, with vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues that enhance its ornamental appeal. It produces inflorescences with showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, adding to its visual impact.

Aechmea platzmannii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea platzmannii has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy, dark green color and a pointed tip.

The stem is short and thick, covered in a smooth, grayish-brown bark, and grows upright from the base of the rosette. The plant produces striking, red-tipped inflorescences with white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, pollinated primarily by bees and hummingbirds. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds through mechanical means when it dries and splits open.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its rocky or dry habitat.
