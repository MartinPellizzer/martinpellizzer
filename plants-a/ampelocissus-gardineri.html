Ampelocissus gardineri

Ampelocissus gardineri, commonly known as the Gardeners' fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Ampelocissus gardineri is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls effectively.
