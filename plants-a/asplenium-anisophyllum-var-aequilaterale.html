Asplenium anisophyllum var. aequilaterale

Asplenium anisophyllum var. aequilaterale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf maidenhair fern.

It is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae, characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that give the plant a finely textured appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
