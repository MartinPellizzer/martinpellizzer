Asparagus buchananii

Asparagus buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's asparagus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
