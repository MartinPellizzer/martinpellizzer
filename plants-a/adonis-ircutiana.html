Adonis ircutiana

Adonis ircutiana, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other showy wildflowers.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
