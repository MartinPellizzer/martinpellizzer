Aloitis quinquefolia

Aloitis quinquefolia, commonly known as the Quinquefolia Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
