Adenandra umbellata var. subpubescens

Adenandra umbellata var. subpubescens, commonly known as the hairy adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are elliptical and often have a bluish-green hue, while the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
