Aegopodium handelii

Aegopodium handelii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ground ivy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
