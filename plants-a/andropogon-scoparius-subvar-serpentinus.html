Andropogon scoparius subvar. serpentinus

Andropogon scoparius subvar. serpentinus, commonly known as serpentine bluestem, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and is often found in rocky, serpentine soils. This variety is adapted to nutrient-poor conditions and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, with tall, erect stems.

Serpentine bluestem plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
