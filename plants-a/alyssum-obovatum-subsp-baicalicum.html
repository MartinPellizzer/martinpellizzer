Alyssum obovatum subsp. baicalicum

Alyssum obovatum subsp.

baicalicum, commonly known as the Baicalian alissum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The leaves are typically ovate to obovate in shape, with a hairy surface that gives the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
