Aridaria watermeyeri

Aridaria watermeyeri, commonly known as the desert bell, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Aridaria watermeyeri is well adapted to high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
