Astragalus schizotropis var. emarginatus

Astragalus schizotropis var. emarginatus, commonly known as the emarginated milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to soil stabilization through its extensive root network.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation strategies and potential medicinal properties.
