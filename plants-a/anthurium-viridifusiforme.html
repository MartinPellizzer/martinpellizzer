Anthurium viridifusiforme

Anthurium viridifusiforme, commonly known as the green-veined anthurium, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with prominent, light green veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium viridifusiforme is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying properties.
