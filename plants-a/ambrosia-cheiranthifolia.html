Ambrosia cheiranthifolia

Ambrosia cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
