Aira kingii

Aira kingii, commonly known as King's aira, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
