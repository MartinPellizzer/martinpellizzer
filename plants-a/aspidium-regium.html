Aspidium regium

Aspidium regium, commonly known as the royal water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a water lily.

Aspidium regium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
