Agropyron cristatum f. calvum

Agropyron cristatum f. calvum, commonly known as the smooth wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the colder regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for erosion control and forage in arid environments.
