Astragalus pinetorum subsp. alamutensis

Astragalus pinetorum subsp.

alamutensis, commonly known as the Almuhut milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia, contributing to soil fertility.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
