Amelanchier vulgaris var. zapateri

Amelanchier vulgaris var. zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
