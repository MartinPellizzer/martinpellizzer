Acer campbellii

Acer campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree typically grows to a height of 10 to 15 meters, with a broad, rounded canopy. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
