Arisaema cumingii

Arisaema cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, palmate leaf structure with lobes radiating from a central point. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a showy, hooded spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowering occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
