Ardisia calophylla

Ardisia calophylla, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Ardisia calophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
