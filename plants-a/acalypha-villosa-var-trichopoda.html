Acalypha villosa var. trichopoda

Acalypha villosa var. trichopoda, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, bright orange to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are often overshadowed by its vibrant bracts, which serve to attract pollinators.
