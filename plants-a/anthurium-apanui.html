Anthurium apanui

Anthurium apanui, commonly known as the Apanui arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces strikingly colorful spathe that can range from white to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its edible roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.
