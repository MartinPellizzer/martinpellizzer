Andropogon gayanus var. cordofanus

Andropogon gayanus var. cordofanus, commonly known as the purple bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to reddish tints in its foliage and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for native landscapes. Its dense growth habit makes it useful for erosion control and wildlife habitat.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, adding ornamental value to the plant.
