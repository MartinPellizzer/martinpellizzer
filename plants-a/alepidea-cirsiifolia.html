Alepidea cirsiifolia

Alepidea cirsiifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf alepidea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
