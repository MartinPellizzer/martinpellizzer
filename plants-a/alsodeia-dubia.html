Alsodeia dubia

Alsodeia dubia, commonly known as the slender alsike, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Alsodeia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
