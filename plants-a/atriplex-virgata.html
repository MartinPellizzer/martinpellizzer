Atriplex virgata

Atriplex virgata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant is adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Atriplex virgata plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
