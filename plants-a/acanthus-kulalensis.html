Acanthus kulalensis

Acanthus kulalensis, commonly known as the Kulal acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
