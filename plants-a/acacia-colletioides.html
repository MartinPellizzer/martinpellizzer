Acacia colletioides

Acacia colletioides, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Acacia colletioides is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
