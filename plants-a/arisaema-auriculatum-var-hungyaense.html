Arisaema auriculatum var. hungyaense

Arisaema auriculatum var. hungyaense, commonly known as the Chinese arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features a distinctive, heart-shaped leaf with a prominent, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
