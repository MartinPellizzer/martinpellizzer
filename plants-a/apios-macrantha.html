Apios macrantha

Apios macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, violet to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods containing several round, edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Apios macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
