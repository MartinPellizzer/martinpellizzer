Astragalus igoschinae

Astragalus igoschinae, commonly known as the Igoschina milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Astragalus igoschinae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
