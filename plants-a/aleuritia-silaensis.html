Aleuritia silaensis

Aleuritia silaensis, commonly known as the Sila alder, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae, which includes birches and alders, and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
