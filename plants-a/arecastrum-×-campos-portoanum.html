Arecastrum × campos-portoanum

Arecastrum × campos-portoanum, commonly known as the hybrid areca palm, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Arecastrum leprosum and Arecastrum romanzoffianum.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is native to Brazil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Arecastrum × campos-portoanum is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer regions.
