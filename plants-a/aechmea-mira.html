Aechmea mira

Aechmea mira, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-colored trichomes, giving the plant its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.

Morphology

Aechmea mira has broad, strap-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, typically measuring up to 30 cm in length and 5 cm in width, with a dark green to bluish-gray coloration.

The plant lacks a true stem, and its base is covered in a dense, fibrous sheath that resembles bark, with a rough texture and a dark brown color. It produces striking inflorescences with long, spadices bearing small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, blooming during the summer months and pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by animals consuming the fruit.

Its root system consists of a central, fibrous root that anchors the plant and absorbs moisture from the surrounding environment.
