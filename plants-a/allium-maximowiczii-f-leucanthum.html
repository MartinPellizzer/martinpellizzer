Allium maximowiczii f. leucanthum

Allium maximowiczii f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered garlic chive, is a cultivated form of Allium maximowiczii, a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and clusters of pale white to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that resemble garlic cloves, which can be harvested for culinary use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like flavor, making it both aesthetically pleasing and edible.
