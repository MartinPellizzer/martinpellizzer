Astragalus yazdii

Astragalus yazdii, commonly known as the Yazd milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus yazdii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
