Arundinaria basiaurita

Arundinaria basiaurita, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, often reddish stems. The plant typically forms dense clumps and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Arundinaria basiaurita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
