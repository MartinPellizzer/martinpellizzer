Abies torano

Abies torano, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Abies in the family Pinaceae, characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool and moist climates. Its foliage is dense and dark green, with a fragrant aroma, and it produces small, cylindrical cones.

The bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental value.
