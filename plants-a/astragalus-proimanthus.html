Astragalus proimanthus

Astragalus proimanthus, commonly known as the Sierra grass vetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, particularly found in mountainous regions of California and Nevada. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus proimanthus is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.
