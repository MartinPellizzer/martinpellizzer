Acacia niopo

Acacia niopo, commonly known as the Niopo tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small leaflets. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. Acacia niopo is adapted to coastal and dry inland habitats, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
