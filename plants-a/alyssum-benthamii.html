Alyssum benthamii

Alyssum benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum benthamii is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
