Andropogon imberbis var. genuinus

Andropogon imberbis var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
