Agropyron pouzolzii var. rottboellioides

Agropyron pouzolzii var. rottboellioides, commonly known as the Rottboell's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
