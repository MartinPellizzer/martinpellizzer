Achyranthes aspera var. late-ovata

Achyranthes aspera var. late-ovata, commonly known as the late-ovate knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and branching stems. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough, textured surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing a distinctive appearance.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
