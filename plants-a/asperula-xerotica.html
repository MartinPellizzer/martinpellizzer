Asperula xerotica

Asperula xerotica, commonly known as the desert azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Asperula xerotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
