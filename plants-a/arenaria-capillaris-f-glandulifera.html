Arenaria capillaris f. glandulifera

Arenaria capillaris f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are linear and covered with glandular hairs, which give the plant its distinguishing feature. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or barren habitats.

This variant is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.
