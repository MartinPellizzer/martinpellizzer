Acer negundo var. trifoliatum

Acer negundo var. trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaf maple, is a variant of the box elder tree characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to North America, typically growing in moist soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The leaves are palmately compound, with three leaflets that are often serrated and have a smooth, shiny surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse via wind.

This plant is valued for its adaptability and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental foliage.
