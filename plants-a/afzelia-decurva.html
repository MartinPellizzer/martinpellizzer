Afzelia decurva

Afzelia decurva, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by legume-like pods containing flat, dark seeds. Afzelia decurva is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
