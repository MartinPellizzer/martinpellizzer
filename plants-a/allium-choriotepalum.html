Allium choriotepalum

Allium choriotepalum, commonly known as the Turk's turban, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of lavender to purple flowers on tall, slender stems, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

Allium choriotepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
