Acalypha eremorum var. capillipeda

Acalypha eremorum var. capillipeda, commonly known as the silky acalypha, is a shrub native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often followed by distinctive, papery bracts. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and reduced leaf surface area.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
