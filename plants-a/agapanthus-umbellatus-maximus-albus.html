Agapanthus umbellatus maximus-albus

Agapanthus umbellatus maximus-albus, commonly known as the white agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features large, showy white flower heads that resemble umbrellas, composed of numerous small, star-shaped blooms. The plant produces strap-like, blue-green leaves that are evergreen in mild climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom reliably in summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the garden.
