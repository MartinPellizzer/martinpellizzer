Agapanthus comptonii subsp. longitubis

Agapanthus comptonii subsp.

longitubis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are evergreen and grow in a rosette pattern. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in horticulture, though it requires minimal maintenance once established.
