Andropogon condensatus var. genuinus

Andropogon condensatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands and marshy areas. Its flowering spikes are narrow and often appear in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
