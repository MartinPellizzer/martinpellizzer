Acicarpha spathulata

Acicarpha spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
