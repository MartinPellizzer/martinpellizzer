Anemone albana f. grandiflora

Anemone albana f. grandiflora, commonly known as the white anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, white, single flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant has finely divided, pinnately lobed leaves that are hairy and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
