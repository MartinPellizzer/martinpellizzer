Aechmea araneosa

Aechmea araneosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Aechmea araneosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.

Morphology

Aechmea araneosa has leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette, and measure up to 30 cm in length, with a dark green color and a waxy surface.

The plant has a short, unbranched stem with a smooth, grayish bark that grows upright from the base. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a colorful bract, with white and yellow hues, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry containing small seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense network near the soil surface, anchoring the plant and aiding in nutrient absorption.
