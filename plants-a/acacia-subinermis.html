Acacia subinermis

Acacia subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warm season. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Acacia subinermis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry regions.
