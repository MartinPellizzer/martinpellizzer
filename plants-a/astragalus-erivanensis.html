Astragalus erivanensis

Astragalus erivanensis, commonly known as the Erivan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in Armenia and parts of Turkey. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Astragalus erivanensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
