Anisifolium poggei

Anisifolium poggei, commonly known as the Pogge's anisifolium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that give it its scientific name, "anisifolium," meaning "unequal leaf.". It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and potential pharmacological value, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
