Acacia brasiliensis

Acacia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. Its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, forming dense, globular clusters.

Acacia brasiliensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
