Arabis amplexicaulis var. japonica

Arabis amplexicaulis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are rosette-like and covered in fine hairs, with small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
