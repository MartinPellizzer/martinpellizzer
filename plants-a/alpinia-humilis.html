Alpinia humilis

Alpinia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features slender, aromatic rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the summer months.

Alpinia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
