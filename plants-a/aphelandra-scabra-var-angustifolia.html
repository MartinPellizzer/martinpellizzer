Aphelandra scabra var. angustifolia

Aphelandra scabra var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
