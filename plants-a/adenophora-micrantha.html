Adenophora micrantha

Adenophora micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender stems that often branch near the base, bearing opposite leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers with five petals, typically in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
