Astragalus porphyrocalyx

Astragalus porphyrocalyx, commonly known as the purple calyx milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple calyx that persists on the seed pod. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it produces slender, hairy seed pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus porphyrocalyx is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations.
