Asarum macanthum

Asarum macanthum, commonly known as the broadleaf wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
