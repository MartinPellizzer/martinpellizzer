Adicea dendrophila

Adicea dendrophila, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form a network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Adicea dendrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
