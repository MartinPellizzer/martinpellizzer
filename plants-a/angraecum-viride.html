Angraecum viride

Angraecum viride, commonly known as the Emerald Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, grass-like foliage and its striking, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Angraecum viride is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare orchids.
