Asteromyrtus brassii

Asteromyrtus brassii, commonly known as the coastal aster, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

Asteromyrtus brassii is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
