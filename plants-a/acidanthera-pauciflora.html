Acidanthera pauciflora

Acidanthera pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered orchid, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and display.
