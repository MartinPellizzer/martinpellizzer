Allium vineale var. typicum

Allium vineale var. typicum, commonly known as wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in umbels during the spring and summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable plant in certain cuisines.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
