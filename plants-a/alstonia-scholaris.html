Alstonia scholaris

Alstonia scholaris, commonly known as the Indian tree of heaven or the ghost tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-green, arranged in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Alstonia scholaris is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
