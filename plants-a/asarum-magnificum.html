Asarum magnificum

Asarum magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate forests of East Asia.

It features a thick, rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense, umbrella-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring. Its aromatic leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Asarum magnificum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
