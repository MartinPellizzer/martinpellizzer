Artemisia nitrosa var. nitrosa

Artemisia nitrosa var. nitrosa, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a strong, bitter scent due to the presence of volatile oils, including thujone. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies.
