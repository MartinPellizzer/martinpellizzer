Agropyron sericeum

Agropyron sericeum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Agropyron sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
