Amsonia jonesii

Amsonia jonesii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and showy, fluffy blue flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.

Amsonia jonesii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
