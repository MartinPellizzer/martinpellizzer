Aeschynomene heurckeana

Aeschynomene heurckeana, commonly known as wild millet, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
