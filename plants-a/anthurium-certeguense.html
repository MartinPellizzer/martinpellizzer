Anthurium certeguense

Anthurium certeguense, commonly known as the red anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is dark green with a glossy texture, and the flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators.

Anthurium certeguense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
