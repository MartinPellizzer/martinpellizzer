Alsine setacea var. parviflora

Alsine setacea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand drought, with a compact growth form and a shallow root system.
