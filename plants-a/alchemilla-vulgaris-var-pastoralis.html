Alchemilla vulgaris var. pastoralis

Alchemilla vulgaris var. pastoralis, commonly known as the softleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, silver-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.
