Aristida adscensionis var. breviseta

Aristida adscensionis var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-bristled threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have short, stiff hairs along the edges.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter bristles compared to other forms of Aristida adscensionis.
