Arthrophyllum comorense

Arthrophyllum comorense, commonly known as the Comorian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros archipelago.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, spatulate leaves and umbellate inflorescence. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Arthrophyllum comorense is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
