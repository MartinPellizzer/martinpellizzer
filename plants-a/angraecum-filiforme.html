Angraecum filiforme

Angraecum filiforme, commonly known as the slender angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and often twisted stems, which can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum filiforme is noted for its unique morphology and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
