Adenandra marginata subsp. mucronata

Adenandra marginata subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the serrated adenandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its acute leaf tips and slightly hairy stems.
