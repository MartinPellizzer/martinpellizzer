Aechmea glomerata

Aechmea glomerata, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the center. Its leaves are typically broad, stiff, and covered in fine, hair-like structures.

Aechmea glomerata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
