Aulomyrcia pusilla

Aulomyrcia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf myrcia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest understories.

Aulomyrcia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
