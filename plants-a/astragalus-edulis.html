Astragalus edulis

Astragalus edulis, commonly known as the edible milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus edulis plays a role in soil stabilization and supports various pollinators with its nectar-rich blooms.
