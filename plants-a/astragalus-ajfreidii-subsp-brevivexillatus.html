Astragalus ajfreidii subsp. brevivexillatus

Astragalus ajfreidii subsp.

brevivexillatus, commonly known as the short-stipitate milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stipules and reduced calyx lobes compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

This plant is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
