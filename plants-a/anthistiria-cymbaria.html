Anthistiria cymbaria

Anthistiria cymbaria, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anthistiria cymbaria is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
