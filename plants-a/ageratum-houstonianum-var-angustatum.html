Ageratum houstonianum var. angustatum

Ageratum houstonianum var. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
