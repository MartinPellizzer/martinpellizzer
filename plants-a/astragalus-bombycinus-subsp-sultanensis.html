Astragalus bombycinus subsp. sultanensis

Astragalus bombycinus subsp.

sultanensis, commonly known as the Sultan's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
