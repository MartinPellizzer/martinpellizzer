Artemisia gracilis

Artemisia gracilis, commonly known as slender wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, aromatic stems and small, gray-green leaves with a strongly pungent scent. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in narrow, spike-like inflorescences.

Artemisia gracilis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
