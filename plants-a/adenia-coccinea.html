Adenia coccinea

Adenia coccinea, commonly known as the red belladonna or red adenium, is a striking shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, often woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid environments.

Adenia coccinea is also known for its toxic properties, making it a plant to handle with care.
