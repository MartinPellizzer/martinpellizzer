Aconitum parcifolium

Aconitum parcifolium, commonly known as the slender monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
