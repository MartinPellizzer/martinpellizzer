Acacia asperulacea

Acacia asperulacea, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless branches and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia asperulacea is often found in dry, open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
