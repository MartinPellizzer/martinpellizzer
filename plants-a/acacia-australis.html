Acacia australis

Acacia australis, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia australis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
