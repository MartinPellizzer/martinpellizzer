Aconitum maximum subsp. kunasilense

Aconitum maximum subsp.

kunasilense, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers.

The plant features a stout, hairy stem that bears alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
