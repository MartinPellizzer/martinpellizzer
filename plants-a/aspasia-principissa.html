Aspasia principissa

Aspasia principissa, commonly known as the royal aspasia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are showy, with white petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming in spring.

Aspasia principissa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance and resilience.
