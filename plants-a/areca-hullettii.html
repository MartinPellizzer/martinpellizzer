Areca hullettii

Areca hullettii, commonly known as the Hullett's areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Areca hullettii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

It plays a role in both ecological systems and traditional landscaping in its native regions.
