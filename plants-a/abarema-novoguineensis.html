Abarema novoguineensis

Abarema novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea bracken.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its fern-like fronds and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are typically compound, with a delicate, lacy appearance.

While not widely cultivated, it is occasionally found in horticultural collections for its ornamental value.
