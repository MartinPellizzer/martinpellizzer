Adelobotrys macrophyllus

Adelobotrys macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leafed starling tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Adelobotrys macrophyllus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
