Arisaema ringens var. glaucescens

Arisaema ringens var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined arisaema, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and prominent veins. The plant produces striking, spadix-like flowers enclosed within a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
