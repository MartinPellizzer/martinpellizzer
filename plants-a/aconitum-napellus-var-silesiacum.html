Aconitum napellus var. silesiacum

Aconitum napellus var. silesiacum, commonly known as the Silesian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant features large, palmate leaves with a distinctive deep green color and serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
