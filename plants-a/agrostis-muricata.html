Agrostis muricata

Agrostis muricata, commonly known as the prickly grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, bristly inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the seed heads are cylindrical and covered in stiff bristles.

Agrostis muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in various ecosystems.
