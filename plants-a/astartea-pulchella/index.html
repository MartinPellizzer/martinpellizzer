Astartea Pulchella

Astartea pulchella is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the temperate regions of eastern North America. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Known for its small, white to pale pink flowers, it blooms in late spring and early summer. The plant's unique leaf structure and subtle beauty make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts. Despite its charm, Astartea pulchella is increasingly threatened by habitat loss and environmental changes.
