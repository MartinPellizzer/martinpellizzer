Anil wightii

Anil wightii, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open woodlands.
