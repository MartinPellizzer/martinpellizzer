Adiantum discolor

Adiantum discolor, commonly known as the variable maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often variegated, with a mix of green and silver or white hues. Adiantum discolor thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and horticultural displays.
