Actinotus superbus

Actinotus superbus, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Actinotus superbus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
