Argyrolobium divaricatum

Argyrolobium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable silver-lobed pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
