Artemisia gmelinii subsp. manshurica

Artemisia gmelinii subsp.

manshurica, commonly known as Manchurian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It has narrow, linear leaves with a strong, pungent odor due to its high concentration of volatile oils.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
