Arundinella crinita

Arundinella crinita, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinella crinita is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
