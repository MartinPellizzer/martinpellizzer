Aconitum shennongjiaense

Aconitum shennongjiaense, commonly known as Shennongjia Aconite, is a perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central China, particularly in the Shennongjia Nature Reserve. The plant features a robust rhizome and produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is handled with caution and is of interest to both botanists and pharmacologists.
