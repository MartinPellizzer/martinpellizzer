Astragalus mario-sousae

Astragalus mario-sousae, commonly known as the Mario-Sousae milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus mario-sousae is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
