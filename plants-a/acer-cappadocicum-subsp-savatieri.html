Acer cappadocicum subsp. savatieri

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

savatieri, commonly known as the Cappadocian maple, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey. It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring palmate leaves with five to seven lobes that turn vibrant red in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its unique foliage and adaptability contribute to its popularity among horticulturists and landscape designers.
