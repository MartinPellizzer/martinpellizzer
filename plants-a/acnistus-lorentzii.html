Acnistus lorentzii

Acnistus lorentzii, commonly known as the silverleaf acnistus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
