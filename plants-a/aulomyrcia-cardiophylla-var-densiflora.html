Aulomyrcia cardiophylla var. densiflora

Aulomyrcia cardiophylla var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered aulomyrcia, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in the spring, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
