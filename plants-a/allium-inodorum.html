Allium inodorum

Allium inodorum, commonly known as the garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves and small, white or purple flowers. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs, which have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Allium inodorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
