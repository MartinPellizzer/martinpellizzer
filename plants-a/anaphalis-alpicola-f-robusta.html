Anaphalis alpicola f. robusta

Anaphalis alpicola f. robusta, commonly known as the Alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, fluffy flower heads that resemble cotton balls. Native to alpine regions of Europe, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
