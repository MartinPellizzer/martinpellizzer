Acacia cochliocarpa

Acacia cochliocarpa, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia cochliocarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
