Asyneuma cordifolium

Asyneuma cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved asyneuma, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Asyneuma cordifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
