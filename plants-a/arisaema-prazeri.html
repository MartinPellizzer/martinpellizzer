Arisaema prazeri

Arisaema prazeri, commonly known as the Pazer's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. Arisaema prazeri typically thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique floral structure plays a role in attracting specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
