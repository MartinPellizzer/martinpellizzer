Abies pinsapo subsp. marocana

Abies pinsapo subsp.

marocana, commonly known as the Moroccan fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Atlas Mountains of Morocco. It is a member of the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds enclosed in woody scales. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
