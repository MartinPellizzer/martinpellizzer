Aristea fimbriata

Aristea fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed aristea, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Aristea fimbriata is well adapted to dry conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
