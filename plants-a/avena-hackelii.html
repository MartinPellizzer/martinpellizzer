Avena hackelii

Avena hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
