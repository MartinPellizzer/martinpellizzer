Annona squamosa

Annona squamosa, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and is known for its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully mature, has a rough, scaly skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
