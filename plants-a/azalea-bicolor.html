Azalea bicolor

Azalea bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that typically display two distinct colors, often pink and white, on the same bloom. The plant belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes other popular ornamental plants. Azalea bicolor thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
