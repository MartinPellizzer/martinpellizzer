Aristida spectabilis

Aristida spectabilis, commonly known as the showy grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
