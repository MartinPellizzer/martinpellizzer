Aquilegia vulgaris var. sylvestris

Aquilegia vulgaris var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are usually hairy and upright. It thrives in woodland or shady areas with well-drained soil, and it is often found in moist, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
