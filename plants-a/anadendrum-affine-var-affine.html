Anadendrum affine var. affine

Anadendrum affine var. affine, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, reddish berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
