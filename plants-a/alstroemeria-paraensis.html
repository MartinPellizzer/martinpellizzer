Alstroemeria paraensis

Alstroemeria paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky soils, and thrives in cooler climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Alstroemeria paraensis is also valued for its ability to adapt to different soil conditions, although it prefers well-drained environments.
