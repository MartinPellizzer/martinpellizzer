Aristolochia peltata var. poitaei

Aristolochia peltata var. poitaei, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often marked with lighter veins. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically purple or brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
