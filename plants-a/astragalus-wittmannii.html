Astragalus wittmannii

Astragalus wittmannii, commonly known as Wittmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in semi-desert environments.
