Aegilops aucheri subsp. polyathera

Aegilops aucheri subsp.

polyathera, commonly known as the Armenian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often grow close to the ground.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
