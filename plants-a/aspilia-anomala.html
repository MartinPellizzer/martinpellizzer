Aspilia anomala

Aspilia anomala, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to yellow florets in its composite flower head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Aspilia anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
