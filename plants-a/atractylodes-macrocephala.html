Atractylodes macrocephala

Atractylodes macrocephala, commonly known as Chinese Atractylodes, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its thick, rhizomatous root system. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic compounds and adaptability to various soil conditions.
