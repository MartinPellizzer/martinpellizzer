Alismorkis angraeciflora

Alismorkis angraeciflora, commonly known as the Madagascar water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Madagascar.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces striking white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance, which attract pollinators such as moths. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
