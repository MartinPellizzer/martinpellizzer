Astragalus nervulosus

Astragalus nervulosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow to brown pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. Its root system is extensive and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.
