Astragalus volckmannii

Astragalus volckmannii, commonly known as the Volckmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitats due to its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
