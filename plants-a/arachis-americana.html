Arachis americana

Arachis americana, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species of legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that produces small, yellow flowers and develops pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. Its root system is capable of fixing atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Arachis americana is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
