Arenaria saxifraga var. burnatii

Arenaria saxifraga var. burnatii, commonly known as Burnat's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features small, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is notable for its ability to withstand cold temperatures and arid conditions, making it a resilient component of high-altitude flora.
