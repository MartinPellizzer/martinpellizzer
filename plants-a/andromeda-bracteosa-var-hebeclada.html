Andromeda bracteosa var. hebeclada

Andromeda bracteosa var. hebeclada, commonly known as the Hebeclad Andromeda, is a variant of the Andromeda genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and the delicate, fragrant flowers that add visual interest to shaded landscapes.
