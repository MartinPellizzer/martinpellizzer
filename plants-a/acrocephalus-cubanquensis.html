Acrocephalus cubanquensis

Acrocephalus cubanquensis, commonly known as the Cuban marsh warbler, is a species of grass that thrives in wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
