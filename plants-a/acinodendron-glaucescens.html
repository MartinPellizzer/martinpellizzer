Acinodendron glaucescens

Acinodendron glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green acacia, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid environments.
