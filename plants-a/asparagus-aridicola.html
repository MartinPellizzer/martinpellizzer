Asparagus aridicola

Asparagus aridicola, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by edible, needle-like spears that resemble true asparagus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in landscaping in regions with hot, dry climates.
