Ancistrum acaena

Ancistrum acaena, commonly known as the red spined orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red and purple flower spikes, which are often adorned with spines. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, growing in dense clusters around the base of the plant.

Ancistrum acaena is often cultivated for its ornamental value in greenhouses and warm climates due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
