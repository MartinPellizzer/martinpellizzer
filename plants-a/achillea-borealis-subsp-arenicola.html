Achillea borealis subsp. arenicola

Achillea borealis subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the sand arnica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of North America. It features dense, compact foliage composed of narrow, gray-green leaves arranged in a rosette.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
