Allium parvulum

Allium parvulum, commonly known as the chive or garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, producing small, white or purple flowers in clusters at the top. The plant has grass-like leaves that are edible and often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. Its bulbs are also edible and can be harvested for use in cooking.

Allium parvulum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
