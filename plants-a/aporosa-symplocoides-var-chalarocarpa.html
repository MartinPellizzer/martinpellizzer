Aporosa symplocoides var. chalarocarpa

Aporosa symplocoides var. chalarocarpa, commonly known as the purple-leafed aporosa, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
