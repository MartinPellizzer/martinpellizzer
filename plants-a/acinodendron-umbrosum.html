Acinodendron umbrosum

Acinodendron umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that often appear in dense clusters, providing a lush, shaded canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Acinodendron umbrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
