Aster shortii f. asper

Aster shortii f. asper, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rough, textured foliage is a distinguishing trait, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Aster shortii f. asper is often found in alpine and rocky habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
