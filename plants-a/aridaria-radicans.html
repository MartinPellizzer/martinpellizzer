Aridaria radicans

Aridaria radicans, commonly known as the creeping desert vine, is a low-growing, succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across rocky or sandy substrates. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during brief periods of rainfall. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, storing water to help it survive prolonged droughts.

Aridaria radicans is often found in desert regions of Africa and the Middle East, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing shelter for small desert animals.
