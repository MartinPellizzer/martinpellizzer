Acacia pseudowightii

Acacia pseudowightii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and it has a distinctive, knobby root system.

Acacia pseudowightii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
