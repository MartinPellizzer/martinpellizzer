Angelica refracta var. multinervis

Angelica refracta var. multinervis, commonly known as the multivenered angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive multivenation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in complex, branched inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing aromatic compounds that give the plant its characteristic fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
