Arundinaria wardii

Arundinaria wardii, commonly known as the Ward's bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight sheen, and are arranged in a dense, upright fashion.

Arundinaria wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
