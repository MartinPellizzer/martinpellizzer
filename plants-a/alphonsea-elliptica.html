Alphonsea elliptica

Alphonsea elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf alphonsea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alphonsea elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
