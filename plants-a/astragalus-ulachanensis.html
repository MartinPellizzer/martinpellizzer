Astragalus ulachanensis

Astragalus ulachanensis, commonly known as the Ulachan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus ulachanensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
