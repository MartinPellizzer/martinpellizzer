Angraecum mahavavense

Angraecum mahavavense, commonly known as the Mahavavy star orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences bearing white or pale pink flowers with prominent, spurred petals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by nocturnal moths, a feature that reflects its evolutionary history.

Angraecum mahavavense is a rare and endemic species, highlighting the unique biodiversity of Madagascar's flora.
