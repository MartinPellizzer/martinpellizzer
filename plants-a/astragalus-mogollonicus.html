Astragalus mogollonicus

Astragalus mogollonicus, commonly known as the Mogollon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, arid, or semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellow to greenish flowers in late spring. Astragalus mogollonicus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and canyon walls.
