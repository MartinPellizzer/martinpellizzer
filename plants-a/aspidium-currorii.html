Aspidium currorii

Aspidium currorii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that help anchor it in moist substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Aspidium currorii is often found in slow-moving streams, ponds, and wetlands, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystem dynamics.
