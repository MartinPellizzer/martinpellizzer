Achillea tenuis

Achillea tenuis, commonly known as slender yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

Achillea tenuis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
