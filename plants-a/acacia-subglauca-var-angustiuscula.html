Acacia subglauca var. angustiuscula

Acacia subglauca var. angustiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, angular stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically a dull green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in spikes and bloom during the autumn season. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry regions.
