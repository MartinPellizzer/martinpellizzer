Aspalathus hispida subsp. hispida

Aspalathus hispida subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the smooth bract bushveld clover, is a South African plant species belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

It is adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of the fynbos vegetation in the Western Cape region.
