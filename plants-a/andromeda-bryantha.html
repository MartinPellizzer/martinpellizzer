Andromeda bryantha

Andromeda bryantha, commonly known as the Bryan's wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its fragrant, white to pink flowers bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging woodland environments.
