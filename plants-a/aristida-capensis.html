Aristida capensis

Aristida capensis, commonly known as Cape grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass species that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, pale green or brown spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aristida capensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate foliage and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
