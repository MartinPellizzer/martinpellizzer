Acridocarpus alexandrinae

Acridocarpus alexandrinae, commonly known as the Alexandrian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
