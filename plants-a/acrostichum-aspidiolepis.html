Acrostichum aspidiolepis

Acrostichum aspidiolepis, commonly known as the broadleaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust, tree-like trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer called the rachis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

The fronds are typically bright green and can be used in traditional practices by some indigenous communities.
