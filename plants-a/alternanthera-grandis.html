Alternanthera grandis

Alternanthera grandis, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to pink foliage, which becomes more intense under strong light conditions. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular in both freshwater and brackish water aquariums.

Its ornamental value makes it a favorite among aquatic plant enthusiasts.
