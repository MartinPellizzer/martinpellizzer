Acacia desmostachya

Acacia desmostachya, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for harsh environments.

The bark and leaves of Acacia desmostachya have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
