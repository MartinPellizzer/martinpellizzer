Aeschynomene scoparia

Aeschynomene scoparia, commonly known as smallleaf aeschynomene, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
