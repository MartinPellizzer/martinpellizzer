Allium parviflorum

Allium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered garlic.

It is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions. Its bulb is edible and has a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
