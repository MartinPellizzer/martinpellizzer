Anil oxalidea

Anil oxalidea, commonly known as the Indian spinach or Oxalis indica, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of spinach. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. Anil oxalidea is known for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.

However, it contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.
