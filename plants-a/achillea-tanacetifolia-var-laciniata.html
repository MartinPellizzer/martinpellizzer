Achillea tanacetifolia var. laciniata

Achillea tanacetifolia var. laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period add both ornamental value and ecological benefit to garden spaces.
