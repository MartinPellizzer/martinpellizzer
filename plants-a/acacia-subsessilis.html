Acacia subsessilis

Acacia subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairless leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Acacia subsessilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals and insects.
