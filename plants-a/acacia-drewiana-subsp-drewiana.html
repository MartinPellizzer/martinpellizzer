Acacia drewiana subsp. drewiana

Acacia drewiana subsp.

drewiana, commonly known as the African silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive, feathery foliage composed of small, opposite leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.
