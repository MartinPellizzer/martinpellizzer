Amaranthus sanguineus var. elatior

Amaranthus sanguineus var. elatior, commonly known as the blood amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into triangular, three-angled seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its vibrant foliage and long blooming period add visual interest to gardens throughout the growing season.
