Aspalathus acanthes

Aspalathus acanthes, commonly known as the Cape tea or African geranium, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky gardens and coastal areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Aspalathus acanthes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
