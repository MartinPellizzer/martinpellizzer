Atriplex cordulata var. erecticaulis

Atriplex cordulata var. erecticaulis, commonly known as the upright saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with cylindrical, spine-tipped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded landscapes. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, triangular seeds.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
