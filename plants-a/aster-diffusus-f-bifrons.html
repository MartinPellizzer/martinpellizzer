Aster diffusus f. bifrons

Aster diffusus f. bifrons, commonly known as the bifrontal aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-faced appearance, with different flower colors on either side of the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in composite heads.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
