Abies delavayi var. faxoniana

Abies delavayi var. faxoniana, commonly known as the Faxon's fir, is a variant of the Delavay fir native to the Himalayas and southwestern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and dense, dark green foliage. The needles are typically short, flat, and have a distinctive white stomatal band underneath. It produces small, yellow-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
