Adenocarpus hispanicus subsp. argyrophyllus

Adenocarpus hispanicus subsp.

argyrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed burr bush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.
