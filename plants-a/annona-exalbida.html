Annona exalbida

Annona exalbida, commonly known as the white annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are white and fragrant, often appearing in clusters, and it bears yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The botanical features of Annona exalbida make it a notable species in tropical horticulture and ecological studies.
