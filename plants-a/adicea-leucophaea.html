Adicea leucophaea

Adicea leucophaea, commonly known as the white-veined fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a pale or white midrib, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Adicea leucophaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
