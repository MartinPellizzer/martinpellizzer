Asplenium filipes

Asplenium filipes, commonly known as the thread fern, is a small, delicate fern native to various parts of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and its ability to form dense mats of foliage. The fronds are typically narrow, with a feather-like appearance, and they often have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in forests and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
