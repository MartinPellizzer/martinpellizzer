Abuta macrocarpa

Abuta macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant abuta, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in South American medicine for their purported health benefits.

Abuta macrocarpa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of the rainforest.
