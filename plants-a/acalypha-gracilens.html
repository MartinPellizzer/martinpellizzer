Acalypha gracilens

Acalypha gracilens, commonly known as slender copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Acalypha gracilens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
