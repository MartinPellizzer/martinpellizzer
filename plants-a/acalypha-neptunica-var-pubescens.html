Acalypha neptunica var. pubescens

Acalypha neptunica var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to red bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in salty, humid environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens and landscapes.
