Astragalus tephrodes var. brachylobus

Astragalus tephrodes var. brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stalked milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellow to brownish flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
