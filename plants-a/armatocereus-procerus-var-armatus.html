Armatocereus procerus var. armatus

Armatocereus procerus var. armatus, commonly known as the armed night-blooming cereus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a robust root system and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators such as moths.

The varietal name "armatus" refers to the dense, bristly spines that cover its stems.
