Aquilegia einseleana f. intermedia

Aquilegia einseleana f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically deep blue or purple in color. The plant features compound leaves with palmate lobes and a short, stout stem that supports its showy blooms. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
