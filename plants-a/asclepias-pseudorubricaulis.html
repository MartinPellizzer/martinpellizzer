Asclepias pseudorubricaulis

Asclepias pseudorubricaulis, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers and velvety, hairy foliage. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a common feature among the milkweed genus. Its flowers are typically clustered in umbels and attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Asclepias pseudorubricaulis plays an important role in supporting monarch butterfly populations by providing a habitat and food source.
