Aloinopsis lodewykii

Aloinopsis lodewykii, commonly known as the Cape goldbos, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange. The plant produces striking, tall flower spikes with yellow to orange flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Aloinopsis lodewykii is also noted for its ornamental value and ease of care.
