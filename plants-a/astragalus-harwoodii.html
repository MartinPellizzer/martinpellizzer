Astragalus harwoodii

Astragalus harwoodii, commonly known as Harwood's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and shrublands. It has small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus harwoodii is often found in areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
