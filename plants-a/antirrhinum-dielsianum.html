Antirrhinum dielsianum

Antirrhinum dielsianum, commonly known as the Diels' snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the genus Antirrhinum, which includes several species of snapdragons, characterized by their distinctive bilabiate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a unique structure that resembles a dragon's mouth, hence the common name.

Antirrhinum dielsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
