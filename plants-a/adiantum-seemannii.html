Adiantum seemannii

Adiantum seemannii, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Adiantum seemannii is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
