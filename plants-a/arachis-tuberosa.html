Arachis tuberosa

Arachis tuberosa, commonly known as the peanut vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that contain edible seeds, which are commonly referred to as peanuts. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with three to five leaflets.

Arachis tuberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of legumes in certain regions.
