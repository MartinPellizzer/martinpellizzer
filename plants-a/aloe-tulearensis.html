Aloe tulearensis

Aloe tulearensis, commonly known as the Tulear aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically green with a pale blue or grayish hue, and they have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces yellowish-green flowers on tall, branching inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine due to its perceived healing properties.
