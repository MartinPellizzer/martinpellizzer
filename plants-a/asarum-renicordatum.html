Asarum renicordatum

Asarum renicordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, irregularly shaped flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
