Acetosa pratensis subsp. arifolia

Acetosa pratensis subsp.

arifolia, commonly known as the meadow sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
