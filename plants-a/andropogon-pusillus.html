Andropogon pusillus

Andropogon pusillus, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that turn golden in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon pusillus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
