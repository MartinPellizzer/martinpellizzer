Alyssum superbum

Alyssum superbum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Alyssum superbum is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
