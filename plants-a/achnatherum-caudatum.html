Achnatherum caudatum

Achnatherum caudatum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
