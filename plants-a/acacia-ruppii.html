Acacia ruppii

Acacia ruppii, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, flat, green pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia ruppii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
