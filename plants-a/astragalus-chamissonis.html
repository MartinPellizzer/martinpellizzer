Astragalus chamissonis

Astragalus chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Astragalus chamissonis is often found in the Pacific Northwest and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
