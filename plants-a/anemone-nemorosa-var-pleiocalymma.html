Anemone nemorosa var. pleiocalymma

Anemone nemorosa var. pleiocalymma, commonly known as the woodland anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features delicate, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has palmate leaves that are deeply divided. It blooms in early spring and is characterized by its ability to form clumps over time.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in garden settings.
