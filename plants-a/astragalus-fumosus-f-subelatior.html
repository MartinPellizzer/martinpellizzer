Astragalus fumosus f. subelatior

Astragalus fumosus f. subelatior, commonly known as the smoky milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The subspecies is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States, particularly in states like Nevada and Utah. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native environment.
