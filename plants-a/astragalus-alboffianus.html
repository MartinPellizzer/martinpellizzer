Astragalus alboffianus

Astragalus alboffianus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow to white flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in arid gardens.
