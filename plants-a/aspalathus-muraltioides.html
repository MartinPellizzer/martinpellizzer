Aspalathus muraltioides

Aspalathus muraltioides, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular plant in native gardens and conservation areas.
