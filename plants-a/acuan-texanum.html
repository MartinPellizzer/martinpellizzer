Acuan texanum

Acuan texanum, commonly known as the Texas acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow to orange flower clusters that bloom in spring. Acuan texanum is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
