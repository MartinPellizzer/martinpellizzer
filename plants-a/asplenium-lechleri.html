Asplenium lechleri

Asplenium lechleri, commonly known as the Amazonian spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in the Amazon basin, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are often clustered closely together. It has a unique ability to absorb moisture through its rhizomes, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Asplenium lechleri is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its healing properties.
