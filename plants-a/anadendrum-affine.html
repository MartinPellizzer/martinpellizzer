Anadendrum affine

Anadendrum affine, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces vibrant orange to red flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
