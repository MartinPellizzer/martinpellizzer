Alchemilla pachyphylla

Alchemilla pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that is often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
