Agrostis alpina subsp. aurata

Agrostis alpina subsp.

aurata, commonly known as the golden alpine grass, is a perennial grass species native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence bears golden-yellow florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
