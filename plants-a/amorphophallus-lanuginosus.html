Amorphophallus lanuginosus

Amorphophallus lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, hairy leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is used as a food source in some regions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
