Astrantia orientalis

Astrantia orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern aster, is a perennial plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
