Allium cyaneum var. deltoides

Allium cyaneum var. deltoides, commonly known as the blue onion or delta garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense clumps that can reach up to 30 cm in height. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
