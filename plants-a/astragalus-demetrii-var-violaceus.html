Astragalus demetrii var. violaceus

Astragalus demetrii var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
