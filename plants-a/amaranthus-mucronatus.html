Amaranthus mucronatus

Amaranthus mucronatus, commonly known as the spiny amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense, spiky inflorescences. It produces small, bead-like seeds that are typically red or black in color.

This plant is often grown as a ornamental due to its striking appearance and resilience in various soil conditions.
