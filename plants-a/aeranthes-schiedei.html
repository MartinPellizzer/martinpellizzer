Aeranthes schiedei

Aeranthes schiedei, commonly known as the Schied's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, making it adaptable to its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Aeranthes schiedei thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded forest understories.
