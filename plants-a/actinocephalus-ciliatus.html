Actinocephalus ciliatus

Actinocephalus ciliatus, commonly known as the featherleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with fine hairs, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some regions.
