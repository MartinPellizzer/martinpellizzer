Acaena microphylla var. pallideolivacea

Acaena microphylla var. pallideolivacea, commonly known as the pale olive acacia, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically pale olive in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spiny fruits. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
