Arachnorchis subglabriphylla

Arachnorchis subglabriphylla, commonly known as the glabrous leaf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and pale green, often clustered in a loose spike, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

This orchid is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
