Acaena adenocarpa var. microphylla

Acaena adenocarpa var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bullion bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
