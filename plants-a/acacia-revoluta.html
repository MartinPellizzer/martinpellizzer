Acacia revoluta

Acacia revoluta, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves with small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and pale, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Acacia revoluta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
