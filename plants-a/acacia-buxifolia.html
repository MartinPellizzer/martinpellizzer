Acacia buxifolia

Acacia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, dark green foliage with leaves that are small, ovate, and have a leathery texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Acacia buxifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
