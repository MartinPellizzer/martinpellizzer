Aconitum chienningense var. lasiocarpum

Aconitum chienningense var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the Hairy-fruited Chinese Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

The plant is notable for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a highly poisonous species requiring caution during handling.
