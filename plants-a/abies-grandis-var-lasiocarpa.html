Abies grandis var. lasiocarpa

Abies grandis var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the subalpine fir, is a variant of the grand fir native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, soft needles that are often covered in a fine, silky white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in high elevation forests, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its cones are usually small and erect, maturing to release seeds that contribute to forest regeneration.

This variety plays an important ecological role in mountain ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
