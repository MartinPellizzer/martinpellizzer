Arctostaphylos stanfordiana

Arctostaphylos stanfordiana, commonly known as Stanford manzanita, is a shrub native to California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
