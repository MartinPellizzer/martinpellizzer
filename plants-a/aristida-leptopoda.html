Aristida leptopoda

Aristida leptopoda, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Aristida leptopoda is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert and coastal ecosystems.
