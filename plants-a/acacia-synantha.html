Acacia synantha

Acacia synantha, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are a characteristic feature of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves.

Acacia synantha is adapted to arid environments and is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness.
