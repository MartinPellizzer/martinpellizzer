Audouinia hispida

Audouinia hispida, commonly known as the rough hairvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Audouinia hispida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in open, sunny habitats.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
