Aster lacinians

Aster lacinians, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with white markings, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Aster lacinians are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant color and unique appearance.
