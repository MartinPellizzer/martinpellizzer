Arum trifidum

Arum trifidum, commonly known as the trifid arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Arum trifidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
