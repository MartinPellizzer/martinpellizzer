Acacia gilliesii

Acacia gilliesii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia gilliesii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and serving as a habitat for various wildlife species.
