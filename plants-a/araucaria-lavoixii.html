Araucaria lavoixii

Araucaria lavoixii, commonly known as the Lavoix's Araucaria, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. Its foliage consists of short, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

Araucaria lavoixii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
