Amorpha pubescens

Amorpha pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent honey-vetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are encased in small, hairy pods that help in seed dispersal.
