Adonis chrysocyathus

Adonis chrysocyathus, commonly known as the golden cup adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
