Agropyron tsukushiense

Agropyron tsukushiense, commonly known as the Japanese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Agropyron tsukushiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
