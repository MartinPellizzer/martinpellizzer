Anemone cathayensis f. hispida

Anemone cathayensis f. hispida, commonly known as the hairy anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from May to June. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its delicate, early-blooming flowers.
