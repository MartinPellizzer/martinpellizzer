Aria betulaefolia

Aria betulaefolia, commonly known as the silver birch, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin, vertical strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped, turning yellow in the autumn.

Aria betulaefolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
