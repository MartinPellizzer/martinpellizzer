Alpinia arctiflora

Alpinia arctiflora, commonly known as the snow lotus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Alpinia arctiflora is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional medicinal uses in herbal remedies.
