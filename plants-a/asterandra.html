Asterandra

Asterandra, commonly known as the golden aster, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plants typically have opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produce clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. Asterandra species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
