Aspilia mexicana

Aspilia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican torch flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow to orange flower heads that resemble torches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Aspilia mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
