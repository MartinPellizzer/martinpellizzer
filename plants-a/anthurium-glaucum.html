Anthurium glaucum

Anthurium glaucum, commonly known as the silver anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium glaucum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
