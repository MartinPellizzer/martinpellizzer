Astragalus thracicus subsp. parnassi

Astragalus thracicus subsp.

parnassi, commonly known as Parnassian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and parts of Eastern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in high-altitude meadows and slopes.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization through its extensive root system.
