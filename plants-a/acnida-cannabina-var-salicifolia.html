Acnida cannabina var. salicifolia

Acnida cannabina var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved hempvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a serrated margin. The leaves are typically pubescent, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance, and they often resemble the leaves of willow trees, hence its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as forest edges and stream banks.
