Artanthe mexicana

Artanthe mexicana, commonly known as Mexican artanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Artanthe mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
