Arethusa secunda

Arethusa secunda, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Arethusa, is a striking aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are usually a deep green with a waxy surface, and its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of these habitats, while its leaves and flowers float on the water's surface.

Arethusa secunda plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
