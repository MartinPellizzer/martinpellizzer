Alyssum brodense

Alyssum brodense, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces numerous tiny, four-petaled blooms along its stems, which are often used in gardens for their ornamental value. Its foliage is dense and silvery-green, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Alyssum brodense is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
