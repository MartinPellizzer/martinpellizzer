Allium microbulbum

Allium microbulbum, commonly known as the dwarf leek, is a small perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a potential culinary herb.

Due to its ornamental value and edible qualities, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
