Aspalathus ferox

Aspalathus ferox, commonly known as the wild African tea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Despite its hardy nature, it is not commonly cultivated for commercial use compared to other Aspalathus species.
