Andropogon procerus

Andropogon procerus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a grass species native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often coated with a fine layer of wax. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats. Andropogon procerus is known for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Its seeds are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.
