Athanasia dentata

Athanasia dentata, commonly known as the toothed everlasting or toothed immortality, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of small, delicate flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and toothed, giving it its scientific name.

Athanasia dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
