Andrachne reverchonii

Andrachne reverchonii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy, greenish-gray leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dry, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Andrachne reverchonii is well-suited to harsh environments and is often found in dry, open habitats such as sand dunes and rocky slopes.
