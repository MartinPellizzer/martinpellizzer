Astragalus ebrahimabadensis

Astragalus ebrahimabadensis, commonly known as Ebrahimabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
