Anemone grandis

Anemone grandis, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features large, showy white to pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are hairy and can grow up to 8 inches in length. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone grandis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
