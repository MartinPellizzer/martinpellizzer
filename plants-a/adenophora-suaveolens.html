Adenophora suaveolens

Adenophora suaveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.

Adenophora suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
