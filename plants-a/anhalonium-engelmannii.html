Anhalonium engelmannii

Anhalonium engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with six petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Anhalonium engelmannii is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant once established.
