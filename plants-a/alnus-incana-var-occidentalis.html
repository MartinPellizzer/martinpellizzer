Alnus incana var. occidentalis

Alnus incana var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western mountain alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
