Absinthium spathulatum

Absinthium spathulatum, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a strongly aromatic, bitter scent due to the presence of essential oils.

This species is valued in traditional medicine and is also used in the preparation of certain herbal beverages.
