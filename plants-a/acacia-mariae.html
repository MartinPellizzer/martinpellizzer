Acacia mariae

Acacia mariae, commonly known as the Marie's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia mariae is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
