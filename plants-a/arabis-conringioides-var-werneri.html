Arabis conringioides var. werneri

Arabis conringioides var. werneri, commonly known as the Werneri's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, rounded capsules as fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
