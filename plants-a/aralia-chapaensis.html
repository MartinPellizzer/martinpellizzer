Aralia chapaensis

Aralia chapaensis, commonly known as the Chinese aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, typically arranged in an alternate pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
