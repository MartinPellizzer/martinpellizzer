Astragalus stipulatus

Astragalus stipulatus, commonly known as the leafy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Astragalus stipulatus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
