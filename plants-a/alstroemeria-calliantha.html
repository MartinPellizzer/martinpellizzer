Alstroemeria calliantha

Alstroemeria calliantha, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, yellow, and white, often with contrasting stripes. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a showy display of blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Alstroemeria calliantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
