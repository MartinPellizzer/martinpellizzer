Abronia salsa

Abronia salsa, commonly known as the coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the sandy shores of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that help it withstand coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Abronia salsa plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
