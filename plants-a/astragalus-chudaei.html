Astragalus chudaei

Astragalus chudaei, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods contain small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus chudaei is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
