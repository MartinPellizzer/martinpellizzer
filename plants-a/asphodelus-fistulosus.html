Asphodelus fistulosus

Asphodelus fistulosus, commonly known as the common asphodel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The common asphodel is also used in traditional medicine and has cultural significance in various regions.
