Acer glabrum f. tripartitum

Acer glabrum f. tripartitum, commonly known as the tripartite maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the smooth maple.

It is characterized by its unique trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
