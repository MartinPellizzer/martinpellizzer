Astragalus Alopecuroides Subsp. Grossii

Astragalus alopecuroides subsp. grossii is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Rocky Mountains. It belongs to the legume family, known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. This subspecies is distinguished by its small, spiny pods and pale yellow to orange flowers. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered in many regions. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique species for future generations.
