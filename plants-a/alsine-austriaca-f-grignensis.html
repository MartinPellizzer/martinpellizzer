Alsine austriaca f. grignensis

Alsine austriaca f. grignensis, commonly known as the Grignan alsine, is a variant of the Austrian alsine, a flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions of Europe. It is a low-growing perennial that often forms dense mats.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
