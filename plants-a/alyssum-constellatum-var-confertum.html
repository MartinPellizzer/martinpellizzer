Alyssum constellatum var. confertum

Alyssum constellatum var. confertum, commonly known as starry alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, star-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and has a mat-forming habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alyssum constellatum var. confertum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, preferring well-drained soils.
