Achillea biebersteinii

Achillea biebersteinii, commonly known as the Siberian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, adding a delicate touch to alpine landscapes.
