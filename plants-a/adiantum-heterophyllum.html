Adiantum heterophyllum

Adiantum heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green with a contrasting lighter midrib. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its fronds are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their graceful appearance.

Adiantum heterophyllum is known for its variability in leaf shape and size, which contributes to its common name.
