Arnica maguirei

Arnica maguirei, commonly known as the Mexican mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica maguirei is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other arnica species.
