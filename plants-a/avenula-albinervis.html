Avenula albinervis

Avenula albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with the inflorescence often displaying a pale or white coloration.

Avenula albinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its unique foliage and adaptability.
