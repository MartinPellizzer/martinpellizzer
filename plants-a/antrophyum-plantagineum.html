Antrophyum plantagineum

Antrophyum plantagineum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
