Anathallis ordinata

Anathallis ordinata, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescence, which resembles a torch, and its unique floral structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, primarily flies, due to their strong, sweet scent.

Anathallis ordinata is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
