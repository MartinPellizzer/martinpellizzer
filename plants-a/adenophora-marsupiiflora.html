Adenophora marsupiiflora

Adenophora marsupiiflora, commonly known as the marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, typically in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Adenophora marsupiiflora is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
