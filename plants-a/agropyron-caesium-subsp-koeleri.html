Agropyron caesium subsp. koeleri

Agropyron caesium subsp.

koeleri, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
