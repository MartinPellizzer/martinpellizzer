Andropogon megapotamicus

Andropogon megapotamicus, commonly known as the Brazilian bluestem, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and forest edges, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon megapotamicus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
