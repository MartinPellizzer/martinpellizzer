Actinidia venosa var. pubescens

Actinidia venosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, contributing to biodiversity in natural environments.
