Astragalus echinatus var. pentaglottis

Astragalus echinatus var. pentaglottis, commonly known as the five-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several oval seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
