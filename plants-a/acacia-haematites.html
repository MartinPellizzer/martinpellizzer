Acacia haematites

Acacia haematites, commonly known as the red acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous tiny leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia haematites is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
