Acrolophus ragusinus

Acrolophus ragusinus, commonly known as the Ragusan grass moth, is a species of moth belonging to the family Acrolophidae.

It is characterized by its small size and distinctive wing patterns, which often feature intricate geometric designs. The plant association of this species is primarily with grasses, as its larvae feed on various grass species. Its life cycle involves a short flight period during the summer months, and it is found in regions with temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and localized species.
