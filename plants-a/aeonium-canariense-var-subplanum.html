Aeonium canariense var. subplanum

Aeonium canariense var. subplanum, commonly known as the Canary Island Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often adorned with a powdery white coating. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with small, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
