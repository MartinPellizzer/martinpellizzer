Asplenium monanthes var. triangularipinnatum

Asplenium monanthes var. triangularipinnatum, commonly known as the triangular fern, is a variant of the common holly fern.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its finely divided, triangular-shaped fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually a vibrant green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
