Artemisia genipi var. godronii

Artemisia genipi var. godronii, commonly known as Godron's tarragon, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, imparting a distinctive anise-like flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
