Adiantum × viridimontanum

Adiantum × viridimontanum, commonly known as the green mountain maidenhair fern, is a hybrid fern species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its fronds are often adorned with small, sporangia clusters that release spores for reproduction.

Adiantum × viridimontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in horticultural settings.
