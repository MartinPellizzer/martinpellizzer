Aloe welmelensis

Aloe welmelensis, commonly known as the Welmel's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellow or orange flowers, which bloom during the summer months. Aloe welmelensis is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry conditions.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
