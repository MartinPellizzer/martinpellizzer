Allium schoenoprasum var. vulgare

Allium schoenoprasum var. vulgare, commonly known as chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible, cylindrical leaves that are widely used in culinary applications for their mild onion flavor. Its bulbs, which are typically harvested in early summer, are also edible and can be used in various dishes.

Chives thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them a popular choice for gardens and herb beds.
