Aulomyrcia maritima

Aulomyrcia maritima, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hibiscus-like flowers, which are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Aulomyrcia maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for local wildlife.
