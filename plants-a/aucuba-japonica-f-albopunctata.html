Aucuba japonica f. albopunctata

Aucuba japonica f. albopunctata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features deep green leaves with white or yellow spots and margins. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its year-round visual interest and hardiness in USDA hardiness zones 6-9.
