Aeschynomene americana

Aeschynomene americana, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 to 2 meters tall, featuring trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique seed pods, which are cylindrical and covered in small, hooked bracts. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Aeschynomene americana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
