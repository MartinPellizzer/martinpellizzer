Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp. canescens

Anchonium elichrysifolium subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf anchonium, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought periods.
