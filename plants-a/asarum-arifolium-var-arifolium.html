Asarum arifolium var. arifolium

Asarum arifolium var. arifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and emit a spicy aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
