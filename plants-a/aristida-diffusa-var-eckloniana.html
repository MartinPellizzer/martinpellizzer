Aristida diffusa var. eckloniana

Aristida diffusa var. eckloniana, commonly known as the Ecklon's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry blades and tufted inflorescences that give it a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
