Alchemilla hybrida subsp. helvetica

Alchemilla hybrida subsp.

helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
