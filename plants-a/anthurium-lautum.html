Anthurium lautum

Anthurium lautum, commonly known as the mountain anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium lautum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
