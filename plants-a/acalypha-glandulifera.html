Acalypha glandulifera

Acalypha glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular spurflower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered within the bracts, which are often the primary attraction for pollinators. Its leaves are typically ovate with prominent veins and may have glandular structures, contributing to its scientific name.

Acalypha glandulifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and showy inflorescences.
