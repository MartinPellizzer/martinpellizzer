Aquilegia vulgaris var. oxysepala

Aquilegia vulgaris var. oxysepala, commonly known as the showy columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically blue or purple and arranged in a cluster. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
