Amomum trachycarpum

Amomum trachycarpum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of dark purple to black, aromatic berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils contain compounds like cineole and limonene, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits.
