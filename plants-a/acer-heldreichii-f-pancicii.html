Acer heldreichii f. pancicii

Acer heldreichii f. pancicii, commonly known as the Balkan maple, is a rare and ornamental tree native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a naturally occurring form of Acer heldreichii, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a vibrant green color that turns brilliant red or orange in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
