Anemia sorbifolia

Anemia sorbifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a species native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Anemia sorbifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
