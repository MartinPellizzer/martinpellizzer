Aristolochia curviflora

Aristolochia curviflora, commonly known as the twisting pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a vine. The plant produces small, dark purple to brown fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
