Albizia forbesii

Albizia forbesii, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Albizia forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
