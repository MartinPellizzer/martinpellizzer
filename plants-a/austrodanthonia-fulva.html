Austrodanthonia fulva

Austrodanthonia fulva, commonly known as the golden grass or kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bright golden yellow. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Austrodanthonia fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
