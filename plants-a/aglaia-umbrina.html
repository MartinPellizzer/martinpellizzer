Aglaia umbrina

Aglaia umbrina, commonly known as the black aglaia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Aglaia umbrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
