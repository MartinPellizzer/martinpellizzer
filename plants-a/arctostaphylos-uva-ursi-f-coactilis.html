Arctostaphylos uva-ursi f. coactilis

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi f. coactilis, commonly known as the dwarf bearberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, evergreen leaves that are typically covered in a whitish, powdery coating, known as a farina. Its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The berries, which are red and edible, are a food source for various wildlife species.
