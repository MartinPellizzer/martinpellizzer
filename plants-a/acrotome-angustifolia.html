Acrotome angustifolia

Acrotome angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acrotome, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its botanical features include a woody stem and fibrous roots, which help it survive in arid conditions.

Acrotome angustifolia is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
