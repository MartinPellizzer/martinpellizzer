Acacia lanigera var. lanigera

Acacia lanigera var. lanigera, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
