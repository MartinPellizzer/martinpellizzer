Amerimnon obovatum

Amerimnon obovatum, commonly known as the Brazilian obovate vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and slightly waxy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
