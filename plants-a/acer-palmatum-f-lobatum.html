Acer palmatum f. lobatum

Acer palmatum f. lobatum, commonly known as the laceleaf maple, is a striking ornamental tree or shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens with moist, cool conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscape design for its aesthetic appeal.
