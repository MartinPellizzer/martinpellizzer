Aquilegia formosa var. kamtshatica

Aquilegia formosa var. kamtshatica, commonly known as the Kamtchatskaya columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Kamchatka in Russia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with five to seven spurred petals, which are characteristic of the genus Aquilegia. The plant produces compound leaves with trifoliate lobes, and its stems are usually hairy and somewhat branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
