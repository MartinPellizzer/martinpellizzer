Anchonium elichrysifolium var. persicum

Anchonium elichrysifolium var. persicum, commonly known as Persian anchonium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
