Andropogon multicaulis

Andropogon multicaulis, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.

Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant for restoration projects.
