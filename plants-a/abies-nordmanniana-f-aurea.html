Abies nordmanniana f. aurea

Abies nordmanniana f. aurea, commonly known as the golden fir, is a unique variant of the Nordmann fir.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with the typical dark green of its parent species. This form is a naturally occurring mutation, often found in specific microclimates where it thrives. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after specimen in horticulture and botanical gardens.
