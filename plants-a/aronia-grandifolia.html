Aronia grandifolia

Aronia grandifolia, commonly known as the red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications. Aronia grandifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Its ecological value includes providing food for birds and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
