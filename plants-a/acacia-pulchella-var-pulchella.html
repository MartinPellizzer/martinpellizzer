Acacia pulchella var. pulchella

Acacia pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give the plant a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. Its bark is smooth and grey, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its shade and aesthetic appeal.
