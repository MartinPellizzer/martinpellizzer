Astragalus rhizanthus subsp. candolleanus

Astragalus rhizanthus subsp.

candolleanus, commonly known as the Sturt's Desert Pea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers with a prominent red or purple blotch on the lower petals.

The plant typically grows from a deep, tuberous root system, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
