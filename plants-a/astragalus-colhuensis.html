Astragalus colhuensis

Astragalus colhuensis, commonly known as the Colhuense milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of northern Mexico, particularly in the states of Chihuahua and Coahuila. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus colhuensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils and along desert margins.
