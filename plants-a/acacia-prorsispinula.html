Acacia prorsispinula

Acacia prorsispinula, commonly known as the slender thorned acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oblong leaflets and is characterized by its yellow, globular flower heads. Its thorns are slender and often arranged in clusters, providing a defensive mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
