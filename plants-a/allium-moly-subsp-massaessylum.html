Allium moly subsp. massaessylum

Allium moly subsp.

massaessylum, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, swollen bulbs that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in a dense umbel.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
