Asplenium subintegrifolium

Asplenium subintegrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely toothed margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are usually short and produce a dense cluster of small, dimorphic fronds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
