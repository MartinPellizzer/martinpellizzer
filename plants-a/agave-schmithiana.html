Agave schmithiana

Agave schmithiana, commonly known as the Schmidth's agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, branched flower stalk that bears yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
