Ardisia eucuneata

Ardisia eucuneata, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright blue berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia eucuneata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
