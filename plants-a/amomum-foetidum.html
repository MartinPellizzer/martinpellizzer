Amomum foetidum

Amomum foetidum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It produces elongated, dark brown pods that contain aromatic, dark brown seeds. The plant is valued for its distinctive spicy and slightly sweet fragrance, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a complex root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Amomum foetidum is closely related to other members of the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.
