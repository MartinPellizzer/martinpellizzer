Anthemis messanensis

Anthemis messanensis, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used historically in traditional medicine.
