Aconitum napellus lusus cernuum

Aconitum napellus lusus cernuum, commonly known as the European garden aconite or the corymbed aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided, compound leaves that are typically deeply lobed. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and requires careful handling.
