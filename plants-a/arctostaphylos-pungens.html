Arctostaphylos pungens

Arctostaphylos pungens, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical, glossy evergreen leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and chaparral habitats.

Arctostaphylos pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
