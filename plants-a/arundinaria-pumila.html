Arundinaria pumila

Arundinaria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, evergreen grass that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet.

It features slender, green culms that are covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense clumps over time.

Arundinaria pumila is well-suited for shaded areas and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
