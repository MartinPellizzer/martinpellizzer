Adeliopsis decumbens

Adeliopsis decumbens, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and alpine areas.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
