Amaryllis × multistriata

Amaryllis × multistriata, commonly known as the multistriate amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisiaica.

It is characterized by its striking, striped leaves that display a combination of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a white or pink center. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This plant is popular as a decorative indoor plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
