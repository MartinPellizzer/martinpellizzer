Azukia radiata

Azukia radiata, commonly known as the radiant azuki, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of small, bright pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round legumes that contain edible seeds, similar to those of the common azuki bean. Azukia radiata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a valuable addition to both ecological and aesthetic landscapes.
