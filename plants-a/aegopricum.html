Aegopricum

Aegopricum, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or pale underside, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and meadows. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Aegopricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
