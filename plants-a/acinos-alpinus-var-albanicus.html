Acinos alpinus var. albanicus

Acinos alpinus var. albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Rosaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
