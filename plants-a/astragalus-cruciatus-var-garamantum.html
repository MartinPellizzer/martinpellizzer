Astragalus cruciatus var. garamantum

Astragalus cruciatus var. garamantum, commonly known as the Garamantian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers clustered in racemes. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
