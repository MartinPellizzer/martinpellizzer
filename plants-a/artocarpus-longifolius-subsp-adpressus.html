Artocarpus longifolius subsp. adpressus

Artocarpus longifolius subsp.

adpressus, commonly known as the flat-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in lowland tropical forests.
