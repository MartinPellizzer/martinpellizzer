Aloe margaritifera

Aloe margaritifera, commonly known as the "pearl aloe," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stem and clusters of fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe margaritifera is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
