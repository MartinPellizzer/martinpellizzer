Artemisia parishii

Artemisia parishii, commonly known as the Parish's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, distinctive scent.

Artemisia parishii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
