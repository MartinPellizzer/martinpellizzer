Acacia gonocarpa

Acacia gonocarpa, commonly known as the African wattle, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia gonocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
