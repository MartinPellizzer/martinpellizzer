Adonis coerulea f. puberula

Adonis coerulea f. puberula, commonly known as the blue adonis or mountain glory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with short, soft pubescence. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender stems. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
