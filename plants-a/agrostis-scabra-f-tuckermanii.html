Agrostis scabra f. tuckermanii

Agrostis scabra f. tuckermanii, commonly known as Tuckerman's rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, bluish-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing tufts. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
