Artemisia × pfaffii

Artemisia × pfaffii, commonly known as golden wormwood, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

Artemisia × pfaffii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
