Astragalus tristis

Astragalus tristis, commonly known as the sad vetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus tristis is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
