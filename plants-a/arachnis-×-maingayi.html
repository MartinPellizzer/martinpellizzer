Arachnis × maingayi

Arachnis × maingayi, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Arachnis × maingayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
