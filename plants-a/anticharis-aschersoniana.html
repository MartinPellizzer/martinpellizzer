Anticharis aschersoniana

Anticharis aschersoniana, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the environment.
