Anemone multifida subsp. saxicola

Anemone multifida subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy, with a compact growth habit.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in early spring, which are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Ranunculaceae family.
