Aloe postgenita

Aloe postgenita, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that has been used traditionally for its soothing properties.

Aloe postgenita is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
