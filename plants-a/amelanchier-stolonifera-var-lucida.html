Amelanchier stolonifera var. lucida

Amelanchier stolonifera var. lucida, commonly known as the shining serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are dark purple to black in color, typically ripening in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing food for birds and pollinators.
