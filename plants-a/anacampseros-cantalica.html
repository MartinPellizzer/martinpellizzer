Anacampseros cantalica

Anacampseros cantalica, commonly known as the Cantalica stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
