Alopecurus behringianus

Alopecurus behringianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.

Alopecurus behringianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
