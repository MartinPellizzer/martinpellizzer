Altensteinia nigrescens

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant's unique coloration is due to the high concentration of anthocyanin pigments in its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Altensteinia nigrescens is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
