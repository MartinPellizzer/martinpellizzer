Artemisia carruthii

Artemisia carruthii, commonly known as Carruth's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
