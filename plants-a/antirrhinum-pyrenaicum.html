Antirrhinum pyrenaicum

Antirrhinum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive bilabiate structure. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Antirrhinum pyrenaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
