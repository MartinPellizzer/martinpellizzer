Aster andohahelensis

Aster andohahelensis, commonly known as the Madagascar aster, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, violet-blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Aster andohahelensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
