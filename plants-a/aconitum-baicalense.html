Aconitum baicalense

Aconitum baicalense, commonly known as the Chinese aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a deeply lobed structure, and the plant has a robust root system.

Aconitum baicalense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling and cultivation.
