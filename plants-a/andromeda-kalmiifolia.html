Andromeda kalmiifolia

Andromeda kalmiifolia, commonly known as the rosebay witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Andromeda kalmiifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
