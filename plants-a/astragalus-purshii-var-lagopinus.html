Astragalus purshii var. lagopinus

Astragalus purshii var. lagopinus, commonly known as mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces legume-like pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, often growing in mountainous regions.

Astragalus purshii var. lagopinus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
