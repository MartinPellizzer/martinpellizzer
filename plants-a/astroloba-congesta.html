Astroloba congesta

Astroloba congesta, commonly known as the star-shaped bulbous plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It features a clustered base of fleshy, bulb-like structures that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces tall, branched stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It blooms during the summer months and is well-suited for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
