Aconitum lycoctonum var. ochranthum

Aconitum lycoctonum var. ochranthum, commonly known as the purple-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking, large, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
