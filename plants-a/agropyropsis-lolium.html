Agropyropsis lolium

Agropyropsis lolium, commonly known as ryegrass, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. Ryegrass is widely distributed in temperate regions and is often found in grasslands, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It plays an important role in agriculture, particularly as a forage crop and in erosion control.
