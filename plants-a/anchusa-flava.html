Anchusa flava

Anchusa flava, commonly known as yellow bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often branched. Anchusa flava thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
