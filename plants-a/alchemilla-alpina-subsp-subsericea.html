Alchemilla alpina subsp. subsericea

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

subsericea, commonly known as the Alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that are softly hairy along the margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright, branched clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in high-altitude environments.
