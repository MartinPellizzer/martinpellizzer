Acacia odoratissima

Acacia odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are compound and feathery in appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds through the wind.

Acacia odoratissima is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in arid environments.
