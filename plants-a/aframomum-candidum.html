Aframomum candidum

Aframomum candidum, commonly known as white cardamom, is a herbaceous plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, yellowish fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
