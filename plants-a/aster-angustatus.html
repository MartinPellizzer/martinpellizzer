Aster angustatus

Aster angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It features slender, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the fall.

Aster angustatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
