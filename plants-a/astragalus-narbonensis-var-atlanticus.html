Astragalus narbonensis var. atlanticus

Astragalus narbonensis var. atlanticus, commonly known as Atlantic milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shores of salt marshes.

This variety is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of coastal plant communities.
