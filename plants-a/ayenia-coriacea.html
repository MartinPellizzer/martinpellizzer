Ayenia coriacea

Ayenia coriacea, commonly known as the rough ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or brown, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Ayenia coriacea is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
