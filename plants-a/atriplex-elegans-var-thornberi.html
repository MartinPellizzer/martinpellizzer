Atriplex elegans var. thornberi

Atriplex elegans var. thornberi, commonly known as the Thornberg's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

This species is often found in saline soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
