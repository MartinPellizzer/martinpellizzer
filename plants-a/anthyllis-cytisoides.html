Anthyllis cytisoides

Anthyllis cytisoides, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis cytisoides is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
