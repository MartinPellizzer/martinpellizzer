Agropyron sinicum

Agropyron sinicum, commonly known as Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and soil-binding properties.

Its seeds are also utilized in traditional medicine and as a nutritious food source for livestock.
