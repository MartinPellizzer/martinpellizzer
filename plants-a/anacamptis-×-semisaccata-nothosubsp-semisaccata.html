Anacamptis × semisaccata nothosubsp.

semisaccata, commonly known as the semi-saccate orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its floral structure includes a three-lobed labellum, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and unique morphology.
