Altensteinia chiogena

Altensteinia chiogena, commonly known as the Chiogena orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The flowers bloom in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
