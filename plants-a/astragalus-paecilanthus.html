Astragalus paecilanthus

Astragalus paecilanthus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Astragalus paecilanthus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
