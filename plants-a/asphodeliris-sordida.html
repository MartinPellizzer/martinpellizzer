Asphodeliris sordida

Asphodeliris sordida, commonly known as the dirty asphodel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spikes of small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Asphodeliris sordida is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
