Aleurites moluccanus var. katoi

Aleurites moluccanus var. katoi, commonly known as the Katoi nut tree, is a variant of the common breadnut tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces oval, hard-shelled fruits containing a single edible kernel, which is rich in oil and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic uses, it is cultivated in various regions for both decorative and practical purposes.
