Arctostaphylos × strigosa

Arctostaphylos × strigosa, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to California and parts of Nevada.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces red or purple berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Arctostaphylos × strigosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
