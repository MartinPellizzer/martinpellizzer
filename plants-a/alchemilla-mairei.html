Alchemilla mairei

Alchemilla mairei, commonly known as the Maire's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, particularly in shaded or moist garden settings.

Alchemilla mairei is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of herbal remedies.
