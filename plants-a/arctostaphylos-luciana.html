Arctostaphylos luciana

Arctostaphylos luciana, commonly known as the Sierra manzanita, is a shrub native to California and parts of Nevada.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Arctostaphylos luciana is an important plant in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
