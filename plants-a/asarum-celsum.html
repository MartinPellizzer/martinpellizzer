Asarum celsum

Asarum celsum, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces a unique, tubular flower that is often mistaken for a leaf due to its shape and color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional and modern pharmacology.
