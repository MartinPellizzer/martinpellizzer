Arum italicum subsp. canariense

Arum italicum subsp.

canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Canary Islands. It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture.

The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral structures.
