Acineta erythroxantha

Acineta erythroxantha, commonly known as the red-flowered acineta, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Acineta erythroxantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
