Anacamptis × timbali nothosubsp. barlae

Anacamptis × timbali nothosubsp.

barlae, commonly known as the Timbali orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It prefers calcareous soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
