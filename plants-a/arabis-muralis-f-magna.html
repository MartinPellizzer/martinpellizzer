Arabis muralis f. magna

Arabis muralis f. magna, commonly known as the giant wallflower, is a variant of the wallflower species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its larger, showy flowers compared to the typical form, often displaying vibrant yellow or orange hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, four-petaled flowers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
