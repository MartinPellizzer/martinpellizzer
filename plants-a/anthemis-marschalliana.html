Anthemis marschalliana

Anthemis marschalliana, commonly known as the marsh marigold, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
