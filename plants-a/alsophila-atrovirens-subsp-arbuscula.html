Alsophila atrovirens subsp. arbuscula

Alsophila atrovirens subsp.

arbuscula, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length.

The fronds are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
