Annona senegalensis var. areolata

Annona senegalensis var. areolata, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to western Africa.

It produces large, edible fruits with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, creamy flesh. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and the plant is valued for both its culinary and medicinal uses.
