Aconitum obtusiflorum

Aconitum obtusiflorum, commonly known as the blunt-flowered aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a coarse texture and a distinctive, waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
