Andromeda ericoides

Andromeda ericoides, commonly known as the Rosebay Witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Andromeda ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
