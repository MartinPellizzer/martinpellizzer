Anisopappus gracilis

Anisopappus gracilis, commonly known as slender pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Anisopappus gracilis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
