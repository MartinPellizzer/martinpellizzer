Alyssopsis mollis

Alyssopsis mollis, commonly known as silverleaf flax, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in spring, which are arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Alyssopsis mollis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
