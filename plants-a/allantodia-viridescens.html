Allantodia viridescens

Allantodia viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered allantodia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, waxy surface. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
