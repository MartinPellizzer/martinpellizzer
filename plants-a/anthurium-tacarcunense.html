Anthurium tacarcunense

Anthurium tacarcunense, commonly known as the Tacarcuna arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the inflorescences that resemble a spatula-shaped structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium tacarcunense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
