Ammi pumilum

Ammi pumilum, commonly known as dwarf cow parsley, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features slender stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a fern-like appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ammi pumilum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
