Acacia mammifera

Acacia mammifera, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly branches and its distinctive yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, needle-like leaflets, and the plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation projects.
