Adipera bicolor

Adipera bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors, with green and white markings on its leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Adipera bicolor is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.

Morphology

Adipera bicolor has leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, alternate in arrangement, and range in size from 5 to 10 centimeters in length, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is slender, with a smooth, pale green bark that becomes slightly rough with age, and it exhibits an erect growth pattern. The flowers are small, clustered in terminal inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in spring and being pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to the plant's preference for well-drained soils.
