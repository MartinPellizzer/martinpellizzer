Abutilon berlandieri

Abutilon berlandieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like hibiscus and cotton.
