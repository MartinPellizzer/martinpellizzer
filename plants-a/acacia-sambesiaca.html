Acacia sambesiaca

Acacia sambesiaca, commonly known as the Brazilian wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
