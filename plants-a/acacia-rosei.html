Acacia rosei

Acacia rosei, commonly known as the pink acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple pairs of small leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
