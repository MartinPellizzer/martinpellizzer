Acacia virgata

Acacia virgata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The plant produces flat, feathery pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia virgata thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
