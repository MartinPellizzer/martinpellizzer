Azalea kendrickii

Azalea kendrickii, commonly known as the Kendrick azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Azalea kendrickii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
