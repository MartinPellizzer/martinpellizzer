Astragalus reinii subsp. mairei

Astragalus reinii subsp.

mairei, commonly known as Maire's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central China. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
