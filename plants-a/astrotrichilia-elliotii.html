Astrotrichilia elliotii

Astrotrichilia elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's star-leaf, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, star-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorl around the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Astrotrichilia elliotii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.
