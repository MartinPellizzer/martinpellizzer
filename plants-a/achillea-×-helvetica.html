Achillea × helvetica

Achillea × helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Achillea × helvetica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
