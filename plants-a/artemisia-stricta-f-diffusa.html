Artemisia stricta f. diffusa

Artemisia stricta f. diffusa, commonly known as the western goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar plants like daisies and sunflowers.

Its aromatic leaves have been used historically for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
