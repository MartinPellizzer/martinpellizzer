Alchemilla subcrenata f. vernalis

Alchemilla subcrenata f. vernalis, commonly known as spring alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a dense mat of narrow, silver-green leaves that are softly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, typically from April to May. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for moisture-retentive soils and garden landscapes.
