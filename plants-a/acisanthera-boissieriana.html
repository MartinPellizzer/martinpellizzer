Acisanthera boissieriana

Acisanthera boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and striking, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
