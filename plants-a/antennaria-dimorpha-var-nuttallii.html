Antennaria dimorpha var. nuttallii

Antennaria dimorpha var. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its basal leaves are typically spoon-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
