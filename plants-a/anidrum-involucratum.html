Anidrum involucratum

Anidrum involucratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anidrum involucratum is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
