Astragalus arkalycensis var. violaceus

Astragalus arkalycensis var. violaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves with a greyish-green hue. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers in late spring, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stems. Its root system is extensive and adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in semi-arid environments.
