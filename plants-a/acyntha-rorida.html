Acyntha rorida

Acyntha rorida, commonly known as the sweet flower or sweet violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Acyntha rorida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
