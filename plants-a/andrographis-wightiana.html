Andrographis wightiana

Andrographis wightiana, commonly known as the Indian toothbrush, is a herbaceous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in dense spikes. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The leaves and stems are often harvested for use in herbal remedies.
