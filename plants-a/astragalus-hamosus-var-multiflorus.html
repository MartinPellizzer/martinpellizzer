Astragalus hamosus var. multiflorus

Astragalus hamosus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
