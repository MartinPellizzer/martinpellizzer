Astragalus neurocarpus subsp. segetalis

Astragalus neurocarpus subsp.

segetalis, commonly known as the field milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the grasslands of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or loamy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
