Amelanchier canadensis var. tomentula

Amelanchier canadensis var. tomentula, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from green to red to deep purple. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and wildlife habitat, as well as its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
