Amygdalus communis var. glabra

Amygdalus communis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth peach.

It is a deciduous tree native to Asia, characterized by its smooth, hairless bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by fuzzy, yellowish fruits that mature in summer. The fruit's skin is thin and edible, while the inner flesh is juicy and sweet.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in various cuisines.
