Anthurium bimarginatum

Anthurium bimarginatum, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its glossy green surface. The plant produces small, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, which is typically a deep red or orange color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium bimarginatum is popular as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and air-purifying properties.
