Aeschynomene aegyptiaca

Aeschynomene aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows in dry, arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikelets. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used for forage.

Aeschynomene aegyptiaca also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
