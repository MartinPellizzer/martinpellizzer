Artanthe leprieurii

Artanthe leprieurii, commonly known as the mountain flax or alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to alpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Artanthe leprieurii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
