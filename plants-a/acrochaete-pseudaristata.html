Acrochaete pseudaristata

Acrochaete pseudaristata, commonly known as the star-shaped daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-like inflorescence composed of white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Acrochaete pseudaristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
