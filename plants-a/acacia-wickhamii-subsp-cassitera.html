Acacia wickhamii subsp. cassitera

Acacia wickhamii subsp.

cassitera, commonly known as the Cassitera acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, pale green phyllodes that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which attract various pollinators. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
