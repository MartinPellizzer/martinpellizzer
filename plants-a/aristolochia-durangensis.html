Aristolochia durangensis

Aristolochia durangensis, commonly known as the Durango birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-shaped flowers. The plant produces a complex array of compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and chemical composition, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
