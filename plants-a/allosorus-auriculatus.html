Allosorus auriculatus

Allosorus auriculatus, commonly known as the ear-shaped liverwort, is a small, leafy liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Pottiaceae and is characterized by its rosette-like growth pattern with closely packed, overlapping leaves. The leaves are typically lanceolate or ovate, with a distinctive auriculate base that gives the plant its common name. It is often found on rocks, soil, or decaying wood in temperate regions.

This non-vascular plant lacks true roots and relies on its dense foliage for water retention and photosynthesis.
