Astragalus keyserlingii

Astragalus keyserlingii, commonly known as Keyserling's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and botanical studies.
