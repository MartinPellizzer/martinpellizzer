Azureocereus hertlingianus subsp. columnaris

Azureocereus hertlingianus subsp.

columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach several meters in height.

The plant produces vibrant, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Its succulent stems store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments.

The subspecies is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience in harsh conditions.
