Aster umbellatus var. latifolius

Aster umbellatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall, with lance-shaped, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
