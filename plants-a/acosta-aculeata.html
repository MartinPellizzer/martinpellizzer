Acosta aculeata

Acosta aculeata, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acosta aculeata is adapted to dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
