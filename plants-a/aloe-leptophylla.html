Aloe leptophylla

Aloe leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers on tall, branched inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

Aloe leptophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
