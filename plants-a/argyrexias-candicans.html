Argyrexias candicans

Argyrexias candicans, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that contrast with its silvery, velvety foliage. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are covered in fine, reflective hairs that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
