Aristolochia clementis

Aristolochia clementis, commonly known as the purple swallowworts, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green berries that contain toxic seeds. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
