Acer palmatum subsp. septemlobum

Acer palmatum subsp.

septemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that typically have seven lobes, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The plant is also valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
