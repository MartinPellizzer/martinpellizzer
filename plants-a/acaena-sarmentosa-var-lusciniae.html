Acaena sarmentosa var. lusciniae

Acaena sarmentosa var. lusciniae, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features wiry, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
