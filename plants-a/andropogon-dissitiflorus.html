Andropogon dissitiflorus

Andropogon dissitiflorus, commonly known as the scattered-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically greenish or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Andropogon dissitiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
