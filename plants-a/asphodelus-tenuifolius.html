Asphodelus tenuifolius

Asphodelus tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asphodel, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like flower clusters. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Asphodelus tenuifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
