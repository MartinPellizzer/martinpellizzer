Aconitum vilmorianum var. altifidum

Aconitum vilmorianum var. altifidum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum vilmorianum var. altifidum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
