Agropyron pallidissimum

Agropyron pallidissimum, commonly known as the pale wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale yellow to greenish flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Agropyron pallidissimum is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
