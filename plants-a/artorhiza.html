Artorhiza

Artorhiza, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to South America, Artorhiza thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant contains toxic milky sap, which can cause skin irritation.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
