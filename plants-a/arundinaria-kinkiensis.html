Arundinaria kinkiensis

Arundinaria kinkiensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kinki bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundinaria kinkiensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
