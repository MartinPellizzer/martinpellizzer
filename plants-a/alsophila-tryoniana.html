Alsophila tryoniana

Alsophila tryoniana, commonly known as the Tryon's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk, which is covered in a fibrous, spongy layer.

The fronds are bi-directionally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance and ecological role in its habitat.
