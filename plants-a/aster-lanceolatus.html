Aster lanceolatus

Aster lanceolatus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woods, thriving in temperate climates. Its lance-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall.

The slender aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
