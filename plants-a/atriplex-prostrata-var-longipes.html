Atriplex prostrata var. longipes

Atriplex prostrata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed saltbush, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert environments. Its small, greenish-gray flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Atriplex prostrata var. longipes plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in harsh climates.
