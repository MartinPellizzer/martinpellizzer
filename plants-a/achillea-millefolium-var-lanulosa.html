Achillea millefolium var. lanulosa

Achillea millefolium var. lanulosa, commonly known as the lanate yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in a soft, woolly layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces flat, composite flower heads in shades of white, pink, or pale yellow, arranged in clusters atop sturdy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
