Acetosa hibernica

Acetosa hibernica, commonly known as the Irish spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Acetosa hibernica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
