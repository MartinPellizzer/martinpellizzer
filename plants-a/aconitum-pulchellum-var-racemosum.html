Aconitum pulchellum var. racemosum

Aconitum pulchellum var. racemosum, commonly known as the racemose monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant features palmately divided, deeply lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in woodland environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
