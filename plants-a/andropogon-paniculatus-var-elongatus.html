Andropogon paniculatus var. elongatus

Andropogon paniculatus var. elongatus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled in the bud. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common species in prairie and woodland habitats.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural landscapes.
