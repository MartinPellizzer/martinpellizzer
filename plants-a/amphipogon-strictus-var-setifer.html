Amphipogon strictus var. setifer

Amphipogon strictus var. setifer, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
