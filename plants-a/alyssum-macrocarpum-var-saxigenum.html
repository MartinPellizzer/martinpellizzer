Alyssum macrocarpum var. saxigenum

Alyssum macrocarpum var. saxigenum, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
