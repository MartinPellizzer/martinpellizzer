Aria corymbiferum

Aria corymbiferum, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the spring. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Aria corymbiferum is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to withstand long periods of drought.
