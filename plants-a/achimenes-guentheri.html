Achimenes guentheri

Achimenes guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's waxplant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a garden plant for its vibrant blooms.

Morphology

Achimenes guentheri has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a moderate size and a slightly waxy texture.

The stem is cylindrical, smooth, and slightly hairy, with a light green to reddish-brown color, growing upright and branching freely. Its flowers are tubular with a spurred shape, displaying vibrant pink to red hues, blooming from summer to autumn, and are pollinated primarily by bees. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and tuberous, helping the plant store nutrients and survive dry periods.
