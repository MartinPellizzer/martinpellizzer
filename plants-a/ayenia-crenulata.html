Ayenia crenulata

Ayenia crenulata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ayenia crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in mountainous areas.
