Acacia hirtella var. inermis

Acacia hirtella var. inermis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flowers arranged in compact, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The silky acacia is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fuelwood in its native habitat.
