Actinodaphne mansonii

Actinodaphne mansonii, commonly known as the Malayan camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it has been historically valued for its essential oils and traditional uses.
