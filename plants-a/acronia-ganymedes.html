Acronia ganymedes

Acronia ganymedes, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a star-like pattern, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Acronia ganymedes is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
