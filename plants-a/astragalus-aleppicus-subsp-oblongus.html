Astragalus aleppicus subsp. oblongus

Astragalus aleppicus subsp.

oblongus, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
