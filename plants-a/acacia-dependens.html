Acacia dependens

Acacia dependens, commonly known as the hanging acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often greyish in color. Acacia dependens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.
