Andropogon nodulibarbis

Andropogon nodulibarbis, commonly known as the knobbed grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant typically produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bracts that are often hairy or scabrous. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a rough texture, with prominent veins.

Andropogon nodulibarbis is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
