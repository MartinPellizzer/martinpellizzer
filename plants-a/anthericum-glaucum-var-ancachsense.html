Anthericum glaucum var. ancachsense

Anthericum glaucum var. ancachsense, commonly known as the Ancacha lily, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. The plant produces striking, white to pale blue flowers with a prominent central spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
