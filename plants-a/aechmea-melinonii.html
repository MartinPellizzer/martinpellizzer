Aechmea melinonii

Aechmea melinonii, commonly known as the Melinon's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients from the environment. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange, often resembling flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Aechmea melinonii thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is popular as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor gardens.

Morphology

Aechmea melinonii has rosette-shaped leaves that are wide, stiff, and leathery, arranged in a dense spiral around the central stem, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating.

The plant lacks a prominent stem, with the base of the leaves forming a tight rosette and the surface of the leaves being smooth and dark green. Its flowers are clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
