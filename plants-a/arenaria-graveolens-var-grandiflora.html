Arenaria graveolens var. grandiflora

Arenaria graveolens var. grandiflora, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant has succulent, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
