Adiantum lutescens

Adiantum lutescens, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that display a striking golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with each leaflet being narrow and elongated.

Adiantum lutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
