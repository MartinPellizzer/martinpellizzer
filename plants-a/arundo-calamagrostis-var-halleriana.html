Arundo calamagrostis var. halleriana

Arundo calamagrostis var. halleriana, commonly known as Haller's reed, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and marshes.

Haller's reed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
