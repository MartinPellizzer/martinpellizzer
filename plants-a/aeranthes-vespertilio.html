Aeranthes vespertilio

Aeranthes vespertilio, commonly known as the evening bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas with mild climates. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive yellowish lip that attracts specific pollinators.

Aeranthes vespertilio is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
