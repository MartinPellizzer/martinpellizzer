Amaranthus guadeloupensis

Amaranthus guadeloupensis, commonly known as the Guadeloupe amaranth, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into spiny capsules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
