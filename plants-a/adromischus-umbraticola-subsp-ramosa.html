Adromischus umbraticola subsp. ramosa

Adromischus umbraticola subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the rock rose or forest rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, bushy growth with fleshy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
