Anoectochilus javanicus

Anoectochilus javanicus, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and epiphytic or lithophytic tendencies. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Anoectochilus javanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
