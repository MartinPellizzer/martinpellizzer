Achillea horanszkyi

Achillea horanszkyi, commonly known as the Hungarian yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Hungary and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Its bloom period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
