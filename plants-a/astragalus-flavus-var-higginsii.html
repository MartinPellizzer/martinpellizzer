Astragalus flavus var. higginsii

Astragalus flavus var. higginsii, commonly known as Higgins' yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is typically fibrous and deep. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
