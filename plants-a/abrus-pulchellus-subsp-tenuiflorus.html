Abrus pulchellus subsp. tenuiflorus

Abrus pulchellus subsp.

tenuiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered scarlet bean, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are typically clustered in loose inflorescences.

The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds with a distinctive black coat. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
