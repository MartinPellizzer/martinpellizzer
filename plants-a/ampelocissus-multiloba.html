Ampelocissus multiloba

Ampelocissus multiloba, commonly known as the African grape or rock fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Ampelocissus multiloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail along structures.
