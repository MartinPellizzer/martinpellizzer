Adenandra humilis

Adenandra humilis, commonly known as the silverleaf adenandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name.

Adenandra humilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
