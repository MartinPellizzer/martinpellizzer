Anthurium pichinchae

Anthurium pichinchae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It features vibrant, heart-shaped bracts that range in color from deep red to orange, often contrasting with its dark green foliage. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, which is enclosed by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pichinchae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
