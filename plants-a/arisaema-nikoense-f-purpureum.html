Arisaema nikoense f. purpureum

Arisaema nikoense f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered arrowhead, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and an inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces showy, violet-colored bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to regions of Southeast Asia.

This cultivar is valued for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display in botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
