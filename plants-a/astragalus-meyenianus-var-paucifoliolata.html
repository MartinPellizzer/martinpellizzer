Astragalus meyenianus var. paucifoliolata

Astragalus meyenianus var. paucifoliolata, commonly known as the few-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with few leaflets, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
