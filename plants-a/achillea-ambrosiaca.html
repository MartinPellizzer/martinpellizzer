Achillea ambrosiaca

Achillea ambrosiaca, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its chemical composition includes compounds such as thujone, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal uses.
