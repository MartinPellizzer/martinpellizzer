Austrostipa scabra subsp. scabra

Austrostipa scabra subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the scabrous wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has wiry, slender stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine, bristly hairs, giving it a scabrous appearance.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as plains, dunes, and rocky outcrops.
