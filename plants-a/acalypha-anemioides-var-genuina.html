Acalypha anemioides var. genuina

Acalypha anemioides var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves with intricate patterns of red, green, and yellow veins. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
