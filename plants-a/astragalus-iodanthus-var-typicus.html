Astragalus iodanthus var. typicus

Astragalus iodanthus var. typicus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often dispersed by wind or animals.
