Acer fulvescens subsp. fupingense

Acer fulvescens subsp.

fupingense, commonly known as the Fuping maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
