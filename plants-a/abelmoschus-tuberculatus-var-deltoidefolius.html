Abelmoschus tuberculatus var. deltoidefolius

Abelmoschus tuberculatus var. deltoidefolius, commonly known as the deltoid-leaf okra, is a variant of the okra species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive deltoid-shaped leaves, which are broad and triangular in appearance. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain edible, fibrous seeds, similar to other okra varieties. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its culinary uses.
