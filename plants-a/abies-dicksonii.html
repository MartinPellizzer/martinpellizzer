Abies dicksonii

Abies dicksonii, commonly known as the Dickson's fir, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often remain attached to the branches for an extended period. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The plant has ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
