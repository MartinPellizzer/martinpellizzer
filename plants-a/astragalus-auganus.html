Astragalus auganus

Astragalus auganus, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Astragalus auganus is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
