Acacia lirellata subsp. lirellata

Acacia lirellata subsp.

lirellata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in globular heads.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender growth habit compared to other acacia species.
