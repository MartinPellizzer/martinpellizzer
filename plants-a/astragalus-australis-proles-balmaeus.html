Astragalus australis proles balmaeus

Astragalus australis proles balmaeus, commonly known as the Balmaean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
