Alternanthera geniculata

Alternanthera geniculata, commonly known as the red-veined or pink-veined beauty, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink veins contrasting with its dark green foliage. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often cultivated in aquariums. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can grow up to 5 inches in length.

Alternanthera geniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for small aquatic life.
