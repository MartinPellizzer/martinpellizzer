Annona gracilis

Annona gracilis, commonly known as the slender soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, resembling a small soursop, is edible and has a mildly acidic, creamy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
