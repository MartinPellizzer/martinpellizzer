Acacia nuperrima

Acacia nuperrima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in cylindrical spikes and bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Acacia nuperrima is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
