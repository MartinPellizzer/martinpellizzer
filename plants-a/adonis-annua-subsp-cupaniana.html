Adonis annua subsp. cupaniana

Adonis annua subsp.

cupaniana, commonly known as the annual adonis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
