Anoda pentaschista var. obtusior

Anoda pentaschista var. obtusior, commonly known as the broadleaf horsegram, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are usually deeply lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
