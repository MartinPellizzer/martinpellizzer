Asarum leucosepalum

Asarum leucosepalum, commonly known as the white-spiked arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence with a white spathe and a yellowish spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family. The plant has heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Asarum leucosepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
