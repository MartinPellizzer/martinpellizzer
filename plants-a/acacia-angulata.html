Acacia angulata

Acacia angulata, commonly known as the angular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia angulata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
