Arum gracile

Arum gracile, commonly known as the slender arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix, often accompanied by a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Arum gracile is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and dramatic floral display.
