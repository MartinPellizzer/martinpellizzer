Anuragia gracilis

Anuragia gracilis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its succulent leaves are adapted for water storage, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Anuragia gracilis is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, making it a characteristic species of its native habitat.
