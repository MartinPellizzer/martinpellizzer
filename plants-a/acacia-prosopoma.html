Acacia prosopoma

Acacia prosopoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery underside, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Acacia prosopoma is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
