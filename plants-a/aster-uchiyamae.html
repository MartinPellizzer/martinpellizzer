Aster uchiyamae

Aster uchiyamae, commonly known as the Uchiyama's aster, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster uchiyamae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
