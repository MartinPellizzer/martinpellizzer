Aristida capensis var. fulviberbis

Aristida capensis var. fulviberbis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a hairy texture. The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
