Amarenus spadiceus

Amarenus spadiceus, commonly known as the golden amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, feather-like leaves that range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Amarenus spadiceus is also valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
