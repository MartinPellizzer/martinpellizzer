Acer campestre var. suberosum

Acer campestre var. suberosum, commonly known as the hairy maple, is a variant of the common maple that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other maple species. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
