Aquilegia formosa var. chrysantha

Aquilegia formosa var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically bright yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant has palmate, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, silky pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often growing in woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
