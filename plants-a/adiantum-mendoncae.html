Adiantum mendoncae

Adiantum mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendo fern, is a species of polypody fern native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and have a finely divided appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in rocky habitats. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Adiantum genus.

The Mendo fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture and light conditions.
