Allophylus rigidus

Allophylus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid alpine holly, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually leathery and dark green. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

Allophylus rigidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
