Allocarya chorisiana var. myriantha

Allocarya chorisiana var. myriantha, commonly known as the clustered thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the summer. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a short, stout flowering stem.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
