Alchornea tiliifolia

Alchornea tiliifolia, commonly known as the silky alchornea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by three-lobed, capsule-like fruits. Alchornea tiliifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
