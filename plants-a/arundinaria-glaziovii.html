Arundinaria glaziovii

Arundinaria glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's bamboo, is a species of grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly rough texture.

This grass is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
