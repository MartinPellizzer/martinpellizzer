Anethum piperitum

Anethum piperitum, commonly known as Indian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant produces oval, dark brown seeds that are rich in essential oils, giving it a distinct aromatic profile. Its leaves are finely divided and often used in culinary applications, while the seeds are widely used as a spice.

Anethum piperitum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common crop in arid and semi-arid regions.
