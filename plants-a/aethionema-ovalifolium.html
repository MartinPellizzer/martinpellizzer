Aethionema ovalifolium

Aethionema ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often upright and branched. Small white or pale yellow flowers appear in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
