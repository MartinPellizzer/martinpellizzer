Astragalus gombo subsp. pseudogombo

Astragalus gombo subsp.

pseudogombo, commonly known as the false gombo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its subspecies status highlights subtle morphological differences from the main species, Astragalus gombo.
