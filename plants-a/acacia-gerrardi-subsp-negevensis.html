Acacia gerrardi subsp. negevensis

Acacia gerrardi subsp.

negevensis, commonly known as the Negev acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Israel and the Negev Desert. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that reduce water loss.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
