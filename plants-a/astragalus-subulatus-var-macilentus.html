Astragalus subulatus var. macilentus

Astragalus subulatus var. macilentus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. The plant has slender, spiny stems and small, compound leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
