Acrocephalus cyaneobracteatus

Acrocephalus cyaneobracteatus, commonly known as the blue-bract bulrush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with blue or purple bracts, giving it its common name. It typically thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and shallow water areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
