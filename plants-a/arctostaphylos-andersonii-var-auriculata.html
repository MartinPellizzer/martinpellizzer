Arctostaphylos andersonii var. auriculata

Arctostaphylos andersonii var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate manzanita, is a shrub native to California's coastal regions.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically pink or white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a red, berry-like drupe that ripens in the summer, providing food for various wildlife species.

This variety is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
