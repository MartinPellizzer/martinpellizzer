Alsophila speciosa

Alsophila speciosa, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dense layer of sori, which are clusters of sporangia used for spore dispersal. The fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive silver or grayish hue on the underside due to the presence of trichomes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
