Andropogon schlechteri

Andropogon schlechteri, commonly known as Schlechter's grass, is a species of perennial grass native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and inflorescence of spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
