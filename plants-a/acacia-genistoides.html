Acacia genistoides

Acacia genistoides, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often mistaken for those of the genus Acacia. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
