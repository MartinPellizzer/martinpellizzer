Allium roylei

Allium roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are used in traditional cuisines, particularly in South Asian countries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, Allium roylei is also cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
