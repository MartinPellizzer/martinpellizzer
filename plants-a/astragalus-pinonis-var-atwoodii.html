Astragalus pinonis var. atwoodii

Astragalus pinonis var. atwoodii, commonly known as the Atwood's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
