Aconitum heptatetalum

Aconitum heptatetalum, commonly known as the seven-leaved monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that supports large, palmately compound leaves with seven leaflets. Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.
