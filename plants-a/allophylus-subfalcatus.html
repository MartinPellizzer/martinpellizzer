Allophylus subfalcatus

Allophylus subfalcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often slightly curled. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Allophylus subfalcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
