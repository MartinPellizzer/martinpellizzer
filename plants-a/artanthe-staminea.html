Artanthe staminea

Artanthe staminea, commonly known as the stamen's artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink to red, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Artanthe staminea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
