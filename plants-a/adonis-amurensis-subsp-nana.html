Adonis amurensis subsp. nana

Adonis amurensis subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf adonis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It features succulent, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes various flowering plants with showy blooms.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
