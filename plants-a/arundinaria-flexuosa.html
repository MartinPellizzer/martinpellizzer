Arundinaria flexuosa

Arundinaria flexuosa, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant is part of the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps. Arundinaria flexuosa typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
