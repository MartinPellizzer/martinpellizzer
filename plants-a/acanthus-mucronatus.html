Acanthus mucronatus

Acanthus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to violet flowers in the summer months. Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Acanthus mucronatus is also valued for its historical use in classical art and architecture as a decorative motif.
