Astragalus polyanthus subsp. vedicus

Astragalus polyanthus subsp.

vedicus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
