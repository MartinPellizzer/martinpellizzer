Asyneuma limonifolium f. canescens

Asyneuma limonifolium f. canescens, commonly known as the woolly lemonleaf, is a variant of the lemonleaf plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a lemony scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
