Anaxagorea petiolata

Anaxagorea petiolata, commonly known as the long petioled evergreen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with long petioles. The leaves are usually elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly pubescent surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red or orange berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
