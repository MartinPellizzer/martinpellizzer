Arabis columbiana

Arabis columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Arabis columbiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
