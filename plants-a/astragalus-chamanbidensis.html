Astragalus chamanbidensis

Astragalus chamanbidensis, commonly known as the Chamanbid Desert Pea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
