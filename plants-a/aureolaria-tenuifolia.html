Aureolaria tenuifolia

Aureolaria tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Aureolaria tenuifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
