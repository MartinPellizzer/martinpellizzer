Achillea × dentatoserrata

Achillea × dentatoserrata, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and wild areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

The species is a natural hybrid between Achillea millefolium and Achillea ptarmica, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.
