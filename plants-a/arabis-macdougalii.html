Arabis macdougalii

Arabis macdougalii, commonly known as the Scottish rock cress, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Arabis macdougalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
