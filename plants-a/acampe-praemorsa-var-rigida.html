Acampe praemorsa var. rigida

Acampe praemorsa var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid acampe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its delicate foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
