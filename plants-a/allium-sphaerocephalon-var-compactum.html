Allium sphaerocephalon var. compactum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. compactum, commonly known as the compact garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with a strong garlic aroma, and its flower heads are spherical, consisting of numerous small, white to pale purple florets. The plant produces bulbils on its stems, which can be used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
