Antrophyum subfalcatum

Antrophyum subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cycad, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and its distinctive, elongated leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants, a feature common in cycads. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable but vulnerable species in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
