Anthurium alvinii

Anthurium alvinii, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped, velvety green leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins. The plant produces spadices, which are the characteristic spathes that enclose the true flowers, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium alvinii is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
