Areca globulifera

Areca globulifera, commonly known as the globular areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits resembling small globes, hence its common name. These fruits are typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

Areca globulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
