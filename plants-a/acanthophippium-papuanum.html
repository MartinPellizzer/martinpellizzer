Acanthophippium papuanum

Acanthophippium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan starfish cactus, is a unique succulent native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, star-shaped stems that store water for survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Acanthophippium papuanum is a rare and specialized species, adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its native habitat.
