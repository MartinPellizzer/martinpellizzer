Abelmoschus manihot var. mindanaensis

Abelmoschus manihot var. mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao okra, is a variant of the okra species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its edible young fruits and its potential use in traditional medicine.
