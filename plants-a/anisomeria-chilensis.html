Anisomeria chilensis

Anisomeria chilensis, commonly known as Chilean anisomeria, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and wind exposure. It is often found in Mediterranean-type climates and can thrive in areas with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Anisomeria chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habits.
