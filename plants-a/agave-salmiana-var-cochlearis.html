Agave salmiana var. cochlearis

Agave salmiana var. cochlearis, commonly known as the cochlear agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
