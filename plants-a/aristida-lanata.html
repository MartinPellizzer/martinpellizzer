Aristida lanata

Aristida lanata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silvery-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, hairy structures on its leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites.

Aristida lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
