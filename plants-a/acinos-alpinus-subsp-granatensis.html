Acinos alpinus subsp. granatensis

Acinos alpinus subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

Its subspecies status reflects distinct morphological and genetic differences from the main species, Acinos alpinus.
