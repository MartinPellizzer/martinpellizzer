Aphelandra heydeana

Aphelandra heydeana, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers clustered beneath the colorful bracts. Its leaves are glossy, elongated, and often have a waxy texture.

Aphelandra heydeana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to semi-shaded environments.
