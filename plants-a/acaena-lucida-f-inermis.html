Acaena lucida f. inermis

Acaena lucida f. inermis, commonly known as the glabrous spinach bush, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves that are often clustered around the stems, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and alpine regions.
