Aulomyrcia panicularis

Aulomyrcia panicularis, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Aulomyrcia panicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal habitats.
