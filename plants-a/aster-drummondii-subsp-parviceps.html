Aster drummondii subsp. parviceps

Aster drummondii subsp.

parviceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it is often found in areas with low precipitation.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
