Aspidium ridleyanum

Aspidium ridleyanum, commonly known as the Ridley's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically borne on long, creeping rhizomes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in dense undergrowth or along stream banks.

Aspidium ridleyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
