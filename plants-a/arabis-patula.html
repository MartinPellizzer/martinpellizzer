Arabis patula

Arabis patula, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arabis patula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
