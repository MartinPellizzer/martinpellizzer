Aconitum canescens

Aconitum canescens, commonly known as the hoary aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely foliated stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered with a fine, whitish down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum canescens is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic to both humans and animals.
