Aquilaria apiculata

Aquilaria apiculata, commonly known as the white sand aquilaria, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its fragrant wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree is also known for its ability to produce agarwood, a resinous substance that develops in response to injury or fungal infection.
