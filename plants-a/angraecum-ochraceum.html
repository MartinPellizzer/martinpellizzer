Angraecum ochraceum

Angraecum ochraceum, commonly known as the ochraceous orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers, which bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Angraecum ochraceum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
