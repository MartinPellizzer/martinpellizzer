Anthericum bolusii

Anthericum bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's lantern, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered around a central axis, giving it a lantern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
