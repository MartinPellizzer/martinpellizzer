Aster gracilis

Aster gracilis, commonly known as slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster gracilis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
