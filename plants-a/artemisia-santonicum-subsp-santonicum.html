Artemisia santonicum subsp. santonicum

Artemisia santonicum subsp.

santonicum, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with finely divided leaves that emit a distinctive, often camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional remedies and as a source of essential oils.
