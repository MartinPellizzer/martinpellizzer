Aspidium viviparum var. bipinnatum

Aspidium viviparum var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the bipinnate water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds, which are divided into smaller pinnae, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in swampy areas. Its reproductive structures, including viviparous sporangia, allow for the development of spores directly on the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
