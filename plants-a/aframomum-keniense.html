Aframomum keniense

Aframomum keniense, commonly known as Kenyan cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to East Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into dark, aromatic fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique scent and botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
