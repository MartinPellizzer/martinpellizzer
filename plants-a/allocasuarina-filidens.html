Allocasuarina filidens

Allocasuarina filidens, commonly known as the slender she-oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique cylindrical, scale-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences, followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain the seeds. It is well adapted to dry and sandy soils, often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
