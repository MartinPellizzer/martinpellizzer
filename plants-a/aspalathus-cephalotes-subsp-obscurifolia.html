Aspalathus cephalotes subsp. obscurifolia

Aspalathus cephalotes subsp.

obscurifolia, commonly known as the dark-leaved mountain tea, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
