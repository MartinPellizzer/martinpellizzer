Abatia brasiliensis

Abatia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian abatia, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 50 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
