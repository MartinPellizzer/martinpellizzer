Acropogon dzumacensis

Acropogon dzumacensis, commonly known as the Dzumacensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Acropogon dzumacensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
