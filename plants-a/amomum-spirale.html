Amomum spirale

Amomum spirale, commonly known as spiral cardamom, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, slightly spicy scent.

Amomum spirale is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
