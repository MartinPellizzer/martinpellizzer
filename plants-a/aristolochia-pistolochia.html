Aristolochia pistolochia

Aristolochia pistolochia, commonly known as the pistol pipevine, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
