Acacia baileyana var. purpurea

Acacia baileyana var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, fluffy yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The variety 'purpurea' is distinguished by its striking purple foliage, which adds a unique visual appeal to the plant.
