Achras tainteriana

Achras tainteriana, commonly known as the Mexican aloe, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces striking red, orange, or yellow flowers on tall, branched inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
