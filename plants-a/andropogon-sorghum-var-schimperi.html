Andropogon sorghum var. schimperi

Andropogon sorghum var. schimperi, commonly known as dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, sturdy stalks and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typical of the Andropogoneae tribe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage or as a ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
