Anthemis tomentosa f. discoidea

Anthemis tomentosa f. discoidea, commonly known as the woolly feverfew, is a cultivar of the Anthemis genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance.

The cultivar is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
