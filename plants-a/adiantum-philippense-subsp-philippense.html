Adiantum philippense subsp. philippense

Adiantum philippense subsp.

philippense, commonly known as the Philippine maidenhair fern, is a species native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its sporangia are arranged in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, a typical feature of the Adiantum genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
