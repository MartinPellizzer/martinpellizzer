Anaphalis javanica f. junghuhniana

Anaphalis javanica f. junghuhniana, commonly known as the Junghuhn's woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features silvery-white, woolly foliage and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
