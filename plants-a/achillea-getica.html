Achillea getica

Achillea getica, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Achillea getica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
