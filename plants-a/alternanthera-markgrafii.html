Alternanthera markgrafii

Alternanthera markgrafii, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a striking aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage, which becomes more intense under bright light conditions. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with moderate to high light exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in both freshwater and saltwater aquariums.
