Ampelopsis wangii

Ampelopsis wangii, commonly known as Chinese ivy, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five lobes, often with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall, attracting various wildlife.

Ampelopsis wangii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to different growing conditions.
