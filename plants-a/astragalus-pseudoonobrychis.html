Astragalus pseudoonobrychis

Astragalus pseudoonobrychis, commonly known as the false goat's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or through animal activity. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
