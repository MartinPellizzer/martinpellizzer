Allium aucheri

Allium aucheri, commonly known as the purple onion or the Siberian leek, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, gray-green leaves and produces tall, slender stems topped with clusters of purple or pinkish flowers. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Allium aucheri is relatively low maintenance and resistant to most pests and diseases.
