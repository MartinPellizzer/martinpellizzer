Adesmia psoraleoides var. glabrata

Adesmia psoraleoides var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth adesmia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, glabrous (hairless) leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
