Apocynum sibiricum var. farwellii

Apocynum sibiricum var. farwellii, commonly known as Farwell's dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

This species is also noted for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.
