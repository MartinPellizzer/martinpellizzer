Aspalathus intricata subsp. intricata

Aspalathus intricata subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
