Acaena pinnatifida subsp. nudiscapa

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

nudiscapa, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand. It features stiff, spiny stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces burr-like fruits with hooked bracts, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to a range of habitats, including forests and coastal areas, and is known for its persistent, woody structure.
