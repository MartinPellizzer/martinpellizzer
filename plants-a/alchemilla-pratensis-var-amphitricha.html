Alchemilla pratensis var. amphitricha

Alchemilla pratensis var. amphitricha, commonly known as the creeping alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, bright green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy powder. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
