Adiantum tetraphyllum var. microphyllum

Adiantum tetraphyllum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the miniature maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like fronds that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and thrives in well-draining, acidic soil. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are typically arranged in a compact, upright cluster.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.
