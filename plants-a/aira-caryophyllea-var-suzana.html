Aira caryophyllea var. suzana

Aira caryophyllea var. suzana, commonly known as the Suzana grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its elegant appearance and ability to add texture to garden landscapes.
