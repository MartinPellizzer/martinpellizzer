Ancylostemon aureus

Ancylostemon aureus, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant display to its canopy. The plant has a fast-growing habit with a straight, smooth trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to the showy blooms.

The golden trumpet tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
