Alseis latifolia

Alseis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alseis, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and blueberries, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a common species in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters.

Alseis latifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
