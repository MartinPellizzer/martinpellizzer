Arabis pubescens var. bracteosa

Arabis pubescens var. bracteosa, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and oval-shaped, with a rosette growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
