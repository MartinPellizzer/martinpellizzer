Anthurium pendulifolium

Anthurium pendulifolium, commonly known as the pendant-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping leaves that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces bright red spathes that enclose its spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium pendulifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique flowering structure.
