Astragalus cabulicus

Astragalus cabulicus, commonly known as the Kabul milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

The seeds of Astragalus cabulicus are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their nutritional content.
