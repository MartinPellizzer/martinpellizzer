Aster chrysogonii

Aster chrysogonii, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
