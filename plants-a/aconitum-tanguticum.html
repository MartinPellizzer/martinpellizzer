Aconitum tanguticum

Aconitum tanguticum, commonly known as the Tibetan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces follicular fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Aconitum tanguticum is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
