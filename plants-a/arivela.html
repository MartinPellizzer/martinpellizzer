Arivela

Arivela, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in long, drooping clusters. The plant produces small, leguminous pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Arivela is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in local herbal remedies.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for reforestation projects in degraded areas.
