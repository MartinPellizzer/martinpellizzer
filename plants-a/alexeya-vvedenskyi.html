Alexeya vvedenskyi

Alexeya vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a coniferous tree native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure, typical of the conifer family. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. Its wood is lightweight and resistant to decay, which has made it valuable for construction and craft.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
