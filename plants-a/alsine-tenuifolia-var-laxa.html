Alsine tenuifolia var. laxa

Alsine tenuifolia var. laxa, commonly known as slender alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
