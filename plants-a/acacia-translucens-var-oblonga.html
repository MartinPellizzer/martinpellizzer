Acacia translucens var. oblonga

Acacia translucens var. oblonga, commonly known as the translucent wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
