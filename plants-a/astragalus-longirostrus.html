Astragalus longirostrus

Astragalus longirostrus, commonly known as the long-beaked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus longirostrus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
