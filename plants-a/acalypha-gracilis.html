Acalypha gracilis

Acalypha gracilis, commonly known as the slender croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish or variegated in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
