Alchemilla westermaieri

Alchemilla westermaieri, commonly known as the Western lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
