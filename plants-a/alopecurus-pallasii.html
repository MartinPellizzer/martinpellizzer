Alopecurus pallasii

Alopecurus pallasii, commonly known as Pallas's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that gives it its common name.

Alopecurus pallasii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
