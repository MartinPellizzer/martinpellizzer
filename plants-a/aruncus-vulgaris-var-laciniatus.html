Aruncus vulgaris var. laciniatus

Aruncus vulgaris var. laciniatus, commonly known as the fern-leaf goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching stems that bear large, feathery, greenish-white plumes of flowers. The leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of a fern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its unique foliage and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
