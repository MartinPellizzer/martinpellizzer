Asplenium ledermannii

Asplenium ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium ledermannii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
