Allium campestre

Allium campestre, commonly known as field garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbils along its flowering stems, which are used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

Allium campestre is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
