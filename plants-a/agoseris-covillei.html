Agoseris covillei

Agoseris covillei, commonly known as the Coville's goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyons. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Agoseris covillei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
