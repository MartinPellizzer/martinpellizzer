Ardisia fulva var. multiflora

Ardisia fulva var. multiflora, commonly known as the red-berried ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
