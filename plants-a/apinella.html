Apinella

Apinella, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Apinella is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
