Aconitum baumgartenii

Aconitum baumgartenii, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers. The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
