Aphelandra jacobinioides

Aphelandra jacobinioides, commonly known as the Jacobine torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Aphelandra jacobinioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and tropical aesthetic.
