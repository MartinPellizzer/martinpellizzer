Allium incensiodorum

Allium incensiodorum, commonly known as the Persian leek or garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like foliage and small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its medicinal properties, which have been recognized in traditional herbal practices.
