Allium paniculatum var. violaceum

Allium paniculatum var. violaceum, commonly known as purple onion grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features grass-like leaves and produces dense, branched inflorescences of small, violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous base and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
