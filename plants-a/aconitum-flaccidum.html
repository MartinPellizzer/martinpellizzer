Aconitum flaccidum

Aconitum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive veined pattern, and its roots are toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Aconitum flaccidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
