Aristida gypsophila f. diffusa

Aristida gypsophila f. diffusa, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
