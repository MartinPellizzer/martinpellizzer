Amorphophallus chevalieri

Amorphophallus chevalieri, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces large, arrow-shaped tubers underground, which are used as a starchy food source. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable in traditional agriculture.
