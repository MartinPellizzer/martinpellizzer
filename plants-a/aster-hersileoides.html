Aster hersileoides

Aster hersileoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Aster hersileoides is also valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
