Amorpha discolor

Amorpha discolor, commonly known as the yellow indigo bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Amorpha discolor is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
