Allomorphia hispida

Allomorphia hispida, commonly known as the rough allomorph, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Allomorphia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
