Acacia foliolosa

Acacia foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in compact spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

Acacia foliolosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
