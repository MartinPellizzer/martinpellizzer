Arenaria ciliata subsp. moehringioides

Arenaria ciliata subsp.

moehringioides, commonly known as the moehringioides sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal and coastal-facing gardens.
