Agrostis eriantha var. planifolia

Agrostis eriantha var. planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, two-lobed glumes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
