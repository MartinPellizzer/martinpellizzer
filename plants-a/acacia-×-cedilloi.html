Acacia × cedilloi

Acacia × cedilloi, commonly known as the hybrid acacia, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Acacia angulata and Acacia longifolia.

It is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Australia. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to spines, and it produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters. It is well adapted to sandy soils and coastal environments, showing tolerance to salt spray and wind.

The hybrid is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Native Distribution

Acacia × cedilloi is primarily native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia, particularly in the Northern Territory and Queensland.

This hybrid species is commonly found in sandy soils along coastal areas and in the mulga woodlands. It thrives in environments with low rainfall and high temperatures, often growing in open habitats. The plant is well adapted to the harsh conditions of the arid and semi-arid zones of its native range.

Its distribution is closely associated with areas where its parent species, Acacia aneura and Acacia retinodes, naturally occur.

Acacia × cedilloi was mainly introduced to South Africa, where it has become naturalized in certain regions.
