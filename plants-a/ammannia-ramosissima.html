Ammannia ramosissima

Ammannia ramosissima, commonly known as prairie willow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia ramosissima is known for its ability to thrive in moist soil conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
