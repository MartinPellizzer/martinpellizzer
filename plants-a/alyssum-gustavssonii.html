Alyssum gustavssonii

Alyssum gustavssonii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill alyssum, is a flowering plant native to California and southern Oregon.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alyssum gustavssonii is often found in open habitats like chaparral and grasslands.
