Asplenium paucifolium

Asplenium paucifolium, commonly known as the few-leaved spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and possesses a limited number of small, lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found on rocks or in crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a delicate, wiry texture.

Asplenium paucifolium is a relatively uncommon species, often requiring specific microclimatic conditions to thrive.
