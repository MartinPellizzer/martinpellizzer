Astragalus deserti

Astragalus deserti, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Astragalus deserti is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
