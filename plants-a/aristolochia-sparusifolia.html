Aristolochia sparusifolia

Aristolochia sparusifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, green leaves with prominent veins and its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex floral structures and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
