Alstroemeria nemorosa

Alstroemeria nemorosa, commonly known as the forest lily, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, often brightly colored leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest areas.

Alstroemeria nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
