Astragalus chaetolobus

Astragalus chaetolobus, commonly known as the hairy lentil, is a species of legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, hairy leaflets that help reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
