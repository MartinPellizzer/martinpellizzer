Amomum kwangsiense

Amomum kwangsiense, commonly known as Guangxi cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a strong, spicy aroma.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in compact spikes, and bloom during the summer months.
