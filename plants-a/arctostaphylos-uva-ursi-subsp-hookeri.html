Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp. hookeri

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a whitish waxy coating.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by red berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
