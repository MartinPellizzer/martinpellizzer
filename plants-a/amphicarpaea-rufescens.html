Amphicarpaea rufescens

Amphicarpaea rufescens, commonly known as redstem false flax, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce both free-standing and root-derived fruits, a trait known as amphicarpy. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
