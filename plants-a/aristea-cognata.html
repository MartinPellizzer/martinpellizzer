Aristea cognata

Aristea cognata, commonly known as the narrowleaf aristea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
