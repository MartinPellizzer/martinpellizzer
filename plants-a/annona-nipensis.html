Annona nipensis

Annona nipensis, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. Its flowers are typically white or greenish-white, with a distinctive five-lobed structure.

The fruit is a large, spiky, green or yellowish berry that ripens to a creamy yellow color.
