Arcytophyllum juniperifolium

Arcytophyllum juniperifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

The species plays a significant role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
