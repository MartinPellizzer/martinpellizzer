Acacia acuminata

Acacia acuminata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spherical heads, which are typical of many Acacia species. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Acacia acuminata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
