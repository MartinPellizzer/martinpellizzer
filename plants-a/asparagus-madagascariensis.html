Asparagus madagascariensis

Asparagus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar asparagus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Asparagus madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
