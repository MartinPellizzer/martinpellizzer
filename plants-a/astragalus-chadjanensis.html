Astragalus chadjanensis

Astragalus chadjanensis, commonly known as Chadjan's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus chadjanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
