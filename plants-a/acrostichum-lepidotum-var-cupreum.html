Acrostichum lepidotum var. cupreum

Acrostichum lepidotum var. cupreum, commonly known as the copper fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf holly fern.

It is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features large, leathery fronds with a distinctive copper-colored hue on the underside. Its reproductive structures are borne on separate plants, a characteristic of ferns.

The fronds are divided into broad, lance-shaped segments, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
