Anthocercis intricata

Anthocercis intricata, commonly known as the intricate anthocercis, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a corkscrew, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet, often clustered in dense, twisting arrangements. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique morphology and limited habitat, it is considered a specialty plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
