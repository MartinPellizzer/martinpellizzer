Atylus linearis

Atylus linearis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, trumpet-shaped petals.

Atylus linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
