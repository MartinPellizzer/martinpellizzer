Astragalus pamiricus

Astragalus pamiricus, commonly known as the Pamir milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
