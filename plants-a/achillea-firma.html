Achillea firma

Achillea firma, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.

Its drought tolerance makes it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and low-maintenance landscapes.
