Aster jemensis

Aster jemensis, commonly known as the Korean aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in the summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster jemensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
