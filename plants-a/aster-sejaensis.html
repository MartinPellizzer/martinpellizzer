Aster sejaensis

Aster sejaensis, commonly known as the Korean aster, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its showy purple or white flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster sejaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
