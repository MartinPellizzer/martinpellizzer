Ascalea lanceolata

Ascalea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf arum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

Ascalea lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types in suitable climates.
