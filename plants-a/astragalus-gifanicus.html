Astragalus gifanicus

Astragalus gifanicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Astragalus gifanicus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
