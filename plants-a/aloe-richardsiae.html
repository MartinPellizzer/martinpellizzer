Aloe richardsiae

Aloe richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional medicine and skincare products.

Aloe richardsiae is well adapted to drought and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
