Aira elegantissima subsp. ambigua

Aira elegantissima subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
