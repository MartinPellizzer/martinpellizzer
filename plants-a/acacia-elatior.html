Acacia elatior

Acacia elatior, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Acacia elatior is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important species in soil enrichment.
