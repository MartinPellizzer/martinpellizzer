Abies picea var. leioclada

Abies picea var. leioclada, commonly known as the white fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that is often covered with a waxy coating. The needles are typically shorter and more rigid compared to other fir species, and they emit a strong, resinous fragrance. The cones are small, cylindrical, and often remain attached to the branches for years.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.
