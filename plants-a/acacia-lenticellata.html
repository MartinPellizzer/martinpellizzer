Acacia lenticellata

Acacia lenticellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.

Acacia lenticellata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
