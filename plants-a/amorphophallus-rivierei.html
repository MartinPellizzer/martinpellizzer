Amorphophallus rivierei

Amorphophallus rivierei, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a thick, underground stem called a tuber. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped flowers that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for its starchy tubers.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
