Aspalathus aciloba

Aspalathus aciloba, commonly known as the small-leaved bushveld pea, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and bushveld.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
