Acrothamnus melaleucoides

Acrothamnus melaleucoides, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often found on sandy soils and cliffs. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
