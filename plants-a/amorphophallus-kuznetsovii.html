Amorphophallus kuznetsovii

Amorphophallus kuznetsovii, commonly known as the giant water chestnut, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tuberous underground stems that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with a single spadix surrounded by a large, purple spathe. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas near water bodies.

The tubers are edible but can be toxic if not prepared properly.
