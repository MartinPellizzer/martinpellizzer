Anabasis tatarica

Anabasis tatarica, commonly known as goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, while its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Anabasis tatarica is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
