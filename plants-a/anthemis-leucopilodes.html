Anthemis leucopilodes

Anthemis leucopilodes, commonly known as the white-rayed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white ray flowers surrounding a central disk, giving it a delicate, daisy-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for dried flower arrangements.
