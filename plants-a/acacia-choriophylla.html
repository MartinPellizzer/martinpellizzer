Acacia choriophylla

Acacia choriophylla, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia choriophylla is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
