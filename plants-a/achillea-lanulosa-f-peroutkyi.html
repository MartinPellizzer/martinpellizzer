Achillea lanulosa f. peroutkyi

Achillea lanulosa f. peroutkyi, commonly known as the Peroutka's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
