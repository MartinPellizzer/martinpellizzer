Ardisia crasseovata

Ardisia crasseovata, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen or the Chinese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is known for its ornamental value, particularly due to its showy, dark purple berries that appear in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ardisia crasseovata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
