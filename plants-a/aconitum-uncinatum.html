Aconitum uncinatum

Aconitum uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be dangerous to humans and animals.
