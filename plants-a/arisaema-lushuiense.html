Arisaema lushuiense

Arisaema lushuiense, commonly known as the Lushui arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, native to Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the genus Arisaema. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring, and it produces berries that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
