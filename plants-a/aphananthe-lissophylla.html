Aphananthe lissophylla

Aphananthe lissophylla, commonly known as the silky ash or silky aphananthe, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and seasonal flowering.
