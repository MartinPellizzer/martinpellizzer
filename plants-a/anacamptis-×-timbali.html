Anacamptis × timbali

Anacamptis × timbali, commonly known as the Timbali orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Anacamptis morio and Anacamptis pyramidalis.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of fragrant, violet-blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The Timbali orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
