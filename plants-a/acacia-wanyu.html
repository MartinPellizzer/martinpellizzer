Acacia wanyu

Acacia wanyu, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, consisting of numerous small leaflets.

Acacia wanyu is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for various wildlife.
