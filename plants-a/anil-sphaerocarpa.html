Anil sphaerocarpa

Anil sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe fruit, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its unique, spherical fruits.

The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture. The fruit, which is typically green when unripe, turns yellow or orange when mature and is edible.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
