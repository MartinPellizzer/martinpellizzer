Aster taxifolius

Aster taxifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

Aster taxifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
