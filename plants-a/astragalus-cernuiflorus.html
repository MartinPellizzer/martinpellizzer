Astragalus cernuiflorus

Astragalus cernuiflorus, commonly known as the nodding milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that hang downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus cernuiflorus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
