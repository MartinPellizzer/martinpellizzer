Acropogon jaffrei

Acropogon jaffrei, commonly known as the Jaffrei's grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps in distinguishing it from other grass species.

Acropogon jaffrei is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as savannas and along roadsides.
