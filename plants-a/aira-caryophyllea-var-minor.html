Aira caryophyllea var. minor

Aira caryophyllea var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf purple grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, silver-gray leaves and produces delicate, purple-pink flower spikes in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
