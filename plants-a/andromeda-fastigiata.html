Andromeda fastigiata

Andromeda fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in erect racemes, blooming in late winter or early spring.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.
