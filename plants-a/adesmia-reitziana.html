Adesmia reitziana

Adesmia reitziana, commonly known as the Ritter's burr, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a hairy, woody pod that contains several seeds, and it often grows in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid regions.
