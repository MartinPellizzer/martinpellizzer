Adenophora onoi

Adenophora onoi, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Adenophora onoi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
