Astragalus decumbens var. convallarius

Astragalus decumbens var. convallarius, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets that are often hairy.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
