Acacia dallachiana

Acacia dallachiana, commonly known as the desert wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, allowing it to reduce water loss in harsh environments. Acacia dallachiana is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.

Its nectar-rich flowers also serve as an important food source for native pollinators.
