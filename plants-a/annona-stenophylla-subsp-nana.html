Annona stenophylla subsp. nana

Annona stenophylla subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf custard apple, is a small, shrub-like plant native to tropical regions of South America. It produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature.

The plant has smooth, glossy leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, creamy pulp that is often used in local cuisines.
