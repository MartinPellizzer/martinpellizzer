Aeonium leucoblepharum f. glandulosum

Aeonium leucoblepharum f. glandulosum, commonly known as the Woolly Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure composed of thick, fleshy, and often woolly leaves. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive powdery coating that gives the plant its common name. It produces striking yellow to orange flowers in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
