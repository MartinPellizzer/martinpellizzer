Acacia moirii subsp. dasycarpa

Acacia moirii subsp.

dasycarpa, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in winter.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust and hairy growth compared to other acacia species.
