Ageratum scorpioideum

Ageratum scorpioideum, commonly known as the scorpionweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
