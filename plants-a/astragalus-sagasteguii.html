Astragalus sagasteguii

Astragalus sagasteguii, commonly known as the Sagastegui milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its small, compound leaves and unique seed pods are key botanical features that aid in its survival in its native habitat.
