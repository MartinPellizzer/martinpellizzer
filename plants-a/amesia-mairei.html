Amesia mairei

Amesia mairei, commonly known as Maire's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest undergrowth. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Amesia mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
