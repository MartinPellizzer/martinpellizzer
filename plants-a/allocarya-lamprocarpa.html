Allocarya lamprocarpa

Allocarya lamprocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that reflect light and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and grasslands.

Its unique foliage and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
