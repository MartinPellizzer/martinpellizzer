Anthurium pulidoae

Anthurium pulidoae, commonly known as the Pulido's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the lowland rainforests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and vibrant green coloration. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with the showy spathe serving as an attractant for pollinators.

Anthurium pulidoae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in humid, indirect light conditions.
