Astragalus rawianus

Astragalus rawianus, commonly known as the Rawian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus rawianus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
