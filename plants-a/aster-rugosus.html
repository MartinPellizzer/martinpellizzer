Aster rugosus

Aster rugosus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and conical, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
