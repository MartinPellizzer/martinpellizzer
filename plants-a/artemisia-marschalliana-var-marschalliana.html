Artemisia marschalliana var. marschalliana

Artemisia marschalliana var. marschalliana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This species is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
