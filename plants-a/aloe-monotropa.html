Aloe monotropa

Aloe monotropa, commonly known as ghost plant or Indian tobacco, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale green, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Aloe monotropa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
