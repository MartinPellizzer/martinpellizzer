Amomum utriculosum

Amomum utriculosum, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or wild ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces erect, branched stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Amomum utriculosum is native to Southeast Asia and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
