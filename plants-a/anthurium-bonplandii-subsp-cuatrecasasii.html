Anthurium bonplandii subsp. cuatrecasasii

Anthurium bonplandii subsp.

cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of Colombia. It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces vibrant red spathes that enclose bright yellow spadices, creating a dramatic floral display. It thrives in warm, humid environments with high light exposure and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
