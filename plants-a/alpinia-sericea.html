Alpinia sericea

Alpinia sericea, commonly known as the silver ginger or velvet ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomatous stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Alpinia sericea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, preferring warm, humid climates.
