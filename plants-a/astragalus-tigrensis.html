Astragalus tigrensis

Astragalus tigrensis, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus tigrensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across suitable habitats.
