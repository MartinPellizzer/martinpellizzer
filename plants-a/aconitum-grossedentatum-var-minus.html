Aconitum grossedentatum var. minus

Aconitum grossedentatum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser toothed monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its root system is tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in home gardens.
