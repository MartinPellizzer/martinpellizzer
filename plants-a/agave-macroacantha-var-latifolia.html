Agave macroacantha var. latifolia

Agave macroacantha var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are broad and often have a bluish-green hue with white to yellowish marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white to greenish flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
