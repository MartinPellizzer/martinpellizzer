Albuca melleri

Albuca melleri, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that resemble tulips, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca melleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
