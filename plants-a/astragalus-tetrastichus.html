Astragalus tetrastichus

Astragalus tetrastichus, commonly known as the four-stem milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several small, oval seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus tetrastichus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ecological resilience.
