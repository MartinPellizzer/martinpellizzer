Amerina ternifolia

Amerina ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved amerina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Amerina ternifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
