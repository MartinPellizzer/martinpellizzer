Adonis autumnalis subsp. carinata

Adonis autumnalis subsp.

carinata, commonly known as the autumn adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, glaucous leaves and produces striking yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn.

The flowers are typically arranged in a loose raceme and have five petals with prominent yellow anthers. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, and is often found in open habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
