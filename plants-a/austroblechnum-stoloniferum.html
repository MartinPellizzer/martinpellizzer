Austroblechnum stoloniferum

Austroblechnum stoloniferum, commonly known as the stoloniferous water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that float on the water surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas through stolons.

Austroblechnum stoloniferum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
