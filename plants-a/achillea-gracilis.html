Achillea gracilis

Achillea gracilis, commonly known as slender yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Achillea gracilis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
