Astragalus schirasicus

Astragalus schirasicus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine ecosystems.
