Ageratum littorale var. hondurense

Ageratum littorale var. hondurense, commonly known as the Honduran sea ageratum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in coastal dunes and estuarine environments. Its foliage is typically silver or gray-green, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils through its extensive root system.
