Aspidium abbreviatum

Aspidium abbreviatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in water or in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its spores are released from sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Aspidium abbreviatum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and aiding in water filtration.
