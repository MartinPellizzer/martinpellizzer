Arctostaphylos canescens subsp. malloryi

Arctostaphylos canescens subsp.

malloryi, commonly known as the Mallory's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Ericaceae family.
