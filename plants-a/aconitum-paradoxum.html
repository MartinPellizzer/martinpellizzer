Aconitum paradoxum

Aconitum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive veined pattern, and its roots are toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids. Aconitum paradoxum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
