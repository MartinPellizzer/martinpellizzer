Aerides huttonii

Aerides huttonii, commonly known as the Hutton's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces inflorescences with several flowers, each featuring three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more showy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Aerides huttonii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively ease of cultivation.
