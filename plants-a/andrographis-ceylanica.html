Andrographis ceylanica

Andrographis ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon Andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Sri Lanka and southern India.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its medicinal value is primarily derived from the bitter principles found in its leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Andrographis ceylanica is often cultivated for its therapeutic properties, particularly in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
