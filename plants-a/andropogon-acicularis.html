Andropogon acicularis

Andropogon acicularis, commonly known as slender grass or needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tufted growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color.

Andropogon acicularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
