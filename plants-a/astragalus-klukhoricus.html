Astragalus klukhoricus

Astragalus klukhoricus, commonly known as Klukhor's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
