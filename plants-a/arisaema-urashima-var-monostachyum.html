Arisaema urashima var. monostachyum

Arisaema urashima var. monostachyum, commonly known as the Japanese flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a tuberous root system and produces striking, orange-red spadices that are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The spathe is typically green with a red or orange inner surface, adding to its visual interest.
