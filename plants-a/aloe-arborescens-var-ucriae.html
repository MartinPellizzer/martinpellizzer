Aloe arborescens var. ucriae

Aloe arborescens var. ucriae, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem with fleshy, dark green leaves that have a waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, resembling tulips, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
