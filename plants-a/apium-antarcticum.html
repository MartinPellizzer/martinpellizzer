Apium antarcticum

Apium antarcticum, commonly known as Antarctic celery, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and small, fern-like leaves. It produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Apium antarcticum is adapted to grow in harsh, alpine environments, often found in South America, Africa, and Antarctica.
