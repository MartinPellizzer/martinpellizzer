Acacia neurocarpa

Acacia neurocarpa, commonly known as the Natal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, clustered leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
