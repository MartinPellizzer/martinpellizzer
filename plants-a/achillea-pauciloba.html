Achillea pauciloba

Achillea pauciloba, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, achene-like fruits and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its growth habit is generally clump-forming, with upright, branching stems.
