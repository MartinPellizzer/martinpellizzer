Anchusa hybrida var. setigera

Anchusa hybrida var. setigera, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also hairy, contributing to its overall rugged look.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
