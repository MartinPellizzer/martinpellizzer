Aspidosperma discolor var. parvifolium

Aspidosperma discolor var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions.
