Aster parvifolius

Aster parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem.

It produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.
