Azalea fulva

Azalea fulva, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea fulva is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
