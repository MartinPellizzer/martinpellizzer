Aquilegia vulgaris var. minor

Aquilegia vulgaris var. minor, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and bright yellow anthers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces clusters of flowers in early spring.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
