Adesmia hispidula var. subnuda

Adesmia hispidula var. subnuda, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
