Amarella californica

Amarella californica, commonly known as the California amara, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Amarella californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
