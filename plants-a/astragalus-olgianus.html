Astragalus olgianus

Astragalus olgianus, commonly known as Olgian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is often deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus olgianus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
