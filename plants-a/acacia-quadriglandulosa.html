Acacia quadriglandulosa

Acacia quadriglandulosa, commonly known as the four-gland acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four glands on each leaflet, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular inflorescences, typically during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and often fissured, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Acacia quadriglandulosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
