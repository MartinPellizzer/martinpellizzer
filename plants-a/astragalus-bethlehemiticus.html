Astragalus bethlehemiticus

Astragalus bethlehemiticus, commonly known as the Holy Land milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
