Aristolochia elevata

Aristolochia elevata, commonly known as the greater Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, with a strong, musty odor. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, winding growth habit.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should not be ingested.
