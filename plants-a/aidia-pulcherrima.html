Aidia pulcherrima

Aidia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
