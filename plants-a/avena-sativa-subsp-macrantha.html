Avena sativa subsp. macrantha

Avena sativa subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the tall oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces panicles of small, two-lipped flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
