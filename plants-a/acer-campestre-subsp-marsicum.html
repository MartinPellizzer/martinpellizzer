Acer campestre subsp. marsicum

Acer campestre subsp.

marsicum, commonly known as the Marsic maple, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are typically green on top and pale underneath, turning vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
