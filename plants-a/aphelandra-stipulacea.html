Aphelandra stipulacea

Aphelandra stipulacea, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright yellow bracts. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in both indoor and outdoor settings. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Aphelandra stipulacea is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually appealing, easy-to-care-for plant.
