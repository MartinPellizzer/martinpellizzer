Alchemilla minor f. typica

Alchemilla minor f. typica, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are shield-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is known for its ability to retain moisture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
