Aulomyrcia polymorpha

Aulomyrcia polymorpha, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It has compound leaves with three to seven elliptical leaflets that are opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Aulomyrcia polymorpha is notable for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its historical use in traditional medicine.
