Anemone pulsatilla var. australis

Anemone pulsatilla var. australis, commonly known as the Southern Pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
