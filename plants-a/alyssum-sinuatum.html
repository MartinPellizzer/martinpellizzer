Alyssum sinuatum

Alyssum sinuatum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features dense clusters of flowers atop slender stems, and its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum sinuatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
