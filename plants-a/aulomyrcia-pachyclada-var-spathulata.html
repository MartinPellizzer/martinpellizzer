Aulomyrcia pachyclada var. spathulata

Aulomyrcia pachyclada var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed aulomyrcia, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its robust, woody stems and broad, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study due to its unique morphology and adaptability.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
