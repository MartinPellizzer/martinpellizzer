Ageratina saxorum

Ageratina saxorum, commonly known as the rock ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, fluffy blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Ageratina saxorum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
