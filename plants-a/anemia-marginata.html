Anemia marginata

Anemia marginata, commonly known as the edge-leaf anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink petals and deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of elegance to the landscape.

Anemia marginata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in cooler climates.
