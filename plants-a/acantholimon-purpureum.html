Acantholimon purpureum

Acantholimon purpureum, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vivid purple or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
