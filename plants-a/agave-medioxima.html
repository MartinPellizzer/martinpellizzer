Agave medioxima

Agave medioxima, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and covered in a blue-gray waxy coating. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy leaves.

Agave medioxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
