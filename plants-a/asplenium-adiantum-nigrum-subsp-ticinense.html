Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp. ticinense

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum subsp.

ticinense, commonly known as the Ticinese water fern, is a fern species native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance.

The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streams. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
