Amaryllis laticoma

Amaryllis laticoma, commonly known as the broadleaf amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a dramatic touch to landscapes or containers.
