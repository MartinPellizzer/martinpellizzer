Ammi capillaceum

Ammi capillaceum, commonly known as bishop's weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and feather-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Bishop's weed is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
