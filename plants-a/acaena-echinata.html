Acaena echinata

Acaena echinata, commonly known as the bristly acaena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny, thorn-like stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its hairy foliage and robust structure make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Acaena echinata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
