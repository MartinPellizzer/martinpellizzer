Aristea alata

Aristea alata, commonly known as the silver carpet plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white to silver hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
