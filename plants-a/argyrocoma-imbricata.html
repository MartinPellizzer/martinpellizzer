Argyrocoma imbricata

Argyrocoma imbricata, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that give it its distinctive appearance.
