Arctium zalewskii

Arctium zalewskii, commonly known as the Polish burdock, is a perennial herb native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, hairy foliage. The plant features a thick, swollen root that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough, serrated edge, and it produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal.

Arctium zalewskii is typically found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
