Aristolochia colimensis

Aristolochia colimensis, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, hollow, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish or reddish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated, though caution is advised due to its potential toxicity.
