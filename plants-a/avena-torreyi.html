Avena torreyi

Avena torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Avena torreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
