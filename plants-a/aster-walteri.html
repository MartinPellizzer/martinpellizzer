Aster walteri

Aster walteri, commonly known as Walter's aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Walter's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
