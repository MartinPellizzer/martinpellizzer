Allium hyalinum

Allium hyalinum, commonly known as the hyacinth bean or white onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant's bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Allium hyalinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
