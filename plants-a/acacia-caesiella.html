Acacia caesiella

Acacia caesiella, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a hardy species in semi-arid environments.

Acacia caesiella is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in dry climates.
