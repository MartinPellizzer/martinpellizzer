Azalea elliptica

Azalea elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or red blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Azalea elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
