Acer hyrcanum f. intermedium

Acer hyrcanum f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate maple, is a unique variant of the Hyrcanian maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green and variegated colors. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, adding visual interest to the landscape.

While not as widely cultivated as other maple species, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability.
