Acacia hayesii

Acacia hayesii, commonly known as the Hayes acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.

Acacia hayesii is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in stabilizing sandy soils.
