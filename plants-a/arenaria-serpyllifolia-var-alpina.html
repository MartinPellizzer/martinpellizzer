Arenaria serpyllifolia var. alpina

Arenaria serpyllifolia var. alpina, commonly known as alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine meadows and tundra habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth form and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
