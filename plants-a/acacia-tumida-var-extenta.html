Acacia tumida var. extenta

Acacia tumida var. extenta, commonly known as the broad-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
