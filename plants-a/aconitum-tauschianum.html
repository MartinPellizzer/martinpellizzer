Aconitum tauschianum

Aconitum tauschianum, commonly known as the Tausch's aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Aconitum tauschianum is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.
