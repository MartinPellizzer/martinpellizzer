Anthurium platylobum

Anthurium platylobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking spadices, which are often red or orange, surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium platylobum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
