Acacia grandiflora

Acacia grandiflora, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Acacia grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.
