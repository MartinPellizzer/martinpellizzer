Astephanus marginatus

Astephanus marginatus, commonly known as the Cape marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a low, spreading habit with silver-gray foliage. Its flowers are white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive yellow center, and bloom from spring to summer.

Astephanus marginatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
