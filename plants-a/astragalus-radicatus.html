Astragalus radicatus

Astragalus radicatus, commonly known as the northern milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus radicatus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
