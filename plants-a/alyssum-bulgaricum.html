Alyssum bulgaricum

Alyssum bulgaricum, commonly known as Bulgarian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum bulgaricum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
