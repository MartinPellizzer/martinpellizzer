Alyssum minutiflorum

Alyssum minutiflorum, commonly known as dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of tiny, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum minutiflorum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
