Andropogon sanguineus var. oligostachyus

Andropogon sanguineus var. oligostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bluestem, is a grass species native to parts of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
