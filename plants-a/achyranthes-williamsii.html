Achyranthes williamsii

Achyranthes williamsii, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Achyranthes williamsii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
