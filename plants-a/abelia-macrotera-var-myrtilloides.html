Abelia macrotera var. myrtilloides

Abelia macrotera var. myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple abelia, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, upright growth with oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
