Alsophila bellisquamata

Alsophila bellisquamata, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are pinnately compound, with a smooth, glossy surface and a golden hue when young. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
