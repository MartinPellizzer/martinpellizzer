Abolboda neblinae

Abolboda neblinae, commonly known as the misty fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude cloud forests of the Andes.

It thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of mist, which is essential for its growth. The plant features narrow, feather-like fronds that are adapted to capture moisture from the air. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many fern species.

Due to habitat destruction, Abolboda neblinae is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
