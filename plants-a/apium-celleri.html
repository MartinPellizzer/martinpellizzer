Apium celleri

Apium celleri, commonly known as the giant parsley or celery fern, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and dill. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fern-like leaves that are finely divided. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

Apium celleri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and subtle fragrance.
