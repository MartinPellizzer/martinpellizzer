Acacia decurrens var. lanigera

Acacia decurrens var. lanigera, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely haired, trifoliate leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are often followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
