Aloe chimanimaniensis

Aloe chimanimaniensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often covered in a whitish bloom, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Aloe chimanimaniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
