Alchemilla delitescens

Alchemilla delitescens, commonly known as the shining lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla delitescens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
