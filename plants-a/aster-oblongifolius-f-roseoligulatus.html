Aster oblongifolius f. roseoligulatus

Aster oblongifolius f. roseoligulatus, commonly known as the rose-ligulate aster, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers consist of two types of florets—ray florets and disc florets—arranged in a composite head.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
