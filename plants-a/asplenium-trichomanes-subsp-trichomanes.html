Asplenium trichomanes subsp. trichomanes

Asplenium trichomanes subsp.

trichomanes, commonly known as the maidenhair spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae. It is characterized by its delicate, glossy, and finely divided fronds that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in both horticulture and botanical studies.
