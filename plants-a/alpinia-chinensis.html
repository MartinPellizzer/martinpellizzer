Alpinia chinensis

Alpinia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and are borne on long, slender stalks.

The rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
