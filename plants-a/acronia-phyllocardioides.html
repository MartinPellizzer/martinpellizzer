Acronia phyllocardioides

Acronia phyllocardioides, commonly known as the heart-shaped fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features fronds that are heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many fern species.

Acronia phyllocardioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
