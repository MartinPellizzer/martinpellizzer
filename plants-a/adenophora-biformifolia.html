Adenophora biformifolia

Adenophora biformifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Adenophora biformifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
