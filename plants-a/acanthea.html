Acanthea

Acanthea, commonly known as the "spiny acanthus," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and showy, often brightly colored flowers. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Acanthea species typically grow as shrubs or herbs, and they often thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Their ornamental value makes them popular in horticulture, though some species can be invasive in certain environments.
