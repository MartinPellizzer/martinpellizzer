Arisaema helleborifolium

Arisaema helleborifolium, commonly known as the helmet fern arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix, and it is typically found in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema helleborifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique floral structure.
