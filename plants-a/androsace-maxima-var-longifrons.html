Androsace maxima var. longifrons

Androsace maxima var. longifrons, commonly known as the long-fruited rock moss, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
