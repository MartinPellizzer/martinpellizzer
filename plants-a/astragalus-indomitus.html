Astragalus indomitus

Astragalus indomitus, commonly known as the Indomitable Milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Astragalus indomitus is often used in traditional medicine and is also of interest for its potential ecological roles in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
