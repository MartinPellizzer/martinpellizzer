Arctium × Pavlovii

Arctium × pavlovii is a fascinating hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species, Arctium lappa and Arctium tomentosum. It is known for its striking purple flowers and spiky seed heads, which make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in meadows. Its unique appearance and hardy nature have made it a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike. Despite its beauty, it can also be invasive in certain regions, requiring careful management.
