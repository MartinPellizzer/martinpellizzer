Aristolochia lozaniana

Aristolochia lozaniana, commonly known as the Lozanian Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a dark purple to brownish coloration. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
