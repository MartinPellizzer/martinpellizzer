Amaranthus hybridus subvar. genuinus

Amaranthus hybridus subvar. genuinus, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

It is commonly found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
