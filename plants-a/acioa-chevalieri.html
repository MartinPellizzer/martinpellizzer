Acioa chevalieri

Acioa chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
