Acacia oraria var. typica

Acacia oraria var. typica, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
