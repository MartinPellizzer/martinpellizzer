Agrostophyllum nidus-avis

Agrostophyllum nidus-avis, commonly known as the bird's nest plant, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that form a cup-like structure to collect rainwater. This water serves as a microhabitat for various small organisms, including insects and frogs.

Its striking appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
