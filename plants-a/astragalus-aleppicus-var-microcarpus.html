Astragalus aleppicus var. microcarpus

Astragalus aleppicus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
