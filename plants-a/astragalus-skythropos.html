Astragalus skythropos

Astragalus skythropos, commonly known as the Greek milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus skythropos is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
