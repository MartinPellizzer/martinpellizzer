Aliella helichrysoides

Aliella helichrysoides, commonly known as the goldenrods, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Aliella helichrysoides plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the late growing season.
