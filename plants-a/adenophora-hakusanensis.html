Adenophora hakusanensis

Adenophora hakusanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.
