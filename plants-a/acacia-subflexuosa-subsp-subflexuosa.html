Acacia subflexuosa subsp. subflexuosa

Acacia subflexuosa subsp.

subflexuosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It features slender, often twisted branches and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes.

The plant produces flat, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
