Amaranthus blitum var. nanus

Amaranthus blitum var. nanus, commonly known as dwarf amaranth, is a compact, annual plant native to the Americas.

It features small, triangular leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that range in color from red to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and modest blooms.
