Allium koreanum

Allium koreanum, commonly known as Korean garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Korea and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves and bulbs are edible, often used in Korean cuisine for their mild garlic flavor.

Allium koreanum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
