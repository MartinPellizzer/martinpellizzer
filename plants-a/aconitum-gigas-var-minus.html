Aconitum gigas var. minus

Aconitum gigas var. minus, commonly known as the lesser giant monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often grown in specialized gardens.
