Aulomyrcia fenzliana

Aulomyrcia fenzliana, commonly known as the Fenzli's myrcia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
