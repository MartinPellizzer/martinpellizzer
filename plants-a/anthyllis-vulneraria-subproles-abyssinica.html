Anthyllis vulneraria subproles abyssinica

Anthyllis vulneraria subproles abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian restharrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes, where it contributes to soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity.
