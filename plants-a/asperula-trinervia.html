Asperula trinervia

Asperula trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined woodbine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, often twining stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Asperula trinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
