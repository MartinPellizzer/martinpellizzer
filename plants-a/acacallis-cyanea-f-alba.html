Acacallis cyanea f. alba

Acacallis cyanea f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered acacallis, is a rare and striking botanical specimen native to southern Africa.

This variant of Acacallis cyanea is distinguished by its pale, almost white petals, which contrast sharply with the typical blue or violet hues of the species. It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Due to its limited distribution and unique floral characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
