Aralia chinensis var. dasyphylloides

Aralia chinensis var. dasyphylloides, commonly known as the Chinese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are usually palmately lobed, with a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant foliage and seasonal changes in appearance.
