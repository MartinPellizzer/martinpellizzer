Acacia mabellae

Acacia mabellae, commonly known as the Mabellae wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. Its flowers are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

The wood of Acacia mabellae is hard and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for tool-making.
