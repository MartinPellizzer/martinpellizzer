Adnaria rhododendron

Adnaria rhododendron, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its long blooming period in spring.

Adnaria rhododendron is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
