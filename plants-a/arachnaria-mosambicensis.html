Arachnaria mosambicensis

Arachnaria mosambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique spider plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of Mozambique and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its foliage is often adorned with delicate, spider-like structures, giving it its common name.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
