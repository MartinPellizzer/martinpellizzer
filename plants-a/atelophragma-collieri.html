Atelophragma collieri

Atelophragma collieri, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Atelophragma collieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
