Amphilophis hirtifolia

Amphilophis hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
