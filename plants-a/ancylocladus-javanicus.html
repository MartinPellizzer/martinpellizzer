Ancylocladus javanicus

Ancylocladus javanicus, commonly known as the Java yew, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant produces woody cones that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-red aril. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
