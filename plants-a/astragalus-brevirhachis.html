Astragalus brevirhachis

Astragalus brevirhachis, commonly known as the short-bract milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features a slender, branched stem with small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow to brownish, arranged in spikes, and are characteristic of the legume family.

Astragalus brevirhachis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
