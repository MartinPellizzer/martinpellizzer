Alyxia oliviformis f. fusiformis

Alyxia oliviformis f. fusiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf alyxia, is a variant of the species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
