Aster erigeroides var. schultesii

Aster erigeroides var. schultesii, commonly known as the Schultes' aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
