Adnaria octosperma

Adnaria octosperma, commonly known as the eight-seeded sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat succulent.

Adnaria octosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
