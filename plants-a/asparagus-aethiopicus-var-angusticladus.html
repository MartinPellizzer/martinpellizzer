Asparagus aethiopicus var. angusticladus

Asparagus aethiopicus var. angusticladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that produce small, inconspicuous white flowers in early summer. The plant's foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique botanical characteristics make it a desirable plant for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
