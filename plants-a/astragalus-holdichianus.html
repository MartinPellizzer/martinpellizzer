Astragalus holdichianus

Astragalus holdichianus, commonly known as the Holdich's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

Astragalus holdichianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
