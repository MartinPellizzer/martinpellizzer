Attalea pindobassu

Attalea pindobassu, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or pindobassu palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, each containing a single large seed. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material, while the trunk is valued for its durability.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
