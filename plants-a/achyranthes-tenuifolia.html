Achyranthes tenuifolia

Achyranthes tenuifolia, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with small, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The roots of Achyranthes tenuifolia are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
