Arctium schmalhausenii

Arctium schmalhausenii, commonly known as the Schmalhausen's burdock, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces spiky, bur-like flowers that are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Arctium schmalhausenii thrives in dry, open habitats and is known for its hardy nature.
