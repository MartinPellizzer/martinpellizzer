Aster tradescanti var. saxatilis

Aster tradescanti var. saxatilis, commonly known as the stonecrop aster, is a hardy perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to store water and thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
