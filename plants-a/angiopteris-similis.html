Angiopteris similis

Angiopteris similis, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive wavy margin.

Angiopteris similis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
