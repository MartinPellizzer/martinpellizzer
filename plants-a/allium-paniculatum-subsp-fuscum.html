Allium paniculatum subsp. fuscum

Allium paniculatum subsp.

fuscum, commonly known as the purple garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features grass-like basal leaves and produces dense, branched inflorescences of small, lavender to purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often grown for its ornamental value and culinary uses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
