Arcyphyllum erectum

Arcyphyllum erectum, commonly known as the tall mountain sage, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high elevations. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Arcyphyllum erectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
