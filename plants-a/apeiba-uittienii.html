Apeiba uittienii

Apeiba uittienii, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits that resemble apples. The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable food source.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
