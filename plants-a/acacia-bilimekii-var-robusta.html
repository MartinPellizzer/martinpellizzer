Acacia bilimekii var. robusta

Acacia bilimekii var. robusta, commonly known as the robust acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and elongated, flat pods that contain seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are followed by the development of woody pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
