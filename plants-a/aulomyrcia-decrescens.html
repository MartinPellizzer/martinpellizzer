Aulomyrcia decrescens

Aulomyrcia decrescens, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish-brown color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical berries.

Due to its high demand in the musical instrument industry, it is also considered an endangered species.
