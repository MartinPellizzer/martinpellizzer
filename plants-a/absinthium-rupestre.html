Absinthium rupestre

Absinthium rupestre, commonly known as rock wormwood, is a perennial herb native to rocky and arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dense clusters, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Absinthium rupestre is often found in limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
