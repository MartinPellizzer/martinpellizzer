Astragalus eriophyllus subsp. chrysophyllus

Astragalus eriophyllus subsp.

chrysophyllus, commonly known as the goldenleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It produces small, hairy pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
