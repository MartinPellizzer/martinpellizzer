Acer negundo f. guttatum

Acer negundo f. guttatum, commonly known as the Variegated Hackberry, is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are broadly ovate with a serrated margin and often display a striking variegation of white, yellow, and green. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different light conditions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
