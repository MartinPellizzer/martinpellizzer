Astragalus chrysostachys var. parisiensis

Astragalus chrysostachys var. parisiensis, commonly known as the golden beard vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
