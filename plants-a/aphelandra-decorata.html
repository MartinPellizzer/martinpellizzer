Aphelandra decorata

Aphelandra decorata, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the late spring to early summer. Aphelandra decorata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
