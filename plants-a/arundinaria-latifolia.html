Arundinaria latifolia

Arundinaria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often smooth and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
