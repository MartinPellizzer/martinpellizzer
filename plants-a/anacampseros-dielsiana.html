Anacampseros dielsiana

Anacampseros dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
