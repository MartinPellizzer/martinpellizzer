Alchornea perrieri

Alchornea perrieri, commonly known as the African mallow, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
