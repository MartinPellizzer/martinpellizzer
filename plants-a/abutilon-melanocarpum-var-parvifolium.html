Abutilon melanocarpum var. parvifolium

Abutilon melanocarpum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value is due to its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
