Aconitum ferdinandi-regis

Aconitum ferdinandi-regis, commonly known as the Ferdinand Regis monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like shape. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it both a potent and potentially dangerous species.
