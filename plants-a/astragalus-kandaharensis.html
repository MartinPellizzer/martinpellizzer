Astragalus kandaharensis

Astragalus kandaharensis, commonly known as Kandahar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Afghanistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
