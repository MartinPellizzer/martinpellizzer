Alyxia septangularis

Alyxia septangularis, commonly known as the square-stemmed alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive square-shaped stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring and early summer. Alyxia septangularis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
