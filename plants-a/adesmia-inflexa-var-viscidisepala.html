Adesmia inflexa var. viscidisepala

Adesmia inflexa var. viscidisepala, commonly known as the viscid-sepaled adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small shrub characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and sepals. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions of Argentina and Chile.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest in botanical studies due to its adaptability to harsh environments.
