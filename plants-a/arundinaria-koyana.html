Arundinaria koyana

Arundinaria koyana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Koyana bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria koyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
