Andropogon liebmannii subvar. raripilus

Andropogon liebmannii subvar. raripilus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the central and southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. This plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences that are pale green to bluish in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
