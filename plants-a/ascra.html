Ascra

Ascra, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. Ascra has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are adapted for insect pollination, and the plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
