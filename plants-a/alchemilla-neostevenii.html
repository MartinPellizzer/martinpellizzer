Alchemilla neostevenii

Alchemilla neostevenii, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
