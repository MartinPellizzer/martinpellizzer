Ampelopsis tweedieana

Ampelopsis tweedieana, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle vine, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its berries are typically orange or red when mature. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

Ampelopsis tweedieana is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
