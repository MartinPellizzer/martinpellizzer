Adiantum caudatum var. capillus-gorgonis

Adiantum caudatum var. capillus-gorgonis, commonly known as the "horse's tail fern," is a striking fern native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that resemble a horse's tail, hence its common name. The plant has a unique rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.
