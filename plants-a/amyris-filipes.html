Amyris filipes

Amyris filipes, commonly known as the Florida mahogany, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with smooth, dark gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by red, fleshy fruits. Amyris filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.

It also has historical significance in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic wood.
