Acanthospermum leptolobum

Acanthospermum leptolobum, commonly known as slender thornweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have glandular dots.

Acanthospermum leptolobum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
