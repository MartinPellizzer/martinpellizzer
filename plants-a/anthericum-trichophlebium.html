Anthericum trichophlebium

Anthericum trichophlebium, commonly known as the velvetleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a rosette of broad, dark green, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist, shaded habitats.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
