Agave bonnetiana

Agave bonnetiana, commonly known as the Bonnet's agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with sharp spines along the edges. The plant produces an inflorescence of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
