Amorphophallus julaihii

Amorphophallus julaihii, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant's flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a strong, unpleasant odor resembling rotting meat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
