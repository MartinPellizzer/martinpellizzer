Aulomyrcia polymorpha var. leucophloea

Aulomyrcia polymorpha var. leucophloea, commonly known as the white-flowered myrcia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
