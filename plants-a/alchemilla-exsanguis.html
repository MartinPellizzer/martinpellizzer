Alchemilla exsanguis

Alchemilla exsanguis, commonly known as the bloody alchemilla, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, silver-green leaves. The plant produces upright stems bearing small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla exsanguis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
