Arundinaria mannii

Arundinaria mannii, commonly known as the African bamboo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination. Its rhizomes are extensively used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Arundinaria mannii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
