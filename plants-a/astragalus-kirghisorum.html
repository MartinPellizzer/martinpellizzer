Astragalus kirghisorum

Astragalus kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods contain small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.
