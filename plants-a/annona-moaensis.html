Annona moaensis

Annona moaensis, commonly known as the moa apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The fruit's flesh is white and edible, though it has a mild, slightly acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its unique fruit.
