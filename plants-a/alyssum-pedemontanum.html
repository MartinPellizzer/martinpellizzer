Alyssum pedemontanum

Alyssum pedemontanum, commonly known as the Alpine Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Alyssum pedemontanum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
