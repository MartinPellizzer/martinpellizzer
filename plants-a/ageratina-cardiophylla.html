Ageratina cardiophylla

Ageratina cardiophylla, commonly known as the heartleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fluffy white or pale blue flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
