Allophylus sericeus

Allophylus sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Indian Ocean islands.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Allophylus sericeus is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
