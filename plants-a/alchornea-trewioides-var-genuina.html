Alchornea trewioides var. genuina

Alchornea trewioides var. genuina, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
