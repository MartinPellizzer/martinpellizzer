Adiantum aethiopicum var. glabrum

Adiantum aethiopicum var. glabrum, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that can form dense mats. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with each segment bearing small, lance-shaped pinnae.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
