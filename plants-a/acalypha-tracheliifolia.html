Acalypha tracheliifolia

Acalypha tracheliifolia, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has palmate leaves that are often hairy and can vary in size depending on the environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Acalypha tracheliifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
