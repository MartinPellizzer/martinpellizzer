Aeonium lancerottense

Aeonium lancerottense, commonly known as the Lanzarote Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking, tall flowering stalks that bear yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions.

Aeonium lancerottense is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
