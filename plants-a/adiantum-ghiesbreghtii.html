Adiantum ghiesbreghtii

Adiantum ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the wet tropical forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth, glossy texture, with a unique ability to curl up when dry.

Adiantum ghiesbreghtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
