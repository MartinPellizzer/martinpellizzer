Astracantha podperae

Astracantha podperae, commonly known as the Canary Islands dragon's head, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiny, spherical fruit that resembles a dragon's head. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
