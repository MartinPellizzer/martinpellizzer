Asplenium ortegae

Asplenium ortegae, commonly known as the Ortega's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces spores in clusters called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. Native to the humid forests of Central and South America, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Asplenium ortegae is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, showcasing its adaptability to epiphytic conditions.
