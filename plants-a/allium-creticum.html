Allium creticum

Allium creticum, commonly known as the Cretan leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of broad, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic-like aroma and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as onions and garlic.

Allium creticum is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional Mediterranean practices.
