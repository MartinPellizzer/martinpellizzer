Astragalus onobrychis subsp. skorpili

Astragalus onobrychis subsp.

skorpili, commonly known as the Greek velvet bean, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
