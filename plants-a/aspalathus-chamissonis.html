Aspalathus chamissonis

Aspalathus chamissonis, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Aspalathus chamissonis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
