Anthericum crinitum

Anthericum crinitum, commonly known as the curly fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its distinctive, curling fronds that give it its common name. The plant features a short rhizome with a cluster of spadices, which are modified leaves that bear the actual flowers. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Anthericum crinitum is also valued for its ability to purify water and support local biodiversity.
