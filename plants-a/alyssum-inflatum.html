Alyssum inflatum

Alyssum inflatum, commonly known as the inflated alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.
