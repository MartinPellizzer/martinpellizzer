Asclepias vestita subsp. parishii

Asclepias vestita subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Parish's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive horn-shaped seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

This species is an important host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing nectar and supporting its life cycle.
