Adiantum pubescens var. tripinnatum

Adiantum pubescens var. tripinnatum, commonly known as the Southern Maidenhair Fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, trifoliate fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to the southeastern United States. The plant features slender, arching fronds with a finely divided, feathery appearance, and its leaves are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant's rhizomes are creeping and produce new fronds from the base, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
