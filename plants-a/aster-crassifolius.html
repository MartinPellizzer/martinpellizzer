Aster crassifolius

Aster crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically thick and leathery. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Aster crassifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
