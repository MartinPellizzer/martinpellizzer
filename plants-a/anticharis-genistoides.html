Anticharis genistoides

Anticharis genistoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control in dry regions.
