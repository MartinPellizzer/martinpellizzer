Agave americana var. oaxacensis

Agave americana var. oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan agave, is a succulent plant native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white teeth along the edges. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
