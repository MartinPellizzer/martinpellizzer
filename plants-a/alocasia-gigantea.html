Alocasia gigantea

Alocasia gigantea, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, featuring a deep green color with a waxy texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. Alocasia gigantea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil to flourish.

Its impressive foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
