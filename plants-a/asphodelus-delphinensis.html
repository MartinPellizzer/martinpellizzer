Asphodelus delphinensis

Asphodelus delphinensis, commonly known as the sea asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asphodelaceae family and typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant produces tall, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.

Its historical use in ancient times included both medicinal and ritual purposes.
