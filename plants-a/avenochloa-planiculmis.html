Avenochloa planiculmis

Avenochloa planiculmis, commonly known as the flat-leaf avena grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny grains, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Avenochloa planiculmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
