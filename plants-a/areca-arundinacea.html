Areca arundinacea

Areca arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its roots are known for their high fiber content, which is used in the production of various industrial materials.

The bamboo palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
