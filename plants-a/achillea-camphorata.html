Achillea camphorata

Achillea camphorata, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust root system and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its name is derived from the Greek mythological figure Achilles, who was said to have used the plant for healing wounds.
