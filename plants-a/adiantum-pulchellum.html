Adiantum pulchellum

Adiantum pulchellum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silky-hairy fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its fronds are typically biennial, with the plant producing spore-bearing structures in the second year.

The botanical features of Adiantum pulchellum make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique texture and aesthetic appeal.
