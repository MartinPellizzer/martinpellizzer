Asperula purpurea subsp. apiculata

Asperula purpurea subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the purple flame azalea, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It features small, dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink, blooming in early spring.

The foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and woodland gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
