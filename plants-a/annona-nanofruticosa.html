Annona nanofruticosa

Annona nanofruticosa, commonly known as the little fruit tree or small tree of annona, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically round or oval, with a smooth, thin skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
