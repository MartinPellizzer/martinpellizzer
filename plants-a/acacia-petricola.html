Acacia petricola

Acacia petricola, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia petricola thrives in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or rocky soils.
