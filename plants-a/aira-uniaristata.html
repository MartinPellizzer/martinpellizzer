Aira uniaristata

Aira uniaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf air grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Botanically, it is classified under the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and cereal crops.
