Abutilon heterotrichum

Abutilon heterotrichum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant grows as an upright shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Abutilon heterotrichum is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
