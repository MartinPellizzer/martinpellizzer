Arundina celebica

Arundina celebica, commonly known as the Celebes bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters, with a smooth, dark green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in panicles, followed by fluffy, grayish-brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wetland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical regions.
