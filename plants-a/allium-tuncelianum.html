Allium tuncelianum

Allium tuncelianum, commonly known as the Tuncel's leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives, and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
