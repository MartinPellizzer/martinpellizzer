Acaena elongata var. gracilis

Acaena elongata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender burr plant, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Acaena. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
