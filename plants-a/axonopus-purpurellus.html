Axonopus purpurellus

Axonopus purpurellus, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, arching stems. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that can turn reddish-purple under stress or during certain seasons. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, pale green or white flowers.

This plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
