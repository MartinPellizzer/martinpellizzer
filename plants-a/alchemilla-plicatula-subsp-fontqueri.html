Alchemilla plicatula subsp. fontqueri

Alchemilla plicatula subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri ladies' mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
