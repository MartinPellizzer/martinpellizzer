Agave teopiscana

Agave teopiscana, commonly known as the Teopiscana agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, succulent leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Agave teopiscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
