Asarum blumei

Asarum blumei, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
