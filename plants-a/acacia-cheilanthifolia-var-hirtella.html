Acacia cheilanthifolia var. hirtella

Acacia cheilanthifolia var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its woody pods contain seeds that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
