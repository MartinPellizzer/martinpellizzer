Asphodelus gracilis

Asphodelus gracilis, commonly known as the slender asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall spikes of pale yellow or greenish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous stem system that aids in its survival in arid conditions.

Asphodelus gracilis is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
