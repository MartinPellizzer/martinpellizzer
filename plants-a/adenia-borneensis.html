Adenia borneensis

Adenia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean Adenia, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals and five petals, often in shades of red and yellow. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains multiple seeds.

Adenia borneensis is typically found in humid, lowland forests and thrives in warm, moist environments.
