Althenia filiformis subsp. orientalis

Althenia filiformis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern threadleaf althenia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, thread-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within the Althenia genus.
