Artemisia kulbadica

Artemisia kulbadica, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its essential oils contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
