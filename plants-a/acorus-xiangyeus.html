Acorus xiangyeus

Acorus xiangyeus, commonly known as the Xiangyeus sweet flag, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
