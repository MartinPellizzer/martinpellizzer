Amelanchier beugesiaca

Amelanchier beugesiaca, commonly known as the Beugesiaca serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring white to pink flowers in spring and edible berries in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters and has compound leaves with 5–9 leaflets. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often used in culinary applications or as a food source for wildlife.

Amelanchier beugesiaca is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
