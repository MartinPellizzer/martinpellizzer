Amphilophium gnaphalanthum subsp. rhynchocarpum

Amphilophium gnaphalanthum subsp.

rhynchocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed woollyweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fibrous roots allow it to thrive in well-drained soils, and it often forms dense mats that stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation strategies and role in local ecosystems.
