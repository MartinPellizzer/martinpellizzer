Arundinaria sadoensis var. infrapilosa

Arundinaria sadoensis var. infrapilosa, commonly known as the Sado bamboo, is a variant of the Sado bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are typically green to yellow-green in color. The plant grows in dense clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as along riverbanks or in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a slightly rough texture.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique growth habit and aesthetic qualities.
