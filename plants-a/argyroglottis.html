Argyroglottis

Argyroglottis, commonly known as the silver-leafed orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often metallic-sheened leaves that give them their common name. They are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Argyroglottis species typically produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

Their unique foliage and ornamental value make them popular among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
