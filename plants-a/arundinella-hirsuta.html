Arundinella hirsuta

Arundinella hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Arundinella hirsuta is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.
