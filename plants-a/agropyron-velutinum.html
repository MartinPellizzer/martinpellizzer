Agropyron velutinum

Agropyron velutinum, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky-haired leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Agropyron velutinum is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
