Achnatherum soboliferum

Achnatherum soboliferum, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Achnatherum soboliferum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
