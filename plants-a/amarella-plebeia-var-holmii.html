Amarella plebeia var. holmii

Amarella plebeia var. holmii, commonly known as the Holm's amarella, is a variant of the amarella plant characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant has small, elongated leaves with a smooth texture and a pale green coloration. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
