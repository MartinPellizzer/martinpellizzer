Alchemilla hoppeana var. pallens

Alchemilla hoppeana var. pallens, commonly known as the silverleaf alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a dense mat of silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
