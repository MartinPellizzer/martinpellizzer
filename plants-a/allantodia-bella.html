Allantodia bella

Allantodia bella, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a star-like pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Allantodia bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
