Aralia indonesica

Aralia indonesica, commonly known as the Indonesian aralia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are usually large, palmately lobed, and can reach several feet in diameter.

Aralia indonesica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
