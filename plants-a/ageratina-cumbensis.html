Ageratina cumbensis

Ageratina cumbensis, commonly known as the Andean blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Ageratina cumbensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
