Acalypha schiedeana f. angustifolia

Acalypha schiedeana f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved flameflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble flames, which are clustered in terminal spikes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is popular in gardens and landscapes.
