Abies hickelii var. hickelii

Abies hickelii var. hickelii, commonly known as the Hickel's fir, is a variant of the fir tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the shoots. The cones are typically pendulous and contain small, winged seeds. This species thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of montane forest ecosystems.
