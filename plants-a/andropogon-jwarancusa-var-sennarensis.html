Andropogon jwarancusa var. sennarensis

Andropogon jwarancusa var. sennarensis, commonly known as the Senner's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a potential bioenergy crop.
