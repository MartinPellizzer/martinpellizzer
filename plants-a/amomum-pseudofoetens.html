Amomum pseudofoetens

Amomum pseudofoetens, commonly known as the black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit, which resembles a capsule, contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cooking.

The plant's unique scent and medicinal properties make it a valuable species in both botanical and culinary contexts.
