Aconitum funckii

Aconitum funckii, commonly known as the Funck's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it highly poisonous to both humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum funckii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
