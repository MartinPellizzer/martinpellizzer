Aspidium braunianum

Aspidium braunianum, commonly known as the broad buckler fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, triangular to ovate fronds that are typically divided into segments resembling a buckler or shield. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Aspidium braunianum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest understories.
