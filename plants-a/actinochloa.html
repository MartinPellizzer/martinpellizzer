Actinochloa

Actinochloa, commonly known as the star grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib. Actinochloa species are often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in areas with abundant water.
