Ascidieria caricifolia var. glabra

Ascidieria caricifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth willow-leaf mallow, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, willow-like leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
