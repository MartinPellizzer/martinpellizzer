Asclepias incarnata f. rosea

Asclepias incarnata f. rosea, commonly known as the Rose Milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its distinctive floral structure includes a crown of hooked spines surrounding a central cluster of seeds, typical of the milkweed genus.
