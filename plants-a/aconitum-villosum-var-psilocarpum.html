Aconitum villosum var. psilocarpum

Aconitum villosum var. psilocarpum, commonly known as the silky monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
