Asphodelus albus var. deseglisei

Asphodelus albus var. deseglisei, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flower stalks with clusters of white, tubular flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
