Acacia cuthbertsonii subsp. linearis

Acacia cuthbertsonii subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the linear acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, linear leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
