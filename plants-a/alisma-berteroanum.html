Alisma berteroanum

Alisma berteroanum, commonly known as the Bertero's water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family members. It thrives in shallow water and muddy soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its aquatic nature, it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
