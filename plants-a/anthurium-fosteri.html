Anthurium fosteri

Anthurium fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's flamingo flower, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a unique structure called a spathe, which encloses the actual flowers, often in bright red or orange hues. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium fosteri is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
