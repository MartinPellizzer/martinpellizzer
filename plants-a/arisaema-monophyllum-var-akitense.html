Arisaema monophyllum var. akitense

Arisaema monophyllum var. akitense, commonly known as the Akitan mountain arum, is a unique variant of the arum family native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological niche.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
