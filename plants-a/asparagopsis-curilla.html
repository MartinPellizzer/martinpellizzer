Asparagopsis curilla

Asparagopsis curilla, commonly known as the red seaweed or *Asparagopsis* species, is a marine algae belonging to the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its bright red coloration and branched thallus structure, which consists of both erect and horizontal axes. This plant thrives in shallow, warm coastal waters and is often found attached to rocky substrates. It plays a significant role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in biotechnology and as a source of bioactive compounds.
