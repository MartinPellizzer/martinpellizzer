Ampelopsis Henryana

Ampelopsis henryana is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia known for its striking foliage and ornamental value. It features heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a popular choice for autumn landscapes. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, followed by bright red berries that attract birds and add visual interest. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adaptable to a variety of soil types. Despite its beauty, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
