Adenanthera intermedia

Adenanthera intermedia, commonly known as the African burr or yellow burr, is a shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruit pods that contain seeds with a hard, angular coat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
