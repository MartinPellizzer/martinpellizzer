Astragalus brevipilus

Astragalus brevipilus, commonly known as the short-hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus brevipilus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
