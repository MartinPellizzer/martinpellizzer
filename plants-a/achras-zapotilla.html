Achras zapotilla

Achras zapotilla, commonly known as the Mexican aloe or zapoteca aloe, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-resistant and suitable for xeriscaping.
