Angelica turcica

Angelica turcica, commonly known as Turkish angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, compound leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica turcica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
