Arenaria ligericina subsp. ligericina

Arenaria ligericina subsp.

ligericina, commonly known as the coastal sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal areas of southern Europe and North Africa. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and well-drained environments.

The plant typically has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It is often found in dune ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing coastal sands.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological characteristics compared to other Arenaria species.
