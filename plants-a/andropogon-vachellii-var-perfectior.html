Andropogon vachellii var. perfectior

Andropogon vachellii var. perfectior, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
