Anil humilis

Anil humilis, commonly known as the slender anil, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of its family. Anil humilis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and aromatic foliage.
