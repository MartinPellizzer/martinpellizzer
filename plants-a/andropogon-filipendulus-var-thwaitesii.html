Andropogon filipendulus var. thwaitesii

Andropogon filipendulus var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
