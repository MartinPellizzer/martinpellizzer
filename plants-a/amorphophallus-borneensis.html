Amorphophallus borneensis

Amorphophallus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, leafy stems and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a unique structure called a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and potential use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
