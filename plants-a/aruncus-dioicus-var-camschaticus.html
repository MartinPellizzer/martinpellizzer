Aruncus dioicus var. camschaticus

Aruncus dioicus var. camschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, feathery flowers resembling a beard. The plant has compound, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its unique appearance in garden settings.
