Aegopogon cenchroides var. abortivus

Aegopogon cenchroides var. abortivus, commonly known as the abortive bluemink, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
