Aster laevis var. strictiflorus

Aster laevis var. strictiflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bluish-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil. It blooms from late summer into early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
