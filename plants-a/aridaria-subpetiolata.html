Aridaria subpetiolata

Aridaria subpetiolata, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its succulent leaves. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy surfaces that reduce water loss.

Aridaria subpetiolata plays a role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
