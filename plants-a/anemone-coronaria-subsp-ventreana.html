Anemone coronaria subsp. ventreana

Anemone coronaria subsp.

ventreana, commonly known as the pink anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, single or double pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, and its stems are usually erect and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is closely related to other Anemone species and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
