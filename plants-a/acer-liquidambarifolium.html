Acer liquidambarifolium

Acer liquidambarifolium, commonly known as the maple-leafed elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Acer genus, which includes various maple species, though it is more closely related to the elm family. The tree is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 20-30 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
