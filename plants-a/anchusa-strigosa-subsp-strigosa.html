Anchusa strigosa subsp. strigosa

Anchusa strigosa subsp.

strigosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
