Acosta cariensis

Acosta cariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom at night. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, and it produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided by livestock and wildlife.
