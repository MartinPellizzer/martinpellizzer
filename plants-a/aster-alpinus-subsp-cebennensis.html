Aster alpinus subsp. cebennensis

Aster alpinus subsp.

cebennensis, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude environments.
