Anthemis anahytae

Anthemis anahytae, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and linear, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the genus Anthemis.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
