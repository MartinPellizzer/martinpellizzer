Acer cordifolium

Acer cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are heart-shaped and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse with the wind. It typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
