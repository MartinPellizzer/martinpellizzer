Alectryon hirsutus

Alectryon hirsutus, commonly known as the rough alectryon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodland areas.

The rough alectryon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
