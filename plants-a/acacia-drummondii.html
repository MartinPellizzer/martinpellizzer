Acacia drummondii

Acacia drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous tiny leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Acacia drummondii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
