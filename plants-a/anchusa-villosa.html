Anchusa villosa

Anchusa villosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas. Anchusa villosa is an annual or biennial species that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
