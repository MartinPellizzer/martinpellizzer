Acer pseudosieboldianum var. microsieboldianum

Acer pseudosieboldianum var. microsieboldianum, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant seasonal foliage, which ranges from yellow-green in spring to orange-red in autumn. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with finely serrated margins and a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
