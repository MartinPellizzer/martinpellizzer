Apocynum filiforme

Apocynum filiforme, commonly known as slender milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with five petals and a distinctive corona.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic, ribbed seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
