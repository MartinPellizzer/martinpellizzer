Asphodeline anatolica

Asphodeline anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, linear leaves that are usually gray-green in color, with a rough texture. The plant produces tall, slender flower stalks that bear clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Asphodeline anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
