Antidesma pentandrum var. rotundisepalum

Antidesma pentandrum var. rotundisepalum, commonly known as the round-sepal antidesma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five rounded sepals. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
