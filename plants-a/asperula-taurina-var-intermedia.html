Asperula taurina var. intermedia

Asperula taurina var. intermedia, commonly known as the Italian heath or creeping evergreen, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy evergreen leaves that remain on the plant year-round, providing dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
