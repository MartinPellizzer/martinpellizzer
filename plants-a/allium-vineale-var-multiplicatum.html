Allium vineale var. multiplicatum

Allium vineale var. multiplicatum, commonly known as the garlic chive or Chinese leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features long, flat, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and easy-to-grow ornamental and edible plant.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to other allium species, including garlic and onions.
