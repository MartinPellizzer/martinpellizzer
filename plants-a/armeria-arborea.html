Armeria arborea

Armeria arborea, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub-like plant that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring woody stems and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and salt spray.
