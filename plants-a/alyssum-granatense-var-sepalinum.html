Alyssum granatense var. sepalinum

Alyssum granatense var. sepalinum, commonly known as the granada allyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
