Argyrolobium sutherlandii

Argyrolobium sutherlandii, commonly known as the Sutherland's silverlobed burr.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa, characterized by its silver-gray foliage and distinctive seed pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique morphology includes a dense mat of leaves and a system of hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal.

Argyrolobium sutherlandii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
