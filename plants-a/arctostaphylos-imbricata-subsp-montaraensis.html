Arctostaphylos imbricata subsp. montaraensis

Arctostaphylos imbricata subsp.

montaraensis, commonly known as Montara manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

Montara manzanita plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
