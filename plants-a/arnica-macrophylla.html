Arnica macrophylla

Arnica macrophylla, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that bear yellow-orange flowers with prominent brown centers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica macrophylla is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
