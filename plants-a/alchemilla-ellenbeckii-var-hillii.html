Alchemilla ellenbeckii var. hillii

Alchemilla ellenbeckii var. hillii, commonly known as the rock alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, basal leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Alchemilla ellenbeckii var. hillii is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry, sunny conditions.
