Achillea × schneideri

Achillea × schneideri, commonly known as the German yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, clustered inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
