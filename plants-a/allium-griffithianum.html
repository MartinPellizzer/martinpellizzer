Allium griffithianum

Allium griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's onion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Allium griffithianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and potential medicinal properties.
