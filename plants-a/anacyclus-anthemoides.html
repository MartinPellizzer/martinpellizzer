Anacyclus anthemoides

Anacyclus anthemoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Anacyclus anthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
