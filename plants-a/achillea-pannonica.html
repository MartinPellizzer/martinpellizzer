Achillea pannonica

Achillea pannonica, commonly known as Pannonian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its medicinal properties have also been historically recognized in traditional herbal practices.
