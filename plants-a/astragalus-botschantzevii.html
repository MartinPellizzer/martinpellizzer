Astragalus botschantzevii

Astragalus botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchantzev milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Mongolia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
