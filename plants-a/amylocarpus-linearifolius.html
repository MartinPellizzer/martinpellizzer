Amylocarpus linearifolius

Amylocarpus linearifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by the development of fleshy, tuberous roots. These roots are the primary edible part of the plant and are used in various culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other plants with similar growth habits and floral structures.
