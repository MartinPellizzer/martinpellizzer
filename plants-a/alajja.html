Alajja

Alajja, commonly known as *Cnicus benedictus*, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Alajja is also known for its historical use in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.

Its botanical classification places it in the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the daisy or sunflower family.
