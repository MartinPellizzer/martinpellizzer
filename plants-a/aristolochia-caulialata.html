Aristolochia caulialata

Aristolochia caulialata, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and striking, hooded flowers that resemble pipes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions, though caution is advised due to its toxic properties.
