Adenophora polymorpha f. integrisepala

Adenophora polymorpha f. integrisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth margin, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
