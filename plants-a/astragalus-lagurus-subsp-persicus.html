Astragalus lagurus subsp. persicus

Astragalus lagurus subsp.

persicus, commonly known as Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
