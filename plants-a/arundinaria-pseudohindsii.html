Arundinaria pseudohindsii

Arundinaria pseudohindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' bamboo, is a rare and endangered grass species native to the wetlands of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves, typical of the grass family Poaceae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. Its unique ecological role makes it an important species for wetland conservation.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and requires specialized conservation efforts.
