Achillea ageratifolia f. breviflora

Achillea ageratifolia f. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered yarrow, is a horticultural form of the yarrow plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more densely packed flower heads compared to the species. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
