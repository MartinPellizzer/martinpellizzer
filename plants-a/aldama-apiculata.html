Aldama apiculata

Aldama apiculata, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
