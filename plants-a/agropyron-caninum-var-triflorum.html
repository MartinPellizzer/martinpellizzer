Agropyron caninum var. triflorum

Agropyron caninum var. triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces compact inflorescences with three to five spikelets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This species is valued for its forage qualities and has been used in ecological restoration projects.
