Agropyron repens var. arvense

Agropyron repens var. arvense, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered with a silky texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in disturbed areas, lawns, and along roadsides. It produces small, pale green spikelets that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family.

Silky wheatgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
