Asparagus aethiopicus

Asparagus aethiopicus, commonly known as Ethiopian asparagus, is a perennial herb native to Africa.

It features slender, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to form dense colonies, often spreading through rhizomes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and unique flowering structure.
