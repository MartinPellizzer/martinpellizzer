Athyrium nigripes var. dissectum

Athyrium nigripes var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected black fern, is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China and Japan. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its dark green, leathery fronds are adorned with a distinctive, intricate pattern of lobes and segments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and its ability to add texture to shaded garden spaces.
