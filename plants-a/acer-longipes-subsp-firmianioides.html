Acer longipes subsp. firmianioides

Acer longipes subsp.

firmianioides, commonly known as the Yunnan maple, is a deciduous tree native to southwestern China. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
