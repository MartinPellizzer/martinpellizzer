Astragalus eriopodus

Astragalus eriopodus, commonly known as the soft-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus eriopodus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
