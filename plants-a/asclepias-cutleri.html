Asclepias cutleri

Asclepias cutleri, commonly known as the Cutler's milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is a valuable host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays an important role in local ecosystems.

Asclepias cutleri is also known for its latex-like sap, which is toxic to many animals.
