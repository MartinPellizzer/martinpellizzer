Adenodaphne corifolia

Adenodaphne corifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered daphne, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its most distinctive feature is its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Adenodaphne corifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
