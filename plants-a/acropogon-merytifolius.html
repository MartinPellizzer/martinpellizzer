Acropogon merytifolius

Acropogon merytifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed acropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
