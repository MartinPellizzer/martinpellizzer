Astragalus microphysa var. paucijugus

Astragalus microphysa var. paucijugus, commonly known as the small-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its deep root system. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique varietal features, including reduced leaflets.

This plant is often found in sagebrush steppe and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
