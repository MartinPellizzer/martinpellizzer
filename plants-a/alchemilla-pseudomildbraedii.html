Alchemilla pseudomildbraedii

Alchemilla pseudomildbraedii, commonly known as the false plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Alchemilla pseudomildbraedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
