Anemone pulsatilla proles amoena

Anemone pulsatilla proles amoena, commonly known as the Pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The Pasque flower is known for its early spring blooming and is considered a symbol of renewal and resilience in many cultures.
