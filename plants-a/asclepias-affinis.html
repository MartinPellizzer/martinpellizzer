Asclepias affinis

Asclepias affinis, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant orange to yellow flowers. The plant features milky sap, which is a distinguishing characteristic of the milkweed genus. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Asclepias affinis serves as a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae, making it ecologically significant in supporting pollinators and native wildlife.
