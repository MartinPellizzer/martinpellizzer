Asphodelus villarsii

Asphodelus villarsii, commonly known as the Canary Island asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features upright, narrow leaves that are usually gray-green in color, and it produces tall spikes of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it resilient to drought and poor soil conditions. It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural and ecological traits.

Asphodelus villarsii is often found in coastal areas and open habitats, contributing to the unique biodiversity of the Canary Islands.
