Anaphalis lorentzii f. angustifolia

Anaphalis lorentzii f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf whitehead, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, and the inflorescence consists of numerous tiny florets arranged in a cylindrical cluster.

This subspecies is often found in the northern regions of North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
