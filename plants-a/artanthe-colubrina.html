Artanthe colubrina

Artanthe colubrina, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, deep blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Artanthe colubrina is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
