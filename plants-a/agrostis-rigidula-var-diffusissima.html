Agrostis rigidula var. diffusissima

Agrostis rigidula var. diffusissima, commonly known as the broadleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-haired, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate, feathery foliage.
