Astracantha plumosa

Astracantha plumosa, commonly known as the feathered burweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Astracantha plumosa is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
