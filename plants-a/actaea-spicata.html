Actaea spicata

Actaea spicata, commonly known as red baneberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces distinctive red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.
