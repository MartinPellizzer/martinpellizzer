Amelanchier integrifolia

Amelanchier integrifolia, commonly known as serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive spring foliage and showy white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in early summer, often attracting wildlife and providing food for birds. Its leaves are typically ovate with smooth edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Amelanchier integrifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
