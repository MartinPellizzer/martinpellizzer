Aegiphila magnifica

Aegiphila magnifica, commonly known as the goldenleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cool, moist climate of its native habitat.
