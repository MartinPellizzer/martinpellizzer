Aconitum thyraicum

Aconitum thyraicum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble a hooded monk's robe. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically green or purple in color. Aconitum thyraicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
