Anacardium mediterraneum

Anacardium mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean cashew, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, edible fruits that are encased in a hard, spiny shell. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a mild, temperate climate.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible seeds, though its cultivation is limited due to specific environmental needs.
