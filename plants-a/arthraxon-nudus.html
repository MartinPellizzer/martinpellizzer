Arthraxon nudus

Arthraxon nudus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Arthraxon nudus is an important component of native ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
