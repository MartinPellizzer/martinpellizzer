Acer ambiguum

Acer ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that vary in shape and size depending on the environment. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Acer ambiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall foliage.
