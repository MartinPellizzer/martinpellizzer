Axonopus rosei

Axonopus rosei, commonly known as the redstem spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers. Axonopus rosei is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to form extensive mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability to wet conditions.
