Andropogon annulatus var. humilis

Andropogon annulatus var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, blue-green blades that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and sustainable landscaping.
