Albizia sherriffii

Albizia sherriffii, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, fragrant, fluffy yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Albizia sherriffii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
