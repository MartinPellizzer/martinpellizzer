Alyxia oliviformis f. ovata

Alyxia oliviformis f. ovata, commonly known as the ovate-leaf alyxia, is a variant of the species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its ovate-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
