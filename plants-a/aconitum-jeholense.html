Aconitum jeholense

Aconitum jeholense, commonly known as the Jehol monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Aconitum jeholense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

It is notable for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant of both ecological and medicinal interest.
