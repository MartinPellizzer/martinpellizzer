Arabis caucasica var. dolichothrix

Arabis caucasica var. dolichothrix, commonly known as the long-stemmed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are arranged in racemes. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
