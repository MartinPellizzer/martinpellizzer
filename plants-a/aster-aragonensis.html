Aster aragonensis

Aster aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon aster, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clustered, blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster aragonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
