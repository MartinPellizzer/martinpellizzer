Atropis festuciformis

Atropis festuciformis, commonly known as the slender grass of the genus Atropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in spikelets. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. It grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying plant diversity and adaptation.
