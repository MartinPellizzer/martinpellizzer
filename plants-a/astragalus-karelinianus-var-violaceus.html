Astragalus karelinianus var. violaceus

Astragalus karelinianus var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a deep violet hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
