Arachnorchis ovata

Arachnorchis ovata, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ovate-shaped pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-white flowers with a yellowish lip, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Arachnorchis ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under suitable conditions.
