Aster arbutoides

Aster arbutoides, commonly known as the rock aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster arbutoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
