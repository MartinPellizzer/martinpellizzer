Asarum sieboldii f. misandrum

Asarum sieboldii f. misandrum, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal studies.
