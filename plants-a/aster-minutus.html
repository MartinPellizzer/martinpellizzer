Aster minutus

Aster minutus, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a small flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to lavender. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruit capsules.

Aster minutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
