Asphodelus audibertii

Asphodelus audibertii, commonly known as the Audibert's asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spikes of yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in full sun.

Asphodelus audibertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
