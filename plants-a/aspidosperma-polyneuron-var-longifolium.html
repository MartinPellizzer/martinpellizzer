Aspidosperma polyneuron var. longifolium

Aspidosperma polyneuron var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved wolfberry, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
