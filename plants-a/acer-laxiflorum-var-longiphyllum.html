Acer laxiflorum var. longiphyllum

Acer laxiflorum var. longiphyllum, commonly known as the long-flowered maple, is a variant of the Chinese maple.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically dark green and turn vibrant red in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is native to China and is valued for its unique floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
