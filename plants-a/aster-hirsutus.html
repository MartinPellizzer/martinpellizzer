Aster hirsutus

Aster hirsutus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Aster hirsutus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
