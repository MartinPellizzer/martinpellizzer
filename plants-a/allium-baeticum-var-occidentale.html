Allium baeticum var. occidentale

Allium baeticum var. occidentale, commonly known as the western garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features a cluster of small, white, spherical bulbs that are often used in Mediterranean cuisine. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.
