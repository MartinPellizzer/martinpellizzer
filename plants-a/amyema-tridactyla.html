Amyema tridactyla

Amyema tridactyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Amyema tridactyla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to the food web and influencing host tree health.
