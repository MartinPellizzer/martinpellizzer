Anemone lancifolia

Anemone lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant has finely cut, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Anemone lancifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
