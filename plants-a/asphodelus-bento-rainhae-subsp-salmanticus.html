Asphodelus bento-rainhae subsp. salmanticus

Asphodelus bento-rainhae subsp.

salmanticus, commonly known as the Salmanticus asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain. It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence.

The plant produces small, pale yellow or greenish flowers that are typically clustered at the tip of the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
