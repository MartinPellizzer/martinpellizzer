Aeollanthus fruticosus

Aeollanthus fruticosus, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with salt spray and strong winds.

Aeollanthus fruticosus is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
