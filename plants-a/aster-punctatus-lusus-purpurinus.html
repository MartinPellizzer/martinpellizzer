Aster punctatus lusus purpurinus

Aster punctatus lusus purpurinus, commonly known as the purple-spotted aster, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent purple-centered disk, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
