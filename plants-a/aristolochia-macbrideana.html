Aristolochia macbrideana

Aristolochia macbrideana, commonly known as the Macbride's birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, saddle-shaped leaves and showy, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which are used in traditional medicine. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
