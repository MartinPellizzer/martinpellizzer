Anchusa sikkimensis

Anchusa sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim Anchusa, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence.

Anchusa sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
