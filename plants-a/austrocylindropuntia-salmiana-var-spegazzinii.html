Austrocylindropuntia salmiana var. spegazzinii

Austrocylindropuntia salmiana var. spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini's barrel cactus, is a spineless cactus native to southern South America.

It features a cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
