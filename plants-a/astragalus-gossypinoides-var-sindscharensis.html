Astragalus gossypinoides var. sindscharensis

Astragalus gossypinoides var. sindscharensis, commonly known as the Sindscharens' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring or early summer. Its fruits are legumes that contain small, hard seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
