Artanthe peduncularis

Artanthe peduncularis, commonly known as the slender artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and delicate, branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, adding visual interest to gardens. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts well with the floral display.

Artanthe peduncularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
