Anthemis comosa

Anthemis comosa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, composite flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
