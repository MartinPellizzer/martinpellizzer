Amblyopogon meyerianus

Amblyopogon meyerianus, commonly known as the Meyer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and savanna habitats, often in regions with moderate to high rainfall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Amblyopogon meyerianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native ecosystems.
