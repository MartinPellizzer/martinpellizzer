Ambaiba concolor

Ambaiba concolor, commonly known as the red-flowered rock fig, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are highly ornamental. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Ambaiba concolor thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
