Aira cespitosa var. firmula

Aira cespitosa var. firmula, commonly known as slender false oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, hairless stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
