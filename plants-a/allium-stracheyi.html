Allium stracheyi

Allium stracheyi, commonly known as the Afghan leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbous roots that are used in local cuisines for their mild, garlic-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Allium stracheyi is also valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
