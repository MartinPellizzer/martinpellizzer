Alyssum microcephalum

Alyssum microcephalum, commonly known as dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compact, succulent-like leaves that are hairy and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum microcephalum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as ladybugs and hoverflies.
