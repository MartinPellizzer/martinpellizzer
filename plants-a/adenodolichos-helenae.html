Adenodolichos helenae

Adenodolichos helenae, commonly known as the Helen's climbing bean, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features tendrils that allow it to climb and attach to supports, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers are typically small and pea-like, with a distinctive pink or purple hue.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
