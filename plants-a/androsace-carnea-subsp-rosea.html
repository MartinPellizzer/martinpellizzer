Androsace carnea subsp. rosea

Androsace carnea subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features a dense mat of foliage with ovate, glaucous leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
