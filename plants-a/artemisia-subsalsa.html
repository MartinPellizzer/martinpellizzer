Artemisia subsalsa

Artemisia subsalsa, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic environments.

Artemisia subsalsa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
