Adenophora verticillata var. canescens

Adenophora verticillata var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
