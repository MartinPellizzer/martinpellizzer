Aeonium × sanctisebastianii

Aeonium × sanctisebastianii, commonly known as the Holy Sebastian Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Aeonium × sanctisebastianii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
