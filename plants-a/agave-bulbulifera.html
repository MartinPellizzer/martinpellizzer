Agave bulbulifera

Agave bulbulifera, commonly known as the hummingbird agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of small, white flowers, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
