Arabis bellidifolia var. soyeriana

Arabis bellidifolia var. soyeriana, commonly known as the Soyer's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, and its stems are usually unbranched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and delicate appearance.
