Anemone baicalensis subsp. glabrata

Anemone baicalensis subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the Baikal anemone, is a perennial herb native to the regions of Siberia and Mongolia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas. Its flowers are composed of numerous petal-like sepals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
