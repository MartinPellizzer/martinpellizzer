Aster pycnophyllus

Aster pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are densely packed and often have a grayish or silvery hue, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Aster pycnophyllus is often found in prairies, open woods, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
