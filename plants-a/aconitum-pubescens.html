Aconitum pubescens

Aconitum pubescens, commonly known as the downy monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and alpine meadows.

Aconitum pubescens contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
