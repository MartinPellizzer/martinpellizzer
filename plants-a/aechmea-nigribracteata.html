Aechmea nigribracteata

Aechmea nigribracteata, commonly known as the black bract aechmea, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking violet to purple inflorescences, with elongated, dark-bracted flowers that are highly ornamental. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea nigribracteata is popular in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor cultivation.

Morphology

Aechmea nigribracteata has leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette, and can reach up to 50 cm in length, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, upright stem with a smooth, pale green bark that grows from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by colorful bracts, and are typically pink or red, blooming during the summer months; they are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by animals feeding on the fruit.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
