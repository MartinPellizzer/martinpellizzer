Alchemilla walasii var. virgata

Alchemilla walasii var. virgata, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in drought-tolerant, decorative plants.
