Anthericum diphyllum

Anthericum diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features two types of leaves—large, heart-shaped leaves and smaller, narrower ones—hence its common name. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Anthericum diphyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
