Argyreia laurifolia

Argyreia laurifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sacred lotus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are often clustered together, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

The plant has cultural significance in various regions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
