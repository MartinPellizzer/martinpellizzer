Auriculardisia furfuracea

Auriculardisia furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy ear orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaf bases that resemble ears, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. The flowers are small and often pale green or yellowish, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
