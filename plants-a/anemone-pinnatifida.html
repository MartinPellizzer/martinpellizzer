Anemone pinnatifida

Anemone pinnatifida, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Anemone pinnatifida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
