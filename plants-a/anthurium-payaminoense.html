Anthurium payaminoense

Anthurium payaminoense, commonly known as the Payaminoense arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium payaminoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and ability to add a dramatic touch to indoor or outdoor gardens.
