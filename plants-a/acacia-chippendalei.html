Acacia chippendalei

Acacia chippendalei, commonly known as the Chippendale wattle, is a small, shrub-like plant native to Western Australia.

It features slender, wiry branches and produces small, yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native landscaping.
