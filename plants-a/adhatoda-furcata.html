Adhatoda furcata

Adhatoda furcata, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Blue Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Adhatoda furcata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
