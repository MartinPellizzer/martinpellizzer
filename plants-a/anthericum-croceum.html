Anthericum croceum

Anthericum croceum, commonly known as the yellow adder's mouth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its scientific name reflects its striking yellow flowers and its historical association with the adder, a venomous snake.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
