Atriplex rosea var. erecta

Atriplex rosea var. erecta, commonly known as silverleaf chenopody, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, aiding in water conservation. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
