Acacia leucophloea

Acacia leucophloea, commonly known as the white-wood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia leucophloea is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
