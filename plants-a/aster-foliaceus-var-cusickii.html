Aster foliaceus var. cusickii

Aster foliaceus var. cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Its foliage is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
