Alyxia grandis

Alyxia grandis, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Alyxia grandis plays an important ecological role, providing food for various wildlife species.
