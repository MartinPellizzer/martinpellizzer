Amelanchier ovalis subvar. comafredensis

Amelanchier ovalis subvar. comafredensis, commonly known as the Comafred's serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. The plant produces showy white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn vibrant yellow in autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
