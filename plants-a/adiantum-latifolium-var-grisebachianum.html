Adiantum latifolium var. grisebachianum

Adiantum latifolium var. grisebachianum, commonly known as the Grisebach's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed maidenhair fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually arching and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
