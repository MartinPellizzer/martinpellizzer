Anacheilium vinaceum

Anacheilium vinaceum, commonly known as the red-stemmed anacheilium, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Anacheilium vinaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
