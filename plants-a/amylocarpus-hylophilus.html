Amylocarpus hylophilus

Amylocarpus hylophilus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces a unique type of underground tuber called an amylocaul, which stores nutrients and aids in its survival. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
