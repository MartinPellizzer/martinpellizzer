Aquilegia euchroma

Aquilegia euchroma, commonly known as the white helmet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
