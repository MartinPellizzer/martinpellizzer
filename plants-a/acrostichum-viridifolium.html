Acrostichum viridifolium

Acrostichum viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound fronds that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila, a distinctive feature of many ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.

Its botanical structure reflects its adaptation to moist, tropical climates, making it a common sight in rainforests and wetlands.
