Arum carratracense

Arum carratracense, commonly known as the Carratracense arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically features arrow-shaped leaves that are broad and pointed, with a waxy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
