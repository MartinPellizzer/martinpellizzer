Achillea grandis

Achillea grandis, commonly known as the giant yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant features upright, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Achillea grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
