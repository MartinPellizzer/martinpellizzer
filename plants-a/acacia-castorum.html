Acacia castorum

Acacia castorum, commonly known as the Castor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The tree is characterized by its flat, bipinnate leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads. It produces a hard, woody pod that contains seeds surrounded by a fibrous, cotton-like material.

Acacia castorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
