Aristea racemosa var. inflata

Aristea racemosa var. inflata, commonly known as the inflated aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are clustered in racemes, giving it its species name. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is noted for its unique inflated bracts, which contribute to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
