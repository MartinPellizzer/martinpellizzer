Adenophora moiwana var. heterotricha

Adenophora moiwana var. heterotricha, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, with five petals and a prominent central tube. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
