Abronia pumila

Abronia pumila, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically features small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry plains.

Abronia pumila plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
