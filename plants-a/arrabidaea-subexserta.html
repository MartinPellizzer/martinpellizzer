Arrabidaea subexserta

Arrabidaea subexserta, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, elliptical leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
