Astragalus dolichopodus

Astragalus dolichopodus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Astragalus dolichopodus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
