Andropogon micranthus var. villosulus

Andropogon micranthus var. villosulus, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
