Aulomyrcia detergens var. depauperata

Aulomyrcia detergens var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-leaved soap tree, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage and distinctive, often fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a milky latex, which has been traditionally used for its cleansing properties. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
