Asparagus duchesnei

Asparagus duchesnei, commonly known as the desert asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, needle-like leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Asparagus duchesnei plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
