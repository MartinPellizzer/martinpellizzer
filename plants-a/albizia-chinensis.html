Albizia chinensis

Albizia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. Albizia chinensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
