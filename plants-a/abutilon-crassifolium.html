Abutilon crassifolium

Abutilon crassifolium, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, velvety leaves. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
