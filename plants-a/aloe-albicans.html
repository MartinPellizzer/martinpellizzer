Aloe albicans

Aloe albicans, commonly known as the white aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are pale green to white in color, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

Aloe albicans is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
