Atriplex rosea var. stipitata

Atriplex rosea var. stipitata, commonly known as the silverleaf chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Atriplex rosea var. stipitata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
