Androsace obtusifolia f. involucrata

Androsace obtusifolia f. involucrata, commonly known as the woolly rock sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving the plant a woolly appearance. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the base of the stems, and bloom in early spring. This plant thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
