Asarum sieboldii var. maculatum

Asarum sieboldii var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, often hidden beneath its foliage. Its aromatic rhizomes have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The varietal name "maculatum" refers to the spotted or mottled appearance of its leaves.
