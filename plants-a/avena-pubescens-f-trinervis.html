Avena pubescens f. trinervis

Avena pubescens f. trinervis, commonly known as the trinerved oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows or along streambanks. Its leaves have a distinct three-veined structure, which is a key identifying feature of this form.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
