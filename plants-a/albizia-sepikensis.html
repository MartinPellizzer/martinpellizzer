Albizia sepikensis

Albizia sepikensis, commonly known as the Sepik acacia, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in the Democratic Republic of the Congo and the Central African Republic.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The tree has a distinctive appearance with feathery, bipinnate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
