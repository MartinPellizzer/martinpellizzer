Arisaema cochleatum

Arisaema cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped spathes that enclose the inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can grow quite large, contributing to its ornamental value.

Arisaema cochleatum is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
