Avena macrantha

Avena macrantha, commonly known as the tall oat, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Avena macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
