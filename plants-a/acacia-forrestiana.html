Acacia forrestiana

Acacia forrestiana, commonly known as the Forest's acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system. Its thorny stems and small, pod-like fruits are typical features of the acacia genus.

Acacia forrestiana also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
