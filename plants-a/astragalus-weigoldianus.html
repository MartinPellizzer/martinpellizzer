Astragalus weigoldianus

Astragalus weigoldianus, commonly known as the Weigold's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and may have potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
