Acalypha subtomentosa

Acalypha subtomentosa, commonly known as silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Acalypha subtomentosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
