Abutilon oxypetalum

Abutilon oxypetalum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
