Aragallus campestris var. johannensis

Aragallus campestris var. johannensis, commonly known as the Johann's field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
