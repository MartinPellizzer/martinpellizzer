Armeniaca mandshurica var. domestica

Armeniaca mandshurica var. domestica, commonly known as the Chinese date or jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, Korea, and parts of Russia.

It produces edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamins and minerals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and shows a preference for well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and it is cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

The fruit matures in late summer to early autumn and is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
