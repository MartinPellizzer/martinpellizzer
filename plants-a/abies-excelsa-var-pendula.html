Abies excelsa var. pendula

Abies excelsa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping fir, is a striking variant of the silver fir.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that give it a unique, almost ethereal appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green color, with a slightly bluish tinge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

This variety is relatively slow-growing and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in optimal conditions.
