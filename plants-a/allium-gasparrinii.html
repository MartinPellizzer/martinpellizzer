Allium gasparrinii

Allium gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the summer. The plant's bulbs are typically small and have a mild, sweet flavor, making them suitable for culinary use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Allium gasparrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
