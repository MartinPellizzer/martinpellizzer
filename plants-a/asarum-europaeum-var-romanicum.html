Asarum europaeum var. romanicum

Asarum europaeum var. romanicum, commonly known as European wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-purple leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a spathaceous bract, which is typically concealed beneath the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes, which contain compounds used in traditional herbal medicine.
