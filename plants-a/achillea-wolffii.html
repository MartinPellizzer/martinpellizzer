Achillea wolffii

Achillea wolffii, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Achillea wolffii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
