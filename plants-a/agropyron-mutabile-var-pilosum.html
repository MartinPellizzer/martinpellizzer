Agropyron mutabile var. pilosum

Agropyron mutabile var. pilosum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and ecological importance, it is often found in alpine meadows and used in restoration projects.
