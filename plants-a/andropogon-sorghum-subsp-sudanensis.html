Andropogon sorghum subsp. sudanensis

Andropogon sorghum subsp.

sudanensis, commonly known as Sudan grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its robust growth and high biomass production, making it suitable for forage and erosion control.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer. It thrives in warm climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Sudan grass is widely cultivated for its nutritional value to livestock and its utility in agricultural systems.
