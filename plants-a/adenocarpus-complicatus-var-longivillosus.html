Adenocarpus complicatus var. longivillosus

Adenocarpus complicatus var. longivillosus, commonly known as the hairy burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, often thorny branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, hairy, spiny fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The hairy foliage and robust structure make it well-suited to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
