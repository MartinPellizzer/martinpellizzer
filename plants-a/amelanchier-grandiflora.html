Amelanchier grandiflora

Amelanchier grandiflora, commonly known as the flowering serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible berries in the summer that are often used in jams and pies. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different light conditions.

Amelanchier grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
