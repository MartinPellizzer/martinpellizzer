Astragalus kuntzei

Astragalus kuntzei, commonly known as Kuntze's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of Mongolia and western China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

Astragalus kuntzei is also noted for its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its native habitat.
