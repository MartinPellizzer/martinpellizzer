Allium margaritaceum var. robustum

Allium margaritaceum var. robustum, commonly known as the robust garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a bulbous base and a strong, aromatic scent, contributing to its popularity among both gardeners and chefs.
