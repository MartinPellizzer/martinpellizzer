Astragalus babakhanloui

Astragalus babakhanloui, commonly known as Babakhanlu milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus babakhanloui is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
