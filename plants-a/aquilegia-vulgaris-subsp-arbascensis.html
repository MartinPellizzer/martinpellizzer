Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. arbascensis

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

arbascensis, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces red or orange seed pods that split open to release seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
