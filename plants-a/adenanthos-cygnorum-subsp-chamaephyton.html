Adenanthos cygnorum subsp. chamaephyton

Adenanthos cygnorum subsp.

chamaephyton, commonly known as the silver leaf chamaephyte, is a subshrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
