Acacia velutina f. ferox

Acacia velutina f. ferox, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a variant of the acacia species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
