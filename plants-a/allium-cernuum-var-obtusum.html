Allium cernuum var. obtusum

Allium cernuum var. obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features grass-like, broad, bluish-green leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities.
