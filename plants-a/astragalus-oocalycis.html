Astragalus oocalycis

Astragalus oocalycis, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

Astragalus oocalycis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for various wildlife species.
