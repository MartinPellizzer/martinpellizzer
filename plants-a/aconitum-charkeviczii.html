Aconitum charkeviczii

Aconitum charkeviczii, commonly known as Charkevich's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed divisions, and its roots are tuberous, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
