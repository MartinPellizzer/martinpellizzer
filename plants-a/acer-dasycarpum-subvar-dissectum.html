Acer dasycarpum subvar. dissectum

Acer dasycarpum subvar. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, red samaras that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
