Aridaria rabiesbergensis

Aridaria rabiesbergensis, commonly known as the Rabiesberg sandthorn, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted for water storage. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Aridaria rabiesbergensis is well-suited to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
