Acer turcomanicum

Acer turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive palmate leaves with five lobes. The leaves are typically green in summer and turn vibrant yellow in autumn. It produces small, red flowers in early spring followed by winged samaras.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
