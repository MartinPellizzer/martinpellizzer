Arundinaria tajimana

Arundinaria tajimana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Arundinaria tajimana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It plays a role in traditional Japanese gardens and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
