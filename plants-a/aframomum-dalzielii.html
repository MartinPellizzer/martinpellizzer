Aframomum dalzielii

Aframomum dalzielii, commonly known as the Dalziel's cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to tropical Africa, particularly found in regions of Uganda, Kenya, and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces berries that contain aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
