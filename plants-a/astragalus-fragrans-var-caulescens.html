Astragalus fragrans var. caulescens

Astragalus fragrans var. caulescens, commonly known as the fragrant milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
