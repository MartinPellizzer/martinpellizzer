Anacamptis × ticinensis

Anacamptis × ticinensis, commonly known as the Ticino Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
