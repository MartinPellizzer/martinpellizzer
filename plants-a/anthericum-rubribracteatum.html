Anthericum rubribracteatum

Anthericum rubribracteatum, commonly known as the red-bracted arum, is a striking perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Anthericum rubribracteatum prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
