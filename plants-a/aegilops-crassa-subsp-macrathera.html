Aegilops crassa subsp. macrathera

Aegilops crassa subsp.

macrathera, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems, with small, awned florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and agricultural research.
