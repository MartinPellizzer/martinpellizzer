Alyssum mesopotamicum

Alyssum mesopotamicum, commonly known as the Mesopotamian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are simple, linear, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alyssum mesopotamicum is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
