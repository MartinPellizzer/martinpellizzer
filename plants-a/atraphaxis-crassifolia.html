Atraphaxis crassifolia

Atraphaxis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved desert barley, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Atraphaxis crassifolia is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.
