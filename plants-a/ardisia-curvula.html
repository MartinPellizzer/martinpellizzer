Ardisia curvula

Ardisia curvula, commonly known as the glossy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Ardisia curvula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
