Acacia pennata var. dregeana

Acacia pennata var. dregeana, commonly known as the dwarf silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
