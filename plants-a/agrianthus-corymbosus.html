Agrianthus corymbosus

Agrianthus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered agave, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Agrianthus corymbosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
