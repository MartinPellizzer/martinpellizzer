Artemisia davurica

Artemisia davurica, commonly known as Siberian tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, contain compounds like thymol and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Artemisia davurica is also valued for its role in traditional herbal medicine and as a hardy ornamental plant.
