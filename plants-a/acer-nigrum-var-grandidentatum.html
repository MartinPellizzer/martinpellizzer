Acer nigrum var. grandidentatum

Acer nigrum var. grandidentatum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The tree produces small, pendulous clusters of greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
