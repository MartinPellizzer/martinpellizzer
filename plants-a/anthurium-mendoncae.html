Anthurium mendoncae

Anthurium mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendoza arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface.

Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a spathe that can vary in color depending on the cultivar. Anthurium mendoncae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
