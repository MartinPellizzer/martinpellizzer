Avena sativa subsp. contracta

Avena sativa subsp.

contracta, commonly known as the slender oat, is a cultivated variety of the oat plant characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to other oat subspecies. It features slender, erect stems with reduced leaf size, which contribute to its distinct appearance.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its botanical traits make it suitable for specific agricultural uses, such as forage or soil stabilization.

The subspecies is valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in sustainable farming practices.
