Aruba alba

Aruba alba, commonly known as the white arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a unique white spathe that resembles a flower. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Aruba alba thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources in its natural habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
