Astragalus ucrainicus

Astragalus ucrainicus, commonly known as the Ukrainian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and steppes. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees.

Astragalus ucrainicus is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and its potential medicinal properties.
