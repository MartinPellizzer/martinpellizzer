Arenaria grandiflora var. valentina

Arenaria grandiflora var. valentina, commonly known as Valentina's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are borne on slender, erect stems.

Valentina's sandwort is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
