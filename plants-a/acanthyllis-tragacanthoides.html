Acanthyllis tragacanthoides

Acanthyllis tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Tragacanth-like Thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive latex that has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
