Amaryllis ferreyrae

Amaryllis ferreyrae, commonly known as the Ferreyra's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features tall, erect stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, waxy petals that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen and persist through much of the year.

Amaryllis ferreyrae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
