Arodes albomaculatum

Arodes albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers feature a combination of white and green hues, with distinctive markings.

Arodes albomaculatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
