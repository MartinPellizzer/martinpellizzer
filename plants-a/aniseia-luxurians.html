Aniseia luxurians

Aniseia luxurians, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Aniseia luxurians is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
