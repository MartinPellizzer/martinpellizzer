Acilepis fysonii

Acilepis fysonii, commonly known as the Fyson's acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The plant is noted for its resilience to drought and is sometimes used in local horticulture for its ornamental value.
