Acianthus sublestus

Acianthus sublestus, commonly known as the slender acianthus, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, which helps collect moisture and nutrients. It produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the summer months.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
