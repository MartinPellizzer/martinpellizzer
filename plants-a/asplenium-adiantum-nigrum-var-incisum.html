Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. incisum

Asplenium adiantum-nigrum var. incisum, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a fern species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery underside. This variety is distinguished by its finely toothed or incised leaf margins, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or shaded forest floors.

The plant is considered a primitive fern, showcasing features typical of early fern evolution.
