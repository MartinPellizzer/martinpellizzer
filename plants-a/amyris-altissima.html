Amyris altissima

Amyris altissima, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by long, narrow, red-brown seed pods. Amyris altissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
