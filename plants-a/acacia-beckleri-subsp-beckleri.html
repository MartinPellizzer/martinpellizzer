Acacia beckleri subsp. beckleri

Acacia beckleri subsp.

beckleri, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
