Aster vahlii var. latifolius

Aster vahlii var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
