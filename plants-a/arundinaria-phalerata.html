Arundinaria phalerata

Arundinaria phalerata, commonly known as the Phaler's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria phalerata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
