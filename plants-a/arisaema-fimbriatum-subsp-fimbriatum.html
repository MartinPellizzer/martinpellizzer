Arisaema fimbriatum subsp. fimbriatum

Arisaema fimbriatum subsp.

fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringe-leaf arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with finely serrated margins.

The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a common feature in the Araceae family. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
