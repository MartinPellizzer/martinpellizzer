Acacia sphacelata subsp. sphacelata

Acacia sphacelata subsp.

sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves composed of numerous thread-like leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
