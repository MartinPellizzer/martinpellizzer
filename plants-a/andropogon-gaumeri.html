Andropogon gaumeri

Andropogon gaumeri, commonly known as Gaumer's grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon gaumeri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
