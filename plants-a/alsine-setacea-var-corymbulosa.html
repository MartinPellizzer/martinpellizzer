Alsine setacea var. corymbulosa

Alsine setacea var. corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered alsike, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
