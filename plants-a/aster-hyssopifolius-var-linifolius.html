Aster hyssopifolius var. linifolius

Aster hyssopifolius var. linifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
