Anthoxanthum aristatum var. welwitschii

Anthoxanthum aristatum var. welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers. This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its growth habit is low and spreading, with a dense mat-like appearance.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
