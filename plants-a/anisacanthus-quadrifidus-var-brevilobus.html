Anisacanthus quadrifidus var. brevilobus

Anisacanthus quadrifidus var. brevilobus, commonly known as the Texas starburst, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody and branched. It is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
