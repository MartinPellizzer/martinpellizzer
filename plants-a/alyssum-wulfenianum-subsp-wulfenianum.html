Alyssum wulfenianum subsp. wulfenianum

Alyssum wulfenianum subsp.

wulfenianum, commonly known as Wulfen's alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
