Acacia limae

Acacia limae, commonly known as the lemon acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its small, oval-shaped seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are used in traditional medicine.

Acacia limae is adapted to arid environments and is often found in shrublands and rocky areas.
