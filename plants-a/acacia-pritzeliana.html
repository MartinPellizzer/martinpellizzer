Acacia pritzeliana

Acacia pritzeliana, commonly known as the Pritzel's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical clusters. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments. Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various purposes.

Acacia pritzeliana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
