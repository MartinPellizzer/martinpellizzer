Appendicula latilabium var. seramica

Appendicula latilabium var. seramica, commonly known as the Seram Island appendicula, is a rare orchid species native to the island of Seram in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and limited geographic distribution.
