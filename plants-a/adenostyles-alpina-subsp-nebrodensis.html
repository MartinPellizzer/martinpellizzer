Adenostyles alpina subsp. nebrodensis

Adenostyles alpina subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Alpine Adenostyles, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, producing small, bell-shaped blooms that are typically pink or purple in color.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
