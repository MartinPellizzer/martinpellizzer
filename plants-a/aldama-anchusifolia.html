Aldama anchusifolia

Aldama anchusifolia, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and produces a milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators in its native habitat.
