Argyreia mollis

Argyreia mollis, commonly known as the velvet flower or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that have a distinctive silver underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by round, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Argyreia mollis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
