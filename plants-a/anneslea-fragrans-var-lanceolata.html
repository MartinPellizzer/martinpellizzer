Anneslea fragrans var. lanceolata

Anneslea fragrans var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate fragrant anneslea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers emit a sweet, spicy aroma, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
