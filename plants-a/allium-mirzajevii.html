Allium mirzajevii

Allium mirzajevii, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features a bulbous stem with long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

The bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in some regions.
