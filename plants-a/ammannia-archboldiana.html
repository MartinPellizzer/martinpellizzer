Ammannia archboldiana

Ammannia archboldiana, commonly known as the Florida bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. It is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to wet or seasonally flooded environments.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
