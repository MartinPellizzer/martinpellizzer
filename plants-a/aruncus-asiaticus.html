Aruncus asiaticus

Aruncus asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian goat's beard, is a striking perennial plant native to Asia.

It features tall, arching stems covered in soft, feathery white to pinkish bracts that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic, early spring display.
