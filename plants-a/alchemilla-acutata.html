Alchemilla acutata

Alchemilla acutata, commonly known as sharpleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla acutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
