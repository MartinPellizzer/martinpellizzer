Azurinia

Azurinia, commonly known as blueberry bush, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Azurinia thrives in acidic soils and requires well-drained conditions for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
