Alternanthera versicolor

Alternanthera versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful foliage, which can range from red, orange, and yellow to pink and violet, depending on light exposure and environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or white.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
