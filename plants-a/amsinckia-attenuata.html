Amsinckia attenuata

Amsinckia attenuata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a distinct scent when crushed.

The showy yellow flowers appear in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
