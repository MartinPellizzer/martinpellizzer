Aframomum verrucosum

Aframomum verrucosum, commonly known as the African cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that produce aromatic, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant's leaves are large, elliptical, and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its fruit is a spindle-shaped, three-lobed capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional remedies for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
