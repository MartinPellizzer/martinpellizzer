Atropis distans var. salinaria

Atropis distans var. salinaria, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. This plant thrives in saline or brackish environments, often found in coastal marshes and tidal flats. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and sprawling.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
