Alchemilla pseudincisa var. polonica

Alchemilla pseudincisa var. polonica, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features a rosette of silvery, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
