Aletris bifolia

Aletris bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved false lily, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a loose inflorescence. Aletris bifolia thrives in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in rich, acidic soils.

It is a low-growing plant that typically reaches a height of less than 10 inches.
