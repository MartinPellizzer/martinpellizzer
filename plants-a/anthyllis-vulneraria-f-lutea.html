Anthyllis vulneraria f. lutea

Anthyllis vulneraria f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the genus Anthyllis. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
