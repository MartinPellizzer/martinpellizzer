Astragalus dielsii

Astragalus dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Astragalus dielsii is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems, where it thrives with minimal water availability.
