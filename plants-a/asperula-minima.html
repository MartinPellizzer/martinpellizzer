Asperula minima

Asperula minima, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Asperula minima is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
