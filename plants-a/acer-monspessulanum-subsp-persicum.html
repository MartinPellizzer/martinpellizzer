Acer monspessulanum subsp. persicum

Acer monspessulanum subsp.

persicum, commonly known as the Persian maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically red or purple in color, turning yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
