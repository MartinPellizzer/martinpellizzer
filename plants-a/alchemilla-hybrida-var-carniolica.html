Alchemilla hybrida var. carniolica

Alchemilla hybrida var. carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
