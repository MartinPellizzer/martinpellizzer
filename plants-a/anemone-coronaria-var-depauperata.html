Anemone coronaria var. depauperata

Anemone coronaria var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with basal leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
