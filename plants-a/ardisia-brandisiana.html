Ardisia brandisiana

Ardisia brandisiana, commonly known as the Java beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its bright red, berry-like fruits that appear in the fall, which are attractive to birds. Ardisia brandisiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
