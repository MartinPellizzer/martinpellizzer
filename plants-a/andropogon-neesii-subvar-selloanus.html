Andropogon neesii subvar. selloanus

Andropogon neesii subvar. selloanus, commonly known as the Selloan switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
