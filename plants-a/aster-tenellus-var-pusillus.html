Aster tenellus var. pusillus

Aster tenellus var. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in gardens with careful attention to soil and moisture conditions.
