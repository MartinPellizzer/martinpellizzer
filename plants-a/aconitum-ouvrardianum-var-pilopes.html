Aconitum ouvrardianum var. pilopes

Aconitum ouvrardianum var. pilopes, commonly known as the Chinese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, conical clusters. Its leaves are compound, with deeply cut lobes, and the plant has a toxic root system containing potent alkaloids.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
