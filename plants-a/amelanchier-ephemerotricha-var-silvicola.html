Amelanchier ephemerotricha var. silvicola

Amelanchier ephemerotricha var. silvicola, commonly known as the dwarf Juneberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
