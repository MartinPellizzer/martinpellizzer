Amelanchier rotundifolia

Amelanchier rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow or red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland habitats.

Amelanchier rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
