Alopecurus pratensis f. obscurus

Alopecurus pratensis f. obscurus, commonly known as the dark meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-bearing branches. Its flowers are typically pale green to brown, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.
