Aponogeton distachyos

Aponogeton distachyos, commonly known as the African water clock, is a tropical aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, typically in late summer or early autumn. Its rhizomes are often used in horticulture for their ability to root and grow in aquatic environments.
