Arabis viridis

Arabis viridis, commonly known as the green rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are oval and slightly hairy, with stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. Arabis viridis thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
