Arnica cordifolia var. eradiata

Arnica cordifolia var. eradiata, commonly known as the heart-leaved arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive tubular structure. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional remedies.
