Arthrochilium alatum

Arthrochilium alatum, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horizontally spreading rhizomes and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. Its unique morphology includes wing-like structures on the stems, giving it its common name.

Arthrochilium alatum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
