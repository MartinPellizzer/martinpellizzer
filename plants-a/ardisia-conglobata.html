Ardisia conglobata

Ardisia conglobata, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible for humans but toxic to livestock and some wildlife.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
