Agrostis vulgaris var. hispida

Agrostis vulgaris var. hispida, commonly known as hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and hairy texture on its leaf blades and stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixtures.
