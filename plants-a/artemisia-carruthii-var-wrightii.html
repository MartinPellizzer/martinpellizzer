Artemisia carruthii var. wrightii

Artemisia carruthii var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its medicinal and aromatic properties have made it of interest in traditional and modern herbal practices.
