Asparagopsis triacantha

Asparagopsis triacantha, commonly known as the red seaweed or *Asparagopsis*, is a species of red algae belonging to the family Halymeniaceae.

It is characterized by its branching, filamentous structure and vibrant red coloration, which is due to the presence of phycoerythrin, a pigment that aids in light absorption. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical marine environments, typically found in shallow, warm waters. It plays an important role in marine ecosystems as a primary producer and provides habitat for various marine organisms.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential applications in aquaculture and as a source of bioactive compounds.
