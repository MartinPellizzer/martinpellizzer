Aria edulis

Aria edulis, commonly known as the edible air plant, is a unique species that thrives in humid environments.

This plant is characterized by its ability to absorb moisture and nutrients through its leaves rather than roots. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist areas. The leaves are broad and fleshy, designed to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Despite its name, it is not a true air plant but a member of the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic species.
