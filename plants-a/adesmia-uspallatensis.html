Adesmia uspallatensis

Adesmia uspallatensis, commonly known as the Uspallata legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and beans, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and has small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Its root system is capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
