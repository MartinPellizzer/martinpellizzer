Asplenium javanicum

Asplenium javanicum, commonly known as the Java spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with closely packed, lance-shaped leaves. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Asplenium javanicum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
