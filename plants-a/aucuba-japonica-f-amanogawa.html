Aucuba japonica f. amanogawa

Aucuba japonica f. amanogawa, commonly known as the Amanogawa aucuba, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a striking yellowish-white variegation on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a valuable addition to both landscape and container gardening.
