Anthyllis montana f. atropurpurea

Anthyllis montana f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple mountain clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
