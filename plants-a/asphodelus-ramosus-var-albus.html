Asphodelus ramosus var. albus

Asphodelus ramosus var. albus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like, linear leaves and produces tall, branched flowering stems with showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. It is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a symbol in ancient Greek and Roman cultures.

The white asphodel is a member of the Asparagaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.
