Aspalathus granulata

Aspalathus granulata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces hard, chestnut-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in both horticulture and herbal practices.
