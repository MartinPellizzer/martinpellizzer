Anchusa calcarea

Anchusa calcarea, commonly known as the limestone hollyhock, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, sparsely branched clusters.

Anchusa calcarea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
