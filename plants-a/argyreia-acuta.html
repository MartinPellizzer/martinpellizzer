Argyreia acuta

Argyreia acuta, commonly known as the sacred lotus or velvet flower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a central yellow or white eye. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Argyreia acuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various Asian traditions.
