Aphelandra longiflora

Aphelandra longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear elongated, bright yellow bracts resembling flowers. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom sequentially along the inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Aphelandra longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
