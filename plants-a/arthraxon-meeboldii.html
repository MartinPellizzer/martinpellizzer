Arthraxon meeboldii

Arthraxon meeboldii, commonly known as the meebold grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
