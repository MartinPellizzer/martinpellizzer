Aconitum nipponicum var. septemcarpum

Aconitum nipponicum var. septemcarpum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood variant with seven-seeded fruits, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a helmet-like structure. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often hairy.

The varietal name 'septemcarpum' refers to its unique feature of producing fruits with seven seeds.
