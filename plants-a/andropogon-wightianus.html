Andropogon wightianus

Andropogon wightianus, commonly known as Wight's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Additionally, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
