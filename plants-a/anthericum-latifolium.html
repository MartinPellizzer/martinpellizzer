Anthericum latifolium

Anthericum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, with a dense, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
