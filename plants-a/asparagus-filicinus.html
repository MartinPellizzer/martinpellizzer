Asparagus filicinus

Asparagus filicinus, commonly known as the fern asparagus or the silver fern, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its fern-like foliage and edible, tender shoots. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into red berries, which are a food source for birds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in well-drained soils.

Asparagus filicinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a vegetable in some regions.
