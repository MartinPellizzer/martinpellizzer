Artabotrys luteus

Artabotrys luteus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling lanterns. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are palmately lobed, providing a lush green canopy.

Artabotrys luteus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
