Atropa aristata

Atropa aristata, commonly known as the death cup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a poisonous berry-like fruit that contains toxic compounds, making it hazardous to humans and animals. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Atropa aristata is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.
