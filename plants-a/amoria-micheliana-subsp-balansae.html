Amoria micheliana subsp. balansae

Amoria micheliana subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Balansae bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
