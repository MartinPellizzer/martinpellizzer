Amaranthus cochleitepalus

Amaranthus cochleitepalus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red to purple bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces tiny green seeds that are often used as a grain in traditional cuisines. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual in arid environments.

Its vibrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
