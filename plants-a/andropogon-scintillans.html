Andropogon scintillans

Andropogon scintillans, commonly known as the glittering grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, and it is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Andropogon scintillans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
