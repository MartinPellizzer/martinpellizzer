Anthoxanthum aethiopicum

Anthoxanthum aethiopicum, commonly known as Ethiopian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It is often used in erosion control due to its dense root system and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential nutritional value and use in livestock feed.
