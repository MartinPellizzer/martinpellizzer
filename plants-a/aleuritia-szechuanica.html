Aleuritia szechuanica

Aleuritia szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Aleuritia szechuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
