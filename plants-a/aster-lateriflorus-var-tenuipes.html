Aster lateriflorus var. tenuipes

Aster lateriflorus var. tenuipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
