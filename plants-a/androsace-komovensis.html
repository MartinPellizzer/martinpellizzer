Androsace komovensis

Androsace komovensis, commonly known as the Komov's rock-moss, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant features a dense mat of prostrate stems covered with small, hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer.

Androsace komovensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
