Atriplex megalotheca

Atriplex megalotheca, commonly known as the largeleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish or yellowish in color, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Atriplex megalotheca plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
