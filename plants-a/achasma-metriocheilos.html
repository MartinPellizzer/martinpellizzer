Achasma metriocheilos

Achasma metriocheilos, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
