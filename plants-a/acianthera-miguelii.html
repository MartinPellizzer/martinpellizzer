Acianthera miguelii

Acianthera miguelii, commonly known as the Miguel's acianthera, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
