Aklema tricolor

Aklema tricolor, commonly known as the three-color bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strikingly colored flowers that display three distinct shades of blue, purple, and white, giving it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Aklema tricolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant coloration and compact growth habit.
