Arisaema peltatum

Arisaema peltatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a distinctive arrow-shaped leaf, which gives it its common name. Its flowering structure consists of a spadix enclosed by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Arisaema peltatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in wetlands and along stream banks.
