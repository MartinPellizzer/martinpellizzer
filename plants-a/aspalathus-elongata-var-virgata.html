Aspalathus elongata var. virgata

Aspalathus elongata var. virgata, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
