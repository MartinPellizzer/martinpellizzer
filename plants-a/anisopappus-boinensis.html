Anisopappus boinensis

Anisopappus boinensis, commonly known as the Baja pineapple cactus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its botanical features include ribbed stems, glochids, and clusters of small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

Anisopappus boinensis is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
