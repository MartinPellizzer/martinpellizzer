Acanthophyllum raphiophyllum

Acanthophyllum raphiophyllum, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert habitats.

Its striking appearance makes it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
