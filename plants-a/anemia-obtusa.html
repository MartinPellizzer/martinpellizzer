Anemia obtusa

Anemia obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemia obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
