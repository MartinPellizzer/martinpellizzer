Adenophora verticillata subf. alternifolia

Adenophora verticillata subf.

alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces that provide a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
