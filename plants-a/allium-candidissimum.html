Allium candidissimum

Allium candidissimum, commonly known as the white garlic or wild garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland areas. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a strong garlic scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The bulb, while not as commonly used as regular garlic, has culinary and medicinal applications in traditional practices.
