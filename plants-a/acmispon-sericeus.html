Acmispon sericeus

Acmispon sericeus, commonly known as silky burr medick, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and desert regions.

Acmispon sericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
