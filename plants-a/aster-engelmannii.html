Aster engelmannii

Aster engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Engelmann's aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
