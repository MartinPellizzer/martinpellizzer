Aconitum kusnezoffii var. yamatsutae

Aconitum kusnezoffii var. yamatsutae, commonly known as the Yamatsutae monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture, and its stem is typically erect and branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
