Ampelaster

Ampelaster, commonly known as the hanging flower, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Ampelaster thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
