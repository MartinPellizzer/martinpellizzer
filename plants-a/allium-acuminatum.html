Allium acuminatum

Allium acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic or the slender leek, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and strap-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs, which are edible, have a mild garlic flavor and are used in various culinary traditions.

Allium acuminatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
