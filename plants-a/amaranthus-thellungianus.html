Amaranthus thellungianus

Amaranthus thellungianus, commonly known as purple amaranth, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its vibrant purple to red pigmentation in its leaves and bracts. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking coloration and upright growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its nutritional properties, as its seeds are rich in protein and minerals.
