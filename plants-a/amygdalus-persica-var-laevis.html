Amygdalus persica var. laevis

Amygdalus persica var. laevis, commonly known as the peach tree, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It produces oval, fuzzy fruits with a sweet, juicy flesh surrounding a single hard pit. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate with a serrated margin. It blooms in spring with pink to white flowers, which are essential for fruit production.

The variety laevis is characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit surface, distinguishing it from other peach varieties.
