Astragalus neplii

Astragalus neplii, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Astragalus neplii belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
