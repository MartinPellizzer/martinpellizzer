Allium trautvetterianum

Allium trautvetterianum, commonly known as the Trautvetter's onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hardy, drought-resistant nature. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium trautvetterianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and edible plant for gardens.
