Artemisia hulteniana

Artemisia hulteniana, commonly known as Hulten's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

Artemisia hulteniana is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
