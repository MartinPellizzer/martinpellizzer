Ageratum lundellii

Ageratum lundellii, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ageratum lundellii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
