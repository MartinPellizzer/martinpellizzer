Amyris texana

Amyris texana, commonly known as Texas tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for various landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
