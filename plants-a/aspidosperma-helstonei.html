Aspidosperma helstonei

Aspidosperma helstonei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua* (though it is actually a different species), is a small to medium-sized tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to withstand dry conditions.
