Aristolochia lineata

Aristolochia lineata, commonly known as the Indian pipe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flower that resembles a pipe, with a long, narrow tube and a broad, rounded lip. The plant has no leaves, relying instead on its white, fleshy stems for photosynthesis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its distinctive morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
