Acacia nilotica var. adansonii

Acacia nilotica var. adansonii, commonly known as the white thorn acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry regions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its timber, gum, and medicinal properties.
