Alsine kabirarum

Alsine kabirarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, pale green flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed areas effectively.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
