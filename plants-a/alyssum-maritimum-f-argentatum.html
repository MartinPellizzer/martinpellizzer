Alyssum maritimum f. argentatum

Alyssum maritimum f. argentatum, commonly known as silver alyssum, is a cultivar of the coastal alyssum species.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, silvery-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features compact, low-growing foliage with a mat-forming habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in coastal environments.
