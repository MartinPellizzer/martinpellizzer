Arctostaphylos glandulosa f. wieriana

Arctostaphylos glandulosa f. wieriana, commonly known as the Wierian manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
