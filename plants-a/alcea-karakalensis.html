Alcea karakalensis

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
