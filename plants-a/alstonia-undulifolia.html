Alstonia undulifolia

Alstonia undulifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf alstonia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which have a distinctive wavy margin, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a gelatinous coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
