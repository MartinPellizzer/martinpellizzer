Alsophila obtusa

Alsophila obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, smooth, greyish-brown stem that is covered in a layer of old leaf bases. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent and have a leathery texture with a deep green color.

Alsophila obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and horticulture.
