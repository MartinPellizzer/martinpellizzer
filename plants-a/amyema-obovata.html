Amyema obovata

Amyema obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring, followed by small, edible fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Amyema obovata is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
