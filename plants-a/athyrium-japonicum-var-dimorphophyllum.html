Athyrium japonicum var. dimorphophyllum

Athyrium japonicum var. dimorphophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Athyrium japonicum species.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that display a striking contrast between the fertile and sterile parts. The plant typically features dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive, finely divided structure. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
