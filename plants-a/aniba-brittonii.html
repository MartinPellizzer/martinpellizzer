Aniba brittonii

Aniba brittonii, commonly known as the Florida aniba, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Aniba brittonii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in hammocks and along stream banks.
