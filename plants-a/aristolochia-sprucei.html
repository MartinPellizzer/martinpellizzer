Aristolochia sprucei

Aristolochia sprucei, commonly known as the purple swallow-wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, purple to brown flowers that resemble a bird's nest. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found in moist woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae, which includes several species with similar morphological features.

However, it is important to note that some species in this genus contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
