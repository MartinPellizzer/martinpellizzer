Astronia mearnsii

Astronia mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns' astronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astronia mearnsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and adaptability.
