Aromadendron macklottii

Aromadendron macklottii, commonly known as the Macklott's sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by small, spherical fruits. It is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in perfumery and traditional crafts.
