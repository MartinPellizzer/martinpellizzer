Acacia prostrata

Acacia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate acacia, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features woody stems that lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the functions of true leaves.

Acacia prostrata is well adapted to harsh environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.
