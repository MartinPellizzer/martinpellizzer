Argyrolobium syriacum

Argyrolobium syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian silverlobed pea, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, inflated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Argyrolobium syriacum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
