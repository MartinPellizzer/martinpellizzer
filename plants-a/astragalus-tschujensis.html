Astragalus tschujensis

Astragalus tschujensis, commonly known as Tschuj's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Astragalus tschujensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the extensive Astragalus genus, which includes many species with medicinal and ecological significance.
