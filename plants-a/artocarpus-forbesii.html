Artocarpus forbesii

Artocarpus forbesii, commonly known as the Java breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is typically fibrous and starchy, similar to breadfruit. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes in its native regions.
