Anthurium pedatoradiatum subsp. pedatoradiatum

Anthurium pedatoradiatum subsp.

pedatoradiatum, commonly known as the Radiata Anthurium, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib.

The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that are often red or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

This subspecies is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
