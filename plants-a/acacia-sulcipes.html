Acacia sulcipes

Acacia sulcipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and compound leaves with small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Acacia sulcipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
