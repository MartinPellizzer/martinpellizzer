Acalypha communis var. hirta

Acalypha communis var. hirta, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Velvetleaf is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
