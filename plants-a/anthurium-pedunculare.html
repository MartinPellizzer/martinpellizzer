Anthurium pedunculare

Anthurium pedunculare, commonly known as the "Red Spurflower," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and spathe. The plant features glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple tinge along the veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a showy, brightly colored spathe.

Anthurium pedunculare is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its striking foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
