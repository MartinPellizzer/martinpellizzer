Astragalus filifoliolatus

Astragalus filifoliolatus, commonly known as the filifoliate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with filament-like leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
