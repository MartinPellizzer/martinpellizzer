Acacia caesia var. subnuda

Acacia caesia var. subnuda, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features slender, often spindly stems that are covered in small, smooth leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers during the winter months, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its foliage is typically gray-green, and it is well adapted to sandy soils and salty coastal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
