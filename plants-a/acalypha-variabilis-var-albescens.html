Acalypha variabilis var. albescens

Acalypha variabilis var. albescens, commonly known as the white-veined spurflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with white veins that contrast sharply against the green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
