Agrostis alba subsp. straminea

Agrostis alba subsp.

straminea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale florets. Agrostis alba subsp.

straminea is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
