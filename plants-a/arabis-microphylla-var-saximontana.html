Arabis microphylla var. saximontana

Arabis microphylla var. saximontana, commonly known as the Sierra Point Arabis, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the Sierra Nevada foothills of California.

It features small, rounded, evergreen leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, upright stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
