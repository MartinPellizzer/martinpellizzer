Anacardium rhinocarpus

Anacardium rhinocarpus, commonly known as the horned cashew, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The tree is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a spiny, woody capsule that contains a single seed, often referred to as a "cashew nut.".

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
