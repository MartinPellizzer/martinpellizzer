Anisogonium

Anisogonium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Anisogonium has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
