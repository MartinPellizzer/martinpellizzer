Agoseris purpurea var. arizonica

Agoseris purpurea var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun. Its stems are usually erect and branched, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
