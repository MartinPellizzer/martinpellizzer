Amomum stoloniflorum

Amomum stoloniflorum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous stems that spread horizontally, giving it a stoloniferous growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface and a distinct, spicy aroma.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences, often hidden beneath the foliage.
