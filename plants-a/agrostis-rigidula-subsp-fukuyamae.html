Agrostis rigidula subsp. fukuyamae

Agrostis rigidula subsp.

fukuyamae, commonly known as the Fukuyama hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
