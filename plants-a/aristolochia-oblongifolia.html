Aristolochia oblongifolia

Aristolochia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, heart-shaped base. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are often large and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical composition, including compounds with potential medicinal properties.
