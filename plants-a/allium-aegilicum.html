Allium aegilicum

Allium aegilicum, commonly known as garlic chives, is a hardy perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs and leaves that are used in various cuisines for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and practical for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
