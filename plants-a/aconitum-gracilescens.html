Aconitum gracilescens

Aconitum gracilescens, commonly known as the slender aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
