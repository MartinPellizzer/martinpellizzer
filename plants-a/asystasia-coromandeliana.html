Asystasia coromandeliana

Asystasia coromandeliana, commonly known as the Indian violet or Coromandel violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming dense clusters of foliage. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while the flowers are borne in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Asystasia coromandeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
