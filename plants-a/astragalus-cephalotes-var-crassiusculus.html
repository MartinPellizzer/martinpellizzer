Astragalus cephalotes var. crassiusculus

Astragalus cephalotes var. crassiusculus, commonly known as the thick-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Its pods contain small, hard seeds that are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed or barren landscapes.
