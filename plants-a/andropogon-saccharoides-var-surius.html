Andropogon saccharoides var. surius

Andropogon saccharoides var. surius, commonly known as sugar cane grass, is a tall perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and can grow up to 6 feet in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential biofuel applications.
