Ammannia striatiflora

Ammannia striatiflora, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and leaves that often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Ammannia striatiflora is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
