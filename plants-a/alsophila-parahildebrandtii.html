Alsophila parahildebrandtii

Alsophila parahildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to New Zealand.

It is a tall, evergreen tree fern characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Alsophila parahildebrandtii is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in shaded garden settings.
