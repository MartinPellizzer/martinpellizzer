Astragalus maximowiczii

Astragalus maximowiczii, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in steppes and rocky areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity.
