Aureolaria pectinata var. eurycarpa

Aureolaria pectinata var. eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a broad, flat capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
