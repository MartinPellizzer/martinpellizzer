Arenaria tetraquetra var. culminicola

Arenaria tetraquetra var. culminicola, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions.

It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in high-altitude environments. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
