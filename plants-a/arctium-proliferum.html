Arctium proliferum

Arctium proliferum, commonly known as the greater burdock, is a tall biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by spiny, bur-like fruits. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

Arctium proliferum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
