Asplenium serratifolium

Asplenium serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Asplenium serratifolium is a hardy species that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
