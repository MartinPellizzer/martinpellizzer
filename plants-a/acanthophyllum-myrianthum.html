Acanthophyllum myrianthum

Acanthophyllum myrianthum, commonly known as the many-flowered acanthophyllum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and water-storing tissues.

This species plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
