Alchemilla kiwuensis var. kandtiana

Alchemilla kiwuensis var. kandtiana, commonly known as the Kandt's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens with appropriate conditions.
