Alopecurus affinis

Alopecurus affinis, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Alopecurus affinis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
