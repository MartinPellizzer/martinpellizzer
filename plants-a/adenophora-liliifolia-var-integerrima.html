Adenophora liliifolia var. integerrima

Adenophora liliifolia var. integerrima, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a fibrous root system. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
