Achillea × conrathii

Achillea × conrathii, commonly known as the German yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in garden settings.
