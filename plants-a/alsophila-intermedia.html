Alsophila intermedia

Alsophila intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are bi-directionally pinnate, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

Alsophila intermedia grows slowly and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions.
