Apochloa subtiramulosa

Apochloa subtiramulosa, commonly known as the silky grass or silky chenopod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and succulent leaves that store water.

Apochloa subtiramulosa is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of native vegetation in its habitat.
