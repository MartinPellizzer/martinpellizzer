Acalypha lagoensis var. grandifolia

Acalypha lagoensis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant swampmallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to its appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found near streams or in swampy areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
