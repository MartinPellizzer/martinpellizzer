Arjona tuberosa var. patagonica

Arjona tuberosa var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian arjona, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its tuberous roots are a key feature, storing water and nutrients to help it survive in dry climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
