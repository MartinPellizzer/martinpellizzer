Acanthostemma puberum

Acanthostemma puberum, commonly known as the roughleaf acanthostemma, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
