Arctagrostis glauca

Arctagrostis glauca, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its compact stature and dense growth make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Arctagrostis glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
