Aster bachtiaricus

Aster bachtiaricus, commonly known as the Bachtiaric aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster bachtiaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
