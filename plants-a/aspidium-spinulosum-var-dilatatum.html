Aspidium spinulosum var. dilatatum

Aspidium spinulosum var. dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Aspidium.

It is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a delicate, lace-like appearance.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the margins of ponds and lakes.
