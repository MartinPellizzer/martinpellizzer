Adiantum hispidulum f. strictum

Adiantum hispidulum f. strictum, commonly known as the stiff frond maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its upright, compact growth habit.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a serrated margin and a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant produces small, dark brown sori on the underside of its leaves, typical of the Adiantum genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
