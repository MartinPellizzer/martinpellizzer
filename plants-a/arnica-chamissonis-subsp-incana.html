Arnica chamissonis subsp. incana

Arnica chamissonis subsp.

incana, commonly known as the hoary arnica, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its subspecific name, *inca*, refers to its soft, fuzzy appearance.
