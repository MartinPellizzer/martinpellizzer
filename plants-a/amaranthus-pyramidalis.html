Amaranthus pyramidalis

Amaranthus pyramidalis, commonly known as the triangular amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial in arid environments.

Amaranthus pyramidalis is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
