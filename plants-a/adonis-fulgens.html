Adonis fulgens

Adonis fulgens, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces striking yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that bloom on tall, slender stems. Adonis fulgens thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
