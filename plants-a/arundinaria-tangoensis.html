Arundinaria tangoensis

Arundinaria tangoensis, commonly known as Tango bamboo, is a species of grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
