Alatiglossum chrysopterum

Alatiglossum chrysopterum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alatiglossum chrysopterum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
