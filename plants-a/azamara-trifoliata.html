Azamara trifoliata

Azamara trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf azamara, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Azamara trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
