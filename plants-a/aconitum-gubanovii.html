Aconitum gubanovii

Aconitum gubanovii, commonly known as the Gubanovii monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence adorned with deep purple, hood-shaped flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with serrated edges, typical of many members of its family. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
