Arthropogon sorengii

Arthropogon sorengii, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist to wet soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a culm that is usually slender and erect.

Arthropogon sorengii is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
