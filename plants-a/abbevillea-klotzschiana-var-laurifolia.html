Abbevillea klotzschiana var. laurifolia

Abbevillea klotzschiana var. laurifolia, commonly known as the laurelleaf abbevillea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in suitable climates.
