Apluda varia var. intermedia

Apluda varia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Apluda, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its botanical characteristics include fibrous roots and a clump-forming growth habit.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
