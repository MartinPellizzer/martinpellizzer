Asprella imbricata

Asprella imbricata, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asprella imbricata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
