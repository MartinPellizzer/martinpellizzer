Ardisia rubricaulis

Ardisia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and distinctive red stems that become more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
