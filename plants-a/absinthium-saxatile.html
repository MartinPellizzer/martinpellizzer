Absinthium saxatile

Absinthium saxatile, commonly known as mountain wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, with a strongly aromatic scent, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

Absinthium saxatile is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.
