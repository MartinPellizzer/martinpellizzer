Asparagus tamaboki

Asparagus tamaboki, commonly known as the Japanese asparagus, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiny berries containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both decorative and culinary purposes.
