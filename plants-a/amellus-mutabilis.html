Amellus mutabilis

Amellus mutabilis, commonly known as the ever-changing flower, is a unique plant species characterized by its ability to alter its floral morphology throughout its blooming cycle.

Its flowers start as white and gradually shift to pink, then red, and finally orange, creating a dynamic visual display. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its striking color transformation, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens and private collections.
