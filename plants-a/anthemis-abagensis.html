Anthemis abagensis

Anthemis abagensis, commonly known as the Abagense ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
