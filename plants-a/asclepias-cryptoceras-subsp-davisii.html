Asclepias cryptoceras subsp. davisii

Asclepias cryptoceras subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, orange-tangerine flowers.

The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain numerous silky-haired seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is an important host plant for the larvae of several butterfly species, including the painted lady.
