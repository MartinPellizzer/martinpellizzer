Amaranthus pendulinus

Amaranthus pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its drooping, branched inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pink to red in color, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
