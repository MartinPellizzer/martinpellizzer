Absinthium canariense

Absinthium canariense, commonly known as Canary wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its historical use in herbal medicine, it has also been cultivated for its aromatic properties.
