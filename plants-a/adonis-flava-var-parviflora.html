Adonis flava var. parviflora

Adonis flava var. parviflora, commonly known as the lesser adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are typically short-stemmed and bloom before the leaves fully develop.

It is valued in horticulture for its early blooming habit and ornamental appeal.
