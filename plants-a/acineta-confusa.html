Acineta confusa

Acineta confusa, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils near the shoreline. It is often found in dune environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind.

Acineta confusa plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for various small insects and birds.
