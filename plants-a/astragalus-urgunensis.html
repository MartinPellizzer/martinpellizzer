Astragalus urgunensis

Astragalus urgunensis, commonly known as the Altai milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces slender, erect stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the broader Astragalus genus, which includes many species with medicinal and forage value.

Its ecological significance is notable in arid and semi-arid regions.
