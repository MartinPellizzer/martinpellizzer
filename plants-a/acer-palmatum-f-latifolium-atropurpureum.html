Acer palmatum f. latifolium-atropurpureum

Acer palmatum f. latifolium-atropurpureum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with broad leaves and purple foliage, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that turn a striking purple-red in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.

Its unique leaf coloration and elegant form make it a standout in horticultural collections.
