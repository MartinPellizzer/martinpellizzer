Abutilon trichodum

Abutilon trichodum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Abutilon trichodum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
