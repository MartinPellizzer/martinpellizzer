Autogenes × biflorus

Autogenes × biflorus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two distinct orchid species.

It exhibits a combination of morphological traits from both parent plants, often displaying unique flower colors and structures. The plant typically features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually borne on terminal racemes, showcasing a range of hues from white to deep purple.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
