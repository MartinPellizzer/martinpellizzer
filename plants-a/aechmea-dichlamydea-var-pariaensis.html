Aechmea dichlamydea var. pariaensis

Aechmea dichlamydea var. pariaensis, commonly known as the Pariensis Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central rosette, which collects rainwater to support its growth. The plant produces inflorescences with vibrant red bracts that enclose small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
