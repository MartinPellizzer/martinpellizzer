Achyranthes argentea var. annua

Achyranthes argentea var. annua, commonly known as silverleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect stems that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

This plant is commonly found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
