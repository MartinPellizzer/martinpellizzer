Artemisia ludoviciana

Artemisia ludoviciana, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to the United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which contains aromatic essential oils that give it a distinctive, minty scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various healing purposes.
