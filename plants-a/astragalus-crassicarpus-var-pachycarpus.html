Astragalus crassicarpus var. pachycarpus

Astragalus crassicarpus var. pachycarpus, commonly known as the thick-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces thick, fleshy pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of forage for livestock.
