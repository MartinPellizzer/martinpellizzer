Allium insubricum

Allium insubricum, commonly known as the Italian garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium insubricum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
