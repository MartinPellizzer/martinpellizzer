Adesmia bracteata

Adesmia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

Adesmia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some areas.
