Aristolochia tarapotina

Aristolochia tarapotina, commonly known as the Andean Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
