Acoridium rufum

Acoridium rufum, commonly known as the red-flowered acoridium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family, which includes aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are long and narrow, typically submerged or floating in water, adapting to its wetland habitat.

Acoridium rufum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
