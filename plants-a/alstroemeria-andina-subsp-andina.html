Alstroemeria andina subsp. andina

Alstroemeria andina subsp.

andina, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and ample sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The species is also valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
