Acacia bahamensis

Acacia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Bahamas.

It features compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces yellow, spherical flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its root system helps stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.

Acacia bahamensis plays a vital role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
