Abutilon glabriflorum

Abutilon glabriflorum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant valued for its unique appearance and adaptability.
