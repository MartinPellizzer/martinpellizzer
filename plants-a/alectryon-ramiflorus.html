Alectryon ramiflorus

Alectryon ramiflorus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Alectryon ramiflorus make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in botanical gardens and landscapes.
