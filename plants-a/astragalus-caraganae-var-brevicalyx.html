Astragalus caraganae var. brevicalyx

Astragalus caraganae var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
