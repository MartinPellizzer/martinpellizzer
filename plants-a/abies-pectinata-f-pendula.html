Abies pectinata f. pendula

Abies pectinata f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping silver fir, is a unique variant of the silver fir tree characterized by its drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance.

It is a deciduous conifer, shedding its needle-like leaves in the fall, which is uncommon among most fir species. The plant features soft, silvery-blue needles that are arranged in flat sprays, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

This cultivar is prized for its graceful form and ability to add a dramatic visual element to landscape designs.
