Astragalus knorringianus

Astragalus knorringianus, commonly known as the Knorring's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus knorringianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
