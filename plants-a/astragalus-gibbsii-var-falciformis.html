Astragalus gibbsii var. falciformis

Astragalus gibbsii var. falciformis, commonly known as Gibbs' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are pinnately divided, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
