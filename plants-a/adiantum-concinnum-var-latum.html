Adiantum concinnum var. latum

Adiantum concinnum var. latum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are dark green and deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
