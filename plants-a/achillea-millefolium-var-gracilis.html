Achillea millefolium var. gracilis

Achillea millefolium var. gracilis, commonly known as slender yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance, and its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species within the Asteraceae family.
