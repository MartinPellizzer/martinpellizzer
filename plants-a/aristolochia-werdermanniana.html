Aristolochia werdermanniana

Aristolochia werdermanniana, commonly known as the Werdermann's birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-like flowers that often have a strong, aromatic scent. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral morphology. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
