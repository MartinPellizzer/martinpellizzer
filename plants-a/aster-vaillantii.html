Aster vaillantii

Aster vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Aster vaillantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
