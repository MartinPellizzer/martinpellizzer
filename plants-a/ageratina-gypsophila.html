Ageratina gypsophila

Ageratina gypsophila, commonly known as the gypsum ageratum, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy inflorescences that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a hairy texture.

Ageratina gypsophila is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
