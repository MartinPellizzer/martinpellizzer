Abietia douglasii var. glauca

Abietia douglasii var. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a variant of the Douglas fir that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States, typically found in mountainous regions. The plant has needle-like leaves that are bluish-gray in color, giving it a striking appearance. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its slow growth and longevity, often living over a century.
