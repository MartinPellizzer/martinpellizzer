Antholyza meriana

Antholyza meriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Antholyza meriana is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in certain environments.
