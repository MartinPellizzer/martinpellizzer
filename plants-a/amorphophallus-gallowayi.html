Amorphophallus gallowayi

Amorphophallus gallowayi, commonly known as the Galloway arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces a large, leafy stem that can grow up to several feet tall, with heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and sometimes variegated. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spadices, a feature common in the Araceae family.

The plant is adapted to moist, rich soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
