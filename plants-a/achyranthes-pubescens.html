Achyranthes pubescens

Achyranthes pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a tropical and subtropical plant native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
