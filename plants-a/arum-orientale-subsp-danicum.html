Arum orientale subsp. danicum

Arum orientale subsp.

danicum, commonly known as the Danubian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Danube region of Europe. It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is typically dark green with a silvery underside.

The plant produces a spatulate, pale green to white spathe that encloses a spadix, which bears small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in garden settings.
