Astragalus drabelliformis

Astragalus drabelliformis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its habitat.

Astragalus drabelliformis is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert grassland ecosystems.
