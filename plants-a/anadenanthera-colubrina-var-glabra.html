Anadenanthera colubrina var. glabra

Anadenanthera colubrina var. glabra, commonly known as the copal tree or sacred tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing a sticky, resinous substance called copal. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
