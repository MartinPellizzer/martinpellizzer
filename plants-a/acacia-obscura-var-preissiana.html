Acacia obscura var. preissiana

Acacia obscura var. preissiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces yellow flowers in winter. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
