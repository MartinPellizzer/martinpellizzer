Anemone Narcissiflora Var. Sibirica

Siberian anemone, scientifically known as Anemone narcissiflora var. sibirica, is a striking perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China. It is celebrated for its early spring blooms, which appear before the leaves unfurl, creating a delicate display of white or pale pink flowers. This hardy species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its frost-resistant nature allows it to survive harsh winters, while its elegant appearance adds a touch of refinement to any garden. With its historical significance in traditional medicine and its role in pollinator support, the Siberian anemone remains a valued and resilient plant in the botanical world.
