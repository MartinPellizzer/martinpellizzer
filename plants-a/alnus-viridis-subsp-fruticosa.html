Alnus viridis subsp. fruticosa

Alnus viridis subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the green alder, is a shrub-like species native to North America. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and catkin-like flowers, which are typically yellow-green in color.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland edges. It has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful plant for erosion control and reforestation efforts.
