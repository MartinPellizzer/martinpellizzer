Achillea halleri

Achillea halleri, commonly known as lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Achillea halleri is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
