Abutilon mauritianum

Abutilon mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a rough, hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often found in disturbed areas and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
