Androcymbium gramineum var. intermedium

Androcymbium gramineum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate grass orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of greenish-white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
