Asarum geophilum

Asarum geophilum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a strong, spicy aroma. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its rhizomatous root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Asarum geophilum thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest floors and woodland areas.
