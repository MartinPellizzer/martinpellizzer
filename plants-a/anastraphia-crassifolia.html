Anastraphia crassifolia

Anastraphia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved anastraphia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring or early summer. Its stems are upright and can grow up to several feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Anastraphia crassifolia is adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
