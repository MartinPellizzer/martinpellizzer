Anabasis iranica

Anabasis iranica, commonly known as the Iranian bladderflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert landscapes.

Anabasis iranica is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
