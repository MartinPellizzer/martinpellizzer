Abelmoschus abelmoschus

Abelmoschus abelmoschus, commonly known as okra, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous edible seeds surrounded by a mucilaginous pulp. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Okra is widely cultivated for its young pods, which are used in various culinary traditions around the world.
