Agapanthus umbellatus var. weillighii

Agapanthus umbellatus var. weillighii, commonly known as the blue agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, umbellate inflorescences composed of numerous blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
