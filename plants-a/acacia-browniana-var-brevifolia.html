Acacia browniana var. brevifolia

Acacia browniana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It features slender, often zigzagging branches and small, phyllodes that are narrow and linear, typically less than 3 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
