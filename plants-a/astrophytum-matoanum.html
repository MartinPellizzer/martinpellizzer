Astrophytum matoanum

Astrophytum matoanum, commonly known as the "Bottle Tree," is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the top of the stems. Its unique appearance and ability to store water make it well adapted to desert environments.

Astrophytum matoanum is also known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
