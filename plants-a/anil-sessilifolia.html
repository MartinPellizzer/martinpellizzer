Anil sessilifolia

Anil sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved anil, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack a petiole. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Anil sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
