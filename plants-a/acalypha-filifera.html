Acalypha filifera

Acalypha filifera, commonly known as silky acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
