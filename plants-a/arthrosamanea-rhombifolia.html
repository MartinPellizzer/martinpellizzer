Arthrosamanea rhombifolia

Arthrosamanea rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
