Asyneuma sibthorpianum

Asyneuma sibthorpianum, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Asyneuma sibthorpianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
