Arundinaria macrosperma

Arundinaria macrosperma, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are typically green and smooth. This plant is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Arundinaria macrosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, making it suitable for both garden and landscape settings.
