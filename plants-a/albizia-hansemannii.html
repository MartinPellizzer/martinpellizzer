Albizia hansemannii

Albizia hansemannii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are compound and consist of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, typically blooming in the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Albizia hansemannii is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
