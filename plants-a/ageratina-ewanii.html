Ageratina ewanii

Ageratina ewanii, commonly known as the velvetleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
