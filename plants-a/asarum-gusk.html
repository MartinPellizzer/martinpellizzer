Asarum gusk

Asarum gusk, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spathaceous flowers. The plant produces a dark brown, berry-like fruit that contains several small seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and rocky areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for various ailments.
