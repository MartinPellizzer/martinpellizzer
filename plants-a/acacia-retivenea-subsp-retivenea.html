Acacia retivenea subsp. retivenea

Acacia retivenea subsp.

retivenea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear in spring. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
