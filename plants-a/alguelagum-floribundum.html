Alguelagum floribundum

Alguelagum floribundum, commonly known as the golden bloom orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
