Aristolochia schulzii

Aristolochia schulzii, commonly known as the Schultze's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a complex, intricate structure. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
