Arachnorchis aestiva

Arachnorchis aestiva, commonly known as the summer spider-orchid, is an Australian orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow labellum. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in spring to early summer, producing fragrant, cream to pale yellow flowers.

Arachnorchis aestiva is noted for its delicate structure and is often found in the southeastern regions of Australia.
