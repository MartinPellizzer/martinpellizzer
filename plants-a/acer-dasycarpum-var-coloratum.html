Acer dasycarpum var. coloratum

Acer dasycarpum var. coloratum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that changes with the seasons, ranging from bright green in spring to fiery red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant has opposite, palmately compound leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that disperse its seeds.

This ornamental tree thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
