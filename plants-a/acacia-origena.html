Acacia origena

Acacia origena, commonly known as the African silver acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Acacia origena is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
