Abronia × alba var. platyphylla

Abronia × alba var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. This variety is a hybrid between Abronia × alba and A. platyphylla, exhibiting traits from both parent species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
