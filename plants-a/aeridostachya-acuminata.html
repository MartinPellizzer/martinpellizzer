Aeridostachya acuminata

Aeridostachya acuminata, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often standing in water or damp soil. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically emerging from the base of the plant.

Aeridostachya acuminata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
