Alyssum tortuosum f. strictum

Alyssum tortuosum f. strictum, commonly known as the compact alyssum, is a compact, low-growing plant with dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and tiny, rounded leaves. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for edging and container gardening.
