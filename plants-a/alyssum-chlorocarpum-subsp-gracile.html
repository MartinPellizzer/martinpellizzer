Alyssum chlorocarpum subsp. gracile

Alyssum chlorocarpum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as slender alyssum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its compact, low-growing habit.

The subspecies gracile is distinguished by its slender stems and reduced leaf size compared to other subspecies of Alyssum chlorocarpum.
