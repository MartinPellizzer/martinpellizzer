Anemone ranunculoides var. gracilis

Anemone ranunculoides var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety *gracilis* is distinguished by its more slender stems and smaller, more refined foliage compared to the typical form.
