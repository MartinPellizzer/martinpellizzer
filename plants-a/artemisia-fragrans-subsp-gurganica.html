Artemisia fragrans subsp. gurganica

Artemisia fragrans subsp.

gurganica, commonly known as the fragrant wormwood, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, medicinal scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive properties.
