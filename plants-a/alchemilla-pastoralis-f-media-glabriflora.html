Alchemilla pastoralis f. media-glabriflora

Alchemilla pastoralis f. media-glabriflora, commonly known as the slender lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters during late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative appearance and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
