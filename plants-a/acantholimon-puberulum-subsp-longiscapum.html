Acantholimon puberulum subsp. longiscapum

Acantholimon puberulum subsp.

longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It is a low-growing perennial with erect, branching stems that bear small, yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy leaves and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
