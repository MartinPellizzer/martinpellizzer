Arrhenatherum kotschyi

Arrhenatherum kotschyi, commonly known as Kotschyi's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
