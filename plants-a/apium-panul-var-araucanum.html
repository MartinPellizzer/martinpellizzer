Apium panul var. araucanum

Apium panul var. araucanum, commonly known as the Araucanian celery, is a lesser-known variant of the celery genus.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, aromatic stems and small, clustered umbels of white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional Andean cuisine for their distinctive flavor.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique morphology and genetic distinctiveness within the Apium species.
