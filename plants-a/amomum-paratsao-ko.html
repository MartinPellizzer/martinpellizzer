Amomum paratsao-ko

Amomum paratsao-ko, commonly known as the Yunnan cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, aromatic stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark purple, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties, often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is cultivated for both its culinary and therapeutic applications.
