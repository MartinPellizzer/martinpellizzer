Alectorurus

Alectorurus, commonly known as the African silvergrass, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It consists of several species, most notably Alectorurus capensis, which is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance. The plants typically grow in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Alectorurus species are known for their slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers.

They play a minor role in local ecosystems but are of interest to botanists due to their unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
