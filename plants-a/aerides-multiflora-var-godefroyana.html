Aerides multiflora var. godefroyana

Aerides multiflora var. godefroyana, commonly known as the Godefroy's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest undergrowth. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, and it blooms during the rainy season.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
