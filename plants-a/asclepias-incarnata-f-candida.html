Asclepias incarnata f. candida

Asclepias incarnata f. candida, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a cultivar of the showy milkweed species.

It is characterized by its pale white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, contrasting with the typical pink or red of the species. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to the eastern and central United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.
