Angelica tschiliensis

Angelica tschiliensis, commonly known as the Chilean angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-like compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which eventually develop into spherical, dry fruits.

Angelica tschiliensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
