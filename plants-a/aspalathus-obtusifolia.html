Aspalathus obtusifolia

Aspalathus obtusifolia, commonly known as African red bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Aspalathus obtusifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
