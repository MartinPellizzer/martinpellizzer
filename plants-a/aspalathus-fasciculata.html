Aspalathus fasciculata

Aspalathus fasciculata, commonly known as the African green bushveld pea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Aspalathus fasciculata is often found in rocky soils and is a valuable component of local ecosystems.
