Atractylis carduus var. angustifolia

Atractylis carduus var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a rough texture.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its aromatic properties.
