Arnica plantaginea

Arnica plantaginea, commonly known as the meadow arnica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are broadly ovate with a rough texture.

Arnica plantaginea has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
