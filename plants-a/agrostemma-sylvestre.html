Agrostemma sylvestre

Agrostemma sylvestre, commonly known as wild flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its seeds are encased in a tough, fibrous coat, and the plant is known for its rapid growth cycle.

Agrostemma sylvestre has historical uses in traditional medicine and has also been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
