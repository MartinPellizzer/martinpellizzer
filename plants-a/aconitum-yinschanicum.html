Aconitum yinschanicum

Aconitum yinschanicum, commonly known as the Yushan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. Aconitum yinschanicum contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
