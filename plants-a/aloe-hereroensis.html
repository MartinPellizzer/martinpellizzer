Aloe hereroensis

Aloe hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero aloe, is a succulent plant native to Namibia and Angola.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

Aloe hereroensis thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
