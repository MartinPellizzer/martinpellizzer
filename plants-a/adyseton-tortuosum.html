Adyseton tortuosum

Adyseton tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted wood fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, branched fronds that give it a distinctive and often whimsical appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, which helps it store water and nutrients.

The fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year of growth.

Morphology

Adyseton tortuosum has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface and a dark green color, typically ranging from 2 to 5 centimeters in length.

The stem is slender and cylindrical, with a rough, grayish-brown bark that peels in thin, vertical strips. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring, and they are pollinated by insects such as bees.
