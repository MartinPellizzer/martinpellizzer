Ateramnus lucidus

Ateramnus lucidus, commonly known as the glossy ateramnus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that reflect light, giving it a shiny appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
