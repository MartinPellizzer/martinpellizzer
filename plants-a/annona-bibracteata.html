Annona bibracteata

Annona bibracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually yellow with a distinctive red or purple calyx, and it produces a fleshy, yellow or orange fruit that is edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which has a flavor similar to that of a papaya.
