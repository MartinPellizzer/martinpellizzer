Annona incana

Annona incana, commonly known as the rough-skinned custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive, fleshy corona.

The fruit is a rough, hairy berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.
