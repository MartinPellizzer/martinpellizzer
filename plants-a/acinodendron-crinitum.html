Acinodendron crinitum

Acinodendron crinitum, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has feathery, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
