Arachniodes × kurosawae

Arachniodes × kurosawae, commonly known as the Japanese spider fern, is a hybrid fern species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is native to Japan, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant is distinguished by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. It produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
