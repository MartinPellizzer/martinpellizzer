Allium tenuicaule

Allium tenuicaule, commonly known as the slender-leaved garlic or dwarf leek, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spherical bulbs. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is increasingly cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
