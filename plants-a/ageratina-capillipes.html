Ageratina capillipes

Ageratina capillipes, commonly known as the silky ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, fluffy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
