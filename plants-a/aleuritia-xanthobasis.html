Aleuritia xanthobasis

Aleuritia xanthobasis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered elder, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the fall.

Aleuritia xanthobasis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
