Anemia dissecta

Anemia dissecta, commonly known as the dissected anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and clematis.
