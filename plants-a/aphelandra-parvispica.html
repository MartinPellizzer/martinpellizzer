Aphelandra parvispica

Aphelandra parvispica, commonly known as the small-spurred flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, fiery orange to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Aphelandra parvispica thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and dramatic inflorescences.
