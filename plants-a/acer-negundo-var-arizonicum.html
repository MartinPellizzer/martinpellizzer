Acer negundo var. arizonicum

Acer negundo var. arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves that split into five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by samaras with two distinct wings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and open woodlands.

The Arizona boxelder is a fast-growing species that can reach heights of up to 30 feet.
