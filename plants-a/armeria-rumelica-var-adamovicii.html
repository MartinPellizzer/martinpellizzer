Armeria rumelica var. adamovicii

Armeria rumelica var. adamovicii, commonly known as the Armenian sandpink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense clusters of small, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to thrive in arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for rock gardens or dry landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to alpine and Mediterranean-style gardens.
