Anagyris latifolia

Anagyris latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by spiny, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Anagyris latifolia is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
