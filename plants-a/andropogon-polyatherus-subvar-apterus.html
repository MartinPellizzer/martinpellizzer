Andropogon polyatherus subvar. apterus

Andropogon polyatherus subvar. apterus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves and tall, slender flowering stalks that produce small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in native plant communities.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
