Achyranthes emersonii

Achyranthes emersonii, commonly known as the Emerson's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish to reddish in color.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
