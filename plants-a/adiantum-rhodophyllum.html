Adiantum rhodophyllum

Adiantum rhodophyllum, commonly known as the red fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Adiantum rhodophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
