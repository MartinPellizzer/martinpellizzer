Anisotes subcoriaceus

Anisotes subcoriaceus, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are thick and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it thrives in arid and rocky environments.
