Achillea marginata

Achillea marginata, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring a stout, hairy stem and basal leaves that are pinnately divided. The plant produces flat, clustered inflorescences of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
