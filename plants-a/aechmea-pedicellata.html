Aechmea pedicellata

Aechmea pedicellata, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy coating, and it thrives in humid, well-lit environments.

Aechmea pedicellata is popular as a ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea pedicellata has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a compact spiral, with a waxy, dark green color and a pointed tip.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, fibrous rhizome that supports the rosette and helps anchor it to the substrate. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence, with tubular, violet-blue petals and yellow anthers, blooming during the rainy season and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals consuming the fruit.
