Aconitum cymbulatum subsp. fastigiatum

Aconitum cymbulatum subsp.

fastigiatum, commonly known as the purple aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.
