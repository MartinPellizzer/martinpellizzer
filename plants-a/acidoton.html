Acidoton

Acidoton, commonly known as the acid-ton plant, is a unique species characterized by its ability to thrive in highly acidic soil conditions.

It possesses waxy, broad leaves that help reduce water loss in its native environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb nutrients from poor soil.

Acidoton is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
