Aceratium versteegii

Aceratium versteegii, commonly known as the Versteeg's acanthus, is a rare and striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. Native to a limited geographic range in South Africa, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
