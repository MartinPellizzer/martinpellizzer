Anisotes trisulcus

Anisotes trisulcus, commonly known as the three-lobed water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its submerged leaves that are deeply lobed, typically with three distinct divisions. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and are borne on short stalks.

Anisotes trisulcus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
