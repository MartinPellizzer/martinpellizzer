Aquilegia vulgaris var. transsilvanica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian columbine, is a variant of the common columbine native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple, spurred flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and lobed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
