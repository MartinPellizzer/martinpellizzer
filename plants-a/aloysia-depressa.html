Aloysia depressa

Aloysia depressa, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is also known for its fragrant foliage, which can be used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
