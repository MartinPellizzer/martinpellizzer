Anaphalis aureopunctata var. aureopunctata

Anaphalis aureopunctata var. aureopunctata, commonly known as the golden dot tickseed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely toothed, silver-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
