Anthopogon procerus var. tonsus

Anthopogon procerus var. tonsus, commonly known as the slender rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
