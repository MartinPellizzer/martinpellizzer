Acacia johnwoodii

Acacia johnwoodii, commonly known as the Johnwood's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and phyllodes that are usually green and sometimes tinged with red. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia johnwoodii is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils.
