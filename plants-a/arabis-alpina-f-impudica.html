Arabis alpina f. impudica

Arabis alpina f. impudica, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
