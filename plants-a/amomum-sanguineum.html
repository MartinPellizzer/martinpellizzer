Amomum sanguineum

Amomum sanguineum, commonly known as the blood ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish hue on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically displaying a mix of white and pink hues.

The rhizomes of Amomum sanguineum are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic and potentially medicinal properties.
