Ansellia nilotica

Ansellia nilotica, commonly known as the African lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faint pinkish tinge. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ansellia nilotica is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a potted plant.
