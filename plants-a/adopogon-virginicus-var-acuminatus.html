Adopogon virginicus var. acuminatus

Adopogon virginicus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
