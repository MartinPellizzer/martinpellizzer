Apteranthes europaea var. schmuckiana

Apteranthes europaea var. schmuckiana, commonly known as the European bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.
