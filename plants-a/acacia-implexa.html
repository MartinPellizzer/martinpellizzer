Acacia implexa

Acacia implexa, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and its unique, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches, giving it a distinctive appearance. Acacia implexa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
