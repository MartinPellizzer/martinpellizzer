Ardisia curtiflora

Ardisia curtiflora, commonly known as the dwarf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, bright blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Ardisia curtiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
