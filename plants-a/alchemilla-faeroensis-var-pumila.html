Alchemilla faeroensis var. pumila

Alchemilla faeroensis var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the volcanic soils of Iceland.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features silvery-white, woolly foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in compact inflorescences.

This hardy species is adapted to harsh alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
