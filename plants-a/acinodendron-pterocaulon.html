Acinodendron pterocaulon

Acinodendron pterocaulon, commonly known as the winged acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces yellowish to greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its unique feature is the presence of winged, flattened pods that split open to release seeds.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
