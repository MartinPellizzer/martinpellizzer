Anthochloa colusana

Anthochloa colusana, commonly known as the white flag grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Anthochloa colusana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
