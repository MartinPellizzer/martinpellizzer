Astragalus affinis

Astragalus affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus affinis is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native pollinators.
