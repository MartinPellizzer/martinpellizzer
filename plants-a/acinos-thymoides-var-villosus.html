Acinos thymoides var. villosus

Acinos thymoides var. villosus, commonly known as woolly thyme.

This plant is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. It features small, aromatic leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in compact clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats across the western United States.
