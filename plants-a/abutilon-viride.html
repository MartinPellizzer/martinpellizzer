Abutilon viride

Abutilon viride, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
