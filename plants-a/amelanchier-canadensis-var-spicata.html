Amelanchier canadensis var. spicata

Amelanchier canadensis var. spicata, commonly known as the spicata serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features opposite, oval to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a hardy landscape plant.

The spicata variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
