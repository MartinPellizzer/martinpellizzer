Aristida gypsophila f. gypsophiloides

Aristida gypsophila f. gypsophiloides, commonly known as the gypsum grass, is a variant of the species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit.

This form is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
