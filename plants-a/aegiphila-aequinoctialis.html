Aegiphila aequinoctialis

Aegiphila aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of violet or purple. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
