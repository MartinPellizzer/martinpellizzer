Asplenium fimbriatum var. sphaeropteroides

Asplenium fimbriatum var. sphaeropteroides, commonly known as the globular shield fern, is a variant of the common holly fern.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit, with leaves that form a dense, rounded cluster. The fronds are typically short and broad, with a distinctive, shield-shaped appearance. This variety is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to botanical gardens and collections.
