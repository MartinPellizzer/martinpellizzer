Allionia incarnata f. obtusilobata

Allionia incarnata f. obtusilobata, commonly known as the blunt-lobed allionia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a bluish-green hue with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, drought-tolerant plants.
