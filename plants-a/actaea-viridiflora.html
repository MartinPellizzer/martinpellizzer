Actaea viridiflora

Actaea viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered bugbane, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often noted for its vibrant green color, which contrasts with the pale flowers.

Actaea viridiflora is typically found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
