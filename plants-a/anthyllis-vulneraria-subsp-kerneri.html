Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. kerneri

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as Kern's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, blue to purple flowers in early spring.

The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
