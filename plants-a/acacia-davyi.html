Acacia davyi

Acacia davyi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads that bloom in summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia davyi is also valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
