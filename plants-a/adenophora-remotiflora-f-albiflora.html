Adenophora remotiflora f. albiflora

Adenophora remotiflora f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered adenophora, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its overall appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
