Argemone mexicana var. subinermis

Argemone mexicana var. subinermis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features showy, orange-red flowers with prominent, spiny seed pods that split open to release its seeds. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a weed and a plant of interest in ecological studies.
