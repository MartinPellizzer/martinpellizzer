Arundinella virgata

Arundinella virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. Arundinella virgata typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
