Alchemilla hirsutopetiolata

Alchemilla hirsutopetiolata, commonly known as the hairy-leaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
