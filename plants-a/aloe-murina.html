Aloe murina

Aloe murina, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing orange or red flowers, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe murina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
