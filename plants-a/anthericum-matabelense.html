Anthericum matabelense

Anthericum matabelense, commonly known as the Matabelense arum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features a tuberous root system and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
