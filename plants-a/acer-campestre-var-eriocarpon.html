Acer campestre var. eriocarpon

Acer campestre var. eriocarpon, commonly known as the downy maple, is a variant of the field maple.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its ovate to elliptic leaves with a hairy underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras that mature in late summer.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
