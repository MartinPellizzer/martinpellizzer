Agropyron tanaiticum var. glabriusculum

Agropyron tanaiticum var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the smooth wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed sites, often forming dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

This species is valued for its forage quality and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
