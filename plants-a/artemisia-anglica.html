Artemisia anglica

Artemisia anglica, commonly known as mugwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which are typically visited by insects. Mugwort is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

It has been used in traditional medicine and as a component in herbal remedies for various ailments.
