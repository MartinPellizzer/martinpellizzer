Agauria salicifolia var. latissima

Agauria salicifolia var. latissima, commonly known as the broad-leaved agauria, is a variant of the agauria species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which are often borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical or subtropical regions.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
