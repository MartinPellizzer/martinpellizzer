Arisaema calcareum

Arisaema calcareum, commonly known as the limestone arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to parts of China and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in limestone-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
