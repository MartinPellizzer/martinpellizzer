Arundinaria kogasensis

Arundinaria kogasensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Arundinaria kogasensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
