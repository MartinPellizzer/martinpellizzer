Acacia flaccida

Acacia flaccida, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Acacia flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
