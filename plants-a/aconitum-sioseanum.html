Aconitum sioseanum

Aconitum sioseanum, commonly known as the Siosean monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmate leaves with a deeply divided structure, and its stems are usually tall and upright. Aconitum sioseanum is known for its toxicity, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
