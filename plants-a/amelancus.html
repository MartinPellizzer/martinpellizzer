Amelancus

Amelancus, commonly known as the false holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Amelancus species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in horticulture.
