Allium anisopodium subsp. argunense

Allium anisopodium subsp.

argunense, commonly known as the Armenian leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem base, which is often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Alliaceae family, which includes other members like onions and garlic.
