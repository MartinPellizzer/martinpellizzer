Aster lanceolatus var. hirsuticaulis

Aster lanceolatus var. hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
