Amblyglottis flava

Amblyglottis flava, commonly known as the yellow root or yellow amblyglottis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its roots are tuberous and have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

Amblyglottis flava thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
