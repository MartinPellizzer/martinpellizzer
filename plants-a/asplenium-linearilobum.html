Asplenium linearilobum

Asplenium linearilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often divided into narrow lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually evergreen, and it produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori.

Asplenium linearilobum is relatively uncommon and is often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
