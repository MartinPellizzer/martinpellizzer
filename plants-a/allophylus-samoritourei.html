Allophylus samoritourei

Allophylus samoritourei, commonly known as the Samorito alpine holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
