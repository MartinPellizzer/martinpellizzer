Asplenium flabellifolium var. dissectum

Asplenium flabellifolium var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maidenhair fern, is a variant of the flabellifolium maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a delicate and intricate appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The fronds are typically bi-directionally pinnate, with a distinctive fan-shaped structure in the mature plant.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
