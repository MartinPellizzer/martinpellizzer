Amelanchier paniculata

Amelanchier paniculata, commonly known as the fragrant crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flower clusters that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in the fall, often turning red or purple. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant yellow or red in autumn.

Amelanchier paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
