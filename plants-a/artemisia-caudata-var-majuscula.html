Artemisia caudata var. majuscula

Artemisia caudata var. majuscula, commonly known as the tall sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid-land ecosystems.
