Anthemis cyllenea

Anthemis cyllenea, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, aromatic scent, which is often described as similar to that of tansy. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, bitter aroma.

Anthemis cyllenea is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.
