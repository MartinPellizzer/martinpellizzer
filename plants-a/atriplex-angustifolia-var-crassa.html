Atriplex angustifolia var. crassa

Atriplex angustifolia var. crassa, commonly known as saltbush or New Zealand spinach, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fleshy seeds. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or inland salt flats.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
