Acetosa olitoria

Acetosa olitoria, commonly known as watercress, is a leafy green vegetable belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, peppery leaves that are typically harvested before the plant flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich environments, often found near streams or in wet soils. Its leaves contain high levels of vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like calcium and iron.

Acetosa olitoria is widely cultivated for its crisp texture and distinctive flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and soups.
