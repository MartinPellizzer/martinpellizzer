Anguria triphylla

Anguria triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Its fruit is a small, spiny cucumber that is often used in local cuisines.

Anguria triphylla thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its edible fruit and ornamental value.
