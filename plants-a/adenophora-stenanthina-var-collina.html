Adenophora stenanthina var. collina

Adenophora stenanthina var. collina, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in cultivating native flora.
