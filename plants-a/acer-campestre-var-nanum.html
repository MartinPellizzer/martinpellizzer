Acer campestre var. nanum

Acer campestre var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, rounded form, making it ideal for small gardens or rockeries. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. It produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.

Native Distribution

Acer campestre var. nanum is primarily native to southern Europe, including countries such as Spain, Portugal, Italy, and parts of France.

It is also found in the Iberian Peninsula and extends into northern Africa, particularly Morocco and Algeria. This variety of the common maple is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often growing in mountainous regions. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Its native distribution reflects its preference for sunny, arid environments across these regions.

Introduced Distribution

Acer campestre var. nanum was mainly introduced to urban and suburban landscapes due to its compact size and ornamental value.
