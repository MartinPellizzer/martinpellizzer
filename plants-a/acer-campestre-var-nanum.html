Acer campestre var. nanum

Acer campestre var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, rounded form, making it ideal for small gardens or rockeries. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. It produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
