Argyroderma citrinum

Argyroderma citrinum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green and silver foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
