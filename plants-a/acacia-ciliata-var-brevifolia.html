Acacia ciliata var. brevifolia

Acacia ciliata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery foliage and produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, stipulate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
