Apium romanum

Apium romanum, commonly known as wild celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its aromatic leaves and stems are used in cooking and have a long history of medicinal use.

Apium romanum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
