Aspalathus lactea var. zeyheri

Aspalathus lactea var. zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its low-growing nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
