Aristolochia roxburghiana var. angustifolia

Aristolochia roxburghiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-brown with a strong, aromatic odor. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
