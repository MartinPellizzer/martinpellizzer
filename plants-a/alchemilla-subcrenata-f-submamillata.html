Alchemilla subcrenata f. submamillata

Alchemilla subcrenata f. submamillata, commonly known as the silverleaf alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of silver-green, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
