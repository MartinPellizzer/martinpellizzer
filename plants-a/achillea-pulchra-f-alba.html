Achillea pulchra f. alba

Achillea pulchra f. alba, commonly known as the White Sneezeweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
