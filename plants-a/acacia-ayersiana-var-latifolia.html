Acacia ayersiana var. latifolia

Acacia ayersiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are broad and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
