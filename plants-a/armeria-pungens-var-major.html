Armeria pungens var. major

Armeria pungens var. major, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and spikes of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. It is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
