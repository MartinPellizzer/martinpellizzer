Aconitum pyrenaicum

Aconitum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pyrenees mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant has dark green, doubly pinnate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Aconitum pyrenaicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant to be handled with care.
