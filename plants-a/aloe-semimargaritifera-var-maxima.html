Aloe semimargaritifera var. maxima

Aloe semimargaritifera var. maxima, commonly known as the giant rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to care for, though it requires minimal water to prevent root rot.
