Amaranthus graecizans subsp. aschersonianus

Amaranthus graecizans subsp.

aschersonianus, commonly known as the Greek amaranth, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is an annual herb characterized by its upright growth habit and feathery, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that transition to red or purple as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
