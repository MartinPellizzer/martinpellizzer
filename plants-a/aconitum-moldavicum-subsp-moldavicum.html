Aconitum moldavicum subsp. moldavicum

Aconitum moldavicum subsp.

moldavicum, commonly known as the Moldavian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region. It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has finely divided, palmately lobed leaves. It produces showy, hooded flowers that are arranged in a raceme, and its roots contain toxic alkaloids.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
