Aster flaccidus subsp. tsarungensis

Aster flaccidus subsp.

tsarungensis, commonly known as the Tsarungensis blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and Nepal. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its blue to violet flower heads are arranged in a compact cluster, often with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native highland ecosystems.
