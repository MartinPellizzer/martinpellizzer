Aristolochia bullata

Aristolochia bullata, commonly known as the Bullate Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often a deep purple or brownish color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
