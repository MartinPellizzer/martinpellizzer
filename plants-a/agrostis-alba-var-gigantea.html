Agrostis alba var. gigantea

Agrostis alba var. gigantea, commonly known as giant whitegrass, is a variant of the whitegrass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to whitish florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its habitat.
