Allium sphaerocephalon var. densiflorum

Allium sphaerocephalon var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense flowering heads, which give it its varietal name "densiflorum.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic scent.
