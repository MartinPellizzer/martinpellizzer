Alliaria lobbii

Alliaria lobbii, commonly known as the Chinese garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. It produces small, round, black seeds that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Alliaria lobbii is valued for its mild garlic-like flavor and is often cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.
