Acianthera gouveiae

Acianthera gouveiae, commonly known as the Gouvea's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small, with colorful petals and a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination.

Acianthera gouveiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
