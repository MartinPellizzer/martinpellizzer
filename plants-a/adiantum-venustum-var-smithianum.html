Adiantum venustum var. smithianum

Adiantum venustum var. smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its intricate fronds are adorned with tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes, which help in water retention.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden areas or as a houseplant.
