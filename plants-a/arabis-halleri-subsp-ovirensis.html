Arabis halleri subsp. ovirensis

Arabis halleri subsp.

ovirensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rockcress, is a flowering plant species native to the Andes region of South America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
