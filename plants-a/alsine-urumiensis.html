Alsine urumiensis

Alsine urumiensis, commonly known as the Urumi sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Alsine urumiensis is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
