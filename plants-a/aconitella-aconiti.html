Aconitella aconiti

Aconitella aconiti, commonly known as the lesser aconite, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its attractive blue or purple flowers and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

Despite its beauty, it should be handled with care as it can be harmful if ingested.
