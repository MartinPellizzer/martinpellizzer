Archidendron kalkmanii

Archidendron kalkmanii, commonly known as the Kalkman's legume, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along forest edges. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces flat, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
