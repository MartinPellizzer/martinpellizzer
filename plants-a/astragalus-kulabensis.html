Astragalus kulabensis

Astragalus kulabensis, commonly known as Kulab milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus kulabensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
