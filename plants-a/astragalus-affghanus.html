Astragalus affghanus

Astragalus affghanus, commonly known as Afghan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus affghanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
