Arundo bifaria

Arundo bifaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, blade-like leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Arundo bifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in landscaping and as a source of material for crafts.
