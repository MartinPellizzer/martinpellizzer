Anthyllis serpentinii

Anthyllis serpentinii, commonly known as the serpentine anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in nutrient-poor, calcareous, or serpentine soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, linear leaflets.

Anthyllis serpentinii is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
