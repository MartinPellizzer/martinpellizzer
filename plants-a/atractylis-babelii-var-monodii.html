Atractylis babelii var. monodii

Atractylis babelii var. monodii, commonly known as the narrowleaf atriplex, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes. It has a fibrous root system that helps it conserve water and survive in harsh conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
