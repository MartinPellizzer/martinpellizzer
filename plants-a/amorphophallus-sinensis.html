Amorphophallus sinensis

Amorphophallus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese Taro or elephant foot, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female parts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible corm, which is a staple in some Asian cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
