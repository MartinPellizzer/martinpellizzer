Aegiphila quinduensis

Aegiphila quinduensis, commonly known as the Quindu sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the high-altitude conditions of its native range.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
