Acer brevilobum

Acer brevilobum, commonly known as the mountain maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its small, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves, and it produces samaras for seed dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
