Astragalus viridis var. elatus

Astragalus viridis var. elatus, commonly known as the green milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus viridis var. elatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
