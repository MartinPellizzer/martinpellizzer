Adesmia conferta

Adesmia conferta, commonly known as the white thorn, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
