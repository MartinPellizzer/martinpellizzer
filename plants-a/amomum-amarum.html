Amomum amarum

Amomum amarum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and dark, aromatic seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that develop into dark brown, elongated pods containing the aromatic seeds. The seeds are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as cineole and limonene, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
