Anamirta racemosa

Anamirta racemosa, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spadix-bearing inflorescences. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, tropical climates. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, fleshy spadix, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Anamirta racemosa has cultural and medicinal significance in traditional Indian practices.
