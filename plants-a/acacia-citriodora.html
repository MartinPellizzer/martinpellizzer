Acacia citriodora

Acacia citriodora, commonly known as lemon acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves make it a popular ornamental plant.

Acacia citriodora is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
