Ammodaucus leucotrichus

Ammodaucus leucotrichus, commonly known as the white thistle or Egyptian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features a robust taproot and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
