Aristolochia nahua

Aristolochia nahua, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate, and often heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that range in color from green to purple, depending on the species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some areas for horticultural and traditional use.
