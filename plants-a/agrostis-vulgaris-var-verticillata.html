Agrostis vulgaris var. verticillata

Agrostis vulgaris var. verticillata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue when fresh. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, with small, two-lobed florets that are typically pale green to brown.

This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.
