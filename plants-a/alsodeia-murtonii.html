Alsodeia murtonii

Alsodeia murtonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It typically forms dense mats of narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to survive in poor, well-drained soils.

Alsodeia murtonii is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in desert scrub and sandy habitats.
