Anthurium clathratum

Anthurium clathratum, commonly known as the "netted anthurium," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense network of veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium clathratum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
