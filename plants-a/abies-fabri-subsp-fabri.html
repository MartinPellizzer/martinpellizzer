Abies fabri subsp. fabri

Abies fabri subsp.

fabri, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive, flattened, scale-like bark.

The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its timber and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
