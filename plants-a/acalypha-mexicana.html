Acalypha mexicana

Acalypha mexicana, commonly known as Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-bracted flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically bright purple or red when ripe. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Acalypha mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structures.
