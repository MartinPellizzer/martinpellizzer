Anisomeria drastica

Anisomeria drastica, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its native habitat. Its leaves are typically small, succulent, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Anisomeria drastica is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
