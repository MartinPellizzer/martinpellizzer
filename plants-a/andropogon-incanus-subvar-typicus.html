Andropogon incanus subvar. typicus

Andropogon incanus subvar. typicus, commonly known as hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
