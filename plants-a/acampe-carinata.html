Acampe carinata

Acampe carinata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
