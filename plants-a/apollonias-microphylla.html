Apollonias microphylla

Apollonias microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved apollonias, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in spring. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
