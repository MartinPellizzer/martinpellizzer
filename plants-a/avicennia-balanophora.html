Avicennia balanophora

Avicennia balanophora, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

The plant has pneumatophores, which are specialized root structures that aid in gas exchange in waterlogged soils. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Avicennia balanophora plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
