Acetosa acetoselloides

Acetosa acetoselloides, commonly known as white clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed soils due to its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
