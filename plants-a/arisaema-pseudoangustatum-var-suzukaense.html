Arisaema pseudoangustatum var. suzukaense

Arisaema pseudoangustatum var. suzukaense, commonly known as the Suzuka arrowhead, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a unique inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions of Japan. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and a highly ornamental appearance that makes it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
