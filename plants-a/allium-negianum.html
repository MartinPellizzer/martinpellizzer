Allium negianum

Allium negianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf garlic.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that are similar in appearance to garlic. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in umbels.

Allium negianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild garlic-like scent.
