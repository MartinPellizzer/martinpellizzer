Acacia ciliata

Acacia ciliata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. Acacia ciliata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
