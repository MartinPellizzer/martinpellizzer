Acanthobotrya pungens

Acanthobotrya pungens, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features spiny bracts that give it its common name, and its leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Acanthobotrya pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
