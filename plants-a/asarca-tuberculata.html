Asarca tuberculata

Asarca tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Asarca tuberculata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
