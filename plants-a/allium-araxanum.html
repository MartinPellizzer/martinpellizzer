Allium araxanum

Allium araxanum, commonly known as the Araxan onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and mild flavor, it is occasionally cultivated for both garden beauty and culinary use.
