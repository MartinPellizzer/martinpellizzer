Antirrhinum majus var. litigiosum

Antirrhinum majus var. litigiosum, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble tiny horns.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This annual species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and botanical collections.
