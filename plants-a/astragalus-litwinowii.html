Astragalus litwinowii

Astragalus litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinow's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as steppes and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus litwinowii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
