Albertisia crassa

Albertisia crassa, commonly known as the rough Albertisia, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Albertisia crassa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
