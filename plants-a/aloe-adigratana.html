Aloe adigratana

Aloe adigratana, commonly known as the Adigrat aloe, is a succulent plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Aloe adigratana is often grown as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
