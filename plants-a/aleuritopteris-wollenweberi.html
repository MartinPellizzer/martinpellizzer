Aleuritopteris wollenweberi

Aleuritopteris wollenweberi, commonly known as the Wollenweber's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sporangia clustered in sori.

Aleuritopteris wollenweberi plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
