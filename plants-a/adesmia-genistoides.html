Adesmia genistoides

Adesmia genistoides, commonly known as the yellow burr acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Adesmia genistoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
