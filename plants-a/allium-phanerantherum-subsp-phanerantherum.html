Allium phanerantherum subsp. phanerantherum

Allium phanerantherum subsp.

phanerantherum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a species of bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces large, fleshy bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are broad and flat, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
