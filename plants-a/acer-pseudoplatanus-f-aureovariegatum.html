Acer pseudoplatanus f. aureovariegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus f. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated maple, is a unique variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its striking leaves that display a mix of yellow, white, and green hues, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges and a smooth underside. This cultivar thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a favored choice for gardens and landscaped areas.
