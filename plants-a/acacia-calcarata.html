Acacia calcarata

Acacia calcarata, commonly known as the sand acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions. It has small, spiny stipules that help protect the plant from herbivores.

Acacia calcarata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which improves soil fertility in its native habitat.
