Adenocline bupleuroides

Adenocline bupleuroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Adenocline bupleuroides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
