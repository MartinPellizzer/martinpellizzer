Aydendron macrophyllum

Aydendron macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family, native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

The fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a soft glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, Aydendron macrophyllum is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
