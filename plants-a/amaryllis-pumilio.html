Amaryllis pumilio

Amaryllis pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis pumilio is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive blooms.
