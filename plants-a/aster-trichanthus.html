Aster trichanthus

Aster trichanthus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

Aster trichanthus is known for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
