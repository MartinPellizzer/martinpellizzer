Acacia glandulifera

Acacia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. Acacia glandulifera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
