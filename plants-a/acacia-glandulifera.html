Acacia glandulifera

Acacia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. Acacia glandulifera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought.

It is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
