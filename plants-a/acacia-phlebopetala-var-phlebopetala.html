Acacia phlebopetala var. phlebopetala

Acacia phlebopetala var. phlebopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves composed of numerous fine, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
