Aconitum khanminthunii

Aconitum khanminthunii, commonly known as the Khomthun aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically deeply lobed and have a waxy texture, adapting to its cool, moist habitat.

Aconitum khanminthunii contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological significance and potential medicinal interest.
