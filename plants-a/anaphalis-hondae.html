Anaphalis hondae

Anaphalis hondae, commonly known as Honduran frostflower, is a flowering plant native to Honduras.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Anaphalis hondae is noted for its frost-like appearance, with delicate, hair-covered leaves that resemble ice crystals.
