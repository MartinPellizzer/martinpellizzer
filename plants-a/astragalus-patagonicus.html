Astragalus patagonicus

Astragalus patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the diverse flora of Patagonia.

Astragalus patagonicus is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
