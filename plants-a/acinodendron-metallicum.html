Acinodendron metallicum

Acinodendron metallicum, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often covered in a silvery or metallic waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
