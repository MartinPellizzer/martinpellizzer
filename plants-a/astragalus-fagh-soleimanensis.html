Astragalus fagh-soleimanensis

Astragalus fagh-soleimanensis, commonly known as the Fagh-Soleiman milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
