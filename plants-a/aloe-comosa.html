Aloe comosa

Aloe comosa, commonly known as the wooly aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. Its root system is adapted for storing water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Aloe comosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
