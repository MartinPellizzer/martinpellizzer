Athyrium × saitoanum

Athyrium × saitoanum, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a hybrid fern species resulting from the cross between Athyrium filipendulum and Athyrium niponicum.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that exhibit a feathery appearance, often with a deep green color and a glossy texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring rich, well-drained soil. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, arranged in distinct clusters.

Athyrium × saitoanum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
