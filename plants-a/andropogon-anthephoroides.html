Andropogon anthephoroides

Andropogon anthephoroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
