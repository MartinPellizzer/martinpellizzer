Arundinaria simonii var. variegata

Arundinaria simonii var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaf patterns, featuring shades of green, white, and yellow. This plant typically grows in clumps, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and has a fast growth rate. Its culms are hollow and segmented, with a smooth, pale green surface.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
