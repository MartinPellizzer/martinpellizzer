Abies gamblei

Abies gamblei, commonly known as the Gambel's fir, is a species of fir tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and dark green needles that are typically two to three millimeters long. The tree produces small, spherical cones that are reddish-brown when mature. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, moist climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of mountain ecosystems in its native range.
