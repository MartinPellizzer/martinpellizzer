Anatherum megapotamicum

Anatherum megapotamicum, commonly known as the South American grass, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile. This plant typically grows in grasslands and shrublands, thriving in temperate climates. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Anatherum megapotamicum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
