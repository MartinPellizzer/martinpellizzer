Albizia katangensis

Albizia katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga acacia, is a deciduous tree native to central Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its bipinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savanna woodlands.

Albizia katangensis plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil fertility.
