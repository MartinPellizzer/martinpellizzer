Amaranthus pringlei

Amaranthus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters. It is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its deep root system.

Amaranthus pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking color and resilience.
