Attalea compta var. acaulis

Attalea compta var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender palm or the dwarf palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into round, edible fruits containing black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various tropical regions for both horticultural and environmental purposes.
