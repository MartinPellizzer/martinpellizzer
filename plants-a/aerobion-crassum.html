Aerobion crassum

Aerobion crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaf air plant, is a unique species of epiphytic plant that thrives in humid environments.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant's roots are adapted for absorbing nutrients from the air rather than soil. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Aerobion crassum is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.
