Alibertia latifolia var. pargueniana

Alibertia latifolia var. pargueniana, commonly known as the broadleaf alibertia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
