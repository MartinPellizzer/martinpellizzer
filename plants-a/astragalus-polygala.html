Astragalus polygala

Astragalus polygala, commonly known as the many-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, this plant often grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus polygala is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
