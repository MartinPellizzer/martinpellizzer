Asplenium crassidens

Asplenium crassidens, commonly known as the broad-leaf spleenwort, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It typically features dark green, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices and forest floors, where it can anchor itself to substrates like stones or wood. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the leaves, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Asplenium crassidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
