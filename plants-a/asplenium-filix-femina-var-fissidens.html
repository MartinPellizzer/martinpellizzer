Asplenium filix-femina var. fissidens

Asplenium filix-femina var. fissidens, commonly known as the Split-leaf Maidenhair Fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
