Allium roseum f. carneum

Allium roseum f. carneum, commonly known as the pink garlic.

This botanical variant is a cultivar of the common garlic plant, characterized by its distinctive pinkish-red bulbs and leaves. It is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists interested in its unique coloration and growth habits.
