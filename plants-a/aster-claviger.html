Aster claviger

Aster claviger, commonly known as the hooked aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Aster claviger is notable for its unique hooked bracts that resemble tiny hooks, giving it its common name.
