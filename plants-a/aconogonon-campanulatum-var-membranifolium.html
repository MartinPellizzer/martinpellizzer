Aconogonon campanulatum var. membranifolium

Aconogonon campanulatum var. membranifolium, commonly known as the membranous-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are thin and membranous, often with a bluish-green hue, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
