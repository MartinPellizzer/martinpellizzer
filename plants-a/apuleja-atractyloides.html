Apuleja atractyloides

Apuleja atractyloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies and wetlands, displaying narrow, grass-like leaves. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
