Alchemilla arvensis f. pusilla

Alchemilla arvensis f. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features a rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or garden settings.

Its foliage is used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to retain moisture.
