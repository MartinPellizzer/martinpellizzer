Archeria traversii

Archeria traversii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows from a basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Archeria traversii is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
