Aster argunensis

Aster argunensis, commonly known as the Argun aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster argunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
