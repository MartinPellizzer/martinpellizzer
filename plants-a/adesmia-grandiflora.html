Adesmia grandiflora

Adesmia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Adesmia grandiflora produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
