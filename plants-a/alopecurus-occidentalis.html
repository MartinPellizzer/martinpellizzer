Alopecurus occidentalis

Alopecurus occidentalis, commonly known as western mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. The plant has fine, hairless leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with greenish or brownish flowers. It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Alopecurus occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
