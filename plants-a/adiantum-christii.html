Adiantum christii

Adiantum christii, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a delicate, evergreen fern characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble those of the common maidenhair fern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, narrow shape.

Adiantum christii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
