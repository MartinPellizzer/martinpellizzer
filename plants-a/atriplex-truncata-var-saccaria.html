Atriplex truncata var. saccaria

Atriplex truncata var. saccaria, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are typically succulent, which helps it conserve water.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
