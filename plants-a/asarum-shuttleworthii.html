Asarum shuttleworthii

Asarum shuttleworthii, commonly known as Shuttleworth's wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves. The plant produces small, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
