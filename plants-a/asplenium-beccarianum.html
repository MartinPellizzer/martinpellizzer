Asplenium beccarianum

Asplenium beccarianum, commonly known as the beccarian spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a compact, rosette-like structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its fronds are evergreen and lack true stems, with spore-producing sorus structures located on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium beccarianum is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor substrates, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
