Arctium hypoleucum

Arctium hypoleucum, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features tall, hollow stems covered in dense, prickly bracts that form bur-like structures. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a spherical cluster at the top of the stem.
