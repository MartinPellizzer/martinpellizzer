Austroliabum glanduliferum

Austroliabum glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, glandular structures on the leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
