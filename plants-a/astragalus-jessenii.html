Astragalus jessenii

Astragalus jessenii, commonly known as Jessen's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Astragalus jessenii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
