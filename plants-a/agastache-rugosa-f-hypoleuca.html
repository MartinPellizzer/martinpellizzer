Agastache rugosa f. hypoleuca

Agastache rugosa f. hypoleuca, commonly known as white hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a minty aroma. The plant produces spikes of tubular, white to pale lavender flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
