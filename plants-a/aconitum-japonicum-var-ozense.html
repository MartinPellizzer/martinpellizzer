Aconitum japonicum var. ozense

Aconitum japonicum var. ozense, commonly known as Japanese monkshood variant ozense, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
