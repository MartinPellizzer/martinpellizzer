Anemone trifoliata

Anemone trifoliata, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, white or pink flowers with a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Anemone trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
