Astragalus pseudorhacodes

Astragalus pseudorhacodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus pseudorhacodes is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of shrublands.
