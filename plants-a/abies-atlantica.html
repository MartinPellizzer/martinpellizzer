Abies atlantica

Abies atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The Atlantic fir plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
