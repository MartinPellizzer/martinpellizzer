Aponogeton boivinianus

Aponogeton boivinianus, commonly known as the Boivin's arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically submerged in water, though some may emerge above the surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season, adding ornamental value to its aquatic habitat. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate to high light exposure.

Aponogeton boivinianus is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
