Argusia gnaphalodes

Argusia gnaphalodes, commonly known as the woolly argusia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
