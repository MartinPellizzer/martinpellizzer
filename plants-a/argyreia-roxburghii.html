Argyreia roxburghii

Argyreia roxburghii, commonly known as the holy night or moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant produces heart-shaped, velvety leaves and has a woody stem covered in fine hairs. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
