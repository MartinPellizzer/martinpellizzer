Anguloa uniflora var. eburnea

Anguloa uniflora var. eburnea, commonly known as the white anguloa, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that often have a faint pink or purple tinge. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
