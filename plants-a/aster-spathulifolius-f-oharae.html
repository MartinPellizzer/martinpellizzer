Aster spathulifolius f. oharae

Aster spathulifolius f. oharae, commonly known as the Ohara's spatulate aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spatulate-shaped leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and blue. Native to Japan, this cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
