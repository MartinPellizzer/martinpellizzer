Aspalathus triquetra

Aspalathus triquetra, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its small, sweet-smelling flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.

Aspalathus triquetra is a key species in the fynbos biome and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
