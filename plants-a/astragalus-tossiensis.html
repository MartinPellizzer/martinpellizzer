Astragalus tossiensis

Astragalus tossiensis, commonly known as the Tossian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its potential uses.
