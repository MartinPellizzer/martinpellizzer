Aspidosperma fendleri

Aspidosperma fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican wolfberry, is a deciduous tree native to the arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its latex contains alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
