Adhatoda oreophila

Adhatoda oreophila, commonly known as the mountain adhatoda, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Adhatoda oreophila is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
