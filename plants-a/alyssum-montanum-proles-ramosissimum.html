Alyssum montanum proles ramosissimum

Alyssum montanum proles ramosissimum, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Alyssum montanum proles ramosissimum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
