Arrhostoxylum curviflorum

Arrhostoxylum curviflorum, commonly known as the curved-flower shrub, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance, while its woody stems contribute to its structural form.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
