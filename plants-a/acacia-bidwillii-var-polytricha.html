Acacia bidwillii var. polytricha

Acacia bidwillii var. polytricha, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow floral spikes that appear during spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The species is well adapted to withstand salt spray and drought, making it a resilient component of coastal ecosystems.
