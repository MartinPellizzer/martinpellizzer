Acinos orontius

Acinos orontius, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense, mat-forming growth with hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually pink or white, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Acinos orontius thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.
