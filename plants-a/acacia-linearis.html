Acacia linearis

Acacia linearis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often mistaken for stems, a characteristic that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry conditions.

The plant is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
