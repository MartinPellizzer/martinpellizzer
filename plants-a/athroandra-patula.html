Athroandra patula

Athroandra patula, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban and rural landscapes.
