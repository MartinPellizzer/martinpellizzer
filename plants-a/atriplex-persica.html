Atriplex persica

Atriplex persica, commonly known as Persian saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its salt tolerance makes it well adapted to saline soils and harsh environmental conditions.

Atriplex persica is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils in degraded areas.
