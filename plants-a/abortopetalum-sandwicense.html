Abortopetalum sandwicense

Abortopetalum sandwicense, commonly known as the Hawaiian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or on rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
