Amphipterygium amplifolium

Amphipterygium amplifolium, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
