Aconitum talassicum var. talassicum

Aconitum talassicum var. talassicum, commonly known as the coastal aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, conical clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a waxy coating.

This variety is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils along coastal cliffs and shores.
