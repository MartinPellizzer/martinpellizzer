Artocarpus bicolor

Artocarpus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored breadfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bi-colored fruit that ranges from green to yellow. The plant produces compound leaves with a rough, hairy texture and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its fruit, which is edible when mature, is often used in traditional cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and timber.
