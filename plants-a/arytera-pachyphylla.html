Arytera pachyphylla

Arytera pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved arylta, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
