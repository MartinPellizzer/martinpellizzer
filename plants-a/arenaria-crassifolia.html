Arenaria crassifolia

Arenaria crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Arenaria crassifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is adapted to withstand extreme cold and wind.
