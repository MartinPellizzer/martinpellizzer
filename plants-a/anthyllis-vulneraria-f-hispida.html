Anthyllis vulneraria f. hispida

Anthyllis vulneraria f. hispida, commonly known as the hispid devil's-bit manroot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and pastures.

Its unique floral structure and medicinal historical use contribute to its ecological and botanical significance.
