Actinea scaposa var. trinervata

Actinea scaposa var. trinervata, commonly known as the three-veined actinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have three distinct veins. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Actinidae family.
