Aloe intermedia var. asperrima

Aloe intermedia var. asperrima, commonly known as the rough aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a rough, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

This species is well-suited for xeriscaping and thrives in full sun with minimal water.
