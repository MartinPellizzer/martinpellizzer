Aconitum tenue

Aconitum tenue, commonly known as the slender aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

Aconitum tenue contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution in handling and cultivation.
