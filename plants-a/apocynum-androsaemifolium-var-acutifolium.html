Apocynum androsaemifolium var. acutifolium

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. acutifolium, commonly known as Indian paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, brush-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
