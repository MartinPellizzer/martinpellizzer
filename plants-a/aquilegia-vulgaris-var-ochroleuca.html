Aquilegia vulgaris var. ochroleuca

Aquilegia vulgaris var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five sepals that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
