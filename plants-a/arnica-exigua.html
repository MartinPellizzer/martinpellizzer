Arnica exigua

Arnica exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

However, it is toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.
