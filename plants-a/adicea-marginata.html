Adicea marginata

Adicea marginata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant features velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often green with a purple or reddish margin. Its flowers are typically showy, with five petals that can be white, pink, or purple in color.

Adicea marginata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
