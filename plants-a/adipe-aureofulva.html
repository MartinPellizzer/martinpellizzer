Adipe aureofulva

Adipe aureofulva, commonly known as the golden-yellow orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow petals and sepals. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Adipe aureofulva thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
