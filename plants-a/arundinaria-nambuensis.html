Arundinaria nambuensis

Arundinaria nambuensis, commonly known as Nambu bamboo, is a species of grass native to southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can be used in traditional practices.

Arundinaria nambuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
