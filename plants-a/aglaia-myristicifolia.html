Aglaia myristicifolia

Aglaia myristicifolia, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine.

Aglaia myristicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has applications in both horticulture and pharmaceutical research.
