Acalypha villosa var. intermedia

Acalypha villosa var. intermedia, commonly known as velvetleaf or red leaf hibiscus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that range in color from green to red or purple, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an adaptable and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
