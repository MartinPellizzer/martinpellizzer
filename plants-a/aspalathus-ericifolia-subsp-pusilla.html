Aspalathus ericifolia subsp. pusilla

Aspalathus ericifolia subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf silver bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for coastal landscaping and erosion control.
