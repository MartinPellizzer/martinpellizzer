Adenandra mundiifolia

Adenandra mundiifolia, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
