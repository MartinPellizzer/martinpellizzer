Abildgaardia odontocarpa

Abildgaardia odontocarpa, commonly known as the toothed-lobed evergreen, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are often lobed and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in horticulture.
