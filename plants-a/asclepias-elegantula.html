Asclepias elegantula

Asclepias elegantula, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender stems that are often hairy and bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive spurred structures. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a characteristic shared with other milkweed species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Asclepias elegantula serves as a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae, supporting biodiversity in its ecosystem.
