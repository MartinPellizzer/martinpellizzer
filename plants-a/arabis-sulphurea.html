Arabis sulphurea

Arabis sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur yellow rocket, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often resembling a cascade of small, clustered blossoms. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Arabis sulphurea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
