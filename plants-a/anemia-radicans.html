Anemia radicans

Anemia radicans, commonly known as bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped red blooms that appear in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces pendulous, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its tendency to "bleed" when cut, as it exudes a sap-like substance.
