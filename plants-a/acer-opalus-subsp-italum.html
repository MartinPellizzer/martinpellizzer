Acer opalus subsp. italum

Acer opalus subsp.

italum, commonly known as the Italian maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and striking red or orange foliage in autumn.

The leaves are palmately lobed with a serrated margin, and the tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.
