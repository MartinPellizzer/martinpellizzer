Astragalus singarensis

Astragalus singarensis, commonly known as the Singarensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. Astragalus singarensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
