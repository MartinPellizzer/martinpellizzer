Amorphophallus cruddasianus

Amorphophallus cruddasianus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, which float on the surface of the water. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or green with a reddish base. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store nutrients and water.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
