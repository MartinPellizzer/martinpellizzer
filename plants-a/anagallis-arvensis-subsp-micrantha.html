Anagallis arvensis subsp. micrantha

Anagallis arvensis subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of open, sunny habitats.
