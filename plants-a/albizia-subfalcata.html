Albizia subfalcata

Albizia subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in racemes, and are fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Albizia subfalcata is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
