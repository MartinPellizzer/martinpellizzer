Aristida hassleri

Aristida hassleri, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are usually light green to brown in color.

Aristida hassleri is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in native plant landscaping.
