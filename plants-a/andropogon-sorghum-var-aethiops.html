Andropogon sorghum var. aethiops

Andropogon sorghum var. aethiops, commonly known as African millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is well adapted to arid environments.

This variety is often used for forage, erosion control, and in traditional agricultural systems.
