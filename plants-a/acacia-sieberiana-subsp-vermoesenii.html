Acacia sieberiana subsp. vermoesenii

Acacia sieberiana subsp.

vermoesenii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets.

The plant produces yellow to pale yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
