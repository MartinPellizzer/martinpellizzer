Astragalus microcephalus var. kandawanensis

Astragalus microcephalus var. kandawanensis, commonly known as the Kandawar Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and a low-growing habit to conserve water. It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.

The plant's botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
