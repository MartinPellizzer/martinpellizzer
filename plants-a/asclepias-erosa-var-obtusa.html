Asclepias erosa var. obtusa

Asclepias erosa var. obtusa, commonly known as the bluntleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly rounded at the tips. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers with distinctive horns, typical of the milkweed family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Asclepias erosa var. obtusa is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and supports a variety of pollinators.
