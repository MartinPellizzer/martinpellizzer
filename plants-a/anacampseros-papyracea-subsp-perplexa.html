Anacampseros papyracea subsp. perplexa

Anacampseros papyracea subsp.

perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing parchment cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in a thin, papery layer.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warm season, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
