Agrostis canina var. flava

Agrostis canina var. flava, commonly known as yellow meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with yellowish to golden florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a component in ecological restoration projects.
