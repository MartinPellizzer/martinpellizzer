Alphitonia ponderosa var. auwahiensis

Alphitonia ponderosa var. auwahiensis, commonly known as the Auwahi tree, is a rare endemic species native to Hawaii.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet forest environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
