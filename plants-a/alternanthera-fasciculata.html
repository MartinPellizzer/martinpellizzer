Alternanthera fasciculata

Alternanthera fasciculata, commonly known as red flame or red leaf sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colored foliage, often displaying shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white.

This species is popular in aquatic and terrestrial gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
