Agathosma erecta var. pilosa

Agathosma erecta var. pilosa, commonly known as the Cape chamomile, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The species is also valued in aromatherapy for its essential oils, which are extracted from the leaves.
