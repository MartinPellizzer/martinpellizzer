Astragalus hamiensis

Astragalus hamiensis, commonly known as the Hami milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Astragalus hamiensis is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
