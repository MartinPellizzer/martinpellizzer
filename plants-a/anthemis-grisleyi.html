Anthemis grisleyi

Anthemis grisleyi, commonly known as the gray anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow with a central disk, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
