Araujia sericifera f. calycina

Araujia sericifera f. calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf moonflower, is a variant of the moonflower species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
