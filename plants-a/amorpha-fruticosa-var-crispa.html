Amorpha fruticosa var. crispa

Amorpha fruticosa var. crispa, commonly known as the curly leaved indigo bush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and distinctive, wavy, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
