Anneslia simulans

Anneslia simulans, commonly known as the mimic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a strikingly similar appearance to other bellflower species. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Anneslia simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
