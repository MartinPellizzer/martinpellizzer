Arundarbor amplexifolia

Arundarbor amplexifolia, commonly known as the leaf-holding orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that wrap around its stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the wet season. Arundarbor amplexifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in its natural range.
