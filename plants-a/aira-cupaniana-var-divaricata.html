Aira cupaniana var. divaricata

Aira cupaniana var. divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tuftgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
