Argusia sibirica

Argusia sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian argusia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Argusia sibirica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
