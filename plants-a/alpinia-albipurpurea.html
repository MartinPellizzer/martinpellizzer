Alpinia albipurpurea

Alpinia albipurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of showy, pale purple to white flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Alpinia albipurpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
