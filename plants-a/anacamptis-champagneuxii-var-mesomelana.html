Anacamptis champagneuxii var. mesomelana

Anacamptis champagneuxii var. mesomelana, commonly known as the Purple Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
