Arabis bucharica

Arabis bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arabis bucharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
