Ageratum salicifolium subsp. annectens

Ageratum salicifolium subsp.

annectens, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and aromatic foliage.

The subspecies is closely related to other ageratum species but is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and floral coloration.
