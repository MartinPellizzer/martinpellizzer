Adiantum lucidum var. anomalum

Adiantum lucidum var. anomalum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, arching fronds with a silvery sheen on the upper surface, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
