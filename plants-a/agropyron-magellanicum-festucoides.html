Agropyron magellanicum festucoides

Agropyron magellanicum festucoides, commonly known as the Magellanic grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically grows in temperate to cold climates and is often found in grasslands and mountainous regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil effectively.

Agropyron magellanicum festucoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in restoration projects.
