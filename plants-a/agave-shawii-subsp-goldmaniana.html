Agave shawii subsp. goldmaniana

Agave shawii subsp.

goldmaniana, commonly known as the Goldman's agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
