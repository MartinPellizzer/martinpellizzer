Acacia triphylla var. pungens

Acacia triphylla var. pungens, commonly known as the spiny wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The plant's thorn-like structures provide protection against herbivores.
