Argyrolobium collinum var. seminudum

Argyrolobium collinum var. seminudum, commonly known as the naked-leaved silverlobed corymbiferous.

It is a flowering plant native to South Africa, characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are typically reduced or scale-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.
