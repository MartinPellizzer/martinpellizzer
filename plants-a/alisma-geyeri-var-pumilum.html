Alisma geyeri var. pumilum

Alisma geyeri var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf water plant, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often near streams, ponds, or wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually floating or partially submerged. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
