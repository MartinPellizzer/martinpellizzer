Aegilops triaristata var. intermedia

Aegilops triaristata var. intermedia, commonly known as intermediate wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and glaucous, blue-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in threes, giving it its species name "triaristata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its fibrous roots and drought tolerance make it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.
