Actaea brachypetala var. microcarpa

Actaea brachypetala var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late summer. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in native plant gardens.
