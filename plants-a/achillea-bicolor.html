Achillea bicolor

Achillea bicolor, commonly known as the purple yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves and a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a range of colors from white to pink and purple.
