Achillea aleppica subsp. altinozlui

Achillea aleppica subsp.

altinozlui, commonly known as the Aleppo thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and produces yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters.

The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a hardy, ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
