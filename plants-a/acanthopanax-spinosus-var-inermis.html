Acanthopanax spinosus var. inermis

Acanthopanax spinosus var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth devil's ivy, is a variant of the devil's ivy plant characterized by its lack of thorns.

It is a climbing shrub native to East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries, which are edible for some bird species.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
