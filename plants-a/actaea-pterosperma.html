Actaea pterosperma

Actaea pterosperma, commonly known as the red-spotted actaea, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features compound, pinnately arranged leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in reddish spots. Actaea pterosperma typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
