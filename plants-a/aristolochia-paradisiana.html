Aristolochia paradisiana

Aristolochia paradisiana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, saddle-shaped leaves and striking, purple-red flowers that resemble a horn or a pipe. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance. Its flowers are often visited by specific pollinators, such as certain species of bees and wasps.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and is not suitable for consumption by humans or animals.
