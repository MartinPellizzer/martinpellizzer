Agropyron spicatum

Agropyron spicatum, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, prairies, and disturbed sites.

Agropyron spicatum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
