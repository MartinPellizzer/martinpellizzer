Astragaloides alopecurus

Astragaloides alopecurus, commonly known as slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Astragaloides alopecurus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
