Abrodictyum setigerum

Abrodictyum setigerum, commonly known as the feather-tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Abrodictyum setigerum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
