Anerincleistus setosus

Anerincleistus setosus, commonly known as the spiny bladder nut, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive spiny leaves that provide a natural defense against herbivores. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ecological research.
