Acacia volubilis

Acacia volubilis, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thin, flexible branches that often trail or hang down, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia volubilis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
