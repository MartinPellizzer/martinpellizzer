Arthraxon hispidus f. langsdorffii

Arthraxon hispidus f. langsdorffii, commonly known as the hairy arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
