Armeria maritima subsp. elongata

Armeria maritima subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense, mat-like clusters of silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces showy clusters of pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-tolerant plant suitable for coastal gardens and erosion control.
