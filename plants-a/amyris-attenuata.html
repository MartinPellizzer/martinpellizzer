Amyris attenuata

Amyris attenuata, commonly known as the candle bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.

Amyris attenuata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
