Astragalus porphyrophysa

Astragalus porphyrophysa, commonly known as the purple-veined milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water and nutrients from deep within the soil.

Astragalus porphyrophysa is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
