Azalea serrulata

Azalea serrulata, commonly known as the serrated azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its blooms, which range in color from white to pink and red, appear in late spring to early summer.

Azalea serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
