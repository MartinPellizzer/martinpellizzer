Alkanna areolata var. sublaevis

Alkanna areolata var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth alkanna, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and produces showy, pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping and rocky garden environments.
