Amitostigma tetralobum

Amitostigma tetralobum, commonly known as the four-lobed larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
