Asplundia clementinae

Asplundia clementinae, commonly known as the Clementinae arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy underground tuber, which is used as a root vegetable in some regions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially useful in traditional cuisine.
