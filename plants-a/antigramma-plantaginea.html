Antigramma plantaginea

Antigramma plantaginea, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Antigramma plantaginea is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
