Allium hookeri var. muliense

Allium hookeri var. muliense, commonly known as the Muli onion or the Tibetan leek, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Asia.

It features long, slender leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous base, which is often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
