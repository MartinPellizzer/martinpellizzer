Annona obovata

Annona obovata, commonly known as the custard apple or sugar apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that produces large, fragrant, green fruits with a smooth, waxy skin. The leaves are ovate to obovate in shape, with a glossy surface and a serrated margin. The flowers are yellow with a red center and are typically borne in clusters.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which has a creamy, sweet flesh and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
