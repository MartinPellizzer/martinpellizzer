Acanthopanax hypoleucus

Acanthopanax hypoleucus, commonly known as the Siberian angel's trumpets, is a deciduous shrub native to the Far East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
