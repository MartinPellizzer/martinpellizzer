Agave longisepala

Agave longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are lined with sharp spines. The plant produces large, white flowers on a tall inflorescence, which can reach several meters in height. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy leaves.

Agave longisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices.
