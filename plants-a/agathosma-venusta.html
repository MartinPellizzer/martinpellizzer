Agathosma venusta

Agathosma venusta, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or African geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, aromatic leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its cultivation in both gardens and horticultural collections.
