Anathallis brittonii

Anathallis brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's anathallis, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain rather than soil. Its flowering period occurs during the wet season, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
