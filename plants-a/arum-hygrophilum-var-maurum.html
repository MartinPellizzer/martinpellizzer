Arum hygrophilum var. maurum

Arum hygrophilum var. maurum, commonly known as the Mauritian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The botanical features of this species make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
