Anthurium coloradense

Anthurium coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium coloradense is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
