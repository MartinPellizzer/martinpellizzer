Anisacantha bicornis

Anisacantha bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces tubular flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anisacantha bicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
