Arctotis interrupta

Arctotis interrupta, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers bloom from spring to autumn, adding color and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
