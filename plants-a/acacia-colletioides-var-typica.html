Acacia colletioides var. typica

Acacia colletioides var. typica, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in drought conditions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
