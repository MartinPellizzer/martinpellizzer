Alchornea adenophila

Alchornea adenophila, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alchornea adenophila is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
