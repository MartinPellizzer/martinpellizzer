Alchemilla splendens var. vetteri

Alchemilla splendens var. vetteri, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, which helps it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
