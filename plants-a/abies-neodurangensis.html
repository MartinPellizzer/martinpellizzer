Abies neodurangensis

Abies neodurangensis, commonly known as the Durango fir, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high elevations, typically above 2,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
