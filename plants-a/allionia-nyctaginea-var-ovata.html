Allionia nyctaginea var. ovata

Allionia nyctaginea var. ovata, commonly known as the moonflower allionia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
