Aloe hazeliana var. hazeliana

Aloe hazeliana var. hazeliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems with narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
