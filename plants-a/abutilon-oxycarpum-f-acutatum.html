Abutilon oxycarpum f. acutatum

Abutilon oxycarpum f. acutatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, round seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
