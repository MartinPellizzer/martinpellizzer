Azalea stenopetala

Azalea stenopetala, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a glossy surface, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soil.

Azalea stenopetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shady garden conditions.
