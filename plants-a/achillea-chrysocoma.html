Achillea chrysocoma

Achillea chrysocoma, commonly known as golden yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its name honors the mythological figure Achilles, who was said to have used the plant to treat wounds.
