Acacia nutans

Acacia nutans, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia nutans is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique visual appeal.
