Adonis ambigua subsp. flava

Adonis ambigua subsp.

flava, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
