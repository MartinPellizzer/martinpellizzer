Acaena digitata var. compacta

Acaena digitata var. compacta, commonly known as the compact thimbleweed, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and features small, spiny leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
