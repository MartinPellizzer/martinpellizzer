Ardisia faberi

Ardisia faberi, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, dark purple berries that appear in clusters, often persisting through winter. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ardisia faberi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and compact growth habit.
