Astragalus holosemius

Astragalus holosemius, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus holosemius plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinators.
