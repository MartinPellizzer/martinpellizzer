Anchusa officinalis var. alba

Anchusa officinalis var. alba, commonly known as white horehound, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and wastelands. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces tall, sparsely branched stems with clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
