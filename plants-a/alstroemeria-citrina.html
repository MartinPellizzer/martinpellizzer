Alstroemeria citrina

Alstroemeria citrina, commonly known as the yellow alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking splash of color to gardens and landscapes.
