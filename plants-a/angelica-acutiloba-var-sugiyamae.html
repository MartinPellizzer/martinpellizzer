Angelica acutiloba var. sugiyamae

Angelica acutiloba var. sugiyamae, commonly known as the Japanese angelica, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, deeply lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are white to pale green and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The roots of this variety are valued for their medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
