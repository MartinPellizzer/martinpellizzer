Amblyopogon atropatanus

Amblyopogon atropatanus, commonly known as the blackleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a dark green to almost black coloration on the upper surface. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Amblyopogon atropatanus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
