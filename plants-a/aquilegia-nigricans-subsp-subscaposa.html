Aquilegia nigricans subsp. subscaposa

Aquilegia nigricans subsp.

subscaposa, commonly known as the black cohosh, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers and compound leaves.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, thriving in partial to full shade. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with five sepals that are often more showy than the petals.

The subspecies subscaposa is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
