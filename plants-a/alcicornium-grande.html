Alcicornium grande

Alcicornium grande, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, waxy leaves that shimmer with a silvery sheen.

Native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high atmospheric moisture. The plant produces elegant, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, often forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Alcicornium grande is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
