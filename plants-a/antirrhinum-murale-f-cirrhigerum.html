Antirrhinum murale f. cirrhigerum

Antirrhinum murale f. cirrhigerum, commonly known as the trailing antirrhinum, is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This cultivar is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from containers or hanging baskets.
