Aspidistra cyathiflora subsp. bifolia

Aspidistra cyathiflora subsp.

bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved aspidistra, is a member of the Araceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy, and elongated leaves that often have a slightly wavy margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
