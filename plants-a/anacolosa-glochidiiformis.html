Anacolosa glochidiiformis

Anacolosa glochidiiformis, commonly known as the spiny acanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spiny bracts and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.

Anacolosa glochidiiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
