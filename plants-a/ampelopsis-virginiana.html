Ampelopsis virginiana

Ampelopsis virginiana, commonly known as Virginia creeper, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that typically have five leaflets, which turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas, along stream banks, and in disturbed sites.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
