Archidendron monadelphum var. gracile

Archidendron monadelphum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender legume tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional uses.
