Asplenium rhizophorum var. alloeopteron

Asplenium rhizophorum var. alloeopteron, commonly known as the rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The fronds are usually unlobed and have a compact, rosette-like growth habit. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in the Aspleniaceae family.
