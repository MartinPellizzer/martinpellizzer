Artemisia capillaris var. acaulis

Artemisia capillaris var. acaulis, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, gray-green, feathery leaves. The plant produces tiny yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
