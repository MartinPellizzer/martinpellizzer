Asperula nitida subsp. mytilinica

Asperula nitida subsp.

mytilinica, commonly known as the silverleaf azalea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that exhibits a striking silver-white underside, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
