Acacia californica subsp. pringlei

Acacia californica subsp.

pringlei, commonly known as the California acacia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets.

The plant produces yellow to golden-green globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
