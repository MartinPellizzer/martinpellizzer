Acosta boissieri subsp. funkii

Acosta boissieri subsp.

funkii, commonly known as the Funk's acosta, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, showing resilience to drought.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed or arid habitats.
