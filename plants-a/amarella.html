Amarella

Amarella, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a small, round fruit belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red color and sweet, juicy flesh, which makes it a popular choice for both fresh consumption and cooking. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing clusters of small tomatoes along its stems. Amarella tomatoes are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, contributing to their health benefits.

They thrive in warm climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
