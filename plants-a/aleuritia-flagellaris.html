Aleuritia flagellaris

Aleuritia flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Hamamelidaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or stream banks.

Its unique, thread-like appearance has earned it the common name "flagellate," referring to its delicate, flagellum-like structures.
