Avena fatua var. ludoviciana

Avena fatua var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds, facilitating its spread across various habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
