Astragalus zahlbruckneri

Astragalus zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahlbruckner's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
