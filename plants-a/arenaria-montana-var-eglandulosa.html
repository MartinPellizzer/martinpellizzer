Arenaria montana var. eglandulosa

Arenaria montana var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, rounded leaves and a fibrous root system adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for harsh environmental conditions.
