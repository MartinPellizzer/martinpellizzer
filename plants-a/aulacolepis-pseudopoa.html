Aulacolepis pseudopoa

Aulacolepis pseudopoa, commonly known as the false poa, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which are often small and inconspicuous.

Aulacolepis pseudopoa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
