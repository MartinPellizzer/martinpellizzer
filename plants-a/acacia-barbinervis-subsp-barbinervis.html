Acacia barbinervis subsp. barbinervis

Acacia barbinervis subsp.

barbinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and phyllodes that are narrow and linear with prominent, parallel veins.

The plant produces yellow globular flowerheads, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native wildlife species.
