Alchemilla ludovitiana

Alchemilla ludovitiana, commonly known as the Hungarian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a single umbel. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla ludovitiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
