Adesmia vesicaria

Adesmia vesicaria, commonly known as the bladderleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, round seeds.

Adesmia vesicaria is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
