Andropogon × coloratus

Andropogon × coloratus, commonly known as the crossbred switchgrass, is a hybrid grass species resulting from the cross between Andropogon gerardii and Andropogon scoparius.

It is native to the central and eastern United States and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. This plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade. Andropogon × coloratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its potential use in bioenergy production.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences that contribute to its ecological and aesthetic appeal.
