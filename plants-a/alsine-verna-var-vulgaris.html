Alsine verna var. vulgaris

Alsine verna var. vulgaris, commonly known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and vegetative reproduction.

Alsine verna var. vulgaris is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in some traditional cuisines.
