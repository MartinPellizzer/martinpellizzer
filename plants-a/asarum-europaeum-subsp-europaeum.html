Asarum europaeum subsp. europaeum

Asarum europaeum subsp.

europaeum, commonly known as European wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy aroma.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The rhizomes of this plant are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and analgesic properties.
