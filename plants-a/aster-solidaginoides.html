Aster solidaginoides

Aster solidaginoides, commonly known as the golden aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
