Aster praealtus var. coerulescens

Aster praealtus var. coerulescens, commonly known as the Blue Aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
