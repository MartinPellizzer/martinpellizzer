Arundo epigeios

Arundo epigeios, commonly known as the creeping reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and ditches. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tall, slender spikes of small, greenish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form dense colonies.

Arundo epigeios is often used in landscaping for erosion control due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
