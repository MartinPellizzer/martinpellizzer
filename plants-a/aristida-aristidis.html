Aristida aristidis

Aristida aristidis, commonly known as the mountain dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce delicate, pale to brownish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Aristida aristidis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize fragile ecosystems.
