Acacia tortilis subsp. spirocarpa

Acacia tortilis subsp.

spirocarpa, commonly known as the spiral acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of branches, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

It is often used for its timber and has cultural significance in some African communities.
