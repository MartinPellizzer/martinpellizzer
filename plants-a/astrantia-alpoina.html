Astrantia alpoina

Astrantia alpoina, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive star-like appearance.

Astrantia alpoina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine environment.
