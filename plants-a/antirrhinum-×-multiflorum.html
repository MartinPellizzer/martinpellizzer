Antirrhinum × multiflorum

Antirrhinum × multiflorum, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
