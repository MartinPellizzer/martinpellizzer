Apium graveolens subsp. butronense

Apium graveolens subsp.

butronense, commonly known as the Butron wild celery, is a wild-growing member of the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels.

This subspecies is native to the western Mediterranean region and thrives in coastal and saline environments. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the seashore.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
