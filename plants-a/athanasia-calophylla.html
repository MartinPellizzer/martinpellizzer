Athanasia calophylla

Athanasia calophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaved evergreen, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that emit a subtle golden sheen under sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Athanasia calophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and resilience in humid climates.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
