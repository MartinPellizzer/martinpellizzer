Astragalus mugosaricus

Astragalus mugosaricus, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus mugosaricus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
