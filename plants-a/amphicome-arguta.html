Amphicome arguta

Amphicome arguta, commonly known as the hardy marsh saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist and wet habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, white or pale pink, arranged in a compact cluster. Amphicome arguta is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its tolerance to cold temperatures.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing food for various insects and small animals.
