Aster leiophyllus var. incisus

Aster leiophyllus var. incisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
