Attalea colenda

Attalea colenda, commonly known as the Amazonian palm or buriti palm, is a tall, slender tree native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that develop into edible, nutrient-rich fruits. The plant thrives in swampy and flooded environments, making it an important species in the Amazon rainforest ecosystem. Its fruits are a vital food source for both wildlife and local human populations.

The plant's ability to tolerate waterlogged soils makes it a key component of the region's wetland vegetation.
