Astragalus adsurgens f. chandonnetii

Astragalus adsurgens f. chandonnetii, commonly known as the Chandonnet's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and Mongolia.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
