Acaena × subglabra

Acaena × subglabra, commonly known as the silky burr.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Acaena, native to New Zealand, characterized by its dense, spiky bracts that form the characteristic burrs. The plant is a low-growing shrub with compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces numerous seeds enclosed in the prickly burrs.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
