Amarella tenuis

Amarella tenuis, commonly known as the slender amarella, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires careful protection.
