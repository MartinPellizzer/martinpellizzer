Andinia hystricosa

Andinia hystricosa, commonly known as the spiny hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs.

Andinia hystricosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
