Aniba magnifica

Aniba magnifica, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Sateré-Mawé people, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, greenish-black berries. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and ethnobotanical research.

The plant's ecological and cultural significance underscores its importance in both scientific and indigenous contexts.
