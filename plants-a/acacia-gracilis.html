Acacia gracilis

Acacia gracilis, commonly known as slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in summer, which are arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Acacia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
