Annona ubatubensis

Annona ubatubensis, commonly known as the Ubatuba custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a smooth, yellowish fruit with a thick, creamy pulp that has a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
