Acalypha wilkesiana f. triumphans

Acalypha wilkesiana f. triumphans, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are typically red, orange, or yellow in color. The plant has palmate leaves with a soft, velvety texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

The bracts are not true flowers but modified leaves that serve to attract pollinators.
