Aniba permollis

Aniba permollis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued in the production of musical instruments. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.
