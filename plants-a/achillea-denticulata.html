Achillea denticulata

Achillea denticulata, commonly known as the toothed yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its common name is derived from the ancient Greek myth of Achilles, who supposedly used the plant to treat wounds.
