Amaranthus urceolatus

Amaranthus urceolatus, commonly known as the cup-shaped amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, spire-like inflorescence that resembles a cup or urn, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Amaranthus urceolatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and is also valued for its resilience in various soil conditions.
