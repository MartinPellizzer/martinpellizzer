Acrocomia fusiformis

Acrocomia fusiformis, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Acrocomia fusiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
