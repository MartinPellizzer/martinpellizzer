Astragalus prattii var. multiflorus

Astragalus prattii var. multiflorus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow to reddish-purple flowers. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
