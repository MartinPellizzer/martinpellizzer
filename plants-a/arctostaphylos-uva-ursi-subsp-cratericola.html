Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp. cratericola

Arctostaphylos uva-ursi subsp.

cratericola, commonly known as the Sierra bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States. It features small, leathery leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and adaptability to harsh mountain conditions.
