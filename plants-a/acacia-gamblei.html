Acacia gamblei

Acacia gamblei, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear phyllodes that are typically white to pale green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear during the autumn and winter seasons. Acacia gamblei is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various native animals.
