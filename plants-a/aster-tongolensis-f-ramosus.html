Aster tongolensis f. ramosus

Aster tongolensis f. ramosus, commonly known as the ramose aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or white. Native to Southeast Asia, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
