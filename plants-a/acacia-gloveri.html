Acacia gloveri

Acacia gloveri, commonly known as the Glove acacia, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, twisted branches and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Acacia gloveri is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides habitat for various desert organisms.
