Acronychia trifoliolata var. trifoliolata

Acronychia trifoliolata var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf acronychia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of three, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often peeling in thin layers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly grown in gardens and as a shade tree in suitable climates.
