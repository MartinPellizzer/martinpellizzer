Anthurium subtruncatum

Anthurium subtruncatum, commonly known as the truncated anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces striking spadix flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from red to orange in hue. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Anthurium subtruncatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
