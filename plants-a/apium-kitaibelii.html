Apium kitaibelii

Apium kitaibelii, commonly known as the Japanese celery or wild celery, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant has slender, hollow stems and finely divided leaves that resemble those of parsley. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the family.

Apium kitaibelii is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
