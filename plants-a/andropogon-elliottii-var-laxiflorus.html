Andropogon elliottii var. laxiflorus

Andropogon elliottii var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the eastern woodland bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, blue-green flowers in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish tint that gives it its common name.

This variety is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
