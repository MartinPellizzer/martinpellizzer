Amsinckia borealis

Amsinckia borealis, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, hairy leaves adapted to arid environments. It produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Amsinckia borealis is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
