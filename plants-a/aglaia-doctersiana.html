Aglaia doctersiana

Aglaia doctersiana, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic uses.
