Acrostichum bifidum

Acrostichum bifidum, commonly known as the bifid fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often divided into two distinct parts, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are typically green and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Acrostichum bifidum plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for small invertebrates and other organisms.
