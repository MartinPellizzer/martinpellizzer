Albizia petersiana subsp. petersiana

Albizia petersiana subsp.

petersiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It features compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stem.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
