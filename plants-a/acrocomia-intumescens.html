Acrocomia intumescens

Acrocomia intumescens, commonly known as the swollen palm or giant pinnate palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, ornamental fruits that are typically clustered in panicles, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for tropical and subtropical regions.
