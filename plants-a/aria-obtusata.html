Aria obtusata

Aria obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like taro and calla lilies. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its underground stems, known as rhizomes, are used to produce a starchy, edible root.

Aria obtusata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
