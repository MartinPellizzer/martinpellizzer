Aster foliaceus var. burkei

Aster foliaceus var. burkei, commonly known as the Burke's leafy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open woodlands and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

The species is also known for its role in supporting local pollinators and contributing to ecological diversity.
