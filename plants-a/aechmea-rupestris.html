Aechmea rupestris

Aechmea rupestris, commonly known as the rock aechmea, is a bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Aechmea rupestris is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Morphology

Aechmea rupestris has narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette formation, with a bright green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, compact stem with a smooth, dark green bark that grows upright. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom during the summer months, and it is pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor the plant in rocky or well-drained soil.
