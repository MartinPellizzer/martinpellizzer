Atropis peisonis

Atropis peisonis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Atropis peisonis make it a unique and sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
