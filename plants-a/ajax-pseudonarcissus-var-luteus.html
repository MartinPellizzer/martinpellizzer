Ajax pseudonarcissus var. luteus

Ajax pseudonarcissus var. luteus, commonly known as the golden daffodil, is a striking variant of the daffodil species characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, tall flower stem with one or more blossoms. Its flowers have a trumpet-shaped corona that is typically a deeper shade of yellow than the tepals.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil with full sun.
