Acer capillipes subsp. insulare

Acer capillipes subsp.

insulare, commonly known as the Ryukyu maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan. It is characterized by its finely serrated, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate climates.
