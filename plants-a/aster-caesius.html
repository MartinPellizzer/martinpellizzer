Aster caesius

Aster caesius, commonly known as the blue aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

Aster caesius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
