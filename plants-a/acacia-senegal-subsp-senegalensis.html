Acacia senegal subsp. senegalensis

Acacia senegal subsp.

senegalensis, commonly known as the gum arabic tree, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa. It is characterized by its thorny, bushy shrub or small tree form, with bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is well-known for producing a resin called gum arabic, which is harvested from its bark. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on sandy soils.

This species plays an important ecological and economic role in its native habitat.
