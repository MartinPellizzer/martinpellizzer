Anacampseros formosa

Anacampseros formosa, commonly known as the Fairy Fan Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
