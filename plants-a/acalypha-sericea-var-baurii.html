Acalypha sericea var. baurii

Acalypha sericea var. baurii, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often concealed by the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a landscape plant in warm climates.
