Andropogon muralis

Andropogon muralis, commonly known as the wall grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, streambanks, and other humid environments. The plant has slender, upright stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Andropogon muralis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
