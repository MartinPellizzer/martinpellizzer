Argemone pyrenaica

Argemone pyrenaica, commonly known as the Spanish buttercup, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces yellow flowers with five petals, and its seed pods are distinctive, resembling small, inflated capsules. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lobed or divided. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
