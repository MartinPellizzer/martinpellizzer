Astragalus follicularis

Astragalus follicularis, commonly known as the follicular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits called follicles, which contain the seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus follicularis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
