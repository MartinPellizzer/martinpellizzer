Apium petroselinum

Apium petroselinum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are used both as a spice and a garnish in cooking. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, forming umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels.

Parsley is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
