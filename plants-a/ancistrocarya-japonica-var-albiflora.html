Ancistrocarya japonica var. albiflora

Ancistrocarya japonica var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese hickory, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk.
