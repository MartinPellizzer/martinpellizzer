Anticona glareophila

Anticona glareophila, commonly known as the golden glory, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Anticona glareophila is often cultivated in gardens for its bold color and ability to add visual interest to landscapes.
