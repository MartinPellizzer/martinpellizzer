Althaea ficifolia

Althaea ficifolia, commonly known as the Persian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Althaea ficifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional medicine.
