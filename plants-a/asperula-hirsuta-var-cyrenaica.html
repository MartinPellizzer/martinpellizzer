Asperula hirsuta var. cyrenaica

Asperula hirsuta var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaic hirsute woodruff, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often described as velvety to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
