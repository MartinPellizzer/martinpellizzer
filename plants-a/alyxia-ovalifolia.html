Alyxia ovalifolia

Alyxia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf alyxia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Alyxia ovalifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
