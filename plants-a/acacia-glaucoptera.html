Acacia glaucoptera

Acacia glaucoptera, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia glaucoptera is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
