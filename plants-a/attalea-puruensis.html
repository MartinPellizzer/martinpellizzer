Attalea puruensis

Attalea puruensis, commonly known as the "palm of the Amazon," is a tall, slender palm native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and a single, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible fruits, which are consumed by both wildlife and humans. Its roots play a crucial role in stabilizing the soil in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
