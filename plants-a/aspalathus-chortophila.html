Aspalathus chortophila

Aspalathus chortophila, commonly known as the mountain tea, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Aspalathus chortophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
