Anacamptis morio subsp. heraclea

Anacamptis morio subsp.

heraclea, commonly known as the Heraclea Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
