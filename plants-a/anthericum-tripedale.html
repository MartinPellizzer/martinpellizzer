Anthericum tripedale

Anthericum tripedale, commonly known as the three-toed arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often mottled with white and green patterns. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Anthericum tripedale is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
