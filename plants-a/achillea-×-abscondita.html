Achillea × abscondita

Achillea × abscondita, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, flat-topped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Achillea × abscondita is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
