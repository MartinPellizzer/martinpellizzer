Atriplex magdalenae

Atriplex magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is well adapted to dry, saline soils. Atriplex magdalenae plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert environments and is often found in areas with low precipitation.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration in arid landscapes.
