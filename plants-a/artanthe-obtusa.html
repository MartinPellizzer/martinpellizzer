Artanthe obtusa

Artanthe obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are small, yellowish to white, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Artanthe obtusa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
