Anisodens arvensis

Anisodens arvensis, commonly known as field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple or pinkish composite flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Anisodens arvensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
