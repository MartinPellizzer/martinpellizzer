Astragalus haydenianus f. nelsonii

Astragalus haydenianus f. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid regions.
