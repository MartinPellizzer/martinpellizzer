Arenaria balfouriana

Arenaria balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
