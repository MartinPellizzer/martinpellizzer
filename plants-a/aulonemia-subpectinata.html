Aulonemia subpectinata

Aulonemia subpectinata, commonly known as the silver flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Aulonemia subpectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
