Agave americana var. picta

Agave americana var. picta, commonly known as the Variegated Century Plant, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green with white or yellow variegation. The leaves are often lined with white or yellow margins, creating a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom once in its lifetime.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
