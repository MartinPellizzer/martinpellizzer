Allium reticulatum var. playanum

Allium reticulatum var. playanum, commonly known as the Playanum leek, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive reticulated pattern on the bulb, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
