Agropyron pectinatum var. imbricatum

Agropyron pectinatum var. imbricatum, commonly known as the needlegrass or prairie grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are often arranged in a compact, brush-like structure. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and maintaining grassland ecosystems.
