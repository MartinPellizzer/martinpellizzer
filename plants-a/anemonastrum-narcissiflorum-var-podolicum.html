Anemonastrum narcissiflorum var. podolicum

Anemonastrum narcissiflorum var. podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic narcissus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure, resembling a daffodil. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
