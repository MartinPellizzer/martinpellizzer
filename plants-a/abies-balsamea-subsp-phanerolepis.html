Abies balsamea subsp. phanerolepis

Abies balsamea subsp.

phanerolepis, commonly known as the eastern white fir, is a coniferous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
