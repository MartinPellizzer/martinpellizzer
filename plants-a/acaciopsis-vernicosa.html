Acaciopsis vernicosa

Acaciopsis vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its bark is thin and can be easily stripped, revealing a fibrous inner layer.

Acaciopsis vernicosa is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in gardens.
