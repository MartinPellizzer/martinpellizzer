Acanthopanax aculeatus

Acanthopanax aculeatus, commonly known as the Chinese angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in forested areas. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese medicine.

The species is also used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
