Amberboa crupinoides

Amberboa crupinoides, commonly known as the golden tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in mild climates.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils adds to its botanical significance.
