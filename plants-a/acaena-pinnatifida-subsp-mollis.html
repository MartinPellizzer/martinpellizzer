Acaena pinnatifida subsp. mollis

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves that are pinnately lobed.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The subspecies mollis is distinguished by its softer, more hairy foliage compared to other subspecies of Acaena pinnatifida.
