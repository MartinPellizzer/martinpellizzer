Alpinia macrostephana

Alpinia macrostephana, commonly known as the giant ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy texture.

Alpinia macrostephana is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its use in traditional medicine.
