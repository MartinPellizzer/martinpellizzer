Artanthe leptura

Artanthe leptura, commonly known as the slender artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

Artanthe leptura is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
