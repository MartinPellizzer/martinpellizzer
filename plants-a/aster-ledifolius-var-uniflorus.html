Aster ledifolius var. uniflorus

Aster ledifolius var. uniflorus, commonly known as the one-flowered aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, showy blue to purple flower at the end of each stem during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and drought tolerance make it an interesting addition to native plant gardens.
