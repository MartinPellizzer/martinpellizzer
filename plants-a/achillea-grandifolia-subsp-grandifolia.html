Achillea grandifolia subsp. grandifolia

Achillea grandifolia subsp.

grandifolia, commonly known as the giant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features a robust root system and upright, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. Its foliage consists of finely divided, silver-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
