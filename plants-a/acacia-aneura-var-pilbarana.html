Acacia aneura var. pilbarana

Acacia aneura var. pilbarana, commonly known as the Pilbara acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, grey-green leaves that are usually reduced to spines. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
