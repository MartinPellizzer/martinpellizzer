Aembilla

Aembilla, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. Aembilla thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
