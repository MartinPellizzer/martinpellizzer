Achras paludosa

Achras paludosa, commonly known as the swamp candle, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy berries. Its roots are adapted to waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in moist garden environments.
