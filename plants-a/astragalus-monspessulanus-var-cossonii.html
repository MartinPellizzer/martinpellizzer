Astragalus monspessulanus var. cossonii

Astragalus monspessulanus var. cossonii, commonly known as the Cosson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, calcareous soils, often in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, round seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its resilience and beauty.
