Argythamnia paludosa

Argythamnia paludosa, commonly known as the marsh hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dense clusters, often in muddy or sandy soils. It is adapted to moist environments and can tolerate periods of flooding.

Argythamnia paludosa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
