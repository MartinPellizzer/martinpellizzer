Aglaia macrocarpa

Aglaia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are oppositely arranged. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in panicles, and it produces large, fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
