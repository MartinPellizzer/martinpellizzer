Artocarpus lanceifolius subsp. clementis

Artocarpus lanceifolius subsp.

clementis, commonly known as the Clemen's breadfruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique fruit with a thick, fibrous rind.

The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its attractive foliage.
