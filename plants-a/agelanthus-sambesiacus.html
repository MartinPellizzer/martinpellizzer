Agelanthus sambesiacus

Agelanthus sambesiacus, commonly known as the Sambesia tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and potential medicinal contexts.
