Astragalus wushanicus

Astragalus wushanicus, commonly known as the Wushan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow to pale purple flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus wushanicus is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
