Aniba citrifolia

Aniba citrifolia, commonly known as the sour orange or citron tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are highly acidic and used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Aniba citrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
