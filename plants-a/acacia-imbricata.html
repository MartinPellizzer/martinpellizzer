Acacia imbricata

Acacia imbricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia imbricata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in reforestation efforts due to its hardiness and ability to fix nitrogen.
