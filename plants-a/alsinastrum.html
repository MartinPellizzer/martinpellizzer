Alsinastrum

Alsinastrum, commonly known as goatweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The flowers are typically greenish or yellowish, with inconspicuous petals, and are often arranged in dense inflorescences. Many species are native to arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in hot and dry climates.

Alsinastrum species are often considered weeds but can also be used in traditional medicine or as forage for livestock.
