Aster leiophyllus var. intermedius

Aster leiophyllus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate blue aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
