Asplenium cuneatum var. laserpitiifolium

Asplenium cuneatum var. laserpitiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spleenwort, is a fern species belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped, and often dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Its intricate leaf structure and ability to thrive in low-light conditions make it a unique and interesting subject for botanical study.
