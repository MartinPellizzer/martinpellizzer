Argemone sulphurea

Argemone sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur flower or yellow argemone, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a sulfur-like scent, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as wastelands and roadsides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branched.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds, contributing to its propagation in various environments.
