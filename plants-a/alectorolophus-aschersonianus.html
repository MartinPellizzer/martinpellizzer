Alectorolophus aschersonianus

Alectorolophus aschersonianus, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in harsh environments. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Alectorolophus aschersonianus is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
