Avenella flexuosa subsp. foliosa

Avenella flexuosa subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved avenella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by triangular, three-lobed fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other subspecies of Avenella flexuosa.
