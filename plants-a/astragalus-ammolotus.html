Astragalus ammolotus

Astragalus ammolotus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, featuring deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across semi-desert habitats.
