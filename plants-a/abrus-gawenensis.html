Abrus gawenensis

Abrus gawenensis, commonly known as the African jequirity, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, red-and-black seed pods. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers and has pinnate leaves with trifoliate leaflets. Its seeds are encased in a hard, brittle pod that splits open when mature, releasing the seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
