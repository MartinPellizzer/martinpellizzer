Arthraxon hispidus var. nudus

Arthraxon hispidus var. nudus, commonly known as the smooth false grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless or only slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
