Arecastrum romanzoffianum var. australe

Arecastrum romanzoffianum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
