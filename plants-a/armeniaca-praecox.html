Armeniaca praecox

Armeniaca praecox, commonly known as the peach, is a deciduous tree native to Asia and widely cultivated for its fruit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers in spring, which give way to fuzzy, yellowish fruits. The fruit's flesh is juicy and sweet, containing a single large seed.

Armeniaca praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
