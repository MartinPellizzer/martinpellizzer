Acnida rhyssocarpa

Acnida rhyssocarpa, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive orange to red berries. Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
