Ajuga candolleana

Ajuga candolleana, commonly known as the Himalayan bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically purple or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Ajuga candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
