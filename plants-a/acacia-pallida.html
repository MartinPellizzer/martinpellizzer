Acacia pallida

Acacia pallida, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia pallida is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various native animals.
