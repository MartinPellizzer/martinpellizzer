Astragalus amalecitanus

Astragalus amalecitanus, commonly known as the Amalecitanus milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of many legumes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astragalus amalecitanus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.
