Astragalus ekbergii

Astragalus ekbergii, commonly known as the Ekberg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Astragalus ekbergii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
