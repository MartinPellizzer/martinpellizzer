Abelia spathulata var. macrophylla

Abelia spathulata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 5 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
