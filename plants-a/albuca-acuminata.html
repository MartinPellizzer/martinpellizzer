Albuca acuminata

Albuca acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant is known for its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca acuminata is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
