Angraecoides rostrata

Angraecoides rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a long, slender spur that extends from the base of the flower. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific nocturnal moths.

Angraecoides rostrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and contributes to the biodiversity of Madagascar's unique flora.
