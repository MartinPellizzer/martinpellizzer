Amorpha fruticosa var. vulgaris

Amorpha fruticosa var. vulgaris, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 8 to 16 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets, and produces showy, pea-shaped yellow flowers in late spring. The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
