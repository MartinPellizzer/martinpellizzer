Arabis hirsuta var. hornungiana

Arabis hirsuta var. hornungiana, commonly known as the Hornung's hairy rockcress, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or arid environments. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
