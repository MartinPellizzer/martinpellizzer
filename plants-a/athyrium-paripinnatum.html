Athyrium paripinnatum

Athyrium paripinnatum, commonly known as the Japanese holly fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a delicate and feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Athyrium paripinnatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
