Aerides savageana

Aerides savageana, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, and its inflorescence is a raceme of numerous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
