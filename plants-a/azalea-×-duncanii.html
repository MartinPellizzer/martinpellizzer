Azalea × duncanii

Azalea × duncanii, commonly known as the Duncans azalea, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or orange-red blooms in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Azalea × duncanii thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
