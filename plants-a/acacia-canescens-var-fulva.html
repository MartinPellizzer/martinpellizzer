Acacia canescens var. fulva

Acacia canescens var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bright yellow flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil rehabilitation efforts.
