Ageratina occidentalis

Ageratina occidentalis, commonly known as whiteflower false fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Ageratina occidentalis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
