Alstroemeria pulchella var. pilosa

Alstroemeria pulchella var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden planting.

This variety is known for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to different growing conditions.
