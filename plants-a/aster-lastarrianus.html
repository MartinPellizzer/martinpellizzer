Aster lastarrianus

Aster lastarrianus, commonly known as the Lastarrian's aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the late summer to early fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
