Astragalus libanotis

Astragalus libanotis, commonly known as the Lebanon milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Astragalus libanotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
