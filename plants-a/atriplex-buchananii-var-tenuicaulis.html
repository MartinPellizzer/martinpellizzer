Atriplex buchananii var. tenuicaulis

Atriplex buchananii var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slenderstem saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of salt-tolerant hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are adapted to the harsh conditions of its environment. It is well-suited to saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland saltbush communities.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in dry, saline habitats.
