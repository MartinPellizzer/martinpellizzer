Acacia brevipes

Acacia brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf acacia or the yellow-wood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like stems that perform the function of leaves, adapting to the harsh, dry environment. Acacia brevipes is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

It also provides habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
