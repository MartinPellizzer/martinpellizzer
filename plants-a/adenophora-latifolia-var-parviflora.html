Adenophora latifolia var. parviflora

Adenophora latifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
