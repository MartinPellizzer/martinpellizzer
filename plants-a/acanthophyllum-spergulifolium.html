Acanthophyllum spergulifolium

Acanthophyllum spergulifolium, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, succulent stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thickened stems and leaves.

Due to its resilience, it plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
