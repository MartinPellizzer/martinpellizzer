Anastraphia ilicifolia

Anastraphia ilicifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Anastraphia ilicifolia is often found in heathlands and is an important component of local ecosystems.
