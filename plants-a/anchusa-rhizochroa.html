Anchusa rhizochroa

Anchusa rhizochroa, commonly known as the purple anchusa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
