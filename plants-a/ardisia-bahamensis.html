Ardisia bahamensis

Ardisia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian evergreen, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Bahamas and parts of the Caribbean.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
