Asparagus filicinus var. microclada

Asparagus filicinus var. microclada, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus fern, is a low-growing, evergreen plant native to southern Africa.

It features feathery, lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
