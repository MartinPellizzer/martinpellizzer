Archidendron parviflorum var. parviflorum

Archidendron parviflorum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered legume, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
