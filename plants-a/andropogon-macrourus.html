Andropogon macrourus

Andropogon macrourus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a tall grass species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, bluish-green leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun. It plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its fibrous roots help prevent erosion, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
