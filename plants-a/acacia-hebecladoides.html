Acacia hebecladoides

Acacia hebecladoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resistance to salt spray.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping in coastal and dry regions.
