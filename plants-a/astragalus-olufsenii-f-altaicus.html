Astragalus olufsenii f. altaicus

Astragalus olufsenii f. altaicus, commonly known as the Altai milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Altai Mountains region of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions.

Astragalus olufsenii f. altaicus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
