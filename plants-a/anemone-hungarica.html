Anemone hungarica

Anemone hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features showy, white or pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it has a short, rhizomatous root system.

Anemone hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
