Acacia elephantorrhiza

Acacia elephantorrhiza, commonly known as the African umbrella thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like crown formed by its long, drooping branches. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that perform photosynthesis. It produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds.

Acacia elephantorrhiza is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
