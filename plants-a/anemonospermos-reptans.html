Anemonospermos reptans

Anemonospermos reptans, commonly known as the creeping anemonosperma, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a fibrous root system and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. It is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to survive in environments with limited water availability.
