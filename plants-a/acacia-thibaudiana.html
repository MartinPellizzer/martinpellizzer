Acacia thibaudiana

Acacia thibaudiana, commonly known as the Tibaud's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and these are typically narrow and linear. Acacia thibaudiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
