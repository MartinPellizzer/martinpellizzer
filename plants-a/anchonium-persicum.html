Anchonium persicum

Anchonium persicum, commonly known as Persian anchovy grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in traditional landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Anchonium persicum is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
