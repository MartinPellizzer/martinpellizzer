Aristida planifolia

Aristida planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, bristly flowers.

Aristida planifolia is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its distinctive flat, broad leaf blades.
