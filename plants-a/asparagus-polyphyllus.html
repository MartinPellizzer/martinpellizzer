Asparagus polyphyllus

Asparagus polyphyllus, commonly known as the giant asparagus or the red asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Asparagus polyphyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
