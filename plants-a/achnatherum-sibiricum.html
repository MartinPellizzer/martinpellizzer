Achnatherum sibiricum

Achnatherum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Achnatherum sibiricum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
