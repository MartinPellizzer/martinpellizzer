Achnatherum parishii

Achnatherum parishii, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spikelet-based flowers in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Achnatherum parishii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
