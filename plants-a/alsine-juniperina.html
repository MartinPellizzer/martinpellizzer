Alsine juniperina

Alsine juniperina, commonly known as the Juniper-like Alsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or low-growing, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
