Arctostaphylos pungens subsp. roofii

Arctostaphylos pungens subsp.

roofii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable environments.
