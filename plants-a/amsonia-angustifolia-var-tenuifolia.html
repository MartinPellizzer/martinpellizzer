Amsonia angustifolia var. tenuifolia

Amsonia angustifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, blue-gray flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long-lasting floral display.
