Alnus orientalis f. puberula

Alnus orientalis f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent oriental alder, is a variant of the oriental alder native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its finely hairy, pubescent stems and leaves. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a serrated margin. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
