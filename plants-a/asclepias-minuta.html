Asclepias minuta

Asclepias minuta, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are covered in fine, white to greenish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Asclepias minuta is a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and is also known for its medicinal and historical uses by Indigenous peoples.
