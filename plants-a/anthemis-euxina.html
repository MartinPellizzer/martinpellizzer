Anthemis euxina

Anthemis euxina, commonly known as the Crimean tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
