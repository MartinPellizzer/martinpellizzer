Aira flexilis

Aira flexilis, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Aira flexilis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
