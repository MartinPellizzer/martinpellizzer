Astragalus craccinopsis

Astragalus craccinopsis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus craccinopsis is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.
