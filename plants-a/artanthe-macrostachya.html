Artanthe macrostachya

Artanthe macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked artanthe, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Artanthe macrostachya is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
