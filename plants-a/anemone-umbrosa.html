Anemone umbrosa

Anemone umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anemone umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
