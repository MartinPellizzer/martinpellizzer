Avena sterilis var. ludoviciana

Avena sterilis var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
