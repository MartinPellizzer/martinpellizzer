Anthistiria andropogonoides

Anthistiria andropogonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers.
