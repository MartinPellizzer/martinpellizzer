Acer kungshanense var. acuminatilobum

Acer kungshanense var. acuminatilobum, commonly known as the Kungshan maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically 5-7 lobed and have a pointed, acutely lobed shape. The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, making it a popular ornamental species. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often growing in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
