Agapetes camelliifolia

Agapetes camelliifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring. Agapetes camelliifolia thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in shaded garden settings.
