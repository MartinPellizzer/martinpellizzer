Astragalus durhamii

Astragalus durhamii, commonly known as Durham's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.

Astragalus durhamii is often found in disturbed or dry areas, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.
