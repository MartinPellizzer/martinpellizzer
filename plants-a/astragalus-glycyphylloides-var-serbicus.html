Astragalus glycyphylloides var. serbicus

Astragalus glycyphylloides var. serbicus, commonly known as Serbian sweet vetch, is a perennial legume native to Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds enclosed in a hairy pod. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
