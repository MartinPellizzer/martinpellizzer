Adesmia obcordata

Adesmia obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Adesmia obcordata is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
