Actaea europaea

Actaea europaea, commonly known as European black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use, containing compounds like actein and tannins.

Actaea europaea thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and pharmacological properties.
