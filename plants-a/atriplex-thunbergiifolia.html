Atriplex thunbergiifolia

Atriplex thunbergiifolia, commonly known as the Thunberg's saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a layer of salt-tolerant hairs, aiding in water retention and reducing transpiration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal areas or inland salt lakes.

Atriplex thunbergiifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
