Alpinia glabrescens

Alpinia glabrescens, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or white ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known gingers such as turmeric and cardamom. The plant features upright, narrow leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are utilized for their aromatic properties and have been valued in herbal remedies.

The botanical characteristics of Alpinia glabrescens make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
