Androsace graminifolia

Androsace graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy androsace, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Androsace graminifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows.
