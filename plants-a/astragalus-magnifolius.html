Astragalus magnifolius

Astragalus magnifolius, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus magnifolius is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
