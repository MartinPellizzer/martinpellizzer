Amaranthus retroflexus var. hybridus

Amaranthus retroflexus var. hybridus, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant produces triangular, smooth-edged leaves that are typically hairless. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and seed dispersal.
