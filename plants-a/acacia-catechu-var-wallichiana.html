Acacia catechu var. wallichiana

Acacia catechu var. wallichiana, commonly known as the Indian blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and bipinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing dark brown seeds. It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of tannins.

The species is also known for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
