Aralia echinops

Aralia echinops, commonly known as the spiny aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are typically divided into several narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. Its growth habit is upright, and it can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

Aralia echinops is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
