Acacia baueri var. aspera

Acacia baueri var. aspera, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown, fissured bark and compound leaves with small, stiff, acicular leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in summer, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
