Anogeissus leiocarpa f. grandifolia

Anogeissus leiocarpa f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered tamanu, is a variant of the tamanu tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, making it visually striking. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland areas.

The botanical features of this plant make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
