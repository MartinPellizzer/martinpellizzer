Aster dysentericus

Aster dysentericus, commonly known as the yellow starwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Aster dysentericus is known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic in high doses.
