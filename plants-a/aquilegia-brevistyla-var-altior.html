Aquilegia brevistyla var. altior

Aquilegia brevistyla var. altior, commonly known as the tall shortstyle columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and heart-shaped. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with five petals and distinctive nectar spurs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
