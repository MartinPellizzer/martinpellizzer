Artemisia capillaris var. sachalinensis

Artemisia capillaris var. sachalinensis, commonly known as Japanese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, gray-green leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in moist or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
