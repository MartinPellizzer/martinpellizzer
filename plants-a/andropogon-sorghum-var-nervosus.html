Andropogon sorghum var. nervosus

Andropogon sorghum var. nervosus, commonly known as narrow-leaf sorghum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and robust, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and as a forage crop in some regions.
