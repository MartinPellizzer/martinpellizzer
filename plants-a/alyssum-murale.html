Alyssum murale

Alyssum murale, commonly known as wall flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, making it ideal for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

Alyssum murale is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile garden plant.
