Acidanthera platypetala

Acidanthera platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves. Its flowers are characterized by their flat, spurred petals and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
