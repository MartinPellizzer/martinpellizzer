Arabis hirsuta f. krausei

Arabis hirsuta f. krausei, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
