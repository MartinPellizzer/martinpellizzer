Aconitum villiferum

Aconitum villiferum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Aconitum villiferum is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
