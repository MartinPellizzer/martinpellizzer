Actinea heterophylla

Actinea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable actinia, is a unique marine invertebrate belonging to the family Actiniidae.

It is characterized by its flexible, elongated body and a crown of tentacles used for capturing prey. The plant exhibits a wide range of morphological variations, with differences in color, size, and shape of its polyps. It thrives in shallow coastal waters and is often found in rocky substrates.

Due to its adaptability, Actinea heterophylla plays an important role in marine ecosystems as both predator and prey.
