Abdulmajidia zainudiniana

Abdulmajidia zainudiniana, commonly known as the Zainudin's ghost orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a strikingly unusual coloration that ranges from pale pink to almost white. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are typically small and ephemeral, blooming only briefly during the wet season.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, it is considered an endangered species with conservation efforts underway to protect its dwindling population.
