Ampelocissus multifoliola

Ampelocissus multifoliola, commonly known as the African fig or nettle fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its vigorous growth makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in ornamental gardens.
