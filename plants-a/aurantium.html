Aurantium

Aurantium, commonly known as bitter orange, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and oval, green fruits that turn orange when mature. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of essential oils from its peel. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The fruit contains a high concentration of flavonoids and is used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
