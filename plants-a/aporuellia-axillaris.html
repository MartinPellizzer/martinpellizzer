Aporuellia axillaris

Aporuellia axillaris, commonly known as the rock wall vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and in open woodlands, where it can climb or trail along the ground. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinct tubular corolla.
