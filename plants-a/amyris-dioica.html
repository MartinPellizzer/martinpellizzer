Amyris dioica

Amyris dioica, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly aromatic scent.

Amyris dioica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
