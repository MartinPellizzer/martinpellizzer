Agave caerulescens var. grisea

Agave caerulescens var. grisea, commonly known as the gray agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, with succulent leaves that store water efficiently.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
