Acer palmatum f. crispum

Acer palmatum f. crispum, commonly known as the Japanese maple with crisped leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, deeply lobed leaves that exhibit a distinctive crinkled or curled texture. The foliage displays a range of colors, from vibrant green in spring to fiery red, orange, and yellow in autumn. This ornamental plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its unique leaf structure and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic appeal in both residential and public gardens.
