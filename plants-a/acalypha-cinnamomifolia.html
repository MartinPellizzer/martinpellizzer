Acalypha cinnamomifolia

Acalypha cinnamomifolia, commonly known as cinnamon leaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and compact growth habit.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a garden plant.
