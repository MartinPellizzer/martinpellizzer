Aira aquatica var. ochroleuca

Aira aquatica var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow bulrush, is a variant of the aquatic plant Aira aquatica.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage and is typically found in wetland environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense tufts, often forming colonies in shallow water. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

This variant is adapted to moist or submerged conditions and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
