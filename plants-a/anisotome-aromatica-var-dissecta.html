Anisotome aromatica var. dissecta

Anisotome aromatica var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected anisotome, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and complex inflorescences. The plant features finely dissected leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
