Acacia drummondii var. typica

Acacia drummondii var. typica, commonly known as the Drummond's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, pea-like flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

The species is often used in revegetation projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
