Astragalus oxyglottis

Astragalus oxyglottis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its pods contain small, rounded seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture availability.

Astragalus oxyglottis plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
