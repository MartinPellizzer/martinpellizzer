Artemisia maritima proles pseudogallica

Artemisia maritima proles pseudogallica, commonly known as the coastal tarragon, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its leaves are used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
