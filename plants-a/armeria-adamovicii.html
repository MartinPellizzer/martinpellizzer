Armeria adamovicii

Armeria adamovicii, commonly known as the Armenian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Armeria adamovicii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
