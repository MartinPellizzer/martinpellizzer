Allium praescissum

Allium praescissum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-shaped, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of purple or pinkish flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible bulb, which is used in culinary applications.

Allium praescissum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
