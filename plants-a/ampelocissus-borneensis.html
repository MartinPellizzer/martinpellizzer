Ampelocissus borneensis

Ampelocissus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean climbing fig, is a vine native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments and is often found growing on trees or shrubs.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems.
