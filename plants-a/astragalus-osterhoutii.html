Astragalus osterhoutii

Astragalus osterhoutii, commonly known as Osterhout's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus osterhoutii is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
