Aconitum refractum

Aconitum refractum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, which are typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant produces dark brown, ribbed seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution. Aconitum refractum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
