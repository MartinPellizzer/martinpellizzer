Astragalus drummondii f. melanocalyx

Astragalus drummondii f. melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black foliage, which sets it apart from other forms of Astragalus drummondii. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
