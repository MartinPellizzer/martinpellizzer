Anthemis rumelica

Anthemis rumelica, commonly known as the Balkan chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable environments.
