Amphilophium magnoliifolium

Amphilophium magnoliifolium, commonly known as the magnolia-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of magnolia trees.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is native to the rainforests of Central and South America. The plant features a climbing habit, with aerial roots that help it attach to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, emerging from a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Amphilophium magnoliifolium thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
