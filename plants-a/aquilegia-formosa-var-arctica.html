Aquilegia formosa var. arctica

Aquilegia formosa var. arctica, commonly known as the Arctic columbine, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of North America.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Ranunculaceae family.
