Andropogon friesii

Andropogon friesii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or reddish in color. It is adapted to tolerate standing water and is often found in areas with high moisture levels.

Andropogon friesii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
