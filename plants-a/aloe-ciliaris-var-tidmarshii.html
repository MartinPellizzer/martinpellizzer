Aloe ciliaris var. tidmarshii

Aloe ciliaris var. tidmarshii, commonly known as the Tidmarsh aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
