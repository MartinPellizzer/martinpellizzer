Ammannia auriculata f. longistaminata

Ammannia auriculata f. longistaminata, commonly known as the long-stamen ammannia, is a variant of the common ammannia species.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with distinctive red or pink bracts that add visual interest.

The f. longistaminata form is notable for its elongated stamens, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
