Andira retusa var. oblonga

Andira retusa var. oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf andira, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptic to oblong leaves. The leaves are typically glabrous and have a leathery texture, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
