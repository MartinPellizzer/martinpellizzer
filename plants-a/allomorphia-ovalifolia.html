Allomorphia ovalifolia

Allomorphia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf allomorph, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Allomorphia ovalifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in tropical ecosystems.
