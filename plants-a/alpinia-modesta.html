Alpinia modesta

Alpinia modesta, commonly known as the modest ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in a spike. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia modesta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
