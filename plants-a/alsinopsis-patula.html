Alsinopsis patula

Alsinopsis patula, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
