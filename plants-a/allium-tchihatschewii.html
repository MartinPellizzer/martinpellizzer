Allium tchihatschewii

Allium tchihatschewii, commonly known as the Tchihatschewii garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
