Alafia congolana

Alafia congolana, commonly known as the Congo alafia, is a flowering plant native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

Alafia congolana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
