Aster leiodes

Aster leiodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to blue-violet petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster leiodes thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
