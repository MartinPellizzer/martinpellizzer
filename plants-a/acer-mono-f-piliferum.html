Acer mono f. piliferum

Acer mono f. piliferum, commonly known as the hairy maple, is a unique variant of the singleleaf maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
