Aeschynomene falcata

Aeschynomene falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf aeschynomene, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sickle-shaped leaflet. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Aeschynomene falcata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in dry conditions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
