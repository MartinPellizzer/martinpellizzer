Adenocarpus samniticus

Adenocarpus samniticus, commonly known as the Sicilian burr, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a dry, hairy capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

Adenocarpus samniticus is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
