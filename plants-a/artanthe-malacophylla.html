Artanthe malacophylla

Artanthe malacophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf artanthe, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
