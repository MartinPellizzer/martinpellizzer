Angraecum confusum

Angraecum confusum, commonly known as the Confused Angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, producing intricate, spurred flowers that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Angraecum confusum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
