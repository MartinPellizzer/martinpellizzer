Alchemilla phalacropoda

Alchemilla phalacropoda, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Alchemilla phalacropoda is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
