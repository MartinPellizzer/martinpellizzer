Astragalus albicalycinus

Astragalus albicalycinus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Astragalus albicalycinus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
