Aleuritia borealis

Aleuritia borealis, commonly known as the northern elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for humans and wildlife.

Aleuritia borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local fauna.
