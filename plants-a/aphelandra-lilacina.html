Aphelandra lilacina

Aphelandra lilacina, commonly known as the lilac torch or lilac flame, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble torches, featuring vibrant purple and yellow bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual display. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Aphelandra lilacina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique and eye-catching appearance.
