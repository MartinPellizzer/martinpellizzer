Adesmia aurantiaca

Adesmia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-flowered legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is notable for its striking orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Adesmia aurantiaca is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various climates.
