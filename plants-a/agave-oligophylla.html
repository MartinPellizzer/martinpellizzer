Agave oligophylla

Agave oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-forming structure and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are often followed by a unique fruit known as a "crown of pearls.". Agave oligophylla is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
