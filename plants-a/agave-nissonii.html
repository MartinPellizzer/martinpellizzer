Agave nissonii

Agave nissonii, commonly known as the Nishina agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave nissonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
