Avenastrum krylovii

Avenastrum krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found at high altitudes.

Avenastrum krylovii is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
