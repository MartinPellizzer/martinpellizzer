Asplenium anisophylloides

Asplenium anisophylloides, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often broader than they are long. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Asplenium anisophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
