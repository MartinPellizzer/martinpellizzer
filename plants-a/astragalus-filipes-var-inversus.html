Astragalus filipes var. inversus

Astragalus filipes var. inversus, commonly known as the reverse filiped milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variant is noted for its unique growth habit, which sets it apart from other members of the Astragalus genus.
