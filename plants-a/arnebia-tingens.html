Arnebia tingens

Arnebia tingens, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is known for containing a red dye called alizarin, which has been historically used in textiles and dyes.

Arnebia tingens is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
