Alopecurus fallacinus

Alopecurus fallacinus, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Alopecurus fallacinus is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
