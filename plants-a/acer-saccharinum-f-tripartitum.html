Acer saccharinum f. tripartitum

Acer saccharinum f. tripartitum, commonly known as the three-lobed silver maple, is a variant of the silver maple tree characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The leaves are typically three-lobed, with a silvery-white underside, giving the plant its common name. This ornamental form is prized for its unique foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to eastern North America.

The plant is relatively fast-growing and can reach heights of up to 30 meters in suitable conditions.
