Allium fimbriatum subsp. parryi

Allium fimbriatum subsp.

parryi, commonly known as Parry's onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white to pale purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable component of alpine and steppe ecosystems.
