Alyssum baumgartnerianum

Alyssum baumgartnerianum, commonly known as the golden alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its compact form and ornamental value.

Alyssum baumgartnerianum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
