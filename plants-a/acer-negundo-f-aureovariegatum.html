Acer negundo f. aureovariegatum

Acer negundo f. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its striking foliage.

The leaves are typically variegated with yellow and green patterns, providing ornamental value in gardens. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in spring.

This cultivar is often used for its aesthetic appeal rather than for timber, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
