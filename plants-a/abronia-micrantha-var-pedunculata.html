Abronia micrantha var. pedunculata

Abronia micrantha var. pedunculata, commonly known as the coastal bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is a low-growing herb with prostrate stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of concern in some areas.
