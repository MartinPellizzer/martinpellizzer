Acer opalus subsp. africanum

Acer opalus subsp.

africanum, commonly known as the African maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa. It features a distinctive, rounded crown and smooth, pale grey bark that becomes fissured with age.

The leaves are palmately lobed, with a bright green upper surface and a lighter green underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
