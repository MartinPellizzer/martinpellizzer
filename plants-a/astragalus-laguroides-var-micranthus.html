Astragalus laguroides var. micranthus

Astragalus laguroides var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
