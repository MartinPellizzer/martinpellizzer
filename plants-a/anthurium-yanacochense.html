Anthurium yanacochense

Anthurium yanacochense, commonly known as the Yanacocha arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
