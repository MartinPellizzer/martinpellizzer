Aconitum leucanthemum

Aconitum leucanthemum, commonly known as the white anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces a tall, upright stem. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
