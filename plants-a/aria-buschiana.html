Aria buschiana

Aria buschiana, commonly known as the Busch's aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the central and eastern regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape during late summer and autumn.

Aria buschiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
