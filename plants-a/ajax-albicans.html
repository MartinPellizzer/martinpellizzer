Ajax albicans

Ajax albicans, commonly known as the white ajax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
