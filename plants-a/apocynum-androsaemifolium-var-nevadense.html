Apocynum androsaemifolium var. nevadense

Apocynum androsaemifolium var. nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with silky appendages.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
