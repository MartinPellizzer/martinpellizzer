Abutilon micranthum

Abutilon micranthum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often growing up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
