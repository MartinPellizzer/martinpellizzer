Amphicalyx

Amphicalyx, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Amphicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
