Actinodaphne speciosa

Actinodaphne speciosa, commonly known as the fragrant laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
