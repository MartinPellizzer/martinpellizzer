Acer leipoense subsp. leucotrichum

Acer leipoense subsp.

leucotrichum, commonly known as the white-haired maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China. It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow pubescent (hairy) leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the veins.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
