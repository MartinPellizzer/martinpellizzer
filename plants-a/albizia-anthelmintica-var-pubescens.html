Albizia anthelmintica var. pubescens

Albizia anthelmintica var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy albizia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, giving it the "hairy" designation. The plant produces yellow or pale green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the wet season. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Albizia anthelmintica var. pubescens is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
