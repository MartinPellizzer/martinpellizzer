Aquilaria microcarpa

Aquilaria microcarpa, commonly known as the gaharu tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its fragrant heartwood, which is highly valued for its unique scent. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers and has elliptical, leathery leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered endangered and is protected in many countries.
