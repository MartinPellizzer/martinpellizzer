Astrochlaena melandrioides

Astrochlaena melandrioides, commonly known as the sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which reduces water loss.

Astrochlaena melandrioides is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
