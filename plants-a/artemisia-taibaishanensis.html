Artemisia taibaishanensis

Artemisia taibaishanensis, commonly known as the Taibai Shan wormwood, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in composite inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche, it is considered a rare and potentially valuable species for botanical research.
