Acaena argentea var. lanigera

Acaena argentea var. lanigera, commonly known as silver burr or silver bristled nettle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny burrs that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
