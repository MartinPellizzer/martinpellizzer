Astragalus fragiformis

Astragalus fragiformis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus fragiformis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
