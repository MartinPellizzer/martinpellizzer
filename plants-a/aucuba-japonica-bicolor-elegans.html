Aucuba japonica bicolor-elegans

Aucuba japonica bicolor-elegans, commonly known as the variegated Japanese aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features striking, variegated foliage with white and yellow markings on its dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive leaves and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
