Aphyllon tacnaense

Aphyllon tacnaense, commonly known as the Tacna spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in small, dry capsules. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
