Allium zergericum

Allium zergericum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant features a bulbous base with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. Its botanical characteristics include a strong garlic scent and the ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Allium zergericum is often used in traditional medicine and has culinary applications in some regions.
