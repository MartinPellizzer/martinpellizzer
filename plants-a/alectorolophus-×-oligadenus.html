Alectorolophus × oligadenus

Alectorolophus × oligadenus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Alectorolophus longiflorus and Alectorolophus oligadenus.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring elongated, showy petals and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in plant taxonomy and horticulture.
