Allium montanum subsp. lusitanicum

Allium montanum subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 500 to 1,500 meters.

The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines and have a mild garlic flavor.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Allium species and is characterized by its distinct floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
