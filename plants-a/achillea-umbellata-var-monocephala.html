Achillea umbellata var. monocephala

Achillea umbellata var. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
