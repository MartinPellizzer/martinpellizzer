Acer pseudoplatanus var. vanvolxemii

Acer pseudoplatanus var. vanvolxemii, commonly known as the Vanvolxemii maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with a unique combination of lobes and serrations. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking autumn coloration.
