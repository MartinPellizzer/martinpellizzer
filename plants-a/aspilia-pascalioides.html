Aspilia pascalioides

Aspilia pascalioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit with narrow, gray-green leaves. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across its natural habitat.

Aspilia pascalioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
