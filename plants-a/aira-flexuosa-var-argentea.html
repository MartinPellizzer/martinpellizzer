Aira flexuosa var. argentea

Aira flexuosa var. argentea, commonly known as silver air grass, is a variant of the common air grass native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a striking, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces delicate, grass-like blades that are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and ease of cultivation in shaded garden settings.
