Amanoa guianensis var. oblonga

Amanoa guianensis var. oblonga, commonly known as the oblong leaf amanoa, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly the Amazon Basin.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in dense forest understories. Its leaves are broad and elongated, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
