Alseodaphne semecarpifolia var. angustifolia

Alseodaphne semecarpifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alseodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries, which are an important food source for various bird species. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary forest regrowth.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
