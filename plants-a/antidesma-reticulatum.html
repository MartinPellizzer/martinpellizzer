Antidesma reticulatum

Antidesma reticulatum, commonly known as the African starfruit or red starfruit, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy, star-shaped fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces a characteristic red, star-shaped fruit.

Antidesma reticulatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
