Acanthus ponticus

Acanthus ponticus, commonly known as the Turkish acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Greece, and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with prominent veins and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.

Acanthus ponticus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
