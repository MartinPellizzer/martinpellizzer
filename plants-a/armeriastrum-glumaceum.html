Armeriastrum glumaceum

Armeriastrum glumaceum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
