Allium valdensium

Allium valdensium, commonly known as the Valdese onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant's bulb is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor similar to that of leeks. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium valdensium is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
