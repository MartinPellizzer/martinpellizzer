Atriplex hypsophila

Atriplex hypsophila, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant has a deep root system that enables it to access water from deeper soil layers. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically wind-pollinated.

Atriplex hypsophila plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
