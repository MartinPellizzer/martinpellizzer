Allium hickmanii

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette near the ground.

Allium hickmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
