Astragalus kukkonenii

Astragalus kukkonenii, commonly known as the Kukkonen milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus kukkonenii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
