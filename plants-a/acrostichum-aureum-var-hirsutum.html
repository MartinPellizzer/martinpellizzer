Acrostichum aureum var. hirsutum

Acrostichum aureum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy golden fern, is a variant of the golden fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy fronds that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests. Its fronds are typically bright green with a yellowish hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
