Aspidium melanocaulon

Aspidium melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, wiry stems and delicate, finely divided fronds that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are typically borne on long, creeping rhizomes that help anchor it to substrates.

Aspidium melanocaulon plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
