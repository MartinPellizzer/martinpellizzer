Arctagrostis parviflora

Arctagrostis parviflora, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny blue to purplish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Arctagrostis parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
