Arenga gracilicaulis

Arenga gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces inflorescences with small, clustered flowers, which eventually develop into edible fruits. Arenga gracilicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in landscaping and as a decorative plant in warm climates.
