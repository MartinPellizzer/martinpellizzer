Allium cristophii subsp. cristophii

Allium cristophii subsp.

cristophii, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant produces a bulbous stem with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
