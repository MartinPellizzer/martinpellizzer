Aerides veitchii

Aerides veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid, characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with high humidity.

Aerides veitchii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
