Ascochilus hirtulus var. porphyrochlamys

Ascochilus hirtulus var. porphyrochlamys, commonly known as the Porphyrochlamys orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
