Acacia latipes

Acacia latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. Acacia latipes produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
