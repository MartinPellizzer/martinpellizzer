Auliza vestita

Auliza vestita, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, velvety green leaves and striking, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of white and pink, are often clustered in loose inflorescences.

Auliza vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
