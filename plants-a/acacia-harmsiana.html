Acacia harmsiana

Acacia harmsiana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
