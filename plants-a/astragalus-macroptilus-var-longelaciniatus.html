Astragalus macroptilus var. longelaciniatus

Astragalus macroptilus var. longelaciniatus, commonly known as the long-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

The variety longelaciniatus is distinguished by its longer leaflets compared to other varieties of Astragalus macroptilus.
