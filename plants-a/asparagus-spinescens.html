Asparagus spinescens

Asparagus spinescens, commonly known as the spiny asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in small, sharp spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, needle-like shoots that are harvested for culinary use. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Asparagus spinescens thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or sandy habitats.
