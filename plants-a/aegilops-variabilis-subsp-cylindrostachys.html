Aegilops variabilis subsp. cylindrostachys

Aegilops variabilis subsp.

cylindrostachys, commonly known as the slender wild wheat, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow leaves, which are typically hairy along the edges.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are enclosed in tough, straw-like glumes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
