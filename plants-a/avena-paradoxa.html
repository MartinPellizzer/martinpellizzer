Avena paradoxa

Avena paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass or the false oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Avena paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
