Alsophila plagiopteris var. columbensis

Alsophila plagiopteris var. columbensis, commonly known as the Columbian tree fern, is a variant of the tree fern native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical forests. Its fronds are dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in shape and size.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
