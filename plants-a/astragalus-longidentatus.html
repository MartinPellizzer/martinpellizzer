Astragalus longidentatus

Astragalus longidentatus, commonly known as the long-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. The plant features compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow to purple. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus longidentatus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
