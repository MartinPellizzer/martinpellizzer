Amygdalus armeniacaria

Amygdalus armeniacaria, commonly known as the Armenian peach, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Anatolia. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to peaches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
