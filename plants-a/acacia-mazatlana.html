Acacia mazatlana

Acacia mazatlana, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is distinguished by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow to orange-pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Acacia mazatlana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
