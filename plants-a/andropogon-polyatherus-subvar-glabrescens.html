Andropogon polyatherus subvar. glabrescens

Andropogon polyatherus subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

It is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
