Arctium gracile

Arctium gracile, commonly known as the slender burdock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces distinctive brown, bristly fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Arctium gracile is typically found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.
