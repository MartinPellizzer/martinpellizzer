Aerobion carinatum

Aerobion carinatum, commonly known as the spiked bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are covered in tiny, bladder-like structures used to trap prey. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving water, where it can absorb nutrients from both water and captured organisms. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne on slender stems that rise from the water's surface.

Aerobion carinatum plays a unique role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling small invertebrate populations and contributing to nutrient cycling.
