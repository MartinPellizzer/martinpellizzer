Aster conyzoides var. plantaginifolius

Aster conyzoides var. plantaginifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy, alternate leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. It produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
