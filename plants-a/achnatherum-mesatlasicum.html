Achnatherum mesatlasicum

Achnatherum mesatlasicum, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Achnatherum mesatlasicum is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
