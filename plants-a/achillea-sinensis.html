Achillea sinensis

Achillea sinensis, commonly known as Chinese yarrow, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
