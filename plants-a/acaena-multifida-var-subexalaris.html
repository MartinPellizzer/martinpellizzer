Acaena multifida var. subexalaris

Acaena multifida var. subexalaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, typical of the genus Acaena. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and montane regions.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
