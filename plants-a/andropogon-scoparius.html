Andropogon scoparius

Andropogon scoparius, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. Switchgrass is a member of the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates. It plays an important role in ecological restoration due to its deep root system and ability to thrive in marginal lands.

The plant is also valued for its potential as a biofuel source.
