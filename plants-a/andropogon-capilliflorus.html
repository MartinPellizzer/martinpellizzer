Andropogon capilliflorus

Andropogon capilliflorus, commonly known as narrowflower bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces small, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant member of the grass family.
