Antimima roseola

Antimima roseola, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along forest edges or in disturbed soils.

Antimima roseola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
