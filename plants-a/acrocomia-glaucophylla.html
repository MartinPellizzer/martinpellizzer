Acrocomia glaucophylla

Acrocomia glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf date palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it its common name.

Acrocomia glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical regions.
