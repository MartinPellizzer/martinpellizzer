Alepidea amatymbica var. aquatica

Alepidea amatymbica var. aquatica, commonly known as the water reed or aquatic reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, featuring long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, usually emerging from the water surface. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the structure of aquatic ecosystems.

Alepidea amatymbica var. aquatica plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
