Abies excelsa var. monstrosa

Abies excelsa var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant Himalayan fir, is a variant of the Himalayan fir native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its exceptionally tall stature, often reaching heights of over 60 meters, making it one of the tallest conifer species in the world. The tree has a narrow, conical shape with long, slender, bluish-green needles that are densely packed. Its cones are typically pendulous and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length.

This variety is noted for its slow growth rate and is often found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.
