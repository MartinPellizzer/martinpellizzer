Alchemilla acutiloba f. altissima

Alchemilla acutiloba f. altissima, commonly known as the tall lady's mantle, is a variant of the lady's mantle plant characterized by its taller growth habit compared to the typical species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, particularly found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late spring to early summer, clustered in loose panicles.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.
