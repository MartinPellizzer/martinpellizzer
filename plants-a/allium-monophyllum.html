Allium monophyllum

Allium monophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
