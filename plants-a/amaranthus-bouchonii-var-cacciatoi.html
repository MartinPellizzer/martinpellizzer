Amaranthus bouchonii var. cacciatoi

Amaranthus bouchonii var. cacciatoi, commonly known as the Cacciatoi amaranth, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, which can range from red to purple hues depending on environmental conditions.
