Ambrosia aptera

Ambrosia aptera, commonly known as smooth ambrosia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and beetles.

Ambrosia aptera is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.
