Apodostachys densiflora

Apodostachys densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense inflorescence, which consists of tightly packed, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, while the flowers are often pale green to reddish in color.

Apodostachys densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
