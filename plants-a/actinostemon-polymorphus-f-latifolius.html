Actinostemon polymorphus f. latifolius

Actinostemon polymorphus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf actinostemon, is a variant of the actinostemon genus characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. The leaves are usually a deep green, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.
