Achillea millefolium var. nobilior

Achillea millefolium var. nobilior, commonly known as the noble yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads in shades of white, pink, or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
