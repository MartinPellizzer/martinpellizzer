Agathosma bowiei

Agathosma bowiei, commonly known as buchu, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves that emit a strong, peppery scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rutaceae and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
