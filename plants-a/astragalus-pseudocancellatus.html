Astragalus pseudocancellatus

Astragalus pseudocancellatus, commonly known as the false lattice milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus pseudocancellatus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
