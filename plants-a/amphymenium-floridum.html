Amphymenium floridum

Amphymenium floridum, commonly known as the Florida sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.

Amphymenium floridum plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for native insects.
