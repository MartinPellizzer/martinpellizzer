Astragalus ibapensis

Astragalus ibapensis, commonly known as the Ibapensian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus ibapensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
