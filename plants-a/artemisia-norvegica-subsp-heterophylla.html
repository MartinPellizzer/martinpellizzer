Artemisia norvegica subsp. heterophylla

Artemisia norvegica subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the Norwegian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often variable in shape, which is reflected in its subspecific name "heterophylla.".

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
