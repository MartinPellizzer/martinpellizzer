Anaphalis margaritacea f. anochlora

Anaphalis margaritacea f. anochlora, commonly known as the silverweed or white feather grass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery, woolly foliage and delicate, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster.

The subspecies anochlora is distinguished by its reduced or absent leaf stalks and a more compact growth form compared to other forms of Anaphalis margaritacea.
