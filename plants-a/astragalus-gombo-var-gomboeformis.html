Astragalus gombo var. gomboeformis

Astragalus gombo var. gomboeformis, commonly known as the Gombo milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
