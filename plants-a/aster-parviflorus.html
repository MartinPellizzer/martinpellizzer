Aster parviflorus

Aster parviflorus, commonly known as the smallflower aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems and a valuable addition to wildlife gardens.
