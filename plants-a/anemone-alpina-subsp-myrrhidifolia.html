Anemone alpina subsp. myrrhidifolia

Anemone alpina subsp.

myrrhidifolia, commonly known as the Alpine Anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, with a hairy texture, and produces showy white or pink flowers in early spring. Its stems are usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs, and the flowers have five to seven petals.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions and is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations.
