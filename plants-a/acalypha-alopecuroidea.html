Acalypha alopecuroidea

Acalypha alopecuroidea, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which is often used in horticultural displays.
