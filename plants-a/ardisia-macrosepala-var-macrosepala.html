Ardisia macrosepala var. macrosepala

Ardisia macrosepala var. macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with four to six petals, and it produces clusters of berries that are an important food source for birds.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit.
