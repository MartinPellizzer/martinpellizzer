Aesculus glabra f. pallida

Aesculus glabra f. pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered bottlebrush buckeye, is a cultivar of the common buckeye.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in upright clusters, contrasting with the typical brown flowers of the species. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique floral display.
