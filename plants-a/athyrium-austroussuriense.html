Athyrium austroussuriense

Athyrium austroussuriense, commonly known as the Southern Athyrium, is a species of fern native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium austroussuriense is valued for its ornamental foliage and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
