Asphodelus albus var. littoralis

Asphodelus albus var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky coastal soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

This variety is often found in dune environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
