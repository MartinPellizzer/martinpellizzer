Aspalathus crassicaulis

Aspalathus crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Aspalathus crassicaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
