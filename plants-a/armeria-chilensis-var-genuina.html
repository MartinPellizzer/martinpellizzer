Armeria chilensis var. genuina

Armeria chilensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Chilean sandstem, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features linear, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and maritime climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
