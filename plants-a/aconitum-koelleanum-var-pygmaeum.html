Aconitum koelleanum var. pygmaeum

Aconitum koelleanum var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a spire-like cluster, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately compound leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is cultivated in gardens but requires caution during handling.
