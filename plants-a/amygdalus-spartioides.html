Amygdalus spartioides

Amygdalus spartioides, commonly known as the rock almond or desert almond, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a popular choice for landscaping in drought-prone areas.
