Aster sampsonii

Aster sampsonii, commonly known as the Sampson's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and features showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster sampsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
