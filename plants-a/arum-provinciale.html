Arum provinciale

Arum provinciale, commonly known as the provincial arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
