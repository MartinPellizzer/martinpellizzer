Aylostera heliosa subsp. cajasensis

Aylostera heliosa subsp.

cajasensis, commonly known as the Cajas sun-cup, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps it withstand cold temperatures.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
