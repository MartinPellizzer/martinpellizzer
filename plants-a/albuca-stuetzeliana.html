Albuca stuetzeliana

Albuca stuetzeliana, commonly known as the Stuetzel's albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Albuca stuetzeliana is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants such as amaryllis and daffodils.
