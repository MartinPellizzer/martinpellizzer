Aster ageratoides var. micranthus

Aster ageratoides var. micranthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its small, pale blue to white flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water requirements.
