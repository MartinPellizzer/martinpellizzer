Anemia sertaneja

Anemia sertaneja, commonly known as *Lactuca serriola*, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. It has a milky sap that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

While not cultivated for food, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
