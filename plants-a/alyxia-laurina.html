Alyxia laurina

Alyxia laurina, commonly known as the Florida holly or silverleaf holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and coastal areas. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Alyxia laurina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
