Azara petiolaris

Azara petiolaris, commonly known as the golden azara, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black berries.

Azara petiolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
