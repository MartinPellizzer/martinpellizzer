Allium cupuliferum var. regelii

Allium cupuliferum var. regelii, commonly known as the Siberian leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous stem is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.
