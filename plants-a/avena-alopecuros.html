Avena alopecuros

Avena alopecuros, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its seeds are small and oval, with a distinctive yellowish hue, and are adapted for wind dispersal.

Avena alopecuros plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
