Anemone multifida var. multifida

Anemone multifida var. multifida, commonly known as the devil's-bit manna, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with deeply divided petals that resemble lace, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and covered in a silky, white down. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
