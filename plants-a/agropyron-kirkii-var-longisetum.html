Agropyron kirkii var. longisetum

Agropyron kirkii var. longisetum, commonly known as the long-haired wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces spikelets that are densely arranged along the stem, with the lemma and palea bearing long, stiff hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
