Anthemis parvifolia

Anthemis parvifolia, commonly known as the smallflower everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are also used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.
