Aster parishii

Aster parishii, commonly known as the Parish's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
