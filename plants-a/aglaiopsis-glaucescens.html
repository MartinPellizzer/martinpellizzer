Aglaiopsis glaucescens

Aglaiopsis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green aglaiopsis, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
