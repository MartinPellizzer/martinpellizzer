Aster coronopifolia

Aster coronopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster coronopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
