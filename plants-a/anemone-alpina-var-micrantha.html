Anemone alpina var. micrantha

Anemone alpina var. micrantha, commonly known as the Alpine anemone, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
