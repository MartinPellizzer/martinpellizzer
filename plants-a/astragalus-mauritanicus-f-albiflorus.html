Astragalus mauritanicus f. albiflorus

Astragalus mauritanicus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The white-flowered variant is distinguished by its pale or white petals, setting it apart from other forms of Astragalus mauritanicus.
