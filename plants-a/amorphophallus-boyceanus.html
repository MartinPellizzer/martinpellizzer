Amorphophallus boyceanus

Amorphophallus boyceanus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant has a distinctive underground tuber, which is used as a food source in some regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and emit a strong odor, similar to decaying flesh, to attract pollinators.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ecological significance in aquatic ecosystems.
