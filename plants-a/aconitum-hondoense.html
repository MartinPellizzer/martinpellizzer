Aconitum hondoense

Aconitum hondoense, commonly known as the Hondo monkshood, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmate leaves with a deep lobed structure, and its stems are typically tall and erect. Aconitum hondoense is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream sides.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
