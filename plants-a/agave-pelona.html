Agave pelona

Agave pelona, commonly known as the Pelona agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale yellow flowers. Agave pelona is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
