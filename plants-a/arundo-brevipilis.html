Arundo brevipilis

Arundo brevipilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it forms dense stands. The plant has long, narrow leaves and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and establish extensive colonies.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
