Aira pallescens

Aira pallescens, commonly known as the pale aira, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue or white flowers appear in dense clusters, making it a distinctive sight in its native environment.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
