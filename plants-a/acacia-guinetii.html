Acacia guinetii

Acacia guinetii, commonly known as the Guinet's wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow floral clusters, typical of the Acacia genus, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

Acacia guinetii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
