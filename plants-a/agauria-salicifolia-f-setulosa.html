Agauria salicifolia f. setulosa

Agauria salicifolia f. setulosa, commonly known as the hairy willow-leaf agauria, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant features narrow, willow-like leaves that are densely covered with fine, hairy pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf structure and adaptability to various growing conditions.
