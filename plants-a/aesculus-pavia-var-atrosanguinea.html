Aesculus pavia var. atrosanguinea

Aesculus pavia var. atrosanguinea, commonly known as the dark red horse chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from deep red to purple, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces large, spiny capsules containing bright orange, hairy seeds. Its leaves are compound, with 5 to 9 ovate leaflets that are dark green and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display and autumn foliage.
