Allium veronense

Allium veronense, commonly known as the Italian garlic or wild garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodlands. Its bulbs are edible and have a strong garlic-like flavor, making it a popular ingredient in various cuisines.

Additionally, it is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
