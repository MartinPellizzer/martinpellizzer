Aiouea napoensis

Aiouea napoensis, commonly known as the Napo quill tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
