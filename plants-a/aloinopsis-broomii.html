Aloinopsis broomii

Aloinopsis broomii, commonly known as the Broomstick Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are fleshy and cylindrical, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Aloinopsis broomii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
