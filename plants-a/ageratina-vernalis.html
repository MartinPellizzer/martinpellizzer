Ageratina vernalis

Ageratina vernalis, commonly known as spring ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, fluffy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ageratina vernalis is often cultivated as a garden plant for its delicate blooms and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
