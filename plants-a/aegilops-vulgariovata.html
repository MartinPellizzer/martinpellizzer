Aegilops vulgariovata

Aegilops vulgariovata, commonly known as the common wild barley, is a wild annual grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Aegilops vulgariovata is considered a weed in agricultural settings but has potential value in genetic research due to its close relation to cultivated barley.
