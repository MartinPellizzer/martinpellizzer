Atragene alpina subsp. sibirica

Atragene alpina subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Siberia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
