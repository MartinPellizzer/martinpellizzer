Aporum babiense

Aporum babiense, commonly known as the Babiense tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a round, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Aporum babiense is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
