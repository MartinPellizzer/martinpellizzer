Argyroxiphium forbesii

Argyroxiphium forbesii, commonly known as the Haleakalā silversword, is a rare endemic plant native to the Hawaiian island of Maui.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking, spiky flower heads. The plant thrives in the high-elevation volcanic soils of Haleakalā Crater, where it has adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Due to its limited habitat and historical overharvesting, it is listed as an endangered species.

Conservation efforts have helped stabilize its population, but it remains a symbol of Hawaii's unique botanical heritage.
