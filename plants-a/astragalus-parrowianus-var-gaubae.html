Astragalus parrowianus var. gaubae

Astragalus parrowianus var. gaubae, commonly known as the Gaubae milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and a low, sprawling growth habit. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological resilience and potential for habitat restoration in degraded landscapes.
