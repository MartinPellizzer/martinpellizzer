Anthericum viridulobrunneum

Anthericum viridulobrunneum, commonly known as the green-brown nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arums. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in rich, loamy soils.
