Anacamptis papilionacea subsp. alibertis

Anacamptis papilionacea subsp.

alibertis, commonly known as the Alibert's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
