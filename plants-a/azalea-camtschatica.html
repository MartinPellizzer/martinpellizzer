Azalea camtschatica

Azalea camtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The Kamchatka azalea is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
