Acalypha amblyodonta var. villosa

Acalypha amblyodonta var. villosa, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, often red or orange, bract-based inflorescences that resemble flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
