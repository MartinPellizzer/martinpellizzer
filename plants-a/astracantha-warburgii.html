Astracantha warburgii

Astracantha warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's thornveld, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
