Agropyron distachyon

Agropyron distachyon, commonly known as the green foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its genetic simplicity and rapid growth, it is widely used in plant biology research.
