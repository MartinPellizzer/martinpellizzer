Aquilegia canadensis f. truncata

Aquilegia canadensis f. truncata, commonly known as the Canadian columbine.

This variant is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The botanical features include heart-shaped leaves with deep lobes and showy blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
