Asplenium herpetopteris var. villosum

Asplenium herpetopteris var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern genus Asplenium.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
