Aloe propagulifera

Aloe propagulifera, commonly known as the mother-of-millions, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, white flowers on tall, branching stems, which bloom during the summer months. One of its most distinctive features is its ability to produce numerous offsets, or "propagules," which allow for easy propagation.

These offsets can be separated and planted to grow into new plants, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
