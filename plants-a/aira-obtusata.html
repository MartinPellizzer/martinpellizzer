Aira obtusata

Aira obtusata, commonly known as rough air grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
