Aquilegia glauca var. himalayica

Aquilegia glauca var. himalayica, commonly known as the Himalayan columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with distinctive spurred petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it withstands cold temperatures and high altitudes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
