Asplenium oblongipinnum

Asplenium oblongipinnum, commonly known as the broad-pinnate spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in groups called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

This species is widely distributed across tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to various microhabitats.
