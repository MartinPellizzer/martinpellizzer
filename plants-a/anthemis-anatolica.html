Anthemis anatolica

Anthemis anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Anthemis anatolica is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
