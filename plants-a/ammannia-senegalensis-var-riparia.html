Ammannia senegalensis var. riparia

Ammannia senegalensis var. riparia, commonly known as river ammannia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems that often root at the nodes, and its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and slightly hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
