Adicea serpyllifolia

Adicea serpyllifolia, commonly known as common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Adicea serpyllifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
