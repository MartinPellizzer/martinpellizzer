Acontias conspurcatus

Acontias conspurcatus, commonly known as the spotted lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Verrucariaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery thallus with distinct, scattered spots of a lighter color. The lichen typically grows on bark, stone, or soil in moist, shaded environments. It is found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Due to its cryptic appearance, it is often overlooked but plays a role in soil stabilization and as an indicator of environmental conditions.
