Anthyllis webbiana f. hirsuta

Anthyllis webbiana f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy anthyllis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "hirsuta," meaning hairy in Latin. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
