Asparagopsis scandens

Asparagopsis scandens, commonly known as the climbing asparagus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, climbing stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves. It produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Asparagopsis scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its ability to trail or climb.
