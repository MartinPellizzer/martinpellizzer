Asphodelus albus var. pyrenaicus

Asphodelus albus var. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean asphodel, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
