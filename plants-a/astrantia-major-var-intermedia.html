Astrantia major var. intermedia

Astrantia major var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate masterwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and large, showy flower heads. The plant produces pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a strong, somewhat aromatic scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.
