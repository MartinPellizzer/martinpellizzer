Allium angolense

Allium angolense, commonly known as the Angolan garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Allium angolense is also valued for its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
