Asterolinon stellatum

Asterolinon stellatum, commonly known as the star-flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine. Asterolinon stellatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
