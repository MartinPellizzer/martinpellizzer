Aesculus chinensis var. chekiangensis

Aesculus chinensis var. chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing glossy, dark brown seeds. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of 5-7 ovate leaflets.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
