Abrus precatorius var. albospermum

Abrus precatorius var. albospermum, commonly known as the white-seed velvet tare, is a variant of the common velvet tare, characterized by its distinctive white seeds.

It is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant produces intricate, showy red or orange flowers that are followed by inflated, papery pods containing the characteristic white seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a hairy stem that gives it a velvety appearance.

The plant is known for its toxic properties, as the seeds contain potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
