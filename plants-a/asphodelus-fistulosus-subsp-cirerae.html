Asphodelus fistulosus subsp. cirerae

Asphodelus fistulosus subsp.

cirerae, commonly known as the Cirera's asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and geographical distribution.
