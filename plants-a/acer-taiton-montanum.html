Acer taiton-montanum

Acer taiton-montanum, commonly known as the Taiton-Montane Maple, is a species of maple native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are typically bright green during the growing season and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
