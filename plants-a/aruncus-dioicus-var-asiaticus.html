Aruncus dioicus var. asiaticus

Aruncus dioicus var. asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian goat's beard, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features striking, feathery white plumes that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has fern-like, bipinnate leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. It prefers moist, shady environments and is typically found in woodland areas.

Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in the ecosystem.
