Avena byzantina subvar. biaristata

Avena byzantina subvar. biaristata, commonly known as the Byzantine oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for soil stabilization.
