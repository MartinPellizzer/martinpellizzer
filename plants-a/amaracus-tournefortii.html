Amaracus tournefortii

Amaracus tournefortii, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Amaracus tournefortii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
