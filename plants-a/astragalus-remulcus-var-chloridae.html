Astragalus remulcus var. chloridae

Astragalus remulcus var. chloridae, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in restoration projects.
