Abies glauca

Abies glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The needles are sharp, four-sided, and have a waxy coating that contributes to its distinctive color. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
