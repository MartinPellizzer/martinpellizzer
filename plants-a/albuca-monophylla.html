Albuca monophylla

Albuca monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf albuca, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a single, thick, fleshy leaf that is often striped with contrasting colors, and it produces tall, erect stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its bulb and leaf to survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
