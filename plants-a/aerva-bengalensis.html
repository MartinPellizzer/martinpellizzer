Aerva bengalensis

Aerva bengalensis, commonly known as Indian nettle or water hemp, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Aerva bengalensis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
