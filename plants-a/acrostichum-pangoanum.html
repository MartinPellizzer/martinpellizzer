Acrostichum pangoanum

Acrostichum pangoanum, commonly known as the Pangoan fern, is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
