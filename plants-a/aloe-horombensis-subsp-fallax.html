Aloe horombensis subsp. fallax

Aloe horombensis subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the East African Aloe, is a succulent plant native to southeastern Tanzania. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a layer of white powder.

The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a serrated margin. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers during the dry season.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
