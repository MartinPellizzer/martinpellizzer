Athyrium imbricatum

Athyrium imbricatum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, bipinnate fronds that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and gold. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its delicate, lace-like foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Athyrium imbricatum is typically propagated through spores or division, and it can be grown both indoors and outdoors in suitable climates.
