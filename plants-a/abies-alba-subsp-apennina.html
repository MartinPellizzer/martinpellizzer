Abies alba subsp. apennina

Abies alba subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine fir, is a subspecies of the silver fir native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically shorter than those of the typical silver fir.

The cones are slender and usually hang downward, with distinctive bract-like structures. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often forming dense forests in the Apennine Mountains.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various mountain species and contributing to soil stabilization in alpine regions.
