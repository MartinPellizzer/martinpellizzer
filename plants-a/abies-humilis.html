Abies humilis

Abies humilis, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with a rounded or conical shape, and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are short and stiff. The tree produces small, rounded cones that typically hang downward, containing edible seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
