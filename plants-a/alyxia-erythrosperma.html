Alyxia erythrosperma

Alyxia erythrosperma, commonly known as the red-fruited alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are a notable feature of its botanical profile. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
