Aster leonis

Aster leonis, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Aster leonis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
