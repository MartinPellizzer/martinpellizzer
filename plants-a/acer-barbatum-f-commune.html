Acer barbatum f. commune

Acer barbatum f. commune, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the maple tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like leaves that display a range of colors from bright green to vibrant red and orange, especially in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a standout feature in gardens and landscapes.
