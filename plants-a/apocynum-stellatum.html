Apocynum stellatum

Apocynum stellatum, commonly known as starry dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain toxic, ribbed seeds. It grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Apocynum stellatum is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed-related species.
