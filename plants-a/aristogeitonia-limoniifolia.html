Aristogeitonia limoniifolia

Aristogeitonia limoniifolia, commonly known as the lemon leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that resemble lemon leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is edible, though less commonly consumed compared to its ornamental appeal.
