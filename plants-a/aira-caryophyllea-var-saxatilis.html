Aira caryophyllea var. saxatilis

Aira caryophyllea var. saxatilis, commonly known as the saxifrage air grass, is a perennial grass-like plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in shades of pink to purple, typically in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
