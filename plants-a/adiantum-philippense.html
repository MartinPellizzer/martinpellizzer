Adiantum philippense

Adiantum philippense, commonly known as the Philippine maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
