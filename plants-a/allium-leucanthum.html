Allium leucanthum

Allium leucanthum, commonly known as the white garlic or white leek, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features bulbous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor, used in various culinary traditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its visual interest.
