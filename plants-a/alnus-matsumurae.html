Alnus matsumurae

Alnus matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese alder, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and catkin-like flowers. The tree produces small, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Alnus matsumurae plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
