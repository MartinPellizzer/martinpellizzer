Ardisia fuliginosa var. scopulata

Ardisia fuliginosa var. scopulata, commonly known as the rock ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible for birds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are leathery and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
