Aeonium × extinctum

Aeonium × extinctum, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a hybrid succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
