Alopecias globiceps

Alopecias globiceps, commonly known as the globular hairless plant, is a unique species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit and is typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant's leaves are small, rounded, and often covered in a similar fuzzy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Alopecias globiceps is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
