Allium oreophiloides

Allium oreophiloides, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Allium oreophiloides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
