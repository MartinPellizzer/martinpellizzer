Anisostachya vohemarensis

Anisostachya vohemarensis, commonly known as the Vohemar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anisostachya vohemarensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
