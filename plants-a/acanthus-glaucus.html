Acanthus glaucus

Acanthus glaucus, commonly known as the blue acanthus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiny leaves with a bluish-gray hue, and its flowers are typically pale blue or white. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with a robust stem and lance-shaped foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The blue acanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in classical architecture.
