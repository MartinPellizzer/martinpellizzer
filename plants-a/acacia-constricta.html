Acacia constricta

Acacia constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, phyllodes that are narrow and often curved. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia constricta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
