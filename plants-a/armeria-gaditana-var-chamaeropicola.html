Armeria gaditana var. chamaeropicola

Armeria gaditana var. chamaeropicola, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The plant has linear, silver-gray leaves that are succulent and adapted to withstand coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardiness and ornamental value make it a popular choice for maritime gardens.
