Acomastylis humilis

Acomastylis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Acomastylis humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
