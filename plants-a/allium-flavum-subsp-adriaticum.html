Allium flavum subsp. adriaticum

Allium flavum subsp.

adriaticum, commonly known as the Adriatic garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has small, white to pale yellow bulbs that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is appreciated by gardeners and botanists alike.
