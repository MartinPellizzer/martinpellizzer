Anacharis pomeranica

Anacharis pomeranica, commonly known as the European waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It has long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often branching out to form dense underwater vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, and it is known for its rapid growth rate in freshwater environments. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, arranged in whorls around the stem, and it is often used in aquariums due to its ability to oxygenate water.

Anacharis pomeranica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
