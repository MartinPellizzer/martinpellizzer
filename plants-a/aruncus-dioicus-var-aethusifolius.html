Aruncus dioicus var. aethusifolius

Aruncus dioicus var. aethusifolius, commonly known as the red stem goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching stems that produce clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
