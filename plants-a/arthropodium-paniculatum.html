Arthropodium paniculatum

Arthropodium paniculatum, commonly known as the New Zealand spider-orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swampy forests and along stream margins. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a panicle-like structure.

Arthropodium paniculatum is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
