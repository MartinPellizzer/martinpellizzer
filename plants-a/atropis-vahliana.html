Atropis vahliana

Atropis vahliana, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with a hairy texture.

The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.
