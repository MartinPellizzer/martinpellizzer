Abies veitchii var. olivacea

Abies veitchii var. olivacea, commonly known as the olive fir, is a rare and ornamental variant of the veitch fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-green foliage, which gives it a unique and elegant appearance. Native to Japan, this conifer is typically found in cool, moist mountainous regions. The leaves are needle-like, short, and arranged spirally along the branches.

It produces small, cone-like structures that mature to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
