Aegilops comosa var. thessalica

Aegilops comosa var. thessalica, commonly known as the Thessalian goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, lemma-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable forage plant for livestock.
