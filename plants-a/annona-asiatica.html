Annona asiatica

Annona asiatica, commonly known as the Asian soursop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a large, spiky fruit with a sweet, aromatic pulp that is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its growth habit includes a short trunk and dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental purposes as well.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
