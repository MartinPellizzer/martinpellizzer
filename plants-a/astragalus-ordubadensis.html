Astragalus ordubadensis

Astragalus ordubadensis, commonly known as the Ordubad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus ordubadensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid ecosystems.
