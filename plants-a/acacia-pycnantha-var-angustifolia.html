Acacia pycnantha var. angustifolia

Acacia pycnantha var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub or tree with slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous tiny leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in ecological restoration projects.
