Astrochlaena solanacea

Astrochlaena solanacea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowers are tubular and often have a bell shape, with five lobes that open to reveal stamens and a pistil.

Astrochlaena solanacea is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some regions for its purported health benefits.
