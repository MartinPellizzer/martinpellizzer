Astragalus sylvaticus

Astragalus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wood vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of diverse plant communities.
