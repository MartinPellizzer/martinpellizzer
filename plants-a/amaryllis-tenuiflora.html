Amaryllis tenuiflora

Amaryllis tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
