Achyranthes bidentata var. tomentosa

Achyranthes bidentata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy cowplant, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive two-toothed appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
