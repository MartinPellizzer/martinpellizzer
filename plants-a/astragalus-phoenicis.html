Astragalus phoenicis

Astragalus phoenicis, commonly known as the red milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods are usually hairy and contain several small, rounded seeds.
