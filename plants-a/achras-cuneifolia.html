Achras cuneifolia

Achras cuneifolia, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species with a distinctive, palm-like appearance, featuring a thick, unbranched stem and clusters of feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits resembling dates. It thrives in warm, arid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and drought.
