Angelica inaequalis

Angelica inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and compound leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica inaequalis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
