Aporum xiphophyllum

Aporum xiphophyllum, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aporum xiphophyllum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add a lush, green texture to gardens.
