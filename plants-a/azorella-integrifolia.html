Azorella integrifolia

Azorella integrifolia, commonly known as the Andean mat grass, is a hardy shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Azorella integrifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
