Athyrium yaku-montanum

Athyrium yaku-montanum, commonly known as the Yaku mountain Athyrium, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
