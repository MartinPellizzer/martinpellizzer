Acacia toondulya

Acacia toondulya, commonly known as the Northern Territory acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Acacia toondulya is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
