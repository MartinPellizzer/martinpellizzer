Anetium crinitum

Anetium crinitum, commonly known as the hairy anetium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mild, spicy scent, similar to other members of the Apiaceae family.

Anetium crinitum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
