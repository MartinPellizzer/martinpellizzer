Artemisia macrocephala

Artemisia macrocephala, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, minty scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
