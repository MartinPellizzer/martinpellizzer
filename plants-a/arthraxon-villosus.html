Arthraxon villosus

Arthraxon villosus, commonly known as the hairy arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often surrounded by bracts.

Arthraxon villosus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
