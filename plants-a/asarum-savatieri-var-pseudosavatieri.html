Asarum savatieri var. pseudosavatieri

Asarum savatieri var. pseudosavatieri, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spathaceous bract, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique floral structure and medicinal properties make it an interesting subject in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
