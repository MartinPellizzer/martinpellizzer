Aporum cultrifolium

Aporum cultrifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its growth habit is typically tree-like, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy of foliage.

Aporum cultrifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
