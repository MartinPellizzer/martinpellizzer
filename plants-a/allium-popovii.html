Allium popovii

Allium popovii, commonly known as the Popov's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous roots and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
