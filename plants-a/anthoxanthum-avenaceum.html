Anthoxanthum avenaceum

Anthoxanthum avenaceum, commonly known as oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Anthoxanthum avenaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
