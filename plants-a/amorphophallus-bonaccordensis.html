Amorphophallus bonaccordensis

Amorphophallus bonaccordensis, commonly known as the Bonaccord arrowleaf, is a rare tropical plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spiny stem. The plant produces a unique inflorescence that resembles a giant, spiky flower, which is often mistaken for a fungus. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
