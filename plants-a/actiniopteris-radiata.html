Actiniopteris radiata

Actiniopteris radiata, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a large, tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor and spread in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, pointed leaflets.

Actiniopteris radiata thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
