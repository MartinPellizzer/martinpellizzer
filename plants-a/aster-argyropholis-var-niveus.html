Aster argyropholis var. niveus

Aster argyropholis var. niveus, commonly known as the white frost aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
