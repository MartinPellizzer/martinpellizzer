Ambulia rigida var. microphylla

Ambulia rigida var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved rigid ambulia, is a unique species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is native to specific regions with well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
