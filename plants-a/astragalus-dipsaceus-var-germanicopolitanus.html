Astragalus dipsaceus var. germanicopolitanus

Astragalus dipsaceus var. germanicopolitanus, commonly known as the Germanicopolitan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
