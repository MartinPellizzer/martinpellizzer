Alliaria gatopensis

Alliaria gatopensis, commonly known as garlic mustard, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant produces small white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a peppery scent. Garlic mustard has been introduced to North America and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and can outcompete native plants for resources.
