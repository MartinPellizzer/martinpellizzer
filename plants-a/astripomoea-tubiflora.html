Astripomoea tubiflora

Astripomoea tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant produces fleshy, tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Astripomoea tubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
