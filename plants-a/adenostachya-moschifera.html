Adenostachya moschifera

Adenostachya moschifera, commonly known as the musk grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive musky scent, which is likely due to the presence of essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Adenostachya moschifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
