Abies alba subsp. pardei

Abies alba subsp.

pardei, commonly known as the Carpathian fir, is a subspecies native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The bark is typically gray to brown and becomes fissured with age, while the cones are cylindrical and often remain attached to the branches for a long time. This subspecies is adapted to cold, mountainous climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
