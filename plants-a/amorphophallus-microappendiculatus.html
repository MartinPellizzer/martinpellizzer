Amorphophallus microappendiculatus

Amorphophallus microappendiculatus, commonly known as the dwarf titan arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a large, showy flower that blooms for only a few days, followed by the development of a spiny, fleshy fruit. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and can reach up to 2 meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its ecological and medicinal potential.
