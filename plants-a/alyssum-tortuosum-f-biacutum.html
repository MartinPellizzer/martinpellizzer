Alyssum tortuosum f. biacutum

Alyssum tortuosum f. biacutum, commonly known as the curlyleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow, curled, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
