Archeria eriocarpa

Archeria eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly archer, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly covering on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and aesthetic appeal.
