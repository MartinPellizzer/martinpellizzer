Asclepias californica subsp. greenei

Asclepias californica subsp.

greenei, commonly known as Greene's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to California. It features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces distinctive, spherical seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds with silky filaments.

This subspecies is valued for its role in supporting local butterfly populations, particularly the monarch butterfly.
