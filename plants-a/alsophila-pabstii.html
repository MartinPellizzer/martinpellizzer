Alsophila pabstii

Alsophila pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, meaning they have a complex, layered structure that enhances its ornamental value.

Alsophila pabstii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
