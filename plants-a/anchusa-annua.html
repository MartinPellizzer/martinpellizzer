Anchusa annua

Anchusa annua, commonly known as the annual bugloss, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anchusa annua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower habitats.
