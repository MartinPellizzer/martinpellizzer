Allium schoenoprasum subsp. riparium

Allium schoenoprasum subsp.

riparium, commonly known as water chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and marshes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it useful in culinary applications.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
