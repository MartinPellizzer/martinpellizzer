Aquilegia ebneri

Aquilegia ebneri, commonly known as the Ebner's columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are upright with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
