Aneilema lanceolatum var. pilosum

Aneilema lanceolatum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy lanceleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its varietal name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of starchy roots.
