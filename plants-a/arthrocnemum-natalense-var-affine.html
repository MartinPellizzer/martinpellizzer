Arthrocnemum natalense var. affine

Arthrocnemum natalense var. affine, commonly known as Natal sandmat, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are pollinated by insects.

This species is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
