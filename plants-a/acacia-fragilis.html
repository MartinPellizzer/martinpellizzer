Acacia fragilis

Acacia fragilis, commonly known as the brittle wattle, is a small, shrub-like plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are typically alternate and bipinnate. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. Acacia fragilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

Its ecological role includes providing food and habitat for various native animals and insects.
