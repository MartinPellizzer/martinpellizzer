Acacia obliqua

Acacia obliqua, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow to white flowers that appear in late winter or early spring, attracting various pollinators. Acacia obliqua is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
