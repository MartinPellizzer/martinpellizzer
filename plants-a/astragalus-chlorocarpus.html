Astragalus chlorocarpus

Astragalus chlorocarpus, commonly known as the green fruit milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive green pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. Astragalus chlorocarpus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various insect species.
