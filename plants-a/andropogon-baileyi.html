Andropogon baileyi

Andropogon baileyi, commonly known as Baileys bluestem, is a tall grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant is a warm-season grass that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
