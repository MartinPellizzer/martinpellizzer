Alocasia aequiloba

Alocasia aequiloba, commonly known as the elephant's ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, featuring a deep green color with a prominent midrib. The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its dramatic appearance.

Alocasia aequiloba is also appreciated for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for interior landscaping.
