Allium baissunense

Allium baissunense, commonly known as the Baissun onion, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Allium baissunense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
