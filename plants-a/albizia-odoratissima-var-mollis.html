Albizia odoratissima var. mollis

Albizia odoratissima var. mollis, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
