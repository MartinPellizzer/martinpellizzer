Aconitum gezaense

Aconitum gezaense, commonly known as the Ethiopian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are tuberous in structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant to handle without proper precautions.
