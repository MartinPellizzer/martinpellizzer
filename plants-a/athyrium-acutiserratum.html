Athyrium acutiserratum

Athyrium acutiserratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Japanese painted fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It reproduces via spores and has a rhizomatous growth habit.
