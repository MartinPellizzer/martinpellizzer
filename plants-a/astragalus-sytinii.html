Astragalus sytinii

Astragalus sytinii, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The seeds of Astragalus sytinii are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
