Aechmea haltonii

Aechmea haltonii, commonly known as the Halton bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect water and nutrients. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Morphology

Aechmea haltonii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, stiff, and dark green with a waxy coating, arranged in a compact spiral pattern around the central stem.

The stem is short and upright, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that remains hidden beneath the dense foliage. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, producing white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated primarily by insects. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature, dispersing seeds through birds and water.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.
