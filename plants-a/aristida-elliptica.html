Aristida elliptica

Aristida elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic threeawn, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often mistaken for seeds, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
