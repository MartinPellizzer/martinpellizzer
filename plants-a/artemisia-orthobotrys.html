Artemisia orthobotrys

Artemisia orthobotrys, commonly known as the Mexican wormwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads. Artemisia orthobotrys is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
