Arabis pubescens subsp. pubescens

Arabis pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the downy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
