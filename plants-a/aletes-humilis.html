Aletes humilis

Aletes humilis, commonly known as the dwarf aletes, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale yellow.

Aletes humilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
