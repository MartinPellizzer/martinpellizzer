Anisopappus upembensis

Anisopappus upembensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is a small, annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant has adapted to extreme aridity by developing a deep taproot system to access groundwater. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Anisopappus upembensis plays a minor role in the local ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for desert fauna.
