Acer palmatum f. euseptemlobum

Acer palmatum f. euseptemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed Japanese maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, with seven distinct lobes that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is valued for its vibrant seasonal color changes, ranging from bright green in spring to fiery red or orange in autumn. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

This cultivar prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
