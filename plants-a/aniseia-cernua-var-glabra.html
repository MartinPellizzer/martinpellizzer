Aniseia cernua var. glabra

Aniseia cernua var. glabra, commonly known as smooth anise, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
