Aphyllon sedi

Aphyllon sedi, commonly known as the desert sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It has small, fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils.

Aphyllon sedi plays a vital role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and preventing soil erosion.
