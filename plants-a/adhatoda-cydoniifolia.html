Adhatoda cydoniifolia

Adhatoda cydoniifolia, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Malabar Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Adhatoda cydoniifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
