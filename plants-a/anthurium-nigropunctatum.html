Anthurium nigropunctatum

Anthurium nigropunctatum, commonly known as the black-spotted anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green with prominent black veins and spots. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium nigropunctatum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
