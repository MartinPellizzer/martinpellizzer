Aspalathus galeata

Aspalathus galeata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Aspalathus galeata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
