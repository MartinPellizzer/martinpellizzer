Aster trinervius var. longifolius

Aster trinervius var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved wood aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

This variety is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
