Astragalus filifolius

Astragalus filifolius, commonly known as the filifoliate milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astragalus filifolius is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
