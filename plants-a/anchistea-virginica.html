Anchistea virginica

Anchistea virginica, commonly known as the Virginia sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Anchistea virginica is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a cluster of tiny bells.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
