Arenaria ligericina subsp. canescens

Arenaria ligericina subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It typically features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers.

The subspecies is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky habitats.

This plant is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
