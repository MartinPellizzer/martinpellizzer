Acacia leptospermoides subsp. obovata

Acacia leptospermoides subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped phyllodes that are typically narrow and slightly curved.

The plant produces yellow floral spikes that bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
