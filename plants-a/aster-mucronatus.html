Aster mucronatus

Aster mucronatus, commonly known as the blunt-tipped aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, and it has hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

Aster mucronatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
