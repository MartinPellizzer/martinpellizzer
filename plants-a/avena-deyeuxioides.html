Avena deyeuxioides

Avena deyeuxioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf oat, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its seeds are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Avena deyeuxioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
