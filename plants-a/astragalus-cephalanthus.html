Astragalus cephalanthus

Astragalus cephalanthus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, and it produces small, rounded seeds enclosed in a hairy pod.

Astragalus cephalanthus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.
