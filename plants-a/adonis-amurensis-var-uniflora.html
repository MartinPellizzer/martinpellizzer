Adonis amurensis var. uniflora

Adonis amurensis var. uniflora, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
