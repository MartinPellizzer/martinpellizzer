Alpinia papuana

Alpinia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often with red or purple markings. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic properties.

Alpinia papuana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, high-altitude environments.
