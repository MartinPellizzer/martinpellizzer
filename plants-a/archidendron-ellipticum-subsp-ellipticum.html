Archidendron ellipticum subsp. ellipticum

Archidendron ellipticum subsp.

ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits.

It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
