Aruncus dioicus subsp. triternatus

Aruncus dioicus subsp.

triternatus, commonly known as the three-parted goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescence that consists of numerous white to pale pink bracts.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with three to five pairs of lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
