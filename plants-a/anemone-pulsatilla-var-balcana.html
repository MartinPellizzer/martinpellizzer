Anemone pulsatilla var. balcana

Anemone pulsatilla var. balcana, commonly known as the Balkan anemone, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that form a dense basal cluster. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
