Adenophora liliifolia subsp. taurica

Adenophora liliifolia subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus and southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. Its root system consists of fibrous roots, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
