Agave heteracantha var. splendens

Agave heteracantha var. splendens, commonly known as the splendens agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves with white to yellowish margins that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and slow growth rate make it a desirable addition to rock gardens and arid landscapes.
