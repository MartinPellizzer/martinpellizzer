Acacia leichhardtii

Acacia leichhardtii, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, followed by long, slender pods. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Acacia leichhardtii is also known for its historical use in the production of tannin-rich wood.
