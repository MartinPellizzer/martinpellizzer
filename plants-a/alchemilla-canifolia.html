Alchemilla canifolia

Alchemilla canifolia, commonly known as the toothed lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla canifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
