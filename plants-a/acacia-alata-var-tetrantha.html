Acacia alata var. tetrantha

Acacia alata var. tetrantha, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow phyllodes that are often curved and covered in a fine layer of white, silky hair. The plant produces small, yellow globular flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
