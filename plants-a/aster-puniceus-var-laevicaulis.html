Aster puniceus var. laevicaulis

Aster puniceus var. laevicaulis, commonly known as the smoothleaf purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, violet-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are generally smooth or slightly hairy along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
