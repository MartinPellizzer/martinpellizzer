Alchemilla pratensis var. acutangula

Alchemilla pratensis var. acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
