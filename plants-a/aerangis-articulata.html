Aerangis articulata

Aerangis articulata, commonly known as the articulated orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, segmented stems that resemble a chain. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
