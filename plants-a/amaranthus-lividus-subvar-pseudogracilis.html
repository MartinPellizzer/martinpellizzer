Amaranthus lividus subvar. pseudogracilis

Amaranthus lividus subvar. pseudogracilis, commonly known as the pale amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, showy inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for beneficial insects.
