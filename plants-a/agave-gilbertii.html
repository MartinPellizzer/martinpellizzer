Agave gilbertii

Agave gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's agave, is a species of agave native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing clusters of white or yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
