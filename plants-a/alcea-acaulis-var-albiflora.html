Alcea acaulis var. albiflora

Alcea acaulis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered common hollyhock, is a variant of the common hollyhock characterized by its pale white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with large, showy flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
