Agave duplicata

Agave duplicata, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating called bloom. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach up to 10 feet in height, bearing small, greenish-white flowers. Agave duplicata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its striking appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
