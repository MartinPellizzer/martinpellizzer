Angraecum verruculosum

Angraecum verruculosum, commonly known as the wrinkled angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dark green, wrinkled leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with long, slender petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It blooms during the rainy season, usually between November and March.

Angraecum verruculosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
