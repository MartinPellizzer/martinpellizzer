Acalypha subbullata

Acalypha subbullata, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, silver-green leaves that give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Acalypha subbullata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and ability to add visual interest to gardens.
