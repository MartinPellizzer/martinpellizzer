Anaxeton arborescens

Anaxeton arborescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Anaxeton arborescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
