Astragalus leontinus var. subacaulis

Astragalus leontinus var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
