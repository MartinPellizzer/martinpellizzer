Asperula tinctoria subsp. tinctoria

Asperula tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to bright red, depending on the variety.

The plant is known for its rapid blooming cycle, often opening and closing its flowers in response to light and temperature changes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
