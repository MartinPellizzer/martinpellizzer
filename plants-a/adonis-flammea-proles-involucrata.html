Adonis flammea proles involucrata

Adonis flammea proles involucrata, commonly known as the flame anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of flowers surrounded by a leafy involucrum.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
