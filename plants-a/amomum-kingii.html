Amomum kingii

Amomum kingii, commonly known as the King's ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and flowering spikes that produce small, white flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
