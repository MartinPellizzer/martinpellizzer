Agastache pringlei var. verticillata

Agastache pringlei var. verticillata, commonly known as the mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a popular choice for native gardens.
