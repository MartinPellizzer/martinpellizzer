Alchemilla krylovii f. mastodonta

Alchemilla krylovii f. mastodonta, commonly known as the giant lady's mantle, is a variant of the alchemilla genus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique foliage and is often used in garden landscapes for its visual appeal.
