Alchemilla hoppeana var. grossidens

Alchemilla hoppeana var. grossidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy, and sometimes lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is known for its ornamental value and ability to retain moisture, making it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
