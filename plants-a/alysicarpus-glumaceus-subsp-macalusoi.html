Alysicarpus glumaceus subsp. macalusoi

Alysicarpus glumaceus subsp.

macalusoi, commonly known as the Macaluso's burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, hairy husk. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in ecological restoration projects.
