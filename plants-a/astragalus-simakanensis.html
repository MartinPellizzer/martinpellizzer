Astragalus simakanensis

Astragalus simakanensis, commonly known as the Simakan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky and arid environments, where it has adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus simakanensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
