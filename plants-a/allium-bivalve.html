Allium bivalve

Allium bivalve, commonly known as the two-valved leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and bulbous stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, often used in culinary applications.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other allium species due to its specific growing requirements.
