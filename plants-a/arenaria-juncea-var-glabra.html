Arenaria juncea var. glabra

Arenaria juncea var. glabra, commonly known as smooth sand-verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features slender, branching stems covered in smooth, hairless foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is distinguished by its lack of pubescence, giving it a sleek, polished appearance.
