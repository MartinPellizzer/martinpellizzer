Astragalus stipulatus var. phulchokiensis

Astragalus stipulatus var. phulchokiensis, commonly known as the Phulchoki milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small stipules at the base of each leaflet, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
