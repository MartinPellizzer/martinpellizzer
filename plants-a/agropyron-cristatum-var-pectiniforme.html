Agropyron cristatum var. pectiniforme

Agropyron cristatum var. pectiniforme, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves that are often curled at the tips. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

This variety is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
