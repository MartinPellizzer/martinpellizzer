Allium winklerianum

Allium winklerianum, commonly known as the Winkler onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting species for horticultural enthusiasts.
