Achillea hillieri f. ramosa

Achillea hillieri f. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
