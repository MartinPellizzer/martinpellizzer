Aeranthes recta

Aeranthes recta, commonly known as the straight-leaved orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a spike, often with a slight fragrance.

Aeranthes recta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
