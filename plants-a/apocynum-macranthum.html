Apocynum macranthum

Apocynum macranthum, commonly known as showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, making it toxic to humans and livestock. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Apocynum macranthum plays a vital role in supporting pollinators, particularly monarch butterflies, by providing nectar and serving as a host plant for their larvae.
