Astragalus arrectus var. leibergii

Astragalus arrectus var. leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to low-nutrient environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife, though it is not widely cultivated.
