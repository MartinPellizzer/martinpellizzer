Alyssum smolikanum subsp. moravense

Alyssum smolikanum subsp.

moravense, commonly known as the Moravian alyssum, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to Central Europe. It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compact, evergreen foliage that remains lush throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and ladybugs.
