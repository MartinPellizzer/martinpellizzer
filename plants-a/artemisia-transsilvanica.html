Artemisia transsilvanica

Artemisia transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Artemisia transsilvanica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
