Alsophila peruviana

Alsophila peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Alsophila peruviana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
