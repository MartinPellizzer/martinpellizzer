Alysicarpus roxburghianus

Alysicarpus roxburghianus, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
