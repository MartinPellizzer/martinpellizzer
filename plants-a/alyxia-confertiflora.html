Alyxia confertiflora

Alyxia confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a woody vine characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and contain seeds. Alyxia confertiflora thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
