Aspilia ramagii

Aspilia ramagii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clustered yellow flowers. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. Aspilia ramagii is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
