Avena elatior var. carpathica

Avena elatior var. carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
