Abrus tenuiflorus

Abrus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically 5-7 lobes, and its distinctive red, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in clusters during the spring. Its seeds contain abrin, a potent toxin that makes them dangerous if ingested.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique seed pods.
