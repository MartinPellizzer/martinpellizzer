Amaranthus mantegazzianus

Amaranthus mantegazzianus, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a tall, annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy inflorescences that range in color from purple to red. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus mantegazzianus is adapted to warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
