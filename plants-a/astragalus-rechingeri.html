Astragalus rechingeri

Astragalus rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and overgrazing.
