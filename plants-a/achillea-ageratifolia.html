Achillea ageratifolia

Achillea ageratifolia, commonly known as the lesser yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Achillea ageratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
