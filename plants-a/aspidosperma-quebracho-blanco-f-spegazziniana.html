Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco f. spegazziniana

Aspidosperma quebracho-blanco f. spegazziniana, commonly known as white quebracho, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fissured bark and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped pods containing flat, dark seeds. It is known for its high tannin content, which makes it valuable for tanning leather and as a source of natural dye.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
