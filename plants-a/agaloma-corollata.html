Agaloma corollata

Agaloma corollata, commonly known as the coral bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Agaloma corollata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
