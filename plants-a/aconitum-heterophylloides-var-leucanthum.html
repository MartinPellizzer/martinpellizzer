Aconitum heterophylloides var. leucanthum

Aconitum heterophylloides var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spore-bearing structures called follicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
