Astus wittweri

Astus wittweri, commonly known as the Wittwer's asters, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy purple to blue flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astus wittweri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
