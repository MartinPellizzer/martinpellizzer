Audibertia pachystachya

Audibertia pachystachya, commonly known as the thick-stemmed tickseed, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with prominent, bract-like structures, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Audibertia pachystachya is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
