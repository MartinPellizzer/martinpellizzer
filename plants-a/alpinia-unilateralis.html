Alpinia unilateralis

Alpinia unilateralis, commonly known as the single-sided ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its flowers are typically pink or white and are clustered in a spiral arrangement.

Alpinia unilateralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
