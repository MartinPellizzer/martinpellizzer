Acetosa conglomerata

Acetosa conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically features small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or desert environments. Acetosa conglomerata is known for its ability to withstand harsh conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic ecosystems.

Its compact growth habit and resilience make it a notable component of its native habitats.
