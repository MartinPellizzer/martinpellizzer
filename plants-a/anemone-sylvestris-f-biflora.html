Anemone sylvestris f. biflora

Anemone sylvestris f. biflora, commonly known as the woodland anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine down. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers with five petals, often in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
