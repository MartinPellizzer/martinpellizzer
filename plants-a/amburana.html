Amburana

Amburana, commonly known as the "sandalwood tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in the production of incense and fine furniture.

Amburana is also known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its role in traditional medicine.
