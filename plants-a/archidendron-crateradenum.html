Archidendron crateradenum

Archidendron crateradenum, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
