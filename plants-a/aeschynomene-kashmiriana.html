Aeschynomene kashmiriana

Aeschynomene kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Aeschynomene kashmiriana is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.
