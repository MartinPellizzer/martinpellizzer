Aegilops speltoides subsp. submutica

Aegilops speltoides subsp.

submutica, commonly known as the rough spikegrass, is a wild wheat species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted, spike-like inflorescence and glabrous or slightly hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a diploid genome and is closely related to cultivated wheat species.

Its genetic diversity has made it an important resource for breeding programs aimed at improving wheat resilience.
