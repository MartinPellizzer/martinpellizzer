Aster lindheimerianus

Aster lindheimerianus, commonly known as Lindheimer's aster, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
