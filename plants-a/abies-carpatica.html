Abies carpatica

Abies carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Abies carpatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
