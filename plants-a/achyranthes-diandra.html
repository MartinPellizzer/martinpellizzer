Achyranthes diandra

Achyranthes diandra, commonly known as wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste lands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
