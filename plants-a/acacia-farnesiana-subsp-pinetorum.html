Acacia farnesiana subsp. pinetorum

Acacia farnesiana subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are grouped in dense clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

The species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
