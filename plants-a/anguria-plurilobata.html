Anguria plurilobata

Anguria plurilobata, commonly known as the multi-lobed melon, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that resemble the wings of a dragonfly, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces a unique, elongated fruit with a thick, waxy rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
