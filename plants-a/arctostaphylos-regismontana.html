Arctostaphylos regismontana

Arctostaphylos regismontana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its woody structure and slow growth rate make it a long-lived species with ecological significance in its native habitat.
