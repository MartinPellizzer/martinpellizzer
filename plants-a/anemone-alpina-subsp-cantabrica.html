Anemone alpina subsp. cantabrica

Anemone alpina subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky, calcareous soils in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and a cluster of showy, white to pale pink flowers. It blooms in spring, usually between April and June, and is often found in mountainous areas with high altitude.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
