Arundinaria pantlingii

Arundinaria pantlingii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Arundinaria pantlingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance and resilience.
