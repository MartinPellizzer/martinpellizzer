Astragalus nurhakdagensis

Astragalus nurhakdagensis, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus nurhakdagensis is often found in steppe and desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
