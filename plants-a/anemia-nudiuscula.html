Anemia nudiuscula

Anemia nudiuscula, commonly known as the naked scale.

It is a small, succulent plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its fleshy, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a thin layer of wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
