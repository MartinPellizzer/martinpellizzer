Aconitum degenii subsp. paniculatum

Aconitum degenii subsp.

paniculatum, commonly known as the paniculate aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in a paniculate inflorescence, which is a complex, branched cluster of flowers. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and it is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This species is known for its toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to both humans and animals.
