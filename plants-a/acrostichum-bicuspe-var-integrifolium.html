Acrostichum bicuspe var. integrifolium

Acrostichum bicuspe var. integrifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and deeply lobed fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with each pinna having a distinctive two-lobed shape.

This variety is well-suited for humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.
