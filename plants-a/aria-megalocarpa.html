Aria megalocarpa

Aria megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant seed tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, woody structure and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds enclosed in a thick, fibrous covering. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
