Apoballis okadae

Apoballis okadae, commonly known as the Okada's acacia, is a rare shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
