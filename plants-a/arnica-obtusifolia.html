Arnica obtusifolia

Arnica obtusifolia, commonly known as bluntleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Arnica obtusifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and easy cultivation.
