Astragalus gerruensis

Astragalus gerruensis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its medicinal properties are not well-documented.
