Argolasia plumosa

Argolasia plumosa, commonly known as the featherleaf argolasia, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forest understories. Its leaves are thin and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
