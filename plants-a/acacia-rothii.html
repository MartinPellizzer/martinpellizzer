Acacia rothii

Acacia rothii, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia rothii is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that access groundwater.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
