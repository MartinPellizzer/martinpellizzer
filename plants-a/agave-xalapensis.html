Agave xalapensis

Agave xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican century plant, is a succulent perennial native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often lined with sharp spines. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for fiber and syrup production.
