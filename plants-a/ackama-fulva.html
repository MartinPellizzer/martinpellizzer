Ackama fulva

Ackama fulva, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of red and purple. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate, strap-like leaves. Ackama fulva thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical collections.
