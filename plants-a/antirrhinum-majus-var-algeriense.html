Antirrhinum majus var. algeriense

Antirrhinum majus var. algeriense, commonly known as the Algerian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
