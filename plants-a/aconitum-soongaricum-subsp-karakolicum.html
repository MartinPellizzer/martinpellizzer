Aconitum soongaricum subsp. karakolicum

Aconitum soongaricum subsp.

karakolicum, commonly known as Karakolic alpine monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces follicles containing toxic alkaloids.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
