Acacia striatifolia

Acacia striatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia striatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
