Aster laxifolius var. laetiflorus

Aster laxifolius var. laetiflorus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with stems that are usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
