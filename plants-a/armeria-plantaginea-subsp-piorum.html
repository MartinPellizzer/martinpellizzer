Armeria plantaginea subsp. piorum

Armeria plantaginea subsp.

piorum, commonly known as the pink sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
