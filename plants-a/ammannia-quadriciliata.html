Ammannia quadriciliata

Ammannia quadriciliata, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands, streambanks, and prairies. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Ammannia quadriciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
