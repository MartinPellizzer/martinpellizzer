Acosta exarata

Acosta exarata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acosta exarata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
