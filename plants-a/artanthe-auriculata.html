Artanthe auriculata

Artanthe auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Artanthe auriculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
