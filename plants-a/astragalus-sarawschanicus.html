Astragalus sarawschanicus

Astragalus sarawschanicus, commonly known as the Sarawak milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, inflated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
