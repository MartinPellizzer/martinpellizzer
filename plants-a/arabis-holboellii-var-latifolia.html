Arabis holboellii var. latifolia

Arabis holboellii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine displays.
