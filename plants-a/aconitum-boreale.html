Aconitum boreale

Aconitum boreale, commonly known as the northern monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a deep taproot and grows in moist, rocky soils, often in alpine or tundra environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat poisonous appearance.

Aconitum boreale is part of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant to be handled with care.
