Aster spathulifolius f. albiflorus

Aster spathulifolius f. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower narrowleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with prominent yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
