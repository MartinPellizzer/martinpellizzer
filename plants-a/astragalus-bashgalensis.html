Astragalus bashgalensis

Astragalus bashgalensis, commonly known as Bashgal Astragalus, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
