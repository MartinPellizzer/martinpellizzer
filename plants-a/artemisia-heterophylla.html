Artemisia heterophylla

Artemisia heterophylla, commonly known as narrowleaf sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils that contribute to its strong, distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture.

Artemisia heterophylla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
