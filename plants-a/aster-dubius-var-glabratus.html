Aster dubius var. glabratus

Aster dubius var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
