Arum dioscoridis f. confluens

Arum dioscoridis f. confluens, commonly known as the confluens arum, is a hybrid form of the genus Arum native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a showy, spatulate inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female structures on separate parts of the spadix.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
