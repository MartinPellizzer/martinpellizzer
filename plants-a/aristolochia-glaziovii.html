Aristolochia glaziovii

Aristolochia glaziovii, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
