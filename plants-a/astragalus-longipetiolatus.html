Astragalus longipetiolatus

Astragalus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions. Astragalus longipetiolatus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife, though it is not widely cultivated.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
