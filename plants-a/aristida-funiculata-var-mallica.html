Aristida funiculata var. mallica

Aristida funiculata var. mallica, commonly known as the Malabar grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles that are often used in traditional floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant in various regions.
