Aphelandra padillana

Aphelandra padillana, commonly known as the Padilla's flameleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant, elongated leaves that range in color from deep green to bronze, often with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, spathiflorous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra padillana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
