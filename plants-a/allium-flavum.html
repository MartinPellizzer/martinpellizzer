Allium flavum

Allium flavum, commonly known as the yellow onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a bulb composed of modified leaves called scales, which are used as a food source. Allium flavum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, and it is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Its essential oils contribute to its distinct aroma and flavor.
