Astragalus lentiginosus var. toyabensis

Astragalus lentiginosus var. toyabensis, commonly known as the Toyabense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of China and Mongolia.

It is a perennial herbaceous species that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender stems with trifoliate leaves, and its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in dense, globular clusters. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
