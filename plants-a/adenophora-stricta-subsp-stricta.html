Adenophora stricta subsp. stricta

Adenophora stricta subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and Canada. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of flowers at the tips.

This species is adapted to moist to mesic habitats, including woodlands and stream banks.
