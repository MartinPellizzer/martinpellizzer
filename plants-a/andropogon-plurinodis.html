Andropogon plurinodis

Andropogon plurinodis, commonly known as the nodding grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that nod toward the ground. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Andropogon plurinodis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
