Amomis oblongata var. occidentalis

Amomis oblongata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 to 3 meters tall. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
