Anaphrenium abyssinicum var. obovatum

Anaphrenium abyssinicum var. obovatum, commonly known as the Abyssinian anaphrenium, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to Ethiopia and thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features slender, branching stems and possesses small, obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as cypselae.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations.
