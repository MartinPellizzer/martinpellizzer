Andrographis rothii

Andrographis rothii, commonly known as the Indian toothbrush, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Andrographis rothii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
