Asperula aristata var. breviflora

Asperula aristata var. breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf coral bell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely toothed, dark green leaves that are evergreen and provide year-round interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
