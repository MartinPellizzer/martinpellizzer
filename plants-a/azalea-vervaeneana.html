Azalea vervaeneana

Azalea vervaeneana, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering shrub that belongs to the Rhododendron genus, characterized by its showy, clustered blooms. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the spring season, which are typically borne on short, dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the colorful flowers.

Azalea vervaeneana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
