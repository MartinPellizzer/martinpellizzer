Alsophila caracasana

Alsophila caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas hakea, is a species of tree fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
