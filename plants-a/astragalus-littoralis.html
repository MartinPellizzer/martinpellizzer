Astragalus littoralis

Astragalus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is adapted to salt spray and windy conditions. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
