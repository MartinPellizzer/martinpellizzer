Arachidna

Arachidna, commonly known as the groundnut or peanut, is an annual legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and compound leaves. The plant produces underground pods that contain the edible seeds, which are rich in protein and oil. Arachidna thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, requiring a long growing season.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agriculture worldwide.
