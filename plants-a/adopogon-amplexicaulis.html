Adopogon amplexicaulis

Adopogon amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it is considered a valuable component of native plant communities.
