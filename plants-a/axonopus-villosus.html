Axonopus villosus

Axonopus villosus, commonly known as hairy axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with hairy leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Axonopus villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
