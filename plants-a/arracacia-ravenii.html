Arracacia ravenii

Arracacia ravenii, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a striking combination of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant produces distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Arracacia ravenii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its visual impact.
