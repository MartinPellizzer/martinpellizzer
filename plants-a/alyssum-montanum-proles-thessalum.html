Alyssum montanum proles thessalum

Alyssum montanum proles thessalum, commonly known as the Thessalian alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its compact growth habit and profuse blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
