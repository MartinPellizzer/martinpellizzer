Acronia pileata

Acronia pileata, commonly known as the mossy lichen, is a symbiotic composite organism composed of a fungus and a green alga.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a vegetative body that lacks true roots, stems, or leaves. Its reproductive structures are formed through the release of spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Acronia pileata plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil formation and providing habitat for microorganisms.
