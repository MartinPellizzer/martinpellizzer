Acacia goadbyi

Acacia goadbyi, commonly known as the Goadby's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow-scented flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and a low water requirement.

Acacia goadbyi plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
