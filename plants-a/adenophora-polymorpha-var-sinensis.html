Adenophora polymorpha var. sinensis

Adenophora polymorpha var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
