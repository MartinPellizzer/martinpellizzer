Albuca lugardii

Albuca lugardii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in coastal climates.

Albuca lugardii is also noted for its resilience to drought and mild frost.
