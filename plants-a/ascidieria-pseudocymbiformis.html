Ascidieria pseudocymbiformis

Ascidieria pseudocymbiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.

Ascidieria pseudocymbiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and resilience.
