Ancipitia odobeniceps

Ancipitia odobeniceps, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers.

Ancipitia odobeniceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
