Alyssum petrophilum

Alyssum petrophilum, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Alyssum petrophilum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
