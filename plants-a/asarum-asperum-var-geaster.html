Asarum asperum var. geaster

Asarum asperum var. geaster, commonly known as the star-shaped asarum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, star-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent and has a complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
