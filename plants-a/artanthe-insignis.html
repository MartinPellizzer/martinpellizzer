Artanthe insignis

Artanthe insignis, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red petals and yellow stamens, which give it a striking appearance. The plant has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Artanthe insignis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
