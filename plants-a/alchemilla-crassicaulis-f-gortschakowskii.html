Alchemilla crassicaulis f. gortschakowskii

Alchemilla crassicaulis f. gortschakowskii, commonly known as the broad-stemmed lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage.

It features rounded, serrated leaves that form a rosette at the base, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, arranged in clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
