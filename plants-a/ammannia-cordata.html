Ammannia cordata

Ammannia cordata, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its roots are reddish in color and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Ammannia cordata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
