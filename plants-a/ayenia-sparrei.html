Ayenia sparrei

Ayenia sparrei, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Ayenia sparrei is part of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting monarch butterfly populations.
