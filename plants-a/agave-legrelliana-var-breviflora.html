Agave legrelliana var. breviflora

Agave legrelliana var. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with white to pale blue markings. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing small, white flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
