Aspidosperma araracanga

Aspidosperma araracanga, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araracanga tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. Its bark is thick and fissured, often featuring a rough, flaky texture.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
