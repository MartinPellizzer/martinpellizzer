Aconitum anthorum

Aconitum anthorum, commonly known as the European monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply cut. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Aconitum anthorum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
