Anisomeris sylvicola

Anisomeris sylvicola, commonly known as the mountain cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, branching stems that form dense, shrub-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to small spines, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Anisomeris sylvicola plays a role in providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
