Ayenia pusilla var. pubescens

Ayenia pusilla var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ayenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with hairy stems and leaves that give it its common name. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ayenia pusilla var. pubescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
