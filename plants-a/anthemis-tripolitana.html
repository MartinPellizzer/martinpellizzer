Anthemis tripolitana

Anthemis tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripoli ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually hairy and aromatic.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptability and potential medicinal properties.
