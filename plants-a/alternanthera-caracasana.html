Alternanthera caracasana

Alternanthera caracasana, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage that turns a brilliant crimson as the plant matures. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are not particularly showy.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in aquatic and bog gardens.
