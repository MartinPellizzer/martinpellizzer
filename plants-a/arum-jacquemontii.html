Arum jacquemontii

Arum jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
