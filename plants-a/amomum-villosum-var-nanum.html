Amomum villosum var. nanum

Amomum villosum var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark brown, three-lobed berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its fragrant seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
