Asterolasia beckersii

Asterolasia beckersii, commonly known as the Koa tree, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in coastal and lowland areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are typically arranged in terminal clusters.

The plant plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
