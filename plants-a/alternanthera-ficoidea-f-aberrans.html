Alternanthera ficoidea f. aberrans

Alternanthera ficoidea f. aberrans, commonly known as the red-leaf alternate plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced under bright light conditions. The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of up to 30 cm, and features small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.
