Asperula gobica

Asperula gobica, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Its evergreen foliage provides ornamental value throughout the year.

Asperula gobica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
