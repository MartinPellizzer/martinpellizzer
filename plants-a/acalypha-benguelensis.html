Acalypha benguelensis

Acalypha benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking copper-colored leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with branched stems and opposite leaf arrangement. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Acalypha benguelensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
