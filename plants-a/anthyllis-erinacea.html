Anthyllis erinacea

Anthyllis erinacea, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and scrublands.

Anthyllis erinacea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in certain ecosystems.
