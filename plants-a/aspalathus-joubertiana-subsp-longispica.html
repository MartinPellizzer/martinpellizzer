Aspalathus joubertiana subsp. longispica

Aspalathus joubertiana subsp.

longispica, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
