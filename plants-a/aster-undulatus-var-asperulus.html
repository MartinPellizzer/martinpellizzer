Aster undulatus var. asperulus

Aster undulatus var. asperulus, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
