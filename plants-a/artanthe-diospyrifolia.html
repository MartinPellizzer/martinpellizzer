Artanthe diospyrifolia

Artanthe diospyrifolia, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, followed by the development of showy, papery bracts. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
