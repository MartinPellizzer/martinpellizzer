Amygdalus arabica

Amygdalus arabica, commonly known as the sweet almond tree, is a species of the Prunus genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The tree produces fruits known as drupes, which contain the edible almond seeds. These seeds are rich in nutrients and are widely used in culinary and cosmetic applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
