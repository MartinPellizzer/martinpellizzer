Allium ochroleucum subsp. suaveolens

Allium ochroleucum subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its fragrant foliage and edible bulbs make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and gastronomy.
