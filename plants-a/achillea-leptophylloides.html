Achillea leptophylloides

Achillea leptophylloides, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
