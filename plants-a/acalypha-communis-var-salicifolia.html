Acalypha communis var. salicifolia

Acalypha communis var. salicifolia, commonly known as silky mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a pale underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
