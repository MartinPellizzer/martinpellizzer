Atriplex sibirica var. centralasiatica

Atriplex sibirica var. centralasiatica, commonly known as Siberian saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
