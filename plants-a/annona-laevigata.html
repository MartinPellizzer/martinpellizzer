Annona laevigata

Annona laevigata, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into round, spiny fruits. The fruit has a creamy, sweet flesh with a distinctive aroma, and the seeds are embedded within the pulp.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
