Anemone pulsatilla var. grandis

Anemone pulsatilla var. grandis, commonly known as the showy anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of silky, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
