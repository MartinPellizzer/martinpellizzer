Avonsera convallarioides

Avonsera convallarioides, commonly known as the sweet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable species like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. It produces small, berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
