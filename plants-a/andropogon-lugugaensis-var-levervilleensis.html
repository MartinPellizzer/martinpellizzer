Andropogon lugugaensis var. levervilleensis

Andropogon lugugaensis var. levervilleensis, commonly known as the Leverville grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This variety is notable for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
