Asyneuma limonifolium var. alpinum

Asyneuma limonifolium var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine lemonleaf, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a citrus-like scent, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique fragrance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
