Allamanda

Allamanda, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Allamanda is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
