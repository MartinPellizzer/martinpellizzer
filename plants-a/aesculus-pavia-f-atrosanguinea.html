Aesculus pavia f. atrosanguinea

Aesculus pavia f. atrosanguinea, commonly known as the Black-veined Horsechestnut, is a cultivar of the Horsechestnut tree.

It is characterized by its striking dark red to black veins on the leaves, which contrast sharply with the white or pale green foliage. The plant produces showy, pendulous clusters of white flowers in spring, followed by spiny, woody fruits. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage and floral display.
