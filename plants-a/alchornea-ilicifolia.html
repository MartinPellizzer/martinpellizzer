Alchornea ilicifolia

Alchornea ilicifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Alchornea ilicifolia is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
