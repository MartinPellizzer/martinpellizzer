Acer truncatum subsp. mono

Acer truncatum subsp.

mono, commonly known as the single-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its unique, palmately lobed leaves that are typically only found on one side of the stem, giving it its subspecies name "mono.".

The leaves are usually green in summer and turn vibrant yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The plant has small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
