Antimima prolongata

Antimima prolongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed antimony plant, is a member of the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
