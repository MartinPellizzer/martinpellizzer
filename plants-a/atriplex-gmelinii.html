Atriplex gmelinii

Atriplex gmelinii, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and ability to thrive in saline soils. The plant has a deep root system that helps it access water in dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Atriplex gmelinii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
