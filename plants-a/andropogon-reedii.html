Andropogon reedii

Andropogon reedii, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking purple tinge on its leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Andropogon reedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant landscaping.
