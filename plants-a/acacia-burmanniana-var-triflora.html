Acacia burmanniana var. triflora

Acacia burmanniana var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
