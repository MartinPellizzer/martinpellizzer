Azalea pittosporifolia

Azalea pittosporifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand azalea, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Azalea pittosporifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
