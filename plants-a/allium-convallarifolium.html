Allium convallarifolium

Allium convallarifolium, commonly known as the coastal garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has a bulbous base that stores nutrients and supports growth. It thrives in coastal areas with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, maritime environments.
