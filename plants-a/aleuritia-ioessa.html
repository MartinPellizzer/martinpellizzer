Aleuritia ioessa

Aleuritia ioessa, commonly known as the Chinese elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Aleuritia ioessa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
