Aphanochilus penduliflorus

Aphanochilus penduliflorus, commonly known as the hanging flower plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they emit a mild fragrance.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
