Acanthorhiza arborea

Acanthorhiza arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has woody stems with thick, leathery leaves that are often lobed or serrated. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a prominent central column, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Acanthorhiza arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
