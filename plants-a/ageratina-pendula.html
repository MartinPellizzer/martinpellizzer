Ageratina pendula

Ageratina pendula, commonly known as the hanging blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy blue or silver-white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Ageratina pendula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
