Agathosma decora var. buchu

Agathosma decora var. buchu, commonly known as buchu, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly succulent leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
