Azalea canadensis

Azalea canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The botanical features of Azalea canadensis make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
