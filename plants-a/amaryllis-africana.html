Amaryllis africana

Amaryllis africana, commonly known as the African amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing multiple blooms. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often form a dense rosette at the base.

Amaryllis africana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
