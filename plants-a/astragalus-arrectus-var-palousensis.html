Astragalus arrectus var. palousensis

Astragalus arrectus var. palousensis, commonly known as Palouse milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
