Amorphophallus koratensis

Amorphophallus koratensis, commonly known as the Korat arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, leafy stems and striking, spatulate-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible tubers.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
