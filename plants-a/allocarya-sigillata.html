Allocarya sigillata

Allocarya sigillata, commonly known as the sigil-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its unique, often intricate leaf patterns that resemble seals or sigils, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Allocarya sigillata produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the rainy season.
