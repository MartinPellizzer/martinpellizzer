Aconitum senanense var. curvipilum

Aconitum senanense var. curvipilum, commonly known as the Senanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its stems are typically hairy and stout. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
