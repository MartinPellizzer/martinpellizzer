Atylosia scarabaeoides var. pedunculata

Atylosia scarabaeoides var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its upright, branching stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
