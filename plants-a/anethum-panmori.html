Anethum panmori

Anethum panmori, commonly known as the Kashmiri coriander or wild cumin, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, spicy aroma, while the seeds are used in traditional medicine and cooking.
