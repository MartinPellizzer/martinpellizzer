Alnus alnobetula

Alnus alnobetula, commonly known as the white birch, is a deciduous tree native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Alnus alnobetula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
