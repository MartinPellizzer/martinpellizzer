Acacia aneura var. microcarpa

Acacia aneura var. microcarpa, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and pale grey bark that resembles the ghostly appearance of its common name. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, narrow leaflets that are arranged in a feathery pattern. It produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, followed by flat, leathery pods that contain several seeds.

This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.
