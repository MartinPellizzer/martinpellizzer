Adiantum dilatatum

Adiantum dilatatum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually light green and have a distinctive, arching growth habit.

Adiantum dilatatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens as a decorative plant.
