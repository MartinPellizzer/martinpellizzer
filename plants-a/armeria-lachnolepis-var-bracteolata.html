Armeria lachnolepis var. bracteolata

Armeria lachnolepis var. bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate thrift, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
