Acacia decora var. macrophylla

Acacia decora var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf wattle, is a striking shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, feathery leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical acacia species. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in gardens and landscaping.
