Alchemilla pastoralis f. adpressepilosa

Alchemilla pastoralis f. adpressepilosa, commonly known as the compact alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with silver-white undersides and bright green upper surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
