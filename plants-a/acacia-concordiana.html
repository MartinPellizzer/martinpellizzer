Acacia concordiana

Acacia concordiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia concordiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
