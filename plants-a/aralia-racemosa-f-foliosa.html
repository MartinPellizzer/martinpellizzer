Aralia racemosa f. foliosa

Aralia racemosa f. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf aralia, is a variant of the common aralia that exhibits distinctive foliage.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are broader and more lobed than those of the typical form. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland environments. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in upright clusters, which are followed by bright red berries.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
