Aegiphila sylvatica

Aegiphila sylvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian pennyroyal, is a perennial shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
