Alliaria halmaheirae

Alliaria halmaheirae, commonly known as the Maltese garlic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a mild garlic-like scent.

It is typically found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
