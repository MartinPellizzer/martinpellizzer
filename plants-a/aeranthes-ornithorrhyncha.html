Aeranthes ornithorrhyncha

Aeranthes ornithorrhyncha, commonly known as the bird-beak orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, bird-like floral structure that resembles a beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The orchid's morphology reflects adaptations for its ecological niche, showcasing the diversity within the orchid family.
