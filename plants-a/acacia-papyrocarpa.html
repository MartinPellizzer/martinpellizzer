Acacia papyrocarpa

Acacia papyrocarpa, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia papyrocarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
