Astragalus iodopetalus

Astragalus iodopetalus, commonly known as the iodine-petaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus iodopetalus is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
