Andreettaea furcatipetala

Andreettaea furcatipetala, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a short, upright stem.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
