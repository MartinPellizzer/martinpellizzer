Arrabidaea decora var. genuina

Arrabidaea decora var. genuina, commonly known as the Florida flame flower, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, red-orange bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is adapted to moist, acidic soils. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens with suitable water conditions.
