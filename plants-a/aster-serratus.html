Aster serratus

Aster serratus, commonly known as the serrated aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Aster serratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
