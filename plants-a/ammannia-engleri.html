Ammannia engleri

Ammannia engleri, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Ammannia engleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
