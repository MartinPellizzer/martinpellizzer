Alpinia arabica

Alpinia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, arranged in spikes, and are accompanied by a strong, spicy aroma.

Alpinia arabica is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
