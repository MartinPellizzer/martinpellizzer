Acacia spathulata

Acacia spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia spathulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture.
