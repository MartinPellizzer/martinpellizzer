Anthereon

Anthereon, commonly known as the "butterfly orchid," is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The anther, a part of the stamen, plays a crucial role in the plant's reproductive process by producing and releasing pollen.
