Amygdalus caryophyllacea

Amygdalus caryophyllacea, commonly known as the wild almond or sweet almond tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by oval, greenish-yellow fruits known as drupes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible seeds, which can be processed into oil or used in culinary applications.
