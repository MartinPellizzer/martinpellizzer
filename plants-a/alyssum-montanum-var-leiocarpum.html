Alyssum montanum var. leiocarpum

Alyssum montanum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seed alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, typically hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is native to regions of the western United States and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
