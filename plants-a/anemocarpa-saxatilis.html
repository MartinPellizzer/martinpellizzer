Anemocarpa saxatilis

Anemocarpa saxatilis, commonly known as the rock anemone, is a flowering plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Anemocarpa saxatilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and supports local pollinator populations.
