Arundinaria bambusina

Arundinaria bambusina, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria bambusina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
