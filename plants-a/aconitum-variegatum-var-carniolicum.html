Aconitum variegatum var. carniolicum

Aconitum variegatum var. carniolicum, commonly known as the Carniolan variegated monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, arranged in a tall spires. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with distinctive white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
