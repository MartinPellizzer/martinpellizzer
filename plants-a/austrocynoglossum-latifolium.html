Austrocynoglossum latifolium

Austrocynoglossum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Austrocynoglossum latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
