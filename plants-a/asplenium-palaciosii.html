Asplenium palaciosii

Asplenium palaciosii, commonly known as the Palacios' spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical climates. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Asplenium palaciosii is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
