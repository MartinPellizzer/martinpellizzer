Astragalus pecten-erinis

Astragalus pecten-erinis, commonly known as the erinaceus milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.

Its seed pods are typically hairy and dehiscent, releasing seeds through mechanical means.
