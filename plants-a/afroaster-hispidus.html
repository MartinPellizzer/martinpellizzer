Afroaster hispidus

Afroaster hispidus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a rough texture, and its flowers are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Afroaster hispidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
