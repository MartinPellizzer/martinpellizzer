Agrimonia canescens

Agrimonia canescens, commonly known as the lesser agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine, grayish hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Agrimonia canescens is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
