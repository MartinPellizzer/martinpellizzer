Astragalus ancistrocarpus var. stepposus

Astragalus ancistrocarpus var. stepposus, commonly known as the steppe milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
