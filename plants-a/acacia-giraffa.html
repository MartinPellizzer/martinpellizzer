Acacia giraffa

Acacia giraffa, commonly known as the giraffe tree, is a species of acacia native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, whip-like branches and its ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds. Its leaves are compound and are often used as forage by livestock and wildlife.

Acacia giraffa is also known for its hardy, thorned structure, which helps protect it from herbivores.
