Acacia giraffa

Acacia giraffa, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its dense, feathery branches. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

Acacia giraffa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in degraded or sandy environments.
