Aria mougeotii var. austriaca

Aria mougeotii var. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant grows from a rhizomatous root system and has linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
