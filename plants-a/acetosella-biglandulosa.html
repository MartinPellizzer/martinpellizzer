Acetosella biglandulosa

Acetosella biglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a small perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the family Violaceae, which includes violets and related species.
