Avena sativa var. obtusata

Avena sativa var. obtusata, commonly known as the obtuse oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its distinct leaf morphology.

This plant typically features broader, more rounded leaves compared to standard oat varieties, which can contribute to its unique appearance. It is often found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small, two-lobed spikelets.

While less commonly cultivated than other oat varieties, it is valued for its potential in ecological and forage applications.
