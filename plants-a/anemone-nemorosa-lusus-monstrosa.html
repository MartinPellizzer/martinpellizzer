Anemone nemorosa lusus monstrosa

Anemone nemorosa lusus monstrosa, commonly known as the giant wood anemone, is a striking perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The flowers emit a subtle fragrance and are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season insects.
