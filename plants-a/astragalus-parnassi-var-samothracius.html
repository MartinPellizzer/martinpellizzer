Astragalus parnassi var. samothracius

Astragalus parnassi var. samothracius, commonly known as the Samothrace milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the flowers are usually pale blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
