Achillea eridania

Achillea eridania, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Achillea eridania is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
