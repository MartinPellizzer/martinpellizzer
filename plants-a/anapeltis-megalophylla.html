Anapeltis megalophylla

Anapeltis megalophylla, commonly known as the giant-leaved anapeltis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
