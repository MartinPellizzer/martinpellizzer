Aerva brachiata

Aerva brachiata, commonly known as Indian spinach or goat's bean, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a three-lobed, hairy capsule, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds.

Aerva brachiata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
