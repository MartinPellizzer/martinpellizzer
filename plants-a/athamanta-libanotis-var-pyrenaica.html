Athamanta libanotis var. pyrenaica

Athamanta libanotis var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
