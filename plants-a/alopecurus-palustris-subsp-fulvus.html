Alopecurus palustris subsp. fulvus

Alopecurus palustris subsp.

fulvus, commonly known as the yellow marsh grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is often distinguished by its yellowish-green coloration and its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
