Andropogon kunthii

Andropogon kunthii, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and forms dense, tufted clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon kunthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
