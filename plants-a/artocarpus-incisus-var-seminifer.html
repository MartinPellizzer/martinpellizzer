Artocarpus incisus var. seminifer

Artocarpus incisus var. seminifer, commonly known as the Indian breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces a fruit with a rough, fibrous exterior. The fruit contains a starchy, edible pulp that is often used in cooking. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is particularly noted for its high nutritional value and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
