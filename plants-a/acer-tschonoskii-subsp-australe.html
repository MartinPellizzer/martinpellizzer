Acer tschonoskii subsp. australe

Acer tschonoskii subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
