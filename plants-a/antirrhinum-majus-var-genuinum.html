Antirrhinum majus var. genuinum

Antirrhinum majus var. genuinum, commonly known as the common snapdragon, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers that resemble a dragon's mouth, hence its common name. The flowers typically come in shades of red, yellow, orange, and pink, and bloom from late spring to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental use and historical significance in traditional medicine.
