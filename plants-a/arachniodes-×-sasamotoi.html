Arachniodes × sasamotoi

Arachniodes × sasamotoi, commonly known as the giant bamboo fern, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species, Arachniodes simplicior and Arachniodes standleyi.

It is characterized by its large, broad fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a dramatic and lush appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically divided into segments with a distinctive, almost spider-like appearance, which contributes to its common name.

Arachniodes × sasamotoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ability to add a tropical feel to indoor or outdoor spaces.
