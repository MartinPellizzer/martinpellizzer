Aristida karelinii

Aristida karelinii, commonly known as Karelin's threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-awned bristles, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Aristida karelinii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
