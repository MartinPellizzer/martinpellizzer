Aloe sororia

Aloe sororia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, upright leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers on tall, branching inflorescences during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Aloe sororia is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
