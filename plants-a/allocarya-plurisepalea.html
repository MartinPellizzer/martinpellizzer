Allocarya plurisepalea

Allocarya plurisepalea, commonly known as the multi-sepal orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, featuring multiple sepals that are often brightly colored and textured. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and exhibits a unique adaptation to its harsh environment, including specialized root systems for nutrient absorption. Its blooms typically appear during the rainy season, adding a striking visual element to the mountainous landscape.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a conservation concern.
