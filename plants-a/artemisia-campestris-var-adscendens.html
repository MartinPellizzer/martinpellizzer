Artemisia campestris var. adscendens

Artemisia campestris var. adscendens, commonly known as ascending wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, aromatic leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compound clusters, which appear in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its strong scent and medicinal properties, it has been used historically in herbal remedies.
