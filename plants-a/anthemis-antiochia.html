Anthemis antiochia

Anthemis antiochia, commonly known as the Antiochian tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Anthemis antiochia is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
