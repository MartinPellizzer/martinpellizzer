Alphonsea gaudichaudiana

Alphonsea gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in panicle inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell.
