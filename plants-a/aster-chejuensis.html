Aster chejuensis

Aster chejuensis, commonly known as the Jeju aster, is a flowering plant native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster chejuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
