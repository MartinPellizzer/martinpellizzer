Artabotrys sahyadricus

Artabotrys sahyadricus, commonly known as the Indian lantern tree, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate-shaped leaves, which are usually dark green in color. Its most distinctive feature is its clustered, lantern-shaped flowers that are pale yellow to white in color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
