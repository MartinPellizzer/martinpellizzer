Argemone mexicana f. leiocarpa

Argemone mexicana f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smoothleaf castor bean, is a variant of the castor oil plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas, particularly Mexico and the southwestern United States. The plant features smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing toxic seeds, similar to the parent species.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphological characteristics and potential use in traditional medicine.
