Antidesma tomentosum var. bangueyense

Antidesma tomentosum var. bangueyense, commonly known as the Bangueyense antidesma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
