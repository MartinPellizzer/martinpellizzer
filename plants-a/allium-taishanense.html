Allium taishanense

Allium taishanense, commonly known as the Tai Shan garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and culinary value, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
