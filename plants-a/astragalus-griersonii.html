Astragalus griersonii

Astragalus griersonii, commonly known as Grierson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Astragalus griersonii is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native range.
