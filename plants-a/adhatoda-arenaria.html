Adhatoda arenaria

Adhatoda arenaria, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Adhatoda arenaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
