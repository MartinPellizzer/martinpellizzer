Anthopogon virgatus

Anthopogon virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's thimble, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Anthopogon virgatus is known for its distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.
