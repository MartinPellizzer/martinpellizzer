Ascolepis speciosa

Ascolepis speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and vibrant hues, which attract specific pollinators.

Ascolepis speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty.
