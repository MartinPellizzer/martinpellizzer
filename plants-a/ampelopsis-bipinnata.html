Ampelopsis bipinnata

Ampelopsis bipinnata, commonly known as silvervine, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known to be a source of a natural stimulant for cats.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
