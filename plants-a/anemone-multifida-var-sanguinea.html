Anemone multifida var. sanguinea

Anemone multifida var. sanguinea, commonly known as the blood anemone, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are dark green and often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
