Anacyclus aureus

Anacyclus aureus, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its aromatic, hairy leaves. It produces small, dandelion-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Anacyclus aureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
