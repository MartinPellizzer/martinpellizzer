Arachnimorpha

Arachnimorpha, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence their common name. They are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Arachnimorpha species have specialized root systems that help them absorb nutrients from poor soil conditions.

Their flowers often have a unique structure with elongated petals and a distinctive spur, which plays a role in pollination by specific insect species.
