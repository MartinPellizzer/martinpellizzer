Aristida spegazzinii var. genuina

Aristida spegazzinii var. genuina, commonly known as the slender threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous bristly lemma-like structures. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to grazing.

This variety is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
