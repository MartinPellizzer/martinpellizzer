Aristolochia mannii

Aristolochia mannii, commonly known as the African birthwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a trumpet, typically in shades of green, yellow, or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are often hairy.

This species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
