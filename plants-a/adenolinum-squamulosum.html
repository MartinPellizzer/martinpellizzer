Adenolinum squamulosum

Adenolinum squamulosum, commonly known as the squamulous adenolinum, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, often hairy or scaly leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or green, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a plant of interest for botanists and conservationists.
