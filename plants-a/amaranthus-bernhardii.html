Amaranthus bernhardii

Amaranthus bernhardii, commonly known as the desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple foliage and upright, branching stems. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into dry, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to drought conditions.

Amaranthus bernhardii is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
