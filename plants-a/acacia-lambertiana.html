Acacia lambertiana

Acacia lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of local ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
