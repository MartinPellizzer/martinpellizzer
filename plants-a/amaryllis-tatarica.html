Amaryllis tatarica

Amaryllis tatarica, commonly known as the Siberian amaryllis, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Amaryllis tatarica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
