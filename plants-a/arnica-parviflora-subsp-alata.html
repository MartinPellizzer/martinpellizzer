Arnica parviflora subsp. alata

Arnica parviflora subsp.

alata, commonly known as the alpine arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high elevations.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with reflexed petals. Its flowers are often clustered in a single inflorescence at the end of the stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its winged stems, which give it its scientific name "alata," meaning "winged" in Latin.
