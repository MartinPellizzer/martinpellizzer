Astragalus stenoceras var. nigrostipulata

Astragalus stenoceras var. nigrostipulata, commonly known as the black-stippled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by pod fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variant is distinguished by its darkly pigmented stipules, which give it its common name.
