Alsophila dielsii

Alsophila dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome system allows it to store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, moist habitats.

The fronds are typically evergreen and can remain on the plant for many years, contributing to its ornamental value.
