Amaranthus roxburghianus var. angustifolius

Amaranthus roxburghianus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf amaranth, is an annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with fine hairs. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that eventually develop into triangular, red or purple seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional cuisines and as a source of nutrition.
