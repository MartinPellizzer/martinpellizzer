Acidoton ramiflorus

Acidoton ramiflorus, commonly known as the branching-flower vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure and rapid growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
