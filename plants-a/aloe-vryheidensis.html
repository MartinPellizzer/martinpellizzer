Aloe vryheidensis

Aloe vryheidensis, commonly known as the Vryheid aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer.

This species is well-suited for arid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
