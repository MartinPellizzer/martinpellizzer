Aloysia gratissima var. paraguariensis

Aloysia gratissima var. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is evergreen, with a woody stem and a dense, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are harvested for the preparation of mate, a traditional infusion consumed throughout the region.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
