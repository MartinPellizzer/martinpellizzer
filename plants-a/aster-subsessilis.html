Aster subsessilis

Aster subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It blooms from late summer into early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster subsessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
