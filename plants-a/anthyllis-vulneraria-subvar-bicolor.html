Anthyllis vulneraria subvar. bicolor

Anthyllis vulneraria subvar. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored heal-all, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and white flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for healing wounds and ailments.
