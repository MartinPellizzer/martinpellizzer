Achillea atrata subsp. carvifolia

Achillea atrata subsp.

carvifolia, commonly known as the dark yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are gray-green and woolly, with finely divided, fern-like upper leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valued plant in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
