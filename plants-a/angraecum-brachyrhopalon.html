Angraecum brachyrhopalon

Angraecum brachyrhopalon, commonly known as the short-stalked star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Angraecum brachyrhopalon is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
