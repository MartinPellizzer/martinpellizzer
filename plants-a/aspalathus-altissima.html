Aspalathus altissima

Aspalathus altissima, commonly known as the African red bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Aspalathus altissima is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
