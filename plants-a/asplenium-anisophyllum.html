Asplenium anisophyllum

Asplenium anisophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely divided. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Asplenium anisophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
