Acer sikkimense subsp. metcalfii

Acer sikkimense subsp.

metcalfii, commonly known as the Metcalf's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high elevations, often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
