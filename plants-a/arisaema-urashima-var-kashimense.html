Arisaema urashima var. kashimense

Arisaema urashima var. kashimense, commonly known as the Kashima urashima arisaema, is a variant of the urashima arisaema native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage and floral structure.
