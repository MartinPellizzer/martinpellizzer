Agave micracantha var. picta

Agave micracantha var. picta, commonly known as the Variegated Century Plant, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces a tall flower stalk with small, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
