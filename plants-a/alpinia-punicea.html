Alpinia punicea

Alpinia punicea, commonly known as the red ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a glossy texture.

Alpinia punicea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
