Aconitum × paniculatum subsp. valesiacum

Aconitum × paniculatum subsp.

valesiacum, commonly known as the Valesiaca monkshood, is a hybrid plant species native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a tall, spire-like inflorescence.

The plant features large, velvety leaves with a distinctive palmate structure, and its roots contain potent alkaloids. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
