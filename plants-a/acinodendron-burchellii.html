Acinodendron burchellii

Acinodendron burchellii, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and forest margins, and is adapted to seasonal droughts.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
