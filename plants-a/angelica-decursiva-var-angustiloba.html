Angelica decursiva var. angustiloba

Angelica decursiva var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features large, compound leaves that are elongated and narrow, often with a pronounced midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic roots and stems have been used historically in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.
