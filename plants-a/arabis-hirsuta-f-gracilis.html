Arabis hirsuta f. gracilis

Arabis hirsuta f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, often blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
