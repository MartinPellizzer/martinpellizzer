Achyranthes elegantissima

Achyranthes elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
