Arnica claytonii

Arnica claytonii, commonly known as Clayton's arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at high elevations. Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous peoples for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Arnica claytonii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
