Aster microcephalus var. microcephalus

Aster microcephalus var. microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf blue aster, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has fine, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
