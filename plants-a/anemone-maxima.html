Anemone maxima

Anemone maxima, commonly known as the giant anemone, is a striking perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a waxy texture, and can grow up to 12 inches long.

Anemone maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
