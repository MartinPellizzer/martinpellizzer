Anemone dichotoma subsp. pennsilvanica

Anemone dichotoma subsp.

pennsilvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system and grows in dense clumps, with lance-shaped leaves that are often divided. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
