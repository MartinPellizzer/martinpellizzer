Adromischus cristatus var. mzimvubuensis

Adromischus cristatus var. mzimvubuensis, commonly known as the Mzimvubu ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
