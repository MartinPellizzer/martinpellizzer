Aster acer

Aster acer, commonly known as the red stem aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Aster acer thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
