Ardisia marginata

Ardisia marginata, commonly known as the red-flowered ardisia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Ardisia marginata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
