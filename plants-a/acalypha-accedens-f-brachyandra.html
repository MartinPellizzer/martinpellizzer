Acalypha accedens f. brachyandra

Acalypha accedens f. brachyandra, commonly known as the short-stalked velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
