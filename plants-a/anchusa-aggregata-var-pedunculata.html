Anchusa aggregata var. pedunculata

Anchusa aggregata var. pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking color and long blooming period.
