Abutilon persicum

Abutilon persicum, commonly known as the Persian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pleasant, sweet scent. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Abutilon persicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
