Artemisia nakaii

Artemisia nakaii, commonly known as Japanese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features silvery-gray foliage with finely divided leaves that emit a strong, aromatic scent. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late summer and are clustered in panicles.

Artemisia nakaii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
