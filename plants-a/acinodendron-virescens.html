Acinodendron virescens

Acinodendron virescens, commonly known as the green acinodendron, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, green berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
