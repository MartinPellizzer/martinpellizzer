Argyrolobium amplexicaule

Argyrolobium amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping silver globular vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often clasping around the stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
