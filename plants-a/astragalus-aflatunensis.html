Astragalus aflatunensis

Astragalus aflatunensis, commonly known as the Aflatun milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, though further research is needed to confirm their efficacy.
