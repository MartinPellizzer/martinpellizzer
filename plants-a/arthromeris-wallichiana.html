Arthromeris wallichiana

Arthromeris wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
