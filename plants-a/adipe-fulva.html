Adipe fulva

Adipe fulva, commonly known as the fat plant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are typically small and rounded, contributing to its overall compact form.

Adipe fulva is well-suited for rock gardens or as a potted plant due to its low maintenance requirements.
