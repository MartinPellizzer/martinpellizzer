Alternanthera ochracea

Alternanthera ochracea, commonly known as the Orange Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its striking foliage and floral display.
