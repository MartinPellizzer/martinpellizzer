Aster mitchellii

Aster mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple.

Aster mitchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
