Athyrium filix-femina var. angustum

Athyrium filix-femina var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
