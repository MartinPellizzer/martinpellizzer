Abies erythrocarpa

Abies erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally around the stems. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a distinctive red color, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The red-fruited fir is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
