Aconitum chiachaense var. chiachaense

Aconitum chiachaense var. chiachaense, commonly known as the Chiapas aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its roots are tuberous and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires cool, temperate climates for optimal growth.
