Aquilegia bernardii

Aquilegia bernardii, commonly known as Bernard's columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar guides. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
