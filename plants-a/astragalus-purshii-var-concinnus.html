Astragalus purshii var. concinnus

Astragalus purshii var. concinnus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, yellow flowers clustered in globular heads. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets.

It is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of forage for wildlife.
