Andryala pinnatifida subsp. mogadorensis

Andryala pinnatifida subsp.

mogadorensis, commonly known as the Mogador rockrose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the Canary Islands.
