Amelanchier crenulata

Amelanchier crenulata, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is characterized by its rounded leaves and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland habitats.

Amelanchier crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
