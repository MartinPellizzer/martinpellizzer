Aeranthes ambrensis

Aeranthes ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrensis Spider-Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a unique, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests, where it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized structures that aid in pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Aeranthes ambrensis is considered a conservation concern.
