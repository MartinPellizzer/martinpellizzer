Asarum franchetianum

Asarum franchetianum, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Due to its medicinal value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
