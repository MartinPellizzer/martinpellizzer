Armeria grandiflora

Armeria grandiflora, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of the United States.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Armeria grandiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
