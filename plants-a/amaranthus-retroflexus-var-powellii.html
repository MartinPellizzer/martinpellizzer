Amaranthus retroflexus var. powellii

Amaranthus retroflexus var. powellii, commonly known as Powell's amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish to reddish flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Powell's amaranth is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
