Allium jesdianum var. latipetalum

Allium jesdianum var. latipetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible, with a mild garlic flavor, and are often used in cooking.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental and culinary plant in temperate climates.
