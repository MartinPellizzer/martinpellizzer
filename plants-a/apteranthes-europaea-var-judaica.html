Apteranthes europaea var. judaica

Apteranthes europaea var. judaica, commonly known as the Judean thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel and the Levant.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against herbivores and water loss.

This variety is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its historical and ecological significance in the region.
