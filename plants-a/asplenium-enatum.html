Asplenium enatum

Asplenium enatum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are densely covered with short, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The leaves are usually divided into narrow segments, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance.
