Alsophila cinerea

Alsophila cinerea, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically a dark green color with a silvery or whitish underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Alsophila cinerea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
