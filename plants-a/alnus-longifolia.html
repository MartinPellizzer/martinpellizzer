Alnus longifolia

Alnus longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf alder, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and compound leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins that are typical of the Betulaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous regions.

Alnus longifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
