Anemone obtusiloba subsp. potentilloides

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

potentilloides, commonly known as the plains anemone, is a flowering plant native to the prairies of North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and stems that bear showy, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a short growing season, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly rounded leaf base and its ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.
