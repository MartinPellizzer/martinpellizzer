Amphicarpaea bracteata var. typica

Amphicarpaea bracteata var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on both surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, indehiscent fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable within its native range.
