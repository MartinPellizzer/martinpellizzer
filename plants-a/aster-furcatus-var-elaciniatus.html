Aster furcatus var. elaciniatus

Aster furcatus var. elaciniatus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to violet in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
