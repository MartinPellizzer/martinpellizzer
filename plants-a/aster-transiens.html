Aster transiens

Aster transiens, commonly known as the transient aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing showy purple to pink flowers.

Due to its short blooming period and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
