Arthraxon quartinianus var. tenellus

Arthraxon quartinianus var. tenellus, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, contributing to aquatic ecosystem stability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland conservation and ecological studies.
