Argemone grandiflora subsp. armata

Argemone grandiflora subsp.

armata, commonly known as the hairy argemone, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with prominent black markings and hairy stems and leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
