Angraecum rothschildianum

Angraecum rothschildianum, commonly known as Rothschild's orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences and large, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a distinctive rosette-shaped growth pattern with broad, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
