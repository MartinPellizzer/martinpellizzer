Actinodaphne engleriana

Actinodaphne engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that contain a single seed, and its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and durability. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
