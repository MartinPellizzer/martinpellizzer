Astragalus striatellus f. macrocarpus

Astragalus striatellus f. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain large, smooth seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
