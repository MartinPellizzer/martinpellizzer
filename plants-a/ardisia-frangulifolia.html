Ardisia frangulifolia

Ardisia frangulifolia, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem structure.

Ardisia frangulifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.
