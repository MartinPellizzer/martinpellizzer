Allium deciduum subsp. retrorsum

Allium deciduum subsp.

retrorsum, commonly known as the backward-facing garlic. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that are often hidden beneath the soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a dense umbel.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential culinary uses.
