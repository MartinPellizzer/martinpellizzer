Arthrophyllum cenabrei

Arthrophyllum cenabrei, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that bear vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, hence its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Arthrophyllum cenabrei is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
