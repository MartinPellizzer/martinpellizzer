Adenocarpus frankenioides

Adenocarpus frankenioides, commonly known as the Frankenioides bursera, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface, and its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
