Agathosma capensis var. brevifolia

Agathosma capensis var. brevifolia, commonly known as buchu, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic, and finely divided leaves that emit a strong, distinctive scent when crushed. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils due to its medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both ecological and commercial contexts.
