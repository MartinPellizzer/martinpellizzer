Arundinella simplex

Arundinella simplex, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rapid growth and ability to form thickets make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native habitats.
