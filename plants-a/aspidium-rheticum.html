Aspidium rheticum

Aspidium rheticum, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its pinnate fronds with serrated margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds.

Aspidium rheticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, feathery appearance.
