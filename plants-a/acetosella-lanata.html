Acetosella lanata

Acetosella lanata, commonly known as downy wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, downy appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Acetosella lanata is often found in nutrient-rich soils and can tolerate partial to full shade.
