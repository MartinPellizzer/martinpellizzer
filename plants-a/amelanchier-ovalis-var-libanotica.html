Amelanchier ovalis var. libanotica

Amelanchier ovalis var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.
