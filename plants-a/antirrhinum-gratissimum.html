Antirrhinum gratissimum

Antirrhinum gratissimum, commonly known as the sweet willie or garden snapdragon, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Antirrhinum gratissimum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
