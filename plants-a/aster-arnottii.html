Aster arnottii

Aster arnottii, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head.

Aster arnottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
