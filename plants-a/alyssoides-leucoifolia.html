Alyssoides leucoifolia

Alyssoides leucoifolia, commonly known as the white-leafed alyssum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
