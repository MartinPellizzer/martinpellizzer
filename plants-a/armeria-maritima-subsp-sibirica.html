Armeria maritima subsp. sibirica

Armeria maritima subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia. It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in coastal and alpine environments due to its tolerance for harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
