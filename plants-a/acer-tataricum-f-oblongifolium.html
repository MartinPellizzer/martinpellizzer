Acer tataricum f. oblongifolium

Acer tataricum f. oblongifolium, commonly known as the Tatarian maple with elongated leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, oblong-shaped leaves that are typically 5-10 cm long and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
