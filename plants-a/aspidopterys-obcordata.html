Aspidopterys obcordata

Aspidopterys obcordata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Aspidopterys obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
