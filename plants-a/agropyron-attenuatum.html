Agropyron attenuatum

Agropyron attenuatum, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Agropyron attenuatum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and for its potential use in forage and erosion control.
