Atriplex tatarica subsp. recurva

Atriplex tatarica subsp.

recurva, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is a perennial shrub native to Eurasia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in compact clusters.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions and is known for its ability to colonize new areas rapidly.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
