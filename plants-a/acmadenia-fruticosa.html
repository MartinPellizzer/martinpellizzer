Acmadenia fruticosa

Acmadenia fruticosa, commonly known as the staghorn acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

Acmadenia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
