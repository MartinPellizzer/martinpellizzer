Abarema limae

Abarema limae, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or false rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments. Its wood is valued for its density and aesthetic qualities, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

However, due to overharvesting, it is considered threatened in some areas.
