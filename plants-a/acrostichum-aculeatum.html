Acrostichum aculeatum

Acrostichum aculeatum, commonly known as the rough hedyotis, is a perennial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, clustered, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
