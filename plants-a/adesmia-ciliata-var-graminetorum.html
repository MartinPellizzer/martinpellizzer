Adesmia ciliata var. graminetorum

Adesmia ciliata var. graminetorum, commonly known as the silky adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

This variant is distinguished by its finely ciliate margins on the leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
