Amaranthus frumentaceus

Amaranthus frumentaceus, commonly known as proso millet, is an annual grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant is well adapted to a wide range of soil types and can thrive in both dry and moist conditions. Its grains are rich in nutrients, including protein, fiber, and essential minerals, making it a valuable crop for human and animal consumption.

Additionally, it is often used in traditional cuisines and has been cultivated for thousands of years.
