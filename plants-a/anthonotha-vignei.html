Anthonotha vignei

Anthonotha vignei, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Anthonotha vignei is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
