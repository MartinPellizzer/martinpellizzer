Aristida pungens var. pennata

Aristida pungens var. pennata, commonly known as the hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
