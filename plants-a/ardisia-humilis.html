Ardisia humilis

Ardisia humilis, commonly known as the low ardisia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible but often considered toxic. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
