Angelica graveolens

Angelica graveolens, commonly known as common angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in summer. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Angelica graveolens is also valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and various medicinal properties.
