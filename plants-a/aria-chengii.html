Aria chengii

Aria chengii, commonly known as the Cheng's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and are often a deep green with a glossy texture.

Aria chengii produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of berries as its fruit.
