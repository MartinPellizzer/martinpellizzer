Annona boliviana

Annona boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian custard apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by a large, spiky fruit. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, aromatic pulp that is often used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and flavor.
