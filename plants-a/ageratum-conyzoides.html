Ageratum conyzoides

Ageratum conyzoides, commonly known as blueweed or flossflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields.

Ageratum conyzoides is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
