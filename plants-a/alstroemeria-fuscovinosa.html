Alstroemeria fuscovinosa

Alstroemeria fuscovinosa, commonly known as the dark vinous alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, often vividly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has strap-like, grassy foliage. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and can come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

Alstroemeria fuscovinosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different growing conditions.
