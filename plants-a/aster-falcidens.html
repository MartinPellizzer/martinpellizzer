Aster falcidens

Aster falcidens, commonly known as the sickle aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are purple to blue in color.

Aster falcidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
