Ammannia kilimandscharica var. hispidula

Ammannia kilimandscharica var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy ammannia, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or grassland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
