Allium gayi

Allium gayi, commonly known as the Gay's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.

Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.
