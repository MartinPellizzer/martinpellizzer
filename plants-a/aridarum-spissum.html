Aridarum spissum

Aridarum spissum, commonly known as the dense desert shrub, is a hardy plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It features a compact growth form with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale or greenish in color, aiding in pollination by wind or insects. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Aridarum spissum plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
