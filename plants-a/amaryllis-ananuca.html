Amaryllis ananuca

Amaryllis ananuca, commonly known as the Brazilian amaryllis, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Amaryllis ananuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown both indoors and outdoors in suitable climates.
