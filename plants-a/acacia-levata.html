Acacia levata

Acacia levata, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, straight pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Acacia levata is also valued for its hardy nature and role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
