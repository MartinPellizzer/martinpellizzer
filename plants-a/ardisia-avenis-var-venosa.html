Ardisia avenis var. venosa

Ardisia avenis var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated evergreen shrub, is a compact, evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its year-round foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
