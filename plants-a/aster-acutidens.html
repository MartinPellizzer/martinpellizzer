Aster acutidens

Aster acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a pointed tip, giving it its scientific name.

Aster acutidens produces showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall.
