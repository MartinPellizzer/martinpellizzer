Acrocentron alpestre

Acrocentron alpestre, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Acrocentron alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging high-altitude environments.
