Acacia uliginosa

Acacia uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp acacia, is a shrub native to wetland habitats in Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, while its bark is rough and fissured.

Acacia uliginosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
