Adiantum tenerum var. farleyense

Adiantum tenerum var. farleyense, commonly known as the Farley's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and slightly curled. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
