Aulomyrcia maraguana

Aulomyrcia maraguana, commonly known as the Guianese myrcia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Guyana, and Suriname. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its bark is thick and rugged.

Aulomyrcia maraguana is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
