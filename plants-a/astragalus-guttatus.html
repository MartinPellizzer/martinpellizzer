Astragalus guttatus

Astragalus guttatus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus guttatus is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for wildlife.
