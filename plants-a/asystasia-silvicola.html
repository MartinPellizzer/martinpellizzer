Asystasia silvicola

Asystasia silvicola, commonly known as the forest asystasia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and understoreys. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and often have two-lipped petals, typical of the Acanthaceae family.
