Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. iberica

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian woundwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval or elliptical leaflets, and it produces narrow, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
