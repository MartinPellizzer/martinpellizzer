Acer campestre var. palmatisectum

Acer campestre var. palmatisectum, commonly known as the palmate suckering maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble a hand, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and hedgerows, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds.

This variety is noted for its suckering habit, forming dense thickets and making it a hardy, low-maintenance plant for landscaping.
