Aegiphila pennellii

Aegiphila pennellii, commonly known as the Mexican pennyroyal, is a shrub native to the dry regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
