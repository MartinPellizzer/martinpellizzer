Aegiphila pennellii

Aegiphila pennellii, commonly known as the Mexican pennyroyal, is a shrub native to the dry regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Morphology

Aegiphila pennellii has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, with a bluish-green color and a slightly hairy texture.

The stem is slender, with a rough, reddish-brown bark that grows upright and branching. Its small, clustered flowers are white with purple markings, blooming in spring and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, helping the plant anchor in rocky or sandy soils.
