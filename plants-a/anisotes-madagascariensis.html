Anisotes madagascariensis

Anisotes madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces showy white flowers with a distinctive, hood-like structure that opens to reveal yellow stamens. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that aids in water retention and light reflection.

Anisotes madagascariensis plays an important role in its native wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
