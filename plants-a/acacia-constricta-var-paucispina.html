Acacia constricta var. paucispina

Acacia constricta var. paucispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in spirals along the stems. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional and contemporary uses.
