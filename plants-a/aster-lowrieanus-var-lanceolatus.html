Aster lowrieanus var. lanceolatus

Aster lowrieanus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
