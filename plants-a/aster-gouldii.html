Aster gouldii

Aster gouldii, commonly known as the Gould's aster, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the western regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Aster gouldii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
