Asparagus officinalis

Asparagus officinalis, commonly known as garden asparagus, is a perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fern-like, compound leaves and edible, needle-like stems that are harvested in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically growing to a height of 1 to 3 feet. Its root system consists of fibrous roots and a central taproot, which helps it anchor and absorb nutrients.

Asparagus officinalis is known for its ability to produce edible spears that are rich in vitamins and minerals.
