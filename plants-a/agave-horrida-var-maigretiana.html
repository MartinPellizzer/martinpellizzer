Agave horrida var. maigretiana

Agave horrida var. maigretiana, commonly known as Maigret's agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with sharp spines along the edges. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
