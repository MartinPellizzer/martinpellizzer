Agropyron firmiculme

Agropyron firmiculme, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous growth system allows it to form dense mats, making it an effective pioneer species in degraded landscapes.

Agropyron firmiculme is also valued for its forage potential and its role in soil stabilization.
