Allium sanbornii var. tuolumnense

Allium sanbornii var. tuolumnense, commonly known as the Tuolumne garlic or Tuolumne leek, is a perennial plant native to California.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is particularly adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
