Amomum pulchellum

Amomum pulchellum, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale green to white, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
