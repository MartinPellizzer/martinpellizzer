Armeria plantaginea var. longibracteata

Armeria plantaginea var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract armeria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and showy bracts that resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and hardy plants.
