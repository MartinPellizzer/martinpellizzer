Alchemilla gymnopoda

Alchemilla gymnopoda, commonly known as the naked-stem lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Alchemilla gymnopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
