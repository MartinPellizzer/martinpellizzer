Allium stephanophorum

Allium stephanophorum, commonly known as the alpine garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and high altitudes, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in certain culinary traditions.

Allium stephanophorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
