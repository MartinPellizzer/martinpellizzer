Aptotheca

Aptotheca, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, sandy soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and sometimes covered in a powdery coating.
