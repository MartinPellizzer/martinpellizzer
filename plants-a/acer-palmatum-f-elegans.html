Acer palmatum f. elegans

Acer palmatum f. elegans, commonly known as the Japanese maple with elegant leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like foliage that turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 8. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and container planting.
