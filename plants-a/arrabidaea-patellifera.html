Arrabidaea patellifera

Arrabidaea patellifera, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
