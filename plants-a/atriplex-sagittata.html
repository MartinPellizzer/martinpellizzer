Atriplex sagittata

Atriplex sagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

Atriplex sagittata plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
