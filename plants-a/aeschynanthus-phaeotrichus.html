Aeschynanthus phaeotrichus

Aeschynanthus phaeotrichus, commonly known as the purple-flowered ivy or velvet ivy, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to western Africa, specifically Nigeria and Cameroon, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its trailing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, typically displaying shades of purple and pink, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
