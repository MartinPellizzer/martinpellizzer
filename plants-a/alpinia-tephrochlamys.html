Alpinia tephrochlamys

Alpinia tephrochlamys, commonly known as the black-veined ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features tall, narrow leaves with dark green veins and produces striking, orange-red flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Alpinia tephrochlamys thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
