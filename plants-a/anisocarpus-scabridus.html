Anisocarpus scabridus

Anisocarpus scabridus, commonly known as roughleaf salsify, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its small, yellow flowers typically bloom in late summer, and it produces distinctive, elongated fruits.

Anisocarpus scabridus is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
