Astragalus sieversianus

Astragalus sieversianus, commonly known as Sievers' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus sieversianus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
