Arenaria anomala

Arenaria anomala, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Arenaria anomala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
