Adiantum concinnum var. pendens

Adiantum concinnum var. pendens, commonly known as the hanging maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that drape gracefully from its stems, giving it a cascading appearance. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its fronds are typically biennial, with the fertile ones appearing in the second year.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
