Andropogon muehlenbergianus

Andropogon muehlenbergianus, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and blue-green foliage, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon muehlenbergianus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
