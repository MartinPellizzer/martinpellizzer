Assonia ferruginea

Assonia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, which give it its common name.

Assonia ferruginea is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.
