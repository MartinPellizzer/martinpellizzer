Arctotis sulphurea

Arctotis sulphurea, commonly known as the golden arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets, creating a striking visual contrast. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Arctotis sulphurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
