Anagallis bella

Anagallis bella, commonly known as the pretty willow or pink willow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often slightly hairy. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Anagallis bella is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in gardens and along roadsides.
