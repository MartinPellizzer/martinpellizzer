Andropogon rectirhachis

Andropogon rectirhachis, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Andropogon rectirhachis plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
