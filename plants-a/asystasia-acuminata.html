Asystasia acuminata

Asystasia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asystasia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The flowers are usually violet or blue in color, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
