Artanthe martinicae

Artanthe martinicae, commonly known as the Martinique artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Martinique.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like structures.

Artanthe martinicae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
