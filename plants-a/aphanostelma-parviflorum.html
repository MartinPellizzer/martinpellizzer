Aphanostelma parviflorum

Aphanostelma parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aphanostelma parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
