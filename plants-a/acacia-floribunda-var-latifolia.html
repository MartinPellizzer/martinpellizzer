Acacia floribunda var. latifolia

Acacia floribunda var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of small, narrow leaves that are often arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and fuel.
