Alsine silvatica

Alsine silvatica, commonly known as woodland alsike, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or ascending. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear to ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Alsine silvatica thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.
