Astragalus citrinus subsp. citrinus

Astragalus citrinus subsp.

citrinus, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves with small, bean-like pods.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep taproot system for water absorption.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
