Alchemilla kupperi

Alchemilla kupperi, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features broad, velvety, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in early summer.

Alchemilla kupperi thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
