Albuca nyikensis

Albuca nyikensis, commonly known as the Nyikeni albuca, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Albuca nyikensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
