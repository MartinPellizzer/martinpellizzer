Acanthophyllum kurdicum

Acanthophyllum kurdicum, commonly known as the Kurdish thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, often inconspicuous flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Acanthophyllum kurdicum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
