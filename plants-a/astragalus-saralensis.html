Astragalus saralensis

Astragalus saralensis, commonly known as the Saralense milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, cylindrical pods, typical of the legume family.

Astragalus saralensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
