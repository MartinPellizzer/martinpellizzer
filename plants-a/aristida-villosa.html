Aristida villosa

Aristida villosa, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Aristida villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
