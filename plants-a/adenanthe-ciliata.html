Adenanthe ciliata

Adenanthe ciliata, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Adenanthe ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
