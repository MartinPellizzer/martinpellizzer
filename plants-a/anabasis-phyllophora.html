Anabasis phyllophora

Anabasis phyllophora, commonly known as the leafy saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in coastal and inland saltbush habitats.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important component of local ecosystems.
