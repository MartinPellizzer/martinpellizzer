Androsace septentrionalis var. ciliata

Androsace septentrionalis var. ciliata, commonly known as the northern mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of northern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine cilia. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
