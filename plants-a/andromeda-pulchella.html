Andromeda pulchella

Andromeda pulchella, commonly known as the rosebay rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Andromeda pulchella is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
