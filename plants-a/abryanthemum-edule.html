Abryanthemum edule

Abryanthemum edule, commonly known as the edible groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine, valued for their mild, slightly peppery flavor.

The plant also has medicinal properties, with some cultures using it to treat ailments like digestive issues and skin conditions.
