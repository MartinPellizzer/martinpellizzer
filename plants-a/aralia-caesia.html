Aralia caesia

Aralia caesia, commonly known as the white aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are palmately arranged, with each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting various pollinators.
