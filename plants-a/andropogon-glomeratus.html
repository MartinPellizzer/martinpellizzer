Andropogon glomeratus

Andropogon glomeratus, commonly known as bush bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often densely covered with small, blue-green leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Andropogon glomeratus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
