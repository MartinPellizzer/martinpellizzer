Adelanthus scandens

Adelanthus scandens, commonly known as the climbing stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-draining soil. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance.

Adelanthus scandens is an evergreen perennial that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.
