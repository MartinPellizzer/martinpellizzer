Adiantum prionophyllum

Adiantum prionophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Adiantum prionophyllum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
