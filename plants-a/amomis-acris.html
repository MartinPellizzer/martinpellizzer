Amomis acris

Amomis acris, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to golden flower heads, which are arranged in panicles and bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Amomis acris is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, prairies, and disturbed areas.
