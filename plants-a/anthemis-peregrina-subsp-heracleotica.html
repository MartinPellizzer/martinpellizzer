Anthemis peregrina subsp. heracleotica

Anthemis peregrina subsp.

heracleotica, commonly known as the Heracleotica ragwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This subspecies is often noted for its low-growing habit and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
