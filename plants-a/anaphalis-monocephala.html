Anaphalis monocephala

Anaphalis monocephala, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white inflorescence that resembles a cloud or a ball of cotton. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a silvery-gray coating that gives it its common name.

Anaphalis monocephala is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique and decorative appearance.
