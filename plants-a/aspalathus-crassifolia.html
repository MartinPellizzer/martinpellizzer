Aspalathus crassifolia

Aspalathus crassifolia, commonly known as the flatbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Aspalathus crassifolia is also known for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
