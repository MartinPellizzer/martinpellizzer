Acalypha fimbriata var. robusta

Acalypha fimbriata var. robusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or coralplant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fimbriated (fringed) leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Acalypha fimbriata var. robusta is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
