Achyronia alternifolia

Achyronia alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf achyronia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its alternate leaf arrangement, with leaves that are typically elliptical and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
