Anathallis ricii

Anathallis ricii, commonly known as the orchid mantis, is a fascinating epiphytic orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a slender, wiry stem and a single, elongated leaf that resembles the body of a mantis. The plant produces striking, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur that serves as a nectar guide for pollinators. Its specialized structure allows it to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of tropical forests.

Anathallis ricii is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its habitat.
