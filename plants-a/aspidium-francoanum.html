Aspidium francoanum

Aspidium francoanum, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.

Aspidium francoanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
