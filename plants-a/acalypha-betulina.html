Acalypha betulina

Acalypha betulina, commonly known as the silver flame or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange and yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and a silver-green underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Acalypha betulina is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
