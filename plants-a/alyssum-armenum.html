Alyssum armenum

Alyssum armenum, commonly known as Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, also known as the mustard family, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Alyssum armenum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
