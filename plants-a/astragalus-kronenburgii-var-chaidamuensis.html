Astragalus kronenburgii var. chaidamuensis

Astragalus kronenburgii var. chaidamuensis, commonly known as the Chaidamu milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments, showcasing features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
