Agave ehrenbergii

Agave ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped arrangement of thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with white teeth along the edges, and they store water to help the plant survive in dry conditions. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and bats.

This plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
