Amaranthus paniculatus var. longespicatus

Amaranthus paniculatus var. longespicatus, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by spiny, triangular fruits. Its roots are edible and have a nutty flavor, making it a valuable crop for both culinary and medicinal uses.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant.
