Albuca volubilis

Albuca volubilis, commonly known as the climbing albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, arching stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns.

Albuca volubilis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
