Acer campbellii subsp. robustum

Acer campbellii subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the Campbell's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central China. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
