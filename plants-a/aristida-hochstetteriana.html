Aristida hochstetteriana

Aristida hochstetteriana, commonly known as the Hochstetter's threeawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aristida hochstetteriana is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low rainfall.
