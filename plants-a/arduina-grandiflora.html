Arduina grandiflora

Arduina grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered arduina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a compact, bushy form. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, often with a bluish-green hue.

Arduina grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
