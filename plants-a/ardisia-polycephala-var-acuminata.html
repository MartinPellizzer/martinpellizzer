Ardisia polycephala var. acuminata

Ardisia polycephala var. acuminata, commonly known as the pointed-leaf ardisia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
