Asphodelus latifolius

Asphodelus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are typically short-lived, blooming from spring to early summer.

Asphodelus latifolius is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
