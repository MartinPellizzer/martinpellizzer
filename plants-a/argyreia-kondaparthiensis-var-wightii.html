Argyreia kondaparthiensis var. wightii

Argyreia kondaparthiensis var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's silver vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, round seeds. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
