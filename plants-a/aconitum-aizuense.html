Aconitum aizuense

Aconitum aizuense, commonly known as the Aizu monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Aconitum aizuense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to handle or ingest.
