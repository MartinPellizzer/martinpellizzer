Armeria maritima subsp. humilis

Armeria maritima subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the sea thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-adapted to salty, windy environments.

The plant features dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
