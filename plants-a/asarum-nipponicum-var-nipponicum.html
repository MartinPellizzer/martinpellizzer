Asarum nipponicum var. nipponicum

Asarum nipponicum var. nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
