Amarella auriculata

Amarella auriculata, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and the flowers are borne on slender stems.

Amarella auriculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
