Arum creticum

Arum creticum, commonly known as the Cretan arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces a spadix, a central spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

The plant has a short growing season and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
