Alyssum montanifolium

Alyssum montanifolium, commonly known as mountain leaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Alyssum montanifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.
