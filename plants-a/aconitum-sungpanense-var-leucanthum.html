Aconitum sungpanense var. leucanthum

Aconitum sungpanense var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers of this variety are predominantly white or pale blue, contrasting with the dark green foliage. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids and requires careful handling due to its potent nature.
