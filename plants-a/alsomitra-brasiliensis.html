Alsomitra brasiliensis

Alsomitra brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazil orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
