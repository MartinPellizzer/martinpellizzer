Aster bellidiastrum var. macroglossus

Aster bellidiastrum var. macroglossus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
