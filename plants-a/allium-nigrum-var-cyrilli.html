Allium nigrum var. cyrilli

Allium nigrum var. cyrilli, commonly known as the Siberian garlic or black garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces small, rounded bulbs with a strong garlic scent and flavor. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is hardy in colder climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to both gardens and traditional herbal practices.
