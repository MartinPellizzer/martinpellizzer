Aster berberifolius

Aster berberifolius, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the dandelion. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Aster berberifolius is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
