Alopecurus agrestis var. creticus

Alopecurus agrestis var. creticus, commonly known as the Cretan mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
