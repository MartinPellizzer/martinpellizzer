Amblynotopsis durangensis

Amblynotopsis durangensis, commonly known as the Durango thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are usually spiny, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Amblynotopsis durangensis plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and serving as a habitat for various small organisms.
