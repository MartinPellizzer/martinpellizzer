Angraecum ramosum var. conchoglossum

Angraecum ramosum var. conchoglossum, commonly known as the conchoglossum angraecum, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, white flowers with prominent, showy lips. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, clinging to trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers open at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
