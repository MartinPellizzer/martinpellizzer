Alomia spilanthoides

Alomia spilanthoides, commonly known as the Mexican buttonweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble buttons, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are usually yellow or white.

Alomia spilanthoides is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
