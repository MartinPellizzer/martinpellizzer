Argyranthemum tenerifae

Argyranthemum tenerifae, commonly known as the Canary Island flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-white fuzzy leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It is a perennial in its native habitat but can be grown as an annual in cooler climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
