Albuca pseudobifolia

Albuca pseudobifolia, commonly known as the African lily or false squill, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice among gardeners.
