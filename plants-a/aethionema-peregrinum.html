Aethionema peregrinum

Aethionema peregrinum, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact size and vibrant flowers make it an attractive addition to alpine gardens.
