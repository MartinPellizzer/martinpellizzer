Anemone ranunculoides var. wockeana

Anemone ranunculoides var. wockeana, commonly known as the Wockeana anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, deeply divided center. The plant has palmately compound leaves that are lobed and often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
