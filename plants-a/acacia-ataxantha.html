Acacia ataxantha

Acacia ataxantha, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, oval pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance. Acacia ataxantha thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
