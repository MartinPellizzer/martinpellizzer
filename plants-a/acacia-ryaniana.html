Acacia ryaniana

Acacia ryaniana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bright yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
