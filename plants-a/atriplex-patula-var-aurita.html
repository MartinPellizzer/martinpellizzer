Atriplex patula var. aurita

Atriplex patula var. aurita, commonly known as the golden saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs that help reduce water loss. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating to further enhance drought tolerance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in compact clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

This plant is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded landscapes.
