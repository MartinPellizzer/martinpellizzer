Aletes tenuifolia

Aletes tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf aletes, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
