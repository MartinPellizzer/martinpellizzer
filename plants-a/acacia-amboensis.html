Acacia amboensis

Acacia amboensis, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow, globular flower heads. The plant is known for its milky sap, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia amboensis plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
