Asplenium × murariiforme

Asplenium × murariiforme, commonly known as the hybrid shield fern, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its compact, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant is native to regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside.

As a hybrid, it exhibits a mix of traits from both parent species, making it a unique subject in botanical studies.
