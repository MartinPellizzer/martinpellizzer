Alpinia biligulata

Alpinia biligulata, commonly known as the black ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of dark purple to black flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a distinct veining pattern.
