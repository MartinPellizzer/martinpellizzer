Alchemilla trabzonica

Alchemilla trabzonica, commonly known as the Trabzon lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the ends of leafy stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla trabzonica is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
