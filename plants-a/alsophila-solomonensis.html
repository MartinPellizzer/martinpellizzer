Alsophila solomonensis

Alsophila solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. Native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, including the Solomon Islands, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
