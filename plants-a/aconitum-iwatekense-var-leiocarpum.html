Aconitum iwatekense var. leiocarpum

Aconitum iwatekense var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the Iwate mountain aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.

It contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.
