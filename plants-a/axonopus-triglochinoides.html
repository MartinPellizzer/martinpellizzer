Axonopus triglochinoides

Axonopus triglochinoides, commonly known as narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly compressed, and its leaves are linear with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
