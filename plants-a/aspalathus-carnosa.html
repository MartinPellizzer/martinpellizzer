Aspalathus carnosa

Aspalathus carnosa, commonly known as the sweet clover or red bush willow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Aspalathus carnosa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
