Aristolochia gracilipedunculata

Aristolochia gracilipedunculata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
