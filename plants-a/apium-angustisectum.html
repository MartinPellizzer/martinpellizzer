Apium angustisectum

Apium angustisectum, commonly known as narrow-leaved celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it often grows in moist, disturbed soils.
