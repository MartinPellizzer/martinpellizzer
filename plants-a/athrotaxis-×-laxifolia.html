Athrotaxis × laxifolia

Athrotaxis × laxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pencil pine, is a hybrid conifer native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly twisted. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in coastal and montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
