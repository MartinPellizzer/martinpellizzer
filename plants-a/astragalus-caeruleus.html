Astragalus caeruleus

Astragalus caeruleus, commonly known as blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus caeruleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
