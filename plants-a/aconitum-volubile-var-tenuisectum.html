Aconitum volubile var. tenuisectum

Aconitum volubile var. tenuisectum, commonly known as the slender-leaved monkshood, is a variant of the monkshood genus characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are blue to purple in color. It produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous if ingested.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
