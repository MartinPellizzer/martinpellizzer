Astracantha karabaghensis subsp. vedica

Astracantha karabaghensis subsp.

vedica, commonly known as the Armenian thornvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology, including the shape and arrangement of its leaves and fruits. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous areas of Armenia and adjacent regions.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insect species.
