Acridocarpus alopecurus subsp. glaber

Acridocarpus alopecurus subsp.

glaber, commonly known as the slender horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in racemes.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable forage species for livestock in arid and semi-arid environments.
