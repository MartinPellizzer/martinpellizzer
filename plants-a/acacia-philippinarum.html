Acacia philippinarum

Acacia philippinarum, commonly known as the Philippine acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands or along coastal areas.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, which can remain on the plant for several years.
