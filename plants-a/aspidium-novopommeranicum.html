Aspidium novopommeranicum

Aspidium novopommeranicum, commonly known as the New Pommeranian water fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in damp woodlands. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Aspidium novopommeranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
