Astragalus nurabadensis

Astragalus nurabadensis, commonly known as the Nurabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of Central Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, compound leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. Astragalus nurabadensis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
