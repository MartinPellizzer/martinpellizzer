Acantholimon diapensioides var. longifolia

Acantholimon diapensioides var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf acantholimon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in racemose inflorescences. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
