Asplenium saulii var. latius

Asplenium saulii var. latius, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family (Aspleniaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often bright green fronds that can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands or rocky crevices, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sporangia, which are clustered in sori.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
