Anemone virginiana f. rubrosepala

Anemone virginiana f. rubrosepala, commonly known as the red-sepaled thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pink flowers with prominent red sepals that contrast vividly with the petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.
