Acacia compressa

Acacia compressa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow globular flowers that appear in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia compressa is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are dispersed by animals and wind.
