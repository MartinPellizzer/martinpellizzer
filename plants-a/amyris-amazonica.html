Amyris amazonica

Amyris amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, resinous bark that exudes a distinctive aromatic compound. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by clustered, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Amyris amazonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of natural resins.
