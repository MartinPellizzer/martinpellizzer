Achillea wilczekiana

Achillea wilczekiana, commonly known as the Polish yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its slender stems and finely divided leaves are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The small white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
