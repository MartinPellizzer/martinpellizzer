Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. subalpina

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

subalpina, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals, which are typically violet or blue in color, and its compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It produces showy flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in gardens.
