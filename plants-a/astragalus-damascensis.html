Astragalus damascensis

Astragalus damascensis, commonly known as the Damascus milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Astragalus damascensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
