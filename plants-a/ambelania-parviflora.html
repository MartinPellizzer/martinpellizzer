Ambelania parviflora

Ambelania parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ambelania, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, often with a slight waxy coating.

Ambelania parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
