Allosorus coriifolius

Allosorus coriifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, coriaceous leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Allosorus coriifolius is often found in mountain meadows and is a member of the Campanulaceae family.
