Archidendron muricarpum

Archidendron muricarpum, commonly known as the rough-fruited legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rough, spiny fruit pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
