Agropyron elongatum var. corsicum

Agropyron elongatum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand arid and coastal conditions.

This grass is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and erosion control capabilities.
