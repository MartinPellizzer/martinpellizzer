Alopecurus hitchcockii

Alopecurus hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, hairless stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in wetland environments.

Alopecurus hitchcockii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
