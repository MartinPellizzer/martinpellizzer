Asarum koreanum

Asarum koreanum, commonly known as Korean wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-brown fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Korean medicine for centuries.
