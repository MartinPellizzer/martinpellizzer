Alchemilla splendens var. infravallesia

Alchemilla splendens var. infravallesia, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy coating, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
