Adesmia fabrisii

Adesmia fabrisii, commonly known as the dwarf silverbush, is a small shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, arid environments.

Adesmia fabrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
