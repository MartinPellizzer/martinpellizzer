Aster schumannii

Aster schumannii, commonly known as Schumann's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues.

Aster schumannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
