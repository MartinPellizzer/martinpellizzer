Acacia penninervis var. angustifolia

Acacia penninervis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are typically green and have a narrow, linear shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some uses in traditional medicine.
