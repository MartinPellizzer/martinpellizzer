Ammodendron karelinii

Ammodendron karelinii, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thick, flaky bark and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits that are edible and used in traditional cuisine.

Ammodendron karelinii is well adapted to harsh desert environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system for water absorption.
