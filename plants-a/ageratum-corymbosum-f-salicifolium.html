Ageratum corymbosum f. salicifolium

Ageratum corymbosum f. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf ageratum, is a variant of the common ageratum characterized by its distinctive, narrow, willow-like leaves.

It is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae), native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, white, and pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Its foliage adds a unique texture to garden landscapes, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.
