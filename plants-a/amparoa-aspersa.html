Amparoa aspersa

Amparoa aspersa, commonly known as the rough amparoa, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
