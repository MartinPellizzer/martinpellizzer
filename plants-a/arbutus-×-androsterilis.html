Arbutus × androsterilis

Arbutus × androsterilis, commonly known as the hybrid madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is a natural hybrid between *Arbutus manca* and *Arbutus unedo*, characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible berries that are dark red when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.
