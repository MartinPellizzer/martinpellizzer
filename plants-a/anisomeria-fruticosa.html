Anisomeria fruticosa

Anisomeria fruticosa, commonly known as desert holly, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with narrow, leathery leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its unique structure and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and wildlife habitat.
