Alternanthera malmeana

Alternanthera malmeana, commonly known as the red-leaf alternate, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from red to purple, depending on light exposure. The plant features opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow.

This ornamental plant is often grown in water gardens or as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
