Anemia incisa

Anemia incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically purple or pink and arranged in clusters.

Anemia incisa is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, sunny environments.
