Aronicum scorpioideum

Aronicum scorpioideum, commonly known as the scarlet bee balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, red-orange flowers resembling bee hives. The plant's foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
