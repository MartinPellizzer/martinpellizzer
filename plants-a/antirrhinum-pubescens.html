Antirrhinum pubescens

Antirrhinum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
