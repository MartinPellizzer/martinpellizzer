Agastache coccinea

Agastache coccinea, commonly known as scarlet bee balm, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features hairy, square stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and vibrant blooms contribute to its ecological and ornamental value.
