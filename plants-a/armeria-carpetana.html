Armeria carpetana

Armeria carpetana, commonly known as the carpeted thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Armeria carpetana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
