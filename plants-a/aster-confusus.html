Aster confusus

Aster confusus, commonly known as the confused aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, purple or white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster confusus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
