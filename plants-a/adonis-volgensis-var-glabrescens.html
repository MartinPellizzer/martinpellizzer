Adonis volgensis var. glabrescens

Adonis volgensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, single flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive trifoliate structure, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

This variety is notable for its reduced pubescence, giving it a smooth, glabrous appearance compared to other adonis species.
