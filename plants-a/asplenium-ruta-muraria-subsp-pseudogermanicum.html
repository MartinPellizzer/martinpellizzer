Asplenium ruta-muraria subsp. pseudogermanicum

Asplenium ruta-muraria subsp.

pseudogermanicum, commonly known as the false rock spleenwort, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family. It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or on tree trunks.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical A. ruta-muraria, particularly in the shape and size of its leaves.
