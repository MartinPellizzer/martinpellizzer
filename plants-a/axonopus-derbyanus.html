Axonopus derbyanus

Axonopus derbyanus, commonly known as the creeping grass or Derby's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. The plant has slender, rhizomatous stems that spread horizontally, forming dense mats. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually a bluish-green color and have a rough texture.

Axonopus derbyanus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
