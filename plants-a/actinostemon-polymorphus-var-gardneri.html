Actinostemon polymorphus var. gardneri

Actinostemon polymorphus var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, branched inflorescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually greenish-white in color, with five petals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
