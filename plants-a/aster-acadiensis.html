Aster acadiensis

Aster acadiensis, commonly known as the eastern aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy purple or pink flowers in the fall. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aster acadiensis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
