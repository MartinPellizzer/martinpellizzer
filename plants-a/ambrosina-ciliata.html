Ambrosina ciliata

Ambrosina ciliata, commonly known as the silky ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators.
