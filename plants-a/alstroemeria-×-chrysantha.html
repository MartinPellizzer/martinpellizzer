Alstroemeria × chrysantha

Alstroemeria × chrysantha, commonly known as the hybrid alstroemeria or the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a hybrid between Alstroemeria aurea and Alstroemeria psittacina, resulting in a diverse range of cultivars with vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring strap-like leaves and clusters of funnel-shaped blooms in various colors. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and commercially valuable in the horticultural industry.
