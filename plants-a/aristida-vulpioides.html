Aristida vulpioides

Aristida vulpioides, commonly known as foxtail grass, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Aristida vulpioides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
