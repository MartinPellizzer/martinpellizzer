Aralia humilis

Aralia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf aralia, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are typically lobed and can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in upright clusters, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Aralia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
