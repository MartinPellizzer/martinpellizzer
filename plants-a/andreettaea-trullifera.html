Andreettaea trullifera

Andreettaea trullifera, commonly known as the "Coral Tree of Brazil," is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a flowering shrub or small tree characterized by its dense clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Andreettaea trullifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
