Acer palmatum subsp. pubescens

Acer palmatum subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese maple with pubescence, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests, and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
