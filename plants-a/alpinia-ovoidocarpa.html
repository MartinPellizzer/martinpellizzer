Alpinia ovoidocarpa

Alpinia ovoidocarpa, commonly known as the mountain ginger or oval-fruit ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and tall, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, oval-shaped berries that are yellow when mature. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
