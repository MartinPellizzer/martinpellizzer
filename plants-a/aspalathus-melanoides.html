Aspalathus melanoides

Aspalathus melanoides, commonly known as the African black tea plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is closely related to the rooibos plant and is known for its unique chemical composition, which includes high levels of antioxidants and flavonoids. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

Due to its ecological importance and potential health benefits, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
