Allium croaticum

Allium croaticum, commonly known as Croatian garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous base and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

Allium croaticum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
