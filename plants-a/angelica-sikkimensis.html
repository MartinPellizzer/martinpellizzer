Angelica sikkimensis

Angelica sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

Native to the eastern Himalayas, it thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in finely divided, feather-like leaves. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically white or pale green, forming dense, spherical clusters.

Angelica sikkimensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
