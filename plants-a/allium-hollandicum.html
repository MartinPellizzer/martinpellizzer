Allium hollandicum

Allium hollandicum, commonly known as the Dutch onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Netherlands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of spherical, pink or purple flowers. The plant is part of the Allium genus, which includes various edible and ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to garden biodiversity.
