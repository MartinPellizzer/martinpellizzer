Acacia woodii

Acacia woodii, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, seed-bearing pods that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
