Adiantum polyphyllum var. rigidum

Adiantum polyphyllum var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid maidenhair fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a rigid, upright growth habit. The plant features small, spore-producing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
