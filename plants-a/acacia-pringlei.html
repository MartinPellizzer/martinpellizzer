Acacia pringlei

Acacia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Acacia pringlei plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
