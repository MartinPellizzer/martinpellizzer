Abies nephrolepis

Abies nephrolepis, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, releasing seeds in the fall. Its foliage provides dense shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The Japanese fir is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
