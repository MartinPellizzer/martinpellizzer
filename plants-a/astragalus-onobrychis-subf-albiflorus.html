Astragalus onobrychis subf. albiflorus

Astragalus onobrychis subf.

albiflorus, commonly known as white-flowered bartsia, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
