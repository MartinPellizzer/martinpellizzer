Anthodiscus gutierrezii

Anthodiscus gutierrezii, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, typical of the family, and bloom in spring.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties.
