Athyrium heterosporum

Athyrium heterosporum, commonly known as the heterosporous lady fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in a clump-forming habit. The plant produces two types of spores—megaspores and microspores—on separate sporangia, a feature that distinguishes it from many other ferns. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance.

Athyrium heterosporum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and stream banks.
