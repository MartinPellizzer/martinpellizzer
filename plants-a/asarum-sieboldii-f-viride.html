Asarum sieboldii f. viride

Asarum sieboldii f. viride, commonly known as the green-flowered wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

The varietal form 'viride' is distinguished by its unique foliage and floral coloration.
