Aloe chortolirioides var. woolliana

Aloe chortolirioides var. woolliana, commonly known as the Woolly Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also popular as ornamental plants due to their unique texture and appearance.
