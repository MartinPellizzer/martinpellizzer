Aspicarpa cynanchoides var. isandra

Aspicarpa cynanchoides var. isandra, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and trifoliate leaves with pink or purple stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by cylindrical, ribbed pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
