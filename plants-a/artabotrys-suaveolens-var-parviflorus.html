Artabotrys suaveolens var. parviflorus

Artabotrys suaveolens var. parviflorus, commonly known as the sweet-scented flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, honey-like aroma. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
