Arabis watsonii

Arabis watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arabis watsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
