Arabis perstellata var. ampla

Arabis perstellata var. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
