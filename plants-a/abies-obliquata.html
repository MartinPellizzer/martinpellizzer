Abies obliquata

Abies obliquata, commonly known as the slant pine, is a species of fir native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a distinctive slanted pattern. The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The botanical features of Abies obliquata make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
