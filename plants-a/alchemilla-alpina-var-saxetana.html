Alchemilla alpina var. saxetana

Alchemilla alpina var. saxetana, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in compact inflorescences.

This hardy species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous habitats.
