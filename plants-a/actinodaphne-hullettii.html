Actinodaphne hullettii

Actinodaphne hullettii, commonly known as the Hullett's camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its essential oils, particularly camphor, have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
