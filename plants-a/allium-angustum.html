Allium angustum

Allium angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chive, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to garlic and onions. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium angustum is valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal in gardens.
