Aeginetia indica var. gracilis

Aeginetia indica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a coastal plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
