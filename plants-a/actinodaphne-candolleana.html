Actinodaphne candolleana

Actinodaphne candolleana, commonly known as the Ceylon camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
