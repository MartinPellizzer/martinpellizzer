Abutilon pachecoanum

Abutilon pachecoanum, commonly known as the Brazilian abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
