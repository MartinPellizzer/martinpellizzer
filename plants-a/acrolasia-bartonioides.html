Acrolasia bartonioides

Acrolasia bartonioides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance that becomes more intense as night progresses.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
