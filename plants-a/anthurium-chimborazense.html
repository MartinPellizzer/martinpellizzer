Anthurium chimborazense

Anthurium chimborazense, commonly known as the Chimborazo anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant green heart-shaped leaves and its showy, spadix-like inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
