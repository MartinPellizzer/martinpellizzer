Aeschynanthus leucothamnos

Aeschynanthus leucothamnos, commonly known as the white-thorned ivy.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its foliage is often glossy and dark green, with opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in gardens.
