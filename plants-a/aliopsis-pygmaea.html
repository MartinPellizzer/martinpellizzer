Aliopsis pygmaea

Aliopsis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown. Its compact size, often reaching only a few inches in height, makes it a unique and delicate member of the Cyperaceae family.

Aliopsis pygmaea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
