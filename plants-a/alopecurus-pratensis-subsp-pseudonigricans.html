Alopecurus pratensis subsp. pseudonigricans

Alopecurus pratensis subsp.

pseudonigricans, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear purple to brown flowers.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes darker towards the base. This subspecies thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows and wetlands.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
