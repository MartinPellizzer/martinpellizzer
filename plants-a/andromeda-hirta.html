Andromeda hirta

Andromeda hirta, commonly known as the hairy andromeda, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are small, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to woodland gardens.
