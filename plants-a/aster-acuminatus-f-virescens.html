Aster acuminatus f. virescens

Aster acuminatus f. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and covered in fine, greenish-white hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pointed tip and a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and bloom in the late summer to early fall.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
