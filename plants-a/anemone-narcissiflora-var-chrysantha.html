Anemone narcissiflora var. chrysantha

Anemone narcissiflora var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like center, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
