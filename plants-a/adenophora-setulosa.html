Adenophora setulosa

Adenophora setulosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Adenophora setulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
