Apium graveolens

Apium graveolens, commonly known as celery, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and aromatic, leafy foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots and stalks are widely used in cooking for their distinct flavor and nutritional value.

Celery thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.
