Acalypha peduncularis var. punctata

Acalypha peduncularis var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and may bear small, yellow or white spots, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
