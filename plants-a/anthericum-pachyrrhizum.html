Anthericum pachyrrhizum

Anthericum pachyrrhizum, commonly known as the thick-rooted fern, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The thick, fleshy roots are an adaptation for storing nutrients and water in its natural habitat.
