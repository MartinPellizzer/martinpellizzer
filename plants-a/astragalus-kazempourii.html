Astragalus kazempourii

Astragalus kazempourii, commonly known as the Kazempour milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Iran and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in challenging habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
