Artemisia caruifolia var. apiacea

Artemisia caruifolia var. apiacea, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features aromatic, silvery-green leaves with a distinctive, slightly sweet and medicinal scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of the compound artemisinin, which is used in antimalarial treatments.
