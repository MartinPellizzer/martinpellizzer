Acalypha crotonoides var. caudata

Acalypha crotonoides var. caudata, commonly known as the trailing croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in spikes.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
