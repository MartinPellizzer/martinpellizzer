Aristotelia pubescens

Aristotelia pubescens, commonly known as the kiwi fruit or actinidia deliciosa, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces fuzzy, brown berries that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular commercial crop.
