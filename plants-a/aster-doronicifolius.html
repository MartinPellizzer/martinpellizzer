Aster doronicifolius

Aster doronicifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall.

Aster doronicifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
