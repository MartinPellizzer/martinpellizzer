Aegilops heldreichii

Aegilops heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan goatgrass, is a wild grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Aegilops heldreichii is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
