Astragalus goeznensis

Astragalus goeznensis, commonly known as the Goetz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native habitat.
