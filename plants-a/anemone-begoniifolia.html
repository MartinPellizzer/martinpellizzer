Anemone begoniifolia

Anemone begoniifolia, commonly known as the begonia-leaved anemone, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that resemble those of a begonia, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Anemone begoniifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
