Armeniaca sibirica var. multipetala

Armeniaca sibirica var. multipetala, commonly known as the Siberian apricot with multiple petals, is a variant of the Siberian apricot native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, white or pink flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while the fruits are small, yellow or orange drupes.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
