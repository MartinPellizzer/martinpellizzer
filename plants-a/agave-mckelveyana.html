Agave mckelveyana

Agave mckelveyana, commonly known as the Mckelvey's agave, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave mckelveyana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
