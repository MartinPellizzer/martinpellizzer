Arundinaria masamuneana

Arundinaria masamuneana, commonly known as the Masamune bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinaria masamuneana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and resilience in various environmental conditions.
