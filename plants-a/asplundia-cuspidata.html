Asplundia cuspidata

Asplundia cuspidata, commonly known as the spiky arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Asplundia cuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
