Aspidium spinescens

Aspidium spinescens, commonly known as the spinous water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in small, spiny structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Aspidium spinescens is notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
