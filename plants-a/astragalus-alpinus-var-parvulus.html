Astragalus alpinus var. parvulus

Astragalus alpinus var. parvulus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, dry environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential immune-supporting effects.
