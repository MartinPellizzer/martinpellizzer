Astragalus douglasii var. piscinus

Astragalus douglasii var. piscinus, commonly known as Douglas's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
