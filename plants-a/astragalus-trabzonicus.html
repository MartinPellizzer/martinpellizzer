Astragalus trabzonicus

Astragalus trabzonicus, commonly known as the Trabzon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
