Acilepis dalzelliana

Acilepis dalzelliana, commonly known as the Dalzell's acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dune environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
