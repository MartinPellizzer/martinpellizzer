Artemisia spicata subsp. villarsii

Artemisia spicata subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine tarragon, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and more robust stems compared to other Artemisia species.
