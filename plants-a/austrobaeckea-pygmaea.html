Austrobaeckea pygmaea

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and white, fleshy berries. The plant attaches itself to the bark of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus species, through a specialized structure called a haustellum. Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and health of its host plants.
