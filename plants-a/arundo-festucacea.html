Arundo festucacea

Arundo festucacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, flowering stalks that produce small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an effective erosion control plant.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
