Athyrium deltoidofrons var. multifidum

Athyrium deltoidofrons var. multifidum, commonly known as the multifidum variety of the delta-faced fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is native to regions in East Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with clustered, lanceolate leaves that are often adorned with a whitish or silver-colored margin. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, creating a lacy appearance that is both ornamental and distinctive.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in shaded garden environments.
