Adelia rotundifolia

Adelia rotundifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Adelia rotundifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
