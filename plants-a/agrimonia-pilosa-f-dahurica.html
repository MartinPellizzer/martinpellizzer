Agrimonia pilosa f. dahurica

Agrimonia pilosa f. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian agrimony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered with soft, grayish-green hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Agrimonia pilosa f. dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
