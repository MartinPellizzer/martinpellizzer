Aridaria suffusa

Aridaria suffusa, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant adapted to arid environments.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves that minimize water loss through transpiration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Aridaria suffusa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
