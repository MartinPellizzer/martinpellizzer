Abarema dalatensis

Abarema dalatensis, commonly known as the Dalat trumpetwood, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
