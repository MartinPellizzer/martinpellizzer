Acrocephalus lilacinus

Acrocephalus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac-headed reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and the flowers are usually a mix of green and lilac in color.

This species plays an important ecological role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
