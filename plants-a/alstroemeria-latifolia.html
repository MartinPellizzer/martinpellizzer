Alstroemeria latifolia

Alstroemeria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with long, strap-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alstroemeria latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
