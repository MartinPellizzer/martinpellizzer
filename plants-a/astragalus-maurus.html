Astragalus maurus

Astragalus maurus, commonly known as the yellow goat's head, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spindle-shaped pod that splits open to release seeds.

Astragalus maurus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
