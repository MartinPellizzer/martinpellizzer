Adyseton altaicum

Adyseton altaicum, commonly known as the Alaska mountain avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and tundra environments. The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Adyseton altaicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its harsh native environment.
