Archiphyllum oblongifolium

Archiphyllum oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf archiphyllum, is a flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

Archiphyllum oblongifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
