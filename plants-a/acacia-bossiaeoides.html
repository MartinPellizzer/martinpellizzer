Acacia bossiaeoides

Acacia bossiaeoides, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Acacia bossiaeoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
