Asplenium spannagelii

Asplenium spannagelii, commonly known as the Spiny Brake, is a fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit. The plant has dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive spiny appearance along the margins. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a kidney-shaped indusium.

Asplenium spannagelii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
