Ajuga orientalis var. orthosiphon

Ajuga orientalis var. orthosiphon, commonly known as Chinese bugloss, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and its ability to spread vegetatively.
