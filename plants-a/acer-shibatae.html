Acer shibatae

Acer shibatae, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant colors during the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and turn red or orange in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
