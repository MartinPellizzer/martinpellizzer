Airochloa parviflora

Airochloa parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears tiny white or pale blue flowers.

Airochloa parviflora is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
