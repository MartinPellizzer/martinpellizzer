Agave vazquezgarciae

Agave vazquezgarciae, commonly known as the Vázquez-García agave, is a species native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with small, white flowers, which appear after several years of growth. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
