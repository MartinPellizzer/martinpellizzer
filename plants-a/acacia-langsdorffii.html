Acacia langsdorffii

Acacia langsdorffii, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are feathery and slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia langsdorffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
