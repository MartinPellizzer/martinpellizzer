Alsophila nicklesii

Alsophila nicklesii, commonly known as the Nickles' tree fern, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Alsophila nicklesii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
