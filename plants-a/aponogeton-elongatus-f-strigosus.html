Aponogeton elongatus f. strigosus

Aponogeton elongatus f. strigosus, commonly known as the hairy elongated arrowhead, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its long, slender leaves that often trail from the water surface into the surrounding water.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated in aquariums for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.

However, it can become invasive in non-native regions if not properly managed.
