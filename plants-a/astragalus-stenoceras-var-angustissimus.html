Astragalus stenoceras var. angustissimus

Astragalus stenoceras var. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous narrow leaflets, and its root system is deep, allowing it to thrive in poor soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
