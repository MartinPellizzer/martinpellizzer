Acanthophyllum paniculatum var. gypsophiloides

Acanthophyllum paniculatum var. gypsophiloides, commonly known as the gypsum bush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow or greenish flowers in panicle-like clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils, often found on rocky or sandy substrates.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and for erosion control in dry landscapes.
