Arundo phragmites var. stolonifera

Arundo phragmites var. stolonifera, commonly known as giant reed, is a perennial grass native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant spreads through rhizomes and stolons, forming dense colonies that dominate aquatic and semi-aquatic environments. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of wetland habitats, making it an important component of many ecosystems.
