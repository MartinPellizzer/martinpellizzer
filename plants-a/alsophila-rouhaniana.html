Alsophila rouhaniana

Alsophila rouhaniana, commonly known as the Rouhan's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and conservatories.
