Acer collinum

Acer collinum, commonly known as the mountain maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows at high elevations, often in subalpine or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The tree has a distinctive appearance with its slender trunk and delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Acer collinum plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
