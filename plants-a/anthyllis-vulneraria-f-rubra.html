Anthyllis vulneraria f. rubra

Anthyllis vulneraria f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
