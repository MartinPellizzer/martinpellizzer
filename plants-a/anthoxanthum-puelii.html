Anthoxanthum puelii

Anthoxanthum puelii, commonly known as the Puel's oatgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed florets. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Anthoxanthum puelii is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
