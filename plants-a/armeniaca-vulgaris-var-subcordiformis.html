Armeniaca vulgaris var. subcordiformis

Armeniaca vulgaris var. subcordiformis, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly waxy texture. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single stone, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is related to the peach and apricot, sharing similar botanical features.
