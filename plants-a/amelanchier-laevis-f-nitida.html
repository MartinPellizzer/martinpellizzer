Amelanchier laevis f. nitida

Amelanchier laevis f. nitida, commonly known as the smooth serviceberry, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
