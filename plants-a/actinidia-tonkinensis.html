Actinidia tonkinensis

Actinidia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
