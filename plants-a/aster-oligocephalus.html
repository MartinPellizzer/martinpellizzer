Aster oligocephalus

Aster oligocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, clustered flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it blooms from late summer into early fall.

Aster oligocephalus is often found in areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
