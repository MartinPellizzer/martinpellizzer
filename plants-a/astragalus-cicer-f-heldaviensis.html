Astragalus cicer f. heldaviensis

Astragalus cicer f. heldaviensis, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a subspecies of the honeyvetch plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along mountain slopes.

Botanically, it is notable for its role in ecological systems and its potential use in traditional medicine.
