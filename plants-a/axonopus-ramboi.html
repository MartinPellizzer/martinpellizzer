Axonopus ramboi

Axonopus ramboi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf axonopus, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Axonopus ramboi is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
