Allium gracile

Allium gracile, commonly known as slender leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear small, white or pale purple flowers in the spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium gracile thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
