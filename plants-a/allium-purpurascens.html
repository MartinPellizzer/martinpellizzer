Allium purpurascens

Allium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant's leaves are long, grass-like, and often have a mild garlic scent. Its bulbs are edible and can be used similarly to regular onions, though they are less commonly cultivated.

Allium purpurascens is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary potential in Mediterranean cuisine.
