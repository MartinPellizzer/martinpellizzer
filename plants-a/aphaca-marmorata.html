Aphaca marmorata

Aphaca marmorata, commonly known as the marble vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that resemble marble, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Aphaca marmorata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
