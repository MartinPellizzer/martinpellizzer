Aralia pergamacea

Aralia pergamacea, commonly known as the giant aralia, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it a dramatic addition to any landscape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in an umbrella-like cluster, which are followed by dark red berries. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments, and it is often found in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
