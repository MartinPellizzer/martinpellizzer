Arnica longifolia subsp. myriadenia

Arnica longifolia subsp.

myriadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, with a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
