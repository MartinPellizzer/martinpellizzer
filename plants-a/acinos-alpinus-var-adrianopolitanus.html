Acinos alpinus var. adrianopolitanus

Acinos alpinus var. adrianopolitanus, commonly known as the Adrianopolitan alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountainous areas with high altitudes and cool climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
