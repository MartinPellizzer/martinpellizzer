Anguria treslingiana

Anguria treslingiana, commonly known as the Trinidadian watermelon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and sprawling stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, pale green leaves with a rough texture and distinct veins. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a large, oblong melon with a thick rind and sweet, juicy flesh, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
