Achyranthes aspera var. villososericea

Achyranthes aspera var. villososericea, commonly known as hairy prickly chaffflower, is a variant of the species Achyranthes aspera, which is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in dense, soft hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and also bear a hairy texture. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments.
