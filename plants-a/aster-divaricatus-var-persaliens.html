Aster divaricatus var. persaliens

Aster divaricatus var. persaliens, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to violet petals and a yellow central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
