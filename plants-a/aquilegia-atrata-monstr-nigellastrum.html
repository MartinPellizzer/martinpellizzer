Aquilegia atrata monstr. nigellastrum

Aquilegia atrata monstr.

nigellastrum, commonly known as the black-flowered columbine, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of deep purple to black, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to diverse plant collections.
