Astragalus montanus proles jacquinii

Astragalus montanus proles jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
