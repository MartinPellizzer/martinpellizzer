Aplostellis velutina

Aplostellis velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which can be white, yellow, or orange, are often clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
