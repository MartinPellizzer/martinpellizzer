Anthyllis hispida f. cavanillesii

Anthyllis hispida f. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
