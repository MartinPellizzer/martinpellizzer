Acacia heterochroa subsp. robertii

Acacia heterochroa subsp.

robertii, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
