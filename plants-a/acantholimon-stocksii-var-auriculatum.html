Acantholimon stocksii var. auriculatum

Acantholimon stocksii var. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate acantholimon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in late spring or early summer. It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-like) lobes on the leaf bases.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
