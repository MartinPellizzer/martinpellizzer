Alyssopsis

Alyssopsis, commonly known as the "bluebells of the desert," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. Alyssopsis species are characterized by their delicate, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling the appearance of true bluebells. They typically grow from bulbs and are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The genus includes several species, such as Alyssopsis capensis and Alyssopsis monticola, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in their native habitats.
