Arcytophyllum laricifolium

Arcytophyllum laricifolium, commonly known as the mountain sage or white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils at high elevations. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and ceremonial practices by Native American communities.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
