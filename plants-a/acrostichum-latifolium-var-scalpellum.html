Acrostichum latifolium var. scalpellum

Acrostichum latifolium var. scalpellum, commonly known as the broadleaf hartiela, is a variant of the broadleaf hartiela, a member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with closely spaced pinnae that give the plant a dense appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to wetland conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in such habitats.
