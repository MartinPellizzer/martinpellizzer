Acalypha rugosa

Acalypha rugosa, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
