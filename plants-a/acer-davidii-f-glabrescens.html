Acer davidii f. glabrescens

Acer davidii f. glabrescens, commonly known as the silver maple, is a variant of the David maple native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery bark and delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden environments.
