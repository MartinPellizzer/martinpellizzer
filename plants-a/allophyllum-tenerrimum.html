Allophyllum tenerrimum

Allophyllum tenerrimum, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, dark berries. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish flowers in early spring.

Allophyllum tenerrimum is known for its resilience to drought and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
