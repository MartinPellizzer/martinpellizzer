Acanthopanax senticosus

Acanthopanax senticosus, commonly known as Siberian ginseng, is a shrub native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, and its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including saponins and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
