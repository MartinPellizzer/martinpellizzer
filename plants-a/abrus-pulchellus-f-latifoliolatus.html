Abrus pulchellus f. latifoliolatus

Abrus pulchellus f. latifoliolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the scarlet runner bean characterized by its broader, more lobed leaves.

It is a climbing legume native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is cultivated for its ornamental value and edible pods. The plant produces vibrant red flowers followed by long, bean-like pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its foliage is dense and lush, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic appeal and edible harvests.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and support for its vining growth habit.
