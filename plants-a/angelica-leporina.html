Angelica leporina

Angelica leporina, commonly known as the rabbit's garlic, is a biennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Angelica leporina thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
