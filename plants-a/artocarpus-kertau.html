Artocarpus kertau

Artocarpus kertau, commonly known as Kertau breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit, known as a "breadfruit," is typically harvested when mature and used in various culinary applications. The plant thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it a significant species in both horticulture and agriculture.
