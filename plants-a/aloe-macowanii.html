Aloe macowanii

Aloe macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow to orange flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Aloe macowanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
