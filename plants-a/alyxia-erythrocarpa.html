Alyxia erythrocarpa

Alyxia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental berries and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color.
