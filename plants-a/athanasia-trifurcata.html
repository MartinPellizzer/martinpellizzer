Athanasia trifurcata

Athanasia trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branch everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive branching pattern, with three main stems emerging from the base.

Athanasia trifurcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
