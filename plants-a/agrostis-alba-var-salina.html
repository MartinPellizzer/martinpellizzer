Agrostis alba var. salina

Agrostis alba var. salina, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal and saline environments.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in soils with high salt content. This plant typically grows in coastal dunes, marshes, and other saline habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Agrostis alba var. salina is an important component of coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
