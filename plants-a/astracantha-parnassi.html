Astracantha parnassi

Astracantha parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its showy purple-blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astracantha parnassi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
