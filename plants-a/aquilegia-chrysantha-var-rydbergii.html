Aquilegia chrysantha var. rydbergii

Aquilegia chrysantha var. rydbergii, commonly known as the Rydberg's columbine, is a variant of the showy columbine.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, typically found in rocky or open woodland habitats. The plant features striking, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided with a serrated edge, and it produces seed pods that split open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
