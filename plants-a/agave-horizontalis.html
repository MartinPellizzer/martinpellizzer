Agave horizontalis

Agave horizontalis, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The leaves are arranged in a tight spiral and can grow up to 3 feet in height. It produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid climates.
