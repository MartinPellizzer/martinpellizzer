Asimina pygmaea

Asimina pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf wild cherry, is a small, deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by yellowish-orange fruits that are edible but generally not widely consumed.
