Astragalus silifkeense

Astragalus silifkeense, commonly known as the Silifke milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Astragalus silifkeense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
