Albizia polyphylla

Albizia polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and can vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Albizia polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
