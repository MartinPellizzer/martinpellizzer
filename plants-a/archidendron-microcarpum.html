Archidendron microcarpum

Archidendron microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
