Aconitum × paniculatum var. cernuum

Aconitum × paniculatum var. cernuum, commonly known as the nodding monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that hang downward. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are blue to purple in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
