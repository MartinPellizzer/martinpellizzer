Asplenium schimperi

Asplenium schimperi, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly Ethiopia and Sudan. The plant features delicate, finely divided, dark green fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Asplenium schimperi is also valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
