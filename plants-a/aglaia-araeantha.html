Aglaia araeantha

Aglaia araeantha, commonly known as the Java aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable timber species. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional uses.
