Actinodaphne borneensis

Actinodaphne borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean actinodaphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the island of Borneo and is typically found in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, characterized by its aromatic leaves and smooth, greyish bark. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
