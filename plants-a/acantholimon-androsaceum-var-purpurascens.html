Acantholimon androsaceum var. purpurascens

Acantholimon androsaceum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered sand-rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable addition to xeriscaping and coastal landscapes.
