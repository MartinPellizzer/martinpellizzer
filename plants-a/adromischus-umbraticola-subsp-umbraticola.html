Adromischus umbraticola subsp. umbraticola

Adromischus umbraticola subsp.

umbraticola, commonly known as the shadowy jade. It is a succulent plant native to South Africa, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and the plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate partial shade.
