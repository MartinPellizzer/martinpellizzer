Amomum padangense

Amomum padangense, commonly known as the Padang ginger or wild ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are followed by small, triangular fruits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in herbal remedies for various health purposes.
