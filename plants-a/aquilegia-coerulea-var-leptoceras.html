Aquilegia coerulea var. leptoceras

Aquilegia coerulea var. leptoceras, commonly known as the blue cohosh, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, particularly found in the eastern United States. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five sepals and two petals. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds.

The plant thrives in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
