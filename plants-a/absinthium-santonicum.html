Absinthium santonicum

Absinthium santonicum, commonly known as the sacred herb of the ancient Greeks, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant is notable for its strong, often bitter scent, which is attributed to compounds like absinthin and santonicin. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Absinthium santonicum thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
