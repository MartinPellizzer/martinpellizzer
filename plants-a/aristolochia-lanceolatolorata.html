Aristolochia lanceolatolorata

Aristolochia lanceolatolorata, commonly known as the lanceolate arrowhead vine, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves and unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a horn. The plant is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are pollinated by flies, which are attracted by their strong, musty odor.
