Astragalus wiedemannianus

Astragalus wiedemannianus, commonly known as the Wiedemann's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary ecological role is as a component of grassland and steppe ecosystems.
