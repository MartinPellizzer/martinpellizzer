Aphelandra chrysops

Aphelandra chrysops, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or bronze underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra chrysops is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
