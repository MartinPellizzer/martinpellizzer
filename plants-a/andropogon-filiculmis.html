Andropogon filiculmis

Andropogon filiculmis, commonly known as the feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, feathery plumes that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Andropogon filiculmis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
