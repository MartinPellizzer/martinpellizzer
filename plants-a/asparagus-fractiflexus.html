Asparagus fractiflexus

Asparagus fractiflexus, commonly known as the flexible asparagus, is a unique species within the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its creeping, prostrate growth habit and the ability to bend and flex, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into red berries. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish tint.

Asparagus fractiflexus is native to southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
