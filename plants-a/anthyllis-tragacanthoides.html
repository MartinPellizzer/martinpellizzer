Anthyllis tragacanthoides

Anthyllis tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Mediterranean hawthorn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in early spring, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Anthyllis tragacanthoides is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
