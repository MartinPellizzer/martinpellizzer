Aspalathus tomentosa

Aspalathus tomentosa, commonly known as the silver bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
