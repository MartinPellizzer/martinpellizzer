Arabis multiflora

Arabis multiflora, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments. It is a perennial species that produces capsules containing numerous small seeds.

Arabis multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
