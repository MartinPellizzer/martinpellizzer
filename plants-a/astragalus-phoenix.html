Astragalus phoenix

Astragalus phoenix, commonly known as the Phoenix milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, compound leaves with numerous leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Astragalus phoenix plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
