Acanthophyllum heratense

Acanthophyllum heratense, commonly known as the Herat spiny gram, is a flowering plant native to Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance through its thick, fleshy stems that store water. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

This species is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of forage in harsh climates.
