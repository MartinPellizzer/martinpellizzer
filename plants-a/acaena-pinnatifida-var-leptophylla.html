Acaena pinnatifida var. leptophylla

Acaena pinnatifida var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces bright red, spherical fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, burr-like structure. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
