Aegilops fausii

Aegilops fausii, commonly known as false wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the tribe Aegilop tribe within the grass family Poaceae. This plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Aegilops fausii has been studied for its potential in genetic research due to its close relationship to wheat species.
