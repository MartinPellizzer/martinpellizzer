Acianthus saxatilis

Acianthus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock iris, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Acianthus saxatilis is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
