Alocasia × mortfontanensis

Alocasia × mortfontanensis, commonly known as the Giant Elephant Ear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Alocasia odora and Alocasia macrorrhiza.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a deep green color and a velvety texture. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm environments with high light exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage rather than its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous.

Due to its size and aesthetic appeal, it requires careful handling and suitable growing conditions to flourish.
