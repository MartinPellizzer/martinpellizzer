Achillea salicifolia subsp. septentrionalis

Achillea salicifolia subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as northern yarrow, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in flat-topped umbels during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.
