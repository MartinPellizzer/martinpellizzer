Adicea alsinifolia

Adicea alsinifolia, commonly known as the slender chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in garden beds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural features.
