Arisaema peninsulae f. atropurpureum

Arisaema peninsulae f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined arisaema, is a variant of the arisaema genus native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with deep purple veins against a dark green background. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a hooded spathe, typical of the arisaema family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
