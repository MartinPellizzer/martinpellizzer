Acer campestre f. bedoioides

Acer campestre f. bedoioides, commonly known as the bedoioides form of the field maple, is a variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that are often a striking green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
