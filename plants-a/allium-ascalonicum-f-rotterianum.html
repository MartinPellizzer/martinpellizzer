Allium ascalonicum f. rotterianum

Allium ascalonicum f. rotterianum, commonly known as the Rotter's leek, is a botanical variant of the leek species.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, leek-like flavor, making it suitable for both garden display and culinary use.
