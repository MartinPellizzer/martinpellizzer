Aspidium glanduliferum

Aspidium glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that are divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, glandular dots on its leaves, which are a distinguishing feature. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Aspidium glanduliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
