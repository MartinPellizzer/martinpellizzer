Arisarum simorrhinum var. pallida

Arisarum simorrhinum var. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered arum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and an inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is distinguished by its pale floral coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties within the species.
