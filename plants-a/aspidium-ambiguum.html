Aspidium ambiguum

Aspidium ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Aspidium ambiguum plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
