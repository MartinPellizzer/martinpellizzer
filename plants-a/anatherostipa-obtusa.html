Anatherostipa obtusa

Anatherostipa obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaf sheaths. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, contributing to its role in stabilizing wetland soils.
