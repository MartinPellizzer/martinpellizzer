Aloe erinacea

Aloe erinacea, commonly known as the cannonball aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiky leaves that form a compact, globular shape, resembling a cannonball. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe erinacea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
