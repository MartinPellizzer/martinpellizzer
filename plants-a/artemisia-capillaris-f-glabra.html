Artemisia capillaris f. glabra

Artemisia capillaris f. glabra, commonly known as Chinese yarrow or hairless wormwood, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, gray-green leaves that are often glabrous, meaning they lack hair. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist or marshy habitats.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
