Aspidosperma cruentum

Aspidosperma cruentum, commonly known as the bloodwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.
