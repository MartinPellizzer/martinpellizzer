Agathosma gonaquensis

Agathosma gonaquensis, commonly known as the Cape garlic or wild garlic, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky, sandy, or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

Agathosma gonaquensis is also cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
