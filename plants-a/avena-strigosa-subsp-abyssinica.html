Avena strigosa subsp. abyssinica

Avena strigosa subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian oat, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for forage or erosion control in arid environments.
