Adyseton campestre

Adyseton campestre, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Adyseton campestre is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
