Aechmea cardenasii

Aechmea cardenasii, commonly known as the Cardenas' bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically green with reddish or purple hues, especially along the margins. It produces vibrant orange or red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

Aechmea cardenasii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Morphology

Aechmea cardenasii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and grass-like, arranged in a compact circle around the central stem.

The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, upright stem with a smooth, pale green bark that grows from the base of the rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.
