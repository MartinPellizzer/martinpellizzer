Armeria maritima subsp. plantaginea

Armeria maritima subsp.

plantaginea, commonly known as sea thrift, is a low-growing evergreen perennial that thrives in coastal environments. It features dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. It is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in maritime climates.

This subspecies is often used in coastal landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
