Ammannia lingulata

Ammannia lingulata, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, pinkish to white flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom from June to September.

Ammannia lingulata is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
