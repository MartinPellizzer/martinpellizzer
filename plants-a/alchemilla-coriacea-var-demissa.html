Alchemilla coriacea var. demissa

Alchemilla coriacea var. demissa, commonly known as the woolly lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
