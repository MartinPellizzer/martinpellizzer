Apium sisarum

Apium sisarum, commonly known as wild celery, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, hollow stems and fern-like, compound leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbrella-shaped inflorescences, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

Apium sisarum is also valued for its edible leaves and roots, which have a mild, celery-like flavor.
