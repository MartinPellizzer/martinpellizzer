Aglaia tomentosa subsp. cordata

Aglaia tomentosa subsp.

cordata, commonly known as the hairy aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
