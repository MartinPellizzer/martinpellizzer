Alopecurus agrestis var. versicolor

Alopecurus agrestis var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its adaptability and widespread distribution, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
