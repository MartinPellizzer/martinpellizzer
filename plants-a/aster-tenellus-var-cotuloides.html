Aster tenellus var. cotuloides

Aster tenellus var. cotuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
