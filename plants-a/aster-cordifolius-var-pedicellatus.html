Aster cordifolius var. pedicellatus

Aster cordifolius var. pedicellatus, commonly known as the pedicellate heartleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
