Alyssum tortuosum var. viridescens

Alyssum tortuosum var. viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight bluish or grayish hue. This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
