Aster chilensis var. invenustus

Aster chilensis var. invenustus, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
