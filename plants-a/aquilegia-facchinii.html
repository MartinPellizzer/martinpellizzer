Aquilegia facchinii

Aquilegia facchinii, commonly known as the Italian columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Italy.

It features striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue, arranged in a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant produces compound leaves with finely toothed edges, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
