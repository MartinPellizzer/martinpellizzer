Allium fimbriatum var. denticulatum

Allium fimbriatum var. denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers in late summer, which are typical of the Allium genus. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued plant in some culinary traditions.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to rocky or arid soils.
