Allium paniculatum var. rifanum

Allium paniculatum var. rifanum, commonly known as the Rif garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found growing in rocky, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional dishes.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
