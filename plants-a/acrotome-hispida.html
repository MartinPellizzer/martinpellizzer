Acrotome hispida

Acrotome hispida, commonly known as the rough acrotome, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
