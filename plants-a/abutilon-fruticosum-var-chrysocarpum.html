Abutilon fruticosum var. chrysocarpum

Abutilon fruticosum var. chrysocarpum, commonly known as the golden-seed abutilon, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that turn golden when mature, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
