Anthemis nobilis

Anthemis nobilis, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with white ray florets, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal medicine.

The species is also known for its strong, aromatic scent, which can vary depending on the time of day.
