Acicalyptus pennelii

Acicalyptus pennelii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a small, evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale blue and appear in clusters.

Acicalyptus pennelii is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
