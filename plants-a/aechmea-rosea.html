Aechmea rosea

Aechmea rosea, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to South America.

Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called bloom, which helps reduce water loss.

Aechmea rosea thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its vibrant appearance.

Aechmea rosea has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a compact circle, typically measuring 20–30 cm in length and 5–8 cm in width, with a bluish-gray color and a waxy surface.

The plant has a short, thick, and often hidden stem with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upright from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of purple or pink flowers that bloom during the summer months, and it is primarily pollinated by insects such as bees and hummingbirds.
