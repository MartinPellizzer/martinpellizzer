Axonopus pseudochloris

Axonopus pseudochloris, commonly known as the narrowleaf axonopus, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green color. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Axonopus pseudochloris is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
