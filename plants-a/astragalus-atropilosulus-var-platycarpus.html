Astragalus atropilosulus var. platycarpus

Astragalus atropilosulus var. platycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
