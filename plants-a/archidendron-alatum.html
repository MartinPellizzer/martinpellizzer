Archidendron alatum

Archidendron alatum, commonly known as the winged maple or winged pod.

It is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves with distinctive wing-like structures on the leaflets. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in racemes, and are usually pale green or yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
