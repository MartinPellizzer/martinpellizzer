Arundinaria suberecta

Arundinaria suberecta, commonly known as the upright bamboo, is a species of grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and ecological benefits, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
