Apocynum pumilum var. plumbeum

Apocynum pumilum var. plumbeum, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that are often tinged with a bluish hue, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by follicles containing toxic, milky sap. It is part of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky latex and showy flowers.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
