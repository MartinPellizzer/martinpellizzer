Allophylus vestitus

Allophylus vestitus, commonly known as the hairy alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and has smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain one or two seeds. Allophylus vestitus is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
