Angraecum defoliatum

Angraecum defoliatum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with dark purple markings. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant blooms during the dry season. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Angraecum defoliatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
