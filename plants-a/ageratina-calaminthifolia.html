Ageratina calaminthifolia

Ageratina calaminthifolia, commonly known as sweetweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in a spherical shape.
