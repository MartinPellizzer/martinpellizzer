Acacia macalusoi

Acacia macalusoi, commonly known as the Macaluso acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, paired leaflets that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its woody structure and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
