Arctostaphylos alpina var. japonica

Arctostaphylos alpina var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese manzanita, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
