Astragalus drepanolobus var. aberrans

Astragalus drepanolobus var. aberrans, commonly known as the bent-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

It is often found in steppes and mountainous areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
