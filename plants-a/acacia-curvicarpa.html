Acacia curvicarpa

Acacia curvicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, demonstrating adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in revegetation projects.
