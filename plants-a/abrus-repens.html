Abrus repens

Abrus repens, commonly known as the wandering beggar or devil's bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces distinctive, showy red or orange flowers that are often followed by intricate, ornate seed pods. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and is typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds contain toxic compounds, making them hazardous if ingested.

The botanical features of Abrus repens make it both ecologically significant and potentially dangerous.
