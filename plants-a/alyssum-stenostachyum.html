Alyssum stenostachyum

Alyssum stenostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stalked alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has succulent, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its compact growth habit.

Alyssum stenostachyum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and ladybugs.
