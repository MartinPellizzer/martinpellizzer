Asplenium lanceolatum subsp. fontanum

Asplenium lanceolatum subsp.

fontanum, commonly known as the Fontana spleenwort, is a fern species native to parts of Europe and North Africa. It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks.

The plant has lance-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. Its spore-bearing structures, or sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to moist, shaded environments and its role in forest ecosystems.
