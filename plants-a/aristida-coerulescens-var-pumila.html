Aristida coerulescens var. pumila

Aristida coerulescens var. pumila, commonly known as bluegrass dropseed, is a low-growing, clump-forming grass native to North America.

It features fine, blue-green foliage that turns a striking coppery hue in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for downy feathers, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for drought-tolerant landscapes.

Its compact size and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
