Antirrhinum pinifolium

Antirrhinum pinifolium, commonly known as the pineleaf snapdragon, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Antirrhinum pinifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
