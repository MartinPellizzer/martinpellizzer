Angraecum eburneum var. virens

Angraecum eburneum var. virens, commonly known as the green angraecum, is a striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescence and vibrant greenish-yellow flowers with prominent white markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by moths, a feature common in the Angraecum genus.

The variety virens is distinguished by its more intensely green coloration compared to other variants of the species.
