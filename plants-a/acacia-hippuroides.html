Acacia hippuroides

Acacia hippuroides, commonly known as the honeywood tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feathery leaflets.

Acacia hippuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
