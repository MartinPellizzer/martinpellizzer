Adenia catharinae

Adenia catharinae, commonly known as the Karijini Adenia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often a deep red or orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Adenia catharinae is also notable for its unique fruit, which is a fleshy capsule containing several seeds.
