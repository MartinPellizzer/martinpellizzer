Aster leiophyllus var. lanceolatus

Aster leiophyllus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that blooms from late summer into early fall.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
