Arundinaria violascens

Arundinaria violascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria violascens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
