Abronia rotundifolia

Abronia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
