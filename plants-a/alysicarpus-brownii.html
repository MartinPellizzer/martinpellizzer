Alysicarpus brownii

Alysicarpus brownii, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
