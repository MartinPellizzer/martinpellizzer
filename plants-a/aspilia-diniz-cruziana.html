Aspilia diniz-cruziana

Aspilia diniz-cruziana, commonly known as the Diniz-Cruziana aster, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It is often found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Aspilia diniz-cruziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
