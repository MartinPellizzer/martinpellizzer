Aster armoricanus

Aster armoricanus, commonly known as the American aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Aster armoricanus plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during the fall season.
