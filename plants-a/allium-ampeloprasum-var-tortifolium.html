Allium ampeloprasum var. tortifolium

Allium ampeloprasum var. tortifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features long, strap-like leaves that are broad and tapering, typically ranging from 30 to 60 centimeters in length. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of white or pale purple flowers in the summer months. Its bulbous stem is often used as a vegetable, similar to leeks and onions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
