Artanthe brunoniana

Artanthe brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale purple to white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring and early summer.

Artanthe brunoniana prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
