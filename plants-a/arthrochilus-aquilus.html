Arthrochilus aquilus

Arthrochilus aquilus, commonly known as the Grey Sun Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between June and September.

Arthrochilus aquilus is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
