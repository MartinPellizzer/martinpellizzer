Alomia cordata

Alomia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf alomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Alomia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
