Acacia pallidiramosa

Acacia pallidiramosa, commonly known as the white-branch acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pale-green branches and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia pallidiramosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
