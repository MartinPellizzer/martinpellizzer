Aplectrum hyemale f. pallidum

Aplectrum hyemale f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale adder's tongue, is a unique orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flower that resembles a snake's head, hence its common name. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces a single, showy flower with a yellowish-green coloration. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating.

This orchid is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, typically in woodlands and along stream banks.
