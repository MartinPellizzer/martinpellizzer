Acacia pulcherrima

Acacia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and its striking, fiery red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its seeds are encased in a woody pod and are dispersed by animals.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
