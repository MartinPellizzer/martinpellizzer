Aa mandonii

Aa mandonii, commonly known as the Mauna Loa dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and is adapted to the humid climate of the Hawaiian Islands. Aa mandonii plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for native pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
