Aridaria celans

Aridaria celans, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom during brief periods of rainfall. The plant has adapted to extreme drought conditions through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. Its flowers often display vibrant colors, attracting specific pollinators like moths and bees.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Aridaria celans is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
