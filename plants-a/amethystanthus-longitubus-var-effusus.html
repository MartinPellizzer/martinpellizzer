Amethystanthus longitubus var. effusus

Amethystanthus longitubus var. effusus, commonly known as the long-tubed amethyst flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, particularly hummingbirds and bees.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
