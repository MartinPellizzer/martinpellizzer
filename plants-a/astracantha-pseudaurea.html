Astracantha pseudaurea

Astracantha pseudaurea, commonly known as the false golden star, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to southern Africa. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disk florets surrounded by white to pale purple ray florets.
