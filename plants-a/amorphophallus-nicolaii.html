Amorphophallus nicolaii

Amorphophallus nicolaii, commonly known as the giant red veined arrowhead, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins and its impressive, spadix-like inflorescence. It thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
