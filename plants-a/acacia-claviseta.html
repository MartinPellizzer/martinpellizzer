Acacia claviseta

Acacia claviseta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, yellow flowers arranged in spherical clusters, typical of the Acacia genus. The plant has thin, linear leaves that are often mistaken for stems, a characteristic feature of many wattles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
