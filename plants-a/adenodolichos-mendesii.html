Adenodolichos mendesii

Adenodolichos mendesii, commonly known as the Mediterranean horsegram, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or arid soils. It has been traditionally used for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
