Acacia centrota

Acacia centrota, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions. Its seeds are encased in a woody pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Acacia centrota plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
