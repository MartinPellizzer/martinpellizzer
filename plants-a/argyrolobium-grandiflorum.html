Argyrolobium grandiflorum

Argyrolobium grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not commonly grown outside its native range.
