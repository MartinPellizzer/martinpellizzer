Arundinaria ramosa var. viridiflora

Arundinaria ramosa var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Arundinaria.

It is characterized by its slender, greenish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white flowers, which are distinct from the more common yellow or brown blooms of other bamboo species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
