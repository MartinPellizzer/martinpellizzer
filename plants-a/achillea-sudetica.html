Achillea sudetica

Achillea sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pinkish in color. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, rocky soils, and open woodlands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Achillea sudetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
