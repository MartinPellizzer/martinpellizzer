Arenaria bomiensis

Arenaria bomiensis, commonly known as the Bomien sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid environments.
