Atylus tripartitus

Atylus tripartitus, commonly known as the three-parted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Atylus tripartitus is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
