Anthericum scabrellum

Anthericum scabrellum, commonly known as the rough anthericum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name.

Anthericum scabrellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
