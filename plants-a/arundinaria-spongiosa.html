Arundinaria spongiosa

Arundinaria spongiosa, commonly known as the sponge bamboo, is a unique grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems that are segmented and covered in a spongy, fibrous material. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and bottomlands. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria spongiosa is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
