Abutilon bedfordianum subsp. concolor

Abutilon bedfordianum subsp.

concolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its fibrous stems and broad leaves contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
