Apium anisoides

Apium anisoides, commonly known as Persian cumin or aniseed cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds are often used as a spice and have a distinct anise-like aroma.

The leaves are finely divided and resemble those of other members of its family.
