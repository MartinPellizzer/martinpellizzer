Aspidium tacticopterum

Aspidium tacticopterum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Aspidium tacticopterum is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
