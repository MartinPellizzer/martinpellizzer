Acmadenia gracilis

Acmadenia gracilis, commonly known as the slender acmadenia, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in heathlands and open forests. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Acmadenia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
