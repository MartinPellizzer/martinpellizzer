Agropyron laevissimum

Agropyron laevissimum, commonly known as the smooth wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of northern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and along roadsides. Agropyron laevissimum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its potential in forage and erosion control due to its dense growth habit.
