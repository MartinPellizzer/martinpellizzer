Agathosma lycopodioides

Agathosma lycopodioides, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stem and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Agathosma lycopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
