Achyrocline disjuncta

Achyrocline disjuncta, commonly known as the Florida aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
