Asplenium laserpitiifolium var. morrisonense

Asplenium laserpitiifolium var. morrisonense, commonly known as the Morrison's maidenhair fern, is a rare fern species native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
