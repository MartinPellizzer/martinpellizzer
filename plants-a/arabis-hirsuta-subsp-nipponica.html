Arabis hirsuta subsp. nipponica

Arabis hirsuta subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as Japanese hairy rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
