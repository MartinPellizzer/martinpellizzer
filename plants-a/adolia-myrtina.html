Adolia myrtina

Adolia myrtina, commonly known as the red-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments.

Adolia myrtina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
