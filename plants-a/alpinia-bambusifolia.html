Alpinia bambusifolia

Alpinia bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem that resembles bamboo, with dense clusters of narrow, arching leaves. The plant produces striking, orange-red spadices that are both ornamental and attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Alpinia bambusifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage structure.
