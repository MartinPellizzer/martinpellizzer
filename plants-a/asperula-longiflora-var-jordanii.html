Asperula longiflora var. jordanii

Asperula longiflora var. jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oval, dark green leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
