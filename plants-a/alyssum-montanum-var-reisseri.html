Alyssum montanum var. reisseri

Alyssum montanum var. reisseri, commonly known as the Sierra Valley alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
