Abutilon bedfordianum var. grandiflorum

Abutilon bedfordianum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
