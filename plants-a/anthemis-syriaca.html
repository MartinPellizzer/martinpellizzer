Anthemis syriaca

Anthemis syriaca, commonly known as Syrian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and linear, aromatic leaves that emit a subtle chamomile scent. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central dark brown disk, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Anthemis syriaca is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
