Artanthe tenuicuspis

Artanthe tenuicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-cusped artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. Its leaves are often narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Artanthe tenuicuspis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
