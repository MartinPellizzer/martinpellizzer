Alyxia euonymifolia

Alyxia euonymifolia, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, resembling those of the genus Euonymus. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring opposite leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Alyxia euonymifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
