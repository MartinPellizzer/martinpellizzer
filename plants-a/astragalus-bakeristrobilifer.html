Astragalus bakeristrobilifer

Astragalus bakeristrobilifer, commonly known as the Baker's burro bean, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a type of legume pod that contains several small, hard seeds.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
