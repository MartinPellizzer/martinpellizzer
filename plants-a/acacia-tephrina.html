Acacia tephrina

Acacia tephrina, commonly known as the stinkwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. Acacia tephrina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
