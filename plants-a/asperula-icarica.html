Asperula icarica

Asperula icarica, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Asperula icarica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
