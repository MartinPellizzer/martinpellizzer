Argyreia lineariloba

Argyreia lineariloba, commonly known as the velvet tangle or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
