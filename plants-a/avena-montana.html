Avena montana

Avena montana, commonly known as mountain oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Avena montana thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
