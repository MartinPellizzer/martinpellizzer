Arabis gredensis

Arabis gredensis, commonly known as the Gredens rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arabis gredensis thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.
