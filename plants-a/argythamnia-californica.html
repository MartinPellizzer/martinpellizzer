Argythamnia californica

Argythamnia californica, commonly known as the California fiddleneck, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and foothills. It produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it an important component of local ecosystems.
