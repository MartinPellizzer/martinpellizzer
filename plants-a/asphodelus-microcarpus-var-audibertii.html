Asphodelus microcarpus var. audibertii

Asphodelus microcarpus var. audibertii, commonly known as the dwarf asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xerophytic gardens.
