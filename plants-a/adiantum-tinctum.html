Adiantum tinctum

Adiantum tinctum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and India. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with finely divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance.

Adiantum tinctum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
