Acacia pellita

Acacia pellita, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green phyllodes that resemble leaves but are actually modified stems. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Acacia pellita thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

It is a hardy species that is drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal or inland gardens.
