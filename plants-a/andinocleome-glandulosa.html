Andinocleome glandulosa

Andinocleome glandulosa, commonly known as the Andean cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Andinocleome glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
