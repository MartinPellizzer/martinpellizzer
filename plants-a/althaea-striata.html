Althaea striata

Althaea striata, commonly known as the striped mallows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale green veins. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Althaea striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its mucilage-rich roots.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
