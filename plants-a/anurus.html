Anurus

Anurus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It has broad, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing shade over water bodies. The plant features showy white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its stems support the floating foliage.

Anurus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
