Adenia ovata

Adenia ovata, commonly known as the African mountain almond, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Adenia ovata thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
