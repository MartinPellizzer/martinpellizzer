Andropogon luembensis

Andropogon luembensis, commonly known as the Central African bluestem, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Andropogon luembensis is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as forage for wildlife.
