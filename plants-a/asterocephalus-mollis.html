Asterocephalus mollis

Asterocephalus mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asterocephalus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
