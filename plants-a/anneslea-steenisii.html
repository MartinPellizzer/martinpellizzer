Anneslea steenisii

Anneslea steenisii, commonly known as the Sunda anneslea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
