Alchemilla tzvelevii

Alchemilla tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla tzvelevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
