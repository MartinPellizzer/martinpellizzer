Alstroemeria salsilla

Alstroemeria salsilla, commonly known as the Salsilla lily, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of grasses or sedges.

Alstroemeria salsilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
