Anthurium libanoense

Anthurium libanoense, commonly known as the Lebanon anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Lebanon.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that can range in color from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the true flowers, often in shades of red or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens worldwide.
