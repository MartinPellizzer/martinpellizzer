Acalypha soratensis

Acalypha soratensis, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its small, inconspicuous true flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Acalypha soratensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
