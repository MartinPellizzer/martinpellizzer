Astragalus owirensis

Astragalus owirensis, commonly known as the Owens Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open grasslands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
