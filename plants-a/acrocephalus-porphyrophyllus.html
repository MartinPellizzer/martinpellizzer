Acrocephalus porphyrophyllus

Acrocephalus porphyrophyllus, commonly known as the purple-leafed marsh warbler, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. Its leaves are typically broad and thick, adapted to retain moisture in waterlogged soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique coloration and ecological niche.
