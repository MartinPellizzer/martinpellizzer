Andropogon barbinodis

Andropogon barbinodis, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, blue-green florets.

Andropogon barbinodis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
