Allium pleianthum var. particolor

Allium pleianthum var. particolor, commonly known as the Purple Stripe Onion, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
