Anacampseros pectinata

Anacampseros pectinata, commonly known as the woolly cushion plant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle floral display. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal.

Anacampseros pectinata thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
