Acacia congesta subsp. cliftoniana

Acacia congesta subsp.

cliftoniana, commonly known as the Clifton's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has small, oblong phyllodes that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
