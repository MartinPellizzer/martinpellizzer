Allium pycnotrichum

Allium pycnotrichum, commonly known as the purple garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and spherical bulb. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically grow in a basal rosette.

Allium pycnotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and medicinal properties.
