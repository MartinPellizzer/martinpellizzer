Arundarbor orientalis

Arundarbor orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Skyroot, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach heights of up to 10 feet, adorned with clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rocky gardens.

Its unique appearance and resilience have made it a popular subject in both botanical studies and horticultural practices.
