Aspidium giganteum

Aspidium giganteum, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a large, aquatic fern native to tropical regions.

It has long, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, which help stabilize the sediment.

The fronds are capable of floating on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis and oxygen production.
