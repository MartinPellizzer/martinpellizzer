Amphorella

Amphorella, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescences, which resemble a bottlebrush due to their dense, cylindrical clusters of small, red or pink flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Amphorella species are typically shrubs or small trees, native to Australia and some parts of Southeast Asia.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
