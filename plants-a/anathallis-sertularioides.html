Anathallis sertularioides

Anathallis sertularioides, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that resembles a spider, aiding in pollination by specific insects.

Anathallis sertularioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
