Agathosma decurrens

Agathosma decurrens, commonly known as wild fennel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves is valued for its strong, camphor-like scent and various commercial applications.
