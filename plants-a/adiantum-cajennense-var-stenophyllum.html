Adiantum cajennense var. stenophyllum

Adiantum cajennense var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, with a pale green to silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year of growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
