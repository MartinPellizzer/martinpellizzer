Acacia gunnii var. angustifolia

Acacia gunnii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
