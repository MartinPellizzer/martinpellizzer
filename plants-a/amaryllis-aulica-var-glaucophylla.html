Amaryllis aulica var. glaucophylla

Amaryllis aulica var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the Glaucophylla Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 3 feet in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens or as a potted plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for various climates.
