Atriplex crassipes var. inappendiculata

Atriplex crassipes var. inappendiculata, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments.

It features succulent, gray-green leaves with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America and is often found in salt marshes and coastal areas.

Its robust root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh conditions.
