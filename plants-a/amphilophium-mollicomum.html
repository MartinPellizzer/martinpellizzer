Amphilophium mollicomum

Amphilophium mollicomum, commonly known as the soft-leaved philophium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Amphilophium mollicomum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
