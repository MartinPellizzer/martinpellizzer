Abutilon mexicanum

Abutilon mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican Abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Abutilon mexicanum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
