Atopoglossum

Atopoglossum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, often silver-tomentose leaves and showy, usually violet or blue flowers. The plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. They are native to regions in Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

Some species within the genus are used in traditional medicine, though many contain toxic compounds.
