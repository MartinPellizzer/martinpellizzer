Agialid ferox

Agialid ferox, commonly known as the fierce marsh fern, is a unique plant species found in wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Agaricaceae and is characterized by its robust, fibrous rhizomes that help it thrive in waterlogged soils. The plant produces large, broad leaves with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. Its reproductive structures, known as spore-bearing cones, are typically found at the base of the plant.

Agialid ferox plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
