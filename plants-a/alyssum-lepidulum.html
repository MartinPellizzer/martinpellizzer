Alyssum lepidulum

Alyssum lepidulum, commonly known as silverleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping.

Alyssum lepidulum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
