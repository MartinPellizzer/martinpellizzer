Anthemis tinctoria subsp. australis

Anthemis tinctoria subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory qualities.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and flowering characteristics compared to the nominate form.
