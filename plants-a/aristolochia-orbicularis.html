Aristolochia orbicularis

Aristolochia orbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, sweet odor. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. Its vines can grow up to 10 feet long, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
