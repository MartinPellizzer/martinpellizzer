Astragalus hypoglottis var. purpureus

Astragalus hypoglottis var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-horned milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Astragalus hypoglottis var. purpureus is known for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
