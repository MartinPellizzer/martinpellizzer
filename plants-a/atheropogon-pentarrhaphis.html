Atheropogon pentarrhaphis

Atheropogon pentarrhaphis, commonly known as the clustered grass or narrowleaf clustered grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
