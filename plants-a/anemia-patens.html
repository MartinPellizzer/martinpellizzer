Anemia patens

Anemia patens, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, graceful appearance. The leaves are broad, ovate, and turn yellow in the autumn before falling. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and ponds.

The plant has a shallow root system and is relatively fast-growing, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
