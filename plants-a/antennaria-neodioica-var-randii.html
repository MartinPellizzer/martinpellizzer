Antennaria neodioica var. randii

Antennaria neodioica var. randii, commonly known as the Sierra bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Compositae (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves. It produces both male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature known as monoecious reproduction.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
