Acanthopanax ricinifolius var. maximowiczi

Acanthopanax ricinifolius var. maximowiczi, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap or the five-leaf aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of five leaflets, which are opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, followed by red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique fruiting display.
