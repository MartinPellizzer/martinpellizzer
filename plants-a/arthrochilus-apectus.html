Arthrochilus apectus

Arthrochilus apectus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinctive midrib, while its flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

Arthrochilus apectus is a relatively rare species, making it a notable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
