Aconitum isidzukae

Aconitum isidzukae, commonly known as the Isidzuka aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Aconitum isidzukae contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.
