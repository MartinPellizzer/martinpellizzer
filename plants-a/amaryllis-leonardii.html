Amaryllis leonardii

Amaryllis leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the summer months. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

Amaryllis leonardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
