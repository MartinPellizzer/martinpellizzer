Artemisia borealis var. bargusinensis

Artemisia borealis var. bargusinensis, commonly known as the Bargusin tamarisk, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Its essential oils have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
