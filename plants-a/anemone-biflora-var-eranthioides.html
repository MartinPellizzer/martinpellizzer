Anemone biflora var. eranthioides

Anemone biflora var. eranthioides, commonly known as the eranthoides anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are basal and often covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for early pollinators.
