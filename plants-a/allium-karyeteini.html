Allium karyeteini

Allium karyeteini, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its aromatic bulbs, which are used in traditional cooking and herbal remedies.

Allium karyeteini is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.
