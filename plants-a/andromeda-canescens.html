Andromeda canescens

Andromeda canescens, commonly known as the coastal rhododendron, is a species native to the western coast of North America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salty air and wind-swept conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, are typically pink to purple in color.

Andromeda canescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
