Acacia tumida

Acacia tumida, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, pale green foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Its thick, waxy leaves help it conserve water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Acacia tumida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
