Armania

Armania, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy blue or purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Armania is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
