Antennaria parlinii subsp. fallax

Antennaria parlinii subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the fallax sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically linear to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
