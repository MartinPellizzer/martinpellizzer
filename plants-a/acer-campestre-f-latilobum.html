Acer campestre f. latilobum

Acer campestre f. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed hedge maple, is a cultivar of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lobed leaves that are often a striking green in the summer and turn vibrant red or orange in the autumn. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the winter months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
