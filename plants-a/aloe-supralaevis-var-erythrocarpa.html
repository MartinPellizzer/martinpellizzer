Aloe supralaevis var. erythrocarpa

Aloe supralaevis var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red fruit aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its unique feature is the bright red or orange fruits that develop from the flowers, which are edible and used in traditional medicine.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive in dry conditions.
