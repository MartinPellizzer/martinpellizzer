Anthemis susiana

Anthemis susiana, commonly known as the Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the genus Anthemis. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
