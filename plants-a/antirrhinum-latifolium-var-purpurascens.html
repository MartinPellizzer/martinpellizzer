Antirrhinum latifolium var. purpurascens

Antirrhinum latifolium var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of bright purple or reddish-pink tubular flowers. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It typically blooms in late spring to early summer and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
