Audibertia nivea

Audibertia nivea, commonly known as the white audibertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has trifoliate leaves with a soft, hairy texture. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Audibertia nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
