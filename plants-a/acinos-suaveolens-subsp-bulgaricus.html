Acinos suaveolens subsp. bulgaricus

Acinos suaveolens subsp.

bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian mountain everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
