Acer mono f. marmoratum

Acer mono f. marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled maple, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a serrated margin and a vibrant mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The variegation is most pronounced in the leaves, which display a marbled pattern of colors throughout the growing season. It is a compact tree or shrub, often reaching heights of 10 to 15 feet, making it suitable for smaller gardens.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, adding a colorful focal point to any landscape.
