Achyroseris albicaulis

Achyroseris albicaulis, commonly known as the white-rayed desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage. Its white petals and yellow center create a striking contrast, making it visually distinctive.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.
