Aristolochia nauseifolia

Aristolochia nauseifolia, commonly known as the stinking vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate, and often aromatic leaves, which can vary in shape and size depending on the environment. The plant produces distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, with a strong, unpleasant odor. Its roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with caution.
