Amomum mala

Amomum mala, commonly known as the small cardamom or lesser cardamom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The rhizomes of Amomum mala are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
