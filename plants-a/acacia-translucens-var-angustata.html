Acacia translucens var. angustata

Acacia translucens var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
