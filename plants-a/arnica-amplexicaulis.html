Arnica amplexicaulis

Arnica amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. Arnica amplexicaulis thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
