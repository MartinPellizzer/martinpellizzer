Adenophora stricta

Adenophora stricta, commonly known as the rigid bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. Adenophora stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
