Acacia macracantha var. glabrescens

Acacia macracantha var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which give it a silky appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.
