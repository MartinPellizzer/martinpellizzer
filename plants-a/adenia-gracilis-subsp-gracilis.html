Adenia gracilis subsp. gracilis

Adenia gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender adenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or wetland habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
