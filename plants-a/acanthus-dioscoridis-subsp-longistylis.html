Acanthus dioscoridis subsp. longistylis

Acanthus dioscoridis subsp.

longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, violet-blue bracts that enclose its small flowers.

Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated floral stamens, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.
