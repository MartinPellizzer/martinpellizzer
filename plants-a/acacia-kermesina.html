Acacia kermesina

Acacia kermesina, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia kermesina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
