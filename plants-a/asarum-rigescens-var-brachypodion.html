Asarum rigescens var. brachypodion

Asarum rigescens var. brachypodion, commonly known as the short-rooted wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are typically concealed within a hooded structure. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
