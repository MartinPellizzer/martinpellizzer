Aster mespilifolius

Aster mespilifolius, commonly known as the sweetwood aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow with purple centers, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster mespilifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
