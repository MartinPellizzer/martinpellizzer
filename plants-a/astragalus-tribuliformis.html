Astragalus tribuliformis

Astragalus tribuliformis, commonly known as the hooked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus tribuliformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
