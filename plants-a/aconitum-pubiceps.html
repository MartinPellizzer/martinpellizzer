Aconitum pubiceps

Aconitum pubiceps, commonly known as the hairy helmet flower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive helmet-shaped upper petal and a spurred lower petal. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed leaflets.

Aconitum pubiceps is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments.
