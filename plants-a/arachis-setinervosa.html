Arachis setinervosa

Arachis setinervosa, commonly known as the hairy peanutgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural interest, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
