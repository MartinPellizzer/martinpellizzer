Aeluropus littoralis subsp. pungens

Aeluropus littoralis subsp.

pungens, commonly known as the coastal arrowgrass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and coastal environments. It typically grows in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines, estuaries, and salt marshes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is well-suited to withstand high salinity and periodic tidal flooding.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
