Agrimonia odorata var. sororia

Agrimonia odorata var. sororia, commonly known as sweet agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by red, fuzzy seed heads. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and serrated, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.
