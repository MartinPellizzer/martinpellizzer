Artemisia cuneata

Artemisia cuneata, commonly known as narrowleaf wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Artemisia cuneata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
