Aspidopterys hutchinsonii

Aspidopterys hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Chilean tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to southern Chile.

It is a large, evergreen tree fern that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, featuring a robust trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive, fibrous trunk and produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
