Aquilegia glandulosa var. bicolor

Aquilegia glandulosa var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored columbine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple with a contrasting white or yellow base. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are hairy and often glandular. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
