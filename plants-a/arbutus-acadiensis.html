Arbutus acadiensis

Arbutus acadiensis, commonly known as the Florida sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a dark purple berry that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Arbutus acadiensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and coastal areas.
