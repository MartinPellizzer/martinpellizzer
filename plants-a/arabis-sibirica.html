Arabis sibirica

Arabis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10-20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Arabis sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early spring blooms.
