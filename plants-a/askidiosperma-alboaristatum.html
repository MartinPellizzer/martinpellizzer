Askidiosperma alboaristatum

Askidiosperma alboaristatum, commonly known as the white-wood tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
