Asparagus leptophyllus

Asparagus leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus leptophyllus is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and delicate blooms.
