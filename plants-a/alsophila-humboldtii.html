Alsophila humboldtii

Alsophila humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Alsophila humboldtii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
