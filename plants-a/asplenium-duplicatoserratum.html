Asplenium duplicatoserratum

Asplenium duplicatoserratum, commonly known as the double-toothed spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth pattern. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive double-toothed margin, giving it its scientific name. It is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

This plant is noted for its delicate foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
