Allionia bracteosa

Allionia bracteosa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

Allionia bracteosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
