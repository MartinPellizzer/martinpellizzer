Alyssum fontqueri

Alyssum fontqueri, commonly known as the coastal alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has succulent, silver-gray foliage that is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its ability to withstand salt spray and harsh conditions.

Alyssum fontqueri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
