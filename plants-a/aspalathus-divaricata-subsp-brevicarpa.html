Aspalathus divaricata subsp. brevicarpa

Aspalathus divaricata subsp.

brevicarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited bushveld clover, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers in spring.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. Its fruit is a small, rounded capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of medicinal compounds.
