Arctotis dimorphocarpa

Arctotis dimorphocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Arctotis dimorphocarpa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
