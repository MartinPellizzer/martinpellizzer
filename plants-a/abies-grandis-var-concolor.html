Abies grandis var. concolor

Abies grandis var. concolor, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage that is typically covered in a white bloom on the underside. The tree produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The white fir plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
