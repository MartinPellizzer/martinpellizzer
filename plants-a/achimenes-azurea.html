Achimenes azurea

Achimenes azurea, commonly known as the blue bell plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for containers or garden borders. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes azurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
