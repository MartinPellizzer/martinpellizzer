Acacia hernandezii

Acacia hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican mimosa, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that resemble the structure of a fern. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a pod-like structure and are dispersed by wind.

Acacia hernandezii is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
