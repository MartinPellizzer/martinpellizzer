Argemone glauca

Argemone glauca, commonly known as blue argemone, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Argemone glauca is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
