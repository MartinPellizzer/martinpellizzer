Astragalus cesarensis

Astragalus cesarensis, commonly known as Cesarean milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Astragalus cesarensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and medicinal research.
