Abarema mataybifolia

Abarema mataybifolia, commonly known as the Mataybifolia or the Brazilian peacock flower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite and palmately lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

Abarema mataybifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
