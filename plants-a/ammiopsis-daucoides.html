Ammiopsis daucoides

Ammiopsis daucoides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.

Ammiopsis daucoides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
