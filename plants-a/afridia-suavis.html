Afridia suavis

Afridia suavis, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its unique aroma, reminiscent of honey and citrus, makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
