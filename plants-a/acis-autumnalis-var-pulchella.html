Acis autumnalis var. pulchella

Acis autumnalis var. pulchella, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
