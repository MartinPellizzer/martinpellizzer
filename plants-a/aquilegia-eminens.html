Aquilegia eminens

Aquilegia eminens, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Rocky Mountains.

It produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aquilegia eminens is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
