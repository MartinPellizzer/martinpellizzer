Astragalus pinetorum

Astragalus pinetorum, commonly known as the pine mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as pine forests and rocky slopes. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.

Astragalus pinetorum is often found in association with other xerophytic species adapted to arid conditions.
