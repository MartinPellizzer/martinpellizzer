Aronicum scorpioides var. carpaticum

Aronicum scorpioides var. carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian aronicum, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered inflorescences produce small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.
