Acer campestre f. aegaeicum

Acer campestre f. aegaeicum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a compact variety of the common maple.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically green in summer and turn vibrant red or orange in the autumn. The plant has a slow growth rate, making it ideal for small spaces or as a decorative shrub. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value comes from its seasonal color changes and compact form.
