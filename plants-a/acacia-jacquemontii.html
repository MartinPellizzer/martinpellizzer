Acacia jacquemontii

Acacia jacquemontii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow floral spikes, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia jacquemontii is also notable for its role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
