Astragalus araneosus

Astragalus araneosus, commonly known as the spider-legged milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Astragalus araneosus is part of the legume family and has adapted to thrive in arid environments.
