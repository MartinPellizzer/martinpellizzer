Astragalus wootonii var. morans

Astragalus wootonii var. morans, commonly known as Morans' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, semi-desert environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
