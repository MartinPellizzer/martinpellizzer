Anthenantia glauca

Anthenantia glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Anthenantia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators.
