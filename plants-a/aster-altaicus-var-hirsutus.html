Aster altaicus var. hirsutus

Aster altaicus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the Hirsute Tundra Aster, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
