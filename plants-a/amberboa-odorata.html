Amberboa odorata

Amberboa odorata, commonly known as the sweet-smelling shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky resin. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its aromatic qualities.

Amberboa odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
