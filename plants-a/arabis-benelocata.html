Arabis benelocata

Arabis benelocata, commonly known as the Benelux rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by fluffy seed pods.

Arabis benelocata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
