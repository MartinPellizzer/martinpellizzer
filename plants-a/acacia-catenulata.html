Acacia catenulata

Acacia catenulata, commonly known as the chain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that perform photosynthesis. Acacia catenulata thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.
