Arundinaria diversifolia

Arundinaria diversifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a distinct golden hue to its leaves. The plant has a clumping growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a bright yellow to golden coloration.

Arundinaria diversifolia is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
