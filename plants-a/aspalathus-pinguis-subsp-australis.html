Aspalathus pinguis subsp. australis

Aspalathus pinguis subsp.

australis, commonly known as the South African sweet bush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in areas with mild, maritime climates. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
