Avenula bromoides subsp. gervaisii

Avenula bromoides subsp.

gervaisii, commonly known as Gervais' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
