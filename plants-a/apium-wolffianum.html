Apium wolffianum

Apium wolffianum, commonly known as Wolff's celery, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, typical of the family.

Wolff's celery is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
