Arenaria verna var. viscidula

Arenaria verna var. viscidula, commonly known as sticky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with sticky, glandular hairs. The leaves are typically linear to lanceolate, with a bluish-green hue and a dense pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
