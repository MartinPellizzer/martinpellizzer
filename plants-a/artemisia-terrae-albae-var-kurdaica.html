Artemisia terrae-albae var. kurdaica

Artemisia terrae-albae var. kurdaica, commonly known as Kurdish mugwort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
