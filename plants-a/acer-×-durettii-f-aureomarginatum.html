Acer × durettii f. aureomarginatum

Acer × durettii f. aureomarginatum, commonly known as the golden-margin maple, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a cultivar of the genus Acer, characterized by its striking foliage with yellow edges that contrast beautifully with its green or red leaves. This plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a rounded canopy and dense branching structure. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The golden-margin maple is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and landscapes where its vibrant coloration adds visual interest.
