Astragalus hoffmeisteri var. pilosus

Astragalus hoffmeisteri var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
