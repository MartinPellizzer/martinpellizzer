Athrixia pulverulenta

Athrixia pulverulenta, commonly known as the dust lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is characterized by its pale to dark grayish-green thallus, which is covered in a fine, powdery layer of dust-like particles. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with a green alga and a fungus, which together provide nutrients through photosynthesis and water absorption. It is often found on rock surfaces and soil in regions with low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a common indicator species in dryland ecosystems.
