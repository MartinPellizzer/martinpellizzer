Aregelia marmorata

Aregelia marmorata, commonly known as the marble plant, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and purple hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Aregelia marmorata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
