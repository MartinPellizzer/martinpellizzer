Amarantellus argentinus

Amarantellus argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, often colorful bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Amarantellus argentinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
