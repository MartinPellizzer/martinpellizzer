Acalypha integrifolia var. concolor

Acalypha integrifolia var. concolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-rooted iron plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking, velvety leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
