Acacia armata f. ornithophora

Acacia armata f. ornithophora, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like stems that perform the function of leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens.
