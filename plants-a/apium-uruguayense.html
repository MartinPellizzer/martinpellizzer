Apium uruguayense

Apium uruguayense, commonly known as the Uruguay celery, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family.

Apium uruguayense is often found in moist, grassy areas and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.
