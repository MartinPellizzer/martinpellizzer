Aucuba japonica f. dentata

Aucuba japonica f. dentata, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba or toothed aucuba, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that have a distinctive serrated or toothed margin, giving it its specific epithet "dentata.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage, which remains vibrant throughout the year.
