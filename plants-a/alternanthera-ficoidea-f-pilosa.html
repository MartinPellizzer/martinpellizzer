Alternanthera ficoidea f. pilosa

Alternanthera ficoidea f. pilosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf alternanthera, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep red to purple, depending on light conditions. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

This variety is prized for its vibrant, textured leaves, making it a popular choice for container gardening and landscape design.
