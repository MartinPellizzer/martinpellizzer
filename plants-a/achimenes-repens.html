Achimenes repens

Achimenes repens, commonly known as the creeping achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground or over surfaces. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening.

Achimenes repens prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers.
