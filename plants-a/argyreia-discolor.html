Argyreia discolor

Argyreia discolor, commonly known as the holy ghost plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that appear in clusters, often surrounded by a dense layer of white bracts. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Argyreia discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
