Aphelexis incana

Aphelexis incana, commonly known as silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Aphelexis incana is also known for its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
