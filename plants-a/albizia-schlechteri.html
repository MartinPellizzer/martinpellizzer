Albizia schlechteri

Albizia schlechteri, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically green with a yellow underside, and its showy, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Albizia schlechteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
