Asplenium lamprocaulon

Asplenium lamprocaulon, commonly known as the shining wall fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds that often have a glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, arranged in a distinctive pattern.

Asplenium lamprocaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens or as a houseplant.
