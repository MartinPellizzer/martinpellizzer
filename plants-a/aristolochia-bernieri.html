Aristolochia bernieri

Aristolochia bernieri, commonly known as the Mexican pipevine, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, maroon-colored flowers that resemble pipes. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas within forests.

The genus Aristolochia is known for containing aristolochic acids, which are nephrotoxic and carcinogenic compounds.
