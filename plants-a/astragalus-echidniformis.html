Astragalus echidniformis

Astragalus echidniformis, commonly known as the echidna milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, and it produces small, cylindrical seed pods.

Astragalus echidniformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
