Astragalus stenoceroides

Astragalus stenoceroides, commonly known as the narrow-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. It produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus stenoceroides is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
