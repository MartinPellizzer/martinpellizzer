Acacia koa var. weimeae

Acacia koa var. weimeae, commonly known as the Koa tree, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow to golden flower clusters. The tree is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities, making it important in both ecological and cultural contexts.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions outside Hawaii.
