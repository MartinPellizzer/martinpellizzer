Aphelandra obtusifolia

Aphelandra obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf angel's trumpet, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Aphelandra obtusifolia is often cultivated for its striking foliage and showy blooms.
