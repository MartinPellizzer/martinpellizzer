Alchemilla procumbens var. hirta

Alchemilla procumbens var. hirta, commonly known as hairy alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a mat-forming habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and wetland restoration projects.
