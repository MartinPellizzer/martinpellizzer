Anemone blanda scythinica

Anemone blanda scythinica, commonly known as the scythinian windflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
