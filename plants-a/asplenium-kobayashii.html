Asplenium kobayashii

Asplenium kobayashii, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a feathery appearance, which contributes to its ornamental value.

Asplenium kobayashii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, indoor setting.
