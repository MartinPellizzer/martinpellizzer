Anemonospermos lanceolata

Anemonospermos lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate anemonosperma, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Anemonaceae.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures include a central flower with numerous stamens and a single pistil.

Anemonospermos lanceolata is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for early pollinators.
