Achyrospermum dasytrichum

Achyrospermum dasytrichum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and sometimes invasive in certain regions.
