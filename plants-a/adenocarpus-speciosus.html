Adenocarpus speciosus

Adenocarpus speciosus, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by inflated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Adenocarpus speciosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
