Atractylis macrocephala var. hunanensis

Atractylis macrocephala var. hunanensis, commonly known as the Hunan giant burr, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its large, hairy, spherical seed heads that can reach up to 5 cm in diameter. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This variant is valued for its ornamental seed heads and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
