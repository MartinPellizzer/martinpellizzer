Aronia ariachamaemespilus

Aronia ariachamaemespilus, commonly known as the black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, bright red berries in the fall. The plant is known for its high antioxidant content and is often cultivated for ornamental and edible purposes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The berries are typically harvested in late summer and are used in jams, juices, and health supplements.
