Acrospira lilioides

Acrospira lilioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a scape that bears multiple flowers along its length.

Acrospira lilioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
