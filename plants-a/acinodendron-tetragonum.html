Acinodendron tetragonum

Acinodendron tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Acinodendron tetragonum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
