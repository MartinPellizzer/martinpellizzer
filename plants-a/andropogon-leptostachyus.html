Andropogon leptostachyus

Andropogon leptostachyus, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, open panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon leptostachyus plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
