Asparagus aphyllus subsp. aphyllus

Asparagus aphyllus subsp.

aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless asparagus, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family. It is characterized by its absence of true leaves, with only modified stems that serve as photosynthetic structures.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is native to regions in southern Africa.
