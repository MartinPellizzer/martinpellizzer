Abarema barnebyana

Abarema barnebyana, commonly known as the Barneby's false acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its seeds are encased in pods and are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in the understory vegetation.

Abarema barnebyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
