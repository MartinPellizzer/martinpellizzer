Arnica aphanactis

Arnica aphanactis, commonly known as mountain arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Arnica aphanactis is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
