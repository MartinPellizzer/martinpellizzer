Andropogon lateralis subsp. cryptopus

Andropogon lateralis subsp.

cryptopus, commonly known as the cryptopus bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
