Arenaria carinata

Arenaria carinata, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its hairy stems and lobed leaves are adapted to withstand sandy soils and harsh weather conditions.

Arenaria carinata is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands, making it a hardy and resilient species.
