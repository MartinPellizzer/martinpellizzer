Aconitum pendulicarpum

Aconitum pendulicarpum, commonly known as the hanging fruit aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Its fruit is unique, consisting of pendulous, inflated follicles that release seeds when mature.

Aconitum pendulicarpum is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.
