Aconitum penninum

Aconitum penninum, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, deeply lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum penninum is known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
