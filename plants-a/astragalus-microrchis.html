Astragalus microrchis

Astragalus microrchis, commonly known as the small-fruit milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus microrchis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
