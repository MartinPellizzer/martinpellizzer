Acacia galioides f. hirsutiuscula

Acacia galioides f. hirsutiuscula, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, flaky bark. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

This variety is noted for its dense growth habit and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
