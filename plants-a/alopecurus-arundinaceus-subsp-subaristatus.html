Alopecurus arundinaceus subsp. subaristatus

Alopecurus arundinaceus subsp.

subaristatus, commonly known as the slender quackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
