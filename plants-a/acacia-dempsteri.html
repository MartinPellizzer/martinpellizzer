Acacia dempsteri

Acacia dempsteri, commonly known as the dwarf black wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry branches and small, dark green leaves that are alternate and pinnately compound. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. Acacia dempsteri is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is a hardy, low-growing plant that is popular in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
