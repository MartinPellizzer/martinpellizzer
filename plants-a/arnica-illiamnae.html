Arnica illiamnae

Arnica illiamnae, commonly known as the Oregon arnica, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant features hairy, basal leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disk. It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Arnica illiamnae is often found in association with other alpine and subalpine flora in its native range.
