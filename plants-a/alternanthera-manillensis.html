Alternanthera manillensis

Alternanthera manillensis, commonly known as the red-veined joyweed, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red veins that contrast with its green foliage, making it a popular choice for aquariums. The plant thrives in freshwater environments and prefers moderate to high light conditions. It has elongated, narrow leaves that can grow up to 10 centimeters in length.

Alternanthera manillensis is often used in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
