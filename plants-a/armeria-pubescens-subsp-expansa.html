Armeria pubescens subsp. expansa

Armeria pubescens subsp.

expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pinkish flowers.

The plant has silver-gray, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in its salty, windy habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in maritime climates.
