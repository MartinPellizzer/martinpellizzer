Aleuritia warshenewskiana

Aleuritia warshenewskiana, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are often pubescent beneath. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Aleuritia warshenewskiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.
