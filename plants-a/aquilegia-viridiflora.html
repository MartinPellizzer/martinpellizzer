Aquilegia viridiflora

Aquilegia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, spurred petals that range in color from blue to purple, often with a greenish tinge. The plant produces compound leaves with lobed segments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aquilegia viridiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, unique blossoms.
