Adventina ciliata

Adventina ciliata, commonly known as the threadleaf spurge, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with fine, hair-like structures along the edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Adventina ciliata is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
