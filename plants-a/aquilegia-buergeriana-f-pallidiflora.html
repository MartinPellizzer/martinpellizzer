Aquilegia buergeriana f. pallidiflora

Aquilegia buergeriana f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered monkshood, is a variant of the monkshood plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. The plant features compound leaves with deeply lobed foliage, and its flowers are arranged in a spire-like cluster. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
