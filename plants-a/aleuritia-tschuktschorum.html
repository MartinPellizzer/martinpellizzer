Aleuritia tschuktschorum

Aleuritia tschuktschorum, commonly known as the Tschuktschorum elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are edible.

Aleuritia tschuktschorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
