Antirrhinum jamaicense

Antirrhinum jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Jamaica.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Antirrhinum jamaicense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
