Acmella parvifolia

Acmella parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered triggerplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its foliage is alternate, with lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture.
