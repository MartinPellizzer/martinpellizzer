Allocasuarina paludosa

Allocasuarina paludosa, commonly known as the swamp she-oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage that forms dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant thrives in wet, swampy or seasonally waterlogged soils, often found in coastal and estuarine areas. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Allocasuarina paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
