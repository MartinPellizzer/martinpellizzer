Achillea millefolium var. borealis

Achillea millefolium var. borealis, commonly known as Arctic yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh climates with short growing seasons.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in extreme environments.
