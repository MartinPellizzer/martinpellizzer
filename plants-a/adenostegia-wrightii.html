Adenostegia wrightii

Adenostegia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's adenostegia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in native gardens.
