Acer decompositum

Acer decompositum, commonly known as the eastern red cedar, is a member of the maple family (Aceraceae).

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is native to eastern North America and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
