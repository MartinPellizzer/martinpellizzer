Aristolochia pohliana

Aristolochia pohliana, commonly known as the Pohli's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are opposite and have a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex floral structures and some species contain toxic compounds.
