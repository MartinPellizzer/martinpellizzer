Achillea dentifera

Achillea dentifera, commonly known as the toothed yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, serrated leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Achillea dentifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
