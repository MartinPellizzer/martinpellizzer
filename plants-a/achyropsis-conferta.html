Achyropsis conferta

Achyropsis conferta, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Achyropsis conferta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
