Astragalus diphtherites subsp. brachyanthus

Astragalus diphtherites subsp.

brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
