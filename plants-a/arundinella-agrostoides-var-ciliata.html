Arundinella agrostoides var. ciliata

Arundinella agrostoides var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy grass or ciliate grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like structure with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, clustered flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The leaves are often covered with fine hairs, giving it its common name and contributing to its ecological adaptability.
