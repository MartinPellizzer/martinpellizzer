Aster undulatus var. abruptifolius

Aster undulatus var. abruptifolius, commonly known as the wavy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple to blue petals and yellow centers, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, wavy-edged leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
