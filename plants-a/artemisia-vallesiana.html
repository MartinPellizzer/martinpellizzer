Artemisia vallesiana

Artemisia vallesiana, commonly known as blue curl, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, blue-gray flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as cliffs and canyons. Its leaves are narrow, silvery-blue, and have a strong, pungent scent when crushed.

Artemisia vallesiana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as a ornamental plant for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
