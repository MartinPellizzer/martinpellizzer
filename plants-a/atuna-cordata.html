Atuna cordata

Atuna cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking white spadix surrounded by a green spathe. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing black seeds. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy, edible roots.

Atuna cordata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
