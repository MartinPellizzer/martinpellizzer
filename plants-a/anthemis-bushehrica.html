Anthemis bushehrica

Anthemis bushehrica, commonly known as the Bushehric tansy, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Anthemis bushehrica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
