Agathosma muizenbergensis var. minor

Agathosma muizenbergensis var. minor, commonly known as African sage, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is a valued plant in both horticulture and herbal practices.
