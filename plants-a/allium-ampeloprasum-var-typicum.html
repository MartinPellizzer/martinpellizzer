Allium ampeloprasum var. typicum

Allium ampeloprasum var. typicum, commonly known as the purple onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and clusters of spherical, purple or pinkish bulbs. The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while the flowers form a loose umbel with white or pale pink petals. This variety is valued for its edible bulbs, which have a mild, sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for both culinary use and ornamental purposes in gardens.
