Athyrium accedens

Athyrium accedens, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a slender, wiry stem that supports the intricate structure of the fronds.

Athyrium accedens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
