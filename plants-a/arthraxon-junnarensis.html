Arthraxon junnarensis

Arthraxon junnarensis, commonly known as the Junnare grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Arthraxon junnarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
