Acetosella florida

Acetosella florida, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in early spring, which bloom in a bell-shaped pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
