Acnistus mollis

Acnistus mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Acnistus mollis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
