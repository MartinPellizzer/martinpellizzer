Alchemilla hybrida subsp. glaucescens

Alchemilla hybrida subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicle-like clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Alchemilla hybrida subsp.

glaucescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
