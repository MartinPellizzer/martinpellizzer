Acanthopanax giraldii var. hispidus

Acanthopanax giraldii var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy angel's trumpet, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, hairy glands. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
