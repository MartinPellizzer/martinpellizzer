Argyrolobium linnaeanum subsp. fallax

Argyrolobium linnaeanum subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features hairy, glandular stems and alternate, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
