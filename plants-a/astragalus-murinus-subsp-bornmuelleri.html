Astragalus murinus subsp. bornmuelleri

Astragalus murinus subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the coastal milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the coastal regions of western North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats, thriving in salty and well-drained environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and providing nectar for pollinators.
