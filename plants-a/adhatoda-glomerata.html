Adhatoda glomerata

Adhatoda glomerata, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale green or white, and they bloom during the summer months.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
