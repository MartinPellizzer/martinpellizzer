Anemone baicalensis subsp. flaccida

Anemone baicalensis subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native environment.
