Allomorphia parvifolia

Allomorphia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf allomorph, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
