Acaena pinnatifida var. glabricupula

Acaena pinnatifida var. glabricupula, commonly known as the smooth cupule acaena, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, spiny fruits that are characteristic of the genus Acaena. The plant has compound leaves with a pinnate structure, and its stems are often covered in short, stiff hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The smooth cupules of this variety distinguish it from other Acaena species, making it a notable botanical feature in its native habitat.
