Anemone exigua

Anemone exigua, commonly known as the dwarf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette of lance-shaped, toothed foliage.

Anemone exigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
