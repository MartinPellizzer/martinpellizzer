Aeranthes crassifolia

Aeranthes crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is often found in shaded, moist habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
