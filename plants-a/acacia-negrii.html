Acacia negrii

Acacia negrii, commonly known as the Negri's wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features narrow, phyllodes that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge, and it produces yellow globular flower heads during the cooler months. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native fauna.

Acacia negrii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
