Alsophila rebeccae

Alsophila rebeccae, commonly known as the Rebeccae tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant belongs to the family Dicksoniaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like structure that contributes to its ornamental value.

Alsophila rebeccae is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
