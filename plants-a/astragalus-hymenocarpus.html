Astragalus hymenocarpus

Astragalus hymenocarpus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Astragalus hymenocarpus is a member of the legume family and has adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
