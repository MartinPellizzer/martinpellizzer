Aristolochia foetens

Aristolochia foetens, commonly known as the stinking beggar's-tick, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, vine-like growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant, and often unpleasant-smelling flower that is typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for cultivation in gardens.
