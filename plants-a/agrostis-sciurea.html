Agrostis sciurea

Agrostis sciurea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Agrostis sciurea is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
