Apocynum cannabinum var. nemorale

Apocynum cannabinum var. nemorale, commonly known as wood lily or green milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive hooded structure.

The plant produces follicles that contain toxic, milky sap, which is characteristic of the Apocynaceae family.
