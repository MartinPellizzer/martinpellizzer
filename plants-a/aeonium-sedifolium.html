Aeonium sedifolium

Aeonium sedifolium, commonly known as the Jade Plant or Christ's Crown, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring or early summer, which emerge from the center of the rosette. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for indoor or outdoor cultivation.
