Arundinella schultzii

Arundinella schultzii, commonly known as the African reed or Schultze's reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes produce small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Arundinella schultzii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
