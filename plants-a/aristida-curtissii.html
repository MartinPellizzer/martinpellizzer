Aristida curtissii

Aristida curtissii, commonly known as the desert dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically tan to brown in color. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
