Acanthus ugandensis

Acanthus ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda spurge, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acanthus ugandensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
