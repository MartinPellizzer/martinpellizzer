Achillea virescens subsp. tenorei

Achillea virescens subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Corsican yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
