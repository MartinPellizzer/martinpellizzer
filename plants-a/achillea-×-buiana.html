Achillea × buiana

Achillea × buiana, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cross between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea ptarmica (chicory yarrow), resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Achillea × buiana is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
