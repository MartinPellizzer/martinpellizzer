Astragalus schurae

Astragalus schurae, commonly known as the Schura's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Astragalus schurae is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
