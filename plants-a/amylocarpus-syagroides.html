Amylocarpus syagroides

Amylocarpus syagroides, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in starch. The plant features a short, upright stem with broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The tubers are often used as a food source and have been traditionally cultivated by indigenous communities.
