Acacia filicioides var. texensis

Acacia filicioides var. texensis, commonly known as Texas acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fern-like, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing flattened seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
