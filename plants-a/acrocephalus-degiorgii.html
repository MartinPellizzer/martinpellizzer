Acrocephalus degiorgii

Acrocephalus degiorgii, commonly known as the Sicilian reed or Sicilian bulrush, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers. It is characterized by its robust root system, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Acrocephalus degiorgii plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic and wetland biodiversity.
