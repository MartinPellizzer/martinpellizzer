Asarca cardioglossa

Asarca cardioglossa, commonly known as the red-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking red-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

Asarca cardioglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
