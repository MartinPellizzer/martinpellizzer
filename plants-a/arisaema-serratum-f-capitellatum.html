Arisaema serratum f. capitellatum

Arisaema serratum f. capitellatum, commonly known as the clustered arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is usually green with a yellowish base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
