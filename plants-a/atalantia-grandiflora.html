Atalantia grandiflora

Atalantia grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican orange or golden glory, is a tropical plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, round, orange-colored berry that contains a single seed.

Atalantia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
