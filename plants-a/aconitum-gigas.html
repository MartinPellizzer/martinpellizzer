Aconitum gigas

Aconitum gigas, commonly known as the giant monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears distinctive helmet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are large, deeply lobed, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Aconitum gigas is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and contains toxic alkaloids, making it both beautiful and dangerous.
