Asterocephalus amoenus

Asterocephalus amoenus, commonly known as the pretty-headed aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a spherical shape, with slender, thread-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Asterocephalus amoenus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
