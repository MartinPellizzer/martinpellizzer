Aglaia stellatopilosa

Aglaia stellatopilosa, commonly known as the star-pilose aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
