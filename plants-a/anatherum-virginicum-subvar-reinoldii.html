Anatherum virginicum subvar. reinoldii

Anatherum virginicum subvar. reinoldii, commonly known as reinold's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

This subvariety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
