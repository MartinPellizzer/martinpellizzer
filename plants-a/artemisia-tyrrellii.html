Artemisia tyrrellii

Artemisia tyrrellii, commonly known as Tyrell's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
