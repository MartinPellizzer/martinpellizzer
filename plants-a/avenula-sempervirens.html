Avenula sempervirens

Avenula sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Avenula sempervirens plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
