Avenula sulcata

Avenula sulcata, commonly known as the sulcate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Avenula sulcata typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along water bodies.
