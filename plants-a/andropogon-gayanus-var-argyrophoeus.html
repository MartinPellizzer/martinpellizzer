Andropogon gayanus var. argyrophoeus

Andropogon gayanus var. argyrophoeus, commonly known as silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence on the leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Silverleaf bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
