Allantodia aspera

Allantodia aspera, commonly known as the rough allantodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
