Acer rufinerve var. albolimbatum

Acer rufinerve var. albolimbatum, commonly known as the white-veined maple, is a variant of the red-veined maple, characterized by its distinctive white veins on its leaves.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with a bright red or orange coloration during the growing season. The tree produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
