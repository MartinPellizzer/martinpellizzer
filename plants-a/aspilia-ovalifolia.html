Aspilia ovalifolia

Aspilia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved torch flower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flower heads that resemble small torches. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Aspilia ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
