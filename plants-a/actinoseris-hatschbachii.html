Actinoseris hatschbachii

Actinoseris hatschbachii, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a robust, branching stem with narrow, hairy leaves adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Actinoseris hatschbachii is well-suited to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
