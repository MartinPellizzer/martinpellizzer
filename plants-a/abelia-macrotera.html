Abelia macrotera

Abelia macrotera, commonly known as the large-flowered abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Abelia macrotera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
