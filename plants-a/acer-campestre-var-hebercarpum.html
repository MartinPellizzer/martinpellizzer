Acer campestre var. hebercarpum

Acer campestre var. hebercarpum, commonly known as the Hebercarpum maple, is a variant of the field maple, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This plant typically exhibits palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges, which are often a vibrant green during the growing season. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. The Hebercarpum maple is known for its ornamental value, particularly in autumn when its foliage displays shades of red and orange.

It is a relatively slow-growing shrub or small tree, often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
