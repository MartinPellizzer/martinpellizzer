Alectryon grandifolius

Alectryon grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf alectryon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Alectryon grandifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
