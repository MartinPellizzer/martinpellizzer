Antirrhinum thymifolium

Antirrhinum thymifolium, commonly known as the thyme-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are narrow and often have a slight aromatic scent, resembling that of thyme. This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
