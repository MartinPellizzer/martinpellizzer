Anaxeton asperum subsp. asperum

Anaxeton asperum subsp.

asperum, commonly known as the rough anaxeton, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive rough texture, contributing to its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
