Arundinella henryi

Arundinella henryi, commonly known as Henry's bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that resemble bamboo. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinella henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
