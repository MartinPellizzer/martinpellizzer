Andropogon stolzii

Andropogon stolzii, commonly known as Stolz's switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for various ecological conditions. It is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

Andropogon stolzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
