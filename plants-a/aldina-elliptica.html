Aldina elliptica

Aldina elliptica, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, hence the species name *elliptica*, and are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Aldina elliptica thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
