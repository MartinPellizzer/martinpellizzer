Asclepias similis

Asclepias similis, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common among milkweeds. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Asclepias similis serves as a vital host plant for monarch butterfly larvae, making it an important species in ecological contexts.
