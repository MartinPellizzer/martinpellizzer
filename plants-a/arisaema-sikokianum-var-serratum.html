Arisaema sikokianum var. serratum

Arisaema sikokianum var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated Japanese arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
