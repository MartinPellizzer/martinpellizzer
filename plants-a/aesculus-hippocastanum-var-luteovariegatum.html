Aesculus hippocastanum var. luteovariegatum

Aesculus hippocastanum var. luteovariegatum, commonly known as the yellow-variegated horse chestnut, is a cultivar of the horse chestnut tree characterized by its distinctive yellow and white variegation on its leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value, featuring showy panicle flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are palmately compound, with each leaflet having a serrated margin. The tree produces large, spiny capsules containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

It is typically grown as a shade tree in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
