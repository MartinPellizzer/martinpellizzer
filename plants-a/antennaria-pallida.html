Antennaria pallida

Antennaria pallida, commonly known as the pale willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, reddish stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in harsh environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Antennaria pallida is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
