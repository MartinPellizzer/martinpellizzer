Anemone davidii

Anemone davidii, commonly known as the Japanese anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Anemone davidii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
