Aconitum tauricum f. taurericum

Aconitum tauricum f. taurericum, commonly known as the Tauricacone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a tall, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
