Acacia meiantha

Acacia meiantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are narrow and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia meiantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
