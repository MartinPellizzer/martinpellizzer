Amelanchier vulgaris var. acutifolia

Amelanchier vulgaris var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval, serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The plant bears small, edible berries that ripen to a bright red or purple color in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
