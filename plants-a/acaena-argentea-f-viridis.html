Acaena argentea f. viridis

Acaena argentea f. viridis, commonly known as the green burr bush, is a variant of the silver burr bush native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces spherical, spiky fruits that are typically green in this form, distinguishing it from the more commonly known silver-furred variety. It thrives in coastal and disturbed soils, often found in rocky or sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in coastal areas.
