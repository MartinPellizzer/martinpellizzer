Arnica angustifolia var. lessingii

Arnica angustifolia var. lessingii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, linear leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
