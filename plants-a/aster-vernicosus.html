Aster vernicosus

Aster vernicosus, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments at high elevations. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Aster vernicosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
