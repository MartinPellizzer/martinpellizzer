Aster glastifolius

Aster glastifolius, commonly known as the slender aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its showy blue to purple flower heads appear in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
