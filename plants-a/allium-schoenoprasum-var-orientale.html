Allium schoenoprasum var. orientale

Allium schoenoprasum var. orientale, commonly known as Persian chives, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, pale purple or white flowers in umbels, which appear in early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
