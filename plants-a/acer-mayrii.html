Acer mayrii

Acer mayrii, commonly known as the Mayrii maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It typically grows to a height of 20 to 30 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

The Mayrii maple is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
