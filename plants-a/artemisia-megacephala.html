Artemisia megacephala

Artemisia megacephala, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to China and other parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, dry fruits called achene. Artemisia megacephala is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

It is used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
