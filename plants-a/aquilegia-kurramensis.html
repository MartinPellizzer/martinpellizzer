Aquilegia kurramensis

Aquilegia kurramensis, commonly known as the Kurram monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Aquilegia kurramensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
