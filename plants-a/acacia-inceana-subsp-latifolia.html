Acacia inceana subsp. latifolia

Acacia inceana subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are broader than those of other acacia species, and its yellow flowers that bloom in compact clusters.

The plant produces flat, woody pods containing seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a drought-tolerant nature, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
