Adenocline pauciflora var. humilis

Adenocline pauciflora var. humilis, commonly known as the low-growing adenocline, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and hairy stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine pubescence, enhancing its adaptability to arid conditions.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its resilience and ecological niche.
