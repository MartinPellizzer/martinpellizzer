Acacia flavescens

Acacia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its seed pods are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia flavescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.
