Amomum loheri

Amomum loheri, commonly known as the Chinese cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, pale green to white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Amomum loheri is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
