Astragalus chaetodon

Astragalus chaetodon, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Astragalus chaetodon is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in arid environments.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across various landscapes.
