Agave eichlamii var. interjecta

Agave eichlamii var. interjecta, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and tall, branched inflorescences that produce showy white to yellow flowers. The plant is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of tequila.

This variety is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
