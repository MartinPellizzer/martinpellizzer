Artemisia gormanii

Artemisia gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, pungent scent. It typically grows in arid and rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Artemisia gormanii has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
