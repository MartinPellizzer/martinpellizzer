Angraecopsis ochyrae

Angraecopsis ochyrae, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native habitat. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
