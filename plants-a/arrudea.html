Arrudea

Arrudea, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Arrudea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
