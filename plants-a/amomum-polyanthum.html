Amomum polyanthum

Amomum polyanthum, commonly known as the Chinese pepper plant or fragrant ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, spicy aroma.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties.
