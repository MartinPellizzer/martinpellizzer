Achasma labellosum

Achasma labellosum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, branched stem that bears numerous blossoms. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species within its ecological niche.
