Aquilegia vulgaris corniculata

Aquilegia vulgaris corniculata, commonly known as the garden crane's-bill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and purple. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces follicles that contain seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a valued addition to many landscapes.
