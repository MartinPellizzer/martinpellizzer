Atropis

Atropis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored petals and a distinctive conical center. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Atropis produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
