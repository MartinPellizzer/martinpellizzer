Anthericum bifoliatum

Anthericum bifoliatum, commonly known as the two-leaved lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in two opposite rows along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in loose clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.

Anthericum bifoliatum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
