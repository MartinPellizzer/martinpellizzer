Adenia hondala

Adenia hondala, commonly known as the Honduran adenium, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces large, pentagonal leaves and has a distinctive, swollen caudex that stores water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow with red filaments, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Adenia hondala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
