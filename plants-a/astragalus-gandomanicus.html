Astragalus gandomanicus

Astragalus gandomanicus, commonly known as the Gandoman mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus gandomanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
