Alchornea haitiensis

Alchornea haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian beautyleaf, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by unique, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some areas for its attractive foliage.
