Allium jesdianum subsp. remediorum

Allium jesdianum subsp.

remediorum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of garlic and onions.

The plant features a bulb composed of several fleshy, white to pale yellow cloves surrounded by a fibrous sheath. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically grow from the base of the plant.

The flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually pink to purple in color.
