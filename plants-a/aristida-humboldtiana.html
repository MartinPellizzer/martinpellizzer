Aristida humboldtiana

Aristida humboldtiana, commonly known as Humboldt's dropseed, is a flowering grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
