Aster tenerrimus

Aster tenerrimus, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Aster tenerrimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
