Acianthera cymbiformis

Acianthera cymbiformis, commonly known as the boat orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, boat-shaped flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinct lip that resembles a boat's hull.

Acianthera cymbiformis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
