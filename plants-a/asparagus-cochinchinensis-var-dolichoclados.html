Asparagus cochinchinensis var. dolichoclados

Asparagus cochinchinensis var. dolichoclados, commonly known as Chinese asparagus or long-stemmed asparagus, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small, oval leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique fruiting structures.
