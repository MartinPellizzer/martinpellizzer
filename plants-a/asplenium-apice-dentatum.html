Asplenium apice-dentatum

Asplenium apice-dentatum, commonly known as the toothed holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are finely toothed along the edges. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky outcrops. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, dark green leaves.

Asplenium apice-dentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
