Angelium

Angelium, commonly known as the bishop's candle, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, composite flower heads.

It features dense clusters of small, tubular florets that form a spherical or conical shape, often resembling a candle flame. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Angelium is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
