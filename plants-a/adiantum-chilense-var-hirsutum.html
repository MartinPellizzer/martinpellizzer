Adiantum chilense var. hirsutum

Adiantum chilense var. hirsutum, commonly known as the Hirsute Maidenhair Fern, is a fern species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its fronds are typically light green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
