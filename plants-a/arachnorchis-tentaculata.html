Arachnorchis tentaculata

Arachnorchis tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Australia.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, which is essential for surviving in its often dry habitat. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, thread-like structure called a tentacle. It typically blooms in spring and is found in rainforest and dry forest regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
