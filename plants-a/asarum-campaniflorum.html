Asarum campaniflorum

Asarum campaniflorum, commonly known as the bellflower asarum, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its aromatic, slightly toxic properties.

The species is valued in both ecological and medicinal contexts due to its unique floral structure and historical use in traditional medicine.
