Anthurium pseudoclavigerum

Anthurium pseudoclavigerum, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a vibrant red or orange stripe along the midrib. The plant produces small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a hallmark feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium pseudoclavigerum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
