Aristida plumosa var. berberica

Aristida plumosa var. berberica, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy seed heads that resemble small, soft plumes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
