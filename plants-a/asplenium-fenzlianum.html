Asplenium fenzlianum

Asplenium fenzlianum, commonly known as the Fenzlian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the shape of a feather, giving it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are typically green, with a slightly glossy texture, and the plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorangia.

Asplenium fenzlianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
