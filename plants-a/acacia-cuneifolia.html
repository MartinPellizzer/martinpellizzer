Acacia cuneifolia

Acacia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are grouped in spherical clusters. Acacia cuneifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
