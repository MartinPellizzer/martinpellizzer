Acridocarpus smeathmannii var. staudtii

Acridocarpus smeathmannii var. staudtii, commonly known as the Staudtii variegated hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
