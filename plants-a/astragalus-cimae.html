Astragalus cimae

Astragalus cimae, commonly known as the Cimarron milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Astragalus cimae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
