Arisaema maximowiczii subsp. tashiroi

Arisaema maximowiczii subsp.

tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the spadix, a common trait in the Araceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral structure.
