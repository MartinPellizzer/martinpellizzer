Abies larix var. pendula

Abies larix var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a unique variant of the larch tree characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance.

It is a deciduous conifer, shedding its needle-like leaves in the fall, which is unusual for conifers. The tree typically grows in cold climates and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage is a soft green, and it produces small, cone-like structures that mature into woody cones.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its visual interest.
