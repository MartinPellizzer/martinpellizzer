Agave disjuncta

Agave disjuncta, commonly known as the Desert Agave, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Agave disjuncta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.
