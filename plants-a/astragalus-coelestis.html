Astragalus coelestis

Astragalus coelestis, commonly known as blue milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, oval legumes as fruits, which contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Astragalus coelestis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
