Albizia fulva

Albizia fulva, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Albizia fulva is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
