Aquilegia vulgaris var. nemoralis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in woodland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, low-maintenance plant.
