Aster australis

Aster australis, commonly known as the New Zealand aster, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and montane regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Aster australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
