Ardisia javanica

Ardisia javanica, commonly known as the Java star or Javan evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ardisia javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
