Ancistrolobus ligustrinus

Ancistrolobus ligustrinus, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum* species, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
