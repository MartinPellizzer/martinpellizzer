Anthemis taygetea

Anthemis taygetea, commonly known as the Greek tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
