Alternanthera nudicaulis

Alternanthera nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed alternanthera, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland habitats.
