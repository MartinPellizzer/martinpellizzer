Arundo indica

Arundo indica, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as riverbanks, marshes, and irrigation ditches. Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an effective erosion control plant.

However, it can also become invasive in non-native regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
