Astragalus drusorum

Astragalus drusorum, commonly known as the Drusy Milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus drusorum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
