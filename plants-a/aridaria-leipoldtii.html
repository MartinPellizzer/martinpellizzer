Aridaria leipoldtii

Aridaria leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's aridaria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapted to survive in low-water environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or yellow, which helps it blend into its surroundings. Its leaves are often small and fleshy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Aridaria leipoldtii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides shelter for small animals and insects.
