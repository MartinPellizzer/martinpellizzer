Acacia multiglandulosa

Acacia multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in small glands. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
