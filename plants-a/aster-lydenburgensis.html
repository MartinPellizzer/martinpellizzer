Aster lydenburgensis

Aster lydenburgensis, commonly known as the Lydenburg aster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually blue or purple in color.

Aster lydenburgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
