Aster graminifolius

Aster graminifolius, commonly known as the meadow aster, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its species name "graminifolius," meaning "grass-like leaves.".

It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
