Aloe krapohliana

Aloe krapohliana, commonly known as the Madagascar aloe, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, which helps it store water in its arid environment. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe krapohliana thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.
