Acacia chartacea

Acacia chartacea, commonly known as the paper acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are narrow and papery, giving the plant its common name, while the bark is smooth and often pale in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
