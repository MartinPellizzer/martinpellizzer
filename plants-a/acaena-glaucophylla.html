Acaena glaucophylla

Acaena glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf acaena, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spiny, spherical fruits. Its leaves and stems are covered in dense, stiff hairs, which provide protection against herbivores.

Acaena glaucophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.
