Agathosma cyminoides

Agathosma cyminoides, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.

Agathosma cyminoides thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.
