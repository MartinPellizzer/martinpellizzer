Aerva javanica var. angustifolia

Aerva javanica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf aerva, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
