Astragalus plumosus var. kurdomerensis

Astragalus plumosus var. kurdomerensis, commonly known as the Kurdomer milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
