Aspidium pallidivenium

Aspidium pallidivenium, commonly known as the pale-veined water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a thin, waxy layer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its leaves, a feature common among ferns.

Aspidium pallidivenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
