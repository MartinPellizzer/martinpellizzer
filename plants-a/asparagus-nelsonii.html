Asparagus nelsonii

Asparagus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces tiny, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Asparagus nelsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
