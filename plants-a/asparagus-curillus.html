Asparagus curillus

Asparagus curillus, commonly known as the golden asparagus or the evergreen asparagus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Asparagus curillus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
