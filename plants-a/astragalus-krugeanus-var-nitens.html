Astragalus krugeanus var. nitens

Astragalus krugeanus var. nitens, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round to oval seeds that are often covered in a hairy coating. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage.
