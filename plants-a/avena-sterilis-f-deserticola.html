Avena sterilis f. deserticola

Avena sterilis f. deserticola, commonly known as the desert oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It has adapted to harsh environments through deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a useful plant for erosion control.
