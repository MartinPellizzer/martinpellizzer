Anisopus efulensis

Anisopus efulensis, commonly known as the East African anisopus, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Anisopus efulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
