Astragalus virgaurea

Astragalus virgaurea, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia, including parts of Central Asia, the Middle East, and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with small leaflets arranged in pairs, and it produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus virgaurea is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
