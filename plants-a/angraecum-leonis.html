Angraecum leonis

Angraecum leonis, commonly known as the lion's orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces large, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
