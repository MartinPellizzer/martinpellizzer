Aristolochia lagesiana

Aristolochia lagesiana, commonly known as the Lagesian Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, earthy scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
