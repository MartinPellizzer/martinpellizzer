Actinocephalus polyanthus

Actinocephalus polyanthus, commonly known as the star-shaped cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-like appearance, with numerous small, fleshy spines radiating from a central stem. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
