Antinoria agrostidea var. annua

Antinoria agrostidea var. annua, commonly known as the annual grass of the genus Antinoria, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered at the base of the stem. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among grass species.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in various habitats, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
