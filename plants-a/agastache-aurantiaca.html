Agastache aurantiaca

Agastache aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of orange and red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas due to their mint-like aroma and mild medicinal properties.

Agastache aurantiaca thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
