Amaracarpus pubescens

Amaracarpus pubescens, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
