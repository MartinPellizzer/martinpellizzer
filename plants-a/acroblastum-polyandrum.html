Acroblastum polyandrum

Acroblastum polyandrum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acroblastum polyandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
