Alstonia esquirolii

Alstonia esquirolii, commonly known as the Philippine candlewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

Alstonia esquirolii is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
