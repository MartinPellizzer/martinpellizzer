Arenaria montana

Arenaria montana, commonly known as mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant has small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Arenaria montana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
