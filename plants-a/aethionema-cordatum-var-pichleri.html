Aethionema cordatum var. pichleri

Aethionema cordatum var. pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
