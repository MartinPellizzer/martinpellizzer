Adiantum dawsonii

Adiantum dawsonii, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant plant native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, lace-like fronds that turn brown and curl during dry periods, a characteristic adaptation to its woodland habitat. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to prevent leaf desiccation. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum dawsonii is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
