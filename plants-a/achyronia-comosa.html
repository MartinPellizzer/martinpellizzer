Achyronia comosa

Achyronia comosa, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its inflorescence is a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Achyronia comosa is often used in ornamental horticulture for its delicate, feathery foliage.
