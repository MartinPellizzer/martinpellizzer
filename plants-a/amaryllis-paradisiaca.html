Amaryllis paradisiaca

Amaryllis paradisiaca, commonly known as the paradise amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, white, or pink. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are often evergreen in warmer climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis paradisiaca is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
