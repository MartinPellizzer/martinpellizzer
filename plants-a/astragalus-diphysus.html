Astragalus diphysus

Astragalus diphysus, commonly known as the two-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus diphysus is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
