Anthyllis vulneraria proles alpestris

Anthyllis vulneraria proles alpestris, commonly known as the alpine viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
