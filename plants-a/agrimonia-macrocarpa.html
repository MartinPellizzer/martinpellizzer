Agrimonia macrocarpa

Agrimonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, large, red-brown seed capsules. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Agrimonia macrocarpa thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
