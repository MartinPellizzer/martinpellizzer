Argemone intermedia

Argemone intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features bright orange to red flowers with four petals, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its striking blooms.
