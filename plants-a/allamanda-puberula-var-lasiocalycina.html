Allamanda puberula var. lasiocalycina

Allamanda puberula var. lasiocalycina, commonly known as the velvet allamanda, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
