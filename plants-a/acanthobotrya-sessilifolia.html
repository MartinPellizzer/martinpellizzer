Acanthobotrya sessilifolia

Acanthobotrya sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthobotrya, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, spiny stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its unique morphology and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
