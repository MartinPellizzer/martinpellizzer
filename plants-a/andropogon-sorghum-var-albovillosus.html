Andropogon sorghum var. albovillosus

Andropogon sorghum var. albovillosus, commonly known as white-haired sorghum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
