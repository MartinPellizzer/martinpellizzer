Acaena lucida var. intermedia

Acaena lucida var. intermedia, commonly known as the slender spinulose burr, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, burr-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
