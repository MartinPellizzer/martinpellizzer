Aster purschianus

Aster purschianus, commonly known as the Pursh aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster purschianus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
