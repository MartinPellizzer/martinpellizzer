Anchusa incarnata

Anchusa incarnata, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to pink flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa incarnata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
