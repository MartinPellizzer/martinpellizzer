Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp. guarensis

Aquilegia pyrenaica subsp.

guarensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five sepals that are often adorned with red or purple markings.

The plant produces a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
