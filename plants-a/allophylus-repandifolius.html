Allophylus repandifolius

Allophylus repandifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf alder, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
