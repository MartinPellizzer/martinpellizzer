Achyronia wurmbeana

Achyronia wurmbeana, commonly known as the Wurmbea lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
