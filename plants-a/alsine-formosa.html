Alsine formosa

Alsine formosa, commonly known as the prettyweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Alsine formosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
