Astragalus villosus

Astragalus villosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus villosus is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
