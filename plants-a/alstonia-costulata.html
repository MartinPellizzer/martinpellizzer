Alstonia costulata

Alstonia costulata, commonly known as the African angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is characterized by its milky sap and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
