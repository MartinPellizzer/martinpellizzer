Astrantia major var. maxima

Astrantia major var. maxima, commonly known as the greater aster or giant asters, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a clump-forming habit with broad, dark green leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Astrantia major var. maxima is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
