Allium hymenorhizum var. hymenorhizum

Allium hymenorhizum var. hymenorhizum, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers in late summer. The plant has a distinct garlic-like aroma and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.
