Aegilops juvenalis var. aristata

Aegilops juvenalis var. aristata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
