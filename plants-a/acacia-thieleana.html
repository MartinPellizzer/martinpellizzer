Acacia thieleana

Acacia thieleana, commonly known as the African wattle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia thieleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
