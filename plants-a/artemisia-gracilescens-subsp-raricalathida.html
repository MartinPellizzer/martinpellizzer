Artemisia gracilescens subsp. raricalathida

Artemisia gracilescens subsp.

raricalathida, commonly known as the rare desert sage, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, pungent aroma.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
