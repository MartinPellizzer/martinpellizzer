Amalia perrinii

Amalia perrinii, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Amalia perrinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
