Allium tauricum

Allium tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous stem, which contains a pungent, edible bulb used in various culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Allium tauricum is valued not only for its flavor but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
