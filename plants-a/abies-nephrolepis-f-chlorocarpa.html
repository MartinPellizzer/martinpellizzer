Abies nephrolepis f. chlorocarpa

Abies nephrolepis f. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the emerald fir, is a variant of the noble fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant features needle-like leaves that are dark green and densely packed, giving it a lush appearance. It produces cones that are typically cylindrical and contain edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
