Alyssum montanum subsp. pedemontanum

Alyssum montanum subsp.

pedemontanum, commonly known as the Alpine Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats. Alyssum montanum subsp.

pedemontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
