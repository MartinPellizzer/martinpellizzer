Anna submontana

Anna submontana, commonly known as the mountain anna, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Anna submontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
