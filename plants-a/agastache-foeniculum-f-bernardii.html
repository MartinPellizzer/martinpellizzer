Agastache foeniculum f. bernardii

Agastache foeniculum f. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet to blue in color, arranged in whorls along the upper stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
