Alchemilla argyrophylla f. keniensis

Alchemilla argyrophylla f. keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan silverleaf, is a variant of the silverleaf plant native to Kenya.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its silvery-white leaves and compact growth habit. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves with a dense covering of fine, silky hairs. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes.

This form is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.
