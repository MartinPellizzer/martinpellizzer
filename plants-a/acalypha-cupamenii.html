Acalypha cupamenii

Acalypha cupamenii, commonly known as the red-leaved ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the island of Guam in the Pacific Ocean.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant during the warmer months. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
