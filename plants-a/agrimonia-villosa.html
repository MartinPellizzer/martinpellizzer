Agrimonia villosa

Agrimonia villosa, commonly known as hairy agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Agrimonia villosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
