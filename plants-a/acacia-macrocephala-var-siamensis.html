Acacia macrocephala var. siamensis

Acacia macrocephala var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden yellow inflorescences, which are clustered and attract various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in woody pods, and the plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
