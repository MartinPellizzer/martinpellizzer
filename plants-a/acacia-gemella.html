Acacia gemella

Acacia gemella, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. Acacia gemella is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for many years.
