Arum corsicum

Arum corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically purple or reddish in hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or along stream banks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
