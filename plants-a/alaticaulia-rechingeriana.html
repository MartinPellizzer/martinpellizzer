Alaticaulia rechingeriana

Alaticaulia rechingeriana, commonly known as the desert thornbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Alaticaulia rechingeriana plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing shelter for small animals.
