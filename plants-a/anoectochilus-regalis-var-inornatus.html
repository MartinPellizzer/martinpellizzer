Anoectochilus regalis var. inornatus

Anoectochilus regalis var. inornatus, commonly known as the unadorned orchid, is a rare and elegant epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in high humidity and shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are short-lived but highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for their delicate beauty.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
