Artemisia mendozana var. mendozana

Artemisia mendozana var. mendozana, commonly known as Mendoza wormwood, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its role in ecological systems.
