Atriplex microsperma var. flavescens

Atriplex microsperma var. flavescens, commonly known as yellow saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of salt-producing glands. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing degraded lands.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to harsh, dry environments.
