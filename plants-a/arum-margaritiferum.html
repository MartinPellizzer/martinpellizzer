Arum margaritiferum

Arum margaritiferum, commonly known as the pearl flower or white arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and a spatulate inflorescence that bears a single, showy white spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
