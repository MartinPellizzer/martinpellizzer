Argentina scorpionis

Argentina scorpionis, commonly known as the Argentine scorpion plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky foliage that resembles the form of a scorpion, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, displaying a drought-tolerant nature. Its leaves are usually green with a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

While it does not produce showy flowers, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
