Agropyron balearicum

Agropyron balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Agropyron balearicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
