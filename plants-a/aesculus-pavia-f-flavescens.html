Aesculus pavia f. flavescens

Aesculus pavia f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered horse chestnut, is a hybrid form of the common horse chestnut.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical white or pink blooms of the parent species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to the southeastern United States. It produces large, spiny capsules that contain glossy, chestnut-like seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
