Acanthopanax fauriei

Acanthopanax fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and thorn-like structures, which are typical of the genus Acanthopanax. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The berries it produces are red and edible, though they are typically consumed by birds rather than humans.
