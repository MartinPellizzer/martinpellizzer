Adenium dhofarense

Adenium dhofarense, commonly known as the Desert Crown, is a striking succulent plant native to the arid regions of Oman and the United Arab Emirates.

It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped trunk and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the cooler months, adding a splash of color to the desert landscape. Its unique morphology makes it well adapted to extreme drought conditions, with deep roots that can access groundwater.

Despite its hardiness, it is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and over-collection.
