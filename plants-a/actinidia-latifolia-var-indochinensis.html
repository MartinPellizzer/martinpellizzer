Actinidia latifolia var. indochinensis

Actinidia latifolia var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochinese broadleaf actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
