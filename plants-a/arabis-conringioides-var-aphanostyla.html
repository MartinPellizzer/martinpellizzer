Arabis conringioides var. aphanostyla

Arabis conringioides var. aphanostyla, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is distinguished by its very short, inconspicuous stem and compact growth habit.
