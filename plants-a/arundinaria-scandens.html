Arundinaria scandens

Arundinaria scandens, commonly known as the climbing bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that can climb and twine around other plants or structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Arundinaria scandens is native to eastern North America and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
