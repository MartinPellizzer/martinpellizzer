Axonopus laxiflorus

Axonopus laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower axonopus, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and the flowers are usually inconspicuous, with small, pale-colored blooms.

Axonopus laxiflorus is valued for its ornamental potential and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
