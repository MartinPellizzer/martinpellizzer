Agnus-castus negundo

Agnus-castus negundo, commonly known as the European privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in hedgerows and landscape design.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
