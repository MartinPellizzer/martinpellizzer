Alchemilla inconcinna

Alchemilla inconcinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, which give them a distinctive appearance.

Alchemilla inconcinna is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
