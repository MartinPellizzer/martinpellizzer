Alopecurus aequalis var. brachytrichus

Alopecurus aequalis var. brachytrichus, commonly known as the short-haired mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
