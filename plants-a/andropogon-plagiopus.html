Andropogon plagiopus

Andropogon plagiopus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses.

Andropogon plagiopus plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
