Alstroemeria ovallei

Alstroemeria ovallei, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Alstroemeria ovallei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
