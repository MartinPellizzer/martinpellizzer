Arthrocereus

Arthrocereus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive segmented stems and spiny or scaly surfaces. They typically grow in arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Arthrocereus species are often cultivated for their unique appearance and ability to bloom with vibrant flowers.

Their botanical features make them popular among cactus enthusiasts and gardeners.
