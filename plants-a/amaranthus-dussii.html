Amaranthus dussii

Amaranthus dussii, commonly known as the desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, clustered green flowers that transition to red or purple as they mature. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Amaranthus dussii is also known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
