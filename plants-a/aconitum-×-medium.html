Aconitum × medium

Aconitum × medium, commonly known as the hybrid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, pleated, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost palmate appearance.

Aconitum × medium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value, though it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
