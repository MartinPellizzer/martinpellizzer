Adiantum discretodenticulatum

Adiantum discretodenticulatum, commonly known as the narrow-toothed maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bi-directionally pinnate, with small, tooth-like projections along the leaflets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
