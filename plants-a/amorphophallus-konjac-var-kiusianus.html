Amorphophallus konjac var. kiusianus

Amorphophallus konjac var. kiusianus, commonly known as the Chinese arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces a starchy tuberous root that is widely used in traditional Asian cuisine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible properties.

Due to its rapid growth and potential invasiveness, it requires careful management in non-native regions.
