Aloysia lycioides var. paraguariensis

Aloysia lycioides var. paraguariensis, commonly known as huacatay, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and herbal applications.
