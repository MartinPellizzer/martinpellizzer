Angophora leiocarpa

Angophora leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-barked apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels away in thin sheets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy upper surface with a pale underside.

The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by edible, round, yellow berries that are a food source for various wildlife.
