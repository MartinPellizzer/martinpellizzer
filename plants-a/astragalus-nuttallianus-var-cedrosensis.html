Astragalus nuttallianus var. cedrosensis

Astragalus nuttallianus var. cedrosensis, commonly known as Cedros Island milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Channel Islands of California.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and coastal habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endangered species.
