Anacardium fruticosum

Anacardium fruticosum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy appearance, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
