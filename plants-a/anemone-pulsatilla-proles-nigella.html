Anemone pulsatilla proles nigella

Anemone pulsatilla proles nigella, commonly known as the common peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically on tall, upright stems. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its roots are fleshy and tuberous. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The common peony is also valued for its fragrance and historical use in traditional medicine.
