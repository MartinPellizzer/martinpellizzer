Acacia meisneri var. angustifolia

Acacia meisneri var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody structure and bipinnate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia meisneri var. angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
