Adesmia prostrata

Adesmia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate adesmia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its pods are typically hairy and contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Adesmia prostrata is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
