Amaranthus brownii

Amaranthus brownii, commonly known as the brown amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Amaranthus brownii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
