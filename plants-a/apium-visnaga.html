Apium visnaga

Apium visnaga, commonly known as cow parsley or wild carrot, is a biennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescences and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and sunny habitats.
