Astragalus albosetulosus

Astragalus albosetulosus, commonly known as the white-tipped milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus albosetulosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
