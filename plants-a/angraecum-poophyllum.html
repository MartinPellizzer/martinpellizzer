Angraecum poophyllum

Angraecum poophyllum, commonly known as the Christmas star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence and numerous fragrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the Christmas season. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. Its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful cultivation to ensure its survival.
