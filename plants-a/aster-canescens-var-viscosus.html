Aster canescens var. viscosus

Aster canescens var. viscosus, commonly known as the viscid aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

The viscid aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
