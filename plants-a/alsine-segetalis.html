Alsine segetalis

Alsine segetalis, commonly known as the field alsike, is an annual grass-like plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Alsine segetalis is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
