Abies gordoniana var. pumila

Abies gordoniana var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a variety of the white fir species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its cones are small and typically hang downward, a distinguishing feature of the genus Abies.

The dwarf white fir is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
