Ampelopsis davidi

Ampelopsis davidi, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Ampelopsis davidi is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
