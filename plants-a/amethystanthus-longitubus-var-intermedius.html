Amethystanthus longitubus var. intermedius

Amethystanthus longitubus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate amethyst anthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The varietal name "intermedius" reflects its intermediate characteristics between the two parent species.
