Anthericum macranthum

Anthericum macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered arum, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a tall, leafy stem topped with a spadix surrounded by a showy, purple-brown spathe. The plant's botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
