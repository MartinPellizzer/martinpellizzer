Asplenium vomeriforme

Asplenium vomeriforme, commonly known as the vomerine spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically found growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a creeping rhizome with densely packed, lanceolate, and often dark green leaves. Its fronds are usually pinnately divided, with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape.

Asplenium vomeriforme is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
