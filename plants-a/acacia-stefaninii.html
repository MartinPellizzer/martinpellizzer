Acacia stefaninii

Acacia stefaninii, commonly known as the Stefanini acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets. Acacia stefaninii is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers.

It is often used in local landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
