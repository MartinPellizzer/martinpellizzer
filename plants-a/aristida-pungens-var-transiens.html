Aristida pungens var. transiens

Aristida pungens var. transiens, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
