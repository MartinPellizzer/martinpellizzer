Aralia monogyna

Aralia monogyna, commonly known as the European spikenard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being lanceolate and serrated. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in an umbel, which are clustered in a spiky arrangement. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
