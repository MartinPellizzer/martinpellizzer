Actaea acuminata subsp. asiatica

Actaea acuminata subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
