Acacia orites

Acacia orites, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, pale green or yellowish leaves that are typically reduced to spines. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Acacia orites is well-suited to harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization.
