Alchemilla akdoganica

Alchemilla akdoganica, commonly known as Turkish lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
