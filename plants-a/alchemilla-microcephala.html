Alchemilla microcephala

Alchemilla microcephala, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are round to heart-shaped with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Alchemilla microcephala is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
