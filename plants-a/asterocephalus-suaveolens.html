Asterocephalus suaveolens

Asterocephalus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented starhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
