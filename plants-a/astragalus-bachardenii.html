Astragalus bachardenii

Astragalus bachardenii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow to greenish-yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface.

Astragalus bachardenii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
