Acer opalus subsp. hispanicum

Acer opalus subsp.

hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
