Artanthe rugosa

Artanthe rugosa, commonly known as the rough artanthe, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Artanthe rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
