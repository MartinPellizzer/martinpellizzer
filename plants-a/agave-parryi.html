Agave parryi

Agave parryi, commonly known as the Parry agave, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Agave parryi is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
