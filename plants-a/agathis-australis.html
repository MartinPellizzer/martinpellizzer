Agathis australis

Agathis australis, commonly known as the kauri tree, is a large evergreen conifer native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The tree can grow up to 50 meters in height and has a lifespan that can exceed 1,000 years. Its wood is dense, durable, and highly valued for its strength and workability.

The kauri also produces large, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
