Astragalus surchanensis

Astragalus surchanensis, commonly known as the Surchan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Astragalus surchanensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
