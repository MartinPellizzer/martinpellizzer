Antrophyum angustatum

Antrophyum angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved antrophyum, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, feathery leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cones, with the male cones appearing at the base of the plant and the female cones emerging higher up. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
