Antennaria porsildii

Antennaria porsildii, commonly known as the Porsild's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in alpine and tundra environments. It has a mat-forming growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh, cold climates.

Antennaria porsildii is adapted to long periods of snow cover and has a deep root system to access nutrients and moisture.
