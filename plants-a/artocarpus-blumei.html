Artocarpus blumei

Artocarpus blumei, commonly known as the Blume's breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, develops a rough, bumpy exterior and contains a soft, edible pulp. The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in various culinary applications, and for its timber, which is durable and versatile.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
