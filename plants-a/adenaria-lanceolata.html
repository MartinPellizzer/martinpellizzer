Adenaria lanceolata

Adenaria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf adenine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Adenaria lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
