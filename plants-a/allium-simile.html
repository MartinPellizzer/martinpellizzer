Allium simile

Allium simile, commonly known as the common leek, is a hardy perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs, which are edible, have a mild onion-like flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

Allium simile thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
