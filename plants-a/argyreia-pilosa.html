Argyreia pilosa

Argyreia pilosa, commonly known as the velvet flower or velvet bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening. Its seeds are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

However, the plant can be invasive in some areas and requires careful management.
