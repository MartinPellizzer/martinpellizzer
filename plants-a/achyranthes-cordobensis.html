Achyranthes cordobensis

Achyranthes cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba knotweed, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and can become quite extensive, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Achyranthes cordobensis is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed due to its aggressive growth.
