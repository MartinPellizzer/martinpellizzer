Aureolaria patula

Aureolaria patula, commonly known as the common sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as fields and woodland edges.

Aureolaria patula is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
