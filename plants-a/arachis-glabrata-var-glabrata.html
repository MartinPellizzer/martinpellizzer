Arachis glabrata var. glabrata

Arachis glabrata var. glabrata, commonly known as the peanut grass or wild peanut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in warm, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
