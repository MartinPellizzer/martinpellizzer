Azalea lapponica

Azalea lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in rocky or mountainous areas, where it forms part of the alpine flora.

Azalea lapponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in early spring.
