Avena flavescens subsp. pindica

Avena flavescens subsp.

pindica, commonly known as the Pindus oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fine, pale green foliage and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
