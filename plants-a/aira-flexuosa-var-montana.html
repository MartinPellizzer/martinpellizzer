Aira flexuosa var. montana

Aira flexuosa var. montana, commonly known as mountain airgrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
