Ascarina reticulata

Ascarina reticulata, commonly known as the paperbark tree, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, papery bark that peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
