Aralia debilis

Aralia debilis, commonly known as the Japanese aralia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

Aralia debilis thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to temperate climates.
