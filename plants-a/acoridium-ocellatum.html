Acoridium ocellatum

Acoridium ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant features a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Acoridium ocellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
