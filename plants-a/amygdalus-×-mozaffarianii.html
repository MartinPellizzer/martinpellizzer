Amygdalus × mozaffarianii

Amygdalus × mozaffarianii, commonly known as the Persian almond or Persian peach, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus dulcis (sweet almond) and Prunus persica (peach).

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy flowers and attractive fruit. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are usually pink or white.

The fruit is a small, fuzzy drupe that matures in late summer, adding to its horticultural appeal.
