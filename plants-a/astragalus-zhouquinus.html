Astragalus zhouquinus

Astragalus zhouquinus, commonly known as Zhou's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
