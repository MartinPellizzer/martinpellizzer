Aeonium × cilifolium

Aeonium × cilifolium, commonly known as the tree aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers, often in the spring. It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Aeonium arboreum and Aeonium haworthii.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping in arid climates.
