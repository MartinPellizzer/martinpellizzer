Achillea grandifolia subsp. hellenica

Achillea grandifolia subsp.

hellenica, commonly known as the Greek yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
