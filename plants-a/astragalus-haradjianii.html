Astragalus haradjianii

Astragalus haradjianii, commonly known as Haradjian's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
