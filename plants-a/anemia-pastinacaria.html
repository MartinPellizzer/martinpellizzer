Anemia pastinacaria

Anemia pastinacaria, commonly known as the liverleaf, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley.
