Andropogon saccharoides subsp. parvispiculus

Andropogon saccharoides subsp.

parvispiculus, commonly known as dwarf switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, pale green blades.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically less than 30 cm in height. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland restoration and bioenergy production.
