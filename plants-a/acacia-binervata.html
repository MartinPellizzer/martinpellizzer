Acacia binervata

Acacia binervata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are compound and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia binervata is also valued for its hardy nature and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
