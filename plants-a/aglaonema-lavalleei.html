Aglaonema lavalleei

Aglaonema lavalleei, commonly known as the Lavalle's arum, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, waxy leaves with prominent, light green veins that create a distinctive pattern. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its ornamental value makes it popular in both indoor and outdoor gardening.
