Actinidia rufa subvar. dulcissima

Actinidia rufa subvar. dulcissima, commonly known as the red actinidia or red kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It produces small, hairy fruits that are typically red or orange in color, with a fuzzy skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The subvariety is closely related to the common kiwifruit but is less commercially grown.
