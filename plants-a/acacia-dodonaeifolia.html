Acacia dodonaeifolia

Acacia dodonaeifolia, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
