Acalypha sabulicola

Acalypha sabulicola, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping.
