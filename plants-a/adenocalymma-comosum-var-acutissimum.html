Adenocalymma comosum var. acutissimum

Adenocalymma comosum var. acutissimum, commonly known as the slender sandblow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate. It is a perennial herb that is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its ability to withstand salt spray, making it suitable for coastal landscaping.
