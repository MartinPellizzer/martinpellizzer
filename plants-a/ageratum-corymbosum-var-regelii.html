Ageratum corymbosum var. regelii

Ageratum corymbosum var. regelii, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and distinctive floral characteristics.
