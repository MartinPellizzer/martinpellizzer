Athamanta collina

Athamanta collina, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Athamanta collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
