Ampelocissus chaffanjonii

Ampelocissus chaffanjonii, commonly known as the yellow fig-leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often exhibit a striking yellow or variegated coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
