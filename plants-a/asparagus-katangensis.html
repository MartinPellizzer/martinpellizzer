Asparagus katangensis

Asparagus katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga asparagus, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, green berries that contain the seeds.

Asparagus katangensis is often found in woodland savannas and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
