Anguloa virginalis var. virginalis

Anguloa virginalis var. virginalis, commonly known as the pink anguloa, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are spurred at the base and have a distinctive, intricate structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

It prefers cool, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and cloud forests.
