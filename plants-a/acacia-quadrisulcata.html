Acacia quadrisulcata

Acacia quadrisulcata, commonly known as the four-groove acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its seed pods are slender and ribbed, containing numerous flattened seeds.

Acacia quadrisulcata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local flora.
