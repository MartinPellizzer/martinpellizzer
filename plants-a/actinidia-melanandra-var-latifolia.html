Actinidia melanandra var. latifolia

Actinidia melanandra var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved actinidia, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright red color in late summer. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.

The leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine, highlighting its multifaceted significance.
