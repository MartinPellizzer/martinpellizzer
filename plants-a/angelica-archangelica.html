Angelica archangelica

Angelica archangelica, commonly known as angelica, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces thick, aromatic roots that are used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent. Angelica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its name is derived from the angelic association with its healing properties.
