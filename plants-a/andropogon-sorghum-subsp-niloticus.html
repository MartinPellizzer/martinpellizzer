Andropogon sorghum subsp. niloticus

Andropogon sorghum subsp.

niloticus, commonly known as Nile grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains.

This subspecies is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
