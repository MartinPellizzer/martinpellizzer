Allanblackia staneriana

Allanblackia staneriana, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by fruiting bodies that resemble small, round nuts. Its fruit contains a high amount of oils, making it valuable for both culinary and industrial uses.

The botanical significance of Allanblackia staneriana lies in its unique chemical composition and ecological role in its native habitat.
