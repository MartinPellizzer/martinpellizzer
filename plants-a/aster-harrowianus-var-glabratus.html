Aster harrowianus var. glabratus

Aster harrowianus var. glabratus, commonly known as the glabrous aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
