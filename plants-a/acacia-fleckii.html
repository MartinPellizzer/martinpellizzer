Acacia fleckii

Acacia fleckii, commonly known as the flecked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate leaves that are often speckled or streaked with contrasting colors. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits. Acacia fleckii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
