Acacia milleriana

Acacia milleriana, commonly known as the Miller's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia milleriana is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
