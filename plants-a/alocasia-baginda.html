Alocasia baginda

Alocasia baginda, commonly known as the Baginda elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, featuring a deep green color with a velvety texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. Alocasia baginda thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
