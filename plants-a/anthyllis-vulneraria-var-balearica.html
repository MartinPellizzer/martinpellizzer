Anthyllis vulneraria var. balearica

Anthyllis vulneraria var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic restharrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, coastal environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
