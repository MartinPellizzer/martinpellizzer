Acacia pseudosocotrana

Acacia pseudosocotrana, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is well-suited to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Acacia pseudosocotrana is also notable for its role in the local ecosystem and its historical significance in traditional uses.
