Alstroemeria chiloensis

Alstroemeria chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and typically grows as a herbaceous perennial.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
