Aspidium emarginatum

Aspidium emarginatum, commonly known as the marginal water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns (Hydropteraceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, finger-like segments. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can receive adequate sunlight and nutrients. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, contributing to water filtration and habitat creation for aquatic organisms.

Aspidium emarginatum plays an important ecological role in maintaining the balance of its aquatic ecosystem.
