Apodytes dimidiata

Apodytes dimidiata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple pairs of opposite leaflets.

Apodytes dimidiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
