Absinthium moxa

Absinthium moxa, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds such as thujone, which contribute to its strong, bitter aroma.

Absinthium moxa has been historically used in traditional medicine and as an ingredient in absinthe.
