Aeranthes virginalis

Aeranthes virginalis, commonly known as the white ginger lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant features upright, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive ginger-like scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Aeranthes virginalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
