Acianthera henrici

Acianthera henrici, commonly known as the Henry's acianthera, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its roots are often covered in a dense, white mycorrhizal sheath. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate appearance and unique fragrance.
