Ajax minor var. angustus

Ajax minor var. angustus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ajax, is a variant of the Ajax minor species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
