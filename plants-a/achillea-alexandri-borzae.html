Achillea alexandri-borzae

Achillea alexandri-borzae, commonly known as Alexandri's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to alpine and rock garden landscapes.
