Achyranthes porrigens

Achyranthes porrigens, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Achyranthes porrigens is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
