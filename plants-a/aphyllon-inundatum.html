Aphyllon inundatum

Aphyllon inundatum, commonly known as the water fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its lack of true leaves.

Instead, it possesses specialized structures called rhizomes that anchor it to submerged surfaces. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores rather than seeds, a trait common among primitive plants.

The botanical study of Aphyllon inundatum provides insights into the adaptation strategies of aquatic flora.
