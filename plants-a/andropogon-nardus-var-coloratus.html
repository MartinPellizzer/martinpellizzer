Andropogon nardus var. coloratus

Andropogon nardus var. coloratus, commonly known as the purple muhly, is a flowering grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pinkish inflorescences that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamp edges, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often bluish-green, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in wetland restoration projects.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
