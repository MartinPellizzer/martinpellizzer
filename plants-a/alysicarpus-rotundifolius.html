Alysicarpus rotundifolius

Alysicarpus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved sida, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are round or heart-shaped with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
