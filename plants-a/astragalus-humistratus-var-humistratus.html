Astragalus humistratus var. humistratus

Astragalus humistratus var. humistratus, commonly known as the lowland milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets arranged in pairs.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
