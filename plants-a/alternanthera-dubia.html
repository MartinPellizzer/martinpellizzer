Alternanthera dubia

Alternanthera dubia, commonly known as the red-veined dock, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red veins that run through its dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Alternanthera dubia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
