Aralidium dentatum

Aralidium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed aralidium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae, which includes a variety of ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
