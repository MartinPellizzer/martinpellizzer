Acrostichum neriifolium

Acrostichum neriifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. Its fronds are often divided into numerous narrow, linear segments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
