Adhatoda kotschyi

Adhatoda kotschyi, commonly known as the African skullcap, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
