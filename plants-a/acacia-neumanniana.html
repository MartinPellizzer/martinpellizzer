Acacia neumanniana

Acacia neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The wood of Acacia neumanniana is hard and durable, historically used by Indigenous Australians for tools and weapons.
