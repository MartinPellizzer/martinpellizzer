Ancistrorhynchus crystalensis

Ancistrorhynchus crystalensis, commonly known as the Crystaline armored catfish, is a freshwater fish native to South America.

It belongs to the Loricariidae family, characterized by its scaleless, armored body covered in bony plates. The species is notable for its elongated, laterally compressed body and the presence of a distinctive, fleshy adipose fin. It is typically found in slow-moving streams and rivers with sandy or muddy substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to both aquarists and researchers.
