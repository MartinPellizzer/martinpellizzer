Acacia arabica var. kraussiana

Acacia arabica var. kraussiana, commonly known as the Arabian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and compound leaves that are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular inflorescences, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to hot, dry climates.

This species is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally for its wood and gum.
