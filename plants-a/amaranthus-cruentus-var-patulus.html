Amaranthus cruentus var. patulus

Amaranthus cruentus var. patulus, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple foliage and striking, showy inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, while the flowers are popular in ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and minerals.
