Albuca chaetopoda

Albuca chaetopoda, commonly known as the woolly albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous stem and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
