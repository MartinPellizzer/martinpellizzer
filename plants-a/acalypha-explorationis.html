Acalypha explorationis

Acalypha explorationis, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that resemble poker chips, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers inside the bracts, which are often overlooked due to the vibrant color of the foliage. Acalypha explorationis typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
