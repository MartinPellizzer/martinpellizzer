Acaena argentea var. humilis

Acaena argentea var. humilis, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spiny, burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
