Anacamptis albertii

Anacamptis albertii, commonly known as the Albert's orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sunny, well-drained habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces tall, erect stems with several lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred lip and are often arranged in a loose raceme.

Anacamptis albertii is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
