Aechmea friedrichsthalii

Aechmea friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the Friedrichsthal's bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea friedrichsthalii is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and unique water-storing structure.
