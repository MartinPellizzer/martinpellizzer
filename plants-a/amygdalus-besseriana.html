Amygdalus besseriana

Amygdalus besseriana, commonly known as the Bessera almond or Bessera almond tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible drupes containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and conservation efforts.
