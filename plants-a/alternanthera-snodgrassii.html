Alternanthera snodgrassii

Alternanthera snodgrassii, commonly known as the red-veined alternate, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, often displaying shades of red, pink, and green in its leaves. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 inches.

Its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for water gardens and pond edges.
