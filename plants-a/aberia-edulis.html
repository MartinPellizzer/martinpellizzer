Aberia edulis

Aberia edulis, commonly known as the edible euphorbia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus. Its latex contains toxic compounds, but certain parts of the plant are used in traditional medicine.

Despite its toxicity, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance in some regions.
