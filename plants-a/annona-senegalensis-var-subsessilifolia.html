Annona senegalensis var. subsessilifolia

Annona senegalensis var. subsessilifolia, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It features a woody stem with rough, fissured bark and produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant bears yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by a fruit that is typically pear-shaped and has a thick, smooth skin.

The fruit contains a white, creamy pulp with numerous small, hard seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
