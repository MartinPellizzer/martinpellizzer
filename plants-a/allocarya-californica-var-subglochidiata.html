Allocarya californica var. subglochidiata

Allocarya californica var. subglochidiata, commonly known as the California false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and short, stiff hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, smooth, brown seeds.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and ecological adaptability in its native habitat.
