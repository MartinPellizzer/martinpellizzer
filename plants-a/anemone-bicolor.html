Anemone bicolor

Anemone bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, palmate leaves that are lobed and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white to pink flowers with a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anemone bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
