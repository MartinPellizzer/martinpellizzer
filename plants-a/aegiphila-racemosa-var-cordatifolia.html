Aegiphila racemosa var. cordatifolia

Aegiphila racemosa var. cordatifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf aegiphila, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically pubescent, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
