Anthericum aphyllum

Anthericum aphyllum, commonly known as the barren lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has no leaves, hence its common name, and relies on its stem and flowers for photosynthesis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
