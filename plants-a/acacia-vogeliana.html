Acacia vogeliana

Acacia vogeliana, commonly known as the Vogel's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The bark and leaves of Acacia vogeliana have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
