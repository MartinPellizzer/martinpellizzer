Asimina campaniflora

Asimina campaniflora, commonly known as the bellflower magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is known for its fragrant, five-petaled blossoms. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

The fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer, providing food for various wildlife species.
