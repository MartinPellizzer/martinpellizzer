Azanza acuminata

Azanza acuminata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central dark spot, which resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, narrow leaves and can reach a height of up to 30 centimeters.

Azanza acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
