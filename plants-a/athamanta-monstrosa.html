Athamanta monstrosa

Athamanta monstrosa, commonly known as the monstrous athamanta, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
