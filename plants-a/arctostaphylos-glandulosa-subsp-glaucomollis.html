Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. glaucomollis

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

glaucomollis, commonly known as the glaucous manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that often has a bluish-gray waxy coating.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in white or pink hues, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and have glandular dots, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
