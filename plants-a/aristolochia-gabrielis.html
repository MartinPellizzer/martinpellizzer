Aristolochia gabrielis

Aristolochia gabrielis, commonly known as the Gabriel's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive, heart-shaped leaf structure. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often strongly scented blooms. Its vines can grow quite long, climbing or trailing along supports, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
