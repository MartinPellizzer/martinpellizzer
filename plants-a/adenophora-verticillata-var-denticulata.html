Adenophora verticillata var. denticulata

Adenophora verticillata var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
