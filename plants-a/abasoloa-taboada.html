Abasoloa taboada

Abasoloa taboada, commonly known as the Mexican thornapple, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.
