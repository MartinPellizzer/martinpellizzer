Aspalathus ramulosa

Aspalathus ramulosa, commonly known as the rooibos plant, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish-brown stems and small, green, opposite leaves. The plant is renowned for its unique red-orange tea, which is produced from its young leaves and stems. Aspalathus ramulosa thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and is economically important for the production of rooibos tea.
