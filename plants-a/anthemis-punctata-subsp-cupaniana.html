Anthemis punctata subsp. cupaniana

Anthemis punctata subsp.

cupaniana, commonly known as the cupani's pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with dark purple centers.

The plant produces small, linear leaves and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is known for its aromatic properties and is cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution compared to other Anthemis species.
