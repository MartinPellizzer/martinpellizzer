Aronia × sargentii

Aronia × sargentii, commonly known as the black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and clusters of small, red berries that turn black when ripe. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its high antioxidant content in its fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Aronia × sargentii is also known for its hardiness and ability to attract birds and other wildlife.
