Allophylus rigidus f. incanus

Allophylus rigidus f. incanus, commonly known as the stiff alpine hakea, is a variant of the Allophylus rigidus species.

It is a shrub native to the alpine regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its rigid, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

This plant thrives in harsh, high-altitude environments and is well-suited to poor, rocky soils.
