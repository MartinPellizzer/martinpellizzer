Arabis drummondii var. brachycarpa

Arabis drummondii var. brachycarpa, commonly known as Drummond's rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant typically features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions make it a hardy ornamental plant.
