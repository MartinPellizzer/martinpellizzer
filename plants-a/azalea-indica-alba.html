Azalea indica-alba

Azalea indica-alba, commonly known as the white azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture for their ornamental value.
