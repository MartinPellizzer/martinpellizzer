Acer campestre f. convergens

Acer campestre f. convergens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maple, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical Acer campestre. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
