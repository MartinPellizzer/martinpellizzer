Aster auriculatus

Aster auriculatus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered seeds.

Aster auriculatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also a source of nectar for pollinators.
