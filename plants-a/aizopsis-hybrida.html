Aizopsis hybrida

Aizopsis hybrida, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy, and durable wood, which is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. Aizopsis hybrida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
