Abies excelsa var. pumila-glauca

Abies excelsa var. pumila-glauca, commonly known as the dwarf blue spruce, is a compact evergreen conifer with a dense, pyramidal shape.

It features needle-like leaves that are short, blue-gray in color, and covered in a fine wax layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue. It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and thrives in cool, moist environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
