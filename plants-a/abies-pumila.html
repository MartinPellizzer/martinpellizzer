Abies pumila

Abies pumila, commonly known as the dwarf fir, is a small coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, making it one of the smallest species in the fir genus. The plant has needle-like leaves that are short, dense, and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small and usually remain attached to the branches for several years.

The dwarf fir is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine zones.
