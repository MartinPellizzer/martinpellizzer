Alternanthera achyrantha var. leiantha

Alternanthera achyrantha var. leiantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep red to purple, depending on light conditions. The plant typically grows in shallow water, forming compact, bushy clusters with broad, ovate leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, which are followed by fluffy, three-lobed seed heads.

This variety is popular in water gardens and ponds for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
