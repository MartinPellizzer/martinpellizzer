Argyroderma luckhoffii

Argyroderma luckhoffii, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, waxy tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
