Allium asarense

Allium asarense, commonly known as the sand garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its bulbous structure stores water and nutrients, enabling survival in harsh environments.

Allium asarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
