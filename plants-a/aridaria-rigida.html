Aridaria rigida

Aridaria rigida, commonly known as the rigid arid flora, is a hardy plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It typically features succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, often inconspicuous flowers, usually in shades of yellow or green, which are adapted for pollination by wind or limited insect activity. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Aridaria rigida is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in regions with minimal rainfall.
