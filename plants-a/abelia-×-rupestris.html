Abelia × rupestris

Abelia × rupestris, commonly known as the creeping abelia, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming a dense, low-growing mat. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

Abelia × rupestris is well-suited for erosion control and thrives in partial shade to full sun with well-drained soil.
