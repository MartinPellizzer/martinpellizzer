Alyssum alpestre var. djurdjurae

Alyssum alpestre var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Alpine Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
