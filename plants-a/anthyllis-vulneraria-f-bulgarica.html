Anthyllis vulneraria f. bulgarica

Anthyllis vulneraria f. bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in dry, calcareous soils in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is native to the Balkan Peninsula and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
