Adhatoda carthaginensis var. minor

Adhatoda carthaginensis var. minor, commonly known as lesser purpleheart, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often blooming during the rainy season. Its bark and leaves contain alkaloids, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
