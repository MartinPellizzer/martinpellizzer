Aira flexuosa f. pallida

Aira flexuosa f. pallida, commonly known as the pale aira, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, white to pale green inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
