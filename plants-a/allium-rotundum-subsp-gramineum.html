Allium rotundum subsp. gramineum

Allium rotundum subsp.

gramineum, commonly known as Persian leek or garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It produces slender, grass-like leaves that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor.

The plant features small, umbel-like clusters of white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbs are typically smaller than those of common garlic and have a more delicate taste.

Botanically, it belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various edible and ornamental plants.
