Amaranthus commutatus

Amaranthus commutatus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species and environmental conditions. Its seeds are small, oval, and often used as a food source by both humans and wildlife.

Amaranthus commutatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
