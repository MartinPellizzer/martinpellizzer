Agrostis tacubayensis

Agrostis tacubayensis, commonly known as Tacubaya grass, is a species of grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with minute flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
