Achillea flava

Achillea flava, commonly known as yellow archangel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Achillea flava is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat wounds and inflammation.
