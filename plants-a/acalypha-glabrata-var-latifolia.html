Acalypha glabrata var. latifolia

Acalypha glabrata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
