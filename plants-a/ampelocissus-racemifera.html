Ampelocissus racemifera

Ampelocissus racemifera, commonly known as the African climbing fig, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It is known for its vigorous climbing habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
