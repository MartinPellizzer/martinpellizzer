Allium fimbriatum subsp. mohavense

Allium fimbriatum subsp.

mohavense, commonly known as the Mohave garlic, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces a tall, leafy scape that terminates in a cluster of small, white to pale purple flowers. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Allium genus.
