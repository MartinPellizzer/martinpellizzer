Albuca corymbosa

Albuca corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that store nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
