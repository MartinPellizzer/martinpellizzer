Angelica japonica var. japonica

Angelica japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese angelica, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
