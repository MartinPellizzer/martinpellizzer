Aframomum singulariflorum

Aframomum singulariflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered cardamom, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional culinary practices.
