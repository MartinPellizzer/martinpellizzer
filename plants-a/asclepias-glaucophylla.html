Asclepias glaucophylla

Asclepias glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-green milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, blue-green coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive, hooded structures that attract various pollinators, including monarch butterflies. Asclepias glaucophylla is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
