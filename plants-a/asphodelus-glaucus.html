Asphodelus glaucus

Asphodelus glaucus, commonly known as the blue asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asphodelus glaucus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
