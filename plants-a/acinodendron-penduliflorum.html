Acinodendron penduliflorum

Acinodendron penduliflorum, commonly known as the hanging flower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique flowering pattern makes it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists.
