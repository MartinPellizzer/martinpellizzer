Argemone mexicana subsp. ochroleuca

Argemone mexicana subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and spiny, hairy stems.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
