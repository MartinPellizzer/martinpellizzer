Aconitum lycoctonum subsp. lasianthum

Aconitum lycoctonum subsp.

lasianthum, commonly known as the meadow aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.
