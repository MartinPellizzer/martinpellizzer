Alopecurus tucheticus

Alopecurus tucheticus, commonly known as slender mouse-ear, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny blue or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Alopecurus tucheticus is also a food source for various insects and wildlife.
