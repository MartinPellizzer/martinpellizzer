Astragalus alopecuroides f. ispirensis

Astragalus alopecuroides f. ispirensis, commonly known as the Ispirensis milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
