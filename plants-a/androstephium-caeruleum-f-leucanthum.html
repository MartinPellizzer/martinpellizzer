Androstephium caeruleum f. leucanthum

Androstephium caeruleum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebells, is a variant of the bluebell plant characterized by its pale blue to white flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in regions like the British Isles. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.
