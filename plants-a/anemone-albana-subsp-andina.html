Anemone albana subsp. andina

Anemone albana subsp.

andina, commonly known as the Andean white anemone, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
