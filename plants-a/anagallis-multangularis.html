Anagallis multangularis

Anagallis multangularis, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or redstem mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with angular, hairy stems and small, showy pink or red flowers. The leaves are usually opposite, oval, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding.
