Anemone variata

Anemone variata, commonly known as the variable anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, white to pink flowers with a distinctive central yellow eye, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are often hairy and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in moist, shaded to partially sunny areas and is often found in woodland habitats.

Anemone variata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
