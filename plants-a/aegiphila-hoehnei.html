Aegiphila hoehnei

Aegiphila hoehnei, commonly known as the Brazilian sand verbena, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Aegiphila hoehnei is adapted to arid conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
