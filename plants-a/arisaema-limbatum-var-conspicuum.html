Arisaema limbatum var. conspicuum

Arisaema limbatum var. conspicuum, commonly known as the showy arrowhead, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green margin, and its most notable characteristic is its large, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique floral structure that includes both male and female flowers.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
