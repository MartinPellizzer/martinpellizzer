Achillea millefolium f. rosea

Achillea millefolium f. rosea, commonly known as the roseate yarrow, is a cultivar of the common yarrow plant.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of pink to rose-colored flowers. This plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for many climates.

The roseate form is prized for its soft, pastel hues, which add a delicate touch to garden landscapes.
