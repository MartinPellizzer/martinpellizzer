Aquilegia taygetea

Aquilegia taygetea, commonly known as the Greek columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Aquilegia taygetea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
