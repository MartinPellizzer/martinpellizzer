Arisaema polydactylum

Arisaema polydactylum, commonly known as the many-fingered arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries, which are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
