Albuca amoena

Albuca amoena, commonly known as the sweet-scented albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Albuca amoena thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ease of cultivation.
