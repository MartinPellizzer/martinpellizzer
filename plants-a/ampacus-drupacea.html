Ampacus drupacea

Ampacus drupacea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ampacus drupacea is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
