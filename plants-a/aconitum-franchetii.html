Aconitum franchetii

Aconitum franchetii, commonly known as Franchet's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves with deep lobes, and its flowers are blue to purple in color, typically arranged in racemes. Aconitum franchetii is known for containing toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal significance.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forested areas at high elevations.
