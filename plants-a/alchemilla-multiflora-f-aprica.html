Alchemilla multiflora f. aprica

Alchemilla multiflora f. aprica, commonly known as the spring lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features rosettes of basal, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, textured appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
