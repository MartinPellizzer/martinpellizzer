Avena abyssinica var. baldratiana

Avena abyssinica var. baldratiana, commonly known as Abyssinian oat, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces awned lemmas and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has been traditionally used for forage and has potential for cultivation in dry regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other oat species.
