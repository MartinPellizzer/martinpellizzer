Astragalus illyricus var. wulfenii

Astragalus illyricus var. wulfenii, commonly known as the Illyrian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, rounded seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
