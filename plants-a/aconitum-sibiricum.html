Aconitum sibiricum

Aconitum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided, compound leaves that are typically deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
