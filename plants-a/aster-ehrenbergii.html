Aster ehrenbergii

Aster ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple, blue, or white, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Aster ehrenbergii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
