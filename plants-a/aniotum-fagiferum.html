Aniotum fagiferum

Aniotum fagiferum, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tasting fruits that resemble small, round berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
