Acrophorus diacalpioides

Acrophorus diacalpioides, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
