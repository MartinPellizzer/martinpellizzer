Ardisia maculosa

Ardisia maculosa, commonly known as the spotted evergreen, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red or purple spots on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in some areas.
