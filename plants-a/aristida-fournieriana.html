Aristida fournieriana

Aristida fournieriana, commonly known as the Fournier's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that consist of numerous narrow, awned florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

Aristida fournieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
