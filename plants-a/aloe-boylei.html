Aloe boylei

Aloe boylei, commonly known as the Boyle's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues, and its leaves are thick, spiny, and covered in a whitish powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Aloe boylei is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular specimen among succulent enthusiasts.
