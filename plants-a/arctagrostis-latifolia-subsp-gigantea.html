Arctagrostis latifolia subsp. gigantea

Arctagrostis latifolia subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant broadleaf panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tall, slender inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a clumping growth habit, making it a hardy species adapted to wet environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
