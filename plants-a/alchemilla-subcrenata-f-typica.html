Alchemilla subcrenata f. typica

Alchemilla subcrenata f. typica, commonly known as the typical alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.

Alchemilla subcrenata f. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
