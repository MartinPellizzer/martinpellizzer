Astragalus yuralicus var. pseudoptilodes

Astragalus yuralicus var. pseudoptilodes, commonly known as the Yuralian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
