Aristida migiurtina

Aristida migiurtina, commonly known as the Mediterranean grass or red grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species.

The botanical features of Aristida migiurtina make it an important plant in ecological restoration and landscaping in arid environments.
