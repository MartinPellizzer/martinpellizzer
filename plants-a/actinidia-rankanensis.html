Actinidia rankanensis

Actinidia rankanensis, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
