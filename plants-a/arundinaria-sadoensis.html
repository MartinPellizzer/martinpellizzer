Arundinaria sadoensis

Arundinaria sadoensis, commonly known as Sado bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
