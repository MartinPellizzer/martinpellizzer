Aira littoralis subvar. rhenana

Aira littoralis subvar. rhenana, commonly known as the coastal air grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and tidal zones. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions. Its small, pale blue to white flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

This subvariety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for specialized wildlife.
