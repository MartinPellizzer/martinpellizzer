Aster bifoliatus

Aster bifoliatus, commonly known as the two-leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It blooms in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster bifoliatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
