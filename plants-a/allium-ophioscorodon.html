Allium ophioscorodon

Allium ophioscorodon, commonly known as the Persian shallot or rock onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Iran, and Pakistan. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The leaves are grass-like and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, making it a distinctive member of the Allium genus.
