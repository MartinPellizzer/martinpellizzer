Achyronia contaminata

Achyronia contaminata, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is known for its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, which can adhere to animals and clothing.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
