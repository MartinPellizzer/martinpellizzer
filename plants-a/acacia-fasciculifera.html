Acacia fasciculifera

Acacia fasciculifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia fasciculifera is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
