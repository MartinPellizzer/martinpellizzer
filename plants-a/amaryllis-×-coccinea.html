Amaryllis × coccinea

Amaryllis × coccinea, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Amaryllis belladonna* and *Amaryllis papilio*.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters on tall, sturdy stems. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It is a tropical plant native to South America, thriving in warm, humid environments.

Amaryllis × coccinea is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its showy blooms and ease of care.
