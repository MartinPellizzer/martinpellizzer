Aralia mexicana

Aralia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican aralia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and it can reach heights of up to 10 feet.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
