Aristolochia microura

Aristolochia microura, commonly known as the small-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, horseshoe-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its striking, often purple or greenish-pink floral structures. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insects, and the plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
