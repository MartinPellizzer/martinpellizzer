Adiantum aethiopicum f. queenslandiae

Adiantum aethiopicum f. queenslandiae, commonly known as the Queensland maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.

The fronds are biennial, with new growth emerging from the base each year.
