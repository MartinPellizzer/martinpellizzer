Acacia sultani

Acacia sultani, commonly known as the Sudanese acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, featuring deep root systems and waxy leaf surfaces. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, leathery pods containing seeds.

Acacia sultani is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
