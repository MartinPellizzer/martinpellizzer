Aristolochia esperanzae var. esperanzae

Aristolochia esperanzae var. esperanzae, commonly known as Esperanza vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe or a hood, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing on trees or shrubs in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
