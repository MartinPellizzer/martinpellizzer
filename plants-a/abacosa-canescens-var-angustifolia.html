Abacosa canescens var. angustifolia

Abacosa canescens var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved abacosa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
