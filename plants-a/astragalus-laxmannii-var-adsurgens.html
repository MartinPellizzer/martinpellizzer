Astragalus laxmannii var. adsurgens

Astragalus laxmannii var. adsurgens, commonly known as the ascending milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
