Astragalus stribrnyi

Astragalus stribrnyi, commonly known as the striated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus stribrnyi is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, and it produces small, woody pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.
