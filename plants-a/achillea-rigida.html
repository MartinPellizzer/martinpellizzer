Achillea rigida

Achillea rigida, commonly known as rigid yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white to pinkish, and arranged in flat-topped clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Achillea rigida is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
