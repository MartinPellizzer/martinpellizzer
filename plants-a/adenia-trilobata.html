Adenia trilobata

Adenia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed adenium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, often reddish bark. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

Adenia trilobata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
