Astragalus hirtellus

Astragalus hirtellus, commonly known as hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus hirtellus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
