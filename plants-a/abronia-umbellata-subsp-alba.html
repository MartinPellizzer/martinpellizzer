Abronia umbellata subsp. alba

Abronia umbellata subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

It is often found in coastal dunes and other disturbed, open habitats.
