Angelica yakusimensis

Angelica yakusimensis, commonly known as Yakutsk angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and parts of Siberia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its aromatic compounds. Its large, compound leaves are deeply lobed, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters.

Angelica yakusimensis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
