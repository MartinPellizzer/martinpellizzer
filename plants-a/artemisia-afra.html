Artemisia afra

Artemisia afra, commonly known as African wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Artemisia afra thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its leaves and flowers contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
