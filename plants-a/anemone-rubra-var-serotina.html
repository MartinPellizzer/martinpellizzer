Anemone rubra var. serotina

Anemone rubra var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering red anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

This variant is noted for its vibrant floral display and is often used in native plant gardens.
