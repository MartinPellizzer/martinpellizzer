Alchemilla scintillans

Alchemilla scintillans, commonly known as the glittering lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that shimmers in the sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf texture and long blooming period.
