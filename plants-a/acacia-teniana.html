Acacia teniana

Acacia teniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia teniana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
