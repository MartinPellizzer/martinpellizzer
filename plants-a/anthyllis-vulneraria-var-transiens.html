Anthyllis vulneraria var. transiens

Anthyllis vulneraria var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Heath, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or reddish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds.

Due to its seasonal blooming pattern, it is often referred to as the Transient Heath, reflecting its brief flowering period.
