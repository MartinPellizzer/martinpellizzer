Abronia parviflora

Abronia parviflora, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils along coastal areas. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Abronia parviflora is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
