Astragalus pulvinatus var. elongatus

Astragalus pulvinatus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is typically fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as plains and foothills.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
