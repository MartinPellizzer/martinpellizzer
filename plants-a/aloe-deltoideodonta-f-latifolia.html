Aloe deltoideodonta f. latifolia

Aloe deltoideodonta f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aloe, is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves.

The leaves are typically wider than they are long, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
