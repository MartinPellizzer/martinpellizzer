Acalypha nusbaumeri

Acalypha nusbaumeri, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha nusbaumeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
