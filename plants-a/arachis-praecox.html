Arachis praecox

Arachis praecox, commonly known as the runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers, followed by pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Arachis praecox is valued both for its nutritional seeds and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
