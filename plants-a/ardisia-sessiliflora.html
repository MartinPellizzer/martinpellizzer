Ardisia sessiliflora

Ardisia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
