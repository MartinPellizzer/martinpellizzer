Astragalus shishkinii

Astragalus shishkinii, commonly known as Shishkin's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus shishkinii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience.
