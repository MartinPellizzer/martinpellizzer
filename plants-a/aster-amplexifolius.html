Aster amplexifolius

Aster amplexifolius, commonly known as the claspingleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Aster amplexifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
