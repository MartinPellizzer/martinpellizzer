Astragalus myriocystis

Astragalus myriocystis, commonly known as the many-cysted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Astragalus myriocystis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
