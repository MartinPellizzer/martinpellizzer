Arabis Sparsiflora Var. Columbiana

Columbian rockcress, scientifically known as Arabis sparsiflora var. columbiana, is a rare and resilient plant native to the rocky highlands of Colombia. It thrives in harsh, arid environments where few other species can survive, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the early spring. Despite its beauty, Columbian rockcress remains little known outside of botanical circles due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat. Protecting this unique species is crucial for preserving the biodiversity of its native ecosystem.
