Alsophila latebrosa var. hemitelioides

Alsophila latebrosa var. hemitelioides, commonly known as the Hemitelioid Hare's-foot fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are creeping and often produce clusters of small, spore-bearing sporangia.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
