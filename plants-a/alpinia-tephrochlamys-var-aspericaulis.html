Alpinia tephrochlamys var. aspericaulis

Alpinia tephrochlamys var. aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-leafed ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces striking yellow and red flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its environment. It thrives in humid, shaded conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine, highlighting its botanical and cultural significance.
