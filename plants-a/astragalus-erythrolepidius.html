Astragalus erythrolepidius

Astragalus erythrolepidius, commonly known as the red-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its distinctive red stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Astragalus erythrolepidius is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
