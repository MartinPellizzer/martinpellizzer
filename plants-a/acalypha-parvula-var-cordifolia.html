Acalypha parvula var. cordifolia

Acalypha parvula var. cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf acalypha, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often resembling a heart shape, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, papery bracts that can range in color from red to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and container planting.
