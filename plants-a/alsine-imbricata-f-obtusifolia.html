Alsine imbricata f. obtusifolia

Alsine imbricata f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing annual herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically forms dense mats of small, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with rounded or blunt tips, giving it its specific epithet "obtusifolia.".

This species is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.
