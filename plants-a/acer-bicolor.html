Acer bicolor

Acer bicolor, commonly known as the twinleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. The tree typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters and has a rounded crown. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind.
