Aloe pongolensis

Aloe pongolensis, commonly known as the Pongola aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
