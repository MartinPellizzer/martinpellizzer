Anoplon

Anoplon, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, nocturnal blooms that open at night. The plant produces milky sap and has smooth, often hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Anoplon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
