Aleuritia waltonii

Aleuritia waltonii, commonly known as the golden wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Aleuritia waltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
