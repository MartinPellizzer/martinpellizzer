Aster staticifolius

Aster staticifolius, commonly known as the sticky leaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are covered in fine, sticky hairs, which give the plant its common name and help deter herbivores. This perennial species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
