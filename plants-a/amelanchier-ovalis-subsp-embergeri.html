Amelanchier ovalis subsp. embergeri

Amelanchier ovalis subsp.

embergeri, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It features oval, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in the summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wildlife habitats.
