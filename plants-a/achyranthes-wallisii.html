Achyranthes wallisii

Achyranthes wallisii, commonly known as Wallis's smartweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

It is often found in moist soils near water bodies and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
