Acacia longifolia var. typica

Acacia longifolia var. typica, commonly known as the coastal wattles, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
