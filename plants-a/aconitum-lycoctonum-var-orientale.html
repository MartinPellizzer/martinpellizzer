Aconitum lycoctonum var. orientale

Aconitum lycoctonum var. orientale, commonly known as the Oriental monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a raceme, with distinctive helmet-shaped sepals. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, giving it a fern-like appearance.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous species to handle.
