Acacia habbas

Acacia habbas, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spikes and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.

Acacia habbas is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood due to its hardy nature.
