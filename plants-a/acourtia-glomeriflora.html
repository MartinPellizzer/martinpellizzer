Acourtia glomeriflora

Acourtia glomeriflora, commonly known as the clustered-flower acourtia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Acourtia glomeriflora is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
