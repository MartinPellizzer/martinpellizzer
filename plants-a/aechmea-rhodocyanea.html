Aechmea rhodocyanea

Aechmea rhodocyanea, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety trichome layer, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces a tall flower spike that bears vibrant red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. Its epiphytic nature allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks.

Aechmea rhodocyanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
