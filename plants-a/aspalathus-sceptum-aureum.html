Aspalathus sceptum-aureum

Aspalathus sceptum-aureum, commonly known as the golden bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant yellow-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
