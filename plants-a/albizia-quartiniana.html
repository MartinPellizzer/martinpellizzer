Albizia quartiniana

Albizia quartiniana, commonly known as the African mimosa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often sensitive to touch, folding shut when disturbed. It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Albizia quartiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
