Aphloia theiformis subsp. madagascariensis

Aphloia theiformis subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar candleplant, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that resemble candles, which are covered in small, waxy leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
