Aristolochia saccata

Aristolochia saccata, commonly known as the saddlebag arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble a saddlebag. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its strong, often unpleasant odor, which attracts specific pollinators. Its vines can grow quite long, making it a striking addition to tropical gardens.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
