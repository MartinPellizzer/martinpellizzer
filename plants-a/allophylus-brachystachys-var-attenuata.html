Allophylus brachystachys var. attenuata

Allophylus brachystachys var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed holly-leaf tree, is a variant of the Allophylus brachystachys species.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in tropical and subtropical climates.
