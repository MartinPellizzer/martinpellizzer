Acalypha poiretii

Acalypha poiretii, commonly known as the silky bush or silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silken leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, or orange, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Acalypha poiretii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
