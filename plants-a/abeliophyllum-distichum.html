Abeliophyllum distichum

Abeliophyllum distichum, commonly known as the fragrant snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and attractive foliage.
