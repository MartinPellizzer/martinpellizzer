Acomis macra

Acomis macra, commonly known as the showy tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Acomis macra typically grows in moist, open habitats like meadows and wetlands.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
