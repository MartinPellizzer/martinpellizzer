Acacia viarum

Acacia viarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which bloom in spring. Acacia viarum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
