Alsophila ohaensis

Alsophila ohaensis, commonly known as the Oha tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and segmented, supporting the growth of its distinctive fronds.

Alsophila ohaensis plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
