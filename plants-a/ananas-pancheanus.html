Ananas pancheanus

Ananas pancheanus, commonly known as the mountain pineapple, is a flowering plant native to the tropical Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces a fleshy, spiky fruit that is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
