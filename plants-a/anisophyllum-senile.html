Anisophyllum senile

Anisophyllum senile, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a rare and unique fern-like plant characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds.

It is native to specific regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, arid environments. The plant's distinctive appearance comes from its long, dangling stipes that bear clusters of small, scale-like leaves. Anisophyllum senile is known for its slow growth and is often found in crevices or on rock faces.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
