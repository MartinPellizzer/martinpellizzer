Acacia mossambicensis

Acacia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Natal acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, flat, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Acacia mossambicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
