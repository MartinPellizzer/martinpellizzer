Ardisia crenata subsp. crassinervosa

Ardisia crenata subsp.

crassinervosa, commonly known as the broad-veined evergreen, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins, which give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
