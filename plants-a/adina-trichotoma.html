Adina trichotoma

Adina trichotoma, commonly known as the African rosewood or African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its fine grain and rich scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, round, reddish-brown fruits.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its wood in traditional crafts and medicine.
