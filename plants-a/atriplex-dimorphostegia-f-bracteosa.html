Atriplex dimorphostegia f. bracteosa

Atriplex dimorphostegia f. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate saltbush, is a variant of the saltbush species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. Its stems are often prostrate or erect, depending on environmental conditions.

This form is adapted to saline soils and is often found in coastal or inland salt flats.
