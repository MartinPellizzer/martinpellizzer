Araliopsis andamanica

Araliopsis andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman araliopsis, is a tropical plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family, which includes a variety of ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub with compound leaves that are often lobed or pinnately arranged. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in umbels, and it produces berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
