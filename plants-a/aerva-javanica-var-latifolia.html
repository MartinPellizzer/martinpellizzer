Aerva javanica var. latifolia

Aerva javanica var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common ornamental and sometimes invasive species in wetland environments.
