Aechmea vanhoutteana

Aechmea vanhoutteana, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, spiky leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The plant produces vibrant, flower-like structures known as inflorescences, which can be red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea vanhoutteana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities.

Morphology

Aechmea vanhoutteana has broad, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with a dark green color and a waxy texture, often featuring a central spine.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are clustered in a terminal spike, with vibrant purple and pink hues, blooming during the summer months and pollinated primarily by bees and hummingbirds. The fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by birds and mammals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the surrounding soil.
