Achillea amanica

Achillea amanica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and scrublands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also known for its aromatic properties and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
