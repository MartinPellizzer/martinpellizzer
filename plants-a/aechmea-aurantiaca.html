Aechmea aurantiaca

Aechmea aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliad family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that resemble a pineapple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to adapt to various light conditions, making it relatively easy to care for.

Morphology

Aechmea aurantiaca has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and leathery, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a reddish or purplish hue on the underside, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a dense, fibrous sheath, and it grows primarily from the base rather than branching extensively. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red or orange, and they bloom during the warmer months, pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that release seeds through wind dispersal, and its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.
