Andropogon castratus

Andropogon castratus, commonly known as the black bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon castratus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
