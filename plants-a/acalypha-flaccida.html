Acalypha flaccida

Acalypha flaccida, commonly known as the softleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Acalypha flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
