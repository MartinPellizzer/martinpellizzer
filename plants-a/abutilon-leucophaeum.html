Abutilon leucophaeum

Abutilon leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine white pubescence. It produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color.

Abutilon leucophaeum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
