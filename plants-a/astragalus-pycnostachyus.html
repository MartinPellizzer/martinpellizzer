Astragalus pycnostachyus

Astragalus pycnostachyus, commonly known as the dense-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus pycnostachyus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
