Alchemilla monticola var. crassa

Alchemilla monticola var. crassa, commonly known as broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded areas, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, hardy plants.
