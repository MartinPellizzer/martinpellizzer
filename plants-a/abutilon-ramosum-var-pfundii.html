Abutilon ramosum var. pfundii

Abutilon ramosum var. pfundii, commonly known as the silverleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, corky texture, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
