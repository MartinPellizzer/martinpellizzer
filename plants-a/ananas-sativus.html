Ananas sativus

Ananas sativus, commonly known as the pineapple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of spiky, waxy leaves that form a central cavity. The plant produces a unique fruit that develops from a flower cluster, with a tough outer skin and a sweet, aromatic interior. The leaves contain bromelain, an enzyme used in food processing and medicine.

Its cultivation requires warm, humid climates and well-drained soil.
