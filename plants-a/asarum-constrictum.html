Asarum constrictum

Asarum constrictum, commonly known as narrowleaf wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a strong, aromatic odor. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported tonic and stimulant properties.

Asarum constrictum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
