Arrabidaea craterophora var. acutifolia

Arrabidaea craterophora var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Acute-leaved Arrabidaea, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with acute leaflets, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the legume family.

The species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
