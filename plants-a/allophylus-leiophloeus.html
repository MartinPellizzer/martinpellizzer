Allophylus leiophloeus

Allophylus leiophloeus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved alder, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Saracinoideae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by fleshy, red or orange berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
