Atylosia scarabaeoides var. argyrophylla

Atylosia scarabaeoides var. argyrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf false heather, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in silvery-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

This variety is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapic gardens.
