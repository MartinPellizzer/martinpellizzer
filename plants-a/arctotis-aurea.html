Arctotis aurea

Arctotis aurea, commonly known as the golden arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are upright and branching. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis aurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
