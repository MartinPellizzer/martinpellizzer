Agalinis decemloba

Agalinis decemloba, commonly known as the ten-lobed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is part of the Oenothera genus, which includes other evening primrose species known for their unique flowering patterns.
