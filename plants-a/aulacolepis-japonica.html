Aulacolepis japonica

Aulacolepis japonica, commonly known as Japanese horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Aulacolepis japonica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
