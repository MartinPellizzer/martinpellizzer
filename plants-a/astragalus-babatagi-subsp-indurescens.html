Astragalus babatagi subsp. indurescens

Astragalus babatagi subsp.

indurescens, commonly known as the hardening milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its subspecies distinction is based on morphological differences, particularly in the texture and coloration of its stems and leaves.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
