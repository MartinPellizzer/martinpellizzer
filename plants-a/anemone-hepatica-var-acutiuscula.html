Anemone hepatica var. acutiuscula

Anemone hepatica var. acutiuscula, commonly known as the sharp-lobed hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, early spring blooms with five to seven petal-like sepals in shades of white, pink, or blue. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that remain throughout the winter. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
