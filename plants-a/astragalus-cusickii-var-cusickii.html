Astragalus cusickii var. cusickii

Astragalus cusickii var. cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
