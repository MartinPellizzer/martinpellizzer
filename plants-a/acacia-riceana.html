Acacia riceana

Acacia riceana, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes and compound leaves composed of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Acacia riceana thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and coastal areas, and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various native animals.
