Arisaema inaense

Arisaema inaense, commonly known as the Ina mountain arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, while the spathe is often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues.

Arisaema inaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
