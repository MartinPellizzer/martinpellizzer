Avicennia germinans f. aberrans

Avicennia germinans f. aberrans, commonly known as the mangrove shrub, is a variant of the black mangrove species native to coastal regions of the Americas.

This plant is characterized by its specialized adaptations to saline and waterlogged soils, including salt-excreting leaves and aerial roots. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms. The form of aberrans is distinguished by its unique morphological traits, such as shorter stature and altered leaf structures.

Its presence is crucial for maintaining the health and biodiversity of mangrove forests.
