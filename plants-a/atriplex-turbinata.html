Atriplex turbinata

Atriplex turbinata, commonly known as the bearded saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has adaptations such as succulent leaves and a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in saline and drought-prone environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Atriplex turbinata is also used in rehabilitation of degraded lands due to its hardy nature.
