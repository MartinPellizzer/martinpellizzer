Acacia sulcata var. sulcata

Acacia sulcata var. sulcata, commonly known as the African screw tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted branches. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia sulcata var. sulcata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
