Albizia flamignii

Albizia flamignii, commonly known as the flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often divided into several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces fluffy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Albizia flamignii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
