Arisaema zebrinum

Arisaema zebrinum, commonly known as the zebra plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe.

Native to Southeast Asia, it features striking, zebra-like patterns on its leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and variegated with contrasting colors. The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence that is often green with red or purple tints, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical gardens.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
