Aechmea joannis

Aechmea joannis, commonly known as the Joannis Bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant flowers, typically in red, orange, or yellow hues. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Aechmea joannis is a popular ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea joannis has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a waxy surface and a deep green color.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, fibrous root system that anchors it to the substrate. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, blooming during the wet season, and is pollinated by insects. The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
