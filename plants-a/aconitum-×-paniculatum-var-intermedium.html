Aconitum × paniculatum var. intermedium

Aconitum × paniculatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate monkshood, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of striking blue or purple hooded flowers. The plant produces large, showy blooms in late summer, which are typically arranged in panicles, giving it its species name. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that are dark green and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
