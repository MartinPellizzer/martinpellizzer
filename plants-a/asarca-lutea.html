Asarca lutea

Asarca lutea, commonly known as yellow hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a yellowish underside. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Asarca lutea is noted for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as protoanemonin, which can be harmful if ingested.
