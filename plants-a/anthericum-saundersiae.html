Anthericum saundersiae

Anthericum saundersiae, commonly known as the Saunders' arum, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to New Zealand.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which serves to attract pollinators. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into red berries, which are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a protected species in its native habitat.
