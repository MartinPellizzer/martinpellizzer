Acacia courtii

Acacia courtii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

Acacia courtii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with low rainfall.
