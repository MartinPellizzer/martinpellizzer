Arundinaria racemosa

Arundinaria racemosa, commonly known as the bamboo cane reed, is a grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and establish itself in moist, shaded environments.

Arundinaria racemosa is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other areas with consistently damp soil.
