Asplenium centrochinense

Asplenium centrochinense, commonly known as the Chinese spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Asplenium centrochinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
