Acaena minor var. minor

Acaena minor var. minor, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, berry-like fruits. The plant is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are similar in taste to spinach.

However, the plant is also known for its ornamental value due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
