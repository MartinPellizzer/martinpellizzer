Acrocephalus fruticosus

Acrocephalus fruticosus, commonly known as the common reed, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters along water edges or in moist soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form extensive stands in wetland habitats.

Acrocephalus fruticosus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various birds and insects.
