Apostasia alba

Apostasia alba, commonly known as the white spatled orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spathelike inflorescence that resembles a white spatula. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a dense cluster. Apostasia alba thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
