Aristida dasydesmis

Aristida dasydesmis, commonly known as the tufted dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often slightly drooping.

Aristida dasydesmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
