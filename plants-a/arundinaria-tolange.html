Arundinaria tolange

Arundinaria tolange, commonly known as the Tolange bamboo, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-white flowers, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Arundinaria tolange is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in basket weaving and construction.
