Acacia latior

Acacia latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces yellowish to pale green flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain flat, compressed seeds.

Acacia latior is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
