Acer × creticum var. cuneifolium

Acer × creticum var. cuneifolium, commonly known as the cuneifolium maple, is a hybrid species within the maple genus, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features broad, ovate to elliptical leaves with a serrated margin, often displaying a vibrant green on the upper surface and a lighter green or sometimes reddish underside. The plant typically produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruits are samaras, which are winged seeds that disperse by wind.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
