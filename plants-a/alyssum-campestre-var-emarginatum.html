Alyssum campestre var. emarginatum

Alyssum campestre var. emarginatum, commonly known as the marginal alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces numerous seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a desirable addition to many garden settings.
