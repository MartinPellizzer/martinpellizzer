Achyranthes panamensis

Achyranthes panamensis, commonly known as Panama knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Due to its adaptability, it can be invasive in certain regions, impacting local ecosystems.
