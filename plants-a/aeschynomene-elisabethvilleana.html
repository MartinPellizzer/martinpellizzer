Aeschynomene elisabethvilleana

Aeschynomene elisabethvilleana, commonly known as the Elizabethville morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
