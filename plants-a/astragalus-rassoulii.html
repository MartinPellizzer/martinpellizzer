Astragalus rassoulii

Astragalus rassoulii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with multiple small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus rassoulii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
