Aubertia argentea

Aubertia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf aubertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Aubertia argentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
