Aristolochia barbata subsp. dictyantha

Aristolochia barbata subsp.

dictyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, saddle-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral morphology and ecological role in supporting specific pollinators.
