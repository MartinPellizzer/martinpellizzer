Acalypha neptunica var. neptunica

Acalypha neptunica var. neptunica, commonly known as the Neptune's Aloe, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
