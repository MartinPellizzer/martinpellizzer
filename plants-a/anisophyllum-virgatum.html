Anisophyllum virgatum

Anisophyllum virgatum, commonly known as the western sandreed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Anisophyllum virgatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
