Acalypha capitata

Acalypha capitata, commonly known as the "Crownleaf" or "White Crown," is a tropical shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal heads.

Acalypha capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
