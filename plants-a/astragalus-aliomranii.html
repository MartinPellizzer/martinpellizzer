Astragalus aliomranii

Astragalus aliomranii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Astragalus aliomranii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
