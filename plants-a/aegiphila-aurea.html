Aegiphila aurea

Aegiphila aurea, commonly known as golden bush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive texture.

Aegiphila aurea is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
