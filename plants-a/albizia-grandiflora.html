Albizia grandiflora

Albizia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant silktree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are often pink or white, arranged in racemes. Its seed pods are long and cylindrical, containing numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Albizia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
