Andropogon gyrans

Andropogon gyrans, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
