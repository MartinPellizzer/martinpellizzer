Alternanthera asterophora

Alternanthera asterophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined sorrel, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental leaves that often display vibrant red and green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in aquatic settings.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
