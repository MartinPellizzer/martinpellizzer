Adnaria nitida

Adnaria nitida, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, while its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs.

Adnaria nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting coastal biodiversity.
