Armeria duriaei var. dasyphylla

Armeria duriaei var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize dune ecosystems.
