Anoiganthus gracilis

Anoiganthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features delicate, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often described as resembling those of a fern. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its graceful, upright growth habit.

Anoiganthus gracilis is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
