Acacia ammobia

Acacia ammobia, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, tolerating saline conditions and strong winds.

Acacia ammobia plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
