Arundinaria cantorii

Arundinaria cantorii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine. Arundinaria cantorii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
