Andropogon schoenanthus var. proximus

Andropogon schoenanthus var. proximus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
