Anthochortus crinalis

Anthochortus crinalis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Anthochortus crinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
