Amarenus sebastiani

Amarenus sebastiani, commonly known as the Sebastian's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its small, clustered flowers range in color from white to pink, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Amarenus sebastiani is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
