Abies coerulescens

Abies coerulescens, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and striking bluish-gray needles that give it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces cones that are typically cylindrical and measure around 2 to 3 inches in length. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round color, and it is well adapted to cold climates and well-drained soils.

The blue spruce is also valued for its ornamental use in landscaping and its role in forest ecosystems.
