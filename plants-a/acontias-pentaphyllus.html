Acontias pentaphyllus

Acontias pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leafed acanthus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a dramatic and architectural appearance. The plant produces showy spikes of violet or purple flowers in the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties displaying a silvery underside that enhances its visual appeal.

Acontias pentaphyllus is well-suited for perennial borders and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
