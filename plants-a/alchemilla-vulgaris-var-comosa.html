Alchemilla vulgaris var. comosa

Alchemilla vulgaris var. comosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of soft, hairy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is used in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
