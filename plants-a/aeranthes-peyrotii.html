Aeranthes peyrotii

Aeranthes peyrotii, commonly known as the Peyrot's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, erect stem and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant has a short flowering period, typically in the spring, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
