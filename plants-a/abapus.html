Abapus

Abapus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a water plant with strikingly large, showy flowers.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in the Nile River and surrounding wetlands. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. Its flowers bloom in a range of colors, from white to pink, and are known for their symbolic significance in Egyptian culture.

The abapus was also used in religious rituals and is considered a symbol of purity and rebirth.
