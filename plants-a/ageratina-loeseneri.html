Ageratina loeseneri

Ageratina loeseneri, commonly known as the mountain ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratina loeseneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural settings.
