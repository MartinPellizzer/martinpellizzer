Achyranthes polystachya

Achyranthes polystachya, commonly known as red sanders, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

Achyranthes polystachya is also notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
