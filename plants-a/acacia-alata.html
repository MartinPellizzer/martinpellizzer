Acacia alata

Acacia alata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are a key feature for identification. Acacia alata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
