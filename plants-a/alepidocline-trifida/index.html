Alepidocline Trifida

Alepidocline trifida is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is known for its unique tripartite inflorescence, which sets it apart from most other members of the Campanulaceae family. Despite its beauty, this species is highly threatened by habitat loss and climate change. Due to its limited distribution, it is listed as critically endangered by the IUCN. Conservation efforts are urgently needed to protect this elusive and ecologically important plant.
