Acacia stanleyi

Acacia stanleyi, commonly known as the Stanley's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden-yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which appear in late winter to early spring. Acacia stanleyi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
