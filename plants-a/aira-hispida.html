Aira hispida

Aira hispida, commonly known as hairy air plant, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

Aira hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
