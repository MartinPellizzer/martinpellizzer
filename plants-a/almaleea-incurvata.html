Almaleea incurvata

Almaleea incurvata, commonly known as the bent-leaf almaleea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, silver-green leaves that curl inward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is adapted to poor, well-drained soils.

Almaleea incurvata is often found in rocky crevices and along dry streambeds, showcasing its drought-resistant traits.
