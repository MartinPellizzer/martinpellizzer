Arrhenatherum elatius subsp. braun-blanquetii

Arrhenatherum elatius subsp.

braun-blanquetii, commonly known as Braun-Blanquet's false oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, thriving in nutrient-rich soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity in wetland habitats.
