Allium burdickii

Allium burdickii, commonly known as Burdick's onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with purple veins. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a potential crop for culinary use.

Allium burdickii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
