Aster elegantulus

Aster elegantulus, commonly known as the elegant aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance.

Aster elegantulus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
