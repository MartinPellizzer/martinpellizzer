Astragalus tetranocarpus

Astragalus tetranocarpus, commonly known as the four-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces four-seeded pods, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and steppe habitats.

Astragalus tetranocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native environment.
