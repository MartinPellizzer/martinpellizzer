Adiantum weatherbyanum

Adiantum weatherbyanum, commonly known as the Florida maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features intricate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive, fern-like structure that resembles the shape of a feather. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are bi-lobed and turn a striking red during the winter months.

Adiantum weatherbyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
