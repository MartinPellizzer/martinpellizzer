Alchemilla glaberrima var. gracilis

Alchemilla glaberrima var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, silvery-green appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
