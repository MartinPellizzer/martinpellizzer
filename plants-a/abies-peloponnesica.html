Abies peloponnesica

Abies peloponnesica, commonly known as the Peloponnesian fir, is a rare and endemic species native to the southern Greek region of Peloponnese.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the fir genus. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
