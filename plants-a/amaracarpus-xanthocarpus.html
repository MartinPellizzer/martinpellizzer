Amaracarpus xanthocarpus

Amaracarpus xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-fruit sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive yellow or orange fruits. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaracarpus xanthocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
