Aster saxomarinus

Aster saxomarinus, commonly known as the saxomarine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its hardiness in cold climates.

The saxomarine aster is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
