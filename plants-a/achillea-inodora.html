Achillea inodora

Achillea inodora, commonly known as sweet yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
