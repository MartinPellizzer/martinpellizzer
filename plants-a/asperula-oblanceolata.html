Asperula oblanceolata

Asperula oblanceolata, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen azalea, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a dense, attractive appearance.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
