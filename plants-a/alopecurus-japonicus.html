Alopecurus japonicus

Alopecurus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese hair vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or pale purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Alopecurus japonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
