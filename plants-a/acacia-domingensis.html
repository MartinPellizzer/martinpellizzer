Acacia domingensis

Acacia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

The tree is adapted to tropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.
