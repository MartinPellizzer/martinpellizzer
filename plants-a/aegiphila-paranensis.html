Aegiphila paranensis

Aegiphila paranensis, commonly known as the Parana shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
