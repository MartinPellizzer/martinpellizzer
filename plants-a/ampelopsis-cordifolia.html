Ampelopsis cordifolia

Ampelopsis cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf vine, is a woody vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in size. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its climbing habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
