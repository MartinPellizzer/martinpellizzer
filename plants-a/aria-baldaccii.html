Aria baldaccii

Aria baldaccii, commonly known as the Baldaccii's lily, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, Aria baldaccii is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
