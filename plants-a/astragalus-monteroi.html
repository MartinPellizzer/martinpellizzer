Astragalus monteroi

Astragalus monteroi, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid, or semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. It has compound leaves and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Astragalus monteroi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
