Alstroemeria lineatiflora

Alstroemeria lineatiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or green veins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Alstroemeria lineatiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
