Aglaia cordata

Aglaia cordata, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange or red berries that contain several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is part of the Meliaceae family, which includes other economically important trees.
