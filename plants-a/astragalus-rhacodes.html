Astragalus rhacodes

Astragalus rhacodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus rhacodes is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
