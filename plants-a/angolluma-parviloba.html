Angolluma parviloba

Angolluma parviloba, commonly known as the small-fruited angolluma, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Angolluma parviloba is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks in its natural habitat.
