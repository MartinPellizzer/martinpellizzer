Austrostipa stipoides

Austrostipa stipoides, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Austrostipa stipoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna in its native range.
