Adhatoda tristiflora

Adhatoda tristiflora, commonly known as the blue tailed cobra plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and a distinctive blue-tipped stem, which gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Adhatoda tristiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
