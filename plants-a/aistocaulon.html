Aistocaulon

Aistocaulon, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, helping to reduce water loss.

Aistocaulon is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
