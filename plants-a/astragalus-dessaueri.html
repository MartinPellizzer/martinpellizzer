Astragalus dessaueri

Astragalus dessaueri, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus dessaueri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
