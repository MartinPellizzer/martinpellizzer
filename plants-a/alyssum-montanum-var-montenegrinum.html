Alyssum montanum var. montenegrinum

Alyssum montanum var. montenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegrin alyssum, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is generally gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
