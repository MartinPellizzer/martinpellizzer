Achillea versecensis

Achillea versecensis, commonly known as the Versecense yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its growth habit is perennial, with a compact form that makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
