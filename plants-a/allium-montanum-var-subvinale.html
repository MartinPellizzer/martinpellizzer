Allium montanum var. subvinale

Allium montanum var. subvinale, commonly known as the purple garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
