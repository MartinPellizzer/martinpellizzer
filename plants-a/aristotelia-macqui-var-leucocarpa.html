Aristotelia macqui var. leucocarpa

Aristotelia macqui var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited copihue, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, white, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a unique, sweet flavor. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, while its bark is thick and rugged.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
