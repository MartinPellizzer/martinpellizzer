Amaranthus abyssinicus

Amaranthus abyssinicus, commonly known as Ethiopian amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, spiky bracts that are often mistaken for petals, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Amaranthus abyssinicus are also edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
