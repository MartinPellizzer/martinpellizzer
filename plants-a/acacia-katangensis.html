Acacia katangensis

Acacia katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorn-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in spherical clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia katangensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
