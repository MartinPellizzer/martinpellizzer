Azalea viscosa var. scabra

Azalea viscosa var. scabra, commonly known as the rough azalea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its small, leathery leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
