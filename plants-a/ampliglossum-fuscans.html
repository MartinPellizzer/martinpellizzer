Ampliglossum fuscans

Ampliglossum fuscans, commonly known as the dark-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green color, often with a bluish or purplish tinge. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a reddish or purple throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
