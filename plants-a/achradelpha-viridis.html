Achradelpha viridis

Achradelpha viridis, commonly known as the green sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and coastal environments. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
