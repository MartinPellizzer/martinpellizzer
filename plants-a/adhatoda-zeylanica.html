Adhatoda zeylanica

Adhatoda zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Malabar Nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. Botanically, it belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their various therapeutic properties.
