Arundinaria attenuata

Arundinaria attenuata, commonly known as the bamboo cane reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a grass-like appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

Arundinaria attenuata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
