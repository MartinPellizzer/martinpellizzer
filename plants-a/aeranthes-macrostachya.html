Aeranthes macrostachya

Aeranthes macrostachya, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing numerous flowers, each with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Aeranthes macrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
