Atragene alpina var. sibirica

Atragene alpina var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subarctic regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often covered in a fine pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
