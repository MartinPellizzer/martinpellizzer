Aechmea rubroaristata

Aechmea rubroaristata, commonly known as the red-bristled pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant features vibrant red and purple inflorescences, which are adorned with soft, hairy bracts that give it its common name. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Aechmea rubroaristata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea rubroaristata has leaves that are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette, with a vibrant green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a fibrous, reddish-brown bark, growing upright from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a dense inflorescence, with red bracts and yellowish-white petals, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by bees and other insects. The fruit is a small, berry-like capsule that disperses seeds by wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
