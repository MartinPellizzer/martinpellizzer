Agrimonia sulcata

Agrimonia sulcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agrimony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Agrimonia sulcata is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
