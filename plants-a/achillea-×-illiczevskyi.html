Achillea × illiczevskyi

Achillea × illiczevskyi, commonly known as the Russian yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale pink. Native to southern Russia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
