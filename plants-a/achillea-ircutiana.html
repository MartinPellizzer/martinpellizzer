Achillea ircutiana

Achillea ircutiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy, and its basal leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a silvery-gray underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Achillea ircutiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
