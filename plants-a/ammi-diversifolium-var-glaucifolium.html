Ammi diversifolium var. glaucifolium

Ammi diversifolium var. glaucifolium, commonly known as the glaucous leaf cowparsley, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and often have a bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
