Arisaema rhizomatum

Arisaema rhizomatum, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spadix and spatula-shaped leaves. The plant produces a cluster of small, white flowers surrounded by a broad, green bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The rhizomes of this plant are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.
