Arctostaphylos patula subsp. patula

Arctostaphylos patula subsp.

patula, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and broad, leathery leaves that turn bright red in the fall.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and dry summers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
