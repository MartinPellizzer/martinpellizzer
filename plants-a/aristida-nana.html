Aristida nana

Aristida nana, commonly known as dwarf dropseed, is a low-growing grass species native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in the wild. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil.

Aristida nana is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
