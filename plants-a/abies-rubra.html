Abies rubra

Abies rubra, commonly known as the red spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Red spruce typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in mountainous regions.

Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, becoming thicker and more furrowed with age.
