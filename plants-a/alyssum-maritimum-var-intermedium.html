Alyssum maritimum var. intermedium

Alyssum maritimum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features silver-green, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salty air and coastal conditions.
