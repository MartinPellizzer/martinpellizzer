Acidosasa longiligula

Acidosasa longiligula, commonly known as the slender-stemmed acidosasa, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant features thin, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are pale green to white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
