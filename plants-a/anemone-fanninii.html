Anemone fanninii

Anemone fanninii, commonly known as the Fannin's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, rounded center that resembles a crown. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Anemone fanninii is often found in woodland settings and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
