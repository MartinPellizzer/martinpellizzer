Aconitum clusianum

Aconitum clusianum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a striking perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant's leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
