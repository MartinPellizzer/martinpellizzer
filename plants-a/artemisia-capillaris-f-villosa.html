Artemisia capillaris f. villosa

Artemisia capillaris f. villosa, commonly known as velvet wormwood, is a variant of the common wormwood plant.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils and are used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
