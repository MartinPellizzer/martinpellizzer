Aster tataricus

Aster tataricus, commonly known as the Tatarian aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact, upright growth habit. Its flowers bloom in the late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster tataricus is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant colors and relatively low maintenance requirements.
