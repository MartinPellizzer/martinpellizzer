Alsophila dubia

Alsophila dubia, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Alsophila dubia plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
