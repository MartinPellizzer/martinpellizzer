Aralia guilfoylei

Aralia guilfoylei, commonly known as the giant aralia, is a striking tropical plant native to the lowland rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can grow up to three meters in length, composed of numerous narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
