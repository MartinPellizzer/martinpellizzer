Aristida mendocina

Aristida mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino threeawn, is a perennial grass native to California.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, which produces small, pale to brown flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
