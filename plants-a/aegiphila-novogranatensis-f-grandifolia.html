Aegiphila novogranatensis f. grandifolia

Aegiphila novogranatensis f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered mountain sage, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
