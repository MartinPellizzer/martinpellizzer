Acer platanoides f. pseudotruncatum

Acer platanoides f. pseudotruncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Norway maple, is a unique foliage variant of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form, with a serrated margin and a lighter green coloration. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, native to Europe, and it thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
