Aconitum chienningense

Aconitum chienningense, commonly known as the Chienning Chinese Aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes and produces follicular fruits containing seeds with tufts of white hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and should be handled with care.
