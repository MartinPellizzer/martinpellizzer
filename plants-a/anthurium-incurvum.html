Anthurium incurvum

Anthurium incurvum, commonly known as the curly anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that curve inward. The plant produces clusters of small, white spadices surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often red, orange, or yellow. Anthurium incurvum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
