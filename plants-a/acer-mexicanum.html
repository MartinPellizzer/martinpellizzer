Acer mexicanum

Acer mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican maple, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The bark of Acer mexicanum is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures as the tree matures.
