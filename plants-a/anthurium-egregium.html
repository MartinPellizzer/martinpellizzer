Anthurium egregium

Anthurium egregium, commonly known as the giant anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often with a deep green color and a glossy texture. The plant produces showy spadices, which are spathes that enclose the actual flowers, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium egregium is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
