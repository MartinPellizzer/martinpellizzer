Allium ucrainicum

Allium ucrainicum, commonly known as the Crimean garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered, spherical bulbs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
