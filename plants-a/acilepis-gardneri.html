Acilepis gardneri

Acilepis gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides habitat for various desert species.

Its seeds are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its survival in harsh environments.
