Abies religiosa subsp. colimensis

Abies religiosa subsp.

colimensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its conical shape and dense foliage.

The needles are dark green, glossy, and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family.

This subspecies thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist climates and is adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its native range.
