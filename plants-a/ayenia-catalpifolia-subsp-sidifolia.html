Ayenia catalpifolia subsp. sidifolia

Ayenia catalpifolia subsp.

sidifolia, commonly known as the rock-rose ayenia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
