Avenula × talaverae

Avenula × talaverae, commonly known as the Talavera grass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or white florets arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
