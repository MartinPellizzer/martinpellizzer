Aspalathus glomerata

Aspalathus glomerata, commonly known as rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is notable for its unique ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils and requires minimal water. Its seeds are harvested and processed to produce the popular herbal tea known as rooibos.

The plant plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its economic significance.
