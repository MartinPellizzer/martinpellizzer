Acaciella goldmanii

Acaciella goldmanii, commonly known as the golden acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to coastal environments and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Acaciella goldmanii plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
