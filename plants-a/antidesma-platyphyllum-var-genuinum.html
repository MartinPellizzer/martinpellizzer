Antidesma platyphyllum var. genuinum

Antidesma platyphyllum var. genuinum, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers and red, berry-like fruits that are often mistaken for true berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
