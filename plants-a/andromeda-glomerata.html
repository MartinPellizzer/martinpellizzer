Andromeda glomerata

Andromeda glomerata, commonly known as the winter beauty or rosebay witch-hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Andromeda glomerata is notable for its ability to survive harsh winter conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
