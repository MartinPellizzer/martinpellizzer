Amellus strigosus

Amellus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy amellus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Amellus strigosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
