Atractylodes erosodentata

Atractylodes erosodentata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ginger or Chinese sand ginger, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the upper part of the stem. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
