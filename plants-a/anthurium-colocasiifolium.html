Anthurium colocasiifolium

Anthurium colocasiifolium, commonly known as the laceleaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are intricately veined and have a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Anthurium colocasiifolium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
