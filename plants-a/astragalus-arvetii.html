Astragalus arvetii

Astragalus arvetii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus arvetii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
