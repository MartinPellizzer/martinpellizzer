Aria carpinifolia

Aria carpinifolia, commonly known as the false hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.

Aria carpinifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial to full shade.
