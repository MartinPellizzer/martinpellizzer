Anthemis tinctoria subsp. triumfetti

Anthemis tinctoria subsp.

triumfetti, commonly known as golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a composite head surrounded by ray florets.

The plant is herbaceous, with a bushy growth habit and aromatic leaves that emit a strong, bitter scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
