Allophylus leucophloeus

Allophylus leucophloeus, commonly known as the white-leafed hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Allophylus leucophloeus is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
