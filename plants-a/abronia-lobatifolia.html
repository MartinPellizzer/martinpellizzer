Abronia lobatifolia

Abronia lobatifolia, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to the sandy shores of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and unique, inflated leaves that resemble bladders. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, saline environments and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
