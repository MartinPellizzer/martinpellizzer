Acrosorus merrillii

Acrosorus merrillii, commonly known as the Singapore tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of fertile fronds that bear spore-bearing structures called sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The botanical features of Acrosorus merrillii make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
