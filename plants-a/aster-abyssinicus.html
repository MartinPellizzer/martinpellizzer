Aster abyssinicus

Aster abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian aster, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster abyssinicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
