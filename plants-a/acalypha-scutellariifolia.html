Acalypha scutellariifolia

Acalypha scutellariifolia, commonly known as the "spider plant" or "false nettle," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that resemble a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are often followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Acalypha scutellariifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
