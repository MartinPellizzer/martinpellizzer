Astragalus skvortsovii

Astragalus skvortsovii, commonly known as the Skvortsov's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed pods contain small, kidney-shaped seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus skvortsovii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
