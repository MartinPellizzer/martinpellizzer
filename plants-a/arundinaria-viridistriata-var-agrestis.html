Arundinaria viridistriata var. agrestis

Arundinaria viridistriata var. agrestis, commonly known as the green-streaked bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often green with distinct white or yellow streaks. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
