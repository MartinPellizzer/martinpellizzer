Allium scorodoprasum subsp. rotundum

Allium scorodoprasum subsp.

rotundum, commonly known as round-leaved garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers.

The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its mild garlic-like aroma and edible leaves.
