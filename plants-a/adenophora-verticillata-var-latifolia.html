Adenophora verticillata var. latifolia

Adenophora verticillata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are broad and often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
