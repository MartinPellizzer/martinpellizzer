Aconitum divergens f. pallidum

Aconitum divergens f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale form of the European monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, helmet-shaped blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
