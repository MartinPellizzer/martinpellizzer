Aconitum franchetii var. glabrescens

Aconitum franchetii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, rough texture.

Aconitum franchetii var. glabrescens is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it both a dangerous and valuable plant in traditional medicine.
