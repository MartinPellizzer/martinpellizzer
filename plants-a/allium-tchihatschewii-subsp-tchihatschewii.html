Allium tchihatschewii subsp. tchihatschewii

Allium tchihatschewii subsp.

tchihatschewii, commonly known as the Tchihatschew's leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers.

The plant has a strong onion-like odor and is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in certain regions.
