Allium bolanderi var. stenanthum

Allium bolanderi var. stenanthum, commonly known as the Bolander's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to California.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and chaparral. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, though they are not as commonly cultivated as other allium species.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native range.
