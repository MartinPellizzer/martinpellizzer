Amaranthus blitum subsp. emarginatus

Amaranthus blitum subsp.

emarginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems covered with dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces small, clustered green flowers that develop into three-angled, bead-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
