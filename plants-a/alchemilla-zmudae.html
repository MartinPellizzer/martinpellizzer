Alchemilla zmudae

Alchemilla zmudae, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla zmudae is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
