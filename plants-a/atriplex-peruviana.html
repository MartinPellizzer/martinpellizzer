Atriplex peruviana

Atriplex peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in dry environments. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded lands. Its small, greenish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Atriplex peruviana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
