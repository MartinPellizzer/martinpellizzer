Acalypha saxicola

Acalypha saxicola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its structure is low-growing, with a sprawling habit that helps it conserve moisture.

Acalypha saxicola is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
