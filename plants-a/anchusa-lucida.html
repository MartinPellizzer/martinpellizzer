Anchusa lucida

Anchusa lucida, commonly known as the blueweed or narrow-leaved anchusa, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Anchusa lucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
