Arrojadoa penicillata

Arrojadoa penicillata, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Arrojadoa penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
