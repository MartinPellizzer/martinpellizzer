Acacia sieberiana f. eusieberiana

Acacia sieberiana f. eusieberiana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are delicate and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its small, woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize open areas.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland restoration projects.
