Acer platanoides var. macrocarpum

Acer platanoides var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant maple, is a variant of the Norway maple.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
