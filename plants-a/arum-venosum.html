Arum venosum

Arum venosum, commonly known as the veiny arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces a spadix, a fleshy spike covered in small, clustered flowers, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Arum venosum is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
