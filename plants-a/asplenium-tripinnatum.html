Asplenium tripinnatum

Asplenium tripinnatum, commonly known as the three-pinnate spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Asplenium tripinnatum is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical collections.
