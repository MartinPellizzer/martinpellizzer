Acacia pennata subsp. kerrii

Acacia pennata subsp.

kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in spikes.

The plant is adapted to dry and sandy soils, often found in coastal and heathland environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender form and specific geographic distribution.
