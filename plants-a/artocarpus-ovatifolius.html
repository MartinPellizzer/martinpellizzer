Artocarpus ovatifolius

Artocarpus ovatifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fruit that is typically oval in shape and has a fibrous, starchy interior. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is well adapted to coastal and lowland environments.
