Alsophila pycnocarpa

Alsophila pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruit tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its fronds, which are typically clustered in dense, compact clusters. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling the plant to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Alsophila pycnocarpa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add a tropical aesthetic to gardens.
