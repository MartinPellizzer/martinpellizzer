Astragalus aleppicus

Astragalus aleppicus, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps it thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Astragalus aleppicus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
