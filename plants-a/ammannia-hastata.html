Ammannia hastata

Ammannia hastata, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, clustered flowers are pink to purple and bloom in late summer.

Ammannia hastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
