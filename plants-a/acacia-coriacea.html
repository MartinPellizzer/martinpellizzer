Acacia coriacea

Acacia coriacea, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, bark that is often fissured and grayish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which appear during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia coriacea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
