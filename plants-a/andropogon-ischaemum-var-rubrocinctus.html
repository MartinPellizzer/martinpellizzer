Andropogon ischaemum var. rubrocinctus

Andropogon ischaemum var. rubrocinctus, commonly known as the red-belted bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with reddish-brown bracts, giving it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
