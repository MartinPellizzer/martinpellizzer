Asparagus lecardii

Asparagus lecardii, commonly known as the narrowleaf asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and unique fruiting structure.
