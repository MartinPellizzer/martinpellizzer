Aegilops macrochaeta subsp. pontica

Aegilops macrochaeta subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic wild rye, is a grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in loose clusters along the stem. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
