Aristolochia oaxacana

Aristolochia oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive pipe-shaped, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
