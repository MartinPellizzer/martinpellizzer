Alopecias vulpinus

Alopecias vulpinus, commonly known as the foxglove, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Alopecias vulpinus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
