Amaranthus albus var. rubicundus

Amaranthus albus var. rubicundus, commonly known as white amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for both ornamental and edible gardening.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its potential use in culinary applications.
