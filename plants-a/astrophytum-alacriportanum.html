Astrophytum alacriportanum

Astrophytum alacriportanum, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that resembles a candle, covered in sparse, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
