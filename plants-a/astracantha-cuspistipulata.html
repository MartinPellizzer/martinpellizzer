Astracantha cuspistipulata

Astracantha cuspistipulata, commonly known as the toothed-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and narrow, toothed leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
