Aster graveolens var. pyramidalis

Aster graveolens var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
