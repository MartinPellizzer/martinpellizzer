Aldama angustissima

Aldama angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf aldama, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
