Aphelandra neesiana

Aphelandra neesiana, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, elongated, bright yellow bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

Aphelandra neesiana is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
