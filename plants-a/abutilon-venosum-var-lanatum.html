Abutilon venosum var. lanatum

Abutilon venosum var. lanatum, commonly known as the woolly abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Native to South America, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
