Acer pubescens

Acer pubescens, commonly known as the downy maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a pubescent underside. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring followed by winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
