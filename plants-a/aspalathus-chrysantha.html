Aspalathus chrysantha

Aspalathus chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and as a garden plant.
