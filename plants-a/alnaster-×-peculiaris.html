Alnaster × peculiaris

Alnaster × peculiaris, commonly known as the hybrid alnaster, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the cross between two different alnaster species.

It exhibits a combination of morphological traits from its parent plants, often displaying distinct foliage and floral characteristics. The species is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, contributing to the genetic diversity of the alnaster genus.

Due to its rarity, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
