Aconitum hebegynum

Aconitum hebegynum, commonly known as the broad-leaved aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.

Morphology

Aconitum hebegynum has leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, arranged alternately along the stem.

The stem is smooth and cylindrical, typically greenish with a slightly hairy texture, and grows upright with a branching pattern. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem, with a complex structure featuring showy petals and spurs, blooming in late summer and attracting pollinators like bees through nectar. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release small, flat seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are thick, fibrous, and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil.
