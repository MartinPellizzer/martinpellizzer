Akebia quinata var. diplochlamys

Akebia quinata var. diplochlamys, commonly known as the Japanese arrow bamboo, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
