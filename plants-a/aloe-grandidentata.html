Aloe grandidentata

Aloe grandidentata, commonly known as the desert aloe or giant aloe, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aloe grandidentata is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive prolonged droughts.

Its resilience and unique appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
