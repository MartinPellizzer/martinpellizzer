Arabidella trisecta var. hybophora

Arabidella trisecta var. hybophora, commonly known as the three-lobed rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are divided into three distinct lobes. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.
