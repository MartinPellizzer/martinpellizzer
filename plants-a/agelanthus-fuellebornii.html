Agelanthus fuellebornii

Agelanthus fuellebornii, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Agelanthus fuellebornii plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
