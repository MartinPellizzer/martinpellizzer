Arisaema akiense var. nakaianum

Arisaema akiense var. nakaianum, commonly known as the Japanese flame spike, is a variant of the arisaema genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a flame, surrounded by a broad, greenish-white spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and distinctive floral structure.
