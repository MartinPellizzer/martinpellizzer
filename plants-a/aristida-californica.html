Aristida californica

Aristida californica, commonly known as California threeawn, is a native grass species found in coastal and inland regions of California.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, hairy texture that aids in water conservation.

Aristida californica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
