Acacia virchowiana

Acacia virchowiana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Acacia virchowiana is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
