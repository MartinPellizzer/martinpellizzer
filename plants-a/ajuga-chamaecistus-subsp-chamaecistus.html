Ajuga chamaecistus subsp. chamaecistus

Ajuga chamaecistus subsp.

chamaecistus, commonly known as the Persian bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread and colonize rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
