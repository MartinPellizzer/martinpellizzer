Acacia michelii

Acacia michelii, commonly known as the Michel's acacia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers that bloom in winter, attracting a variety of pollinators. Acacia michelii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
