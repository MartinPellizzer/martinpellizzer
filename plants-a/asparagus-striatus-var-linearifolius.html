Asparagus striatus var. linearifolius

Asparagus striatus var. linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow close to the ground. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its characteristic appearance. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
