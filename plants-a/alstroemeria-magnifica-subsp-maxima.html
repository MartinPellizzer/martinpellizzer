Alstroemeria magnifica subsp. maxima

Alstroemeria magnifica subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

The plant produces numerous narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms contribute to its value in both horticulture and botanical studies.
