Acer platanoides f. rubrum

Acer platanoides f. rubrum, commonly known as the red maple, is a cultivar of the red maple tree known for its vibrant autumn foliage.

It is a deciduous tree with a broad, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with coarsely toothed margins and a prominent midrib. In spring, it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to a range of soil types and climates.
