Atalantia floribunda

Atalantia floribunda, commonly known as the golden glory tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
