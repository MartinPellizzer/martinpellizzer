Aconitum wagneri

Aconitum wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Aconitum wagneri is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it both a dangerous and valuable plant in botanical and medicinal studies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
