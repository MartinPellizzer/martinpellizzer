Astragalus garbancillo var. mandonii

Astragalus garbancillo var. mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
