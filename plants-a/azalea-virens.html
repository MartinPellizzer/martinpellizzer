Azalea virens

Azalea virens, commonly known as the greenleaf azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Azalea virens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form thickets in its natural habitat.
