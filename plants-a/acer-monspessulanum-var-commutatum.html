Acer monspessulanum var. commutatum

Acer monspessulanum var. commutatum, commonly known as the Spanish maple, is a deciduous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
