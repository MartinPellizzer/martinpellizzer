Actinidia callosa var. pilosula

Actinidia callosa var. pilosula, commonly known as the downy actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.

The variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
