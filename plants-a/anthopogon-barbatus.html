Anthopogon barbatus

Anthopogon barbatus, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, which contribute to its common name.

Anthopogon barbatus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
