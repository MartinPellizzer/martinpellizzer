Acacia isenbergiana

Acacia isenbergiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Acacia isenbergiana is also valued for its hardiness and use in landscaping.
