Arisaema sprengerianum

Arisaema sprengerianum, commonly known as the Sprenger's arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, hence the common name, and its flowers are usually green or yellowish.

Arisaema sprengerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
