Azara crassifolia

Azara crassifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *azara*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. Azara crassifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
