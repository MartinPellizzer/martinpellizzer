Acronema mukherjeeanum

Acronema mukherjeeanum, commonly known as the Mukherjee's acronema, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of other members of its family. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
