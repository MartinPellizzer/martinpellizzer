Actinocyclus secundus

Actinocyclus secundus, commonly known as the secondary actinocyst, is a species of diatom belonging to the class Bacillariophyceae.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated shell composed of silica, which forms intricate patterns on both the valve and the raphe. The organism is typically found in freshwater environments, where it plays a role in the aquatic food web. Its small size and delicate structure make it a subject of interest in ecological and paleontological studies.

The species is often used as an indicator of water quality due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
