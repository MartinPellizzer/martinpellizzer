Amaranthus retroflexus subsp. patulus

Amaranthus retroflexus subsp.

patulus, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that develop into three-angled, spiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a competitive weed in various ecosystems.
