Aristida pallida

Aristida pallida, commonly known as pale needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed sites. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a pale green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer to early fall.

Aristida pallida is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
