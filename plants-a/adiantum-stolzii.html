Adiantum stolzii

Adiantum stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi-lobed. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Adiantum stolzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
