Acacia euthyphylla

Acacia euthyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and its distinctive, narrow, phyllode-like leaves that are typically green and hairless. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in winter, which are arranged in spikes and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia euthyphylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a capacity for resprouting after fire.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is an important component of local ecosystems.
