Aster inornatus

Aster inornatus, commonly known as the white aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Aster inornatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
