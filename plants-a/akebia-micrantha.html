Akebia micrantha

Akebia micrantha, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its unique, fragrant, and tubular flowers that bloom before the leaves appear. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Akebia micrantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
