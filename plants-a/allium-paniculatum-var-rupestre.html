Allium paniculatum var. rupestre

Allium paniculatum var. rupestre, commonly known as the rock garlic or mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright, branched inflorescences of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or arid environments. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and poor soil conditions.
