Amorphophallus campanulatus var. blumei

Amorphophallus campanulatus var. blumei, commonly known as the Blume's elephant's foot, is a striking plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, swollen, tuberous stem that resembles an elephant's foot, which stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern, often with a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habit, it is also valued in traditional medicine and horticulture.
