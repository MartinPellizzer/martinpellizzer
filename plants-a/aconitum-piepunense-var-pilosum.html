Aconitum piepunense var. pilosum

Aconitum piepunense var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy piepun aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided, silky-hairy leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance.

This species is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
