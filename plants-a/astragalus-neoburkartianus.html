Astragalus neoburkartianus

Astragalus neoburkartianus, commonly known as the Neoburkartian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
