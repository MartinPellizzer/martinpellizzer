Artemisia vulgaris subsp. michauxiana

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

michauxiana, commonly known as white mugwort, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray foliage that is aromatic when crushed.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Its chemical composition includes volatile oils, sesquiterpene lactones, and other compounds that contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal uses.
