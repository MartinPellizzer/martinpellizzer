Arabidopsis mollissima var. griffithiana

Arabidopsis mollissima var. griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's hairy Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The species is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential for genetic research.
