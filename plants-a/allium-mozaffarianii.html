Allium mozaffarianii

Allium mozaffarianii, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, onion-like scent.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
