Acacia sentis var. victoriae

Acacia sentis var. victoriae, commonly known as the Victoria acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow globular flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
