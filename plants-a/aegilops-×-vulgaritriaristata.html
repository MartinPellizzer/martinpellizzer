Aegilops × vulgaritriaristata

Aegilops × vulgaritriaristata, commonly known as the wild rye grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This species is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improving wheat genetics through hybridization.
