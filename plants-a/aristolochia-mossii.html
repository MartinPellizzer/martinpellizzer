Aristolochia mossii

Aristolochia mossii, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a snake. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are typically vine-like.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
