Acacia tetragonocarpa var. scabra

Acacia tetragonocarpa var. scabra, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular, ridged stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. Its small, hard pods contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
