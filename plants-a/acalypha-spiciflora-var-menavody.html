Acalypha spiciflora var. menavody

Acalypha spiciflora var. menavody, commonly known as the Menavody spurflower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and vibrant, spiculate inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and often have a reddish or purple tinge. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
