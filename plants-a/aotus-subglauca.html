Aotus subglauca

Aotus subglauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
