Anomospermum japurense

Anomospermum japurense, commonly known as the Japurensis tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
