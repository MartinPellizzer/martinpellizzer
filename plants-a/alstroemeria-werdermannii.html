Alstroemeria werdermannii

Alstroemeria werdermannii, commonly known as the Werdermann's alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Alstroemeria werdermannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
