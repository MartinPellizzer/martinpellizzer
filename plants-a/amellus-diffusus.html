Amellus diffusus

Amellus diffusus, commonly known as the spreading amellus, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Amellus diffusus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
