Artemisia samoiedorum

Artemisia samoiedorum, commonly known as the Samoied SAGE, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Artemisia samoiedorum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in various cultures for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
