Argemone mexicana var. typica

Argemone mexicana var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features bright orange to red flowers with prominent yellow markings, which bloom in late summer. The plant has spiny, lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and medicinal studies.
