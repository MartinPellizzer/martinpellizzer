Amaryllis × cuprea

Amaryllis × cuprea, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis × cuprea is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
