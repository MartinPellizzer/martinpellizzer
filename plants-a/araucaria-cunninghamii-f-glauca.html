Araucaria cunninghamii f. glauca

Araucaria cunninghamii f. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous Norfolk pine, is a cultivar of the Norfolk pine native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant has a tall, slender trunk with a conical shape and produces small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
