Andropogon inermis

Andropogon inermis, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Switchgrass is a hardy plant that thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly drought-resistant. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.

Additionally, it is a valuable source of biomass for renewable energy production.
