Arisaema dolosum

Arisaema dolosum, commonly known as the deceptive arum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, hooded spathe and a spadix that bears small, clustered flowers. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a glossy surface.

Arisaema dolosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
