Aristida plumosa subsp. kyzylkumica

Aristida plumosa subsp.

kyzylkumica, commonly known as the Kyzylkum feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
