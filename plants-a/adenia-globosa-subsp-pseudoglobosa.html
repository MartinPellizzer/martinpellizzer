Adenia globosa subsp. pseudoglobosa

Adenia globosa subsp.

pseudoglobosa, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped flowers.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
