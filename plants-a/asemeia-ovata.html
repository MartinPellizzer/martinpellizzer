Asemeia ovata

Asemeia ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are ovate in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed berries that are dispersed by water.

Asemeia ovata plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
