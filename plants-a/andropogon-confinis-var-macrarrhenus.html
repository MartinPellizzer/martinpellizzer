Andropogon confinis var. macrarrhenus

Andropogon confinis var. macrarrhenus, commonly known as the long-awned bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
