Anthemis candicans

Anthemis candicans, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in chaparral and grassland habitats. Its showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage, which contains compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
