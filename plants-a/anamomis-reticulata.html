Anamomis reticulata

Anamomis reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, reticulate venation pattern on its leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
