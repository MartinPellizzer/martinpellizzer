Anthyllis × gamisansii

Anthyllis × gamisansii, commonly known as the hybrid sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellow or reddish flowers in spike-like clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is well-developed, enabling it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Due to its ornamental and forage qualities, it is cultivated in some parts of Europe and the Middle East.
