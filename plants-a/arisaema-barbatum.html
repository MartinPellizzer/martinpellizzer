Arisaema barbatum

Arisaema barbatum, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. Native to East Asia, particularly China, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
