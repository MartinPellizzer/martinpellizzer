Achyrophorus helveticus

Achyrophorus helveticus, commonly known as the Swiss alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Achyrophorus helveticus is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing habitat for various insects.
