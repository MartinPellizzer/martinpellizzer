Acanthocarpea scabrida

Acanthocarpea scabrida, commonly known as the rough acanthocarpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers partial shade.
