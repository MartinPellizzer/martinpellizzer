Acacia sieberiana var. rehmanniana

Acacia sieberiana var. rehmanniana, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and compound leaves composed of numerous small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are encased in woody pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in seed dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
