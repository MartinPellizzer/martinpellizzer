Apocynum × pseudomedium

Apocynum × pseudomedium, commonly known as the Indian hemp or wild jimsonweed, is a hybrid plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open woodlands and along roadsides. Its leaves are usually opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are trumpet-shaped, often pink or purple, and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
