Astragalus preussii

Astragalus preussii, commonly known as Preuss's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems that help it survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to drought and nutrient-poor soils.
