Acacia melalobiata

Acacia melalobiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and narrow, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typically followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Acacia melalobiata is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
