Anthemis zaianica

Anthemis zaianica, commonly known as the Zaian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are linear and aromatic, emitting a mild chamomile scent.

The species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
