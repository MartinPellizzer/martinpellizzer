Alpinia zerumbet

Alpinia zerumbet, commonly known as flame grass or shell flower, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.

Alpinia zerumbet is also valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine.
