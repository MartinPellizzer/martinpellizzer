Ammannia coccinea subsp. longifolia

Ammannia coccinea subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features striking red stems that are particularly prominent during the growing season.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
