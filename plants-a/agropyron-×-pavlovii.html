Agropyron × pavlovii

Agropyron × pavlovii, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a polyploid grass species resulting from the cross between Agropyron cristatum and Agropyron elongatum.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization, making it valuable for erosion control.

Agropyron × pavlovii is also cultivated for forage and has potential agricultural applications.
