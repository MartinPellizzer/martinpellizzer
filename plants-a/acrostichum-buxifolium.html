Acrostichum buxifolium

Acrostichum buxifolium, commonly known as the box-leaved fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that are oval in shape and have a distinctive boxwood-like texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
