Aspidium auriculatum var. submarginale

Aspidium auriculatum var. submarginale, commonly known as the marginal maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with the margins often showing a distinctive pale or brownish border. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
