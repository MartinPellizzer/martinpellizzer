Adiantum killipii

Adiantum killipii, commonly known as the Killip's maidenhair, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Adiantaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the wings of a bird. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which is a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This rare and striking fern is of interest to botanists due to its unusual ecological traits and limited distribution.
