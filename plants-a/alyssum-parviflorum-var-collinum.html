Alyssum parviflorum var. collinum

Alyssum parviflorum var. collinum, commonly known as the California alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the western United States.

It features tiny, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a slightly fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is particularly valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
