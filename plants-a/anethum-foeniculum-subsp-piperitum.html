Anethum foeniculum subsp. piperitum

Anethum foeniculum subsp.

piperitum, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features feathery, aromatic leaves and slender, yellowish-green stems.

The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are often used as a spice, known for their licorice-like flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
