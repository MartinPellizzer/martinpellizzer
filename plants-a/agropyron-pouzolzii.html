Agropyron pouzolzii

Agropyron pouzolzii, commonly known as the mountain wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Agropyron pouzolzii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
