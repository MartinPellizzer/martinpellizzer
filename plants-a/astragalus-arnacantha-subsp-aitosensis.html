Astragalus arnacantha subsp. aitosensis

Astragalus arnacantha subsp.

aitosensis, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is known for producing a milky sap, which gives it its common name.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in its native range.
