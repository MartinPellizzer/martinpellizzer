Anacheilium marcilianum

Anacheilium marcilianum, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
