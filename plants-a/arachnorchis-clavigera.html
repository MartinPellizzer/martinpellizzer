Arachnorchis clavigera

Arachnorchis clavigera, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin sepals and petals that resemble legs. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom during the spring and summer months.

Arachnorchis clavigera is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological specificity.
