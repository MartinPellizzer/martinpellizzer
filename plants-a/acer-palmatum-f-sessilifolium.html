Acer palmatum f. sessilifolium

Acer palmatum f. sessilifolium, commonly known as the Japanese maple with sessile leaves, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely cut leaves that are short-stemmed or sessile, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its foliage changes color through the seasons, displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall.

It prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
