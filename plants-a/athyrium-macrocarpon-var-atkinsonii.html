Athyrium macrocarpon var. atkinsonii

Athyrium macrocarpon var. atkinsonii, commonly known as the Atkinson's mountain shield fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. The plant thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a sensitive indicator species for forest health and biodiversity.
