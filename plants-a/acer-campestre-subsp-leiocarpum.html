Acer campestre subsp. leiocarpum

Acer campestre subsp.

leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-leaf horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its oval, glossy, dark green leaves that remain smooth and hairless on both sides.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by spiny, orange-brown fruits. Its bark is typically smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
