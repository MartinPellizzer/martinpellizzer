Actinopappus drummondii

Actinopappus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. Its leaves are generally lobed or toothed, and the flowers are usually yellow, with a distinctive purple or brown coloration on the bracts.

The plant produces a crown of persistent, woolly bracts that give it a unique appearance and aid in seed dispersal.
