Artocarpus cuspidatus

Artocarpus cuspidatus, commonly known as the giant breadfruit, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit, which can grow up to 30 cm in length, develops from an inflorescence and contains a fibrous, starchy pulp. The plant is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction, as well as for its ornamental qualities.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
