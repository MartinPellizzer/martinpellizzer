Alopecurus alpinus var. stejnegeri

Alopecurus alpinus var. stejnegeri, commonly known as the alpine meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows, rocky slopes, and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
