Asplenium parvulum grandidentatum

Asplenium parvulum grandidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with small, serrated leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Asplenium parvulum grandidentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
