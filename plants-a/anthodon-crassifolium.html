Anthodon crassifolium

Anthodon crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved anthodon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
