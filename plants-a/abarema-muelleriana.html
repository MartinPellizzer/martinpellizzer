Abarema muelleriana

Abarema muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's sensitive plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its rapid leaf movement in response to touch, a trait called thigmonastic response. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats within tropical forests.
