Astragalus absentivus

Astragalus absentivus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus absentivus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
