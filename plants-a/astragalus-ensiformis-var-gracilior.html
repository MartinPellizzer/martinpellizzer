Astragalus ensiformis var. gracilior

Astragalus ensiformis var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several small, rounded seeds.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
