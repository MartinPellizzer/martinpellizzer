Anthurium digitatum var. connatum

Anthurium digitatum var. connatum, commonly known as the Connatum Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and garden displays.
