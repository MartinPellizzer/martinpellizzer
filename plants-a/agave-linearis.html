Agave linearis

Agave linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and long, narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or yellowish flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave linearis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
