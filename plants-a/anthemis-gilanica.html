Anthemis gilanica

Anthemis gilanica, commonly known as Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
