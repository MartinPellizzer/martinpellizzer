Aloe prostrata subsp. pallida

Aloe prostrata subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale aloe, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green to blue-gray in color, often with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in branched clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also popular as ornamental plants.
