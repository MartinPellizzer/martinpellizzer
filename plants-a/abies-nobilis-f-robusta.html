Abies nobilis f. robusta

Abies nobilis f. robusta, commonly known as the White Fir, is a subspecies of the Noble Fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones that hang from the branches. The tree can reach heights of up to 80 meters, making it one of the tallest fir species. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing some protection against forest fires.

This evergreen is often found in high-altitude forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
