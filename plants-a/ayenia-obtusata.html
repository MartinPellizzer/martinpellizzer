Ayenia obtusata

Ayenia obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf ayenia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow to orange composite flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ayenia obtusata is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
