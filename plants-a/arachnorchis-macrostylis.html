Arachnorchis macrostylis

Arachnorchis macrostylis, commonly known as the large-styled spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with a slender, wiry stem and small, greenish-brown flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of delicate, pale yellow to brownish flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or under dense vegetation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
