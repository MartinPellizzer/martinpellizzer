Acacia adjutrices

Acacia adjutrices, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Acacia adjutrices is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
