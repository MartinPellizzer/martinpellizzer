Alpinia velveta

Alpinia velveta, commonly known as velvet ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Alpinia velveta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
