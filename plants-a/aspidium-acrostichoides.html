Aspidium acrostichoides

Aspidium acrostichoides, commonly known as the broad fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The fronds are typically divided into a series of pinnae, which are arranged in a pinnate pattern. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
