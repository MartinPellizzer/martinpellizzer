Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. palustris

Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. palustris, commonly known as swamp millet, is a perennial grass native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
