Astragalus wallii

Astragalus wallii, commonly known as the Turkish milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus wallii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
