Anigozanthos bicolor var. minor

Anigozanthos bicolor var. minor, commonly known as the small-stemmed emu bush, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters, which are attracted to its nectar.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
