Achillea tyrolensis

Achillea tyrolensis, commonly known as the Tyrol yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, often forming dense, low-growing mats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
