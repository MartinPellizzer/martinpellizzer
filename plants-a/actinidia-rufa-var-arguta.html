Actinidia rufa var. arguta

Actinidia rufa var. arguta, commonly known as the red actinidia or the red kiwi, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, fuzzy, orange-red berries that are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a rough texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
