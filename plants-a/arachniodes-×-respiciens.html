Arachniodes × respiciens

Arachniodes × respiciens, commonly known as the spider fern, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Arachniodes felix-marmoratus and Arachniodes standishii.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The fronds are bi-pinnate, with a distinctive spider-like appearance due to their finely divided, arching pinnae. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create a lush, tropical ambiance in gardens and greenhouses.
