Arachnorchis rosella

Arachnorchis rosella, commonly known as the Rosella Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble delicate webbing. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rainforest undergrowth, where it thrives in the cool, humid climate. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Arachnorchis rosella is a rare and protected species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its delicate ecosystem.
