Aspidium barberi

Aspidium barberi, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and form dense colonies, which help it spread vegetatively.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its natural habitat.
