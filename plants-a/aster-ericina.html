Aster ericina

Aster ericina, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink petals surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aster ericina typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
