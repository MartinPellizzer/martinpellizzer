Arundinaria kanayamensis

Arundinaria kanayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Kanayama bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinaria kanayamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
