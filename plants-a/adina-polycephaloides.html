Adina polycephaloides

Adina polycephaloides, commonly known as the clustered adina, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense clusters of small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Adina polycephaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
