Aechmea saxicola

Aechmea saxicola, commonly known as the rock aecharis, is a bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a compact, terrestrial plant that typically grows on rocky substrates, showcasing a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a flowering spike that bears small, violet-colored flowers, which are clustered in a spadix surrounded by modified leaves. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Aechmea saxicola is appreciated for its unique appearance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Morphology

Aechmea saxicola has rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and sharply pointed, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem, with a waxy blue-green color.

The stem is short and often hidden beneath the leaves, with a rough, fibrous texture and a light green to reddish-brown color. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with white to pale purple petals, blooming in the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate.
