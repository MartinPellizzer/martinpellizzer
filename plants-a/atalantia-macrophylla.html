Atalantia macrophylla

Atalantia macrophylla, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red fruits that resemble lanterns, which are often used in traditional festivals. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
