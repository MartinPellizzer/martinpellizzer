Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp. zacaensis

Arctostaphylos glandulosa subsp.

zacaensis, commonly known as the Zaca manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and reddish-brown bark.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
