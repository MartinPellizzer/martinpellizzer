Amaryllis aurea

Amaryllis aurea, commonly known as the golden amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a golden or yellowish hue. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis aurea is popular for its long blooming period and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
