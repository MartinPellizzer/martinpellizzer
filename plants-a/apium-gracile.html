Apium gracile

Apium gracile, commonly known as slender parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and celery. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, green leaves that are finely divided. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in umbels, typical of the family.

Apium gracile is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and aromatic foliage.
