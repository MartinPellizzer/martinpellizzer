Asarum sieboldii f. koreanum

Asarum sieboldii f. koreanum, commonly known as Korean wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the forests of Korea.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
