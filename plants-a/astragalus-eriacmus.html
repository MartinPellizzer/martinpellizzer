Astragalus eriacmus

Astragalus eriacmus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous narrow, linear leaflets.

Astragalus eriacmus is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
