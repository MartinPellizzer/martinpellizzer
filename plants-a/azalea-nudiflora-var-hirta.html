Azalea nudiflora var. hirta

Azalea nudiflora var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered with fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
