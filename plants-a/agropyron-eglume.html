Agropyron eglume

Agropyron eglume, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in dry, nutrient-poor environments. Its seeds are wind-dispersed, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
