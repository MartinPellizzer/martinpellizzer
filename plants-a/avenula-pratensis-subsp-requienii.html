Avenula pratensis subsp. requienii

Avenula pratensis subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the slender field sedge, is a grass-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and wetlands. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and a preference for temperate climates.

It plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
