Aconitum gracile f. saxonicum

Aconitum gracile f. saxonicum, commonly known as the Saxony slender monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care.
