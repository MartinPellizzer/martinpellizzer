Alepidea coarctata

Alepidea coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are narrow and somewhat compressed, with a fibrous root system. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on upright flowering stems.

Alepidea coarctata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
