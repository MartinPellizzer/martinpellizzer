Astragalus tmoleus var. bounacanthus

Astragalus tmoleus var. bounacanthus, commonly known as the Bouna-canthus milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
