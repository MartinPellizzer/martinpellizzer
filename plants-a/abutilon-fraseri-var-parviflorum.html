Abutilon fraseri var. parviflorum

Abutilon fraseri var. parviflorum, commonly known as the narrowflower abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
