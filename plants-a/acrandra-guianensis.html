Acrandra guianensis

Acrandra guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Acrandra guianensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
