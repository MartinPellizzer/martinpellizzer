Ancistrophyllum robustum

Ancistrophyllum robustum, commonly known as the robust leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Ancistrophyllum robustum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun conditions.
