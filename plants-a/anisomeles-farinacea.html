Anisomeles farinacea

Anisomeles farinacea, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sticky leaves that exude a viscous, resinous substance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Anisomeles farinacea thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
