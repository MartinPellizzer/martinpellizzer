Aldama sodiroi

Aldama sodiroi, commonly known as the Mexican alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters. Aldama sodiroi is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
