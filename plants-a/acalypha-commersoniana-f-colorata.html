Acalypha commersoniana f. colorata

Acalypha commersoniana f. colorata, commonly known as the Crimson Cudweed, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and container planting.
