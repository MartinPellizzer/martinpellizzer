Artemisia ludoviciana var. americana

Artemisia ludoviciana var. americana, commonly known as American tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and historical use in herbal medicine.
