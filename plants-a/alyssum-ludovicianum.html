Alyssum ludovicianum

Alyssum ludovicianum, commonly known as the purple alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant features silvery-gray foliage and produces numerous tiny, fragrant blooms throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum ludovicianum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
