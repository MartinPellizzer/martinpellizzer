Adenophora gmelinii subsp. subjenisseensis

Adenophora gmelinii subsp.

subjenisseensis, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to northeastern Asia. It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching near the top.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest clearings and rocky slopes.
