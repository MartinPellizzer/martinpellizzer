Arundinaria fortunei f. viridis

Arundinaria fortunei f. viridis, commonly known as the green bamboo or green bamboo fern, is a variant of the bamboo plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green shoots and dense, grass-like foliage. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its culms are flexible and can reach several feet in height, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for water gardens and shaded landscapes.
