Agoseris subalpina

Agoseris subalpina, commonly known as the alpine goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Agoseris subalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
