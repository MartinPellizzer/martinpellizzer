Anthurium harrisii var. assimile

Anthurium harrisii var. assimile, commonly known as the red-tipped anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent red or pink vein running down the center. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a brightly colored spathe, which is often a vibrant red or orange. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
