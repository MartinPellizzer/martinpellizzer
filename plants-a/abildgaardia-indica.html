Abildgaardia indica

Abildgaardia indica, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood that is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now classified as an endangered species.
