Anthericum benguellense

Anthericum benguellense, commonly known as the Benguella arum, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features a tall, leafless stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting a large, showy spathe that is typically green with a purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers inside the spathe, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Anthericum benguellense is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, coastal environments.
