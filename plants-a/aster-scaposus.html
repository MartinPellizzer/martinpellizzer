Aster scaposus

Aster scaposus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
