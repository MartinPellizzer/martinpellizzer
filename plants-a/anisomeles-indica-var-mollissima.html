Anisomeles indica var. mollissima

Anisomeles indica var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky anise plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is known for its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
