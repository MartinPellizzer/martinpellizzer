Agave integrifolia

Agave integrifolia, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are blue-gray in color and have smooth margins. It produces an inflorescence of white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Agave integrifolia is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional uses.
