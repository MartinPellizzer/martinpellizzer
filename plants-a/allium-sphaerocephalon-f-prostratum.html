Allium sphaerocephalon f. prostratum

Allium sphaerocephalon f. prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate leek, is a botanical variety characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit.

It features elongated, cylindrical leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are often clustered at the base. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a dense umbel.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its compact growth and ornamental appeal.
