Arundinaria tenuifolia

Arundinaria tenuifolia, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative plant. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often used in floral arrangements.

Arundinaria tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
