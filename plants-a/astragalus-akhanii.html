Astragalus akhanii

Astragalus akhanii, commonly known as the Akhan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is often deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus akhanii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
