Apollonias canariensis

Apollonias canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a tropical plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, although it is not as productive as other date palm species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Apollonias canariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in the Canary Islands.
