Ardisia diffusa

Ardisia diffusa, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval-shaped and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Ardisia diffusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
