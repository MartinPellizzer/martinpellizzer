Abrus stictosperma

Abrus stictosperma, commonly known as the red bittersweet.

It is a climbing legume native to parts of Africa and Asia, characterized by its distinctive red, bead-like pods that contain toxic seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with numerous leaflets. Its seeds contain a potent toxin, abrin, which makes them highly dangerous if ingested.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine, though with caution due to its toxic properties.
