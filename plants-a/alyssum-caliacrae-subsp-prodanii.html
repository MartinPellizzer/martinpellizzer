Alyssum caliacrae subsp. prodanii

Alyssum caliacrae subsp.

prodanii, commonly known as the Cilicia Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and hairy, adapted to thrive in arid or rocky environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
