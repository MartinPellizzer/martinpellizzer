Alcea striata var. assyriaca

Alcea striata var. assyriaca, commonly known as the Assyrian hollyhock, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, rose-like flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmate leaves that are lobed and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.
