Allium drusorum

Allium drusorum, commonly known as the bulbous leek, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a bulb composed of clustered, fleshy, white or pale green cloves, which are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor. The plant produces tall, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
