Adesmia verrucosa

Adesmia verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly legume, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Adesmia verrucosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
