Abies montana

Abies montana, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking silver-white underneath. The tree produces small, spherical cones that release seeds in the spring, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its bark is typically gray and furrowed, providing a distinctive texture.

Abies montana thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-elevation forests.
