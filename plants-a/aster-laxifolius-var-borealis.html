Aster laxifolius var. borealis

Aster laxifolius var. borealis, commonly known as the northern aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and tundra. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
