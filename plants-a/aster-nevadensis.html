Aster nevadensis

Aster nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its purple to pinkish flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This perennial species typically grows in moist meadows and stream banks, thriving in cooler climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
