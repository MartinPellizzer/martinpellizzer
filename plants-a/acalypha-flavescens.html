Acalypha flavescens

Acalypha flavescens, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green leaves, which often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Acalypha flavescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
