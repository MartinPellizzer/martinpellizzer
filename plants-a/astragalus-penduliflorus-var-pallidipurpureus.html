Astragalus penduliflorus var. pallidipurpureus

Astragalus penduliflorus var. pallidipurpureus, commonly known as the purple-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a distinctive violet tinge. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
