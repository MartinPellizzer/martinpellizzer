Artemisia rutifolia var. altaica

Artemisia rutifolia var. altaica, commonly known as the Altai wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, rounded shapes. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its resilience to harsh climates makes it a notable species in arid and alpine ecosystems.
