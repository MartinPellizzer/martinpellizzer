Alchemilla kolaensis

Alchemilla kolaensis, commonly known as the African lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla kolaensis is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
