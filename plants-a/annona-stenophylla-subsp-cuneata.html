Annona stenophylla subsp. cuneata

Annona stenophylla subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and fragrant, white flowers.

The plant produces a large, spiny fruit that is typically harvested when fully mature. The fruit's interior contains a soft, white pulp with numerous black seeds, and it is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
