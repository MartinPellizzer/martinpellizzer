Alyssum fulvescens var. fulvescens

Alyssum fulvescens var. fulvescens, commonly known as the golden alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has finely divided, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and profuse blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
