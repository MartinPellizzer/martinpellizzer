Arabis scapigera

Arabis scapigera, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, glaucous leaves and upright, branching stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and arctic regions. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
