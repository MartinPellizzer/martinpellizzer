Aconitum napellus var. densum

Aconitum napellus var. densum, commonly known as the dense aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features large, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, arranged in dense, conical clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and its roots contain potent toxins belonging to the alkaloid family.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a dangerous plant and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
