Acrocephalus kundelungensis

Acrocephalus kundelungensis, commonly known as the Kundelungu reed warbler, is a small passerine bird native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is characterized by its slender body, long tail, and distinctive yellowish-brown plumage. This species inhabits wetland areas, including marshes and swamps, where it nests in dense vegetation. The male exhibits a more vibrant yellow coloration during the breeding season, which is used to attract mates.

It plays an important role in the ecosystem by contributing to seed dispersal and insect control.
