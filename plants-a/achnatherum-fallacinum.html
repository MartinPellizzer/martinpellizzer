Achnatherum fallacinum

Achnatherum fallacinum, commonly known as the fall panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.
