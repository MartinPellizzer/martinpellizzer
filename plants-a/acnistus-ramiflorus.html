Acnistus ramiflorus

Acnistus ramiflorus, commonly known as the branched-flower acnistus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in branched clusters, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
