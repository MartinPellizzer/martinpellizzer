Aconitum orochryseum

Aconitum orochryseum, commonly known as the golden aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a spire-like cluster. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a deep green color and a velvety texture.

The golden aconite is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
