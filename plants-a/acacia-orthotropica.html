Acacia orthotropica

Acacia orthotropica, commonly known as the straight-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Acacia orthotropica is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
