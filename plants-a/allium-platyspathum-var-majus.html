Allium platyspathum var. majus

Allium platyspathum var. majus, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, grass-like foliage and produces large, showy purple or pink flowers in summer. The plant is characterized by its swollen, bulbous base that stores nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary use.
