Aster puniceus f. glabratus

Aster puniceus f. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features hairy, upright stems that become glabrous (smooth) with age, giving it its specific epithet "glabratus.". The plant produces showy, violet-blue flower heads in the late summer and early fall, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
