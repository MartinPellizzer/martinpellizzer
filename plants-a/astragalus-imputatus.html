Astragalus imputatus

Astragalus imputatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Astragalus imputatus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and botanical studies.
