Acacia macdonnelliensis subsp. macdonnelliensis

Acacia macdonnelliensis subsp.

macdonnelliensis, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which appear in spring and early summer. Its leaves are reduced to phyllodes, a common adaptation in acacia species to reduce water loss.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and along coastal dunes.
