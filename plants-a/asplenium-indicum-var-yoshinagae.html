Asplenium indicum var. yoshinagae

Asplenium indicum var. yoshinagae, commonly known as the Yoshinaga's spleenwort, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lanceolate or ovate, with a finely toothed margin.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and is of interest to botanists studying fern diversity.
