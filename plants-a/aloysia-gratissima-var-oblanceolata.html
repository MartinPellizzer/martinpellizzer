Aloysia gratissima var. oblanceolata

Aloysia gratissima var. oblanceolata, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves with a strongly aromatic, lemon-scented aroma. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and ornamental use.

Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy due to their calming properties.
