Aristolochia tournefortii var. macroglossa

Aristolochia tournefortii var. macroglossa, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musky odor. The leaves are heart-shaped and can grow quite large, often reaching up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are initially green and turn red when mature.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
