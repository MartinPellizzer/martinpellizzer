Azalea cumberlandensis

Azalea cumberlandensis, commonly known as the Cumberland azalea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Azalea cumberlandensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in woodland habitats.
