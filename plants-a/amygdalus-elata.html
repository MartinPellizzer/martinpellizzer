Amygdalus elata

Amygdalus elata, commonly known as the desert peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive bluish-gray color due to a dense covering of hairs.

Amygdalus elata is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in drought conditions.
