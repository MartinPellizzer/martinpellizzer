Allophylus brevipetiolaris

Allophylus brevipetiolaris, commonly known as the short-stemmed holly-leaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
