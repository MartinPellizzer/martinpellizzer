Acer bicolor var. serratifolium

Acer bicolor var. serratifolium, commonly known as the red maple with serrated leaves, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red and green foliage, which displays vibrant color changes in autumn. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
