Acrostichum assurgens

Acrostichum assurgens, commonly known as the giant reed or common reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, with a fibrous, hollow stem that is often used in construction and crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution across various habitats.
