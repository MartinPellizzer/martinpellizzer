Alstroemeria capixaba

Alstroemeria capixaba, commonly known as the Brazilian lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
