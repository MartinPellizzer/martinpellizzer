Astragalus dictamnoides

Astragalus dictamnoides, commonly known as the fragrant milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish to reddish pea-like flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus dictamnoides is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
