Anoectochilus setaceopictus

Anoectochilus setaceopictus, commonly known as the Orchid with Feathery Leaves, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
