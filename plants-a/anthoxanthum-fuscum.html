Anthoxanthum fuscum

Anthoxanthum fuscum, commonly known as the brown oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, pale brown florets.

Anthoxanthum fuscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
