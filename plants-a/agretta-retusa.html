Agretta retusa

Agretta retusa, commonly known as the slender agretta, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Agretta retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
