Anthyllis vulneraria proles pulchella

Anthyllis vulneraria proles pulchella, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or yellow sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, hairy pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
