Armeniaca vulgaris var. variegata

Armeniaca vulgaris var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree known for its distinctive foliage.

It features leaves with white or yellow variegation, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically smaller than standard cherries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and edible yield.
