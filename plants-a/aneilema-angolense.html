Aneilema angolense

Aneilema angolense, commonly known as the Angola arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aneilema angolense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
