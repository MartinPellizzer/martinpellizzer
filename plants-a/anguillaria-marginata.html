Anguillaria marginata

Anguillaria marginata, commonly known as the marginated anguillaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinct white margin, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Anguillaria marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
