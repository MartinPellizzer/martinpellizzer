Astragalus subangustidens

Astragalus subangustidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Astragalus subangustidens is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
