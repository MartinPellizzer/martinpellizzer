Aerva sericea

Aerva sericea, commonly known as silky aerva, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aerva sericea is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
