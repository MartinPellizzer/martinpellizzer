Anathallis napintzae

Anathallis napintzae, commonly known as the Napintza orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its small, clustered inflorescences and specialized root system are adaptations to its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
