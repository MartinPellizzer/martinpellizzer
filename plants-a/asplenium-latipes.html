Asplenium latipes

Asplenium latipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that can form dense mats. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Asplenium latipes is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor settings.
