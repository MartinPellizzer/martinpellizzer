Amsinckia abramsii

Amsinckia abramsii, commonly known as the desert smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, fluffy spheres. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pale yellow to white and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
