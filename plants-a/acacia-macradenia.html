Acacia macradenia

Acacia macradenia, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and its distinctive, flat, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically followed by flat, leathery pods. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
