Anisacanthus andersonii

Anisacanthus andersonii, commonly known as the Texas starburst, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Anisacanthus andersonii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
