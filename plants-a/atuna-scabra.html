Atuna scabra

Atuna scabra, commonly known as the rough atuna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Atuna scabra is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
