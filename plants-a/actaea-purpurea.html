Actaea purpurea

Actaea purpurea, commonly known as red baneberry, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking red berries that appear in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces white, clustered flowers in early summer. Its toxic properties make it a plant to be handled with care.

The berries are particularly poisonous to humans and animals, containing cardiotoxic compounds.
