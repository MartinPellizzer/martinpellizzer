Astragalus karamasicus

Astragalus karamasicus, commonly known as Karamajian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
