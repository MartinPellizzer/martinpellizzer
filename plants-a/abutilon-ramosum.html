Abutilon ramosum

Abutilon ramosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Abutilon ramosum is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant flowers.
