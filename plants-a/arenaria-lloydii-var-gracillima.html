Arenaria lloydii var. gracillima

Arenaria lloydii var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the coastal dunes of southern California.

It is characterized by its prostrate habit, with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

This variety is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
