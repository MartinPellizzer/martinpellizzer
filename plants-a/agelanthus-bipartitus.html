Agelanthus bipartitus

Agelanthus bipartitus, commonly known as the two-part agelanthus, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a striking, branched stem structure with fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
