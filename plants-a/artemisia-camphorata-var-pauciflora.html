Artemisia camphorata var. pauciflora

Artemisia camphorata var. pauciflora, commonly known as Chinese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a volatile oil containing camphor, which contributes to its strong scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky habitats.

Its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils highlights its botanical and economic significance.
