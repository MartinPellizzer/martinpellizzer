Acer campestre var. schwerinii

Acer campestre var. schwerinii, commonly known as the Schwerin maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its oval to elliptical leaves that are typically green on top and silver-white underneath, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, red or orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its colorful autumn foliage, which ranges from yellow to red.
