Aleuritopteris pseudoargentea

Aleuritopteris pseudoargentea, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silver-colored fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Aleuritopteris pseudoargentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
