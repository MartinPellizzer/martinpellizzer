Asarum arifolium var. callifolium

Asarum arifolium var. callifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that emerge from the base of the leaves in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
