Astiria rosea

Astiria rosea, commonly known as the rose aster, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy pink to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astiria rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils in full sun.
