Asplenium trichomanes subsp. pachyrachis

Asplenium trichomanes subsp.

pachyrachis, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The fronds are typically divided into smaller, finger-like segments, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
