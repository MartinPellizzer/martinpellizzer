Atriplex reichei

Atriplex reichei, commonly known as desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, salt-tolerant leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and stabilize sandy soils.

Atriplex reichei plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
