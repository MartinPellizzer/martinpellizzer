Avena sempervirens var. pubescens

Avena sempervirens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, awned florets. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
