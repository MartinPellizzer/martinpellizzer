Acalypha granulata

Acalypha granulata, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves, which display a range of colors from green to reddish-brown.
