Acacia aristulata

Acacia aristulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Acacia aristulata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
