Ammopiptanthus nanus

Ammopiptanthus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese peashrub, is a small shrub native to arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Ammopiptanthus nanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
