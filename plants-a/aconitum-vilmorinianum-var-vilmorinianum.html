Aconitum vilmorinianum var. vilmorinianum

Aconitum vilmorinianum var. vilmorinianum, commonly known as the Vilmorin'saconite, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal racemes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, palmate appearance.

Aconitum vilmorinianum var. vilmorinianum is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
