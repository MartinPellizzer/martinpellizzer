Amelanchier alnifolia var. nitens

Amelanchier alnifolia var. nitens, commonly known as the silverleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
