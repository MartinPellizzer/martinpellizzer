Aster calocephalus

Aster calocephalus, commonly known as the purplehead aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, purple to violet flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Aster calocephalus is also an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
