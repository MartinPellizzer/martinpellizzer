Alpinia oxyphylla

Alpinia oxyphylla, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or small ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant is characterized by its upright, rhizomatous growth and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Alpinia oxyphylla is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.
