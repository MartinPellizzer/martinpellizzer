Allium podolicum

Allium podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant's bulbs are often used as a culinary herb, similar to garlic and onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium podolicum is valued for its aromatic qualities and is often grown in gardens for both ornamental and edible purposes.
