Astragalus arenarius var. sabulosus

Astragalus arenarius var. sabulosus, commonly known as the sand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellow to pale orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
