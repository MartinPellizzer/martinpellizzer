Acacia farnesiana var. curvispina

Acacia farnesiana var. curvispina, commonly known as the sweet acacia or the honey bush, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
