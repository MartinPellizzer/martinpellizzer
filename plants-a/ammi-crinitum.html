Ammi crinitum

Ammi crinitum, commonly known as the flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, silky white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Ammi crinitum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
