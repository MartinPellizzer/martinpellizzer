Anthurium decipiens

Anthurium decipiens, commonly known as the false peace lily, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Anthurium decipiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
