Ancistrocactus crassihamatus

Ancistrocactus crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its environment. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

Overall, it is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant well adapted to harsh desert climates.
