Aristida riparia var. andina

Aristida riparia var. andina, commonly known as the Andean ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
