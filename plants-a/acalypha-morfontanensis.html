Acalypha morfontanensis

Acalypha morfontanensis, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha morfontanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
