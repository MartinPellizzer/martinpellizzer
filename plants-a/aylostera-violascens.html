Aylostera violascens

Aylostera violascens, commonly known as the violet-flowered aylostera, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Aylostera violascens thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
