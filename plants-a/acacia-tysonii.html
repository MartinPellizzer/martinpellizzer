Acacia tysonii

Acacia tysonii, commonly known as the Tysons acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Acacia tysonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
