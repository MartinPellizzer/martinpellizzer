Abelia biflora var. alpina

Abelia biflora var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine abelia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that remain throughout the year, providing ornamental value in both garden and natural settings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand colder climates, contributing to its popularity among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
