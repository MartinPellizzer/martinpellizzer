Androcorys pusillus

Androcorys pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf mossy orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
