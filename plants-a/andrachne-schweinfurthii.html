Andrachne schweinfurthii

Andrachne schweinfurthii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy soils and access deep water sources.

Andrachne schweinfurthii plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
