Astragalus concinnus

Astragalus concinnus, commonly known as the elegant milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus concinnus is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
