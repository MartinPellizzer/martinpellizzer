Anemia nana

Anemia nana, commonly known as dwarf anemone, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish down. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a rounded inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its small size and the characteristic appearance of its flowers.
