Adiantum braunii var. obovatum

Adiantum braunii var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-ovate maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds with a distinctive blackish midrib, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that produce new shoots. Its leaves are typically bright green and turn a reddish hue in the autumn.

It prefers shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor settings.
