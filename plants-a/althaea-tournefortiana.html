Althaea tournefortiana

Althaea tournefortiana, commonly known as the Turkishmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Althaea tournefortiana is known for its mucilage-rich roots, which have been used in traditional medicine.
