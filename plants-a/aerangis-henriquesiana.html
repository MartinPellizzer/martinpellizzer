Aerangis henriquesiana

Aerangis henriquesiana, commonly known as the Henriques' orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.
