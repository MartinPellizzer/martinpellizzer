Aquilegia alpina var. sternbergii

Aquilegia alpina var. sternbergii, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in cold climates.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
