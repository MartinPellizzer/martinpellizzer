Amelanchier canadensis lancifolia

Amelanchier canadensis lancifolia, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a red or pink color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open woods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
