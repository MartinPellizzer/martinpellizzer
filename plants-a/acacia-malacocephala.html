Acacia malacocephala

Acacia malacocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers that bloom in compact clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Acacia malacocephala is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
