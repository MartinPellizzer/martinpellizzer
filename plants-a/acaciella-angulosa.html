Acaciella angulosa

Acaciella angulosa, commonly known as the angular acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are compound, typically with three leaflets. Acaciella angulosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
