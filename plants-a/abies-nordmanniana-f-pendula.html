Abies nordmanniana f. pendula

Abies nordmanniana f. pendula, commonly known as the hanging fir, is a unique variant of the Nordmann fir.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, weeping appearance. This form is a natural mutation of the parent species, Abies nordmanniana, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant retains the typical coniferous features of the species, including needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structures.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
