Acamptopappus sphaerocephalus var. hirtellus

Acamptopappus sphaerocephalus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
