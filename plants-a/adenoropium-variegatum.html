Adenoropium variegatum

Adenoropium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its fronds are typically variegated with shades of green, white, and brown, adding to its ornamental value.

Adenoropium variegatum requires well-draining soil and regular misting to maintain its health and vibrant appearance.
