Aspilia ciliata

Aspilia ciliata, commonly known as the silky aspilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with fine hairs along the edges, giving it a silky texture. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray flowers, creating a striking visual display.

Aspilia ciliata is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
