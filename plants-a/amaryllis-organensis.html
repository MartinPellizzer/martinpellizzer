Amaryllis organensis

Amaryllis organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and strap-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Amaryllis organensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
