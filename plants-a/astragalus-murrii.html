Astragalus murrii

Astragalus murrii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils and withstand periods of drought.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
