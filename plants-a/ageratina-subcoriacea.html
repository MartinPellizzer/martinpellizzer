Ageratina subcoriacea

Ageratina subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ageratina subcoriacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
