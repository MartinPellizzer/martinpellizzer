Aconitum × cammarum var. beskidense

Aconitum × cammarum var. beskidense, commonly known as the Beskid Aconite, is a hybrid plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs.

This variety is adapted to moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountain meadows and forest clearings.
