Arenaria sphaerocarpa

Arenaria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-flower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its spherical, fleshy fruits are distinctive features. It is often found in open, well-drained habitats and is adapted to cold climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
