Asplenium truncatum var. crenulatum

Asplenium truncatum var. crenulatum, commonly known as the notched maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species characterized by its distinctive leaf shape.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The fronds are typically lanceolate with a notched or crenulate margin, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
