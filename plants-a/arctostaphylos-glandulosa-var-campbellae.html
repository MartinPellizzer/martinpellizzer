Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. campbellae

Arctostaphylos glandulosa var. campbellae, commonly known as Campbell's manzanita, is a shrub native to California, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of white to pink flowers.

It typically grows in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its compact form and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
