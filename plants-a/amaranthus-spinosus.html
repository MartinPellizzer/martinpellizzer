Amaranthus spinosus

Amaranthus spinosus, commonly known as prickly amaranth, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and tall, branched stalks that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-lobed, triangular seed capsules. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
