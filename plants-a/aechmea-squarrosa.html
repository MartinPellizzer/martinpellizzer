Aechmea squarrosa

Aechmea squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces a central inflorescence that bears showy, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in its native humid and often rainy environment.

Aechmea squarrosa thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Morphology

Aechmea squarrosa has leaves that are long, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, with a bluish-green color and a waxy coating.

The plant has a short, sturdy stem that is often hidden beneath the foliage, with a smooth, pale green bark. Its flowers are clustered in a panicle inflorescence, with violet or purple petals, and bloom during the dry season, primarily pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its natural habitat.
