Artemisia cina

Artemisia cina, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils, including thujone, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. Artemisia cina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

It has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various cultures.
