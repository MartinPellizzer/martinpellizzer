Astragalus iselyi

Astragalus iselyi, commonly known as the Isely's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.

Astragalus iselyi is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional practices.
