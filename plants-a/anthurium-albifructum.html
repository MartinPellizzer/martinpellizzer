Anthurium albifructum

Anthurium albifructum, commonly known as the white-fruit anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its flowers are typically white or pale in color, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Anthurium albifructum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
