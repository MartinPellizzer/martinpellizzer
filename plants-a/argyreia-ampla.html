Argyreia ampla

Argyreia ampla, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Argyreia ampla thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
