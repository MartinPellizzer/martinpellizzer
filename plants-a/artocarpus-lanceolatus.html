Artocarpus lanceolatus

Artocarpus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The tree produces a large, spiky fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The fruit is edible, with a starchy, bread-like pulp surrounding the seeds.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and its ornamental value.
