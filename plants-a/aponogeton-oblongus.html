Aponogeton oblongus

Aponogeton oblongus, commonly known as the broad-leaf water lettuce, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features floating leaves that are broad and arrowhead-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its roots are fibrous and anchor it to the substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Aponogeton oblongus is often used in ornamental ponds and wetland restoration projects due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
