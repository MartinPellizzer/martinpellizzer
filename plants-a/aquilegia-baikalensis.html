Aquilegia baikalensis

Aquilegia baikalensis, commonly known as the Baikal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Lake Baikal region in Siberia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and thrives in cool climates. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring, range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on the variety.

Aquilegia baikalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native environment.
