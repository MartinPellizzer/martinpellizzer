Allium sphaerocephalon subsp. durandoi

Allium sphaerocephalon subsp.

durandoi, commonly known as the Turk's cap leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spherical, purple to pink flower heads that resemble a turban.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often grown as an ornamental due to its striking appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

The subspecies durandoi is distinguished by its slightly larger bulbs and more robust foliage compared to the typical form of A. sphaerocephalon.
