Adesmia cordobensis var. appendiculata

Adesmia cordobensis var. appendiculata, commonly known as the Cordoba legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.
