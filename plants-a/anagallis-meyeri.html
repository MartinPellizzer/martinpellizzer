Anagallis meyeri

Anagallis meyeri, commonly known as the mouse-ear or fairy clock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant typically blooms from spring to early summer, and its flowers open in the morning, closing by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The leaves are hairy and heart-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
