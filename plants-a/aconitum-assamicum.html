Aconitum assamicum

Aconitum assamicum, commonly known as the Assam aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

Its roots contain potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring careful handling.

Aconitum assamicum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of India and Nepal.

The flowers are blue to violet in color, with showy, spurred petals, and bloom in late summer to early autumn; they are pollinated primarily by insects. The roots are tuberous and fibrous, serving as storage organs for nutrients.
