Anthriscus nemorosa

Anthriscus nemorosa, commonly known as woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are aromatic and have been historically used in herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent.

The plant's unique appearance and pleasant scent make it a notable component of natural landscapes.
