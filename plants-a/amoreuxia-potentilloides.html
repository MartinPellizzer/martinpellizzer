Amoreuxia potentilloides

Amoreuxia potentilloides, commonly known as the common lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a rough texture, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Amoreuxia potentilloides is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and drought tolerance.
