Aloysia unifacialis

Aloysia unifacialis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Aloysia unifacialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
