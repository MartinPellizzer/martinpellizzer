Asarum taitonense

Asarum taitonense, commonly known as Taitonga or Taitonese wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Islands, particularly the Mariana Islands.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, characterized by its unique, often tubular-shaped flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy aroma. Its flowers are typically greenish-brown with a distinctive hooded structure, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Asarum taitonense is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or lowland forests.
