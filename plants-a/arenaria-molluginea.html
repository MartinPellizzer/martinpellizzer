Arenaria molluginea

Arenaria molluginea, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Arenaria molluginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
