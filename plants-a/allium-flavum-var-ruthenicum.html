Allium flavum var. ruthenicum

Allium flavum var. ruthenicum, commonly known as the Ruthenian leek, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are typically small and are used in local cuisines for their mild, onion-like flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
