Astragalus sempervirens var. granatensis

Astragalus sempervirens var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
