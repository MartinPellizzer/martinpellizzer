Andryala cossyrensis

Andryala cossyrensis, commonly known as the Canary Island caryota, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and striking inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a common feature in the Araceae family.

Andryala cossyrensis is adapted to coastal and rocky environments, showing resilience to saline and windy conditions.
