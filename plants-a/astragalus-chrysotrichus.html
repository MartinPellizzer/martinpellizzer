Astragalus chrysotrichus

Astragalus chrysotrichus, commonly known as golden-hair vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has small, compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus chrysotrichus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
