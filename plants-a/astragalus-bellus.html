Astragalus bellus

Astragalus bellus, commonly known as the beautiful milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
