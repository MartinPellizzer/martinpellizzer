Acacia incongesta

Acacia incongesta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia incongesta is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its wood is lightweight and has been traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
