Antirrhinum nuttallianum var. subsessile

Antirrhinum nuttallianum var. subsessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive bilabiate (two-lipped) structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
