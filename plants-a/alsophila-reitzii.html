Alsophila reitzii

Alsophila reitzii, commonly known as the Reitz's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila reitzii is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying properties.
