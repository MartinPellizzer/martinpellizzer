Astronia dioica

Astronia dioica, commonly known as the dioecious aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinct male and female flowers, with separate plants for each sex, a feature known as dioecy. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its showy yellow flowers appear in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Astronia dioica plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
