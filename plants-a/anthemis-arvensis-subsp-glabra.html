Anthemis arvensis subsp. glabra

Anthemis arvensis subsp.

glabra, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with linear leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets.

The plant produces a strong, aromatic scent, which is often attributed to its essential oils. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
