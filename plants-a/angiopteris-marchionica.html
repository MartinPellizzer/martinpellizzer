Angiopteris marchionica

Angiopteris marchionica, commonly known as the Marchionni's shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive pattern of sori on the underside.

Angiopteris marchionica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
