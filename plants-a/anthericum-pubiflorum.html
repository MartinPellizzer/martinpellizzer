Anthericum pubiflorum

Anthericum pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered arum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered on the spadix, with the female flowers located at the base and the male flowers above. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a waxy texture.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
