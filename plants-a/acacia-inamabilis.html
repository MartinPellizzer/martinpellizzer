Acacia inamabilis

Acacia inamabilis, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive feathery foliage and yellow or golden flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to drought conditions. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acacia inamabilis also plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
