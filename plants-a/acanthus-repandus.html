Acanthus repandus

Acanthus repandus, commonly known as the broadleaf acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy spikes of violet or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Acanthus repandus is also valued for its historical use in decorative art and architecture.
