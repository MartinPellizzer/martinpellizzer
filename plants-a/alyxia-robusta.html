Alyxia robusta

Alyxia robusta, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Alyxia robusta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
