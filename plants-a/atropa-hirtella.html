Atropa hirtella

Atropa hirtella, commonly known as poison berry or white henbane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
