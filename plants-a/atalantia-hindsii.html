Atalantia hindsii

Atalantia hindsii, commonly known as Hinds' atalantia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor, similar to citrus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
