Acer cappadocicum var. tripartitum

Acer cappadocicum var. tripartitum, commonly known as the three-parted maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, red to orange flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a flaky texture that becomes more pronounced with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
