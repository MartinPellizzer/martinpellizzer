Abutilon chapelieri

Abutilon chapelieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Abutilon chapelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
