Acacia dealbata subsp. dealbata

Acacia dealbata subsp.

dealbata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
