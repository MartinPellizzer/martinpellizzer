Aristolochia karwinskii

Aristolochia karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are often dark purple or maroon in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are covered in small, glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
