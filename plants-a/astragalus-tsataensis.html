Astragalus tsataensis

Astragalus tsataensis, commonly known as the Tsata Valley milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. Astragalus tsataensis is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and serving as a food source for various insects and small animals.
