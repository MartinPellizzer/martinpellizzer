Allium margaritaceum var. affine

Allium margaritaceum var. affine, commonly known as the silverleaf garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground storage organs, which are used for propagation and culinary purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to other Allium species, particularly those with similar floral and structural features.
