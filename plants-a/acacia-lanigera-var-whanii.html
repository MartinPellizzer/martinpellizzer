Acacia lanigera var. whanii

Acacia lanigera var. whanii, commonly known as the Whanganui acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in coastal and lowland environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
