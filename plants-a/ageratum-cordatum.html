Ageratum cordatum

Ageratum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which are arranged in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped, hence the species name "cordatum," and are covered in fine hairs.

Ageratum cordatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
