Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. inconcinna

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

inconcinna, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
