Agave crassispina var. cultra

Agave crassispina var. cultra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a blue-gray wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces narrow, spiny leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with prominent white teeth along the edges. It blooms with yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the plant, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
