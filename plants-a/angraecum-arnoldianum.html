Angraecum arnoldianum

Angraecum arnoldianum, commonly known as the Arnold's star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with white petals and a deep violet lip. The plant is adapted to grow on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on rainfall for moisture. Its flowers are pollinated by specific moths, showcasing a unique co-evolutionary relationship.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
