Amelanchier alnifolia

Amelanchier alnifolia, commonly known as Saskatoon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and often used in culinary applications. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.
