Aptosimum scaberrimum var. glabrum

Aptosimum scaberrimum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth aptosimum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often form tussocks. The leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets. This variety is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
