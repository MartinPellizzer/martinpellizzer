Acacia calamistrata

Acacia calamistrata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, feathery foliage composed of small, silky-haired leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are encased in elongated, woody pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia calamistrata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
