Ascocentrum himalaicum var. roseolum

Ascocentrum himalaicum var. roseolum, commonly known as the rose-colored arrowroot plant, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into yellowish berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shady garden environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and adaptability to humid tropical and subtropical climates.
