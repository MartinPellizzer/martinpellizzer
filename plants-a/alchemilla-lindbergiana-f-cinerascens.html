Alchemilla lindbergiana f. cinerascens

Alchemilla lindbergiana f. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens alkanet, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a sought-after plant in native gardens and conservation habitats.
