Ambrosia lindheimeriana

Ambrosia lindheimeriana, commonly known as Lindheimer's ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Ambrosia lindheimeriana is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
