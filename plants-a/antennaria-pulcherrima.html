Antennaria pulcherrima

Antennaria pulcherrima, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, fluffy flowers that resemble miniature clouds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates. The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
