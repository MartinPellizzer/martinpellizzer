Agapetes variegatum var. macrantha

Agapetes variegatum var. macrantha, commonly known as the variegated agapetes, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green on its leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
