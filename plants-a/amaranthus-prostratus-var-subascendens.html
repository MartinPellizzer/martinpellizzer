Amaranthus prostratus var. subascendens

Amaranthus prostratus var. subascendens, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is a low-growing annual plant native to warm climates.

It features spreading stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of green or red. It is often cultivated as a ground cover or for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.

This species is also valued in some regions for its edible leaves and seeds.
