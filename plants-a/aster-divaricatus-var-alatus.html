Aster divaricatus var. alatus

Aster divaricatus var. alatus, commonly known as the winged blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often branched and winged, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological landscapes.
