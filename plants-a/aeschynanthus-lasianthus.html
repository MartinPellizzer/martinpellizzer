Aeschynanthus lasianthus

Aeschynanthus lasianthus, commonly known as the silver bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Aeschynanthus lasianthus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
