Achimenes lanata

Achimenes lanata, commonly known as the woolly achimenes, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers well-draining soil.

Achimenes lanata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
