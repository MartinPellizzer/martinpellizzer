Acacia deltoidea subsp. ampla

Acacia deltoidea subsp.

ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in arid environments.
