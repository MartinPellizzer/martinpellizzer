Amygdalus leiocarpa

Amygdalus leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth almond.

It is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its small, showy white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a thick, woody shell. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a fruit that is a drupe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
