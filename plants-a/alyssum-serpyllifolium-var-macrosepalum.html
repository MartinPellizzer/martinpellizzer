Alyssum serpyllifolium var. macrosepalum

Alyssum serpyllifolium var. macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit with dense, silver-gray foliage that is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels, and the leaves are linear to ovate, often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
