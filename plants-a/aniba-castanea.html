Aniba castanea

Aniba castanea, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark brown, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Aniba castanea is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and as a natural remedy.
