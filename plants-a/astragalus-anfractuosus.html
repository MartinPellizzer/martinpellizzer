Astragalus anfractuosus

Astragalus anfractuosus, commonly known as the wrinkled milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, rounded seeds.
