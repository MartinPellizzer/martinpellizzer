Aster lentus

Aster lentus, commonly known as the broadleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Aster lentus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
