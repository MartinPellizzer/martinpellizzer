Acacia raddiana var. pubescens

Acacia raddiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, feathery, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, hairless leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The plant is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various animals.
