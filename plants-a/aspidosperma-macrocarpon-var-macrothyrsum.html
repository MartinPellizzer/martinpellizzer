Aspidosperma macrocarpon var. macrothyrsum

Aspidosperma macrocarpon var. macrothyrsum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratonia siliqua* var. macrothyrsum, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, showy red fruits that resemble cherries. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
