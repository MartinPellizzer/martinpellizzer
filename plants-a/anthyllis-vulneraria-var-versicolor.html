Anthyllis vulneraria var. versicolor

Anthyllis vulneraria var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable woundwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow or orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
