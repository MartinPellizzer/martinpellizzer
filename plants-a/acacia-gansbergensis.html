Acacia gansbergensis

Acacia gansbergensis, commonly known as the Gansberg acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Acacia gansbergensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance and heat tolerance.
