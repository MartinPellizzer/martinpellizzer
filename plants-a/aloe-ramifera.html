Aloe ramifera

Aloe ramifera, commonly known as the branched aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that has been used for its soothing properties.

Aloe ramifera is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry conditions.
