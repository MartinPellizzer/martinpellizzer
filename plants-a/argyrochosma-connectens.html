Argyrochosma connectens

Argyrochosma connectens, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features a compact, mat-like growth form with small, fleshy, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Argyrochosma connectens is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the edges of deserts and in coastal areas.
