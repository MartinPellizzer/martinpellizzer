Aglaia rubiginosa

Aglaia rubiginosa, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
