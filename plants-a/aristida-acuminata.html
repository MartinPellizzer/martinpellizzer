Aristida acuminata

Aristida acuminata, commonly known as narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bristly flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
