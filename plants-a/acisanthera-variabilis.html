Acisanthera variabilis

Acisanthera variabilis, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is known for its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for local wildlife.
