Alnus crispa var. elongata

Alnus crispa var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated alder, is a variant of the alder tree native to parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forest edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, and it produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
