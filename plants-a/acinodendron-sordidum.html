Acinodendron sordidum

Acinodendron sordidum, commonly known as the dirty acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a drought-resistant growth habit. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, a typical feature of the legume family.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
