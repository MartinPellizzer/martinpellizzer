Asplenium × gastoni-gautieri

Asplenium × gastoni-gautieri, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Asplenium gautieri* and *Asplenium tomentosum*.

It is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
