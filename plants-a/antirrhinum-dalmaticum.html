Antirrhinum dalmaticum

Antirrhinum dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, and they open in the morning, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
