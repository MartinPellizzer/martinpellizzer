Annona glauca

Annona glauca, commonly known as the white custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces a large, green, spiny fruit that turns yellow when mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a strong, unpleasant odor.

The fruit is edible, though its texture is mealy and its taste is often described as bland or slightly sweet.
