Arctotis caulescens

Arctotis caulescens, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming from late winter to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include alternate, lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system.
