Acaciella talpana

Acaciella talpana, commonly known as the Mexican acacia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Acaciella talpana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
