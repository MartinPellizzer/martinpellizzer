Acacia ramiflora

Acacia ramiflora, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and phyllodes that are narrow and often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia ramiflora is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

It plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
