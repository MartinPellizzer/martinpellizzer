Anthurium bromelicola subsp. bahiense

Anthurium bromelicola subsp.

bahiense, commonly known as the Bahia anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Brazil. It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its foliage is usually dark green with a glossy texture, and the inflorescence features a colorful, fleshy spathe.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
