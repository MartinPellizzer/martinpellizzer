Aristida shawii

Aristida shawii, commonly known as Shaw's threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous bristly florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Shaw's threeawn is often found in disturbed soils and can serve as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
