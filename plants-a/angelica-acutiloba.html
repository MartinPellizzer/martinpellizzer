Angelica acutiloba

Angelica acutiloba, commonly known as Japanese angelica, is a biennial or perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Angelica acutiloba is valued for its aromatic roots and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
