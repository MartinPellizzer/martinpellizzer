Abelia sanguinea

Abelia sanguinea, commonly known as the redleaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features attractive, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value. It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.

Abelia sanguinea is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal and moderate care requirements.

Native Distribution

Abelia sanguinea is primarily native to eastern North America, including the United States and Canada.

It is commonly found in moist, woodland areas and along stream banks. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and is often seen in the southeastern United States. It is known for its showy clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Abelia sanguinea is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.

Introduced Distribution

Abelia sanguinea was mainly introduced to North America as an ornamental plant due to its attractive pink flowers and hardy nature.
