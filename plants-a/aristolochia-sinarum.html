Aristolochia sinarum

Aristolochia sinarum, commonly known as Chinese birthwort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically purple or greenish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex chemical composition, including various aristolochic acids. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
