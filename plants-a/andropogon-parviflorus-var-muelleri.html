Andropogon parviflorus var. muelleri

Andropogon parviflorus var. muelleri, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, making it a common component of tallgrass prairie ecosystems. The plant typically grows in full sun and is adapted to well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
