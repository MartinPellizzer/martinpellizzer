Arisaema rhizomatum var. nudum

Arisaema rhizomatum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a unique, often fragrant bloom that attracts pollinators such as beetles and flies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
