Alpinia corneri

Alpinia corneri, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.
