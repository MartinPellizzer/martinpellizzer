Adenophora polymorpha var. latifolia

Adenophora polymorpha var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are typically surrounded by a ring of narrow, pointed sepals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
