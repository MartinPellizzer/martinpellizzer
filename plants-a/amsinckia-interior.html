Amsinckia interior

Amsinckia interior, commonly known as the interior prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, spiny calyx. It is often found in rocky soils and dry slopes, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
