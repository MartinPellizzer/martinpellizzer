Atropis laevis var. rigida

Atropis laevis var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid smooth bellflower, is a variant of the smooth bellflower species native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or lavender in color. The leaves are smooth and lanceolate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a notable addition to collections of succulent and semi-succulent flora.
