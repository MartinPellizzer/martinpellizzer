Abronia villosa var. pinetorum

Abronia villosa var. pinetorum, commonly known as the coastal bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the sandy soils of the Pacific coast.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandy beaches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
