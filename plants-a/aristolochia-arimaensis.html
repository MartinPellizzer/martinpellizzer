Aristolochia arimaensis

Aristolochia arimaensis, commonly known as the Japanese Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
