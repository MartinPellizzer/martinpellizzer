Aridaria dyeri

Aridaria dyeri, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to survive with minimal water. Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Aridaria dyeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
