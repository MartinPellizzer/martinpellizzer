Andropogon exaristatus

Andropogon exaristatus, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems and open woodlands.

Andropogon exaristatus plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
