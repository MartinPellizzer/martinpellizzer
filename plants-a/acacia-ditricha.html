Acacia ditricha

Acacia ditricha, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia ditricha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salinity.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
