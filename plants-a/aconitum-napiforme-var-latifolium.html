Aconitum napiforme var. latifolium

Aconitum napiforme var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
