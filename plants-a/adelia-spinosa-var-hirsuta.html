Adelia spinosa var. hirsuta

Adelia spinosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy adelia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a thick, branching stem covered in short, stiff hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention.

This variety is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
