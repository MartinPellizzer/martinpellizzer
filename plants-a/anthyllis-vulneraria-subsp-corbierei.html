Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. corbierei

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

corbierei, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
