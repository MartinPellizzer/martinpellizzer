Adonis ambigua

Adonis ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Adonis ambigua is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
