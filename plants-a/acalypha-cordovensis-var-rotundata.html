Acalypha cordovensis var. rotundata

Acalypha cordovensis var. rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaf copperleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with reddish veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
