Aster suborbiculatus

Aster suborbiculatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators in its native range.
