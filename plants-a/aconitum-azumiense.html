Aconitum azumiense

Aconitum azumiense, commonly known as Azumisideaconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Aconitum azumiense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a potentially dangerous plant to handle or ingest.
