Andropogon longiberbis

Andropogon longiberbis, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Tall bluestem plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
