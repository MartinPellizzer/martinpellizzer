Amomum cevuga

Amomum cevuga, commonly known as the lesser galangal, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, pungent aroma.

Amomum cevuga is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
