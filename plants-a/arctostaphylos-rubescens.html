Arctostaphylos rubescens

Arctostaphylos rubescens, commonly known as the red-berried manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its adaptability to drought and fire makes it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
