Adenophora manshurica f. albiflora

Adenophora manshurica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mandarin bellflower, is a variant of the species native to the mountainous regions of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or rocky habitats. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
