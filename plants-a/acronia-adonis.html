Acronia adonis

Acronia adonis, commonly known as the Adonis lily, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms. Acronia adonis is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in gardens, though it requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive.
