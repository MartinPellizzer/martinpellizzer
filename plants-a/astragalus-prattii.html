Astragalus prattii

Astragalus prattii, commonly known as Pratt's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Astragalus prattii is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
