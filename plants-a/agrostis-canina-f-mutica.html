Agrostis canina f. mutica

Agrostis canina f. mutica, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant is typically found in temperate regions, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The inflorescence consists of compact spikelets, and the plant is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated in lawns and turfgrass areas due to its fine texture and resilience.
