Aristida vagans var. gracillima

Aristida vagans var. gracillima, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, grass-like blades that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is usually erect and spike-like.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
