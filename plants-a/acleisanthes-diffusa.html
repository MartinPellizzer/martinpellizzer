Acleisanthes diffusa

Acleisanthes diffusa, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet and are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is well-suited to dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
