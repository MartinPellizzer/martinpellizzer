Adiantopsis hickeyi

Adiantopsis hickeyi, commonly known as the Hickey's maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae, which includes other maidenhair ferns characterized by their finely divided, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its fronds are usually bright green, with a distinctive, finely serrated margin.

Adiantopsis hickeyi is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its unique appearance and rarity.
