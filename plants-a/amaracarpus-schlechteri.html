Amaracarpus schlechteri

Amaracarpus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, colorful heads.

Amaracarpus schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
