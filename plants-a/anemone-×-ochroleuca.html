Anemone × ochroleuca

Anemone × ochroleuca, commonly known as the white anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized areas.
