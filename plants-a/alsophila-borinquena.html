Alsophila borinquena

Alsophila borinquena, commonly known as the Puerto Rican tree fern, is a species native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of large, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to two meters. Alsophila borinquena thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts in its native range.
