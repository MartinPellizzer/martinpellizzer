Aristea latifolia

Aristea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aristea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aristea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
