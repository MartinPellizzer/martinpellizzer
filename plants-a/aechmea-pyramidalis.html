Aechmea pyramidalis

Aechmea pyramidalis, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant's inflorescence consists of a tall, spiky flower stem topped with colorful bracts that resemble a pineapple. Its foliage is often adorned with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Aechmea pyramidalis is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea pyramidalis has rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff, narrow, and sword-like, arranged in a compact spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy coating.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a fibrous, rough texture and a dark brown color. Its flowers are borne in a spike at the center of the rosette, with bright red bracts and white petals, blooming during the summer months and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that produces small, black seeds dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its natural habitat.
