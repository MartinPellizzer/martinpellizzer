Anguria dentata

Anguria dentata, commonly known as the toothed cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like melons and gourds. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with serrated edges that give it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.
