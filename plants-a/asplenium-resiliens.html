Asplenium resiliens

Asplenium resiliens, commonly known as the resilient spleenwort, is a member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, humid environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into smaller segments, giving it a feathery appearance. It is native to various regions in the Americas and is known for its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.

Asplenium resiliens plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
