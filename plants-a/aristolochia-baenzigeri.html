Aristolochia baenzigeri

Aristolochia baenzigeri, commonly known as the Baenziger's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, fragrant flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisted floral structure. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, spicy aroma, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
