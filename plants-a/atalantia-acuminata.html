Atalantia acuminata

Atalantia acuminata, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which has a sweet, honey-like flavor.
