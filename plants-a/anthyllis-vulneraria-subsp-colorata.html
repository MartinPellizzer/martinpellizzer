Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. colorata

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the red bugloss, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, and the flowers are borne in dense clusters.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
