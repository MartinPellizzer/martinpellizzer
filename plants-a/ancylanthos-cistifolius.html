Ancylanthos cistifolius

Ancylanthos cistifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping.
