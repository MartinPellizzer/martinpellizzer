Amelanchier ovalis var. subcordata

Amelanchier ovalis var. subcordata, commonly known as the western serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
