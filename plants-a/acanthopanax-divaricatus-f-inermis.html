Acanthopanax divaricatus f. inermis

Acanthopanax divaricatus f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth devil's ivy, is a variant of the genus Acanthopanax, characterized by its lack of thorns.

It is a climbing shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in forested areas. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its roots are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The f. inermis form is distinguished by its smooth stems, making it distinct from other forms of the species.
