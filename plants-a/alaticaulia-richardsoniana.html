Alaticaulia richardsoniana

Alaticaulia richardsoniana, commonly known as the Richardson's alpine fir, is a rare and specialized conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving branches and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments with well-drained soil, typically found in alpine and subalpine zones. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
