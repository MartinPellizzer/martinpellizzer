Astragalus woldemarii

Astragalus woldemarii, commonly known as the Ethiopian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
