Arabis × landaueri

Arabis × landaueri, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Arabis × landaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
