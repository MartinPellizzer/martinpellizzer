Anthriscus sylvestris var. alpestris

Anthriscus sylvestris var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a mild, aromatic scent.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
