Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. intermedia

Austrocylindropuntia vestita var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Pencil Cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spineless stems that resemble pencils, which are segmented and covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
