Agave × bundrantii

Agave × bundrantii, commonly known as the hybrid agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a hybrid between Agave americana and Agave lechuguilla, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and typically have a bluish-green hue with white or grayish marginal teeth. It produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in the plant's lifetime.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid landscapes for its drought tolerance and architectural form.
