Acacia rubida

Acacia rubida, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia rubida is adapted to drought conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
