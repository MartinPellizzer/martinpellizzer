Acaena argentea var. gracillima

Acaena argentea var. gracillima, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective ground cover in ecological restoration projects.
