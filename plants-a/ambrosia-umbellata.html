Ambrosia umbellata

Ambrosia umbellata, commonly known as the common ragweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters called umbels. It is an annual plant that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

Ambrosia umbellata is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause severe respiratory issues in sensitive individuals.
