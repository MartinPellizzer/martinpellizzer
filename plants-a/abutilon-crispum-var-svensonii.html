Abutilon crispum var. svensonii

Abutilon crispum var. svensonii, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a velvety texture, and the flowers are often clustered in terminal panicles.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
