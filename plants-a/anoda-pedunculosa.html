Anoda pedunculosa

Anoda pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Anoda pedunculosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
