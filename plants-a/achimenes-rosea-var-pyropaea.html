Achimenes rosea var. pyropaea

Achimenes rosea var. pyropaea, commonly known as the flame bellflower, is a tropical perennial herb native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant features velvety, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
