Acer pseudoplatanus var. erythrocarpum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking red berries, which contrast with its palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage and fruit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
