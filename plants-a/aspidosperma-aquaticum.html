Aspidosperma aquaticum

Aspidosperma aquaticum, commonly known as the water aspidosperma, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, clustered white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water.
