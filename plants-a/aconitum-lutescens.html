Aconitum lutescens

Aconitum lutescens, commonly known as the yellow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
