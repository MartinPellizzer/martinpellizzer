Albizia pedicellata

Albizia pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow albizia, is a deciduous legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Albizia pedicellata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability in soil.
