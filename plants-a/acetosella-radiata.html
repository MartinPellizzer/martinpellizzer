Acetosella radiata

Acetosella radiata, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a small, low-growing plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and garden cultivation.
