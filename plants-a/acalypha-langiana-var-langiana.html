Acalypha langiana var. langiana

Acalypha langiana var. langiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-flowered acalypha, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
