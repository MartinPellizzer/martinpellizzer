Aechmea sprucei

Aechmea sprucei, commonly known as the pink pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, and often colorful leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is typically green with a pink or red hue, especially along the leaf margins. It produces a striking inflorescence with red bracts and small white flowers that bloom in the summer.

Aechmea sprucei is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.

Morphology

Aechmea sprucei has rosette-shaped leaves that are typically narrow, strap-like, and arranged in a dense spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are dark green with a waxy coating, and they can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant has a short, underground stem with a thick, fibrous root system that anchors it in the soil. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with white to pinkish petals and a blooming period during the rainy season.

The fruit is a fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.
