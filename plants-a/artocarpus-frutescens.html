Artocarpus frutescens

Artocarpus frutescens, commonly known as the rough fig or wild breadfruit, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other figs and mulberries. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the tree is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in traditional medicine.
