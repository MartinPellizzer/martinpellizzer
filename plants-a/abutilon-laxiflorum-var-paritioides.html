Abutilon laxiflorum var. paritioides

Abutilon laxiflorum var. paritioides, commonly known as the silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
