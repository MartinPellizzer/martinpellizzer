Arisaema heterocephalum var. okinawaense

Arisaema heterocephalum var. okinawaense, commonly known as the Okinawan arrowhead, is a unique variant of the arisaema genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a tuberous rhizome and showy, umbellate flowers that attract pollinators.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological traits.
