Alyssum thymops

Alyssum thymops, commonly known as coastal alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with tiny, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Alyssum thymops is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy or coastal soils.
