Atalantia aurantia

Atalantia aurantia, commonly known as the golden apple or the atalanta tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
