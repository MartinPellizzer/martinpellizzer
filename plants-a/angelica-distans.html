Angelica distans

Angelica distans, commonly known as the European angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in an umbel, which are followed by oval, ridged fruits. Angelica distans is often cultivated for its aromatic roots and leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and cooking.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable member of the Apiaceae family.
