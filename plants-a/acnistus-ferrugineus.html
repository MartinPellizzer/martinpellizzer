Acnistus ferrugineus

Acnistus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty acnistus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems covered in a rusty-colored bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture with a reddish underside.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
