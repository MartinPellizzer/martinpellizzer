Abildgaardia hispidula var. filiformis

Abildgaardia hispidula var. filiformis, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, thriving in full sun and drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
