Acacia intorta

Acacia intorta, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a woody, sometimes thorny stem.

Acacia intorta is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique form and drought tolerance.
