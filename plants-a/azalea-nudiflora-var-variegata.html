Azalea nudiflora var. variegata

Azalea nudiflora var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated winter azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The leaves are typically oval and have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in woodland gardens.
