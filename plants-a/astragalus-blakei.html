Astragalus blakei

Astragalus blakei, commonly known as Blake's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Astragalus blakei is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
