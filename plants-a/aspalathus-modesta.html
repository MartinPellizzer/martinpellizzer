Aspalathus modesta

Aspalathus modesta, commonly known as the small green bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus modesta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
