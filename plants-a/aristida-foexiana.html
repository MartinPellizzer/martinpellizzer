Aristida foexiana

Aristida foexiana, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is widely used in landscaping and garden design.
