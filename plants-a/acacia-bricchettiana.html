Acacia bricchettiana

Acacia bricchettiana, commonly known as the Briccett's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Acacia bricchettiana plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
