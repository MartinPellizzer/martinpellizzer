Astragalus verrucosus

Astragalus verrucosus, commonly known as the warty milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus verrucosus is often found in prairies and open woodlands, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local wildlife.
