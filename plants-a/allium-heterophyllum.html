Allium heterophyllum

Allium heterophyllum, commonly known as the Sierra onion, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that store nutrients, typical of the Allium genus. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
