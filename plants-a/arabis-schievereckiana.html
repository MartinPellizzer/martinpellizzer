Arabis schievereckiana

Arabis schievereckiana, commonly known as the Schievereck's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard and cabbages. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
