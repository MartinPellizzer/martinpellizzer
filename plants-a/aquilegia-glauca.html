Aquilegia glauca

Aquilegia glauca, commonly known as the blue columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aquilegia glauca is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
