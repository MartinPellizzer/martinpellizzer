Abies pectinata var. podolica

Abies pectinata var. podolica, commonly known as the Podolic fir, is a variant of the European fir native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The cones are typically pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
