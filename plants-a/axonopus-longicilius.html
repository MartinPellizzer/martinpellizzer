Axonopus longicilius

Axonopus longicilius, commonly known as the long-haired axonopus, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Axonopus longicilius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
