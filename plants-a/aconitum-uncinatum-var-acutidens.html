Aconitum uncinatum var. acutidens

Aconitum uncinatum var. acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of blue or purple, arranged in a helmet-like shape with spurred petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle this plant with care and avoid ingestion.
