Asplenium gravesii

Asplenium gravesii, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium gravesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
