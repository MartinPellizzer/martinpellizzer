Antirrhinum tricolor

Antirrhinum tricolor, commonly known as the three-color snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored flowers that typically display red, yellow, and white in a single bloom. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in meadows and gardens.

Antirrhinum tricolor is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.
