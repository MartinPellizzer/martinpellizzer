Artemisia bejdemaniae

Artemisia bejdemaniae, commonly known as the Kashmiri wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan and India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, rocky, and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
