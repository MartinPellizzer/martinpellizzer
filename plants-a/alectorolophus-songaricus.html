Alectorolophus songaricus

Alectorolophus songaricus, commonly known as the Songaric sunbird, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.
