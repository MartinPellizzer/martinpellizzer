Actinodaphne grandis

Actinodaphne grandis, commonly known as the large-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental gardening and reforestation projects.
