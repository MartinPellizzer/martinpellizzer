Aechmea beeriana

Aechmea beeriana, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and serves as a habitat for small organisms. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture and can grow up to 1 meter in length. It produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
