Alopecurus purpurascens

Alopecurus purpurascens, commonly known as purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

Alopecurus purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
