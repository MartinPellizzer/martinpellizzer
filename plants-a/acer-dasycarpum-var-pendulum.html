Acer dasycarpum var. pendulum

Acer dasycarpum var. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping maple, is a variant of the hairy maple characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It is native to China and is valued for its ornamental appearance, with delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant colors in autumn. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are inconspicuous, producing small greenish-yellow catkins, while the fruit is a samara with a winged structure.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
