Acer nigrum f. villosum

Acer nigrum f. villosum, commonly known as the silky maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the black maple.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, or pubescent, leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant is native to the northeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
