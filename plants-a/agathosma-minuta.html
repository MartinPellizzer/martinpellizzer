Agathosma minuta

Agathosma minuta, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in garden landscapes.
