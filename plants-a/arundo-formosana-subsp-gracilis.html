Arundo formosana subsp. gracilis

Arundo formosana subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender reed, is a grass-like plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and wetlands.
