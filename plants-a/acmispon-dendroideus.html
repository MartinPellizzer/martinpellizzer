Acmispon dendroideus

Acmispon dendroideus, commonly known as the tree burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to form dense, thorny thickets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Acmispon dendroideus is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
