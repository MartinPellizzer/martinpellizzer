Alismorkis natalensis

Alismorkis natalensis, commonly known as Natal water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Alismorkis natalensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
