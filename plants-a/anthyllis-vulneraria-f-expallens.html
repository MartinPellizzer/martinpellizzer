Anthyllis vulneraria f. expallens

Anthyllis vulneraria f. expallens, commonly known as the pale clover, is a variant of the common viper's bugloss.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
