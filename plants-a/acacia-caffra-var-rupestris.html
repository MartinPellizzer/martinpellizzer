Acacia caffra var. rupestris

Acacia caffra var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant's hardy nature makes it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control in dry landscapes.
