Amomum latifolium

Amomum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clustered, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and culinary applications.
