Amygdalus aitchisonii

Amygdalus aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison almond, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, almond-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Amygdalus aitchisonii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
