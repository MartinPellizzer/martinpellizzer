Anthocercis viscosa

Anthocercis viscosa, commonly known as the sticky anthocercis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain the seeds.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
