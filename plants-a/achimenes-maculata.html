Achimenes maculata

Achimenes maculata, commonly known as the spotted achimenes, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings.

Achimenes maculata is typically grown as a summer annual in temperate climates, thriving in warm, humid conditions.
