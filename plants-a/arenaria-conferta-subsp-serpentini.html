Arenaria conferta subsp. serpentini

Arenaria conferta subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as serpent's herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has a compact habit with densely packed, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its subsp.

serpentini is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
