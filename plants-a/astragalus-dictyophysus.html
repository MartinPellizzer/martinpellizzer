Astragalus dictyophysus

Astragalus dictyophysus, commonly known as the lacepod or desert milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability, featuring deep taproots and drought-resistant foliage. Its flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow or green, and the plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus dictyophysus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
