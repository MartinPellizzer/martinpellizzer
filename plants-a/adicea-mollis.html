Adicea mollis

Adicea mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the warm seasons. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Adicea mollis is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
