Acrostichum obductum

Acrostichum obductum, commonly known as the broadleaf hanger, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in dense forests. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with each leaflet having a smooth, waxy surface.

Acrostichum obductum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
