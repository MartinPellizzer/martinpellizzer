Acacia etbaica subsp. australis

Acacia etbaica subsp.

australis, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
