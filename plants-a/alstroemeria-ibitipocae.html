Alstroemeria ibitipocae

Alstroemeria ibitipocae, commonly known as the Ibitipoca lily, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Alstroemeria ibitipocae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
