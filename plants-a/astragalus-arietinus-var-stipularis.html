Astragalus arietinus var. stipularis

Astragalus arietinus var. stipularis, commonly known as the stipular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its stipules, which are leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks.
