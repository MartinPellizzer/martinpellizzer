Aquilegia brevistyla

Aquilegia brevistyla, commonly known as the short-styled columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two upper petals that are elongated into distinctive spurs. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and it produces follicles that contain seeds with silky appendages. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
