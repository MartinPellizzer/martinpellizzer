Aconitum forrestii var. albovillosum

Aconitum forrestii var. albovillosum, commonly known as the White-veined Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of blue to purple, with distinctive white veins on the petals. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
