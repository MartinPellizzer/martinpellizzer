Anemonoides amurensis

Anemonoides amurensis, commonly known as the Amur anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with numerous petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

Anemonoides amurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
