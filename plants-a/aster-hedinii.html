Aster hedinii

Aster hedinii, commonly known as the Hedin's aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically bloom in shades of blue to purple, and its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Aster hedinii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
