Arctium degenii var. tibidatensis

Arctium degenii var. tibidatensis, commonly known as the Tibetan burweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny bracts and composite flower heads, typical of the dandelion family. The plant produces a rough, hairy stem that often branches near the base, and its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is notable for its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient weed in certain regions.
