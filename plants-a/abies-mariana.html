Abies mariana

Abies mariana, commonly known as the white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to northern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive silvery-blue foliage. The needles are flat, soft, and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically cylindrical and range in color from green to reddish-brown.

This species plays an important ecological role in boreal and subarctic forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
