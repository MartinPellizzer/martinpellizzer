Andropogon salzmannii var. aristatus

Andropogon salzmannii var. aristatus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to six feet. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, elongated leaf blades.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in native habitats.
