Artemisia vulgaris var. umbrosa

Artemisia vulgaris var. umbrosa, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that appear in late summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry meadows.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
