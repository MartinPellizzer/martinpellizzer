Acacia strigosa var. endlicheri

Acacia strigosa var. endlicheri, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and yellow flowers that appear in spikes. The leaves are small, phyllodes, and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in providing habitat for native wildlife.
