Aeschynomene rehmannii

Aeschynomene rehmannii, commonly known as blue stylo, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, dehiscent pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Aeschynomene rehmannii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and forage production.
