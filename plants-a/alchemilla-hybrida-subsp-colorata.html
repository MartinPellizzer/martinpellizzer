Alchemilla hybrida subsp. colorata

Alchemilla hybrida subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of yellowish-green flowers.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers.
