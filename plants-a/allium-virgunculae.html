Allium virgunculae

Allium virgunculae, commonly known as the slender leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulbous stem that is typically slender and elongated, with a fibrous, white or pale yellow interior. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly curled, often resembling those of leeks or onions.

Allium virgunculae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
