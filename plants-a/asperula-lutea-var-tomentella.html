Asperula lutea var. tomentella

Asperula lutea var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
