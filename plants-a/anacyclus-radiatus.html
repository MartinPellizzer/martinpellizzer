Anacyclus radiatus

Anacyclus radiatus, commonly known as the radiate dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, typical of the sunflower family.

Anacyclus radiatus is often found in rocky soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
