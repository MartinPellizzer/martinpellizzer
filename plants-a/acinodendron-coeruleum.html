Acinodendron coeruleum

Acinodendron coeruleum, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
