Astragalus heterotrichus

Astragalus heterotrichus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many species in the genus Astragalus. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States.

Astragalus heterotrichus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
