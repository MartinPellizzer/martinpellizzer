Angiopteris intricata

Angiopteris intricata, commonly known as the intricate water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pellaeaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are often described as intricate and ornate. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically near streams or in dense forests. Its leaves are bi-directionally divided, creating a complex, fern-like structure that distinguishes it from other fern species.

Angiopteris intricata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is valued in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
