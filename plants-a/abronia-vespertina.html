Abronia vespertina

Abronia vespertina, commonly known as the evening geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Abronia vespertina is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
