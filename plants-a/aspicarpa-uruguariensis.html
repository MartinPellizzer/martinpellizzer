Aspicarpa uruguariensis

Aspicarpa uruguariensis, commonly known as the Uruguay persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, orange-red berries. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its berries are edible, though they are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
