Arum arborescens

Arum arborescens, commonly known as the tree arum, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are typically greenish-white in color and are followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds.
