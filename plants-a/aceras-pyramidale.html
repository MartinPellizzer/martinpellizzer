Aceras pyramidale

Aceras pyramidale, commonly known as the mountain fescue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Aceras pyramidale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
