Anatherum cyrtocladum

Anatherum cyrtocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

Anatherum cyrtocladum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
