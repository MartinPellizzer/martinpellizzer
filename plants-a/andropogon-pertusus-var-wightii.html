Andropogon pertusus var. wightii

Andropogon pertusus var. wightii, commonly known as Wight's switchgrass, is a variant of switchgrass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
