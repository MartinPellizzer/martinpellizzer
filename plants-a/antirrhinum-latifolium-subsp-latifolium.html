Antirrhinum latifolium subsp. latifolium

Antirrhinum latifolium subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf snapdragon, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers that range from red to orange and yellow.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
