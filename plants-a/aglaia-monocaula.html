Aglaia monocaula

Aglaia monocaula, commonly known as the single-stem aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody fruits containing aromatic seeds.

Aglaia monocaula is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
