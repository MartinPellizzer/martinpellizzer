Andropogon pungens

Andropogon pungens, commonly known as purple bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and striking purple-tinted inflorescence that appears in late summer. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
