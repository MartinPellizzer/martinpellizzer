Achras gaumeri

Achras gaumeri, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Achariaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a spherical fruit with a thick, fibrous outer layer and a sweet, edible pulp containing a single large seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and for local consumption.
