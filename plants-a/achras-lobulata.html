Achras lobulata

Achras lobulata, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces a valuable oil-rich fruit. The plant grows as a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit contains a hard-shelled seed surrounded by a fleshy, yellowish pulp.

The species is important in both ecological and economic contexts due to its oil production and role in local ecosystems.
