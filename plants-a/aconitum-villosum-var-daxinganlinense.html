Aconitum villosum var. daxinganlinense

Aconitum villosum var. daxinganlinense, commonly known as the Daxinganlin monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the forests of northeastern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers. The plant features a stout, hairy stem with alternate, palmately compound leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
