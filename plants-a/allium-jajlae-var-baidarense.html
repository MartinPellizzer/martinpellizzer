Allium jajlae var. baidarense

Allium jajlae var. baidarense, commonly known as the Baidaren onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, round bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is gaining popularity among horticultural enthusiasts.
