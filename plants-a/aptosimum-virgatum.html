Aptosimum virgatum

Aptosimum virgatum, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its slender, upright stems bear small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aptosimum virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
