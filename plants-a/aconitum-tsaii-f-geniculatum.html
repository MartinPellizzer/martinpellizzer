Aconitum tsaii f. geniculatum

Aconitum tsaii f. geniculatum, commonly known as the Geniculate Aconite, is a hybrid form of the Aconitum genus, characterized by its unique, twisting stems.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spiky clusters. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rugged texture and a strong, toxic aroma.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling due to its potent alkaloid content.
