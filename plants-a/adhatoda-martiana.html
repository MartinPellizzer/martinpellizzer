Adhatoda martiana

Adhatoda martiana, commonly known as the Indian Trumpet or Malabar Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is known for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Adhatoda martiana is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic and indigenous systems of medicine.
