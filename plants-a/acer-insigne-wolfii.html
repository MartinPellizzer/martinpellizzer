Acer insigne wolfii

Acer insigne wolfii, commonly known as the Sierra sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Acer, which includes various maple species known for their ornamental value. The tree is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and orange in autumn. Its flowers are small and green, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming more fissured with age, and the tree is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
