Amoora cumingiana

Amoora cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's evergreen fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
