Allium daninianum

Allium daninianum, commonly known as the Danin's garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are used in traditional herbal medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in various cultural contexts.
