Achneria capensis var. capillacea

Achneria capensis var. capillacea, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm climates.
