Aster tephrodes

Aster tephrodes, commonly known as the tephrodes aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
