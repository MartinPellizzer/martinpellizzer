Alpinia phoenicea

Alpinia phoenicea, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Alpinia phoenicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
