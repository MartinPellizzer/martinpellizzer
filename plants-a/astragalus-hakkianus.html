Astragalus hakkianus

Astragalus hakkianus, commonly known as the Tibetan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and neighboring areas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to harsh, arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
