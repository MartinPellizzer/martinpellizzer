Alyssum lapeyrouseanum var. angustifolium

Alyssum lapeyrouseanum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
