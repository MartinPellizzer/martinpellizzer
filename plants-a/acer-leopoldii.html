Acer leopoldii

Acer leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold maple, is a species of maple native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmately lobed leaves, which are typically red or yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
