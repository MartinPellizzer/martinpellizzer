Acoridium oreophilum

Acoridium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain acorus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
