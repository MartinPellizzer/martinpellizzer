Arisaema consanguineum

Arisaema consanguineum, commonly known as the Chinese threadleaf arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a short, stout rhizome. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Arisaema consanguineum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
