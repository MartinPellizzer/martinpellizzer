Asplenium macrolobium

Asplenium macrolobium, commonly known as the large-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a finely toothed margin and a pale, glossy underside.

Asplenium macrolobium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
