Anagallis longicaulis

Anagallis longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed pink, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Anagallis longicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along streambanks, and in disturbed habitats.
