Alsophila marianna

Alsophila marianna, commonly known as the Marianna tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a large, evergreen fern characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.

Alsophila marianna plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
