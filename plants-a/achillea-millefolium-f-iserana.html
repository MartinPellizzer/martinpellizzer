Achillea millefolium f. iserana

Achillea millefolium f. iserana, commonly known as the Iseran yarrow, is a subspecies of the common yarrow.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to the alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Asteraceae family.
