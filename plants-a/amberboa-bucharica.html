Amberboa bucharica

Amberboa bucharica, commonly known as the Turk's Cap lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers resembling small caps. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to full sun and arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding a bold splash of color to the landscape.
