Aleuritia wilsonii

Aleuritia wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Aleuritia wilsonii is also appreciated for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
