Abies microphylla

Abies microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf mountain fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its short, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it a dense, compact appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments. Its slow growth rate and resilience to harsh conditions make it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.

The plant plays an important role in providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native mountainous regions.
