Avena pratensis var. gexiana

Avena pratensis var. gexiana, commonly known as the slender oat, is a variant of the common oat species characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

It is a perennial grass that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a panicle-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, two-lobed spikelets that are often tinged with purple, and it is known for its fine, silky florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
