Abies fraseri

Abies fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America.

The cones of the Fraser fir are typically slender and elongated, with short, sticky bracts that help in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high elevations where the climate is temperate and humid.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in the Christmas tree industry.
