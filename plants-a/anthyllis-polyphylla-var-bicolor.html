Anthyllis polyphylla var. bicolor

Anthyllis polyphylla var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

This species is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.
