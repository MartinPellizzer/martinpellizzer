Astragalus hinkei

Astragalus hinkei, commonly known as the Hinkes milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Astragalus hinkei is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological significance.
