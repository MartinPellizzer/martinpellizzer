Aretia charpentieri

Aretia charpentieri, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that typically feature a dark purple or violet coloration. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, tropical environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical significance of Aretia charpentieri lies in its ecological role and its value in horticultural collections.
