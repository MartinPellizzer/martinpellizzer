Actinostemon glabrescens

Actinostemon glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed star-leaf, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Actinostemon glabrescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
