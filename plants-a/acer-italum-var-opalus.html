Acer italum var. opalus

Acer italum var. opalus, commonly known as the Italian maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It features a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree produces small, red or yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a pointed tip.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
