Agropyron czimganicum

Agropyron czimganicum, commonly known as the Czimgan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with small, awned florets. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Agropyron czimganicum is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
