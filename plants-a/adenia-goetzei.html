Adenia goetzei

Adenia goetzei, commonly known as the African mountain cucumber, is a striking climbing plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
