Amphipappus spinosus

Amphipappus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, spiny bracts that surround its flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer.

Amphipappus spinosus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
