Angianthus preissianus

Angianthus preissianus, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, erect stem that bears the showy blooms. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Angianthus preissianus is often found in sandy soils and is a symbol of resilience in the Australian outback.
