Acrocephalus indicus var. spicatus

Acrocephalus indicus var. spicatus, commonly known as the spiculate marsh warbler, is a variant of the marsh warbler native to wetland habitats in South and Southeast Asia.

It is a small, insectivorous bird with a distinctive, slender body and a long, pointed bill adapted for foraging in dense vegetation. The plant's botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
