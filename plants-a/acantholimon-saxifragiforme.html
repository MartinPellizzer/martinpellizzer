Acantholimon saxifragiforme

Acantholimon saxifragiforme, commonly known as the saxifrage-like thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features dense, grayish-blue foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Acantholimon saxifragiforme is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
