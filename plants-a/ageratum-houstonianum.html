Ageratum houstonianum

Ageratum houstonianum, commonly known as Mexican marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors including purple, blue, white, and pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum houstonianum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
