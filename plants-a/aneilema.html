Aneilema

Aneilema, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves and showy, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. Aneilema species typically thrive in humid, shady environments and are popular as ornamental plants. They have a fibrous root system and are often propagated through stem cuttings.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their decorative foliage and blooms.
