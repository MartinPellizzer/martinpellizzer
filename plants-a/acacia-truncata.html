Acacia truncata

Acacia truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia truncata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
