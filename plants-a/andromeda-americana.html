Andromeda americana

Andromeda americana, commonly known as the American wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are pink to red and bloom in early spring.

The plant is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
