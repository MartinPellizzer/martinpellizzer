Arabis sinaica

Arabis sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, coastal environments. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
