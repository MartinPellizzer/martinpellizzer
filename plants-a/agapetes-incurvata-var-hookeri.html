Agapetes incurvata var. hookeri

Agapetes incurvata var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's silverleaf, is a shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
