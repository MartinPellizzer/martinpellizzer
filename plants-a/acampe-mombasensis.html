Acampe mombasensis

Acampe mombasensis, commonly known as the Mombasa acacia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and yellow flowers. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ecological and potential medicinal importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
