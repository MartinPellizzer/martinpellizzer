Aruncus acuminatus

Aruncus acuminatus, commonly known as the mountain gothic, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of white to pinkish bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in a panicle inflorescence. Its foliage consists of large, lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a dramatic appearance.

Aruncus acuminatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
