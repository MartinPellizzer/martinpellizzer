Acianthera aberrans

Acianthera aberrans, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they attach to trees without parasitizing them.

Acianthera aberrans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
