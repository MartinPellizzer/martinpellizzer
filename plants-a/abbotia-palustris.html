Abbotia palustris

Abbotia palustris, commonly known as the swamp abbotia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It features striking, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
