Annona warmingiana

Annona warmingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a rough, spiky berry that ripens to a yellowish-green color and contains a creamy, sweet pulp. It is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
