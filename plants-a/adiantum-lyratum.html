Adiantum lyratum

Adiantum lyratum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are wiry and often covered in a fine, silky coating.

Adiantum lyratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
