Abutilon indicum subsp. indicum

Abutilon indicum subsp.

indicum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian abutilon, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
