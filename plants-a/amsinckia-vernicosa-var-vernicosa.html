Amsinckia vernicosa var. vernicosa

Amsinckia vernicosa var. vernicosa, commonly known as the stickyleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky resin when touched. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
