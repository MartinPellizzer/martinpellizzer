Asplenium mosetenense

Asplenium mosetenense, commonly known as the Moseten fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and humid understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori and indusia, are typical of ferns and contribute to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Asplenium mosetenense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
