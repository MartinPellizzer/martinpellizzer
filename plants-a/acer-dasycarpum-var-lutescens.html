Acer dasycarpum var. lutescens

Acer dasycarpum var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden-flowered maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in early spring, preceding the foliage. The leaves are palmately lobed, typically with five to seven segments, and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, clustered fruits known as samaras, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental display and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
