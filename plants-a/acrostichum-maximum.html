Acrostichum maximum

Acrostichum maximum, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, forming dense clumps near water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in moist environments.

Acrostichum maximum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
