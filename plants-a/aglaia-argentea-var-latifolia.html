Aglaia argentea var. latifolia

Aglaia argentea var. latifolia, commonly known as the silver aglaia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or pale green waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
