Acalypha elegantula

Acalypha elegantula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha elegantula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
