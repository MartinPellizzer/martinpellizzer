Astragalus kolymensis

Astragalus kolymensis, commonly known as the Kolyma milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are compound with numerous small leaflets. Astragalus kolymensis is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native range.
