Atriplex paludosa subsp. tridentata

Atriplex paludosa subsp.

tridentata, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant typically grows in saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hard seeds. Atriplex paludosa subsp.

tridentata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
