Adenophora manshurica

Adenophora manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with toothed edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Adenophora manshurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
