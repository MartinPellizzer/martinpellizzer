Alsine helmii

Alsine helmii, commonly known as the smooth bladderwort, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, aiding in its survival in damp environments.

Alsine helmii is also notable for its ability to reproduce both sexually and through vegetative propagation.
