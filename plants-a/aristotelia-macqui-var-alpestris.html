Aristotelia macqui var. alpestris

Aristotelia macqui var. alpestris, commonly known as the Andean wineberry, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its small, fleshy berries that are typically red or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, mountainous climates with well-drained soils, often growing at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and white or pink.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
