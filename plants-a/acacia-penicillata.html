Acacia penicillata

Acacia penicillata, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and heathlands.

Acacia penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Native Distribution

Acacia penicillata is primarily native to eastern Australia, including the states of New South Wales, Queensland, and Victoria.

It is commonly found in coastal regions and sandy soils along the eastern seaboard. This species thrives in temperate and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands and heathlands. It is also present in some parts of South Australia and Tasmania.

Acacia penicillata is well adapted to coastal conditions and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
