Astragalus cognatus

Astragalus cognatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus cognatus is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a food source for various wildlife species.
