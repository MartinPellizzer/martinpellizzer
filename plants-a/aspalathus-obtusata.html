Aspalathus obtusata

Aspalathus obtusata, commonly known as the sweet pea bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has woody stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Aspalathus obtusata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
