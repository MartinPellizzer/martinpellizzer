Agraphis serotina

Agraphis serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering sheetgrass, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Agraphis serotina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.
