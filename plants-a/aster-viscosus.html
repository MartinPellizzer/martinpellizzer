Aster viscosus

Aster viscosus, commonly known as sticky aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swamps.

Aster viscosus is often used in wetland restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
