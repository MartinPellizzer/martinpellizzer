Amatula flava

Amatula flava, commonly known as the yellow amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Amatula flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
