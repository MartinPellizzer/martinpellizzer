Acer mono subsp. ambiguum

Acer mono subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as the Mongolian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, clustered green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
