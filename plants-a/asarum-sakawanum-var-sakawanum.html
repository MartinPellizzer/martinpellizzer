Asarum sakawanum var. sakawanum

Asarum sakawanum var. sakawanum, commonly known as the Sakawa wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories and is often found in humid tropical environments. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its unique floral structure and foliage.
