Aechmea surinamensis

Aechmea surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam pineapple, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup, known as a rosette. The plant produces vibrant, spathaceous flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling a pineapple in appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Aechmea surinamensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage structure.

Morphology

Aechmea surinamensis has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered with fine trichomes, arranged in a compact rosette at the base of the plant.

The leaves are dark green with a silvery underside, measuring up to 40 cm in length and 5 cm in width. The plant has a short, unbranched stem that is covered with a thick, fibrous sheath resembling bark, and it grows primarily from the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of red or purple flowers with spadices, blooming during the dry season, and it is pollinated by insects such as beetles and flies.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind and water.
