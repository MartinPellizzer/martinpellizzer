Arabis tibetica

Arabis tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs to reduce water loss.

Arabis tibetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
