Allophylus pallidus

Allophylus pallidus, commonly known as the pale alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
