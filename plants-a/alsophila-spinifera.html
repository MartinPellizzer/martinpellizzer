Alsophila spinifera

Alsophila spinifera, commonly known as the spinifera tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, bipinnate fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in soil while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Alsophila spinifera plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
