Apluda scirpoides

Apluda scirpoides, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Apluda scirpoides plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
