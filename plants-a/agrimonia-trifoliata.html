Agrimonia trifoliata

Agrimonia trifoliata, commonly known as three-leaved agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has serrated, trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Agrimonia trifoliata is known for its astringent properties and has been used historically to treat digestive and skin ailments.
