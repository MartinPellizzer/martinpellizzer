Astragalus marosportensis

Astragalus marosportensis, commonly known as the Marosportensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small pods that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
