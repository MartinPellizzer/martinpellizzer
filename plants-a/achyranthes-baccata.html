Achyranthes baccata

Achyranthes baccata, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Achyranthes baccata is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
