Actinodaphne procera

Actinodaphne procera, commonly known as the giant laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and aromatic bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its timber and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
