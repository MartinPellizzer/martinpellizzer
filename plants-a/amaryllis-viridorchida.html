Amaryllis viridorchida

Amaryllis viridorchida, commonly known as the green-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a greenish tinge. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.

The bulbs are perennial, capable of surviving through winter dormancy and regenerating each growing season.
