Aristolochia nana

Aristolochia nana, commonly known as the dwarf Dutchman's pipe, is a small herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and produces unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically purple or brown in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, hooded flowers that attract specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
