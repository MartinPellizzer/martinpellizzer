Aira major subsp. alpina

Aira major subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine airygrass, is a perennial grass species native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
