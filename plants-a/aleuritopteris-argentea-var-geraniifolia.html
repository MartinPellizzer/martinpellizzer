Aleuritopteris argentea var. geraniifolia

Aleuritopteris argentea var. geraniifolia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a fern with a silver sheen.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features bipinnate fronds with a striking silvery hue on the underside, which is due to the presence of trichomes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The fronds are typically arching and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
