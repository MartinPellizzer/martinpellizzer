Amerimnon ecastaphyllum

Amerimnon ecastaphyllum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, violet-pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a tall, branching stem that supports its composite flower heads. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
