Aridaria vespertina

Aridaria vespertina, commonly known as the evening desert orchid, is a rare flowering plant adapted to arid environments.

It features succulent leaves and produces striking, nocturnal blooms that open in response to temperature changes. The plant's roots are deep and specialized to access water from underground sources. It thrives in sandy soils with minimal rainfall, often found in desert regions.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a keystone species in its ecosystem.
