Auxopus letouzeyi

Auxopus letouzeyi, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped blooms that emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Auxopus letouzeyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
