Astragalus micracme

Astragalus micracme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus micracme plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
