Amaranthus graecizans var. blitum

Amaranthus graecizans var. blitum, commonly known as wild amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

This species is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
