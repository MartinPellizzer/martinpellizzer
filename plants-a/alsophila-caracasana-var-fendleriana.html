Alsophila caracasana var. fendleriana

Alsophila caracasana var. fendleriana, commonly known as the Fendler's tree fern, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Venezuela.

It is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically bright green and exhibit a distinctive, elegant appearance.
