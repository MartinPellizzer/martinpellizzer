Aster multiflorus var. commutatus

Aster multiflorus var. commutatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
