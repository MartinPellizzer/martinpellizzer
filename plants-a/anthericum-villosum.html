Anthericum villosum

Anthericum villosum, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Anthericum villosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
