Alsophila similis

Alsophila similis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound fronds. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rainforests or along stream banks. Its rhizome system supports its growth, and it can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
