Arachnorchis anthracina

Arachnorchis anthracina, commonly known as the black orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves and its showy, fragrant flowers that range from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers are borne on tall, erect stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Arachnorchis anthracina is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
