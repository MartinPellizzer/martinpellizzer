Acoridium lineare

Acoridium lineare, commonly known as the linear acorus, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Acoraceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that float on the water's surface, with a distinct midrib running along the length of each leaf. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, a feature common in many monocot plants. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands.

Acoridium lineare plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
