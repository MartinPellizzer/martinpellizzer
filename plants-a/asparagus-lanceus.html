Asparagus lanceus

Asparagus lanceus, commonly known as the lanceleaf asparagus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
