Abutilon crinitum

Abutilon crinitum, commonly known as the silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its fibrous bark and hairy foliage make it a distinctive and ornamental species in botanical gardens.
