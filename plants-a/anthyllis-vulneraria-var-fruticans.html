Anthyllis vulneraria var. fruticans

Anthyllis vulneraria var. fruticans, commonly known as the creeping yellow sweet clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean flora and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
