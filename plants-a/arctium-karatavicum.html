Arctium karatavicum

Arctium karatavicum, commonly known as the Karatavicum burdock, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
