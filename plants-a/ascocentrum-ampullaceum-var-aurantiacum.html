Ascocentrum ampullaceum var. aurantiacum

Ascocentrum ampullaceum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden arrow orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces striking, golden-yellow flowers that resemble arrows, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
