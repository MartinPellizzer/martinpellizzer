Ailanthus glandulosa var. erythocarpa

Ailanthus glandulosa var. erythocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tree of heaven, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 12 to 30 pairs of pinnate leaflets and yellowish-green flowers that appear in early summer. The tree produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

The plant has a long taproot and can tolerate urban environments, making it a common invasive species in many regions.
