Achyranthes zaririi

Achyranthes zaririi, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red or brown fruits. It is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
