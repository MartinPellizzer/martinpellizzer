Apium elatum

Apium elatum, commonly known as giant parsley, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow stems and umbrella-like inflorescences. The plant can grow up to 3 meters in height, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, smooth, yellowish fruits.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
