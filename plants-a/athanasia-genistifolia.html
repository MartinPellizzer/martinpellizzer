Athanasia genistifolia

Athanasia genistifolia, commonly known as the golden geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Athanasia genistifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
