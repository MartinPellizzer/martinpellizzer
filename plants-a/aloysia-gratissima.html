Aloysia gratissima

Aloysia gratissima, commonly known as the Cape vermouth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aloysia gratissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
