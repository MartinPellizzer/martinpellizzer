Amaranthus palmeri var. glomeratus

Amaranthus palmeri var. glomeratus, commonly known as Palmer's amaranth, is a highly branched annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences and lanceolate, often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-winged, triangular seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and competitive nature.

This species is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its invasive potential and ability to reduce crop yields.
