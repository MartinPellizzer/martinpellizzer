Artemisia nitrosa var. kasakorum

Artemisia nitrosa var. kasakorum, commonly known as the Karakorum wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It contains volatile oils and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various pollinators and small animals.
