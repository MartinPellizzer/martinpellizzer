Amorphophallus rivierei var. konjac

Amorphophallus rivierei var. konjac, commonly known as konjac, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves and a swollen, underground corm that serves as its storage organ. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Konjac is particularly valued for its starchy corm, which is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
