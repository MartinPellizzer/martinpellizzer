Alchemilla salmoniana

Alchemilla salmoniana, commonly known as the salmon alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to rounded, with a soft, velvety texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early summer, clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Alchemilla salmoniana is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
