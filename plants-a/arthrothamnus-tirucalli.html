Arthrothamnus tirucalli

Arthrothamnus tirucalli, commonly known as the Indian fire bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice in dryland gardens.
