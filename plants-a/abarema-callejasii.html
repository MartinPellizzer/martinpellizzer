Abarema callejasii

Abarema callejasii, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, ornamental flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance, it is cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
