Altensteinia argyrolepis

Altensteinia argyrolepis, commonly known as the silver-leaved altensteinia, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, silky hairs on the leaf surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered spore cases known as soruses, which are typically located on the undersides of the leaves. Altensteinia argyrolepis thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
