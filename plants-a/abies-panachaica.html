Abies panachaica

Abies panachaica, commonly known as the Panachea fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Abies panachaica is adapted to cold, mountainous climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
