Agave seemanniana

Agave seemanniana, commonly known as the Mexican hat agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white to yellowish marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears showy, bell-shaped flowers, often in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Agave seemanniana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
