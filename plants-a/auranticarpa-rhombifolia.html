Auranticarpa rhombifolia

Auranticarpa rhombifolia, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is notable for its high vitamin C content and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
