Anisacantha erinacea

Anisacantha erinacea, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a spiny, branched shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, globular to cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a succulent, drought-tolerant nature.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
