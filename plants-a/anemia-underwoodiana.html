Anemia underwoodiana

Anemia underwoodiana, commonly known as the golden spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Anemia underwoodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
