Astragalus alaarczensis

Astragalus alaarczensis, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Astragalus alaarczensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
