Astragalus praelongus var. praelongus

Astragalus praelongus var. praelongus, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
