Arrabidaea decora var. macrophylla

Arrabidaea decora var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf arrabidaea, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features robust, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often prominently veined. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically large, up to 10 inches long, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
