Ammannia tenella

Ammannia tenella, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered pink or red flowers during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-rich environments.

Ammannia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
