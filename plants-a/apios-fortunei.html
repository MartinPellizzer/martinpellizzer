Apios fortunei

Apios fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese wild bean, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It features a tuberous root system that resembles a yam, which is edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. The plant produces upright stems with pinnately compound leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. Its fruit is a hairy pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Apios fortunei is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
