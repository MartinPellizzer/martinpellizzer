Atriplex leptoclada

Atriplex leptoclada, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant has small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its stems are often prostrate or erect, and the leaves are usually fleshy and arranged oppositely.

Atriplex leptoclada plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
