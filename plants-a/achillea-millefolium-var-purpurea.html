Achillea millefolium var. purpurea

Achillea millefolium var. purpurea, commonly known as Purple Yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are gray-green in color and emit a slight aromatic scent. The plant produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in medicine and as a symbol of love and devotion adds to its cultural significance.
