Astragalus calamistratus

Astragalus calamistratus, commonly known as the scaly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hooked spines and its showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Astragalus calamistratus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
