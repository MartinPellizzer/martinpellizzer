Acalypha brasiliensis var. asterotricha

Acalypha brasiliensis var. asterotricha, commonly known as the star-tipped gloryflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small star clusters, composed of numerous tiny, fleshy flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and often exhibit a vibrant green color. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
