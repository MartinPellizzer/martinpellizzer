Agropyron pseudocaesium

Agropyron pseudocaesium, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Agropyron pseudocaesium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
