Achillea millefolium var. magna

Achillea millefolium var. magna, commonly known as the giant yarrow, is a variant of the common yarrow plant.

It is characterized by its larger, more robust foliage compared to the standard variety, with broad, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
