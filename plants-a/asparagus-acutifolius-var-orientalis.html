Asparagus acutifolius var. orientalis

Asparagus acutifolius var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern asparagus or oriental asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, spiny stems and small, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its edible shoots, which are harvested in the spring.
