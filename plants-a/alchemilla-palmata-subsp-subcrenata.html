Alchemilla palmata subsp. subcrenata

Alchemilla palmata subsp.

subcrenata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, rounded clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
