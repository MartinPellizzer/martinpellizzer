Acalypha grandis var. amboynensis

Acalypha grandis var. amboynensis, commonly known as the Amboyna beautyleaf, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that range from red to yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
