Aster novi-belgii f. roseus

Aster novi-belgii f. roseus, commonly known as the Roseum Aster, is a cultivar of the New Belgium Aster, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fluffy flower heads.

It produces vibrant, rose-colored flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant is native to the United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. It is a hardy perennial that is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic value.

Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.
