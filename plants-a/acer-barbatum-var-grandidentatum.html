Acer barbatum var. grandidentatum

Acer barbatum var. grandidentatum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant red, orange, and yellow colors during the fall season. The leaves are palmately lobed with serrated edges, and they are typically large, reaching up to 10 inches in length. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The tree is also valued for its elegant form and seasonal beauty, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
