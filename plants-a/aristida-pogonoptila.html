Aristida pogonoptila

Aristida pogonoptila, commonly known as the blackbrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils make it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Aristida pogonoptila plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
