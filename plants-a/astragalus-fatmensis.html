Astragalus fatmensis

Astragalus fatmensis, commonly known as the Fatmen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat due to its deep root system.

Astragalus fatmensis is also of interest to botanists for its potential medicinal properties and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
