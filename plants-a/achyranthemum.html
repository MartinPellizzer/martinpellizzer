Achyranthemum

Achyranthemum, commonly known as the bloodwort, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their erect or prostrate growth habit and are often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and have a rough or hairy texture. The flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a reddish or purple hue.

Achyranthemum species are adapted to a variety of climates and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
