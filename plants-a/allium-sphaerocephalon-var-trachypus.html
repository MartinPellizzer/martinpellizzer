Allium sphaerocephalon var. trachypus

Allium sphaerocephalon var. trachypus, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a compact, spherical bulb with a strong garlic odor, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. The plant produces umbels of white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its culinary uses and ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
