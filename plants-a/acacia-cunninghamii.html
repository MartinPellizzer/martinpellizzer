Acacia cunninghamii

Acacia cunninghamii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that serve as the primary photosynthetic organs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia cunninghamii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
