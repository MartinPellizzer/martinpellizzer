Alchornea grandis

Alchornea grandis, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries containing seeds. It is well adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a valuable species in arid landscapes.

Its ecological and medicinal uses are also of interest in traditional practices.
