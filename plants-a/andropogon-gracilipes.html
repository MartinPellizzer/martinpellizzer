Andropogon gracilipes

Andropogon gracilipes, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Andropogon gracilipes is an important component of native grassland ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
