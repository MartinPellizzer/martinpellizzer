Athyrium caespitosum

Athyrium caespitosum, commonly known as the dense fern or Japanese painted fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and striking, finely divided, bluish-green fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile ones appearing in the second year.

Athyrium caespitosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
