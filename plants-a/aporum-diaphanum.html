Aporum diaphanum

Aporum diaphanum, commonly known as the glassy fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a unique and ethereal appearance. The plant's fronds are typically slender and arching, with a soft, feathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and indirect light.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
