Arbutus furiens

Arbutus furiens, commonly known as the "fury" or "devil's paintbrush," is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but have a strong, musky flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and seasonal blooms, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
