Arthraxon amplexifolium

Arthraxon amplexifolium, commonly known as the clasping grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering units typical of grasses.

Arthraxon amplexifolium is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
