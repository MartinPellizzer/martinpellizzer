Ardisia ternatensis var. ternatensis

Ardisia ternatensis var. ternatensis, commonly known as the Java ardisia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The berries are edible but can be toxic in large quantities, making it both a decorative and potentially hazardous plant.
