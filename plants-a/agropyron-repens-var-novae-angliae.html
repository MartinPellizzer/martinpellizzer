Agropyron repens var. novae-angliae

Agropyron repens var. novae-angliae, commonly known as New England bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green colored leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
