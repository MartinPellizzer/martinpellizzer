Austronea ciliolata

Austronea ciliolata, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hair-like structures.

Austronea ciliolata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
