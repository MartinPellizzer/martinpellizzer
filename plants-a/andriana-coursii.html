Andriana coursii

Andriana coursii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its native habitat.

Andriana coursii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
