Andropogon ascinodis

Andropogon ascinodis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Andropogon ascinodis is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
