Aspidogyne nobilis var. argyrocentra

Aspidogyne nobilis var. argyrocentra, commonly known as the silver-centered aspidogyne, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring broad, elliptical leaves with a distinctive silvery-white midrib. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
