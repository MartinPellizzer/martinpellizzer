Arenaria capillaris subsp. americana

Arenaria capillaris subsp.

americana, commonly known as American sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry, open habitats.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often prostrate or ascending.

It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
