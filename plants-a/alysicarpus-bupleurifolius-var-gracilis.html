Alysicarpus bupleurifolius var. gracilis

Alysicarpus bupleurifolius var. gracilis, commonly known as slender leaf bupleurifolius, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and trifoliate or pinnate leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in racemes, and the fruit is a hairy, three-lobed pod that dehisces to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
