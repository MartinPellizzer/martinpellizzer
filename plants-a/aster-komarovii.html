Aster komarovii

Aster komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
