Aspalathus rostripetala

Aspalathus rostripetala, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Aspalathus rostripetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
