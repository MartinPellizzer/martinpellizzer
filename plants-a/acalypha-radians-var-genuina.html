Acalypha radians var. genuina

Acalypha radians var. genuina, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically bright yellow or orange, often with a red or pink margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is popular in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
