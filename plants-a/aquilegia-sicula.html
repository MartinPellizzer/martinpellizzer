Aquilegia sicula

Aquilegia sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian columbine, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It features showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, supporting various pollinators.
