Archibaccharis peninsularis

Archibaccharis peninsularis, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in coastal areas and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
