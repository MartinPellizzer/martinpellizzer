Alternanthera microphylla

Alternanthera microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often bright red or purple in color. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
