Arcangelisia tympanopoda

Arcangelisia tympanopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Arcangelisia tympanopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and floral display.
