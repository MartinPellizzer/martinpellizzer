Acritopappus jacobaeus

Acritopappus jacobaeus, commonly known as Jacob's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
