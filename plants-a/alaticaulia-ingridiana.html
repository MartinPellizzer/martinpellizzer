Alaticaulia ingridiana

Alaticaulia ingridiana, commonly known as the Ingrid's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warm season, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Alaticaulia ingridiana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
