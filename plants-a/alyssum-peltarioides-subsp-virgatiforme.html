Alyssum peltarioides subsp. virgatiforme

Alyssum peltarioides subsp.

virgatiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
