Arabis Hirsuta Var. Allionii

Arabis hirsuta var. allionii is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe. It is known for its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. This variety is closely related to the common rockcress, but it has distinct hairy leaves and a more compact growth habit. Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species. Conservation efforts are essential to preserve this unique plant for future generations.
