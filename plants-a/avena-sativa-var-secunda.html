Avena sativa var. secunda

Avena sativa var. secunda, commonly known as slender oat, is a lesser-known variant of the common oat species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and reduced leaf size compared to typical oats. This variety is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are small and have a high nutritional value, making it a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of the Poaceae family, including hollow stems and parallel-veined leaves.
