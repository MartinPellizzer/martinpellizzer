Astragalus dianat-nejadii

Astragalus dianat-nejadii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for potential medicinal uses due to its biochemical properties.
