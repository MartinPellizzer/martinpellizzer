Archidendron kunstleri subsp. ashtonii

Archidendron kunstleri subsp.

ashtonii, commonly known as the Ashton's legume, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems through soil enrichment and habitat provision.
