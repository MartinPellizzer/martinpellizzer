Agastache urticifolia

Agastache urticifolia, commonly known as green giant hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive minty aroma. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry slopes and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a nectar source for pollinators.
