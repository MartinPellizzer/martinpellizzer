Agathosma sedifolia

Agathosma sedifolia, commonly known as African germander, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
