Anathallis simpliciglossa

Anathallis simpliciglossa, commonly known as the simple-tongued anathallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which plays a role in its specific pollination strategy.
