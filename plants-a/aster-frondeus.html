Aster frondeus

Aster frondeus, commonly known as the fronded aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Aster frondeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
