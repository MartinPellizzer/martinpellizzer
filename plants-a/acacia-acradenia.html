Acacia acradenia

Acacia acradenia, commonly known as the African bristlenut, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feathery leaflets.

The wood of Acacia acradenia is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.
