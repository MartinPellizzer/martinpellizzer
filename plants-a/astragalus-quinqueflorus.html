Astragalus quinqueflorus

Astragalus quinqueflorus, commonly known as the five-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus quinqueflorus plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
