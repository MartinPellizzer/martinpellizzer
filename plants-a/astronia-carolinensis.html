Astronia carolinensis

Astronia carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
