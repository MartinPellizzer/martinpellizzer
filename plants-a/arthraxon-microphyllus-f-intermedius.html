Arthraxon microphyllus f. intermedius

Arthraxon microphyllus f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate small-leaved arthraxon, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often in wetlands or along water bodies. Its leaves are usually shorter and more compact compared to the typical species of the genus Arthraxon.

This form is considered a natural variant, showing intermediate traits between the standard species and other related forms.
