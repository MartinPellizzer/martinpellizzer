Aloe macroclada

Aloe macroclada, commonly known as the giant aloe or large-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Aloe macroclada is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
