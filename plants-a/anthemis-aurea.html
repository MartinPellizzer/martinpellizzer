Anthemis aurea

Anthemis aurea, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by pale green or white ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Anthemis aurea is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
