Achillea stricta

Achillea stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and rocky habitats.

Its historical use in herbal medicine is linked to its ability to promote wound healing and reduce inflammation.
