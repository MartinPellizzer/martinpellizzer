Arctostaphylos hispidula

Arctostaphylos hispidula, commonly known as the hairy manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, gnarled branches and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a small, persistent capsule that contains several seeds.

Arctostaphylos hispidula is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
