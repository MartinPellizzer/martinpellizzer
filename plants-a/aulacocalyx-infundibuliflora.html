Aulacocalyx infundibuliflora

Aulacocalyx infundibuliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
