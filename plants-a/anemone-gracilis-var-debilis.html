Anemone gracilis var. debilis

Anemone gracilis var. debilis, commonly known as the slender anemone, is a delicate flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender stems with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often used in woodland gardens.
