Aster acris subsp. trinervis

Aster acris subsp.

trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined aster, is a perennial herb native to North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have three distinct veins. Its showy flowers are usually blue to purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
