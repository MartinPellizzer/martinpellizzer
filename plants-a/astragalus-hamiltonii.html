Astragalus hamiltonii

Astragalus hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus hamiltonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
