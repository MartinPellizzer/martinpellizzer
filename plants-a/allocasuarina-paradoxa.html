Allocasuarina paradoxa

Allocasuarina paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Sheoak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like phyllodes that are typically blue-green in color, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Allocasuarina paradoxa plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
