Astragalus macrourus subsp. pseudotauricola

Astragalus macrourus subsp.

pseudotauricola, commonly known as the Tauric milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
