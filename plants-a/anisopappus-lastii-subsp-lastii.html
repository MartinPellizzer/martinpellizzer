Anisopappus lastii subsp. lastii

Anisopappus lastii subsp.

lastii, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy stems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
