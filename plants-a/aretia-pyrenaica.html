Aretia pyrenaica

Aretia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean aretia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Aretia pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
