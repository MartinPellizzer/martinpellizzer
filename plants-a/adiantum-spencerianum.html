Adiantum spencerianum

Adiantum spencerianum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of North America, particularly the southeastern United States. The plant features finely divided, bi-lobed leaves that are typically bright green and turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil.

Adiantum spencerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in indoor settings.
