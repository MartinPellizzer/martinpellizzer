Astragalus klementzii

Astragalus klementzii, commonly known as the Klementz's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus klementzii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
