Aechmea aquilega

Aechmea aquilega, commonly known as the velvet leaf bromeliad, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and covered in a dense, velvety coating of trichomes, which helps in water retention. It produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Morphology

Aechmea aquilega has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a compact spiral, with a waxy, bluish-green color and a pointed tip.

The plant has a short, fibrous stem with a smooth, dark green bark that grows upward from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red and orange, and bloom during the dry season, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
