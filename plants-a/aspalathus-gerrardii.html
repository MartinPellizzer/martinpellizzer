Aspalathus gerrardii

Aspalathus gerrardii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
