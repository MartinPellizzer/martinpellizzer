Arisaema neglectum

Arisaema neglectum, commonly known as the neglected arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a broad, leaf-like spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Arisaema neglectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
