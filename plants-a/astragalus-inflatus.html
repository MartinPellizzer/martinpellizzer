Astragalus inflatus

Astragalus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated milkvetch, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to pale purple flowers.

Astragalus inflatus is often found in regions of the western United States and parts of Canada.
