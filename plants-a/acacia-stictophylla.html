Acacia stictophylla

Acacia stictophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to grey hairs. Acacia stictophylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

It produces small yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by flat, ribbed pods containing seeds.
