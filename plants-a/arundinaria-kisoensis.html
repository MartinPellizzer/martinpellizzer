Arundinaria kisoensis

Arundinaria kisoensis, commonly known as the Kiso bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, bluish-green appearance.

Arundinaria kisoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
