Aquilegia vulgaris var. saccocentra

Aquilegia vulgaris var. saccocentra, commonly known as the Saccocentra columbine, is a variant of the common columbine characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two elongated nectar-filled spurs. The leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets arranged radiantly around a central stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland and meadow habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
