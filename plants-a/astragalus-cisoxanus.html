Astragalus cisoxanus

Astragalus cisoxanus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied for potential health benefits.
