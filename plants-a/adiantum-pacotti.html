Adiantum pacotti

Adiantum pacotti, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a subtle golden hue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its fronds are bi-lobed and often have a distinctive, curling appearance.

Adiantum pacotti is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
