Allium xanthicum

Allium xanthicum, commonly known as the yellow garlic or wild leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbous stem is often used in cooking, similar to other allium species.

Allium xanthicum is valued for its aromatic qualities and is also a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
