Anthemis montana var. collina

Anthemis montana var. collina, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
