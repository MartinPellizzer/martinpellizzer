Aplotaxis denticulata var. hypoleuca

Aplotaxis denticulata var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-stemmed African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its stems are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
