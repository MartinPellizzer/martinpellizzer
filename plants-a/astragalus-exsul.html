Astragalus exsul

Astragalus exsul, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to brown flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus exsul plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
