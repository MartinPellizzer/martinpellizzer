Agapetes loranthiflora var. loranthiflora

Agapetes loranthiflora var. loranthiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
