Anthyllis vulneraria subsp. weldeniana

Anthyllis vulneraria subsp.

weldeniana, commonly known as the Welden's viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
