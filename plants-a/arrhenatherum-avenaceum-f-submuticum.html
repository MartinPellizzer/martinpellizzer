Arrhenatherum avenaceum f. submuticum

Arrhenatherum avenaceum f. submuticum, commonly known as the submutic form of meadow foxtail, is a variant of the grass species characterized by its slightly hairy leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
