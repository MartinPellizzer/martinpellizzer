Anacamptis pyramidalis f. angistiloba

Anacamptis pyramidalis f. angistiloba, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed pyramidal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers arranged in a pyramidal shape. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This orchid is native to southern Europe and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
