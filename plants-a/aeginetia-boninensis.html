Aeginetia boninensis

Aeginetia boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin Islands thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands in the Pacific Ocean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy habitats, showing adaptation to saline and wind-exposed environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
