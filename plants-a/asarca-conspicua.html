Asarca conspicua

Asarca conspicua, commonly known as the showy asarum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive, complex structure that resembles a pipe or a flower.

Asarca conspicua is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
