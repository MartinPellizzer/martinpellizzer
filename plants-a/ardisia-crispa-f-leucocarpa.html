Ardisia crispa f. leucocarpa

Ardisia crispa f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited beautyberry, is a variant of the common beautyberry.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. The berries are notably smaller and have a lighter color compared to the typical variety.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
