Aster marylandicus

Aster marylandicus, commonly known as Maryland aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head.

Maryland aster is often found in the wild and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
