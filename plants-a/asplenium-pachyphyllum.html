Asplenium pachyphyllum

Asplenium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often thick foliage.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped and have a dark green color with a rough texture.

Asplenium pachyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
