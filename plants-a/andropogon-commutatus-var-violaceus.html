Andropogon commutatus var. violaceus

Andropogon commutatus var. violaceus, commonly known as violet bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which turns reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or violet in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies.

Violet bluestem is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
