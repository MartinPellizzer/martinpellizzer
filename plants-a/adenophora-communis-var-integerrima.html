Adenophora communis var. integerrima

Adenophora communis var. integerrima, commonly known as the common adenophora, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
