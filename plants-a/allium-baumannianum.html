Allium baumannianum

Allium baumannianum, commonly known as the bulbous leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous base and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its mild, garlic-like scent.
