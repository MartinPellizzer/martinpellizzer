Andropogon nardus subsp. flexuosus

Andropogon nardus subsp.

flexuosus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, silky leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and swampy areas. Its slender, tufted inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
