Aster laevis var. falcatus

Aster laevis var. falcatus, commonly known as the smooth aster or falcate aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
