Astragalus conjunctus var. conjunctus

Astragalus conjunctus var. conjunctus, commonly known as the western jointleaf paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
