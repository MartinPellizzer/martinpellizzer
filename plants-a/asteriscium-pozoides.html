Asteriscium pozoides

Asteriscium pozoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Asteriscium pozoides is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
