Astragalus argophyllus var. pephragmenoides

Astragalus argophyllus var. pephragmenoides, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is deep and fibrous. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
