Abies williamsonii

Abies williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cones. The tree typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
