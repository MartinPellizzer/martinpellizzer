Aster eliasii

Aster eliasii, commonly known as the Elias's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-green color. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, blooming in late summer.

Aster eliasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
