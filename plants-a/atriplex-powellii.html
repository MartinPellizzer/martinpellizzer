Atriplex powellii

Atriplex powellii, commonly known as Powell's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, sage-green leaves that are coated with a waxy layer to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.

Powell's saltbush plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
