Agnus-castus incisa

Agnus-castus incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chaste tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Agnus-castus incisa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and historical associations with chastity and fertility.
