Amphilophis compressa

Amphilophis compressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Its growth habit involves trailing or climbing, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
