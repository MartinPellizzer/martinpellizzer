Apios bodinieri

Apios bodinieri, commonly known as the yellow wild bean, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Apios bodinieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples.
