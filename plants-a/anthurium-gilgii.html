Anthurium gilgii

Anthurium gilgii, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking spadix inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from deep red to purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium gilgii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
