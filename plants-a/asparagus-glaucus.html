Asparagus glaucus

Asparagus glaucus, commonly known as the silver asparagus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive silver-gray appearance. The plant produces small, white to green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, needle-like spears that are harvested before they fully elongate. Asparagus glaucus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
