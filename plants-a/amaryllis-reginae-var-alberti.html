Amaryllis reginae var. alberti

Amaryllis reginae var. alberti, commonly known as the Alberti's Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and succulent, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This variety is prized for its showy blooms and ease of cultivation in both indoor and outdoor settings.
