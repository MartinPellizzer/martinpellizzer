Arundina graminifolia subsp. caespitosa

Arundina graminifolia subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the bamboo reed, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae). It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and long, slender blades that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-like, with small, pale green to brownish flowers. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wet or marshy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
