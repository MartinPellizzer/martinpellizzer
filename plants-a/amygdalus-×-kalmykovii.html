Amygdalus × kalmykovii

Amygdalus × kalmykovii, commonly known as the Kalmykovii peach, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus persica (peach) and Prunus davidiana (Davidian apricot).

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its moderate growth habit and ornamental value, with showy pink to white flowers in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruits are small, round, and typically have a fuzzy skin.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of China and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential in horticultural breeding programs.
