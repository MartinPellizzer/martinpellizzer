Ageratum mexicanum var. nanum

Ageratum mexicanum var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf ageratum, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features small, fluffy flowers in shades of white, purple, and blue, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
