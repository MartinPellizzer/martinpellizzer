Acanthus hirsutus

Acanthus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy acanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in soft, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, spiny flower spikes that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and architectural form make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
