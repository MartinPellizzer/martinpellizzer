Ageratum confertum

Ageratum confertum, commonly known as the common ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of blue, purple, or white flowers. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Ageratum confertum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
