Arenaria mirdamadii

Arenaria mirdamadii, commonly known as the Mirdamad sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
