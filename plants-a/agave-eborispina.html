Agave eborispina

Agave eborispina, commonly known as the Eborispina agave, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with sharp spines along the edges, providing both protection and a distinctive appearance. It produces tall flowering stalks that bear small, white flowers, which are followed by clusters of fleshy, spiky fruits.

This plant is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
