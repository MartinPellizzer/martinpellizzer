Appunia megalantha

Appunia megalantha, commonly known as the giant bluebell, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
