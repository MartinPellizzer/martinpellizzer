Asteriscus daltonii subsp. daltonii

Asteriscus daltonii subsp.

daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's asterisk, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually purple to pink, and it produces tiny, capsule-like fruits.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in drought-tolerant gardens.
