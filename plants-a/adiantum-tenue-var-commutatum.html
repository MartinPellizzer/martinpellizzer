Adiantum tenue var. commutatum

Adiantum tenue var. commutatum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species characterized by its finely divided, lacy fronds.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, narrow, and arching appearance. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for indoor or outdoor gardens with proper humidity and light conditions.
