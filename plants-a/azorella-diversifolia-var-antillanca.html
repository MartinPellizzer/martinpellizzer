Azorella diversifolia var. antillanca

Azorella diversifolia var. antillanca, commonly known as the Antillean Azorella, is a perennial cushion plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves. Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is an important component of the alpine flora in its native range.
