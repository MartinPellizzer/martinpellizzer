Anchietea pyrifolia

Anchietea pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow as a small tree or shrub. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

Anchietea pyrifolia is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to attract pollinators.
