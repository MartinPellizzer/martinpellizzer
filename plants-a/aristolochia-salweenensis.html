Aristolochia salweenensis

Aristolochia salweenensis, commonly known as the Salween Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a distinctive, aromatic scent. It is often found in moist, shaded environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
