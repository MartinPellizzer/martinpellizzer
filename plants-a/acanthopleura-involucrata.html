Acanthopleura involucrata

Acanthopleura involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an invasive species in many areas.
