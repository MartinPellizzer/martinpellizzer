Aloysia uruguayensis

Aloysia uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Aloysia uruguayensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
