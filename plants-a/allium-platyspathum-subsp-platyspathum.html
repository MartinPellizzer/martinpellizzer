Allium platyspathum subsp. platyspathum

Allium platyspathum subsp.

platyspathum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the summer.

The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as garlic and onions. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable plant for culinary and medicinal use.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
