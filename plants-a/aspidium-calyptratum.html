Aspidium calyptratum

Aspidium calyptratum, commonly known as the helmet fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a unique, helmet-like appearance on the underside.

Aspidium calyptratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
