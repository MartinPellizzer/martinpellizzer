Adonis dahurica

Adonis dahurica, commonly known as the Caucasian glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with prominent central spurs. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and larkspurs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Adonis dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
