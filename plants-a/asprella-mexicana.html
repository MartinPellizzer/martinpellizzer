Asprella mexicana

Asprella mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican starfruit or Mexican asprella, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
