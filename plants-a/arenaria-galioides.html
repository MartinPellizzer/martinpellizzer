Arenaria galioides

Arenaria galioides, commonly known as the sand sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in harsh, well-drained environments such as rocky soils and sandy habitats. The plant has small, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in cold, dry conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Arenaria galioides is adapted to survive in extreme climates, making it a hardy member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
