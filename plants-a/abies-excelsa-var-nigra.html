Abies excelsa var. nigra

Abies excelsa var. nigra, commonly known as the black fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which gives it its common name. The tree has conical, dense foliage with needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
