Androcymbium rechingeri

Androcymbium rechingeri, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a rare and specialized plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its roots are highly specialized for water storage, allowing it to survive in extreme drought.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
