Asphodelus microcarpus subsp. affinis

Asphodelus microcarpus subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
