Abarema microcarpa

Abarema microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited burr legume, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
