Anethum graveolens var. parvifolium

Anethum graveolens var. parvifolium, commonly known as dwarf parsley, is a cultivar of the parsley plant characterized by its smaller, more compact leaves compared to the standard variety.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as carrots and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a mild, fresh parsley scent. Its foliage is often used in cooking for its subtle flavor and is also valued in traditional medicine.

This variety is well-suited for container gardening due to its compact growth habit.
