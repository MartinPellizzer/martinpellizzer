Agave robusta

Agave robusta, commonly known as the robust agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have sharp, spiny edges. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, white flowers, which are often followed by the development of a large, spiky fruit. Agave robusta is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
