Acrothamnus maccraei

Acrothamnus maccraei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, which are followed by distinctive burrs that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acrothamnus maccraei is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
