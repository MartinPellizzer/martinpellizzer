Artemisia rhodantha

Artemisia rhodantha, commonly known as red sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and vibrant red flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, pungent scent. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Artemisia rhodantha thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
