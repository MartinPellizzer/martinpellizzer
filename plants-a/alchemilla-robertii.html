Alchemilla robertii

Alchemilla robertii, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with a soft, velvety texture, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Alchemilla robertii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
