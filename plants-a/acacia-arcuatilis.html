Acacia arcuatilis

Acacia arcuatilis, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved and warty pod-like fruits, which are a key feature of the species. The plant has phyllodes rather than true leaves, which are narrow and strap-like, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds.

Acacia arcuatilis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
