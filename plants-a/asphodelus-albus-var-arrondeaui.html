Asphodelus albus var. arrondeaui

Asphodelus albus var. arrondeaui, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom reliably in Mediterranean climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
