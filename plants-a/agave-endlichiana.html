Agave endlichiana

Agave endlichiana, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which eventually give way to edible berries. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave endlichiana is widely cultivated for its use in the production of tequila and other agave-based spirits.
