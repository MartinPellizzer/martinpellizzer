Astragalus simonii

Astragalus simonii, commonly known as Simon's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture.

Astragalus simonii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
