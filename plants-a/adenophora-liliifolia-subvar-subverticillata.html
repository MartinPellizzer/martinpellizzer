Adenophora liliifolia subvar. subverticillata

Adenophora liliifolia subvar. subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while the plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
