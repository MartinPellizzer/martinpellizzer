Argemone platyceras var. hispida

Argemone platyceras var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid prickly poppy, is a variant of the prickly poppy species native to certain regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain numerous small, black seeds.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
