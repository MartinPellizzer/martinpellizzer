Aglaia leptantha

Aglaia leptantha, commonly known as the slender aglaia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Meliaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers and smooth, glossy leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
