Aristolochia albertiana

Aristolochia albertiana, commonly known as the Albertian Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often maroon or purple in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, often twisted floral structure. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The plant is also notable for containing aristolochic acids, which are toxic to humans and animals.
