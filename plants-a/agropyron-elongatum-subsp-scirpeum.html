Agropyron elongatum subsp. scirpeum

Agropyron elongatum subsp.

scirpeum, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass subspecies scirpeum, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged along the stem, with small, awned flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
