Algarobia dulcis

Algarobia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Algarobia dulcis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
