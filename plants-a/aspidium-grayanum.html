Aspidium grayanum

Aspidium grayanum, commonly known as the grayanum water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The spores are released from sori that are located on the undersides of the fronds.
