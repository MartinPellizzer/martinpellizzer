Alchemilla gubanovii

Alchemilla gubanovii, commonly known as the Siberian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in branched cymes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Alchemilla gubanovii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
