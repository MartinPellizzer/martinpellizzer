Acetosa intermedia

Acetosa intermedia, commonly known as common chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along pathways.

Acetosa intermedia is a hardy species that can spread rapidly through seed dispersal and is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
