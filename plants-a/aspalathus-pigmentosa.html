Aspalathus pigmentosa

Aspalathus pigmentosa, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, bean-like pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Aspalathus pigmentosa is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
