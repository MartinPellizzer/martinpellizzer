Alsophila andersonii

Alsophila andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's tree fern, is a species native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in favorable conditions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the forest floor, while its roots absorb moisture and nutrients from the soil.

Alsophila andersonii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
