Anemone pulsatilla var. chinensis

Anemone pulsatilla var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features a rosette of finely divided, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is often cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
