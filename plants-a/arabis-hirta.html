Arabis hirta

Arabis hirta, commonly known as hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters. It produces capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Arabis hirta thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
