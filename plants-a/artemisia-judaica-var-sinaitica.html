Artemisia judaica var. sinaitica

Artemisia judaica var. sinaitica, commonly known as Sinai wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the rocky and arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It features gray-green, aromatic leaves that are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to dry, hot climates and is often found in rocky soils and mountainous areas.

This variety is closely related to other Artemisia species and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
