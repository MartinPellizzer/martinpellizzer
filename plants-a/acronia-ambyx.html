Acronia ambyx

Acronia ambyx, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Acronia ambyx plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
