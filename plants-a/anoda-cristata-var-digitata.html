Anoda cristata var. digitata

Anoda cristata var. digitata, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery purple or violet flower spikes that rise above narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
