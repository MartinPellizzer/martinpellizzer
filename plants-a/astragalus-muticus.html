Astragalus muticus

Astragalus muticus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus muticus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
