Allium cydni

Allium cydni, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic or Cydni garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in traditional cuisine for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

Allium cydni is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in both culinary and herbal applications.
