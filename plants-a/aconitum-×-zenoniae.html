Aconitum × zenoniae

Aconitum × zenoniae, commonly known as the hybrid monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, compound leaves with serrated edges. Its roots are tuberous and contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

Aconitum × zenoniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
