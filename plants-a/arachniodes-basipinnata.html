Arachniodes basipinnata

Arachniodes basipinnata, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its pinnate leaves and a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread across forest floors. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The botanical features of Arachniodes basipinnata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
