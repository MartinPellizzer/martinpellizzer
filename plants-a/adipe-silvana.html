Adipe silvana

Adipe silvana, commonly known as the forest fat plant, is a rare and elusive species native to the dense, humid rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves that store moisture, allowing it to thrive in low-light conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its roots are known to secrete a substance that aids in nutrient absorption from poor soil.

Due to its unique adaptations, Adipe silvana is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
