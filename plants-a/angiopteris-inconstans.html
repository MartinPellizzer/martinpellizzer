Angiopteris inconstans

Angiopteris inconstans, commonly known as the variable water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant has a distinctive, finely divided leaf structure with a light green color and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests.

Angiopteris inconstans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
