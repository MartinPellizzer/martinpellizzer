Atriplex lindleyi subsp. inflata

Atriplex lindleyi subsp.

inflata, commonly known as the inflated saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves allows it to survive in low-water conditions.
