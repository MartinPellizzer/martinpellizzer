Aster iostemma

Aster iostemma, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a blue-violet center, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Aster iostemma is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
