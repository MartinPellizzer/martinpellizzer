Astragalus ovatus

Astragalus ovatus, commonly known as the oval milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Astragalus ovatus is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
