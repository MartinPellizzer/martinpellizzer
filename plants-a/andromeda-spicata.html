Andromeda spicata

Andromeda spicata, commonly known as the coastal azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandhill habitats.

Andromeda spicata is notable for its ability to tolerate salt spray and sandy conditions.
