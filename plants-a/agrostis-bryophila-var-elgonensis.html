Agrostis bryophila var. elgonensis

Agrostis bryophila var. elgonensis, commonly known as Elgon mountain grass, is a rare and endemic grass species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa, particularly around Mount Elgon.

It is a low-growing, tufted perennial grass that thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous and forested areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. It is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its habitat, including high elevation and nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
