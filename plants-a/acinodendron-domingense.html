Acinodendron domingense

Acinodendron domingense, commonly known as the Dominican acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds, and it often grows in coastal or disturbed habitats. However, it is considered rare and faces threats from habitat loss and environmental changes.
