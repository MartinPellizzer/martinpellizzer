Anthemis boveana var. jahandiezii

Anthemis boveana var. jahandiezii, commonly known as the Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers with white centers. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
