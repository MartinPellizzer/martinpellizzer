Astragalus leushanensis

Astragalus leushanensis, commonly known as the Leushan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
