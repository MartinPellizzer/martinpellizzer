Aster campestris subsp. suksdorfii

Aster campestris subsp.

suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's aster, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
