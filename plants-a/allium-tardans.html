Allium tardans

Allium tardans, commonly known as the late garlic, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, rounded berries. Allium tardans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic-like scent.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
