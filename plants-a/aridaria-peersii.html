Aridaria peersii

Aridaria peersii, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, often purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping to conserve water in drought-prone areas.
