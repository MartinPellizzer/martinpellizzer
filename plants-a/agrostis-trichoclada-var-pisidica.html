Agrostis trichoclada var. pisidica

Agrostis trichoclada var. pisidica, commonly known as the Crimean bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense, mat-like growth habit, making it well-suited for moist, shaded environments. The plant typically thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brownish spikelets.

Agrostis trichoclada var. pisidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
