Andropogon tristachyus

Andropogon tristachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each branch, giving it the species name "tristachyus," which means "three-branched.".

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
