Amphiglossa nitidula

Amphiglossa nitidula, commonly known as the glossy leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, glossy green leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a unique shape that resembles a small lip or hood.

Amphiglossa nitidula requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
