Adiantum moritzianum

Adiantum moritzianum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, arching structure, and its fronds are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. Adiantum moritzianum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are located on the undersides of the fronds.
