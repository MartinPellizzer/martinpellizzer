Aristolochia tithonusiana

Aristolochia tithonusiana, commonly known as the Tithonus arrow vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a vine-like structure. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex and sometimes toxic chemical compounds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
