Artemisia camphorata var. canescens

Artemisia camphorata var. canescens, commonly known as white mugwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its aromatic properties make it a notable species in both ecological and historical contexts.
