Acmispon strigosus var. tomentellus

Acmispon strigosus var. tomentellus, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The variety tomentellus is distinguished by its dense pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance.
