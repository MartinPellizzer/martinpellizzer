Asplenium × poscharskyanum

Asplenium × poscharskyanum, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of its parent species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of its leaves, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
