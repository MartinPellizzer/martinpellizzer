Anthyllis vulneraria f. albida

Anthyllis vulneraria f. albida, commonly known as the white woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its pale yellow or white pea-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is notable for its reduced flower color compared to the typical form, which contributes to its unique appearance and ecological niche.
