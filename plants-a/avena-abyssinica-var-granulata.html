Avena abyssinica var. granulata

Avena abyssinica var. granulata, commonly known as Abyssinian oat, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant with potential agricultural and ecological significance.
