Acacia baileyana var. aurea

Acacia baileyana var. aurea, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellowish-green foliage and its iconic yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is well adapted to various soil types and is drought-tolerant once established.

The golden wattle is also a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
