Artemisia campestris var. brachyphylla

Artemisia campestris var. brachyphylla, commonly known as rough tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone, which contribute to its strong, herbaceous scent.
