Asplenium × binaraense

Asplenium × binaraense, commonly known as the hybrid bird's nest fern, is a rare fern species resulting from the cross between *Asplenium trichomanes* and *Asplenium bulbiferum*.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like fronds that form a compact, nest-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, with a distinctive pattern of small, scale-like structures along the fronds.

Asplenium × binaraense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
