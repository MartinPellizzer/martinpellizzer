Aster chilensis subsp. ascendens

Aster chilensis subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as the Andean aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, adapting to the cold and harsh conditions of its environment. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
