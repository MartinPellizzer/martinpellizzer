Aesculus austrina

Aesculus austrina, commonly known as the Australian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that appear in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
