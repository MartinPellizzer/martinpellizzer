Arabis menziesii

Arabis menziesii, commonly known as the coastal rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas, rocky soils, and disturbed sites. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Arabis menziesii is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
