Acaena antarctica

Acaena antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic hairweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America and the Antarctic Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy structures that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Acaena antarctica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native ecosystems.
