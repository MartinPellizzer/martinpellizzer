Aralidium

Aralidium, commonly known as the lace fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araliaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. Aralidium species typically thrive in humid, shady environments and require well-drained soil.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their attractive foliage.
