Austromyrtus shepherdii

Austromyrtus shepherdii, commonly known as the Australian tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.

Austromyrtus shepherdii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
