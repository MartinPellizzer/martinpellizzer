Acacia farnesiana var. cavenia

Acacia farnesiana var. cavenia, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological settings.
