Aosa grandis

Aosa grandis, commonly known as the giant aosa, is a striking member of the Araceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, making it a dramatic focal point in any garden. The plant produces impressive inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Aosa grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
