Asclepias modesta

Asclepias modesta, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. Asclepias modesta is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for monarch caterpillars.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
