Antirrhinum graniticum subsp. graniticum

Antirrhinum graniticum subsp.

graniticum, commonly known as the granitic snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Antirrhinum graniticum subsp.

graniticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
