Anthurium monserratense

Anthurium monserratense, commonly known as the Montserrat anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Montserrat.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often a deep green with prominent veins, and the inflorescence can range in color from red to orange.

Anthurium monserratense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
