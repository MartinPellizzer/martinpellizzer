Astragalus pseudokurrumensis

Astragalus pseudokurrumensis, commonly known as the Kurrum Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its unique adaptations.
