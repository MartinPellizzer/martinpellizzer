Astracantha mitchelliana

Astracantha mitchelliana, commonly known as Mitchell's thornveld, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Astracantha mitchelliana is often found in shrubland and grassland habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
