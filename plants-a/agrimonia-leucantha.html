Agrimonia leucantha

Agrimonia leucantha, commonly known as white agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive seed heads that resemble a crown. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Agrimonia leucantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
