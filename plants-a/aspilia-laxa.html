Aspilia laxa

Aspilia laxa, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Aspilia laxa is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
