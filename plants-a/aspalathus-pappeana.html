Aspalathus pappeana

Aspalathus pappeana, commonly known as the African red bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown foliage and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with small leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus pappeana plays an important role in local ecosystems and is used in traditional medicine.
