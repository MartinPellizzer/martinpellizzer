Amblyanthus

Amblyanthus, commonly known as the Indian champa or champa flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and includes species such as Amblyanthus capensis and Amblyanthus trifoliatus. The plants are characterized by their delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellow or orange hue. They have compound leaves and typically grow as shrubs or small trees.

Amblyanthus species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
