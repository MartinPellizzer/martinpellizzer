Atriplex nelsonii

Atriplex nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's saltbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in saline soils and rocky habitats. The plant has small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other salt-tolerant plants.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
