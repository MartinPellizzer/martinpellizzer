Agropyron integrum

Agropyron integrum, commonly known as the wheatgrasses, is a grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its coarse, upright growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a fibrous texture.

Agropyron integrum is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop due to its resilience and nutritional value.
