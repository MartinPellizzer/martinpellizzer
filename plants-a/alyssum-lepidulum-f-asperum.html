Alyssum lepidulum f. asperum

Alyssum lepidulum f. asperum, commonly known as the rough alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, rough-textured foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "asperum.". It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
