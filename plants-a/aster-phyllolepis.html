Aster phyllolepis

Aster phyllolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Aster phyllolepis is often found in rocky or grassy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
