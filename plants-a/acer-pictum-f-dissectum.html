Acer pictum f. dissectum

Acer pictum f. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maple, is a cultivar of the red maple tree known for its striking ornamental value.

This plant features delicate, finely cut leaves that resemble lace, giving it a unique and ethereal appearance. The leaves are typically bright green in summer and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

While it is relatively low maintenance, it may require protection from strong winds and extreme weather conditions.
