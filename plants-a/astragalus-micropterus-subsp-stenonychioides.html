Astragalus micropterus subsp. stenonychioides

Astragalus micropterus subsp.

stenonychioides, commonly known as the narrow-nail milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, elongated leaflets and unique floral morphology. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
