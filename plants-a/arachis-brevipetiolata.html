Arachis brevipetiolata

Arachis brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing one or two round seeds. It is adapted to warm, dry climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
