Arenaria segetalis

Arenaria segetalis, commonly known as the field sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are linear and grass-like. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate dry conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also known for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
