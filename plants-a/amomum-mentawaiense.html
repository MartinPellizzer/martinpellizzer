Amomum mentawaiense

Amomum mentawaiense, commonly known as Mentawai cardamom, is a species of ginger native to the Indonesian island of Mentawai.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elongated fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. The plant grows in moist, shaded forest environments and is part of the Zingiberaceae family. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, spicy fragrance.

This plant is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
