Arabis glabra var. furcatipilis

Arabis glabra var. furcatipilis, commonly known as the forked-hair rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, pinnately divided, and covered with fine, forked hairs, giving it its scientific name.

It is often found in open habitats with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
