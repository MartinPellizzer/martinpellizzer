Acacia homomalla

Acacia homomalla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical clusters, which are followed by flat, linear pods. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Acacia homomalla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
