Aristolochia sellowiana

Aristolochia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and striking, hooded flowers that resemble pipes. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, intricate floral structure. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a deep purple or brown interior, attracting specific pollinators.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
