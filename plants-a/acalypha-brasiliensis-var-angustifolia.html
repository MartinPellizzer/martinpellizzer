Acalypha brasiliensis var. angustifolia

Acalypha brasiliensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a shrub native to South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
