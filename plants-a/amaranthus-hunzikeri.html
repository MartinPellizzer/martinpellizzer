Amaranthus hunzikeri

Amaranthus hunzikeri, commonly known as the Hunziker's amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Amaranthus hunzikeri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
