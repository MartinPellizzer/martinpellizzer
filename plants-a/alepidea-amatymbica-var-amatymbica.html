Alepidea amatymbica var. amatymbica

Alepidea amatymbica var. amatymbica, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
