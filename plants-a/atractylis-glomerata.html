Atractylis glomerata

Atractylis glomerata, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are often gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Atractylis glomerata is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
