Acacia nilotica var. tomentosa

Acacia nilotica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grayish-green branches and small, yellow, globular flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and the plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.
