Aster sagittifolius var. drummondii

Aster sagittifolius var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's arrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves and showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and grasslands. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
