Antidesma pubescens var. moritzii

Antidesma pubescens var. moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often pubescent leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, fleshy berries that are usually red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
