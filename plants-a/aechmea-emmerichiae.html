Aechmea emmerichiae

Aechmea emmerichiae, commonly known as the Emerald Star, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features a rosette of broad, dark green leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for its epiphytic growth. The plant produces vibrant red and yellow floral bracts that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Aechmea emmerichiae is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance.

Morphology

Aechmea emmerichiae has rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy, sword-like, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the central rosette, with a dark green to blue-green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, thick stem covered in a dense, fibrous bark that is typically brownish in color. Its flowers are clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette, with red or pink bracts and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season, primarily pollinated by birds. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds through wind and water.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding environment.
