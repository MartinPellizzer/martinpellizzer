Agave justosierrana

Agave justosierrana, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave justosierrana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
