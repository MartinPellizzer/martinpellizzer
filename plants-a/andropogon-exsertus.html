Andropogon exsertus

Andropogon exsertus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon exsertus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
