Astianthus

Astianthus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their vibrant appearance.

Astianthus species are adapted to a variety of soil types but thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.
