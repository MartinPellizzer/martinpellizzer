Amentotaxus hatuyenensis

Amentotaxus hatuyenensis, commonly known as the Hatuyen pine, is a rare and endangered species native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Taxaceae family, which includes yews and related conifers. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and unique cone structure, which contains seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril. It thrives in humid, mountainous environments and is adapted to acidic soils.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
