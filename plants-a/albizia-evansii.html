Albizia evansii

Albizia evansii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Albizia evansii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
