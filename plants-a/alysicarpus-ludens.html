Alysicarpus ludens

Alysicarpus ludens, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
