Acaena anserinifolia var. paucidens

Acaena anserinifolia var. paucidens, commonly known as the slender silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and along streams.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
