Amethystanthus macrophyllus

Amethystanthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant amethyst flower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of amethyst crystals. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. Its blooms, which appear in late summer, consist of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers that aggregate into a dense, spire-like structure.

Amethystanthus macrophyllus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and dramatic appearance.
