Albizia meyeri

Albizia meyeri, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Albizia meyeri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
