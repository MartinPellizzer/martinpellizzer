Arctotis aspleniifolia

Arctotis aspleniifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved arctotis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in temperate climates.

Arctotis aspleniifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
