Astragalus rahiminejadii

Astragalus rahiminejadii, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
