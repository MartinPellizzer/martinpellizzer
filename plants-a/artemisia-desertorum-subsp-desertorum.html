Artemisia desertorum subsp. desertorum

Artemisia desertorum subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as the desert sagebrush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots and a dense, drought-resistant structure. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential medicinal and insect-repelling properties.
