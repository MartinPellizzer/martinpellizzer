Abies concolor var. pendens

Abies concolor var. pendens, commonly known as the pendulous white fir, is a variant of the white fir species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The cones are typically cylindrical and mature to a reddish-brown color, releasing seeds in the fall. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in higher elevation forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of mountain ecosystems.
