Acanthophyllum stocksianum var. pubescens

Acanthophyllum stocksianum var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent acanthophyllum, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to high levels of sunlight and temperature fluctuations. Its flowers are small and usually pale green or yellow, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

This variety is notable for its hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss and protect against extreme weather conditions.
