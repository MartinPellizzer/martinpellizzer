Amphicarpaea edgeworthii var. japonica

Amphicarpaea edgeworthii var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces both above-ground and underground fruits, a trait known as amphicarpous growth. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
