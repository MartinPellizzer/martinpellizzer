Amberboa crupinoides var. immarginata

Amberboa crupinoides var. immarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf amberboa, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicinal practices by indigenous communities.
