Asterosedum spurium

Asterosedum spurium, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners seeking drought-resistant landscaping options.
