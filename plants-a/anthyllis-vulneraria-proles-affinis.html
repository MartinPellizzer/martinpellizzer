Anthyllis vulneraria proles affinis

Anthyllis vulneraria proles affinis, commonly known as the lesser vetchling, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
