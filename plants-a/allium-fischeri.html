Allium fischeri

Allium fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
