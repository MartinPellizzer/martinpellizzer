Acacia trigonophylla

Acacia trigonophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Acacia trigonophylla is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
