Artemisia lagocephala var. lagocephala

Artemisia lagocephala var. lagocephala, commonly known as bluegreen wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a fine, downy texture, and it produces fluffy, spherical seed heads that are highly ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
