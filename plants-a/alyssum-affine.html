Alyssum affine

Alyssum affine, commonly known as narrowleaf alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its branched stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Alyssum affine thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period make it a valuable addition to many garden landscapes.
