Amblyanthera crassifolia

Amblyanthera crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved amblyanthera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent leaves that are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
