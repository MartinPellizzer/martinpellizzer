Anthurium punctatum

Anthurium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive red or purple markings. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is a modified leaf. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium punctatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
