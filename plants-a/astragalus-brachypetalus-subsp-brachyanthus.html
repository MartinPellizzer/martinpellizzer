Astragalus brachypetalus subsp. brachyanthus

Astragalus brachypetalus subsp.

brachyanthus, commonly known as the short-petaled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
