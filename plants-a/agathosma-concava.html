Agathosma concava

Agathosma concava, commonly known as African Geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Agathosma concava is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
