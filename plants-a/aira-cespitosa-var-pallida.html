Aira cespitosa var. pallida

Aira cespitosa var. pallida, commonly known as pale false oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue to white flowers that appear in summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It forms dense clumps with rhizomes, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
