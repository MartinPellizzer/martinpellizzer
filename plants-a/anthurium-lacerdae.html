Anthurium lacerdae

Anthurium lacerdae, commonly known as the split-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that often have a deeply veined, almost split appearance. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium lacerdae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
