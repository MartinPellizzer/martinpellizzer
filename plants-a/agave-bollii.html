Agave bollii

Agave bollii, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. Its leaves contain a high concentration of sap, which is used in the production of tequila.

Agave bollii is well adapted to harsh environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive in drought conditions.
