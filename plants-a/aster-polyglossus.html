Aster polyglossus

Aster polyglossus, commonly known as the starry everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

Aster polyglossus is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
