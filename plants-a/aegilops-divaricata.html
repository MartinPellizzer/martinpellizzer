Aegilops divaricata

Aegilops divaricata, commonly known as the slender wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are often used in agricultural research due to their potential for improving wheat varieties.

Aegilops divaricata plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.

Aegilops divaricata has narrow, linear leaves that are alternate in arrangement, with a bluish-green color and a slightly hairy texture.
