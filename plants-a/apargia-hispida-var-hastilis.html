Apargia hispida var. hastilis

Apargia hispida var. hastilis, commonly known as the roughleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
