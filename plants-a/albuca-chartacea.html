Albuca chartacea

Albuca chartacea, commonly known as the parchment lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive, parchment-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring or early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Albuca chartacea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
