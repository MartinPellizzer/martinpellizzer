Astragalus chamardiensis

Astragalus chamardiensis, commonly known as the Chamard's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus chamardiensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
