Anthurium cuneatissimum

Anthurium cuneatissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are bright red or orange in color, surrounded by a leaf-like structure called a spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium cuneatissimum is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and unique floral structures.
