Agave macroacantha var. macrantha

Agave macroacantha var. macrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit and long, narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers in terminal panicles, which can reach several meters in height. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
