Arctium serratulodes

Arctium serratulodes, commonly known as the hairy burdock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features rough, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowering stems produce spiky, bur-like structures that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
