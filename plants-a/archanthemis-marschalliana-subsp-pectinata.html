Archanthemis marschalliana subsp. pectinata

Archanthemis marschalliana subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the woolly archanthemis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique texture.
