Artanthe parkeriana

Artanthe parkeriana, commonly known as Parker's artanthe, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Artanthe parkeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in arid landscapes.
