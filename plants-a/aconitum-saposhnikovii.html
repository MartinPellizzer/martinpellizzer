Aconitum saposhnikovii

Aconitum saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which make it potentially dangerous to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and natural habitats.
