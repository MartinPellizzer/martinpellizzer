Astragalus talasseus

Astragalus talasseus, commonly known as the Talassian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Astragalus talasseus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
