Aconitum tenuisectum

Aconitum tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. Aconitum tenuisectum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
