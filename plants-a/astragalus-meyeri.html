Astragalus meyeri

Astragalus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus meyeri is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
