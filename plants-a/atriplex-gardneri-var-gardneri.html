Atriplex gardneri var. gardneri

Atriplex gardneri var. gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it a common component of desert ecosystems.
