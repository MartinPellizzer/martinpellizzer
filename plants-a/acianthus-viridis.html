Acianthus viridis

Acianthus viridis, commonly known as the green acianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
