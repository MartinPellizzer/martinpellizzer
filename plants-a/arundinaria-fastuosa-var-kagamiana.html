Arundinaria fastuosa var. kagamiana

Arundinaria fastuosa var. kagamiana, commonly known as the Kagamiya bamboo, is a variant of the fastuosa bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that often have a smooth, almost glassy surface. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in humid conditions.
