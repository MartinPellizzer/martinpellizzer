Argythamnia calycina

Argythamnia calycina, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Argythamnia calycina is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
