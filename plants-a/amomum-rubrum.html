Amomum rubrum

Amomum rubrum, commonly known as red cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown seed pods. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elongated, spiky fruits that contain aromatic seeds. These seeds are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine for their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum rubrum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
