Arabis sagittata var. sagittata

Arabis sagittata var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf wintercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are arrow-shaped, with a distinctive lobed structure, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
