Alepidea ciliaris var. lanceolata

Alepidea ciliaris var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandbroom, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
