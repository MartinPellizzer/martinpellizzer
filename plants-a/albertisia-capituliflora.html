Albertisia capituliflora

Albertisia capituliflora, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
