Actinodaphne quercina

Actinodaphne quercina, commonly known as the Burmese camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface and a fragrant aroma when crushed.

Actinodaphne quercina is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
