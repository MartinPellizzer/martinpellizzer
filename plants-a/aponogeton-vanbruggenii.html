Aponogeton vanbruggenii

Aponogeton vanbruggenii, commonly known as the Vanbruggen's arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, slender roots that extend into the substrate. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, adding ornamental value to ponds and aquariums.

Aponogeton vanbruggenii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in aquatic settings.
