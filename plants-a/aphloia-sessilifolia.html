Aphloia sessilifolia

Aphloia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf aphloia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Aphloia sessilifolia is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
