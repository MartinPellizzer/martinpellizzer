Adonis aestivalis subsp. cretica

Adonis aestivalis subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Cretan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
