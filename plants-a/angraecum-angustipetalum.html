Angraecum angustipetalum

Angraecum angustipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, slender sepals and petals, typically in shades of white and violet. It blooms during the dry season, relying on specific pollinators such as moths.

This orchid is noted for its unique adaptations to its arid environment and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
