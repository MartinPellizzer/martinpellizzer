Adenophora verbenifolia

Adenophora verbenifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, creating a mist-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Adenophora verbenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
