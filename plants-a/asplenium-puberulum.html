Asplenium puberulum

Asplenium puberulum, commonly known as the hairy spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or forest floors. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

Asplenium puberulum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
