Acacia pittieriana

Acacia pittieriana, commonly known as the Madagascar thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are often followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia pittieriana is also known for its thorny stems, which provide some protection from herbivores.
