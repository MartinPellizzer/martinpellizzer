Aceranthus sagittatus

Aceranthus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae, characterized by its spatheous inflorescence. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a showy, leaf-like structure called a spathe.

Aceranthus sagittatus is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
