Allium gusaricum

Allium gusaricum, commonly known as the Crimean leek, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and umbel-shaped flower clusters. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbous stem is often used as a vegetable in local cuisines, similar to garlic and leeks.

Allium gusaricum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes as well.
