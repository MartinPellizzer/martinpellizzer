Akebia × pentaphylla

Akebia × pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf akebia, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Akebia quinata and Akebia trifoliata. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often lobed and can vary in shape. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by red to orange berries that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal fruit.
