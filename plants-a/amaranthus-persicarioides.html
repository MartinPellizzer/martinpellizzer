Amaranthus persicarioides

Amaranthus persicarioides, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often turn reddish as the plant matures. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces numerous tiny, three-angled seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
