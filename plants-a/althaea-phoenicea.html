Althaea phoenicea

Althaea phoenicea, commonly known as the Cyprus marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in wet or marshy habitats, often found near rivers and lakes. Its roots produce a mucilaginous substance, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Althaea phoenicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
