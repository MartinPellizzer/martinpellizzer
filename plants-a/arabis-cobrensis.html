Arabis cobrensis

Arabis cobrensis, commonly known as the Cobren's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This species is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
