Astragalus globiflorus

Astragalus globiflorus, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers range in color from white to pink.

Astragalus globiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
