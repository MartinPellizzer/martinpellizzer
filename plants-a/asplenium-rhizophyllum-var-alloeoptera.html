Asplenium rhizophyllum var. alloeoptera

Asplenium rhizophyllum var. alloeoptera, commonly known as the rock fern, is a variant of the common rock fern found in rocky and calcareous soils.

It is a small, evergreen fern with a compact, creeping growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The fronds are typically divided into narrow, linear segments that are often curved or twisted. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in crevices.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its distinctive foliage structure.
