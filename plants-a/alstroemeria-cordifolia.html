Alstroemeria cordifolia

Alstroemeria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in a compact shrub form, with stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and come in shades of red, orange, yellow, and white.

Alstroemeria cordifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
