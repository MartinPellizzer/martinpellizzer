Agave scabra subsp. potosiensis

Agave scabra subsp.

potosiensis, commonly known as the Potosi agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
