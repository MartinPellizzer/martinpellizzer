Alstroemeria pudica

Alstroemeria pudica, commonly known as the slender alstroemeria, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of colorful, tubular flowers. The flowers typically display a range of hues, including red, orange, yellow, and pink, and are often used in floral arrangements. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
