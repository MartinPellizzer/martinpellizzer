Astragalus gillii

Astragalus gillii, commonly known as Gill's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus gillii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
