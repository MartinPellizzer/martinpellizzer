Astrocodon auritus

Astrocodon auritus, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, ear-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive red lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare tropical plants.
