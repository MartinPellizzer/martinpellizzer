Acacia lucida

Acacia lucida, commonly known as the shining acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are delicate and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

Acacia lucida thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
