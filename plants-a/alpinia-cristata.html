Alpinia cristata

Alpinia cristata, commonly known as the crowned ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental, palm-like leaves that are often used in horticulture for their aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Alpinia cristata thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
