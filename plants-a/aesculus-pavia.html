Aesculus pavia

Aesculus pavia, commonly known as the red buckeye, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in early spring, which are clustered in upright panicles. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. The fruit is a spiny capsule that contains one to three elliptical seeds.

Aesculus pavia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
