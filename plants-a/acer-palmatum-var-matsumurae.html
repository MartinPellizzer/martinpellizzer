Acer palmatum var. matsumurae

Acer palmatum var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lace-like leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a rounded or spreading crown. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The species is well-suited for ornamental gardening due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
