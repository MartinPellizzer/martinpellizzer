Agave albicans var. mediopicta

Agave albicans var. mediopicta, commonly known as the White Agave with Median Stripes, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white or grayish markings. The leaves are lined with sharp spines along their edges, which serve as a defense mechanism. This plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears yellow flowers, which appear in the spring.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
