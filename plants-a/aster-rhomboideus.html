Aster rhomboideus

Aster rhomboideus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats. It blooms in the summer and is an important nectar source for pollinators.

Aster rhomboideus is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
