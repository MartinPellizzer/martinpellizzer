Antennaria carpatica subsp. carpatica

Antennaria carpatica subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features a mat-forming habit with hairy, often glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, and the species is adapted to rocky or calcareous soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine and subalpine habitats.
