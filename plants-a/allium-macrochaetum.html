Allium macrochaetum

Allium macrochaetum, commonly known as the large-hair onion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a cultivated crop in some regions.

Allium macrochaetum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
