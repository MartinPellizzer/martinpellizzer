Astragalus barrelieri var. queraltii

Astragalus barrelieri var. queraltii, commonly known as the Barrelier's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
