Andropogon brongniartii

Andropogon brongniartii, commonly known as giant bluestem, is a tall grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, bluish-green foliage and can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has a robust root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often found in wetland and marshy habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences.

Andropogon brongniartii plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
