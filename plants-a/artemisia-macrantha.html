Artemisia macrantha

Artemisia macrantha, commonly known as the largeflower wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains volatile oils and sesquiterpene lactones. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Artemisia macrantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
