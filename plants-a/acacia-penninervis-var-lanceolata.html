Acacia penninervis var. lanceolata

Acacia penninervis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
