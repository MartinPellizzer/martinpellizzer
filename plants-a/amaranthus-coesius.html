Amaranthus coesius

Amaranthus coesius, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red foliage and striking, showy flower heads that resemble plumes. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines, while the seeds are valued for their nutritional content.

The plant is also cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in diverse climates.
