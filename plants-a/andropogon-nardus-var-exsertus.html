Andropogon nardus var. exsertus

Andropogon nardus var. exsertus, commonly known as narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
