Artemisia vulgaris subsp. coarctata

Artemisia vulgaris subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in compact spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and wastelands.

Its essential oils, particularly thujone, contribute to its historical use in medicine and as a flavoring agent.
