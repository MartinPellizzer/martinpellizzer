Alyxia acuminata var. lancifolia

Alyxia acuminata var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alyxia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy floral clusters that attract pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
