Aparinella ruricola

Aparinella ruricola, commonly known as the rural aparinella, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Aparinella ruricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
