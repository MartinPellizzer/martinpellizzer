Aspidium flaccidum

Aspidium flaccidum, commonly known as the flexible water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with small, oval-shaped pinnae that are arranged alternately along the rachis.

Aspidium flaccidum is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types, making it a versatile species in both natural and cultivated settings.
