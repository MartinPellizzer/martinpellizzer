Australopyrum pectinatum var. retrofractum

Australopyrum pectinatum var. retrofractum, commonly known as the southern frond fern, is a unique variant of the genus Australopyrum native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive frond structure, with long, pinnately divided leaves that exhibit a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are clustered in spore-bearing cones, typical of ferns.

The varietal name "retrofractum" refers to the backward-bending nature of its leaf segments, a feature that aids in distinguishing it from other similar species.
