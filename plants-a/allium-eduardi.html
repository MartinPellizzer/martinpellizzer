Allium eduardi

Allium eduardi, commonly known as Edward's leek, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and hollow, cylindrical stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, flat, and grass-like, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Allium eduardi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
