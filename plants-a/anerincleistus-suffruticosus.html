Anerincleistus suffruticosus

Anerincleistus suffruticosus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
