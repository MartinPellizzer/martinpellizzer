Artemisia maritima var. astrachanica

Artemisia maritima var. astrachanica, commonly known as the Astrachan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes. It has a strong, camphor-like scent and is known for its medicinal properties.

This variety is closely related to other artemisia species and is valued for its adaptability to harsh maritime environments.
