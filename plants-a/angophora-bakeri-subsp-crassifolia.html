Angophora bakeri subsp. crassifolia

Angophora bakeri subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved angophora, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae. It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in the coastal regions of New South Wales.

The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, often in sandy or rocky soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other subspecies of Angophora bakeri.
