Amana edulis

Amana edulis, commonly known as the edible amana, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn a vibrant red or orange. These fruits are rich in nutrients and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional recipes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
