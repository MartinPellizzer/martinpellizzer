Arctium crispum

Arctium crispum, commonly known as the velvetleaf burdock, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a thick, hairy stem and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its most distinctive feature is the spiky, bur-like fruit that contains seeds covered in tiny hooks.

Arctium crispum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
