Astragalus splendidissimus

Astragalus splendidissimus, commonly known as the splendid milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus splendidissimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
