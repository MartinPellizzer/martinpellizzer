Andropogon carinatus var. exserens

Andropogon carinatus var. exserens, commonly known as the ridge grass or tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
