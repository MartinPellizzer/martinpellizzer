Amomum arunachalense

Amomum arunachalense, commonly known as the Arunachal cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family and is native to the northeastern regions of India and parts of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its aromatic, elongated fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested environments.

The plant is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
