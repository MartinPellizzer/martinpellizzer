Aristolochia bracteata var. basitruncata

Aristolochia bracteata var. basitruncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. The plant produces small, dark brown fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and unique foliage.
