Aconitum virgatum

Aconitum virgatum, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy and erect. Aconitum virgatum thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
