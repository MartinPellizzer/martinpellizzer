Astragalus fausicola

Astragalus fausicola, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils with limited water availability.
