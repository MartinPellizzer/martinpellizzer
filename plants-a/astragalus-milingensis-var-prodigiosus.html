Astragalus milingensis var. prodigiosus

Astragalus milingensis var. prodigiosus, commonly known as the red-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
