Arenaria leroyana

Arenaria leroyana, commonly known as the Leroy's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually succulent and adapted to conserve water, reflecting its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Arenaria leroyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging garden conditions.
