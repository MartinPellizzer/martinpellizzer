Abutilon vesicarium

Abutilon vesicarium, commonly known as the bladderleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in small, bladder-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
