Anthemis montana var. thracica

Anthemis montana var. thracica, commonly known as the Balkan tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It produces small, aromatic seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
