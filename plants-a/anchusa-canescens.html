Anchusa canescens

Anchusa canescens, commonly known as hoary bluebell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies.

Anchusa canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
