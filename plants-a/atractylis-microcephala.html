Atractylis microcephala

Atractylis microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed marsh thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, and bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Atractylis microcephala is noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
