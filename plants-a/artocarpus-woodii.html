Artocarpus woodii

Artocarpus woodii, commonly known as the wild breadfruit or breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, is typically green when mature and turns yellow or orange when ripe. The plant is valued for its edible fruit and has been used traditionally for food and medicine.
