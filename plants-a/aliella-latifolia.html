Aliella latifolia

Aliella latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf aliella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in the spring. Aliella latifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
