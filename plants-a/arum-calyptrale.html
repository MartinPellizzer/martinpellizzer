Arum calyptrale

Arum calyptrale, commonly known as the hooded arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply veined and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowers are typically green or yellowish with a distinctive hood-like structure, which houses the true flowers.

Arum calyptrale is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its dramatic appearance.
