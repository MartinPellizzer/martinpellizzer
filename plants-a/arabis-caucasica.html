Arabis caucasica

Arabis caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers well-drained conditions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Arabis caucasica is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
