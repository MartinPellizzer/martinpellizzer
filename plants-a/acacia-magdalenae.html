Acacia magdalenae

Acacia magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalenae acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Acacia magdalenae thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
