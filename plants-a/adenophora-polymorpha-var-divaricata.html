Adenophora polymorpha var. divaricata

Adenophora polymorpha var. divaricata, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct basal rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
