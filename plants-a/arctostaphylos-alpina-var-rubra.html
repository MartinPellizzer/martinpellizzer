Arctostaphylos alpina var. rubra

Arctostaphylos alpina var. rubra, commonly known as red alpine manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for ecological restoration projects.
