Aralia chinensis var. intermedia

Aralia chinensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese aralia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound, pinnate leaves that are often serrated and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
