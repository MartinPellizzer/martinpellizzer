Andropogon apricus var. indicus

Andropogon apricus var. indicus, commonly known as the Indian switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to Asia, particularly India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon apricus var. indicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
