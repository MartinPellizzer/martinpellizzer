Acer septemlobum var. marginatum

Acer septemlobum var. marginatum, commonly known as the Red River maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red to orange leaves that turn vibrant colors in the fall. The leaves are typically seven-lobed, with a serrated margin and a striking red or pink border. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
