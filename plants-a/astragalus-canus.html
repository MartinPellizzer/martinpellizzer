Astragalus canus

Astragalus canus, commonly known as the pale milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Astragalus canus is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
