Achyranthemum affine

Achyranthemum affine, commonly known as the common bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming from summer to autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Achyranthemum affine is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
