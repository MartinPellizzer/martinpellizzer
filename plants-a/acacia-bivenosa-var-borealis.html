Acacia bivenosa var. borealis

Acacia bivenosa var. borealis, commonly known as the northern acacia, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to permafrost conditions. Its root system helps stabilize soil in harsh, nutrient-poor habitats.

It plays a vital role in providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
