Astragalus monachorum

Astragalus monachorum, commonly known as the monk's bean, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several round, hard seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Astragalus monachorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
