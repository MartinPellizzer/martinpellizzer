Agapetes variegata var. speciosa

Agapetes variegata var. speciosa, commonly known as the variegated silver fir, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and strikingly variegated foliage, which often features a mix of green, white, and silver hues. The leaves are typically leathery and elliptical, providing a textured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, red berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady, moist garden environments.
