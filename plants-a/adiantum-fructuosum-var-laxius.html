Adiantum fructuosum var. laxius

Adiantum fructuosum var. laxius, commonly known as the lax leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family (Adiantaceae).

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are more loosely arranged compared to other varieties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream sides, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

This fern is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
