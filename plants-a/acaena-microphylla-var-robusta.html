Acaena microphylla var. robusta

Acaena microphylla var. robusta, commonly known as the robust bladder nut, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that contain hard, edible seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
