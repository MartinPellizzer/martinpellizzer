Aster × tardiflorus var. lancifolius

Aster × tardiflorus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the late-flowering aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals that bloom late in the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators in late summer and early fall.
