Adonis aestivalis var. cupaniana

Adonis aestivalis var. cupaniana, commonly known as the cupaniana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, succulent leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and pollinator attraction.
