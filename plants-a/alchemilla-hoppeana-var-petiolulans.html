Alchemilla hoppeana var. petiolulans

Alchemilla hoppeana var. petiolulans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves with soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
