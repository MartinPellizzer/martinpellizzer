Amberboa xanthocephala

Amberboa xanthocephala, commonly known as the goldenhead beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Amberboa xanthocephala thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
