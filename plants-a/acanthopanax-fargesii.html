Acanthopanax fargesii

Acanthopanax fargesii, commonly known as the Yunnan angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicle inflorescences, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
