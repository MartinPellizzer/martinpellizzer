Alcea sulphurea var. crassifolia

Alcea sulphurea var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved sulfur flower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its showy appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
