Allium elegans

Allium elegans, commonly known as the slender leek, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
