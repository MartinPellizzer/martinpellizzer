Antirrhinum meonanthum var. huetii

Antirrhinum meonanthum var. huetii, commonly known as the Huet's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that resemble a dragon's mouth, hence its common name. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
