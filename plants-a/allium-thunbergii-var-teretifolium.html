Allium thunbergii var. teretifolium

Allium thunbergii var. teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and emit a mild garlic scent. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in the summer, which are followed by bulbils that can be used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, often used in culinary applications.
