Astragalus gigantostegius

Astragalus gigantostegius, commonly known as the giant-stem milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supported by a robust root system. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus gigantostegius is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
