Androrchis × ehdenica

Androrchis × ehdenica, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Androrchis caerulea and Androrchis scabrosa.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, upright stem that bears a single spike of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and the flowers are characterized by their intricate, spurred structure.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
