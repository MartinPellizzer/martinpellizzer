Asarum porphyronotum

Asarum porphyronotum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a unique, pipe-shaped flower that is often covered in a reddish-purple hue, which gives it its common name. Its rhizomatous root system is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Asarum porphyronotum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
