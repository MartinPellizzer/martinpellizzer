Alpinia caribaea

Alpinia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

Alpinia caribaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
