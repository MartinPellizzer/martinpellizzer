Anagallis amplexicaulis

Anagallis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the spreading jeweledwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has a distinctive habit of wrapping its stems around other plants, giving it a creeping or trailing appearance. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Anagallis amplexicaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
