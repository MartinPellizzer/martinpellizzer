Arctostaphylos costaricensis

Arctostaphylos costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican manzanita, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Arctostaphylos costaricensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and mountainous regions.
