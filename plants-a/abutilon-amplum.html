Abutilon amplum

Abutilon amplum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woollypod abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in tropical and subtropical regions.
