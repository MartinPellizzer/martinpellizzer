Amyema curranii

Amyema curranii, commonly known as the Curran's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in eastern Australia.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain one or two seeds.

Amyema curranii plays an important ecological role by providing food for native birds and insects.
