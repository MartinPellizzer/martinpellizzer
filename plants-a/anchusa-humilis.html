Anchusa humilis

Anchusa humilis, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
