Austromyrtus metrosideros

Austromyrtus metrosideros, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The tree is characterized by its silver-gray, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elliptical, dark red berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
