Aconitum eustachium

Aconitum eustachium, commonly known as the Eustachian aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, waxy appearance.

Aconitum eustachium contains potent alkaloids, making it toxic and requiring careful handling.
