Ardisia oblanceolata

Ardisia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are a key feature of its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage make it a versatile plant for landscaping and horticultural use.
