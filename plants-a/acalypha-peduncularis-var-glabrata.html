Acalypha peduncularis var. glabrata

Acalypha peduncularis var. glabrata, commonly known as smoothleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its botanical features include a robust stem with alternate leaves and a showy inflorescence that attracts pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
