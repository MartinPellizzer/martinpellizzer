Ageratum corymbosum f. album

Ageratum corymbosum f. album, commonly known as the white ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale blue, often with a subtle violet hue, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and floral arrangements due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
