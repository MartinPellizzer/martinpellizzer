Asplenium nidus var. multilobum

Asplenium nidus var. multilobum, commonly known as the basket fern, is a fern species characterized by its unique, clustered growth habit and feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plant's fronds are typically divided into multiple lobes, giving it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to form dense colonies, making it a popular ornamental plant in moist, shaded garden settings.
