Arrhostoxylum jussieuoides

Arrhostoxylum jussieuoides, commonly known as the Andean cholla, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting succulent leaves and a deep root system for water retention. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
