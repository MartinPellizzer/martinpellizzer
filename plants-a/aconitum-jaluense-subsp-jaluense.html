Aconitum jaluense subsp. jaluense

Aconitum jaluense subsp.

jaluense, commonly known as the Jaluense monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that adds to its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to both animals and humans.
