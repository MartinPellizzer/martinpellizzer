Acmella nudicaulis

Acmella nudicaulis, commonly known as the triggerplant, is a small flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred petals that resemble a trigger mechanism, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often surrounded by green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

Acmella nudicaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
