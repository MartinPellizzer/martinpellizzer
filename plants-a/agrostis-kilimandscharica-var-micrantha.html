Agrostis kilimandscharica var. micrantha

Agrostis kilimandscharica var. micrantha, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It typically grows in rocky and mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly packed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
