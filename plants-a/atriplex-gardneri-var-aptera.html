Atriplex gardneri var. aptera

Atriplex gardneri var. aptera, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces greenish to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in soils with high salinity.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
