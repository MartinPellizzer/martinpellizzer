Avena americana

Avena americana, commonly known as wild oat, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in a tough, husky covering, and the plant is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Avena americana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
