Acaena tenera subsp. epilis

Acaena tenera subsp.

epilis, commonly known as the slender burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, typical of the genus Acaena.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing food for native birds and insects.
