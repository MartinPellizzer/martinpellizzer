Acanthophippium yamamotoi

Acanthophippium yamamotoi, commonly known as the Yamamoto's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
