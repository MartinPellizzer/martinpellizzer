Aeschynanthus volubilis

Aeschynanthus volubilis, commonly known as the trailing gloxinia, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers that range from red to orange. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
