Acacia obliquinervia

Acacia obliquinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bhutan, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves with narrow, parallel veins. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

Acacia obliquinervia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
