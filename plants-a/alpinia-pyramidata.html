Alpinia pyramidata

Alpinia pyramidata, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. The plant produces striking red or pink flower spikes that emerge from the leaf axils, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Alpinia pyramidata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
