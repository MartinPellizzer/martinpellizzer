Alopecurus agrestis var. pulchellus

Alopecurus agrestis var. pulchellus, commonly known as purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
