Anemia hirsuta var. schwackeana

Anemia hirsuta var. schwackeana, commonly known as the Schwacke's hairy anemia, is a variant of the Anemia hirsuta species, which belongs to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
