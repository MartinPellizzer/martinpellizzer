Anaphalis nepalensis

Anaphalis nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and clustered, forming a fluffy appearance that resembles a cotton ball.
