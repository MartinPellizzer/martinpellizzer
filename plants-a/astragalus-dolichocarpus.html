Astragalus dolichocarpus

Astragalus dolichocarpus, commonly known as the long-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Astragalus dolichocarpus is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
