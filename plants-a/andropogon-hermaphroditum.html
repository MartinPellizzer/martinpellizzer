Andropogon hermaphroditum

Andropogon hermaphroditum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence that turns golden in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon hermaphroditum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in native plant landscaping.
