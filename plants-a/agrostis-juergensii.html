Agrostis juergensii

Agrostis juergensii, commonly known as the bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense, sod-like turf. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open woodlands and meadows.

Agrostis juergensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
