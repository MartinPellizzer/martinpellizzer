Aconitum formosum var. clusianum

Aconitum formosum var. clusianum, commonly known as the Clusian Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hooded blue or purple flowers. The plant features a tall, erect stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
