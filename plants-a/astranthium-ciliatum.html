Astranthium ciliatum

Astranthium ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive thimble-like shape, giving it its common name.

Astranthium ciliatum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
