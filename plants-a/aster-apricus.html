Aster apricus

Aster apricus, commonly known as the meadow sweet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flower heads that resemble small clusters of stars. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Aster apricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
