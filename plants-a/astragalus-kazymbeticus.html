Astragalus kazymbeticus

Astragalus kazymbeticus, commonly known as Kazymbetian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
