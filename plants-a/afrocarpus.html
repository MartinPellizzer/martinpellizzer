Afrocarpus

Afrocarpus, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a genus of evergreen trees native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage. The trees typically grow in mountainous regions and are adapted to cool, moist climates. Afrocarpus species are known for their slow growth and longevity, often living over 100 years.

The genus includes several species, such as Afrocarpus falcatus and Afrocarpus gracilinelius, which are valued for their timber and ecological importance.
