Anguillaria zeylanica

Anguillaria zeylanica, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Anguillaria zeylanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
