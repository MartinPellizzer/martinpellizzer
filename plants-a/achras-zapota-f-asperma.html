Achras zapota f. asperma

Achras zapota f. asperma, commonly known as the chicle tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces large, palm-like leaves and has a distinctive fruit with a hard outer shell. Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid, warm climates.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its latex, which is used in chewing gum.
