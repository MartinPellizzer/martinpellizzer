Annona haematantha

Annona haematantha, commonly known as the red-veined custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red vein pattern on the underside. Its flowers are white with a yellow center and are followed by edible, yellow, fuzzy fruits.

The fruit has a creamy, sweet pulp with a subtle flavor, making it a popular fruit in its native regions.
