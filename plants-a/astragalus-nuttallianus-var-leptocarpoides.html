Astragalus nuttallianus var. leptocarpoides

Astragalus nuttallianus var. leptocarpoides, commonly known as the slender pod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces slender, elongated pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native habitat's flora.
