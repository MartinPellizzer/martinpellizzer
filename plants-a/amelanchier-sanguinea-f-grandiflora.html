Amelanchier sanguinea f. grandiflora

Amelanchier sanguinea f. grandiflora, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its attractive white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
