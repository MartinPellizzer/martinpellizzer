Arabis gracilescens

Arabis gracilescens, commonly known as the slender rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, basal leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
