Asyneuma pulvinatum

Asyneuma pulvinatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a basal rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Asyneuma pulvinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
