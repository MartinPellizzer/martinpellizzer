Acacia leprosa var. tenuifolia

Acacia leprosa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
