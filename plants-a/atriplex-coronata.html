Atriplex coronata

Atriplex coronata, commonly known as silverleaf chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. Its succulent stems and leaves make it well adapted to drought conditions, allowing it to thrive in saline and alkaline soils.

Atriplex coronata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
