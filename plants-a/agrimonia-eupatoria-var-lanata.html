Agrimonia eupatoria var. lanata

Agrimonia eupatoria var. lanata, commonly known as woolly agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
