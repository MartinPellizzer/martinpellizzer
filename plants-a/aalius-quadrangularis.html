Aalius quadrangularis

Aalius quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-sided plant, is a unique species characterized by its quadrangular stems.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The species is native to arid regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
