Amomum kandariense

Amomum kandariense, commonly known as Kandari cardamom, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are oblong, three-lobed capsules that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
