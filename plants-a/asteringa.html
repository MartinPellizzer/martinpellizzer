Asteringa

Asteringa, commonly known as the starflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically feature a central disk surrounded by ray florets. These plants are native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America. Asteringa species often thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making them popular choices for gardens and wildflower meadows.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value and others for their ecological benefits to pollinators.
