Acacia cupeyensis

Acacia cupeyensis, commonly known as the Cuban acacia, is a shrub native to Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, thorn-like structures that help protect it from herbivores. Its seeds are encased in a woody pod, and it is often found in dry, coastal areas.

Acacia cupeyensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
