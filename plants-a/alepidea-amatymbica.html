Alepidea amatymbica

Alepidea amatymbica, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Alepidea amatymbica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
