Aster germanicus

Aster germanicus, commonly known as the German aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in the late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Aster germanicus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
