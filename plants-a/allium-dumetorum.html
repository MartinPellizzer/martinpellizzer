Allium dumetorum

Allium dumetorum, commonly known as wild leek, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Allium dumetorum is valued for its mild garlic-like flavor and has been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
