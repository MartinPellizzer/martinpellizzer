Archidendron tonkinense

Archidendron tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin legume, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.

Archidendron tonkinense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
