Abutilon uratocarpum

Abutilon uratocarpum, commonly known as the Brazilian abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
