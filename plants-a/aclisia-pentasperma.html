Aclisia pentasperma

Aclisia pentasperma, commonly known as the five-seeded acanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, bract-based inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Aclisia pentasperma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
