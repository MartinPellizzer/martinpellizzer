Anadenanthera falcata

Anadenanthera falcata, commonly known as the "Bottle Tree," is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to dry climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
