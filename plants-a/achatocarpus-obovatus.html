Achatocarpus obovatus

Achatocarpus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, bracteate inflorescences with clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Achatocarpus obovatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
