Acalypha weddelliana var. genuina

Acalypha weddelliana var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by three-lobed, winged fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
