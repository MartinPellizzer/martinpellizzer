Alyssum tortuosum

Alyssum tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted alyssum, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has delicate, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum tortuosum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings.
