Acalypha brasiliensis var. maxima

Acalypha brasiliensis var. maxima, commonly known as the Brazil flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its rapid growth rate makes it suitable for use as a hedge or screen in landscaping.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
