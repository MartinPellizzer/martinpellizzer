Ampelopsis gilgilensis

Ampelopsis gilgilensis, commonly known as the Korean grapevine, is a climbing vine native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark blue as they mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
