Abies nobilis var. prostrata

Abies nobilis var. prostrata, commonly known as the creeping white fir, is a low-growing variant of the white fir.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, forming dense mats of foliage on the ground. The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.
