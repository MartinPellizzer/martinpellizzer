Anaphalis pseudocinnamomea

Anaphalis pseudocinnamomea, commonly known as the false cinnamon plant, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that resemble clouds, giving it a unique visual appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
