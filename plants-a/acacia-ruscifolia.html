Acacia ruscifolia

Acacia ruscifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia ruscifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
