Aristolochia eurystoma

Aristolochia eurystoma, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure that resembles a pipe or a hood. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is not recommended for cultivation or consumption.
