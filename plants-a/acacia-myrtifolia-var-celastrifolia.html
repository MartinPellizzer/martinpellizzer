Acacia myrtifolia var. celastrifolia

Acacia myrtifolia var. celastrifolia, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is closely related to other acacia species but is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and floral arrangement.
