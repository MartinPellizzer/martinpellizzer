Acanthocereus marnierianus

Acanthocereus marnierianus, commonly known as the Marnier's night bloomer, is a cactus species native to the desert regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing transpiration and allowing it to thrive in extreme conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and succulent gardens.
