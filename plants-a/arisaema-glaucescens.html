Arisaema glaucescens

Arisaema glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-veined arum, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a showy, spatulate leaf-like structure called a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered around the spadix. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Arisaema glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
