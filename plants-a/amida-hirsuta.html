Amida hirsuta

Amida hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy amida, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amidoaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
