Abutilon mucronatum

Abutilon mucronatum, commonly known as the Mexican maple or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, spire-like clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
