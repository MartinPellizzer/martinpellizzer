Acacia obtecta

Acacia obtecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. Acacia obtecta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
