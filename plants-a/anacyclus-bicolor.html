Anacyclus bicolor

Anacyclus bicolor, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pale green, hairy leaves and a single, yellow, dandelion-like flower that blooms in summer. The plant produces a capsule containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Anacyclus bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
