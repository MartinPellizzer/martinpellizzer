Arachnorchis suffusa

Arachnorchis suffusa, commonly known as the purple spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, underground rhizome and its distinctive, spider-like appearance of the flower spikes. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in heathlands or under forest canopies.

Arachnorchis suffusa is a rare and protected species, with limited distribution and specific habitat requirements.
