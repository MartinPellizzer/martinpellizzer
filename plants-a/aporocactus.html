Aporocactus

Aporocactus, commonly known as the "nipple cactus," is a genus of flowering plants in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

These plants are native to Mexico and are characterized by their short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, rounded tubercles. They produce vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in the spring. Aporocactus species are typically deciduous, shedding their leaves during the dry season to conserve water.

Their unique appearance and relatively easy care make them popular among cactus enthusiasts.
