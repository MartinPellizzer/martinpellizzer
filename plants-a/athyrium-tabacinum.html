Athyrium tabacinum

Athyrium tabacinum, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Athyrium tabacinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
