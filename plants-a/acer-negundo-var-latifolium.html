Acer negundo var. latifolium

Acer negundo var. latifolium, commonly known as the box elder, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five leaflets, and its smooth, light gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It is often found in riparian areas and can tolerate a variety of soil types.

The species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to urban environments.
