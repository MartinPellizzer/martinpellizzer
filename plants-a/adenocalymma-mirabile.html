Adenocalymma mirabile

Adenocalymma mirabile, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in shrublands and grasslands. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, with the ray florets being the most prominent and colorful.

Adenocalymma mirabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
