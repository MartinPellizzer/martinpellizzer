Acacia mucronata subsp. mucronata

Acacia mucronata subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by hard, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
