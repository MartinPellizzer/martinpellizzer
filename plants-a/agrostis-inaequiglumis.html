Agrostis inaequiglumis

Agrostis inaequiglumis, commonly known as the unequal-glume grass, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with distinctive, asymmetrical glumes. It is typically found in moist, temperate regions, often in meadows or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
