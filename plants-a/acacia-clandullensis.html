Acacia clandullensis

Acacia clandullensis, commonly known as the Clandullensis wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
