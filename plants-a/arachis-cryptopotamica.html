Arachis cryptopotamica

Arachis cryptopotamica, commonly known as the cryptopotamica groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other groundnut species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
