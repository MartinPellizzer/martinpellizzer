Aristolochia baetica var. bicolor

Aristolochia baetica var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-white with a deep purple or brown interior. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
