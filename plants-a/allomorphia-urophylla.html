Allomorphia urophylla

Allomorphia urophylla, commonly known as the leafy broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender branches and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
