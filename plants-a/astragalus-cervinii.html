Astragalus cervinii

Astragalus cervinii, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus cervinii is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
