Anemone griffithii

Anemone griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Anemone griffithii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
