Allium khorasanicum

Allium khorasanicum, commonly known as the Khorasan garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, valued for their pungent aroma and flavor.

Allium khorasanicum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
