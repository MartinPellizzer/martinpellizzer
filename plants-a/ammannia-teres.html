Ammannia teres

Ammannia teres, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features smooth, reddish stems that are often tinged with purple, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture with prominent veins. This plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in wetland ecosystems.
