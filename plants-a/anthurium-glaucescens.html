Anthurium glaucescens

Anthurium glaucescens, commonly known as the blue anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, dark green leaves with a bluish tint, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Anthurium glaucescens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
