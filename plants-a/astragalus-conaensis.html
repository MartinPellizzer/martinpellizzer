Astragalus conaensis

Astragalus conaensis, commonly known as the Cona milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, particularly found in China and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus conaensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also of interest for its potential ecological roles in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
