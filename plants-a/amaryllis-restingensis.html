Amaryllis restingensis

Amaryllis restingensis, commonly known as the Restinga amaryllis, is a species native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the Atlantic coast, where it thrives in humid, coastal climates. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Amaryllis restingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
