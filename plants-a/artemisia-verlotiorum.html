Artemisia verlotiorum

Artemisia verlotiorum, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic, often pungent, scent. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss.

Artemisia verlotiorum is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of compounds used in herbal remedies.
