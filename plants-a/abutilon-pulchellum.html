Abutilon pulchellum

Abutilon pulchellum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves that give it a soft, tactile appearance. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Abutilon pulchellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
