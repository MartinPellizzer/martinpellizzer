Aster ageratoides var. potaninii

Aster ageratoides var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
