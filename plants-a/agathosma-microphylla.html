Agathosma microphylla

Agathosma microphylla, commonly known as buchu, is a small shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular hairs that produce a distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its leaves are narrow and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters.

Agathosma microphylla is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for treating digestive and respiratory ailments.
