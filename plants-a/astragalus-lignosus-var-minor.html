Astragalus lignosus var. minor

Astragalus lignosus var. minor, commonly known as the small shrub milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
