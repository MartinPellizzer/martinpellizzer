Achillea pauciloba var. halacsyana

Achillea pauciloba var. halacsyana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flat, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers, which range from white to pale yellow, bloom in summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
