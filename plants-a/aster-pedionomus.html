Aster pedionomus

Aster pedionomus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy purple or blue flowers make it a distinctive wildflower.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
