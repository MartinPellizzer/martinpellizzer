Artemisia odoratissima

Artemisia odoratissima, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-green leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
