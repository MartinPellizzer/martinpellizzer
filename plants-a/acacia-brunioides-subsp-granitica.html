Acacia brunioides subsp. granitica

Acacia brunioides subsp.

granitica, commonly known as the granite acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are often glandular and emit a strong, unpleasant odor when crushed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
