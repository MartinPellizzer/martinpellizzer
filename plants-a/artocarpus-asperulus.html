Artocarpus asperulus

Artocarpus asperulus, commonly known as the rough-fig or wild fig, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
