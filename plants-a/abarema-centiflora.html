Abarema centiflora

Abarema centiflora, commonly known as the hundred-flower plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant typically grows as an erect shrub.

Abarema centiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
