Arundinaria albomarginata

Arundinaria albomarginata, commonly known as the white-edged bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive white margin that sets it apart from other bamboo species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
