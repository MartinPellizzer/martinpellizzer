Astragalus interruptus

Astragalus interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Astragalus interruptus is also of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and limited distribution.
