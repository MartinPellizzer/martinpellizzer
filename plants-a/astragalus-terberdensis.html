Astragalus terberdensis

Astragalus terberdensis, commonly known as milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Additionally, some species within the genus Astragalus are used in traditional medicine.
