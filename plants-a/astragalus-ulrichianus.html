Astragalus ulrichianus

Astragalus ulrichianus, commonly known as the Ulrich's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Astragalus ulrichianus is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in arid ecosystems.
