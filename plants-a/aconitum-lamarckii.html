Aconitum lamarckii

Aconitum lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in moist, woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like shape. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that resembles a hand, and it produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.

Aconitum lamarckii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
