Artemisia eriocarpa

Artemisia eriocarpa, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Artemisia eriocarpa is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
