Aeonium decorum

Aeonium decorum, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in the spring, which are borne on tall, branching stalks. Aeonium decorum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental value.
