Ansellia congoensis

Ansellia congoensis, commonly known as the Congo arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a corm that is used as a starchy root vegetable, similar to taro. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Ansellia congoensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.
