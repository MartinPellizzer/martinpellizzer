Astragalus conspicuus

Astragalus conspicuus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to blue-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus conspicuus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
