Amoria micheliana

Amoria micheliana, commonly known as the Michel's amoria, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Amoria micheliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
