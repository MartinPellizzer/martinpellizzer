Adesmia lambertiana

Adesmia lambertiana, commonly known as Lambert's legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
