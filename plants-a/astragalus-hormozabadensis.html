Astragalus hormozabadensis

Astragalus hormozabadensis, commonly known as Hormozabad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
