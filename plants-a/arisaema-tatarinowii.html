Arisaema tatarinowii

Arisaema tatarinowii, commonly known as the Tatarian Cobra Lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features a distinctive, hooded spathe that resembles a cobra's head, which is a key characteristic of the Arisaema genus. The plant produces a spadix, a dense cluster of tiny, inconspicuous flowers, enclosed within the spathe. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Arisaema tatarinowii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its unique and striking appearance.
