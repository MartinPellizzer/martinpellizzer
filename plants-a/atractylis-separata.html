Atractylis separata

Atractylis separata, commonly known as Japanese ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a thick, rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in branched clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their aromatic properties.
