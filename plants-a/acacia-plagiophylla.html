Acacia plagiophylla

Acacia plagiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, narrow phyllodes that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which appear during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Acacia plagiophylla is a hardy shrub that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
