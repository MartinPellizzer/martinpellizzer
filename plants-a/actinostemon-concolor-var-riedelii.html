Actinostemon concolor var. riedelii

Actinostemon concolor var. riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's concolor, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, glaucous leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare plants.
