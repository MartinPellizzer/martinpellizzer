Acacia angustissima subsp. suffrutescens

Acacia angustissima subsp.

suffrutescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
