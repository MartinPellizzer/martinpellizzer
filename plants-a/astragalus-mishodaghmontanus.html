Astragalus mishodaghmontanus

Astragalus mishodaghmontanus, commonly known as the Mishodaghmontan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus mishodaghmontanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
