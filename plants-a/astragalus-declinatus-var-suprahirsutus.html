Astragalus declinatus var. suprahirsutus

Astragalus declinatus var. suprahirsutus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has hairy foliage and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
