Agathosma blaerioides

Agathosma blaerioides, commonly known as Blaerioides sage, is a member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
