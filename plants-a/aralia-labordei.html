Aralia labordei

Aralia labordei, commonly known as the Mexican angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the Datura genus. The plant has compound, feather-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Aralia labordei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.
