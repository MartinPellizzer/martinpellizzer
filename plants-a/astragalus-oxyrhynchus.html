Astragalus oxyrhynchus

Astragalus oxyrhynchus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the genus Astragalus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus oxyrhynchus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
