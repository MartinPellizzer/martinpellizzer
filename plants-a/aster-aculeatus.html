Aster aculeatus

Aster aculeatus, commonly known as the prickly aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance.

Aster aculeatus is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.
