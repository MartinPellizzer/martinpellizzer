Amyema squarrosa

Amyema squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or gray pubescence on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on the fleshy, red berries it produces.

Amyema squarrosa plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, influencing the growth and health of its host trees.
