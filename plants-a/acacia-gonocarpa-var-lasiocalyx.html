Acacia gonocarpa var. lasiocalyx

Acacia gonocarpa var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-barked acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its pale green, bipinnate leaves and yellow spherical flowerheads that appear in winter. The plant has a woody, fissured bark that becomes rough and flaky with age. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

The seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often dispersed by animals or through wind.
