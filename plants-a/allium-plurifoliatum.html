Allium plurifoliatum

Allium plurifoliatum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous roots and thick, fleshy bulbs. The plant produces grass-like leaves that are broad and flat, often reaching several inches in width. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in umbels, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Allium plurifoliatum is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
