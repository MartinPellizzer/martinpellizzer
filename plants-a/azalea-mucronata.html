Azalea mucronata

Azalea mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. Azalea mucronata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form thickets in suitable conditions.
