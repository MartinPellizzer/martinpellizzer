Ageratum hondurense

Ageratum hondurense, commonly known as the Honduras ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum hondurense is often used in floral arrangements and as a bedding plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
