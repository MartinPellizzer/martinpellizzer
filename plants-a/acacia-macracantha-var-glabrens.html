Acacia macracantha var. glabrens

Acacia macracantha var. glabrens, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in globular clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its lack of thorns, distinguishing it from other acacia species.
