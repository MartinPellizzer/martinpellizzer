Archidendron trifoliolatum

Archidendron trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leaved legume or the pink shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
