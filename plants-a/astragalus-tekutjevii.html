Astragalus tekutjevii

Astragalus tekutjevii, commonly known as the Tekutjev's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus tekutjevii is also noted for its role in traditional medicine in some regions of its native habitat.
