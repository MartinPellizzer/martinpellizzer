Alchemilla plicatula

Alchemilla plicatula, commonly known as the wrinkled lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled, shield-shaped leaves that resemble a lady's mantle. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla plicatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
