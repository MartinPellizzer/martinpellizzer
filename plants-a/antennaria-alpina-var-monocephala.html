Antennaria alpina var. monocephala

Antennaria alpina var. monocephala, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically linear to oblanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
