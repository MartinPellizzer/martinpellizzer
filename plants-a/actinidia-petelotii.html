Actinidia petelotii

Actinidia petelotii, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its palmately compound leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, green fruits that turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
