Asclepias ruthiae

Asclepias ruthiae, commonly known as the Ruth's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in umbels. Asclepias ruthiae is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
