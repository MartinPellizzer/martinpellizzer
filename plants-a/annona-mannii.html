Annona mannii

Annona mannii, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that produces large, green, spiny fruits with a thick, yellowish pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped or elliptical. Its flowers are typically white or greenish-white and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, creamy flesh and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
