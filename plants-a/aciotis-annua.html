Aciotis annua

Aciotis annua, commonly known as common blueweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its small, composite flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aciotis annua is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
