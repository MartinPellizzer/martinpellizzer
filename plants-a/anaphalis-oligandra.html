Anaphalis oligandra

Anaphalis oligandra, commonly known as the silverleaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and conical inflorescence. The plant produces small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its frost-resistant nature and ability to attract pollinators.
