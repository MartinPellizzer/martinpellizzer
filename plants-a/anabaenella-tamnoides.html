Anabaenella tamnoides

Anabaenella tamnoides, commonly known as the water silk, is a filamentous cyanobacterium that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, flexible, and often spirally coiled filaments, which give it a silky appearance. This species is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems. It often forms dense mats on the surface of still waters, such as ponds and lakes.

Anabaenella tamnoides is also notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions, making it a common inhabitant of slow-moving or stagnant water bodies.
