Ailanthus peekelii var. glabra

Ailanthus peekelii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth tree of heaven, is a variant of the tree of heaven species native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, palmately branched roots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by winged fruits that disperse seeds via wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Its rapid growth and hardiness contribute to its ecological impact.
