Arsenjevia rossii

Arsenjevia rossii, commonly known as the Rossii's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence that resembles a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, particularly beetles.

Arsenjevia rossii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens for its exotic appearance.
