Aceratium hypoleucum

Aceratium hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered acanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, spire-like inflorescences that bear white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include hairy stems and bracts that help protect the flowers.

Aceratium hypoleucum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
