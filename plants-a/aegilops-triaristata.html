Aegilops triaristata

Aegilops triaristata, commonly known as the three-awned wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with three awns, which are bristle-like structures at the base of each floret. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Aegilops triaristata is considered a weed in agricultural settings but has also been studied for its potential in breeding programs for improved wheat varieties.

Morphology
