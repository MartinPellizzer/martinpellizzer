Agrifolium

Agrifolium, commonly known as the candlewood plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that often have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain seeds. Agrifolium species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and their ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
