Arrhostoxylum fulgidum var. angustissimum

Arrhostoxylum fulgidum var. angustissimum, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert willow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators in desert ecosystems.
