Aegilops aucheri f. nudiglumis

Aegilops aucheri f. nudiglumis, commonly known as the naked lemma aegilops, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. Its unique feature is the absence of glumes on the spikelets, giving it the "nudiglumis" designation, which means "naked glumes" in Latin.

This variety is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration.
