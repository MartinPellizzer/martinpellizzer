Avena fatua subvar. pseudonana

Avena fatua subvar. pseudonana, commonly known as slender wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with bisexual flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in many regions.
