Aristida mutabilis subsp. nigritiana

Aristida mutabilis subsp.

nigritiana, commonly known as blackstem wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and riparian areas.

The plant has slender, wiry stems that often turn dark purple or black as they mature, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
