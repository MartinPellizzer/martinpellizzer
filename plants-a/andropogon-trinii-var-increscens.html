Andropogon trinii var. increscens

Andropogon trinii var. increscens, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
