Arctotis rotundifolia

Arctotis rotundifolia, commonly known as the Round-leaf Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, rounded leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
