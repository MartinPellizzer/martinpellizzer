Aster barbellatus

Aster barbellatus, commonly known as the fringed aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pale blue to violet flowers with distinctive fringed petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Aster barbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
