Alchemilla sudetica

Alchemilla sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Alchemilla sudetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
