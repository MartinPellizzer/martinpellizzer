Acacia albicans

Acacia albicans, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia albicans is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
