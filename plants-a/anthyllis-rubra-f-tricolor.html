Anthyllis rubra f. tricolor

Anthyllis rubra f. tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored broom.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers typically display a combination of red, white, and yellow, giving it its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
