Astragalus thompsoniae

Astragalus thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellowish to reddish pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus thompsoniae is often found in open grasslands and along mountain slopes.
