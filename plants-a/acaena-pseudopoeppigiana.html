Acaena pseudopoeppigiana

Acaena pseudopoeppigiana, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prickly stems and small, serrated leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain areas.
