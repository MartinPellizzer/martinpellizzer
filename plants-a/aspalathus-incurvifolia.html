Aspalathus incurvifolia

Aspalathus incurvifolia, commonly known as the African red bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Aspalathus incurvifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of cut flowers.
