Argythamnia pavoniana

Argythamnia pavoniana, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is often found in sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall.

Argythamnia pavoniana is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
