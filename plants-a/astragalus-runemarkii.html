Astragalus runemarkii

Astragalus runemarkii, commonly known as the runemark milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus runemarkii is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
