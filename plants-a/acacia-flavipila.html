Acacia flavipila

Acacia flavipila, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements and are a symbol of Australian identity.

Acacia flavipila is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
