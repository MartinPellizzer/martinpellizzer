Annona africana

Annona africana, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a fragrant, yellowish-green fruit with a thick, creamy pulp that is edible. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong scent that attracts pollinators.

The plant is often grown for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
