Acacia eggelingii

Acacia eggelingii, commonly known as the Eeling's acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the dry season. Its woody pods contain flat, edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisine.

Acacia eggelingii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
