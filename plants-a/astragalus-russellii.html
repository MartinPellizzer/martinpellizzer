Astragalus russellii

Astragalus russellii, commonly known as Russell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus russellii is often found in sagebrush communities and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
