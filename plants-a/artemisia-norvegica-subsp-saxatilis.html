Artemisia norvegica subsp. saxatilis

Artemisia norvegica subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the Norwegian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine regions of northern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower clusters.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. It has a taproot system and is often found in mountainous and coastal areas.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential medicinal and aromatic uses.
