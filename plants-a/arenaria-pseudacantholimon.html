Arenaria pseudacantholimon

Arenaria pseudacantholimon, commonly known as the sandpink, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pinkish or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Arenaria pseudacantholimon thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
