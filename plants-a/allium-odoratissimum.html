Allium odoratissimum

Allium odoratissimum, commonly known as the garlic chive or Chinese leek, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are often used in culinary applications for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and farms.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and edible, contributing to its widespread use in various cuisines.
