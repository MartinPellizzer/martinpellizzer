Agrostis phleoides

Agrostis phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is a hardy species that can tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
