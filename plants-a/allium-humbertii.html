Allium humbertii

Allium humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert onion, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Corsica.

It is a bulbous plant that produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, alpine conditions of its native habitat. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable plant for both culinary and ecological purposes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Allium humbertii is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human activity.
