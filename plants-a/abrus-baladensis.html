Abrus baladensis

Abrus baladensis, commonly known as the Balad bean, is a climbing legume native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It produces distinctive red, ornate pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Due to the toxicity of its seeds, it is important to handle the plant with care.
