Apluda zeygites

Apluda zeygites, commonly known as the red lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, red or pink flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in the spring, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The red lily is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
