Arisaema maekawae

Arisaema maekawae, commonly known as the Kuma River arisaema, is a herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Arisaema maekawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
