Anemone obtusiloba subsp. ovalifolia

Anemone obtusiloba subsp.

ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf anemone, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a waxy, hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
