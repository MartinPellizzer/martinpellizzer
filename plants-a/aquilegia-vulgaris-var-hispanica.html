Aquilegia vulgaris var. hispanica

Aquilegia vulgaris var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
