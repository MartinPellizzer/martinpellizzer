Aspidium monotis

Aspidium monotis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Dennstaedtiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches, where it forms dense mats on the water's surface. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Aspidium monotis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
