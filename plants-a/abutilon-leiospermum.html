Abutilon leiospermum

Abutilon leiospermum, commonly known as velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often found in moist, tropical environments and is adapted to thrive in partial shade.
