Anaxagorea crassipetala

Anaxagorea crassipetala, commonly known as the thick-leaved evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Anaxagorea crassipetala is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
