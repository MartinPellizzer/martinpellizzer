Astragalus straussii var. albiflorus

Astragalus straussii var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
