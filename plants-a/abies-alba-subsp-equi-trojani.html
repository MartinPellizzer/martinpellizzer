Abies alba subsp. equi-trojani

Abies alba subsp.

equi-trojani, commonly known as the Carpathian fir, is a subspecies of the European silver fir native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves.

The foliage is typically dark green, with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces cones that are cylindrical and often hang downward, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, mountainous climates and plays an important role in the forest ecosystems of Central and Eastern Europe.
