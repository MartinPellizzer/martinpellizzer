Astragalus swatensis

Astragalus swatensis, commonly known as Swat milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the summer months. Astragalus swatensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ecological significance includes providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization in its native range.
