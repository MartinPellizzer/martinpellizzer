Aganisia cyanea

Aganisia cyanea, commonly known as the blue aganisia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth texture.

Aganisia cyanea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
