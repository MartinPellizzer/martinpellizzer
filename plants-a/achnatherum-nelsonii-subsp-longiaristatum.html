Achnatherum nelsonii subsp. longiaristatum

Achnatherum nelsonii subsp.

longiaristatum, commonly known as the long-bract needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
