Asphodelopsis arangadinensis

Asphodelopsis arangadinensis, commonly known as the Arangadina asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family, which includes many flowering plants adapted to arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
