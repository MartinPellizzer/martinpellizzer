Anisopappus grangeoides

Anisopappus grangeoides, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adapted growth habits.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in desert regions.
