Asterocephalus sericeus

Asterocephalus sericeus, commonly known as the silky headplant, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, and often silvery appearance.

Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, almost velvety texture. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

Due to its specialized growth requirements, Asterocephalus sericeus is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
