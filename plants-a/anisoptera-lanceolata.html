Anisoptera lanceolata

Anisoptera lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf acanthus, is a striking plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often adorned with distinctive, spiny bracts. The plant produces showy, spiky flowers that range in color from purple to red, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Anisoptera lanceolata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its dramatic foliage and long-lasting blooms.
