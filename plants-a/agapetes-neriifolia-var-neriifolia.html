Agapetes neriifolia var. neriifolia

Agapetes neriifolia var. neriifolia, commonly known as the white-leafed agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the montane forests of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically white or pale green on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found at high altitudes in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
