Aspidoglossum albocoronatum

Aspidoglossum albocoronatum, commonly known as the white-capped snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a white or yellowish stripe along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its low maintenance and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and air-purifying purposes.
