Acacia pterocaulon

Acacia pterocaulon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Acacia pterocaulon plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
