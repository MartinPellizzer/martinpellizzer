Agropyron subsecundum var. andinum

Agropyron subsecundum var. andinum, commonly known as the Andean needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry culms that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Agropyron subsecundum var. andinum plays a role in stabilizing soils in mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
