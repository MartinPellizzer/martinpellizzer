Asplenium dielpallidum

Asplenium dielpallidum, commonly known as the pale shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters known as sori.

Asplenium dielpallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
