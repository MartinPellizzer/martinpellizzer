Aniseia trichantha

Aniseia trichantha, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or greenish flowers and its ability to thrive in arid conditions. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "trichantha," meaning "hairy stem.". It produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
