Aneurolepidium multicaule

Aneurolepidium multicaule, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of 30 to 80 centimeters. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairless or slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

Aneurolepidium multicaule plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
