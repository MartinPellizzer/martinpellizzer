Amorpha gaertneri

Amorpha gaertneri, commonly known as the yellow false indigo, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Amorpha gaertneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
