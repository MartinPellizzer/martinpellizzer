Arthropteris glabra

Arthropteris glabra, commonly known as the smooth holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its leaves in clusters called sori.

Arthropteris glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, green foliage.
