Aponogeton appendiculatus

Aponogeton appendiculatus, commonly known as the black water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, with roots that hang below. The plant thrives in soft, acidic water and is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds. It is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water, adding a delicate touch to its underwater environment.
