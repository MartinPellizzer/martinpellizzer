Allium welwitschii

Allium welwitschii, commonly known as the giant yellow garlic, is a striking plant native to Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Allium welwitschii is adapted to arid environments and is known for its resilience in harsh climates.
