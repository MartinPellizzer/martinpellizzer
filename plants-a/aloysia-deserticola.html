Aloysia deserticola

Aloysia deserticola, commonly known as the desert verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system for water conservation. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Aloysia deserticola is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
