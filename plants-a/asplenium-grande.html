Asplenium grande

Asplenium grande, commonly known as the giant spleenwort, is a fern that belongs to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often lobed fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium grande is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and is often found in temperate regions.
