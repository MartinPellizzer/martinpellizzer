Asystasia kalantha

Asystasia kalantha, commonly known as the red-flowered asystasia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asystasia kalantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
