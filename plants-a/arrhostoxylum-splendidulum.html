Arrhostoxylum splendidulum

Arrhostoxylum splendidulum, commonly known as the Andean beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue berries that are visually appealing and attract various pollinators. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
