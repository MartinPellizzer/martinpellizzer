Angiopteris cumingii

Angiopteris cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Angiopteris cumingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
