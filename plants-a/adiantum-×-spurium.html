Adiantum × spurium

Adiantum × spurium, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a hybrid species within the Adiantum genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streams. Its fronds are usually dark green and turn a reddish-brown when dry, which is a notable feature.

Adiantum × spurium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
