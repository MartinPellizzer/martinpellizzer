Altamiranoa stricta

Altamiranoa stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure known as a spadix, often enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered around the spadix.

Altamiranoa stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
