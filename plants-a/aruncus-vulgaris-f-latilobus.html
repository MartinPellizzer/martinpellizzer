Aruncus vulgaris f. latilobus

Aruncus vulgaris f. latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed goat's beard, is a decorative perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of white to cream-colored bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The leaves are large, compound, and deeply lobed, giving the plant a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture and color to garden landscapes.
