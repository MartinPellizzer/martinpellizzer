Allium curtum subsp. aegyptiacum

Allium curtum subsp.

aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its strong garlic scent and is often found growing in dry, rocky soils. It is closely related to other Allium species and shares similar botanical traits such as bulb formation and inflorescence structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
