Anaphalis pannosa

Anaphalis pannosa, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and finely divided leaves. The plant is distinguished by its silvery-white, woolly foliage that resembles a cloud or frost, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Anaphalis pannosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
