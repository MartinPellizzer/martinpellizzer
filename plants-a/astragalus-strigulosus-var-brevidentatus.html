Astragalus strigulosus var. brevidentatus

Astragalus strigulosus var. brevidentatus, commonly known as the short-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in disturbed or open habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and taprooted, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
