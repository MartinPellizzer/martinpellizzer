Aster leptocladus

Aster leptocladus, commonly known as the slender-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers in the fall.

Aster leptocladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
