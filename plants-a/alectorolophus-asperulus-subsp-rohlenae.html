Alectorolophus asperulus subsp. rohlenae

Alectorolophus asperulus subsp.

rohlenae, commonly known as the Rohlen's rough lichen, is a member of the Parmeliaceae family. It is a foliose lichen that forms a loosely attached, crust-like thallus with a pale to dark green or brown coloration.

The species is characterized by its distinct, irregularly shaped lobes and a dense, granular texture. It typically grows on bark, stone, or soil in temperate and boreal forest regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant lichen species.
