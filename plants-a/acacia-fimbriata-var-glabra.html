Acacia fimbriata var. glabra

Acacia fimbriata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and trifoliate leaves composed of three elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
