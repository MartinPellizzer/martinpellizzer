Acanthocereus sicariguensis

Acanthocereus sicariguensis, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny appearance. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is well adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Its slow growth rate and unique flowering pattern make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
