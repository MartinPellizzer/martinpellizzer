Atriplex patula proles delilei

Atriplex patula proles delilei, commonly known as the smooth saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, making it well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soils in harsh environments.
