Anthericum graecum

Anthericum graecum, commonly known as the Greek arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant blooms with a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is characteristic of the Araceae family. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Anthericum graecum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
