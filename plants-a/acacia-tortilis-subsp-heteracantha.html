Acacia tortilis subsp. heteracantha

Acacia tortilis subsp.

heteracantha, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its dense, spreading branches.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves, and it produces yellow flowers followed by pod fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This species is also known for its role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
