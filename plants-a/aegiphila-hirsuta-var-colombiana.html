Aegiphila hirsuta var. colombiana

Aegiphila hirsuta var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian hairy beggarticks, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. It has a woody stem with persistent, lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
