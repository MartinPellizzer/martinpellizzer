Anemia madagascariensis

Anemia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a unique species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
