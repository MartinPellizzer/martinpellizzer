Azalea henryi

Azalea henryi, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Azalea henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
