Acosta jabukensis

Acosta jabukensis, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along its climbing stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often found growing in humid, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Acosta jabukensis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing nectar for pollinators.
