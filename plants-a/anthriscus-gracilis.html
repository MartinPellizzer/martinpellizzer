Anthriscus gracilis

Anthriscus gracilis, commonly known as slender chervil, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white, umbelliferous flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anthriscus gracilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and mild, anise-like flavor.
