Astragalus schimperi var. aravensis

Astragalus schimperi var. aravensis, commonly known as the Aravah milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits are typical of the Fabaceae family.

Astragalus schimperi var. aravensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments and is of interest for ecological and botanical studies.
