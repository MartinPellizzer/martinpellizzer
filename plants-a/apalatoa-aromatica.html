Apalatoa aromatica

Apalatoa aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful blooms. The plant produces small, aromatic flowers that emit a sweet, spicy scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture in its humid habitat.

Apalatoa aromatica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its use in traditional herbal remedies.
