Asplenium erectum f. serratum

Asplenium erectum f. serratum, commonly known as the serrated maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds.

The fronds are typically upright and have a distinctive serrated edge, which gives the plant its common name. It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is native to regions in Asia, including China and Japan. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
