Anatherum setifolium

Anatherum setifolium, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in dense clusters at the top of the stalks. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a distinctive hairy texture along the edges.

Anatherum setifolium thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
