Astragalus cossonii

Astragalus cossonii, commonly known as the Cosson's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus cossonii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
