Acalypha firmula

Acalypha firmula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Acalypha firmula is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in some areas.
