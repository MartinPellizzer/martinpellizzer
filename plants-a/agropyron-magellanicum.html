Agropyron magellanicum

Agropyron magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy blades that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, including arid and semi-arid regions, and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Agropyron magellanicum plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
