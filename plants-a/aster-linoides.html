Aster linoides

Aster linoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its narrow, linear leaves and showy purple to pink flowers make it a distinctive species within its habitat.

Aster linoides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils and open landscapes.
