Anemone pseudoaltaica var. katonis

Anemone pseudoaltaica var. katonis, commonly known as the Katonai anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
