Asterocephalus micranthus

Asterocephalus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asterhead, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in a compact, spherical cluster. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Asterocephalus micranthus is typically found in grasslands and open habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
