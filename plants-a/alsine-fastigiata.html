Alsine fastigiata

Alsine fastigiata, commonly known as the upright alsike, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Alsine fastigiata is typically found in moist, disturbed soils in meadows and along stream banks.
