Anemone magellanica

Anemone magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan anemone, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white petals that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

Anemone magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
