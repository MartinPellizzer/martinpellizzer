Astragalus calliphysa subsp. calliphysa

Astragalus calliphysa subsp.

calliphysa, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive bluish-green color.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow leaflets and reduced floral structures compared to other Astragalus species.
