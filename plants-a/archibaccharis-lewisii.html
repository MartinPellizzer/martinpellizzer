Archibaccharis lewisii

Archibaccharis lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's baccharis, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. It has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for its medicinal properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
