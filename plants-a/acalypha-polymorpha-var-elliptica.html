Acalypha polymorpha var. elliptica

Acalypha polymorpha var. elliptica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist, shady environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
