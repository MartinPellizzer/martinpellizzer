Agati cannabina

Agati cannabina, commonly known as the West Indian hemp, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family, which includes other hemp species. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles.

Agati cannabina is notable for containing psychoactive compounds, similar to those found in Cannabis sativa.
