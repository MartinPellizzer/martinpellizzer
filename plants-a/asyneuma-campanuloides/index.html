Asyneuma Campanuloides

Asyneuma campanuloides is a striking flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America. Its most notable feature is its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. This species thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively unknown to the general public.
