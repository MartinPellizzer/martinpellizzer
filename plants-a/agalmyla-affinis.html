Agalmyla affinis

Agalmyla affinis, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin.

The flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, round fruits.
