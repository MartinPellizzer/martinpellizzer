Aloe semimargaritifera var. major

Aloe semimargaritifera var. major, commonly known as the giant aloe or rock aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
