Acer palmatum subvar. formosanum

Acer palmatum subvar. formosanum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with a rounded or conical shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The tree prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
