Achillea atrata subsp. halleri

Achillea atrata subsp.

halleri, commonly known as the black yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
