Achyranthes robusta

Achyranthes robusta, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a robust annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a stout, branched stem that is often covered with short, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and its seed capsules are triangular in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
