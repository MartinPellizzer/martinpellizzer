Achillea teretifolia

Achillea teretifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
