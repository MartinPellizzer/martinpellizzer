Adiantum varium

Adiantum varium, commonly known as the variable maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the feathers of a bird. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with each segment ending in a small, spoon-shaped pinna.

Adiantum varium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
