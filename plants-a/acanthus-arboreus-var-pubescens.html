Acanthus arboreus var. pubescens

Acanthus arboreus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy acanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features large, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, foamy appearance on the underside. The plant produces showy, spires of purple or pink flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, though it can be somewhat invasive in certain regions.
