Acacia glabra

Acacia glabra, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are a key feature for identification. Acacia glabra is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or loamy soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
