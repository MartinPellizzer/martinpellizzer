Anthericum rhodesianum

Anthericum rhodesianum, commonly known as the Rhodesian arum, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, providing a dramatic visual presence. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy, spatulate spathe, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also notable for its ability to produce a strong, unpleasant odor, which attracts certain pollinators.
