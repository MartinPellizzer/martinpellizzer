Alchemilla exigua

Alchemilla exigua, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive velvety texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Alchemilla exigua is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
