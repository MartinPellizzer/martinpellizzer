Alpagrostis setacea var. setacea

Alpagrostis setacea var. setacea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant typically grows in clumps, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are arranged in spikelets.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
