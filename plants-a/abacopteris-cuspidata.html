Abacopteris cuspidata

Abacopteris cuspidata, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
