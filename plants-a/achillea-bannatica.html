Achillea bannatica

Achillea bannatica, commonly known as meadow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white, arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
