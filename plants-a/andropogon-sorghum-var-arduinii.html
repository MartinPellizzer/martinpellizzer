Andropogon sorghum var. arduinii

Andropogon sorghum var. arduinii, commonly known as the dwarf sorghum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
