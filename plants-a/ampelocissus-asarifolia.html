Ampelocissus asarifolia

Ampelocissus asarifolia, commonly known as the asarifolia moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, black, berry-like fruits. Its tendrils allow it to climb and spread along fences or trees.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
