Anil aspalathoides

Anil aspalathoides, commonly known as the sweet tea plant, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its aromatic foliage, which emits a sweet, tea-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Anil aspalathoides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
