Anthomeles stipulosa

Anthomeles stipulosa, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clumps near water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-parted flowers, and it produces small, wind-pollinated blooms.

Anthomeles stipulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
