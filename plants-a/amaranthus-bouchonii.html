Amaranthus bouchonii

Amaranthus bouchonii, commonly known as the Bouchon's amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or open habitats. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their nutritional value.

Amaranthus bouchonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
