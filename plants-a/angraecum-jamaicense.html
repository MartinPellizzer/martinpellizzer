Angraecum jamaicense

Angraecum jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican orchid, is a rare and unique species native to Jamaica.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue with long, slender petals and a prominent lip.

Angraecum jamaicense is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
