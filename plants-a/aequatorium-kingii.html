Aequatorium kingii

Aequatorium kingii, commonly known as the King's aequatorium, is a rare and striking plant native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy soils along the shorelines, where it thrives in the unique microclimates provided by the ocean breeze and salt spray. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months. Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh coastal environments and its role in local ecosystems.
