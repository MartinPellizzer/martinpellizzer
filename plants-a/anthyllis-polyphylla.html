Anthyllis polyphylla

Anthyllis polyphylla, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow or reddish flowers in clusters. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
