Astragalus inyoensis

Astragalus inyoensis, commonly known as Inyo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Great Basin region of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
