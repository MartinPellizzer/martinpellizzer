Acaena californica var. polyarthrotricha

Acaena californica var. polyarthrotricha, commonly known as the California burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by distinctive burrs containing seeds. Its fruiting structures are adapted for dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

This species is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is part of the Rosaceae family.
