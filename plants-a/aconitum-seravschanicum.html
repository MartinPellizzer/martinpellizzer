Aconitum seravschanicum

Aconitum seravschanicum, commonly known as the Altai aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Aconitum seravschanicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
