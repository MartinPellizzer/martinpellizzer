Agrimonia coreana

Agrimonia coreana, commonly known as Korean agrimony, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as achenes, which are surrounded by persistent sepals. Its leaves are typically pinnately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Agrimonia coreana is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
