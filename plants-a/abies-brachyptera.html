Abies brachyptera

Abies brachyptera, commonly known as the dwarf white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its short, rounded cones and dense, dark green needles that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree typically grows in high-elevation forests and is adapted to cold, moist environments. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it produces aromatic resin.

Abies brachyptera plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
