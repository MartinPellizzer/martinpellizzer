Astragalus acrocarpus

Astragalus acrocarpus, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across rugged landscapes.
