Aristolochia glaucescens

Aristolochia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-winged Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-blue in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
