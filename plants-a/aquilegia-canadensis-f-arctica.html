Aquilegia canadensis f. arctica

Aquilegia canadensis f. arctica, commonly known as the Arctic columbine, is a botanical variant of the Canadian columbine.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America, particularly in alpine and arctic environments. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five sepals that resemble petals. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and subarctic habitats.
