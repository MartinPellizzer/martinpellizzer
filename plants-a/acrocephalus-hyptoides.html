Acrocephalus hyptoides

Acrocephalus hyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetland environments in Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, usually blooming during the spring and summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland ecosystems.
