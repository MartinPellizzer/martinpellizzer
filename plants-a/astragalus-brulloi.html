Astragalus brulloi

Astragalus brulloi, commonly known as the Brulloi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus brulloi is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
