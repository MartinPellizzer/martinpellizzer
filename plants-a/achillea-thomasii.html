Achillea thomasii

Achillea thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Thomas' yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
