Abutilon rufescens

Abutilon rufescens, commonly known as the red abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
