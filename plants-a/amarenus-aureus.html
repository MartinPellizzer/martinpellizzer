Amarenus aureus

Amarenus aureus, commonly known as the golden amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant grows up to 1-2 meters tall, with branching stems that often produce multiple flower clusters. Its seeds are small, oblong, and typically have a reddish or brownish hue.

Amarenus aureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
