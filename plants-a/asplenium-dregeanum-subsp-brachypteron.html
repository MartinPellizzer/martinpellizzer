Asplenium dregeanum subsp. brachypteron

Asplenium dregeanum subsp.

brachypteron, commonly known as the short-veined rock fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa. It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows on rocky outcrops and in shaded, moist environments.

The plant has a compact rosette of leathery, dark green leaves with a distinctive, short-stemmed structure. Its fronds are usually narrowly lance-shaped and have a finely toothed margin.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its use in traditional medicine by local communities.
