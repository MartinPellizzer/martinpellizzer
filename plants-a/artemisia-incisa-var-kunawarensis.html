Artemisia incisa var. kunawarensis

Artemisia incisa var. kunawarensis, commonly known as the Japanese tarragon or Kunawara artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It features silvery-gray, finely divided leaves that emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in the summer, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued in traditional herbal practices.
