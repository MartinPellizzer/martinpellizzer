Anthyllis circinnata

Anthyllis circinnata, commonly known as the spiral broom.

It is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Anthyllis circinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
