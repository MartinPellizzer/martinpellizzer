Acacia shapelleae

Acacia shapelleae, commonly known as the Shapellea acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.

Acacia shapelleae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
