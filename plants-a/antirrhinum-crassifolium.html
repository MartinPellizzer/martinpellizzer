Antirrhinum crassifolium

Antirrhinum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
