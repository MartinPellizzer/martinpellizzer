Acacia fuscaneura

Acacia fuscaneura, commonly known as the narrow-veined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia fuscaneura is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
