Ampelocissus nervosa

Ampelocissus nervosa, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, waxy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
