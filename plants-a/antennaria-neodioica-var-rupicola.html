Antennaria neodioica var. rupicola

Antennaria neodioica var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
