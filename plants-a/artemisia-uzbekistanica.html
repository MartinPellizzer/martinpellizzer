Artemisia uzbekistanica

Artemisia uzbekistanica, commonly known as the Uzbek wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typical of the composite flower structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
