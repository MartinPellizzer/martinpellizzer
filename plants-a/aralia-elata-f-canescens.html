Aralia elata f. canescens

Aralia elata f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy aralia, is a variant of the Asian aralia species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant features compound leaves with a feathery appearance, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

This cultivar is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
