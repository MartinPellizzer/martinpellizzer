Anatherum brachystachyum

Anatherum brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
