Aiphanes ulei

Aiphanes ulei, commonly known as the blue panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in coastal areas.

Aiphanes ulei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
