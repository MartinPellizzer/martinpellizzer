Acacia tortilis subsp. campoptila

Acacia tortilis subsp.

campoptila, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by its long, drooping branches.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it is well adapted to drought conditions.

The acacia also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing its ability to thrive in poor soils.
