Artemisia canariensis

Artemisia canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flower clusters. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its strong, camphor-like scent. Artemisia canariensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
