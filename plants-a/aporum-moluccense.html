Aporum moluccense

Aporum moluccense, commonly known as the Moluccan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Aporum moluccense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
