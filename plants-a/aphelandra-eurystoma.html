Aphelandra eurystoma

Aphelandra eurystoma, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Aphelandra eurystoma is popular as a ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and showy blooms.
