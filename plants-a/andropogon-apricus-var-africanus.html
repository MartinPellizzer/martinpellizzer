Andropogon apricus var. africanus

Andropogon apricus var. africanus, commonly known as the African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used for erosion control.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in sustainable landscaping.
