Alstroemeria albescens

Alstroemeria albescens, commonly known as the white alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, featuring strap-like leaves and a compact, bushy habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Alstroemeria albescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
