Astragalus aestivorum

Astragalus aestivorum, commonly known as the summer milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across suitable habitats.
