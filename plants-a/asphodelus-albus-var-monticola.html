Asphodelus albus var. monticola

Asphodelus albus var. monticola, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to dry, harsh environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
