Astragalus pentapetaloides

Astragalus pentapetaloides, commonly known as the five-petaled milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus pentapetaloides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
