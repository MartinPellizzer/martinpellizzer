Acidanthera fourcadei

Acidanthera fourcadei, commonly known as the Fourcade's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with white or pale pink petals and a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
