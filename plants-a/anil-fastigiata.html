Anil fastigiata

Anil fastigiata, commonly known as the upright anil, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in whorls along the stems.

Anil fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique form and floral display.
