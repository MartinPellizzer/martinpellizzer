Abutilon nelsonii

Abutilon nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fibrous bark and hairy foliage are notable botanical features, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
