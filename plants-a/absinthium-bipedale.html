Absinthium bipedale

Absinthium bipedale, commonly known as the two-footed wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height, covered in fine, silver-white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in complex panicles, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and covered in a dense layer of fine, glandular hairs.

Absinthium bipedale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
