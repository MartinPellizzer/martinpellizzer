Asteranthera chiloensis

Asteranthera chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. Its showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Asteranthera chiloensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in coastal environments.
