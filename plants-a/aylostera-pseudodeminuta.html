Aylostera pseudodeminuta

Aylostera pseudodeminuta, commonly known as the dwarf aylostera, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
