Amaranthus blitoides var. crassior

Amaranthus blitoides var. crassior, commonly known as thickleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range from red to orange. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is rich in nutrients, making it a valuable forage crop for livestock.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
