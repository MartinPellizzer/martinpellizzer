Aechmea pseudonudicaulis

Aechmea pseudonudicaulis, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a pine-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help collect and store water in the humid environments it inhabits.

This bromeliad thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
