Aspilia heringeriana

Aspilia heringeriana, commonly known as the Heringer's candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Aspilia heringeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
