Asperula diminuta

Asperula diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pepper or small woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Asperula diminuta is also valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
