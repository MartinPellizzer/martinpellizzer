Ajuga chamaepitys subsp. mardinensis

Ajuga chamaepitys subsp.

mardinensis, commonly known as Mardin ajuga, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to violet flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
