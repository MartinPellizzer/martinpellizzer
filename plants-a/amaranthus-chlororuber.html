Amaranthus chlororuber

Amaranthus chlororuber, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into three-angled, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.

Amaranthus chlororuber is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
