Avena fatua subsp. macrantha

Avena fatua subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the slender oat, is a wild grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
