Aureolaria pedicularia subsp. intercedens

Aureolaria pedicularia subsp.

intercedens, commonly known as the common yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

The subspecies intercedens is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Aureolaria pedicularia.
