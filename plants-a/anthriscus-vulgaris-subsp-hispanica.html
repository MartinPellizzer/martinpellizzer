Anthriscus vulgaris subsp. hispanica

Anthriscus vulgaris subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish parsley, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its aromatic foliage is often used in cooking, similar to its closely related species.

It is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
