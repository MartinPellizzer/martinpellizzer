Astragalus jamzadiae

Astragalus jamzadiae, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The seeds of Astragalus jamzadiae are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
