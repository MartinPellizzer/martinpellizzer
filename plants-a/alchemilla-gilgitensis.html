Alchemilla gilgitensis

Alchemilla gilgitensis, commonly known as the Gilgit lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Alchemilla gilgitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
