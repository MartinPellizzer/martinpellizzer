Aegiphila cuspidata

Aegiphila cuspidata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant yellowish-white flowers. It is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
