Asclepias gonalis

Asclepias gonalis, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common among milkweeds. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Asclepias gonalis serves as a critical host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the lifecycle of this iconic species.
