Albizia subrhombea

Albizia subrhombea, commonly known as the African honeythorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Albizia subrhombea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.
