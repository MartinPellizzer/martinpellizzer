Ardisia fletcheri

Ardisia fletcheri, commonly known as the Fletcher's beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red berries that are a striking contrast to its foliage, attracting birds and other wildlife. Ardisia fletcheri thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
