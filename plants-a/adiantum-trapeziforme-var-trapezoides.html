Adiantum trapeziforme var. trapezoides

Adiantum trapeziforme var. trapezoides, commonly known as the trapezoid maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, lanceolate fronds that are typically bright green and have a distinctive trapezoidal shape. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to rocky or soil surfaces.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
