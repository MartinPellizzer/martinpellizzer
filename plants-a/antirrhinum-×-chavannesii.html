Antirrhinum × chavannesii

Antirrhinum × chavannesii, commonly known as the hybrid snapdragon, is a perennial flowering plant that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species, Antirrhinum majus and Antirrhinum campanulatum.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to yellow, often with intricate patterns resembling the face of a human or animal. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Antirrhinum × chavannesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
