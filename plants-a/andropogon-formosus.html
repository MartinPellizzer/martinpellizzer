Andropogon formosus

Andropogon formosus, commonly known as the Chinese switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Andropogon formosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
