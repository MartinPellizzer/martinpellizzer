Agrostis vulgaris var. alpestris

Agrostis vulgaris var. alpestris, commonly known as alpine bentgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to mountainous regions.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in high-altitude gardens.
