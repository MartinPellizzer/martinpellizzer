Acaciella polypodioides

Acaciella polypodioides, commonly known as the slender acaciella, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
