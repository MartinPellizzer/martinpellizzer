Acantholimon laxiusculum

Acantholimon laxiusculum, commonly known as the slender flaxvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Acantholimon laxiusculum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and wildlife.
