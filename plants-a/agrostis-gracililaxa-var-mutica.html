Agrostis gracililaxa var. mutica

Agrostis gracililaxa var. mutica, commonly known as the silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for turfgrass applications. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and often covered in soft, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
