Alchemilla lessingiana

Alchemilla lessingiana, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of shiny, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
