Astragalus scoparius subsp. intarrensis

Astragalus scoparius subsp.

intarrensis, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and is adapted to grow in rocky or alpine environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
