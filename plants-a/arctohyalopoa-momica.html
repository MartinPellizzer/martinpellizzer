Arctohyalopoa momica

Arctohyalopoa momica, commonly known as the Momica mountain palm, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that contains a single seed. Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for studies on plant adaptation to extreme climates.
