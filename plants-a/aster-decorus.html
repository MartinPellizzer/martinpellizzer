Aster decorus

Aster decorus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. Aster decorus thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
