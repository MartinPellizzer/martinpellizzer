Aconitum callibotryon

Aconitum callibotryon, commonly known as the European aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, conical clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed foliage that is often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Aconitum callibotryon is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
