Acanthocereus haackeanus

Acanthocereus haackeanus, commonly known as the night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and is part of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

The plant's unique blooming pattern and striking appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
