Arctostaphylos nummularia subsp. mendocinoensis

Arctostaphylos nummularia subsp.

mendocinoensis, commonly known as Mendocino madrone, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of northern California. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This subspecies is notable for its unique ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to environmental stressors.
