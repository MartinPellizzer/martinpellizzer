Alsine filifolia var. schimperi

Alsine filifolia var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sandspurry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by small, round, edible fruits. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
