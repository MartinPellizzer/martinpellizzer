Asplenium quaylei

Asplenium quaylei, commonly known as Quay's spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Asplenium quaylei is native to parts of Central and South America and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
