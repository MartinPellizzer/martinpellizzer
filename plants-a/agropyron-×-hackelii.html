Agropyron × hackelii

Agropyron × hackelii, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species that combines traits from both Agropyron cristatum and Agropyron elongatum.

It is a perennial plant native to the North American prairie regions, characterized by its tufted growth habit and coarse, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, with each spikelet containing several small, awned lemmas. Agropyron × hackelii is valued for its high forage quality and its potential as a forage crop due to its adaptability to various soil conditions.

It also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
