Absinthium virescens

Absinthium virescens, commonly known as green wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are aromatic and used in traditional herbal medicine.

Absinthium virescens is also noted for its historical use in the production of absinthe, though it is not the primary ingredient in modern versions.
