Alyssum cochlearioides

Alyssum cochlearioides, commonly known as the rock Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Alyssum cochlearioides is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
