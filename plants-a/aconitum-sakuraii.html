Aconitum sakuraii

Aconitum sakuraii, commonly known as Sakurai's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that give it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum sakuraii is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
