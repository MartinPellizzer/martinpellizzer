Aristida riedeliana

Aristida riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's threeawn, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
