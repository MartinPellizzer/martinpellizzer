Abies sibirica

Abies sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the stems. The cones of this species are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, maturing over several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also an important species in the ecological systems of its native range.
