Adenarium peploides

Adenarium peploides, commonly known as the velvet adenium, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its ability to store water in its stems makes it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant is also noted for its slow growth and unique, sometimes spiny, foliage structure.
