Adiantum duale

Adiantum duale, commonly known as the twin fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and finely divided. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Adiantum duale is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
