Aconitum dhwojii

Aconitum dhwojii, commonly known as the Nepal aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with rough, hairy leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a spires and are highly ornamental, though the plant is toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids.

Aconitum dhwojii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
