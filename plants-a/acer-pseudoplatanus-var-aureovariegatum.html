Acer pseudoplatanus var. aureovariegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking leaves that display a mix of yellow, white, and green, creating a variegated pattern. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree.

Its unique foliage makes it a desirable addition to gardens and landscapes.
