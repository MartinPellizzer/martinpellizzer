Arabis sagittata var. lusitanica

Arabis sagittata var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
