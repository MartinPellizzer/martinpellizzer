Arisaema setosum

Arisaema setosum, commonly known as the hairy arisaema, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a showy, hooded spathe. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
