Anthemis rigida subsp. amorgina

Anthemis rigida subsp.

amorgina, commonly known as the Amorgian anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in coastal areas and is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
