Abies nigra

Abies nigra, commonly known as the black fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy needles and conical shape, with bark that becomes furrowed and dark as it matures. The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The black fir is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
