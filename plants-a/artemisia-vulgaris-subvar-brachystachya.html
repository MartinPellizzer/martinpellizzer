Artemisia vulgaris subvar. brachystachya

Artemisia vulgaris subvar. brachystachya, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, often pungent, essential oils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species within the Asteraceae family.
