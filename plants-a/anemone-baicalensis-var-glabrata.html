Anemone baicalensis var. glabrata

Anemone baicalensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the Siberian anemone, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of eastern Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that become glabrous as they mature. Its leaves are palmately divided with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish-brown underside.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and forested areas.
