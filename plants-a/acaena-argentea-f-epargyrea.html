Acaena argentea f. epargyrea

Acaena argentea f. epargyrea, commonly known as silver weed, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its silver-gray foliage.

It features small, spiny fruits that are often used as a food source for birds and small mammals. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of South America. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a common weed in arid and semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rosaceae and is notable for its unique, clustered inflorescences.
