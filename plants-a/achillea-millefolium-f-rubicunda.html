Achillea millefolium f. rubicunda

Achillea millefolium f. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-stemmed yarrow, is a variant of the common yarrow.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
