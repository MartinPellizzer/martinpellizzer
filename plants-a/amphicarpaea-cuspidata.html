Amphicarpaea cuspidata

Amphicarpaea cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to the central and southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces two types of fruits: one above ground and one below ground, a unique adaptation known as amphicarpy. Its root system is capable of fixing nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
