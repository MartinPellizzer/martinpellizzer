Astragalus hamosus var. volubilitanus

Astragalus hamosus var. volubilitanus, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial species characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
