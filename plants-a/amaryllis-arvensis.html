Amaryllis arvensis

Amaryllis arvensis, commonly known as the field amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Amaryllis arvensis is also known for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
