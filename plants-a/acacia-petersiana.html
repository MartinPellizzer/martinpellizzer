Acacia petersiana

Acacia petersiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. It produces long, straight pods that contain seeds used for food and traditional medicine.

Acacia petersiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
