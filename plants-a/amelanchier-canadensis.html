Amelanchier canadensis

Amelanchier canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Amelanchier canadensis is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
