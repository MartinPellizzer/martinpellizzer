Astragalus oxytropifolius

Astragalus oxytropifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and small, efficient leaves. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
