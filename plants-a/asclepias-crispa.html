Asclepias crispa

Asclepias crispa, commonly known as the zigzag milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces follicles that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Asclepias crispa is a member of the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and is known for its milky sap.

It serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly and other milkweed-dependent species.
