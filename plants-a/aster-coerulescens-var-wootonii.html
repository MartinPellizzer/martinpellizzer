Aster coerulescens var. wootonii

Aster coerulescens var. wootonii, commonly known as the Wooton's blue aster, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its value in native plant gardens.
