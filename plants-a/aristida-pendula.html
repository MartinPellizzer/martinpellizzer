Aristida pendula

Aristida pendula, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping panicles that give it a distinctive, weeping appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer.

Aristida pendula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
