Aegilops ovata var. eventricosa

Aegilops ovata var. eventricosa, commonly known as the swollen spikelet wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen spikelets that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters.

It is a perennial plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
