Asparagus fallax

Asparagus fallax, commonly known as the silver asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Asparagus fallax is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
