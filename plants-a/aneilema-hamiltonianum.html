Aneilema hamiltonianum

Aneilema hamiltonianum, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, drooping inflorescences that produce clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly velvety texture.

Aneilema hamiltonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
