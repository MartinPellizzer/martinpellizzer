Aeschynomene nyikensis var. gracilis

Aeschynomene nyikensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender sesban, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its potential in soil improvement and as a forage crop.
