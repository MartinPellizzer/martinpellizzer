Arundinaria viscosa

Arundinaria viscosa, commonly known as bamboo cane or Chinese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Arundinaria viscosa is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
