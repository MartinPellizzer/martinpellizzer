Acanthophyllum xanthoporphyranthum

Acanthophyllum xanthoporphyranthum, commonly known as the yellow porphyranthum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to reddish-pink. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
