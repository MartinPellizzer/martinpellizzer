Arnica japonica

Arnica japonica, commonly known as Japanese arnica, is a perennial herb native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Arnica japonica is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
