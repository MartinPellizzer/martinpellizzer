Asplenium mutilum

Asplenium mutilum, commonly known as the fragmented spleenwort, is a fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered fronds that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant typically grows on rocks or soil in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive pattern of veins.

Asplenium mutilum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
