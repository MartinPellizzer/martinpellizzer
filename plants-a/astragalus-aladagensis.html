Astragalus aladagensis

Astragalus aladagensis, commonly known as the Aladagan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus aladagensis is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
