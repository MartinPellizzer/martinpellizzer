Agropyron × mayebaranum f. villosulum

Agropyron × mayebaranum f. villosulum, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species characterized by its fine, soft foliage.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
