Acaena microphylla subsp. eumicrophylla

Acaena microphylla subsp.

eumicrophylla, commonly known as the small-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length.

The plant produces bright orange to red berries that contain numerous seeds enclosed in a fibrous, burr-like structure. These berries are an important food source for native birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including coastal areas, forest margins, and rocky soils.
