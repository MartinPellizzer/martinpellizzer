Aria

Aria, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by elongated, woody pods. Aria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its fast growth and adaptability to urban environments.
