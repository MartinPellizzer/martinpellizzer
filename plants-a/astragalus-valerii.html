Astragalus valerii

Astragalus valerii, commonly known as Valerian goat's head, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Astragalus valerii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
