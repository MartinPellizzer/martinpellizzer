Acacia enervia subsp. enervia

Acacia enervia subsp.

enervia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
