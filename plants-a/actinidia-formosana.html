Actinidia formosana

Actinidia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan kiwifruit, is a deciduous vine native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to common kiwifruit but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens worldwide.
