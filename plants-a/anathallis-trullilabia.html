Anathallis trullilabia

Anathallis trullilabia, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flower that resembles a small slipper, with a pinkish hue and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers bloom during the summer months, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
