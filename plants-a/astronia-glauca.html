Astronia glauca

Astronia glauca, commonly known as the blue astronia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers humid climates with well-drained soil.
