Amorphophallus calcicola

Amorphophallus calcicola, commonly known as the limestone arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the limestone regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a tall, spathate inflorescence with a single, showy white spathe and a spadix that bears small, greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat specificity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
