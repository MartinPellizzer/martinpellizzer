Angraecum erectocalcaratum

Angraecum erectocalcaratum, commonly known as the erect calyx angraecum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of pale green to white flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically open at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This orchid is adapted to grow in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in areas with high humidity and filtered sunlight.
