Artemisia campestris var. fuscata

Artemisia campestris var. fuscata, commonly known as yellow wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
