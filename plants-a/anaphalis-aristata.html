Anaphalis aristata

Anaphalis aristata, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
