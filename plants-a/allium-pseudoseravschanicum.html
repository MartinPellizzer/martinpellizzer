Allium pseudoseravschanicum

Allium pseudoseravschanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and umbel-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines and have a mild garlic flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native region.
