Artemisia tecti-mundi

Artemisia tecti-mundi, commonly known as the tarragon weed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features gray-green, aromatic leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas.

Its chemical composition includes essential oils, making it useful in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
