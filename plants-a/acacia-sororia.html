Acacia sororia

Acacia sororia, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia sororia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
