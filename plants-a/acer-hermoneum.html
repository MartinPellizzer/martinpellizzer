Acer hermoneum

Acer hermoneum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that change color with the seasons, often displaying vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues in autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, with a spreading crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens and parks.
