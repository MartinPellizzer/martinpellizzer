Aster scaber

Aster scaber, commonly known as rough aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces showy purple or pink flowers in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aster scaber is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
