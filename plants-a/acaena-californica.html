Acaena californica

Acaena californica, commonly known as the California burr bush, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features woody stems covered in dense, spiny bracts that form a prickly, spherical structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its adaptations include hairy leaves and a deep root system, which help it survive in coastal and sandy soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
