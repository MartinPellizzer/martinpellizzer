Aster dumosus var. foliosus

Aster dumosus var. foliosus, commonly known as the dwarf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its flowers bloom in the late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
