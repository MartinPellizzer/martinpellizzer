Agapetes symplocifolia

Agapetes symplocifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that often has a silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.

Agapetes symplocifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
