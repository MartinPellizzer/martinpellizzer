Athyrium × pseudobipinnatum

Athyrium × pseudobipinnatum, commonly known as the false bird's nest fern, is a hybrid fern species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Athyrium filix-femina and Athyrium niponicum.

It is characterized by its intricate, feathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, giving it a delicate and lacy appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both garden and indoor cultivation.
