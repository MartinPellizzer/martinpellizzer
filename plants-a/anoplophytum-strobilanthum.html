Anoplophytum strobilanthum

Anoplophytum strobilanthum, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red inflorescences that appear during the monsoon season. The plant is a shrub or small tree with smooth, greyish bark and alternate, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in local traditions.
