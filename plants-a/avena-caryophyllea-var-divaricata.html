Avena caryophyllea var. divaricata

Avena caryophyllea var. divaricata, commonly known as the purple grass or purple oat grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, violet to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
