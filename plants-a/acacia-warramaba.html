Acacia warramaba

Acacia warramaba, commonly known as the Warramaba acacia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters, characterized by its dense, thorny branches. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Acacia warramaba is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and a capacity to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
