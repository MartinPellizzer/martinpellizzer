Anthurium subaequans

Anthurium subaequans, commonly known as the "Emerald Queen," is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Anthurium subaequans is prized for its ornamental value and air-purifying qualities.
