Aetheopappus concinnus

Aetheopappus concinnus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows as a low, mat-forming shrub with silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aetheopappus concinnus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
