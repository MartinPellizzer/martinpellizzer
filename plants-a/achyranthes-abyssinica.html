Achyranthes abyssinica

Achyranthes abyssinica, commonly known as African knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a common weed in various parts of its native range.
