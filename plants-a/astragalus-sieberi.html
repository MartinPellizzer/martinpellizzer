Astragalus sieberi

Astragalus sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Astragalus sieberi is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a potential source of medicinal compounds.
