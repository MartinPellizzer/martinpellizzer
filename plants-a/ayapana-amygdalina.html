Ayapana amygdalina

Ayapana amygdalina, commonly known as the Brazilian almond or wild almond, is a deciduous shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble almonds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Ayapana amygdalina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
