Astragalus vepres

Astragalus vepres, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus vepres is often found in open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.
