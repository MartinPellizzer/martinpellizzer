Arachis douradiana

Arachis douradiana, commonly known as the wild peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It belongs to the genus *Arachis* and is closely related to the domesticated peanut (*Arachis hypogaea*). The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruits are pods containing one or two seeds, which are often used for propagation.

This species is notable for its potential agricultural and nutritional value.
