Acacia excelsa subsp. excelsa

Acacia excelsa subsp.

excelsa, commonly known as the tall golden wattle, is a striking tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its tall, slender form and golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous narrow, linear leaflets. It produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and ecologically important plant.
