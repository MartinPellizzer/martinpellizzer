Albuca longifolia

Albuca longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Albuca longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
