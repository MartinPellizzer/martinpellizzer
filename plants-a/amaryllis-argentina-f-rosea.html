Amaryllis argentina f. rosea

Amaryllis argentina f. rosea, commonly known as the Rosea Amaryllis, is a hybrid cultivar of the amaryllis genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
