Adenia lobata

Adenia lobata, commonly known as the mountain glorybower, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, woody stems and alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, often appearing in late winter to early spring.

Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, including rocky and sandy soils.
