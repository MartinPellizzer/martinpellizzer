Astragalus rhizocephalus subsp. hindukushensis

Astragalus rhizocephalus subsp.

hindukushensis, commonly known as the Hindukush milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct geographical and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
