Acanthophoenix grandis

Acanthophoenix grandis, commonly known as the giant date palm, is a striking palm native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible dates, which are a significant agricultural product in its native region. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and economic importance, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
