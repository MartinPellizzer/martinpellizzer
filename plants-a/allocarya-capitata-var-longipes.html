Allocarya capitata var. longipes

Allocarya capitata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The inflorescence consists of a single, elongated raceme bearing yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.
