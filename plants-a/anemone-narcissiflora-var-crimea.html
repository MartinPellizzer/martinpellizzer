Anemone narcissiflora var. crimea

Anemone narcissiflora var. crimea, commonly known as the Crimean anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, lobed leaves that are basal and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
