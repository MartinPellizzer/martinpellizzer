Adesmia dessaueri

Adesmia dessaueri, commonly known as the desert bean, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate. Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which help protect them from harsh environmental conditions.

Adesmia dessaueri plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
