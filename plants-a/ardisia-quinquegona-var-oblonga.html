Ardisia quinquegona var. oblonga

Ardisia quinquegona var. oblonga, commonly known as the oblong-leaf beautyberry, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Following flowering, it produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
