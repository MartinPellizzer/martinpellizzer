Andropogon purpureosericeus var. calomelas

Andropogon purpureosericeus var. calomelas, commonly known as the silky bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. This variety thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it forms dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are typically purple-tinged, adding ornamental value to the plant.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
