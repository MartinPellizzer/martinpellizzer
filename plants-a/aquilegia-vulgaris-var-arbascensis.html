Aquilegia vulgaris var. arbascensis

Aquilegia vulgaris var. arbascensis, commonly known as the Alpine columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in mountainous habitats.
