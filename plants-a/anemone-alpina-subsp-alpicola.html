Anemone alpina subsp. alpicola

Anemone alpina subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five to seven petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its distinctive floral structure and often toxic sap.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
