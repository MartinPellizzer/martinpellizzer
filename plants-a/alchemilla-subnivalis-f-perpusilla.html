Alchemilla subnivalis f. perpusilla

Alchemilla subnivalis f. perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to round, with a velvety underside covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in loose clusters, typical of the genus Alchemilla.

This variety is particularly adapted to cold, high-altitude environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
