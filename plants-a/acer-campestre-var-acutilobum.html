Acer campestre var. acutilobum

Acer campestre var. acutilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red or orange-red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
