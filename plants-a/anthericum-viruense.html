Anthericum viruense

Anthericum viruense, commonly known as the Viruense onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Anthericum viruense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
