Achyranthes fruticulosa

Achyranthes fruticulosa, commonly known as the red root or Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Morphology

Achyranthes fruticulosa has compound leaves that are alternate in arrangement, with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are usually 2–5 cm long and 1–2 cm wide, having a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The stem is cylindrical, with a rough, greyish-brown bark that is slightly furrowed, and it grows upright with some branching. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, blooming from June to September, and are wind-pollinated. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping to stabilize the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.
