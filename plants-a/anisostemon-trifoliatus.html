Anisostemon trifoliatus

Anisostemon trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade.

Anisostemon trifoliatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
