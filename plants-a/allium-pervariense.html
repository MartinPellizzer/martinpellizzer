Allium pervariense

Allium pervariense, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves and clusters of pale purple or white flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful for culinary purposes.
