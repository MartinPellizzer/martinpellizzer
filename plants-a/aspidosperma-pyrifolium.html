Aspidosperma pyrifolium

Aspidosperma pyrifolium, commonly known as the yellow wood or African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, simple leaves that are alternate and elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

The plant also contains alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
