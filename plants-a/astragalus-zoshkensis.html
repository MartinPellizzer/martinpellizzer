Astragalus zoshkensis

Astragalus zoshkensis, commonly known as the Zoshkenskaya milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climates. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus zoshkensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
