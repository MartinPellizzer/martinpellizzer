Alsophila negrosiana

Alsophila negrosiana, commonly known as the Negros tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while its fronds are arranged in a crown at the top.

Alsophila negrosiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
