Astragalus divandarrehensis

Astragalus divandarrehensis, commonly known as the Divandarreh milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native habitat.
