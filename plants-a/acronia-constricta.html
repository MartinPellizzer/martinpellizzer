Acronia constricta

Acronia constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
