Achillea mucronata

Achillea mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Achillea mucronata is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
