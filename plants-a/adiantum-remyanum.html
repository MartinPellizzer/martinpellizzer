Adiantum remyanum

Adiantum remyanum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that resemble the shape of a heart or a sickle. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Adiantum remyanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
