Augusta parvifolia

Augusta parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved Augusta, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Augusta parvifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
