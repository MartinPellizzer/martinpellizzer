Acacia hereroensis

Acacia hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, thread-like segments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, often appearing in late winter or early spring. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia hereroensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of savanna ecosystems.
