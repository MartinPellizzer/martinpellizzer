Acacia oligophleba

Acacia oligophleba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to ecological balance.

Acacia oligophleba is also valued for its hardy nature and use in revegetation projects.
