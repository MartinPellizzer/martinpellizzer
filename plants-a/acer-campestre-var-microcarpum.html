Acer campestre var. microcarpum

Acer campestre var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a low-growing variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces tiny, red or orange fruits that are clustered in umbels, distinguishing it from other maple species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
