Achillea nana

Achillea nana, commonly known as the dwarf yarrow, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Achillea nana is also valued for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat wounds and digestive issues.
