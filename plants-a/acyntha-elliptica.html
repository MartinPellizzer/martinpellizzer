Acyntha elliptica

Acyntha elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acynthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have a violet or blue color.

Acyntha elliptica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
