Astragalus purpurascens

Astragalus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus purpurascens is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
