Acer campestre f. tauricum

Acer campestre f. tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric hedge maple, is a compact shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is a variant of the common hedge maple, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, ovate leaves with coarsely toothed margins. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter beneath, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
