Astragalus clusii

Astragalus clusii, commonly known as the Clusii milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod, typical of the legume family.

Astragalus clusii is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
