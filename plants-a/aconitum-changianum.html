Aconitum changianum

Aconitum changianum, commonly known as the Chinese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, hood-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Aconitum changianum is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
