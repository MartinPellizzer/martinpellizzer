Allium canadense var. fraseri

Allium canadense var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to North America, characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

This variety is closely related to other Allium species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
