Ambrosia fruticosa var. canescens

Ambrosia fruticosa var. canescens, commonly known as sand ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub-like species with grayish-green foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and dune environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

The plant is also recognized for its role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing erosion.
