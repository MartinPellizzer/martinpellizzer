Alchornea subrotunda

Alchornea subrotunda, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's structure and adaptations reflect its ability to survive in humid, aquatic environments.
