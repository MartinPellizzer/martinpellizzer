Astragalus pendulinus

Astragalus pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Astragalus pendulinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
