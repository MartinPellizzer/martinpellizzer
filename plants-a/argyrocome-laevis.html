Argyrocome laevis

Argyrocome laevis, commonly known as the smooth silverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Argyrocome laevis is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
