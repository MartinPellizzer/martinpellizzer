Allium lopadusanum

Allium lopadusanum, commonly known as the bulbous leek, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces large, bulbous underground stems that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer.
