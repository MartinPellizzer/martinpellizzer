Aster sodiroi var. mutisianus

Aster sodiroi var. mutisianus, commonly known as the Mutisia aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
