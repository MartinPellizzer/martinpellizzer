Astragalus panjaoensis

Astragalus panjaoensis, commonly known as the Panjao milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus panjaoensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
