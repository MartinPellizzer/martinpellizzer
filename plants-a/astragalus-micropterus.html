Astragalus micropterus

Astragalus micropterus, commonly known as the small-winged milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus micropterus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
