Ammannia multiflora var. parviflora

Ammannia multiflora var. parviflora, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface and red stems that are often tinged with purple. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
