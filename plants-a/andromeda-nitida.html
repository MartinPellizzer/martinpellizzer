Andromeda nitida

Andromeda nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf andromeda, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Andromeda nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
