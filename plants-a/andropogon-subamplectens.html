Andropogon subamplectens

Andropogon subamplectens, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its flowering stems are often more robust.

Silver bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
