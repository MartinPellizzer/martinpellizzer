Amomum macrodons

Amomum macrodons, commonly known as the large cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its rhizomes and roots are the primary parts used, valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Amomum macrodons is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and traditional medicine applications.
