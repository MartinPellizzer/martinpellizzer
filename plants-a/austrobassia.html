Austrobassia

Austrobassia, commonly known as the Australian bottlebrush, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with narrow, needle-like leaves. Austrobassia species are often found in coastal and heathland environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

They play an important role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for native pollinators.
