Alstroemeria lactilutea

Alstroemeria lactilutea, commonly known as the yellow alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and grows in a clumping habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Alstroemeria lactilutea is also known for its long-lasting blooms and is cultivated for ornamental purposes.
