Amelanchier arborea var. austromontana

Amelanchier arborea var. austromontana, commonly known as the Australian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark blue berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest margins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
