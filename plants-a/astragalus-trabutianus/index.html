Astragalus Trabutianus

Astragalus trabutianus is a rare and distinctive plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family, known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. This species is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and deeply lobed leaves. Due to its limited habitat and threats from human activity, it is considered endangered. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical species.
