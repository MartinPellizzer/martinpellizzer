Amaryllis parodii

Amaryllis parodii, commonly known as the red amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and can grow up to 1 meter in length.

Amaryllis parodii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
