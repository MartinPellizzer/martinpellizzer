Acacia praecox f. inermis

Acacia praecox f. inermis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its flowers are often visited by various pollinators, including bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
