Abildgaardia pachyptera

Abildgaardia pachyptera, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean and temperate climates.
