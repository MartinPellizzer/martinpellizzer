Agave coetocapnia subsp. pueblensis

Agave coetocapnia subsp.

pueblensis, commonly known as the Puebla agave, is a species native to Mexico. It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardiness.
