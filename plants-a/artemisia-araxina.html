Artemisia araxina

Artemisia araxina, commonly known as Armenian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in flat-topped inflorescences, which bloom in late summer.

Artemisia araxina is known for its strong, camphor-like scent and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
