Asparagus pachyrrhizus

Asparagus pachyrrhizus, commonly known as the thick-root asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy roots that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, needle-like spears. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its culinary value.

Asparagus pachyrrhizus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
