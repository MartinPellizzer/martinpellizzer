Aristida adscensionis var. coerulescens

Aristida adscensionis var. coerulescens, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
