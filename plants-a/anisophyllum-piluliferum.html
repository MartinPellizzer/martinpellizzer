Anisophyllum piluliferum

Anisophyllum piluliferum, commonly known as the pill-berry plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of tiny, red berries that resemble tiny pills, hence its common name. The plant is typically found in acidic, well-drained soils in forested areas. It produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months.

Anisophyllum piluliferum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
