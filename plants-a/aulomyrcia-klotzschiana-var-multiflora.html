Aulomyrcia klotzschiana var. multiflora

Aulomyrcia klotzschiana var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora aulomyrcia, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
