Abutilon dispermum

Abutilon dispermum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, narrow pods containing two seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
