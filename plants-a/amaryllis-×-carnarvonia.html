Amaryllis × carnarvonia

Amaryllis × carnarvonia, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically produces large, fleshy leaves that are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This hybrid is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
