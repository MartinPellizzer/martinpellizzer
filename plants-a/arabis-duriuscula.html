Arabis duriuscula

Arabis duriuscula, commonly known as the rough rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Arabis duriuscula is often found in meadows and on slopes at high elevations.
