Andropogon laxatus

Andropogon laxatus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a native North American grass species that thrives in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and loose, feathery panicles that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant is well adapted to prairie ecosystems and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits. Switchgrass is also a valuable source of biomass for renewable energy production.

Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil structure.
