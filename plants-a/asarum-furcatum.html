Asarum furcatum

Asarum furcatum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaf that is often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spicate inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The rhizomatous root system is thick and fleshy, contributing to its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
