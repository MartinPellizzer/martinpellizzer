Anemone rupestris var. spatulata

Anemone rupestris var. spatulata, commonly known as the spoon-leaved anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in the southwestern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broad and spoon-shaped, with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are typically clustered in a single umbel. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and withstand drought conditions.

The leaves and flowers are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations.
