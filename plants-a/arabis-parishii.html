Arabis parishii

Arabis parishii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to thrive in harsh, alpine environments.
