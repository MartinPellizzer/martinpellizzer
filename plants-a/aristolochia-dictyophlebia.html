Aristolochia dictyophlebia

Aristolochia dictyophlebia, commonly known as the lace-veined Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves with intricate venation patterns. The plant produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
