Atractylis kentrophylloides

Atractylis kentrophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish or grayish hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The species is known for its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.
