Aridarum caulescens var. angustifolium

Aridarum caulescens var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed desert stalk, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is well adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

This variant is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
