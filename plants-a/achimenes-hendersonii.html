Achimenes hendersonii

Achimenes hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's stormy bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces showy bracts that often resemble the actual flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Achimenes hendersonii is often cultivated as a bedding plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
