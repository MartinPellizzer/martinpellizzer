Astragalus sciureus

Astragalus sciureus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus sciureus is often found in prairies, sagebrush steppe, and along dry streambeds.
