Arisaema translucens

Arisaema translucens, commonly known as the translucent arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like bracts that enclose the spadix, a central flowering structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense cluster on the spadix. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
