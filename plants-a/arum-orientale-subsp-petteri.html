Arum orientale subsp. petteri

Arum orientale subsp.

petteri, commonly known as the Persian arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and sometimes red, adding visual interest to gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique foliage and floral structure.
