Aster myrtifolius

Aster myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a subtle myrtle scent, giving it its common name. It produces showy blue to purple flowers in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster myrtifolius is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
