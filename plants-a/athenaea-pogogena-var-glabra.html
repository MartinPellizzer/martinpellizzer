Athenaea pogogena var. glabra

Athenaea pogogena var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered athenaea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its smooth, glabrous leaves and erect stems make it easily distinguishable from other similar species.

It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
