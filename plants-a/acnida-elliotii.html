Acnida elliotii

Acnida elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family.

Acnida elliotii is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
