Arisaema roxburghii

Arisaema roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian cobra plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with white and green patterns. Its flowers are typically yellow or green, and it produces berries that turn red when mature.

Arisaema roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
