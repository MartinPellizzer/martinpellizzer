Acaciella carbonaria

Acaciella carbonaria, commonly known as the black acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody structure and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it resilient in harsh environments.

Acaciella carbonaria is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
