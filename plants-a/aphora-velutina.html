Aphora velutina

Aphora velutina, commonly known as the velvet aphora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Aphora velutina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
