Austrocedrus chilensis

Austrocedrus chilensis, commonly known as the Patagonian cypress, is a coniferous tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and narrow, pyramidal crown. The tree has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, and it produces small, spherical cones. It is adapted to grow in dry, mountainous environments and is often found in the Andes.

Austrocedrus chilensis is valued for its durable wood and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
