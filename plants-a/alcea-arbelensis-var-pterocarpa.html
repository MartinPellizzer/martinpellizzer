Alcea arbelensis var. pterocarpa

Alcea arbelensis var. pterocarpa, commonly known as the Jerusalem bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
