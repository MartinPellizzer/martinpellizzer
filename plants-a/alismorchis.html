Alismorchis

Alismorchis, commonly known as the water plantain, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Alismarceae.

These plants are typically aquatic or semi-aquatic, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. They have long, narrow leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with small white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The roots of Alismorchis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

These plants play an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
