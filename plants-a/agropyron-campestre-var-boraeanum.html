Agropyron campestre var. boraeanum

Agropyron campestre var. boraeanum, commonly known as the Boreal wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to cold climates of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and tundra. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation across harsh environments.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
