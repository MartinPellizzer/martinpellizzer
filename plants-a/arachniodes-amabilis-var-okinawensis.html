Arachniodes amabilis var. okinawensis

Arachniodes amabilis var. okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa spider fern, is a species native to Japan, particularly the Ryukyu Islands.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with intricate, spiderweb-like patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are bi-lobed and can reach lengths of up to 50 centimeters, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
