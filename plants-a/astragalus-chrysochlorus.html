Astragalus chrysochlorus

Astragalus chrysochlorus, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus chrysochlorus is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
