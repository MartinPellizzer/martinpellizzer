Acacia falciformis var. propinqua

Acacia falciformis var. propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping phyllodes that are typically 5–15 cm long and 1–2 cm wide. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
