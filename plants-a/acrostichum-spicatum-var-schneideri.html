Acrostichum spicatum var. schneideri

Acrostichum spicatum var. schneideri, commonly known as the Schneider's fern, is a variant of the common bracken fern.

It is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia. The plant features large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its ability to spread and form dense colonies.
