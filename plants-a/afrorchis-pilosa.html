Afrorchis pilosa

Afrorchis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grass-like foliage and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a spurred lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

The botanical features of Afrorchis pilosa make it a notable species in the Orchidaceae family.
