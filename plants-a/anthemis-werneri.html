Anthemis werneri

Anthemis werneri, commonly known as the Werneri's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its yellow-orange flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
