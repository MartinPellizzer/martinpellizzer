Aster kayserianus

Aster kayserianus, commonly known as the Kashmir aster, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Aster kayserianus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
