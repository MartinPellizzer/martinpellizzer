Astragalus thaumatothamnus

Astragalus thaumatothamnus, commonly known as the fairy thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny, thistle-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. Astragalus thaumatothamnus is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
