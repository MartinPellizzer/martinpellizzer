Athamanta hungarica

Athamanta hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Athamanta hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
