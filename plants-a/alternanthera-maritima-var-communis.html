Alternanthera maritima var. communis

Alternanthera maritima var. communis, commonly known as salt marsh fleabane, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a slightly succulent texture, adapted to withstand salty and windy conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local ecosystems.
