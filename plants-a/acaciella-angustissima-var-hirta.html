Acaciella angustissima var. hirta

Acaciella angustissima var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy acaciella, is a small, shrub-like plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions. Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil, and it serves as a habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
