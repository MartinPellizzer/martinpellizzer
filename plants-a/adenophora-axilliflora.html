Adenophora axilliflora

Adenophora axilliflora, commonly known as the axillary bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and showy inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Adenophora axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
