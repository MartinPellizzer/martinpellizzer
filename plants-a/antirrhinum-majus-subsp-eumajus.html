Antirrhinum majus subsp. eumajus

Antirrhinum majus subsp.

eumajus, commonly known as the red-flowered snapdragon, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are typically red or orange, with two lips, and are bilaterally symmetrical, a characteristic feature of the Scrophulariaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a serrated margin.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
