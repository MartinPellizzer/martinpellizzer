Aegilops sharonensis

Aegilops sharonensis, commonly known as Sharon grass, is a wild relative of wheat native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets. It has been studied for its potential in wheat breeding programs due to its genetic diversity and useful traits.

Aegilops sharonensis thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Aegilops sharonensis has leaves that are narrow and grass-like, with a linear shape, alternate arrangement, and a bluish-green color.
