Agropyron rigidum var. glabrifolium

Agropyron rigidum var. glabrifolium, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically hairless, giving it the specific epithet *glabrifolium*, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, nutrient-poor environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
