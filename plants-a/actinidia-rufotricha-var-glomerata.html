Actinidia rufotricha var. glomerata

Actinidia rufotricha var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered hairy actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, fuzzy berries.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential culinary uses.
