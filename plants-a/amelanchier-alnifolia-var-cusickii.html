Amelanchier alnifolia var. cusickii

Amelanchier alnifolia var. cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
