Achillea tomentosa

Achillea tomentosa, commonly known as white yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as meadows and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, medicinal properties.

Its common name is derived from the Greek myth of Achilles, who was said to have used the plant to treat wounds.
