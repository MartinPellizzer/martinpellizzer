Arabidopsis kamchatica subsp.

kawasakiana, commonly known as the Sakhalin Arabidopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and northern Japan. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops like cabbage and mustard.

This subspecies is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and a growth habit that is typically rosette-like. It is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to temperate climates.
