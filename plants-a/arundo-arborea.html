Arundo arborea

Arundo arborea, commonly known as the desert reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in environments with limited water availability.

Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an important species for erosion control and habitat provision.
