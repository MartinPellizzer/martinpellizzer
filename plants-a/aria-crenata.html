Aria crenata

Aria crenata, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Aria crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
