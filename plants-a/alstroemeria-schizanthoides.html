Alstroemeria schizanthoides

Alstroemeria schizanthoides, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a range of colors including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and typically grows in a clumping form.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
