Amperea spicata

Amperea spicata, commonly known as the clustered ampere, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Amperea spicata produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators.
