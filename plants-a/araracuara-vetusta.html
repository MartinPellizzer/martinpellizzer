Araracuara vetusta

Araracuara vetusta, commonly known as the ancient araracuara, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the dense, humid forests of the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered.
