Acacia incurvaneura

Acacia incurvaneura, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often curved thorns. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, rounded leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Acacia incurvaneura is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
