Asplenium herpetopteris

Asplenium herpetopteris, commonly known as the snake fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, leathery leaves that are often divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, with the leaves curling tightly at the base.

Asplenium herpetopteris is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in temperate to tropical regions.
