Acacia dentifera

Acacia dentifera, commonly known as the serrated acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers that appear in dense, globular clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia dentifera is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
