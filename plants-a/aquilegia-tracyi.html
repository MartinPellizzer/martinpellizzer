Aquilegia tracyi

Aquilegia tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's columbine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant flower colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Tracy's columbine is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
