Anemia oblanceolata

Anemia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead shape, typically ranging from 2 to 6 inches in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
