Alchornea grandiflora var. lehmannii

Alchornea grandiflora var. lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinct yellowish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
