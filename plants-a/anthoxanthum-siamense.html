Anthoxanthum siamense

Anthoxanthum siamense, commonly known as Siam oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Anthoxanthum siamense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions.
