Auricula crenata

Auricula crenata, commonly known as the wrinkled auricula, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
