Alternanthera linearis

Alternanthera linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that often display vibrant red, purple, or pink hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is popular in aquatic and wetland environments due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
