Ageratina vacciniifolia

Ageratina vacciniifolia, commonly known as the pineapple weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun. Its foliage has a slightly aromatic scent, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Ageratina vacciniifolia is also a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
