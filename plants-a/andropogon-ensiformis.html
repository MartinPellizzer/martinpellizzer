Andropogon ensiformis

Andropogon ensiformis, commonly known as tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often folded in a V-shape. This plant thrives in full sun and is commonly found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon ensiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
