Allomorphia procursa

Allomorphia procursa, commonly known as the creeping allomorph, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal spikes.

Allomorphia procursa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
