Alsine macrocarpa var. jooi

Alsine macrocarpa var. jooi, commonly known as the large-seeded alsine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed capsules that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
