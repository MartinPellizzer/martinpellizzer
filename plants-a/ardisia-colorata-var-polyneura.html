Ardisia colorata var. polyneura

Ardisia colorata var. polyneura, commonly known as the variegated beautyberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white veins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright purple berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and ornamental berries make it a desirable addition to gardens and landscapes.
