Artocarpus tamaran

Artocarpus tamaran, commonly known as the tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit, which is encased in a rough, woody husk. The fruit contains a sweet, sticky pulp surrounded by astringent seeds. The tree is often cultivated for its edible fruit and its timber, which is valued for its durability.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
