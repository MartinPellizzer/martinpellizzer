Astranthium integrifolium var. ciliatum

Astranthium integrifolium var. ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy astranthium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features showy, pink to purple ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have soft, hairy margins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
