Asplenium crinicaule

Asplenium crinicaule, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, trailing rhizomes that produce clusters of small, dark green, needle-like leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its unique appearance resembles a tuft of horse's tail, hence its common name.

Asplenium crinicaule is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
