Alsophila palembanica

Alsophila palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, and it plays a role in the ecological structure of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens around the world.
