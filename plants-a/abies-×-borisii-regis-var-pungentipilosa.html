Abies × borisii-regis var. pungentipilosa

Abies × borisii-regis var. pungentipilosa, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a hybrid conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. The cones are typically erect and bear small, spiky scales. This variety is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
