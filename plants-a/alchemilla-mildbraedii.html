Alchemilla mildbraedii

Alchemilla mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
