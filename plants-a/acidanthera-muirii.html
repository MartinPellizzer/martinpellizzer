Acidanthera muirii

Acidanthera muirii, commonly known as the Muir's orchid, is a striking South African orchid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions, and requires full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip, a feature common in many orchid species, which aids in pollination by specific insects.

Acidanthera muirii is a relatively rare and localized species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
