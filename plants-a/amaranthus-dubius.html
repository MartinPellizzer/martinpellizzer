Amaranthus dubius

Amaranthus dubius, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into three-angled, spiny seed capsules. Its seeds are small and can be a valuable source of protein and nutrients.

Amaranthus dubius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown as a weed in agricultural fields.
