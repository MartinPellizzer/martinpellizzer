Amomum somniculosum

Amomum somniculosum, commonly known as the sleepy ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing a distinctive, spicy aroma.

The rhizomes of Amomum somniculosum are used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.
