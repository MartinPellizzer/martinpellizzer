Aegiphila alba

Aegiphila alba, commonly known as white aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features aromatic, glandular leaves and produces small, pale purple to white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Aegiphila alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
