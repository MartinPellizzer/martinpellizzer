Antirrhinum majus var. hendersonii

Antirrhinum majus var. hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's snapdragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically bilaterally symmetrical, with two lips that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
