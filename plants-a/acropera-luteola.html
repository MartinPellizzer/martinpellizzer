Acropera luteola

Acropera luteola, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Acropera luteola is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
