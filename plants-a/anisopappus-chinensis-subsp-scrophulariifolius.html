Anisopappus chinensis subsp. scrophulariifolius

Anisopappus chinensis subsp.

scrophulariifolius, commonly known as the Chinese bitterweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that are arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
