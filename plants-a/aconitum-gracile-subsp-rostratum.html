Aconitum gracile subsp. rostratum

Aconitum gracile subsp.

rostratum, commonly known as the slender aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, terminal racemes. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in moist, well-drained soils.
