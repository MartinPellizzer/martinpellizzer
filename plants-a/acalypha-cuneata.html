Acalypha cuneata

Acalypha cuneata, commonly known as the arrowleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-yellow and are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules.

Acalypha cuneata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
