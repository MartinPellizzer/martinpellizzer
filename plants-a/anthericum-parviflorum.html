Anthericum parviflorum

Anthericum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a striking inflorescence composed of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters on the spadix. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
