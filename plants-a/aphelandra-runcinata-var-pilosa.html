Aphelandra runcinata var. pilosa

Aphelandra runcinata var. pilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
