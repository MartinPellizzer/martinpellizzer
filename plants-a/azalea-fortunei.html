Azalea fortunei

Azalea fortunei, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, elliptical leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce abundant flowers in spring.
