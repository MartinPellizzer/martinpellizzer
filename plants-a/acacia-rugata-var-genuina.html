Acacia rugata var. genuina

Acacia rugata var. genuina, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
