Aristolochia nashii

Aristolochia nashii, commonly known as Nash's birthwort, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and a striking, vine-like growth habit. Its flowers are distinctive, with a complex, pipe-like structure that resembles a helmet or a tube, often with a purple or greenish hue.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to thrive.
