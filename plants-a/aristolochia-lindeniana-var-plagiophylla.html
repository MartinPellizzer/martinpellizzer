Aristolochia lindeniana var. plagiophylla

Aristolochia lindeniana var. plagiophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed Dutchman's pipe, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture. This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using tendrils.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
