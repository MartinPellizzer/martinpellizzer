Acalypha deltoidea

Acalypha deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid ironleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its deltoid-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Acalypha deltoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
