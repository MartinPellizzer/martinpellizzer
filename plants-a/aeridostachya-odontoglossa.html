Aeridostachya odontoglossa

Aeridostachya odontoglossa, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
