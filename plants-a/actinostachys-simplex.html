Actinostachys simplex

Actinostachys simplex, commonly known as the narrowleaf basket grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it well-suited for use in ornamental landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
