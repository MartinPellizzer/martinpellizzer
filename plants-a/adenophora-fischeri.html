Adenophora fischeri

Adenophora fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Adenophora fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
