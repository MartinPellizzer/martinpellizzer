Astragalus kisylkumi

Astragalus kisylkumi, commonly known as the Kyzylkum milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Astragalus kisylkumi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
