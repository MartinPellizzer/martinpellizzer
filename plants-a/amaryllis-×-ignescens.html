Amaryllis × ignescens

Amaryllis × ignescens, commonly known as the fire lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
