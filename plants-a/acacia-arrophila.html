Acacia arrophila

Acacia arrophila, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Acacia arrophila plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
