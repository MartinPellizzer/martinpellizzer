Artanthe lanceata

Artanthe lanceata, commonly known as the lanceleaf artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Artanthe lanceata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
