Ampelocissus salmonea

Ampelocissus salmonea, commonly known as the golden fig vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant's leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Ampelocissus salmonea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in landscaping or as a climbing plant.
