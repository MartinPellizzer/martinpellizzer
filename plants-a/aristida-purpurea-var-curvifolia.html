Aristida purpurea var. curvifolia

Aristida purpurea var. curvifolia, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a distinctive purple tinge along the edges.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
