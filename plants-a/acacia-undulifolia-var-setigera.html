Acacia undulifolia var. setigera

Acacia undulifolia var. setigera, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in coastal stabilization and landscaping.
