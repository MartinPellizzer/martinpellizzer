Adenocarpus complicatus var. commutatus

Adenocarpus complicatus var. commutatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds used for various traditional purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a forage plant.
