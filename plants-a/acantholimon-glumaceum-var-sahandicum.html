Acantholimon glumaceum var. sahandicum

Acantholimon glumaceum var. sahandicum, commonly known as the Sahand sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Iran.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, adapting to the arid and rocky environments it inhabits.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
