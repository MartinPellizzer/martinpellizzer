Arachis prostrata subf. brevicalyx

Arachis prostrata subf.

brevicalyx, commonly known as the short-leaf groundnut, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing one or two seeds. It is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in warm, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
