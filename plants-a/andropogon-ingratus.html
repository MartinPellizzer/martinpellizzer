Andropogon ingratus

Andropogon ingratus, commonly known as bush bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, bluish-green leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Andropogon ingratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wildlife habitat.
