Aloe reynoldsii

Aloe reynoldsii, commonly known as Reynolds' aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, and they may have small teeth or spines along their edges. It produces yellowish-green flowers on tall, branched stems during the warmer months.

This plant is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often cultivated as an ornamental species.
