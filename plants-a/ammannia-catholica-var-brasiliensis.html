Ammannia catholica var. brasiliensis

Ammannia catholica var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ammannia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright red or pink flowers in dense inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
