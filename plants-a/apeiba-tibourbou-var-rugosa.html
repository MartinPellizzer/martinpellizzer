Apeiba tibourbou var. rugosa

Apeiba tibourbou var. rugosa, commonly known as the rugose apeiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps, giving them a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiky fruits containing edible seeds. Its bark is rich in tannins and has been used traditionally in folk medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
