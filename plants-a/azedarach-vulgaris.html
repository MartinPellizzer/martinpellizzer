Azedarach vulgaris

Azedarach vulgaris, commonly known as the Chinese frangipani, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10 to 20 centimeters long, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers are usually pink or white and are borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain several flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world.
