Arisaema concinnum

Arisaema concinnum, commonly known as the dwarf Indian turnip, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces a single, spadix surrounded by a leaf-like bract. The plant's flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers located on separate parts of the spadix. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
