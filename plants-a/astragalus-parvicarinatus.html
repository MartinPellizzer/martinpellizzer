Astragalus parvicarinatus

Astragalus parvicarinatus, commonly known as the small-ribbed milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant has small, palmately lobed leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus parvicarinatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
