Athamanta cretensis var. tomentosa

Athamanta cretensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Cretan tomentose bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Crete.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and showy blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
