Amelanchier ovalis subsp. vulgaris

Amelanchier ovalis subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America. It produces showy white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
