Aster miser var. diffusus

Aster miser var. diffusus, commonly known as the spreading aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems that often branch near the base, supporting clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
