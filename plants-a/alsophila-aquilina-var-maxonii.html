Alsophila aquilina var. maxonii

Alsophila aquilina var. maxonii, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a towering fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, tree-like trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer called the rhizome. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique structure and slow growth make it a striking and long-lived component of tropical ecosystems.
