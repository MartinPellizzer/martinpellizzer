Acacia karroo

Acacia karroo, commonly known as the white thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. Acacia karroo is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
