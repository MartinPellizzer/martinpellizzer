Archidendron tjendana

Archidendron tjendana, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
