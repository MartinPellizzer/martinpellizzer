Anthurium crassinervium var. caatingae

Anthurium crassinervium var. caatingae, commonly known as the Caatinga anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Brazil, specifically the Caatinga biome.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces vibrant, spatulate-shaped spathes that can range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow or white lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
