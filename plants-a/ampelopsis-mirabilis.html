Ampelopsis mirabilis

Ampelopsis mirabilis, commonly known as the beautiful grapevines, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, woodland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative climbing plant.
