Adiantum lorentzii

Adiantum lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year of growth.

Adiantum lorentzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic qualities.
