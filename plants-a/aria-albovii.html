Aria albovii

Aria albovii, commonly known as the white-flowered aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Aria albovii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
