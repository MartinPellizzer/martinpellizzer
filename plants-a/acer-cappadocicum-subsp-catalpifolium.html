Acer cappadocicum subsp. catalpifolium

Acer cappadocicum subsp.

catalpifolium, commonly known as the Catalpa-leaved maple, is a unique subspecies native to Turkey. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which resemble those of the catalpa tree, featuring broad, heart-shaped lobes with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
