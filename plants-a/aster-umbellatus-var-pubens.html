Aster umbellatus var. pubens

Aster umbellatus var. pubens, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its varietal name "pubens," which means hairy in Latin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
