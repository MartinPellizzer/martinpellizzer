Asparagus acutifolius subsp. aragonensis

Asparagus acutifolius subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and fern-like, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, needle-like berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
