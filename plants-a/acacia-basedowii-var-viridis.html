Acacia basedowii var. viridis

Acacia basedowii var. viridis, commonly known as the green acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, greenish-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
