Aquilegia × helenae

Aquilegia × helenae, commonly known as the Helen's columbine, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and western North America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue, arranged in a distinctive spurred pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring compound leaves with a finely toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
