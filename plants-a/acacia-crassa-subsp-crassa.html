Acacia crassa subsp. crassa

Acacia crassa subsp.

crassa, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its straight, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
