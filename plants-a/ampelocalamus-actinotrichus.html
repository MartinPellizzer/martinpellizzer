Ampelocalamus actinotrichus

Ampelocalamus actinotrichus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a perennial grass native to China.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often hidden among the foliage. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
