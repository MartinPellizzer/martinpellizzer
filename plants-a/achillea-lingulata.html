Achillea lingulata

Achillea lingulata, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and covered in a soft, woolly texture. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and are arranged in flat-topped clusters, typical of the daisy family.

Achillea lingulata is often found in dry meadows, rocky soils, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
