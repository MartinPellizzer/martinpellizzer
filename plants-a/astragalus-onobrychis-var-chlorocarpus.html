Astragalus onobrychis var. chlorocarpus

Astragalus onobrychis var. chlorocarpus, commonly known as yellow bartsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and pea-like, typical of the Fabaceae family, and are arranged in clusters.

The seed pods are slender and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.
