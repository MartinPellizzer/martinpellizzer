Aristida streptachne

Aristida streptachne, commonly known as slender three-awn, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in association with other native grasses.

Botanically, it is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological adaptability.
