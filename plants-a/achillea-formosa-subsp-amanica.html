Achillea formosa subsp. amanica

Achillea formosa subsp.

amanica, commonly known as the showy yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
