Adicea fontana

Adicea fontana, commonly known as the Fontana's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
