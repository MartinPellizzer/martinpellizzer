Aloe horombensis subsp. amboahangyensis

Aloe horombensis subsp.

amboahangyensis, commonly known as the Amboahangyensis aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves that form a dense rosette, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, typical of many aloe species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
