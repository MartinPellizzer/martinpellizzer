Aconitum hispanicum

Aconitum hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, conical clusters. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are hairy and serrated.

Aconitum hispanicum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
