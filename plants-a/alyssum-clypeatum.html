Alyssum clypeatum

Alyssum clypeatum, commonly known as the flatleaf alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dense, silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum clypeatum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
