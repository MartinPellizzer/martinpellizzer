Aristida adscendens

Aristida adscendens, commonly known as the ascending threeawn, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Aristida adscendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
