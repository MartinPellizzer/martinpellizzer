Asperula glomerata subsp. bracteata

Asperula glomerata subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate woodruff, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive bracts that surround the flower clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
