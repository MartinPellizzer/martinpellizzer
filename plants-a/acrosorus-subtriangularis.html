Acrosorus subtriangularis

Acrosorus subtriangularis, commonly known as the triangular fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped fronds. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces erect, lanceolate leaves with a serrated margin. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Acrosorus subtriangularis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
