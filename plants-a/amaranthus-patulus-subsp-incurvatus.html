Amaranthus patulus subsp. incurvatus

Amaranthus patulus subsp.

incurvatus, commonly known as the curlleaf amaranth, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and lanceolate leaves that often curve inward.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically followed by triangular, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
