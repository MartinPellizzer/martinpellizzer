Adenopodia minutiflora

Adenopodia minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered adenopodia, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Adenopodia minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
