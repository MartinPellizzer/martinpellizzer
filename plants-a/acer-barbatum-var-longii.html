Acer barbatum var. longii

Acer barbatum var. longii, commonly known as the long-leaf maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, serrated leaves that are often tinged with red or orange during the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
