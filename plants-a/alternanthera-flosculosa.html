Alternanthera flosculosa

Alternanthera flosculosa, commonly known as the clustered Alternanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
