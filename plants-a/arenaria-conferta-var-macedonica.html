Arenaria conferta var. macedonica

Arenaria conferta var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid or rocky habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
