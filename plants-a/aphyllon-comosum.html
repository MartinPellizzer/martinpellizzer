Aphyllon comosum

Aphyllon comosum, commonly known as the woolly burr, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its spherical, spiky seed heads, which are covered in bristly hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Aphyllon comosum is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance.
