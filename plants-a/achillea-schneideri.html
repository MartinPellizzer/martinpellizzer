Achillea schneideri

Achillea schneideri, commonly known as Schneider's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are small, clustered in flat-topped umbels, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Achillea schneideri is often used in herbal medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
