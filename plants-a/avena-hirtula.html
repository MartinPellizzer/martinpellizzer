Avena hirtula

Avena hirtula, commonly known as hairy oat, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. Avena hirtula is often found in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
