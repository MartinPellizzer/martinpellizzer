Anthospermum aethiopicum var. ternifolium

Anthospermum aethiopicum var. ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf anthospermum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, trifoliate leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
