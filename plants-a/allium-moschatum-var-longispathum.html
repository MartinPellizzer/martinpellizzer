Allium moschatum var. longispathum

Allium moschatum var. longispathum, commonly known as the long-spurred leek, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine for their mild, garlic-like flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and culinary significance, it is a valued species in both horticultural and ethnobotanical contexts.
