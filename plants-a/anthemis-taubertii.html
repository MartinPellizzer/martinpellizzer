Anthemis taubertii

Anthemis taubertii, commonly known as the Taubert's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads. Its flowers typically bloom in the summer months and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild aromatic properties.
