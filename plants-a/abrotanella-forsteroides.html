Abrotanella forsteroides

Abrotanella forsteroides, commonly known as the Forster's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with soft hairs, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
