Abacopteris repanda

Abacopteris repanda, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a distinctive evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a thick, fibrous trunk that stores water and nutrients. Its fronds are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique feature that aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
