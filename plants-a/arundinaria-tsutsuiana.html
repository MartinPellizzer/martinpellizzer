Arundinaria tsutsuiana

Arundinaria tsutsuiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Tsutsuian bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, forming a compact and upright structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
