Anisomeris longiflora

Anisomeris longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered anisomeris, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
