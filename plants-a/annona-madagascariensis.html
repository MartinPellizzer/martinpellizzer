Annona madagascariensis

Annona madagascariensis, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a large, round fruit with a thick, yellow rind and a soft, creamy pulp that is edible. The fruit is rich in nutrients and is often used in traditional cuisines across Africa and the Caribbean.

It also has medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
