Achyranthes novae-brasilianae

Achyranthes novae-brasilianae, commonly known as Brazilian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and potential as an invasive species in certain regions.
