Aster sanczirii

Aster sanczirii, commonly known as the Armenian aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Aster sanczirii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
