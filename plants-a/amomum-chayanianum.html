Amomum chayanianum

Amomum chayanianum, commonly known as the Thai cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spindle-shaped, reddish-brown fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its culinary and medicinal uses.
