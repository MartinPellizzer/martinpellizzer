Adiantum pedatum var. kamtschaticum

Adiantum pedatum var. kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschaticum maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace, with a light green to bronze coloration. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
