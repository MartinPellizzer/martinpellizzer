Astragalus barba-jovis var. candicans

Astragalus barba-jovis var. candicans, commonly known as the shining milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its sparsely hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are typically slender and contain several small, hard seeds.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in stabilizing sandy or gravelly landscapes.
