Arum rupicola var. conophalloides

Arum rupicola var. conophalloides, commonly known as the rock arum, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply veined and can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces a unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically a deep red or purple color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is a sought-after species among botanists and plant enthusiasts.
