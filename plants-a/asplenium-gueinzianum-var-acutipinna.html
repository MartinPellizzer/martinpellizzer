Asplenium gueinzianum var. acutipinna

Asplenium gueinzianum var. acutipinna, commonly known as the acute pinna shield fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery fronds with a distinctive, shield-like shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a rough texture, with spore-producing structures located on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
