Asperula maximoviczii var. latifolia

Asperula maximoviczii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen azalea, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are broader than the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
