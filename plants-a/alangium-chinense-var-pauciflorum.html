Alangium chinense var. pauciflorum

Alangium chinense var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the Chinese alangium, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
