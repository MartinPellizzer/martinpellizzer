Astragalus zarghumensis

Astragalus zarghumensis, commonly known as the Zarghun Astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments, showcasing deep root systems and drought tolerance. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
