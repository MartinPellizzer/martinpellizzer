Arenaria crilloniana

Arenaria crilloniana, commonly known as the Crillon's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and arid environments, typically found in alpine and subalpine zones. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
