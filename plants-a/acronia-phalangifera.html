Acronia phalangifera

Acronia phalangifera, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it often grows in alpine or arctic environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
