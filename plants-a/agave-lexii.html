Agave lexii

Agave lexii, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of white flowers, which bloom after many years. Agave lexii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
