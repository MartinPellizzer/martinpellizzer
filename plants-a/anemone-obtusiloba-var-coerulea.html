Anemone obtusiloba var. coerulea

Anemone obtusiloba var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
