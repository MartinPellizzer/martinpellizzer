Adhatoda latibracteata

Adhatoda latibracteata, commonly known as the Indian dove tree, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble small dove wings, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by brightly colored bracts that enhance its ornamental value.
