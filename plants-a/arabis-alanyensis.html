Arabis alanyensis

Arabis alanyensis, commonly known as the Alanya rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Arabis alanyensis is a low-growing perennial that is often found in mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.
