Albuca bracteata

Albuca bracteata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that resemble tulips, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca bracteata is known for its long blooming period, often lasting several weeks during the summer months.
