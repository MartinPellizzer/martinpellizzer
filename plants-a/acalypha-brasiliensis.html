Acalypha brasiliensis

Acalypha brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. It produces small, yellowish fruits that contain three winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Acalypha brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
