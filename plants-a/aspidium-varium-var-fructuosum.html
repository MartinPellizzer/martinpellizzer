Aspidium varium var. fructuosum

Aspidium varium var. fructuosum, commonly known as the fruit-bearing water fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

This variety is notable for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
