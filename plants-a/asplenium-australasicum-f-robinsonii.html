Asplenium australasicum f. robinsonii

Asplenium australasicum f. robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to Australia and New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a layer of brownish, scaly tissue.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
