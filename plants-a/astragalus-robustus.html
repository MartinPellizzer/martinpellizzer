Astragalus robustus

Astragalus robustus, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus robustus is often found in grasslands, shrublands, and rocky areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
