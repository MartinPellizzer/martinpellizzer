Agapetes hirsuta

Agapetes hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows at high elevations, often in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Agapetes hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
