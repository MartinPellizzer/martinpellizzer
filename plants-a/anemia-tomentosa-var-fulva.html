Anemia tomentosa var. fulva

Anemia tomentosa var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Caesalpinaceae family.
