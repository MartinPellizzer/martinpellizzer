Adenochlaena mallotiformis

Adenochlaena mallotiformis, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Adenochlaena mallotiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
