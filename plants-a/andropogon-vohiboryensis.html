Andropogon vohiboryensis

Andropogon vohiboryensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
