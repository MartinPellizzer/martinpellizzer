Anguillaria excelsa

Anguillaria excelsa, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Anguillaria excelsa is valued for its edible rhizomes, which have a starchy texture similar to taro.
