Alatiglossum ciliatum

Alatiglossum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silvery-green leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Alatiglossum ciliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
