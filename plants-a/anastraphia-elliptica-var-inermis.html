Anastraphia elliptica var. inermis

Anastraphia elliptica var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-leafed anastraphia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features elliptical, smooth-edged leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Its foliage is characterized by a glossy surface and a slightly succulent texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
