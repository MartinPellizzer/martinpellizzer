Allium acutangulum

Allium acutangulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
