Acacia sakalava

Acacia sakalava, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers that are arranged in cylindrical spikes, and its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small leaflets. Acacia sakalava is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various local wildlife species.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used by indigenous communities.
