Aster erigeroides var. proliferus

Aster erigeroides var. proliferus, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit with a mat-forming structure that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
