Asparagus altilis subsp. oxycarpus

Asparagus altilis subsp.

oxycarpus, commonly known as the giant asparagus, is a robust perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant produces large, fern-like leaves and has clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible young shoots.
