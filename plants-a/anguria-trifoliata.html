Anguria trifoliata

Anguria trifoliata, commonly known as the trifoliate watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its sprawling vines and large, lobed leaves that often have three leaflets, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces bright green fruits with a thick rind and juicy, pale pink or red flesh. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and mild flavor, it is cultivated in some regions for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
