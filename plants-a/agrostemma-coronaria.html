Agrostemma coronaria

Agrostemma coronaria, commonly known as the crown vetch, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread widely.
