Anthurium trujilloi

Anthurium trujilloi, commonly known as the Trujillo anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in cloud forests. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Anthurium trujilloi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
