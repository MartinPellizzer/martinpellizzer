Alhagi karduchorum

Alhagi karduchorum, commonly known as the Karadzhur grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems that help it survive in drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used for forage in livestock grazing.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitats where it contributes to biodiversity and land management.
