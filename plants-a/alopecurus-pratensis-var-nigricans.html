Alopecurus pratensis var. nigricans

Alopecurus pratensis var. nigricans, commonly known as black meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, fine-textured leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows and wetlands. It has a clumping growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.

The variety is distinguished by its darker foliage compared to the typical form of Alopecurus pratensis.
