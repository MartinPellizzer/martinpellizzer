Astragalus sepultipes

Astragalus sepultipes, commonly known as the burial milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
