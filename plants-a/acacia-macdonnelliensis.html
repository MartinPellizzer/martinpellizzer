Acacia macdonnelliensis

Acacia macdonnelliensis, commonly known as the Macdonnell's wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically phyllodes, and its yellow floral spikes that bloom during the warmer months. The plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, exhibiting drought resistance and efficient water use. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia macdonnelliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
