Aspalathus patens

Aspalathus patens, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of long, narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The botanical features of Aspalathus patens make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
