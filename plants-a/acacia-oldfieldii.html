Acacia oldfieldii

Acacia oldfieldii, commonly known as the oldfield acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves, adapting to the dry climate. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Acacia oldfieldii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
