Agrostis reuteri subsp. botelhoi

Agrostis reuteri subsp.

botelhoi, commonly known as the Botelho's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with closely spaced leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in nutrient-poor soils.
