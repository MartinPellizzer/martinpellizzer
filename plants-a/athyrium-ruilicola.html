Athyrium ruilicola

Athyrium ruilicola, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy tissue.

Athyrium ruilicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
