Ammannia nana

Ammannia nana, commonly known as the dwarf ammannia, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from June to September. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

Ammannia nana is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
