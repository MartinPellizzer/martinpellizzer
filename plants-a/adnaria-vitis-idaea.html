Adnaria vitis-idaea

Adnaria vitis-idaea, commonly known as crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue or black berries that are edible but often tart. The plant thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in boreal and tundra environments. Its leaves are leathery and remain green throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
