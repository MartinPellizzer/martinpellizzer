Aristolochia dabieshanensis

Aristolochia dabieshanensis, commonly known as the Dabieshan arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
