Aster incisus var. macrodon

Aster incisus var. macrodon, commonly known as the large-headed aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The varietal name "macrodon" refers to its large, showy flower heads, which make it a notable ornamental plant.
