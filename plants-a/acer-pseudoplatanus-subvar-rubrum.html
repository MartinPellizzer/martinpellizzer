Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. rubrum

Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. rubrum, commonly known as the red maple, is a variant of the common maple tree native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn season. The plant has palmate leaves with five lobes, and its flowers are small and green, appearing in clusters. It produces samaras, which are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
