Araliopsis tabouensis

Araliopsis tabouensis, commonly known as the African aralia, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive trifoliate structure, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
