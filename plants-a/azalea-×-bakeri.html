Azalea × bakeri

Azalea × bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring with flowers ranging in color from white to deep pink, depending on the variety. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Azalea × bakeri is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
