Artemisia valdostana

Artemisia valdostana, commonly known as the Valdostan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to cold, rocky environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in mountainous areas of Italy, France, and Switzerland.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
