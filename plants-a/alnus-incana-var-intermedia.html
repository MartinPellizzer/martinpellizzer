Alnus incana var. intermedia

Alnus incana var. intermedia, commonly known as the gray alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and small, opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces catkin-like flowers and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
