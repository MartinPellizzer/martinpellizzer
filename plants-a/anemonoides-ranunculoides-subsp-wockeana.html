Anemonoides ranunculoides subsp. wockeana

Anemonoides ranunculoides subsp.

wockeana, commonly known as the Wocke's anemonoid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
