Ageratum rhytidophyllum

Ageratum rhytidophyllum, commonly known as the rough ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, purple, and blue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
