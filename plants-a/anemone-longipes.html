Anemone longipes

Anemone longipes, commonly known as the long-stalked anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often with five to seven petals. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can reach up to 10 inches in diameter.

Anemone longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
