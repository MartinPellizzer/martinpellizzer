Amelanchier venulosa

Amelanchier venulosa, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, turning from green to red or purple. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, and it typically grows in woodland or open areas.

Amelanchier venulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
