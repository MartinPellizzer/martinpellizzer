Achillea partheniflora

Achillea partheniflora, commonly known as the golden yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has feathery, aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its common name reflects its historical association with healing and its striking floral display.
