Ancistrocladus ileboensis

Ancistrocladus ileboensis, commonly known as the African poisonwood, is a flowering plant native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the family Ancistrocladaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and woody stems. The plant is notable for containing toxic compounds, particularly in its bark and leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine despite the risks. Its leaves are often used in local rituals and as a source of dye.

Due to its toxic properties, handling the plant requires caution.
