Anthoxanthum nitens subsp. balticum

Anthoxanthum nitens subsp.

balticum, commonly known as Baltic false brome, is a perennial grass species native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers.

It plays a role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
