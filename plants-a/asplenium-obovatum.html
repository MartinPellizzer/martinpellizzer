Asplenium obovatum

Asplenium obovatum, commonly known as the broad-ovate spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to ovate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or soil in temperate regions.

Asplenium obovatum is a small, hardy fern that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
