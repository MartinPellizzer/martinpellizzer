Astragalus chionophyllus

Astragalus chionophyllus, commonly known as the snowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates.

Astragalus chionophyllus is often found in alpine and subalpine environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
