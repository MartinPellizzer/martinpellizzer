Acacia nigripilosa

Acacia nigripilosa, commonly known as the black-haired wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems with a distinctive dark reddish-brown color. Its flowers are typically arranged in cylindrical spikes and are pollinated by insects.

Acacia nigripilosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
