Acacia undulata var. longispina

Acacia undulata var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by long, straight pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
