Acacia inaequilatera

Acacia inaequilatera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Acacia inaequilatera is well adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate salt spray, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
