Apuleia grazielana

Apuleia grazielana, commonly known as the Grazielana ceiba, is a species of leguminous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is often found in conservation programs.
