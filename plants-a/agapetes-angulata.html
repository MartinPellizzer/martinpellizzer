Agapetes angulata

Agapetes angulata, commonly known as the angular agapetes, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This evergreen shrub typically grows in humid, cool mountain forests at high elevations. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive angular appearance, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in late winter to early spring.
