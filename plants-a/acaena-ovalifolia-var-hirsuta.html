Acaena ovalifolia var. hirsuta

Acaena ovalifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy burrplant, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, often in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
