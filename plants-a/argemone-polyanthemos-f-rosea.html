Argemone polyanthemos f. rosea

Argemone polyanthemos f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered argemone, is a variant of the argemone plant characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically growing in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish to orange seeds in its capsules. It has a distinctive, somewhat pungent odor and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is related to other members of the argemone genus.
