Alpinia rolfei

Alpinia rolfei, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or Rolfe's ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features rhizomatous stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow with red or purple markings, and they bloom in clusters at the base of the plant.

Alpinia rolfei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic rhizomes.
