Amicia glandulosa

Amicia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular amicia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. Its stems are often hairy and bear glandular structures, which can secrete oils or other compounds.

Amicia glandulosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
