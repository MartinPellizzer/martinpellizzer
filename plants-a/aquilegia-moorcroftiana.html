Aquilegia moorcroftiana

Aquilegia moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moors' Crown, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are usually hairy and upright. Aquilegia moorcroftiana thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
