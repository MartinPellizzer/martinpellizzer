Aulomyrcia campestris var. brunnea

Aulomyrcia campestris var. brunnea, commonly known as the brown bracted aulomyrcia, is a variant of the aulomyrcia plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive brown bracts that distinguish it from other varieties of the species. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
