Asperula occidentalis

Asperula occidentalis, commonly known as Western azalea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Asperula occidentalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in native plant gardens.
