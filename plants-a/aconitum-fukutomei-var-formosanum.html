Aconitum fukutomei var. formosanum

Aconitum fukutomei var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound, palmately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
