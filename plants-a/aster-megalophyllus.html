Aster megalophyllus

Aster megalophyllus, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological gardens.
