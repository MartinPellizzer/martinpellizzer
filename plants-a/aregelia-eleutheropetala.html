Aregelia eleutheropetala

Aregelia eleutheropetala, commonly known as the silverleaf aregelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Aregelia eleutheropetala is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly environments.
