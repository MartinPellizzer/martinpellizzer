Astragalus sumbari

Astragalus sumbari, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the cold climates of Central Asia, including regions of Russia and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and mountainous environments.

Astragalus sumbari is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
