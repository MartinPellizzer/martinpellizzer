Astragalus acceptus

Astragalus acceptus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus acceptus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators.
