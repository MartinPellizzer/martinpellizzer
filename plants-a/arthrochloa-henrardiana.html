Arthrochloa henrardiana

Arthrochloa henrardiana, commonly known as the African grass or Henrard's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
