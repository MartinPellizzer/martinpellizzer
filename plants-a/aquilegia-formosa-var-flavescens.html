Aquilegia formosa var. flavescens

Aquilegia formosa var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and distinctive spurred sepals, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with lobes that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
