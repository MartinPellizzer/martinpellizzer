Aster divergens var. pendulus

Aster divergens var. pendulus, commonly known as the hanging aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often hang downward, giving it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited for moist, partially shaded environments and is often used in garden settings for its aesthetic appeal.
