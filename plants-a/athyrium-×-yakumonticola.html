Athyrium × yakumonticola

Athyrium × yakumonticola, commonly known as the Yakumonticola Japanese shield fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Athyrium niponicum and Athyrium yakubense.

It is a compact, clump-forming fern with dark green, lance-shaped fronds that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, spore-bearing clusters on the underside of its fronds, typical of the fern family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its foliage provides a lush, textured appearance, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
