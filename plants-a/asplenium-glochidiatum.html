Asplenium glochidiatum

Asplenium glochidiatum, commonly known as the glochidiate spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic plant, typically found growing on rocks or trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a dark green color and a slightly glossy surface. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Asplenium glochidiatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
