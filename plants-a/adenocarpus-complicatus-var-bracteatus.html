Adenocarpus complicatus var. bracteatus

Adenocarpus complicatus var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate burr grass, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often branched stems, which are covered with small, leaf-like bracts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular heads, followed by spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
