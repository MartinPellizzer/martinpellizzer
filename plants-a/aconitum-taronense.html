Aconitum taronense

Aconitum taronense, commonly known as the Taronese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue, helmet-shaped flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with deeply lobed segments, and its roots are tuberous. Aconitum taronense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
