Anagallis nummulariifolia

Anagallis nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverweed or pennywort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Anagallis nummulariifolia is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
