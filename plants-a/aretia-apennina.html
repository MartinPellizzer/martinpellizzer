Aretia apennina

Aretia apennina, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Aretia apennina is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
