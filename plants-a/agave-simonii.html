Agave simonii

Agave simonii, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Agave simonii is prized for its drought tolerance and ornamental value in xeriscaping.
