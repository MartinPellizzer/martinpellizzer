Arisaema ovale var. inaense

Arisaema ovale var. inaense, commonly known as the Inaense Arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and an unusual, spadix-like inflorescence that is often covered in a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include a tuberous root system and a short, stout stem.

The Inaense Arrowhead is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
