Allium melananthum

Allium melananthum, commonly known as the black nightshade onion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbous underground stem, or bulb, is edible and has a mild, garlic-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
