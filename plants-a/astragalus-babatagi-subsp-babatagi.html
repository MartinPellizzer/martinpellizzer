Astragalus babatagi subsp. babatagi

Astragalus babatagi subsp.

babatagi, commonly known as the Babatagi milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
