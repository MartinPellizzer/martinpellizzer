Acacia arabica var. adansonii

Acacia arabica var. adansonii, commonly known as the Sudanese acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a flat, fan-like pattern, and its distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry climates, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is also valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as a source of timber.
