Alternanthera coerilis

Alternanthera coerilis, commonly known as red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly veined leaves that range from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Alternanthera coerilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
