Asparagopsis abyssinica

Asparagopsis abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by berry-like fruits containing tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
