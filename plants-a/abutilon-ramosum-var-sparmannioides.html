Abutilon ramosum var. sparmannioides

Abutilon ramosum var. sparmannioides, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.
