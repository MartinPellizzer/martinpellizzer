Astragalus brevipes

Astragalus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.

Astragalus brevipes is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid environments.
