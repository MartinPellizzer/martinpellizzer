Angelica archangelica subsp. himalaica

Angelica archangelica subsp.

himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, compound leaves that are often aromatic.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine and have a strong, spicy scent.
