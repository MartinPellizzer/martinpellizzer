Anemone caroliniana f. violacea

Anemone caroliniana f. violacea, commonly known as the purple swamp holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it a distinctive and valuable species in wetland ecosystems.
