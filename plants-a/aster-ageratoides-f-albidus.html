Aster ageratoides f. albidus

Aster ageratoides f. albidus, commonly known as the White Daisy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry prairies and open woodlands.

This ornamental plant is valued for its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
