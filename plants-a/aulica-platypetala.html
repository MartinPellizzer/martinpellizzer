Aulica platypetala

Aulica platypetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved aulica, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
