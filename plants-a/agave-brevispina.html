Agave brevispina

Agave brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a rosette of leaves with short, spiny margins, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Agave brevispina is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
