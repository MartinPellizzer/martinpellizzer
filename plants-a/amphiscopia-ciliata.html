Amphiscopia ciliata

Amphiscopia ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and relatively rare species within the Orchidaceae family.
