Aspidium macrocarpon

Aspidium macrocarpon, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Its rhizomes are commonly harvested for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

Aspidium macrocarpon thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
