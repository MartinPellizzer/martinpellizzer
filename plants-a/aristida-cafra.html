Aristida cafra

Aristida cafra, commonly known as the African dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Aristida cafra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
