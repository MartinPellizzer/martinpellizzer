Allium calyptratum

Allium calyptratum, commonly known as the meadow garlic or purple garlic.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, bulbous structures that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and meadows.

Allium calyptratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
