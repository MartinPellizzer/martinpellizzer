Acalypha veronicoides

Acalypha veronicoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Acalypha veronicoides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
