Arabis gerardi var. borealis

Arabis gerardi var. borealis, commonly known as the northern rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
