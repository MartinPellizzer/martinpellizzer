Astragalus smithianus

Astragalus smithianus, commonly known as Smith's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus smithianus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
