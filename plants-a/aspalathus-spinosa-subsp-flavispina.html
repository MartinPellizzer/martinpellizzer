Aspalathus spinosa subsp. flavispina

Aspalathus spinosa subsp.

flavispina, commonly known as the yellow thornveld pea, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and spiny stems, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates. It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain small, edible seeds.

This subspecies is often found in grassland ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
