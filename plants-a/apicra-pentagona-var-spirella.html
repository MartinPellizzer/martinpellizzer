Apicra pentagona var. spirella

Apicra pentagona var. spirella, commonly known as the spiral pentagon plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves.

This variation exhibits a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped foliage that spirals upward along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rare occurrence, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and scientific interest.
