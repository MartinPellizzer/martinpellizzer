Astragalus thracicus subsp. monachorum

Astragalus thracicus subsp.

monachorum, commonly known as the Thracian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local biodiversity.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Astragalus species.
