Acer glabrum subsp. typicum

Acer glabrum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the smooth maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
