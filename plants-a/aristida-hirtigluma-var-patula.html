Aristida hirtigluma var. patula

Aristida hirtigluma var. patula, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, wiry stems that produce dense clusters of tiny, silky, hair-like leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and ability to form attractive, low-maintenance ground covers.
