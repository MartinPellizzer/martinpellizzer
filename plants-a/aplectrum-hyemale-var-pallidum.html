Aplectrum hyemale var. pallidum

Aplectrum hyemale var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale adder's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hooded flower that resembles a snake's head. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in its habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge, adding to its ornamental value.

The pale adder's tongue is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used by indigenous peoples for various ailments.
