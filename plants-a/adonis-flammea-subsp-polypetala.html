Adonis flammea subsp. polypetala

Adonis flammea subsp.

polypetala, commonly known as the golden bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive bell shape.

The flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
