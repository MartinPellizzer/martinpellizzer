Antirrhinum aphyllum

Antirrhinum aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless snapdragon, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and yellow.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant is notable for its lack of leaves on the flowering stems, which gives it its common name. It produces numerous small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Antirrhinum aphyllum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
