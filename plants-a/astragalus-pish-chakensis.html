Astragalus pish-chakensis

Astragalus pish-chakensis, commonly known as the Pish-chakensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have potential medicinal uses, though research on its botanical properties is limited.
