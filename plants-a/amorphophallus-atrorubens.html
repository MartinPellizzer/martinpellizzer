Amorphophallus atrorubens

Amorphophallus atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red arrowhead, is a striking plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, often spiky underground tuber. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. Its tuberous roots are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in seasonal or dry environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be found in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
