Ampliglossum insigne

Ampliglossum insigne, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange petals that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ampliglossum insigne is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
