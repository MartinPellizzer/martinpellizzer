Alnus vilmoriana

Alnus vilmoriana, commonly known as the African alder, is a deciduous tree native to Africa, particularly found in regions such as Somalia, Kenya, and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and catkin-like flowers. The tree produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Alnus vilmoriana is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including riverbanks and forest edges.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
