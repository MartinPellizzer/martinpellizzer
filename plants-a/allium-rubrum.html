Allium rubrum

Allium rubrum, commonly known as the red onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a fibrous root system and a cluster of cylindrical, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients. The plant produces grass-like leaves and, in summer, spikes of small, pink to purple flowers. Its bulbs are rich in sulfur compounds, which contribute to its pungent flavor.

Allium rubrum is cultivated for culinary use and has historical significance in various cuisines.
