Aspidium singaporianum

Aspidium singaporianum, commonly known as the Singapore water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense underwater colonies. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged, contributing to its ornamental appeal in aquatic settings. It plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
