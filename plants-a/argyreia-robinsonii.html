Argyreia robinsonii

Argyreia robinsonii, commonly known as the "Crimson beauty" or "Rainbow vine," is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and colorful bracts that range from pink to red, orange, and yellow, which are not true flowers but modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
