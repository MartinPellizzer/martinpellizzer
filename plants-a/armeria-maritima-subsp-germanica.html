Armeria maritima subsp. germanica

Armeria maritima subsp.

germanica, commonly known as the German thrift, is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that thrives in coastal and sandy environments. It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh coastal conditions. It is native to the Atlantic coast of Europe and parts of North America.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in maritime climates.
