Astragalus forrestii

Astragalus forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets.

Astragalus forrestii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at higher elevations, where it thrives in dry, temperate climates.
