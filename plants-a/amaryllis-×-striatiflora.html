Amaryllis × striatiflora

Amaryllis × striatiflora, commonly known as the streaked amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisicola.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

The leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette that adds to the plant's visual appeal.
