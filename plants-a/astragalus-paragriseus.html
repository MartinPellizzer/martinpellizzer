Astragalus paragriseus

Astragalus paragriseus, commonly known as the gray-green milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Astragalus paragriseus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
