Aerangis muscicola

Aerangis muscicola, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with a unique structure that resembles moss, giving it its common name.

Aerangis muscicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
