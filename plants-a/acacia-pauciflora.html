Acacia pauciflora

Acacia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Acacia pauciflora also plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
