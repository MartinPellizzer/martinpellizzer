Arctium decipiens

Arctium decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces spiny, bristly fruits called burrs, which are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Arctium decipiens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
