Astragalus reconditus

Astragalus reconditus, commonly known as the hidden milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Astragalus reconditus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
