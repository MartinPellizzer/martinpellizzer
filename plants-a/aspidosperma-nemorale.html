Aspidosperma nemorale

Aspidosperma nemorale, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds.

It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
