Armeria bifida

Armeria bifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
