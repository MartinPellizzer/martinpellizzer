Alpinia purpurata

Alpinia purpurata, commonly known as the red ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tint. The plant produces clusters of showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full shade.

Alpinia purpurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
