Alsophila haughtii

Alsophila haughtii, commonly known as the Haught's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are covered in a thick layer of sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures.

Alsophila haughtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
