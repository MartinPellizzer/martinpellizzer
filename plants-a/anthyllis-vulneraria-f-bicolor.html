Anthyllis vulneraria f. bicolor

Anthyllis vulneraria f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored honey vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, which typically display a combination of white and pink or purple hues. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in dry, calcareous soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and coastal areas.
