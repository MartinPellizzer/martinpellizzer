Adenopetalum pictum

Adenopetalum pictum, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Adenopetalum pictum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
