Aspalathus lebeckioides

Aspalathus lebeckioides, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus lebeckioides is drought-tolerant and has a slow growth rate, making it a hardy plant suited to arid environments.
