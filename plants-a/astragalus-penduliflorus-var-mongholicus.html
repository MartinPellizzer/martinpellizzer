Astragalus penduliflorus var. mongholicus

Astragalus penduliflorus var. mongholicus, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often found in steppe and mountainous environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seed pods are typically hairy and release seeds through a mechanism that aids in wind dispersal.
