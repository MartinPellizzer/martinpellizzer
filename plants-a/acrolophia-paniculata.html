Acrolophia paniculata

Acrolophia paniculata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange-red flowers, which are showy and attract pollinators. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Acrolophia paniculata is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions once established.
