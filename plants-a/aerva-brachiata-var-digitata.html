Aerva brachiata var. digitata

Aerva brachiata var. digitata, commonly known as the finger grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
