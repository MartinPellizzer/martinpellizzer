Ageratinastrum polyphyllum var. polyphyllum

Ageratinastrum polyphyllum var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and fluffy, white to blue flower clusters that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while the stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
