Asparagus corruda

Asparagus corruda, commonly known as the Mexican asparagus, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, edible stalks that emerge from a dense cluster of fern-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its growth habit is similar to other asparagus species, with a dormant period during the winter months.

The botanical features of Asparagus corruda make it both a useful vegetable and an ornamental plant in suitable environments.
