Abutilon sellowianum

Abutilon sellowianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
