Arundinaria hirtula

Arundinaria hirtula, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to moist, swampy environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Arundinaria hirtula is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
