Aplotaxis gossypina

Aplotaxis gossypina, commonly known as the cotton bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that resemble cotton. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a notable species in its native region.
