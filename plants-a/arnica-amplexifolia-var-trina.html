Arnica amplexifolia var. trina

Arnica amplexifolia var. trina, commonly known as the three-leaf arnica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically arranged in threes, giving it the specific epithet "trina.".

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.
