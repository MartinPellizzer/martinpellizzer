Acaena subtusvillosula

Acaena subtusvillosula, commonly known as the silky burr, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, spiny fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are inconspicuous, producing small, yellowish blooms that attract pollinators.

Acaena subtusvillosula thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
