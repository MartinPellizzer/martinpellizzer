Abarema dolichadena

Abarema dolichadena, commonly known as the long-stemmed bush bean, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub with compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and is valued in traditional agriculture.
