Aloe paludicola

Aloe paludicola, commonly known as the swamp aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.
