Aristolochia montana

Aristolochia montana, commonly known as the mountain Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-brown in color. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
