Albizia toona

Albizia toona, commonly known as the white cedar or white albizia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, bean-like pods. Albizia toona is often valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
