Acetosella squamata

Acetosella squamata, commonly known as the scaly spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has a delicate appearance with tiny, clustered flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, scaly texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Acetosella squamata is valued in horticulture for its early spring bloom and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
