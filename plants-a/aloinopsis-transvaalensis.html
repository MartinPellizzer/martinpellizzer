Aloinopsis transvaalensis

Aloinopsis transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
