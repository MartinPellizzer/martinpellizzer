Astragalus africanus

Astragalus africanus, commonly known as the African milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus africanus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ecological role in its native habitat.
