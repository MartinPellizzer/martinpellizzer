Aconitum adriaticum

Aconitum adriaticum, commonly known as the Adriatic aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are covered in a dense layer of glandular hairs.

Aconitum adriaticum contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
