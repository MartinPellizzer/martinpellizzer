Amorphophallus excentricus

Amorphophallus excentricus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, hence its common name. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The underground tuberous roots of this plant are also used in traditional medicine and cuisine in some regions.
