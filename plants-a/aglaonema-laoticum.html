Aglaonema laoticum

Aglaonema laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian arrowroot or Laotian evergreen, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and slightly waxy. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant's rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient and hardy species.
