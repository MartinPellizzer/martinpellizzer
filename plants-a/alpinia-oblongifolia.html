Alpinia oblongifolia

Alpinia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its rhizomes, similar to ginger, are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.

Alpinia oblongifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic and practical benefits.
