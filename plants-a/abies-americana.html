Abies americana

Abies americana, commonly known as the American fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green with a white stomatal band. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The American fir plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
