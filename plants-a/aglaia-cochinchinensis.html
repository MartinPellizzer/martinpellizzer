Aglaia cochinchinensis

Aglaia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the white wood or white aglaia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid and warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
