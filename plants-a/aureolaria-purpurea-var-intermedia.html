Aureolaria purpurea var. intermedia

Aureolaria purpurea var. intermedia, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
