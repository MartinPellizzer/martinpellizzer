Acantholimon ulicinum var. ulicinum

Acantholimon ulicinum var. ulicinum, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime chaparral habitats. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species in harsh coastal environments.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
