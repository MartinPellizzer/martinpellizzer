Athyrium × sanjappae

Athyrium × sanjappae, commonly known as the hybrid Japanese forest fern, is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Athyrium nipponicum and Athyrium sinense.

It is a compact, rhizomatous fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and mountainous regions. The plant features finely divided, feather-like fronds with a delicate, lacey appearance, often displaying a vibrant green color. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in shaded gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in horticulture.
