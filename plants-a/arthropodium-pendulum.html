Arthropodium pendulum

Arthropodium pendulum, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a flowering spike that bears small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by distinctive, club-shaped fruits. Arthropodium pendulum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
