Aporum incurvociliatum

Aporum incurvociliatum, commonly known as the curling-leaf aporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, enclosed by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often curl at the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Aporum incurvociliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
