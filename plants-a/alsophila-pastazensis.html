Alsophila pastazensis

Alsophila pastazensis, commonly known as the Pastaza hortensia, is a species of tree fern native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive trunk that is covered with remnants of old leaf bases, giving it a unique appearance. Its fronds are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

Alsophila pastazensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
