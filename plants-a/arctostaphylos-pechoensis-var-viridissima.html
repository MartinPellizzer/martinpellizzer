Arctostaphylos pechoensis var. viridissima

Arctostaphylos pechoensis var. viridissima, commonly known as the greenleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to a striking blue-green hue. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
