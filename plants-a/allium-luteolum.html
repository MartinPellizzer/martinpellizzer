Allium luteolum

Allium luteolum, commonly known as the yellow onion, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are widely used in culinary applications for their pungent flavor.

Botanically, it is notable for its sulfur-containing compounds that contribute to its distinctive aroma and taste.
