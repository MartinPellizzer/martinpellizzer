Achyranthes lanata

Achyranthes lanata, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Achyranthes lanata is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

The stem is erect, hairy, and somewhat woody, with a rough texture and a reddish-brown color, growing in a branching pattern. The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, with pale green to white petals, and bloom from late spring to early summer, primarily pollinated by wind. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and extensive, helping to stabilize the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Native Distribution

Achyranthes lanata is primarily native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid climates and is commonly found in moist soils along riverbanks and in forest understories. This plant is also present in parts of Africa and the Pacific Islands. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
