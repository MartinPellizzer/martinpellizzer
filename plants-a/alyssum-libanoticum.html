Alyssum libanoticum

Alyssum libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon alissum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has silvery-gray foliage and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry climates.

Alyssum libanoticum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
