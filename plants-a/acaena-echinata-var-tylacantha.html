Acaena echinata var. tylacantha

Acaena echinata var. tylacantha, commonly known as the spiny burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky environments. Its fruit contains seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in seed dispersal.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
