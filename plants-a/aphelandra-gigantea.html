Aphelandra gigantea

Aphelandra gigantea, commonly known as the giant flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with bright yellow or orange bracts that resemble flames. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra gigantea is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
