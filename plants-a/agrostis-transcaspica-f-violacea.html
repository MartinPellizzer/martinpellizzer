Agrostis transcaspica f. violacea

Agrostis transcaspica f. violacea, commonly known as the violet false bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, violet-colored inflorescences, which distinguish it from other members of the Agrostis genus. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
