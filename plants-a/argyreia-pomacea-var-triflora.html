Argyreia pomacea var. triflora

Argyreia pomacea var. triflora, commonly known as the tri-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant produces three to five flowers per inflorescence, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal form. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
