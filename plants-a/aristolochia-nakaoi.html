Aristolochia nakaoi

Aristolochia nakaoi, commonly known as the Japanese birthwort, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and a unique, intricate flower structure that resembles a pipe or a horn. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, smooth seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
