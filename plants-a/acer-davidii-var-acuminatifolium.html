Acer davidii var. acuminatifolium

Acer davidii var. acuminatifolium, commonly known as the pointed-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are finely serrated and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
