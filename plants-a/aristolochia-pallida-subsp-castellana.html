Aristolochia pallida subsp. castellana

Aristolochia pallida subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana Dutchman's pipe, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family. It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe.

The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musty odor, which attracts specific pollinators. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant contains aristolochic acids, which are toxic and have been linked to kidney damage in humans.
