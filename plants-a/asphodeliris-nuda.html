Asphodeliris nuda

Asphodeliris nuda, commonly known as the naked asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in suitable habitats.

Asphodeliris nuda is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
