Astragalus balansae

Astragalus balansae, commonly known as the Balansae milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Astragalus balansae is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
