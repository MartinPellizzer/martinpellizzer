Albizia occidentalis var. plurijuga

Albizia occidentalis var. plurijuga, commonly known as the West African silk tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnately compound, and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Albizia occidentalis var. plurijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
