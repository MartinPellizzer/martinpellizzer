Alpinia abundiflora

Alpinia abundiflora, commonly known as the abundant-flower ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright rhizomes and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Alpinia abundiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
