Acacia mucronata var. acuta

Acacia mucronata var. acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils.
