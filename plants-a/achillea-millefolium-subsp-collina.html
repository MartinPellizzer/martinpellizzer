Achillea millefolium subsp. collina

Achillea millefolium subsp.

collina, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features a compact, upright growth habit with silver-green, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and aromatic.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or pale yellow, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
