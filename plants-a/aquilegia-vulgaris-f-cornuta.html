Aquilegia vulgaris f. cornuta

Aquilegia vulgaris f. cornuta, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a variant of the common columbine characterized by its distinctive, elongated sepals that resemble horns.

This plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with five spurred petals that extend outward, creating a unique and striking appearance. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

It produces showy, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
