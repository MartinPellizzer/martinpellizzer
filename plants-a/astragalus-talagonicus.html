Astragalus talagonicus

Astragalus talagonicus, commonly known as the Talagonic milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus talagonicus is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecologically sensitive areas.
