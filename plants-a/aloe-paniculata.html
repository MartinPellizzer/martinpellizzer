Aloe paniculata

Aloe paniculata, commonly known as the tree aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in summer. Aloe paniculata is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
