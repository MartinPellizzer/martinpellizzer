Azalea poukhanensis

Azalea poukhanensis, commonly known as the Poukhan azalea, is a flowering plant native to the island of Poukhan in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea poukhanensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in local horticulture.
