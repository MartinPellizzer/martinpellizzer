Achyrocline deflexa

Achyrocline deflexa, commonly known as the creeping sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and well-drained soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. It is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of coastal ecosystems.
