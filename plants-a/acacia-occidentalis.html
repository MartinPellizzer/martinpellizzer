Acacia occidentalis

Acacia occidentalis, commonly known as the western acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flower spikes that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hardy nature makes it suitable for coastal and rocky soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh climates.
