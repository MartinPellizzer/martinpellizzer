Aegilops virescens

Aegilops virescens, commonly known as green false wheat, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Aegilops virescens is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat breeding programs.

Morphology

Aegilops virescens has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically linear in shape, arranged alternately along the stem, and have a bluish-green color.

The stem is erect, with a smooth texture and a light green to reddish-brown color, growing in a clump-like pattern. Its flowers are spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to white florets, blooming in early summer, and are pollinated by wind. The fruit is a spikelet containing small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and well-developed, helping the plant anchor itself and absorb nutrients from the soil.
