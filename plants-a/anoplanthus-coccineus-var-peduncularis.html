Anoplanthus coccineus var. peduncularis

Anoplanthus coccineus var. peduncularis, commonly known as the red-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in dense spikes during the wet season. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for aquatic gardens and water features.

The inflorescence is typically terminal, adding a vibrant splash of color to its environment.
