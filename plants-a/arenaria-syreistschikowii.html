Arenaria syreistschikowii

Arenaria syreistschikowii, commonly known as the Siberian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of Siberia.

It typically features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. The plant has a compact, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often succulent-like leaves.
