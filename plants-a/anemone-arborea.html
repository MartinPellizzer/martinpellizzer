Anemone arborea

Anemone arborea, commonly known as the tree anemone, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows on moist, wooded slopes and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to seven white or pale pink petals.

Anemone arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
