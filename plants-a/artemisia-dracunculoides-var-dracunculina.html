Artemisia dracunculoides var. dracunculina

Artemisia dracunculoides var. dracunculina, commonly known as wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer. It is characterized by its strong, distinctive scent and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal and historical uses.
