Amorphophallus schliebenii

Amorphophallus schliebenii, commonly known as the giant swamp arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate inflorescence. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form large clumps. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a showy, often fragrant flower that attracts pollinators.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
