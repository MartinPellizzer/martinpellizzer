Andropogon hallii var. grandiflorus

Andropogon hallii var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
