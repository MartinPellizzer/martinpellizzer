Acalypha mollis var. polystachya

Acalypha mollis var. polystachya, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky ironweed, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, woolly seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
