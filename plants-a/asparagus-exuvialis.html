Asparagus exuvialis

Asparagus exuvialis, commonly known as the white asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, edible stalks that are harvested before they fully develop, similar to traditional asparagus. The plant has compound leaves with small, feather-like leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Asparagus exuvialis is valued for its culinary use and is often cultivated for its tender, crisp shoots.
