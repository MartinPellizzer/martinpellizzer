Antirrhinum tortuosum var. genuinum

Antirrhinum tortuosum var. genuinum, commonly known as the twisted snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted flower spikes that resemble the shape of a dragon's snout. The plant produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
