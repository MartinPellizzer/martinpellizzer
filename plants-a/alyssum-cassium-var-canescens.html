Alyssum cassium var. canescens

Alyssum cassium var. canescens, commonly known as hoary alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
