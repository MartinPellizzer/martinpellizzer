Astragalus depressus var. ioleucus

Astragalus depressus var. ioleucus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to reddish, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
