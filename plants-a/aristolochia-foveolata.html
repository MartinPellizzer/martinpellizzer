Aristolochia foveolata

Aristolochia foveolata, commonly known as the spotted Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow with purple veins. Its fruit is a dark, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
