Agave bulbifera

Agave bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces an underground bulb from which it derives its common name. It is known for its ability to store water in its leaves and stem, making it drought-tolerant.

Agave bulbifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
