Armeniaca mandshurica

Armeniaca mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but typically less flavorful than common apricots. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
