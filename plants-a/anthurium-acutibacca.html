Anthurium acutibacca

Anthurium acutibacca, commonly known as the sharp-angled anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a glossy texture. The plant produces spadices, which are the characteristic spathes that enclose the small, clustered flowers. Anthurium acutibacca thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
