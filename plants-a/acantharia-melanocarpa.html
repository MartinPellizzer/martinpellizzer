Acantharia melanocarpa

Acantharia melanocarpa, commonly known as the black acantharian, is a species of marine protist belonging to the order Acantharia.

It is characterized by its intricate, calcareous skeletal structure, which forms a complex, spiny shell. The organism is typically found in coastal waters and is part of the plankton community. Its life cycle involves both asexual and sexual reproduction, with the latter producing resting spores.

Acantharia melanocarpa plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as a food source for various marine organisms.
