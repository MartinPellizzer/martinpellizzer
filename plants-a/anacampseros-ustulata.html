Anacampseros ustulata

Anacampseros ustulata, commonly known as the fireplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species for xerophytic conditions.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscaping.
