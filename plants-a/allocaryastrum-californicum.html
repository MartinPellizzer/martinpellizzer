Allocaryastrum californicum

Allocaryastrum californicum, commonly known as the California barbed wire vine, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that often coil around other vegetation or structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive barbed edge, contributing to its common name.

Allocaryastrum californicum thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
