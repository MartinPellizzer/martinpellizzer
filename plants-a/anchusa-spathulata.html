Anchusa spathulata

Anchusa spathulata, commonly known as broadleaf anchusa, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, spatulate leaves and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is part of the Boraginaceae family, known for its hairy stems and medicinal properties in some species.
