Agrostis robusta

Agrostis robusta, commonly known as roughstalk bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and have a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.
