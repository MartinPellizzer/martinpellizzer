Anthemis urvillaei

Anthemis urvillaei, commonly known as the Peruvian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets. It is often found growing in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
