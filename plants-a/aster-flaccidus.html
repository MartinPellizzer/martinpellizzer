Aster flaccidus

Aster flaccidus, commonly known as the weak aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Aster flaccidus is known for its delicate, often sprawling growth habit and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
