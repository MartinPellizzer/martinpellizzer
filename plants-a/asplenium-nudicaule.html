Asplenium nudicaule

Asplenium nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stem maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, slender fronds.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and often covered with a layer of brown, fibrous tissue.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, narrow pinnae that give the plant its distinctive, lace-like appearance.
