Asarum glabrum

Asarum glabrum, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a stout, rhizomatous root system and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
