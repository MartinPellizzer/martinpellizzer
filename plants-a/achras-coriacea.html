Achras coriacea

Achras coriacea, commonly known as the Socotra cactus, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has a distinctive crown of large, waxy leaves that are green and sometimes covered in a whitish coating. Its flowers are large, yellowish, and appear at the top of the plant during the rainy season.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its stem and leaves to survive in its harsh environment.
