Aruncus dioicus var. acuminatus

Aruncus dioicus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped goat's beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching stems that bear clusters of soft, white, feathery flowers. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
