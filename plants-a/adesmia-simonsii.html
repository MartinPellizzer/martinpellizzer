Adesmia simonsii

Adesmia simonsii, commonly known as the desert burr plant, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or green. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals or through mechanical means. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
