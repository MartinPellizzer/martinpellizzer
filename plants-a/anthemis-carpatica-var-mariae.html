Anthemis carpatica var. mariae

Anthemis carpatica var. mariae, commonly known as the Marie's Carpathian ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region.

It features basal and upright stems that are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disc and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged texture.

This variety is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
