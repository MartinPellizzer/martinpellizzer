Avena sterilis var. culta

Avena sterilis var. culta, commonly known as cultivated oat, is a domesticated variant of the wild oat species.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and spikelet clusters that bear small, two-lobed leathery bracts. The plant produces awned spikelets with three to five florets, which are typically sterile in the cultivated form. It thrives in temperate climates and is widely grown for its nutritious grains.

This variety is valued for its high protein content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
