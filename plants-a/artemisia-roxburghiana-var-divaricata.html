Artemisia roxburghiana var. divaricata

Artemisia roxburghiana var. divaricata, commonly known as the Kashmiri wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayan regions.

It features silvery-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that appear in late summer. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its essential oils have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.
