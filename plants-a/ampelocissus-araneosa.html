Ampelocissus araneosa

Ampelocissus araneosa, commonly known as the spider plant vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. It is known for its ability to climb using tendrils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
