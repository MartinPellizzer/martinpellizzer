Anthyllis jacquinii

Anthyllis jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's ladders, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from spring to summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Anthyllis jacquinii is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
