Astragalus eremiticus

Astragalus eremiticus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has deep taproots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are typically small and hairy, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Astragalus eremiticus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
