Anaphalis gracilis var. ulophylla

Anaphalis gracilis var. ulophylla, commonly known as the slender woolly Aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a soft, woolly texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and clustered, producing a fluffy, cloud-like effect.
