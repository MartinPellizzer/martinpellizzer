Antirrhinum murale var. ramosissimum

Antirrhinum murale var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse displays of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
