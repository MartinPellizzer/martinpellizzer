Agave commelyni

Agave commelyni, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by spiny fruit capsules. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave commelyni is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
