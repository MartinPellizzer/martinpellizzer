Acaena adscendens var. macrochaeta

Acaena adscendens var. macrochaeta, commonly known as the tall bentweed, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in coastal and subalpine environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

The variety macrochaeta is distinguished by its longer, more robust bracts compared to other varieties of Acaena adscendens.
