Astragalus utriger f. ovatus

Astragalus utriger f. ovatus, commonly known as the ovate milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to pale purple flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that dehisces to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
