Alyssum trapeziforme

Alyssum trapeziforme, commonly known as the Mexican alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and the plant produces small, rounded seed pods that contain several seeds.
