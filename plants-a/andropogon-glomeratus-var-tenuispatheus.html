Andropogon glomeratus var. tenuispatheus

Andropogon glomeratus var. tenuispatheus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The variety tenuispatheus is distinguished by its narrower leaf blades compared to the typical form of Andropogon glomeratus.
