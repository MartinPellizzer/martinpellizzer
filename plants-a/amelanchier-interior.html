Amelanchier interior

Amelanchier interior, commonly known as the Allegheny serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It produces showy white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Amelanchier interior is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
