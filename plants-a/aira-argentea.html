Aira argentea

Aira argentea, commonly known as silver aira, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, white to pale blue florets. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides.

Aira argentea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
