Anodontea arenaria

Anodontea arenaria, commonly known as the sandy beach liverwort, is a small, non-vascular plant that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its leafy shoots and is typically found in moist, sandy soils along shorelines. The plant has a simple structure with no true roots, stems, or leaves, and instead consists of thalloid bodies. It plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for microorganisms.

Anodontea arenaria is often used as an indicator species for assessing the health of coastal ecosystems.
