Amomum masticatorium

Amomum masticatorium, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red fruits containing a single seed. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is also a key ingredient in the preparation of betel quid.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
