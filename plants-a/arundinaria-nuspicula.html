Arundinaria nuspicula

Arundinaria nuspicula, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often display a striking purple hue, particularly in the lower nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria nuspicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and resilience.
