Allium saralicum

Allium saralicum, commonly known as the Saratov leek, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features long, cylindrical leaves that are often bluish-green in color and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbous stem is edible and has a mild, onion-like flavor.

Allium saralicum is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
