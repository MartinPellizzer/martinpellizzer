Aconitum delphinense

Aconitum delphinense, commonly known as the Delphinine Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in dense, spires. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are hairy and often have a distinctive silvery sheen. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
