Acetosa officinalis

Acetosa officinalis, commonly known as common sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its tart, sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid. It typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Acetosa officinalis has a long history of use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
