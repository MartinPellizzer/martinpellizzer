Austrodanthonia popinensis

Austrodanthonia popinensis, commonly known as the Popinensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open woodlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate salt spray, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
