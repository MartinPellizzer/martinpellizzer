Acalypha sehnemii

Acalypha sehnemii, commonly known as the silver mist plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in fine, dense hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, followed by spherical, fuzzy seed capsules.

This plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
