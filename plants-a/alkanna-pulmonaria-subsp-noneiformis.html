Alkanna pulmonaria subsp. noneiformis

Alkanna pulmonaria subsp.

noneiformis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy roots that are highly valued for their medicinal and dyeing properties.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats on the ground.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
