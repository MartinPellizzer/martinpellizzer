Alisma reniforme

Alisma reniforme, commonly known as broadleaf water plantain, is a semi-aquatic herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in waterlogged soils and absorb nutrients. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that float on the water surface or grow partially submerged. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Alisma reniforme plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
