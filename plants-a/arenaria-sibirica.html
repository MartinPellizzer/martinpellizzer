Arenaria sibirica

Arenaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Arenaria sibirica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
