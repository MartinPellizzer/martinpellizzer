Arthraxon cuspidatus var. micans

Arthraxon cuspidatus var. micans, commonly known as the shining arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant features narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a shiny appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
