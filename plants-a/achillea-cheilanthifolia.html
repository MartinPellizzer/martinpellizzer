Achillea cheilanthifolia

Achillea cheilanthifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
