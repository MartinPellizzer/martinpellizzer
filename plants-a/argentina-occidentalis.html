Argentina occidentalis

Argentina occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Argentine plant, is a flowering species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Argentina occidentalis is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally by local communities for its healing benefits.
