Asarum callifolium

Asarum callifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Aristolochiaceae family. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
