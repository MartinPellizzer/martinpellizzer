Actinidia asymmetrica

Actinidia asymmetrica, commonly known as the Chinese may apple, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often asymmetrical leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries. Its vines can climb or trail along the ground, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
