Adenophora verticillata var. glabra

Adenophora verticillata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth false bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
