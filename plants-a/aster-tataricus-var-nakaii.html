Aster tataricus var. nakaii

Aster tataricus var. nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's blue aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
