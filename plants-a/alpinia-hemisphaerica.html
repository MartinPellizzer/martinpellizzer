Alpinia hemisphaerica

Alpinia hemisphaerica, commonly known as the mountain ginger or golden ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Alpinia hemisphaerica is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
