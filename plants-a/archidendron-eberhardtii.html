Archidendron eberhardtii

Archidendron eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale yellow.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which dry and split open to release the seeds, aiding in dispersal.
