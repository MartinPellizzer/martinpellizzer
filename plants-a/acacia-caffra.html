Acacia caffra

Acacia caffra, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, feathery leaves that turn a striking silver-white color when dry. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

Acacia caffra is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
