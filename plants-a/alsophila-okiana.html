Alsophila okiana

Alsophila okiana, commonly known as the Okinawan tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Japan and the Philippines.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its growth in moist, shaded environments.

Alsophila okiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
