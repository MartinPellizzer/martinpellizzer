Adenophora fusifolia

Adenophora fusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, with five lobes. Its leaves are elongated and lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs.

Adenophora fusifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
