Asarum sieboldii var. sieboldii

Asarum sieboldii var. sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, thick, fleshy root system that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued for its potential therapeutic uses.
