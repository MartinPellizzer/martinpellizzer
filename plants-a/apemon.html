Apemon

Apemon, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, white, ghost-like appearance. The plant lacks leaves and instead relies on its roots to absorb nutrients from the air and decaying organic matter. It typically grows on the trunks of trees in wet, shaded environments.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, the apemon is considered endangered and is protected in many areas.
