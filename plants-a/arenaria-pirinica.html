Arenaria pirinica

Arenaria pirinica, commonly known as the Pyrenean sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically thrives in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are usually succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Arenaria pirinica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation sites.
