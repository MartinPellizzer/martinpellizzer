Aster ageratoides var. tenuifolius

Aster ageratoides var. tenuifolius, commonly known as slenderleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name, while the flowers are small and blue to purple in color.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
