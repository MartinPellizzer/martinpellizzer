Antiphylla latiflora

Antiphylla latiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved antiphylla, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Antiphylla latiflora is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
