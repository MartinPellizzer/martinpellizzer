Agropyron krylovianum

Agropyron krylovianum, commonly known as the Krylovian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, semi-arid environments and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Agropyron krylovianum is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in restoration ecology projects.
