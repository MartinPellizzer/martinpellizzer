Abelia biflora var. coreana

Abelia biflora var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers in the summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.

Its natural habitat is characterized by cool, moist climates and well-drained soils.
