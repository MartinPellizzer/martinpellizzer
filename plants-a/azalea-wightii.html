Azalea wightii

Azalea wightii, commonly known as the Wight's azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink to red blossoms. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
