Andropogon exilis var. glabrescens

Andropogon exilis var. glabrescens, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along creek banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster with two to three small, pale blue flowers.

Andropogon exilis var. glabrescens plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
