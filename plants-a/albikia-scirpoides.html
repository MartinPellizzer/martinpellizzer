Albikia scirpoides

Albikia scirpoides, commonly known as the silverstem bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks. The plant has tall, slender stems that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while small, inconspicuous flowers appear in spike-like clusters during the summer months.

Albikia scirpoides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
