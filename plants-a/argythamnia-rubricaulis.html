Argythamnia rubricaulis

Argythamnia rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its striking red stems and purple flowers make it a distinctive feature in its natural habitat.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
