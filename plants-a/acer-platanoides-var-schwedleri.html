Acer platanoides var. schwedleri

Acer platanoides var. schwedleri, commonly known as the Norway maple, is a deciduous tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing capabilities.
