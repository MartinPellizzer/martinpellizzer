Adiantum sulphureum var. majus

Adiantum sulphureum var. majus, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a strikingly ornamental fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are bright gold to yellow in color, with a distinctive frilly edge. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense, carpet-like colonies.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and elegant foliage.
