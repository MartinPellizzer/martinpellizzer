Azalea ledifolia var. noordtiana

Azalea ledifolia var. noordtiana, commonly known as the Noordtiana azalea, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its botanical features include small, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a desirable specimen for specialized gardens.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
