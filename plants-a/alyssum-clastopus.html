Alyssum clastopus

Alyssum clastopus, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming growth habit with succulent, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Alyssum clastopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
