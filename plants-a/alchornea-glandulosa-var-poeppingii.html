Alchornea glandulosa var. poeppingii

Alchornea glandulosa var. poeppingii, commonly known as the glandular alchornea, is a shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that often emit a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural compounds.
