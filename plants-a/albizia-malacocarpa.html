Albizia malacocarpa

Albizia malacocarpa, commonly known as the Malacocarpa Albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often arranged in a feathery pattern, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and crafts.
