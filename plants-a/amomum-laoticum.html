Amomum laoticum

Amomum laoticum, commonly known as Laotian cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its medicinal value and aromatic qualities.
