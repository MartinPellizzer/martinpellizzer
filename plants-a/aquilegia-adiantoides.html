Aquilegia adiantoides

Aquilegia adiantoides, commonly known as the fern-leaf columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shade beds.
