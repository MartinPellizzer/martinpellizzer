Aristolochia malmeana

Aristolochia malmeana, commonly known as the Malme's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, musty odor. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological traits and ecological preferences.
