Armeniaca mume

Armeniaca mume, commonly known as the Chinese date or Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and orange-red berries. The fruit, which is edible, has a sweet, honey-like flavor when ripe. The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and a temperate climate for optimal growth.
