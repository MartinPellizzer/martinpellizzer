Anemone pentaphylla

Anemone pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Anemone pentaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
