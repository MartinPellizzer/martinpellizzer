Artemisia dracunculus subsp. glauca

Artemisia dracunculus subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a strong, camphor-like scent when crushed.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
