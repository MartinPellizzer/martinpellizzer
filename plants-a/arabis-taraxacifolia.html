Arabis taraxacifolia

Arabis taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelionleaf rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant typically has basal leaves that are elongated and resemble those of dandelions, hence its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Arabis taraxacifolia thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
