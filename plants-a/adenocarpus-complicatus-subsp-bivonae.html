Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. bivonae

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

bivonae, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has a taproot system and produces small, hairy seeds in its fruiting bodies. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
