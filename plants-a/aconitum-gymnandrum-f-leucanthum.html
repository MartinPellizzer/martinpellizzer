Aconitum gymnandrum f. leucanthum

Aconitum gymnandrum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered monkshood, is a hybrid form of the genus Aconitum.

It is characterized by its striking pale blue to white flowers that are arranged in a spire-like cluster. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive palmate shape, typical of the Aconitum genus.

The plant contains potent alkaloids and is considered toxic, requiring caution during handling and cultivation.
