Aspidium ellipticum

Aspidium ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Water Ferns.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that are often submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a rhizome that anchors it to the substrate, and its leaves are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. Aspidium ellipticum thrives in slow-moving or still water, such as ponds and marshes, and is often found in temperate regions.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
