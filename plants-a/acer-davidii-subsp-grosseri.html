Acer davidii subsp. grosseri

Acer davidii subsp.

grosseri, commonly known as the Chinese maple or David's maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically green on top and silvery-white underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
