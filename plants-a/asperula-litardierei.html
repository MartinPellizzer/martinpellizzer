Asperula litardierei

Asperula litardierei, commonly known as the French azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern France.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Asperula litardierei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form thickets in its natural habitat.
