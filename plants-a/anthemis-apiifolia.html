Anthemis apiifolia

Anthemis apiifolia, commonly known as the parsleyleaf ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are finely divided, resembling parsley, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.
