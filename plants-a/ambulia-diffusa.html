Ambulia diffusa

Ambulia diffusa, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that give it a ghost-like appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae and is known for its simple, unbranched stem and small, inconspicuous flowers.
