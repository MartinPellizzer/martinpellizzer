Adiantum plicatum

Adiantum plicatum, commonly known as the fan-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble a fan, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, humus-rich soil. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a glossy sheen, with a unique structure that allows for efficient water retention.

Adiantum plicatum is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
