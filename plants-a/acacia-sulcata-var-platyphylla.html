Acacia sulcata var. platyphylla

Acacia sulcata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
