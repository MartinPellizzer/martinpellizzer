Asperula glomerata subsp. dasycarpa

Asperula glomerata subsp.

dasycarpa, commonly known as the woolly woodruff, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The subspecies dasycarpa is distinguished by its more robust growth and thicker, hairy stems compared to the typical form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
