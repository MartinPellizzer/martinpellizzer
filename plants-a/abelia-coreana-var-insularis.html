Abelia coreana var. insularis

Abelia coreana var. insularis, commonly known as the Jeju abelia, is a flowering shrub native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It features attractive, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to garden settings.
