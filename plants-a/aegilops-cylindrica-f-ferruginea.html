Aegilops cylindrica f. ferruginea

Aegilops cylindrica f. ferruginea, commonly known as the red spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This grass species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
