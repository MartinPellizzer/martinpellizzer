Aconitum multifidum var. callibotryon

Aconitum multifidum var. callibotryon, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum multifidum var. callibotryon is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is also noted for its toxic properties.
