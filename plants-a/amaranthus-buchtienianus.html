Amaranthus buchtienianus

Amaranthus buchtienianus, commonly known as the Buchtienian amaranth, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to harsh climates.

Amaranthus buchtienianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
