Abutilon andrewsianum

Abutilon andrewsianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Abutilon andrewsianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
