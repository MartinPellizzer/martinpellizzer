Agapetes desmogyne

Agapetes desmogyne, commonly known as the silverleaf evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. This shrub typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring or early summer.
