Astragalus hirtus

Astragalus hirtus, commonly known as hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus hirtus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
