Agrostis laxiflora f. tuckermanii

Agrostis laxiflora f. tuckermanii, commonly known as Tuckerman's slender grass, is a variant of the slender grass species native to North America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and often tinged with reddish hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
