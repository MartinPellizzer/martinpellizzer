Alchemilla heptagona f. autumnalis

Alchemilla heptagona f. autumnalis, commonly known as the autumnal lady's mantle, is a perennial plant characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe, particularly in the mountainous areas of the Alps. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.

The species is known for its adaptability to moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
