Anemone afghanica

Anemone afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting center. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually hairy and lobed.

Anemone afghanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
