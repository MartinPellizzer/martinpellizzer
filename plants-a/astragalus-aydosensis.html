Astragalus aydosensis

Astragalus aydosensis, commonly known as Aydos milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus aydosensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
