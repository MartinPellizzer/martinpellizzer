Acer insigne f. perckense

Acer insigne f. perckense, commonly known as the Perckens maple, is a unique variant of the Acerspecies native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 15 feet. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its seasonal color and elegant form.
