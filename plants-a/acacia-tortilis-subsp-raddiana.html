Acacia tortilis subsp. raddiana

Acacia tortilis subsp.

raddiana, commonly known as the umbrella thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown formed by dense, thorny branches.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of leaves, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

The acacia also plays a significant role in providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
