Astragalus guinanicus f. albiflorus

Astragalus guinanicus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are predominantly white, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
