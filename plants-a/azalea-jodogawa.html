Azalea jodogawa

Azalea jodogawa, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to Japan and belongs to the Rhododendron genus.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea jodogawa prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
