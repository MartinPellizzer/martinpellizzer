Alypum

Alypum, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, or purple. Alypum species are native to regions of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, and they thrive in well-drained soils. The plants are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Some species are also cultivated for their edible seeds, which can be used in cooking or as a spice.
