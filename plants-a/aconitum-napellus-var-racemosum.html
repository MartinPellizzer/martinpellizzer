Aconitum napellus var. racemosum

Aconitum napellus var. racemosum, commonly known as the Italian monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, upright stems and striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply lobed edges, and its roots are tuberous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
