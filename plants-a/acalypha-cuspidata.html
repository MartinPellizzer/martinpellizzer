Acalypha cuspidata

Acalypha cuspidata, commonly known as the spikeless copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, copper-colored bracts that resemble leaves, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

Acalypha cuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
