Arrhenatherum sempervirens subsp. setaceum

Arrhenatherum sempervirens subsp.

setaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often slightly folded and have a distinct, grassy aroma. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native environments.
