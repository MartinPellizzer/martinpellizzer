Aloe kwasimbana

Aloe kwasimbana, commonly known as the Natal aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.

Aloe kwasimbana thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
