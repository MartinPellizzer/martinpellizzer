Adodendron chamaecistus

Adodendron chamaecistus, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
