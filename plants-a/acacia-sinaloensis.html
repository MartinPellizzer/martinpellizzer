Acacia sinaloensis

Acacia sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloa acacia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
