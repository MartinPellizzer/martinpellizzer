Asperula digyna

Asperula digyna, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like rhododendrons and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Asperula digyna thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact growth.
