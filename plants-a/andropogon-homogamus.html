Andropogon homogamus

Andropogon homogamus, commonly known as bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are arranged in a panicle structure.

Andropogon homogamus plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local wildlife.
