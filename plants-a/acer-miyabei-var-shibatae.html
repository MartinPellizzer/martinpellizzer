Acer miyabei var. shibatae

Acer miyabei var. shibatae, commonly known as the Shibata maple, is a variant of the Miyabei maple native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a serrated edge.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
