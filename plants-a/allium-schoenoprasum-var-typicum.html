Allium schoenoprasum var. typicum

Allium schoenoprasum var. typicum, commonly known as chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which are typically followed by spherical, dry fruits containing numerous seeds. Chives are widely cultivated for their aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herb beds.
