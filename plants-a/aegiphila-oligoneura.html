Aegiphila oligoneura

Aegiphila oligoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Greater Antilles.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that helps reflect sunlight.

The flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or white, contributing to its ornamental value.
