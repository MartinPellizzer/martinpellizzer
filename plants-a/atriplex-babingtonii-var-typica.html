Atriplex babingtonii var. typica

Atriplex babingtonii var. typica, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is adapted to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Atriplex babingtonii var. typica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
