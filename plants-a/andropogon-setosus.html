Andropogon setosus

Andropogon setosus, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon setosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
