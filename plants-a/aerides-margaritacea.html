Aerides margaritacea

Aerides margaritacea, commonly known as the pearl orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, erect stem with several fleshy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its flowers have a distinctive, waxy texture and emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent.

Aerides margaritacea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
