Aristida lembaensis

Aristida lembaensis, commonly known as the Lembu grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like clusters.

Aristida lembaensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
