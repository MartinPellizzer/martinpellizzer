Acacia bussei var. benadirensis

Acacia bussei var. benadirensis, commonly known as the Benadire acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to golden flowers arranged in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
