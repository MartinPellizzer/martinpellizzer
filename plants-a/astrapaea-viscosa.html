Astrapaea viscosa

Astrapaea viscosa, commonly known as the roughleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Astrapaea viscosa is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
