Avena fatua var. elongata

Avena fatua var. elongata, commonly known as slender wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and inflorescence.

It is an annual grass native to temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that mature into three-branched, awned grains. Its growth habit is typically prostrate to erect, with the stems often lying close to the ground.

This variant is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
