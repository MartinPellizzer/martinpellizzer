Adonis aestivalis var. dentata

Adonis aestivalis var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant features delicate, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent yellow stamens, and its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
