Aria torminalis

Aria torminalis, commonly known as the red tailed tropicbird, is a tropical plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects and birds.

Aria torminalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
