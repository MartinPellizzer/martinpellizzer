Agropyron × sitanioides

Agropyron × sitanioides, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the Great Plains of North America.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant has a robust root system, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces spike-like inflorescences.

Agropyron × sitanioides is valued for its potential in forage and as a candidate for developing new wheat varieties due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
