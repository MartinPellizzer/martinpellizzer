Aspalathus pycnantha

Aspalathus pycnantha, commonly known as the South African green tea or rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are harvested and processed to produce a caffeine-free herbal tea.

The plant's unique botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a significant crop in the tea industry.
