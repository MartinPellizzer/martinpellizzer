Aconitum vulparia var. lasianthum

Aconitum vulparia var. lasianthum, commonly known as the paleflower monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spire-like stems that bear clusters of dark blue to purple, hood-shaped flowers with prominent yellow markings. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in cooler climates.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display in gardens.
