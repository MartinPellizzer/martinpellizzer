Atriplex davidsonii

Atriplex davidsonii, commonly known as Davidson's saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland salt-affected areas.

Davidson's saltbush is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
