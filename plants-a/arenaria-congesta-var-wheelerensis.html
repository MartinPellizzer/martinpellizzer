Arenaria congesta var. wheelerensis

Arenaria congesta var. wheelerensis, commonly known as Wheeler's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
