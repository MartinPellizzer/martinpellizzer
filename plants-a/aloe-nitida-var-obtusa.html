Aloe nitida var. obtusa

Aloe nitida var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
