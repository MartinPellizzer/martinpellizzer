Arabis sagittata var. nipponica

Arabis sagittata var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
