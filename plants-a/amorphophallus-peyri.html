Amorphophallus peyri

Amorphophallus peyri, commonly known as the Peyri's arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a large, fleshy underground tuber that serves as its primary storage organ. The leaves are typically large, arrow-shaped, and can reach several meters in length.

It is valued for its edible tuber, which is used in various culinary applications in its native regions.
