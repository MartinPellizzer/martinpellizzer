Avenella flexuosa subsp. corsica

Avenella flexuosa subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are slightly folded and may have a bluish-gray appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by small, three-lobed seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
