Amomum acre

Amomum acre, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by dark, oblong fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical features of Amomum acre make it a valuable species in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
