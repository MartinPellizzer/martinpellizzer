Amaranthus japonicus

Amaranthus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese amaranth, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, three-lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
