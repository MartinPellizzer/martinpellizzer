Antirrhinum breweri

Antirrhinum breweri, commonly known as the Brewer's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.
