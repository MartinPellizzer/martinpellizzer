Aster lanceolatus var. simplex

Aster lanceolatus var. simplex, commonly known as the smooth aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features lance-shaped, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
