Acacia pulchella var. reflexa

Acacia pulchella var. reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous fine, thread-like leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
