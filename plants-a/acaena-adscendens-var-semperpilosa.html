Acaena adscendens var. semperpilosa

Acaena adscendens var. semperpilosa, commonly known as the evergreen acaena, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that are an important food source for native birds. The plant has finely divided, needle-like leaves that are evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
