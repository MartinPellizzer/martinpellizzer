Aegilops crassa var. flavescens

Aegilops crassa var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often tinged with a yellowish hue. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is notable for its potential role in agricultural systems due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
