Abies × insignis var. mastersiana

Abies × insignis var. mastersiana, commonly known as the Masters' fir, is a hybrid coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Abies balsamea and Abies lasiocarpa, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. The tree typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, providing a striking visual contrast.

The cones are usually small and conical, maturing to release seeds that aid in its propagation.
