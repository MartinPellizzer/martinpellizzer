Atheropogon

Atheropogon, commonly known as the needle grass, is a genus of grasses native to various regions of the world, including North America and parts of Asia.

These plants typically have tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Atheropogon species are usually found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

The genus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
