Aconitum tatsienense var. divaricatum

Aconitum tatsienense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Yunnan aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often hairy.

This variant is closely related to other Aconitum species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
