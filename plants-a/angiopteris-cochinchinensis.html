Angiopteris cochinchinensis

Angiopteris cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Cochinchina shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, almost metallic sheen.

Angiopteris cochinchinensis is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
