Achyrospermum scandens

Achyrospermum scandens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
