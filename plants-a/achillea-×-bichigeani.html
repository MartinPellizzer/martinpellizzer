Achillea × bichigeani

Achillea × bichigeani, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea ptarmica (chicory yarrow), resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and produces clusters of small, usually white or pinkish flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in herbal medicine.

Achillea × bichigeani thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
