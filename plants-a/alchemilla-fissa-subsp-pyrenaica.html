Alchemilla fissa subsp. pyrenaica

Alchemilla fissa subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
