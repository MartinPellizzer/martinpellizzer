Anthemis turolensis

Anthemis turolensis, commonly known as the Turolan chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Anthemis turolensis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
