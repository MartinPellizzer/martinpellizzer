Aridaria aurea

Aridaria aurea, commonly known as the golden aridaria, is a flowering plant native to arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access groundwater.

Aridaria aurea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
