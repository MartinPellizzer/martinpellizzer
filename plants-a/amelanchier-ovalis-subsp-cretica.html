Amelanchier ovalis subsp. cretica

Amelanchier ovalis subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Crete serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, appearing in late summer.

The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

The flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.
