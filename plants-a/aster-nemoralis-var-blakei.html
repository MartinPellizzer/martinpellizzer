Aster nemoralis var. blakei

Aster nemoralis var. blakei, commonly known as the Blake's wood aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
