Adesmia sulina

Adesmia sulina, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Adesmia sulina is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
