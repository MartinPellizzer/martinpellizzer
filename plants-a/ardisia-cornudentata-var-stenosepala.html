Ardisia cornudentata var. stenosepala

Ardisia cornudentata var. stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled beautyberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright purple to red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
