Appendicularia thymifolia

Appendicularia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved appendicularia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and aromatic, fragrant leaves that resemble those of thyme. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
