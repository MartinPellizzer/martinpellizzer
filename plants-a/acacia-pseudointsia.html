Acacia pseudointsia

Acacia pseudointsia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and glandular. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Acacia pseudointsia is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

It is an important plant in local ecosystems, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
