Achyranthes aspera var. aspera

Achyranthes aspera var. aspera, commonly known as prickly chaffflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
