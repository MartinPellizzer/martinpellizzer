Acacia nubica var. erythraea

Acacia nubica var. erythraea, commonly known as the Erythraean acacia, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves with thorny tips. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to dry climates. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its role in preventing soil erosion in degraded landscapes.
