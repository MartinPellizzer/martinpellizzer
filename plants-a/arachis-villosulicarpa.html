Arachis villosulicarpa

Arachis villosulicarpa, commonly known as the hairy pod groundnut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its unique morphology and potential nutritional value, it has attracted interest for agricultural and botanical research.
