Arenaria Querioides Subsp. Cavanillesiana

Arenaria querioides subsp. cavanillesiana is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of the southwestern United States. It is known for its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, adding a touch of beauty to rocky and arid landscapes. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to harsh environments. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Protecting this plant is essential for preserving the biodiversity of its native habitat.
