Anachyris paspaloides

Anachyris paspaloides, commonly known as the slender grass or paspaloid grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in arid conditions.
