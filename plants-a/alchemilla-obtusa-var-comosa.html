Alchemilla obtusa var. comosa

Alchemilla obtusa var. comosa, commonly known as the woolly alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a low-growing rosette of soft, fuzzy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
