Allium kyrenium

Allium kyrenium, commonly known as the Cyrenaica onion, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bulbous root system is similar to other allium species, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Allium kyrenium thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
