Amomum kayserianum

Amomum kayserianum, commonly known as the Kayser's cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by dark red berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
