Astragalus kofensis

Astragalus kofensis, commonly known as Kofen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Astragalus kofensis is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivorous animals.
