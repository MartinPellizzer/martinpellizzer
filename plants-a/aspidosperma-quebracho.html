Aspidosperma quebracho

Aspidosperma quebracho, commonly known as quebracho, is a large evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense, dark-green foliage. The tree produces small, white to greenish flowers and is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture.

It also contains alkaloids, making it significant in traditional medicine and pharmacology.
