Astephanus multiflorus

Astephanus multiflorus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other similar plants. The plant is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Astephanus multiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
