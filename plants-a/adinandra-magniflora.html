Adinandra magniflora

Adinandra magniflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a robust trunk with smooth, greyish bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its leaves are pinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Adinandra magniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
