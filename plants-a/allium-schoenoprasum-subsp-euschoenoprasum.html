Allium schoenoprasum subsp. euschoenoprasum

Allium schoenoprasum subsp.

euschoenoprasum, commonly known as garden chives, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green and have a strong onion-like aroma.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.

This subspecies is closely related to other chive varieties and is often used in cooking for its mild, garlic-like flavor.
