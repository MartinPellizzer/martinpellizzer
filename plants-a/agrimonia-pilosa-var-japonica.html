Agrimonia pilosa var. japonica

Agrimonia pilosa var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese agrimony, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pome, which aids in seed dispersal.

Agrimonia pilosa var. japonica is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
