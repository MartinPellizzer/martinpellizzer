Alstroemeria involucrosa

Alstroemeria involucrosa, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or green markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Alstroemeria involucrosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
