Achimenes hirsuta

Achimenes hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy achimenes, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Achimenes hirsuta prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
