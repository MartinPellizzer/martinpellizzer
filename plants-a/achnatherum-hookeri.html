Achnatherum hookeri

Achnatherum hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, rocky slopes, and alpine environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Achnatherum hookeri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small mammals.
