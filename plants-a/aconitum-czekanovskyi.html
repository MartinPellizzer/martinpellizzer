Aconitum czekanovskyi

Aconitum czekanovskyi, commonly known as the Czekanovsky aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, conical clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Aconitum czekanovskyi is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
