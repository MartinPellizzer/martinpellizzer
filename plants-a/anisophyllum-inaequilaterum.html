Anisophyllum inaequilaterum

Anisophyllum inaequilaterum, commonly known as the unequal-sided fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that have varying lengths and widths along their margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

The botanical features of this fern make it an interesting subject for studying fern morphology and adaptation to humid habitats.
