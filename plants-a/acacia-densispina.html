Acacia densispina

Acacia densispina, commonly known as the dense thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods and are dispersed by animals.

Acacia densispina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
