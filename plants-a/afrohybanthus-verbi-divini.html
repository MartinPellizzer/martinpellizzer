Afrohybanthus verbi-divini

Afrohybanthus verbi-divini, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
