Acalypha indica var. bailloniana

Acalypha indica var. bailloniana, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers within the bract clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
