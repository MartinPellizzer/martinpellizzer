Asclepias nummularia

Asclepias nummularia, commonly known as the milkweed pennywort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, round, green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae), known for its milky sap and ecological importance to monarch butterflies.

Asclepias nummularia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
