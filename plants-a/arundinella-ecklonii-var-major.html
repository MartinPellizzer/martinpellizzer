Arundinella ecklonii var. major

Arundinella ecklonii var. major, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with feathery flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its erosion control and ornamental qualities.
