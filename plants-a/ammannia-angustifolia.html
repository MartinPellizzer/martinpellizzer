Ammannia angustifolia

Ammannia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in moist, sunny environments.

Ammannia angustifolia is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
