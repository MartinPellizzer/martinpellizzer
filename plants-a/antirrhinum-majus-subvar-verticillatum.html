Antirrhinum majus subvar. verticillatum

Antirrhinum majus subvar. verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow and orange. The flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature typical of the plant family Plantaginaceae. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
