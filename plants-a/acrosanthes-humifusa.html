Acrosanthes humifusa

Acrosanthes humifusa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, featuring linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or along streamlines.

Acrosanthes humifusa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
