Anemone whyteana

Anemone whyteana, commonly known as the White Anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone whyteana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
