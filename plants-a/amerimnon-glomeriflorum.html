Amerimnon glomeriflorum

Amerimnon glomeriflorum, commonly known as the clustered-flower amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of small, often red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits called utricles that contain one or two seeds.

Amerimnon glomeriflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
