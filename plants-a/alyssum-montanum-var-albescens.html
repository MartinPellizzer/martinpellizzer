Alyssum montanum var. albescens

Alyssum montanum var. albescens, commonly known as the white alpine alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-green foliage and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Alyssum montanum var. albescens is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to cold climates.
