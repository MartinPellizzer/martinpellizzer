Arctostaphylos canescens f. candissima

Arctostaphylos canescens f. candissima, commonly known as the candissima form of the whiteleaf manzanita, is a subspecies native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a slow growth rate and forms low, spreading shrubs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
