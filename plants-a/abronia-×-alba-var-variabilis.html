Abronia × alba var. variabilis

Abronia × alba var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable sand verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is known for its variability in flower color and form, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
