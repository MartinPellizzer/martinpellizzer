Ardisia viridiflora

Ardisia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered ardisia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a bright red berry, which is edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
