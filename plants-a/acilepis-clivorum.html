Acilepis clivorum

Acilepis clivorum, commonly known as the cliff heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its compact growth form and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions make it well adapted to its natural environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
