Amygdalus sweginzowii

Amygdalus sweginzowii, commonly known as the Sweginzow's almond, is a species of shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hard nuts enclosed in a thick, woody shell. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a fuzzy texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential for genetic research.
