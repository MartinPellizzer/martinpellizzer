Acanthocalycium glaucum

Acanthocalycium glaucum, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue-gray, ribbed stems and spiny areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Acanthocalycium glaucum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
