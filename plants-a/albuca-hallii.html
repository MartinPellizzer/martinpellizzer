Albuca hallii

Albuca hallii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is part of the Asparagaceae family and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the summer months and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Albuca hallii is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
