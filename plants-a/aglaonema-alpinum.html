Aglaonema alpinum

Aglaonema alpinum, commonly known as the Alpine Canna, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright orange or red flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Aglaonema alpinum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden landscapes and indoor cultivation.
