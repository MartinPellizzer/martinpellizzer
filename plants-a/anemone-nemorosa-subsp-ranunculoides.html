Anemone nemorosa subsp. ranunculoides

Anemone nemorosa subsp.

ranunculoides, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It features delicate, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear.

The plant has trifoliate leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky texture. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for naturalized gardens and conservation efforts.
