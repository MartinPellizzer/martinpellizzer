Arisarum vulgare subsp. simorrhinum

Arisarum vulgare subsp.

simorrhinum, commonly known as the common arum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a distinctive inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe, which is characteristic of the Araceae family.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
