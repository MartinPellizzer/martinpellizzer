Avena fatua var. microtricha

Avena fatua var. microtricha, commonly known as slender wild oat, is a grass species characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is an annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields and roadsides, and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which develop into bract-like structures that enclose the seeds. Its leaves are usually shorter and more slender compared to other oat species, and it has a shallow root system that allows it to spread quickly.

This variation of wild oat is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and persistent weed.
