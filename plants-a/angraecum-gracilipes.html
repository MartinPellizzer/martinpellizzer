Angraecum gracilipes

Angraecum gracilipes, commonly known as the slender star orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and often pendulous flower spikes that bear numerous small, star-shaped blossoms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks in the rainforest. Its flowers are pollinated by the giant hawkmoth, which has a long proboscis adapted to reach the nectar deep within the flower.

Angraecum gracilipes is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
