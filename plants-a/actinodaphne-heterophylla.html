Actinodaphne heterophylla

Actinodaphne heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, ranging from elliptical to ovate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and berries, which are an important part of its reproductive cycle. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
