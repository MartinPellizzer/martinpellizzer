Asplenium resectum f. adiantifrons

Asplenium resectum f. adiantifrons, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant exhibits a distinctive silvery hue on its fronds due to the presence of trichomes, which give it a unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens.
