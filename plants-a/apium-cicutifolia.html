Apium cicutifolia

Apium cicutifolia, commonly known as cow parsley or wild carrot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbrella-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a hairy stem and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, oval fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
