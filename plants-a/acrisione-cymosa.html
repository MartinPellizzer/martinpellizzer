Acrisione cymosa

Acrisione cymosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense clusters of blooms.

Acrisione cymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
