Achillea lanata

Achillea lanata, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a soft, hairy texture, and its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Achillea lanata is often used in herbal medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
