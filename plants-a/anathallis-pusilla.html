Anathallis pusilla

Anathallis pusilla, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Anathallis pusilla is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
