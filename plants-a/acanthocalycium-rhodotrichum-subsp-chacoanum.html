Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum subsp. chacoanum

Acanthocalycium rhodotrichum subsp.

chacoanum, commonly known as the Chacoan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense clusters of spines.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
