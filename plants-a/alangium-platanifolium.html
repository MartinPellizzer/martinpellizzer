Alangium platanifolium

Alangium platanifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf alangium, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
