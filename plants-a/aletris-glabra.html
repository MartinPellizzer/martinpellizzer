Aletris glabra

Aletris glabra, commonly known as the smooth aletris, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, white to pale purple flowers.

Aletris glabra is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
