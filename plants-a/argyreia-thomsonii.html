Argyreia thomsonii

Argyreia thomsonii, commonly known as the "Morning Glory of the South," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Argyreia thomsonii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
