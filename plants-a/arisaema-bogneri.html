Arisaema bogneri

Arisaema bogneri, commonly known as the Bogner's arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Arisaema bogneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking foliage.
