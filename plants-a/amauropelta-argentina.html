Amauropelta argentina

Amauropelta argentina, commonly known as the Argentine shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions of South America. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Amauropelta argentina plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
