Acalypha commersoniana var. parvifolia

Acalypha commersoniana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, finely toothed leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
