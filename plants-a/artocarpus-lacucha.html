Artocarpus lacucha

Artocarpus lacucha, commonly known as the Java apple or lacucha, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, spiky fruits. The fruit, known as a "breadfruit," is typically green when immature and turns yellow or orange when ripe. It produces a sweet, starchy pulp that is edible and often used in various culinary preparations.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and timber.
