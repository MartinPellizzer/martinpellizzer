Agrostis pectinata

Agrostis pectinata, commonly known as the silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage and dense, mat-like growth habit, which makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and lawns. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in a silky, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Agrostis pectinata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in natural landscapes.
