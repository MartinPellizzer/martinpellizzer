Aconitum naviculare

Aconitum naviculare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, spire-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be lethal if ingested.
