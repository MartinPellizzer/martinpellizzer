Archidendron glandulosum

Archidendron glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along roadsides.

Its hairy texture and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
