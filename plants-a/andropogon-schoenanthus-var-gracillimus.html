Andropogon schoenanthus var. gracillimus

Andropogon schoenanthus var. gracillimus, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
