Aconitum sukaczevii

Aconitum sukaczevii, commonly known as the Sukaczev's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with deeply lobed segments, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
