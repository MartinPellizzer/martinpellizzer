Apium petroselinum var. variegatum

Apium petroselinum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated celery.

This variant is a cultivated form of celery characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns, featuring white and green stripes. It is typically grown for its ornamental value rather than its edible parts, though the stems can be used in cooking. The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, thriving in temperate regions.

Its foliage is often used in gardens to add visual interest and can also be incorporated into floral arrangements.
