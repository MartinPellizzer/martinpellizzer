Adenophora pereskiifolia var. curvidens

Adenophora pereskiifolia var. curvidens, commonly known as the curly-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy or curled margin. The plant produces bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
