Apargia arenaria

Apargia arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry, open habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt exposure, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Apargia arenaria plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing nectar for pollinators.
