Alvesia tomentosa

Alvesia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly alvesia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Alvesia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
