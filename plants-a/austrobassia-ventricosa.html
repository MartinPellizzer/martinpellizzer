Austrobassia ventricosa

Austrobassia ventricosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, often thornless foliage and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Austrobassia ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
