Anatherum virginicum subvar. glaziovii

Anatherum virginicum subvar. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
