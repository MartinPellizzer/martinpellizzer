Amaranthus cristulatus

Amaranthus cristulatus, commonly known as the crested amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescence that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, greenish or reddish flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Amaranthus cristulatus is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
