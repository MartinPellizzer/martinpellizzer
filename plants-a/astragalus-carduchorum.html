Astragalus carduchorum

Astragalus carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
