Atriplex glauca subsp. palaestina

Atriplex glauca subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as palestine saltbush, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its succulent, gray-green leaves that are adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded lands.

Its robust root system helps prevent soil erosion in harsh climates.
