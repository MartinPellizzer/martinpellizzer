Aster vaseyi

Aster vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that bloom in the fall.

Aster vaseyi is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
