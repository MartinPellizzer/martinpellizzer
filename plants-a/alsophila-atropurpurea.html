Alsophila atropurpurea

Alsophila atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple hickory fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically dark green on the upper surface and a striking purple or reddish hue on the underside, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique coloration and elegant form make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
