Arracacia atropurpurea

Arracacia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple heart, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its striking violet to purple tubular flowers and velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
