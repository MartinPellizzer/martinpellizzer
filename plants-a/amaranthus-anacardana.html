Amaranthus anacardana

Amaranthus anacardana, commonly known as the cardamom amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Amaranthus anacardana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
