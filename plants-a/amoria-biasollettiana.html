Amoria biasollettiana

Amoria biasollettiana, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
