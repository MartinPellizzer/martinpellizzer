Achyrophorus cryptocephalus

Achyrophorus cryptocephalus, commonly known as the black root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, fleshy roots and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
