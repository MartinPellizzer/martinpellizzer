Asarum wulingense

Asarum wulingense, commonly known as Wuling wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a strong, spicy odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various ailments.
