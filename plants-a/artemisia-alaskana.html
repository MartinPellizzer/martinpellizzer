Artemisia alaskana

Artemisia alaskana, commonly known as Alaska wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the circumpolar Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is well adapted to cold climates. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. It produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes.

Artemisia alaskana is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
