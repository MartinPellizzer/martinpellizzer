Astragalus viridiflorus

Astragalus viridiflorus, commonly known as greenflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus viridiflorus is part of the legume family and can fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
