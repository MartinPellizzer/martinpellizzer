Anthurium ophites

Anthurium ophites, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically a striking red or orange color. Anthurium ophites thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
