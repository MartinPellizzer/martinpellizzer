Achillea sieheana

Achillea sieheana, commonly known as Siehe's yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of white to pale yellow, are arranged in flat-topped clusters, making it an attractive addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
