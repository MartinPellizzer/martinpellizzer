Astragalus velenovskyi

Astragalus velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and southern Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.

Astragalus velenovskyi is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
