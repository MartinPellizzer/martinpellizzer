Acacia laccata

Acacia laccata, commonly known as the black wattle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its dark, woody pods that contain seeds, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Acacia laccata is also notable for its use in the timber industry and as a source of natural dyes.
