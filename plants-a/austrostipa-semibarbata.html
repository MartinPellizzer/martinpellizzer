Austrostipa semibarbata

Austrostipa semibarbata, commonly known as the slender bearded grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Austrostipa semibarbata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna in its native range.
