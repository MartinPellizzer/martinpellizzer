Achras excelsa

Achras excelsa, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a large tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small bread loaves. The fruit is rich in carbohydrates and has been a staple food source in many Pacific island cultures.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Moraceae and is closely related to the fig and mulberry trees.
