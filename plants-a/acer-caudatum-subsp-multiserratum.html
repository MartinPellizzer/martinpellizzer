Acer caudatum subsp. multiserratum

Acer caudatum subsp.

multiserratum, commonly known as the multi-toothed maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are finely toothed along the margins.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow furrows that become more pronounced with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
