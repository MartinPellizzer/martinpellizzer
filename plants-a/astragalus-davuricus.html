Astragalus davuricus

Astragalus davuricus, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Astragalus davuricus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
