Avenochloa asiatica

Avenochloa asiatica, commonly known as Asian wild rice, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, two-lobed grains that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Avenochloa asiatica is valued for its potential as a food source and has been studied for its nutritional and ecological benefits.
