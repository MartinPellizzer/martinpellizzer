Aragallus verticillaris

Aragallus verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with whorled leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

The spiral gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
