Aglaia mackiana

Aglaia mackiana, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, glossy green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by ornamental, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
