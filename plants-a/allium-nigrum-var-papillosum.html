Allium nigrum var. papillosum

Allium nigrum var. papillosum, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that are spherical and covered in small, papillate (bump-like) structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
