Astracantha brachyptera

Astracantha brachyptera, commonly known as the short-winged aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and the plant produces small, bract-like structures that give it its scientific name.

Astracantha brachyptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
