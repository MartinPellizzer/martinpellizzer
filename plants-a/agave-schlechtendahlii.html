Agave schlechtendahlii

Agave schlechtendahlii, commonly known as the Blue Agave, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are blue-gray in color, with sharp spines along the edges.

Agave schlechtendahlii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
