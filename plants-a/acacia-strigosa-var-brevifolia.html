Acacia strigosa var. brevifolia

Acacia strigosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive greyish-green color and a rough texture.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sandy soils.
