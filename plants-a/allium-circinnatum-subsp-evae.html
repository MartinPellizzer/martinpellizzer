Allium circinnatum subsp. evae

Allium circinnatum subsp.

evae, commonly known as the Eave's onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, curled inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
