Andropogon chrysatherus

Andropogon chrysatherus, commonly known as golden panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered closely together. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Andropogon chrysatherus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
