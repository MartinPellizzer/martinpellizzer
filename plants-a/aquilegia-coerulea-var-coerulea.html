Aquilegia coerulea var. coerulea

Aquilegia coerulea var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
