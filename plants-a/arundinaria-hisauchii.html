Arundinaria hisauchii

Arundinaria hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or hisauchii bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of many grass species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria hisauchii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
