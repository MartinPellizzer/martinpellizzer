Amblyanthera fluminensis var. claussenii

Amblyanthera fluminensis var. claussenii, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. It produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
