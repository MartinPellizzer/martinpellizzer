Actinidia chinensis var. chinensis

Actinidia chinensis var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow-green berries that ripen to red. The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a cold winter dormancy period.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in the production of kiwifruit.
