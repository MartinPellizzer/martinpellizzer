Asclepias katangensis

Asclepias katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga milkweed, is a species native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, orange-to-red flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators. It belongs to the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae, and is known for its latex sap.

This species plays a crucial role in supporting local ecosystems, particularly for monarch butterflies and other pollinators.
