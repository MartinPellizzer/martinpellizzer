Astragalus hostilis

Astragalus hostilis, commonly known as the hostile milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, small trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas due to its deep root system.

Astragalus hostilis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
