Avena barbata subsp. segetalis

Avena barbata subsp.

segetalis, commonly known as the Mediterranean wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and fields.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, and it produces small, two-rowed grains.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
