Azalea veitchiana

Azalea veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Azalea veitchiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
