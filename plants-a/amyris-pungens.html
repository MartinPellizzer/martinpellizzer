Amyris pungens

Amyris pungens, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring dense, aromatic foliage with a spicy, resinous scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Amyris pungens is well-suited for USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9, making it a hardy and adaptable choice for landscaping.
