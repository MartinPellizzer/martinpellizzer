Asteriscium crambe

Asteriscium crambe, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Asteriscium crambe is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
