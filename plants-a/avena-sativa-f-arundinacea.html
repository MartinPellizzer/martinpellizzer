Avena sativa f. arundinacea

Avena sativa f. arundinacea, commonly known as soft oatgrass, is a variant of the common oat plant characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It features narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with florets that are typically wind-pollinated. This plant is often found in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a hollow stem, which contribute to its adaptability in various soil conditions.

The plant is notable for its potential use in erosion control and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.
