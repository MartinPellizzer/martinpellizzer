Allium maximowiczii var. shibutsuense

Allium maximowiczii var. shibutsuense, commonly known as the Japanese garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are often used in culinary applications, similar to garlic and onions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and early spring bloom.
