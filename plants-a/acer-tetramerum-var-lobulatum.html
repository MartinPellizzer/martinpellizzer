Acer tetramerum var. lobulatum

Acer tetramerum var. lobulatum, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lobed leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique foliage and bark make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its habitat.
