Arabis hirsuta var. nipponica

Arabis hirsuta var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
