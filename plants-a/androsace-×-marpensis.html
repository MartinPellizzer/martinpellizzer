Androsace × marpensis

Androsace × marpensis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
