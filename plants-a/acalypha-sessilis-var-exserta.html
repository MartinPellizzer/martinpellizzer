Acalypha sessilis var. exserta

Acalypha sessilis var. exserta, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in some regions.
