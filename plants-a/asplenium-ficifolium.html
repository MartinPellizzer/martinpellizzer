Asplenium ficifolium

Asplenium ficifolium, commonly known as the fig-leaf spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves that are typically clustered in a rosette formation. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Asplenium ficifolium plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
