Acacia serra

Acacia serra, commonly known as the serrated acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in pairs. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers that are typically clustered in rounded or cylindrical inflorescences. Acacia serra is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
