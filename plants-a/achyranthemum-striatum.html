Achyranthemum striatum

Achyranthemum striatum, commonly known as the striped spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinct reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid or semi-arid gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in xeriscapes.
