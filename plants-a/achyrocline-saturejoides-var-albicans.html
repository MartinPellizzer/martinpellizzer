Achyrocline saturejoides var. albicans

Achyrocline saturejoides var. albicans, commonly known as the white achyrocline, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant is notable for its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
