Astragalus leiophysa

Astragalus leiophysa, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple and are clustered in small, spike-like inflorescences.

Astragalus leiophysa plays a role in stabilizing soils and provides forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
