Aridaria splendens

Aridaria splendens, commonly known as the golden spire cactus, is a succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, slender stem with a distinctive golden hue, which becomes more pronounced in full sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its environment. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
