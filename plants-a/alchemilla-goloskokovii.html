Alchemilla goloskokovii

Alchemilla goloskokovii, commonly known as the golden tutsan, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are covered with fine, white hairs that give them a silvery appearance.

Alchemilla goloskokovii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
