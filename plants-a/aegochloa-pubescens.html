Aegochloa pubescens

Aegochloa pubescens, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Aegochloa pubescens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
