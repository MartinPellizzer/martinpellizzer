Anemone punica

Anemone punica, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a short, sturdy stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically solitary and have a distinctive, lantern-like shape with numerous petals.

Anemone punica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
