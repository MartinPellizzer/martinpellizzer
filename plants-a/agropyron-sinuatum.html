Agropyron sinuatum

Agropyron sinuatum, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.

Agropyron sinuatum is also valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop.
