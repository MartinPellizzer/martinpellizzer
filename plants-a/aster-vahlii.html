Aster vahlii

Aster vahlii, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Aster vahlii is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
