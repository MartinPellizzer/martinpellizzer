Amaryllis andersonii

Amaryllis andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's amaryllis, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
