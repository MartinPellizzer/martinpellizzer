Aster eminens

Aster eminens, commonly known as the tall aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall, covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Aster eminens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
