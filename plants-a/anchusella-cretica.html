Anchusella cretica

Anchusella cretica, commonly known as the Cretan anchusa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Anchusella cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
