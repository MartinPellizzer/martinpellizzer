Anthurium sodiroanum

Anthurium sodiroanum, commonly known as the Sodiro's anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often deep green with a glossy texture, and the spathe can range in color from red to orange, depending on the variety.

Anthurium sodiroanum thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.
