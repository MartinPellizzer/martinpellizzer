Abutilon grevilleanum

Abutilon grevilleanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Abutilon grevilleanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
