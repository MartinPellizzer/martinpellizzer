Astragalus aytatchii

Astragalus aytatchii, commonly known as the Aytatchi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has hairy stems and produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus aytatchii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
