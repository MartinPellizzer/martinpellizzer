Actinostemon cuneatus var. latifolius

Actinostemon cuneatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf actinostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
