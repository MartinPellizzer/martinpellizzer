Adiantum formosissimum

Adiantum formosissimum, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive black or dark brown midrib.

Adiantum formosissimum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
