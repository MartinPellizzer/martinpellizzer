Artemisia turczaninoviana var. altaica

Artemisia turczaninoviana var. altaica, commonly known as the Altai wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves contain essential oils and are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
