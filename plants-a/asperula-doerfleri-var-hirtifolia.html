Asperula doerfleri var. hirtifolia

Asperula doerfleri var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest undergrowth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
