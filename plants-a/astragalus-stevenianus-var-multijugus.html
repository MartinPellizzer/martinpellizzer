Astragalus stevenianus var. multijugus

Astragalus stevenianus var. multijugus, commonly known as the Steven's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
