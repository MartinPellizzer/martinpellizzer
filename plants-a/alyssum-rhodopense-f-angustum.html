Alyssum rhodopense f. angustum

Alyssum rhodopense f. angustum, commonly known as narrowleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
