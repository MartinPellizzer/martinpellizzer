Ageratum corymbosum var. longipetiolatum

Ageratum corymbosum var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled ageratum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are ovate to lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the petioles are notably long, giving the plant its distinguishing feature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

This variety is also popular in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.
