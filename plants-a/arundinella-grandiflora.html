Arundinella grandiflora

Arundinella grandiflora, commonly known as the giant bamboo or large-flowered bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that are often arranged in panicle-like structures. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Arundinella grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
