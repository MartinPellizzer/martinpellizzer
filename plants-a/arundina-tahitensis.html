Arundina tahitensis

Arundina tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Arundina tahitensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense stands.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
