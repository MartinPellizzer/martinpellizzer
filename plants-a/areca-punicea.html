Areca punicea

Areca punicea, commonly known as the red areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into red, fleshy fruits. Areca punicea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
