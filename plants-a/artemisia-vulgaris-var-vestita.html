Artemisia vulgaris var. vestita

Artemisia vulgaris var. vestita, commonly known as velvetleaf wormwood, is a variant of the common wormwood plant.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its aromatic leaves contain volatile oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its medicinal and historical uses, it has been cultivated for its therapeutic properties.
