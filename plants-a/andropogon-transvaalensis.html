Andropogon transvaalensis

Andropogon transvaalensis, commonly known as Transvaal grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its erect, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are often green or purplish in color.

Andropogon transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
