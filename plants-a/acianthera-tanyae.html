Acianthera tanyae

Acianthera tanyae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acianthera, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in a spike, which are typical of many orchid species. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
