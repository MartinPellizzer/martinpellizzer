Austrodanthonia laevis

Austrodanthonia laevis, commonly known as silky grass or silky wallaby grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in a clump form, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Austrodanthonia laevis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
