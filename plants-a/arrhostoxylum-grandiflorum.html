Arrhostoxylum grandiflorum

Arrhostoxylum grandiflorum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
