Achasma sphaerocephalum var. grandiflorum

Achasma sphaerocephalum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant globe lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, globe-shaped inflorescences that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a sweet, spicy fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialty collections.
