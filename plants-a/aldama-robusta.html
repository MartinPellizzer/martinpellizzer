Aldama robusta

Aldama robusta, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by legume-like pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
