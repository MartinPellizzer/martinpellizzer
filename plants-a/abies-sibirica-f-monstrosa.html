Abies sibirica f. monstrosa

Abies sibirica f. monstrosa, commonly known as the Siberian fir dwarf form, is a compact variant of the Siberian fir, characterized by its slow growth and dense, conical shape.

It features dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more clustered than those of the typical species. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit with a cushion-like appearance, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative specimen. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, preferring cooler climates.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging garden conditions.

This variety is commonly found in the taiga biome, where it thrives in cold, boreal climates with well-drained soils. It is particularly associated with the Altai Mountains and the Ural Mountains, where it grows at high altitudes. The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in these regions.

Its distribution is largely restricted to the northern and eastern parts of its native range.
