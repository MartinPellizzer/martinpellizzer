Agouticarpa hirsuta

Agouticarpa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy agouti fruit, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small shrub characterized by its hairy, woody fruits that resemble the seeds of the agouti rodent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is typically dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in seed propagation.
