Allium viridialbum

Allium viridialbum, commonly known as the green leek, is a perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of long, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears a single, spherical cluster of small, white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland areas.

Allium viridialbum is valued for its edible leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a culinary herb.
