Abutilon rufinerve var. latifolium

Abutilon rufinerve var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its growth habit is similar to that of a shrub or small tree, with multiple stems and a bushy appearance.
