Amaranthus interruptus

Amaranthus interruptus, commonly known as the redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which become more prominent as the plant matures. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences. This plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional cuisines.
