Aurinia microcarpa

Aurinia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited aurinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains several tiny seeds.

Aurinia microcarpa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
