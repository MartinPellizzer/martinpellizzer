Anagallis arvensis var. tridyma

Anagallis arvensis var. tridyma, commonly known as the trailing strawberry.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, bright red berries. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
