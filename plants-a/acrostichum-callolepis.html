Acrostichum callolepis

Acrostichum callolepis, commonly known as the broadleaf tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and basket weaving in some cultures.
