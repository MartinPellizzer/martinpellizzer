Aria caloneura

Aria caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant's leaves are elongated and have a distinctive narrowing toward the tip, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its edible tubers, which are similar in texture to yams and can be cooked in various ways.
