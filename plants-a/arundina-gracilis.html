Arundina gracilis

Arundina gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo or bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like in appearance. Arundina gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
