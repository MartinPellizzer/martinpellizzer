Ageratum houstonianum f. versicolor

Ageratum houstonianum f. versicolor, commonly known as the multicolored ageratum, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, blue, purple, and pink. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is aromatic and often covered in fine hairs, which can help deter some pests.

This cultivar is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
