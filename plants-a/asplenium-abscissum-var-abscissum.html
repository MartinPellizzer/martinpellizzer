Asplenium abscissum var. abscissum

Asplenium abscissum var. abscissum, commonly known as the broken fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fan-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous small, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its ornamental value in shaded gardens.
