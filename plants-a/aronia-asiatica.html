Aronia asiatica

Aronia asiatica, commonly known as the Asian chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is known for its high antioxidant content, particularly anthocyanins, which give the berries their vibrant color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Aronia asiatica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
