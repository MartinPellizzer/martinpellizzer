Aster homolepis

Aster homolepis, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, white to pale purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
