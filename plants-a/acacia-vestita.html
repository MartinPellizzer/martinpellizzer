Acacia vestita

Acacia vestita, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Acacia vestita is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
