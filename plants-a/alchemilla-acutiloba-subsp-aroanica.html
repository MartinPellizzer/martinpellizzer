Alchemilla acutiloba subsp. aroanica

Alchemilla acutiloba subsp.

aroanica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
