Arnica parryi var. crinita

Arnica parryi var. crinita, commonly known as the hairy arnica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sensitive species in its native range.
