Aristolochia costaricensis var. zamorensis

Aristolochia costaricensis var. zamorensis, commonly known as the Zamora's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
