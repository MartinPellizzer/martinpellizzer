Astrophytum buenekeri

Astrophytum buenekeri, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on its surface. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. Its spines are typically short and white, providing protection from herbivores.

Blooming in spring, it produces yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and moths.
