Aklema cotinifolia

Aklema cotinifolia, commonly known as the American beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking clusters of violet-pink berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and long-lasting fruit, which attract birds and add visual interest throughout the seasons.
