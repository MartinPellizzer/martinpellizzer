Alophia stricta

Alophia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf lily, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking white or pale pink flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

Alophia stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
