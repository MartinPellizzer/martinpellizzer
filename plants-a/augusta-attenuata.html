Augusta attenuata

Augusta attenuata, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It features delicate, elongated leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a slight fragrance. The plant grows up to 3 feet tall, with stems that are slender and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Augusta attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
