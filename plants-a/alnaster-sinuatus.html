Alnaster sinuatus

Alnaster sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuous alnaster, is a lesser-known plant species with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the family Alnasteraceae and is characterized by its unique, sinuous leaf patterns that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, while its leaves are broad and serrated.

Alnaster sinuatus is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance.
