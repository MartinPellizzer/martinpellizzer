Aerva triangularifolia

Aerva triangularifolia, commonly known as Indian nettle or arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found growing in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The leaves are edible and have been used in various culinary and therapeutic applications.
