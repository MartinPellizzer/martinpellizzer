Adesmia clarenii

Adesmia clarenii, commonly known as the Clarenii burro bush, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals and insects.
