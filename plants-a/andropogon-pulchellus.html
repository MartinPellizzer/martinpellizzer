Andropogon pulchellus

Andropogon pulchellus, commonly known as purple false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that contribute to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.

Andropogon pulchellus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
