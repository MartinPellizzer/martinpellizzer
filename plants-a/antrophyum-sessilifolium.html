Antrophyum sessilifolium

Antrophyum sessilifolium, commonly known as the giant dayflower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant features a robust, upright stem with broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive purple throat, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Antrophyum sessilifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its dramatic floral display.
