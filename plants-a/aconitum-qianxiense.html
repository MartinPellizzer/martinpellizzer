Aconitum qianxiense

Aconitum qianxiense, commonly known as Qianxiense Monkshood, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its roots are tuberous.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is handled with caution and is not commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes.
