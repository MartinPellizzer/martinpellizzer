Allium fedschenkoanum var. elatum

Allium fedschenkoanum var. elatum, commonly known as the giant bulb onion, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces clusters of small, spherical, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

The botanical features of this plant make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and botanical collections.
