Adesmia unifoliolata

Adesmia unifoliolata, commonly known as the single leaf legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a single compound leaf and small, pea-like flowers. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Adesmia unifoliolata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to improve soil fertility.
