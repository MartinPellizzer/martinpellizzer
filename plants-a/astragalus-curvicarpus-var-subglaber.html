Astragalus curvicarpus var. subglaber

Astragalus curvicarpus var. subglaber, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to low moisture conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
