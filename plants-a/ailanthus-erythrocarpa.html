Ailanthus erythrocarpa

Ailanthus erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited ailanthus, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant foliage and unique fruit.
