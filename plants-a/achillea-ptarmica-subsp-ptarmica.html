Achillea ptarmica subsp. ptarmica

Achillea ptarmica subsp.

ptarmica, commonly known as common yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a robust root system and upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish-white hairs.

The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are pinnately divided into narrow, linear segments with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and disturbed areas.
