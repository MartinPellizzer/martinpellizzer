Acaciella painteri var. painteri

Acaciella painteri var. painteri, commonly known as the Painted Acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of tiny leaflets, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
