Argentina achillea

Argentina achillea, commonly known as the Andean yarrow, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Argentina achillea is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
