Argemone mexicana

Argemone mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, spiny stems. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid regions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid spread and potential toxicity.
