Acanthopanax spinifolius

Acanthopanax spinifolius, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap or five-leaf aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, often with serrated edges and a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Acanthopanax spinifolius is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
