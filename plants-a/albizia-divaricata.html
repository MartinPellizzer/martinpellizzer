Albizia divaricata

Albizia divaricata, commonly known as the yellow albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer.

Its pods are long and slender, containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and soil enrichment benefits.
