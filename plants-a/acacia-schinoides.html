Acacia schinoides

Acacia schinoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Acacia schinoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance and efficient water use.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
