Andesanthus lepidotus var. congestiflorus

Andesanthus lepidotus var. congestiflorus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
