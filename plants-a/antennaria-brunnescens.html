Antennaria brunnescens

Antennaria brunnescens, commonly known as the brown sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats. Its flowers are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter and are arranged in dense, compact inflorescences.

Antennaria brunnescens is a member of the rockcress family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
