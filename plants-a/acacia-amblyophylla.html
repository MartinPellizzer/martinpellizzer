Acacia amblyophylla

Acacia amblyophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
