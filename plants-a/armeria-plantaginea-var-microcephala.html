Armeria plantaginea var. microcephala

Armeria plantaginea var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf sea thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to coastal areas of southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has succulent, silver-gray foliage that is adapted to tolerate salty air and coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often used in coastal landscaping.

Its compact size and hardy nature make it an attractive and resilient addition to gardens.
