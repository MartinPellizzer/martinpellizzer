Ailuroglossum triste

Ailuroglossum triste, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant has delicate, hairy leaves and produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
