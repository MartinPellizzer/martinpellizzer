Anil indica

Anil indica, commonly known as Indian spinach or Malabar spinach, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves. It is a fast-growing plant that can reach lengths of up to 10 feet.

Anil indica is valued for its nutritional content and is used in various traditional cuisines.
