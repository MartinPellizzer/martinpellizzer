Achillea × rozaliae

Achillea × rozaliae, commonly known as the Rozalia yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects like butterflies and bees.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders in well-drained soil.
