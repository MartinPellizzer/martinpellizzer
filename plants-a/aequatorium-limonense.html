Aequatorium limonense

Aequatorium limonense, commonly known as the lemon-scented aquilegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with a distinctive lemon-like fragrance, which is released when the foliage is crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
