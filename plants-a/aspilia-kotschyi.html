Aspilia kotschyi

Aspilia kotschyi, commonly known as the African torch flower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Aspilia kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
