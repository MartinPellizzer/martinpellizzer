Agave deserti

Agave deserti, commonly known as the desert agave, is a succulent plant native to the deserts of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white or yellow flowers, typically blooming only once in its lifetime. Its leaves contain a high concentration of sap, which is used in the production of agave nectar and tequila.

Agave deserti is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
