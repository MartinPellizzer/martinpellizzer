Acrostylia

Acrostylia, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are soft to the touch, giving the plant its common name. Acrostylia species are often used in ornamental horticulture for their attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
