Allium subbiflorum

Allium subbiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures similar to garlic, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Allium subbiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal practices.
