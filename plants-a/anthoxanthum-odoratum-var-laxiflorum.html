Anthoxanthum odoratum var. laxiflorum

Anthoxanthum odoratum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including dry and disturbed sites. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
