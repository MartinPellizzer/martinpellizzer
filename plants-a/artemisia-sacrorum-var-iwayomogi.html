Artemisia sacrorum var. iwayomogi

Artemisia sacrorum var. iwayomogi, commonly known as Japanese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its distinctive scent and therapeutic uses.
