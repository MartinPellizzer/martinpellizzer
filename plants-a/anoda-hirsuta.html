Anoda hirsuta

Anoda hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

Anoda hirsuta is often used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
