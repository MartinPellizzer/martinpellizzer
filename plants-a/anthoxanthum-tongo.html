Anthoxanthum tongo

Anthoxanthum tongo, commonly known as the tongo grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Anthoxanthum tongo is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
