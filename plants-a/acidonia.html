Acidonia

Acidonia, commonly known as the Indian almond tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Acidonia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for both decorative and practical purposes.
