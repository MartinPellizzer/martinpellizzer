Alpinia velutina

Alpinia velutina, commonly known as the velvet ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma.
