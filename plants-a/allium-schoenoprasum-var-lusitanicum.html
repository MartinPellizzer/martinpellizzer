Allium schoenoprasum var. lusitanicum

Allium schoenoprasum var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian chive, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in umbels. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species.

Its leaves and flowers are edible and have a mild garlic or onion-like flavor.
