Andinopuntia lagopus

Andinopuntia lagopus, commonly known as the Andean flamingo cactus, is a species of cactus native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has a columnar stem with spines and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in cool, arid environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and intense sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
