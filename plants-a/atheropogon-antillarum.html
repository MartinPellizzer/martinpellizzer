Atheropogon antillarum

Atheropogon antillarum, commonly known as the Caribbean grass or Antillean grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing coastal environments and preventing erosion.
