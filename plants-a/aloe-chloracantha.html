Aloe chloracantha

Aloe chloracantha, commonly known as the yellow aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow-orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its purported medicinal properties.

Aloe chloracantha is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
