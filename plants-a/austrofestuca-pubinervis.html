Austrofestuca pubinervis

Austrofestuca pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy nervegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense tussocks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the veins, giving it its scientific name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Austrofestuca pubinervis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
