Acer palmatum f. rubrum

Acer palmatum f. rubrum, commonly known as the red maple, is a cultivar of the Japanese maple known for its striking red foliage.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
