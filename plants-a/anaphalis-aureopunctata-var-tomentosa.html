Anaphalis aureopunctata var. tomentosa

Anaphalis aureopunctata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish-white woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family, valued for both its ornamental qualities and ecological role.
