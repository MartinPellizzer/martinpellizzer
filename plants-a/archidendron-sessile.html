Archidendron sessile

Archidendron sessile, commonly known as the sessile legume, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale green flowers. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Archidendron sessile is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
