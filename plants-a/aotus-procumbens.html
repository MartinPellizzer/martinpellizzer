Aotus procumbens

Aotus procumbens, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that typically grows along the ground, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its rapid growth habit.

Aotus procumbens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
