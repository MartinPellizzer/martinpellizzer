Aster ferrugineus

Aster ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a rusty-colored down, giving it its common name.

Aster ferrugineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
