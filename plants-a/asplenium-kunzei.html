Asplenium kunzei

Asplenium kunzei, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to spread slowly through vegetative propagation.

Asplenium kunzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
