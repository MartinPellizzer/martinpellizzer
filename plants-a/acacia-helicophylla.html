Acacia helicophylla

Acacia helicophylla, commonly known as the spiral-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-arranged phyllodes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia helicophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in ecological restoration projects.
