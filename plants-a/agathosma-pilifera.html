Agathosma pilifera

Agathosma pilifera, commonly known as the Cape spearmint, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in its native habitat.
