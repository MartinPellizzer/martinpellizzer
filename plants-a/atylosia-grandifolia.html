Atylosia grandifolia

Atylosia grandifolia, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a compact rosette growth habit. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Atylosia grandifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
