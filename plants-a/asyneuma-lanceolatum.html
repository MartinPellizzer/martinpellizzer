Asyneuma lanceolatum

Asyneuma lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
