Astrocaryum acanthopodium

Astrocaryum acanthopodium, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti, is a palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, black, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
