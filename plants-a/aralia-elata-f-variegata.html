Aralia elata f. variegata

Aralia elata f. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Aralia, is a cultivar of the Aralia elata species, native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.
