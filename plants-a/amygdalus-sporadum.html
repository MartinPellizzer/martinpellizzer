Amygdalus sporadum

Amygdalus sporadum, commonly known as the sporadic almond, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of small, edible nuts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated edge, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Amygdalus sporadum is considered a botanically interesting but endangered species.
