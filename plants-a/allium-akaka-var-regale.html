Allium akaka var. regale

Allium akaka var. regale, commonly known as the royal onion, is a striking perennial plant native to Hawaii.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy purple or pink flower clusters, which resemble a crown, giving it its regal appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale purple bulbs that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique beauty and cultural significance, it is often cultivated as both an ornamental and a culinary plant.
