Andropogon sorghum subvar. splendidus

Andropogon sorghum subvar. splendidus, commonly known as the purpletop sorghum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple inflorescence, which is a key feature for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both garden and landscape settings.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a robust stem that supports its tall, showy flower heads.
