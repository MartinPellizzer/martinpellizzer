Astragalus rudbaricus

Astragalus rudbaricus, commonly known as the Rudbar milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late summer. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

Astragalus rudbaricus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
