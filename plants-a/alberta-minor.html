Alberta minor

Alberta minor, commonly known as the prairie smoke, is a flowering plant native to the central and western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery plumes that resemble smoke rising from the ground, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Alberta minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
