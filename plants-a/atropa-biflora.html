Atropa biflora

Atropa biflora, commonly known as deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its dark purple berries and bell-shaped flowers. The plant contains tropane alkaloids, such as atropine and scopolamine, which are highly poisonous to humans and animals. Its leaves and berries are often used in traditional medicine, though with extreme caution due to their toxicity.

Despite its harmful properties, it has historical significance in medicine, poisons, and folklore.
