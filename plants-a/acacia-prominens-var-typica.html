Acacia prominens var. typica

Acacia prominens var. typica, commonly known as the typical wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated phyllodes that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
