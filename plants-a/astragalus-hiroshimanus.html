Astragalus hiroshimanus

Astragalus hiroshimanus, commonly known as Hiroshima milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
