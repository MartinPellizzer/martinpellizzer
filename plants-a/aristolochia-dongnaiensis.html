Aristolochia dongnaiensis

Aristolochia dongnaiensis, commonly known as the Dong Nai arrow vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a vine or a horn. The plant is notable for its aromatic, often fragrant blossoms that attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on the growth conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
