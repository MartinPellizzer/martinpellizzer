Amelanchier canadensis var. obovalis

Amelanchier canadensis var. obovalis, commonly known as the oval serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers, attractive foliage, and fruit-bearing capabilities.
