Agrostis gigantea subsp. glaucescens

Agrostis gigantea subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as blue tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
