Aconitum limprichtii

Aconitum limprichtii, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
