Acer caudatum var. multiserratum

Acer caudatum var. multiserratum, commonly known as the multilobed maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are finely toothed along the margins. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange samaras that mature in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming furrowed with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
