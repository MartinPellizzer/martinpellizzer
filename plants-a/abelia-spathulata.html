Abelia spathulata

Abelia spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Abelia spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
