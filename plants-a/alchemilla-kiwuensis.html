Alchemilla kiwuensis

Alchemilla kiwuensis, commonly known as the African lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla kiwuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
