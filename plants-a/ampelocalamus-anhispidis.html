Ampelocalamus anhispidis

Ampelocalamus anhispidis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved reed, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
