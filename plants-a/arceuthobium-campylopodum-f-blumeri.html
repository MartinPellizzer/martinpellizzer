Arceuthobium campylopodum f. blumeri

Arceuthobium campylopodum f. blumeri, commonly known as the Blumer's dwarf broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique mechanism for seed dispersal, where the seeds are propelled by the explosive release of the fruit. It is often found in disturbed or open habitats, such as meadows and pastures.
