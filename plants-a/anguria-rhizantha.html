Anguria rhizantha

Anguria rhizantha, commonly known as the root vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, hairy stems that produce small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its tuberous roots, which are often harvested for their medicinal properties. Anguria rhizantha thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique root structure and potential pharmacological applications.
