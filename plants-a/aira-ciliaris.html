Aira ciliaris

Aira ciliaris, commonly known as slenderawn, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, with pale green to brown bracts.

Aira ciliaris is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
