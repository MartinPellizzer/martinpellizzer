Aulomyrcia prunifolia

Aulomyrcia prunifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Aulomyrcia prunifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
