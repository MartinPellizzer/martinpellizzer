Acer cinerascens f. medicum

Acer cinerascens f. medicum, commonly known as the medicinal maple, is a rare and distinctive variant of the smoke tree maple.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, dark green leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and unique foliage, it is often sought after by horticulturists and botanists alike.
