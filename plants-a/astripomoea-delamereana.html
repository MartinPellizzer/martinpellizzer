Astripomoea delamereana

Astripomoea delamereana, commonly known as Delamere's asters, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, grassy habitats and is known for its showy, violet-blue to purple flowers. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine.

Astripomoea delamereana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
