Artemisia rubripes

Artemisia rubripes, commonly known as rough tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Artemisia rubripes is known for its strong, camphor-like scent and has been used traditionally for various medicinal purposes.
