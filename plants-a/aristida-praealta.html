Aristida praealta

Aristida praealta, commonly known as the tall grass or tall lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Aristida praealta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
