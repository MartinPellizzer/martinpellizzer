Alsine pinifolia

Alsine pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that resemble pine needles. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by tiny, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique morphology, it is an important component of desert ecosystems.
