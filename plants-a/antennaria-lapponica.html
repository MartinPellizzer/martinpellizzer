Antennaria lapponica

Antennaria lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
