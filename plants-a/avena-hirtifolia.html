Avena hirtifolia

Avena hirtifolia, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its fibrous roots make it a valuable plant for erosion control and soil stabilization.

Avena hirtifolia is also used in some traditional medicinal practices for its potential health benefits.
