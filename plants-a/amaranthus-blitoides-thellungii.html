Amaranthus blitoides thellungii

Amaranthus blitoides thellungii, commonly known as the silverleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and erect, branched stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
