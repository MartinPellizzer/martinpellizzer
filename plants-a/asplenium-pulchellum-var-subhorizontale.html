Asplenium pulchellum var. subhorizontale

Asplenium pulchellum var. subhorizontale, commonly known as the horizontal maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horizontally spreading fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant features delicate, dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid indoor setting.
