Adeliopsis

Adeliopsis, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica and the southern tip of South America.

It is a small, low-growing perennial that thrives in harsh, cold climates with minimal soil. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are often hidden among its dense, hairy foliage. Adeliopsis has adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme seasonal variations.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an important subject in studies of plant adaptation to extreme environments.
