Adiantum fournieri

Adiantum fournieri, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fan-shaped fronds with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are bi-directionally branching, and the sori, where spores are produced, are located on the underside of the leaflets.

Adiantum fournieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
