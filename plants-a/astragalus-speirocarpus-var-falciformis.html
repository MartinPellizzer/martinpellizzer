Astragalus speirocarpus var. falciformis

Astragalus speirocarpus var. falciformis, commonly known as the falcate milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
