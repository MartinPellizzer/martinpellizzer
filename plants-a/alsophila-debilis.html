Alsophila debilis

Alsophila debilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish outer layer. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that grow in a spiral pattern around the trunk. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Alsophila debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
