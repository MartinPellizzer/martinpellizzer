Acis fabrei

Acis fabrei, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Acis fabrei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
