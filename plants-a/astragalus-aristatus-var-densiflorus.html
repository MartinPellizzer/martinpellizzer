Astragalus aristatus var. densiflorus

Astragalus aristatus var. densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its compound leaves are pinnately divided, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
