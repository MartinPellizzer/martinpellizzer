Amaryllis atamasco var. minor

Amaryllis atamasco var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a compact, flowering bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, growing from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
