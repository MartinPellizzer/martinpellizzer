Ascoglossum calopterum

Ascoglossum calopterum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
