Achilus siamensis

Achilus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam Achillea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
