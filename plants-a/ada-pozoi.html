Ada pozoi

Ada pozoi, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and fern-like, deeply lobed leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant contains toxic compounds and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its historical use in herbal medicine has been noted, though its poisonous nature limits modern applications.
