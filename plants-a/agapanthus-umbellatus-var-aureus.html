Agapanthus umbellatus var. aureus

Agapanthus umbellatus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden agapanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, spherical clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
