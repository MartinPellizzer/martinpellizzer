Achillea grandifolia

Achillea grandifolia, commonly known as mountain yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves and its clusters of small, pale yellow to white ray flowers. The plant produces numerous slender, branching stems that rise from the base, bearing the flower heads at the tips. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
