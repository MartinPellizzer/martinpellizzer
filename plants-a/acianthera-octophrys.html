Acianthera octophrys

Acianthera octophrys, commonly known as the eight-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and several vertical pseudobulbs. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in two rows along the stem.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with intricate patterns, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
