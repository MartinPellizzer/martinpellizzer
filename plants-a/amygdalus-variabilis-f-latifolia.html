Amygdalus variabilis f. latifolia

Amygdalus variabilis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf almond.

This variant is characterized by its broader, more rounded leaves compared to the typical almond species. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces small white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
