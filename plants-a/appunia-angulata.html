Appunia angulata

Appunia angulata, commonly known as the angular acanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, angular leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Appunia angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
