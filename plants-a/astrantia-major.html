Astrantia major

Astrantia major, commonly known as the greater masterwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear large, showy, pinkish-red to white flowers in late summer. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Astrantia major is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
