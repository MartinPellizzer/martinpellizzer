Achyranthes fruticosa var. pubescens

Achyranthes fruticosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

The hairy false brome is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
