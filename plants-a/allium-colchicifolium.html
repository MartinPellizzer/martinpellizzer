Allium colchicifolium

Allium colchicifolium, commonly known as the sea garlic or coastal garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to salty, windy environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in Mediterranean cuisine.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
