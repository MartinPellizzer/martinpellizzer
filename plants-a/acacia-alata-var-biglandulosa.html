Acacia alata var. biglandulosa

Acacia alata var. biglandulosa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The species is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
