Ananthopus cordatus

Ananthopus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette, with the lower leaves often curling inward, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically green or brown, and it is often found in shallow water or moist soil. Ananthopus cordatus has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
