Agrimonia pilosa subsp. dahurica

Agrimonia pilosa subsp.

dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian agrimony, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its fruits are dry, indehiscent capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

Agrimony is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
