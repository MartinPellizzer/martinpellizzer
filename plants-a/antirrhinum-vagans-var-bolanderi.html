Antirrhinum vagans var. bolanderi

Antirrhinum vagans var. bolanderi, commonly known as the Bolander's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
