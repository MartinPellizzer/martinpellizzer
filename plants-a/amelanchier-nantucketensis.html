Amelanchier nantucketensis

Amelanchier nantucketensis, commonly known as Nantucket serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Amelanchier nantucketensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
