Anoda zuccagnii

Anoda zuccagnii, commonly known as the Zuccagni's bluemink, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Anoda zuccagnii is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
