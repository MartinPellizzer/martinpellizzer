Andropogon pseudoschinzii

Andropogon pseudoschinzii, commonly known as the false Schinz grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Andropogon pseudoschinzii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
