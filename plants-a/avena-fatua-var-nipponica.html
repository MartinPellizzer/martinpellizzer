Avena fatua var. nipponica

Avena fatua var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese wild oat, is a variant of the wild oat species native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to beige flowers. Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed soils and agricultural fields.

This variant is often considered a weed due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
