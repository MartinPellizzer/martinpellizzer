Aster lateriflorus var. grandis

Aster lateriflorus var. grandis, commonly known as the showy aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its showy, violet-blue to lavender flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.
