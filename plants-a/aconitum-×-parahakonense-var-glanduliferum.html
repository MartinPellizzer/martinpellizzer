Aconitum × parahakonense var. glanduliferum

Aconitum × parahakonense var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular-flowered aconitum, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, blue to purple hooded flowers. The plant features palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are tuberous and somewhat fibrous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
