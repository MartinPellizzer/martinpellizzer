Avena glomerata

Avena glomerata, commonly known as slender oat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelet clusters. The plant produces small, two-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Avena glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
