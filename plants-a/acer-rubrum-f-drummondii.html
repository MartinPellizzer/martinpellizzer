Acer rubrum f. drummondii

Acer rubrum f. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's red maple, is a distinctive cultivar of the red maple tree.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the autumn, which is more intense than that of the typical red maple. The leaves are typically smaller and more elongated compared to the standard species, giving it a unique appearance. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in eastern North America.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
