Agoseris aurantiaca var. purpurea

Agoseris aurantiaca var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
