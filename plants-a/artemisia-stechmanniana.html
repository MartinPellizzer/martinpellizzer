Artemisia stechmanniana

Artemisia stechmanniana, commonly known as Stechmann's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal remedies.
