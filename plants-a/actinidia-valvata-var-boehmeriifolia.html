Actinidia valvata var. boehmeriifolia

Actinidia valvata var. boehmeriifolia, commonly known as the Bohmeriifolia Actinidia, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by edible, fuzzy berries that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in native landscaping.
