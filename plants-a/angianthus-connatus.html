Angianthus connatus

Angianthus connatus, commonly known as the slender angianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Angianthus connatus is also valued for its resilience in arid conditions.
