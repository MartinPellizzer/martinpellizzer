Alangium villosum subsp. pilosum

Alangium villosum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy alangium, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
