Agapetes affinis

Agapetes affinis, commonly known as the silverleaf agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath family, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

Agapetes affinis is often found in cool, moist forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
