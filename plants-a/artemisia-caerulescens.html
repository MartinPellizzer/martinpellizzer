Artemisia caerulescens

Artemisia caerulescens, commonly known as blue artemisia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the branches. Artemisia caerulescens is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
