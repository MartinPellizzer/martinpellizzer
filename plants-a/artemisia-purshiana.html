Artemisia purshiana

Artemisia purshiana, commonly known as Pursh's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a distinct, sharp scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that develop into fluffy, brown seed clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Artemisia purshiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
