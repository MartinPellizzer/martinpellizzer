Actinea chrysanthemoides

Actinea chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the golden star, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that resemble chrysanthemums, arranged in a star-like pattern. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are divided into numerous small, pointed lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Actinea chrysanthemoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
