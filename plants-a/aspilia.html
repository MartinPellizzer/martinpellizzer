Aspilia

Aspilia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Africa, with species found across various regions, including Madagascar and the Arabian Peninsula. The plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers with a distinctive yellow center and white or pink petals. Aspilia species are typically shrubs or herbs, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.
