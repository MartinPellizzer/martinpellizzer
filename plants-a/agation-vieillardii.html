Agation vieillardii

Agation vieillardii, commonly known as the Vieillard's agate plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
