Aconitum tokusanense

Aconitum tokusanense, commonly known as Tokusan's aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in fine hairs.

Aconitum tokusanense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
