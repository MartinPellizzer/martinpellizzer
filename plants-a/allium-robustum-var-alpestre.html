Allium robustum var. alpestre

Allium robustum var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine leek, is a hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
