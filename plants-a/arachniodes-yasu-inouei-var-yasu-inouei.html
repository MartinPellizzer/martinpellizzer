Arachniodes yasu-inouei var. yasu-inouei

Arachniodes yasu-inouei var. yasu-inouei, commonly known as the Japanese spider fern, is a species native to Japan.

It is a fern characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble spider webs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, finger-like segments that give it a intricate appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
