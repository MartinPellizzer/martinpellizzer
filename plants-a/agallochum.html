Agallochum

Agallochum, commonly known as the gharial tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, lowland areas and thrives in humid climates. Its seeds are dispersed by animals that eat its fruit, contributing to its spread.

The botanical features of Agallochum make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
