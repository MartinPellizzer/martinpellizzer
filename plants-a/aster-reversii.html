Aster reversii

Aster reversii, commonly known as the reverse aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Aster reversii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
