Alocasia principiculus

Alocasia principiculus, commonly known as the "Princess Alocasia," is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, featuring a deep green color with a glossy texture. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces small, pale green to white flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

Alocasia principiculus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to purify indoor air.
