Acer × neglectum var. annae

Acer × neglectum var. annae, commonly known as the Ann Arbor maple, is a hybrid species within the maple family (Aceraceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring lobed leaves with a serrated margin that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow catkins that appear in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
