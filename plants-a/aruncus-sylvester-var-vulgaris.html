Aruncus sylvester var. vulgaris

Aruncus sylvester var. vulgaris, commonly known as the mountain gothwart, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its tall, branched stems that bear clusters of white to pinkish bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of large, lobed leaves that are deeply veined and often have a reddish or bronze tinge.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add texture and color to shaded landscapes.
