Aulomyrcia ramulosa var. colorata

Aulomyrcia ramulosa var. colorata, commonly known as the colorful flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like inflorescences that display a range of colors from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer.
