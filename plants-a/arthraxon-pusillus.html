Arthraxon pusillus

Arthraxon pusillus, commonly known as the minute grass.

It is a small annual grass species native to Australia, characterized by its tiny stature and dense growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its size and adaptability, it plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is not widely cultivated.
