Ardisia colorata var. salicifolia

Ardisia colorata var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf beautyberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
