Ada glumacea

Ada glumacea, commonly known as the spike grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and the inflorescence is usually tall and spike-like.

Ada glumacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
