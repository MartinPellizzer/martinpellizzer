Aquilegia vulgaris monstr. stellata

Aquilegia vulgaris monstr.

stellata, commonly known as the starry cress, is a striking perennial plant characterized by its showy, spurred flowers. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces bright red or purple flowers with elongated, spurred petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration.
