Allium antonii-bolosii subsp. eivissanum

Allium antonii-bolosii subsp.

eivissanum, commonly known as the Eivissanum garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers.

The plant is characterized by its distinct, aromatic bulbs that are often used in local culinary traditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic lineage and ecological role in Mediterranean ecosystems.
