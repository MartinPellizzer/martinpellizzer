Acacia decipiens var. elongata

Acacia decipiens var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
