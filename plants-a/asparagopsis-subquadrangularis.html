Asparagopsis subquadrangularis

Asparagopsis subquadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular asparagus, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry conditions with its water-storing tissues.

Its botanical features make it a resilient plant suitable for xerophytic environments.
