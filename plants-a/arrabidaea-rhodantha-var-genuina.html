Arrabidaea rhodantha var. genuina

Arrabidaea rhodantha var. genuina, commonly known as the Cape iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems produce clusters of delicate, iris-like blooms.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
