Agrostis laxissima

Agrostis laxissima, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has fine, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Agrostis laxissima is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
