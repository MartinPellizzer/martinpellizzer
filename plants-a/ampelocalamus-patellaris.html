Ampelocalamus patellaris

Ampelocalamus patellaris, commonly known as the Patellar reed, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous spikelets that are clustered at the tips.

Ampelocalamus patellaris plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
