Anacyclus × malvesiensis

Anacyclus × malvesiensis, commonly known as the hybrid sunflower, is a composite plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Anacyclus polyanthemus and Helianthus annuus, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
