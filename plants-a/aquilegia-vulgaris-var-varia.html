Aquilegia vulgaris var. varia

Aquilegia vulgaris var. varia, commonly known as the variable columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to red, often with a distinctive upward-curving shape. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is typically found in woodland edges and meadows, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden cultivation and ecological studies.
