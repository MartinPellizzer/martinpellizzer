Antholyza watsonia

Antholyza watsonia, commonly known as Watson's antholyza, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

Antholyza watsonia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
