Alyssum murale var. chrysanthum

Alyssum murale var. chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden alyssum, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is drought-tolerant and attracts beneficial insects like bees and ladybugs.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
