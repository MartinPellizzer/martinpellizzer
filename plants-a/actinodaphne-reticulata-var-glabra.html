Actinodaphne reticulata var. glabra

Actinodaphne reticulata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth actinodaphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
