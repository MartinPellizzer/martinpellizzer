Astragalus leiocarpus

Astragalus leiocarpus, commonly known as the smoothpod milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the United States, particularly found in the Great Plains and western regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to greenish flowers. The plant produces small, smooth pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus leiocarpus is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
