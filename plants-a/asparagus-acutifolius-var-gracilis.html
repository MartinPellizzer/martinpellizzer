Asparagus acutifolius var. gracilis

Asparagus acutifolius var. gracilis, commonly known as slender asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that produce small, white or greenish-white flowers in early spring. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance. Its berries are typically red or black and contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
