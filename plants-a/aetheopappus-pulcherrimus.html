Aetheopappus pulcherrimus

Aetheopappus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the beautiful globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spherical seed pods that split open when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
