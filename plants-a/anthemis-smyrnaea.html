Anthemis smyrnaea

Anthemis smyrnaea, commonly known as the Smyrna chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are hairy and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
