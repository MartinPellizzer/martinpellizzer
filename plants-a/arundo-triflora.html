Arundo triflora

Arundo triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Arundo triflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
