Asarina petrophila

Asarina petrophila, commonly known as the mountain asarum, is a rare flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and distinctive, aromatic foliage.
