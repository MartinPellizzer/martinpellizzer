Acanthopanax baviensis

Acanthopanax baviensis, commonly known as Bavi's spurge.

It is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its woody structure and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
