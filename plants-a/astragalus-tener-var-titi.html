Astragalus tener var. titi

Astragalus tener var. titi, commonly known as the Titi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to harsh environments, possessing deep root systems to access water and nutrients.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
