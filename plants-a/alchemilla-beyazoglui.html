Alchemilla beyazoglui

Alchemilla beyazoglui, commonly known as Turkish Lady's Mantle, is a perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that give them a distinctive, almost velvety appearance.

Alchemilla beyazoglui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.
