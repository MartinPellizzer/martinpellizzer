Angraecum dorotheae

Angraecum dorotheae, commonly known as the Dorothea orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescence bearing numerous fragrant, white flowers with prominent yellow lips. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific nocturnal moths, showcasing an intricate co-evolutionary relationship.

Angraecum dorotheae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
