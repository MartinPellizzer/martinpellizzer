Acer campestre f. platypterum

Acer campestre f. platypterum, commonly known as the flat-leaf field maple, is a cultivar of the European field maple.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smaller and flatter than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that disperse by wind. It is a hardy, deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
