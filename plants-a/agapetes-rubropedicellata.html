Agapetes rubropedicellata

Agapetes rubropedicellata, commonly known as the red-stalked agapetes, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. This evergreen shrub is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and produces berries that are edible for certain bird species.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
