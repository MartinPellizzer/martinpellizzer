Allium iranshahrii

Allium iranshahrii, commonly known as the Iranian onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional Iranian cuisine for their pungent flavor.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Allium species such as garlic and leeks, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sunlight.
