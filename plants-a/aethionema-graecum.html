Aethionema graecum

Aethionema graecum, commonly known as Greek candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its compact size and ornamental value.

Aethionema graecum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
