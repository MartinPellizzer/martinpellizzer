Armeniaca cathayana

Armeniaca cathayana, commonly known as the Chinese peach or Chinese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits that are edible but have a bitter taste due to the presence of amygdalin. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
