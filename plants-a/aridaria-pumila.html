Aridaria pumila

Aridaria pumila, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its resilience and low maintenance make it a popular choice for landscaping in desert regions.
