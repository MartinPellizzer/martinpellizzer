Apocynum eximium

Apocynum eximium, commonly known as the Eastern dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in summer, which are followed by cylindrical, green berries that turn red as they mature. Its roots contain toxic compounds, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.

Apocynum eximium is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
