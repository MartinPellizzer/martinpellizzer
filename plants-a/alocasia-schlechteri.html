Alocasia schlechteri

Alocasia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spathe and spadix. Alocasia schlechteri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
