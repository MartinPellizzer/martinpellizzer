Aconitum napellus nothosubsp. polatschekii

Aconitum napellus nothosubsp.

polatschekii, commonly known as the Polatschek's monkshood, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Aconitum napellus and Aconitum carpathicum. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and is native to the Carpathian Mountains. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
