Asperula matrisylva

Asperula matrisylva, commonly known as the European sweetbox, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its berries are small, red, and edible.

Asperula matrisylva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
