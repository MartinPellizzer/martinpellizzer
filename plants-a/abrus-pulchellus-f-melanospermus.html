Abrus pulchellus f. melanospermus

Abrus pulchellus f. melanospermus, commonly known as the blackseed velvet tare, is a variant of the velvet tare plant characterized by its dark-colored seeds.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seeds, which are encased in a bright red aril, are notable for their contrasting black seeds and are sometimes used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
