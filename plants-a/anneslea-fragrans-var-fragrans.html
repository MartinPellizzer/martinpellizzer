Anneslea fragrans var. fragrans

Anneslea fragrans var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant anneslea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom during the summer months and are known for their sweet, spicy aroma.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
