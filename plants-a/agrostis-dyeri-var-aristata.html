Agrostis dyeri var. aristata

Agrostis dyeri var. aristata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
