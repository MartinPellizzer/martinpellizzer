Acianthera raduliglossa

Acianthera raduliglossa, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and scent.
