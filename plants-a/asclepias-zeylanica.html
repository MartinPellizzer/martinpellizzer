Asclepias zeylanica

Asclepias zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, bean-like seed pods. It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Asclepias zeylanica also serves as a host plant for the monarch butterfly.
