Allohemia purpurea

Allohemia purpurea, commonly known as the purple alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine white pubescence.

Allohemia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
