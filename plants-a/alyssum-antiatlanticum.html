Alyssum antiatlanticum

Alyssum antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Canary Islands alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Alyssum antiatlanticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
