Aristida elytrophoroides

Aristida elytrophoroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences with bristly spikelets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
