Ammannia capitellata subsp. lupembensis

Ammannia capitellata subsp.

lupembensis, commonly known as the African blushing root, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, red or purple roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
