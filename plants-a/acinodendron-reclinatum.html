Acinodendron reclinatum

Acinodendron reclinatum, commonly known as the hanging acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
