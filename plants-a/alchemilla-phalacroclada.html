Alchemilla phalacroclada

Alchemilla phalacroclada, commonly known as the rock alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a waxy, silver-green surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Alchemilla phalacroclada is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
