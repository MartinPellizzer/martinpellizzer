Asteriscus

Asteriscus, commonly known as the starwort, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, star-shaped flowers that often form dense clusters, giving them a distinctive appearance. They are native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and Africa, and many species thrive in dry, rocky habitats. Asteriscus species typically have narrow, linear leaves and produce small, inconspicuous fruits called achenes.

They are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
