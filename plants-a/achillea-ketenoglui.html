Achillea ketenoglui

Achillea ketenoglui, commonly known as the Ketenoglui yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
