Acyntha massae

Acyntha massae, commonly known as the Massa thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive lobed shape.

Acyntha massae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
