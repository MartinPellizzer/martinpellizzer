Asyneuma canescens var. foliosum

Asyneuma canescens var. foliosum, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, and its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its botanical features include alternate, lance-shaped leaves and a branching structure that supports its showy inflorescence.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
