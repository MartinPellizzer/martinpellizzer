Aegilops squarrosa var. pubescens

Aegilops squarrosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its fibrous roots and drought-resistant nature make it a resilient species in various soil types.
