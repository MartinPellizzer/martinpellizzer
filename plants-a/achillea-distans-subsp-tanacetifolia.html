Achillea distans subsp. tanacetifolia

Achillea distans subsp.

tanacetifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features basal rosettes of linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long flowering period make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
