Averrhoa acutangula

Averrhoa acutangula, commonly known as the acerola cherry, is a tropical fruit-bearing shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red fruits with a tart, acidic flavor. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often used in juices, supplements, and traditional medicine.
