Araiostegia hopei

Araiostegia hopei, commonly known as the Hope's rock-rush, is a member of the Xerophytes family, native to South Africa.

It is a small, mat-forming succulent that thrives in arid and rocky environments. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, and its leaves are reduced to scales, a common adaptation in xerophytic plants. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and succulent collections.
