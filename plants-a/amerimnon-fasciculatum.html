Amerimnon fasciculatum

Amerimnon fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered dwarf sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, spike-like clusters of flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dense colonies.
