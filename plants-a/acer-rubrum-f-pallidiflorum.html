Acer rubrum f. pallidiflorum

Acer rubrum f. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered red maple, is a rare and ornamental variant of the red maple tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast strikingly with its deep green foliage. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils. It typically grows as a medium-sized tree, reaching heights of 15 to 30 feet.

Due to its unique floral display, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value.
