Acacia kraussiana

Acacia kraussiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in winter. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
