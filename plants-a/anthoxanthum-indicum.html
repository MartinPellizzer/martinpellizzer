Anthoxanthum indicum

Anthoxanthum indicum, commonly known as Indian oats or false oat, is a perennial grass native to Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Botanically, it is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
