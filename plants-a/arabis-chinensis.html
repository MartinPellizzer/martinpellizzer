Arabis chinensis

Arabis chinensis, commonly known as Chinese rockcress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many economically important crops. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a slightly hairy texture.
