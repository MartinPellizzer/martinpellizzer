Antidesma polyanthum

Antidesma polyanthum, commonly known as the golden antidesma, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red or orange berries. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

Antidesma polyanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
