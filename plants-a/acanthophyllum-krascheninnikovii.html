Acanthophyllum krascheninnikovii

Acanthophyllum krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's thornsteppe, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, succulent appearance with dense clusters of spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves that help conserve water.
