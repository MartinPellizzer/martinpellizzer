Acinos alpinus var. hirsutus

Acinos alpinus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hirsute alpine acino, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against cold temperatures. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
