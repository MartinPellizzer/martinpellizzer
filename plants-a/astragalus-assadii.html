Astragalus assadii

Astragalus assadii, commonly known as Assad's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
