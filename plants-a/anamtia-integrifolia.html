Anamtia integrifolia

Anamtia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed anamtia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Anamtia integrifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
