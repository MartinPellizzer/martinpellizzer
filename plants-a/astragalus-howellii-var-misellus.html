Astragalus howellii var. misellus

Astragalus howellii var. misellus, commonly known as Howell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
