Arabis rugosa

Arabis rugosa, commonly known as the rough rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes. Arabis rugosa is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

It blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch of white to the landscape.
