Adiantum capillus-veneris var. grande

Adiantum capillus-veneris var. grande, commonly known as the "Maiden's Tears," is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lacy appearance.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in humid forests. Its fronds are typically green, with a distinctive central vein and a soft, feathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
