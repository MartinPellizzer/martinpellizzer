Arachnorchis macroclavia

Arachnorchis macroclavia, commonly known as the broad-lobed spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like floral structure and its unique, broad-lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, preferring rich, organic soil. Its flowers are usually violet to purple in color, with a distinct, elongated spur.

Arachnorchis macroclavia is a federally endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect its dwindling habitat.
