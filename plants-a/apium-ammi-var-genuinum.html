Apium ammi var. genuinum

Apium ammi var. genuinum, commonly known as cow parsley, is a biennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbel-shaped flower clusters and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, feather-like leaves. Its flowers are white or pale green, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
