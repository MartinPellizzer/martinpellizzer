Asparagus brevifolius

Asparagus brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact, cylindrical clusters called panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
