Ambrosia longifolia

Ambrosia longifolia, commonly known as the slender ambrosia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Ambrosia longifolia typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and it is known for its ability to spread rapidly in certain environments.
