Antiostelma lantsangense

Antiostelma lantsangense, commonly known as the Lantsang wild rice, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters at the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Antiostelma lantsangense plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
