Acacia diadenia

Acacia diadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Acacia diadenia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small fauna.
