Arachis ipaensis

Arachis ipaensis, commonly known as the peanut plant, is a leguminous species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that produces underground pods containing edible seeds, known as peanuts. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic peanut pods. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enriches the soil.

The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is cultivated globally for its high nutritional value.
