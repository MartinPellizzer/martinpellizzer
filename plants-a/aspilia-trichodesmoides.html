Aspilia trichodesmoides

Aspilia trichodesmoides, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, woody structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used for construction and tool-making.
