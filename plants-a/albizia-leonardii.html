Albizia leonardii

Albizia leonardii, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

Albizia leonardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
