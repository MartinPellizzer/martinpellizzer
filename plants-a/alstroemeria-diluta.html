Alstroemeria diluta

Alstroemeria diluta, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in a clump-forming habit, with narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are known for their long-lasting bloom and are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Alstroemeria diluta prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
