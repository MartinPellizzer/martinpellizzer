Arundinaria vicina

Arundinaria vicina, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Arundinaria vicina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
