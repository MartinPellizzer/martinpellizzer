staufferi, commonly known as the Stauffer's shield fern, is a rare and hybrid fern species native to specific regions of the southeastern United States. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or acidic soils.
