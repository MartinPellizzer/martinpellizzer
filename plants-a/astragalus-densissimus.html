Astragalus densissimus

Astragalus densissimus, commonly known as the dense astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus densissimus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
