Antirrhinum rhodium

Antirrhinum rhodium, commonly known as the silver snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Antirrhinum rhodium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
