Asarum kurosawae

Asarum kurosawae, commonly known as Kurosawa's wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Asarum kurosawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
