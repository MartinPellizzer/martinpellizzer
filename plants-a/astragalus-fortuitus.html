Astragalus fortuitus

Astragalus fortuitus, commonly known as the lucky pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Astragalus fortuitus is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
