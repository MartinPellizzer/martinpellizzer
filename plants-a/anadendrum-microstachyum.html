Anadendrum microstachyum

Anadendrum microstachyum, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.

The bark is smooth and gray, and the plant is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
