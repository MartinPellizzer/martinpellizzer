Acer dasycarpum f. weiri

Acer dasycarpum f. weiri, commonly known as the Weiri maple, is a unique variant of the genus Acer, characterized by its distinct foliage and growth habits.

This plant is native to specific regions in China and is valued for its ornamental qualities, particularly its vibrant autumn colors. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin, and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
