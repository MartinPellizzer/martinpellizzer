Arachis retusa

Arachis retusa, commonly known as the hairy peanut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
