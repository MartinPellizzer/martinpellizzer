Adiantum triquetrum

Adiantum triquetrum, commonly known as the fan-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble the shape of a fan, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky soils or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive triangular shape at the base of each frond.

Adiantum triquetrum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
