Ardisia volubilis

Ardisia volubilis, commonly known as the creeping ardisia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Following flowering, it develops red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it a popular choice for ground cover in shaded gardens.
