Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. vernicosenitens

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

vernicosenitens, commonly known as the glossy burrplant, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, spiny fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating, giving them a shiny appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glossy foliage and unique fruit structure compared to other Acaena species.
