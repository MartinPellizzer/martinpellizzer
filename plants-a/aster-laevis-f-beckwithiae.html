Aster laevis f. beckwithiae

Aster laevis f. beckwithiae, commonly known as the Beckwith's smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing a showy display of blue to purple petals.
