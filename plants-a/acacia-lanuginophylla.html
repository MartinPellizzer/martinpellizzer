Acacia lanuginophylla

Acacia lanuginophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish, silky-hairy stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Acacia lanuginophylla plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
