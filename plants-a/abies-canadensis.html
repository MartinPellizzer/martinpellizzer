Abies canadensis

Abies canadensis, commonly known as the balsam fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color, and distinctive cylindrical cones. The tree produces a fragrant resin that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal and mixed forests.

The balsam fir plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
