Achillea pellati

Achillea pellati, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

Achillea pellati is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
