Armeria plantaginea var. scorzonerifolia

Armeria plantaginea var. scorzonerifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of silver-white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in mild climates.
