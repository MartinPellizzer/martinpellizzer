Acanthopanax acerifolius

Acanthopanax acerifolius, commonly known as the maple-leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that resemble those of maple trees, with elongated leaflets and serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
