Astragalus lazicus proles foucaudii

Astragalus lazicus proles foucaudii, commonly known as the Foucaud's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

It is often found in rocky soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
