Arisaema hungyaense

Arisaema hungyaense, commonly known as the Korean arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of arum species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema hungyaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in traditional Korean horticulture.
