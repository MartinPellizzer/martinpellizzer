Alliaria golodhara

Alliaria golodhara, commonly known as the Indian garlic or wild garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, aromatic leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its bulbs are edible and have a pungent, garlic-like flavor, making it a valued herb in traditional cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it well-adapted to its natural habitat, with its growth pattern and chemical composition aiding in its survival.
