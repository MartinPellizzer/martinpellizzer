Allowissadula lozanoi

Allowissadula lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozano's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Allowissadula lozanoi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
