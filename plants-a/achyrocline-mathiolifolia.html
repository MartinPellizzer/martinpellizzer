Achyrocline mathiolifolia

Achyrocline mathiolifolia, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, pink to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
