Actinea leptoclada

Actinea leptoclada, commonly known as the slender actinia, is a rare and delicate species of sea anemone found in the cold waters of the North Atlantic.

It is characterized by its long, slender tentacles and a pale, translucent body that allows for the observation of its internal structures. This species typically inhabits rocky substrates and is often found in association with other marine organisms. Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to its fragile nature and specific habitat requirements, Actinea leptoclada is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
