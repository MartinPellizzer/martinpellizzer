Asarum porphyronotum var. porphyronotum

Asarum porphyronotum var. porphyronotum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a deep purple underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
