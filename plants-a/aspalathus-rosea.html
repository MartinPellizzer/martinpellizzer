Aspalathus rosea

Aspalathus rosea, commonly known as the rosy bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Aspalathus rosea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
