Achyronia ulicina

Achyronia ulicina, commonly known as the green velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types make it a common weed in agricultural settings.

The species is also known for its adaptability to disturbed environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
