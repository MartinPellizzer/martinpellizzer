Acalypha schiedeana var. genuina

Acalypha schiedeana var. genuina, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins that contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by three-lobed, red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
