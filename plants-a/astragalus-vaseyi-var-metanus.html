Astragalus vaseyi var. metanus

Astragalus vaseyi var. metanus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval leaflets arranged in pairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
