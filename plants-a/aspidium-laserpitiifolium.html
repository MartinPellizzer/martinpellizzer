Aspidium laserpitiifolium

Aspidium laserpitiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to substrates such as soil or rock.

The spores are released from sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
