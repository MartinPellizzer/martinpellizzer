Anathallis seriata

Anathallis seriata, commonly known as the slender spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that are often clustered in a loose inflorescence. Anathallis seriata is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without being parasitic.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
