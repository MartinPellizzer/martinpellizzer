Arisaema amurense f. denticulatum

Arisaema amurense f. denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, a typical feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in exotic foliage.
