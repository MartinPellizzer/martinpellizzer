Anemone narcissiflora var. villosissima

Anemone narcissiflora var. villosissima, commonly known as the woolly anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust root system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
