Amethystanthus inflexus

Amethystanthus inflexus, commonly known as the violet flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, violet-hued flowers that resemble flames, with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of showy, fragrant flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.
