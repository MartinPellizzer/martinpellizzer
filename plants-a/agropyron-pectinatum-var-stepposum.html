Agropyron pectinatum var. stepposum

Agropyron pectinatum var. stepposum, commonly known as the steppe wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and steppe environments.

Agropyron pectinatum var. stepposum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
