Actinodaphne myriantha

Actinodaphne myriantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
