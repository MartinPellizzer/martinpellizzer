Acer saccharinum subsp. floridanum

Acer saccharinum subsp.

floridanum, commonly known as the Florida maple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and distinctive palmate leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically important species in its native range.
