Arctostaphylos patula subsp. gankinii

Arctostaphylos patula subsp.

gankinii, commonly known as the Gankin's manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often with a reddish or bronze hue.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, shiny seeds.
