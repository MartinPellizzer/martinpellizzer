Ageratina pomaderrifolia

Ageratina pomaderrifolia, commonly known as white crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that resemble a crown, which are composed of small, tubular florets. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Ageratina pomaderrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
