Aster barcinonensis

Aster barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcinonese aster, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers display a mix of blue and purple hues.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
