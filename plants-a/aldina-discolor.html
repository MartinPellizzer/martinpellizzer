Aldina discolor

Aldina discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf Aldina, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
