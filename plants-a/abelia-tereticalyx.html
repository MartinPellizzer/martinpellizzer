Abelia tereticalyx

Abelia tereticalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems and small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Abelia tereticalyx is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
