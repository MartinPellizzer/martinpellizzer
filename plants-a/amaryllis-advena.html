Amaryllis advena

Amaryllis advena, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, and its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves. Amaryllis advena prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
