Acetosa woodii

Acetosa woodii, commonly known as the Wood's penny-cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

Acetosa woodii is also noted for its medicinal properties, having been used in traditional herbal remedies.
