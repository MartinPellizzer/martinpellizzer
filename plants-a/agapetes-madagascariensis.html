Agapetes madagascariensis

Agapetes madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical with a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

Agapetes madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
