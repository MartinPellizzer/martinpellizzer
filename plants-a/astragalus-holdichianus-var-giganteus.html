Astragalus holdichianus var. giganteus

Astragalus holdichianus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its habitat and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
