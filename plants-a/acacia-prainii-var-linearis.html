Acacia prainii var. linearis

Acacia prainii var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spherical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
