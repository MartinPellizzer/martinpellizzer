Astragalus missouriensis var. cuspidocarpus

Astragalus missouriensis var. cuspidocarpus, commonly known as the Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
