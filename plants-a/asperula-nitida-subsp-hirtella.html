Asperula nitida subsp. hirtella

Asperula nitida subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the hairy woodruff, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are covered with fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
