Amomum bimaculatum

Amomum bimaculatum, commonly known as the two-spotted cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to dark brown, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Amomum bimaculatum is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal applications.
