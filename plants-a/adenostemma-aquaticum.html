Adenostemma aquaticum

Adenostemma aquaticum, commonly known as water bladdernut, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and often have a pale yellow to greenish color.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal in aquatic environments.
