Alsophila compta

Alsophila compta, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a smooth, dark green surface and a leathery texture.

Alsophila compta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
