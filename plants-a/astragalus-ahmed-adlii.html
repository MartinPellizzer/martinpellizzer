Astragalus ahmed-adlii

Astragalus ahmed-adlii, commonly known as the Ahmed-Adli milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its scientific classification and ecological significance make it an important subject in botanical studies.
