Allium trifurcatum

Allium trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-lobed garlic or clustered garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of strap-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are bulbous and have a strong garlic aroma, often used in traditional medicine.

Allium trifurcatum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
