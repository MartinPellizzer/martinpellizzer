Azadirachta excelsa

Azadirachta excelsa, commonly known as the Indian banyan, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its extensive network of aerial roots that form a dense canopy, creating a unique structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood and is often found in tropical rainforests.
