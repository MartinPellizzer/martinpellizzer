Anemone caerulea var. typica

Anemone caerulea var. typica, commonly known as the blue anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
