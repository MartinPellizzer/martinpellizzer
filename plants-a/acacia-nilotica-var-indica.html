Acacia nilotica var. indica

Acacia nilotica var. indica, commonly known as the Indian acacia or khair, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned branches and bipinnate leaves that are compound and feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by long, flat pods containing hard, angular seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree has been traditionally used for its timber, gum, and medicinal properties.
