Asplenium pseudocaudatum

Asplenium pseudocaudatum, commonly known as the false cow's tail, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually short and clustered, with a dark green color and a leathery texture.

Asplenium pseudocaudatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
