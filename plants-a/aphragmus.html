Aphragmus

Aphragmus, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species that are native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They produce small, yellow flowers that are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Aphragmus species are often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including meadows and roadsides.
