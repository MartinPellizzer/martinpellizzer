Artemisia waltonii var. waltonii

Artemisia waltonii var. waltonii, commonly known as the Walton's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, downy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
