Achras obovata

Achras obovata, commonly known as the African silver cluster tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmate leaves that are obovate in shape. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible for some animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
