Aristolochia brunneomaculata

Aristolochia brunneomaculata, commonly known as the brown-spotted Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serpentine-shaped leaves and fragrant, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
