Amorphophallus venustus

Amorphophallus venustus, commonly known as the "beautiful corpse," is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a phallic shape, which is why it earned its dramatic common name. The plant produces a single, large, white spathaceous flower that opens at night, emitting a strong, unpleasant odor to attract pollinators. Its underground corm is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Amorphophallus venustus is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
