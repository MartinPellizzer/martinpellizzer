Acaena obtusiloba

Acaena obtusiloba, commonly known as the soft burr plant, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, fluffy, spherical fruits known as achenes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Acaena obtusiloba is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
