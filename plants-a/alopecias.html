Alopecias

Alopecias, commonly known as goatweed, are a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

They are characterized by their upright growth habit and often have small, narrow leaves. The flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences and are often greenish or reddish in color. These plants are native to various regions in the Americas and are adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Alopecias are sometimes used in traditional medicine and have ornamental value in gardens.
