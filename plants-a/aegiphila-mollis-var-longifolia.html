Aegiphila mollis var. longifolia

Aegiphila mollis var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, open woodlands.

Its aromatic foliage and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
