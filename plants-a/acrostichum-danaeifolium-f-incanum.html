Acrostichum danaeifolium f. incanum

Acrostichum danaeifolium f. incanum, commonly known as the silver frond fern, is a varietal form of the common brake fern.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy appearance due to dense, fine hairs covering the fronds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a leathery texture.

This fern is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive, textured foliage.
