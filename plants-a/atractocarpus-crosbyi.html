Atractocarpus crosbyi

Atractocarpus crosbyi, commonly known as Crosby's sycamore, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers and fruiting structures called syconia, which are typical of the family. The bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.
