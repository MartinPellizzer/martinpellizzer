Ampelamus volubilis

Ampelamus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ampelamus volubilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
