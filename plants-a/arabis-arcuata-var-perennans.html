Arabis arcuata var. perennans

Arabis arcuata var. perennans, commonly known as the creeping rockcress, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions of Europe. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
