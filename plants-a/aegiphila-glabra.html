Aegiphila glabra

Aegiphila glabra, commonly known as smooth aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Aegiphila glabra is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
