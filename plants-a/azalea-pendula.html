Azalea pendula

Azalea pendula, commonly known as the weeping azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of bright pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Azalea pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
