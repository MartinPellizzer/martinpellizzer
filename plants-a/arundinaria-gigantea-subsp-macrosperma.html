Arundinaria gigantea subsp. macrosperma

Arundinaria gigantea subsp.

macrosperma, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its long, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant produces large, elongated inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
