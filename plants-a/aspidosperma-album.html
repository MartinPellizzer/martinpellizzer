Aspidosperma album

Aspidosperma album, commonly known as the white aspidosperma, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing elongated seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
