Andropogon campyloracheus

Andropogon campyloracheus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and blue-green foliage, which turn golden in the fall. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. It plays a vital role in maintaining soil stability and supporting biodiversity in its native range.

Andropogon campyloracheus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscaping.
