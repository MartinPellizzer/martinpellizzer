Amomum rivale

Amomum rivale, commonly known as black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by dark, aromatic berries that contain the spice used in cooking. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like cineole and limonene, contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Amomum rivale is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
