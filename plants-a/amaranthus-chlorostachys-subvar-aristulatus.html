Amaranthus chlorostachys subvar. aristulatus

Amaranthus chlorostachys subvar. aristulatus, commonly known as the silverleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and upright, branched stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them their distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its potential as a forage crop and for its historical use in traditional cuisines.
