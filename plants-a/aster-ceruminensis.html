Aster ceruminensis

Aster ceruminensis, commonly known as the wax aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy, glandular leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Aster ceruminensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
