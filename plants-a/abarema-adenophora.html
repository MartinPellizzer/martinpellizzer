Abarema adenophora

Abarema adenophora, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into multiple small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Abarema adenophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
