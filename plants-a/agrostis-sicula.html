Agrostis sicula

Agrostis sicula, commonly known as Sicilian bentgrass, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a prominent midrib, while the inflorescence consists of a single spikelet.

Agrostis sicula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience to environmental stress.
