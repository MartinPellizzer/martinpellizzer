Arnopogon dalechampii

Arnopogon dalechampii, commonly known as the Dalechamp's tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

Arnopogon dalechampii is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
