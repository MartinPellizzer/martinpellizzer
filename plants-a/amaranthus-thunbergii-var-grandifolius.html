Amaranthus thunbergii var. grandifolius

Amaranthus thunbergii var. grandifolius, commonly known as the Japanese amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with large, lance-shaped leaves that are often bright green. The plant produces showy, feather-like inflorescences in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are highly ornamental. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
