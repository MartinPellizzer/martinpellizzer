Anemonoides trifolia subsp. albida

Anemonoides trifolia subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in regions with temperate climates.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is relatively hardy in various soil types.
