Acer pictum f. tricuspis

Acer pictum f. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed maple, is a striking ornamental tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a naturally occurring variant of the striped maple, characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and have a tricuspate shape. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with a yellow or orange margin, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. In the fall, the foliage turns a brilliant red or orange, enhancing its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its unique leaf structure and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
