Albizia mossambicensis

Albizia mossambicensis, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its pods are elongated, woody, and contain several seeds.

Albizia mossambicensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
