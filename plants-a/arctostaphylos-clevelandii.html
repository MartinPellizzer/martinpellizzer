Arctostaphylos clevelandii

Arctostaphylos clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and dry summers.

Cleveland manzanita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
