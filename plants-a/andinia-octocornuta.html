Andinia octocornuta

Andinia octocornuta, commonly known as the eight-horned andinia, is a unique species of flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a cluster of horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical head. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its distinctive morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
