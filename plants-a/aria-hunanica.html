Aria hunanica

Aria hunanica, commonly known as the Hunan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Aria hunanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
