Astragalus luteolus

Astragalus luteolus, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky slopes. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices.
