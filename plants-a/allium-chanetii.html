Allium chanetii

Allium chanetii, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple or white flowers. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium chanetii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
