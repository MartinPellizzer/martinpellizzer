Ammochloa unispiculata

Ammochloa unispiculata, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and survive prolonged droughts.

Ammochloa unispiculata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
