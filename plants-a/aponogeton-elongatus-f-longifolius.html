Aponogeton elongatus f. longifolius

Aponogeton elongatus f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved aquatic plant, is a variant of the aquatic plant Aponogeton elongatus.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. This plant is typically found in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are often submerged, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

The plant is also noted for its ability to produce showy, white flowers that emerge above the water surface.
