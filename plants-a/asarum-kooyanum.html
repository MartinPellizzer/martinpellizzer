Asarum kooyanum

Asarum kooyanum, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a showy, hooded flower that is often mistaken for a true ginger plant due to its similar appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Asarum kooyanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
