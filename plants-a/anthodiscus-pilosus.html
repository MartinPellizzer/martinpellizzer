Anthodiscus pilosus

Anthodiscus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy disk daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a central yellow disk surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. Its hairy stems and leaves are adaptations to its arid environment, helping to reduce water loss.

Anthodiscus pilosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
