Acacia mountfordiae

Acacia mountfordiae, commonly known as the Mountford's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense spikes at the ends of the branches. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Acacia mountfordiae is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
