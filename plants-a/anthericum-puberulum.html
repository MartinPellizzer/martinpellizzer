Anthericum puberulum

Anthericum puberulum, commonly known as the hairy yellow flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along riverbanks and in damp woodlands. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of yellow, spear-shaped flowers in the summer. Its leaves are narrow, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
