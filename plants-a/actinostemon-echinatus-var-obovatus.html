Actinostemon echinatus var. obovatus

Actinostemon echinatus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed thornplant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are obovate in shape, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are spiny, providing a natural defense against herbivores.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
