Acacia dumosa

Acacia dumosa, commonly known as the straggly wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted branches and small, yellow flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia dumosa is drought-tolerant and is used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
