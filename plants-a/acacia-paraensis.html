Acacia paraensis

Acacia paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wattle, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, zigzagging branches and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acacia paraensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and woodland areas.
