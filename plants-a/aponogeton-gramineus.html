Aponogeton gramineus

Aponogeton gramineus, commonly known as the African waterweed, is a flowering aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Aponogeton gramineus is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
