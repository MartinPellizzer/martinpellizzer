Annona annonoides

Annona annonoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it ripens.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and slightly acidic flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations.
