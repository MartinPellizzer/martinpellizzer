Andropogon caesius

Andropogon caesius, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Andropogon caesius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
