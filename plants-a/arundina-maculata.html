Arundina maculata

Arundina maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms covered in distinctive purple or red spots. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. Its foliage is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Arundina maculata is also valued for its air-purifying properties and can be grown both indoors and outdoors.
