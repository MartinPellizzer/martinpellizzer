Achillea ageratifolia subsp. ageratifolia

Achillea ageratifolia subsp.

ageratifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, and it is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of medicinal use in traditional remedies.
