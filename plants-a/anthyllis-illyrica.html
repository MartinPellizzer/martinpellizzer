Anthyllis illyrica

Anthyllis illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis illyrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
