Agropyron × pseudorepens var. sennii

Agropyron × pseudorepens var. sennii, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic tundra environments.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
