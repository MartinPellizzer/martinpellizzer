Acacia lycopodiifolia var. typica

Acacia lycopodiifolia var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features narrow, linear leaves arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
