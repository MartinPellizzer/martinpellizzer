Artabotrys oblanceolatus

Artabotrys oblanceolatus, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oblanceolate in shape, with a smooth margin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
