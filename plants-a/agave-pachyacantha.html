Agave pachyacantha

Agave pachyacantha, commonly known as the thick-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of yellow-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
