Abrus precatorius var. villosula

Abrus precatorius var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy maple bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces intricate, bean-like pods that contain dark red, ornamental seeds with a prominent black spot. These seeds are toxic due to the presence of abrin, a potent lectin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in various cultures.
