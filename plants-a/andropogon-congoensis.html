Andropogon congoensis

Andropogon congoensis, commonly known as Congo bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves with a blue-green sheen, and it produces panicles of small, pale blue to white flowers. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
