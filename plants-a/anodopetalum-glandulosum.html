Anodopetalum glandulosum

Anodopetalum glandulosum, commonly known as the glossy-leafed beautybush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring, which are borne on short, glandular pedicels. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it has a compact growth habit.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
