Acacia macrocephala

Acacia macrocephala, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are elongated and often curved, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Acacia macrocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
