Aster bietii

Aster bietii, commonly known as the Alpine aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments at high elevations. Its foliage is usually hairy and gray-green, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

Aster bietii produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
