Astragalus diphysus var. albiflorus

Astragalus diphysus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
