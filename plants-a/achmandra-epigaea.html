Achmandra epigaea

Achmandra epigaea, commonly known as the Eastern Starflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for early-season pollinators.
