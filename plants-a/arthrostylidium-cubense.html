Arthrostylidium cubense

Arthrostylidium cubense, commonly known as the Cuban feather grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue florets. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
