Alsophila junghuhniana

Alsophila junghuhniana, commonly known as the Junghuhn's tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome system allows it to anchor firmly in the soil while supporting its tall structure.

The fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to the plant's ornamental value.
