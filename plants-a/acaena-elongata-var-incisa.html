Acaena elongata var. incisa

Acaena elongata var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, serrated leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
