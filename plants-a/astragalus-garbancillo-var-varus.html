Astragalus garbancillo var. varus

Astragalus garbancillo var. varus, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
