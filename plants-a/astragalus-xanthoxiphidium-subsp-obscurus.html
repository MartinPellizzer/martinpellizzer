Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp. obscurus

Astragalus xanthoxiphidium subsp.

obscurus, commonly known as the darkleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with a grayish-green hue, and its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
