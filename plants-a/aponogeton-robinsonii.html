Aponogeton robinsonii

Aponogeton robinsonii, commonly known as the golden duckweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are typically bright green to golden in color. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that emerge from the water surface, often in clusters. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Aponogeton robinsonii is valued in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
