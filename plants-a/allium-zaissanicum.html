Allium zaissanicum

Allium zaissanicum, commonly known as the Zaissan garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy bulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, sturdy stem.

Allium zaissanicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
