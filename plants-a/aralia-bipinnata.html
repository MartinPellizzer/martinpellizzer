Aralia bipinnata

Aralia bipinnata, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are bipinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Aralia bipinnata is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and wildlife value.
