Acianthera hartwegiifolia

Acianthera hartwegiifolia, commonly known as the Hartweg's violet, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
