Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. epoligotricha

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

epoligotricha, commonly known as the red burr bush, is a flowering plant species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, red-brown fruits that contain seeds.

The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.
