Aldama imbricata

Aldama imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican bristle grass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
