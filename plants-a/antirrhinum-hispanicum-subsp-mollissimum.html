Antirrhinum hispanicum subsp. mollissimum

Antirrhinum hispanicum subsp.

mollissimum, commonly known as the Spanish snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
