Angelica shikokiana var. mayebarana

Angelica shikokiana var. mayebarana, commonly known as the Mayebarana angelica, is a rare and distinctive variety of angelica native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often adorned with large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
