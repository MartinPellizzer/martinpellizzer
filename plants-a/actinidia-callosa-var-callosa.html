Actinidia callosa var. callosa

Actinidia callosa var. callosa, commonly known as the fuzzy actinidia or the rough actinidio, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature, typically in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscaping and horticultural research.
