Averrhoa carambola

Averrhoa carambola, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive five-pointed fruits, which are yellow when ripe and have a tart, acidic flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, while its flowers are small and white with a subtle fragrance.

The fruit is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its nutritional value and unique taste.
