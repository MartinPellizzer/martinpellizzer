Acaena pinnatifida subsp. brevispica

Acaena pinnatifida subsp.

brevispica, commonly known as the short-styled acaena, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, with a serrated margin, and the stems are often covered in stiff, hooked bracts.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.
