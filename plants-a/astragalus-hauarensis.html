Astragalus hauarensis

Astragalus hauarensis, commonly known as the Huarpe milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to thrive in arid and high-altitude environments, often found in rocky soils.

Astragalus hauarensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
