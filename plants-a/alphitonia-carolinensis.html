Alphitonia carolinensis

Alphitonia carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina ash or white ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has smooth, light gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing one or two seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal and urban environments.
