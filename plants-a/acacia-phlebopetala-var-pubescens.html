Acacia phlebopetala var. pubescens

Acacia phlebopetala var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow floral spikes and small, feathery leaves. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
