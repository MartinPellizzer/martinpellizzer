Ardisia maestrensis

Ardisia maestrensis, commonly known as the Colombian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in undergrowth layers of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
