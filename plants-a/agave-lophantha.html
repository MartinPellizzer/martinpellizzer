Agave lophantha

Agave lophantha, commonly known as the Mexican woolly agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its rosette shape helps it conserve water efficiently, a key feature of many desert plants.

The flowers, which appear on a tall stalk, are usually yellow or green and bloom in late spring or early summer.
