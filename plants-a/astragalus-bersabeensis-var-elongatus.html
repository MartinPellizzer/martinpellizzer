Astragalus bersabeensis var. elongatus

Astragalus bersabeensis var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

This variety is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
