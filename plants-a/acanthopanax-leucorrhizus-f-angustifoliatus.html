Acanthopanax leucorrhizus f. angustifoliatus

Acanthopanax leucorrhizus f. angustifoliatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf five-angled aralia, is a variant of the Chinese five-angled aralia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by dark blue berries. It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
