Aconitum angulatum

Aconitum angulatum, commonly known as the angular aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided, deeply cut leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Aconitum angulatum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
