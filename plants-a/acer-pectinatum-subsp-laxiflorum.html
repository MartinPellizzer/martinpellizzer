Acer pectinatum subsp. laxiflorum

Acer pectinatum subsp.

laxiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China. It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
