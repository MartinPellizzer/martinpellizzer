Alnus glutinosa lusus quercifolia

Alnus glutinosa lusus quercifolia, commonly known as the European black alder, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, simple leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
