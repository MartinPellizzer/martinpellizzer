Amaranthus fatine

Amaranthus fatine, commonly known as the red amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or orange bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. It grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Amaranthus fatine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional cuisines.
