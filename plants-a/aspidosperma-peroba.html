Aspidosperma peroba

Aspidosperma peroba, commonly known as Brazilwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that was historically valuable for making musical instruments and dyes. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds.

The tree is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
