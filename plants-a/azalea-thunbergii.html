Azalea thunbergii

Azalea thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink, red, or white blossoms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Azalea thunbergii is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and its role in attracting pollinators.
