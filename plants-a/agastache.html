Agastache

Agastache, commonly known as hyssop, is a genus of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to North America and includes species such as Agastache foeniculum and Agastache rugosa. These plants are characterized by their tall spikes of tubular flowers, which range in color from blue to purple. Agastache species are often found in moist soils in prairies, meadows, and woodland edges.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and also for their use in traditional herbal medicine.
