Asteropsis megapotamica

Asteropsis megapotamica, commonly known as the Andean aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Asteropsis megapotamica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
