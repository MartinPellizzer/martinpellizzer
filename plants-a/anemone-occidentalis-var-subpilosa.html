Anemone occidentalis var. subpilosa

Anemone occidentalis var. subpilosa, commonly known as the western anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with petal-like sepals in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to temperate climates and forest understory environments.
