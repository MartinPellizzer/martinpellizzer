Astragalus trichocarpus

Astragalus trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy pod milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus trichocarpus plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
