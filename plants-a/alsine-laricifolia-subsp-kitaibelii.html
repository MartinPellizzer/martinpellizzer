Alsine laricifolia subsp. kitaibelii

Alsine laricifolia subsp.

kitaibelii, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
