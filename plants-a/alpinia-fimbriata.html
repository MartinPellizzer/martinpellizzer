Alpinia fimbriata

Alpinia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental leaves, which are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a wavy or fringed edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Alpinia fimbriata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
