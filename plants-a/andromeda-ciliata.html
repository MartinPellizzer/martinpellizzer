Andromeda ciliata

Andromeda ciliata, commonly known as the silky andromeda, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical with finely toothed margins, and it produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds.

Andromeda ciliata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
