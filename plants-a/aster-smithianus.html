Aster smithianus

Aster smithianus, commonly known as the Smith's aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its purple to blue flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Aster smithianus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
