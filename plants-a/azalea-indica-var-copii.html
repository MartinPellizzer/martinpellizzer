Azalea indica var. copii

Azalea indica var. copii, commonly known as the Chinese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers in spring, which are often accompanied by a pleasant fragrance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
