Alstroemeria gouveiana

Alstroemeria gouveiana, commonly known as the Gouvea alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity and striking appearance.

Alstroemeria gouveiana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
