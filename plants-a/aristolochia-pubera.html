Aristolochia pubera

Aristolochia pubera, commonly known as the hairy birthwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical composition.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
