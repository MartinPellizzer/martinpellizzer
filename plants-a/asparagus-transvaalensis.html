Asparagus transvaalensis

Asparagus transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
