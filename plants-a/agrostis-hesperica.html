Agrostis hesperica

Agrostis hesperica, commonly known as the western bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, tufted shoots and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

Agrostis hesperica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
