Achras ferruginea

Achras ferruginea, commonly known as the red cedar or bloodwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the dry regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its thick, rugged trunk and small, clustered leaves. The tree produces yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and traditional crafts.
