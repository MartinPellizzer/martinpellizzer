Asparagus francisci

Asparagus francisci, commonly known as the California asparagus, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, succulent stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Asparagus francisci is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
