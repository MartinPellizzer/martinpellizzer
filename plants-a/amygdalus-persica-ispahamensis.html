Amygdalus persica ispahamensis

Amygdalus persica ispahamensis, commonly known as the Persian almond.

It is a species of the Prunus genus, characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits and delicate pink or white flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Iran and Afghanistan. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

The fruit, which is botanically classified as a drupe, contains a single seed encased in a hard shell.
