Abutilon periplocifolium var. albicans

Abutilon periplocifolium var. albicans, commonly known as the white-leaf abutilon, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
