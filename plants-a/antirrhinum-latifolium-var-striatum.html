Antirrhinum latifolium var. striatum

Antirrhinum latifolium var. striatum, commonly known as the striped toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
