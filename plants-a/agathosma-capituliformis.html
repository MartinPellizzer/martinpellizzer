Agathosma capituliformis

Agathosma capituliformis, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a small, aromatic shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in compact, rounded clusters, giving it its scientific name "capituliformis," which refers to its head-like inflorescence. The plant has silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
