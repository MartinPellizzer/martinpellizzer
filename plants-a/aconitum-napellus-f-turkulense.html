Aconitum napellus f. turkulense

Aconitum napellus f. turkulense, commonly known as the Turcullan monkshood, is a botanical variant of the European monkshood.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and deeply lobed leaves, typical of the Aconitum genus. This ferny perennial prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, thriving in temperate climates. The plant is native to the Alps and has a long history of use in traditional medicine, though it contains potent toxins.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
