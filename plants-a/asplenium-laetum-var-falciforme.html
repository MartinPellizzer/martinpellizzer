Asplenium laetum var. falciforme

Asplenium laetum var. falciforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often arching fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped appearance.

Asplenium laetum var. falciforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
