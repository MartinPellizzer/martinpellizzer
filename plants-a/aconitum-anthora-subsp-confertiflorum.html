Aconitum anthora subsp. confertiflorum

Aconitum anthora subsp.

confertiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy. Aconitum anthora subsp.

confertiflorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
