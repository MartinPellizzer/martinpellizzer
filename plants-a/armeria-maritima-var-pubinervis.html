Armeria maritima var. pubinervis

Armeria maritima var. pubinervis, commonly known as the coastal thrift, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and dense clusters of bright pink or white flowers. The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is highly tolerant of salt spray and wind.

This variety is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
