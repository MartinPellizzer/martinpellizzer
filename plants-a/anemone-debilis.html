Anemone debilis

Anemone debilis, commonly known as the common anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are slender and often hairy. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Anemone debilis is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable conditions.
