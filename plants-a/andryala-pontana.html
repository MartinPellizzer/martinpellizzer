Andryala pontana

Andryala pontana, commonly known as the Himalayan thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Andryala pontana plays an important role in the ecological balance of alpine ecosystems.
