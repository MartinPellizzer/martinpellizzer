Acronychia elliptica

Acronychia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf apple or white ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It features smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin, papery layers, and its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and typically narrow. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, woody fruits containing a single seed.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
