Asplenium sellowianum

Asplenium sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of narrow, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Asplenium sellowianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
