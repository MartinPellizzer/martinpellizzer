Aconitum dolomiticum

Aconitum dolomiticum, commonly known as the Dolomite Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Aconitum dolomiticum is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
