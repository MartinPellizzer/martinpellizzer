Angophora cordata

Angophora cordata, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Victoria. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyll forests.
