Acer × creticum var. minor

Acer × creticum var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf red maple, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Acer rubrum and Acer trichotomum.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub with distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters and features showy red to orange flowers in early spring. Its foliage is often more compact and dense compared to its parent species, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The tree is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively resistant to pests and diseases.
