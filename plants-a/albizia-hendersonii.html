Albizia hendersonii

Albizia hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's silk tree, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feathery leaflets, and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Albizia hendersonii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
