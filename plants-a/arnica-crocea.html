Arnica crocea

Arnica crocea, commonly known as the common arnica, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, rough leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its medicinal properties have been historically used for treating bruises and inflammation.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
