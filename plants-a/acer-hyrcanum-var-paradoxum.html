Acer hyrcanum var. paradoxum

Acer hyrcanum var. paradoxum, commonly known as the Turkmenian maple, is a rare and distinctive species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring palmate leaves with a striking yellow-green coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, clustered flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
