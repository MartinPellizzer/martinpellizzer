Abutilon vitifolium

Abutilon vitifolium, commonly known as the wine-leaf abutilon, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
