Abies balsamea var. hudsonia

Abies balsamea var. hudsonia, commonly known as Hudson's fir, is a variant of the balsam fir native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically shorter than those of the typical balsam fir. The plant produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in forest ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
