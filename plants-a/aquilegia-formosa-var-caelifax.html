Aquilegia formosa var. caelifax

Aquilegia formosa var. caelifax, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its showy, butterfly-shaped petals.

This variety is particularly adapted to the Mediterranean climate of California, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
