Aucuba chinensis var. variegata

Aucuba chinensis var. variegata, commonly known as Japanese laurel or variegated aucuba, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow and white variegation, enhancing its ornamental value. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for hedges, borders, or container gardening.
