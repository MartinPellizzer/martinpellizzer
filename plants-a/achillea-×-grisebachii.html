Achillea × grisebachii

Achillea × grisebachii, commonly known as the gray yarrow, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
