Artemisia nana

Artemisia nana, commonly known as dwarf mugwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features silver-gray foliage with small, rounded leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Artemisia nana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
