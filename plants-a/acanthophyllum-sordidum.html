Acanthophyllum sordidum

Acanthophyllum sordidum, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, pale green leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its structure and growth habits make it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.

Acanthophyllum sordidum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
