Abies religiosa var. hirtella

Abies religiosa var. hirtella, commonly known as the Mexican silver fir, is a variant of the silver fir species native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and distinctive silver undersides on its needles. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
