Ambrosia crithmifolia

Ambrosia crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its leaves are aromatic and often have a silvery or grayish hue due to the presence of fine hairs.

Ambrosia crithmifolia produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
