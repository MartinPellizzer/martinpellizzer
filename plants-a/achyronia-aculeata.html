Achyronia aculeata

Achyronia aculeata, commonly known as the spiny poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the center of the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular choice for festive decorations.
