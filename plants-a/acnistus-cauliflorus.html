Acnistus cauliflorus

Acnistus cauliflorus, commonly known as the climbing acnistus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or vine that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves and its unique flowering pattern, where flowers emerge from the axils of the leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
