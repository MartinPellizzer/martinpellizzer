Astragalus tragacantha subsp. vicentinus

Astragalus tragacantha subsp.

vicentinus, commonly known as the Vicentine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. It produces a distinctive resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and its potential for medicinal applications.
