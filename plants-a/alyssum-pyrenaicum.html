Alyssum pyrenaicum

Alyssum pyrenaicum, commonly known as Pyrenean alyssum, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Alyssum pyrenaicum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
