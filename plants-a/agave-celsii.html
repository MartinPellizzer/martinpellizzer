Agave celsii

Agave celsii, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave celsii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional practices.
