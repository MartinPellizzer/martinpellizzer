Arnica scaberrima

Arnica scaberrima, commonly known as rough arnica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving at higher altitudes. Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous communities for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
