Annesorhiza angustifolia

Annesorhiza angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf false larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, sparsely branched stems. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with long, narrow petals and are arranged in clusters.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
