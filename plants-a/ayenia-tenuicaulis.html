Ayenia tenuicaulis

Ayenia tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers make it a distinctive species within its habitat.

Ayenia tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
