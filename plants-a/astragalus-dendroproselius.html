Astragalus dendroproselius

Astragalus dendroproselius, commonly known as the tree milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that resemble small trees or shrubs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus dendroproselius is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species found in rocky and sandy soils.
