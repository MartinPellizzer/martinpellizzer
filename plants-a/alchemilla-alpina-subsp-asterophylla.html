Alchemilla alpina subsp. asterophylla

Alchemilla alpina subsp.

asterophylla, commonly known as alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
