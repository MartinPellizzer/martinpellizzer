Agrimonia eupatoria f. rosulata

Agrimonia eupatoria f. rosulata, commonly known as roseless agrimony, is a botanical variant of the common agrimony.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins and a distinctive hairy texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.
