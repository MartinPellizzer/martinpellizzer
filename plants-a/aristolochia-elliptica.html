Aristolochia elliptica

Aristolochia elliptica, commonly known as the Indian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces intricate, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, hence its common name. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant blossoms. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
