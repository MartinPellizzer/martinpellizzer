Abrus precatorius var. latifoliolatus

Abrus precatorius var. latifoliolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, red-and-black ornamental pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive, toxic seeds enclosed in fleshy, reddish pods. The seeds contain potent alkaloids, making the plant potentially hazardous if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
