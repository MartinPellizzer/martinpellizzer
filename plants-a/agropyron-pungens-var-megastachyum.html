Agropyron pungens var. megastachyum

Agropyron pungens var. megastachyum, commonly known as the giant wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences with spikelets that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it valuable for ecological restoration and as a forage crop in arid environments.
