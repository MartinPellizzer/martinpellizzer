Asperula sherardia var. typica

Asperula sherardia var. typica, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy inflorescences of pink or white blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and long blooming period.
