Acinodendron ovatum

Acinodendron ovatum, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
