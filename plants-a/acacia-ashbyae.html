Acacia ashbyae

Acacia ashbyae, commonly known as Ashby's wattle, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Acacia ashbyae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
