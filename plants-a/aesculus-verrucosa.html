Aesculus verrucosa

Aesculus verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spiny, woody fruits containing shiny, brown seeds. The leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are often hairy or pubescent. The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
