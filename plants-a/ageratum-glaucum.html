Ageratum glaucum

Ageratum glaucum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum glaucum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
