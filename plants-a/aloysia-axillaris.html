Aloysia axillaris

Aloysia axillaris, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Aloysia axillaris is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
