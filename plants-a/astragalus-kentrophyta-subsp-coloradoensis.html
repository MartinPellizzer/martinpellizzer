Astragalus kentrophyta subsp. coloradoensis

Astragalus kentrophyta subsp.

coloradoensis, commonly known as the Colorado milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.
