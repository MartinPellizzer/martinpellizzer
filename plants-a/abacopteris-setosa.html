Abacopteris setosa

Abacopteris setosa, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds. Its fibrous leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing dunes.

Abacopteris setosa is an important component of coastal ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
