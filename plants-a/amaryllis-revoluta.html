Amaryllis revoluta

Amaryllis revoluta, commonly known as the spiral amaryllis, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Amaryllis revoluta is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires periods of dormancy for optimal growth.

Its unique spiral arrangement of petals makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
