Aucuba japonica var. latimaculata

Aucuba japonica var. latimaculata, commonly known as the Japanese aucuba or variegated aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental foliage make it a versatile plant for both residential and commercial landscapes.
