Astragalus arbuscula var. microphyllus

Astragalus arbuscula var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in harsh conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
