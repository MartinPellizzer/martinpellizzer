Acinodendron spicellatum

Acinodendron spicellatum, commonly known as the spiky acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also contributes to soil fertility in nutrient-poor regions.
