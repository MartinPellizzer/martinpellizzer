Astilbe glaberrima lusus terrestris

Astilbe glaberrima lusus terrestris, commonly known as the creeping aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.
