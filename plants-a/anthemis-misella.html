Anthemis misella

Anthemis misella, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically features small, yellow flowers surrounded by pale green or white ray florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
