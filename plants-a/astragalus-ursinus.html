Astragalus ursinus

Astragalus ursinus, commonly known as bear's milk, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Astragalus ursinus is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found growing in rocky soils.

Its name is derived from the historical belief that it was used to treat ailments in bears.
