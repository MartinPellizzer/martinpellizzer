Amauropelta ptarmica

Amauropelta ptarmica, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically golden in color, especially when young. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and it produces spores in clusters on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
