Acer platanoides f. cuneatum

Acer platanoides f. cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Norway maple, is a cultivar of the Norway maple species.

It is characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical Norway maple, which gives it a distinct appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree that produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
