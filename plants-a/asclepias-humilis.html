Asclepias humilis

Asclepias humilis, commonly known as the low milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches, featuring slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces pale yellow to greenish flowers with distinctive, horn-like seed pods that develop after blooming. It is a host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential nourishment for monarch caterpillars.

Asclepias humilis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
