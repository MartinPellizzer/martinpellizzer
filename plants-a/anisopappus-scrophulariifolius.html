Anisopappus scrophulariifolius

Anisopappus scrophulariifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
