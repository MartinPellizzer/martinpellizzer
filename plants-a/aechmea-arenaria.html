Aechmea arenaria

Aechmea arenaria, commonly known as the sand aechmea, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers, which are typically pink or red, bloom in the summer and attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Aechmea arenaria is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea arenaria has rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy, dark green with a metallic sheen, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are narrow and pointed, typically reaching lengths of up to 30 cm, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant has a short, thick, and somewhat woody stem that is smooth and light green in color, growing upright from the base of the leaf rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, typically pink or red, and bloom during the summer months; they are pollinated by insects such as bees and beetles.

The fruit is a bright red berry that contains small, black seeds, which are dispersed by animals feeding on the fruit.
