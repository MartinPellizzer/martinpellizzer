Acacia glaucissima

Acacia glaucissima, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, rounded phyllodes that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Acacia glaucissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
