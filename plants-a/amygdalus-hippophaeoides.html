Amygdalus hippophaeoides

Amygdalus hippophaeoides, commonly known as the Chinese almond or wild almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often referred to as "Chinese almonds," though they are not true almonds.

Amygdalus hippophaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
