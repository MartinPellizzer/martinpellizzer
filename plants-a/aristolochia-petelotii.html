Aristolochia petelotii

Aristolochia petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and unique, intricate flowers that resemble a pipe, often with a deep purple or maroon coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that are typically toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
