Areca bakeri

Areca bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Areca bakeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight to thrive.
