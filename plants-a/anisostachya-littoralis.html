Anisostachya littoralis

Anisostachya littoralis, commonly known as the coastal reed, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found along shores and estuaries. The plant has slender, erect culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and wind. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Anisostachya littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
