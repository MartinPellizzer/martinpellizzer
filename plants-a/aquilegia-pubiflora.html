Aquilegia pubiflora

Aquilegia pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers and palmate leaves. The plant features distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with a hairy texture on the petals. Aquilegia pubiflora thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to alpine environments.

Its unique floral structure and hairy foliage make it a notable species in botanical studies.
