Aspidosperma resonans

Aspidosperma resonans, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red fruits that split open to reveal seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in riverine forests and savanna woodlands.

Aspidosperma resonans is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
