Ada brachypus

Ada brachypus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to wetland environments. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize soil in aquatic and moist habitats.

Ada brachypus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and support diverse wildlife.
