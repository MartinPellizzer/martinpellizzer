Astragalus turbat-haidariensis

Astragalus turbat-haidariensis, commonly known as the Haidari's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access water from lower soil layers.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
