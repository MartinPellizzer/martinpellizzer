Anthemis kitaibelii

Anthemis kitaibelii, commonly known as the Armenian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers sunny, well-drained conditions. Its leaves are aromatic and have a mild chamomile scent, which is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
