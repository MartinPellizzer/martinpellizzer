Aronia melanocarpa

Aronia melanocarpa, commonly known as chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Aronia melanocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its edible fruits used in jams, juices, and health supplements.
