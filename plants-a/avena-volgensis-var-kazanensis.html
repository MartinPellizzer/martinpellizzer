Avena volgensis var. kazanensis

Avena volgensis var. kazanensis, commonly known as Kazan oat, is a variant of the oat species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
