Aconitum rockii var. ramosum

Aconitum rockii var. ramosum, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Aconitum rockii var. ramosum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
