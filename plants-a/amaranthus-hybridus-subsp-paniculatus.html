Amaranthus hybridus subsp. paniculatus

Amaranthus hybridus subsp.

paniculatus, commonly known as red amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy bracts that surround the flowering spikes.

The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled capsules. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a nutritious grain crop.
