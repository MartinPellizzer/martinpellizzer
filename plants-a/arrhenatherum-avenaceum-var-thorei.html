Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. thorei

Arrhenatherum avenaceum var. thorei, commonly known as Thore's meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained to slightly waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, bearing narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with brown or purple hues.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small mammals.
