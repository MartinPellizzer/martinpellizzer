Asplenium shepherdioides

Asplenium shepherdioides, commonly known as the Shepherd's Spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. Native to various regions across the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and woodland areas. The plant's fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with sporangia borne on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium shepherdioides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
