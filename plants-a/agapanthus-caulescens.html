Agapanthus caulescens

Agapanthus caulescens, commonly known as the slender agapanthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, narrow stems that bear dense clusters of striking blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden planting.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
