Arnebia euchroma subsp. caespitosa

Arnebia euchroma subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau. It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to poor, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically basal, lanceolate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its traditional medicinal uses and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates.
