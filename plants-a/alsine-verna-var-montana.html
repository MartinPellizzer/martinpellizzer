Alsine verna var. montana

Alsine verna var. montana, commonly known as mountain alsike, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
