Acaena trifida var. brachyphylla

Acaena trifida var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in groups of five. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter stems compared to the typical form of Acaena trifida.
