Aconitum napellus lusus hoppeanum

Aconitum napellus lusus hoppeanum, commonly known as the Hoppean monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers and large, palmately lobed leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids such as aconitine.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often cultivated in gardens with caution.
