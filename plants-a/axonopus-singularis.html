Axonopus singularis

Axonopus singularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf axonopus, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact growth habit. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Axonopus singularis is known for its ability to form dense mats, making it a common ground cover in its native habitats.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various ecological conditions, though it is not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
