Ardisia singaporensis

Ardisia singaporensis, commonly known as the Singapore ardisia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Ardisia singaporensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
