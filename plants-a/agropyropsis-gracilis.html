Agropyropsis gracilis

Agropyropsis gracilis, commonly known as slender hairgrass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and is often found in wet or moist environments. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, forming low, compact tufts. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

Agropyropsis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in aquatic or wetland landscaping.
