Aquilegia vulgaris subsp. nivalis

Aquilegia vulgaris subsp.

nivalis, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
