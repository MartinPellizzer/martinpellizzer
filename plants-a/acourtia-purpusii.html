Acourtia purpusii

Acourtia purpusii, commonly known as the purple thimble cactus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, spiny stems and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
