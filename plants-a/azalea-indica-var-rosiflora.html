Azalea indica var. rosiflora

Azalea indica var. rosiflora, commonly known as the roseleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
