Andromeda globulifera

Andromeda globulifera, commonly known as the Indian alder, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in horticulture and natural ecosystems.
