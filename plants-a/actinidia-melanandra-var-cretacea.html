Actinidia melanandra var. cretacea

Actinidia melanandra var. cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous actinidia, is a rare and ancient species of vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible berries, although these are not as widely cultivated as other actinidia species.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
