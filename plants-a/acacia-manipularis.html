Acacia manipularis

Acacia manipularis, commonly known as the wattle tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia manipularis is often used in reforestation projects due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
