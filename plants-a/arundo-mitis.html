Arundo mitis

Arundo mitis, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in wet environments such as marshes and riverbanks. Its hollow stems are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of reed for musical instruments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
