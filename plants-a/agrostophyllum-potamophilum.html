Agrostophyllum potamophilum

Agrostophyllum potamophilum, commonly known as the riverbed grass.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in parts of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for small organisms.
