Achrysum glomeratum

Achrysum glomeratum, commonly known as clustered achrysum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Achrysum glomeratum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
