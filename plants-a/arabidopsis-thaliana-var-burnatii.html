Arabidopsis thaliana var. burnatii

Arabidopsis thaliana var. burnatii, commonly known as the Burnatii thale cress, is a variant of the widely studied model plant Arabidopsis thaliana.

It is a small, annual flowering plant native to Central Asia, characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant exhibits variations in leaf shape and color compared to the standard variety, which makes it an interesting subject for botanical research. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its genetic makeup is similar to the standard Arabidopsis, making it valuable for studying plant adaptation and evolution.
