Anthericum graptophyllum

Anthericum graptophyllum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
