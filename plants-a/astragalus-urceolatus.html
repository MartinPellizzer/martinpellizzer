Astragalus urceolatus

Astragalus urceolatus, commonly known as the cup-shaped milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped flower cluster, which is a key feature in its classification within the Fabaceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus urceolatus is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
