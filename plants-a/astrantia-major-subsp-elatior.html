Astrantia major subsp. elatior

Astrantia major subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater masterwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features showy, pinkish-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched. Its flowers are composed of numerous small, tubular florets arranged in a spherical cluster.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
