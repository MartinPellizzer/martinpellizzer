Anthonotha crassifolia

Anthonotha crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved anthonotha, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for survival in dry conditions.

Anthonotha crassifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
