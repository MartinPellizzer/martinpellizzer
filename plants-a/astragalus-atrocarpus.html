Astragalus atrocarpus

Astragalus atrocarpus, commonly known as black-horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several small, hard seeds.

Astragalus atrocarpus plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
