Allium confusum

Allium confusum, commonly known as the confused garlic, is a bulbous plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces a bulb that is similar in appearance to garlic but has a milder flavor. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Allium confusum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
