Amaranthus flavus var. ovalifolius

Amaranthus flavus var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate to oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are edible and can be used in cooking, similar to other amaranth species.
