Astragalus muelleri

Astragalus muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role and unique adaptations.
