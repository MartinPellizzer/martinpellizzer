Acacia kalkora

Acacia kalkora, commonly known as the Kalkora wattle, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green phyllodes that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense clusters. Acacia kalkora is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Its ecological role includes providing food and habitat for various native animals and insects.
