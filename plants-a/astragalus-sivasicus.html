Astragalus sivasicus

Astragalus sivasicus, commonly known as the Sivash mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, typical of many legumes.

Astragalus sivasicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
