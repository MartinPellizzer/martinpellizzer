Astrantia saniculifolia

Astrantia saniculifolia, commonly known as the lesser dropwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically arranged in umbels, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
