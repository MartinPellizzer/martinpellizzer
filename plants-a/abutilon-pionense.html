Abutilon pionense

Abutilon pionense, commonly known as the golden abutilon, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Abutilon pionense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
