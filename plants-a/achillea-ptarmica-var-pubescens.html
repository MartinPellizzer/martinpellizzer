Achillea ptarmica var. pubescens

Achillea ptarmica var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The variety 'pubescens' is distinguished by its dense, hairy leaves, which give it a unique appearance compared to other yarrow species.
