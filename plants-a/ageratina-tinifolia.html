Ageratina tinifolia

Ageratina tinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, fluffy blue or purple flower clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to diverse ecosystems.
