Aeschynomene glutinosa

Aeschynomene glutinosa, commonly known as sticky top, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oblong seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Aeschynomene glutinosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
