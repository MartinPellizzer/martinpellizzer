Asystasia laticapsula

Asystasia laticapsula, commonly known as the broad-capped asystasia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asystasia laticapsula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
