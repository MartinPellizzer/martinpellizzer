Alcea biennis

Alcea biennis, commonly known as the common sunhemp, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules as its fruiting structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Alcea biennis is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
