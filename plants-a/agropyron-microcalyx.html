Agropyron microcalyx

Agropyron microcalyx, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, needle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Agropyron microcalyx plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
