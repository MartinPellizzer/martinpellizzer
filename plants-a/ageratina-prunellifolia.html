Ageratina prunellifolia

Ageratina prunellifolia, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The showy white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
