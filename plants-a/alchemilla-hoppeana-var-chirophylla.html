Alchemilla hoppeana var. chirophylla

Alchemilla hoppeana var. chirophylla, commonly known as the golden alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
