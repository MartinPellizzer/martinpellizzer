Aralia thomsonii var. glabrescens

Aralia thomsonii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and often have a smooth, glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiny fruits. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.
