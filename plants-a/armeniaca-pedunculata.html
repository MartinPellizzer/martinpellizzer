Armeniaca pedunculata

Armeniaca pedunculata, commonly known as the Persian apricot, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to the domesticated apricot (Prunus armeniaca). The plant produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
