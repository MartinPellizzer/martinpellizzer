Anemia coccinea

Anemia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and edible bean pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with compound leaves and tendrils that help it climb. Its botanical features include showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The seeds, enclosed in the pods, are rich in nutrients and widely used in culinary applications.
