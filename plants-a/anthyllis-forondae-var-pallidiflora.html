Anthyllis forondae var. pallidiflora

Anthyllis forondae var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils.

This species is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and rocky slopes.
