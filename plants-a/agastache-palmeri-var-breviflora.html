Agastache palmeri var. breviflora

Agastache palmeri var. breviflora, commonly known as Palmer's hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces fragrant, violet to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter floral bracts compared to other varieties of Agastache palmeri.
