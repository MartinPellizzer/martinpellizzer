Adenophora denticulata

Adenophora denticulata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Adenophora denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
