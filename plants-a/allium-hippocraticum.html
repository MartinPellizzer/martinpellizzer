Allium hippocraticum

Allium hippocraticum, commonly known as the Greek leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for both its culinary value and ornamental appeal.
