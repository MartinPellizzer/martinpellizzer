Allophylus macrobotrys

Allophylus macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or mountain arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. Its roots are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its culinary uses.
