Arrhenatherum milanjianum

Arrhenatherum milanjianum, commonly known as Milanjian's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
