Achillea × roseoalba

Achillea × roseoalba, commonly known as the pink yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of pink to pale pink flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.

Its common name honors the mythological figure Achilles, who was said to have used yarrow in treating wounds.
