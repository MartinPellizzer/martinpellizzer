Anthemis blancheana

Anthemis blancheana, commonly known as the white anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in a single head, resembling a dandelion. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Anthemis blancheana is typically found in dry, sunny habitats and is known for its aromatic properties.
