Arthragrostis aristispicula

Arthragrostis aristispicula, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that bears small, two-lobed flowers.

Arthragrostis aristispicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
