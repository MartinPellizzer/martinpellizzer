Aronia

Aronia, commonly known as chokeberry, is a genus of shrubs native to North America and East Asia.

It includes several species, the most well-known being Aronia melanocarpa and Aronia arbutifolia. These plants are characterized by their dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plants produce small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring.

Aronia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and edible fruits.
