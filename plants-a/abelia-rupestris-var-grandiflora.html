Abelia rupestris var. grandiflora

Abelia rupestris var. grandiflora, commonly known as the rock abelia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a dense, mounding growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive flowers and compact size.

Native Distribution

Abelia rupestris var. grandiflora is primarily native to the southeastern United States, specifically Florida and parts of Georgia.

It thrives in areas with high humidity and mild winters, making it well adapted to the subtropical climate of its native range. The plant is often seen in open woodlands and along the edges of wetlands.

Its native distribution is limited to these specific regions due to its particular ecological requirements.

Introduced Distribution
