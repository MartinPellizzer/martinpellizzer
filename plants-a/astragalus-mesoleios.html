Astragalus mesoleios

Astragalus mesoleios, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Astragalus mesoleios plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
