Acacia scirpifolia

Acacia scirpifolia, commonly known as the swamp acacia, is a shrub native to wetland areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and small, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its moist habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are visited by various pollinators. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Acacia scirpifolia is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in swampy environments.
