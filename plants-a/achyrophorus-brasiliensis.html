Achyrophorus brasiliensis

Achyrophorus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and long, slender leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that resembles a starfruit in appearance. The plant is adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
