Aphelandra oostachya

Aphelandra oostachya, commonly known as the Oosthuyse's flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and showy, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as birds and bats. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts in its native habitat.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
