Annona neoecuadorensis

Annona neoecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian soursop, is a tropical tree native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit similar to the soursop, with a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
