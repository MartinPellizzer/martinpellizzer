Asplenium amaurolobum

Asplenium amaurolobum, commonly known as the black spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery, and often glossy leaves, which are typically arranged in a rosette or clustered form. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a sorus, which contains the spores necessary for its propagation. It is often found on rock surfaces or in crevices, thriving in humid and cool conditions.

Asplenium amaurolobum is a member of the Aspleniaceae family and is known for its resilience in various ecological niches.
