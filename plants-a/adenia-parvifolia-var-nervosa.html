Adenia parvifolia var. nervosa

Adenia parvifolia var. nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined adenia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features woody stems with a distinctive, branched structure and dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Adenaceae family.
