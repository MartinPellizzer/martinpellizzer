Anisostachya pubescens

Anisostachya pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wild millet, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in arid regions for landscaping purposes.
