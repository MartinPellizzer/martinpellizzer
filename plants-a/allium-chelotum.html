Allium chelotum

Allium chelotum, commonly known as the Persian onion, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces a pungent odor when crushed, similar to other members of the Allium genus. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible bulbs.
