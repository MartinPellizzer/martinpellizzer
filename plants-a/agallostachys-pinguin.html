Agallostachys pinguin

Agallostachys pinguin, commonly known as the silver pine or silver plant, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by small, round fruits. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
