Acer rufinerve f. marmoratum

Acer rufinerve f. marmoratum, commonly known as the marbled maple, is a variant of the red-veined maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive marbled patterns on the leaves, which display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of China and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with prominent red veins that contrast with the lighter foliage.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
