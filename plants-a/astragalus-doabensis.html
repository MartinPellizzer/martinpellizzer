Astragalus doabensis

Astragalus doabensis, commonly known as the Doab milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in regions of Pakistan and India. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Astragalus doabensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
