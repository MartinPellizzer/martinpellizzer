Astragalus wulfenii

Astragalus wulfenii, commonly known as Wulfen's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its root system is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Astragalus wulfenii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
