Aerangis alcicornis

Aerangis alcicornis, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble horns, hence its common name. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Aerangis alcicornis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
