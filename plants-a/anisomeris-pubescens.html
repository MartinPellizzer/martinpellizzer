Anisomeris pubescens

Anisomeris pubescens, commonly known as the hairy anisomeris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

The yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by small, spiny fruits that disperse seeds by wind.
