Arum normannii

Arum normannii, commonly known as the Norman's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a velvety texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Arum normannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and dramatic appearance.
