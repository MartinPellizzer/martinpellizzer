Allantoma integrifolia

Allantoma integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf allantoin, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves contain allantoin, a compound known for its soothing properties, which has been used in traditional medicine.

Allantoma integrifolia thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.
