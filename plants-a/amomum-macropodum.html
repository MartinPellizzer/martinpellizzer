Amomum macropodum

Amomum macropodum, commonly known as the large-leafed cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elongated leaves and spikes of pale green to yellow flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically cultivated in tropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
