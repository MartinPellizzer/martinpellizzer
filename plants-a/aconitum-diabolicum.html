Aconitum diabolicum

Aconitum diabolicum, commonly known as the devil's helmet, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces large, helmet-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Aconitum diabolicum is known for its high toxicity, containing potent alkaloids that can be lethal to humans and animals.

Due to its extreme toxicity, it is often avoided in gardens and requires careful handling.
