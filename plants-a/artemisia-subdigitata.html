Artemisia subdigitata

Artemisia subdigitata, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Artemisia subdigitata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
