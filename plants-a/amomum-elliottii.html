Amomum elliottii

Amomum elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's cardamom, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, three-lobed berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Amomum elliottii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
