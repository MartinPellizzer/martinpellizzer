Angraecopsis burttii

Angraecopsis burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's star orchid, is a rare and striking terrestrial orchid native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its star-shaped flowers, which are predominantly white with deep purple or violet markings. The plant produces its flowers on tall, slender stems, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a period of dormancy during the dry season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
