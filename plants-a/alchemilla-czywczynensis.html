Alchemilla czywczynensis

Alchemilla czywczynensis, commonly known as the Polish lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla czywczynensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
