Acetosella diffusa

Acetosella diffusa, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers throughout the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Acetosella diffusa is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
