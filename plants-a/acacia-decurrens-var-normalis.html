Acacia decurrens var. normalis

Acacia decurrens var. normalis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow, globular flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water uptake. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
