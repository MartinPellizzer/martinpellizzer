Aconitum pseudoproliferum

Aconitum pseudoproliferum, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions, and are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Aconitum pseudoproliferum is adapted to cold, moist environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
