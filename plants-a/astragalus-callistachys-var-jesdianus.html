Astragalus callistachys var. jesdianus

Astragalus callistachys var. jesdianus, commonly known as the Jesdian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
