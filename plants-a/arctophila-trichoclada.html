Arctophila trichoclada

Arctophila trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy arctophila, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in water retention.

The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
