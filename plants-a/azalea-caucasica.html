Azalea caucasica

Azalea caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of pink to red blossoms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Azalea caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
