Aquilegia flabellata f. globularis

Aquilegia flabellata f. globularis, commonly known as the globular form of the peacock flower, is a compact, ornamental cultivar of the genus Aquilegia.

It features densely packed, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, purple, and white, which bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, typical of the genus. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its unique, rounded growth habit and showy floral display.
