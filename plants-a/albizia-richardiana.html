Albizia richardiana

Albizia richardiana, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a deciduous legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and fragrant, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for container gardening. It is often used as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Albizia richardiana is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which benefit soil fertility.
