Axinandra zeylanica

Axinandra zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Axinandra zeylanica also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
