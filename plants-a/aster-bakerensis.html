Aster bakerensis

Aster bakerensis, commonly known as the Baker's aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, blue to purple florets arranged in a composite head. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces slender, branching stems.

Aster bakerensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
