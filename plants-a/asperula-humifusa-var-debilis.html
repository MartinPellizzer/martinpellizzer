Asperula humifusa var. debilis

Asperula humifusa var. debilis, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, dense clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
