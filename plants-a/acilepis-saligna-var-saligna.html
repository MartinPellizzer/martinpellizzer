Acilepis saligna var. saligna

Acilepis saligna var. saligna, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The silky acacia is notable for its ecological role in supporting various insect and bird species.
