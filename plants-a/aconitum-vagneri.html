Aconitum vagneri

Aconitum vagneri, commonly known as the Vagner's monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that resemble a hood, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Aconitum vagneri is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value despite its dangerous nature.
