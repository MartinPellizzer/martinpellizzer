Anthemis macedonica subsp. thracica

Anthemis macedonica subsp.

thracica, commonly known as the Thrace goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, glandular hairs on its stems and foliage. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
