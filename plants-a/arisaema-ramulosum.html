Arisaema ramulosum

Arisaema ramulosum, commonly known as the clustered arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like bracts that enclose its spadix, a fleshy, spiky structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are enclosed within the spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Arisaema ramulosum is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in some cultures.
