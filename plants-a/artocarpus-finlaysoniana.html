Artocarpus finlaysoniana

Artocarpus finlaysoniana, commonly known as the Bornean jackfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, spiky fruit that matures to a yellowish color. The plant produces a distinctive, compound fruit that contains multiple seeds surrounded by a fibrous, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and has cultural significance in the regions where it is native.
