Artemisia maritima var. pseudogallica

Artemisia maritima var. pseudogallica, commonly known as the coastal tarragon, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It has a creeping growth habit with upright flowering stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
