Acer nigrum var. glaucum

Acer nigrum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous maple, is a variant of the black maple native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically palmately lobed with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. This deciduous tree prefers moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

It is often found in forested areas and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
