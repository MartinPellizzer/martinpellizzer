Agave breviscapa

Agave breviscapa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, broad, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces an upright, branched inflorescence with small, white to pale yellow flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave breviscapa is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
