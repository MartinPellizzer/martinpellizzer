Aloe vanrooyenii

Aloe vanrooyenii, commonly known as the Van Rooyen's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in the spring. Aloe vanrooyenii is adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
