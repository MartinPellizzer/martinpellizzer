Anthurium cordifolium

Anthurium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking spadix flowers surrounded by a vibrant, often red or orange, spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium cordifolium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
