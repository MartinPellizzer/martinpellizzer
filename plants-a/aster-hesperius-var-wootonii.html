Aster hesperius var. wootonii

Aster hesperius var. wootonii, commonly known as the Wooton's blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
