Acalypha oblancifolia

Acalypha oblancifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, spiky seed capsules. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
