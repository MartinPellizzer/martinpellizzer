Aethionema persicum

Aethionema persicum, commonly known as Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant has compact, mat-forming foliage with gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and hardy nature.
