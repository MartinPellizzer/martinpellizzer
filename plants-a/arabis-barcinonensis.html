Arabis barcinonensis

Arabis barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcinonensis rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow or white flowers in early spring, and its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
