Aerides Quinquevulnera Var. Farmeri

Farmer's vanda, scientifically known as Aerides quinquevulnera var. farmeri, is a rare and striking orchid native to Southeast Asia. This hybrid orchid is celebrated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms that come in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant is named after the botanist who first identified it, adding to its historical significance. Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
