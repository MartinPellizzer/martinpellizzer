Acalypha lepinei

Acalypha lepinei, commonly known as the red-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy red leaves that contrast with its pale green stems. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acalypha lepinei prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
