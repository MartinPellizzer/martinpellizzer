Aloe juttae

Aloe juttae, commonly known as the Jutta's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers. Aloe juttae is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive dry conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
