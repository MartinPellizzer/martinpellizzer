Ammannia coccinea

Ammannia coccinea, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly toothed margin.

Ammannia coccinea is often found in moist soils along streambanks and in wetlands, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
