Anthericum hereroense

Anthericum hereroense, commonly known as the Herero onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces white to pale purple flowers in umbels, which bloom in spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.
