Anthyllis vulneraria f. anglica

Anthyllis vulneraria f. anglica, commonly known as English stitchwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is typically found in calcareous soils, often in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This plant is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in supporting pollinators.
