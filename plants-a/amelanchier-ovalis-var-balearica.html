Amelanchier ovalis var. balearica

Amelanchier ovalis var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balearic Islands.

It produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, offering a nutritious food source for wildlife and humans. The plant has distinctive oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its ornamental value, combined with its ecological benefits, makes it a valuable addition to local ecosystems.
