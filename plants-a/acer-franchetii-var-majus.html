Acer franchetii var. majus

Acer franchetii var. majus, commonly known as the giant maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter, often with a yellowish-green underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree in suitable climates.
