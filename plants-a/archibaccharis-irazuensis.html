Archibaccharis irazuensis

Archibaccharis irazuensis, commonly known as the Irazu baccharis, is a shrub native to the highland regions of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
