Adesmia radicifolia

Adesmia radicifolia, commonly known as the root-bearing legume, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces lens-shaped seeds enclosed in hairy pods.

Adesmia radicifolia is adapted to harsh environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
