Aronia nigra var. decumbens

Aronia nigra var. decumbens, commonly known as red chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and clusters of small, red berries that turn dark purple when mature. The plant produces flowers that are white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The shrub thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
