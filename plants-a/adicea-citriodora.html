Adicea citriodora

Adicea citriodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented or lemon tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon-like aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, yellow fruits with a thin, leathery skin. The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Its ornamental value and unique fragrance make it a popular choice for both horticultural and therapeutic applications.
