Asplenium gueinzianum var. subintegrifolium

Asplenium gueinzianum var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often deeply lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The fronds are bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that gives it a refined look. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
