Agropyron tianschanicum

Agropyron tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tian Shan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Agropyron tianschanicum plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
