Andropogon fulvibarbis

Andropogon fulvibarbis, commonly known as the yellow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive yellowish stripe along the midrib, which gives it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Andropogon fulvibarbis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in native habitats.
