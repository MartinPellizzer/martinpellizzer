Amaranthus hybridus var. hypochondriacus

Amaranthus hybridus var. hypochondriacus, commonly known as the Chinese spinach or Jerusalem artichoke, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into tuberous roots, which are the edible part. It is often cultivated for its nutrient-rich roots, which are high in inulin and minerals.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
