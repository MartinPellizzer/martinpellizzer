Arundinaria ibityensis

Arundinaria ibityensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a unique species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
