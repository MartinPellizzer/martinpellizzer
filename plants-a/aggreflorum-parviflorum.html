Aggreflorum parviflorum

Aggreflorum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered aggregate, is a unique plant species characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences.

It typically features small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow, adding a subtle aesthetic to its foliage. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
