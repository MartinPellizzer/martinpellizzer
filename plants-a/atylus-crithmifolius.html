Atylus crithmifolius

Atylus crithmifolius, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-blue foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Atylus crithmifolius is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
