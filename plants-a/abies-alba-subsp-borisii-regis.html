Abies alba subsp. borisii-regis

Abies alba subsp.

borisii-regis, commonly known as the Bosnian fir, is a subspecies of the European fir native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the shoots.

The cones are typically pendulous, with reddish-brown bracts and woody scales, and they mature to a dark brown color. This subspecies is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the mountain forest ecosystems in the Balkan Peninsula.
