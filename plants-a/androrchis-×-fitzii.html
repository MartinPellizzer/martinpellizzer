Androrchis × fitzii

Androrchis × fitzii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Androrchis scrobiculatus and Androrchis maculatus.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that are often pollinated by insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
