Allosorus Auriculatus

Golden ear, scientifically known as Allosorus auriculatus, is a strikingly unique fern native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia. Its most distinctive feature is its large, ear-shaped fronds that give it its common name. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively obscure species among fern enthusiasts. Its delicate yet resilient nature makes it a fascinating subject for both botanists and nature lovers alike.
