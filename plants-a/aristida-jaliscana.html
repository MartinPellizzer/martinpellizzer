Aristida jaliscana

Aristida jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in arid conditions.
