Aria rotundifolia

Aria rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf aralia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with rounded lobes, typically arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Aria rotundifolia thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
