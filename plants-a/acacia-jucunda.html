Acacia jucunda

Acacia jucunda, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Acacia jucunda is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
