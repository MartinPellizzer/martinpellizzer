Aegiphila umbellata

Aegiphila umbellata, commonly known as the Umbellate Aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink, often arranged in umbel-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, minty aroma.

Aegiphila umbellata is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
