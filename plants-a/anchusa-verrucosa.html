Anchusa verrucosa

Anchusa verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grey-green foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Anchusa verrucosa is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in full sun.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wildflower gardens.
