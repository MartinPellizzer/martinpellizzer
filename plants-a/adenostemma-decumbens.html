Adenostemma decumbens

Adenostemma decumbens, commonly known as creeping milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate and hairy.

Adenostemma decumbens is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
