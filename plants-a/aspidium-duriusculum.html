Aspidium duriusculum

Aspidium duriusculum, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, scaly texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in groups called sori. Aspidium duriusculum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

It is a hardy plant that can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
