Arctostaphylos pyrifolia

Arctostaphylos pyrifolia, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and lobed, ovate leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

The Pacific madrone is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native habitats.
