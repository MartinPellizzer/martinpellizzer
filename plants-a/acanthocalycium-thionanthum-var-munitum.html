Acanthocalycium thionanthum var. munitum

Acanthocalycium thionanthum var. munitum, commonly known as the Munitum Hedgehog Cactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered with a fine, white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
