Astragalus habamontis

Astragalus habamontis, commonly known as the Habamontis milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus habamontis is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
