Allium roseum var. grandiflorum

Allium roseum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant purple onion, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces large, showy purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
