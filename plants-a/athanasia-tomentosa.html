Athanasia tomentosa

Athanasia tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Athanasia tomentosa is also known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
