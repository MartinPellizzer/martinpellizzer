Aristolochia velutina

Aristolochia velutina, commonly known as the velvet pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble pipes. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its aromatic, often fragrant blossoms. Its unique floral structure is adapted to attract specific pollinators, such as flies and beetles.

However, it contains toxic compounds and is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
