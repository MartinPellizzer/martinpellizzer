Aegopogon fiebrigii

Aegopogon fiebrigii, commonly known as the Fiebrig's goat's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Aegopogon fiebrigii is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions where it can thrive in suitable conditions.
