Aster multiflorus var. exiguus

Aster multiflorus var. exiguus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
