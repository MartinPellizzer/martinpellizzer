Areca wallichiana

Areca wallichiana, commonly known as the Chinese areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually bear edible fruits. Its botanical features include palmate leaves with multiple leaflets and a fibrous, starchy fruit pulp.

Areca wallichiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
