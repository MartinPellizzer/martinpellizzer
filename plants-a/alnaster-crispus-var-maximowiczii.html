Alnaster crispus var. maximowiczii

Alnaster crispus var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean boxwood, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It is valued for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture as a decorative plant.

The leaves have a leathery texture and are arranged in a dense, upright manner.
