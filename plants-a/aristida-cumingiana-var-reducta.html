Aristida cumingiana var. reducta

Aristida cumingiana var. reducta, commonly known as the narrowleaf threeawn, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in open grasslands or along the edges of woodlands. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is loosely arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
