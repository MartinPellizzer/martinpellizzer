Acacia aemula subsp. muricata

Acacia aemula subsp.

muricata, commonly known as the rough acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are often followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a useful plant for erosion control.
