Abelia adenotricha

Abelia adenotricha, commonly known as the silky abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture, which contributes to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting floral display.
