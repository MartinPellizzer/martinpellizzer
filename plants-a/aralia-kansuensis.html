Aralia kansuensis

Aralia kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features compound leaves with a pinnate structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
