Aloe mirabilis

Aloe mirabilis, commonly known as the bishop's candle, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in spines and produces striking, orange-red flowers during the summer months. The plant's leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water once established.

Aloe mirabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden or indoor setting.
