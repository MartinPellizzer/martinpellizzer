Acrocomia glaucescens

Acrocomia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue acrocomia, is a tropical palm native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Acrocomia glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.
