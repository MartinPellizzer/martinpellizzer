Allium eriocoleum

Allium eriocoleum, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces a tall, slender stem topped with a cluster of small, pale purple flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
