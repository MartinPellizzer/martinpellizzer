Achyranthes brasiliana

Achyranthes brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian knotweed, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous root system and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
