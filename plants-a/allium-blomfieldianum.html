Allium blomfieldianum

Allium blomfieldianum, commonly known as the Blomfield's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a noteworthy species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
