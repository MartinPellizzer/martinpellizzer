Aeschynomene upembensis

Aeschynomene upembensis, commonly known as the African horsegram or small cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop for livestock.

Aeschynomene upembensis is also appreciated for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
