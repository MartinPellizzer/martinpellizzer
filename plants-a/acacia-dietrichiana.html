Acacia dietrichiana

Acacia dietrichiana, commonly known as the dwarf silver wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Acacia dietrichiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal or sandy soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
