Azalea microphyton

Azalea microphyton, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.

Azalea microphyton is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
