Adonis scrobiculata

Adonis scrobiculata, commonly known as the mountain adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and produces showy yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has a taproot system and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
