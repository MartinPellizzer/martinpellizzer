Aconitum variegatum lusus judenbergense

Aconitum variegatum lusus judenbergense, commonly known as the Judenburg variant of the variegated aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sparsely leafy stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with finely toothed margins.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant contain potent alkaloids.
