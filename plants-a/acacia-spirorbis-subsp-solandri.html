Acacia spirorbis subsp. solandri

Acacia spirorbis subsp.

solandri, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The subspecies solandri is distinguished by its more compact growth and yellow floral spikes.
