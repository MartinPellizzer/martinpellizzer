Aspalathus anthylloides

Aspalathus anthylloides, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.
