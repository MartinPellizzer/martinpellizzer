Aristida uniplumis

Aristida uniplumis, commonly known as the single-fruit grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with reddish or brown hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Aristida uniplumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments with minimal water requirements.
