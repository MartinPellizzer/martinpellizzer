Angianthus strictus var. laniger

Angianthus strictus var. laniger, commonly known as the woolly angianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
