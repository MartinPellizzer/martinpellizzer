Agapetes cuneifolia

Agapetes cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf silverton, is a species of shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. Agapetes cuneifolia thrives in cool, moist environments, typically at high altitudes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
