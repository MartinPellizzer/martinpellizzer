Amaranthus graecizans subsp. thellungianus

Amaranthus graecizans subsp.

thellungianus, commonly known as the Greek amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the stage of development. Its seeds are small, triangular, and typically reddish in color, which contribute to its ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering pattern.
