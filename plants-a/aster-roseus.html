Aster roseus

Aster roseus, commonly known as the rose campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pink to purple in color, with a central yellow disk, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Aster roseus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
