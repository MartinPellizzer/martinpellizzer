Asparagus chimanimanensis

Asparagus chimanimanensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
