Arthraxon prionodes

Arthraxon prionodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arthraxon, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Arthraxon prionodes is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
