Alnus japonica var. borealis

Alnus japonica var. borealis, commonly known as the northern alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Japan.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, elliptical leaves and catkins that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ecological importance is notable in forest ecosystems where it contributes to nutrient cycling.
