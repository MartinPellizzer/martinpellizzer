Abies veitchii subsp. sikokiana

Abies veitchii subsp.

sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate climates.
