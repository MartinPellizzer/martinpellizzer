Anathallis abbreviata

Anathallis abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stalked spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance with small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short, erect stem.

Anathallis abbreviata is an epiphytic species, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.
