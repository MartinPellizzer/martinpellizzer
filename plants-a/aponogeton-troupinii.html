Aponogeton troupinii

Aponogeton troupinii, commonly known as the African water sword, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical Africa.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, making it a popular choice for large water features. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in both freshwater and brackish environments, adapting well to various water conditions. It produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, adding an ornamental touch to ponds and aquariums.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, making it both a decorative and potentially invasive species in non-native habitats.
