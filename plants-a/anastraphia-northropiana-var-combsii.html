Anastraphia northropiana var. combsii

Anastraphia northropiana var. combsii, commonly known as the Comb's Anastraphia, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky inflorescence that resembles a comb, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
