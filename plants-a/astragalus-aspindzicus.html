Astragalus aspindzicus

Astragalus aspindzicus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Astragalus aspindzicus plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
