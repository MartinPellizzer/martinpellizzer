Acalypha baurii

Acalypha baurii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from silver to deep green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, woolly seed capsules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Acalypha baurii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage.
