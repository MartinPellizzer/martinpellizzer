Arenaria tmolea var. kotschyana

Arenaria tmolea var. kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschyan sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually succulent, helping it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
