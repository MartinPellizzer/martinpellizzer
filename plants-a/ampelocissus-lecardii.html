Ampelocissus lecardii

Ampelocissus lecardii, commonly known as the leopard's ear vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ampelocissus lecardii is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or walls.
