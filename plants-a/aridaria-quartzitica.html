Aridaria quartzitica

Aridaria quartzitica, commonly known as the quartzite desert lily, is a rare and resilient plant adapted to extreme arid environments.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils typical of desert regions, often found in areas with limited rainfall. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of white and pale pink, are small but striking, appearing during brief periods of moisture.

Due to its specialized habitat, Aridaria quartzitica is considered a vulnerable species, facing threats from habitat degradation and climate change.
