Aeonium × mascaense

Aeonium × mascaense, commonly known as the Chocolate Cactus, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
