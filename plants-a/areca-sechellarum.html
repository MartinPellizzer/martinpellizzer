Areca sechellarum

Areca sechellarum, commonly known as the Seychelles areca, is a flowering plant native to the Seychelles archipelago.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Areca sechellarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its growth is adapted to the tropical climate of the Seychelles, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
