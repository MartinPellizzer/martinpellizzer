Aeranthes henrici

Aeranthes henrici, commonly known as the Henry's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers, which can be white or pale pink, are known for their intricate structure and subtle fragrance.
