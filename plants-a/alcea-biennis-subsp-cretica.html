Alcea biennis subsp. cretica

Alcea biennis subsp.

cretica, commonly known as the Crete hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete. It is a biennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its sturdy stems and large, showy flowers.

The leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in racemes at the top of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
