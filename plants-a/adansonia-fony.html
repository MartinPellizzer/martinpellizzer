Adansonia fony

Adansonia fony, commonly known as the Fony baobab, is a species of the genus Adansonia, native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, bottle-shaped trunk and thick, flaky bark that provides insulation against temperature extremes. The tree produces large, white flowers that bloom in the rainy season, followed by woody pods containing inedible seeds. Its roots can store significant amounts of water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The Fony baobab plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
