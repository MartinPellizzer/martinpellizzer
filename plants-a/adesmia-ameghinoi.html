Adesmia ameghinoi

Adesmia ameghinoi, commonly known as the Argentine acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring bipinnate leaves and yellow or white flowers. Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
