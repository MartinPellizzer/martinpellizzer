Artocarpus cerifer

Artocarpus cerifer, commonly known as the candle tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces fruit that resembles candles, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. The fruit, which is inedible for humans, is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
