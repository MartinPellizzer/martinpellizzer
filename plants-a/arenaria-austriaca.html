Arenaria austriaca

Arenaria austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant has small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
