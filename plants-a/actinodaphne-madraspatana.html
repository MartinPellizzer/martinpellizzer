Actinodaphne madraspatana

Actinodaphne madraspatana, commonly known as the Madras laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in Ayurvedic practices.
