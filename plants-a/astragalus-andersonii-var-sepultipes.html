Astragalus andersonii var. sepultipes

Astragalus andersonii var. sepultipes, commonly known as the Anderson's milkvetch, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Mojave Desert.

It is a low-growing perennial forb with yellow flowers and hairy stems, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in creosote bush and blackbrush communities.
