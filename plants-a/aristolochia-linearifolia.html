Aristolochia linearifolia

Aristolochia linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
