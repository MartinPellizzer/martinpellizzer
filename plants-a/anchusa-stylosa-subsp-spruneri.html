Anchusa stylosa subsp. spruneri

Anchusa stylosa subsp.

spruneri, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Anchusa stylosa subsp.

spruneri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
