Aulaya grandiflora

Aulaya grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered aulaya, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a prominent yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Aulaya grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
