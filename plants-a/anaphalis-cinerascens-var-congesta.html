Anaphalis cinerascens var. congesta

Anaphalis cinerascens var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of numerous tiny white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
