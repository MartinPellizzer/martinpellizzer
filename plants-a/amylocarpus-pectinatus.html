Amylocarpus pectinatus

Amylocarpus pectinatus, commonly known as the pectinate arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and are used as a food source in some regions. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a unique structure called a spadix, which is surrounded by a spathe.

Amylocarpus pectinatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
