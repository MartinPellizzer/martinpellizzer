Agropyron cristatum var. fragile

Agropyron cristatum var. fragile, commonly known as the fragile wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
