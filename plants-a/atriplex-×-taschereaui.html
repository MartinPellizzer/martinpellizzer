Atriplex × taschereaui

Atriplex × taschereaui, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Atriplex × taschereaui is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in harsh environments.
