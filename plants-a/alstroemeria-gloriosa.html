Alstroemeria gloriosa

Alstroemeria gloriosa, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking South American plant belonging to the Alstroemeriaceae family.

It is characterized by its vivid orange and red flowers, which resemble lilies, and its long, narrow leaves. The plant is native to regions of Chile and Argentina, where it thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria gloriosa is also noted for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
