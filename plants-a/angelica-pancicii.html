Angelica pancicii

Angelica pancicii, commonly known as the Balkan angelica, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbellate flowers. Angelica pancicii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
