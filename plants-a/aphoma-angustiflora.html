Aphoma angustiflora

Aphoma angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered aphoma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
