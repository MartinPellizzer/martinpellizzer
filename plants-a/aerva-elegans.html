Aerva elegans

Aerva elegans, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in a single terminal head and bloom during the cooler months.

Aerva elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
