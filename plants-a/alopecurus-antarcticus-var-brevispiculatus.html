Alopecurus antarcticus var. brevispiculatus

Alopecurus antarcticus var. brevispiculatus, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its short, dense tufts of hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine and subantarctic regions. Its compact growth form and frost-resistant nature make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The variety brevispiculatus is distinguished by its shorter and more robust spikelets compared to other varieties of Alopecurus antarcticus.
