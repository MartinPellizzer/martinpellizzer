Acacia stricta var. pleiocephala

Acacia stricta var. pleiocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
