Alyssum minimum

Alyssum minimum, commonly known as dwarf alyssum, is a low-growing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white, pink, or purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with finely textured, silver-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum minimum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
