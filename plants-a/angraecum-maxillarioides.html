Angraecum maxillarioides

Angraecum maxillarioides, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, narrow leaf and produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant blooms during the dry season, typically from September to November. Its flowers have a unique structure with long, slender sepals and petals, and a prominent, spurred labellum.

Angraecum maxillarioides is adapted to arid conditions and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.
