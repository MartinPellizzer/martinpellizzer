Acacia pygmaea

Acacia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Aboriginal diets. Acacia pygmaea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
