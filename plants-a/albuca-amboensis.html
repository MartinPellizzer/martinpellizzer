Albuca amboensis

Albuca amboensis, commonly known as the East African albuca, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants such as lilies and daffodils.

Albuca amboensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
