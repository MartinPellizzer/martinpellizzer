Aciotis dichotoma var. longifolia

Aciotis dichotoma var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf aciotis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native environment, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
