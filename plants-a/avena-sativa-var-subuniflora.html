Avena sativa var. subuniflora

Avena sativa var. subuniflora, commonly known as slender oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and reduced inflorescence.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which develop into oval, straw-colored grains. Its botanical features make it suitable for forage and soil stabilization.

It is less commonly cultivated than other oat varieties but holds ecological and agricultural significance.
