Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp. stanfordiana

Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp.

stanfordiana, commonly known as Stanford manzanita, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces small, leathery berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
