Adenophora verticillata var. media

Adenophora verticillata var. media, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features upright stems that bear whorled clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
