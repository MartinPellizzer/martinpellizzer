Aster infirmus

Aster infirmus, commonly known as the weak aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators.

Aster infirmus is often found in alpine and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
