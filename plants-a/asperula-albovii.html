Asperula albovii

Asperula albovii, commonly known as the white-flowered woodbine, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, blooming in late spring to early summer. Asperula albovii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
