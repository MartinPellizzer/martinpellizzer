Adonis cyllenea var. cyllenea

Adonis cyllenea var. cyllenea, commonly known as the Greek Adonis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during early spring. The flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stems and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
