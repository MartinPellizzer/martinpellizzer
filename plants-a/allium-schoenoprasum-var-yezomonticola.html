Allium schoenoprasum var. yezomonticola

Allium schoenoprasum var. yezomonticola, commonly known as the Yezomonticola chives, is a lesser-known variant of the common chive.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Asia, particularly in the Yezo region of Japan. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in local cuisines. Its flowers are small, white or pale purple, and typically bloom in clusters at the top of the stems.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
