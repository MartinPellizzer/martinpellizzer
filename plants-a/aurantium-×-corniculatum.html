Aurantium × corniculatum

Aurantium × corniculatum, commonly known as the citron orange, is a hybrid citrus plant resulting from the cross between sweet orange (Citrus sinensis) and mandarin orange (Citrus reticulata).

It is characterized by its thick, leathery rind and large, segmented fruit that is typically green to yellow in color. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its flowers are fragrant and white, typical of many citrus species.

The fruit is used in both culinary and medicinal applications, and the plant is valued for its adaptability to various climates.
