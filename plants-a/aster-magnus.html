Aster magnus

Aster magnus, commonly known as the Great Aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is sturdy and erect.

Aster magnus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
