Achnatherum lemmonii subsp. pubescens

Achnatherum lemmonii subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent needlegrass, is a grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
