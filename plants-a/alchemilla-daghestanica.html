Alchemilla daghestanica

Alchemilla daghestanica, commonly known as the Caucasian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Alchemilla daghestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
