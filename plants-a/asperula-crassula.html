Asperula crassula

Asperula crassula, commonly known as the dwarf candytuft, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that remain throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Asperula crassula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
