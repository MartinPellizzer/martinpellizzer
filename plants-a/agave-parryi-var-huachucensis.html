Agave parryi var. huachucensis

Agave parryi var. huachucensis, commonly known as the Huachucensis Agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and large, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, white to yellowish flowers in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
