Aechmea callichroma

Aechmea callichroma, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It features a rosette of dark green, spiky leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant red bracts that surround its showy yellow flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Aechmea callichroma is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea callichroma has rosette-shaped leaves that are stiff, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy surface.

The plant lacks a typical stem, instead having a short, fibrous base with a thick, spongy, and pale green pseudobark. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by bright red bracts, with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, dispersing seeds through animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.
