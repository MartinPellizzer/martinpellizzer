Amellus flosculosus

Amellus flosculosus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Amellus flosculosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
