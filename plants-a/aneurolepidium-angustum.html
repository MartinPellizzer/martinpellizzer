Aneurolepidium angustum

Aneurolepidium angustum, commonly known as narrowleaf brome, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a perennial species that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting insect biodiversity.
