Artanthe colubrina var. glandulosa

Artanthe colubrina var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular colubrina, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
