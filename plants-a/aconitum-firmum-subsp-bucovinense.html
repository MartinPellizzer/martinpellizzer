Aconitum firmum subsp. bucovinense

Aconitum firmum subsp.

bucovinense, commonly known as the Bucovina monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple hooded flowers.

The plant has deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling.
