Aconitum firmum subsp. maninense

Aconitum firmum subsp.

maninense, commonly known as the Maninense Monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant features striking blue to purple spurred flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Like other members of the Ranunculaceae family, it contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
