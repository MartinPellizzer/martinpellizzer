Aeschynanthus radicans var. robustior

Aeschynanthus radicans var. robustior, commonly known as the wax plant or hanging waxflower, is a trailing evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers warm, humid conditions. It is often cultivated as a hanging plant due to its cascading growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardens and greenhouses.
