Ardisia littoralis

Ardisia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal ardisia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant produces small, glossy green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its berries, which are dark red when mature, are an important food source for various bird species.

Ardisia littoralis plays a significant role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
