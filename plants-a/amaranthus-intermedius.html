Amaranthus intermedius

Amaranthus intermedius, commonly known as redroot amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bisexual flowers that are typically green to reddish in hue. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Amaranthus intermedius is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
