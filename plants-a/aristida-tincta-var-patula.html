Aristida tincta var. patula

Aristida tincta var. patula, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and spikelets that are typically tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, bristly flowers that are wind-pollinated, and it thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in grasslands, deserts, and along roadsides, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
