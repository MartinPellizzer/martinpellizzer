Amaryllis × glaucescens

Amaryllis × glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebell amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis bellona and Amaryllis belladonna.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep blue, often with a bluish hue. The plant produces thick, fleshy bulbs that store energy for flowering and growth. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
