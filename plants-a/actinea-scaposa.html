Actinea scaposa

Actinea scaposa, commonly known as the slender actinio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale green flowers in late spring. Its flowers are clustered in a spike-like arrangement and are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

Actinea scaposa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
