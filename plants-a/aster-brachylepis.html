Aster brachylepis

Aster brachylepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It produces small, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its distinguishing appearance.
