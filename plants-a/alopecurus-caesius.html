Alopecurus caesius

Alopecurus caesius, commonly known as silver hair vetch, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired inflorescence and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Alopecurus caesius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
