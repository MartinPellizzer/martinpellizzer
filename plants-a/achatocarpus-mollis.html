Achatocarpus mollis

Achatocarpus mollis, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its soft, silky texture on the underside of its leaves, which gives it its common name. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Achatocarpus mollis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
