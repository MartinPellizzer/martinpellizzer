Anthurium ochranthum

Anthurium ochranthum, commonly known as the red-veined anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of spadices, which are inflorescences that are often white or pale yellow with a red spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

Anthurium ochranthum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
