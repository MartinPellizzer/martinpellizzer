Asystasia chelonoides var. variabilis

Asystasia chelonoides var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable asystasia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is notable for its variability in leaf shape and flower color, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
