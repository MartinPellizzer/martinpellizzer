Anemone nemorosa robinsoniana

Anemone nemorosa robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and features delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and basal leaves that are heart-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually 2-4 cm in diameter and have five to nine petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
