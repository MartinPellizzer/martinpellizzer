Acer negundo var. pseudocalifornicum

Acer negundo var. pseudocalifornicum, commonly known as the California boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The tree is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in riparian zones and can tolerate urban environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across different habitats.
