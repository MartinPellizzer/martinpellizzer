Anthemis triumfetti subsp. aligulata

Anthemis triumfetti subsp.

aligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets.

The plant is known for its aromatic, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is finely serrated and covered in a dense layer of glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and strong scent.
