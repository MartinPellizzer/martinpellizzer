Anemia wightiana

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
