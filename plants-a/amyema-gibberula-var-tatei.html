Amyema gibberula var. tatei

Amyema gibberula var. tatei, commonly known as the Tasmanian thornbush, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Amyema gibberula var. tatei is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.
