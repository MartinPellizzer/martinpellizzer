Andropogon pubescens var. breviaristata

Andropogon pubescens var. breviaristata, commonly known as the narrow-awn switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear bristle-like awns, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and bioenergy production.
