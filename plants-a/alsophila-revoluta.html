Alsophila revoluta

Alsophila revoluta, commonly known as the revolute tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow quite long, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila revoluta plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
