Abies georgei

Abies georgei, commonly known as the George fir, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. The cones of Abies georgei are typically pendulous, with a reddish-brown color and a distinctive, elongated shape. This species thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in its native range.
