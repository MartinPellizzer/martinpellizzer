Aconogonon campanulatum var. fulvidum

Aconogonon campanulatum var. fulvidum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
