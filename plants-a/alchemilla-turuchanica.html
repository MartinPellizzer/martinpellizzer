Alchemilla turuchanica

Alchemilla turuchanica, commonly known as Turuchan lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Alchemilla turuchanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
