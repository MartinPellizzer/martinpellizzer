Astragalus onobrychis var. orientalis

Astragalus onobrychis var. orientalis, commonly known as oriental horseshoe vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain several seeds, and it has small, compound leaves with stipules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
