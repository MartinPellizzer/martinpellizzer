Acicarpha tribuloides var. pinnatifida

Acicarpha tribuloides var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant is herbaceous, with slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its fruit is a dry, indehiscent capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

This species is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
