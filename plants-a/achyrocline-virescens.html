Achyrocline virescens

Achyrocline virescens, commonly known as green everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is known for its resilience to salt spray and wind. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The flowers are small and white to pale blue, blooming in clusters that resemble a cluster of tiny stars.
