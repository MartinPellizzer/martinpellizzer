Anacampseros linguifolia

Anacampseros linguifolia, commonly known as the tongue-shaped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tongue-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, usually during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique leaf shape and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and easy to care for.
