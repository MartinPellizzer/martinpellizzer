Armeniaca longifolia

Armeniaca longifolia, commonly known as the common jujube, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnate with 10 to 20 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, reddish-brown fruits. The fruits are rich in nutrients, including vitamins and antioxidants, and are valued for their sweet, chewy texture.

Armeniaca longifolia is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
