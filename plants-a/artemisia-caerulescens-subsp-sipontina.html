Artemisia caerulescens subsp. sipontina

Artemisia caerulescens subsp.

sipontina, commonly known as the Sipontine wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features silver-gray, aromatic leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in narrow, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
