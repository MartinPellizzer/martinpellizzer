Amylocarpus

Amylocarpus, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in starch and used as a food source in various parts of the world. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a spathe and spadix structure typical of the Araceae family.
