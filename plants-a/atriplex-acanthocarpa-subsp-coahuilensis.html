Atriplex acanthocarpa subsp. coahuilensis

Atriplex acanthocarpa subsp.

coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuilan saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, spine-tipped leaves adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant produces yellow flowers in compact clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well-suited for harsh desert environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for wildlife in its native range.
