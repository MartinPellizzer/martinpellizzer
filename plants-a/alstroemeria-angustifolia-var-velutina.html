Alstroemeria angustifolia var. velutina

Alstroemeria angustifolia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf alstroemeria, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
