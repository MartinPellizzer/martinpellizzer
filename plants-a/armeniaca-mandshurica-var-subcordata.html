Armeniaca mandshurica var. subcordata

Armeniaca mandshurica var. subcordata, commonly known as the Manchurian apricot.

This variant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China and the Russian Far East. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. The leaves are ovate with a slightly heart-shaped base, and the plant is adapted to temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
