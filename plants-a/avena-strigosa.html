Avena strigosa

Avena strigosa, commonly known as slender oat, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in moist environments. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types.

Avena strigosa is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
