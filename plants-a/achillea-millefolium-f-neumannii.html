Achillea millefolium f. neumannii

Achillea millefolium f. neumannii, commonly known as the Neumann's yarrow, is a horticultural form of the common yarrow.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, flat-topped flower heads. This variant typically exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
