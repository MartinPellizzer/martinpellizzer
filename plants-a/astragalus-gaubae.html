Astragalus gaubae

Astragalus gaubae, commonly known as the Gaubae milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
