Acacia caffra var. namaquensis

Acacia caffra var. namaquensis, commonly known as theNamaqua thornveld acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and phyllodes that are narrow and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
