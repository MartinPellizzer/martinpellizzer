Aechmea catendensis

Aechmea catendensis, commonly known as the Catendens bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Aechmea catendensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
