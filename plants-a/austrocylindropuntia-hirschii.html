Austrocylindropuntia hirschii

Austrocylindropuntia hirschii, commonly known as the Hirsch's columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This cactus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
