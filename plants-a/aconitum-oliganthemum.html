Aconitum oliganthemum

Aconitum oliganthemum, commonly known as the few-flowered monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple hood-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring palmately lobed leaves and a tuberous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aconitum oliganthemum is noted for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution during handling.
