Asystasia congensis

Asystasia congensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with contrasting coloration on the inner petals. Its leaves are typically velvety and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Asystasia congensis is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its long-lasting blooms.
