Aspidium pulcherrimum

Aspidium pulcherrimum, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside.

Aspidium pulcherrimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
