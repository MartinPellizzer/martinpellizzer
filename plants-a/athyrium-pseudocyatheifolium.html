Athyrium pseudocyatheifolium

Athyrium pseudocyatheifolium, commonly known as the false cyatheifolium fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The foliage exhibits a striking coloration, with young leaves displaying a vibrant green that matures to a deeper, more subdued hue.
