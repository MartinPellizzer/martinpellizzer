Acacia coolgardiensis

Acacia coolgardiensis, commonly known as the Coolgardie acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, flat, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Acacia coolgardiensis is adapted to arid conditions and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
