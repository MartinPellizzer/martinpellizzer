Amblynotopsis humilis

Amblynotopsis humilis, commonly known as the lowland amblynotopsis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and are arranged in clusters.

Amblynotopsis humilis is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
