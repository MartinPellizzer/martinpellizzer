Asterocephalus urceolatus

Asterocephalus urceolatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive inflorescence.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is native to specific regions in southern Africa. The plant features dense, cylindrical flower clusters that resemble a bottlebrush, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often woody and branched.

Asterocephalus urceolatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
