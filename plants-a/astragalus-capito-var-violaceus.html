Astragalus capito var. violaceus

Astragalus capito var. violaceus, commonly known as purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or violet. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
