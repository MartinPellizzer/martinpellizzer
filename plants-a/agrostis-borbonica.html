Agrostis borbonica

Agrostis borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon grass, is a species of grass native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Agrostis borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
