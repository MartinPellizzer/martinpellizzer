Aster lindleyanus var. ciliolatus

Aster lindleyanus var. ciliolatus, commonly known as the ciliate aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple-to-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with finely toothed margins, and its stems are typically hairy and glandular. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
