Amaranthus dubius var. crassespicatus

Amaranthus dubius var. crassespicatus, commonly known as rough amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and branched, often hairy stems. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences. This species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a food source.
