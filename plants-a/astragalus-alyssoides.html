Astragalus alyssoides

Astragalus alyssoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves that help reduce water loss.

Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
