Arundo boivinii

Arundo boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along water bodies.

Its root system is extensive, making it an effective plant for erosion control in aquatic habitats.
