Avena sativa var. barbata

Avena sativa var. barbata, commonly known as the barbed oat, is a variant of the common oat characterized by its distinctive barbed awns on the lemma.

This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is an annual grass that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, glabrous stems. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
