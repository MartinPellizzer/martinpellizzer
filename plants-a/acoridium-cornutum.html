Acoridium cornutum

Acoridium cornutum, commonly known as the horned acoridium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Acoraceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
