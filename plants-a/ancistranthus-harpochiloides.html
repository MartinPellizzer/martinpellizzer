Ancistranthus harpochiloides

Ancistranthus harpochiloides, commonly known as the Harpochilus orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil, and they absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

The orchid's unique floral structure and adaptability to specific ecological niches make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
