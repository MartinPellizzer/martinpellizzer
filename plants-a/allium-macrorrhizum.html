Allium macrorrhizum

Allium macrorrhizum, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy bulbs and tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight sheen.

Allium macrorrhizum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
