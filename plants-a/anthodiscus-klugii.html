Anthodiscus klugii

Anthodiscus klugii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, velvety flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its leaves are thick, fleshy, and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native arid environment.

Anthodiscus klugii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
