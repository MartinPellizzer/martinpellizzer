Acacia solenota

Acacia solenota, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Acacia solenota is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
