Acinos × jahnianus

Acinos × jahnianus, commonly known as the hybrid alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
