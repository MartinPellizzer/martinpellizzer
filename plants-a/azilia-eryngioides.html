Azilia eryngioides

Azilia eryngioides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its spiny, blue-gray leaves and showy, blue or purple composite flowers that bloom in summer. Azilia eryngioides is adapted to withstand salty air and strong coastal winds, making it a hardy species.

Its unique appearance and ecological resilience make it a notable plant in coastal flora.
