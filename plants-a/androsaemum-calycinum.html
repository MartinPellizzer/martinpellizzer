Androsaemum calycinum

Androsaemum calycinum, commonly known as the California lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a distinctive, aromatic fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and native plant collections.
