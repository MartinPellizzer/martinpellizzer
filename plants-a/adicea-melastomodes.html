Adicea melastomodes

Adicea melastomodes, commonly known as the red-flowered melastome, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display vibrant red or purple hues. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often woody and branching. Adicea melastomodes produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
