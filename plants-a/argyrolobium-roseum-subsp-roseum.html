Argyrolobium roseum subsp. roseum

Argyrolobium roseum subsp.

roseum, commonly known as the pink burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume morphology.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as a forage plant.
