Amorphophallus campanulatus f. darnleyensis

Amorphophallus campanulatus f. darnleyensis, commonly known as the Darnley's elephant's foot, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy underground tuber, which can grow to impressive sizes, often resembling a giant, gnarled root. The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence with large, white, bell-shaped flowers that emit a strong, unpleasant odor, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a cool, dry climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
