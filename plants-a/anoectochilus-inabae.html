Anoectochilus inabae

Anoectochilus inabae, commonly known as the Izu orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and possesses lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers with yellow lips, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
