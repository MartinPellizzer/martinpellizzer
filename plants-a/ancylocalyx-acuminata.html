Ancylocalyx acuminata

Ancylocalyx acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
