Aster arguta

Aster arguta, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, while its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

Aster arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
