Anemone ambigua

Anemone ambigua, commonly known as the common anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and forest floors.

Its botanical name reflects its ambiguous flower structure, which can resemble both a star and a bell.
