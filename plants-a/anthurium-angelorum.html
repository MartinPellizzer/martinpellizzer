Anthurium angelorum

Anthurium angelorum, commonly known as the angel's tears or the white anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and its striking white spadix surrounded by a spathe that resembles a tear. The plant is part of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium angelorum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
