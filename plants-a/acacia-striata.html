Acacia striata

Acacia striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and pairs of small, narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes during the spring, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. Acacia striata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
