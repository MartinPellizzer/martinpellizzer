Antennaria aprica var. aureola

Antennaria aprica var. aureola, commonly known as the golden mouse ear.

It is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are small, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in flat-topped inflorescences, typically blooming in early spring.

This plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
