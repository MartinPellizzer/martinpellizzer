Adriana tomentosa

Adriana tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Adriana tomentosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
