Arracacia ternata

Arracacia ternata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and trifoliate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
