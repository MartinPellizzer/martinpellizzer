Aristida fredscholzii

Aristida fredscholzii, commonly known as the Fredscholz's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
