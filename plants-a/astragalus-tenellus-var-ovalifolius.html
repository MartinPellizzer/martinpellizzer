Astragalus tenellus var. ovalifolius

Astragalus tenellus var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are oval-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
