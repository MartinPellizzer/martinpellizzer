Astragalus sohrevardianus

Astragalus sohrevardianus, commonly known as the Sohrevardian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Astragalus sohrevardianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
