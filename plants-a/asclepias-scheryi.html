Asclepias scheryi

Asclepias scheryi, commonly known as Schery's milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Schery's milkweed is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its role in supporting monarch butterfly populations.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
