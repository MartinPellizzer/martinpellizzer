Astragalus pauciflorus

Astragalus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus pauciflorus is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
