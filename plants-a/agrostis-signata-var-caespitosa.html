Agrostis signata var. caespitosa

Agrostis signata var. caespitosa, commonly known as bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms low-growing, compact clumps and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
