Androsace geraniifolia var. pedicellata

Androsace geraniifolia var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate rock jasmine, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, cushion-like clusters of foliage. The leaves are typically spoon-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, which are borne on slender pedicels.

This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
