Alternanthera hirtula subvar. straminea

Alternanthera hirtula subvar. straminea, commonly known as the golden Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant golden to orange-red foliage, which becomes more intense under strong light conditions. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in warm, shallow waters and is often used in aquascaping for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth rate makes it suitable for both freshwater and brackish water environments.
