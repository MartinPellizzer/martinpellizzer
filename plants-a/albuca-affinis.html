Albuca affinis

Albuca affinis, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tulip-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Albuca affinis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
