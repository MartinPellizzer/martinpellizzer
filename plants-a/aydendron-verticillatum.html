Aydendron verticillatum

Aydendron verticillatum, commonly known as the spiralwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves around its stems, a feature that gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often aromatic and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

Aydendron verticillatum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
