Annona xylopiifolia

Annona xylopiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian custard apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, greenish-yellow fruits with a rough, spiny exterior.

The fruit contains a soft, creamy pulp surrounding a central core of seeds, and it is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
