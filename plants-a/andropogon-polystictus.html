Andropogon polystictus

Andropogon polystictus, commonly known as the striped grass or black panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a distinctive striped appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Andropogon polystictus is a hardy species that is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil conditions.
