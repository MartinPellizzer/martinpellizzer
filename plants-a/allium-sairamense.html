Allium sairamense

Allium sairamense, commonly known as the Sairam onion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spherical bulbs that are often used in local cuisines. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in umbels, and bloom in the summer months.

Allium sairamense is valued for its aromatic properties and is also studied for its potential medicinal uses.
