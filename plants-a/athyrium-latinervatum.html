Athyrium latinervatum

Athyrium latinervatum, commonly known as the narrow-veined lady fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds bearing sporangia on the undersides of the pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves have a distinctive, refined appearance with finely divided, linear segments and a pale green color.
