Arundinaria burchellii

Arundinaria burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's bamboo, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense clumps.

Arundinaria burchellii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
