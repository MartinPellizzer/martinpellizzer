Amaryllis × fulva

Amaryllis × fulva, commonly known as the orange amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papilio.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep orange to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Amaryllis × fulva is popular as a ornamental plant, especially during the holiday season due to its vibrant blooms.
