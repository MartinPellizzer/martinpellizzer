Acacia multijuga

Acacia multijuga, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia multijuga is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
