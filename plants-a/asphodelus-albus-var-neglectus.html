Asphodelus albus var. neglectus

Asphodelus albus var. neglectus, commonly known as the white asphodel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flower stalks with small, white, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the Asphodelaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Its structure and growth habits make it a hardy plant suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.
