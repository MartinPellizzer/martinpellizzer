Anthemis montana subsp. chrysocephala

Anthemis montana subsp.

chrysocephala, commonly known as the goldenhead wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
