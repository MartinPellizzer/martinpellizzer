Aquilegia jonesii

Aquilegia jonesii, commonly known as Jones' columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant floral colors. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces seed pods that burst open to disperse seeds.
