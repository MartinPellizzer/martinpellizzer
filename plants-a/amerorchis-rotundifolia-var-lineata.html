Amerorchis rotundifolia var. lineata

Amerorchis rotundifolia var. lineata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation areas due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
