Aeschynomene patagonica

Aeschynomene patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Aeschynomene patagonica is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
