Arenaria affinis

Arenaria affinis, commonly known as sand pink, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal dunes or rocky habitats.

Arenaria affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy environments.
