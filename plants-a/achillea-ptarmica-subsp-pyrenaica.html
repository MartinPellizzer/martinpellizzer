Achillea ptarmica subsp. pyrenaica

Achillea ptarmica subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as Pyrenean yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region. It features finely divided, silver-gray leaves and flat, clustered inflorescences of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.
