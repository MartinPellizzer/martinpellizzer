Astragalus kialensis

Astragalus kialensis, commonly known as the Kialensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and may have traditional uses in local herbal medicine.

Its ecological significance is linked to its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
