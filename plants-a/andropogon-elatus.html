Andropogon elatus

Andropogon elatus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Switchgrass is known for its ability to thrive in both wet and dry conditions, making it a resilient species.

It is also valued for its potential as a biofuel crop due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements.
