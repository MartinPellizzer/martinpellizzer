Abarema poilanei

Abarema poilanei, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, ornamental flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
