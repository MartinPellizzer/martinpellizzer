Ambrosia humi

Ambrosia humi, commonly known as the common ragweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its widespread distribution. Ambrosia humi is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause seasonal allergies in humans.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
