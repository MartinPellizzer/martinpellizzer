Amorphophallus barteri

Amorphophallus barteri, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a dramatic visual presence. The plant produces a spadix, a fleshy, spikelike structure surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is often mistaken for a flower. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

The plant has both ornamental and potential medicinal uses, though it is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
