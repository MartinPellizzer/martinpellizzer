Ageratum peckii

Ageratum peckii, commonly known as Mexican Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, fluffy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture.

Ageratum peckii is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
