Aspalathus pendula

Aspalathus pendula, commonly known as the weeping bushveld pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, slender branches that bear small, pea-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Aspalathus pendula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
