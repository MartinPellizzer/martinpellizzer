Acacia leucadendron

Acacia leucadendron, commonly known as the white candle bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are typically followed by woody, cone-like seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
