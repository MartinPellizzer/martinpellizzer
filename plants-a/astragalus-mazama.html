Astragalus mazama

Astragalus mazama, commonly known as Mazama milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

Astragalus mazama is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity.
