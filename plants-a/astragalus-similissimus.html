Astragalus similissimus

Astragalus similissimus, commonly known as the similar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species in some regions.
