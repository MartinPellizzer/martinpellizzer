Aquilegia hybrida

Aquilegia hybrida, commonly known as the garden columbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces clusters of small, seed-like fruits known as achenes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Aquilegia hybrida is native to Europe and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
