Anchusa montelinasana

Anchusa montelinasana, commonly known as the Alboraya flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anchusa montelinasana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
