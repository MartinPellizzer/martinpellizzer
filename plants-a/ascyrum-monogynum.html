Ascyrum monogynum

Ascyrum monogynum, commonly known as the single-seed orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant typically produces a single, showy flower that is often brightly colored, attracting specific pollinators. Its growth habit is terrestrial, with a slender stem and a single leaf.

Ascyrum monogynum is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
