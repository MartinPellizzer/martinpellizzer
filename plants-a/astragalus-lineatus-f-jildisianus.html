Astragalus lineatus f. jildisianus

Astragalus lineatus f. jildisianus, commonly known as the Jildisian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
