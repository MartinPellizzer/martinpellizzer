Acacia drewiana subsp. minor

Acacia drewiana subsp.

minor, commonly known as the dwarf golden wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It is often found in sandy soils and is a component of the native flora in the region.

The subspecies is distinguished from other acacia species by its compact growth habit and smaller size.
