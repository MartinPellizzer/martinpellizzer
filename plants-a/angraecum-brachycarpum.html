Angraecum brachycarpum

Angraecum brachycarpum, commonly known as the short-stemmed star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, showcasing its adaptive strategies to the arid environment. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss, and its roots are adapted for efficient nutrient absorption.

Angraecum brachycarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
