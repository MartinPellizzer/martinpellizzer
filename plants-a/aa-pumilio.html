Aa pumilio

Aa pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy form with a short trunk and a dense crown of feathery fronds. The plant features a root system that helps anchor it in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically light green and can grow up to several feet in length.

Aa pumilio thrives in humid climates and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Taxonomy

Aa pumilio belongs to the kingdom Plantae, phylum Magnoliophyta, class Equisetopsida, subclass Equisetidae, order Equisetales, family Equisetaceae, genus *Equisetum*, and species *Equisetum pumilum*.

It is a type of horsetail, commonly found in moist environments. This plant is known for its unique morphology, featuring jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
