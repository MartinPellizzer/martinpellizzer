Acacia tingoorensis

Acacia tingoorensis, commonly known as the Tingoorensis wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Acacia tingoorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
