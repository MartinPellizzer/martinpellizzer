Arundinaria amabilis var. sativa

Arundinaria amabilis var. sativa, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a variety of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a material for crafting. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental value.

This bamboo species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a versatile plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
