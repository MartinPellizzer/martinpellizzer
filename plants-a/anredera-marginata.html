Anredera marginata

Anredera marginata, commonly known as silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a trailing or climbing perennial that produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a ground cover or hanging plant.

Silver vine is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
