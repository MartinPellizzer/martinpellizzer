Aegopogon bryophilus

Aegopogon bryophilus, commonly known as the moss-feeding grass, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical significance lies in its unique ecological role as a host plant for specific insect species.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
