Asplenium aethiopicum subsp. dodecaploideum

Asplenium aethiopicum subsp.

dodecaploideum, commonly known as the African maidenhair fern, is a member of the Aspleniaceae family. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous narrow segments.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical significance lies in its polyploid nature, which contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
