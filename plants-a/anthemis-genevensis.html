Anthemis genevensis

Anthemis genevensis, commonly known as the Genevan everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
