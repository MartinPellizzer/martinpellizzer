Aechmea phanerophlebia

Aechmea phanerophlebia, commonly known as the showy pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for hydration. The plant produces vibrant, spathiflorous inflorescences with showy, red or orange bracts. Its leaves are typically broad, leathery, and covered in fine trichomes, which help in water retention.

Aechmea phanerophlebia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Morphology

Aechmea phanerophlebia has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, arranged in a compact circle, with a waxy, dark green surface and a silvery underside.

The stem is short and stout, with a smooth, pale green bark that grows upright from the base of the plant. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of yellow or red, and bloom during the dry season, relying on wind pollination. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
