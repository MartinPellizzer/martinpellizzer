Anathallis linearifolia

Anathallis linearifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf anathallis, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid, shaded understory of the forest. Its flowers are usually short-lived, blooming during the wet season.

Anathallis linearifolia plays a role in the ecosystem by providing nectar for certain pollinators, though it remains relatively understudied.
