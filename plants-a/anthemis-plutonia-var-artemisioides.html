Anthemis plutonia var. artemisioides

Anthemis plutonia var. artemisioides, commonly known as the Artemisia-like anthemis, is a variant of the Anthemis genus characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance and aromatic properties.
