Acacia cochliacantha f. houghii

Acacia cochliacantha f. houghii, commonly known as the Hough's acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing food for various insects and animals.
