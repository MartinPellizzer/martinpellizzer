Azalea rosmarinifolia var. alba

Azalea rosmarinifolia var. alba, commonly known as the white rosemary azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has evergreen, leathery leaves that are aromatic, resembling those of rosemary. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and pleasant fragrance make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
