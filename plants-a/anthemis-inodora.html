Anthemis inodora

Anthemis inodora, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in summer. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, thriving in sunny conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
