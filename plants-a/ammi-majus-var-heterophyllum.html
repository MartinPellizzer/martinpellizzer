Ammi majus var. heterophyllum

Ammi majus var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the lesser cow parsley, is a biennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a notable plant in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
