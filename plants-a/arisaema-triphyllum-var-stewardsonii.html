Arisaema triphyllum var. stewardsonii

Arisaema triphyllum var. stewardsonii, commonly known as the Stewartsont's Indian turnip, is a variant of the Indian turnip native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and a spadix surrounded by a hooded spathe. The plant produces a unique, fleshy underground tuber that is often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a short, stout rhizome and a complex inflorescence structure typical of the Araceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
