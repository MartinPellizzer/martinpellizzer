Aleuritopteris aurantiaca

Aleuritopteris aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically bright yellow to orange in color, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Aleuritopteris aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
