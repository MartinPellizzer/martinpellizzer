Allium calabrum

Allium calabrum, commonly known as the Calabrian garlic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a bulb composed of several fleshy, white or pale yellow cloves encased in a papery skin. The plant produces upright, hollow stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spherical, purple or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its aromatic foliage and edible bulbs make it both ornamental and culinary valuable.
