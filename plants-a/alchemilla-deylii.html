Alchemilla deylii

Alchemilla deylii, commonly known as the Deylii Lady's Mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive basal rosette of velvety, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Alchemilla deylii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
