Artemisia obtusiloba var. glabra

Artemisia obtusiloba var. glabra, commonly known as whiteleaf sage, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
