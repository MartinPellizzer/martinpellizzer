Adonis cullellii

Adonis cullellii, commonly known as the Cretan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky or dry soils.

Adonis cullellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.

Morphology

Adonis cullellii has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with lanceolate leaflets that are typically green and slightly hairy.

The stems are upright, woody, and have a rough, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its flowers are showy, with yellow petals and a red or orange center, blooming in late spring to early summer, and are pollinated by insects such as bees. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
