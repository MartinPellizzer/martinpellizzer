Abarema fournieri

Abarema fournieri, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces showy, pea-like flowers in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
