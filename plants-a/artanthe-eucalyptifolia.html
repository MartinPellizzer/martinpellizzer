Artanthe eucalyptifolia

Artanthe eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus-leaved artanthe, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that emit a faintly aromatic scent, reminiscent of eucalyptus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Artanthe eucalyptifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
