Agyneia flexuosa

Agyneia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible agyneia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Agyneia flexuosa is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
