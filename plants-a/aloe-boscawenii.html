Aloe boscawenii

Aloe boscawenii, commonly known as the Boscowen's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, designed to store water. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Aloe boscawenii thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
