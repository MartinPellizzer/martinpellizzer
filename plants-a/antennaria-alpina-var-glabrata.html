Antennaria alpina var. glabrata

Antennaria alpina var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, pale green to yellowish leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabrata.". The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as meadows and scree slopes.
