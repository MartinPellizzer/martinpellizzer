Acacia cornigera var. americana

Acacia cornigera var. americana, commonly known as the American acacia, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It features slender, thorned stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow, globular inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
