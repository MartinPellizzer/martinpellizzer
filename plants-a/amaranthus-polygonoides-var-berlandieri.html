Amaranthus polygonoides var. berlandieri

Amaranthus polygonoides var. berlandieri, commonly known as desert amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with branched stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by oblong, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
