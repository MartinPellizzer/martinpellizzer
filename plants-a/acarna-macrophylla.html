Acarna macrophylla

Acarna macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces a showy inflorescence with a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
