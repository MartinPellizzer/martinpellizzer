Alchemilla vulgaris var. wichurae

Alchemilla vulgaris var. wichurae, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a dense rosette of basal, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often silver-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in loose panicles. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is frequently used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to retain moisture.
