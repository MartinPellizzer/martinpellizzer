Arisaema nikoense var. kaimontanum

Arisaema nikoense var. kaimontanum, commonly known as the Kaiman Island arisaema, is a rare and distinctive member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, specifically found in the Kaiman Islands. The plant features a striking, hooded spathe that encloses a spadix, a characteristic structure common in arum species. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, with a deep green color and a rough texture.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
