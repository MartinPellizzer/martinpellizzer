Astragalus alopecuroides var. hookeri

Astragalus alopecuroides var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
