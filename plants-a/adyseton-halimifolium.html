Adyseton halimifolium

Adyseton halimifolium, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in marshy environments and is often found near water sources. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Adyseton halimifolium plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
