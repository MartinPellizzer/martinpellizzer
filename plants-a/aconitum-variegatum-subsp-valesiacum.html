Aconitum variegatum subsp. valesiacum

Aconitum variegatum subsp.

valesiacum, commonly known as the Valesiacean monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple, helmet-shaped flowers.

The plant features palmately compound leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and showy blooms.
