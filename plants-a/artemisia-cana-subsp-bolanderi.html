Artemisia cana subsp. bolanderi

Artemisia cana subsp.

bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong, distinctive scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal properties.
