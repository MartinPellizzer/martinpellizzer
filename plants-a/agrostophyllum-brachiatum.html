Agrostophyllum brachiatum

Agrostophyllum brachiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to potential host trees.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals that disperse its seeds.
