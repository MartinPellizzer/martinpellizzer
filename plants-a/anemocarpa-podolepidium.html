Anemocarpa podolepidium

Anemocarpa podolepidium, commonly known as the mountain anemone, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, hairy seeds enclosed in a distinctive, elongated fruit known as a follicle. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
