Acacia bilaura

Acacia bilaura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. Its yellow flowers are clustered in spherical heads, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Acacia bilaura is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
