Aethionema grandiflorum var. sintenisii

Aethionema grandiflorum var. sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of fleshy, lobed leaves that help it conserve water.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in drought-prone landscapes.
