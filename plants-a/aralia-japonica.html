Aralia japonica

Aralia japonica, commonly known as Japanese angelica tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive feather-like appearance, typically reaching lengths of up to 30 cm. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
