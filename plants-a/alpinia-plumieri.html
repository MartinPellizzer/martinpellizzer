Alpinia plumieri

Alpinia plumieri, commonly known as the red ginger or flame ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish underside. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its foliage. Alpinia plumieri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical settings.
