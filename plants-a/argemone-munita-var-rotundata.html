Argemone munita var. rotundata

Argemone munita var. rotundata, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers with prominent red veins. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
