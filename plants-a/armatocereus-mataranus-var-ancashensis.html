Armatocereus mataranus var. ancashensis

Armatocereus mataranus var. ancashensis, commonly known as the Cashan night-blooming cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
