Aster × angustus

Aster × angustus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it blooms from late summer into early autumn.

Aster × angustus is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
