Agave geminiflora var. sonorae

Agave geminiflora var. sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran agave, is a succulent plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a rosette of thick, blue-gray leaves that are often lined with white teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence bearing numerous yellowish-green flowers. It is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a drought-tolerant species in desert ecosystems.
