Aceras angustifolium

Aceras angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
