Adhatoda robusta

Adhatoda robusta, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant contains various alkaloids, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
