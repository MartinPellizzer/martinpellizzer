Alpinia purpurata var. grandis

Alpinia purpurata var. grandis, commonly known as the red ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
