Allocassine laurifolia

Allocassine laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Allocassine laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
