Aopla reniformis

Aopla reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Aopla reniformis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
