Allium cupani

Allium cupani, commonly known as the cupani garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered bulbs and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium cupani is relatively easy to cultivate and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
