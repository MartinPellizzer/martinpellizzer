Arachis hypogaea var. aequatoriana

Arachis hypogaea var. aequatoriana, commonly known as the Andean groundnut, is a variant of the peanut plant native to South America.

This species is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with three leaflets. The flowers are typically yellow and are followed by pods that develop underground, a unique trait of the Arachis genus. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

The seeds, or peanuts, are rich in protein and oil, making them an important crop in certain regions.
