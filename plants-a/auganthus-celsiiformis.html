Auganthus celsiiformis

Auganthus celsiiformis, commonly known as the mountain bells, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Auganthus celsiiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
