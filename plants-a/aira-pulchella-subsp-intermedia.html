Aira pulchella subsp. intermedia

Aira pulchella subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate false oat, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
