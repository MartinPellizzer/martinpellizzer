Acacia plectocarpa subsp. tanumbirinensis

Acacia plectocarpa subsp.

tanumbirinensis, commonly known as the Tanumbirinensis wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that are typically narrow and linear.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are a notable feature during the flowering season. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other acacia species.
