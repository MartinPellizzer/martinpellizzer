Acalypha punctata var. rogersii

Acalypha punctata var. rogersii, commonly known as the red-leaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that contrast with its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for adding vibrant color to landscapes.
