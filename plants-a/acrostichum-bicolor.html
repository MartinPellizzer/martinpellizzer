Acrostichum bicolor

Acrostichum bicolor, commonly known as the golden fern, is a tropical fern native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The fronds are typically divided into broad, lance-shaped pinnae, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

The plant reproduces via spores and is an important component of riparian ecosystems.
