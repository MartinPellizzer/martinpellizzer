Austrostipa blakei

Austrostipa blakei, commonly known as Blake's wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. Austrostipa blakei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sandy habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
