Aerva leubnitziae

Aerva leubnitziae, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and palmate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Aerva leubnitziae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
