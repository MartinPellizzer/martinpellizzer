Andropogon minor

Andropogon minor, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted seed heads that turn copper-red in the fall. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon minor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
