Acacia hermannii

Acacia hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often shed during the dry season, and its flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to arid environments and can thrive in poor soils.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
