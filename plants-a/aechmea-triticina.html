Aechmea triticina

Aechmea triticina, commonly known as the grass-leaf bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms a rosette structure, which helps collect water and nutrients from the air. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears showy, violet-colored flowers, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow and stiff, with a waxy coating that aids in water retention.

Aechmea triticina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the wild.

Morphology

Aechmea triticina has broad, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette, with a bluish-green color and a waxy surface.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, compact growth pattern with a dense, fibrous base. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically pink or red, and bloom during the rainy season, pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that releases small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its natural habitat.
