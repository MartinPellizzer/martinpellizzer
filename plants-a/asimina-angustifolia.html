Asimina angustifolia

Asimina angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed custard apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, custard-like pulp.
