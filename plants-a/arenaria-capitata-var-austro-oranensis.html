Arenaria capitata var. austro-oranensis

Arenaria capitata var. austro-oranensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean sandflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thin, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
