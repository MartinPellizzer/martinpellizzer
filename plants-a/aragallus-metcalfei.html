Aragallus metcalfei

Aragallus metcalfei, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and somewhat succulent.

Aragallus metcalfei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in high-altitude gardens.
