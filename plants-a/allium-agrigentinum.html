Allium agrigentinum

Allium agrigentinum, commonly known as the Sicilian leek, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and chives. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor similar to leeks.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
