Artanthe jalapensis

Artanthe jalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

Artanthe jalapensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
