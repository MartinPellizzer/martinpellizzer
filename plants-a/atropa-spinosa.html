Atropa spinosa

Atropa spinosa, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or white. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its berries are toxic but have been historically used in traditional medicine and witchcraft.

The plant contains tropane alkaloids, which contribute to its potent and dangerous properties.
