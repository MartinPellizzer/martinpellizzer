Abatia microphylla

Abatia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved abatia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas with rich soil. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are small in size. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
