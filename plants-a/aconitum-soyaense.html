Aconitum soyaense

Aconitum soyaense, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has compound, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in soft, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
