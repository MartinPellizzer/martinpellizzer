Astragalus azizii

Astragalus azizii, commonly known as the Aziz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Astragalus azizii is often found in mountainous areas, where it thrives in full sun.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its habitat.
