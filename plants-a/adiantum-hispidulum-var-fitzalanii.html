Adiantum hispidulum var. fitzalanii

Adiantum hispidulum var. fitzalanii, commonly known as the Fitzalan's maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, bright green fronds that are typically arching and gracefully drooping. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil. Its fronds are characterized by their slender, lance-shaped pinnae with a slightly hairy underside.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
