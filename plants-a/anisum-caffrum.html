Anisum caffrum

Anisum caffrum, commonly known as wild anise, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Anisum caffrum is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
