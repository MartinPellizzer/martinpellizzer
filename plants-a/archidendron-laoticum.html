Archidendron laoticum

Archidendron laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian legume, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, which are typically hairy and contain several seeds.

Archidendron laoticum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
