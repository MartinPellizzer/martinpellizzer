Aster obliquus

Aster obliquus, commonly known as the leaning aster, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Aster obliquus is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found in areas with poor soil drainage.
