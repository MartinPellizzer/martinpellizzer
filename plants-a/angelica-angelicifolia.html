Angelica angelicifolia

Angelica angelicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

Angelica angelicifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
