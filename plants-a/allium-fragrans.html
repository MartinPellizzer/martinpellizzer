Allium fragrans

Allium fragrans, commonly known as the sweet garlic or the fragrant garlic, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its bulbous structure, which is composed of layered, fleshy leaves that store nutrients. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose umbel, and emit a mild, sweet scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild culinary uses.
