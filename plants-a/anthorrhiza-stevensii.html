Anthorrhiza stevensii

Anthorrhiza stevensii, commonly known as the Stevens' ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with pale greenish-white flowers and a single, elongated spur. This epiphytic plant grows on tree trunks or branches in the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia. Its leaves are strap-like and often covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hair.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
