Andropogon glomeratus subsp. reinoldii

Andropogon glomeratus subsp.

reinoldii, commonly known as Reinold's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often rolled at the edges.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Reinold's bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
