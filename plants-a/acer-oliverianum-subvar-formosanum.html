Acer oliverianum subvar. formosanum

Acer oliverianum subvar. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
