Alectorolophus ramosus

Alectorolophus ramosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as tropical forests. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
