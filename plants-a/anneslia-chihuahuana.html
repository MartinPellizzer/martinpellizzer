Anneslia chihuahuana

Anneslia chihuahuana, commonly known as the Chihuahua's anemone, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua Desert in Mexico.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Anneslia chihuahuana is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
