Andira paniculata

Andira paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian white wood or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue to black berries. The species also has medicinal uses in traditional South American medicine.
