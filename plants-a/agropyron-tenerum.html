Agropyron tenerum

Agropyron tenerum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seed heads are slender and often bend downward, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Agropyron tenerum is valued for its ornamental use and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping and erosion control.
