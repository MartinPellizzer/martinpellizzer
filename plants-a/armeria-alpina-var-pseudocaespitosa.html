Armeria alpina var. pseudocaespitosa

Armeria alpina var. pseudocaespitosa, commonly known as the alpine thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, compact clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in cooler climates.
