Arum modicense

Arum modicense, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot or simply arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a starchy underground tuber that is widely used as a food source. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
