Astragalus merxmuelleri

Astragalus merxmuelleri, commonly known as Merxmueller's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and often extensive. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus merxmuelleri is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and plays a role in nitrogen fixation in its ecosystem.
