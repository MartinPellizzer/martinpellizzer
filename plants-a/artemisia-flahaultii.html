Artemisia flahaultii

Artemisia flahaultii, commonly known as Flahault's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a distinctive, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in drought-tolerant gardens and natural habitats.
