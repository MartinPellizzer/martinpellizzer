Astragalus vuralii

Astragalus vuralii, commonly known as the Vural milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Astragalus vuralii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
