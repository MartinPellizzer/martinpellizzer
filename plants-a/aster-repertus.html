Aster repertus

Aster repertus, commonly known as the discovered aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy purple to blue flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

Aster repertus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
