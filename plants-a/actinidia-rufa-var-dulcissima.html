Actinidia rufa var. dulcissima

Actinidia rufa var. dulcissima, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that transition from green to red as they mature, often reaching a size comparable to a cherry. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

It is widely cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of nutrition.
