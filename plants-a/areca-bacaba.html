Areca bacaba

Areca bacaba, commonly known as the bacaba palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, green fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, while the fruit is consumed for its sweet, juicy pulp.

The bacaba palm is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
