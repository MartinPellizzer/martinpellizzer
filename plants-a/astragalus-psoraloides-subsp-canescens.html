Astragalus psoraloides subsp. canescens

Astragalus psoraloides subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers in late spring. Its seed pods are slender and hairy, containing lens-shaped seeds.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
