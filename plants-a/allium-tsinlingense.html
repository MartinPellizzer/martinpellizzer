Allium tsinlingense

Allium tsinlingense, commonly known as the Tsingling onion, is a bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces underground bulbs that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
