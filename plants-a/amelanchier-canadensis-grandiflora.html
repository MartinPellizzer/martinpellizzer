Amelanchier canadensis grandiflora

Amelanchier canadensis grandiflora, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces edible berries in the summer, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Amelanchier canadensis grandiflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
