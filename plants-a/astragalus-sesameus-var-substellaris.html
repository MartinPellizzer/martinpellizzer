Astragalus sesameus var. substellaris

Astragalus sesameus var. substellaris, commonly known as the starry milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
