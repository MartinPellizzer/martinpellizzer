Acanthophippium parviflorum

Acanthophippium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered acanthophippium, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces berries that are adapted for animal dispersal, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.
