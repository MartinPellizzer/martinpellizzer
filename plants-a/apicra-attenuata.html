Apicra attenuata

Apicra attenuata, commonly known as the slender apicra, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

Apicra attenuata is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
