Aerva lanata var. robusta

Aerva lanata var. robusta, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
