Alyssum eriophyllum var. umbellatum

Alyssum eriophyllum var. umbellatum, commonly known as the silverleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it often forms mats or cushions on rocky soils. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant in ecological gardens.
