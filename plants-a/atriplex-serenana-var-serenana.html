Atriplex serenana var. serenana

Atriplex serenana var. serenana, commonly known as silverleaf chenopod, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its succulent stems and leaves store water, aiding in its survival in drought conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
