Ammannia capitellata subsp. capitellata

Ammannia capitellata subsp.

capitellata, commonly known as prairie ammannia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
