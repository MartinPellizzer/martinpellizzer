Asparagus mozambicus

Asparagus mozambicus, commonly known as the Mozambique asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems that are often branched and covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Asparagus mozambicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry conditions.
