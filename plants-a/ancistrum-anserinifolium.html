Ancistrum anserinifolium

Ancistrum anserinifolium, commonly known as the white-leafed arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking spadices, which are inflorescences that consist of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in some regions.
