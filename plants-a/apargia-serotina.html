Apargia serotina

Apargia serotina, commonly known as late white bead.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to North America, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in loose racemes, blooming in late summer to early fall.

Apargia serotina is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants with umbel-like inflorescences.
