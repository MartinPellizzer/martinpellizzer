Agave sobria var. roseana

Agave sobria var. roseana, commonly known as the roseate agave, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color with a distinctive pink or rose-colored margin. The plant produces a tall, branching flower stalk that bears yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
