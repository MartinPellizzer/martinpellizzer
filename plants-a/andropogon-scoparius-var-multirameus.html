Andropogon scoparius var. multirameus

Andropogon scoparius var. multirameus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This variant is known for its dense growth habit and ability to form extensive grasslands. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and savanna ecosystems.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
