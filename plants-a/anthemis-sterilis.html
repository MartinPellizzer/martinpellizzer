Anthemis sterilis

Anthemis sterilis, commonly known as common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features aromatic yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by a crown of pale green to white ray florets. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Anthemis sterilis is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
