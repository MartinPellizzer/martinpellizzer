Aconitum hians

Aconitum hians, commonly known as the Alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers, which are arranged in a helmet-like structure. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a waxy coating. Aconitum hians thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, typically growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is also known for its toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
