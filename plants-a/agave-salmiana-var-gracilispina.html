Agave salmiana var. gracilispina

Agave salmiana var. gracilispina, commonly known as the narrow-spined agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a thick, rosette-shaped stem covered in sharp, spiny leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
