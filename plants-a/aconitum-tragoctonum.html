Aconitum tragoctonum

Aconitum tragoctonum, commonly known as the meadow aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply divided lobes, and its roots are tuberous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be lethal to humans and animals.
