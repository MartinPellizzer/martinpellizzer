Arundinaria imadatensis

Arundinaria imadatensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Arundinaria imadatensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.

Its growth habit and appearance make it a popular choice for both landscaping and horticultural purposes.
