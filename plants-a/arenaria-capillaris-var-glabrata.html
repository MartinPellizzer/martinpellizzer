Arenaria capillaris var. glabrata

Arenaria capillaris var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and rocky soils in temperate regions.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, creeping stems covered with fine, soft hairs that are less prominent than in other varieties. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in dense clusters near the ground.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy and resilient species suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
