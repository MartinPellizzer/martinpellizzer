Acicalyptus seemannii

Acicalyptus seemannii, commonly known as the mountain pepper.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white to grayish powdery coating. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark blue to black, fleshy berries.

The species is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
