Aeschynomene schindleri

Aeschynomene schindleri, commonly known as Schindler's Aeschynomene, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
