Axonopus poiretii

Axonopus poiretii, commonly known as velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features fine, soft, and densely tufted foliage with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense growth habit and attractive appearance.
