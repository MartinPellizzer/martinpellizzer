Agoseris heterophylla var. turgida

Agoseris heterophylla var. turgida, commonly known as the swollenleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, ranging from basal rosettes to alternate leaves along the stem. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are typically hairy as well.

This species is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
