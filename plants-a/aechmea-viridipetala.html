Aechmea viridipetala

Aechmea viridipetala, commonly known as the green-petaled bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a type of bromeliad characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding cup. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that can range from pink to red, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically long, narrow, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

Aechmea viridipetala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Morphology

Aechmea viridipetala has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, dark green with a waxy texture, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, greenish-brown bark that grows upward from the base. The plant produces striking inflorescences with purple and white flowers, blooming in late spring to early summer, and is pollinated primarily by birds and insects. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring the plant in its native habitat.
