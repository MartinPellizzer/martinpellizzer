Anthyllis vulnerarioides

Anthyllis vulnerarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Anthyllis vulnerarioides is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.
