Aloe pachygaster

Aloe pachygaster, commonly known as the Cape aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a feature typical of many succulents. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe pachygaster thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
