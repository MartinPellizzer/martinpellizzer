Anisotome aromatica var. lanuginosa

Anisotome aromatica var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly anisotome, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are densely covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its aromatic qualities.

This species is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
