Antimima argentea

Antimima argentea, commonly known as silver antimima, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Antimima argentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
