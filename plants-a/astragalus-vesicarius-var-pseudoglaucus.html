Astragalus vesicarius var. pseudoglaucus

Astragalus vesicarius var. pseudoglaucus, commonly known as the smooth milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert environments. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, arranged in small clusters, and are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

The seeds are enclosed in hairy pods that split open to release the mature seeds.
