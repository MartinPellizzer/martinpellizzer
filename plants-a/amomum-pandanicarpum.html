Amomum pandanicarpum

Amomum pandanicarpum, commonly known as the gingerbread plant, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and tall, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by oval, fleshy fruits that resemble pineapples in appearance. Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.

The plant's growth habit and morphology are adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
