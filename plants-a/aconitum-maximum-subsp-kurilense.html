Aconitum maximum subsp. kurilense

Aconitum maximum subsp.

kurilense, commonly known as the Kuril monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and the islands of the Kuril chain. It features striking blue-violet to purple flowers arranged in a raceme, with hooded petals that resemble a bishop's mitre.

The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Like other members of the genus Aconitum, it contains potent alkaloids and is toxic to humans and animals.
