Agave datylio var. datylio

Agave datylio var. datylio, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
