Amaryllis belladonna blanda

Amaryllis belladonna blanda, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a bulbous stem and has strap-like, evergreen leaves that persist throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which can last several weeks during the growing season.
