Artemisia obtusiloba subsp. martjanovii

Artemisia obtusiloba subsp.

martjanovii, commonly known as the Martjanov's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to grazing.

Its aromatic properties and chemical composition make it of interest in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
