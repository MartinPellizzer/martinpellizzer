Agave antillarum var. grammontensis

Agave antillarum var. grammontensis, commonly known as the Grammont's agave, is a succulent plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which appear in the summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
