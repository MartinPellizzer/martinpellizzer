Abutilon vexillarium

Abutilon vexillarium, commonly known as the flag plant or Mexican bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, ovate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Abutilon vexillarium is also used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
