Anemone altaica var. latiloba

Anemone altaica var. latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant features a basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, white to pale pink flowers bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
