Amaryllis aulica

Amaryllis aulica, commonly known as the mountain amaryllis, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically red or pink, with six petals and prominent stamens, creating a dramatic visual impact. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold floral displays.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are broad and dark green, adding to its ornamental value.
