Aster Chilensis Var. Bernardinus

Aster chilensis var. bernardinus is a unique and rare plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its striking blue or violet flower heads. This variant is distinguished by its compact growth and distinctive coloration, making it a prized species among botanists and gardeners. It thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates, often found in areas with strong winds and salt spray. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for conservation and botanical study.
