Astragalus avromanicus

Astragalus avromanicus, commonly known as the Armenian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution.
