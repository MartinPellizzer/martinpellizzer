Acacia apiculata

Acacia apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

Acacia apiculata is well adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
