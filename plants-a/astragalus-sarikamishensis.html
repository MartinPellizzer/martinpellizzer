Astragalus sarikamishensis

Astragalus sarikamishensis, commonly known as the Sarikamish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
