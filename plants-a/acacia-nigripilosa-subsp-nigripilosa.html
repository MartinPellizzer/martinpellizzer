Acacia nigripilosa subsp. nigripilosa

Acacia nigripilosa subsp.

nigripilosa, commonly known as the black-hair wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and grey-green foliage.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, needle-like leaflets, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

The species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
