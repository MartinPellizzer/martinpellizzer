Allium monadelphum var. fedschenkoanum

Allium monadelphum var. fedschenkoanum, commonly known as the Fedschenkoanum garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulbous stem with a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild garlic-like aroma.
