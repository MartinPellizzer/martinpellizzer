Acacia richii

Acacia richii, commonly known as the Socotra acacia, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like crown and feathery, thornless branches. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in the arid environment.

Acacia richii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a resilient species of the Arabian Peninsula.
