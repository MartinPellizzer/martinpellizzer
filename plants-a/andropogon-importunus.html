Andropogon importunus

Andropogon importunus, commonly known as the common bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy, and its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets. It is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in areas with full sun.

Andropogon importunus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
