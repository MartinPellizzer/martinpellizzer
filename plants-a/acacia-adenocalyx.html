Acacia adenocalyx

Acacia adenocalyx, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature.
