Astragalus epiglottis

Astragalus epiglottis, commonly known as the epiglottic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Astragalus epiglottis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
