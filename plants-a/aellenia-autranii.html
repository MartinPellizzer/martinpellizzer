Aellenia autranii

Aellenia autranii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of elliptic, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a showy inflorescence during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.

Morphology

Aellenia autranii has leaves that are elliptical in shape, alternate in arrangement, and range from 5 to 10 centimeters in length, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is slender, woody, and slightly hairy, with a reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and display a pale yellow color, blooming during the spring and early summer, and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in sandy or rocky soils.
