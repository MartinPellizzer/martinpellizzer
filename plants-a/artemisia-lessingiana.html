Artemisia lessingiana

Artemisia lessingiana, commonly known as Lessing's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and narrow, silvery-blue leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in rounded or oblong shapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Artemisia lessingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
