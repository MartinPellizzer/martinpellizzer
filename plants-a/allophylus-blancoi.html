Allophylus blancoi

Allophylus blancoi, commonly known as the white-flowered alder, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soils, often found in forest understories. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Allophylus blancoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
