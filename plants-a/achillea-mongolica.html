Achillea mongolica

Achillea mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a creeping rhizome and erect, hairy stems that bear narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels, which bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert habitats.

Achillea mongolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
