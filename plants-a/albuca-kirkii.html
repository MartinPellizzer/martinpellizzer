Albuca kirkii

Albuca kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's albuca, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, strap-shaped leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca kirkii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
