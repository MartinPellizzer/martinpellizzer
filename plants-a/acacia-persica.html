Acacia persica

Acacia persica, commonly known as Persian acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in pairs. Its yellow flowers are borne in dense, globular clusters and are highly fragrant.

Acacia persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
