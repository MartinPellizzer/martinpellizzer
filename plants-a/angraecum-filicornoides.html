Angraecum filicornoides

Angraecum filicornoides, commonly known as the feather-stemmed orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often nocturnal, attracting specific pollinators like moths.

Angraecum filicornoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
