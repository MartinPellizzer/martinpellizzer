Aridaria tetramera

Aridaria tetramera, commonly known as the four-leaf desert plant, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, adapting to the low-nutrient soils of its habitat. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well-suited for drought conditions.

Aridaria tetramera plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert creatures.
