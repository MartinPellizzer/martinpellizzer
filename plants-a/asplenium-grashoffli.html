Asplenium grashoffli

Asplenium grashoffli, commonly known as the Grashoff's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, with a delicate, fern-like structure. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its rhizomes help it anchor to surfaces such as tree bark or rocks.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
