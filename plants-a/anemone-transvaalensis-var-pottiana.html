Anemone transvaalensis var. pottiana

Anemone transvaalensis var. pottiana, commonly known as the Transvaal anemone, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
