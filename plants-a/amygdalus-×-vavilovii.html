Amygdalus × vavilovii

Amygdalus × vavilovii, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus sibirica.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East and parts of eastern Siberia. The plant is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common apricots. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in breeding programs to improve the hardiness of apricot cultivars.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cold climates, making it an important plant in temperate regions.
