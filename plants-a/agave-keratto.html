Agave keratto

Agave keratto, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence topped with yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lined with sharp spines, making it a striking and somewhat dangerous plant.

Agave keratto is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal water once established.
