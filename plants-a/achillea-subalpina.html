Achillea subalpina

Achillea subalpina, commonly known as alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with finely divided, gray-green leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Achillea subalpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
