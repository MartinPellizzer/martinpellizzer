Australina capensis

Australina capensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with simple, ovate leaves and small, white flowers.

Its fruit is edible, often used in jams and desserts, and is rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
