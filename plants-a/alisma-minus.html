Alisma minus

Alisma minus, commonly known as lesser water plantain, is a wetland plant native to Asia, North America, and Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its small white or greenish flowers appear in clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Alisma minus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
