Allium bertolonii

Allium bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Allium bertolonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
