Amygdalus × saviczii

Amygdalus × saviczii, commonly known as the wild almond or sweet almond hybrid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus dulcis (sweet almond) and Prunus armeniaca (apricot).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible drupes with a sweet, nutty flavor, similar to its parent species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential in horticultural breeding programs.
