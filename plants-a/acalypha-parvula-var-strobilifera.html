Acalypha parvula var. strobilifera

Acalypha parvula var. strobilifera, commonly known as the coral bells, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, colorful bracts that resemble small coral-like structures, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, cone-shaped inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in wet soils.

Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, red, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
