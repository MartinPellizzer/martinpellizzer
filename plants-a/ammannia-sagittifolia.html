Ammannia sagittifolia

Ammannia sagittifolia, commonly known as arrowleaf ammannia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically alternate and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ammannia sagittifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
