Arabis kazbegi

Arabis kazbegi, commonly known as the Caucasian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus Mountains.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.
