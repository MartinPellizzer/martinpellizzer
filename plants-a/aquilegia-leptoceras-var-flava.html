Aquilegia leptoceras var. flava

Aquilegia leptoceras var. flava, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a variant of the aquilegia genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This perennial plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five petals that curve backward. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
