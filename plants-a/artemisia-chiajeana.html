Artemisia chiajeana

Artemisia chiajeana, commonly known as the Chiajean wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its essential oils have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
