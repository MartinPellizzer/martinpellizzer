Anthemis sphacelata

Anthemis sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and attract various pollinators.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some regions.
