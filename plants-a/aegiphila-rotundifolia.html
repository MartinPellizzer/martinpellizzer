Aegiphila rotundifolia

Aegiphila rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed aegiphila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tubular, pink or purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.

Morphology

Aegiphila rotundifolia has round or oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a glossy dark green color and a moderate size.

The stem is slender, with a smooth, slightly woody texture and a reddish-brown bark that becomes more prominent with age. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, with white or pale pink petals, blooming in the spring and summer, and are pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to the plant's growth in dry, rocky environments.
