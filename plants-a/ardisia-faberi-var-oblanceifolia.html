Ardisia faberi var. oblanceifolia

Ardisia faberi var. oblanceifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ardisia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical to oblanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in tropical and subtropical regions.
