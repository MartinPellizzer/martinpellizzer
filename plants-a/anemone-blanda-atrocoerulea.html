Anemone blanda atrocoerulea

Anemone blanda atrocoerulea, commonly known as the dark blue anemone, is a delicate perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, nodding flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet, with the species name "atrocoerulea" reflecting its dark blue hue. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and grayish in appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a subtle yet striking floral display to the landscape.
