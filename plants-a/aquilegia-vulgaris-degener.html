Aquilegia vulgaris degener

Aquilegia vulgaris degener, commonly known as the common columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, blue, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower habitats.
