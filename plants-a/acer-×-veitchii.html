Acer × veitchii

Acer × veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Sapindaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters and has a rounded crown with stout, upright branches. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This ornamental species is valued for its attractive foliage and is often planted in gardens and parks for its seasonal beauty.
