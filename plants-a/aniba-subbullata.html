Aniba subbullata

Aniba subbullata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian sandarac, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible for certain wildlife.

Due to its unique fragrance and historical use in traditional medicine, it is also valued for its wood in crafting musical instruments.
