Acanthocalycium peitscherianum

Acanthocalycium peitscherianum, commonly known as the Peitscher's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
