Apostates

Apostates, commonly known as *Apostasia*, are a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. These plants typically have rhizomatous growth, with tuberous roots that store nutrients. Their flowers are often showy, with intricate structures that facilitate pollination by insects.

Apostates are prized in horticulture for their ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
