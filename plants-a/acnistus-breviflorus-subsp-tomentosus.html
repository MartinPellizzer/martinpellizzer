Acnistus breviflorus subsp. tomentosus

Acnistus breviflorus subsp.

tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly acnistus, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
