Amphicarpum purshii

Amphicarpum purshii, commonly known as Pursh's false brome, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant is known for its ability to produce two types of fruits: one above ground and one below ground, a trait known as amphicarpy. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it conserve water.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer plant in ecological succession.
