Athanasia crithmifolia

Athanasia crithmifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in coastal environments, often growing on rocky shores or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Athanasia crithmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
