Alchemilla kerneriana

Alchemilla kerneriana, commonly known as the Kerner's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-veined leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Alchemilla kerneriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
