Anatherum abortivum

Anatherum abortivum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with reduced or absent flowers, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Anatherum. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it can become a weed in cultivated areas, though it plays a role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
