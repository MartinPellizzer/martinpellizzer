Areca flavescens

Areca flavescens, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits containing a hard, starchy seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional practices, while the seeds are cultivated for their nut-like fruit.

Areca flavescens thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
