Aristida oligantha var. nervata

Aristida oligantha var. nervata, commonly known as the narrow-veined threeawn, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and sandy soils, preferring well-drained conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of bracteate, spikelets that are tightly pressed against the stem.

The species is characterized by its fine, thread-like lemma hairs, which are more pronounced in this variant compared to the typical form.
