Astragalus iranshahrii

Astragalus iranshahrii, commonly known as Iranian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Iran and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus iranshahrii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
