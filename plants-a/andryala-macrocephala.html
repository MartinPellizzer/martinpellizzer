Andryala macrocephala

Andryala macrocephala, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Andryala macrocephala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
