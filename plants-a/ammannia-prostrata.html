Ammannia prostrata

Ammannia prostrata, commonly known as redroot ammannia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats along the ground. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to moist, sandy soils.
