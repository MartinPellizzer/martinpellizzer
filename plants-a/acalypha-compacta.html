Acalypha compacta

Acalypha compacta, commonly known as the compact flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and striking red or orange bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which are often surrounded by the showy bracts. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Acalypha compacta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
