Arundinaria schomburgkii

Arundinaria schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk bamboo, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth in texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
