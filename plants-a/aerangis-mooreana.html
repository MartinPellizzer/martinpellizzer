Aerangis mooreana

Aerangis mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's Aerangis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Aerangis mooreana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
