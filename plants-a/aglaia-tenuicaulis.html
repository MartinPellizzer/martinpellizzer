Aglaia tenuicaulis

Aglaia tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Aglaia tenuicaulis is often valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
