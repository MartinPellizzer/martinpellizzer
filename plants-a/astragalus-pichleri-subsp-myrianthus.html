Astragalus pichleri subsp. myrianthus

Astragalus pichleri subsp.

myrianthus, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, typical of the legume family Fabaceae. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
