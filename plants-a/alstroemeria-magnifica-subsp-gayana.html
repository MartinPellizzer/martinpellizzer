Alstroemeria magnifica subsp. gayana

Alstroemeria magnifica subsp.

gayana, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both floral arrangements and landscape design.
