Anthodendron ponticum

Anthodendron ponticum, commonly known as the Turkish bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
