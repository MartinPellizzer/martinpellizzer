Asarum fauriei var. fauriei

Asarum fauriei var. fauriei, commonly known as Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-brown hairy leaves that are typically found growing close to the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
