Astragalus burkeanus

Astragalus burkeanus, commonly known as Burke's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus burkeanus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
