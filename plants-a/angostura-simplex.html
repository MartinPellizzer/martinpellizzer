Angostura simplex

Angostura simplex, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
