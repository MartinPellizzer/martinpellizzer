Anemone multisepala

Anemone multisepala, commonly known as the many-sepal anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with numerous sepals that resemble petals, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Anemone multisepala is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and wooded areas, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
