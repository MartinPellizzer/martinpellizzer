Adenocarpus complicatus nothosubsp. federici

Adenocarpus complicatus nothosubsp.

federici, commonly known as the Federici's complex thornveld, is a subspecies native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a hairy, three-lobed capsule that dehisces to release seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands.
