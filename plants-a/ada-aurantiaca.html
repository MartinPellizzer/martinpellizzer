Ada aurantiaca

Ada aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces long, slender leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and as a climbing plant.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
