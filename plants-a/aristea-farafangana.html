Aristea farafangana

Aristea farafangana, commonly known as the Madagascar starwort, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a star-like arrangement.

Aristea farafangana is often found in coastal areas and is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
