Acantholimon avenaceum

Acantholimon avenaceum, commonly known as the dwarf flax or sand flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its drought tolerance.

Acantholimon avenaceum plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinators.
