Acalypha amboynensis

Acalypha amboynensis, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Acalypha amboynensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in local medicine.
