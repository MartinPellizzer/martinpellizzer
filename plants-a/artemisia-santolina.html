Artemisia santolina

Artemisia santolina, commonly known as Spanish sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of sage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in xeriscaping and herbal gardens.
