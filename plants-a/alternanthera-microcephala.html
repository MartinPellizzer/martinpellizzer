Alternanthera microcephala

Alternanthera microcephala, commonly known as small-headed alternanthera, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often red or pink, foliage that changes with light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in water gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
