Arachnorchis citrina

Arachnorchis citrina, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands. Its flowers are borne on erect, hairy stems and are known for their intricate, spider-like appearance.

Arachnorchis citrina is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
