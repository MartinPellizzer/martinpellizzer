Athamanta capillacea

Athamanta capillacea, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, hair-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Athamanta capillacea is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to thrive in humid, temperate climates.

Its botanical structure reflects its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
