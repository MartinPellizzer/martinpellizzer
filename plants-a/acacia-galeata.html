Acacia galeata

Acacia galeata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Acacia galeata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
