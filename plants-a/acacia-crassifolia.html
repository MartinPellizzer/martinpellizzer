Acacia crassifolia

Acacia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are often visited by insects and birds. Acacia crassifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance through its thick, waxy leaves.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
