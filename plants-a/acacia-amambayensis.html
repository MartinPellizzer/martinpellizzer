Acacia amambayensis

Acacia amambayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.

Additionally, its wood and bark have been used traditionally by indigenous communities for medicinal and practical purposes.
