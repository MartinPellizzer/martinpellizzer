Arabis hirsuta var. rigidula

Arabis hirsuta var. rigidula, commonly known as the rigid hairy rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It features basal leaves that are hairy and linear, while the upper leaves are more lanceolate.

Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring.
