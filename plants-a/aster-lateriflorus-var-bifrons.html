Aster lateriflorus var. bifrons

Aster lateriflorus var. bifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
