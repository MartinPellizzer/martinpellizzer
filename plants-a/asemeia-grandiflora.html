Asemeia grandiflora

Asemeia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered asemeia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a whorled pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Asemeia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
