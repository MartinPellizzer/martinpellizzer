Agathis moorei

Agathis moorei, commonly known as the Moore's agathis, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Araucariaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of New Guinea and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces large, conical cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing natural protection against pests and environmental stress.

Agathis moorei plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat, supporting a diverse array of flora and fauna.
