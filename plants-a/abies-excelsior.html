Abies excelsior

Abies excelsior, commonly known as the noble fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are coated with a waxy layer. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

Abies excelsior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
