Aristolochia cremersii

Aristolochia cremersii, commonly known as the Cremers' Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
