Andropogon iwarancusa

Andropogon iwarancusa, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a robust rhizome system that helps it thrive in wet environments.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
