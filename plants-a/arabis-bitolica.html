Arabis bitolica

Arabis bitolica, commonly known as the two-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are usually basal, with a rosette formation, and the plant is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
