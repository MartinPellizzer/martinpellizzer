Aegilops crassa var. macranthera

Aegilops crassa var. macranthera, commonly known as the broad-leafed goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically borne in clusters, with lemmas that are often rough and hairy. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a flowering structure that is typical of the Poaceae family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
