Arisaema angustifoliolatum var. integrifolium

Arisaema angustifoliolatum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive arrow-shaped leaf that is broad and undivided, giving it its common name. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
