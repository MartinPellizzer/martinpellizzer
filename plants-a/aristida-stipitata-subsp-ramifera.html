Aristida stipitata subsp. ramifera

Aristida stipitata subsp.

ramifera, commonly known as the ramifera false beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often tinged with red or brown, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
