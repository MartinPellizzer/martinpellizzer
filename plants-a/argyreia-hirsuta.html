Argyreia hirsuta

Argyreia hirsuta, commonly known as the velvet flower or hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Argyreia hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
