Amellus alternifolius subsp. alternifolius

Amellus alternifolius subsp.

alternifolius, commonly known as the alternate-leaved amellus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in terminal racemes during the summer months. Its leaves are often glandular and emit a strong, aromatic scent.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
