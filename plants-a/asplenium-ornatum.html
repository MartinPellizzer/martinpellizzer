Asplenium ornatum

Asplenium ornatum, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and intricate frond structure. The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, shield-like appearance, with a dense covering of tiny, scale-like structures. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
