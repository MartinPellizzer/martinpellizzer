Acacia leptospermoides subsp. leptospermoides

Acacia leptospermoides subsp.

leptospermoides, commonly known as the slender-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, forming part of the local flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
