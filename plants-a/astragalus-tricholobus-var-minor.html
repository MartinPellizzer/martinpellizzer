Astragalus tricholobus var. minor

Astragalus tricholobus var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Astragalus tricholobus var. minor is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
