Arytera brackenridgei

Arytera brackenridgei, commonly known as the Brackenridge's lily, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
