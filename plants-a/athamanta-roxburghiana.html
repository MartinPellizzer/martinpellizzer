Athamanta roxburghiana

Athamanta roxburghiana, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Athamanta roxburghiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
