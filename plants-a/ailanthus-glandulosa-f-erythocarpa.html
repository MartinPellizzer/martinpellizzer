Ailanthus glandulosa f. erythocarpa

Ailanthus glandulosa f. erythocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tree of heaven, is a variant of the tree of heaven species native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly adaptable to different climates.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a problematic species in non-native regions.
