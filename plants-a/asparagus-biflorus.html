Asparagus biflorus

Asparagus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered asparagus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Asparagus biflorus is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and resilience in various soil conditions.
