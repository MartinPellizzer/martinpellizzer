Asparagus orientalis

Asparagus orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese asparagus fern, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It features feathery, arching leaves that form a dense, lush foliage, giving it a fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.

Asparagus orientalis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
