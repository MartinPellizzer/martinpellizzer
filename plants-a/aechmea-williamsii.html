Aechmea williamsii

Aechmea williamsii, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with silver or white markings, and it produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea williamsii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
