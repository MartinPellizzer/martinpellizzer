Aechmea williamsii

Aechmea williamsii, commonly known as the pineapple bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater and nutrients. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with silver or white markings, and it produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aechmea williamsii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea williamsii has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a silver-white underside and can grow up to 60 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem that is covered in a fibrous, pale green to brown bark, and it grows upright with a compact form. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful bracts, with flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late winter to early spring and pollinated by insects.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
