Alocasia insignis

Alocasia insignis, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 feet in length, often with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Alocasia insignis is popular as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
