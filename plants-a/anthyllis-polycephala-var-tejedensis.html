Anthyllis polycephala var. tejedensis

Anthyllis polycephala var. tejedensis, commonly known as the Tejedan sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clustered, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
