Alchemilla argyrophylloides

Alchemilla argyrophylloides, commonly known as silver-leaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered with fine, soft hairs that give them a metallic sheen.

Alchemilla argyrophylloides is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
