Anemirhiza

Anemirhiza, commonly known as the windroot, is a rare and elusive plant species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forest understories. Its root system is unique, forming a network of tubers that store nutrients and aid in its survival during dry periods.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Anemirhiza is considered a conservation concern.
