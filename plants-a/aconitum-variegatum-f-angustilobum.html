Aconitum variegatum f. angustilobum

Aconitum variegatum f. angustilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed variegated monkshood, is a hybrid form of the variegated monkshood.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and kept away from livestock and children.
