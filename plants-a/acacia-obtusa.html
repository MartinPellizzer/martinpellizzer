Acacia obtusa

Acacia obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract a variety of pollinators. Acacia obtusa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
