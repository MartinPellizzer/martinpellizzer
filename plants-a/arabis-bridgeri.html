Arabis bridgeri

Arabis bridgeri, commonly known as Bridger's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Arabis bridgeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
