Agropyron magellanicum condensatum

Agropyron magellanicum condensatum, commonly known as the Patagonian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous short, awned spikelets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for grazing animals.
