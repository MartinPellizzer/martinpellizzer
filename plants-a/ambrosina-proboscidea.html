Ambrosina proboscidea

Ambrosina proboscidea, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical plant characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to regions of Central and South America. The plant features elongated, waxy leaves and produces clusters of small, pendulous blooms in various colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Its unique structure allows it to thrive as an epiphyte, often growing on trees in humid environments.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
