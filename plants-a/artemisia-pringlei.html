Artemisia pringlei

Artemisia pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, narrow, and often fuzzy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Artemisia pringlei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
