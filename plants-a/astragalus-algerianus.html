Astragalus algerianus

Astragalus algerianus, commonly known as Algerian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
