Anathallis caudatipetala

Anathallis caudatipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled anathallis, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Anathallis caudatipetala plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing nectar to pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
