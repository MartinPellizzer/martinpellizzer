Alchemilla propinqua f. pastoraloides

Alchemilla propinqua f. pastoraloides, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in loose, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
