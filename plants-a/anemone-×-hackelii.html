Anemone × hackelii

Anemone × hackelii, commonly known as the hybrid anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting central disc. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
