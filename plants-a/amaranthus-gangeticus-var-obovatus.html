Amaranthus gangeticus var. obovatus

Amaranthus gangeticus var. obovatus, commonly known as the Indian millet or red amaranth, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, clustered flowers are typically green or reddish, and it produces edible seeds that are rich in nutrients.

This species is valued for its resilience to poor soil conditions and its use in both culinary and ornamental applications.
