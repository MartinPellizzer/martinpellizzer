Adonis pyrenaica proles alpina

Adonis pyrenaica proles alpina, commonly known as the Alpine Adonis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces striking yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
