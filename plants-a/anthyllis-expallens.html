Anthyllis expallens

Anthyllis expallens, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, hairy leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis expallens is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or arid regions.
