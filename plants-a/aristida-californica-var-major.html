Aristida californica var. major

Aristida californica var. major, commonly known as the California dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and disturbed areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
