Abrodictyum cylindratum

Abrodictyum cylindratum, commonly known as the cylindrical orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique cylindrical shape and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
