Aristida delicatula

Aristida delicatula, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the United States, particularly in the southwestern regions.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are typically arranged in panicle-like clusters, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed areas.

Aristida delicatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
