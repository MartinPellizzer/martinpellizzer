Alsine anatolica subsp. macedonica

Alsine anatolica subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian alsike, is a flowering plant species native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and regional distribution.
