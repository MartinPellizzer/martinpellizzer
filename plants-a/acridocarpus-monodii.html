Acridocarpus monodii

Acridocarpus monodii, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Its essential oil, derived from the heartwood, is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in perfumery, traditional medicine, and incense.
