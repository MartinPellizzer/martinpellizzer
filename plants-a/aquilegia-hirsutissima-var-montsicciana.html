Aquilegia hirsutissima var. montsicciana

Aquilegia hirsutissima var. montsicciana, commonly known as the Alpine Columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals arranged in a distinctive spurred pattern. The plant has compound leaves with lobed segments, and its stems are often covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
