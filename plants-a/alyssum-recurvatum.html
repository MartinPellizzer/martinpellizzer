Alyssum recurvatum

Alyssum recurvatum, commonly known as the curled alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue.

Alyssum recurvatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
