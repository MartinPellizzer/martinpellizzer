Anotis wightiana var. compressa

Anotis wightiana var. compressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallaby bush, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
