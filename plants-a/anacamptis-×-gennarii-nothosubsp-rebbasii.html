Anacamptis × gennarii nothosubsp. rebbasii

Anacamptis × gennarii nothosubsp.

rebbasii, commonly known as the Rebbas' spider orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, spider-like flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pale yellow to greenish-white petals with a distinctive, narrow shape. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
