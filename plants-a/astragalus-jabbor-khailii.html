Astragalus jabbor-khailii

Astragalus jabbor-khailii, commonly known as the Karakoram milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from underground sources.

Astragalus jabbor-khailii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
