Adenia lanceolata subsp. scheffleri

Adenia lanceolata subsp.

scheffleri, commonly known as the serrated adenia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges.

The plant produces striking, pendulous yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and floral display.
