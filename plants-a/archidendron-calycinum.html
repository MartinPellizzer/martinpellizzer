Archidendron calycinum

Archidendron calycinum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its seeds in traditional medicine.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.
