Annona axillaris

Annona axillaris, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, clustered in inflorescences, and are followed by pear-shaped fruits.

The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, custard-like pulp that is edible.
