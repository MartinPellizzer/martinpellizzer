Aspidium extensum

Aspidium extensum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Aspidium extensum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and greenhouses.
