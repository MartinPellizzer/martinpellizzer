Anthericum longituberosum

Anthericum longituberosum, commonly known as the long-tubed arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

Anthericum longituberosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
