Aphelandra chamissoniana

Aphelandra chamissoniana, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated, orange-yellow bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, tubular, white to pink flowers that are typically hidden beneath the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Aphelandra chamissoniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
