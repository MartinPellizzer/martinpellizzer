Angelica pubescens f. muratae

Angelica pubescens f. muratae, commonly known as the Japanese angelica or mountain angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
