Achillea pallida

Achillea pallida, commonly known as white yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial. Its pale yellow to white flowers bloom in summer, and the plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Achillea pallida is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
