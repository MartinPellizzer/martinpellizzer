Acacia prosopoides

Acacia prosopoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that serve as leaves, reducing water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

Acacia prosopoides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
