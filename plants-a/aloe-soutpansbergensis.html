Aloe soutpansbergensis

Aloe soutpansbergensis, commonly known as the Soutpansberg aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
