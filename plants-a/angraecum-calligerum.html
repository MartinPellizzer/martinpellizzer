Angraecum calligerum

Angraecum calligerum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stem and large, showy white flowers with a distinctive central red stripe. The plant produces intricate, fragrant blooms that are primarily pollinated by the hawkmoth, which has a long proboscis adapted for accessing nectar. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Angraecum calligerum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
