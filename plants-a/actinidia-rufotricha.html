Actinidia rufotricha

Actinidia rufotricha, commonly known as the red-haired actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible berries, which are usually yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
