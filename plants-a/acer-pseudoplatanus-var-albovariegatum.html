Acer pseudoplatanus var. albovariegatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. albovariegatum, commonly known as the white-variegated maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking white and green variegated leaves, which provide a distinctive visual appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded crown. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and they turn yellow in the autumn, adding seasonal interest.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
