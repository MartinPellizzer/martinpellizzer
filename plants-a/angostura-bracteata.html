Angostura bracteata

Angostura bracteata, commonly known as the red bract orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red bracts that enclose the actual flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and riverbanks. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

Angostura bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
