Ardisia montana var. selangorensis

Ardisia montana var. selangorensis, commonly known as the Selangorensis Pomegranate, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries resembling pomegranates. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The berries are edible and have a tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in both ornamental and culinary contexts.
