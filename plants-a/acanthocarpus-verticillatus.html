Acanthocarpus verticillatus

Acanthocarpus verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled burr grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, spiny fruits that form in whorls around the stem. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
