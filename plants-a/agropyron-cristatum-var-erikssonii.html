Agropyron cristatum var. erikssonii

Agropyron cristatum var. erikssonii, commonly known as the Eriksson's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with bristle-like structures. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
