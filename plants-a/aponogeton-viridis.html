Aponogeton viridis

Aponogeton viridis, commonly known as the green water clover, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its submerged leaves that are often used in aquariums due to their aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate water. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface. It thrives in warm, freshwater environments with moderate light exposure.

Its unique leaf structure allows it to float and anchor itself in aquatic habitats.
