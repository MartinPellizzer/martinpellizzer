Aster diplostephioides var. falconeri

Aster diplostephioides var. falconeri, commonly known as the Falconer's aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
