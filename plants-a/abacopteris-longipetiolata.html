Abacopteris longipetiolata

Abacopteris longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that arise from a creeping rhizome. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, lanceolate pinnae that give it a finely divided appearance.

The spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in humid, tropical climates.
