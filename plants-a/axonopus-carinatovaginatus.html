Axonopus carinatovaginatus

Axonopus carinatovaginatus, commonly known as the hairy leaved axonopus, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves with prominent veins and a hairy texture on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small flowering structures.

Axonopus carinatovaginatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
