Anemone vernalis

Anemone vernalis, commonly known as the spring anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces delicate, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive central cluster of stamens, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Anemone vernalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early-season blooms.
