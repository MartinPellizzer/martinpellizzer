Aloe bargalensis

Aloe bargalensis, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Aloe bargalensis is well adapted to hot, sunny environments and requires minimal water once established.
