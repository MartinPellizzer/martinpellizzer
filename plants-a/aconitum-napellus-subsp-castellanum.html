Aconitum napellus subsp. castellanum

Aconitum napellus subsp.

castellanum, commonly known as the Castellano monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees and southern France. It features striking blue-violet flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape, characteristic of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has compound, pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of potent alkaloids such as aconitine.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
