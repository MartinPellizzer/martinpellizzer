Acanthoxanthium spinosum

Acanthoxanthium spinosum, commonly known as the spiny goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant features spiny, hairy stems and leaves with a rough, textured surface, which aid in reducing water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Acanthoxanthium spinosum is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture availability.
