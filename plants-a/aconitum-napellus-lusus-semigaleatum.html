Aconitum napellus lusus semigaleatum

Aconitum napellus lusus semigaleatum, commonly known as the semi-galeate monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, hood-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features large, pleated leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive greenish-white underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
