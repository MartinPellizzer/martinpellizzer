Altingia chinensis

Altingia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese alder, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Altingia chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
