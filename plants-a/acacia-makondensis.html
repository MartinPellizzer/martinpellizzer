Acacia makondensis

Acacia makondensis, commonly known as the Makondensis Acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its bark and wood are often used in traditional medicine and for crafting tools.

The species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
