Acacia sphaerostachya var. angustior

Acacia sphaerostachya var. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, often spiny stems and compound leaves with small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are characteristic of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
