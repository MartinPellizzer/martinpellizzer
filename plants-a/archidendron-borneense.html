Archidendron borneense

Archidendron borneense, commonly known as the Bornean legume or Bornean acacia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or white flowers arranged in racemes. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.

The plant is valued for its timber and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
