Arctium bucharicum

Arctium bucharicum, commonly known as the Bukharan burdock, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the daisy family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Arctium bucharicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
