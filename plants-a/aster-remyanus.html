Aster remyanus

Aster remyanus, commonly known as the silverleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-gray underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Aster remyanus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
