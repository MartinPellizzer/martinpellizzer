Aspidophyllum clypeatum

Aspidophyllum clypeatum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped fronds, which are typically broad and leathery in texture. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, helping it store water and nutrients.

The plant is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in greenhouses and shaded gardens.
