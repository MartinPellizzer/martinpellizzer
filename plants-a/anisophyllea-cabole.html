Anisophyllea cabole

Anisophyllea cabole, commonly known as the broadleaf anise tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that can vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
