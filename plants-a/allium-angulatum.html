Allium angulatum

Allium angulatum, commonly known as the Persian shallot or angular leek, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, tubular bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and edible, adding value to both gardens and kitchens.
