Ardisia forbesii

Ardisia forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' evergreen shrub, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries that are visually striking. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in the understory of dense vegetation.

Ardisia forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
