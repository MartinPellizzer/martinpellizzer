Astragalus bozghoushensis

Astragalus bozghoushensis, commonly known as the Bozghoush milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
