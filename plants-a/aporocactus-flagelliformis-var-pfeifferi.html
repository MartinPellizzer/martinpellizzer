Aporocactus flagelliformis var. pfeifferi

Aporocactus flagelliformis var. pfeifferi, commonly known as the Pfeiffer's candelabra cactus, is a unique species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems that resemble a candelabra, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements in suitable climates.
