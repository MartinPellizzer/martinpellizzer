Alchemilla alpestris f. latiloba

Alchemilla alpestris f. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved alpine lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
