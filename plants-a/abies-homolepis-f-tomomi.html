Abies homolepis f. tomomi

Abies homolepis f. tomomi, commonly known as the Tomomi fir, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the fir tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly curved. The cones are typically small and reddish-brown when mature, and they release seeds that are dispersed by wind. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
