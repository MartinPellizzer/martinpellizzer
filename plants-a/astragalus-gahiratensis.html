Astragalus gahiratensis

Astragalus gahiratensis, commonly known as the Gahiratensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus gahiratensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
