Anacamptis × gennarii nothosubsp.

orientecaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian marsh orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
