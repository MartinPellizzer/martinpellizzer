Anethum graveolens var. tenerifrons

Anethum graveolens var. tenerifrons, commonly known as the Canary Island dill, is a variant of the dill plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable addition to both gardens and kitchens.
