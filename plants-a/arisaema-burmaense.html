Arisaema burmaense

Arisaema burmaense, commonly known as the Burmese arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a prominent vein pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

Arisaema burmaense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
