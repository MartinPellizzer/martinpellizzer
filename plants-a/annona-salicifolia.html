Annona salicifolia

Annona salicifolia, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a spiky exterior and a soft, creamy white pulp that is highly aromatic. The fruit is rich in nutrients and contains several bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical horticulture and traditional medicine.
