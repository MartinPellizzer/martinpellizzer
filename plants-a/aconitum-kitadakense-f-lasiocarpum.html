Aconitum kitadakense f. lasiocarpum

Aconitum kitadakense f. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood.

This plant is a variant of the Aconitum genus, characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers and striking blue or purple hues. It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream sides. The leaves are deeply lobed, and the plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators.

Botanically, it is notable for its toxic alkaloids, which are present throughout the plant and require careful handling.
