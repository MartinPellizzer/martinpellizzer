Aletes anisatus

Aletes anisatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming a compact rosette structure. Its flowers are usually borne on long, slender stems and come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white.

Aletes anisatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
