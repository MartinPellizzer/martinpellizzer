Alyssum strigosum

Alyssum strigosum, commonly known as rough alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum strigosum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
