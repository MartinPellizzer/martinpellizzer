Artemisia absinthium f. argentea

Artemisia absinthium f. argentea, commonly known as silver wormwood, is a variant of the common wormwood plant.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, densely hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, linear, and have a strong, aromatic scent. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
