Ardisia argenticaulis

Ardisia argenticaulis, commonly known as silverleaf ardisia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are densely covered with silvery-white hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
