Astragalus pannosus var. flavescens

Astragalus pannosus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing food for native wildlife.
