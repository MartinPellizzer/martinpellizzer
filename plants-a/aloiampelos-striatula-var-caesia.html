Aloiampelos striatula var. caesia

Aloiampelos striatula var. caesia, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue-gray foliage with distinctive white to pale blue markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
