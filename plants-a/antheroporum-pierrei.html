Antheroporum pierrei

Antheroporum pierrei, commonly known as the Corsican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Antheroporum pierrei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
