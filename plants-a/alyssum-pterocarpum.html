Alyssum pterocarpum

Alyssum pterocarpum, commonly known as the rock alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming throughout the summer months. The plant has compact, silver-gray foliage that is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Alyssum pterocarpum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
