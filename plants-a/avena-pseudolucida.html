Avena pseudolucida

Avena pseudolucida, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, two-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Avena pseudolucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
