Atropis distans var. festuciformis

Atropis distans var. festuciformis, commonly known as the slender grass veldt, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, grassy habitats such as savannas and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with rhizomes that help it spread and establish in suitable environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
