Astragalus dasycephalus

Astragalus dasycephalus, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters at the top of the stems. Its root system is extensive and adapted to survive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Astragalus dasycephalus is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
