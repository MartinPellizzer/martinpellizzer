Allium haematochiton

Allium haematochiton, commonly known as the blood-red onion, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its striking purple to red flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

The bulbs of Allium haematochiton are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a cultivated plant in some regions for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
