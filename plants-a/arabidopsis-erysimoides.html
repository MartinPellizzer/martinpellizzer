Arabidopsis erysimoides

Arabidopsis erysimoides, commonly known as the rock cress, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small white or yellow flowers and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Due to its genetic simplicity and short life cycle, it is sometimes used in botanical research.
