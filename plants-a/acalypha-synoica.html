Acalypha synoica

Acalypha synoica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican broom.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

Acalypha synoica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
