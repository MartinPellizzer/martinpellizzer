Ammannia koehnei

Ammannia koehnei, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that often turn reddish in color, particularly at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Ammannia koehnei is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
