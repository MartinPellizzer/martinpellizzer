Alepidea massaica

Alepidea massaica, commonly known as the Massaica lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained environments. Alepidea massaica is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
