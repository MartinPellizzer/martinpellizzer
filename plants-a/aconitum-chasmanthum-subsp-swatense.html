Aconitum chasmanthum subsp. swatense

Aconitum chasmanthum subsp.

swatense, commonly known as the Swat aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, wavy outline. Aconitum chasmanthum subsp.

swatense is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
