Ampelopsis striata

Ampelopsis striata, commonly known as the striped grapevine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive striped pattern on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and seasonal fruit display.
