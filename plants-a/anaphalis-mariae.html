Anaphalis mariae

Anaphalis mariae, commonly known as the silverleaf plant or snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Anaphalis mariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
