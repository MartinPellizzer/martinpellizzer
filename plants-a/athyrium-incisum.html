Athyrium incisum

Athyrium incisum, commonly known as the dissected fern or Japanese spleenwort, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, giving the plant its distinctive, lace-like appearance.
