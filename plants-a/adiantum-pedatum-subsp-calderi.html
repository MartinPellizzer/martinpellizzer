Adiantum pedatum subsp. calderi

Adiantum pedatum subsp.

calderi, commonly known as the California maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided, feathery appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a distinctive rachis that is slightly hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
