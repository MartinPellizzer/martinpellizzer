Agapetes poilanei

Agapetes poilanei, commonly known as the Pailane's silverleaf, is a shrub native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that often exhibits a silvery or grayish hue due to the presence of fine, soft hairs on the leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Agapetes poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
