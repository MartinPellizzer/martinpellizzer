Aristida leiocalycina

Aristida leiocalycina, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its resilience and adaptability, it is considered an important species in ecological restoration projects.
