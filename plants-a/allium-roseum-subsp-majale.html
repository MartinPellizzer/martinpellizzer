Allium roseum subsp. majale

Allium roseum subsp.

majale, commonly known as the purple onion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a bulbous base with long, strap-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, spherical, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and culinary use.

This subspecies is well-suited to well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
