Aspilia avilensis

Aspilia avilensis, commonly known as the African crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers surrounded by white bracts that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its height can range from 30 to 100 centimeters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aspilia avilensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various health purposes.
