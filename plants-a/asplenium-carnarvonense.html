Asplenium carnarvonense

Asplenium carnarvonense, commonly known as the Carnarvon spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the Aspleniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky or limestone substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Asplenium carnarvonense is native to southern Africa and is considered a rare and restricted species.
