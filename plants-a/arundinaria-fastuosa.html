Arundinaria fastuosa

Arundinaria fastuosa, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often tinged with a distinctive purple hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Arundinaria fastuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
