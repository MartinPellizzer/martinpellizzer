Acalypha lantanifolia

Acalypha lantanifolia, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
