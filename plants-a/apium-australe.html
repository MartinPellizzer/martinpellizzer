Apium australe

Apium australe, commonly known as the Australian cowparsley, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can be found in sandy or rocky soils. Its slender stems are often hairy, and it produces small white or greenish flowers.

Apium australe is also known for its edible leaves, which have a mild, parsley-like flavor.
