Adonis preslii

Adonis preslii, commonly known as the Preslia's glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, with a silver-gray underside, and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

Adonis preslii is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
