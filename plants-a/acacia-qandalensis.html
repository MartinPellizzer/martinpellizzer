Acacia qandalensis

Acacia qandalensis, commonly known as the Sudanese acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

The plant produces yellow to golden-green flowers, which are clustered in spikes and attract various pollinators. Acacia qandalensis is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
