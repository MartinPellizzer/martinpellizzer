Andropogon hypogynus var. conjugens

Andropogon hypogynus var. conjugens, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-ranked spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
