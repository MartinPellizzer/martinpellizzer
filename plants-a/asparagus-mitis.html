Asparagus mitis

Asparagus mitis, commonly known as the sweet asparagus or the white asparagus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Asparagus mitis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
