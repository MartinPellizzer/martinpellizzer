Acosta calolepis

Acosta calolepis, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually bright yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a hairy texture.

Acosta calolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
