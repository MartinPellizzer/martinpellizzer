Agropyron smithii var. typica

Agropyron smithii var. typica, commonly known as the prairie grass or wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

This species is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration ecology and as a forage plant.
