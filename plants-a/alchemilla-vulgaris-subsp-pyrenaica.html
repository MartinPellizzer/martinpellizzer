Alchemilla vulgaris subsp. pyrenaica

Alchemilla vulgaris subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as Pyrenean lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenees region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in late spring to early summer on slender, erect stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
