Aconitum rockii

Aconitum rockii, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a robust root system and tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a waxy texture, typical of the genus Aconitum.

Aconitum rockii is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to humans and animals.
