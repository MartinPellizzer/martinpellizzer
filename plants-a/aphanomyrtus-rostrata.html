Aphanomyrtus rostrata

Aphanomyrtus rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Aphanomyrtus rostrata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
