Achillea alpina subsp. camtschatica

Achillea alpina subsp.

camtschatica, commonly known as the Caucasian yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features upright, slender stems that bear narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
