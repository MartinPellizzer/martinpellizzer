Asparagus aethiopicus var. ternifolius

Asparagus aethiopicus var. ternifolius, commonly known as the African asparagus or the fern asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and its ability to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, needle-like spears, similar to common asparagus. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a vegetable in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-resistant gardens.
