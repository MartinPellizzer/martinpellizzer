Anemia × didicusana

Anemia × didicusana, commonly known as the hybrid anemia plant, is a rare botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two distinct species.

It exhibits a unique combination of morphological traits from its parent plants, often displaying vibrant foliage and unusual growth patterns. The plant is characterized by its clustered growth habit and striking, variegated leaves that range from green to shades of red and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by botanists and plant enthusiasts for its aesthetic and scientific interest.
