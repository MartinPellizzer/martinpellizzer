Alectorolophus × brigantiacus

Alectorolophus × brigantiacus, commonly known as the hybrid larkspur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinct, sometimes hairy appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful handling as it contains toxic alkaloids.
