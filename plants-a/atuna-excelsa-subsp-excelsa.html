Atuna excelsa subsp. excelsa

Atuna excelsa subsp.

excelsa, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces a distinctive fruit with a spiky exterior.

The fruit contains a single large, inedible seed and a soft, white, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
