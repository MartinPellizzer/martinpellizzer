Acer kobakoense

Acer kobakoense, commonly known as the Kobako maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and compact size.
