Abutilon texense

Abutilon texense, commonly known as velvetleaf or Texas abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Abutilon texense is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.
