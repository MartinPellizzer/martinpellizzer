Amarella theiantha

Amarella theiantha, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Amarella theiantha is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
