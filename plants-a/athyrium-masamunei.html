Athyrium masamunei

Athyrium masamunei, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a species native to Japan and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Athyriaceae and is known for its finely divided, arching leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

Athyrium masamunei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
