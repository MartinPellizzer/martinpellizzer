Acer saccharum f. villosum

Acer saccharum f. villosum, commonly known as the silky maple, is a variant of the sugar maple characterized by its distinctive silky, hairy texture on the leaves and stems.

This form exhibits a more delicate appearance compared to the typical sugar maple, with leaves that are often broader and more lobed. The plant is native to eastern North America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in forested areas, growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
