Astragalus chrysostachys var. sericeus

Astragalus chrysostachys var. sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Astragalus chrysostachys var. sericeus is adapted to drought conditions and is considered a valuable plant for xeriscaping and wildlife habitat.
