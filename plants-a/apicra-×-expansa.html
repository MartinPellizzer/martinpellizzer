Apicra × expansa

Apicra × expansa, commonly known as the hybrid dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, lance-shaped leaves and a hollow, leafy stem that supports a single, elongated flower head. The plant produces a composite flower made up of numerous yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tiny tubular flowers. Apicra × expansa is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its rapid growth cycle, making it a common weed in gardens and fields.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in various habitats.
