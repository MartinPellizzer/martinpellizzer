Athysanus pusillus

Athysanus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf thistle, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with a rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spiny flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant.
