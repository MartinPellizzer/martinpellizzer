Alsophila ludoviciana

Alsophila ludoviciana, commonly known as the Ludovician tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forests and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, supporting its growth and enabling it to withstand periodic flooding.

The fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.
