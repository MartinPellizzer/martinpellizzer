Airidium elegantulum

Airidium elegantulum, commonly known as the elegant lily of the Nile, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage is typically broad and glossy, adding visual interest to gardens and landscapes.
