Anoda caudatifolia

Anoda caudatifolia, commonly known as the hairy false flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and bracts that are often mistaken for leaves.

Anoda caudatifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
