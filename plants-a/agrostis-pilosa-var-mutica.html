Agrostis pilosa var. mutica

Agrostis pilosa var. mutica, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for turfgrass applications. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-rowed flowers.

Agrostis pilosa var. mutica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
