Aloe comptonii

Aloe comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Aloe comptonii is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
