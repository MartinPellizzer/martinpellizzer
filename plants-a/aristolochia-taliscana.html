Aristolochia taliscana

Aristolochia taliscana, commonly known as the Mexican birthwort, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces striking, hooded flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
