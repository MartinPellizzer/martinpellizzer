Athanasia ebracteata

Athanasia ebracteata, commonly known as the naked-leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is herbaceous, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often absent or reduced, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Athanasia ebracteata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
