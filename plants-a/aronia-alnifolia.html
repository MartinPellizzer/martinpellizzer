Aronia alnifolia

Aronia alnifolia, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that develop into edible berries. The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, juices, and supplements. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
