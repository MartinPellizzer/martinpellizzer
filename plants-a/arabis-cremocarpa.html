Arabis cremocarpa

Arabis cremocarpa, commonly known as the creeping rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters, while its leaves are basal and spoon-shaped.

Arabis cremocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens.
