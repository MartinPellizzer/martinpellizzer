Asclepias blanchetii

Asclepias blanchetii, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the milkweed family, Asclepiadaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces unique seed pods that contain numerous fluffy seeds attached to silky filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Asclepias blanchetii is also a critical host plant for the monarch butterfly, providing essential food and shelter for its larvae.
