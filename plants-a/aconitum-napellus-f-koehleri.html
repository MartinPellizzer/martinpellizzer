Aconitum napellus f. koehleri

Aconitum napellus f. koehleri, commonly known as the Koehleri's monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
