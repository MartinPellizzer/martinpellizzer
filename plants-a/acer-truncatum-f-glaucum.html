Acer truncatum f. glaucum

Acer truncatum f. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous maple, is a cultivar of the maple tree characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a smooth margin and a striking coloration that turns yellow in autumn. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities, including its compact growth habit and vibrant seasonal color changes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
