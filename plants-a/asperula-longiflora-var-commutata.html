Asperula longiflora var. commutata

Asperula longiflora var. commutata, commonly known as the communis variety of the evergreen shrub, is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and oval in shape, providing an attractive evergreen appearance. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a ground cover.
