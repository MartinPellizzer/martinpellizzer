Acer ginnala f. aidzuense

Acer ginnala f. aidzuense, commonly known as the Aidzu maple, is a rare variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and is typically found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. It prefers partial to full shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
