Astragalus monspessulanus

Astragalus monspessulanus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
