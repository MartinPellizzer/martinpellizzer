Arundinella vaginata

Arundinella vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
