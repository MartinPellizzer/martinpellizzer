Atriplex lampa

Atriplex lampa, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and salt-tolerant properties, allowing it to thrive in saline soils. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in desert environments. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Atriplex lampa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
