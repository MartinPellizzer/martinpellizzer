Asplenium × litardierei

Asplenium × litardierei, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Asplenium trichomanes and Asplenium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that often exhibit a distinctive zigzag pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are usually located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium × litardierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
