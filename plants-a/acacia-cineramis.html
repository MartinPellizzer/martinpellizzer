Acacia cineramis

Acacia cineramis, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pale grey to silver-green branches and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Acacia cineramis is a hardy species that is drought-tolerant and well adapted to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.
