Acronychia laevis var. longiflora

Acronychia laevis var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered silky oak, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a pale underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native environment.
