Agonis luehmannii

Agonis luehmannii, commonly known as the Luehmann's she-oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and distinctive, small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a rounded habit with smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age. It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dunes.

Agonis luehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
