Aniseia cernua var. ambigua

Aniseia cernua var. ambigua, commonly known as the weeping aniseia, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its drooping, slender stems.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
