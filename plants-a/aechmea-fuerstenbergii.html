Aechmea fuerstenbergii

Aechmea fuerstenbergii, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture, and they are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom briefly before fading.

Morphology

Aechmea fuerstenbergii has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, fleshy, and sword-like, arranged in a compact spiral, with a dark green to blue-green color and a waxy texture.

The plant lacks a traditional stem, instead having a short, underground rhizome that supports the rosette structure. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small white to pale purple flowers that bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that contains seeds dispersed by wind and animals.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air and soil, typical of its epiphytic growth habit.
