Aquilegia alba

Aquilegia alba, commonly known as the white columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features striking, spurred blossoms that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

Aquilegia alba is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
