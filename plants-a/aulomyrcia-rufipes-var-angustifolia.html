Aulomyrcia rufipes var. angustifolia

Aulomyrcia rufipes var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aulomyrcia, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
