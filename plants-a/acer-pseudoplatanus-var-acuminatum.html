Acer pseudoplatanus var. acuminatum

Acer pseudoplatanus var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminata maple, is a variant of the common maple, characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and coloration.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically elongated and pointed, with a serrated margin, and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
