Astragalus sinicus f. rosaceus

Astragalus sinicus f. rosaceus, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environments.
