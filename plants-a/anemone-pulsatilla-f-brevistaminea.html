Anemone pulsatilla f. brevistaminea

Anemone pulsatilla f. brevistaminea, commonly known as the short-stamen pasqueflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, showy purple to pink flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. Its reduced stamens and unique floral structure make it a notable example of floral variation within the genus Anemone.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early spring blooms.
