Anthyllis vulneraria var. allionii

Anthyllis vulneraria var. allionii, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, spherical seed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in soil stabilization.
