Ampelopsis heterophylla var. bungei

Ampelopsis heterophylla var. bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's Ampelopsis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that vary in shape, with upper leaves typically trifoliate and lower ones usually pinnately lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to gardens and natural habitats.
