Andropogon parvispicus

Andropogon parvispicus, commonly known as the small-leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Andropogon parvispicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
