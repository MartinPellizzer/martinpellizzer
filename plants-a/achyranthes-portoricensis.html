Achyranthes portoricensis

Achyranthes portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Achyranthes portoricensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
