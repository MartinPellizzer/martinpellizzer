Acanthocereus baxaniensis

Acanthocereus baxaniensis, commonly known as the Baxan Thorn cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and spiny, with a ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
