Agathis beccarii

Agathis beccarii, commonly known as the Philippine cedar, is a large evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The tree produces large, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in local industries.

The plant also plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various forms of wildlife.
