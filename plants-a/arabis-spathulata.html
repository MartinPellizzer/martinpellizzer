Arabis spathulata

Arabis spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are broad and spoon-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes.

Arabis spathulata is often found in alpine or mountainous regions, where it contributes to the flora of rocky habitats.
