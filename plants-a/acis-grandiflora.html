Acis grandiflora

Acis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered acis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers with prominent yellow stamens. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Acis grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
