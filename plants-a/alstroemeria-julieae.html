Alstroemeria julieae

Alstroemeria julieae, commonly known as Julieae Alstroemeria, is a rare and striking plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces an upright growth habit with multiple flower spikes.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
