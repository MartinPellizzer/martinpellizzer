Aphelandra tetroicia

Aphelandra tetroicia, commonly known as the four-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by berries that mature to a red or orange hue.

Aphelandra tetroicia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shady environments.
