Anguria inaequalis

Anguria inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal cucumber, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped fruits that vary in size and color, often displaying a mix of green and yellow hues. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers being essential for fruit development. Its vines are vigorous climbers, requiring support as they grow.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a mature yellow color, indicating full ripeness.
