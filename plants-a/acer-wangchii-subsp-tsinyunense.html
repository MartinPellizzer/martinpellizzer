Acer wangchii subsp. tsinyunense

Acer wangchii subsp.

tsinyunense, commonly known as the Yunnan maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
