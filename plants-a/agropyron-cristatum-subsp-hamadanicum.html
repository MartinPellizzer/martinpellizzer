Agropyron cristatum subsp. hamadanicum

Agropyron cristatum subsp.

hamadanicum, commonly known as the Hamadanic needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its foliage is often used for forage in livestock grazing.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
