Antennaria petaloidea var. noveboracensis

Antennaria petaloidea var. noveboracensis, commonly known as the New York bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with hairy stems and leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and contributes to its ecological significance in native habitats.
