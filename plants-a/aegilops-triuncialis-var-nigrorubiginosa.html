Aegilops triuncialis var. nigrorubiginosa

Aegilops triuncialis var. nigrorubiginosa, commonly known as the dark red rye grass, is a wild grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and dark red to purple-colored glumes and lemma. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has been studied for its potential in agricultural systems due to its adaptability and resilience.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and genetic research.
