Argyreia purpuricarpa

Argyreia purpuricarpa, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a distinctive purple-pink hue, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, red or orange aril, which is often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Argyreia purpuricarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a decorative vine in gardens and greenhouses.
