Arsenjevia baicalensis

Arsenjevia baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the Lake Baikal region of Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It contains various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and iridoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Arsenjevia baicalensis is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
