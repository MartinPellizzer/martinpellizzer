Arctium × ambiguum

Arctium × ambiguum, commonly known as the common burdock.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces spiky, brown burrs that contain seeds, which are adapted for attachment to animals and clothing. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its fibrous roots have been historically used in traditional medicine and for fiber production.
