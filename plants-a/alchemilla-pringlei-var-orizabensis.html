Alchemilla pringlei var. orizabensis

Alchemilla pringlei var. orizabensis, commonly known as the Orizaba alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
