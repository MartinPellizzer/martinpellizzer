Arabis dacica

Arabis dacica, commonly known as the Balkan rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Arabis dacica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
