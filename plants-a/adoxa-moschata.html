Adoxa moschata

Adoxa moschata, commonly known as sweet pepper or musk root, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Adoxaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble tiny peppers, which are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often reddish in color.

Adoxa moschata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant aroma.
