Acalypha japonica

Acalypha japonica, commonly known as Japanese ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct reddish or purple underside.

Acalypha japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
