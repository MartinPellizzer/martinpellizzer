Azalea indica var. garibaldii

Azalea indica var. garibaldii, commonly known as the Garibaldi azalea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink to red hues that attract pollinators. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for both landscape design and container cultivation.
