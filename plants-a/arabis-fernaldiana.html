Arabis fernaldiana

Arabis fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a rough texture.

Arabis fernaldiana is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
