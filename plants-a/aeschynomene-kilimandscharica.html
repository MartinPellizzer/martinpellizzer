Aeschynomene kilimandscharica

Aeschynomene kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro burr, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in regions with poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it resilient in harsh climates.

Its seeds are encased in a rough, spiny pod, which aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or human clothing.
