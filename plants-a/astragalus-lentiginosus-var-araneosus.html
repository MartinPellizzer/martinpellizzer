Astragalus lentiginosus var. araneosus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. araneosus, commonly known as the spider-net milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is known for its distinctive, net-like pattern on its leaves, which gives it its common name.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
