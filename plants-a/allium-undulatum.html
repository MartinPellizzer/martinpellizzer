Allium undulatum

Allium undulatum, commonly known as the bent-grass garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its elongated, curved bulbs that resemble twisted roots. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky environments.

Allium undulatum is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
