Acalypha parvula f. chathamensis

Acalypha parvula f. chathamensis, commonly known as the Chatham Islands copperleaf, is a unique variant of the copperleaf genus native to the Chatham Islands.

It is a small shrub characterized by its distinctive, copper-colored foliage that gives it its common name. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
