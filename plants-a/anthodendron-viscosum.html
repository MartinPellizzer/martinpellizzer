Anthodendron viscosum

Anthodendron viscosum, commonly known as sticky thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sticky, glandular leaves that give it a unique texture. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Anthodendron viscosum is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects.
