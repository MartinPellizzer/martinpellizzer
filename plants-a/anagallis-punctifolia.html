Anagallis punctifolia

Anagallis punctifolia, commonly known as the dotted willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant blooms from spring to early summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
