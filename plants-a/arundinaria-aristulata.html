Arundinaria aristulata

Arundinaria aristulata, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically golden in color, hence its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Arundinaria aristulata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
