Aeginetia trimenii

Aeginetia trimenii, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in its native habitat.
