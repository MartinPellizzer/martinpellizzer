Aquilegia canadensis var. flavescens

Aquilegia canadensis var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow corydalis, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed, and it produces erect, branched stems.

The yellow corydalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
