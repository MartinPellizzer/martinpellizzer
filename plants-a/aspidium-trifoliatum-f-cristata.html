Aspidium trifoliatum f. cristata

Aspidium trifoliatum f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate water fern, is a cultivar of the water fern genus Aspidium.

It is characterized by its unique, crinkled or crisped fronds that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. This fern thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The fronds are typically lance-shaped and have a delicate, feathery texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
