Adhatoda densiflora

Adhatoda densiflora, commonly known as the African skullcap, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant contains various alkaloids, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their bronchodilatory properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest margins.

Adhatoda densiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
