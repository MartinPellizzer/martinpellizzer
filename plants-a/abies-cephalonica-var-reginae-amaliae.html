Abies cephalonica var. reginae-amaliae

Abies cephalonica var. reginae-amaliae, commonly known as the Queen Amalia fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the mountainous regions of northern Greece.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The cones are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is highly valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
