Aesculus lantsangensis

Aesculus lantsangensis, commonly known as the Lantsang horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that appear in panicles during the spring. The tree produces large, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds each. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Aesculus lantsangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
