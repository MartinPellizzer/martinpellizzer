Anthurium paraguayense

Anthurium paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay arrowroot or Paraguay anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it produces colorful, showy inflorescences that range from red to orange.

Anthurium paraguayense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
