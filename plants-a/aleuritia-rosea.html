Aleuritia rosea

Aleuritia rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Aleuritia rosea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
