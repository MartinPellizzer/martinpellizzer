Aster lanceolatus subsp. hesperius

Aster lanceolatus subsp.

hesperius, commonly known as the western aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to blue flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its structure supports pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
