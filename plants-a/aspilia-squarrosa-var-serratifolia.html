Aspilia squarrosa var. serratifolia

Aspilia squarrosa var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
