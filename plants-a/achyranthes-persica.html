Achyranthes persica

Achyranthes persica, commonly known as Persian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

The roots of Achyranthes persica are thick and fleshy, and they contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
