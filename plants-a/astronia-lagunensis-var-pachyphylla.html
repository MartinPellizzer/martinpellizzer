Astronia lagunensis var. pachyphylla

Astronia lagunensis var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved astrophytum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
