Astragalus echinatus var. stenorrhinus

Astragalus echinatus var. stenorrhinus, commonly known as the narrow-nosed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is part of the legume family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
