Astragalus epiglossus

Astragalus epiglossus, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions, and it is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

Astragalus epiglossus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
