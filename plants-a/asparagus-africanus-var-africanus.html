Asparagus africanus var. africanus

Asparagus africanus var. africanus, commonly known as the African asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems with small, scale-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
