Albizia amara subsp. amara

Albizia amara subsp.

amara, commonly known as the bitter acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are compound and divided into small, feather-like segments.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its pods are slender, elongated, and contain several seeds. Albizia amara subsp.

amara is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
