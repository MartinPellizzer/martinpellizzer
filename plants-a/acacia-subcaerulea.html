Acacia subcaerulea

Acacia subcaerulea, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces flat, elongated seed pods that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The blue acacia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
