Acantholimon halophilum var. coloratum

Acantholimon halophilum var. coloratum, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray foliage and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, saline soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize sandy soils in coastal environments.
