Anemone rupicola var. glabriuscula

Anemone rupicola var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous rock anemone, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often hairy but become glabrous with age, and it produces showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or xeriscaping.
