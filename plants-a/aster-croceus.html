Aster croceus

Aster croceus, commonly known as the yellow aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tiny florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Aster croceus is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it blooms in late summer to early autumn.
