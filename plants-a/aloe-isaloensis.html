Aloe isaloensis

Aloe isaloensis, commonly known as the Isalo Aloe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color with a waxy coating, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season.

Aloe isaloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
