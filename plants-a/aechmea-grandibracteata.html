Aechmea grandibracteata

Aechmea grandibracteata, commonly known as the sword fern bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater, which serves as a habitat for various microorganisms and small animals. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically narrow and sword-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Aechmea grandibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Morphology

Aechmea grandibracteata has rosette-shaped leaves that are sword-like, stiff, and covered in fine white hairs, arranged in a tight spiral around the central stem.

The leaves are typically dark green with a bluish tint, and can grow up to 40 cm in length. The plant has a short, thick stem with a fibrous, woody bark that is usually greenish-brown in color. It produces striking red or pink flowers in a spadix-like structure, blooming during the summer months, and is pollinated by insects such as bees and beetles.
