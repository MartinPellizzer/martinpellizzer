Asplenium glaberrimum

Asplenium glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth holly fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are arranged in a distinctive pattern along the margins of the fronds. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
