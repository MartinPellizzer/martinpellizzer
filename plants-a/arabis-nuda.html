Arabis nuda

Arabis nuda, commonly known as the naked-stemmed rockcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in open, sunny environments. Its simple, alternate leaves are usually hairless, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

Arabis nuda is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
