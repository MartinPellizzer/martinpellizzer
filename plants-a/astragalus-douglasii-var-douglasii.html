Astragalus douglasii var. douglasii

Astragalus douglasii var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
