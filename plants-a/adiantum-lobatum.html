Adiantum lobatum

Adiantum lobatum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and slightly curled. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Adiantum lobatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
