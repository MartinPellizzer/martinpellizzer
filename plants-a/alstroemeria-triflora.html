Alstroemeria triflora

Alstroemeria triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered alstroemeria, is a member of the Alstroemeriaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Alstroemeria triflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
