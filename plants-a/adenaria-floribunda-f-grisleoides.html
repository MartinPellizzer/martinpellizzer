Adenaria floribunda f. grisleoides

Adenaria floribunda f. grisleoides, commonly known as the Grisleoides form, is a botanical variant of the genus Adenaria, which belongs to the Iridaceae family.

This plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
