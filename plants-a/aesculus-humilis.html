Aesculus humilis

Aesculus humilis, commonly known as the bottlebrush buckeye, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble bottle brushes, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a spiny, spherical capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
