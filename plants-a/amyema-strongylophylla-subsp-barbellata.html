Amyema strongylophylla subsp. barbellata

Amyema strongylophylla subsp.

barbellata, commonly known as the barbellate acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, barbed stipules on the leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
