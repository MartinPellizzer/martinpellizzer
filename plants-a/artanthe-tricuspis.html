Artanthe tricuspis

Artanthe tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Artanthe tricuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
