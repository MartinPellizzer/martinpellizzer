Abutilon glaucum var. cavanillesii

Abutilon glaucum var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the silverleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Abutilon glaucum var. cavanillesii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
