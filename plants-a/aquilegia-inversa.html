Aquilegia inversa

Aquilegia inversa, commonly known as the upside-down crownflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where the flowers are oriented downward, giving it its common name. The plant features compound leaves with deeply cut lobes and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, purple, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is valued in both horticulture and botanical studies.
