Agave micracantha var. albidior

Agave micracantha var. albidior, commonly known as the white-flowered agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
