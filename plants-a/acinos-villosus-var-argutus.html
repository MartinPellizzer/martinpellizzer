Acinos villosus var. argutus

Acinos villosus var. argutus, commonly known as the hairy mountain aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with purple centers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
