Acetosa hortensis

Acetosa hortensis, commonly known as garden sorrel, is a perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to their high content of oxalic acid.
