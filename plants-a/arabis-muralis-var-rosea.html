Arabis muralis var. rosea

Arabis muralis var. rosea, commonly known as the pink wallflower, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to rose. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing numerous flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
