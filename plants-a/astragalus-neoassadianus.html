Astragalus neoassadianus

Astragalus neoassadianus, commonly known as the Neoassadian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in harsh environments.
