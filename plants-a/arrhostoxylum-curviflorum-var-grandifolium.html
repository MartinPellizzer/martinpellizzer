Arrhostoxylum curviflorum var. grandifolium

Arrhostoxylum curviflorum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved chilco, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. These flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous habitats.
