Aphomonix hederacea

Aphomonix hederacea, commonly known as the common ground ivy, is a perennial weed belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It spreads rapidly through both vegetative growth and seed dispersal, making it a persistent invasive species in lawns and disturbed soils.

Its creeping habit and ability to form dense mats contribute to its success in various environments.
