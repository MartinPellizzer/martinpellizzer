Astragalus kermanicus

Astragalus kermanicus, commonly known as Kerman milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its biological features make it an important species in ecological and botanical studies.
