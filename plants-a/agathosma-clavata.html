Agathosma clavata

Agathosma clavata, commonly known as African geranium, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Geraniaceae family and is often grown for its ornamental value and fragrant properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Agathosma clavata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
