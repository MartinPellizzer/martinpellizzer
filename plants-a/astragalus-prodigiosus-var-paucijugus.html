Astragalus prodigiosus var. paucijugus

Astragalus prodigiosus var. paucijugus, commonly known as the red milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
