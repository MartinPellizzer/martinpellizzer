Anisostachya debilis

Anisostachya debilis, commonly known as the slender reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Anisostachya debilis is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and adaptability to moist environments.
