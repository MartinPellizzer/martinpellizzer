Atractogyne gabonii

Atractogyne gabonii, commonly known as the Gabon arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Central and West Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves and produces striking, spadix-like flowers. Its rhizomes are used for their starchy roots, which are edible and similar to yams.

Atractogyne gabonii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
