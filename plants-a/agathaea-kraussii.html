Agathaea kraussii

Agathaea kraussii, commonly known as the Cape mountain thorn, is a shrub native to the fynbos biome of South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Agathaea kraussii plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
