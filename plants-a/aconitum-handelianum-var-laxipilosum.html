Aconitum handelianum var. laxipilosum

Aconitum handelianum var. laxipilosum, commonly known as the soft-hairy aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are compound, with deeply lobed or divided foliage, and are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
