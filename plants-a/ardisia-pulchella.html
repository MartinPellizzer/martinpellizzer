Ardisia pulchella

Ardisia pulchella, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Following flowering, it develops bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and unique fruiting structure.
