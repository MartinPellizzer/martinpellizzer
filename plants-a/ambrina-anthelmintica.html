Ambrina anthelmintica

Ambrina anthelmintica, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many plants with milky sap. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its milky latex is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anthelmintic properties.
