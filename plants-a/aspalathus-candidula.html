Aspalathus candidula

Aspalathus candidula, commonly known as the white bushveld clover, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Aspalathus candidula is often found in grassland ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
