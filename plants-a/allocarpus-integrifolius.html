Allocarpus integrifolius

Allocarpus integrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems that store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system is extensive and helps it thrive in sandy soils with low moisture.

Allocarpus integrifolius is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
