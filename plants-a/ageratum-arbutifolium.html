Ageratum arbutifolium

Ageratum arbutifolium, commonly known as the bearberry ageratum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Ageratum arbutifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
