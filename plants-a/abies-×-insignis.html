Abies × insignis

Abies × insignis, commonly known as the noble fir, is a hybrid conifer native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Abies amabilis and Abies lasiocarpa, resulting in a tree that exhibits characteristics of both parent species. The plant has a tall, slender trunk and a conical shape, with dark green, needle-like leaves that are soft to the touch. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and release seeds in the fall.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
