Anerincleistus setulosus var. suffruticosus

Anerincleistus setulosus var. suffruticosus, commonly known as the creeping spiny bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its small, bladder-like structures used for trapping prey.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it anchors itself to substrates with its root system. The plant exhibits a succulent, herbaceous habit with thin, branched stems covered in tiny, spiny appendages. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

This variety is notable for its specialized adaptations to aquatic life, including its ability to absorb nutrients from both water and trapped prey.
