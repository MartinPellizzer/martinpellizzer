Aira aristulata

Aira aristulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aira, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Aira aristulata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
