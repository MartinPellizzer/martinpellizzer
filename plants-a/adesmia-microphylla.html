Adesmia microphylla

Adesmia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clustered, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small size and compact growth habit make it well adapted to arid environments.

Adesmia microphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
