Austromyrtus bidwillii

Austromyrtus bidwillii, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a peppermint-like scent when crushed. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.
