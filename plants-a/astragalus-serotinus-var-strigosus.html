Astragalus serotinus var. strigosus

Astragalus serotinus var. strigosus, commonly known as the late-flowering milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus serotinus var. strigosus is also notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
