Actinodaphne sesquipedalis var. cambodiana

Actinodaphne sesquipedalis var. cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
