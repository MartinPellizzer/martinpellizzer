Angelica gigas var. minor

Angelica gigas var. minor, commonly known as lesser angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features large, compound leaves that are often deeply lobed, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces tall, slender stems topped with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
