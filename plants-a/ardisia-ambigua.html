Ardisia ambigua

Ardisia ambigua, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen or the Christmas berry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrsinaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
