Aphelandra castanifolia

Aphelandra castanifolia, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling torches, hence its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Aphelandra castanifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
