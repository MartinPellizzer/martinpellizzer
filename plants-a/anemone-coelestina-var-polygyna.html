Anemone coelestina var. polygyna

Anemone coelestina var. polygyna, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high elevations of the American West.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in alpine ecosystems.
