Aphanochilus fruticosus var. ochroleucus

Aphanochilus fruticosus var. ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
