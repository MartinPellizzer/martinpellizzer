Angraecum forcipatum

Angraecum forcipatum, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving the hawkmoth. Its flowers have a long, tubular nectar spur that is adapted for pollination by specific nocturnal insects.

Angraecum forcipatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
