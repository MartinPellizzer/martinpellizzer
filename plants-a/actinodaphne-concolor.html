Actinodaphne concolor

Actinodaphne concolor, commonly known as the smooth-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its aromatic leaves and bark contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
