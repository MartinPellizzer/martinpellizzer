Alpinia jingxiensis

Alpinia jingxiensis, commonly known as the Jingxi ginger, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in inflorescences at the top of the plant.

Alpinia jingxiensis is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
