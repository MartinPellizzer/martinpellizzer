Abrus aureus

Abrus aureus, commonly known as the flame bean or Java coffee, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain toxic seeds, which are often used in traditional crafts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Abrus aureus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
