Acacia murrumboensis

Acacia murrumboensis, commonly known as the Murrumbidgee wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, phyllodes that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear during the warmer months. Acacia murrumboensis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
