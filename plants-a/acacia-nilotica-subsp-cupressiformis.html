Acacia nilotica subsp. cupressiformis

Acacia nilotica subsp.

cupressiformis, commonly known as the Egyptian acacia or the black acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Nile Valley and parts of the Sahel. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thorny branches, which provide habitat for various animals.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The wood is hard and durable, historically used for construction and tool-making.
