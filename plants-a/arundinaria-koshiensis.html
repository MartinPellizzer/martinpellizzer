Arundinaria koshiensis

Arundinaria koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Arundinaria koshiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
