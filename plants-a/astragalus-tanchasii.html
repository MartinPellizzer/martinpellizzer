Astragalus tanchasii

Astragalus tanchasii, commonly known as Tanchas' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many plants known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Astragalus tanchasii is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
