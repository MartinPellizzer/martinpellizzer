Artocarpus chama

Artocarpus chama, commonly known as the little breadfruit or dwarf breadfruit, is a small tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fruit-bearing trees like the mulberry and fig. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed when ripe, though they can be used in various culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the tree is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Artocarpus chama thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
