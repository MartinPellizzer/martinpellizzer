Allionia cucullata

Allionia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Allionia cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
