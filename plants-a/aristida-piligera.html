Aristida piligera

Aristida piligera, commonly known as the slender dropseed, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Aristida piligera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
