Andrographis tenera

Andrographis tenera, commonly known as the small bitter grass, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Andrographis tenera is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine systems.
