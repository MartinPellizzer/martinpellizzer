Anthericum unifolium

Anthericum unifolium, commonly known as the single-leaved lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a single, narrow, grass-like leaf that is often basal and persists through the growing season. The plant produces a tall, slender stem bearing a single, bell-shaped flower with six petals. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, shady environments.

Anthericum unifolium is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland areas.
