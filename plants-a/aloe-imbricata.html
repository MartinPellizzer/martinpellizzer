Aloe imbricata

Aloe imbricata, commonly known as the Zulu aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spiky leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers on tall, branched stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Aloe imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
