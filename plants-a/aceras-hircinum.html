Aceras hircinum

Aceras hircinum, commonly known as the goat's bean, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal studies.
