Aloe nordaliae

Aloe nordaliae, commonly known as the Nordalia aloe, is a species native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of succulents. The leaves are usually blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
