Allium rosenbachianum subsp. kwakense

Allium rosenbachianum subsp.

kwakense, commonly known as the Kwakense leek, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest undergrowth. It produces bulbous structures that serve as storage organs and are used for propagation.
