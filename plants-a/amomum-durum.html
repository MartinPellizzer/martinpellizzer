Amomum durum

Amomum durum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, dark brown, three-lobed berries that are used as a spice. The berries are harvested when fully mature and dried for use in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic properties.
