Areca camarinensis

Areca camarinensis, commonly known as the Malayan areca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which eventually develop into elongated, three-lobed fruits. Areca camarinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
