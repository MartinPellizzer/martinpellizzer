Aster viscidulus

Aster viscidulus, commonly known as sticky aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aster viscidulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
