Angadenia valenzuelana

Angadenia valenzuelana, commonly known as the Valenzuelan orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
