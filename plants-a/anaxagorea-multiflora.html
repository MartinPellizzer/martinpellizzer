Anaxagorea multiflora

Anaxagorea multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered evergreen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in panicles. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Anaxagorea multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
