Acacia verticillata subsp. cephalantha

Acacia verticillata subsp.

cephalantha, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dunes or along the coast.

The species is well adapted to coastal environments and is an important part of the local flora.
