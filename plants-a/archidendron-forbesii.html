Archidendron forbesii

Archidendron forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
