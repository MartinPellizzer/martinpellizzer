Aster gremialis

Aster gremialis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster gremialis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
