Acronia phyllocardia

Acronia phyllocardia, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Acronia phyllocardia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
