Acacia rostriformis

Acacia rostriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, needle-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Acacia rostriformis is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
