Acacia diversifolia

Acacia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Acacia diversifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a range of morphological variations depending on environmental conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
