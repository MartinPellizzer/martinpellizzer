Angorkis eburnangis

Angorkis eburnangis, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a deciduous species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that resembles polished ivory, giving it its scientific name. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, round fruits.

Angorkis eburnangis is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in low rainfall and high temperatures.
