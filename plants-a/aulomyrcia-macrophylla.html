Aulomyrcia macrophylla

Aulomyrcia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Aulomyrcia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
