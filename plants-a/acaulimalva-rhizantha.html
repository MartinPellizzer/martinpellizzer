Acaulimalva rhizantha

Acaulimalva rhizantha, commonly known as the creeping mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes and form a dense mat. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Acaulimalva rhizantha is often found in wet meadows, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.
