Aristolochia bonatii

Aristolochia bonatii, commonly known as the Sicilian birthwort, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its striking, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a horn. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
