Attalea seabrensis

Attalea seabrensis, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish flowers that develop into orange or red fruits. The plant thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often found in the Amazon rainforest. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

The plant also has traditional uses by indigenous communities for its fibrous materials and nutritional value.
