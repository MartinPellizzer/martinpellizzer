Amyris marshii

Amyris marshii, commonly known as the Florida sand pine, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Amyris marshii is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
