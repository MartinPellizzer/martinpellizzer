Astragalus chingianus

Astragalus chingianus, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its pods are slender and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus chingianus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
