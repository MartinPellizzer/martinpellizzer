Antiphylla pulvinata

Antiphylla pulvinata, commonly known as the cushion lichen, is a unique plant that thrives in arid and rocky environments.

It is a lichen, which means it is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. The plant forms dense, cushion-like growths that help it retain moisture and protect against harsh weather conditions. Its structure is adapted for survival in extreme climates, making it a common sight in deserts and high-altitude regions.

Antiphylla pulvinata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
