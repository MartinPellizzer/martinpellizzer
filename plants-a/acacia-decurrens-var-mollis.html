Acacia decurrens var. mollis

Acacia decurrens var. mollis, commonly known as soft-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous or softly hairy stems and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular inflorescences, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
