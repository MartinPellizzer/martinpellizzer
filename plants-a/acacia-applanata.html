Acacia applanata

Acacia applanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Acacia applanata is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
