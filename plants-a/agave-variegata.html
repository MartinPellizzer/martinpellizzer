Agave variegata

Agave variegata, commonly known as the variegated agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features a combination of green, white, and yellow hues. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant store water efficiently. It produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, white flowers, though it is rarely cultivated for its blooms.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.
