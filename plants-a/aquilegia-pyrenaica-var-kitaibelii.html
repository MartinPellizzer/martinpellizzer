Aquilegia pyrenaica var. kitaibelii

Aquilegia pyrenaica var. kitaibelii, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
