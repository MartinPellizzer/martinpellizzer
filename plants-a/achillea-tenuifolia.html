Achillea tenuifolia

Achillea tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
