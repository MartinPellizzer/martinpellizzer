Allophylus spragueanus

Allophylus spragueanus, commonly known as the Sprague's alder, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Allophylus spragueanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
