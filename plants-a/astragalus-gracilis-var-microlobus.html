Astragalus gracilis var. microlobus

Astragalus gracilis var. microlobus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus gracilis var. microlobus is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a keystone species in its habitat.
