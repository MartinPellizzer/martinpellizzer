Athyrium fimbriatum var. sphaeropteroides

Athyrium fimbriatum var. sphaeropteroides, commonly known as the round-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually a dark green color with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fern-like appearance.
