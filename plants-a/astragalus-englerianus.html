Astragalus englerianus

Astragalus englerianus, commonly known as Engler's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus englerianus is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
