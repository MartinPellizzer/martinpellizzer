Anthemis cornucopiae

Anthemis cornucopiae, commonly known as the Cornucopia flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
