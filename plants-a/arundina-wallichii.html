Arundina wallichii

Arundina wallichii, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Himalayas.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are usually pale green to white in color.

Arundina wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
