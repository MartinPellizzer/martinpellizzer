Atriplex kataf

Atriplex kataf, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, green or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is highly adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a valuable species for soil stabilization and phytoremediation. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.

Atriplex kataf plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems by preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
