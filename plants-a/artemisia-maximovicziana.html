Artemisia maximovicziana

Artemisia maximovicziana, commonly known as the Siberian wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
