Aronia canadensis

Aronia canadensis, commonly known as Canadian chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces bright red to black berries in the fall, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Aronia canadensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
