Aster patens var. phlogifolius

Aster patens var. phlogifolius, commonly known as the flameleaf aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, lobed, and often reddish-orange leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
