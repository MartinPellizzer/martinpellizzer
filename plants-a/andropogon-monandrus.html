Andropogon monandrus

Andropogon monandrus, commonly known as inland sea oats, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Andropogon monandrus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
