Androrchis × kretzschmariorum

Androrchis × kretzschmariorum, commonly known as the Kretzschmar's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Androrchis luteus and Androrchis maculatus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, erect spikes bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive orange and yellow coloration. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in temperate climates.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
