Alsine macrocarpa var. imbricata

Alsine macrocarpa var. imbricata, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

The flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
