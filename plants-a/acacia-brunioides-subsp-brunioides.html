Acacia brunioides subsp. brunioides

Acacia brunioides subsp.

brunioides, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces yellow globular flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in dune environments.

The species is adapted to withstand salt spray and moderate drought conditions.
