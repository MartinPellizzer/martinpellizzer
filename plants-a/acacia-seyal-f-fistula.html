Acacia seyal f. fistula

Acacia seyal f. fistula, commonly known as the knobthorn tree, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya, Somalia, and Sudan. The plant is characterized by its thick, knobby trunk and small, compound leaves that help reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers are clustered in cylindrical spikes, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Acacia seyal f. fistula is well adapted to harsh environments and plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional practices.
