Aster heterophyllus var. glabriusculus

Aster heterophyllus var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the smoothleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with some leaves being narrow and others broader, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed soils.
