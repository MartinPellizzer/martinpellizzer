Amyris polymorpha

Amyris polymorpha, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Amyris polymorpha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and landscape settings.
