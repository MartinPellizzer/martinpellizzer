Astragalus siahcheshmehensis

Astragalus siahcheshmehensis, commonly known as the Siahcheshmeh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
