Atopocarpus papillosus

Atopocarpus papillosus, commonly known as the hairy false kareedoo, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits known as cypsela, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
