Agrostis clavata var. putoranica

Agrostis clavata var. putoranica, commonly known as the Putoranica bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a tufted growth habit and a preference for temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
