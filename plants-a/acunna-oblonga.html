Acunna oblonga

Acunna oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf acunna, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Acunna oblonga thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
