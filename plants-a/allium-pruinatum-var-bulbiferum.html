Allium pruinatum var. bulbiferum

Allium pruinatum var. bulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbous garlic.

This plant is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and has a short, stout stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild garlic-like scent.
