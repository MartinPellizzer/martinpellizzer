Avena versicolor f. rigida

Avena versicolor f. rigida, commonly known as the rigid oat, is a variant of the oat species characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant produces spikelets that are usually green to reddish in color, with leathery bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
