Anthericum exellii var. angustifolium

Anthericum exellii var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early summer. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, nodding blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
