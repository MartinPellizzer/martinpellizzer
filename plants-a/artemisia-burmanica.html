Artemisia burmanica

Artemisia burmanica, commonly known as Burmese wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a strong, camphor-like scent. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in panicles and bloom in the summer months.

Artemisia burmanica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
