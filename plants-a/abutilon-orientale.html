Abutilon orientale

Abutilon orientale, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Abutilon orientale is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
