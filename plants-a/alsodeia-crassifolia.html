Alsodeia crassifolia

Alsodeia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alsodeia, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
