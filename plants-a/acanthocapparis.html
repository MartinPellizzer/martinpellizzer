Acanthocapparis

Acanthocapparis, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Capparaceae.

These plants are characterized by their thorny stems and small, often yellow or green flowers. They are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in rocky or sandy soils. The fruit, which is usually a small berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Acanthocapparis species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
