Alchemilla hybrida subsp. pubescens

Alchemilla hybrida subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the velvet alkanet, is a perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
