Allium altoatlanticum

Allium altoatlanticum, commonly known as the highland garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Allium altoatlanticum is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates.
