Allium birkinshawii

Allium birkinshawii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley garlic.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers in late spring. Its bulbs are small and have a mild garlic scent, though they are not typically cultivated for culinary use.

Allium birkinshawii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
