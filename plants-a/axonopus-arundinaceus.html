Axonopus arundinaceus

Axonopus arundinaceus, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, tufted culms that are often mistaken for bamboo due to their similar appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense clump. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Axonopus arundinaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
