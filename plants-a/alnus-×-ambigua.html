Alnus × ambigua

Alnus × ambigua, commonly known as the paper birch, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its white, papery bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Betulaceae family and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Alnus × ambigua plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
