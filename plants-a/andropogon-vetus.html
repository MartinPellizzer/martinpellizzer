Andropogon vetus

Andropogon vetus, commonly known as old bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local wildlife.

Andropogon vetus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional landscaping.
