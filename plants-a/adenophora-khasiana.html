Adenophora khasiana

Adenophora khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Adenophora khasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
