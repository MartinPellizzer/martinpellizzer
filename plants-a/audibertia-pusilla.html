Audibertia pusilla

Audibertia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf audibertia, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and attractive flowers, it is often cultivated in gardens as a decorative plant.
