Alloplectus tessmannii

Alloplectus tessmannii, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, arranged in a showy inflorescence during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

Alloplectus tessmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
