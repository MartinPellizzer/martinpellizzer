Aragallus songoricus

Aragallus songoricus, commonly known as the Songorica lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and upright flowering stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
