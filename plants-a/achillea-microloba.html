Achillea microloba

Achillea microloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of tiny white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and its ornamental value make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
