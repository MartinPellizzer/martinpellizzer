Acanthospora juncea

Acanthospora juncea, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Acanthospora juncea is well adapted to maritime environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
