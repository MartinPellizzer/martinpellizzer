Adelia peduncularis

Adelia peduncularis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed adelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Adelia peduncularis is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in semi-desert landscapes.
