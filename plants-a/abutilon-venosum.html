Abutilon venosum

Abutilon venosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a prominent central vein, and they are often green with a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are yellow or orange, clustered in loose inflorescences, and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields.
