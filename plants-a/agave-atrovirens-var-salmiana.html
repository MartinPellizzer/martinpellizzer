Agave atrovirens var. salmiana

Agave atrovirens var. salmiana, commonly known as the Salmiana agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, dark green leaves with a bluish tint. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a waxy texture, which helps the plant retain moisture. It produces tall flower stalks with yellowish-green flowers, which bloom after several years.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid environments.
