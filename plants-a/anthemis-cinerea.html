Anthemis cinerea

Anthemis cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
