Adiantum amelianum

Adiantum amelianum, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are typically grayish-white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil to flourish. Its fronds are composed of small, overlapping pinnae that create a finely textured, feathery look.

Adiantum amelianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
