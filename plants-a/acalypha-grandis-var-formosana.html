Acalypha grandis var. formosana

Acalypha grandis var. formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan gloryflower, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround small yellow flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
