Astracantha eriocephala

Astracantha eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly aster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which provides protection against herbivores and moisture loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astracantha eriocephala is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
