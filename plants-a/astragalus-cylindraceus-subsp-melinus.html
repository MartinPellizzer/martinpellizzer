Astragalus cylindraceus subsp. melinus

Astragalus cylindraceus subsp.

melinus, commonly known as the cylindrical milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

The plant features slender, erect stems and small, yellowish to greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
