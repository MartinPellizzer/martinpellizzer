Acacia kraussiana subsp. madagascariensis

Acacia kraussiana subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, and its yellow, globular flower heads.

The plant produces long, thin pods that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hair. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Madagascar.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
