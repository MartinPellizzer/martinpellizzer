Anthyllis rustica

Anthyllis rustica, commonly known as the rough anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Anthyllis rustica is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
