Aegiphila arborea

Aegiphila arborea, commonly known as the tree aegiphila, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Aegiphila arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
