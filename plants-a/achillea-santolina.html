Achillea santolina

Achillea santolina, commonly known as Spanish thyme or santolina, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its compact, silver-gray foliage and small, aromatic yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary applications and as a decorative plant in gardens.

Santolina is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract beneficial insects.
