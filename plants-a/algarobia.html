Algarobia

Algarobia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several round, smooth seeds. Algarobia is native to the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural areas.

It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types and environmental conditions.
