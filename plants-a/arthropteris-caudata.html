Arthropteris caudata

Arthropteris caudata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are protected by indusia.

Arthropteris caudata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
