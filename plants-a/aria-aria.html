Aria aria

Aria aria, commonly known as the common arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It has heart-shaped leaves that are typically arrowhead-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Aria aria thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
