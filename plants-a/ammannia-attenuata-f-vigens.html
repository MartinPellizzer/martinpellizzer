Ammannia attenuata f. vigens

Ammannia attenuata f. vigens, commonly known as the vigorous ammannia, is a variant of the ammannia plant characterized by its more robust growth habit.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems that often trail along the ground, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink or red, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
