Asraoa

Asraoa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to blue flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Asraoa is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value lies in its soft, mist-like appearance and extended blooming period.
