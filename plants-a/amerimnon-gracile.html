Amerimnon gracile

Amerimnon gracile, commonly known as the slender American orchid, is a delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
