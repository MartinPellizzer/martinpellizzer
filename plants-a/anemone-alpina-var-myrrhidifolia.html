Anemone alpina var. myrrhidifolia

Anemone alpina var. myrrhidifolia, commonly known as the Alpine Anemone, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its ornamental value.
