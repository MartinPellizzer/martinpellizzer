Andropogon acutispathaceus

Andropogon acutispathaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Andropogon acutispathaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
