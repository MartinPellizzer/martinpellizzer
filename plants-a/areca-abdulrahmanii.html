Areca abdulrahmanii

Areca abdulrahmanii, commonly known as the Abdul Rahman areca, is a rare and unique palm species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, round fruits. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species.
