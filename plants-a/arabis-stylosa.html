Arabis stylosa

Arabis stylosa, commonly known as the clustered rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in clusters, giving it its common name.

Arabis stylosa is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
