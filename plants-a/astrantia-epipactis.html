Astrantia epipactis

Astrantia epipactis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Astrantia epipactis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
