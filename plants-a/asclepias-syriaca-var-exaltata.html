Asclepias syriaca var. exaltata

Asclepias syriaca var. exaltata, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a striking variant of the common milkweed.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range from pink to orange-red in color. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common to the Asclepias genus. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
