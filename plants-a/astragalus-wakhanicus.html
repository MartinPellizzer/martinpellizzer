Astragalus wakhanicus

Astragalus wakhanicus, commonly known as the Wakhan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
