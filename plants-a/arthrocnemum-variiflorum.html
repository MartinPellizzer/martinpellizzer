Arthrocnemum variiflorum

Arthrocnemum variiflorum, commonly known as the variable flower jointweed, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in arid and coastal ecosystems.
