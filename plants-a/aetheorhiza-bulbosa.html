Aetheorhiza bulbosa

Aetheorhiza bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous root orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique bulb-like structures that store nutrients and water, enabling it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators. Its growth is slow and requires a specialized mycorrhizal relationship with fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
