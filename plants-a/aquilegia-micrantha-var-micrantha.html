Aquilegia micrantha var. micrantha

Aquilegia micrantha var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved columbine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petal-like sepals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ecological and ornamental landscapes.
