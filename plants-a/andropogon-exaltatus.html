Andropogon exaltatus

Andropogon exaltatus, commonly known as tall bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon exaltatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
