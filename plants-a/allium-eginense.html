Allium eginense

Allium eginense, commonly known as the Eginian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium eginense is valued for its aromatic properties and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
