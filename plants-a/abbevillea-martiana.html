Abbevillea martiana

Abbevillea martiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf abbevillea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
