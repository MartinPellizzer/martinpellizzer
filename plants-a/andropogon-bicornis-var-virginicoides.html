Andropogon bicornis var. virginicoides

Andropogon bicornis var. virginicoides, commonly known as the tall bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that turn golden in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
