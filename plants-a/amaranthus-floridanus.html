Amaranthus floridanus

Amaranthus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and production of small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
