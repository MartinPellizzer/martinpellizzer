Allium sinaiticum

Allium sinaiticum, commonly known as the Sinai garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces clusters of round, fleshy bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to rocky and sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
