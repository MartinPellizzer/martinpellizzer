Astragalus aksuensis

Astragalus aksuensis, commonly known as the Aksu milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the Xinjiang region of China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to thrive in harsh conditions and support local biodiversity.
