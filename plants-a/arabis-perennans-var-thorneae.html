Arabis perennans var. thorneae

Arabis perennans var. thorneae, commonly known as Thorne's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme, blooming in early spring.
