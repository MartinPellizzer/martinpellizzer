Abronia gracilis subsp. platyphylla

Abronia gracilis subsp.

platyphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
