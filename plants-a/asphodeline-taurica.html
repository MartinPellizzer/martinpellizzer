Asphodeline taurica

Asphodeline taurica, commonly known as the Crimean asphodel, is a perennial plant native to the steppes of southern Russia and Crimea.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Asphodeline taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and visual interest.
