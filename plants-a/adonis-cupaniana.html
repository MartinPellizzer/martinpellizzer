Adonis cupaniana

Adonis cupaniana, commonly known as the cupuanian adonis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Adonis cupaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
