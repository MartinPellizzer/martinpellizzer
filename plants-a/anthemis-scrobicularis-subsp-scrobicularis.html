Anthemis scrobicularis subsp. scrobicularis

Anthemis scrobicularis subsp.

scrobicularis, commonly known as the rough anthemis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are usually rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
