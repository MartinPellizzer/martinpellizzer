Andropogon tremulus

Andropogon tremulus, commonly known as the trembling grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Andropogon tremulus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
