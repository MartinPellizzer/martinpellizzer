Abutilon oxycarpum f. incanum

Abutilon oxycarpum f. incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf abutilon, is a variant of the common abutilon species.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and visual appeal.
