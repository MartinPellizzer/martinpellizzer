Acacia nilotica var. cupressiformis

Acacia nilotica var. cupressiformis, commonly known as the weeping acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its pendulous, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, followed by long, flat pods that contain seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and charcoal.
