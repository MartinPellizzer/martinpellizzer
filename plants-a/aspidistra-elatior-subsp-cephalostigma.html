Aspidistra elatior subsp. cephalostigma

Aspidistra elatior subsp.

cephalostigma, commonly known as the black-leaf aspidistra, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to eastern China. It features dark, glossy, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep, almost blackish-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers with a reddish-brown spathe, which appear in late spring or early summer. It thrives in low-light conditions and prefers moist, well-draining soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in shaded garden settings or as a houseplant.
