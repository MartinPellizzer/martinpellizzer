Acacia trispinosa

Acacia trispinosa, commonly known as the three-spined acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia trispinosa is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
