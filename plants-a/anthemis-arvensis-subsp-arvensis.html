Anthemis arvensis subsp. arvensis

Anthemis arvensis subsp.

arvensis, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that form flat, composite heads.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
