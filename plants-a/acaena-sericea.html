Acaena sericea

Acaena sericea, commonly known as silver burr, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. Acaena sericea thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests, shrublands, and coastal areas.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
