Astragalus racemosus

Astragalus racemosus, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus racemosus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
