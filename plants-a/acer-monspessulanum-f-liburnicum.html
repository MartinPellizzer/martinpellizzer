Acer monspessulanum f. liburnicum

Acer monspessulanum f. liburnicum, commonly known as the Liburnian maple, is a rare and unique variant of the sycamore maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that often display a striking combination of green and red hues. This form is native to the Adriatic region and is typically found in coastal areas with a humid climate. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, with its vibrant foliage making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

However, it is relatively uncommon in cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
