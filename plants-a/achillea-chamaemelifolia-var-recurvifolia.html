Achillea chamaemelifolia var. recurvifolia

Achillea chamaemelifolia var. recurvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or pale yellow, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
