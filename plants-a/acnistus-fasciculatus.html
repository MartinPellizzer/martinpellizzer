Acnistus fasciculatus

Acnistus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in the spring. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
