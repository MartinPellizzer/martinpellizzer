Acrocarpus densifolius

Acrocarpus densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved acrocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
