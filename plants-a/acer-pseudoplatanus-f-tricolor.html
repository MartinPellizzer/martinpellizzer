Acer pseudoplatanus f. tricolor

Acer pseudoplatanus f. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color maple, is a variant of the common maple tree.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays three distinct colors—typically green, yellow, and red—on the same leaf. This variation is due to a genetic mutation that affects chlorophyll production. The plant is native to the British Isles and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
