Alchemilla minor var. hirsuta

Alchemilla minor var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy alchemilla, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early summer, typically arranged in dense clusters atop slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various garden settings.
