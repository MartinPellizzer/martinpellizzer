Amphipogon pentacraspedon

Amphipogon pentacraspedon, commonly known as the five-spurred orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which features five spurred petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from white to pink, and are adapted for pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
