Aquilegia canadensis f. flaviflora

Aquilegia canadensis f. flaviflora, commonly known as the Canada columbine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
