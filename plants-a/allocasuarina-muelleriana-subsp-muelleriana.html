Allocasuarina muelleriana subsp. muelleriana

Allocasuarina muelleriana subsp.

muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's sheoak, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its unique, needle-like phyllodes that are arranged in spirals along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
