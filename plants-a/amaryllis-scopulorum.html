Amaryllis scopulorum

Amaryllis scopulorum, commonly known as the rock amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Amaryllis scopulorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
