Albizia perrieri

Albizia perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Albizia perrieri is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native region.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ecological research.
