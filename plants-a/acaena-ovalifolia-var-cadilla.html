Acaena ovalifolia var. cadilla

Acaena ovalifolia var. cadilla, commonly known as the Cadilla burr bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's unique feature is its fruiting bodies, which are spherical, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. These capsules are often referred to as "burs" and are adapted for dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
