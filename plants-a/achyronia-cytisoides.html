Achyronia cytisoides

Achyronia cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
