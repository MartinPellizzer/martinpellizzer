Arrabidaea cinerea

Arrabidaea cinerea, commonly known as the pale bract euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its gray-green, fleshy leaves and branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, papery bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
