Adeneleuterophora ligularis

Adeneleuterophora ligularis, commonly known as the ligular leaf fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ligulate (strap-like) leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are often covered in small, scale-like structures that aid in nutrient absorption.

The reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on the underside of the leaves, contributing to its propagation in moist, shaded habitats.
