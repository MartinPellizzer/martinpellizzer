Actinidia arguta f. platyphylla

Actinidia arguta f. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in herbal medicine.
