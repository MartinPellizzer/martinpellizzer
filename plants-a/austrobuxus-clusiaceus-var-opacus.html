Austrobuxus clusiaceus var. opacus

Austrobuxus clusiaceus var. opacus, commonly known as the opacous boxwood, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse morphology and ecological adaptability. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that provide a glossy appearance. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in the understory of tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable component of forest ecosystems, offering habitat and resources to various wildlife species.
