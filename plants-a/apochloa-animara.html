Apochloa animara

Apochloa animara, commonly known as the African grass or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves. Its yellow flowers are clustered in a composite head, often surrounded by pale green bracts.

Apochloa animara is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
