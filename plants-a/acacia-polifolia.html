Acacia polifolia

Acacia polifolia, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, flat leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It is often used in coastal dune stabilization due to its strong root system.

Acacia polifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sandy soils.
