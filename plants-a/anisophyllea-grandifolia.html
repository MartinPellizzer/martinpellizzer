Anisophyllea grandifolia

Anisophyllea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf anisophyllea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anisophylleaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical or ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by globose, woody fruits containing several seeds. Anisophyllea grandifolia is typically found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental leaves and potential medicinal properties.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative element in horticulture.
