Artemisia japonica var. purandharensis

Artemisia japonica var. purandharensis, commonly known as the Himalayan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, camphor-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
