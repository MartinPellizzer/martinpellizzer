Acaena closiana

Acaena closiana, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds with tufts of white filaments, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and coastal areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
