Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp. chamissonianum

Aconitum delphiniifolium subsp.

chamissonianum, commonly known as the Chamisson's monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
