Alpinia scabra

Alpinia scabra, commonly known as the rough ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by hairy, three-lobed berries. Alpinia scabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
