Agrostis canina f. elatior

Agrostis canina f. elatior, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
