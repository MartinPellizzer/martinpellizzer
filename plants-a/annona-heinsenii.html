Annona heinsenii

Annona heinsenii, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are fragrant, white, and have five petals, typically appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a rough, green, egg-shaped berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a white, creamy pulp with numerous seeds.
