Achyranthes geminata

Achyranthes geminata, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on the upper part of the plant. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
