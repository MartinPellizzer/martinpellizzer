Amaracarpus muscifer

Amaracarpus muscifer, commonly known as the muskwood plant, is a member of the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is notable for its distinctive, fragrant wood, which emits a strong musk-like scent when cut. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Amaracarpus muscifer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance, making it a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
