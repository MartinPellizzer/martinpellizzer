Agrianthus leutzelburgii

Agrianthus leutzelburgii, commonly known as the Cape rose or red agapanthus, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are evergreen and often form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Agrianthus leutzelburgii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
