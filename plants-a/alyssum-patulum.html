Alyssum patulum

Alyssum patulum, commonly known as the creeping alyssum, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and a mat-forming growth habit, making it ideal for ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum patulum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
