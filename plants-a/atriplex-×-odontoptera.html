Atriplex × odontoptera

Atriplex × odontoptera, commonly known as the saltbush hybrid, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a natural hybrid between Atriplex hymnocrassifolia and Atriplex aptera, characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves. The plant typically grows in saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Atriplex × odontoptera is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
