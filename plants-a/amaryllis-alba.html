Amaryllis alba

Amaryllis alba, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are broad and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Amaryllis alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
