Aria Subdanubialis

Aria subdanubialis is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the banks of the Danube River. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its small, pale blue flowers bloom in late spring, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat. The plant is known for its resilience to flooding and its ability to stabilize riverbanks. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this species from habitat loss and environmental changes.
