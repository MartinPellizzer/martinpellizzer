Arum maculatum subsp. pyrenaeum

Arum maculatum subsp.

pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It features a distinctive purple-spotted leaf and a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in spring, and it is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
