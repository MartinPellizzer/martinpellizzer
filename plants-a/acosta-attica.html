Acosta attica

Acosta attica, commonly known as the sacred plant of the Andean people, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers and dark green, waxy leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional Andean rituals. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and the ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

Acosta attica is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine practices.
