Acacia pteridifolia

Acacia pteridifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaf acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Acacia pteridifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
