Allium kiiense

Allium kiiense, commonly known as the Kii onion, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and strap-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local Japanese cuisine.

Allium kiiense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary contributions.
