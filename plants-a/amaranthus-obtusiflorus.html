Amaranthus obtusiflorus

Amaranthus obtusiflorus, commonly known as the broadflower amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy annual in many regions.

Amaranthus obtusiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown as a decorative plant in gardens.
