Acmena grata

Acmena grata, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Acmena grata is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
