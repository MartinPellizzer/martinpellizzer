Arctophila similis

Arctophila similis, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
