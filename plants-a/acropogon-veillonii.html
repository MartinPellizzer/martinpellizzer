Acropogon veillonii

Acropogon veillonii, commonly known as the veillonii grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Acropogon veillonii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is used in some regions for forage or traditional purposes.
