Aspidistra minutiflora

Aspidistra minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf aspidistra, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shady, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
