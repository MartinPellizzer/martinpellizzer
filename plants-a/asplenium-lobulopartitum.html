Asplenium lobulopartitum

Asplenium lobulopartitum, commonly known as the lobed shield fern, is a member of the family Aspleniaceae and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive lobed structure, giving it its common name. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of ferns.

Asplenium lobulopartitum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
