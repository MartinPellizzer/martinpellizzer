Aristida libyca

Aristida libyca, commonly known as the Libyan threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of multiple bract-like structures. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it often forms dense mats that stabilize soil in harsh environments.

Aristida libyca is valued for its ecological role in preventing erosion and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
