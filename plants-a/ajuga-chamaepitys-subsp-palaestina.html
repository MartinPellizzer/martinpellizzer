Ajuga chamaepitys subsp. palaestina

Ajuga chamaepitys subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as Palestinian bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ground cover in shaded landscapes.
