Adenophora capillaris subsp. leptosepala

Adenophora capillaris subsp.

leptosepala, commonly known as the slender-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to violet in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
