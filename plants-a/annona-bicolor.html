Annona bicolor

Annona bicolor, commonly known as the white sapote, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow when ripe. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces a sweet, creamy fruit with a texture similar to a pear.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit in home gardens.
