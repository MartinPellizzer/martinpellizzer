Acacia disparrima

Acacia disparrima, commonly known as the narrowleaf wattle, is a small shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia disparrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
