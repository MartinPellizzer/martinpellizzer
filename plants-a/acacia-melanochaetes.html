Acacia melanochaetes

Acacia melanochaetes, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and compound leaves with small, needle-like phyllodes. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, followed by flat, woody pods. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia melanochaetes is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has historical significance in Australian ecosystems.
