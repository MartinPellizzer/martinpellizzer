Alternanthera brasiliana var. strigosa

Alternanthera brasiliana var. strigosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep red to dark purple. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a dense, hairy texture that gives it a unique appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and containers.
