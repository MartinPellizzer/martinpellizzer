Allophylus litoralis

Allophylus litoralis, commonly known as the coastal alpine, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Allophylus litoralis is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dune environments.
