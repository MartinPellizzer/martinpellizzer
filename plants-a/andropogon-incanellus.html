Andropogon incanellus

Andropogon incanellus, commonly known as hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that are covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Andropogon incanellus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
