Achnatherum ordubadense

Achnatherum ordubadense, commonly known as the Ordubad needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Achnatherum ordubadense plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant flora.
