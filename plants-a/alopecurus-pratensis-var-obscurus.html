Alopecurus pratensis var. obscurus

Alopecurus pratensis var. obscurus, commonly known as dark meadow foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained to moderately wet soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with brown or purple hues. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize open areas.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
