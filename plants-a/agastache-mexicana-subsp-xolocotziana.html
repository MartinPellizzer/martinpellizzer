Agastache mexicana subsp. xolocotziana

Agastache mexicana subsp.

xolocotziana, commonly known as the Xolocotziana hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of central Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its foliage has a strong mint-like scent, which is a common trait among members of its family.
