Arthraxon hispidus subsp. langsdorffii

Arthraxon hispidus subsp.

langsdorffii, commonly known as the hairy arthraxon, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
