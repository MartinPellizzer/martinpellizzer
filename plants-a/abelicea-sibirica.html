Abelicea sibirica

Abelicea sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and tundra environments.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
