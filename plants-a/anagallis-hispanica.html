Anagallis hispanica

Anagallis hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish eyebright, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive dark eye-like spot at the center. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
