Anisophyllum chamissonis

Anisophyllum chamissonis, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in brackish or freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are often inflated and resemble bladders, which help it stay afloat. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is adapted to coastal areas and can tolerate fluctuating salinity levels.

Anisophyllum chamissonis plays a role in stabilizing water surfaces and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
