Alternanthera filifolia subsp. typica

Alternanthera filifolia subsp.

typica, commonly known as the threadleaf Alternanthera, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often a striking red or purple color, especially under bright light.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
