Acacia biaciculata

Acacia biaciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves composed of numerous thread-like leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia biaciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
