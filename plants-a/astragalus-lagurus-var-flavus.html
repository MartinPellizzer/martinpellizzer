Astragalus lagurus var. flavus

Astragalus lagurus var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
