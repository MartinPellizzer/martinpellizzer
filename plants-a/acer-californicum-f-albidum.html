Acer californicum f. albidum

Acer californicum f. albidum, commonly known as the white-leaf maple, is a variant of the California maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale green leaves, which contrast with the typical green foliage of the species. This plant is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas.

The tree has a rounded canopy and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a notable feature in its native habitat.
