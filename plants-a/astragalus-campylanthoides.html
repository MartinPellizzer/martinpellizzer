Astragalus campylanthoides

Astragalus campylanthoides, commonly known as the bent-flower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, rounded seeds.

Astragalus campylanthoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
