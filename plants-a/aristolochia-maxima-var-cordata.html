Aristolochia maxima var. cordata

Aristolochia maxima var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a gelatinous coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
