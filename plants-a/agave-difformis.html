Agave difformis

Agave difformis, commonly known as the twisted agave, is a striking succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of long, narrow, blue-green leaves that curve dramatically, creating a unique and eye-catching appearance. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its architectural form and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
