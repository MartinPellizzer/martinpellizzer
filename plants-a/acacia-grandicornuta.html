Acacia grandicornuta

Acacia grandicornuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in globular heads. Acacia grandicornuta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
