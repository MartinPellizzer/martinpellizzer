Aspidium nemophilum

Aspidium nemophilum, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble the leaves of the common sorrel plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its fronds are usually biennial, with the sporangia developing on the underside of the leaves.

Aspidium nemophilum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
