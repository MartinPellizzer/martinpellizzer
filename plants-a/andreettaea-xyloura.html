Andreettaea xyloura

Andreettaea xyloura, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants such as cotton and hibiscus.
