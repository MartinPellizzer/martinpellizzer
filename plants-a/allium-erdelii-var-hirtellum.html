Allium erdelii var. hirtellum

Allium erdelii var. hirtellum, commonly known as the Hirtell's garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are typically small and have a pungent, garlicky aroma, though they are not widely cultivated for consumption.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
