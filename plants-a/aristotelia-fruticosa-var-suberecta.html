Aristotelia fruticosa var. suberecta

Aristotelia fruticosa var. suberecta, commonly known as the silver vine, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are a food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
