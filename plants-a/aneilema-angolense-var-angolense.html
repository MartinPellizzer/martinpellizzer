Aneilema angolense var. angolense

Aneilema angolense var. angolense, commonly known as the Angola arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, branched stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
