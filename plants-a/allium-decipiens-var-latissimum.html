Allium decipiens var. latissimum

Allium decipiens var. latissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used in culinary applications, similar to leeks or garlic, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
