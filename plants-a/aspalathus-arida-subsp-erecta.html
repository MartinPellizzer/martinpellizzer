Aspalathus arida subsp. erecta

Aspalathus arida subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bushveld protea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
