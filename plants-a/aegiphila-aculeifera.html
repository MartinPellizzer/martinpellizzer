Aegiphila aculeifera

Aegiphila aculeifera, commonly known as the spiny aegiphila, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its stems are woody and may bear small spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aegiphila aculeifera is adapted to coastal and limestone soils, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical environments.
