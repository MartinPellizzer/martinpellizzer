Acacia triptycha var. pungens

Acacia triptycha var. pungens, commonly known as the spiked wattle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves composed of three leaflets, which are usually narrow and slightly serrated. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds and insects.
