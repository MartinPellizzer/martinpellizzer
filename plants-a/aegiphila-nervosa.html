Aegiphila nervosa

Aegiphila nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined aegiphila, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and clusters of tubular, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Aegiphila nervosa is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Morphology

Aegiphila nervosa has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The stem is slender and upright, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that remains relatively unbranched. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, featuring pinkish-white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in the spring and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a dry, capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, anchoring the plant in rocky or sandy soils.
