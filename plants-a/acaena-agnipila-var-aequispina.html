Acaena agnipila var. aequispina

Acaena agnipila var. aequispina, commonly known as the New Zealand bristled burr, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, woody husk. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

The species is known for its ability to form dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.
