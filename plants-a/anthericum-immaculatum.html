Anthericum immaculatum

Anthericum immaculatum, commonly known as the white adder's tongue, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. The plant features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Anthericum immaculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
