Argyreia nellygherya

Argyreia nellygherya, commonly known as the sacred lotus of India, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens or ponds.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also hold cultural and religious significance in various traditions.
