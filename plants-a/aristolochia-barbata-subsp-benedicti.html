Aristolochia barbata subsp. benedicti

Aristolochia barbata subsp.

benedicti, commonly known as the Benedict's Dutchman's pipe, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family. It is characterized by its unique, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinctive hooded shape.

The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia.

The species is known for its complex floral structure and is often found in moist, wooded habitats.
