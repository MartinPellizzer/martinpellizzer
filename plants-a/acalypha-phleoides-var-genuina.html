Acalypha phleoides var. genuina

Acalypha phleoides var. genuina, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features distinctive leaves with striking red veins against a dark green background, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its growth habit is usually upright, with a somewhat bushy appearance, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Acalypha phleoides var. genuina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
