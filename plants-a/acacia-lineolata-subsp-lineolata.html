Acacia lineolata subsp. lineolata

Acacia lineolata subsp.

lineolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, linear leaflets.

The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Acacia lineolata subsp.

lineolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
