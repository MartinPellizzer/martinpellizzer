Alstroemeria angustifolia subsp. velutina

Alstroemeria angustifolia subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf alstroemeria, is a perennial herb native to South America. It features narrow, elongated leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
