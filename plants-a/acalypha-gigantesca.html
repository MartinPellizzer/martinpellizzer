Acalypha gigantesca

Acalypha gigantesca, commonly known as the giant spurflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with bright red bracts that attract birds and insects. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
