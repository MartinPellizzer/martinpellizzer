Artemisia maccallae

Artemisia maccallae, commonly known as the Maccall's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. Artemisia maccallae thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
