Agave guadarramae

Agave guadarramae, commonly known as the Guadarrama agave, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white marginal teeth. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, white flowers, which are followed by spiky, blue-green fruits.

Agave guadarramae is adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
