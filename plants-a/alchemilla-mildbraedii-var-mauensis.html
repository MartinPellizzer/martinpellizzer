Alchemilla mildbraedii var. mauensis

Alchemilla mildbraedii var. mauensis, commonly known as the purple alchemilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge, especially on the underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
