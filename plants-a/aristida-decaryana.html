Aristida decaryana

Aristida decaryana, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences, which give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Aristida decaryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
