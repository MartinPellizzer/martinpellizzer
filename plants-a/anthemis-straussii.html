Anthemis straussii

Anthemis straussii, commonly known as the Strauss's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
