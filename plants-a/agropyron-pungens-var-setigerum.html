Agropyron pungens var. setigerum

Agropyron pungens var. setigerum, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear short, bristly hairs. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture. It produces spikelet-like inflorescences with awned florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
