Acacia angustissima subsp. smithii

Acacia angustissima subsp.

smithii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Acacia angustissima subsp.

smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
