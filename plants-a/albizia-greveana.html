Albizia greveana

Albizia greveana, commonly known as the African silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often feathery in appearance, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or white. Albizia greveana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
