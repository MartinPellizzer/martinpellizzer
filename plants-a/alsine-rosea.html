Alsine rosea

Alsine rosea, commonly known as the pink sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Alsine rosea is adapted to thrive in harsh, low-nutrient conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
