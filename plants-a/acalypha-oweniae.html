Acalypha oweniae

Acalypha oweniae, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Hawaiian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Acalypha oweniae is an important plant in Hawaiian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
