Artemisia campestris var. laxa

Artemisia campestris var. laxa, commonly known as looseleaf tarragon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, silvery-green leaves that are finely divided and have a distinctive, slightly sharp scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in herbal remedies.
