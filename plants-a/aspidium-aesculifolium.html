Aspidium aesculifolium

Aspidium aesculifolium, commonly known as the horse's tail fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and dense, dark green fronds that resemble a horse's tail. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped pinnae that are tightly clustered.

Aspidium aesculifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9.
