Arenaria congesta var. parishiorum

Arenaria congesta var. parishiorum, commonly known as the Parry's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, arid environments and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms.
