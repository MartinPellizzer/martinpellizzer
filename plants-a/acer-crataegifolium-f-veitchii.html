Acer crataegifolium f. veitchii

Acer crataegifolium f. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's maple, is a unique variant of the crataegifolium maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble the leaves of hawthorn, giving it its specific epithet. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly its vibrant fall coloration. It typically thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and specific horticultural requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
