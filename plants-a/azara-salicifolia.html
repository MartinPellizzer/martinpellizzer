Azara salicifolia

Azara salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf azara, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
