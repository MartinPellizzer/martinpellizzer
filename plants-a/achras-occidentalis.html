Achras occidentalis

Achras occidentalis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and a smooth, pale green trunk. The tree produces a fleshy, spiny fruit that contains a starchy, edible pulp and a central core of seeds. The plant is well adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and human agriculture, particularly in Pacific island cultures.
