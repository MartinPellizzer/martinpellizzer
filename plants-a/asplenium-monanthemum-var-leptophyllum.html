Asplenium monanthemum var. leptophyllum

Asplenium monanthemum var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
