Aster albescens var. limprichtii

Aster albescens var. limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's white aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

This variant is noted for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
