Aconitum × vulgare var. pubescens

Aconitum × vulgare var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking blue to purple hooded flowers that resemble a monk's habit, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, downy pubescence, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
