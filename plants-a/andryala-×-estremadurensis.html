Andryala × estremadurensis

Andryala × estremadurensis, commonly known as the Estremaduran andryala, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Andryala nemorosa and Andryala fragilis.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly in Spain. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Andryala × estremadurensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
