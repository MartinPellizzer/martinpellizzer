Aretia palinuri

Aretia palinuri, commonly known as the Pacific lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable climates.

Aretia palinuri is also noted for its resilience to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
