Allium mishtshenkoanum

Allium mishtshenkoanum, commonly known as the Mishtshenko onion, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence bearing small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs that are similar in taste to garlic and shallots. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is gaining popularity among gardeners and food enthusiasts.
