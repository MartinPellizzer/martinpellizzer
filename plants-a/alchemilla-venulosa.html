Alchemilla venulosa

Alchemilla venulosa, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Alchemilla venulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
