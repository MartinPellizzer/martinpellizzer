Acilepis peninsularis var. peninsularis

Acilepis peninsularis var. peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular acilepis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
