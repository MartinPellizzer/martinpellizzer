Anemone subpinnata

Anemone subpinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anemone, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink petals and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and blooms in early spring. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Anemone subpinnata is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
