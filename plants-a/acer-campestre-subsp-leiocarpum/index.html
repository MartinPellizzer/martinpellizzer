Acer Campestre Subsp. Leiocarpum

Acer campestre subsp. leiocarpum is a lesser-known variant of the common horse chestnut. It is native to parts of eastern europe and western asia. this subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, light-colored bark and smaller, more rounded leaves. it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas. due to its unique characteristics, it is gaining interest among botanists and gardeners alike.
