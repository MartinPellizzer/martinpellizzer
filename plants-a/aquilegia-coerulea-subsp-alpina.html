Aquilegia coerulea subsp. alpina

Aquilegia coerulea subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes at high elevations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
