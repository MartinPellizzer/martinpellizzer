Asplenium imbricatum

Asplenium imbricatum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or crevice environments. The fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a leathery texture and a deep green color. It produces spores in clusters on the underside of its fronds, which are typically found in moist, shaded areas.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate low light conditions.
