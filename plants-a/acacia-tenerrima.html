Acacia tenerrima

Acacia tenerrima, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which consists of small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia tenerrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
