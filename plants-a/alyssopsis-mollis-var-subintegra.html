Alyssopsis mollis var. subintegra

Alyssopsis mollis var. subintegra, commonly known as the softleaf alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
