Anathallis angulosa

Anathallis angulosa, commonly known as the angular anathallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

Anathallis angulosa is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
