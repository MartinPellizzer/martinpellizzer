Adesmia atacamensis

Adesmia atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its desert habitat. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, a feature that helps minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Adesmia atacamensis is adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in the Atacama Desert.
