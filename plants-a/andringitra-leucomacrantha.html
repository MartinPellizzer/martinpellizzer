Andringitra leucomacrantha

Andringitra leucomacrantha, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals with contrasting green veins. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
