Aconitum soongaricum var. jeholense

Aconitum soongaricum var. jeholense, commonly known as the Jehol aconite, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces large, complex flowers with numerous stamens and a central pistil, typical of the genus Aconitum. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
