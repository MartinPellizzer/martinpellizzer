Amylocarpus formosus

Amylocarpus formosus, commonly known as the Taiwan arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in starch. The plant features a short, stocky stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The tubers are used as a starchy food source and are also valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
