Aegilops triuncialis var. glabrispica

Aegilops triuncialis var. glabrispica, commonly known as the smooth awned wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This plant is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential genetic contributions to wheat improvement.
