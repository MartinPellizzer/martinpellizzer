Abutilon terminale

Abutilon terminale, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of lantern-shaped, orange-red bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Abutilon terminale is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
