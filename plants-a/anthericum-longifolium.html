Anthericum longifolium

Anthericum longifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the spadix, with the spathe serving as a colorful, showy bract. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

Anthericum longifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
