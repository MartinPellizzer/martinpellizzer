Acacia ehrenbergii

Acacia ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert thorn acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned branches and small, inconspicuous green or yellowish flowers. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various animals. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

Its leaves are typically small and feathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.
