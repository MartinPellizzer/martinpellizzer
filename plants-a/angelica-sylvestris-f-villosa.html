Angelica sylvestris f. villosa

Angelica sylvestris f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy wild angelica, is a variant of the wild angelica characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its large, umbrella-shaped leaves and tall, spire-like flower clusters are typical of the Angelica genus.

The roots are often harvested for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
