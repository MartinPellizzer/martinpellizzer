Acacia gracillima

Acacia gracillima, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching form and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its small, woody pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Acacia gracillima is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.
