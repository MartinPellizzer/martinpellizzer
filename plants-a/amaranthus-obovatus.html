Amaranthus obovatus

Amaranthus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf amaranth, is a species of flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by triangular, three-angled seed capsules.

Amaranthus obovatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional cuisines in some regions.
