Achillea × similis

Achillea × similis, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and flat, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a range of colors from white to pink and red.

Achillea × similis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
