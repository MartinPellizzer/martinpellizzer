Argemone versicolor

Argemone versicolor, commonly known as the variable argemone, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Argemone versicolor is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
