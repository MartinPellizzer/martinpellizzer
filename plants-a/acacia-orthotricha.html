Acacia orthotricha

Acacia orthotricha, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The wood of Acacia orthotricha is hard and durable, making it useful for timber and fuel.
