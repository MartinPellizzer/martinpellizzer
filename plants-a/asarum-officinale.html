Asarum officinale

Asarum officinale, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-brown leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, dull green flowers that are clustered in a spike, often hidden beneath its foliage. Its roots are aromatic and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Asarum officinale thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
