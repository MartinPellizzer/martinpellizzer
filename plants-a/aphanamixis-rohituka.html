Aphanamixis rohituka

Aphanamixis rohituka, commonly known as the Khejri tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree is well-adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable species for afforestation and soil conservation.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine, and the plant plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
