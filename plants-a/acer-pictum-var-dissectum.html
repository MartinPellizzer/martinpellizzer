Acer pictum var. dissectum

Acer pictum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely cut, feathery leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
