Adiantum princeps

Adiantum princeps, commonly known as the royal maidenhair fern, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a delicate, evergreen fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive blackish midrib, giving it a striking visual appearance. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Adiantum princeps is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
