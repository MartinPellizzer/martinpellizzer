Acacia paniculiflora

Acacia paniculiflora, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, pendulous clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to sandy soils and saline environments, making it a hardy species.

Acacia paniculiflora plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
