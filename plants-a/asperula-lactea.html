Asperula lactea

Asperula lactea, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and can be toxic if ingested. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Asperula lactea is typically found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
