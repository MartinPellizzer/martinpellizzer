Aphelandra sericantha

Aphelandra sericantha, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated, waxy leaves that are often adorned with a golden or yellowish sheen. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens in warm climates.
