Aceranthus triphyllus

Aceranthus triphyllus, commonly known as the three-leafed acanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to white. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
