Aconitum vulparia f. myoctonum

Aconitum vulparia f. myoctonum, commonly known as the dwarf white aconite, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid ingestion.
