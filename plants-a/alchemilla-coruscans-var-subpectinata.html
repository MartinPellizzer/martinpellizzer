Alchemilla coruscans var. subpectinata

Alchemilla coruscans var. subpectinata, commonly known as the silverweed or lesser silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

Alchemilla coruscans var. subpectinata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
