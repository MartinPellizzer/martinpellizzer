Aristida adscensionis subvar. condensata

Aristida adscensionis subvar. condensata, commonly known as the dense bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically tan to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
