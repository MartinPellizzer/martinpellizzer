Acer × creticum var. macrophyllum

Acer × creticum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the big-leaf maple, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, providing a striking visual display in the autumn. The tree typically grows to a height of 40–60 feet and has a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant seasonal color.
