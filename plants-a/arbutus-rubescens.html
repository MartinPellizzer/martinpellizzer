Arbutus rubescens

Arbutus rubescens, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dark green, leathery leaves that turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, urn-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests and chaparral habitats.

Arbutus rubescens is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native range.
