Adenophora potaninii var. bockiana

Adenophora potaninii var. bockiana, commonly known as the Bock's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.
