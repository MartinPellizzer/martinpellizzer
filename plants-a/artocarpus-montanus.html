Artocarpus montanus

Artocarpus montanus, commonly known as the mountain breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces a unique fruit with a fibrous outer husk and a starchy, edible interior. Its fruit is often used in traditional cuisines and has cultural significance in various regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
