Anaxagorea manausensis

Anaxagorea manausensis, commonly known as the Manaus tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of the rainforest.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
