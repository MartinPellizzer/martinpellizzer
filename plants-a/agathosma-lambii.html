Agathosma lambii

Agathosma lambii, commonly known as the Cape mountain thyme, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils are often used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine due to their therapeutic properties.
