Aristolochia subsagittata

Aristolochia subsagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, serpentine-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical compounds, some of which have medicinal uses.
