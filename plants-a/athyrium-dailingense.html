Athyrium dailingense

Athyrium dailingense, commonly known as the Dailing fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its leaves, typical of ferns.

Athyrium dailingense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
