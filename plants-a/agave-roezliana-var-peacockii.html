Agave roezliana var. peacockii

Agave roezliana var. peacockii, commonly known as the peacock agave, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The leaves are adorned with prominent white and blue markings, resembling the feathers of a peacock, hence its common name. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of yellow flowers in the spring.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
