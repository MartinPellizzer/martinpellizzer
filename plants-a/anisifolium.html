Anisifolium

Anisifolium, commonly known as narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are narrow and have a pointed tip, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Anisifolium is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ability to thrive in various soil conditions makes it a common weed in agricultural fields.
