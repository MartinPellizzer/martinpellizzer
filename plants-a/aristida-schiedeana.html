Aristida schiedeana

Aristida schiedeana, commonly known as Schiede's threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous bristly spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is adapted to hot, dry climates with low rainfall. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage grass in dryland agriculture.
