Astragalus sibthorpianus

Astragalus sibthorpianus, commonly known as the Sibthorp's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, enriching the soil in harsh environments.
