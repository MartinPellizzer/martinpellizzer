Alsophila lamprocaulos

Alsophila lamprocaulos, commonly known as the silver-stemmed tree fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dense layer of silvery, scale-like indumentum. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
