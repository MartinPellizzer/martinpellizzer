Archidendron chrysocarpum

Archidendron chrysocarpum, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain edible seeds enclosed in a hard, yellowish shell. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
