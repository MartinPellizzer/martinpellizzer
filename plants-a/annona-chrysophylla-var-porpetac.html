Annona chrysophylla var. porpetac

Annona chrysophylla var. porpetac, commonly known as the golden leaf annona, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking golden-yellow coloration, especially under sunlight. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
