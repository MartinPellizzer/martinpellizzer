Arenaria longifolia var. grandiflora

Arenaria longifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal areas.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental species valued in horticulture.
