Adesmia muricata var. dentata

Adesmia muricata var. dentata, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that matures to a reddish-brown color, containing several elliptical seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in temperate climates.
