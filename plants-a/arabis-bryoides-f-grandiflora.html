Arabis bryoides f. grandiflora

Arabis bryoides f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and features small, basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or arctic regions.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cold climates.
