Acacia guanacastensis

Acacia guanacastensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste acacia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its bark and wood are used in traditional medicine and for construction due to their durability.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
