Astragalus krasnovii

Astragalus krasnovii, commonly known as Krasnov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
