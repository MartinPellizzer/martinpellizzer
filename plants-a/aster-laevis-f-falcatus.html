Aster laevis f. falcatus

Aster laevis f. falcatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of purple and blue, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

This perennial plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
