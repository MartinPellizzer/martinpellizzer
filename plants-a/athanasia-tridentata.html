Athanasia tridentata

Athanasia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cosmos, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Athanasia tridentata is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
