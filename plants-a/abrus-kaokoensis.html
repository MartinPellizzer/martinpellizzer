Abrus kaokoensis

Abrus kaokoensis, commonly known as the Kaoko velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds covered in a silky, reddish-brown coating. These seeds are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional practices.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves and a preference for well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
