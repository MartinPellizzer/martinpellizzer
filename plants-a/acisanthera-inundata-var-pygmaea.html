Acisanthera inundata var. pygmaea

Acisanthera inundata var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf water hibiscus, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and wetland landscapes.
