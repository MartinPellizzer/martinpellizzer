Alepidea ciliaris var. caffrorum

Alepidea ciliaris var. caffrorum, commonly known as the Cape mountain lily, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a tall stem bearing several pale blue to purple flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
