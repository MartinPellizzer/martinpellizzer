Astragalus apiculatus

Astragalus apiculatus, commonly known as the sharp-crested milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus apiculatus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
