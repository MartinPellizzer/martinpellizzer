Adenophora wawreana var. lanceifolia

Adenophora wawreana var. lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands.

This variant is distinguished by its narrower leaf shape compared to other varieties of Adenophora.
