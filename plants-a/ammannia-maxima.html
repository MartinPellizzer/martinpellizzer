Ammannia maxima

Ammannia maxima, commonly known as the redroot ammannia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ammannia maxima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.
