Aristida condylifolia

Aristida condylifolia, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray sheen, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Aristida condylifolia is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance.
