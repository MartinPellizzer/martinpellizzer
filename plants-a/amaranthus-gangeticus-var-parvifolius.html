Amaranthus gangeticus var. parvifolius

Amaranthus gangeticus var. parvifolius, commonly known as red amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on the stage of development. Its seeds are small and oval, with a slightly textured surface, and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content, making it a versatile plant in both gardens and traditional agriculture.
