Achillea neilreichii

Achillea neilreichii, commonly known as Neilreich's yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
