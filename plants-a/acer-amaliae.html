Acer amaliae

Acer amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia maple, is a species of maple native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features palmately lobed leaves with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge in spring.

The fruit is a samara, which aids in the dispersal of its seeds.
