Amaracarpus grandifolius var. grandifolius

Amaracarpus grandifolius var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf amaracarpus, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are dry capsules containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
