Astragalus tugarinovii

Astragalus tugarinovii, commonly known as Tugarinov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
