Aster ericoides var. randii

Aster ericoides var. randii, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
