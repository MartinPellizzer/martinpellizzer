Amygdalopsis

Amygdalopsis, commonly known as the "false almond plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a legume pod containing seeds that resemble almonds, though they are not edible.

Amygdalopsis has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
