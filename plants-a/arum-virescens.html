Arum virescens

Arum virescens, commonly known as the green arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and striking spadix inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or forest understories.

Arum virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
