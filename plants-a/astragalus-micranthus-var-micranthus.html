Astragalus micranthus var. micranthus

Astragalus micranthus var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus micranthus var. micranthus is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
