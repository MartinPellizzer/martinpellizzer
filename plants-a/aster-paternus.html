Aster paternus

Aster paternus, commonly known as the common aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster paternus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
