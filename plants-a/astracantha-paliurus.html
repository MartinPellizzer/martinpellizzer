Astracantha paliurus

Astracantha paliurus, commonly known as the hairy thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its woody stems and spiny branches. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, hairy fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
