Albuca

Albuca, commonly known as the star lily or false onion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asparagaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their strap-shaped leaves and showy, star-like flowers. Albuca species typically grow in sandy or rocky soils and prefer full sun to partial shade. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.
