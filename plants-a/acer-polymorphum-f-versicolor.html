Acer polymorphum f. versicolor

Acer polymorphum f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable maple, is a unique variant of the polychrome maple.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays a range of colors from green to red and purple throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a serrated margin, and can vary in shape and size. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
