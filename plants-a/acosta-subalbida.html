Acosta subalbida

Acosta subalbida, commonly known as the white-flowered acosta, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are white to pale yellow, with prominent central disc florets surrounded by ray florets.

Acosta subalbida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
