Agropyron glaucum subsp. barbulatum

Agropyron glaucum subsp.

barbulatum, commonly known as the blue wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, bluish-green leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid landscapes.
