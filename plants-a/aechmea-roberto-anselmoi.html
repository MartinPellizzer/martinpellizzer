Aechmea roberto-anselmoi

Aechmea roberto-anselmoi, commonly known as the Roberto-Anselmo bromeliad, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a striking epiphytic plant characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and often display intricate patterns. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.

Morphology

Aechmea roberto-anselmoi has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, sword-like, and arranged in a tight spiral, with a dark green color and a waxy texture.

The plant has a short, compact stem with a smooth, pale green bark that grows close to the ground. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bracts, producing small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom during the dry season and are pollinated by insects. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense mat around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb nutrients.
