Arundinaria armata

Arundinaria armata, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height, with long, narrow leaves arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it relies on seed dispersal for propagation.

Arundinaria armata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats, making it a valuable component of native ecosystems.
