Amphinomia lupinifolia var. villosa

Amphinomia lupinifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant features compound leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its varietal name "villosa.". It produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
