Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp. bakeri

Arctostaphylos stanfordiana subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as Stanford manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
