Alopecurus vaginatus var. dasyanthus

Alopecurus vaginatus var. dasyanthus, commonly known as the hairy goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
