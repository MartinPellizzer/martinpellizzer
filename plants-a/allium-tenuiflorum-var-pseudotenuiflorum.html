Allium tenuiflorum var. pseudotenuiflorum

Allium tenuiflorum var. pseudotenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic chives, is a variant of the garlic chive plant.

It is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it popular in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardens for its attractive foliage and easy maintenance.
