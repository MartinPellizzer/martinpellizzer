Acer amoenum f. matsumurae

Acer amoenum f. matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura's maple, is a variant of the Amur maple.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges and a pointed tip. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticulture.
