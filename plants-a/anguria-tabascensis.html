Anguria tabascensis

Anguria tabascensis, commonly known as the Tabasco cucumber, is a unique species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit that resembles a cucumber but has a distinct, spicy flavor. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized botanical species.
