Anisostachya rivalis

Anisostachya rivalis, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with fine, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, whitish to purplish flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and as a landscape plant in arid regions.
