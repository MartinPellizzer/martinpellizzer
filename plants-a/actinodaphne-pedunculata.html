Actinodaphne pedunculata

Actinodaphne pedunculata, commonly known as the fragrant sandalwood tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its fragrant wood, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
