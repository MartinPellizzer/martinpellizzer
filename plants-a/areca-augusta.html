Areca augusta

Areca augusta, commonly known as the golden areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into oval, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while the palm is also valued for its ornamental beauty in landscaping.

Areca augusta thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
