Achimenes alba

Achimenes alba, commonly known as the white flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. Achimenes alba thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.
