Avena varia

Avena varia, commonly known as variable oat, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from narrow to broad depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with both spikelets and racemes, which are often bracted.

Avena varia is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
