Agrimonia eupatoria var. hirsuta

Agrimonia eupatoria var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by red, velvety seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs as well.

Hairy agrimony is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
