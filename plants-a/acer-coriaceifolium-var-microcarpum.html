Acer coriaceifolium var. microcarpum

Acer coriaceifolium var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf maple, is a rare and compact variant of the common maple.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces tiny, red-orange samaras, which are its characteristic seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable ornamental and conservation species.
