Andreettaea claviculata

Andreettaea claviculata, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its use in horticulture, though it remains relatively less common in global gardens.
