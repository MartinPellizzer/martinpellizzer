Anthericum filiforme

Anthericum filiforme, commonly known as the slender twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and reduced, non-showy flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its lack of distinct petals, with the flowers being predominantly green and inconspicuous.
