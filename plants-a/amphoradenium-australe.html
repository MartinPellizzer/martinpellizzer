Amphoradenium australe

Amphoradenium australe, commonly known as the Australian bottle tree, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, swollen stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are small and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

The species is well-suited for cultivation in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements and resilience to harsh climates.
