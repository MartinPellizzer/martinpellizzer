Achras zapota var. lobata

Achras zapota var. lobata, commonly known as the African pear or cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and smooth, grayish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by large, round fruits with a thick, spiny rind.

The fruit contains a creamy, aromatic pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a unique, sweet-tart flavor.
