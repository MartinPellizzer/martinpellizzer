Aglaia exstipulata

Aglaia exstipulata, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
