Adiantum festum

Adiantum festum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble flowing hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive U-shaped sori on the underside.

Adiantum festum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
