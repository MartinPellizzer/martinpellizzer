Astragalus seatonii var. crucis

Astragalus seatonii var. crucis, commonly known as the cross-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, cruciform (cross-shaped) leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.

Astragalus seatonii var. crucis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
