Adelphia platyrachis

Adelphia platyrachis, commonly known as the broad-leafed adelphia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Adelphia platyrachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
