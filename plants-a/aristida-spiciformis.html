Aristida spiciformis

Aristida spiciformis, commonly known as the spikeless threeawn, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, pale green to brown flowers.

Aristida spiciformis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
