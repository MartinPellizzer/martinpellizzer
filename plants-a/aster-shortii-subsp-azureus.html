Aster shortii subsp. azureus

Aster shortii subsp.

azureus, commonly known as the Azure Aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the fall.

The plant typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 inches and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
