Artemisia austrohimalayana

Artemisia austrohimalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong medicinal and aromatic properties.

Artemisia austrohimalayana is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
