Astragalus berytheus

Astragalus berytheus, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Astragalus berytheus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
