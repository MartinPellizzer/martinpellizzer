Aerangis seegeri

Aerangis seegeri, commonly known as the Seeger's orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, Aerangis seegeri is highly valued in horticultural circles.
