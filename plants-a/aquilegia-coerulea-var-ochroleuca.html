Aquilegia coerulea var. ochroleuca

Aquilegia coerulea var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically spurred at the base and arranged in a cup-like formation. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
