Alysicarpus glumaceus var. patlopedicellatus

Alysicarpus glumaceus var. patlopedicellatus, commonly known as the hairy sida, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This plant is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
