Abrus cyaneus

Abrus cyaneus, commonly known as the Java bean or crimson devil, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red pods that contain toxic seeds covered in a silky, white coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Due to the high toxicity of its seeds, it is important to handle the plant with care.
