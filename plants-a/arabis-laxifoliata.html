Arabis laxifoliata

Arabis laxifoliata, commonly known as the slender-leaved rockcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal leaves that are elongated and often hairy. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
