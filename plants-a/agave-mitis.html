Agave mitis

Agave mitis, commonly known as the Mexican button agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that can reach up to 3 meters in height, bearing white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Agave mitis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional practices.
