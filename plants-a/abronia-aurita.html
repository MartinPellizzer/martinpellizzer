Abronia aurita

Abronia aurita, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
