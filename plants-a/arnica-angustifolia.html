Arnica angustifolia

Arnica angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf Arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers in the summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Arnica angustifolia is typically harvested for its flowers, which are used in herbal remedies.
