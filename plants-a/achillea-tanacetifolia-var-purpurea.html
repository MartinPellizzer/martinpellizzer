Achillea tanacetifolia var. purpurea

Achillea tanacetifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as purple yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in narrow, silver-gray leaves with a finely toothed margin. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.
