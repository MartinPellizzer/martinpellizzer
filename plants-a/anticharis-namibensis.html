Anticharis namibensis

Anticharis namibensis, commonly known as the Namibian anticharis, is a flowering plant native to the Namib Desert.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found growing in rocky outcrops or along dry riverbeds.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in the desert ecosystem.
