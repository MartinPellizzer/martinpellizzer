Abelmoschus guineensis

Abelmoschus guineensis, commonly known as okra, is a flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Africa and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used for propagation. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The plant is valued for its edible pods, which are rich in nutrients and commonly used in various cuisines.
