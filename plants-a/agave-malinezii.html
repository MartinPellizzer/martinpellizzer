Agave malinezii

Agave malinezii, commonly known as the Huizachal agave, is a species of agave native to central Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and can be processed to make fibers for weaving.
