Aniba rosodora var. amazonica

Aniba rosodora var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
