Afzelia cambodensis

Afzelia cambodensis, commonly known as Cambodian ironwood, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, typically appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of tropical forest ecosystems.
