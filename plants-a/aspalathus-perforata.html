Aspalathus perforata

Aspalathus perforata, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves with a distinctive perforated texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Aspalathus perforata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
