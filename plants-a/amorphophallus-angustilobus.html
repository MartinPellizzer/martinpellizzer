Amorphophallus angustilobus

Amorphophallus angustilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its spatheous inflorescence and unique morphology. The plant produces a large, fleshy underground tuber that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. Its leaves are typically large and palmately lobed, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its edible tuber, which is used in various culinary applications.
