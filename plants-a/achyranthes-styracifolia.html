Achyranthes styracifolia

Achyranthes styracifolia, commonly known as Indian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes. The fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas.
