Artemisia senecionis

Artemisia senecionis, commonly known as roughleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
