Archidendron chevalieri

Archidendron chevalieri, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, flat, clustered pods. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its wood is used for timber and fuel, while its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
