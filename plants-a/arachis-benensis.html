Arachis benensis

Arachis benensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peanut, is a species of legume native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Arachis, which includes various peanut species, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds, which are used for food and oil production. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Arachis benensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
