Arachis africana

Arachis africana, commonly known as the African groundnut, is a species of legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in sandy or loamy soils and requires a warm climate.

Arachis africana is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and oil.
