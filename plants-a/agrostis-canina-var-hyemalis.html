Agrostis canina var. hyemalis

Agrostis canina var. hyemalis, commonly known as winter meadowgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact inflorescences. This variety is well adapted to cool, moist environments and often thrives in meadows and pastures. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland and grassland habitats.
