Amygdalus korshinskyi

Amygdalus korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky almond, is a species of shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental flowers and deciduous foliage. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble almonds, though they are not edible. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
