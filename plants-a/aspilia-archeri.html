Aspilia archeri

Aspilia archeri, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in clusters, often forming dense stands. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Aspilia archeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
