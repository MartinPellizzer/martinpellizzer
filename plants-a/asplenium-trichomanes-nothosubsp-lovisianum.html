Asplenium trichomanes nothosubsp. lovisianum

Asplenium trichomanes nothosubsp.

lovisianum, commonly known as the Lovisian fern, is a rare and hybrid variant of the common rock fern. It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in dense, low mats on rocky or shaded substrates.

The plant exhibits a unique combination of morphological traits from its parent species, making it distinct in appearance. It is often found in humid, temperate regions with high moisture levels and moderate light conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
