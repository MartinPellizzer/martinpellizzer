Azalea chrysantha

Azalea chrysantha, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, often with a reddish or pinkish hue at the base. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Azalea chrysantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
