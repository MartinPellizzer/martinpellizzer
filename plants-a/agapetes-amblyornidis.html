Agapetes amblyornidis

Agapetes amblyornidis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silverleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Agapetes amblyornidis is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with partial shade.
