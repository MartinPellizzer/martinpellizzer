Avena hungarica

Avena hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian oat, is a species of grass native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, white to pale green flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Avena hungarica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
