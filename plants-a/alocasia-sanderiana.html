Alocasia sanderiana

Alocasia sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Alocasia sanderiana thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
