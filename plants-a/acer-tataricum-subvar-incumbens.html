Acer tataricum subvar. incumbens

Acer tataricum subvar. incumbens, commonly known as the Siberian maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically green on top and greyish-white underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
