Allium thunbergii var. thunbergii

Allium thunbergii var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often used in Asian cuisine for their mild garlic flavor. The plant produces small, spherical clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and aromatic foliage.
