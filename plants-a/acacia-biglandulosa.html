Acacia biglandulosa

Acacia biglandulosa, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, pale green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, glandular structures on its stems and leaves, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Acacia biglandulosa is also valued for its role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
