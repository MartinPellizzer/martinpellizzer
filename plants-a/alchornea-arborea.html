Alchornea arborea

Alchornea arborea, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it the common name. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Alchornea arborea is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
