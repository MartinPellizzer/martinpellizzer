Acer pictum subsp. taishakuense

Acer pictum subsp.

taishakuense, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its ornamental foliage, which displays vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues during the autumn season.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a serrated edge. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This subspecies is valued for its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes, enhancing its aesthetic appeal in landscaped settings.
