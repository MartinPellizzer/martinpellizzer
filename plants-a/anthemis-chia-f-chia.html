Anthemis chia f. chia

Anthemis chia f. chia, commonly known as chia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant produces tiny, nut-like seeds that are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, protein, and fiber. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
