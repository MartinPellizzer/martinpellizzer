Adesmia calocarpa

Adesmia calocarpa, commonly known as the sweetpea tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Adesmia calocarpa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
