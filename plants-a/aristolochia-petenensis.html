Aristolochia petenensis

Aristolochia petenensis, commonly known as the Mexican pipevine, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
