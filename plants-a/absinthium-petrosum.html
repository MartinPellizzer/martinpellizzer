Absinthium petrosum

Absinthium petrosum, commonly known as rock wormwood, is a perennial herb native to arid and rocky regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dense mats, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, grayish-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Absinthium petrosum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
