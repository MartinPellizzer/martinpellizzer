Astragalus aegobromus

Astragalus aegobromus, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, hard seeds. Astragalus aegobromus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
