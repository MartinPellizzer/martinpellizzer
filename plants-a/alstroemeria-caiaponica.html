Alstroemeria caiaponica

Alstroemeria caiaponica, commonly known as the Chilean lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with strap-like leaves and showy inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
