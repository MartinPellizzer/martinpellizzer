Aerva sansibarica

Aerva sansibarica, commonly known as African senna, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Aerva sansibarica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
