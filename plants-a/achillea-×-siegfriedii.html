Achillea × siegfriedii

Achillea × siegfriedii, commonly known as the Siegfried yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and flat, clustered inflorescences of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects such as butterflies and bees.

Achillea × siegfriedii is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
