Acacia luederitzii var. retinens

Acacia luederitzii var. retinens, commonly known as the Luederitz acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are composed of numerous small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden flowers in cylindrical inflorescences, which appear during the warmer months. Its seed pods are long, slender, and woody, containing several seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
