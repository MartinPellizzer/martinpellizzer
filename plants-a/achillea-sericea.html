Achillea sericea

Achillea sericea, commonly known as silver yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Silver yarrow is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
