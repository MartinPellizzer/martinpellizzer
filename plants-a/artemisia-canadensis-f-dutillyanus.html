Artemisia canadensis f. dutillyanus

Artemisia canadensis f. dutillyanus, commonly known as the Dutilly's wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
