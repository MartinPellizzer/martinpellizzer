Andropogon isostachyus

Andropogon isostachyus, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive purple-tinted flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Andropogon isostachyus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
