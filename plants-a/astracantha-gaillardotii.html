Astracantha gaillardotii

Astracantha gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thick, spiny stems and gray-green, woolly leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astracantha gaillardotii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
