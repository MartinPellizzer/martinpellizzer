Acacia neurocarpa var. glabrata

Acacia neurocarpa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in spherical inflorescences, which are followed by woody pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
