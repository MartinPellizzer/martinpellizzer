Aplexia virgata

Aplexia virgata, commonly known as the Virginia aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the fall.

Aplexia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
