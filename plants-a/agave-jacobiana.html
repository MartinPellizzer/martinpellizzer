Agave jacobiana

Agave jacobiana, commonly known as the Jacob's agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color with white marginal teeth. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Agave jacobiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
