Adesmia lotoides var. vaginata

Adesmia lotoides var. vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features small, compound leaves that are typically alternate and have a distinctive narrow shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
