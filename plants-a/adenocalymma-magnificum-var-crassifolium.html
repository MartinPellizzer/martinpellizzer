Adenocalymma magnificum var. crassifolium

Adenocalymma magnificum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf adenocalymma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to flourish.
