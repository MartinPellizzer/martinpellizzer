Astragalus barbidens

Astragalus barbidens, commonly known as the bearded milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Astragalus barbidens is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
