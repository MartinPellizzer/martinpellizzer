Anamirta septemnervia

Anamirta septemnervia, commonly known as the seven-veined waterleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with seven distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-green color.

Anamirta septemnervia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
