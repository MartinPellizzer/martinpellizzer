Acacia roigii

Acacia roigii, commonly known as the Guatemalan wattle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Acacia roigii is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.
