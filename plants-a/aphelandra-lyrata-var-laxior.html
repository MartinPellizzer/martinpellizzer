Aphelandra lyrata var. laxior

Aphelandra lyrata var. laxior, commonly known as the broad-leafed torch plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with bright yellow bracts and small white flowers, which bloom throughout the year in suitable conditions. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
