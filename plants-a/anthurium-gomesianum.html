Anthurium gomesianum

Anthurium gomesianum, commonly known as the Gomesian arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which can range from white to red or purple. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

Anthurium gomesianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
