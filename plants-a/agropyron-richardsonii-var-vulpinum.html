Agropyron richardsonii var. vulpinum

Agropyron richardsonii var. vulpinum, commonly known as the prickly wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and sharp, spiny leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
