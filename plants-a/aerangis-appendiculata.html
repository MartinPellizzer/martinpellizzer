Aerangis appendiculata

Aerangis appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-shaped, aiding in efficient water retention.

Aerangis appendiculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
