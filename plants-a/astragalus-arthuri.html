Astragalus arthuri

Astragalus arthuri, commonly known as the Arthur's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Astragalus arthuri is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
