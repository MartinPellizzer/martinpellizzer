Alopecurus nigricans var. humilis

Alopecurus nigricans var. humilis, commonly known as black oatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense mats of slender, hairless stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
