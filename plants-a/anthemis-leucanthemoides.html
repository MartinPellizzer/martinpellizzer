Anthemis leucanthemoides

Anthemis leucanthemoides, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
