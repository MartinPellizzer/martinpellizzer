Agrostis alba var. diffusa

Agrostis alba var. diffusa, commonly known as silvergrass or whitegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, pale spikelets. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.

Agrostis alba var. diffusa is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
