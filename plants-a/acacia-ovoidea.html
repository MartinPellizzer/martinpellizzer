Acacia ovoidea

Acacia ovoidea, commonly known as the oval acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, oval-shaped phyllodes that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Acacia ovoidea is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
