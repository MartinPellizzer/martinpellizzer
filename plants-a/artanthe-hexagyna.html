Artanthe hexagyna

Artanthe hexagyna, commonly known as the six-angled artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pink ray florets. Its leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Artanthe hexagyna thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
