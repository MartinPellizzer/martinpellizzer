Arctagrostis macrophylla

Arctagrostis macrophylla, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and dense growth habit, making it a popular choice for lawns and turfgrass. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its fine texture and ability to form a dense sod make it ideal for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.

Arctagrostis macrophylla is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its low maintenance requirements.
