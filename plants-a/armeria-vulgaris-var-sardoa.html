Armeria vulgaris var. sardoa

Armeria vulgaris var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian thrift, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has wiry, succulent stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its compact form and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
