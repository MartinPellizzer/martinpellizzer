Amonia agrimonioides

Amonia agrimonioides, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are valued for their licorice-like flavor and have been used in traditional medicine.

Amonia agrimonioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
