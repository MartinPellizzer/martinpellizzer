Aletris chinensis

Aletris chinensis, commonly known as Chinese false onion, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in a loose cluster during the spring or early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aletris chinensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
