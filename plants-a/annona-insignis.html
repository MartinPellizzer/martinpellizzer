Annona insignis

Annona insignis, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a pear-shaped fruit with a rough, hairy exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp. Its leaves are large, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Annona insignis is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
