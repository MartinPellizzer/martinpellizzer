Anarthria dioica

Anarthria dioica, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Anarthria dioica is often found in dry meadows and grasslands, and it is known for its aromatic properties.
