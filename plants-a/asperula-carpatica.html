Asperula carpatica

Asperula carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian beauty or Carpathian azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Asperula carpatica is well-suited for woodland gardens and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.
