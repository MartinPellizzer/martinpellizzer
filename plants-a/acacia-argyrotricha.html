Acacia argyrotricha

Acacia argyrotricha, commonly known as the silver-haired wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, contributing to its distinctive silver hue.

Acacia argyrotricha is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
