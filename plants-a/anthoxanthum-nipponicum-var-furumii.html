Anthoxanthum nipponicum var. furumii

Anthoxanthum nipponicum var. furumii, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale yellow to brown florets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration.
