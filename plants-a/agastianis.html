Agastianis

Agastianis, commonly known as the Agastache plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. These plants are native to North America and are commonly found in grasslands, woodlands, and along stream banks. Agastache species are known for their ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them popular choices for xeriscaping.

They also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
