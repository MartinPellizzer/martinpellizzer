Adiantum amblyopteridium

Adiantum amblyopteridium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
