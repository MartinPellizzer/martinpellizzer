Aletris spicata var. fargesii

Aletris spicata var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Aletris spicata var. fargesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
