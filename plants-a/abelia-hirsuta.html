Abelia hirsuta

Abelia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hair, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Abelia hirsuta is well-suited for partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

It is also found in parts of Southeast Asia, including Vietnam and the Philippines. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in forested areas and mountainous regions. It is known for its clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer.
