Actinostemon polymorphus f. minor

Actinostemon polymorphus f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf actinostemon, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact size and delicate floral display.
