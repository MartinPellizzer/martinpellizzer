Aristida nigritiana

Aristida nigritiana, commonly known as blackstem dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental quality to gardens. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its fine texture and ability to form dense clumps make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
