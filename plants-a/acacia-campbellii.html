Acacia campbellii

Acacia campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's acacia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the winter months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, thread-like leaflets.

Acacia campbellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
