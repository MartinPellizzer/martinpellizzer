Areca triginticollina

Areca triginticollina, commonly known as the thirty-horned areca, is a unique palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which bears up to 30 small, clustered flowers, giving it its name. The plant has a slender trunk and feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
