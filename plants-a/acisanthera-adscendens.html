Acisanthera adscendens

Acisanthera adscendens, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, twining stems that allow it to climb and form dense thickets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

Acisanthera adscendens is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife.
