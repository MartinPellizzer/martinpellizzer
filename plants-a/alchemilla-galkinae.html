Alchemilla galkinae

Alchemilla galkinae, commonly known as Galkina's lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the ends of leafy stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Alchemilla galkinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
