Arundinaria leucorhoda var. kanayamensis

Arundinaria leucorhoda var. kanayamensis, commonly known as the Kanayama bamboo, is a rare variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it its scientific name. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique appearance.
