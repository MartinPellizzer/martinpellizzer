Ambrina carinata

Ambrina carinata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink, white, or blue. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant.

Ambrina carinata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of care, though it requires well-draining soil and moderate watering.
