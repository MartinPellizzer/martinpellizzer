Achimenes occidentalis

Achimenes occidentalis, commonly known as the western flame flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and bloom throughout the growing season.
