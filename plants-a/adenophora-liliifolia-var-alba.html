Adenophora liliifolia var. alba

Adenophora liliifolia var. alba, commonly known as the white adenophora, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
