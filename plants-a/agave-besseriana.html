Agave besseriana

Agave besseriana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are blue-green in color and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing clusters of white or pale green flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Agave besseriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its potential in traditional uses.
