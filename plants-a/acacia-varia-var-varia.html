Acacia varia var. varia

Acacia varia var. varia, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale green flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
