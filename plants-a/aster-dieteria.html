Aster dieteria

Aster dieteria, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically features slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

Aster dieteria is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
