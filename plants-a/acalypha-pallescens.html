Acalypha pallescens

Acalypha pallescens, commonly known as the silverleaf or silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Acalypha pallescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
