Agave capensis

Agave capensis, commonly known as the Cape agave, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white or yellowish flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Agave capensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
