Anemone rupestris var. latifolia

Anemone rupestris var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rock anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped, with a hairy surface, and grows in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, typically with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
