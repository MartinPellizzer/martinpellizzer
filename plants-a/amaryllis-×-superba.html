Amaryllis × superba

Amaryllis × superba, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis paradisica.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are typically broad and dark green, forming a dense rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Amaryllis × superba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor planting.
