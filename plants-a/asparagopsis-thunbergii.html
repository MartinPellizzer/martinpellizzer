Asparagopsis thunbergii

Asparagopsis thunbergii, commonly known as sea asparagus, is a halophytic plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It thrives in coastal environments, often found in intertidal zones where it tolerates high salinity and fluctuating water levels. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves, and it produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. Its roots are adapted to anchor in sandy or muddy substrates, and it plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Sea asparagus is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of potassium and amino acids.
