Alyssum styriacum

Alyssum styriacum, commonly known as the Styrian alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lance-shaped, with a silvery-blue underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Alyssum styriacum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
