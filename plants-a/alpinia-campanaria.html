Alpinia campanaria

Alpinia campanaria, commonly known as the bell ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Alpinia campanaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
