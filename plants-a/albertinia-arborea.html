Albertinia arborea

Albertinia arborea, commonly known as the Natal apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
