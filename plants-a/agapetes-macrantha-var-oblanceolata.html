Agapetes macrantha var. oblanceolata

Agapetes macrantha var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved silver pine, is a variant of the Agapetes genus native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities for their purported healing properties.
