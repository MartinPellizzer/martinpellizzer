Argyrodendron trifoliolatum var. macrophyllum

Argyrodendron trifoliolatum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf argyrodendron, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often marked with distinct ridges.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
