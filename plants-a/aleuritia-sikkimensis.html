Aleuritia sikkimensis

Aleuritia sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Aleuritia sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
