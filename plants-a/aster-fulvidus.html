Aster fulvidus

Aster fulvidus, commonly known as the yellow wood aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and meadows, preferring partial to full shade. Its vibrant yellow flowers bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
