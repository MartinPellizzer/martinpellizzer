Astragalus bisulcatus var. bisulcatus

Astragalus bisulcatus var. bisulcatus, commonly known as the two-ridged milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
