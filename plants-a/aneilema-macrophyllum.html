Aneilema macrophyllum

Aneilema macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as taro and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
