Anthurium albidum

Anthurium albidum, commonly known as the white anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white spathes and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium albidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
