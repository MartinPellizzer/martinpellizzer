Astragalus canovirens

Astragalus canovirens, commonly known as the Canary Island milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Astragalus canovirens is often found in open, sunny areas such as rocky slopes and grasslands.
