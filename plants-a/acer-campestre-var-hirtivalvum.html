Acer campestre var. hirtivalvum

Acer campestre var. hirtivalvum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf maple, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves that are often glandular-pubescent on the underside. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with coarsely toothed margins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
