Adamanthus graminifolius

Adamanthus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leafed Adamanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, commonly known as the palm family, and is characterized by its slender, tree-like structure with a single trunk. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by the development of elongated, feather-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to habitat loss, Adamanthus graminifolius is considered threatened and is of interest for conservation efforts.
