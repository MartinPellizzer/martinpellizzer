Acinodendron vaccinioides

Acinodendron vaccinioides, commonly known as the African cranberry bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in which it grows.
