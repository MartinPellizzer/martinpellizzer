Amaranthus ascendens subsp. polygonoides

Amaranthus ascendens subsp.

polygonoides, commonly known as the prostrate amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit, with branching stems that often lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
