Astragalus straussii

Astragalus straussii, commonly known as Strauss's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
