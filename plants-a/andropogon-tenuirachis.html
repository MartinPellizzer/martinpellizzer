Andropogon tenuirachis

Andropogon tenuirachis, commonly known as narrow-leaved bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. It is a perennial plant that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
