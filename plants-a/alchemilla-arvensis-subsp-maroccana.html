Alchemilla arvensis subsp. maroccana

Alchemilla arvensis subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It features basal rosettes of velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
