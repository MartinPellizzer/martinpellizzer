Anthyllis spinosa

Anthyllis spinosa, commonly known as the spiny anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Anthyllis spinosa is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often found in Mediterranean climates where it thrives in well-drained soils.
