Aloe × corifolia

Aloe × corifolia, commonly known as the hybrid aloe, is a succulent plant resulting from the cross between Aloe arborescens and Aloe ferox.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and covered in a protective layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by the development of red, fleshy fruits. Aloe × corifolia is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses similar to other aloe species.
