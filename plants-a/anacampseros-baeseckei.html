Anacampseros baeseckei

Anacampseros baeseckei, commonly known as the Baeseck's ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It is a low-growing shrub that forms dense mats, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
