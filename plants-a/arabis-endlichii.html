Arabis endlichii

Arabis endlichii, commonly known as the Endlich's rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
