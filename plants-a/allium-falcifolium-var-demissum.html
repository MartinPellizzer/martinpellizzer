Allium falcifolium var. demissum

Allium falcifolium var. demissum, commonly known as the bent-leaf garlic.

This plant is a perennial member of the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its grass-like leaves that curve downward. It produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The bulbs are typically smaller than those of common garlic and have a milder flavor.

It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
