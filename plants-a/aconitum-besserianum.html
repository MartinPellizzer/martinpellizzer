Aconitum besserianum

Aconitum besserianum, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Aconitum besserianum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
