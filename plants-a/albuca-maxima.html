Albuca maxima

Albuca maxima, commonly known as the giant albuca, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade.

Albuca maxima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
