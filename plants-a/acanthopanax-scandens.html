Acanthopanax scandens

Acanthopanax scandens, commonly known as climbing devil's ivy, is a vigorous climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its strong climbing habit makes it suitable for trellises and vertical gardens.
