Amomum capitatum

Amomum capitatum, commonly known as the fragrant cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces dark brown, three-lobed seeds that are highly valued for their intense aroma and flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The essential oils found in its leaves and seeds contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a spice.
