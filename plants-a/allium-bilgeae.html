Allium bilgeae

Allium bilgeae, commonly known as the Turkish leek, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces upright, cylindrical flower stalks that bear small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful crop in Mediterranean cuisine.

Allium bilgeae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.
