Anisostachya ambositrensis

Anisostachya ambositrensis, commonly known as the Malagasy reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in sustainable land management.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the grass family, Poaceae.
