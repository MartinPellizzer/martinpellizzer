Anthemis parnassica

Anthemis parnassica, commonly known as the Parnassian anthemis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically arranged in a flat, disc-like structure. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Anthemis parnassica is often found in alpine meadows and is noted for its resilience in harsh climates.
