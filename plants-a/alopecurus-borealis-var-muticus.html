Alopecurus borealis var. muticus

Alopecurus borealis var. muticus, commonly known as the silent northern feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless blades and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spike-like flowers. This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of the vegetation in its native habitat.
