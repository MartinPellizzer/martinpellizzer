Aristolochia fulvicoma

Aristolochia fulvicoma, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a strong, often unpleasant odor to attract its pollinators. Its flowers are specialized to accommodate specific pollinators, such as flies, and the plant is known for its complex floral morphology.

Aristolochia fulvicoma contains various bioactive compounds and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
