Acharagma roseanum

Acharagma roseanum, commonly known as the pink stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its upright stems covered in fine, irritating hairs that release formic acid when touched. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are typically pale green to pinkish in color.

Acharagma roseanum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
