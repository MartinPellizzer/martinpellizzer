Alchemilla andina

Alchemilla andina, commonly known as Andean lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla andina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
