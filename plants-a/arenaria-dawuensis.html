Arenaria dawuensis

Arenaria dawuensis, commonly known as the Dawu sandwort, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Arenaria dawuensis is often found in alpine or arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
