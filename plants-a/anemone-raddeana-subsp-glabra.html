Anemone raddeana subsp. glabra

Anemone raddeana subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a basal rosette of smooth, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, arranged in a cup-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
