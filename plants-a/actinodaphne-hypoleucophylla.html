Actinodaphne hypoleucophylla

Actinodaphne hypoleucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, and often white-veined leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish outer layer.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
