Astragalus garaensis

Astragalus garaensis, commonly known as the Garaensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Astragalus garaensis is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
