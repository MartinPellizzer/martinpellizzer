Ailuroschia amygdaliger

Ailuroschia amygdaliger, commonly known as the almond-leaved lichen, is a species of foliose lichen that thrives in temperate climates.

It is characterized by its leaf-like thallus, which is typically green or yellow-green in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which provide essential nutrients through photosynthesis and decomposition. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in moist environments.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for ecological health.
