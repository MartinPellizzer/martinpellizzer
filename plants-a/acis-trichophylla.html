Acis trichophylla

Acis trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly acis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Acis trichophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
