Amauropelta steyermarkii

Amauropelta steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically broad and leathery. The plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
