Anthemis Marschalliana Subsp. Sosnowskyana

Marschall's anthemis, scientifically known as Anthemis marschalliana subsp. sosnowskyana, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. This subspecies is characterized by its small, yellow daisy-like flowers and finely divided, silver-green leaves. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and lesser-known member of the Asteraceae family.
