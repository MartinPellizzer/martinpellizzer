Astragalus australis var. bucsecsi

Astragalus australis var. bucsecsi, commonly known as the Southern Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties.
