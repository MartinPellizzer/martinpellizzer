Amyris axilliflora

Amyris axilliflora, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its root system is fibrous and moderately deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Amyris axilliflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
