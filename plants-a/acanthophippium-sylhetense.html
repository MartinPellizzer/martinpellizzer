Acanthophippium sylhetense

Acanthophippium sylhetense, commonly known as Sylhet spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Sylhet region of Bangladesh.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
