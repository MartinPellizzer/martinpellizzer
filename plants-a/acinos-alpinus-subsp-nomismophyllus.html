Acinos alpinus subsp. nomismophyllus

Acinos alpinus subsp.

nomismophyllus, commonly known as the alpine acino, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and surrounding mountain ranges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is distinguished by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
