Acourtia cordata

Acourtia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf acourtia, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Acourtia cordata is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub environments.

It is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
