Arisaema pradhanii

Arisaema pradhanii, commonly known as the Pradhan's arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It is native to the mountainous regions of northeastern India, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its spadix, which is typically yellowish-green, is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Arisaema pradhanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
