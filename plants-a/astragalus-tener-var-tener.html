Astragalus tener var. tener

Astragalus tener var. tener, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
