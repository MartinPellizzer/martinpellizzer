Agave paniculata

Agave paniculata, commonly known as the Mexican weeping agave, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, blue-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces an impressive inflorescence of white to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, with thick leaves that store water.

Agave paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low-maintenance in suitable climates.
