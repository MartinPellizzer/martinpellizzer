Arundinaria amabilis var. farinosa

Arundinaria amabilis var. farinosa, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo genus Arundinaria.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in a fine, silky down, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface that adds to its ornamental value.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
