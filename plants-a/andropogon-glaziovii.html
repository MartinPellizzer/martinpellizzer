Andropogon glaziovii

Andropogon glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
