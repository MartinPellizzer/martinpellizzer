Artabotrys gossweileri

Artabotrys gossweileri, commonly known as the African star jasmine, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Artabotrys gossweileri is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
