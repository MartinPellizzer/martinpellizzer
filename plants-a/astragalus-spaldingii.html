Astragalus spaldingii

Astragalus spaldingii, commonly known as Spalding's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

Astragalus spaldingii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
