Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. selemiensis

Astragalus bethlehemiticus var. selemiensis, commonly known as the Bethlehem astragalus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow or white pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to dry climates. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its environment.
