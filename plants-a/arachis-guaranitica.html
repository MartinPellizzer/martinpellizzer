Arachis guaranitica

Arachis guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani peanut, is a species of legume native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that produces small, yellow flowers and edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves and is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical climates. Its seeds are rich in protein and oil, making it a potential crop for nutritional and agricultural purposes.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other peanut species.
