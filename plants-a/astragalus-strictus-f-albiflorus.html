Astragalus strictus f. albiflorus

Astragalus strictus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typical of the Astragalus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and herbalist collections.
