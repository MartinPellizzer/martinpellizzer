Amsonia hubrichtii

Amsonia hubrichtii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and fluffy, blue-pink flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and pond edges. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, particularly in wetland landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
