Aristolochia rumphii

Aristolochia rumphii, commonly known as the Java pipevine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-like flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often found in tropical rainforests.

The genus Aristolochia includes many species with similar morphological features and ecological roles.
