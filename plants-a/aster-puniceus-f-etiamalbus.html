Aster puniceus f. etiamalbus

Aster puniceus f. etiamalbus, commonly known as the White-Flowered Purple Coneflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a cultivar of the native Aster puniceus, characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that contrast with its deep purple foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping, making it a popular choice in native plant gardens.

The white flowers bloom in late summer, adding a unique visual appeal to the landscape.
