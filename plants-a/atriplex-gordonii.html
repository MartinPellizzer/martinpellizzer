Atriplex gordonii

Atriplex gordonii, commonly known as Gordon's saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its salt-tolerant nature allows it to thrive in saline soils, making it a valuable species for stabilizing coastal and desert landscapes.

Atriplex gordonii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
