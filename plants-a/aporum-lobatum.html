Aporum lobatum

Aporum lobatum, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Aporum lobatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
