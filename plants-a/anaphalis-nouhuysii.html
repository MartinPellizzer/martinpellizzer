Anaphalis nouhuysii

Anaphalis nouhuysii, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
