Alsophila furcata

Alsophila furcata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, bi-lobed fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a silvery sheen. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
