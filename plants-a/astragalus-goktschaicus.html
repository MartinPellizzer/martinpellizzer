Astragalus goktschaicus

Astragalus goktschaicus, commonly known as the Goktschaic milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
