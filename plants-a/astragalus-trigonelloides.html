Astragalus trigonelloides

Astragalus trigonelloides, commonly known as the three-lobed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus trigonelloides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
