Aconitum ohmorii

Aconitum ohmorii, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a helmet-like shape. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically erect and hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Aconitum ohmorii is toxic due to the presence of potent alkaloids, making it a plant that requires careful handling.
