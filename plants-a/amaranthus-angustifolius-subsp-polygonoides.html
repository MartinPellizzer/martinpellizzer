Amaranthus angustifolius subsp. polygonoides

Amaranthus angustifolius subsp.

polygonoides, commonly known as the slender amaranth, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and upright growth habit, often reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiky, bristly seed heads. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for drought-tolerant landscaping.

The seeds are edible and have been used historically by indigenous peoples for food and ceremonial purposes.
