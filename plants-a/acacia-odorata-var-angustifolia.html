Acacia odorata var. angustifolia

Acacia odorata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are often fragrant and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
