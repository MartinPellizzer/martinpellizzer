Astrochlaena malvacea var. parviflora

Astrochlaena malvacea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mallows, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves, with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
