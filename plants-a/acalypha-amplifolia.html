Acalypha amplifolia

Acalypha amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved spurflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences with small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Acalypha amplifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
