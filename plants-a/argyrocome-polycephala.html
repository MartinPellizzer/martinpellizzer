Argyrocome polycephala

Argyrocome polycephala, commonly known as the silver-headed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones being more rounded. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable and vulnerable species in its native habitat.
