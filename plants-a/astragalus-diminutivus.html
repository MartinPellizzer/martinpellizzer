Astragalus diminutivus

Astragalus diminutivus, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a small perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus diminutivus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
