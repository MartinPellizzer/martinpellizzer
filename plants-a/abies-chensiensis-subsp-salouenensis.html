Abies chensiensis subsp. salouenensis

Abies chensiensis subsp.

salouenensis, commonly known as the Salouenensis fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the mountainous regions of eastern China. It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and a dense, pyramidal crown.

The plant thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil, typically found in high-altitude forests. Its bark is thin and smooth, often covered in a layer of moss, while its cones are small and typically hang downward.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
