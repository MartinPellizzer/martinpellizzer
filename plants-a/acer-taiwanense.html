Acer taiwanense

Acer taiwanense, commonly known as the Taiwan maple, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
