Archibaccharis schiedeana

Archibaccharis schiedeana, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or *Babassu* palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, woody fruits containing edible seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its economic uses, including the production of oil from its seeds.
