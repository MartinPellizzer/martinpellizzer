Acacia insignis

Acacia insignis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Acacia insignis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is a symbol of Australia's national identity.
