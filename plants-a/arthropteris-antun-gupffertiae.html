Arthropteris antun-gupffertiae

Arthropteris antun-gupffertiae, commonly known as the Antun-Gupffertia fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping, and the fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinct, glossy appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in humid, shaded gardens.
