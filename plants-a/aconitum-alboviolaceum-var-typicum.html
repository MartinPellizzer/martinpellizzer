Aconitum alboviolaceum var. typicum

Aconitum alboviolaceum var. typicum, commonly known as the white and violet aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with white markings, which are typically arranged in a spire-like inflorescence. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves that are deeply cut and covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a dangerous plant and should be handled with care.
