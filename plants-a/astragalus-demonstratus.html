Astragalus demonstratus

Astragalus demonstratus, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus demonstratus is also notable for its role in supporting local pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
