Adesmia graminidea

Adesmia graminidea, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Adesmia graminidea is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment and its potential medicinal properties.
