Astragalus angustiflorus subsp. filamentosus

Astragalus angustiflorus subsp.

filamentosus, commonly known as the filamentous milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
