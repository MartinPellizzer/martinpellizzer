Aechmea geminiflora

Aechmea geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces showy inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and provide a unique texture.

Aechmea geminiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.

Morphology

Aechmea geminiflora has rosette-shaped leaves that are narrow, strap-like, and arranged in a compact spiral, with a waxy, bright green color and a pointed tip.

The plant lacks a true stem, with a short, fibrous, and often covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. Its inflorescence is a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The fruit is a capsule that produces small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.

The roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, with some rhizomes forming new plants.
