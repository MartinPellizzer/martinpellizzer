Aechmea geminiflora

Aechmea geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces showy inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and provide a unique texture.

Aechmea geminiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant.
