Aria japonica f. denudata

Aria japonica f. denudata, commonly known as the Japanese spurge.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan, characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
