Anathallis fasciculata

Anathallis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive in the presence of mycorrhizal fungi. Anathallis fasciculata is a rare and delicate species, often found in specific microhabitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
