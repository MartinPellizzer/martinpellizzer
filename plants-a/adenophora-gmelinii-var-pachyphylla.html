Adenophora gmelinii var. pachyphylla

Adenophora gmelinii var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
