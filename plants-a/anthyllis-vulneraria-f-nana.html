Anthyllis vulneraria f. nana

Anthyllis vulneraria f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf yellow sweetclover, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and arctic regions. Its compact form and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a hardy species.

The plant is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
