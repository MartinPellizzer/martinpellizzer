Agropyron violaceum var. andinum

Agropyron violaceum var. andinum, commonly known as the Andean bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that often bear a bluish hue. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic environments, where it thrives in cold, dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its adaptation to extreme climates and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
