Ancylostemon vulpinus

Ancylostemon vulpinus, commonly known as the red torch ginger, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has aromatic leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ancylostemon vulpinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
