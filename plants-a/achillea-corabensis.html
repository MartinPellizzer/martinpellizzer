Achillea corabensis

Achillea corabensis, commonly known as the Corsican yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Achillea corabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
