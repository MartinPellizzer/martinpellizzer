Abacopteris holophylla

Abacopteris holophylla, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that radiate from a central stem, giving it a star-like appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are typically bright green and covered in fine, hair-like structures that help it absorb nutrients from the water.

Abacopteris holophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
