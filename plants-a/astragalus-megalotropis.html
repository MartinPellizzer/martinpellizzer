Astragalus megalotropis

Astragalus megalotropis, commonly known as the giant milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Astragalus megalotropis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
