Asarum hatsushimae

Asarum hatsushimae, commonly known as Japanese wild betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are typically greenish-brown with a strong, spicy odor, which attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Japanese medicine for various ailments.
