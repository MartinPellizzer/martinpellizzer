Acacia didyma

Acacia didyma, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia didyma is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
