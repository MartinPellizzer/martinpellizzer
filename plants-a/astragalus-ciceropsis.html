Astragalus ciceropsis

Astragalus ciceropsis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus ciceropsis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which benefits surrounding vegetation.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
