Arenaria fendleri var. loisiae

Arenaria fendleri var. loisiae, commonly known as the Desert Sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

The species is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
