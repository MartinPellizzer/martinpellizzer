Afzelia ramosissima

Afzelia ramosissima, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a spreading crown of lanceolate leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing flat, oval seeds.

Afzelia ramosissima is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an adaptable species in its native habitat.
