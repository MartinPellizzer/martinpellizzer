Arctotis carduifolia

Arctotis carduifolia, commonly known as the bluebottle or blue coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Arctotis carduifolia is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to reseed itself, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
