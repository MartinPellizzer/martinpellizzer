Anabasis abolinii

Anabasis abolinii, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems. It produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Anabasis abolinii is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
