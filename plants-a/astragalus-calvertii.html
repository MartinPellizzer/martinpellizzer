Astragalus calvertii

Astragalus calvertii, commonly known as Calvert's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets. Astragalus calvertii is part of the legume family (Fabaceae), which is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
