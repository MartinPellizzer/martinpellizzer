Asparagus graminifolius

Asparagus graminifolius, commonly known as the grassy asparagus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Asparagus graminifolius thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
