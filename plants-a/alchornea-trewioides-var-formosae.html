Alchornea trewioides var. formosae

Alchornea trewioides var. formosae, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered, violet-blue berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife. These berries are typically harvested in the fall and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
