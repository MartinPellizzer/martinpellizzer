Aster bakerianus

Aster bakerianus, commonly known as the Baker's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster bakerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
