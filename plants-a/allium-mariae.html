Allium mariae

Allium mariae, commonly known as the Marie's onion, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes various species of onions and garlic. The plant produces small, spherical bulbs that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, growing from a basal rosette.

Allium mariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and potential culinary uses.
