Arabis albida var. umbrosa

Arabis albida var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving rockcress, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, cruciform flowers. This variety is characterized by its pale white flowers and compact, low-growing habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and shady borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, often found in alpine and woodland environments.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate moist, rocky conditions.
