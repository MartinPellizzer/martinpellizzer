Alchemilla porrectidens

Alchemilla porrectidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broad, wavy-edged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Alchemilla porrectidens is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
