Acacia leptospermoides

Acacia leptospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and narrow, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Acacia leptospermoides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
