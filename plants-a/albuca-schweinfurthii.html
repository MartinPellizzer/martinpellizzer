Albuca schweinfurthii

Albuca schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that resemble tulips, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Albuca schweinfurthii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
