Alsinanthus obtusus

Alsinanthus obtusus, commonly known as the woolly phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for arid landscapes.
