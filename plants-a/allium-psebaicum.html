Allium psebaicum

Allium psebaicum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Allium psebaicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
