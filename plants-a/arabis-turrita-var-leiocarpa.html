Arabis turrita var. leiocarpa

Arabis turrita var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are borne on slender, branching stems.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
