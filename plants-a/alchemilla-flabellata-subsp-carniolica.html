Alchemilla flabellata subsp. carniolica

Alchemilla flabellata subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carniolan lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the Alps and surrounding regions. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in upright panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
