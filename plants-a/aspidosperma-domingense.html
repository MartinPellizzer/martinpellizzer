Aspidosperma domingense

Aspidosperma domingense, commonly known as the Dominican cherry or palo de indio, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic effects.
