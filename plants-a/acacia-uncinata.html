Acacia uncinata

Acacia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which attract nectar-feeding insects and birds. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Acacia uncinata is well-suited to dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
