Astragalus cusickii var. sterilis

Astragalus cusickii var. sterilis, commonly known as the Cusick's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, pinnate leaves. It is notable for its limited seed production, which contributes to its rarity and conservation status.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
