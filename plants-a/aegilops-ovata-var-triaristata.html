Aegilops ovata var. triaristata

Aegilops ovata var. triaristata, commonly known as the three-awned wild oat, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces three awns on each lemma, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is closely related to other wild oat species and is of interest in agricultural and ecological studies.
