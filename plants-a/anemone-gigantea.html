Anemone gigantea

Anemone gigantea, commonly known as the giant anemone, is a striking aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Anemone gigantea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water clarity.
