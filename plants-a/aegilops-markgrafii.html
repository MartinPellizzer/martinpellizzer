Aegilops markgrafii

Aegilops markgrafii, commonly known as the Markgraf's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Aegilops markgrafii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
