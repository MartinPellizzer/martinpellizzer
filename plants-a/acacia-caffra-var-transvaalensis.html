Acacia caffra var. transvaalensis

Acacia caffra var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
