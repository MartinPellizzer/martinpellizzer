Augusta grandiflora

Augusta grandiflora, commonly known as the Southern Magnolia, is a large evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves and its large, showy white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a sturdy trunk, dense foliage, and a long blooming period, typically from spring to summer.

Augusta grandiflora is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
