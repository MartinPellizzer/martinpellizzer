Aquilegia sibirica var. spectabilis

Aquilegia sibirica var. spectabilis, commonly known as the Siberian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
