Adiantopsis seemannii

Adiantopsis seemannii, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique member of the fern family (Polypodiaceae).

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it a distinct appearance. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically delicate and can be either evergreen or deciduous depending on the climate.

Adiantopsis seemannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
