Asphodelus bento-rainhae subsp. bento-rainhae

Asphodelus bento-rainhae subsp.

bento-rainhae, commonly known as the Bento-Rainha asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a valuable component of local flora due to its resilience and ornamental appeal.
