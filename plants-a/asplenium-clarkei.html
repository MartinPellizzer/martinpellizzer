Asplenium clarkei

Asplenium clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's spleenwort, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a delicate, feathery appearance, with a dark green color and a slightly glossy surface.

Asplenium clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in indoor gardens due to its low maintenance requirements.
