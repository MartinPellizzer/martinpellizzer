Alchemilla lindebergiana

Alchemilla lindebergiana, commonly known as Lindberg's lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in a dense, rounded inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
