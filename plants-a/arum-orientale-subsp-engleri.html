Arum orientale subsp. engleri

Arum orientale subsp.

engleri, commonly known as the Engler's arrowhead, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns.

The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
