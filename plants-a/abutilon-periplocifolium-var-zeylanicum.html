Abutilon periplocifolium var. zeylanicum

Abutilon periplocifolium var. zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, panicle-like inflorescences. Its fibrous stems are valued for their use in traditional textiles and papermaking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
