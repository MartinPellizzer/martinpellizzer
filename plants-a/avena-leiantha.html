Avena leiantha

Avena leiantha, commonly known as the silky oat, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, silky-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Avena leiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and ecological restoration.
