Arrhenatherum exserens

Arrhenatherum exserens, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

Arrhenatherum exserens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
