Amomum labellosum

Amomum labellosum, commonly known as the Chinese cardamom or wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in terminal inflorescences. Its aromatic seeds, which are harvested when mature, are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Amomum labellosum make it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal applications.
