Aristolochia ungulifolia

Aristolochia ungulifolia, commonly known as the horned pipe, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that contain the seeds. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical compounds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for cultivation in gardens or for medicinal use without professional guidance.
