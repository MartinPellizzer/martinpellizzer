Atylus axillaris

Atylus axillaris, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and white, waxy flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and other animals. Its growth is often limited to the branches of its host, where it derives some of its nutrients through its specialized roots.

Atylus axillaris plays a role in the ecosystem by providing sustenance for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of forested areas.
