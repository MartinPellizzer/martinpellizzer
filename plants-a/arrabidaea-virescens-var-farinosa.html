Arrabidaea virescens var. farinosa

Arrabidaea virescens var. farinosa, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
