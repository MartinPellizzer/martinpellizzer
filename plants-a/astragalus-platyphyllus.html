Astragalus platyphyllus

Astragalus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, making it ecologically significant.

Astragalus platyphyllus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
