Aspalathus glossoides

Aspalathus glossoides, commonly known as the small-leaved bushveld clover, is a small, erect shrub native to South Africa.

It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and bushveld habitats.

Aspalathus glossoides is a hardy plant that is well adapted to the semi-arid conditions of its native region.
