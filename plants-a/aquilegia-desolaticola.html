Aquilegia desolaticola

Aquilegia desolaticola, commonly known as the desert columbine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred petals and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant typically grows from a basal rosette of leaves and produces tall, sparsely branched stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
