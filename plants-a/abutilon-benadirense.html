Abutilon benadirense

Abutilon benadirense, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a waxy texture.

Abutilon benadirense thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
