Acaena buchananii subsp. longissimefilamentosa

Acaena buchananii subsp.

longissimefilamentosa, commonly known as the long-filamentous bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its long, slender filaments in the flower structure, which give it its specific epithet. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and provides habitat for native insects and birds.
