Aipyanthus echioides

Aipyanthus echioides, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Aipyanthus echioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
