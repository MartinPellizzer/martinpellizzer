Arundinaria cubensis

Arundinaria cubensis, commonly known as Cuban bamboo, is a species of grass native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth in texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Arundinaria cubensis plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
