Artemisia gansuensis var. oligantha

Artemisia gansuensis var. oligantha, commonly known as the Gansu wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It contains volatile oils and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
