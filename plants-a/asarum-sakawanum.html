Asarum sakawanum

Asarum sakawanum, commonly known as the Sakawanum wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, reddish-brown, irregularly shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.

Asarum sakawanum is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
