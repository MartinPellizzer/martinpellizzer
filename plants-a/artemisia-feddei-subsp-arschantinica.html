Artemisia feddei subsp. arschantinica

Artemisia feddei subsp.

arschantinica, commonly known as the Armenian tarragon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its medicinal properties and distinctive aroma, it is occasionally cultivated for use in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
