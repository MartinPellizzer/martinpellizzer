Acantholimon capitatum subsp. capitatum

Acantholimon capitatum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf jointvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
