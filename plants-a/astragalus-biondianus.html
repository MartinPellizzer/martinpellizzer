Astragalus biondianus

Astragalus biondianus, commonly known as the Biondi's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus biondianus is known for its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
