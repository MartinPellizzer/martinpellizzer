Astragalus misellus var. misellus

Astragalus misellus var. misellus, commonly known as the small milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces small, yellow to greenish flowers in early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
