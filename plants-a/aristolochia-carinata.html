Aristolochia carinata

Aristolochia carinata, commonly known as the spurred arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein. The plant produces unique, spiral-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids.
