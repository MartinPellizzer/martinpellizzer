Amaranthus macrocarpus

Amaranthus macrocarpus, commonly known as the scarlet amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Amaranthus macrocarpus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
