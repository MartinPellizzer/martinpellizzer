Aspalathus suffruticosa

Aspalathus suffruticosa, commonly known as African red bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is known for its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

Aspalathus suffruticosa is also recognized for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
