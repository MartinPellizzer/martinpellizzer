Acer mono var. truncatulum

Acer mono var. truncatulum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring vibrant foliage that changes color throughout the seasons. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a truncate or notched base, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its compact size and ability to adapt to various climatic conditions.
