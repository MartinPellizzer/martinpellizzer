Andropogon monticola var. trinii

Andropogon monticola var. trinii, commonly known as the eastern gamagrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
