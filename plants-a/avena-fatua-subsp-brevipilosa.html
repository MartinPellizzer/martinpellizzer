Avena fatua subsp. brevipilosa

Avena fatua subsp.

brevipilosa, commonly known as slender wild oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 5 mm in width.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it is considered a weed in many cultivated environments.
