Achras novozelandica

Achras novozelandica, commonly known as the New Zealand aloe, is a succulent plant native to New Zealand.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, with a bluish-green color and serrated edges.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
