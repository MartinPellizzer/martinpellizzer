Adiantum × villosolucidum

Adiantum × villosolucidum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a hybrid fern species that combines traits from its parent species, Adiantum venustum and Adiantum capillus-veneris.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, drooping sori that are typically located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Adiantum × villosolucidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
