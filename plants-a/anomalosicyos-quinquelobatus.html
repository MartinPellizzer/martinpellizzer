Anomalosicyos quinquelobatus

Anomalosicyos quinquelobatus, commonly known as the five-lobed horned cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, ribbed stems that are often branched and covered in clusters of sharp, curved spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

This cactus is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for small animals.
