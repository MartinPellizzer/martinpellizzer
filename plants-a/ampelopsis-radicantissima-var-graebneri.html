Ampelopsis radicantissima var. graebneri

Ampelopsis radicantissima var. graebneri, commonly known as the Graebner's Ampelopsis, is a climbing vine native to parts of East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and vibrant green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a decorative and hardy climbing plant.
