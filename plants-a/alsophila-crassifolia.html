Alsophila crassifolia

Alsophila crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense, fibrous mat, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with long, narrow pinnae that give the plant a lush, green appearance.

Alsophila crassifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along stream banks.
