Achyranthes uncinulata

Achyranthes uncinulata, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and medicinal properties. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

The small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are accompanied by specialized bracts.
