Annona hostmannii

Annona hostmannii, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that produces a distinctive, fragrant fruit with a rough, greenish skin. The fruit contains a creamy, white, custard-like pulp that is sweet and aromatic. Botanically, it belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
