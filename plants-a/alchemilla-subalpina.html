Alchemilla subalpina

Alchemilla subalpina, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine climates.

Alchemilla subalpina is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental foliage and hardiness.
