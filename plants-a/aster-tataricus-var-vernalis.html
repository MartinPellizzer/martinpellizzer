Aster tataricus var. vernalis

Aster tataricus var. vernalis, commonly known as the spring aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and it produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant typically blooms in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a slightly fuzzy texture.
