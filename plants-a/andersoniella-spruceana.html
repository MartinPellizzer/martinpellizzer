Andersoniella spruceana

Andersoniella spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's Andersonia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
