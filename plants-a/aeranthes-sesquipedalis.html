Aeranthes sesquipedalis

Aeranthes sesquipedalis, commonly known as the long-flowered spider-orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting a dense cluster of long, narrow, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces numerous showy, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting specific pollinators like bees and wasps. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
