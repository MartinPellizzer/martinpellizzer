Aciotis longifolia

Aciotis longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, pinnately arranged leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Aciotis longifolia is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
