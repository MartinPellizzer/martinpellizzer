Alchemilla georgica

Alchemilla georgica, commonly known as the Georgian lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Alchemilla georgica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in woodland settings.
