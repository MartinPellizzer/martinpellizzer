Acosta busambarensis

Acosta busambarensis, commonly known as the Bornean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
