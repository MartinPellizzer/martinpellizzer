Acacia anadenia

Acacia anadenia, commonly known as the red acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in globular clusters. The leaves are typically small and feathery, with a pale green color. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
