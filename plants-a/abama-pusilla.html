Abama pusilla

Abama pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf abama, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slight pubescence.

Abama pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
