Allium subhirsutum var. album

Allium subhirsutum var. album, commonly known as the white garlic chive, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale lavender flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its mild garlic scent and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is closely related to other Allium species and is valued in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
