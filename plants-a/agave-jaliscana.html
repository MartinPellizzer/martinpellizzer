Agave jaliscana

Agave jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a species native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Agave jaliscana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
