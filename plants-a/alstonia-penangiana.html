Alstonia penangiana

Alstonia penangiana, commonly known as the Penang alstonia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
