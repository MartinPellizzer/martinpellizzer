Astragalus landbeckii

Astragalus landbeckii, commonly known as the landbeck's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
