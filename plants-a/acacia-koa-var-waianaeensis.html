Acacia koa var. waianaeensis

Acacia koa var. waianaeensis, commonly known as the Koa ‘Waianaeensis’ or a rare subspecies of the Koa tree, is a native Hawaiian plant characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers.

It is typically found in moist, mesic habitats on the windward slopes of the island of Oahu. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind or animals. It plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian forests and is considered a culturally significant species.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, it is also a species of conservation concern.
