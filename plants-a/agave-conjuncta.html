Agave conjuncta

Agave conjuncta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf agave, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears numerous white or pale yellow flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while the plant itself is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Agave conjuncta is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and desert gardens.
