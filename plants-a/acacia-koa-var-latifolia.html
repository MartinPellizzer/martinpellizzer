Acacia koa var. latifolia

Acacia koa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf koa, is a native Hawaiian tree species belonging to the legume family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to golden pod-like fruits. The plant is well adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is also cultivated for landscaping and traditional uses.
