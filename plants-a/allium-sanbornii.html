Allium sanbornii

Allium sanbornii, commonly known as Sanborn's onion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild onion flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium sanbornii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
