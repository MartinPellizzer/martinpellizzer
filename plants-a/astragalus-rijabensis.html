Astragalus rijabensis

Astragalus rijabensis, commonly known as the Jordanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East, particularly Jordan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Astragalus rijabensis is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
