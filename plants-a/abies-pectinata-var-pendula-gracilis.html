Abies pectinata var. pendula-gracilis

Abies pectinata var. pendula-gracilis, commonly known as the slender silver fir, is a rare variant of the silver fir native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its pendulous, slender branches that give it a weeping appearance. The leaves are typically needle-like, short, and glaucous, with a silvery-blue underside. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
