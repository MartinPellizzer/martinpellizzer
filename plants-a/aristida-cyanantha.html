Aristida cyanantha

Aristida cyanantha, commonly known as bluegrass dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue to silver flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and erosion control.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal in ecological landscaping.
