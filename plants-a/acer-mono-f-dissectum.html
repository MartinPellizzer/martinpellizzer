Acer mono f. dissectum

Acer mono f. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected maple, is a cultivar of the red maple tree.

It is characterized by its finely cut, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows to a height of 15 to 25 feet and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its vibrant autumn foliage adds significant visual interest to gardens and parks.
