Albizia henryi

Albizia henryi, commonly known as Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant, cream-colored flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

Albizia henryi is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration.
