Arnica attenuata

Arnica attenuata, commonly known as narrowleaf arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica attenuata is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
