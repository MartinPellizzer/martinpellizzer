Alternanthera kurtzii subsp. kurtzii

Alternanthera kurtzii subsp.

kurtzii, commonly known as the red-veined alternate, is a species of flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking red veins on its dark green leaves, which make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually pink or white and appear in clusters.
