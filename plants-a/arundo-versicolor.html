Arundo versicolor

Arundo versicolor, commonly known as the variable reed, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green color and a distinctive blue or purple tinge on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Arundo versicolor is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
