Aureolaria flava var. integrifolia

Aureolaria flava var. integrifolia, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically entire, giving it its varietal name "integrifolia.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in arid landscapes.
