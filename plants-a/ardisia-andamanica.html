Ardisia andamanica

Ardisia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many species of shrubs and trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries. The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit.
