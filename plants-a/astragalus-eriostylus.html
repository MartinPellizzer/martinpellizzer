Astragalus eriostylus

Astragalus eriostylus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Astragalus eriostylus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
