Astragalus reventoides

Astragalus reventoides, commonly known as the Andean milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Astragalus reventoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
