Albuca stapffii

Albuca stapffii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-like leaves that are often blue-green in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Albuca stapffii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
