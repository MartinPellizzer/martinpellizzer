Alloberberis haematocarpa

Alloberberis haematocarpa, commonly known as the red-stemmed berberis, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems, which become more pronounced with age, and its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste due to the presence of berberine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Alloberberis haematocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid landscapes.
