Asphodelus cerasifer f. virgatus

Asphodelus cerasifer f. virgatus, commonly known as the pink asphodel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asphodelaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking pink or purple flower spikes in the spring. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and low maintenance requirements.
