Arundinaria trinii

Arundinaria trinii, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and capable of rapid vegetative propagation.

Arundinaria trinii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and resilience.
