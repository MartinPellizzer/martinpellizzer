Anguloa clowesii var. macrantha

Anguloa clowesii var. macrantha, commonly known as the Macrantha Anguloa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white, with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
