Astragalus amherstianus

Astragalus amherstianus, commonly known as Amherst's milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus amherstianus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
