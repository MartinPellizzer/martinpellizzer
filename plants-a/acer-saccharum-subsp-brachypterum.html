Acer saccharum subsp. brachypterum

Acer saccharum subsp.

brachypterum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

The tree produces small, red flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained sites.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
