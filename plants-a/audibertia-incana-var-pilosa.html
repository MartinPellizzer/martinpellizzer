Audibertia incana var. pilosa

Audibertia incana var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly sandmat, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to thrive in sandy soils, making it a hardy species suited for xerophytic conditions.

Overall, it is an important plant in its native habitat for both ecological and potential medicinal uses.
