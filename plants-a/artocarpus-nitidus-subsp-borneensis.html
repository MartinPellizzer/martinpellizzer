Artocarpus nitidus subsp. borneensis

Artocarpus nitidus subsp.

borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the island of Borneo. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a unique fruit with a fibrous outer husk and a starchy, edible interior. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional dishes and as a source of nutrition.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
