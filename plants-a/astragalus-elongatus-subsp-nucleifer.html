Astragalus elongatus subsp. nucleifer

Astragalus elongatus subsp.

nucleifer, commonly known as the nutleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces distinctive, flattened seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
