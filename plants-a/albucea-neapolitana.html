Albucea neapolitana

Albucea neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is typically found growing in open, sunny areas. It is a climbing vine that produces edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Albucea neapolitana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
