Arenaria muscipula

Arenaria muscipula, commonly known as the sandwort catchfly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments.

Arenaria muscipula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in rocky or coastal areas.
