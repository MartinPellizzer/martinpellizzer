Agave guccifolia

Agave guccifolia, commonly known as the Guaycura agave, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Baja California and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often lined with spines. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences of yellow flowers, which bloom once in its lifetime. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Agave guccifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional practices.
