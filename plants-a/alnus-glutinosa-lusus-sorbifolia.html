Alnus glutinosa lusus sorbifolia

Alnus glutinosa lusus sorbifolia, commonly known as the sticky alder or sweet alder, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, sticky seeds and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or wetlands. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by fruiting bodies that release its sticky seeds.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
