Antennaria carpatica var. pulcherrima

Antennaria carpatica var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features delicate, mat-forming stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
