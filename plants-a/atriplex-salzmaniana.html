Atriplex salzmaniana

Atriplex salzmaniana, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, salt-tolerant leaves that help it thrive in saline soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the spring. Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, making it drought-resistant.

Atriplex salzmaniana plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
