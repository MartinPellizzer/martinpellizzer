Anplectrum suluense

Anplectrum suluense, commonly known as the Sulu spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and is adapted to humid tropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Anplectrum suluense plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal value.
