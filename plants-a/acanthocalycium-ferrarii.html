Acanthocalycium ferrarii

Acanthocalycium ferrarii, commonly known as the Ferrari's spiny cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, sharp spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, where it adapts to extreme conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
