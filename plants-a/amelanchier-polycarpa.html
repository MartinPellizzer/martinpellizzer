Amelanchier polycarpa

Amelanchier polycarpa, commonly known as the multi-fruited serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries in the summer, which are a food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Amelanchier polycarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
