Annona riparia

Annona riparia, commonly known as the river annona, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It typically grows near rivers and moist soils, thriving in humid, warm climates. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is edible, is similar in appearance to a pineapple but has a distinct, creamy flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
