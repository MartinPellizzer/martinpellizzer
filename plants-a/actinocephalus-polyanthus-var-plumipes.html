Actinocephalus polyanthus var. plumipes

Actinocephalus polyanthus var. plumipes, commonly known as the plumiped plum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a sweet, edible pulp and a hard, woody pit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive form and seasonal blooms.
