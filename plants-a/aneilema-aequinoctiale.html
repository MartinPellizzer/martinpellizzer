Aneilema aequinoctiale

Aneilema aequinoctiale, commonly known as the equinox lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its botanical classification places it in the family Commelinaceae, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants.

Aneilema aequinoctiale is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
