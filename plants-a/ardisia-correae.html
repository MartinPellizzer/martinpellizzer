Ardisia correae

Ardisia correae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Christmas berry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged oppositely on the stems.

Ardisia correae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit.
