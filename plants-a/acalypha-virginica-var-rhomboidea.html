Acalypha virginica var. rhomboidea

Acalypha virginica var. rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features alternate, rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage, though it can be invasive in some regions.
