Astrotricha parvifolia

Astrotricha parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and ecological significance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
