Astragalus lycaonicus

Astragalus lycaonicus, commonly known as the Lycaonia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in rocky or arid habitats. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution in suitable environments.
