Anthurium incurvatum

Anthurium incurvatum, commonly known as the curling anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that curl inward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-shaped inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium incurvatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
