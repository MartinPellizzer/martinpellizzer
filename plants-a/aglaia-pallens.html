Aglaia pallens

Aglaia pallens, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Aglaia pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.
