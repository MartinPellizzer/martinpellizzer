Aster albescens var. megaphyllus

Aster albescens var. megaphyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
