Aster okanoganus

Aster okanoganus, commonly known as Okanogan aster, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to purple flower heads in the summer. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
