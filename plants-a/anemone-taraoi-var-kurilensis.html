Anemone taraoi var. kurilensis

Anemone taraoi var. kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Kuril Islands.

It features showy, single or double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to native plant collections.
