Astragalus rarissimus

Astragalus rarissimus, commonly known as the rare milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant is notable for its extremely limited geographic distribution, making it one of the rarest species within the Astragalus genus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected by conservation efforts.
