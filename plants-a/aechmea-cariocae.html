Aechmea cariocae

Aechmea cariocae, commonly known as the Carioca Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant's foliage is typically dark green with a metallic sheen, and it produces vibrant red or orange inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and moderate light.

Aechmea cariocae is popular as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea cariocae has leaves that are sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette, with a bright green color and a waxy surface.

The plant has a short, thick stem that is often hidden beneath the leaves, with a smooth, dark green bark. Its inflorescence consists of clustered, showy flowers in shades of red and orange, blooming during the summer months, and is pollinated primarily by hummingbirds. The fruit is a bright red, fleshy capsule that disperses seeds through both wind and animal consumption.

The roots are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing moisture from the environment.
