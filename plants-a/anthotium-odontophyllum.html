Anthotium odontophyllum

Anthotium odontophyllum, commonly known as the toothed arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its modified leaves called bracts and its distinctive inflorescence. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, enclosed by a spathe. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, with a serrated or toothed margin, giving it its common name.

Anthotium odontophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
