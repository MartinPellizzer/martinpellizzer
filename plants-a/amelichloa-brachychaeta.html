Amelichloa brachychaeta

Amelichloa brachychaeta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand grass, is a species of grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
