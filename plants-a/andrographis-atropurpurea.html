Andrographis atropurpurea

Andrographis atropurpurea, commonly known as purple milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to reddish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect with a somewhat woody texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
