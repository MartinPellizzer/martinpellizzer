Arabis fendleri var. spatifolia

Arabis fendleri var. spatifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
