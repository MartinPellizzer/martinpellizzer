Aristida glumaris

Aristida glumaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf dropseed, is a flowering grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil.

Aristida glumaris is also noted for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
