Antennaria neglecta

Antennaria neglecta, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Antennaria neglecta is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
