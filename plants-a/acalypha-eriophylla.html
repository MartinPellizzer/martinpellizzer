Acalypha eriophylla

Acalypha eriophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often heart-shaped with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Acalypha eriophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
