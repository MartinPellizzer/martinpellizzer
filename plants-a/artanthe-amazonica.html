Artanthe amazonica

Artanthe amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in the humid environment.

Artanthe amazonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
