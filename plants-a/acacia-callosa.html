Acacia callosa

Acacia callosa, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are compound, with thread-like phyllodes that are rough to the touch, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
