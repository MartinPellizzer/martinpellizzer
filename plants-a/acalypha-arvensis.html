Acalypha arvensis

Acalypha arvensis, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on bracteate inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it an invasive species in some areas.
