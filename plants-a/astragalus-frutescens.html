Astragalus frutescens

Astragalus frutescens, commonly known as honeyvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Astragalus frutescens plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
