Astragalus angustifolius subsp. echinoides

Astragalus angustifolius subsp.

echinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves with a distinctive narrow, linear shape. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, forming pea-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
