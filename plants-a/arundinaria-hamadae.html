Arundinaria hamadae

Arundinaria hamadae, commonly known as the Hamada bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps. The plant typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Arundinaria hamadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional Japanese horticulture.
