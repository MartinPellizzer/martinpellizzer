Achillea barrelieri

Achillea barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
