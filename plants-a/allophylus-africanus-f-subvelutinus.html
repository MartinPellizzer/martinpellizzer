Allophylus africanus f. subvelutinus

Allophylus africanus f. subvelutinus, commonly known as the velvet-leaved alderleaf, is a variant of the African alderleaf plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture. This subspecies thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for taxonomic study and ecological research.
