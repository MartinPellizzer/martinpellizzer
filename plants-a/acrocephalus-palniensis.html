Acrocephalus palniensis

Acrocephalus palniensis, commonly known as the Palni reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India, particularly in the Palni Hills region. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Acrocephalus palniensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.
