Amomum zambesiacum

Amomum zambesiacum, commonly known as the Zambesia cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, herbaceous stem and large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, three-lobed flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by spherical, three-lobed fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils, particularly those containing cineole, are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species, prompting conservation efforts in its native regions.
