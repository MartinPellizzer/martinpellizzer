Arrabidaea obliqua var. hirsuta

Arrabidaea obliqua var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy arrabidaea, is a perennial shrub native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
