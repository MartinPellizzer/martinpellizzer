Adesmia muricata var. gilliesii

Adesmia muricata var. gilliesii, commonly known as the Gillies' hairy legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
