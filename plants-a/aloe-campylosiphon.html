Aloe campylosiphon

Aloe campylosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. A. campylosiphon is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and having a slow growth rate.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
