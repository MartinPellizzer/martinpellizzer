Acalypha commersoniana f. discolor

Acalypha commersoniana f. discolor, commonly known as the variegated ironweed, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and pink hues on its leaves, which create a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays due to its vibrant coloration and ease of cultivation.
