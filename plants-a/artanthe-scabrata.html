Artanthe scabrata

Artanthe scabrata, commonly known as the rough artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, often grayish leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is often described as rough to the touch, giving it its common name.

Artanthe scabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
