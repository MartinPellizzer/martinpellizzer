Aconitum napellus var. semigaleatum

Aconitum napellus var. semigaleatum, commonly known as the semi-galeate monkshood, is a variant of the European monkshood plant.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers arranged in a tall, spire-like inflorescence. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments, typical of the genus Aconitum. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Europe.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
