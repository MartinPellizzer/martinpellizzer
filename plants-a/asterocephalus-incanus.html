Asterocephalus incanus

Asterocephalus incanus, commonly known as the white ghost flower, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white, fluffy blooms that resemble a ghostly apparition, hence its common name. The plant is native to arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, silver-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Asterocephalus incanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
