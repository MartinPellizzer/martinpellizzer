Asperula oligantha var. conferta-elongata

Asperula oligantha var. conferta-elongata, commonly known as the clustered or elongated evergreen azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and remain evergreen throughout the year. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
