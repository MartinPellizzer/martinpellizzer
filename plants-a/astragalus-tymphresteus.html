Astragalus tymphresteus

Astragalus tymphresteus, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Astragalus tymphresteus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
