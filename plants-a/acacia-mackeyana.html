Acacia mackeyana

Acacia mackeyana, commonly known as the Mackey's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia mackeyana is often found in coastal and sandy soils, contributing to local ecological diversity.
