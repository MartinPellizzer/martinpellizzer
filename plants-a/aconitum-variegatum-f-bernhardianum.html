Aconitum variegatum f. bernhardianum

Aconitum variegatum f. bernhardianum, commonly known as the Bernhard's variegated monkshood, is a cultivar of the monkshood plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored leaves, featuring a mix of green and white, and its tall, spiky blue-purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and dramatic floral display.
