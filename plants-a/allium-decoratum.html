Allium decoratum

Allium decoratum, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, purple flower heads that resemble small globes, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Allium decoratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
