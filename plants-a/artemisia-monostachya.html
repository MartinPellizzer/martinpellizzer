Artemisia monostachya

Artemisia monostachya, commonly known as silverleaf wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are clustered in a single, elongated inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Artemisia monostachya is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
