Annona cherimolioides var. amplifolia

Annona cherimolioides var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, green, spiky berry that ripens to a yellowish color.

The leaves and fruit are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
