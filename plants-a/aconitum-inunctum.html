Aconitum inunctum

Aconitum inunctum, commonly known as the alpine aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes that are often covered in fine hairs.

Aconitum inunctum is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
