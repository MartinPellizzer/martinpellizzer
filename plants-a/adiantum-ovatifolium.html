Adiantum ovatifolium

Adiantum ovatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, ovate-shaped fronds that are typically green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a reputation for being relatively easy to care for in a home setting. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.

Adiantum ovatifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
