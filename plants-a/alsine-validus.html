Alsine validus

Alsine validus, commonly known as the slender alsike, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically smooth and lance-shaped. The plant produces numerous tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils in meadows and along roadsides.

Alsine validus is a short-lived perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
