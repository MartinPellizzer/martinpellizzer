Allium striatellum

Allium striatellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garlic.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful crop in some regions.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
