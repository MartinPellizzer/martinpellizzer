Allium przewalskianum var. planifolium

Allium przewalskianum var. planifolium, commonly known as the flat-leaf garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in culinary applications, similar to other Allium species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and edible qualities make it a valued plant in both gardens and traditional cuisines.
