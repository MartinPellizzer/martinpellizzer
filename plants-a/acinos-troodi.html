Acinos troodi

Acinos troodi, commonly known as the alpine aster, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Acinos troodi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
