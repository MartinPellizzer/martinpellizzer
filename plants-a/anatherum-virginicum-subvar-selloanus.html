Anatherum virginicum subvar. selloanus

Anatherum virginicum subvar. selloanus, commonly known as the Selloan panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread and survival in wet environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
