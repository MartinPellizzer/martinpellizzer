Amaracarpus compactus

Amaracarpus compactus, commonly known as the compact umbrella tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy of dark green, leathery leaves that give it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Amaracarpus compactus is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
