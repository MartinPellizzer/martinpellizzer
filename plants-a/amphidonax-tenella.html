Amphidonax tenella

Amphidonax tenella, commonly known as the small silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and forest edges. The plant has slender, silver-gray blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often overlooked. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a clump-forming growth habit.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
