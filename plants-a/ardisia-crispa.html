Ardisia crispa

Ardisia crispa, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Ardisia crispa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It has a compact growth habit and is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for gardens and landscaping.
