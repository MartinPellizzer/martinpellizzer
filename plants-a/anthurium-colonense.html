Anthurium colonense

Anthurium colonense, commonly known as the Colombian anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant spadix flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Anthurium colonense requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
