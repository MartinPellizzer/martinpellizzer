Aristida asplundii

Aristida asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
