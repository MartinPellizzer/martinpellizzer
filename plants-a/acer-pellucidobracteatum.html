Acer pellucidobracteatum

Acer pellucidobracteatum, commonly known as the translucent bract maple, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, with leaves that are often tinged with a pale, translucent hue, especially along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive samaras that have elongated, winged seeds. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and dense branching.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
