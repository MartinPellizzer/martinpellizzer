Anthemis montana var. chrysocephala

Anthemis montana var. chrysocephala, commonly known as the goldenhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are often reddish in color.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like appearance.
