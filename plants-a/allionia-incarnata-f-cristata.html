Allionia incarnata f. cristata

Allionia incarnata f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate Indian pink, is a unique cultivar of the Indian pink plant.

It is characterized by its striking, crinkled, and ruffled foliage, which gives it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
