Amaryllis × compacta

Amaryllis × compacta, commonly known as the compact amaryllis, is a hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papillosa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, making it ideal for container gardening. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

Amaryllis × compacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
