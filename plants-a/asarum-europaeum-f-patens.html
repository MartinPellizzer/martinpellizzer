Asarum europaeum f. patens

Asarum europaeum f. patens, commonly known as the spreading European wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
