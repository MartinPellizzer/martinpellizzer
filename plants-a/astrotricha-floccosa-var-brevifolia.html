Astrotricha floccosa var. brevifolia

Astrotricha floccosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved starry aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, white flower heads that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety brevifolia is distinguished by its shorter leaves compared to the typical form of Astrotricha floccosa.
