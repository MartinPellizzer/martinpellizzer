Ageratina kupperi

Ageratina kupperi, commonly known as the Kupper's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ageratina kupperi is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
