Astragalus solandri var. bubaloceras

Astragalus solandri var. bubaloceras, commonly known as the Bubaloceras milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is often found in steppe and desert margins, contributing to local biodiversity.
