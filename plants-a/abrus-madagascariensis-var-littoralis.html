Abrus madagascariensis var. littoralis

Abrus madagascariensis var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to coastal regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red and black seed pods, which contain toxic seeds used in traditional practices. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are compound and trifoliate, with a velvety texture.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization along coastlines.
