Acer obtusatum var. anomalum

Acer obtusatum var. anomalum, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically red or orange in the fall. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but its vibrant foliage makes it a popular choice in gardens.
