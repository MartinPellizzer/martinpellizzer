Ayenia euphrasiifolia subsp. euphrasiifolia

Ayenia euphrasiifolia subsp.

euphrasiifolia, commonly known as the showy ayenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable addition to native plant gardens.
