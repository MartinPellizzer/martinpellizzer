Arisaema sinanoense

Arisaema sinanoense, commonly known as the Japanese arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a metallic sheen. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
