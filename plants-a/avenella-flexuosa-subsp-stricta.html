Avenella flexuosa subsp. stricta

Avenella flexuosa subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf avenella, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, erect stems that are often branched.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks in arid regions.
