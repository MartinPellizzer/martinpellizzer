Alstroemeria foliosa var. humilior

Alstroemeria foliosa var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf alstroemeria, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
