Arisaema smithii

Arisaema smithii, commonly known as Smith's arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and have a rough texture, while the spathe is usually green or reddish in color.

Arisaema smithii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowering structure.
