Androsaemum vulgare

Androsaemum vulgare, commonly known as the common cow parsnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped basal leaves and finely divided upper leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

The plant contains compounds such as sesquiterpene lactones, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
