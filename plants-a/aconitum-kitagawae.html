Aconitum kitagawae

Aconitum kitagawae, commonly known as the Japanese monkshood, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces follicular fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
