Aquilegia pyrenaica

Aquilegia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with five petals and two elongated nectar spurs. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces follicles as its fruit type. It thrives in woodland and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Aquilegia pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
