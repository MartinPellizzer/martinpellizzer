Acer opalus var. granatense

Acer opalus var. granatense, commonly known as the Granate Maple, is a variant of the Opalus Maple native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange-red foliage, which becomes more vivid in the fall. The plant features palmate leaves with five lobes, and its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or grayish hue. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The Granate Maple is relatively slow-growing and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.
