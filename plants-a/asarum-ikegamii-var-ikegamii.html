Asarum ikegamii var. ikegamii

Asarum ikegamii var. ikegamii, commonly known as the Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive, mace-like berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest undergrowth.

Its aromatic rhizomes have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
