Artemisia borealis var. purshii

Artemisia borealis var. purshii, commonly known as white artemisia, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and aromatic, bitter scent. The plant typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

This species plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
