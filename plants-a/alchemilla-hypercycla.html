Alchemilla hypercycla

Alchemilla hypercycla, commonly known as the mountain lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its purported medicinal properties.

Alchemilla hypercycla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
