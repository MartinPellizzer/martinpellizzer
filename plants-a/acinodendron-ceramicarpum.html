Acinodendron ceramicarpum

Acinodendron ceramicarpum, commonly known as the ceramic-fruit tree, is a rare and unique plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often colorful fruits that resemble small ceramic vessels, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
