Acoridium latifolium

Acoridium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in various cuisines.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
