Anchusa exasperata

Anchusa exasperata, commonly known as the spurred bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa exasperata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
