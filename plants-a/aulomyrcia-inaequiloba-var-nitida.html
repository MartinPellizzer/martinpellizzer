Aulomyrcia inaequiloba var. nitida

Aulomyrcia inaequiloba var. nitida, commonly known as the glossy alderleaf, is a variant of the alderleaf plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or near water bodies. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in catkins, and it produces small, winged fruits for seed dispersal.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
