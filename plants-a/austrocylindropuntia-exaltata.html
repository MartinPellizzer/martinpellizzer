Austrocylindropuntia exaltata

Austrocylindropuntia exaltata, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
