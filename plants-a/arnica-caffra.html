Arnica caffra

Arnica caffra, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, and its basal leaves are hairy and lobed. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Arnica caffra is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
