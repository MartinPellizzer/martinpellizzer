Allenia blackiana var. microphylla

Allenia blackiana var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Allenia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.
