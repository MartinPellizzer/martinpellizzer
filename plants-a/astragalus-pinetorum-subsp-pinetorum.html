Astragalus pinetorum subsp. pinetorum

Astragalus pinetorum subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open woodlands and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has adapted to harsh, arid environments through its deep root system and drought tolerance.
