Andropogon amplus

Andropogon amplus, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stature and wide, flat leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Andropogon amplus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
