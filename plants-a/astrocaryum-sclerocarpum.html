Astrocaryum sclerocarpum

Astrocaryum sclerocarpum, commonly known as the Brazilian buriti, is a tall, palm-like tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its large, fibrous fruits, which contain edible seeds and are an important food source for local wildlife. The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.

Its growth is typically found in humid, lowland areas with rich, well-drained soils.
