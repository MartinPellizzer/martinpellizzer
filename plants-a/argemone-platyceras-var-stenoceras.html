Argemone platyceras var. stenoceras

Argemone platyceras var. stenoceras, commonly known as the narrow-lobed prickly poppy, is a variant of the prickly poppy species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain oily seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, glandular leaves, and a distinctive floral structure.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.
