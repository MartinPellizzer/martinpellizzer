Acacia discolor var. unijuga

Acacia discolor var. unijuga, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has small, flat, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sand dunes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
