Alsine fimbriata

Alsine fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed carpetweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters and features small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed or toothed edge. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Alsine fimbriata is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
