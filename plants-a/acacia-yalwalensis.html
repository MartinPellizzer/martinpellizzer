Acacia yalwalensis

Acacia yalwalensis, commonly known as the Yalwal wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its yellow flowers are often clustered in cylindrical spikes, adding ornamental value.

Acacia yalwalensis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
