Avena insubrica

Avena insubrica, commonly known as the slender oat, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces fluffy, white or pale blue seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
