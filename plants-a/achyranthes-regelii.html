Achyranthes regelii

Achyranthes regelii, commonly known as the red root or Chinese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Achyranthes regelii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
