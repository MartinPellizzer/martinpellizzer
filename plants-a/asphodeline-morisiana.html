Asphodeline morisiana

Asphodeline morisiana, commonly known as the Morisiana asphodel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
