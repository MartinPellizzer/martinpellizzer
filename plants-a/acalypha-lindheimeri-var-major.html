Acalypha lindheimeri var. major

Acalypha lindheimeri var. major, commonly known as the Lindheimer's spurflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that are often red or purple in color, which serve to attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in spikes, which are borne on the ends of the branches.
