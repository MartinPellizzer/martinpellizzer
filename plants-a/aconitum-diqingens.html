Aconitum diqingens

Aconitum diqingens, commonly known as the Yunnan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are palmately compound with serrated edges. Aconitum diqingens is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and studied for its chemical composition.
