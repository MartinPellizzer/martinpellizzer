Ascolepis protea subsp. chrysocephala

Ascolepis protea subsp.

chrysocephala, commonly known as the golden-headed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous pale yellow to golden flowers arranged in a spherical shape.

The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
