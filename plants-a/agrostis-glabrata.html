Agrostis glabrata

Agrostis glabrata, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Agrostis glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
