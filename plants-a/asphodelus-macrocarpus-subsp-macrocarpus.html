Asphodelus macrocarpus subsp. macrocarpus

Asphodelus macrocarpus subsp.

macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded asphodel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with pale yellow to orange flowers. Its bulbs are fleshy and serve as storage organs, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
