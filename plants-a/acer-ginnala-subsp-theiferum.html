Acer ginnala subsp. theiferum

Acer ginnala subsp.

theiferum, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental tree in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage and graceful form.
