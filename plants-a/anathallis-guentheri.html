Anathallis guentheri

Anathallis guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinct, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Anathallis guentheri is a rare and delicate plant, often found in humid, shaded environments where it thrives in the cool, misty conditions of its natural habitat.
