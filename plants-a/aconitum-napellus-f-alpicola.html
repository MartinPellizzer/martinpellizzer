Aconitum napellus f. alpicola

Aconitum napellus f. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a poisonous plant and requires careful handling.
