Anguloa superba

Anguloa superba, commonly known as the royal anguloa, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically arranged in a racemose inflorescence, with each bloom featuring a distinct lip that aids in pollination. Anguloa superba thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a pollinator attractant and its ornamental value in horticulture.
