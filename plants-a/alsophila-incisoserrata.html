Alsophila incisoserrata

Alsophila incisoserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive serrated edge that gives it its specific epithet.

Alsophila incisoserrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types in shaded environments.
