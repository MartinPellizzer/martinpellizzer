Adenospermum tuberculatum

Adenospermum tuberculatum, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides.

Adenospermum tuberculatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
