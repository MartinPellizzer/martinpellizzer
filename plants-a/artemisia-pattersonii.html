Artemisia pattersonii

Artemisia pattersonii, commonly known as Patterson's wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It contains essential oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic properties.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators in its native range.
