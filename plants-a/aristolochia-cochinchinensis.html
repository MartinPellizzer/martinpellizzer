Aristolochia cochinchinensis

Aristolochia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Indochina Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are often greenish or brownish in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The genus Aristolochia is known for its complex chemical composition, including various alkaloids.
