Allium kossoricum var. araratense

Allium kossoricum var. araratense, commonly known as the Ararat garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
