Allium szurulense

Allium szurulense, commonly known as the Szurulensk garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces large, rounded bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and medicinal research.
