Allium humile

Allium humile, commonly known as the slender leek, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Allium humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
