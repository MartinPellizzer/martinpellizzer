Alsodeia cinerea var. hirsutiflora

Alsodeia cinerea var. hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered alsodeia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are characteristic of its varietal name, hirsutiflora. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
