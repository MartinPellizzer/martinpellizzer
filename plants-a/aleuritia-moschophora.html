Aleuritia moschophora

Aleuritia moschophora, commonly known as the musk aleurita, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Aleuritia moschophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic qualities.
