Alstroemeria dulcis

Alstroemeria dulcis, commonly known as the sweet alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing showy blooms from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Alstroemeria dulcis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
