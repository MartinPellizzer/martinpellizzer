Alternanthera pulverulenta

Alternanthera pulverulenta, commonly known as the dustymoon plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from pink to purple, which darken as the plant matures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
