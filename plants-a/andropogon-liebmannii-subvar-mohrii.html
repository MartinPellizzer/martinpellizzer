Andropogon liebmannii subvar. mohrii

Andropogon liebmannii subvar. mohrii, commonly known as Mohri's bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
