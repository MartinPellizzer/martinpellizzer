Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. smaragdinum

Acer pseudoplatanus subvar. smaragdinum, commonly known as the Emerald Queen maple, is a rare variant of the sycamore maple.

It is characterized by its striking, bright green leaves that remain vibrant throughout the growing season. This subvariety is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and Alps. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard sycamore maple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
