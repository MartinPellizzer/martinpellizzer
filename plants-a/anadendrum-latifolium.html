Anadendrum latifolium

Anadendrum latifolium, commonly known as the Texas buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
