Andrographis lobelioides

Andrographis lobelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf andrographis, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Andrographis lobelioides is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
