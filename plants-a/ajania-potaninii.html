Ajania potaninii

Ajania potaninii, commonly known as the alpine ajania, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and have a distinctive blue-green hue with a velvety texture.

Ajania potaninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
