Arctotis canaliculata

Arctotis canaliculata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble misty clouds. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Arctotis canaliculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
