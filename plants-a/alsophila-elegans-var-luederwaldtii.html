Alsophila elegans var. luederwaldtii

Alsophila elegans var. luederwaldtii, commonly known as the Luederwaldt's tree fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are pinnate, with a soft texture and a delicate, almost translucent quality.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
