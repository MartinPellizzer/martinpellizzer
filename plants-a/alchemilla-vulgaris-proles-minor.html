Alchemilla vulgaris proles minor

Alchemilla vulgaris proles minor, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, basal leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
