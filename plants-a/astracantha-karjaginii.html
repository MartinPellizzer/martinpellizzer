Astracantha karjaginii

Astracantha karjaginii, commonly known as the Karjagin's thornveld, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its drought-resistant features. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Astracantha karjaginii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
