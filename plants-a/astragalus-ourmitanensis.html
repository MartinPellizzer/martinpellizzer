Astragalus ourmitanensis

Astragalus ourmitanensis, commonly known as the Ourmitan milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to adapt to extreme climates and support local biodiversity.
