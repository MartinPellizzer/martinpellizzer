Aesculus × rubicunda briotii

Aesculus × rubicunda briotii, commonly known as the Red Horse Chestnut, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, red-bracted flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in upright panicles. The fruit is a spiny capsule that contains one or two chestnut-like seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks.
