Arctostaphylos stanfordiana var. repens

Arctostaphylos stanfordiana var. repens, commonly known as creeping manzanita, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to California.

It features small, oval, leathery leaves that are typically reddish-brown to gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink during the spring. Its fruit is a small, leathery capsule that contains several seeds.

This variety is well adapted to rocky soils and is often found in coastal and chaparral habitats.
