Aspilia pascaloides

Aspilia pascaloides, commonly known as the Pascal's spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads that resemble a spider's web. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Aspilia pascaloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its unique appearance.
