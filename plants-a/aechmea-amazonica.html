Aechmea amazonica

Aechmea amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian urn plant, is a bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and they can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom from the center of the rosette.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Morphology

Aechmea amazonica has leaves that are typically sword-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern, and can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a waxy, dark green color.

The stem is short and thick, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that grows upward from the base of the plant. Its flowers are clustered in a spike at the center of the rosette, featuring bright red bracts with white petals, blooming during the dry season and pollinated by wind and insects. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense mat around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb moisture from the soil.
