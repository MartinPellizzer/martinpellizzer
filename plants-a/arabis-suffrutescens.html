Arabis suffrutescens

Arabis suffrutescens, commonly known as rock白菜, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Arabis suffrutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
