Aspilia ioletae

Aspilia ioletae, commonly known as the Ioleth's aster, is a flowering plant native to the island of Ioleth in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are often mistaken for a single flower. Aspilia ioletae is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in coastal environments with sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
