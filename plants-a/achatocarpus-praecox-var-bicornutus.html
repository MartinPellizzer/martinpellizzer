Achatocarpus praecox var. bicornutus

Achatocarpus praecox var. bicornutus, commonly known as the two-horned acacia, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, typical of the legume family.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
