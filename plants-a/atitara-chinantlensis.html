Atitara chinantlensis

Atitara chinantlensis, commonly known as the Chinantla atitara, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Atitara chinantlensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.
