Aloysia chamaedryoides

Aloysia chamaedryoides, commonly known as Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and coastal landscaping.
