Arabis suecica

Arabis suecica, commonly known as Swedish rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic regions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Arabis suecica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
