Ammannia pedroi

Ammannia pedroi, commonly known as the Brazilian water spinach, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender, edible leaves that grow on long, trailing stems. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped with a smooth surface and can vary in color from light green to reddish.

Ammannia pedroi is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
