Acanthocalycium oreopepon

Acanthocalycium oreopepon, commonly known as the Oaxaca nightshade, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming at night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
