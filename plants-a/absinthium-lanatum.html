Absinthium lanatum

Absinthium lanatum, commonly known as woolly wormwood, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in complex inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic properties are attributed to compounds such as thujone and other essential oils.

Absinthium lanatum is often found in dry, sunny habitats like meadows and rocky slopes.
