Arundinella purpurea

Arundinella purpurea, commonly known as purple bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, hollow stems that are typically purple to pink in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can be both a valuable ornamental plant and an invasive species in certain regions.
