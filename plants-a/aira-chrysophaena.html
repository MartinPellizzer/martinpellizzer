Aira chrysophaena

Aira chrysophaena, commonly known as the golden airgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Aira chrysophaena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
