Appendicula buxifolia

Appendicula buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf appendicula, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with leaves that are oval and slightly waxy, giving it a compact and tidy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
