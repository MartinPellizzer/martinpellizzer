Alsodeia macrocarpa

Alsodeia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited alsodeia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
