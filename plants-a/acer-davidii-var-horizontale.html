Acer davidii var. horizontale

Acer davidii var. horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive horizontal branching pattern, which gives it a unique and elegant appearance. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a bright green color that turns red or orange in the fall. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
