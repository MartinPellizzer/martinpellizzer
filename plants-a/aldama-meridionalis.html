Aldama meridionalis

Aldama meridionalis, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy blooms during the spring and summer months.
