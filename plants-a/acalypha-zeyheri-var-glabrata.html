Acalypha zeyheri var. glabrata

Acalypha zeyheri var. glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or glossy abutilon, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, palmate leaves that are often a deep green with a soft texture. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
