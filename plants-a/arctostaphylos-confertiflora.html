Arctostaphylos confertiflora

Arctostaphylos confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower manzanita, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and chaparral environments.
