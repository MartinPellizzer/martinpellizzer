Acacia incana

Acacia incana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly branches and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Acacia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
