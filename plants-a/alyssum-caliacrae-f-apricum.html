Alyssum caliacrae f. apricum

Alyssum caliacrae f. apricum, commonly known as the apricot-flowered alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
