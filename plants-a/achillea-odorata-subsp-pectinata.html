Achillea odorata subsp. pectinata

Achillea odorata subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the fragrant yarrow, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are finely divided and aromatic.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in gardens.
