Aster oblongifolius

Aster oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces numerous small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and slightly toothed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Aster oblongifolius is typically found in moist habitats such as wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
