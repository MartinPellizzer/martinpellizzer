Antirrhinum braun-blanquetii

Antirrhinum braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet's snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of brightly colored, tubular flowers that typically range from red to orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Antirrhinum braun-blanquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
