Aconitum toxicum subsp. bosniacum

Aconitum toxicum subsp.

bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers.

The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its high toxicity due to the presence of potent alkaloids.

Despite its dangerous properties, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
