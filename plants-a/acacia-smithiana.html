Acacia smithiana

Acacia smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Acacia smithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
