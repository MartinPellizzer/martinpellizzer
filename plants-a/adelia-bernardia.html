Adelia bernardia

Adelia bernardia, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and light brown, with a tendency to flake in older trees.

Adelia bernardia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
