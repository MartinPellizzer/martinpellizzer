Annona neochrysocarpa

Annona neochrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden star soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, green fruits with a spiny exterior. The fruit's interior contains a white, creamy pulp surrounded by a layer of fibrous material, and it is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor.

Annona neochrysocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
