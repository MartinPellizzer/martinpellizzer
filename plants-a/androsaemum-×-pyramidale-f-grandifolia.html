Androsaemum × pyramidale f. grandifolia

Androsaemum × pyramidale f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered white honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, often covered in fine hairs. It is typically found in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
