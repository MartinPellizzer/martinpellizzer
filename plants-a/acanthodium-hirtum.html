Acanthodium hirtum

Acanthodium hirtum, commonly known as the hairy acanthodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
