Astragalus funereus

Astragalus funereus, commonly known as the death camis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
