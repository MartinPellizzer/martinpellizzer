Asplenium fontanum subsp. fontanum

Asplenium fontanum subsp.

fontanum, commonly known as the Fontana's spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices or on tree trunks.

The plant has slender, dark green fronds with a delicate, lace-like appearance. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is notable for its adaptability to various microhabitats and its role in forest ecosystems.
