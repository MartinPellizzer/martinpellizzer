Athanasia virgata

Athanasia virgata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

Athanasia virgata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
