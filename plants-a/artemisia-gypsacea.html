Artemisia gypsacea

Artemisia gypsacea, commonly known as big sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of small, aromatic leaves with a distinctive pungent aroma. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Artemisia gypsacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
