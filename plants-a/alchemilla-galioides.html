Alchemilla galioides

Alchemilla galioides, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Alchemilla galioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
