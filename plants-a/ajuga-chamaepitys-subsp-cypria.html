Ajuga chamaepitys subsp. cypria

Ajuga chamaepitys subsp.

cypria, commonly known as Cyprian bugleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is often used in ecological landscaping.
