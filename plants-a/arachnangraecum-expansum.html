Arachnangraecum expansum

Arachnangraecum expansum, commonly known as the broadleaf spider plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescences that resemble intricate webs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Native to South Africa, this plant is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
