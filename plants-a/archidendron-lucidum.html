Archidendron lucidum

Archidendron lucidum, commonly known as the shining legume, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow or white, followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
