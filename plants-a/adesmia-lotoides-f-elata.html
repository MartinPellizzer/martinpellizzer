Adesmia lotoides f. elata

Adesmia lotoides f. elata, commonly known as the slender lotus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its root system is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
