Alisma sagittifolium

Alisma sagittifolium, commonly known as arrowleaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early summer.

Alisma sagittifolium plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
