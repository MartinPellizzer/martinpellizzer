Aglaia canariifolia

Aglaia canariifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release bright red, fleshy seeds.

Aglaia canariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
