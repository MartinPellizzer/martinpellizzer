Astragalus tarletonis

Astragalus tarletonis, commonly known as Tarleton's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the Great Basin and surrounding regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
