Aster cabulicus

Aster cabulicus, commonly known as the Persian aster, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Aster cabulicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
