Aristolochia micrantha

Aristolochia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered Dutchman's pipe, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular, maroon-colored flowers that resemble a pipe, with a distinct hooded shape. The plant produces small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
