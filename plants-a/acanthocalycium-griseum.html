Acanthocalycium griseum

Acanthocalycium griseum, commonly known as the gray cactus or the Mexican nightshade cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a layer of grayish-white wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
