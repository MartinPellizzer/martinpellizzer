Anthocleista urbaniana

Anthocleista urbaniana, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are follicles that release seeds with tufts of hair. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
