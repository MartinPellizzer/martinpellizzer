Amarenus schreberi

Amarenus schreberi, commonly known as the Schreber's amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant red or orange bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding visual appeal. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Amarenus schreberi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
