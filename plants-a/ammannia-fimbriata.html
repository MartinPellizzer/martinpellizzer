Ammannia fimbriata

Ammannia fimbriata, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

Ammannia fimbriata is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
