Allium myrianthum

Allium myrianthum, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.

Allium myrianthum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
