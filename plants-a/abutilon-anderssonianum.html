Abutilon anderssonianum

Abutilon anderssonianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.

Abutilon anderssonianum prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for warm climate gardens.
