Aporum scirpoides

Aporum scirpoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow from rhizomes, often forming dense clumps in marshy or waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, with a smooth surface and parallel veins.

Aporum scirpoides plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
