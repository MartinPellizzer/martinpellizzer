Arrabidaea rhodantha var. praecox

Arrabidaea rhodantha var. praecox, commonly known as the early pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
