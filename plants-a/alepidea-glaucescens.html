Alepidea glaucescens

Alepidea glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebark tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-green foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody trunk and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is adapted to dry conditions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Alepidea glaucescens is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
