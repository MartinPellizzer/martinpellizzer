Abeliophyllum distichum f. eburneum

Abeliophyllum distichum f. eburneum, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its finely textured, silvery-gray foliage.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and emit a subtle, fragrant aroma. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
