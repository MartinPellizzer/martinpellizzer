Avonia quinaria subsp. alstonii

Avonia quinaria subsp.

alstonii, commonly known as the Alston's avonia, is a flowering plant species native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its attractive blooms and adaptability to various garden environments.
