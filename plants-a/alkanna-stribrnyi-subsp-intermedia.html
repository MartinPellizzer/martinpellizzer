Alkanna stribrnyi subsp. intermedia

Alkanna stribrnyi subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red ink plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family. It is native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive red, woody roots that exude a reddish dye when cut. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in natural dyeing processes.
