Alsine recurva proles thevenaei

Alsine recurva proles thevenaei, commonly known as the slender alsike.

This annual herb is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in the summer months. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
