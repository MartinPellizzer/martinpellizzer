Acacia hyaloneura

Acacia hyaloneura, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. Acacia hyaloneura is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
