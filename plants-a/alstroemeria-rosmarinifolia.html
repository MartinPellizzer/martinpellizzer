Alstroemeria rosmarinifolia

Alstroemeria rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that resemble rosemary, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Alstroemeria rosmarinifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in bouquets and as a border plant.
