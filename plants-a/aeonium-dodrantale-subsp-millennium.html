Aeonium dodrantale subsp. millennium

Aeonium dodrantale subsp.

millennium, commonly known as the Millennium Aeonium, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features striking, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues along the edges.

The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
