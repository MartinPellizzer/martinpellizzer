Astragalus lentilobus f. subalpinus

Astragalus lentilobus f. subalpinus, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting features such as a compact growth form and deep root systems to withstand harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in alpine ecosystems.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to cold, arid climates.
