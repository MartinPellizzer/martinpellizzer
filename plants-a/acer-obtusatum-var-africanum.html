Acer obtusatum var. africanum

Acer obtusatum var. africanum, commonly known as the African maple, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are typically green on top and grayish-white underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
