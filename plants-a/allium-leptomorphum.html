Allium leptomorphum

Allium leptomorphum, commonly known as the slender leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers on slender stems during the spring and summer months. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Allium leptomorphum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
