Artocarpus paloensis

Artocarpus paloensis, commonly known as the Palos Island breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when mature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and has a fibrous, starchy pulp. The plant is valued for its edible fruit and is often found in lowland forests.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
