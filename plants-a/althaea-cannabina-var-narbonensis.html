Althaea cannabina var. narbonensis

Althaea cannabina var. narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonne marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its mucilaginous sap has been historically used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.
