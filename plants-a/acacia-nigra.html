Acacia nigra

Acacia nigra, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and long, slender phyllodes that are usually green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by flat, woody pods. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia nigra is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
