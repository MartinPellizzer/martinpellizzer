Anagallis coerulea

Anagallis coerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anagallis coerulea is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
