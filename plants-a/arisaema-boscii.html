Arisaema boscii

Arisaema boscii, commonly known as the Bosc's arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous underground stem and produces striking, often colorful bracts that attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Arisaema boscii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
