Allium subhirsutum var. brachystemon

Allium subhirsutum var. brachystemon, commonly known as the woolly leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears umbels of small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
