Atylosia cajanifolia

Atylosia cajanifolia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces bright orange to red ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Atylosia cajanifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
