Allium sativum var. vulgare

Allium sativum var. vulgare, commonly known as garlic, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia and widely cultivated for its pungent cloves.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous, layered bulbs composed of numerous small, white to purple cloves. The plant produces basal leaves that are linear and hollow, and its flowering stem bears clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Garlic is rich in sulfur compounds, which contribute to its distinctive aroma and medicinal properties.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
