Acacia salicina var. varians

Acacia salicina var. varians, commonly known as the River Red Gum, is a variable form of the Australian native acacia species.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed habit and long, pendulous phyllodes that can vary in shape and size. The plant typically grows along watercourses and in open woodlands, adapting to a range of soil types but preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or cream, appearing in globular or cylindrical clusters, and it produces hard, woody pods that contain seeds.

This species is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
