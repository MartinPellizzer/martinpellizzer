Arctotheca populifolia

Arctotheca populifolia, commonly known as the "skeggs" or "poplar-leaved arctotheca," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, rounded leaves that resemble those of a poplar tree, hence its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Arctotheca populifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
