Artemisia pallasii

Artemisia pallasii, commonly known as Pallas' wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats. Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The essential oils derived from its leaves contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
