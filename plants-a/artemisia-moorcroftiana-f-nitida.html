Artemisia moorcroftiana f. nitida

Artemisia moorcroftiana f. nitida, commonly known as the silver artemisia, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
