Acantholimon bromifolium var. iranicum

Acantholimon bromifolium var. iranicum, commonly known as the Iranian thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants adapted to dry environments. The plant features erect, branched stems covered with dense, woolly hairs, and its leaves are typically narrow and linear. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

This variety is noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy plant in harsh climates.
