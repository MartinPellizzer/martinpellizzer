Azalea vaccinioides

Azalea vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf azalea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions with acidic, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Azalea vaccinioides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
