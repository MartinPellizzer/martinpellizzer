Adenandra uniflora var. pubescens

Adenandra uniflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy adenandra, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, white, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" designation. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
