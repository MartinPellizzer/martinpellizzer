Ardisia copelandii

Ardisia copelandii, commonly known as the Christmas berry or glossy abelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is glossy and dark green, and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various climates.

Ardisia copelandii prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
