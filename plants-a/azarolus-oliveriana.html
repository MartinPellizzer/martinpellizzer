Azarolus oliveriana

Azarolus oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's azarolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Azarolus oliveriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal climates.
