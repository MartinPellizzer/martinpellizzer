Athanasia imbricata

Athanasia imbricata, commonly known as the Cape ironwood, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by small, round fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Athanasia imbricata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
