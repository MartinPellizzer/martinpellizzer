Angraecum pulchellum

Angraecum pulchellum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with prominent purple or red markings. The plant produces a single, tall inflorescence bearing numerous fragrant flowers, which bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Angraecum pulchellum is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
