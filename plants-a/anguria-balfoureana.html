Anguria balfoureana

Anguria balfoureana, commonly known as the Balfour's cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits resembling cucumbers. It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
