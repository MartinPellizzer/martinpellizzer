Aster nelsonii

Aster nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster nelsonii is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid environments.
