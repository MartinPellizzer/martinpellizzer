Adiantum reniforme subsp. reniforme

Adiantum reniforme subsp.

reniforme, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a fern species characterized by its soft, velvety fronds. It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is native to South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant features delicate, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of trichomes, giving it a silky texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
