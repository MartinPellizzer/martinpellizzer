Asplenium latedens

Asplenium latedens, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often a deep green color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of roots, which help anchor it to its substrate.

Asplenium latedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
