Asparagus subscandens

Asparagus subscandens, commonly known as the climbing asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length, often twining around supports. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its berries are red and contain toxic seeds, making it a plant to handle with care.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
