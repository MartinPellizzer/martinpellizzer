Aristida multiramea

Aristida multiramea, commonly known as the slender wiregrass, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
