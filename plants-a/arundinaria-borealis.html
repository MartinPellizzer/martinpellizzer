Arundinaria borealis

Arundinaria borealis, commonly known as the northern bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread through vegetative propagation.

Arundinaria borealis is well adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.
