Aralia calyculata

Aralia calyculata, commonly known as the Mexican giant aralia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features large, compound leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive palmate shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
