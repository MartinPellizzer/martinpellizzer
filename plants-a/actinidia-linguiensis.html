Actinidia linguiensis

Actinidia linguiensis, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that ripen to a dark red or black color, containing seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
