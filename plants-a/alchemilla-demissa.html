Alchemilla demissa

Alchemilla demissa, commonly known as lesser lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal medicine.
