Abarema sessiliflora

Abarema sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, peacock-like inflorescences that display a vibrant array of colors. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in some regions.
