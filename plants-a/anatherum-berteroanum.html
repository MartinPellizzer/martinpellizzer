Anatherum berteroanum

Anatherum berteroanum, commonly known as the Bertero's grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Anatherum berteroanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
