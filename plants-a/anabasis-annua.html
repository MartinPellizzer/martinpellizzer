Anabasis annua

Anabasis annua, commonly known as the annual saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. It is often found in saline soils and can tolerate high levels of salt.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, enabling it to spread across vast, open landscapes.
