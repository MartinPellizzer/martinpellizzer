Andropogon platybasis

Andropogon platybasis, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a tall, perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are narrow, bluish-green, and have a distinctive, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to landscapes.

Broadleaf bluestem is valued for its erosion control capabilities and its role in supporting native wildlife.
