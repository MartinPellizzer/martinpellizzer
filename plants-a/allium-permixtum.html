Allium permixtum

Allium permixtum, commonly known as the mixed leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, hollow stems and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Allium permixtum thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.
