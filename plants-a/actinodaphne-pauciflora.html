Actinodaphne pauciflora

Actinodaphne pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Actinodaphne pauciflora is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
