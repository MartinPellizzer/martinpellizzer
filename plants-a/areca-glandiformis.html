Areca glandiformis

Areca glandiformis, commonly known as the glandular areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in inflorescences, followed by clustered fruits that are typically reddish when mature. Areca glandiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.
