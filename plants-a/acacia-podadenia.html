Acacia podadenia

Acacia podadenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, and the seed pods are slender and ribbed.

Acacia podadenia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native vegetation.
