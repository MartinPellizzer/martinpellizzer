Astragalus johnstonii

Astragalus johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Astragalus johnstonii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
