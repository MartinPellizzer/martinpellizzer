Aloe perfoliata var. arborescens

Aloe perfoliata var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branching, tree-like structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The leaves are typically narrow and have a waxy texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. It produces yellowish-green flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This variety is well-suited for xeriscaping and thrives in full sun with minimal water.
