Alchemilla pubescens proles plicata

Alchemilla pubescens proles plicata, commonly known as the hairy lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and traditional herbal uses.
