Arundinaria matake

Arundinaria matake, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many bamboo species. Its rhizomes are robust and help in the propagation of the plant through vegetative means.

Arundinaria matake is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
