Antirrhinum chalepense

Antirrhinum chalepense, commonly known as the Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It is often found in gardens and natural habitats where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
