Aralia veitchii gracillima

Aralia veitchii gracillima, commonly known as the Veitch's aralia, is a rare and striking plant native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and sought-after specimen in botanical gardens.
