Agathis loranthifolia

Agathis loranthifolia, commonly known as the white sand agathis, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pollinated by insects, followed by the development of woody, elongated cones. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a habitat for various epiphytic plants and insects.

Agathis loranthifolia is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
