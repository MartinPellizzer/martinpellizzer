Anthemis praecox

Anthemis praecox, commonly known as early tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its small yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green leaves with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Anthemis praecox is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in herbal remedies.
