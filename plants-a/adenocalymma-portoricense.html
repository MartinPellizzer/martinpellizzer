Adenocalymma portoricense

Adenocalymma portoricense, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the dry season, which are clustered in panicles and attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Adenocalymma portoricense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
