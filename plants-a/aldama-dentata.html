Aldama dentata

Aldama dentata, commonly known as the toothed aldama, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, serrated leaves and clustered flower heads make it easily identifiable in its native habitat.

Aldama dentata is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest for its potential ecological and pharmacological properties.
