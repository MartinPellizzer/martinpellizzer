Actinodaphne sulcata

Actinodaphne sulcata, commonly known as the ridged laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lanceolate leaves with prominent midribs and a distinctive, ridged texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Actinodaphne sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for landscaping purposes.
