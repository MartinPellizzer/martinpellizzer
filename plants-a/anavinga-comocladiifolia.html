Anavinga comocladiifolia

Anavinga comocladiifolia, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and tidal environments.

It possesses specialized root systems, including pneumatophores, which allow for gas exchange in waterlogged soils. The plant's leaves are thin and blade-like, reducing water loss while facilitating photosynthesis in brackish conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces woody fruits that disperse via water.

Anavinga comocladiifolia plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
