Abrotanella pusilla

Abrotanella pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sand verbena, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. The plant features tiny, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Abrotanella pusilla is well adapted to harsh climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
