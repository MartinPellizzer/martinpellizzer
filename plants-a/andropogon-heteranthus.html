Andropogon heteranthus

Andropogon heteranthus, commonly known as the heteranthus grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Andropogon heteranthus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
