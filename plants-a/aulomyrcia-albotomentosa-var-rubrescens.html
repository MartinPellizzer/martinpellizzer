Aulomyrcia albotomentosa var. rubrescens

Aulomyrcia albotomentosa var. rubrescens, commonly known as the red-fruited silver-moth plant, is a flowering species native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage and small, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
