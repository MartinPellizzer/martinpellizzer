Amphipappus fremontii

Amphipappus fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's burroweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits called achenes, which are surrounded by bracts that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and shrubland habitats.

Its structure and reproductive strategy reflect adaptations to arid environments.
