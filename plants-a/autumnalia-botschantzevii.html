Autumnalia botschantzevii

Autumnalia botschantzevii, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh environments.

Autumnalia botschantzevii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
