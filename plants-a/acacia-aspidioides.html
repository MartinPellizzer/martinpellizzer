Acacia aspidioides

Acacia aspidioides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Acacia aspidioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
