Aira flexuosa var. parviflora

Aira flexuosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the slender oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
