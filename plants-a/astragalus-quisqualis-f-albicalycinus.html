Astragalus quisqualis f. albicalycinus

Astragalus quisqualis f. albicalycinus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a variant of the species Astragalus quisqualis, characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers.

This plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and the plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
