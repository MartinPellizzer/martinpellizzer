Althaea micans

Althaea micans, commonly known as the silverleaf hollyhock, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, rose-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has velvety, silver-green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Althaea micans is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
