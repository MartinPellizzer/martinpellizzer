Acacia albizioides

Acacia albizioides, commonly known as the white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a greyish or whitish underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
