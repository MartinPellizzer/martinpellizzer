Aerangis crassipes

Aerangis crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
