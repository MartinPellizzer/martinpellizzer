Anaphalis margaritacea var. yedoensis

Anaphalis margaritacea var. yedoensis, commonly known as the Yedo snowbell, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that resemble tiny snowballs. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
