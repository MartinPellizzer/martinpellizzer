Aristida swartziana

Aristida swartziana, commonly known as the slender threeawn, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy spikelets that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Aristida swartziana is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
