Anthurium purpuribacca

Anthurium purpuribacca, commonly known as the red-bellied arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble spikes, often with a contrasting coloration. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium purpuribacca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
