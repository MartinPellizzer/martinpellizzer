Aster spectabilis var. bellidifolius

Aster spectabilis var. bellidifolius, commonly known as the New England aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with rough textures. Its flowering stems are usually hairy, and the flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

This species is often found in moist meadows, prairies, and woodland edges, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
