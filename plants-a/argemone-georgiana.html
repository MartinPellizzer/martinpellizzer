Argemone georgiana

Argemone georgiana, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features striking orange to red flowers with four to six petals, often accompanied by narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and yellow stamens that contrast with the vibrant petals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.
