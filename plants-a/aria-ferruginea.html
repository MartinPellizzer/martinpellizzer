Aria ferruginea

Aria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown hue on the underside, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Aria ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
