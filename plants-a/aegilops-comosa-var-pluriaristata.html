Aegilops comosa var. pluriaristata

Aegilops comosa var. pluriaristata, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
