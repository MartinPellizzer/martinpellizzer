Acaciopsis conzattii

Acaciopsis conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access underground water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
