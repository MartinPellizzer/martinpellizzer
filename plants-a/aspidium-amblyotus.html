Aspidium amblyotus

Aspidium amblyotus, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated fronds that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile leaves, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Aspidium amblyotus is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
