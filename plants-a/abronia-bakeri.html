Abronia bakeri

Abronia bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh coastal climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
