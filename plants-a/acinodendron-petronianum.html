Acinodendron petronianum

Acinodendron petronianum, commonly known as the Natal umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, umbrella-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique foliage and seasonal blooms, it is valued in both horticulture and ecological studies.
