Amomum raoi var. raoi

Amomum raoi var. raoi, commonly known as Rao's cardamom, is a species of ginger native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elongated fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in tropical climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
