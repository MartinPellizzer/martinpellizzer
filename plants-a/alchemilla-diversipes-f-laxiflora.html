Alchemilla diversipes f. laxiflora

Alchemilla diversipes f. laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers on slender stems, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in shady gardens.
