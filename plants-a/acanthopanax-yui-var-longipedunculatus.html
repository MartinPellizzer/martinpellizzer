Acanthopanax yui var. longipedunculatus

Acanthopanax yui var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long pedunculate Chinese angel's trumpet, is a woody shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement and serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
