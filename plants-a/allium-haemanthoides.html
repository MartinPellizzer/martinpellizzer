Allium haemanthoides

Allium haemanthoides, commonly known as the red-flowered garlic.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its striking red or pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, clustered bulbs that are edible and have a mild garlic flavor. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while its stems are short and sturdy.

Allium haemanthoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
