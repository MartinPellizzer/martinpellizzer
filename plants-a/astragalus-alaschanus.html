Astragalus alaschanus

Astragalus alaschanus, commonly known as the Alaska milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus alaschanus is often found in tundra and subarctic habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
