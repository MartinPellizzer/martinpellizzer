Andropogon saccharoides var. pulvinatus

Andropogon saccharoides var. pulvinatus, commonly known as the tall bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical panicle that produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
