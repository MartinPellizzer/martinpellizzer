Aster dubius

Aster dubius, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster dubius is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
