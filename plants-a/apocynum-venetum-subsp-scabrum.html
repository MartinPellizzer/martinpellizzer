Apocynum venetum subsp. scabrum

Apocynum venetum subsp.

scabrum, commonly known as the rough dogbane, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are follicles containing seeds with a tuft of white hairs.

It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.
