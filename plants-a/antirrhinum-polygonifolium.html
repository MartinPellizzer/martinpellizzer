Antirrhinum polygonifolium

Antirrhinum polygonifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Antirrhinum polygonifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
