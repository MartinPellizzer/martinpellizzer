Arabis divaricarpa var. pinetorum

Arabis divaricarpa var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or pine forest habitats. It has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in the spring.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
