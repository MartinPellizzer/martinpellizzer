Aristolochia hitchcockii

Aristolochia hitchcockii, commonly known as the Hitchcock's Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
