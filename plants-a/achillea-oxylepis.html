Achillea oxylepis

Achillea oxylepis, commonly known as the alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and flat, aromatic flower heads that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Achillea oxylepis is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates and high altitudes.
