Agoseris retrorsa

Agoseris retrorsa, commonly known as the bentleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Agoseris retrorsa is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
