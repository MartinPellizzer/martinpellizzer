Alyxia myrtoides

Alyxia myrtoides, commonly known as the myrtle-like alyxia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a woody vine characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy texture with a slight aromatic scent. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing along coastal areas or in dense undergrowth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
