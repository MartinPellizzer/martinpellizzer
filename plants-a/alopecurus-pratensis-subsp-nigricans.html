Alopecurus pratensis subsp. nigricans

Alopecurus pratensis subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as black meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, coarse leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and wetlands.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in supporting biodiversity and soil stabilization in its habitat.
