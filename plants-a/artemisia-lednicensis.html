Artemisia lednicensis

Artemisia lednicensis, commonly known as the Lednica wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the sunflower family. Artemisia lednicensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

It is used in traditional medicine and has a strong, camphor-like scent.
