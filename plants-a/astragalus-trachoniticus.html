Astragalus trachoniticus

Astragalus trachoniticus, commonly known as the Trachonite milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Astragalus trachoniticus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
