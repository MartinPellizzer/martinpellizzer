Anthyllis vulneraria f. picta

Anthyllis vulneraria f. picta, commonly known as the painted woundwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The 'picta' variety is distinguished by its unique, mottled leaf patterns, which give it its common name.
