Arctostaphylos subcordata

Arctostaphylos subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish bark, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture, and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates, often found in coastal regions and chaparral habitats.
