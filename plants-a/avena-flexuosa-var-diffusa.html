Avena flexuosa var. diffusa

Avena flexuosa var. diffusa, commonly known as the soft oat or velvet oat, is a variant of the common oat species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy, flexible stems. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each containing two to three small, bracteate flowers.

This variety is adapted to moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
