Anthriscus sicula

Anthriscus sicula, commonly known as Sicilian parsley, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Anthriscus sicula is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
