Acacia undulifolia var. sertiformis

Acacia undulifolia var. sertiformis, commonly known as the wavy-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, wavy-edged leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine, and it also serves as a source of nectar for pollinators.
