Atractylis arbuscula

Atractylis arbuscula, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of gray-green, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Atractylis arbuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
