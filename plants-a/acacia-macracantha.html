Acacia macracantha

Acacia macracantha, commonly known as the large-leaf wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and pinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that appear in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.

Acacia macracantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
