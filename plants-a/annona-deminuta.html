Annona deminuta

Annona deminuta, commonly known as the small annona or Mexican custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are followed by green, spiny fruits resembling small apples. The fruit's flesh is sweet and creamy, with a texture similar to custard, and it is edible when fully ripe.

Annona deminuta is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
