Astragalus hybridus

Astragalus hybridus, commonly known as the field milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Astragalus hybridus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
