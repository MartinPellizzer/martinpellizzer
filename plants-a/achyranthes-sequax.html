Achyranthes sequax

Achyranthes sequax, commonly known as the Indian spinach or wild spinach, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Achyranthes sequax is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
