Astragalus gibberosus

Astragalus gibberosus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Astragalus gibberosus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for wildlife.
