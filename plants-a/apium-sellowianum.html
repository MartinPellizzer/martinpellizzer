Apium sellowianum

Apium sellowianum, commonly known as Brazilian parsley, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, feather-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and has a distinctive, spicy flavor.
