Alysicarpus glumaceus var. intermedius

Alysicarpus glumaceus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive burrs that aid in seed dispersal through attachment to animals or clothing. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and soil stabilization efforts.
