Agropyron tenerum var. longifolium

Agropyron tenerum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that bear small, awned grains.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
