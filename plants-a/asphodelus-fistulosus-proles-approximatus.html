Asphodelus fistulosus proles approximatus

Asphodelus fistulosus proles approximatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved asphodel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, this species thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant is perennial, with a compact growth habit, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and resilience.
