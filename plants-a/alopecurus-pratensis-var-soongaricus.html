Alopecurus pratensis var. soongaricus

Alopecurus pratensis var. soongaricus, commonly known as the Siberian meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow foxtail.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia, including Siberia and parts of China. This plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent central vein, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
