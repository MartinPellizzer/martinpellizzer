Arctagrostis

Arctagrostis, commonly known as the bluegrass, is a genus of grasses native to North America.

These plants are characterized by their fine, bluish-green foliage and are often found in moist, well-drained soils. Arctagrostis species typically grow in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and along stream banks. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in landscaping.

The genus includes several species, with Arctagrostis latifolia being one of the most widely recognized.
