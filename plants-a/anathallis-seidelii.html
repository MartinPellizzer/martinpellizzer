Anathallis seidelii

Anathallis seidelii, commonly known as the Seidel's ghost orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its pale, ghostly white flowers that resemble small, translucent petals. The plant typically grows on tree branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air rather than soil. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
