Aconitum lihsienense

Aconitum lihsienense, commonly known as the Lihsien monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features palmate leaves with lobes that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
