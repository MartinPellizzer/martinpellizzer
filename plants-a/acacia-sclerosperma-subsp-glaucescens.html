Acacia sclerosperma subsp. glaucescens

Acacia sclerosperma subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the grey acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its grey-green foliage and slender, often spiny branches.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its seed pods are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of local ecosystems.
