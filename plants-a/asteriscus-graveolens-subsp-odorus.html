Asteriscus graveolens subsp. odorus

Asteriscus graveolens subsp.

odorus, commonly known as the sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

The subspecies odorus is distinguished by its stronger fragrance compared to other subspecies of Asteriscus graveolens.
