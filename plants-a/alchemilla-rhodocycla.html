Alchemilla rhodocycla

Alchemilla rhodocycla, commonly known as the pink lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, gray-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Alchemilla rhodocycla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
