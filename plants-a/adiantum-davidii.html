Adiantum davidii

Adiantum davidii, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in small, round structures called sorus.

Adiantum davidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
