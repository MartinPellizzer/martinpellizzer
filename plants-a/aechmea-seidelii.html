Aechmea seidelii

Aechmea seidelii, commonly known as the Seidel's pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, spiky leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with vibrant red bracts that surround a spadix of small white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Aechmea seidelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Morphology

Aechmea seidelii has leaves that are broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, with a waxy, dark green color and a sharp, pointed tip.

The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, grayish bark that grows upright from the base of the rosette. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a colorful bract, typically in shades of red or orange, and bloom during the summer months, pollinated primarily by insects. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.

The roots are fibrous and form a dense mat around the base of the plant, helping to anchor it and absorb moisture from the environment.
