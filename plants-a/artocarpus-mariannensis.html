Artocarpus mariannensis

Artocarpus mariannensis, commonly known as the Mariannese breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The fruit, which resembles a breadfruit, is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or orange when mature. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in carbohydrates and used in various traditional cuisines.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in island ecosystems.
