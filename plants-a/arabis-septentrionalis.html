Arabis septentrionalis

Arabis septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern rockcress, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This perennial species typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a low, spreading growth habit.
