Acacia chamelensis

Acacia chamelensis, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soils.

Acacia chamelensis is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of firewood and traditional medicine.
