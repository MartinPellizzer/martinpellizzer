Anthriscus cerefolium

Anthriscus cerefolium, commonly known as cow parsley, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, a characteristic feature of the carrot family (Apiaceae). It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
