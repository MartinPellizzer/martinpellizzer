Alstroemeria litterata

Alstroemeria litterata, commonly known as the mountain alstroemeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool and moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Alstroemeria litterata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
