Amorphophallus warneckei

Amorphophallus warneckei, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can span up to three meters in diameter, which float on the surface of still water. The plant's flowers are large and dramatic, often resembling a phallic shape, which has led to its alternative name, the "corpse flower.". It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of both scientific study and botanical fascination.
