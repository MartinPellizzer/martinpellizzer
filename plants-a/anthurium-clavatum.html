Anthurium clavatum

Anthurium clavatum, commonly known as the velvet leaf anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and often display a deep green to dark red coloration. The plant produces clusters of spadices, which are inflorescences that resemble spikes, typically in shades of red or orange. Anthurium clavatum thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
