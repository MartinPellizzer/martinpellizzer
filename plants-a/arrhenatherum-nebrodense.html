Arrhenatherum nebrodense

Arrhenatherum nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrod grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
