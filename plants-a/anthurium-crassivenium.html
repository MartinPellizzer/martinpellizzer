Anthurium crassivenium

Anthurium crassivenium, commonly known as the broad-veined anthurium, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, dark green veins. The plant produces striking spadices, which are typically green or red, surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Anthurium crassivenium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
