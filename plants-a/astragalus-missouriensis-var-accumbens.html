Astragalus missouriensis var. accumbens

Astragalus missouriensis var. accumbens, commonly known as Missouri milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the central United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. It is characterized by its hairy stems and is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

This variety is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
