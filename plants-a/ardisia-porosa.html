Ardisia porosa

Ardisia porosa, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, clustered berries that turn a striking red-purple color in the fall. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Ardisia porosa thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
