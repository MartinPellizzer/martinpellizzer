Abelia mairei

Abelia mairei, commonly known as the Chinese abelia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range from white to pink, often with a red throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Abelia mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Abelia mairei is primarily native to China, specifically the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan.

It thrives in mountainous regions with a temperate climate and is often found in forests and shrublands. This plant is well adapted to the humid and cool conditions of these areas. It is also occasionally found in neighboring regions such as Vietnam.

Abelia mairei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens outside its native range.
