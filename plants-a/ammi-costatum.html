Ammi costatum

Ammi costatum, commonly known as the cow parsley or bishop's weed, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley.

Ammi costatum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
