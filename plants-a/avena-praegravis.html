Avena praegravis

Avena praegravis, commonly known as early barley, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Avena praegravis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
