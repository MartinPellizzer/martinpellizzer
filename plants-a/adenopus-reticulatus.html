Adenopus reticulatus

Adenopus reticulatus, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes other members commonly referred to as nettles. The plant is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems, which can cause irritation upon contact. It typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Adenopus reticulatus is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
