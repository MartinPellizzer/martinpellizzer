Acropogon paagoumenensis

Acropogon paagoumenensis, commonly known as the Paagoumenensis grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
