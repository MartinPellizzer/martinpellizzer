Acacia oligophylla

Acacia oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia oligophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
