Aristolochia angustifolia var. longifolia

Aristolochia angustifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant in the Aristolochiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and elongated, giving it its varietal name "longifolia.". It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
