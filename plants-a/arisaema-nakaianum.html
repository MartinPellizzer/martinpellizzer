Arisaema nakaianum

Arisaema nakaianum, commonly known as the Nakaian mountain arisaema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a single, showy white spathe that encloses a dense cluster of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Arisaema nakaianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
