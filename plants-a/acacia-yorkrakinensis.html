Acacia yorkrakinensis

Acacia yorkrakinensis, commonly known as the Yorkrakin acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its slender, thornless stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia yorkrakinensis is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
