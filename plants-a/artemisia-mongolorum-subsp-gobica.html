Artemisia mongolorum subsp. gobica

Artemisia mongolorum subsp.

gobica, commonly known as Mongolian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including extreme temperatures and low precipitation. It is often found in steppe and desert steppe ecosystems, contributing to the vegetation structure of these regions.

Due to its resilience and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
