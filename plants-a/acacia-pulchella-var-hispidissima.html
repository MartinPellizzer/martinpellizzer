Acacia pulchella var. hispidissima

Acacia pulchella var. hispidissima, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
