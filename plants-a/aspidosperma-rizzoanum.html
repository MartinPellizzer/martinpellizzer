Aspidosperma rizzoanum

Aspidosperma rizzoanum, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. It is often found in humid tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Aspidosperma rizzoanum is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
