Allium meliophilum

Allium meliophilum, commonly known as the pink onion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
