Acacia erioclada

Acacia erioclada, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stem and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically reduced to leaflets. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in compact, globular heads, which are a notable feature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

The plant is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil rehabilitation.
