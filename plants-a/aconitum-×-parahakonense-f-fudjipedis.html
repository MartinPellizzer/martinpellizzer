Aconitum × parahakonense f. fudjipedis

Aconitum × parahakonense f. fudjipedis, commonly known as the Fudjipedis hybrid, is a hybrid plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spires of flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
