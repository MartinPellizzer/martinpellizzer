Aerva edulis

Aerva edulis, commonly known as the edible aerva, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its edible leaves, which are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, slightly sweet flavor. Aerva edulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

It is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in some cultures.
