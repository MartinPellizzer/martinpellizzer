Allium retrorsum

Allium retrorsum, commonly known as backward garlic, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, strap-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor.

Allium retrorsum is often grown for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
