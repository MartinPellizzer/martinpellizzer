Acrocomia antiguana

Acrocomia antiguana, commonly known as the windmill palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and savanna habitats.

The windmill palm is valued for its ornamental appearance and its use in traditional medicine and cuisine.
