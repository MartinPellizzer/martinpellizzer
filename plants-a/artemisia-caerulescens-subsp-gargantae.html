Artemisia caerulescens subsp. gargantae

Artemisia caerulescens subsp.

gargantae, commonly known as the giant blue sage, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking blue-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and unique appearance make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
