Avenula pungens

Avenula pungens, commonly known as the sharpleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, sharp hairs, giving it its common name. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps.

Avenula pungens produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-lobed, three-chambered berries.
