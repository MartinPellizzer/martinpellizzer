Astragalus villosidentatus

Astragalus villosidentatus, commonly known as the hairy-toothed milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Astragalus villosidentatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators in its native range.
