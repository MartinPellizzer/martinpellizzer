Acaena sanguisorbae subsp. papuana

Acaena sanguisorbae subsp.

papuana, commonly known as the Papuan bentthorn, is a species native to New Guinea. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense growth habit and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown seeds enclosed in prickly burrs, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
