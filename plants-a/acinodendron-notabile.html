Acinodendron notabile

Acinodendron notabile, commonly known as the showy acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for its ecological benefits.
