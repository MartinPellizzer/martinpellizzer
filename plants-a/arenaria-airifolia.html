Arenaria airifolia

Arenaria airifolia, commonly known as the air-puff grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to arctic and alpine regions, where it thrives in cold, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Arenaria airifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitats and is often found in tundra and rocky areas.
