Arthraxon figarii

Arthraxon figarii, commonly known as the African grass or fig-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Arthraxon figarii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
