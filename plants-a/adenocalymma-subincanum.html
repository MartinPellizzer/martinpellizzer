Adenocalymma subincanum

Adenocalymma subincanum, commonly known as the silky sandbur, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in heads.

Adenocalymma subincanum is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
