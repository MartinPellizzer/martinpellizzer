Astragalus beathii

Astragalus beathii, commonly known as Beath's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus beathii is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub ecosystems.
