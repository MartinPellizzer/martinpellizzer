Amaranthus texidoris

Amaranthus texidoris, commonly known as the Texas amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spiny, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
