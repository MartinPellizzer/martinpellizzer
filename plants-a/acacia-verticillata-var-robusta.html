Acacia verticillata var. robusta

Acacia verticillata var. robusta, commonly known as the robust acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading crown and feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces yellow, globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
