Acacia stereophylla var. cylindrata

Acacia stereophylla var. cylindrata, commonly known as the cylindrical wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical phyllodes that are typically green with a pale midrib. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads during the warmer months, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
