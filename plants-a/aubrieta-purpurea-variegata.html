Aubrieta purpurea variegata

Aubrieta purpurea variegata, commonly known as the variegated rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically gray-green with white or yellow variegation, adding visual interest to rock gardens or borders. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it ideal for dry, rocky environments.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
