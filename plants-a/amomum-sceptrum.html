Amomum sceptrum

Amomum sceptrum, commonly known as the sacred cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark, triangular berries containing aromatic seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmaceutical research.
