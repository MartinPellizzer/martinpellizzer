Azalea nudiflora var. rutilans

Azalea nudiflora var. rutilans, commonly known as the golden azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
