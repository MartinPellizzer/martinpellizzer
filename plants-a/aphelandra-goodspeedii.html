Aphelandra goodspeedii

Aphelandra goodspeedii, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Aphelandra goodspeedii is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
