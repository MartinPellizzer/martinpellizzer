Arrabidaea divaricata

Arrabidaea divaricata, commonly known as the African flame or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical and subtropical regions.
