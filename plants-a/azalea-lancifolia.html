Azalea lancifolia

Azalea lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, depending on the specific variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Azalea lancifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
