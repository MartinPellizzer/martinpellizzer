Acer campestre f. hederifolium

Acer campestre f. hederifolium, commonly known as the creeping maple, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Europe.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. The plant produces small, red-orange flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to form dense, mat-like growth.
