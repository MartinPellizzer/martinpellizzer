Anemone × pittonii

Anemone × pittonii, commonly known as the Himalayan anemone, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southeast Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils, often growing in woodland or alpine environments. Its leaves are basal, with a distinctive heart-shaped form and a hairy texture.

Anemone × pittonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
