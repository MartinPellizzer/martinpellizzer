Amaranthus flavus

Amaranthus flavus, commonly known as the yellow amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and feathery leaves, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils. Its seeds are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop for both food and forage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is closely related to other pigweed species.
