Anthemis cossoniana

Anthemis cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and often have a subtle chamomile scent, which contributes to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential calming properties.
