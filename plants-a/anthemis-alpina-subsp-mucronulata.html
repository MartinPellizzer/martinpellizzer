Anthemis alpina subsp. mucronulata

Anthemis alpina subsp.

mucronulata, commonly known as the Alpine ragwort, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers arranged in flat, composite heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.
