Allium cupuliferum

Allium cupuliferum, commonly known as the Spanish onion or bulbous garlic, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and clusters of grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valued plant in both culinary and ornamental contexts.

Allium cupuliferum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate climates.
