Annesorhiza bracteosa

Annesorhiza bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted annesorhiza, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and elongated flowering stems. The plant produces small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Annesorhiza bracteosa is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
