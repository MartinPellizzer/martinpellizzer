Aristida hystricula

Aristida hystricula, commonly known as the hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and grassland habitats.

Aristida hystricula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
