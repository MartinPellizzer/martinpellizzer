Aquilegia oxysepala var. yabeana

Aquilegia oxysepala var. yabeana, commonly known as Yabe's columbine, is a rare and unique variant of the columbine genus native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in a range of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with finely toothed margins.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its limited distribution and distinctive morphology.
