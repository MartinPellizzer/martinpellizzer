Aglaia polyphylla

Aglaia polyphylla, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are yellow when ripe and contain a single seed. Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture, while its wood is also valued for its durability.

Aglaia polyphylla thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
