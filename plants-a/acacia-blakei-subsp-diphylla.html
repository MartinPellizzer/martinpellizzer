Acacia blakei subsp. diphylla

Acacia blakei subsp.

diphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that appear in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often included in conservation and revegetation efforts.
