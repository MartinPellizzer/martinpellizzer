Alyssum calycinum var. vagum

Alyssum calycinum var. vagum, commonly known as the rock alpine alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-blue foliage with a woolly texture, and its stems are often prostrate or semi-upright. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
