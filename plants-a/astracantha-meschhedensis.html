Astracantha meschhedensis

Astracantha meschhedensis, commonly known as the Armenian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its scientific name reflects its geographical origin and distinctive features.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
