Axonopus chrysites

Axonopus chrysites, commonly known as golden axonopus, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and the presence of golden-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight bluish hue.

Axonopus chrysites is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground cover.
