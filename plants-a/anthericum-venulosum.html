Anthericum venulosum

Anthericum venulosum, commonly known as the veined ladies' tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, dense, and often fragrant inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in a spiral along the stem.

Anthericum venulosum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
