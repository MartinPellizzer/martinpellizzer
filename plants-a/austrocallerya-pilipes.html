Austrocallerya pilipes

Austrocallerya pilipes, commonly known as the Chinese winged bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often adorned with small, wing-like projections on the leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
