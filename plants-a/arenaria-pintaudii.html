Arenaria pintaudii

Arenaria pintaudii, commonly known as the Pintaud's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments.

Arenaria pintaudii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
