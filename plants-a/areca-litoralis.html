Areca litoralis

Areca litoralis, commonly known as the coastal areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt-laden air and sandy soils. Its fruit, which resembles a coconut, contains a single seed surrounded by a fibrous husk.

Areca litoralis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
