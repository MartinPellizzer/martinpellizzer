Aristolochia tuitensis

Aristolochia tuitensis, commonly known as the New Zealand Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble a pipe, which are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex, twisting flower structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
