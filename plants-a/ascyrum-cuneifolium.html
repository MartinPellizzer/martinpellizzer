Ascyrum cuneifolium

Ascyrum cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-gray underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves contain volatile oils, contributing to its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Ascyrum cuneifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
