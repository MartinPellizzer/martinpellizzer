Amelanchier sanguinea var. gaspensis

Amelanchier sanguinea var. gaspensis, commonly known as the Gaspé serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Canada.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and showy white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries in the summer that are rich in antioxidants and often used in local cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas along the Gaspé Peninsula.
