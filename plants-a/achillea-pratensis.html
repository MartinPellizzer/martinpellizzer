Achillea pratensis

Achillea pratensis, commonly known as field yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has feathery, aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
