Astragalus englerianus var. longiflorus

Astragalus englerianus var. longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It has small, hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
