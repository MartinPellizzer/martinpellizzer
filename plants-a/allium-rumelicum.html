Allium rumelicum

Allium rumelicum, commonly known as the meadow garlic, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous roots and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Allium rumelicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
