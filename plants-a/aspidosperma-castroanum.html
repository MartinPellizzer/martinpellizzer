Aspidosperma castroanum

Aspidosperma castroanum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerocarpus castroanum*, is a species of flowering plant in the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Aspidosperma castroanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
