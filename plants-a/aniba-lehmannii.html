Aniba lehmannii

Aniba lehmannii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Its aromatic leaves and bark contain compounds used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
