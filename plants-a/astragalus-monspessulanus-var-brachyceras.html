Astragalus monspessulanus var. brachyceras

Astragalus monspessulanus var. brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-capsuled milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and soil stabilization.
