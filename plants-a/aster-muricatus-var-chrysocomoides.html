Aster muricatus var. chrysocomoides

Aster muricatus var. chrysocomoides, commonly known as the golden-leaf aster, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinct, slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
