Achillea beckiana

Achillea beckiana, commonly known as Beck's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
