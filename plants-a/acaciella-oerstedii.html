Acaciella oerstedii

Acaciella oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent stems and reduced leaves.

This unique species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
