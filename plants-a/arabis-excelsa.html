Arabis excelsa

Arabis excelsa, commonly known as the tall rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Arabis excelsa thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
