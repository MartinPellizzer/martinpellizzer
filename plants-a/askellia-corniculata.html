Askellia corniculata

Askellia corniculata, commonly known as the horned morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped calyx that encases the flower, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests.

Askellia corniculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
