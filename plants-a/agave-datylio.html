Agave datylio

Agave datylio, commonly known as the narrowleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flower stalk that bears small, white flowers, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
