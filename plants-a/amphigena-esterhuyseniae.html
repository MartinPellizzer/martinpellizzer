Amphigena esterhuyseniae

Amphigena esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia orchid, is a unique orchid species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are usually fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, making it a popular choice for cultivation.
