Adenosma africana

Adenosma africana, commonly known as the African adenostema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
