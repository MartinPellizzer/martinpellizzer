Aconitum yezoense

Aconitum yezoense, commonly known as Yezo monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Aconitum yezoense contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially dangerous to humans and animals.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
