Avena macrocarpa proles ludoviciana

Avena macrocarpa proles ludoviciana, commonly known as the Mexican oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
