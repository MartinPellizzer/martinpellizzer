Arctostaphylos columbiana var. virgata

Arctostaphylos columbiana var. virgata, commonly known as the Oregon madrone, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
