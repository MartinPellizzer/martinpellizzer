Andropogon provincialis var. chrysocomus

Andropogon provincialis var. chrysocomus, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a distinctive golden hue along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
