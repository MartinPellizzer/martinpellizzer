Alyssum eximum

Alyssum eximum, commonly known as the common alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compact, succulent-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum eximum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
