Argythamnia schiedeana var. major

Argythamnia schiedeana var. major, commonly known as the desert gum plant, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It possesses woody stems that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually small, linear, and may be reduced to scales in some varieties.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and efficient water storage mechanisms.
