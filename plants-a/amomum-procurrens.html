Amomum procurrens

Amomum procurrens, commonly known as small cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by oval, dark brown fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The essential oils extracted from its rhizomes and fruits are valued for their distinct spicy and fragrant properties.
