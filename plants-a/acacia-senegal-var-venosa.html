Acacia senegal var. venosa

Acacia senegal var. venosa, commonly known as the gum arabic acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are followed by long, slender pods containing a viscous, resinous gum. This gum, known as gum arabic, is widely used in food, pharmaceutical, and industrial applications.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important resource in dryland ecosystems.
