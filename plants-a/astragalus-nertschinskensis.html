Astragalus nertschinskensis

Astragalus nertschinskensis, commonly known as the Nertschinsk milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia and northern China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus nertschinskensis is part of the Fabaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
