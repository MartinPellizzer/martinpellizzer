Acacia karroo var. transvaalensis

Acacia karroo var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
