Aylthonia viscosissima

Aylthonia viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky bryophyte, is a species of moss that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is known for its dense, cushion-like growth form and the presence of a viscous, sticky substance on its shoots, which may aid in water retention. This moss typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are borne on specialized stalks called setae.

The plant's ability to retain moisture makes it an important component of humid ecosystems.
