Acacia erythrantha

Acacia erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, bright red flower spikes that appear during the warmer months. The plant produces small, flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Acacia erythrantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
