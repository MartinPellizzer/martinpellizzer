Anacamptis × parvifolia

Anacamptis × parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
