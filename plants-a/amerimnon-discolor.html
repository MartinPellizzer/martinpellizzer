Amerimnon discolor

Amerimnon discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
