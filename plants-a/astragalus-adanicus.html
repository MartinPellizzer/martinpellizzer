Astragalus adanicus

Astragalus adanicus, commonly known as the Adanica milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water and nutrients from deep within the soil.

Astragalus adanicus is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
