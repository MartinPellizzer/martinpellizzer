Anthyllis fuersteinii

Anthyllis fuersteinii, commonly known as the Fuerstein's anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has a low-growing habit with trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Anthyllis fuersteinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
