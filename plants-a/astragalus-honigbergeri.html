Astragalus honigbergeri

Astragalus honigbergeri, commonly known as Honigberger's milkvetch, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. It produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus honigbergeri is often found in scrubland and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
