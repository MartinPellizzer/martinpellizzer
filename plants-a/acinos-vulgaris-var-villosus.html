Acinos vulgaris var. villosus

Acinos vulgaris var. villosus, commonly known as the woolly acinos, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
