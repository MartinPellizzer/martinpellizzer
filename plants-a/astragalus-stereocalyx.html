Astragalus stereocalyx

Astragalus stereocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various health benefits.
