Aphyllorchis anomala

Aphyllorchis anomala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and lack of true leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a spike.

Aphyllorchis anomala is known for its mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake.
