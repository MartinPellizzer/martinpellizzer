Acetosella acuminata

Acetosella acuminata, commonly known as sharp-leaved sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers during the spring season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Acetosella acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
