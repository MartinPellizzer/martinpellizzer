Acmella trilobata

Acmella trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble small triggers. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Acmella trilobata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
