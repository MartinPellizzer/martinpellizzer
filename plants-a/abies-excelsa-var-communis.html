Abies excelsa var. communis

Abies excelsa var. communis, commonly known as the Colombian silver fir, is a coniferous tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the stems. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, typically found on the lower branches. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high elevations, often growing in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
