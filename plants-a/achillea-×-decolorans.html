Achillea × decolorans

Achillea × decolorans, commonly known as the purple yarrow, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining traits from both Achillea millefolium and Achillea ptarmica.
