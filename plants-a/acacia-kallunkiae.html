Acacia kallunkiae

Acacia kallunkiae, commonly known as the Western Australian acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to golden spherical flower heads, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia kallunkiae is also valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
