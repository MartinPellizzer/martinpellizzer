Aconitum sachalinense var. lasiocarpum

Aconitum sachalinense var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the Siberian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers arranged in tall, sparsely branched spikes. The plant features palmately compound leaves with lobes that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is notable for its toxic alkaloids, which make it a plant to be handled with care.
