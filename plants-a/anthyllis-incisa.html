Anthyllis incisa

Anthyllis incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.
