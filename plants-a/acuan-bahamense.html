Acuan bahamense

Acuan bahamense, commonly known as the Bahamian acuan, is a flowering plant native to the Bahamas and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near coastal areas and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

Acuan bahamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
