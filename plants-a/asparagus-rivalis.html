Asparagus rivalis

Asparagus rivalis, commonly known as the red asparagus fern, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both indoor and outdoor settings.
