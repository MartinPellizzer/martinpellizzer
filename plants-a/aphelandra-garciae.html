Aphelandra garciae

Aphelandra garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's torch ginger, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that resemble torches, with bright yellow bracts and red-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Aphelandra garciae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
