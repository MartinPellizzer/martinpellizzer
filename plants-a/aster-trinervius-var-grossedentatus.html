Aster trinervius var. grossedentatus

Aster trinervius var. grossedentatus, commonly known as the toothed aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its botanical features include a branching stem and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
