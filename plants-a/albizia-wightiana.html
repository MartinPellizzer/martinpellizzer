Albizia wightiana

Albizia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Albizia wightiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes planted for soil stabilization.
