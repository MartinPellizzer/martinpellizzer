Adenogyne rotundifolia

Adenogyne rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf adenogyne, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
