Astragalus varius f. albiflorus

Astragalus varius f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a variant of the Astragalus genus, which is part of the legume family Fabaceae.

This plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The most distinctive feature is its white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in small racemes. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
