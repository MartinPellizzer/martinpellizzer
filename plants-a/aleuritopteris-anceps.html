Aleuritopteris anceps

Aleuritopteris anceps, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are divided into numerous pinnae, which are often covered in a fine, silky texture.

Aleuritopteris anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, feathery appearance.
