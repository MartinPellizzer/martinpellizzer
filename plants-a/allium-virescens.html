Allium virescens

Allium virescens, commonly known as garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible bulbs and leaves that are used in various cuisines for their mild garlic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.

Allium virescens is also valued for its ability to deter pests due to its strong aroma.
