Astragalus multispina var. parviflorus

Astragalus multispina var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a slender, branched stem that grows from a taproot system, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
