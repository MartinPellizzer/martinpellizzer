Astragalus durudensis

Astragalus durudensis, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Astragalus durudensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
