Astragalus decurrens subsp. dichroanthus

Astragalus decurrens subsp.

dichroanthus, commonly known as the two-colored milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture.

The flowers are typically yellow to orange, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for pollination by insects. This subspecies is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions.
