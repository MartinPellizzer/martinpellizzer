Arum cyprium

Arum cyprium, commonly known as the Cyprian arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces a striking, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a showy, purple spathe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Arum cyprium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
