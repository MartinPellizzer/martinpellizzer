Ampomele triphylla

Ampomele triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small figs. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ampomele triphylla thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
