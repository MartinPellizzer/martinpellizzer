Aster hyssopifolius var. canescens

Aster hyssopifolius var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
