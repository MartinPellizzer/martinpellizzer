Aspalathus rectistyla

Aspalathus rectistyla, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are arranged in a compact, rounded shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, brown fruits. It is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in gardens and for erosion control.
