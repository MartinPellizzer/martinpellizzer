Acacia lanigera var. venulosa

Acacia lanigera var. venulosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage composed of small, glandular, silky hairs that give it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with each leaflet having a distinctive, narrow shape.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique texture and drought tolerance.
