Astragalus schistocalyx

Astragalus schistocalyx, commonly known as the sand milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Astragalus schistocalyx is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
