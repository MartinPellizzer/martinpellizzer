Ayenia rotundifolia

Ayenia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are rounded and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ayenia rotundifolia is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
