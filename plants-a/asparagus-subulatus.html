Asparagus subulatus

Asparagus subulatus, commonly known as the needle asparagus, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, cylindrical berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
