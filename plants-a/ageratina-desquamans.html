Ageratina desquamans

Ageratina desquamans, commonly known as the silky ageratum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Ageratina desquamans is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
