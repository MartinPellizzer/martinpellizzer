Acacia acuminata var. glaucescens

Acacia acuminata var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular heads. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with numerous fine, feathery leaflets. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive floral display and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
