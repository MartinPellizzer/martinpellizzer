Adike glaberrima

Adike glaberrima, commonly known as the African wild spinach, is a leafy vegetable native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant is perennial and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in riverine areas and swampy regions. Its leaves are edible, rich in nutrients, and widely used in traditional cuisines across sub-Saharan Africa.

The plant also has cultural significance and is sometimes used in local herbal remedies.
