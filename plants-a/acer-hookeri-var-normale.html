Acer hookeri var. normale

Acer hookeri var. normale, commonly known as the Hooker's maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and parks.
