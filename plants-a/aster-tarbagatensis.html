Aster tarbagatensis

Aster tarbagatensis, commonly known as the Tarbagatay aster, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, violet-blue flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
