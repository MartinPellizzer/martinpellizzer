Acer fabri var. megalocarpum

Acer fabri var. megalocarpum, commonly known as the giant-seeded maple, is a variant of the Chinese maple.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces substantial, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Acer genus.
