Agrimonia suffrutescens

Agrimonia suffrutescens, commonly known as common agrimony, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its branched, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-brown fruits called achenes, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, sunny habitats.

Agrimony is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically for its purported medicinal benefits.
