Arceuthobium cyanocarpum

Arceuthobium cyanocarpum, commonly known as the blueberry dodder, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the roots of blueberry plants.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients and water. The plant produces small, blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, where it can significantly weaken the host plant.

Due to its parasitic nature, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
