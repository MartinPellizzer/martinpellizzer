Astragalus semilunatus

Astragalus semilunatus, commonly known as the moonflower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Astragalus semilunatus is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
