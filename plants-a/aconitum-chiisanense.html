Aconitum chiisanense

Aconitum chiisanense, commonly known as the Japanese aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum chiisanense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
