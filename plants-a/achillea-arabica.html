Achillea arabica

Achillea arabica, commonly known as the Arabian yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Achillea arabica is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat various ailments.
