Asarum sieboldii

Asarum sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic underground tuber that is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
