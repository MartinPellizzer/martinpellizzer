Alyssum calycinum

Alyssum calycinum, commonly known as the rock allyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The plant has compact, silver-green foliage with a mat-forming growth habit, making it ideal for rock gardens and borders. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Alyssum calycinum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
