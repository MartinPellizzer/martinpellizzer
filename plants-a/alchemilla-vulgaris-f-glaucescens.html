Alchemilla vulgaris f. glaucescens

Alchemilla vulgaris f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous lady's mantle, is a variant of the common lady's mantle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are broad, lobed, and covered with a distinctive grayish-blue waxy coating. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
