Actinidia rudis var. rudis

Actinidia rudis var. rudis, commonly known as the rough actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple color. These berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and are also valued for their nutritional content.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and autumn coloration.
