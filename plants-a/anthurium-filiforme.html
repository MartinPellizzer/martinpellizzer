Anthurium filiforme

Anthurium filiforme, commonly known as the thread-leaved anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often hairless leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are the modified leaves that enclose the true flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Anthurium filiforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
