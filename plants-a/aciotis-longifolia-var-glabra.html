Aciotis longifolia var. glabra

Aciotis longifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth aciotis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
