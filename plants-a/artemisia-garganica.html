Artemisia garganica

Artemisia garganica, commonly known as Garganica wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas.

Artemisia garganica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
