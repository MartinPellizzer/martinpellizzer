Anguria pedata

Anguria pedata, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. The plant produces small, round berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
