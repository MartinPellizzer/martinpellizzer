Amauropelta amphioxypteris

Amauropelta amphioxypteris, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the order Ophioglossales.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, broad lamina. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a sporangium, which contains spores for reproduction. It thrived in swampy and wetland environments during the Carboniferous period.

Fossil records indicate that it played a significant role in the early development of forest ecosystems.
