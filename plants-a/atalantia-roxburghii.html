Atalantia roxburghii

Atalantia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian atalantia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its leaves are simple, ovate, and leathery in texture. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
