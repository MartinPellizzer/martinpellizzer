Amaranthus pendulus

Amaranthus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging amaranth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its drooping inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually red or orange, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
