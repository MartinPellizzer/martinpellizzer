Acetosa suffruticosa

Acetosa suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping thimbleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Acetosa suffruticosa is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
