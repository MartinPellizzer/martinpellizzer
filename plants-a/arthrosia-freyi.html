Arthrosia freyi

Arthrosia freyi, commonly known as the freyi's arthrosia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, clustered growth habit and striking, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, capsule-like fruits. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.

Its botanical study contributes to the understanding of tropical flora and ecological resilience.
