Acalypha macrostachya var. sidifolia

Acalypha macrostachya var. sidifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both landscapes and botanical collections.
