Alsophila janseniana

Alsophila janseniana, commonly known as the Jansen's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, and it plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
