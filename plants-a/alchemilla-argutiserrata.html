Alchemilla argutiserrata

Alchemilla argutiserrata, commonly known as the serrated lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Alchemilla argutiserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
