Agelanthus terminaliae

Agelanthus terminaliae, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deep underground.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in various African cultures for its healing benefits.
