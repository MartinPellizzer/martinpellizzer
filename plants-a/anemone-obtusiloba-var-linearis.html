Anemone obtusiloba var. linearis

Anemone obtusiloba var. linearis, commonly known as the linear-leaved anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and narrow, with a waxy surface, and produces showy, white or pale pink flowers in spring. Its flowers are usually borne on slender, leafless stems and have five to seven petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
