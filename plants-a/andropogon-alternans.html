Andropogon alternans

Andropogon alternans, commonly known as the alternate bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or pink.

Andropogon alternans plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
