Aster perrugosus

Aster perrugosus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers in the fall, typically from September to November. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Aster perrugosus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
