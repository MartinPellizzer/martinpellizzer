Acalypha heterodonta var. hirsuta

Acalypha heterodonta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy iron plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are typically bright green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some subtropical and tropical gardens.
