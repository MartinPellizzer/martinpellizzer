Albertinia bardanoides

Albertinia bardanoides, commonly known as the Natal starwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

The species is also valued for its ornamental use in rock gardens and as a container plant.
