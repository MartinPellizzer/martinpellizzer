Athanasia annua

Athanasia annua, commonly known as common milkweed, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in follicles that release fine, white hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Athanasia annua is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
