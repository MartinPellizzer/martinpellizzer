Arabis muralis subsp. rosea

Arabis muralis subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and gravelly habitats in Europe. It features basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to rose-red, which bloom in early spring. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
