Andropogon tener var. neesii

Andropogon tener var. neesii, commonly known as Nees' silky grass, is a variant of the silky grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its growth habit makes it well-suited for ornamental use in gardens or as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in warm climates.
