Anthochloa lepidula

Anthochloa lepidula, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a small, mat-forming perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive velvety texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually inconspicuous, producing small, greenish flowers.

Anthochloa lepidula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
