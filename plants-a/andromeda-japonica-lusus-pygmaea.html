Andromeda japonica lusus pygmaea

Andromeda japonica lusus pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese andromeda, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, providing year-round interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate pinkish-white blooms and its ability to form a tidy, compact mound.
