Astragalus leucocyaneus

Astragalus leucocyaneus, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus leucocyaneus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
