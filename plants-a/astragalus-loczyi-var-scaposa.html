Astragalus loczyi var. scaposa

Astragalus loczyi var. scaposa, commonly known as the scapose milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its root system is deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native habitat.
