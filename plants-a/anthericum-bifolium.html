Anthericum bifolium

Anthericum bifolium, commonly known as the two-leaved lily, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces a single, upright flower stalk bearing a single, showy white or pale blue flower with six petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and its role in early spring ecosystems.
