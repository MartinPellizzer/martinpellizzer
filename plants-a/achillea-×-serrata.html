Achillea × serrata

Achillea × serrata, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and flat-topped clusters of small, often white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its hybrid nature results from crosses between Achillea millefolium and Achillea ptarmica.
