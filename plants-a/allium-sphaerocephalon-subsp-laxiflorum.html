Allium sphaerocephalon subsp. laxiflorum

Allium sphaerocephalon subsp.

laxiflorum, commonly known as the looseflower garlic. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

The plant features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its spherical, clustered inflorescence composed of small, white to pale purple flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
