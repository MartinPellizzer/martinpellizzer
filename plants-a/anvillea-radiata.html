Anvillea radiata

Anvillea radiata, commonly known as the yellow woodlot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its bark is often fissured and exudes a milky sap.
