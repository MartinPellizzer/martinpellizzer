Anthurium denudatum

Anthurium denudatum, commonly known as the naked anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of green, red, or purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium denudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
