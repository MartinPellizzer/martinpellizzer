Aronia atropurpurea

Aronia atropurpurea, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Aronia atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
