Asparagus medeoloides var. falciformis

Asparagus medeoloides var. falciformis, commonly known as the zigzag asparagus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features wiry, ascending stems that often exhibit a zigzag or undulating pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or greenish-white flowers in compact, branched clusters during the spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly wavy or zigzagged margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
