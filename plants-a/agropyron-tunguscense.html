Agropyron tunguscense

Agropyron tunguscense, commonly known as the Tungus wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and tolerating low temperatures.

Agropyron tunguscense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
