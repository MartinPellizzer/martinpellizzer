Albuca pruinosa

Albuca pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet plant or African lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and delicate blossoms make it a striking addition to any botanical collection.
