Anabasis alopecuroides

Anabasis alopecuroides, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations include deep root systems and waxy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
