Asparagus aggregatus

Asparagus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Its adaptability to rocky soils and drought conditions makes it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and medicinal plants.
