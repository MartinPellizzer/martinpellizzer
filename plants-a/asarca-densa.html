Asarca densa

Asarca densa, commonly known as the dense asarum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, dark purple to reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its underground rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds.

Asarca densa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for certain pollinators.
