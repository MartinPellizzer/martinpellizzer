Arisaema bottae

Arisaema bottae, commonly known as the Bott's arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a spadix surrounded by a brightly colored spathe, which serves to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are arrowhead-shaped, with a heart-shaped base, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
