Athanasia filiformis

Athanasia filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found in disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule-like fruit that contains numerous small seeds.

Athanasia filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
