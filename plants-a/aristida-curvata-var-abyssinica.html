Aristida curvata var. abyssinica

Aristida curvata var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian threeawn, is a grass species native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often growing in low densities.

Due to its drought tolerance and hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in arid regions.
