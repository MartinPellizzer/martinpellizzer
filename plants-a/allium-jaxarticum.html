Allium jaxarticum

Allium jaxarticum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many species of onions and garlic. The plant produces spherical bulbs composed of numerous small, white or pale pink cloves. Its leaves are grass-like, and it typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters.

It is valued for its aromatic qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
