Amerina tomentosa

Amerina tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly amerina, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Amerina tomentosa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
