Atropis glauca

Atropis glauca, commonly known as the bluebell of the Mediterranean, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dark blue berries in the fall.

Atropis glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
