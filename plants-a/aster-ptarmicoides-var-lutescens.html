Aster ptarmicoides var. lutescens

Aster ptarmicoides var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
