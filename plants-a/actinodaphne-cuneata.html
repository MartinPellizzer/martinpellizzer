Actinodaphne cuneata

Actinodaphne cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its wood and essential oils.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
