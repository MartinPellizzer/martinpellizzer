Arabis mexicana

Arabis mexicana, commonly known as Mexican rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are basal, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
