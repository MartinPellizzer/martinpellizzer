Arnebia grandiflora

Arnebia grandiflora, commonly known as the greater betony, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of bright red or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
