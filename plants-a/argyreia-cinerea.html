Argyreia cinerea

Argyreia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in some areas.
