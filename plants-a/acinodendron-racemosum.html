Acinodendron racemosum

Acinodendron racemosum, commonly known as the racemose acinodendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Anacardiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
