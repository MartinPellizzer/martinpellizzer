Acalypha communis var. puberula

Acalypha communis var. puberula, commonly known as velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it can become an invasive species in certain areas.
