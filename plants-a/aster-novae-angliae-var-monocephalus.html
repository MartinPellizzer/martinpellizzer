Aster novae-angliae var. monocephalus

Aster novae-angliae var. monocephalus, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, gray-green leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
