Acalypha digyneia

Acalypha digyneia, commonly known as the two-seeded glory flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that form showy inflorescences, often displaying vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Acalypha digyneia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
