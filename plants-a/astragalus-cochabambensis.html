Astragalus cochabambensis

Astragalus cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Bolivia.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are compound with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus cochabambensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
