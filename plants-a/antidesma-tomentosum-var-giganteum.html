Antidesma tomentosum var. giganteum

Antidesma tomentosum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant antidesma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
