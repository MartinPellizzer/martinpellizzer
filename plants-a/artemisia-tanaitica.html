Artemisia tanaitica

Artemisia tanaitica, commonly known as the Tanaitic wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds like thujone and camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
