Acalypha simplicissima

Acalypha simplicissima, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage. Acalypha simplicissima is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
