Alchemilla polonica

Alchemilla polonica, commonly known as Polish lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent properties.

Alchemilla polonica thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
