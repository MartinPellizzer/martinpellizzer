Arisaema filiforme f. pedatisectum

Arisaema filiforme f. pedatisectum, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowroot, is a variant of the arrowhead plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a delicate, lacy appearance due to the pedatisect leaf structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white spadices that are not showy but are typical of the Araceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers.
