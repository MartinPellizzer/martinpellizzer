Acca lanuginosa

Acca lanuginosa, commonly known as the silver currant, is a small shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Acca lanuginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique fruit.
