Andropogon tsaratananensis

Andropogon tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, bract-like structures.

Andropogon tsaratananensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest for botanical research due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences.
