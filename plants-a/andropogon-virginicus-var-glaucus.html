Andropogon virginicus var. glaucus

Andropogon virginicus var. glaucus, commonly known as bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bluish-green foliage and tall, slender culms that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Bluejoint grass plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
