Alopecurus aristatus var. paniceus

Alopecurus aristatus var. paniceus, commonly known as panicled bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive paniculate inflorescence, which consists of dense, branched clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces light blue to purple flowers in late summer.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
