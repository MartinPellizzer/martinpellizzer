Achillea × huteri

Achillea × huteri, commonly known as the hybrid yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Achillea millefolium (common yarrow) and Achillea ptarmica (meadow yarrow), resulting in a plant with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. The leaves are finely divided and feather-like, while the flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in flat-topped clusters. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
