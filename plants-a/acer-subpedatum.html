Acer subpedatum

Acer subpedatum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, red flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It typically grows to a height of 20 to 40 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Acer subpedatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes.
