Armeria sicula

Armeria sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a mat-like growth pattern.

Armeria sicula is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
