Andinia xenion

Andinia xenion, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white petals and a unique, elongated flower spike that resembles a ghostly apparition. The plant thrives in swampy, nutrient-poor environments and is often found growing on the roots of mangrove trees. Its botanical structure includes a slender, root-like stem and a single, showy flower that blooms once a year.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, Andinia xenion is considered a vulnerable species.
