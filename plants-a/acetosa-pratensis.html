Acetosa pratensis

Acetosa pratensis, commonly known as meadow sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is commonly found in meadows and along stream banks.

Acetosa pratensis is also known for its edible leaves, which have a tart, sour flavor.
