Abrodictyum clathratum

Abrodictyum clathratum, commonly known as the glassy orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent or glassy appearance, which is due to the intricate, lattice-like structure of its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a delicate, almost ethereal look.

Abrodictyum clathratum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses due to its specific care requirements.
