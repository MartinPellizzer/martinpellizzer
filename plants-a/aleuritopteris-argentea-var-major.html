Aleuritopteris argentea var. major

Aleuritopteris argentea var. major, commonly known as the silver fern, is a variant of the silver fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fern characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in silvery, scale-like structures, giving it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a broad, leathery texture and a striking silver hue.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
