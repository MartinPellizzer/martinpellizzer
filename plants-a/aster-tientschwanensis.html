Aster tientschwanensis

Aster tientschwanensis, commonly known as the Tientschwan aster, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces showy blue to purple flowers. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Aster tientschwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
