Asarum gracilipes

Asarum gracilipes, commonly known as slender wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to the forests of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions with rich, organic soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
