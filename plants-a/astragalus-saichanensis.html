Astragalus saichanensis

Astragalus saichanensis, commonly known as the Saichan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Astragalus saichanensis is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
