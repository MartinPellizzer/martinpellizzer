Absinthium mutellina

Absinthium mutellina, commonly known as the slender wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
