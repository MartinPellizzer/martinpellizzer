Amaryllis chionedyantha

Amaryllis chionedyantha, commonly known as the white amaryllis, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms that typically appear in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and ease of care.
