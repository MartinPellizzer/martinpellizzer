Aira caryophyllea var. curta

Aira caryophyllea var. curta, commonly known as the dwarf purplegrass, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear dense clusters of small, lavender-pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form attractive, low-growing clumps in gardens.
