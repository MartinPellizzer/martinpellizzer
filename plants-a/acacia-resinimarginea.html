Acacia resinimarginea

Acacia resinimarginea, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to golden yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Acacia resinimarginea is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Its resin has been traditionally used for medicinal and industrial purposes in various cultures.
