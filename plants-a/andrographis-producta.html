Andrographis producta

Andrographis producta, commonly known as the horsegram or Indian pennywort, is a medicinal plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a legume in various cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its traditional medicinal properties, particularly in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine.
