Alsophila guimaraensis

Alsophila guimaraensis, commonly known as the Guimaraes tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique combination of upright growth and a fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Alsophila guimaraensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
