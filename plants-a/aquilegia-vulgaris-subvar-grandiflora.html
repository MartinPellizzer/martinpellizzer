Aquilegia vulgaris subvar. grandiflora

Aquilegia vulgaris subvar. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered columbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or purple, with five petals and two prominent, elongated nectar-rich spurs. The plant has compound leaves with palmate lobes and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
