Adesmia papposa var. radicifolia

Adesmia papposa var. radicifolia, commonly known as the root-hairy burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by pods that contain small, hairy seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
