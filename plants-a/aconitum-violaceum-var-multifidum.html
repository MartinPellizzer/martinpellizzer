Aconitum violaceum var. multifidum

Aconitum violaceum var. multifidum, commonly known as the purple aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets, giving it a fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
