Acacia zapatensis

Acacia zapatensis, commonly known as the Zapatilla acacia, is a shrub native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Acacia zapatensis is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance and a deep root system.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
