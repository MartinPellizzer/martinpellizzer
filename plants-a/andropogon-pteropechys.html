Andropogon pteropechys

Andropogon pteropechys, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Andropogon pteropechys plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
