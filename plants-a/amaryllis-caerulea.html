Amaryllis caerulea

Amaryllis caerulea, commonly known as the blue amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
