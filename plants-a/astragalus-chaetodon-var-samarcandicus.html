Astragalus chaetodon var. samarcandicus

Astragalus chaetodon var. samarcandicus, commonly known as the Samarcand bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
