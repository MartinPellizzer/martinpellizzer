Aframomum hanburyi

Aframomum hanburyi, commonly known as Hanbury's cardamom, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by dark purple fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are valued for their spicy, aromatic qualities, similar to cardamom.

Due to its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
