Anthurium xanthostachyum

Anthurium xanthostachyum, commonly known as the yellow-stalked anthurium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored spathe and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces elongated, yellowish-green bracts that surround a central spadix, which bears small, inconspicuous flowers. Anthurium xanthostachyum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
