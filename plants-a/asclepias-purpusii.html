Asclepias purpusii

Asclepias purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Asclepias purpusii is an important host plant for the monarch butterfly, supporting the life cycle of this iconic species.
