Aeschynomene rueppellii

Aeschynomene rueppellii, commonly known as Ruppell's aeschynomene, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by cylindrical, hairy pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Aeschynomene rueppellii is often used in forage systems due to its nutritional value and drought tolerance.
