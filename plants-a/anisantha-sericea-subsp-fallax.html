Anisantha sericea subsp. fallax

Anisantha sericea subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the silky grass veldt, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a clump-forming habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early to mid-summer, which are arranged in panicle-like inflorescences.
