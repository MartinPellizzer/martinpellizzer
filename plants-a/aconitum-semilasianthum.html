Aconitum semilasianthum

Aconitum semilasianthum, commonly known as the Himalayan aconite, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue or purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.

Morphology

Aconitum semilasianthum has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with serrated edges and a moderate size, typically reaching up to 30 cm in length.

The stem is upright, smooth, and has a bluish-green bark that becomes rougher with age, growing in a straight, unbranched pattern. Its flowers are showy, blue to purple in color, with a spurred shape, blooming in late summer to early autumn, and are primarily pollinated by bees.
