Astragalus kitianus

Astragalus kitianus, commonly known as the Kitian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus kitianus is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
