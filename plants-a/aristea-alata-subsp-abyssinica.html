Aristea alata subsp. abyssinica

Aristea alata subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian arrowhead, is a succulent plant native to Ethiopia. It features rosettes of fleshy, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
