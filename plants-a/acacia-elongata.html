Acacia elongata

Acacia elongata, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features slender, zigzagging stems and small, phyllodes that are typically linear to narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are modified into phyllodes, a characteristic feature of many Acacia species.

Acacia elongata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
