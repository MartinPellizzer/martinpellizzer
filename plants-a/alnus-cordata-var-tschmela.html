Alnus cordata var. tschmela

Alnus cordata var. tschmela, commonly known as the narrowleaf alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
