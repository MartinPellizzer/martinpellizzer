Arhynchium

Arhynchium, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence that consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface.

Arhynchium species are often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
