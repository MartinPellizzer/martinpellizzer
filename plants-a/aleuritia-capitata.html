Aleuritia capitata

Aleuritia capitata, commonly known as the snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. Aleuritia capitata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
