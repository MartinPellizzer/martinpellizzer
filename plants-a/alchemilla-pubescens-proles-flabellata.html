Alchemilla pubescens proles flabellata

Alchemilla pubescens proles flabellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf lady's mantle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of soft, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
