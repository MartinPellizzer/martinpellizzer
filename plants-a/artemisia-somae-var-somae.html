Artemisia somae var. somae

Artemisia somae var. somae, commonly known as the Tibetan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, yellowish-gray flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand strong sunlight and low temperatures. Its leaves contain essential oils and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its resilience and medicinal potential.
