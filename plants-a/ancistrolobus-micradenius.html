Ancistrolobus micradenius

Ancistrolobus micradenius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small shrub characterized by its spiny stems and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with hot, dry climates.
