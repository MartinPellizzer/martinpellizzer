Angraecum micranthum

Angraecum micranthum, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive star-like shape. The plant is characterized by its intricate floral structure, including a long, slender spur that houses nectar. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Angraecum micranthum is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and unique blooming pattern.
