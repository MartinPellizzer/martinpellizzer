Alyssum maritimum var. genuinum

Alyssum maritimum var. genuinum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and silver-gray, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Alyssum maritimum var. genuinum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
