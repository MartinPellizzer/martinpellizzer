Adenocarpus complicatus subsp. complicatus

Adenocarpus complicatus subsp.

complicatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The species is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to livestock and humans.
