Acer oxyodon

Acer oxyodon, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which typically have five lobes and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The tree produces small, red flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Acer oxyodon is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
