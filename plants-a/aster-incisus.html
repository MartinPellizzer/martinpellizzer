Aster incisus

Aster incisus, commonly known as the cutleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces showy purple or blue flower heads that bloom in the late summer and early fall.

Aster incisus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
