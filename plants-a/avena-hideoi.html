Avena hideoi

Avena hideoi, commonly known as the Japanese oat, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Avena hideoi is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
