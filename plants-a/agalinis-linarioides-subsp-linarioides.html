Agalinis linarioides subsp. linarioides

Agalinis linarioides subsp.

linarioides, commonly known as the slender false flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from June to September. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
