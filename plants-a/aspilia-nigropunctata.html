Aspilia nigropunctata

Aspilia nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-spotted aster, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black central disk surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
