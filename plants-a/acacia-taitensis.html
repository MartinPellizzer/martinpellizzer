Acacia taitensis

Acacia taitensis, commonly known as the Taiti acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands, particularly Tahiti and Moorea.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences, which are often visited by native pollinators. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia taitensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various species.
