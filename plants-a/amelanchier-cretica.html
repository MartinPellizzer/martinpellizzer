Amelanchier cretica

Amelanchier cretica, commonly known as the Canadian serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, dense foliage and showy white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries in the summer, which are often used in culinary applications. Amelanchier cretica typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
