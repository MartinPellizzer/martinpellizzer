Abrotanella moseleyi

Abrotanella moseleyi, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features woolly, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It has a fibrous root system adapted to retain moisture in dry soils.

The species is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping.
