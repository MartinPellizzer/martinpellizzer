Aglaia urophylla

Aglaia urophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
