Amaranthus cararia

Amaranthus cararia, commonly known as purple amaranth, is an annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red bracts and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into three-angled, seed-filled capsules. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.

Amaranthus cararia is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
