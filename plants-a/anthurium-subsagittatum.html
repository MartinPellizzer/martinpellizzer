Anthurium subsagittatum

Anthurium subsagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with striking veins. The plant produces small, spadix-like flowers enclosed within a colorful spathe, typically in shades of white, red, or orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Anthurium subsagittatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
