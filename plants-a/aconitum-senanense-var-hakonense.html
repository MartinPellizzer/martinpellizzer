Aconitum senanense var. hakonense

Aconitum senanense var. hakonense, commonly known as the Hakonens' aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments, and are covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and is often found in moist, shaded environments at elevations above 3,000 meters.
