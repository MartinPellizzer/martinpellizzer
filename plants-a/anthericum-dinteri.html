Anthericum dinteri

Anthericum dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's ladies' tresses, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and a dense cluster of pale green to white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions to survive.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is highly vulnerable and protected in many regions.
