Anchusa crispata

Anchusa crispata, commonly known as the hardy blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, silver-gray hairs, and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and have a distinctive crinkled or wavy edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
