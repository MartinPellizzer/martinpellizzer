Abutilon sinense

Abutilon sinense, commonly known as Chinese Abutilon, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hairy seeds.
