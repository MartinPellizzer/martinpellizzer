Asperula hoffmeisteri

Asperula hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Asperula hoffmeisteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
