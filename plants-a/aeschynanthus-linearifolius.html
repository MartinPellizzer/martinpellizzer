Aeschynanthus linearifolius

Aeschynanthus linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved ivy creeper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Aeschynanthus linearifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb or trail in hanging baskets.
