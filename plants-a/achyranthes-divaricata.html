Achyranthes divaricata

Achyranthes divaricata, commonly known as Indian knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste lands.

Its adaptability and rapid growth make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.

Morphology

Achyranthes divaricata has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually 3–8 cm long and 1–3 cm wide, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and a dark green upper surface with a pale underside.

The stems are cylindrical, slightly hairy, and have a reddish-brown bark that becomes rough and fissured with age. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, with a wind-pollination method. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and rhizomatous, helping the plant spread vegetatively.
