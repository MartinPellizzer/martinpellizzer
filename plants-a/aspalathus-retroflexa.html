Aspalathus retroflexa

Aspalathus retroflexa, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
