Annona glabra

Annona glabra, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces a distinctive, spiky fruit with a fragrant, creamy flesh. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has a history of traditional medicinal use.
