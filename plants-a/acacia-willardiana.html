Acacia willardiana

Acacia willardiana, commonly known as the Mexican white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feather-like and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Acacia willardiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
