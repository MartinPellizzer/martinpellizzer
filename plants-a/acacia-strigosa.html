Acacia strigosa

Acacia strigosa, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The leaves are small, narrow, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Acacia strigosa is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
