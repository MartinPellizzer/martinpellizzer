Argyranthemum escarrei

Argyranthemum escarrei, commonly known as the scarlet marsh marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Argyranthemum escarrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
