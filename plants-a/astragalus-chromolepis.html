Astragalus chromolepis

Astragalus chromolepis, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Astragalus chromolepis has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
