Acacia ligulata

Acacia ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Acacia ligulata is valued for its hardiness and is used in landscaping and for soil stabilization.
