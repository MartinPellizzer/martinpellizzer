Achillea taurica

Achillea taurica, commonly known as Caucasian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called corymbs. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine绒毛, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Achillea taurica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
