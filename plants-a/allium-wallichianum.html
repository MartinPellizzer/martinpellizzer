Allium wallichianum

Allium wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan leek, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in late summer. Its bulbous base is often used in traditional cuisines for its mild, onion-like flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
