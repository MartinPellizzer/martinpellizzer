Asplenium cesatianum

Asplenium cesatianum, commonly known as the rock spleenwort, is a small, evergreen fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and its ability to colonize rocky surfaces, walls, and soil. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. It reproduces through spores, which are released from the sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium cesatianum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky habitats.
