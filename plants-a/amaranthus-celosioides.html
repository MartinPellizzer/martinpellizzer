Amaranthus celosioides

Amaranthus celosioides, commonly known as the red amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor, making it a popular choice in some cuisines.

Amaranthus celosioides thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
