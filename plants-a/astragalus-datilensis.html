Astragalus datilensis

Astragalus datilensis, commonly known as the Canary Island milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate. Astragalus datilensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ecological role includes supporting local pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.
