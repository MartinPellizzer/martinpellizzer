Astragalus deterior

Astragalus deterior, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has small, hairy stems and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy conditions.

Astragalus deterior is adapted to drought and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
