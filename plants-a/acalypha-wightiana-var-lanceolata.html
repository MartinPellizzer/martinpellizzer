Acalypha wightiana var. lanceolata

Acalypha wightiana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by spiky, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
