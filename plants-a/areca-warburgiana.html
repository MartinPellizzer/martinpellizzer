Areca warburgiana

Areca warburgiana, commonly known as the Warburg's areca, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
