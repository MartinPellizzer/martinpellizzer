Arum nigrum var. schottii

Arum nigrum var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's black arum, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy purple spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
