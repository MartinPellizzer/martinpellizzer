Acanthophyllum polyrhaphium

Acanthophyllum polyrhaphium, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
