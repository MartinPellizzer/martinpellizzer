Aster latissimifolius var. serotinus

Aster latissimifolius var. serotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
