Asphodelus pratensis

Asphodelus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow asphodel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of pale yellow to orange flowers. Its flowers are composed of three petals and three sepals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Asphodelus pratensis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
