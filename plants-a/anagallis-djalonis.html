Anagallis djalonis

Anagallis djalonis, commonly known as the African waterweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features opposite, slender leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often borne in clusters at the base of the stem, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.

Anagallis djalonis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
