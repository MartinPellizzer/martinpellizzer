Arnoglossum sulcatum

Arnoglossum sulcatum, commonly known as the cutleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, ridged textures. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Arnoglossum sulcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
