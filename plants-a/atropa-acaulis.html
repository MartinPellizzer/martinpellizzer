Atropa acaulis

Atropa acaulis, commonly known as the death cap lily, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features a rosette of dark green, waxy leaves that form a dense cluster near the ground. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive bell-shaped structure.

Despite its beauty, it is toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
