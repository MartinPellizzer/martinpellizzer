Astragalus confusus

Astragalus confusus, commonly known as the confused milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Astragalus confusus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
