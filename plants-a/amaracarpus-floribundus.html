Amaracarpus floribundus

Amaracarpus floribundus, commonly known as the showy amara, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Amaracarpus floribundus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
