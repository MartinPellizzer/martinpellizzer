Acaena torilicarpa var. gracilis

Acaena torilicarpa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender burr grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and inconspicuous. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins, and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
