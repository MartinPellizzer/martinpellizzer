Acer cinnamomifolium var. microphyllum

Acer cinnamomifolium var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf cinnamon leaf maple, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, palmate leaves that resemble those of the cinnamon tree, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 3 meters, with a compact, bushy form. Its leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
