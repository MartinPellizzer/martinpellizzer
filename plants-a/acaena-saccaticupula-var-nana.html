Acaena saccaticupula var. nana

Acaena saccaticupula var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf bull's-eyes, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, spiky, red-brown burrs that mature into persistent, spherical fruits. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
