Amphithalea incurvifolia

Amphithalea incurvifolia, commonly known as the curl-leaf euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often succulent leaves that curl inward, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in complex inflorescences called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Amphithalea incurvifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
