Asperula boryana

Asperula boryana, commonly known as the Boryan evergreen azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop for the blooms.

Asperula boryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
