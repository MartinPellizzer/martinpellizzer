Arundinaria acutissima

Arundinaria acutissima, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant forms dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 3 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Arundinaria acutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
