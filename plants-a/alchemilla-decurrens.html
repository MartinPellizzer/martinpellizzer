Alchemilla decurrens

Alchemilla decurrens, commonly known as the running alchemilla, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Alchemilla decurrens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to form dense ground cover.
