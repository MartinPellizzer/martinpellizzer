Anemone × volgensis

Anemone × volgensis, commonly known as the hybrid anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Anemone × volgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
