Allium dictyoscordum

Allium dictyoscordum, commonly known as the giant bulbous onion, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, bulbous stem and clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, edible bulbs that are often used in cooking. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Allium dictyoscordum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
