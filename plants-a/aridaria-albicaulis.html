Aridaria albicaulis

Aridaria albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed aridaria, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant has small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically pale or white, which helps attract pollinators in its native habitat.

Aridaria albicaulis is often found in rocky or sandy soils in desert regions.
