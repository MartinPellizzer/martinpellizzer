Astragalus bombycinus var. sinaicus

Astragalus bombycinus var. sinaicus, commonly known as the Sinai milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments and is often found in steppe and desert habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
