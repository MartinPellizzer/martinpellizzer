Anagallis myrtifolia

Anagallis myrtifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf or redstem mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of delicate, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Anagallis myrtifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
