Ageratina choricephaloides

Ageratina choricephaloides, commonly known as the narrow-headed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ageratina choricephaloides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
