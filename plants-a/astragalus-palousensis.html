Astragalus palousensis

Astragalus palousensis, commonly known as the Palouse milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus palousensis is often found in areas with well-drained soil and is adapted to the semi-arid climate of its native range.
