Aria danubialis

Aria danubialis, commonly known as the Danube arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as rivers, lakes, and wetlands. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually submerged, with a distinctive arrowhead shape when viewed from above. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Aria danubialis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
