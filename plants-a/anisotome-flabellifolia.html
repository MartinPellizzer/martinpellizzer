Anisotome flabellifolia

Anisotome flabellifolia, commonly known as the fan-leaved grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Anisotome flabellifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
