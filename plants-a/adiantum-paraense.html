Adiantum paraense

Adiantum paraense, commonly known as the Paraense maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Adiantaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and stream sides. Its fronds are bipinnate, with finely divided segments that give it a feathery appearance.

Adiantum paraense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
