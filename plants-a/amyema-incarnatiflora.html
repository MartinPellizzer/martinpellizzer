Amyema incarnatiflora

Amyema incarnatiflora, commonly known as the pink-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its fleshy, red berries.

Amyema incarnatiflora is an important part of the ecosystem, providing food for native wildlife.
