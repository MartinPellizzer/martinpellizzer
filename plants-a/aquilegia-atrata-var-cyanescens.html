Aquilegia atrata var. cyanescens

Aquilegia atrata var. cyanescens, commonly known as the dark blue columbine, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals that range from deep blue to almost black in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with finely toothed edges, often appearing dark green.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
