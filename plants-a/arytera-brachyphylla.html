Arytera brachyphylla

Arytera brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Arytera brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
