Alyssanthus corymbosus

Alyssanthus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Alyssanthus corymbosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in garden settings for its delicate floral display.
