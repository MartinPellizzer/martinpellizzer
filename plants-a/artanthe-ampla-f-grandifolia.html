Artanthe ampla f. grandifolia

Artanthe ampla f. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora artanthe, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
