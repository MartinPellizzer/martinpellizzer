Anthurium angustilaminatum subsp. angustilaminatum

Anthurium angustilaminatum subsp.

angustilaminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen.

The plant produces vibrant, spatulate-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which are borne on a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
