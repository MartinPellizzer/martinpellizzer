Alstroemeria werdermannii subsp. werdermannii

Alstroemeria werdermannii subsp.

werdermannii, commonly known as the Werdermann's alstroemeria, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of colorful, tubular flowers that range from red to orange and yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The subspecies is noted for its resilience and ability to withstand cooler temperatures.
