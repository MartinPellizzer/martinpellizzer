Adiantum martinii

Adiantum martinii, commonly known as the Chinese maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to China.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically bright green and have a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of the Dryopteridaceae family.

Adiantum martinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
