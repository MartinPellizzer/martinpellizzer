Axyris amaranthoides var. nana

Axyris amaranthoides var. nana, commonly known as dwarf amaranth, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge, and it produces tiny, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its short stature and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
