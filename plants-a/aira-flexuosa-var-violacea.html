Aira flexuosa var. violacea

Aira flexuosa var. violacea, commonly known as the violet false oat, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, grass-like blades with a bluish-green hue and produces small, violet-colored flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are often matted together, forming dense clumps that help retain soil moisture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
