Arundinaria retropila

Arundinaria retropila, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color. This plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Arundinaria retropila is a clump-forming plant that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
