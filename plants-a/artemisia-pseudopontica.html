Artemisia pseudopontica

Artemisia pseudopontica, commonly known as the Crimean wormwood, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Artemisia pseudopontica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
