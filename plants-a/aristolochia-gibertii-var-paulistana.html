Aristolochia gibertii var. paulistana

Aristolochia gibertii var. paulistana, commonly known as the Brazilian pipevine, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble pipes. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though caution is necessary due to its toxic properties.
