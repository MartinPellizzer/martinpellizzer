Aralia concinna

Aralia concinna, commonly known as the American aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, palmately arranged structure, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Aralia concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
