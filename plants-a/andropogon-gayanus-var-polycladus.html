Andropogon gayanus var. polycladus

Andropogon gayanus var. polycladus, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to form dense clumps and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of wildlife, including insects and small mammals.
