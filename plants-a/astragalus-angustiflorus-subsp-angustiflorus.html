Astragalus angustiflorus subsp. angustiflorus

Astragalus angustiflorus subsp.

angustiflorus, commonly known as narrow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as Nevada and California.

The plant has slender stems and small, compound leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are pale yellow to greenish-yellow, with a distinctive spurred structure typical of the Fabaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
