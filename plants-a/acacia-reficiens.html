Acacia reficiens

Acacia reficiens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in cylindrical spikes. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Acacia reficiens thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
