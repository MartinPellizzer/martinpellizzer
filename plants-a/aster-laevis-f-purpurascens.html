Aster laevis f. purpurascens

Aster laevis f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf smooth aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive purple or reddish tinge on the underside. It produces showy purple to pink flowers in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
