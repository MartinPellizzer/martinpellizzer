Anatherum glabrum

Anatherum glabrum, commonly known as the smooth grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Anatherum glabrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
