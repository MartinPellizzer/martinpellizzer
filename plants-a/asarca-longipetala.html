Asarca longipetala

Asarca longipetala, commonly known as the long-petaled arnica, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Asarca longipetala is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
