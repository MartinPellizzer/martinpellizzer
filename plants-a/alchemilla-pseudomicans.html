Alchemilla pseudomicans

Alchemilla pseudomicans, commonly known as the lesser lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.

Alchemilla pseudomicans thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
