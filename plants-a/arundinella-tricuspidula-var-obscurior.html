Arundinella tricuspidula var. obscurior

Arundinella tricuspidula var. obscurior, commonly known as the dark three-angled reed, is a variant of the arundinella genus native to certain regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, three-angled flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
