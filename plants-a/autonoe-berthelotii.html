Autonoe berthelotii

Autonoe berthelotii, commonly known as the Berthelot's autonoe, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is classified as endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
