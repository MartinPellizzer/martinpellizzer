Apocynum occidentale

Apocynum occidentale, commonly known as western dogbane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful to humans and livestock.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
