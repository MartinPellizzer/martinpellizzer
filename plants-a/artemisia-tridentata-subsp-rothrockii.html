Artemisia tridentata subsp. rothrockii

Artemisia tridentata subsp.

rothrockii, commonly known as the Utah sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and aromatic, silvery leaves that contain essential oils.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer, which are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological systems it inhabits.
