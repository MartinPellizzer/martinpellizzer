Acaena splendens var. brachyphylla

Acaena splendens var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bull's-ear, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense clusters of small, spiny leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
