Amsinckia remotifructis

Amsinckia remotifructis, commonly known as the desert tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Amsinckia remotifructis is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
