Allium sprengeri

Allium sprengeri, commonly known as the Persian onion, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its swollen, onion-like bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Allium sprengeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
