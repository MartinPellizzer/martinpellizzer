Achyranthes tomentosa

Achyranthes tomentosa, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Achyranthes tomentosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
