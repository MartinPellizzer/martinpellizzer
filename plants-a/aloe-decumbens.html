Aloe decumbens

Aloe decumbens, commonly known as the desert aloe, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy, cylindrical leaves that lie horizontally along the ground, which helps it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is sometimes used for its soothing properties.

Aloe decumbens is well adapted to survive in harsh, low-moisture conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
