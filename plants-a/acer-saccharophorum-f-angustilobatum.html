Acer saccharophorum f. angustilobatum

Acer saccharophorum f. angustilobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sugar maple, is a variant of the sugar maple with distinctive narrow, elongated leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
