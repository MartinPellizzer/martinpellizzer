Achyropappus queretarensis

Achyropappus queretarensis, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, showcasing its resilience in harsh environments.
