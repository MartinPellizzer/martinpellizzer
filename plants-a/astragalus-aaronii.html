Astragalus aaronii

Astragalus aaronii, commonly known as Aaron's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Astragalus aaronii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
