Acacia smilacifolia

Acacia smilacifolia, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces yellow to golden yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it has a woody, aromatic scent.

Acacia smilacifolia is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
