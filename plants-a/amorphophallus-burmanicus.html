Amorphophallus burmanicus

Amorphophallus burmanicus, commonly known as the Burmese arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a large, spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many aroid species. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
