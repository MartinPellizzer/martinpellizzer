Adonis pilosa

Adonis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy adonis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to withstand cold temperatures and arid conditions.

Adonis pilosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
