Agrostis alba subsp. glaucescens

Agrostis alba subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the bluish meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and sandy soils.

The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of wax. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a component in native plant restoration projects.
