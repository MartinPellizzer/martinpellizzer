Asclepias polyphylla

Asclepias polyphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Asclepias polyphylla is an important host plant for monarch butterfly larvae and plays a vital role in local ecosystems.
