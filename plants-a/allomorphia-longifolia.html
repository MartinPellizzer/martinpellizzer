Allomorphia longifolia

Allomorphia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf allomorph, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Allomorphia longifolia is often found in shaded, humid environments such as tropical forests.
