Arabis nemorensis

Arabis nemorensis, commonly known as the woodland rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

Arabis nemorensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
