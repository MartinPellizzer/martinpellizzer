Aster trinervis

Aster trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have three distinct veins, giving it its common name.

The showy purple or blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
