Astragalus hypoglottis subsp. gremlii

Astragalus hypoglottis subsp.

gremlii, commonly known as the Gremli's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone habitats.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
