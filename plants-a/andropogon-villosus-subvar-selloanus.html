Andropogon villosus subvar. selloanus

Andropogon villosus subvar. selloanus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This grass is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
