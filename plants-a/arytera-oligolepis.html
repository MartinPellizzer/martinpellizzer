Arytera oligolepis

Arytera oligolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its humid habitat. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, with a prominent spadix that bears both male and female flowers.

Arytera oligolepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in shady, moist environments.
