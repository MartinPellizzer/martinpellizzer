Anchusa formosa

Anchusa formosa, commonly known as the blueweed or purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Anchusa formosa is also known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
