Argemone mexicana subvar. inermis

Argemone mexicana subvar. inermis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a weed in some regions but also has traditional uses in folk medicine.
