Aspidium macrochlamys

Aspidium macrochlamys, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with fertile pinnae bearing sori on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
