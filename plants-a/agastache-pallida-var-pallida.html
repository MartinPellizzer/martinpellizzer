Agastache pallida var. pallida

Agastache pallida var. pallida, commonly known as narrowleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, minty fragrance.

Agastache pallida var. pallida is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and pollinator attraction.
