Alangium vitiense var. tomentosum

Alangium vitiense var. tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet alangium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and its distinctive, velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
