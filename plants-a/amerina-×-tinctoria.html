Amerina × tinctoria

Amerina × tinctoria, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a hybrid species that combines traits from its parent plants, *Abelmoschus manihot* and *Abelmoschus trilobus*.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its vibrant orange to red, papery bracts that enclose small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring moderate water and warm temperatures for optimal growth. Its unique foliage and striking inflorescences make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and containers.

The species is also valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used historically for textile production.
