Astragalus hamadryadis

Astragalus hamadryadis, commonly known as the Hamadryad's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Astragalus hamadryadis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
