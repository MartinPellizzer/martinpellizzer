Actaea spicata subsp. arguta

Actaea spicata subsp.

arguta, commonly known as the white bugbane, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white, spiky flowers.

The plant has dark green, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes, which are used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
