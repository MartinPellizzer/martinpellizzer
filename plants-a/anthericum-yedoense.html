Anthericum yedoense

Anthericum yedoense, commonly known as Japanese arum, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Anthericum yedoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
