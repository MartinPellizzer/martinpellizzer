Aechmea winkleri

Aechmea winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's Bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and provide a unique texture.

Aechmea winkleri is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
