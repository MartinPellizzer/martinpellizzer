Acacia stipuligera var. glabrifolia

Acacia stipuligera var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
