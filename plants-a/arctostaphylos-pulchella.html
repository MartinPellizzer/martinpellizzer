Arctostaphylos pulchella

Arctostaphylos pulchella, commonly known as the alpine bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Arctostaphylos pulchella is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in harsh climates.
