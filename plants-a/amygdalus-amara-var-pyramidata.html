Amygdalus amara var. pyramidata

Amygdalus amara var. pyramidata, commonly known as the Pyramidal Peach, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinctive pyramidal shape.

It produces sweet, edible fruits with a fuzzy skin and a juicy, flavorful pulp. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It has small, ovate leaves and white to light pink flowers that bloom in the spring.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
