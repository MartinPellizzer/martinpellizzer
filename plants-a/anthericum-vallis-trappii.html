Anthericum vallis-trappii

Anthericum vallis-trappii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
