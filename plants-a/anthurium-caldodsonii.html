Anthurium caldodsonii

Anthurium caldodsonii, commonly known as the Caldocondo anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a spathe, a typical feature of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Anthurium caldodsonii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
