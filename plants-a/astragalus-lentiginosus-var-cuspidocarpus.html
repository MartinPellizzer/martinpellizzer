Astragalus lentiginosus var. cuspidocarpus

Astragalus lentiginosus var. cuspidocarpus, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its small, hairy pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
