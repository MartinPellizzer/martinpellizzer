Alchemilla monticola var. hungarica

Alchemilla monticola var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
