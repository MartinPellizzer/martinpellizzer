Astragalus amphioxys var. cymbellus

Astragalus amphioxys var. cymbellus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
