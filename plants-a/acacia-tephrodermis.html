Acacia tephrodermis

Acacia tephrodermis, commonly known as the blackwood acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which gives it its common name. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in winter. Acacia tephrodermis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
