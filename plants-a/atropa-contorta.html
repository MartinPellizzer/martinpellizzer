Atropa contorta

Atropa contorta, commonly known as the narrowleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers.
