Astragalus krugeanus var. karagoelldaghensis

Astragalus krugeanus var. karagoelldaghensis, commonly known as the Karagoelldagh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky outcrops. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod, which aids in seed dispersal in its harsh environment.
