Antillanthus subsquarrosus

Antillanthus subsquarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose antillanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
