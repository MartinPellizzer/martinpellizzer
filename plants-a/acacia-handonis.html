Acacia handonis

Acacia handonis, commonly known as the Christmas tree acacia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and yellow floral clusters that bloom during the holiday season. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its unique morphology and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
