Amomum carnosum

Amomum carnosum, commonly known as wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Amomum carnosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
