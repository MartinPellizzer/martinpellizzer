Arenaria pungens subsp. parviflora

Arenaria pungens subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and sandy soils in arid regions. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to full sun and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
