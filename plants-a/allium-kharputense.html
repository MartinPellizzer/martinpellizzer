Allium kharputense

Allium kharputense, commonly known as the Kharput leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other allium species due to its specific growing conditions.
