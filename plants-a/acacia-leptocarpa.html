Acacia leptocarpa

Acacia leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Acacia leptocarpa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
