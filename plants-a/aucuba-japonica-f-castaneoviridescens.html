Aucuba japonica f. castaneoviridescens

Aucuba japonica f. castaneoviridescens, commonly known as the variegated aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking variegation of cream and gold, which provide a vibrant contrast. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and is well-suited for woodland gardens or as a foundation plant.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
