Aegilops parvula

Aegilops parvula, commonly known as the small spikelet wild oat, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, hairless stems with narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of small, spikelet-like clusters, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Aegilops parvula is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
