Agave filifera var. pannosa

Agave filifera var. pannosa, commonly known as the threadleaf agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are lined with fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
