Abbevillea neriiflora

Abbevillea neriiflora, commonly known as the black-flowered abbevillea, is a striking member of the Ericaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bell-shaped flowers that contrast beautifully with its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic plants.

Abbevillea neriiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
