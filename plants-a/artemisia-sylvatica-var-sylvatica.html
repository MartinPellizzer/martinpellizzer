Artemisia sylvatica var. sylvatica

Artemisia sylvatica var. sylvatica, commonly known as common tarragon, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, particularly in seasoning dishes.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
