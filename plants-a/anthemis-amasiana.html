Anthemis amasiana

Anthemis amasiana, commonly known as the Amasian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
