Astragalus semiglabricarpus

Astragalus semiglabricarpus, commonly known as the smoothpod milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific epithet. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus semiglabricarpus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
