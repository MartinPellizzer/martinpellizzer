Acacia arrecta

Acacia arrecta, commonly known as the feverfew tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Acacia arrecta is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, though its use is less common today.
