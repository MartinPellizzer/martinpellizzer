Acacia lacertensis

Acacia lacertensis, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, bipinnate leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Acacia lacertensis is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access underground water.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
