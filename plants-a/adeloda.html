Adeloda

Adeloda, commonly known as the mountain mahogany, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in rocky soils. Adeloda has small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.

Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various medicinal purposes.
