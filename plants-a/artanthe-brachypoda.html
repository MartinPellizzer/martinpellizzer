Artanthe brachypoda

Artanthe brachypoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf artanthe, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Artanthe brachypoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
