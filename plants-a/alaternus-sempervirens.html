Alaternus sempervirens

Alaternus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with spiny leaves that provide natural protection. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Alaternus sempervirens is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to create privacy screens.
