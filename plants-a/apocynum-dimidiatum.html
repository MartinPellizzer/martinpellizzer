Apocynum dimidiatum

Apocynum dimidiatum, commonly known as the Indian hemp or dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its distinctive seed pods are cylindrical and split open to release tiny, hooked seeds.

Apocynum dimidiatum contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and livestock.
