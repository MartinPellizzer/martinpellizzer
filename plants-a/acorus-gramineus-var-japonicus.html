Acorus gramineus var. japonicus

Acorus gramineus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps, often forming a rosette structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, and is often found in shallow water or damp soil.
