Ardisia maculosa var. symplocifolia

Ardisia maculosa var. symplocifolia, commonly known as the spotted evergreen ardisia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green with a subtle reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
