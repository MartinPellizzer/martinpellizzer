Amphistelma henriqueanum

Amphistelma henriqueanum, commonly known as the Henry's star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, colorful, and arranged in clusters, making it a visually striking species.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
