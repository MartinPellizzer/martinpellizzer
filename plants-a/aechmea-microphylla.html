Aechmea microphylla

Aechmea microphylla, commonly known as the silver pine bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of narrow, silver-blue leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant's foliage is covered in fine, white trichomes that give it a silvery appearance and help reduce water loss. Aechmea microphylla produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which are typically followed by vibrant red or orange berries.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
