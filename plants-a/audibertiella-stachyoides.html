Audibertiella stachyoides

Audibertiella stachyoides, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Audibertiella stachyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
