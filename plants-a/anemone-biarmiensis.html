Anemone biarmiensis

Anemone biarmiensis, commonly known as the Carpathian anemone, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Anemone biarmiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
