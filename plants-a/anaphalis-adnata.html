Anaphalis adnata

Anaphalis adnata, commonly known as the silverleaf frostflower, is a herbaceous plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that resemble miniature daisies, typically blooming in late summer. Anaphalis adnata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptive features make it a distinctive and resilient member of its ecosystem.
