Acer crassipes

Acer crassipes, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The tree has opposite, palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 lobes, with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

Acer crassipes thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained environments.
