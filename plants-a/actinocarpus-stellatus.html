Actinocarpus stellatus

Actinocarpus stellatus, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed star-shaped fruit when sliced. The plant is a small evergreen shrub with opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters.

The fruit develops from the flower and is typically harvested when it turns yellow or orange.
