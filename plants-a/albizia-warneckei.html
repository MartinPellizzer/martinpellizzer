Albizia warneckei

Albizia warneckei, commonly known as the pink-velvet acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky white or pink down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or pale yellow, arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Albizia warneckei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
