Anemone brevipedunculata

Anemone brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stemmed anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its flowering stems are short and often branched. Anemone brevipedunculata is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
