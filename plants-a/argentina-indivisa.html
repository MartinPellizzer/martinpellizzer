Argentina indivisa

Argentina indivisa, commonly known as the "tulipan de los Andes," is a striking flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that resemble tulips, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Argentina indivisa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
