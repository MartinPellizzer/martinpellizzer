Anaxeton asperum var. laeve

Anaxeton asperum var. laeve, commonly known as the rough-hairy mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features hairy, ovate leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
