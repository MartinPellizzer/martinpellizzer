Astragalus kindlii

Astragalus kindlii, commonly known as Kindli's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus kindlii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
