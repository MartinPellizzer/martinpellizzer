Arabis sabulosa var. colorata

Arabis sabulosa var. colorata, commonly known as the colored sandstone mustard, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh maritime conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to coastal landscapes.

This variety is distinguished by its vivid yellow flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage.
