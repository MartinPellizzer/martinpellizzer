Arctostaphylos cratericola

Arctostaphylos cratericola, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or mountainous environments.

Arctostaphylos cratericola is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
