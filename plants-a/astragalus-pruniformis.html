Astragalus pruniformis

Astragalus pruniformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with pairs of narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Astragalus pruniformis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
