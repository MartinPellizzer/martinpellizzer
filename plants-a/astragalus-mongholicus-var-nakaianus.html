Astragalus mongholicus var. nakaianus

Astragalus mongholicus var. nakaianus, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid climates.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
