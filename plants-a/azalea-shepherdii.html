Azalea shepherdii

Azalea shepherdii, commonly known as the mountain azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Azalea shepherdii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
