Arthraxon quartinianus subsp. vriesii

Arthraxon quartinianus subsp.

vriesii, commonly known as the Vries' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It is characterized by its robust root system, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
