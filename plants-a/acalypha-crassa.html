Acalypha crassa

Acalypha crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often with bracts that are colorful and showy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha crassa is known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it a common species in riparian zones.
