Acanthus humilis

Acanthus humilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that enclose small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural designs.
