Acanthus humilis

Acanthus humilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acanthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, spiny bracts that enclose small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural designs.

Native Distribution

Acanthus humilis is primarily native to southern Europe, including countries such as Spain, Portugal, and parts of southern France.

It is also found in North Africa, particularly in regions like Morocco and Algeria. This plant thrives in rocky or coastal areas and is often associated with Mediterranean climates. It is commonly found in dry, sunny habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and along the edges of forests.

Its native range extends into the Canary Islands, where it grows in similar environmental conditions.

Introduced Distribution

Acanthus humilis was mainly introduced to ornamental horticulture and garden design due to its striking foliage and floral display.
