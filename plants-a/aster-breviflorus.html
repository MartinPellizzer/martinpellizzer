Aster breviflorus

Aster breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its small, clustered flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it blooms from late summer into early fall.

Aster breviflorus is often found in California and parts of Nevada, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.
