Ardisia byrsonimae

Ardisia byrsonimae, commonly known as the Byrsonimae beautyberry, is a member of the Myrsinaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are clustered in groups of four.

These berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
