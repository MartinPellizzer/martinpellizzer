Arrojadoa rhodantha var. anguinea

Arrojadoa rhodantha var. anguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered arrojadoa, is a variant of the arrojadoa species native to parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its botanical features include a woody structure with smooth, often reddish bark.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
