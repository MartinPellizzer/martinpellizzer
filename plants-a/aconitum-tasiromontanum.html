Aconitum tasiromontanum

Aconitum tasiromontanum, commonly known as the Tasiromon Aconite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply cut lobes, and its roots are toxic, containing various alkaloids.

Due to its habitat in extreme environments, it is a rare and ecologically significant species in alpine ecosystems.
