Aidia sinensis

Aidia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Aidia sinensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade for optimal growth.
