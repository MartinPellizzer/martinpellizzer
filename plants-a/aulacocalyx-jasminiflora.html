Aulacocalyx jasminiflora

Aulacocalyx jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine-flowered acanthus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that resemble jasmine, giving it its common name. These flowers are typically borne on long, drooping peduncles, adding a decorative element to the plant.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its striking foliage and pleasant scent.
