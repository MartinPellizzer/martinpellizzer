Abies mariesii f. hayachinensis

Abies mariesii f. hayachinensis, commonly known as the Hayachinensis fir, is a rare and ornamental variant of the white fir.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical species. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
