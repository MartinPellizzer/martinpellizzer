Achyranthes obovata

Achyranthes obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
