Amaracarpus buxifolius

Amaracarpus buxifolius, commonly known as the boxleaf acanthus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for use in ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
