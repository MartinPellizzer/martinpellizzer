Aspalathus succulenta

Aspalathus succulenta, commonly known as African green oat, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Aspalathus succulenta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
