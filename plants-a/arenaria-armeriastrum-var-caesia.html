Arenaria armeriastrum var. caesia

Arenaria armeriastrum var. caesia, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
