Agrostis reuteri subsp. scabriglumis

Agrostis reuteri subsp.

scabriglumis, commonly known as the rough bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
