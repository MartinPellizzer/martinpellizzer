Aster eulae

Aster eulae, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple or pink ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by insects. Aster eulae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower habitats.

Its blooms typically appear in the summer months, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
