Acacia sibirica

Acacia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring or early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, arid environments.

Acacia sibirica is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with cold winters.
