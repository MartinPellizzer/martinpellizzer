Ageratum adscendens

Ageratum adscendens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has fine, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ageratum adscendens is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
