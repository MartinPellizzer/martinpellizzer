Arabis charbonnelii

Arabis charbonnelii, commonly known as the alpine rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. This species is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its small, pinnately divided leaves and delicate appearance make it a distinctive member of its ecological community.
