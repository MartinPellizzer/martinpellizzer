Axinanthera bellucia

Axinanthera bellucia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Axinanthera bellucia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
