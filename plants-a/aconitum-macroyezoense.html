Aconitum macroyezoense

Aconitum macroyezoense, commonly known as the Yezo aconite, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer, arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed segments that are often hairy.

Aconitum macroyezoense contains toxic alkaloids, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
