Anemone pratensis var. micrantha

Anemone pratensis var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and often have a hairy surface, while its flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of delicate, cup-shaped flowers.

The species is characterized by its relatively small size and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
