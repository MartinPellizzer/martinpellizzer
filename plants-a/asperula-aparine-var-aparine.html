Asperula aparine var. aparine

Asperula aparine var. aparine, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and small greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant contains fine hairs that can cause a stinging sensation upon contact with the skin. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Asperula aparine var. aparine is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
