Anisostachya taviala

Anisostachya taviala, commonly known as the Taviala grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland environments.

Anisostachya taviala is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
