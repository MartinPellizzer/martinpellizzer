Avicennia schaueriana

Avicennia schaueriana, commonly known as the mangrove shrub, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas where it tolerates high salt concentrations in the soil and water. The plant has specialized root systems, including pneumatophores, which aid in respiration in waterlogged soils. Its leaves are small, thick, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Avicennia schaueriana plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and supporting diverse marine and terrestrial ecosystems.
