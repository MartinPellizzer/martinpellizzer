Asarum parvifolium

Asarum parvifolium, commonly known as small-leaved wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Asarum parvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
