Achillea crithmifolia var. gubinensis

Achillea crithmifolia var. gubinensis, commonly known as the Gubin's yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, particularly for rock gardens and alpine displays.
