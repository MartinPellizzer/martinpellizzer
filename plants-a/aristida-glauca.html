Aristida glauca

Aristida glauca, commonly known as bluegrass or western bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Aristida glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
