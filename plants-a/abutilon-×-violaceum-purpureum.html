Abutilon × violaceum-purpureum

Abutilon × violaceum-purpureum, commonly known as the purple velvet abutilon, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a combination of characteristics from its parent species.

It is a shrub-like plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is notable for its fuzzy, velvety leaves that are typically a deep purple or reddish-purple in color. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they appear in clusters, adding visual interest to the plant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
