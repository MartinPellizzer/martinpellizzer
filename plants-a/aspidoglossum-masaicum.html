Aspidoglossum masaicum

Aspidoglossum masaicum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Aspidoglossum masaicum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
