Angraecum yuccifolium

Angraecum yuccifolium, commonly known as the Yucca-leaved Orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that resemble those of yucca plants, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant night-blooming flowers in shades of white and purple, which attract moths for pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
