Aeschynomene nana

Aeschynomene nana, commonly known as dwarf leadplant, is a small herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers, which are often borne in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Aeschynomene nana is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
