Adenophora marsupiiflora var. korshinskiana

Adenophora marsupiiflora var. korshinskiana, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant is characterized by its showy inflorescences and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Adenophora genus, which are valued for their horticultural and medicinal properties.
