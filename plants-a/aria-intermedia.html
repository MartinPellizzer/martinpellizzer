Aria intermedia

Aria intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Aria intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help stabilize wetland ecosystems.
