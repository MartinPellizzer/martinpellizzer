Argentina cardotiana

Argentina cardotiana, commonly known as the Argentine cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water. The plant produces yellow or white flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to harsh environments make it a resilient and hardy species.

Argentina cardotiana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
