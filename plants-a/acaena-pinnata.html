Acaena pinnata

Acaena pinnata, commonly known as the New Zealand thistle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions. The plant features small, spiny leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are burrs with hooked spines, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

Acaena pinnata plays a role in its ecosystem as a food source for certain insects and birds.
