Aloysia attenuata

Aloysia attenuata, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and as a source of essential oils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in both ornamental gardening and herbal medicine.
