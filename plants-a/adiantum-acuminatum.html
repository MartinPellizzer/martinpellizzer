Adiantum acuminatum

Adiantum acuminatum, commonly known as the southern maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Adiantaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spore-bearing sorus structures on the underside of its fronds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
