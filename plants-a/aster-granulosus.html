Aster granulosus

Aster granulosus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy, violet-blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are covered in a fine, granular texture, giving it its common name.

Aster granulosus is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
