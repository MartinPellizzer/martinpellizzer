Ampacus celastracea

Ampacus celastracea, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by bright orange or red berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
