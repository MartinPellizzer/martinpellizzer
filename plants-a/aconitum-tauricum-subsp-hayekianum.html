Aconitum tauricum subsp. hayekianum

Aconitum tauricum subsp.

hayekianum, commonly known as the Caucasian monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features striking blue to purple hooded flowers that are typical of the genus Aconitum.

The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, often toxic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
