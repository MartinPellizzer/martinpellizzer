Alsophila sollyana

Alsophila sollyana, commonly known as the Solly's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and roots, while its fronds play a crucial role in photosynthesis.

Alsophila sollyana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.
