Aster incisus var. australis

Aster incisus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian aster, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
