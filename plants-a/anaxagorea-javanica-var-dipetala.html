Anaxagorea javanica var. dipetala

Anaxagorea javanica var. dipetala, commonly known as the two-petaled satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with two petals, giving it its varietal name. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
