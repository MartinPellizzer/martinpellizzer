Aechmea mollis

Aechmea mollis, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The leaves are thick, leathery, and covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Morphology

Aechmea mollis has rosette-shaped leaves that are broad, sword-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The leaves are arranged in a compact rosette, typically reaching lengths of 30 to 60 centimeters, and are a deep green color with a bluish tint. The plant lacks a prominent stem, with the base of the leaves forming a dense, compact rosette that resembles a cushion. It produces striking inflorescences with spathaceous bracts, bearing showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the summer months and are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds.

The fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.
