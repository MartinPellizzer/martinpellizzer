Astragalus brevicalycinus

Astragalus brevicalycinus, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Astragalus brevicalycinus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
