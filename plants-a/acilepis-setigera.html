Acilepis setigera

Acilepis setigera, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by a spherical structure called a "cyst" or "cyst-like" structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
