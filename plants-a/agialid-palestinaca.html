Agialid palestinaca

Agialid palestinaca, commonly known as the Palestinian marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the coastal wetlands of the eastern Mediterranean.

It thrives in brackish and freshwater marshes, often found in areas with high salinity and fluctuating water levels. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and salt spray. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Agialid palestinaca plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
