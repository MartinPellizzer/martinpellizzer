Alyssum lapeyrouseanum

Alyssum lapeyrouseanum, commonly known as Lapeyrouse's alyssum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It has a low-growing habit with silvery-green foliage and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Alyssum lapeyrouseanum is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is drought-tolerant once established.
