Athamanta pubescens

Athamanta pubescens, commonly known as the hairy athamanta, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Athamanta pubescens is known for its resilience to drought and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
