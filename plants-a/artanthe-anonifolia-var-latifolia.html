Artanthe anonifolia var. latifolia

Artanthe anonifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved artanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features basal leaves that are broad and lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
