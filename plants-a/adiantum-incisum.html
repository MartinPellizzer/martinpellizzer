Adiantum incisum

Adiantum incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that are finely divided and often have a feathery appearance. The plant prefers shady, moist environments and is typically found in woodland areas. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Adiantum incisum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
