Adicea latifolia

Adicea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf adicea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Adicea latifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
