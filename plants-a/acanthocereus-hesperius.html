Acanthocereus hesperius

Acanthocereus hesperius, commonly known as the night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines and have a ribbed structure. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its adaptations include a succulent structure for water storage and a nocturnal blooming cycle to conserve moisture.

The plant thrives in arid environments and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
