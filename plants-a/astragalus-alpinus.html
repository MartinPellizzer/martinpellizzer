Astragalus alpinus

Astragalus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Astragalus alpinus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature in harsh environments.
