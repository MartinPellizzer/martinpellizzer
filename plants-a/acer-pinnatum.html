Acer pinnatum

Acer pinnatum, commonly known as the laceleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a striking addition to landscapes, especially in the spring and fall.
