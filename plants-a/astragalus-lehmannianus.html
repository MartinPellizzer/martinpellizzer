Astragalus lehmannianus

Astragalus lehmannianus, commonly known as Lehmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Astragalus lehmannianus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
