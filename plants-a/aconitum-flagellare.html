Aconitum flagellare

Aconitum flagellare, commonly known as the whip aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are palmately compound, with deeply lobed divisions that give it a distinctive appearance.

Aconitum flagellare contains potent alkaloids, making it highly toxic and requiring caution during handling or cultivation.
