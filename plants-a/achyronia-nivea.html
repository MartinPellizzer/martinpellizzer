Achyronia nivea

Achyronia nivea, commonly known as the white orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a slender, erect stem. It typically blooms during the summer months, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
