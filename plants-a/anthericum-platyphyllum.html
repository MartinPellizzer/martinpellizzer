Anthericum platyphyllum

Anthericum platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf arum, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf with a broad, flat base and a narrow, pointed tip. The plant produces a spadix, which is a dense cluster of tiny flowers surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. Its flowers are typically greenish-white in color and are arranged in a complex, clustered structure.

Anthericum platyphyllum prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
