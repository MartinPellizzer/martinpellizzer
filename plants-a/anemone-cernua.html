Anemone cernua

Anemone cernua, commonly known as the white avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped or kidney-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers have five to eight petals and are surrounded by a cluster of sepals.

Anemone cernua thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
