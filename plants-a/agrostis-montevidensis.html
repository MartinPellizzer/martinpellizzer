Agrostis montevidensis

Agrostis montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, soft leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, which is both ornamental and important for pollinators.

Agrostis montevidensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
