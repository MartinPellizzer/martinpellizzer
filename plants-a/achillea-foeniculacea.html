Achillea foeniculacea

Achillea foeniculacea, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The common name "sweet everlasting" refers to its fragrant scent and the durability of its dried flowers.
