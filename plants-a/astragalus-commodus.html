Astragalus commodus

Astragalus commodus, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to various regions across Asia and North America, it thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Astragalus commodus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
