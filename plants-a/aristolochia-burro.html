Aristolochia burro

Aristolochia burro, commonly known as burro's pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, waxy, pipe-like flowers that range in color from green to purple. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
