Albertisia scandens

Albertisia scandens, commonly known as the climbing Albertisia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
