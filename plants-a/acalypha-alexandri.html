Acalypha alexandri

Acalypha alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian curlleaf, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
