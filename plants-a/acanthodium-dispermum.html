Acanthodium dispermum

Acanthodium dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded thornplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces two seeds per fruit, which are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange capsule that splits open when mature. Acanthodium dispermum is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep root systems and waxy leaves to reduce water loss.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
