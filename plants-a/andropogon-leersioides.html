Andropogon leersioides

Andropogon leersioides, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Andropogon leersioides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to waterlogged conditions.
