Acacia stipuligera subsp. glabrifolia

Acacia stipuligera subsp.

glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and pinnate leaves with small, reduced stipules.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glabrous (smooth) leaflets and reduced stipules, setting it apart from other acacia species.
