Allium derderianum

Allium derderianum, commonly known as the Armenian garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, narrow leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces spherical bulbs that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Allium derderianum is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
