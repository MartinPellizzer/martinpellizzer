Allium tolmiei var. tolmiei

Allium tolmiei var. tolmiei, commonly known as the Tolmiei onion, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bulbs, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic traits and potential horticultural value.
