Astragalus atrosanguineus

Astragalus atrosanguineus, commonly known as the black blood milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Astragalus atrosanguineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
