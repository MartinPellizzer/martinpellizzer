Acacia saponaria

Acacia saponaria, commonly known as the soapbark acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing small, hard seeds. Acacia saponaria is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh conditions.

Its name derives from the soap-like substance found in its bark, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
