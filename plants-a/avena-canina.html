Avena canina

Avena canina, commonly known as dog's grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it blooms with tiny, greenish flowers.

Avena canina is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas where it can thrive in poor soil conditions.
