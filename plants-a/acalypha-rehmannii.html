Acalypha rehmannii

Acalypha rehmannii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant typically has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Acalypha rehmannii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
