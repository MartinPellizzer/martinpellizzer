Agave atrovirens var. cochlearis

Agave atrovirens var. cochlearis, commonly known as the cochlear agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk with small, white flowers, which appears after several years of growth. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.
