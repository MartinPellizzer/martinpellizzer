Astragalus pinardii

Astragalus pinardii, commonly known as the Pinard's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus pinardii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
