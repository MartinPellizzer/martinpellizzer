Acacia donaldi

Acacia donaldi, commonly known as the white wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and clusters of small, fluffy white flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as true leaves, reducing water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
