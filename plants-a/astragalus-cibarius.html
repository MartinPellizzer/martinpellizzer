Astragalus cibarius

Astragalus cibarius, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas.

Astragalus cibarius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
