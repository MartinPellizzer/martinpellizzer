Acacia cygnorum

Acacia cygnorum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow globular flowerheads that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Acacia cygnorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
