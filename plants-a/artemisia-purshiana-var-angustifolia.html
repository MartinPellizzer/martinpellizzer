Artemisia purshiana var. angustifolia

Artemisia purshiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sagebrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its essential oils contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
