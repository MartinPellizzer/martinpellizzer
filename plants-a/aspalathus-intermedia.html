Aspalathus intermedia

Aspalathus intermedia, commonly known as the African red bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.

However, it is not typically cultivated for commercial purposes.
