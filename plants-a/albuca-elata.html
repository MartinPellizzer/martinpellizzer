Albuca elata

Albuca elata, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pale yellow, with a distinctive purple stripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Albuca elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
