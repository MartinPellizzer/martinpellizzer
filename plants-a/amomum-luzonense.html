Amomum luzonense

Amomum luzonense, commonly known as Luzon cardamom, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and spikes of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark brown, three-lobed berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
