Aphanocalyx pteridophyllus

Aphanocalyx pteridophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often palmately lobed. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
