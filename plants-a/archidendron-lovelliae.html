Archidendron lovelliae

Archidendron lovelliae, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
