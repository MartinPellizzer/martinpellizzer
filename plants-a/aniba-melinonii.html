Aniba melinonii

Aniba melinonii, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is valued for its fragrant wood, which has been traditionally used in the production of incense and other aromatic products.
