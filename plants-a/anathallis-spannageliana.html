Anathallis spannageliana

Anathallis spannageliana, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence that resembles a spider, with long, slender floral stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Anathallis spannageliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
