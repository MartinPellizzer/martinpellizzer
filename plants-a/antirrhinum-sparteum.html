Antirrhinum sparteum

Antirrhinum sparteum, commonly known as the mountain snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Antirrhinum sparteum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.
