Aira saburralis

Aira saburralis, commonly known as the Japanese grass or sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional practices in its native regions.
