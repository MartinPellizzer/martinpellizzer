Amorphophallus sumawongii

Amorphophallus sumawongii, commonly known as the Sumawong arrowhead, is a tuberous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a large, spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of its family. Its underground tubers are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in some regions.
