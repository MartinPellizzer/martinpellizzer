Allium hymenorhizum

Allium hymenorhizum, commonly known as the Persian leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and clustered, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbous roots are edible and have a mild, garlic-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other allium species due to its specific growing requirements.
