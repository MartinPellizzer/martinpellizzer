Albuca thermarum

Albuca thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring albuca, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Albuca thermarum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
