Anacamptis papilionacea subsp. aegaea

Anacamptis papilionacea subsp.

aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple flowers with a spurred lip.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems bear a single spike of flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
