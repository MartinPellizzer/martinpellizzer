Anisocereus

Anisocereus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to Central and South America.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and have a tubular shape with prominent stamens. Anisocereus species are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

They are popular among cactus enthusiasts for their striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
