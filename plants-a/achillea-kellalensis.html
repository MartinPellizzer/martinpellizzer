Achillea kellalensis

Achillea kellalensis, commonly known as the Kellal yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pale yellow to white in color.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
