Aria nivea var. incisa

Aria nivea var. incisa, commonly known as the white snowflake, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
