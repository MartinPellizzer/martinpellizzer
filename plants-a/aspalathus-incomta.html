Aspalathus incomta

Aspalathus incomta, commonly known as the silver leaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Aspalathus incomta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
