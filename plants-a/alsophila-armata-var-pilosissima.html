Alsophila armata var. pilosissima

Alsophila armata var. pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a variant of the silver tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dense layer of brown, fibrous roots. The fronds are pinnately compound, with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinctive, hairy appearance on the undersides. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
