Agrostis alba var. aristata

Agrostis alba var. aristata, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, silvery-white inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, fuzzy texture that contributes to its ornamental value.

Agrostis alba var. aristata is often used in native plant gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
