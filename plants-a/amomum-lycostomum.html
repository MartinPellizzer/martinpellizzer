Amomum lycostomum

Amomum lycostomum, commonly known as the Indian cardamom or wild cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, aromatic fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a unique flavor to dishes.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
