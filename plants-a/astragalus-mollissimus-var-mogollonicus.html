Astragalus mollissimus var. mogollonicus

Astragalus mollissimus var. mogollonicus, commonly known as the Mogollon milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
