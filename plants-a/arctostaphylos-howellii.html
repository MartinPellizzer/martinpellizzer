Arctostaphylos howellii

Arctostaphylos howellii, commonly known as Howell's manzanita, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
