Acaena multifida var. ramificans

Acaena multifida var. ramificans, commonly known as the branched wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
