Anguloa brevilabris

Anguloa brevilabris, commonly known as the short-lipped anguloa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a short, robust stem with a few leaves, and its flowers are often clustered in a loose inflorescence. Anguloa brevilabris thrives in high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a prized ornamental plant in horticultural collections.
