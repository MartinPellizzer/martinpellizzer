Achillea nobilis subsp. kurdica

Achillea nobilis subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces flat-topped clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
