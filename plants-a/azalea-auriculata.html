Azalea auriculata

Azalea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate azalea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Azalea auriculata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
