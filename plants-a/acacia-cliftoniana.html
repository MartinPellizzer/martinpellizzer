Acacia cliftoniana

Acacia cliftoniana, commonly known as the Clifton's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces yellow, globular flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils.

Acacia cliftoniana is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
