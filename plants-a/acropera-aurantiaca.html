Acropera aurantiaca

Acropera aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette around the base.

Acropera aurantiaca is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
