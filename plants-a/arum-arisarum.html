Arum arisarum

Arum arisarum, commonly known as the Italian arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a striking, arrow-shaped leaf that is usually dark green with a prominent white midrib. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is typically bright yellow or orange. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and unique flowering structure.
