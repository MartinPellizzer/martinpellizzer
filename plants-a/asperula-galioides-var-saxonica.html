Asperula galioides var. saxonica

Asperula galioides var. saxonica, commonly known as the Saxon woodruff, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a woody rootstock. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
