Agathophytum rubrum

Agathophytum rubrum, commonly known as the red agathophytum, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to minimize water loss.

Agathophytum rubrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a favorite among succulent enthusiasts.
