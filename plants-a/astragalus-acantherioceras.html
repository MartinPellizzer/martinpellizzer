Astragalus acantherioceras

Astragalus acantherioceras, commonly known as the hooked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, bean-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to thrive in dry soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus acantherioceras plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
