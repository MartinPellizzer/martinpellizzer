Ampelopsis veitchii

Ampelopsis veitchii, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that transition into edible, yellow-orange berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and fruit.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
