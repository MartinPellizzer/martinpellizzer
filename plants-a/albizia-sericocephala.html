Albizia sericocephala

Albizia sericocephala, commonly known as the silk-capped acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in savanna and woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, fluffy, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its pods are elongated and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Albizia sericocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
