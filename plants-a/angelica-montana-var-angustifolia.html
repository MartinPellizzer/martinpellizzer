Angelica montana var. angustifolia

Angelica montana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves that have a distinctive pinnate structure. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

This variety is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
