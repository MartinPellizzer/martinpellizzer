Amomum pubimarginatum

Amomum pubimarginatum, commonly known as the hairy-edge cardamom, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is cultivated for both its culinary and medicinal uses.
