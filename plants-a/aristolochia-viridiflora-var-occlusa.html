Aristolochia viridiflora var. occlusa

Aristolochia viridiflora var. occlusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, twisting, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking foliage and unusual floral structure.
