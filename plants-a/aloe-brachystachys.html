Aloe brachystachys

Aloe brachystachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of narrow, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with tubular, yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Aloe brachystachys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
