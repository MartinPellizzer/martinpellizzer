Alstroemeria bilabiata

Alstroemeria bilabiata, commonly known as the pink alstroemeria, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features long, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
