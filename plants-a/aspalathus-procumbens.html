Aspalathus procumbens

Aspalathus procumbens, commonly known as African red bush tea, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bright red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a popular herbal tea.

The botanical features of Aspalathus procumbens make it well-suited for both ornamental gardening and commercial tea production.
