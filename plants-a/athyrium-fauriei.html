Athyrium fauriei

Athyrium fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Athyriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a distinctive, shield-shaped pattern.

Athyrium fauriei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often used in shaded garden settings.
