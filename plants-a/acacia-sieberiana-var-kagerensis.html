Acacia sieberiana var. kagerensis

Acacia sieberiana var. kagerensis, commonly known as the Kagera acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to orange-pink globular flower heads, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
