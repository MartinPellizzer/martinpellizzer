Alchemilla parodii

Alchemilla parodii, commonly known as the silverleaf alchemilla, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Alchemilla parodii is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
