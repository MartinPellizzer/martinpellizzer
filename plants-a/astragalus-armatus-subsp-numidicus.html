Astragalus armatus subsp. numidicus

Astragalus armatus subsp.

numidicus, commonly known as the Numidian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
