Arisaema peerumedense

Arisaema peerumedense, commonly known as the Malayan arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are typically large, arrow-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its starchy tubers, which are used in local cuisines.
