Abarema maestrensis

Abarema maestrensis, commonly known as the Maestrensis legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, elongated pods, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
