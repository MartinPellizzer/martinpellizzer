Arabis rhaetica

Arabis rhaetica, commonly known as Rhaetian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately divided.

Arabis rhaetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
