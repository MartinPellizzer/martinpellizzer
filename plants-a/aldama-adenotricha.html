Aldama adenotricha

Aldama adenotricha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glandular-hairy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
