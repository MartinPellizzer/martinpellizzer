Andropogon lumeneensis

Andropogon lumeneensis, commonly known as the Lumeneensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
