Alchemilla glaberrima var. incisa

Alchemilla glaberrima var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features a low-growing habit with a dense rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are clustered in open, branched inflorescences. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a waxy surface, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
