Abies kansouensis

Abies kansouensis, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Abies in the family Pinaceae, characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage is dense and dark green, often retaining its color throughout the winter.

The cones are small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
