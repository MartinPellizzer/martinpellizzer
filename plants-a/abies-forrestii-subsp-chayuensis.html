Abies forrestii subsp. chayuensis

Abies forrestii subsp.

chayuensis, commonly known as the Chayu fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a compact, sub-opposite pattern.

The tree produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is of significant botanical interest due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
