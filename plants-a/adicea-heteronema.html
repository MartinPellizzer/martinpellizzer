Adicea heteronema

Adicea heteronema, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features soft, silvery-blue foliage and produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Adicea heteronema thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
