Amaryllis stilosa

Amaryllis stilosa, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring or early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Amaryllis stilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
