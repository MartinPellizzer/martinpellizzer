Astracantha gaziantepica

Astracantha gaziantepica, commonly known as the Gaziantep thistle, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant is known for its spiny, globular inflorescence, which consists of both ray and disk flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
