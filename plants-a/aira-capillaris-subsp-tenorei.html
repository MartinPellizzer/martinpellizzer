Aira capillaris subsp. tenorei

Aira capillaris subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Tuscan grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and delicate, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, awned seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological and botanical studies in Mediterranean ecosystems.
