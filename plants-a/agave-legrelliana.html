Agave legrelliana

Agave legrelliana, commonly known as the Mexican blue agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, blue-gray leaves with sharp spines along the edges. It produces a tall flowering stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers.

Agave legrelliana is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
