Aralia erinacea

Aralia erinacea, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features a tall, unbranched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Aralia erinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
