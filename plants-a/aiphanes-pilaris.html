Aiphanes pilaris

Aiphanes pilaris, commonly known as the pilar palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay.

It is a small to medium-sized palm, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with a slender trunk and a crown of feathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating its wind-pollinated reproductive cycle. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.

Aiphanes pilaris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
