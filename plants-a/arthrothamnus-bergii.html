Arthrothamnus bergii

Arthrothamnus bergii, commonly known as the Berg's thorny shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically inconspicuous and arranged in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient species in semi-desert and rocky habitats.
