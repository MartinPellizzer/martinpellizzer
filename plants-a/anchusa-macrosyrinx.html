Anchusa macrosyrinx

Anchusa macrosyrinx, commonly known as the tall blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Anchusa macrosyrinx is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils.
