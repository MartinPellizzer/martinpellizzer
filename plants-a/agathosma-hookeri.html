Agathosma hookeri

Agathosma hookeri, commonly known as the Cape star or African geranium, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers clustered in star-like formations, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated for essential oils and traditional herbal use.
