Anthriscus chaerophyllus

Anthriscus chaerophyllus, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and delicate, umbellate flowers. The plant produces small, aromatic seeds that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Anthriscus chaerophyllus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
