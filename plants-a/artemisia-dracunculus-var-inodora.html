Artemisia dracunculus var. inodora

Artemisia dracunculus var. inodora, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting beneficial insects. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its potential therapeutic properties.
