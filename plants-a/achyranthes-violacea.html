Achyranthes violacea

Achyranthes violacea, commonly known as purple borage, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
