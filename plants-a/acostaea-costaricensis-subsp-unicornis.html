Acostaea costaricensis subsp. unicornis

Acostaea costaricensis subsp.

unicornis, commonly known as the unicorn fern, is a unique member of the Araceae family. It is native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica, and is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often spiral-shaped fronds.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix and enclosed by a spathe. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive appearance and is often cultivated for its visual interest in shaded garden environments.
