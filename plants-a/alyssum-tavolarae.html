Alyssum tavolarae

Alyssum tavolarae, commonly known as Tavolara alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers throughout the growing season. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Alyssum tavolarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
