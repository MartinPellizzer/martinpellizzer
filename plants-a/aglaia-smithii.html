Aglaia smithii

Aglaia smithii, commonly known as the white aglaia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and ash trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing several winged seeds, which aid in wind dispersal.

Aglaia smithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
