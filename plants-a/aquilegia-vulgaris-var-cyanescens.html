Aquilegia vulgaris var. cyanescens

Aquilegia vulgaris var. cyanescens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a variant of the common columbine that features striking blue to violet flowers.

This perennial plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. The flowers are typically arranged in a spire-like cluster, with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
