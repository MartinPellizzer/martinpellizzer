Arbutus × andrachnoides

Arbutus × andrachnoides, commonly known as the coastal manzanita, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in maritime climates.
