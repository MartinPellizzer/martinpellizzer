Astracantha consentanea

Astracantha consentanea, commonly known as the Brazilian starbursts, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Astracantha consentanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
