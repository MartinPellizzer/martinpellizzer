Anthyllis webbiana f. alpina

Anthyllis webbiana f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine anthyllis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, yellow or reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to harsh, rocky environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
