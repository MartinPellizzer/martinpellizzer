Amaranthus caesius

Amaranthus caesius, commonly known as the purple amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense, branching clusters. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the seeds are rich in nutrients.

Amaranthus caesius is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
