Alsophila muricata

Alsophila muricata, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a thick layer of brown, fibrous roots. The plant has a crown of feathery, light green fronds that grow in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in horticulture.
