Atriplex boecheri

Atriplex boecheri, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Atriplex boecheri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
