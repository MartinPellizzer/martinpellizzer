Andropogon cymbarius var. lepidus

Andropogon cymbarius var. lepidus, commonly known as the slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
