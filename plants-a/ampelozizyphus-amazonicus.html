Ampelozizyphus amazonicus

Ampelozizyphus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian grapefruit tree, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to citrus fruits but have a distinct flavor profile. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
