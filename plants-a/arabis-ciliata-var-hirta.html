Arabis ciliata var. hirta

Arabis ciliata var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive ciliate margin, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in racemes, and bloom in early spring.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine environments, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these harsh ecosystems.
