Abrus wittei

Abrus wittei, commonly known as the African snail bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, inflated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
