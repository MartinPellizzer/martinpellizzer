Adhatoda capensis var. arenosa

Adhatoda capensis var. arenosa, commonly known as the sand adhatoda, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported respiratory benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
