Achras meridionalis

Achras meridionalis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces a fruit that is botanically classified as a syncarp, formed from the fusion of multiple berries. The fruit is rich in carbohydrates and has been a vital food source for indigenous communities.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
