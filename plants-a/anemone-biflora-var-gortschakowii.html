Anemone biflora var. gortschakowii

Anemone biflora var. gortschakowii, commonly known as the Gortschakow's anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed, with hairy surfaces, and stems that are upright and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and early blooming habit.
