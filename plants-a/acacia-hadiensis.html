Acacia hadiensis

Acacia hadiensis, commonly known as the white wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments.

Acacia hadiensis is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
