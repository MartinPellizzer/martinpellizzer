Aquilegia ottonis var. magellensis

Aquilegia ottonis var. magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic bellflower, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant, often red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces seed pods that burst open to disperse its seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
