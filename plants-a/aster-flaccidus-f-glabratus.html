Aster flaccidus f. glabratus

Aster flaccidus f. glabratus, commonly known as the glabrous aster, is a flowering plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to the eastern United States. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
