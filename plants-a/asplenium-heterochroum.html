Asplenium heterochroum

Asplenium heterochroum, commonly known as the variegated shield fern, is a species of polypod fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with contrasting white or silver veins. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with small, ovate leaflets arranged along the rachis.

Asplenium heterochroum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
