Aristida mutabilis var. senegalensis

Aristida mutabilis var. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and along roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually pale green to golden in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
