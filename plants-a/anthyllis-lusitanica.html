Anthyllis lusitanica

Anthyllis lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian anthyllis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Anthyllis lusitanica is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, supporting soil fertility in arid environments.
