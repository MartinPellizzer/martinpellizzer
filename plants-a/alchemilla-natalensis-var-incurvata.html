Alchemilla natalensis var. incurvata

Alchemilla natalensis var. incurvata, commonly known as Natal lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are softly hairy and have a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
