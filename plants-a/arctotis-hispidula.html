Arctotis hispidula

Arctotis hispidula, commonly known as the roughleaf arctotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces vibrant blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid climates.

Arctotis hispidula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
