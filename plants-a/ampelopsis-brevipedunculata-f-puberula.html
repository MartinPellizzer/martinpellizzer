Ampelopsis brevipedunculata f. puberula

Ampelopsis brevipedunculata f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy grape ivy, is a varietal form of the species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to climb or cascade from supports. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.
