Allium lancipetalum

Allium lancipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed garlic chive, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and leeks, and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, typical of many Allium species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its culinary and medicinal uses are similar to other Allium plants, though it is less commonly cultivated than its more famous relatives.
