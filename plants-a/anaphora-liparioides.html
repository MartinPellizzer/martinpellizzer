Anaphora liparioides

Anaphora liparioides, commonly known as the Lipari Island bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
