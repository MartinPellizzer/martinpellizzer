Avena abyssinica var. chiovendae

Avena abyssinica var. chiovendae, commonly known as the Abyssinian oat, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically borne on long, drooping stalks, with small, pale flowers. It thrives in high-altitude regions and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
