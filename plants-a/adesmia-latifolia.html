Adesmia latifolia

Adesmia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Adesmia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in its native habitats.
