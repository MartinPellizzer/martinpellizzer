Acacia collina

Acacia collina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and yellow-green in color. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Acacia collina is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of timber and fuel.
