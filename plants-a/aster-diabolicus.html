Aster diabolicus

Aster diabolicus, commonly known as the devil's aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet to purple petals that surround a central disk of smaller florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its striking appearance and remote habitat, it is considered a rare and somewhat mysterious species.
