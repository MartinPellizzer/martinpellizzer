Alloberberis trifoliolata var. glauca

Alloberberis trifoliolata var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous three-leaf berberis, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
