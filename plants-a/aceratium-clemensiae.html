Aceratium clemensiae

Aceratium clemensiae, commonly known as the Clemens' acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of spikelike clusters of small, tubular flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in warm climates.
