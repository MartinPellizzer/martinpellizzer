Aspalathus pinguis

Aspalathus pinguis, commonly known as the fat bonnet, is a species of legume native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated pod-like fruits that resemble small, rounded bells. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal regions. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Aspalathus pinguis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
