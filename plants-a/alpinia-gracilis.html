Alpinia gracilis

Alpinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Alpinia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
