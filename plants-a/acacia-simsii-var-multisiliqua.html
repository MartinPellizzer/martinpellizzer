Acacia simsii var. multisiliqua

Acacia simsii var. multisiliqua, commonly known as the multibract acacia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular inflorescences, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts. Its root system is highly adapted to drought, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
