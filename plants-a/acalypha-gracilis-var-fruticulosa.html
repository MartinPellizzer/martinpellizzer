Acalypha gracilis var. fruticulosa

Acalypha gracilis var. fruticulosa, commonly known as the red-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and striking, velvety red or purple leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is suitable for container gardening and small garden spaces.
