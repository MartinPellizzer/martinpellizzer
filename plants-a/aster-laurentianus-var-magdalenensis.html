Aster laurentianus var. magdalenensis

Aster laurentianus var. magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalen Aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

It produces showy purple to blue flowers in the fall, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
