Achillea moschata var. haussknechtiana

Achillea moschata var. haussknechtiana, commonly known as the Persian chamomile, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow-centered flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, chamomile-like scent, which comes from volatile oils in its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
