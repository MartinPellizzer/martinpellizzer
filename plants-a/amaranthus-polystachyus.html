Amaranthus polystachyus

Amaranthus polystachyus, commonly known as the Indian spinach or love lies beneath, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into spiny, three-lobed seed capsules. The plant produces edible leaves that are rich in nutrients, making it a valuable crop in some parts of the world. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Amaranthus polystachyus is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
