Aloe brunneopunctata

Aloe brunneopunctata, commonly known as the brown-spotted aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green with distinctive brown or reddish spots. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellow flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and birds. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Aloe brunneopunctata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
