Angraecoides panicifolia

Angraecoides panicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the inflorescence consists of multiple flower spikes.

Angraecoides panicifolia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
