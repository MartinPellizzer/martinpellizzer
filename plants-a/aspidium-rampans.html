Aspidium rampans

Aspidium rampans, commonly known as the wandering spleen, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often appear in a cascading or trailing manner. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Aspidium rampans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
