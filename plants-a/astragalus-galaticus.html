Astragalus galaticus

Astragalus galaticus, commonly known as the star vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Astragalus galaticus is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
