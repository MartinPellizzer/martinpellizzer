Alocasia fornicata

Alocasia fornicata, commonly known as the hooded arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Alocasia fornicata is popular as a ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and unique appearance.
