Astragalus cornu-bovis

Astragalus cornu-bovis, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. The plant is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped seed pods and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Astragalus cornu-bovis plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
