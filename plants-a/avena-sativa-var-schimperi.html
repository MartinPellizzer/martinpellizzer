Avena sativa var. schimperi

Avena sativa var. schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian oat, is a variant of the common oat that is native to Ethiopia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, whitish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its seeds are used for both human consumption and animal feed, and it plays a role in traditional agriculture in its native region.
