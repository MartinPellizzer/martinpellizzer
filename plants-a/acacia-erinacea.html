Acacia erinacea

Acacia erinacea, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
