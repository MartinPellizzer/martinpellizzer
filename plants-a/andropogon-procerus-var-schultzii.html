Andropogon procerus var. schultzii

Andropogon procerus var. schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze bluestem, is a variant of the tall bluestem grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
