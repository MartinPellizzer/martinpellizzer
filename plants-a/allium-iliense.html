Allium iliense

Allium iliense, commonly known as the Ili Valley garlic, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Allium genus, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs underground. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in a dense umbel.

Allium iliense is valued for its potential culinary and medicinal uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other garlic varieties.
