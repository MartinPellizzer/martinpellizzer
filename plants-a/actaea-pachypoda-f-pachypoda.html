Actaea pachypoda f. pachypoda

Actaea pachypoda f. pachypoda, commonly known as the white spiderling, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking white, spider-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features a tall, slender stem with large, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
