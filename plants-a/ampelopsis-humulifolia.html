Ampelopsis humulifolia

Ampelopsis humulifolia, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that change from bright green to vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to orange-red berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and striking seasonal coloration make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
