Abies fraseri f. prostrata

Abies fraseri f. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate Fraser fir, is a rare and unique form of the Fraser fir.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit, which distinguishes it from the typical upright growth of the species. The plant features scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, mat-like appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

This variant is often found in specific microhabitats, reflecting its adaptation to particular environmental conditions.
