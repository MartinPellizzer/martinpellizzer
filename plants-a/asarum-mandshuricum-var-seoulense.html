Asarum mandshuricum var. seoulense

Asarum mandshuricum var. seoulense, commonly known as Seoul asarum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, reddish-purple leaves. The plant produces small, irregularly shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white with a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
