Astragalus mouterdeianus

Astragalus mouterdeianus, commonly known as the Mouterde's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Astragalus mouterdeianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinator populations.
