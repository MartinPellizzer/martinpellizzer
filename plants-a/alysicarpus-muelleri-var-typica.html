Alysicarpus muelleri var. typica

Alysicarpus muelleri var. typica, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by cylindrical, woody pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often grown as a forage crop or for its edible seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
