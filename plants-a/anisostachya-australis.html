Anisostachya australis

Anisostachya australis, commonly known as the Australian reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Anisostachya australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
