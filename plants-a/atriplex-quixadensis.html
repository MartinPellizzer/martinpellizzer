Atriplex quixadensis

Atriplex quixadensis, commonly known as the Quixadensis saltbush, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent leaves, which are often covered in a whitish powder, a feature that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
