Amaryllis × splendens

Amaryllis × splendens, commonly known as the belladonna lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis papaya.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.

Amaryllis × splendens is popular as a decorative plant, often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
