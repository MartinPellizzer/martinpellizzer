Asarum pubitessellatum

Asarum pubitessellatum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf asarum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, pubescent leaves. The plant produces a unique, tubular flower that is often mistaken for a leaf due to its shape and color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
