Asperula Arvensis Subsp. Orientalis

Asperula arvensis subsp. orientalis is a lesser-known member of the Ericaceae family known for its delicate appearance and adaptability. Native to parts of eastern Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers bloom in spring, adding a touch of color to woodland gardens. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated compared to more popular ornamental plants. This subspecies offers both ecological value and potential for use in native landscaping.
