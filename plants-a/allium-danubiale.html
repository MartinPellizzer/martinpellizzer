Allium danubiale

Allium danubiale, commonly known as the Danube garlic, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
