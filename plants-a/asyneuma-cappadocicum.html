Asyneuma cappadocicum

Asyneuma cappadocicum, commonly known as the Cappadocian ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Asyneuma cappadocicum is often found in open habitats such as steppes and mountain slopes.
