Acalypha magistri

Acalypha magistri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sacred plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by distinctive, spiky seed capsules. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
