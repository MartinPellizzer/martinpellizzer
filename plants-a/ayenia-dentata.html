Ayenia dentata

Ayenia dentata, commonly known as the toothed ayenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ayenia dentata is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
