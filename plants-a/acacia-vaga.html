Acacia vaga

Acacia vaga, commonly known as the wandering acacia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that are often reduced to spines. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. Acacia vaga is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
