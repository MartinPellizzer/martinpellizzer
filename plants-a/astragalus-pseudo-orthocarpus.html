Astragalus pseudo-orthocarpus

Astragalus pseudo-orthocarpus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

Astragalus pseudo-orthocarpus is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has adapted to thrive in arid environments.
