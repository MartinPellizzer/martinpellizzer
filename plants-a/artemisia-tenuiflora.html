Artemisia tenuiflora

Artemisia tenuiflora, commonly known as narrowleaf sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and deserts. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, pungent scent.

Artemisia tenuiflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
