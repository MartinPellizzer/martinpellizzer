Allium seirotrichum

Allium seirotrichum, commonly known as the purple garlic.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, purple flowers. Its bulbs are similar to garlic but are less commonly cultivated.

Botanically, it belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
