Atalopteris aspidioides

Atalopteris aspidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It has a distinctive sporophyte structure with a short, thick stem and clusters of sporangia at the base of the leaves.

This species is notable for its primitive morphology and is often found in temperate to subtropical regions.
