Aster britannicus

Aster britannicus, commonly known as the British aster, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple-blue petals and a yellow center, blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Aster britannicus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
