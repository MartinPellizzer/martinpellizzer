Aspalathus benthamii

Aspalathus benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's bushveld pea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying shades of white to pale pink.

Aspalathus benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
