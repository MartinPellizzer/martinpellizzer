Acharitea tenuis

Acharitea tenuis, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with specific mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, Acharitea tenuis is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
