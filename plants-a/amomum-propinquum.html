Amomum propinquum

Amomum propinquum, commonly known as small cardamom, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces spindle-shaped fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Its essential oils are valued for their antimicrobial and digestive benefits.
