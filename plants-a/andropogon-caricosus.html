Andropogon caricosus

Andropogon caricosus, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas. Switchgrass is a valuable species for erosion control and is also used in biofuel production.

Its flowering inflorescence consists of a loose panicle that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
