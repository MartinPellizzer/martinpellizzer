Antimion

Antimion, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Antimion has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette.
