Actinidia acuminata

Actinidia acuminata, commonly known as the Chinese mayapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Actinidiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
