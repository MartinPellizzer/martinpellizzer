Adesmia subsericea

Adesmia subsericea, commonly known as the silky adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Adesmia subsericea is noted for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and fragrant blooms.
