Acacia chariessa

Acacia chariessa, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical inflorescences, which are highly attractive to bees and other pollinators. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Acacia chariessa is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and fodder.
