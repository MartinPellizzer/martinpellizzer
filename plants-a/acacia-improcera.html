Acacia improcera

Acacia improcera, commonly known as the giant acacia or umbrella thorn acacia, is a large, deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and long, straight branches that form a wide, umbrella-like canopy. The tree produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets, and it is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Acacia improcera is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in providing shade and habitat for various wildlife species.
