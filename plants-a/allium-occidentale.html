Allium occidentale

Allium occidentale, commonly known as the western garlic, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous structure and grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium occidentale thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
