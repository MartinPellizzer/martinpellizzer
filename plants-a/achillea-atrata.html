Achillea atrata

Achillea atrata, commonly known as the black yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, dark purple to black flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. Achillea atrata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
