Acalypha multicaulis

Acalypha multicaulis, commonly known as the silky touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from red to yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a distinctive velvety texture.

Acalypha multicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
