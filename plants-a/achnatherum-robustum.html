Achnatherum robustum

Achnatherum robustum, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Achnatherum robustum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
