Archidendron contortum

Archidendron contortum, commonly known as the twisted acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often contorted branches and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets, and the plant is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique branching pattern and flowering habit.
