Agave maigretiana

Agave maigretiana, commonly known as the Mexican giant agave, is a striking plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like rosette of thick, blue-green leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The leaves are lined with sharp spines and have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces an impressive flowering stalk that can reach heights of over 5 meters, bearing numerous white to yellowish flowers.

This species is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in the production of agave-based products.
