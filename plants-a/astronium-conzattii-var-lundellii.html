Astronium conzattii var. lundellii

Astronium conzattii var. lundellii, commonly known as the Lundell's sycamore, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and broad, palmate leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
