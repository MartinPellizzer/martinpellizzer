Acacia zygia

Acacia zygia, commonly known as the white acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a small, shrub-like tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a robust root system adapted to drought conditions.

Acacia zygia is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
