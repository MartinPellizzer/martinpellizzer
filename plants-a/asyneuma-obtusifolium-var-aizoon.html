Asyneuma obtusifolium var. aizoon

Asyneuma obtusifolium var. aizoon, commonly known as the Aizoon false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands. The plant features basal rosettes of silvery-gray, woolly leaves that are lanceolate in shape and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster at the top of the plant, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its succulent-like foliage.
