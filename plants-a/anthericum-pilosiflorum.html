Anthericum pilosiflorum

Anthericum pilosiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered arum, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf known as a spathe. The plant features showy, greenish-white spathe that encloses the spadix, which contains both male and female flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Anthericum pilosiflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
