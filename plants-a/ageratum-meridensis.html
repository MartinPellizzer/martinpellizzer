Ageratum meridensis

Ageratum meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Ageratum meridensis is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
