Aquilegia nikolicii

Aquilegia nikolicii, commonly known as the Nikolic's columbine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred petals that come in shades of red, purple, and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces distinctive, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with finely divided lobes, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
