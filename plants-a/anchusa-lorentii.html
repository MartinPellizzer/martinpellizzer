Anchusa lorentii

Anchusa lorentii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bright blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Anchusa lorentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
