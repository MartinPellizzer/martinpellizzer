Aquilegia beata var. discolor

Aquilegia beata var. discolor, commonly known as the white and red columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically a mix of white and red, with five petals and two elongated nectar-rich spurs. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are palmately arranged and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant colors and unique flower structure.
