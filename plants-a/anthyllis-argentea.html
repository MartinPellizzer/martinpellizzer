Anthyllis argentea

Anthyllis argentea, commonly known as silver vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It has a creeping growth habit with erect stems that produce clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

Anthyllis argentea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
