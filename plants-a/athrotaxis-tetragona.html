Athrotaxis tetragona

Athrotaxis tetragona, commonly known as the mountain ash or the yellow mountain ash, is a species of conifer native to Tasmania.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
