Aegle tamilnadensis

Aegle tamilnadensis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Aegle tamilnadensis is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
