Astragalus tauricus

Astragalus tauricus, commonly known as the Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its root system is fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas.

Astragalus tauricus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
