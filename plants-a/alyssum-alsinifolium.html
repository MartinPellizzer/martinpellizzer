Alyssum alsinifolium

Alyssum alsinifolium, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in clusters. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Alyssum alsinifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
