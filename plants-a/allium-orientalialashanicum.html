Allium orientalialashanicum

Allium orientalialashanicum, commonly known as the Caucasian leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a tall, hollow stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are long, cylindrical, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical umbel, which bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a valuable crop in some regions.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous areas.
