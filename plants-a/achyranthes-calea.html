Achyranthes calea

Achyranthes calea, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or red in color, which develop into hard, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Achyranthes calea is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
