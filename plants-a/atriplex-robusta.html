Atriplex robusta

Atriplex robusta, commonly known as saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in saline and nutrient-poor soils. Its small, green or yellowish flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Atriplex robusta plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.
