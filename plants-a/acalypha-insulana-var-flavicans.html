Acalypha insulana var. flavicans

Acalypha insulana var. flavicans, commonly known as the golden flame acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers clustered within the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
