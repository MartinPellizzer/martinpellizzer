Alyssum tortuosum subsp. sawranicum

Alyssum tortuosum subsp.

sawranicum, commonly known as the Kashmiri alyssum, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has wiry, branched stems and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
