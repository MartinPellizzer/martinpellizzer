Alchemilla cadinensis

Alchemilla cadinensis, commonly known as the Armenian lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Alchemilla cadinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
