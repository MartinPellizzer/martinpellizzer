Asplenium japonicum var. oldhamii

Asplenium japonicum var. oldhamii, commonly known as the Japanese spleenwort, is a fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rock surfaces or in forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
