Anarthrophyllum cumingii var. angustifolia

Anarthrophyllum cumingii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved anarthrophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the cooler months. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in poor soil conditions and low rainfall.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
