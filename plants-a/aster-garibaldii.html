Aster garibaldii

Aster garibaldii, commonly known as the Garibaldi aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky habitats, such as cliffs and slopes, and prefers well-drained soils. It produces showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
