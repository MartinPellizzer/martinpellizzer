Aconitum meloctonum

Aconitum meloctonum, commonly known as the Melesotonic Aconite, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a hazardous plant and should be handled with care.
