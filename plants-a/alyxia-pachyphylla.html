Alyxia pachyphylla

Alyxia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved alyxia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Alyxia pachyphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
