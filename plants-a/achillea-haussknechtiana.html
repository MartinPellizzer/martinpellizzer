Achillea haussknechtiana

Achillea haussknechtiana, commonly known as the Alpine yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a compact rosette of silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
