Artocarpus obtusus

Artocarpus obtusus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive fruit. The fruit, which is typically green when immature, turns yellow or orange when ripe and has a fibrous, starchy pulp. The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.

Its growth habit includes a tall, straight trunk with a broad canopy, making it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
