Astragalus grahamii

Astragalus grahamii, commonly known as Graham's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Astragalus grahamii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
