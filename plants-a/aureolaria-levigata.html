Aureolaria levigata

Aureolaria levigata, commonly known as the smooth auricle.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

Aureolaria levigata is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
