Atropa physaloides

Atropa physaloides, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its dark purple berries and bell-shaped flowers. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, such as atropine and scopolamine, which can be lethal if ingested. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and gardens.

Despite its toxicity, it has been historically used in medicine and as a source of hallucinogenic compounds.
