Annona praetermissa

Annona praetermissa, commonly known as the Mexican custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a smooth, yellowish skin and a creamy, sweet-tart pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The tree is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for various tropical and subtropical regions.
