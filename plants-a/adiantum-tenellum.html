Adiantum tenellum

Adiantum tenellum, commonly known as the maidenhair fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bi-pinnate fronds that resemble flowing hair, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive rachis that is slightly hairy.

Adiantum tenellum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
