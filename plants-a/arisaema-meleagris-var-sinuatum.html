Arisaema meleagris var. sinuatum

Arisaema meleagris var. sinuatum, commonly known as the Chinese arrowhead, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the flowering spikes. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers that are often surrounded by a colorful bract, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.

This variant is closely related to other arisaema species and is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
