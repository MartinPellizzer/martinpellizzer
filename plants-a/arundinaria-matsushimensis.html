Arundinaria matsushimensis

Arundinaria matsushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Matsushimense bamboo, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
