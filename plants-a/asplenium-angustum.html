Asplenium angustum

Asplenium angustum, commonly known as the narrow fern or narrow shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Asplenium angustum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
