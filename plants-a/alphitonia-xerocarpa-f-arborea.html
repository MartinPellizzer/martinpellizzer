Alphitonia xerocarpa f. arborea

Alphitonia xerocarpa f. arborea, commonly known as the tree form of the desert fig, is a variant of the species native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or red in color.

This form is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.
