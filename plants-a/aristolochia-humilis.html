Aristolochia humilis

Aristolochia humilis, commonly known as the lesser Dutchman's pipe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe, with a deep purple to brown coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can reach up to 10 inches in length.

The species is part of the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure and ecological significance.
