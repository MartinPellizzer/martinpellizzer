Allium porrum var. kurrat

Allium porrum var. kurrat, commonly known as the Kurrat leek, is a cultivated variety of the leek plant characterized by its elongated, cylindrical leaves and bulbous stem.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is closely related to other allium species such as onions and garlic. The plant is typically grown for its edible, mild-flavored stalks, which are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Kurrat leek is valued for its robust flavor and is often used in soups, stews, and as a garnish.
