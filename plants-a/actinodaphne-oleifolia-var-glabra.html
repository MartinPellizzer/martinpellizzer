Actinodaphne oleifolia var. glabra

Actinodaphne oleifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its timber and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
