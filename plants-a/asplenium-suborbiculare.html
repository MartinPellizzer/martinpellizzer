Asplenium suborbiculare

Asplenium suborbiculare, commonly known as the round-lobed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive rounded lobes. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium suborbiculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
