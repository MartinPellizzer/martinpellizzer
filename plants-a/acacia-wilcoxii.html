Acacia wilcoxii

Acacia wilcoxii, commonly known as the Wilcox acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, globular clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. Acacia wilcoxii is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
