Adiantum paradiseae

Adiantum paradiseae, commonly known as the paradise maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist, well-drained soil.

Adiantum paradiseae is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
