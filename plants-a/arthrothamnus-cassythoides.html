Arthrothamnus cassythoides

Arthrothamnus cassythoides, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.
