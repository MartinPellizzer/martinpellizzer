Adenandra odoratissima subsp. odoratissima

Adenandra odoratissima subsp.

odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented adenandra, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed.

The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
