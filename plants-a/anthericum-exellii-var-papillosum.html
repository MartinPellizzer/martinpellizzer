Anthericum exellii var. papillosum

Anthericum exellii var. papillosum, commonly known as the woolly nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Araliaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
