Apium petraeum

Apium petraeum, commonly known as the rock celery, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Western Asia, often growing in rocky or limestone habitats. The plant features a slender, branching stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Apium petraeum is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
