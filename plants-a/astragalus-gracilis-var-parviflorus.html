Astragalus gracilis var. parviflorus

Astragalus gracilis var. parviflorus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish in color. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
