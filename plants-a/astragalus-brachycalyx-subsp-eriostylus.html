Astragalus brachycalyx subsp. eriostylus

Astragalus brachycalyx subsp.

eriostylus, commonly known as the short-styled milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its short, slender styles, which are a key taxonomic feature. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
