Agathosma hirtella

Agathosma hirtella, commonly known as African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Agathosma hirtella is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
