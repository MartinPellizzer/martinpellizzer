Astragalus aleppicus subsp. megaloceras

Astragalus aleppicus subsp.

megaloceras, commonly known as the Aleppo milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its seeds are small and typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.
