Anthriscus sylvestris var. gracilis

Anthriscus sylvestris var. gracilis, commonly known as slender woodland parsley, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
