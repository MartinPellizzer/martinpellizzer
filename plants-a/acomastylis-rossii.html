Acomastylis rossii

Acomastylis rossii, commonly known as the New Zealand starflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its star-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features a rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal or subalpine regions.

Acomastylis rossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
