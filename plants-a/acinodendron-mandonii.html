Acinodendron mandonii

Acinodendron mandonii, commonly known as the Madagascar cactus, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny, succulent stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with branches that can spread outwards.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
