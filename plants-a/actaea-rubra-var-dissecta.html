Actaea rubra var. dissecta

Actaea rubra var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected black cohosh, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive, finely divided appearance, often resembling lace, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its showy foliage makes it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
