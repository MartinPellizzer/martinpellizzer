Achyrophorus taraxacifolius

Achyrophorus taraxacifolius, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
