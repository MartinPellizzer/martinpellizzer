Alchornea kelungensis

Alchornea kelungensis, commonly known as the Kelung tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical conditions.
