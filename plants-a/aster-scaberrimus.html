Aster scaberrimus

Aster scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing purple to pinkish-blue florets.

Aster scaberrimus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
