Acacia kulinensis

Acacia kulinensis, commonly known as the Kulin acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in spring. The plant has phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that function as leaves, a common adaptation in acacia species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

Acacia kulinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
