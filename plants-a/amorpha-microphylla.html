Amorpha microphylla

Amorpha microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved honeyvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often used for erosion control due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its small, elliptical leaves and clustered flowers make it a distinctive species within its ecological niche.
