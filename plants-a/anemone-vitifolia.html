Anemone vitifolia

Anemone vitifolia, commonly known as the grape-leaved anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, palmate leaves that resemble grape leaves, with a deep green color and serrated edges. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, typically in clusters at the base of the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Anemone vitifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
