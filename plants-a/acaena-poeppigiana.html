Acaena poeppigiana

Acaena poeppigiana, commonly known as the New Zealand burr bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds. Its foliage consists of alternate, narrow, and leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Acaena poeppigiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
