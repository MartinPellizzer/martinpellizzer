Aristolochia hypoglauca

Aristolochia hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue-veined Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent blue veins. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a strong, unpleasant odor. Its fruit is a smooth, greenish-brown capsule that contains elliptical seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in warm climates.
