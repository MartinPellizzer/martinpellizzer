Allium hyacinthoides

Allium hyacinthoides, commonly known as the woodland garlic or purple onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are linear and grass-like, with a strong garlic scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

Allium hyacinthoides thrives in woodland or shady garden environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.
