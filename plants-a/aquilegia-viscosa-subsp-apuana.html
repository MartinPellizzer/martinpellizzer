Aquilegia viscosa subsp. apuana

Aquilegia viscosa subsp.

apuana, commonly known as the Apuan rock columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Italy. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and vibrant blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with a hairy texture that gives it a rough appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.
