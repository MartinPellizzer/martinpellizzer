Acacia chrysopoda

Acacia chrysopoda, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, yellow-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
