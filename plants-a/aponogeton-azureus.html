Aponogeton azureus

Aponogeton azureus, commonly known as the blue water clover, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue-green leaves that are often tinged with a striking blue hue, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes. Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental aquatic gardening.

Aponogeton azureus is also known for its ability to flower, producing small, white, star-shaped blooms that add to its aesthetic appeal.
