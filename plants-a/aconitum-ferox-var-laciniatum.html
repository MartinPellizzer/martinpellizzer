Aconitum ferox var. laciniatum

Aconitum ferox var. laciniatum, commonly known as the shredded monkshood, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, spiky stems that bear large, hood-shaped purple flowers, which are characteristic of the Aconitum genus. The leaves are deeply lobed and often resemble those of a fern, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

However, it is highly toxic, containing potent alkaloids that can be dangerous to humans and animals.
