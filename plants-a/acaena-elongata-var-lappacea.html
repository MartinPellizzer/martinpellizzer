Acaena elongata var. lappacea

Acaena elongata var. lappacea, commonly known as the long-stemmed burweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a reddish or purple hue.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for native fauna and stabilizing soil in its habitat.
