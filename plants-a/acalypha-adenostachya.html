Acalypha adenostachya

Acalypha adenostachya, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf acalypha, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often silver or gray in color due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Acalypha adenostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
