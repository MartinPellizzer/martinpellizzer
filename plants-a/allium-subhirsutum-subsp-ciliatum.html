Allium subhirsutum subsp. ciliatum

Allium subhirsutum subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical bulbs that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and aromatic foliage.
