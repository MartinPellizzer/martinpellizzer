Acalypha amentacea

Acalypha amentacea, commonly known as the silverleaf acalypha, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are clustered in dense, catkin-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Acalypha amentacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
