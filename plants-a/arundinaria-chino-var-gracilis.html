Arundinaria chino var. gracilis

Arundinaria chino var. gracilis, commonly known as Chinese bamboo or slender bamboo, is a variety of the genus Arundinaria, native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
