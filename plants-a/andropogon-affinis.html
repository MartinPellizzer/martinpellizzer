Andropogon affinis

Andropogon affinis, commonly known as inland needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is a valuable forage plant for livestock and also supports a variety of wildlife.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for insects and birds.
