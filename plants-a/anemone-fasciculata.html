Anemone fasciculata

Anemone fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch of color to its natural habitat.
