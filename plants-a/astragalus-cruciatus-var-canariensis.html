Astragalus cruciatus var. canariensis

Astragalus cruciatus var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
