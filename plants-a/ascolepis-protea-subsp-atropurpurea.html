Ascolepis protea subsp. atropurpurea

Ascolepis protea subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered protea, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that range from pink to deep purple in color.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are composed of numerous petal-like bracts and are adapted for insect pollination.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to thrive.
