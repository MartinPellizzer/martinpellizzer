Apium graveolens convar. rapaceum

Apium graveolens convar. rapaceum, commonly known as wild celery or turnip root.

This plant is a biennial or perennial herb characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and pinnately lobed leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. The roots are fleshy and often swollen, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along waterways.
