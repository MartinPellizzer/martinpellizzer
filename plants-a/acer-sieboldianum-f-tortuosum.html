Acer sieboldianum f. tortuosum

Acer sieboldianum f. tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted maple, is a unique variant of the Japanese maple characterized by its distinctive, curling foliage.

The leaves are typically narrow and exhibit a twisted, spiraled growth pattern, giving the plant a whimsical appearance. It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange samaras in the fall.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and bonsai cultivation.
