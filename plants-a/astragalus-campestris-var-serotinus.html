Astragalus campestris var. serotinus

Astragalus campestris var. serotinus, commonly known as late-flowering field milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with leaflets that are oblong and slightly hairy.

The seeds are enclosed in small, leathery pods, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.
