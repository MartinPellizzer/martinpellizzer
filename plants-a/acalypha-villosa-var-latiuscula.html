Acalypha villosa var. latiuscula

Acalypha villosa var. latiuscula, commonly known as silky ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are usually borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
