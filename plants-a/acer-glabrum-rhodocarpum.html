Acer glabrum rhodocarpum

Acer glabrum rhodocarpum, commonly known as the red maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and urban forestry.
