Adonis apennina var. sibirica

Adonis apennina var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian Adonis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Siberia.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers in early summer, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its stems are upright and can grow up to 30 cm in height, often adorned with glandular hairs.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
