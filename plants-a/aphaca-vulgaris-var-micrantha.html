Aphaca vulgaris var. micrantha

Aphaca vulgaris var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered aphaca, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
