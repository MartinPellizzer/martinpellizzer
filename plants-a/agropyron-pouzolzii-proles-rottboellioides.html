Agropyron pouzolzii proles rottboellioides

Agropyron pouzolzii proles rottboellioides, commonly known as the Rottboell's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Agropyron pouzolzii proles rottboellioides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
