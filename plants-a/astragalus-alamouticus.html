Astragalus alamouticus

Astragalus alamouticus, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran, Afghanistan, and Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Astragalus alamouticus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
