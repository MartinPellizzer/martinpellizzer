Agastache tomentosa

Agastache tomentosa, commonly known as honeyweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of lavender to pinkish-purple tubular flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Agastache tomentosa is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both traditional herbal medicine and as an ornamental plant.
