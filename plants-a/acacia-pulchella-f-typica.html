Acacia pulchella f. typica

Acacia pulchella f. typica, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australia.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
