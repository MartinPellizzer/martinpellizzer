Acriulus madagascariensis

Acriulus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spider orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate and often striking floral structure. The plant typically features long, slender stems and produces small, delicate flowers with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in forest understories.

Due to its restricted habitat and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
