Acacia acantholoba

Acacia acantholoba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are grouped in compact, spherical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Acacia acantholoba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
