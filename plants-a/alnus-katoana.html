Alnus katoana

Alnus katoana, commonly known as the Katoana alder, is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and cone-like fruits. The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in humid tropical environments. Its root system forms associations with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Alnus katoana plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
