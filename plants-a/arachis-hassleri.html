Arachis hassleri

Arachis hassleri, commonly known as the Hassler groundnut, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers and cylindrical pods containing one or two seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
