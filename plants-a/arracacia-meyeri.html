Arracacia meyeri

Arracacia meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's arracacha, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and edible roots.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soils. The plant features a robust, branched root system that produces a crisp, white tuberous root used in culinary applications. Its leaves are broad, pinnately compound, and often have a mild, spicy scent.

It is cultivated for both its edible roots and ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
