Aira intermedia

Aira intermedia, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in prairies and sandy soils.

Blue grama is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
