Amomum irosinense

Amomum irosinense, commonly known as the Irosin nutmeg or Irosin cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant produces aromatic, seed-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in cooking, adding a distinctive flavor to various dishes.

The plant's botanical features include rhizomatous growth, lanceolate leaves, and small, clustered flowers.
