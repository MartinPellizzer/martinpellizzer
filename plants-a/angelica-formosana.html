Angelica formosana

Angelica formosana, commonly known as the Formosan angelica, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and is often found in moist, shaded forest areas. Its roots and leaves are used to treat various ailments, including digestive issues and respiratory conditions.
