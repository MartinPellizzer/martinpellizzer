Alternanthera brasiliana var. villosa

Alternanthera brasiliana var. villosa, commonly known as velvetleaf Alternanthera, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to purple, depending on light exposure and water conditions. The plant thrives in warm, shallow waters and is often used in ornamental ponds and aquariums for its vibrant foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, and it spreads through both runners and seeds.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for aquatic horticulture.
