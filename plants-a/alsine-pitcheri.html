Alsine pitcheri

Alsine pitcheri, commonly known as the plains alpine, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It has a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil in disturbed areas.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
