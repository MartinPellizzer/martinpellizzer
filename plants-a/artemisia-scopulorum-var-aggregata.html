Artemisia scopulorum var. aggregata

Artemisia scopulorum var. aggregata, commonly known as silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its aromatic leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
