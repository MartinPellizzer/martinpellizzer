Arnica brevifolia

Arnica brevifolia, commonly known as short-leaved arnica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the roots and rhizomes containing compounds used in traditional remedies.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should not be ingested without proper guidance.
