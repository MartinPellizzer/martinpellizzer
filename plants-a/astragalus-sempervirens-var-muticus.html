Astragalus sempervirens var. muticus

Astragalus sempervirens var. muticus, commonly known as the silent everlasting pea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and a deep root system.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than its counterparts but is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
