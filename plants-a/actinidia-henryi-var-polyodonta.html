Actinidia henryi var. polyodonta

Actinidia henryi var. polyodonta, commonly known as the Chinese Actinidia, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into 5 to 9 leaflets, and its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are dark red to black in color, containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and potential for cultivation make it a notable species in horticulture.
