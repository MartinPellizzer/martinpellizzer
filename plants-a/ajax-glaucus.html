Ajax glaucus

Ajax glaucus, commonly known as the blue glaucus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact, low-growing habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaping efforts.
