Asplenium normale

Asplenium normale, commonly known as the common shield fern, is a species of fern native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with spores produced on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium normale is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
