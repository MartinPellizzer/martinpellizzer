Acrocephalus kaessneri

Acrocephalus kaessneri, commonly known as the Kaessner's reed warbler, is a small passerine bird native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Acrocephalidae, which includes various reed warblers and grass warblers. This species is typically found in wetland habitats such as reed beds and marshes, where it nests in dense vegetation. Its diet mainly consists of insects and other small invertebrates, which it forages from the water's surface.

The bird is known for its distinctive song and its role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
