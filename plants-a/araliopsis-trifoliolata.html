Araliopsis trifoliolata

Araliopsis trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved araliopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Araliaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
