Agapetes acuminata

Agapetes acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped agapete, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of South America.

The plant typically grows at high elevations, often in humid, shaded environments, and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Agapetes acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
