Alchemilla steudneri

Alchemilla steudneri, commonly known as Steudner's lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Alchemilla steudneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
