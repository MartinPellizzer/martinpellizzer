Acer dissectum var. tenuifolium

Acer dissectum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, elongated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental species is valued for its delicate foliage and seasonal color changes in gardens and parks.
