Aphragmia rotundifolia

Aphragmia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved aphragmia, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 5 to 10 centimeters. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Aphragmia rotundifolia is adapted to cold climates and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.
