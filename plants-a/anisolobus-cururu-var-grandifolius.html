Anisolobus cururu var. grandifolius

Anisolobus cururu var. grandifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its upright growth habit and strap-like leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
