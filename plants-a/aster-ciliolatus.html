Aster ciliolatus

Aster ciliolatus, commonly known as the slender aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, and it produces small blue to purple flowers in the fall.

Aster ciliolatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
