Astragalus hamosus subsp. embergeri

Astragalus hamosus subsp.

embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert steppe habitats.

The subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
