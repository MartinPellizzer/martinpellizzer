Acaciella umbellifera

Acaciella umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
