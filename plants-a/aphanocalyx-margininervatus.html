Aphanocalyx margininervatus

Aphanocalyx margininervatus, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a soft, velvety texture with a margin that is finely serrated. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
