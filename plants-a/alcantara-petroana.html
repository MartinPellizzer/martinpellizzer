Alcantara petroana

Alcantara petroana, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats on rocky outcrops.

Alcantara petroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
