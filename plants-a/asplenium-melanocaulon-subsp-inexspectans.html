Asplenium melanocaulon subsp. inexspectans

Asplenium melanocaulon subsp.

inexspectans, commonly known as the black-stem spleenwort, is a fern species characterized by its dark, almost black stems and dense, velvety fronds. It belongs to the family Aspleniaceae and is typically found growing on rock surfaces or in shaded, moist environments.

The leaves are usually lanceolate and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive arrangement of veins. This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in shaded garden areas.
