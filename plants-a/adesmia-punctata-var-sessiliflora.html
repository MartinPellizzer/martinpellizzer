Adesmia punctata var. sessiliflora

Adesmia punctata var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered adesmia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.
