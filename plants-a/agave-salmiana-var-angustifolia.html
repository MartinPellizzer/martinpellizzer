Agave salmiana var. angustifolia

Agave salmiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf century plant, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features tall, narrow leaves that are blue-green in color with a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces an impressive flower stalk that can reach several meters in height, bearing yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
