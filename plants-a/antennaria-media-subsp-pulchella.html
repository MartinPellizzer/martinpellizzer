Antennaria media subsp. pulchella

Antennaria media subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the pretty mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its hairy stems.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are typically hairy and spoon-shaped, adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and naturalized areas.
