Ammannia woodii

Ammannia woodii, commonly known as the redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes and along stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its small, showy flowers are typically pink or red and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
