Amaranthus macrocarpus subsp. pallidus

Amaranthus macrocarpus subsp.

pallidus, commonly known as the pale amaranth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, showy inflorescences that range from red to yellow in color.

The plant produces small, bead-like seeds that are often used as a grain or for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and can be eaten as a leaf vegetable in some regions.
