Ardisia sublanceolata

Ardisia sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, narrow elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is notable for its ornamental berries, which are typically bright red and appear in late summer. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ardisia sublanceolata is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
