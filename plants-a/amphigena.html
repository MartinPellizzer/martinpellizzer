Amphigena

Amphigena, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded areas. Amphigena has a long history of use in traditional medicine, though it is toxic and should be handled with care.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the daisy family.
