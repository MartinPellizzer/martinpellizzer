Andropogon doloensis

Andropogon doloensis, commonly known as the African switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
