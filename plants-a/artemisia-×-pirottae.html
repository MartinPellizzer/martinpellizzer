Artemisia × pirottae

Artemisia × pirottae, commonly known as the Pirottae mugwort, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish to brownish flower heads. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as thujone and camphor, contributing to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal properties.
