Aquilegia pyrenaica var. decipiens

Aquilegia pyrenaica var. decipiens, commonly known as the Pyrenean columbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred petals that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with finely divided lobes, and it produces seed capsules that release numerous small seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
