Ammannia pentandra var. fimbriata

Ammannia pentandra var. fimbriata, commonly known as the pink rush or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink stems and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
