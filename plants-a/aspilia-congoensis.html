Aspilia congoensis

Aspilia congoensis, commonly known as Congo flame or African flame, is a flowering plant native to central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking red-orange flower heads with yellow centers, surrounded by narrow, pale green bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Aspilia congoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
