Amoria nigrescens subsp. petrisavii

Amoria nigrescens subsp.

petrisavii, commonly known as the dark amoria, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black leaves, which are often veined with contrasting white or pale green markings.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of violet or blue.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in botanical conservation efforts.
