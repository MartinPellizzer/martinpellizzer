Agelaea pseudobliqua

Agelaea pseudobliqua, commonly known as the slender false arum, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as calla lilies and peace lilies. The plant is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Agelaea pseudobliqua is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
