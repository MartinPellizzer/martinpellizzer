Antirrhinum ruscinonense

Antirrhinum ruscinonense, commonly known as the Ruscinonese Snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
