Acacia browniana var. endlicheri

Acacia browniana var. endlicheri, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, which provides a striking visual contrast.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southeastern Australia.
