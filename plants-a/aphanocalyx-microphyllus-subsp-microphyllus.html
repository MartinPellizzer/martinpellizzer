Aphanocalyx microphyllus subsp. microphyllus

Aphanocalyx microphyllus subsp.

microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months.

The leaves are small, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. The plant prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
