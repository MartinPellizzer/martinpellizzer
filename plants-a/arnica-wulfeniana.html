Arnica wulfeniana

Arnica wulfeniana, commonly known as the alpine tansy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Arnica wulfeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
