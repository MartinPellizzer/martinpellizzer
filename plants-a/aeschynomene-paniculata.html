Aeschynomene paniculata

Aeschynomene paniculata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Aeschynomene paniculata is often used as a forage crop and for soil stabilization due to its ability to fix nitrogen.
