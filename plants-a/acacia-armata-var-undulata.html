Acacia armata var. undulata

Acacia armata var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its unique leaf structure and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
