Ammannia mauritiana subsp. lupembensis

Ammannia mauritiana subsp.

lupembensis, commonly known as the African enkianthus, is a shrub native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
