Alcea kurdica var. kurdica

Alcea kurdica var. kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, rose-like flowers. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
