Austrocephalocereus dyhowskii

Austrocephalocereus dyhowskii, commonly known as the Mexican candle bush, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and flowering pattern make it a notable species in the Cactaceae family.
