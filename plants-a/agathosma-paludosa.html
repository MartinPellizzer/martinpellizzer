Agathosma paludosa

Agathosma paludosa, commonly known as marsh agathosma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid and cool conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in swampy or boggy areas.

Agathosma paludosa is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
