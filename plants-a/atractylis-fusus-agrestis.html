Atractylis fusus-agrestis

Atractylis fusus-agrestis, commonly known as the roughleaf atractylis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

It is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
