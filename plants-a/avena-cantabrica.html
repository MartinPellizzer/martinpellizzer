Avena cantabrica

Avena cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Avena cantabrica thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
