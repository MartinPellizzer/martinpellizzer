Aconitum thelyphonum

Aconitum thelyphonum, commonly known as the Tibetan monkshood, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayas and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features large, palmately divided leaves that are dark green and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
