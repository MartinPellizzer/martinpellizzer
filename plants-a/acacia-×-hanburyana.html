Acacia × hanburyana

Acacia × hanburyana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a hybrid plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in reforestation efforts.
