Aristolochia pontica

Aristolochia pontica, commonly known as the Greek birthwort, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often covered in a dense, hairy texture. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
