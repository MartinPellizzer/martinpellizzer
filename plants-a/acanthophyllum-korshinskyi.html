Acanthophyllum korshinskyi

Acanthophyllum korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's jointweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Acanthophyllum korshinskyi is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperature fluctuations.
