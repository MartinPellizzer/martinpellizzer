Acalypha chamaedrifolia

Acalypha chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the creeping false nettle, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
