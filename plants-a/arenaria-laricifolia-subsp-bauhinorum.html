Arenaria laricifolia subsp. bauhinorum

Arenaria laricifolia subsp.

bauhinorum, commonly known as the Alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are adapted to harsh alpine conditions.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for high-altitude environments with strong winds and limited nutrients.
