Acacia subtiliformis

Acacia subtiliformis, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, feathery leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across sandy soils.

Acacia subtiliformis is adapted to low rainfall and can thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
