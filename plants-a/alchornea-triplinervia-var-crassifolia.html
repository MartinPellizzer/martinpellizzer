Alchornea triplinervia var. crassifolia

Alchornea triplinervia var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf alchornea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
