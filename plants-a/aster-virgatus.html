Aster virgatus

Aster virgatus, commonly known as the eastern aster, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces showy purple to blue flowers in the late summer and early fall.

Aster virgatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
