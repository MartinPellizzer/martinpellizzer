Andropogon sorghum subsp. sativus

Andropogon sorghum subsp.

sativus, commonly known as sorghum, is a tall, warm-season grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its sturdy stalks, which are used for grain production, animal feed, and biofuel.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in dry to semi-arid climates and is highly drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in many parts of the world.
