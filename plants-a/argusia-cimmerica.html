Argusia cimmerica

Argusia cimmerica, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Argusia cimmerica is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its hardiness makes it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects in arid landscapes.
