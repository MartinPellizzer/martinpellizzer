Aloe punctata

Aloe punctata, commonly known as the spotted aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with distinct white or yellow spots. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Aloe punctata is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
