Acrostichum teijsmannianum

Acrostichum teijsmannianum, commonly known as the giant reed or giant arundo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and coastal areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
