Angophora bakeri subsp. bakeri

Angophora bakeri subsp.

bakeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved angophora, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
