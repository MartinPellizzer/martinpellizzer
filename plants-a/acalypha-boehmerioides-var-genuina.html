Acalypha boehmerioides var. genuina

Acalypha boehmerioides var. genuina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican beauty, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown, often with a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
