Anemone apennina var. albiflora

Anemone apennina var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are basal and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
