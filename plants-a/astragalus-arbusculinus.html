Astragalus arbusculinus

Astragalus arbusculinus, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its distinctive clustered growth habit and yellow to reddish flowers make it a notable species in its ecological community.
