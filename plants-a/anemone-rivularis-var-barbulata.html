Anemone rivularis var. barbulata

Anemone rivularis var. barbulata, commonly known as the water anemone, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has delicate, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water's surface. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread across shallow water areas, forming dense colonies.

The species is adapted to aquatic conditions, with specialized structures that aid in buoyancy and nutrient absorption.
