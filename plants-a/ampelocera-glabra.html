Ampelocera glabra

Ampelocera glabra, commonly known as the smooth rock-rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Ampelocera glabra is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
