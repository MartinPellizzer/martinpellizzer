Acacia odorata

Acacia odorata, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in hard, pod-like structures that split open when mature.

Acacia odorata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
