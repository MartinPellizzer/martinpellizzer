Ailanthus moluccana

Ailanthus moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan ailanthus, is a deciduous tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Simaroubaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with 10 to 20 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its hardiness and rapid growth, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its potential medicinal properties.
