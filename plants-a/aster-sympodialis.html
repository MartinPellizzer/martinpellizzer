Aster sympodialis

Aster sympodialis, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late summer to early fall.

Aster sympodialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
