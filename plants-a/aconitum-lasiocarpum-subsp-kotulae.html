Aconitum lasiocarpum subsp. kotulae

Aconitum lasiocarpum subsp.

kotulae, commonly known as the Kotula's monkshood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a waxy surface, while its stems are usually covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
