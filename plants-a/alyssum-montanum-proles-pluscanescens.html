Alyssum montanum proles pluscanescens

Alyssum montanum proles pluscanescens, commonly known as the mountain alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive blooms and resilience.

Alyssum montanum proles pluscanescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
