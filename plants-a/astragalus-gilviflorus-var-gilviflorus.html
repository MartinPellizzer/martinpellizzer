Astragalus gilviflorus var. gilviflorus

Astragalus gilviflorus var. gilviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological significance lies in its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding plant life.
