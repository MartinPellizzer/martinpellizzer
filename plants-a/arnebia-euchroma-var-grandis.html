Arnebia euchroma var. grandis

Arnebia euchroma var. grandis, commonly known as the Tibetan rhubarb or Indian madder, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a notable species in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
