Agathosma involucrata

Agathosma involucrata, commonly known as wild fennel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's essential oils, derived from its leaves and stems, are valued for their aromatic and therapeutic uses.
