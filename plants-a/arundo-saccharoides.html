Arundo saccharoides

Arundo saccharoides, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height. This plant thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an effective pioneer species in disturbed soils.

The plant is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a source of biomass.
