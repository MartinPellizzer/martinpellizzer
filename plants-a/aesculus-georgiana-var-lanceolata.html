Aesculus georgiana var. lanceolata

Aesculus georgiana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf horsechestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lance-shaped lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces showy, white or pale pink, clustered flowers in spring, followed by spiny, orange-brown fruits. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a rough texture that becomes more fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
