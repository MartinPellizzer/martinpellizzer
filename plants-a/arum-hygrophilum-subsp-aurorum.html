Arum hygrophilum subsp. aurorum

Arum hygrophilum subsp.

aurorum, commonly known as the golden arrow arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces a spatulate inflorescence with a spathe that can range in color from yellow to orange, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
