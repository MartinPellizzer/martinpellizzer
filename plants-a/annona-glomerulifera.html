Annona glomerulifera

Annona glomerulifera, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a rough, bark-like trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruits that are typically round or pear-shaped. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, sweet pulp with a distinctive aroma.

It is valued for its edible fruit and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
