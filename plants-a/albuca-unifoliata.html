Albuca unifoliata

Albuca unifoliata, commonly known as the single-leaf albuca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Albuca unifoliata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
