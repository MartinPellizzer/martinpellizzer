Alloplectus rubidus

Alloplectus rubidus, commonly known as the red-stemmed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic habit, often growing in shallow water or moist soil. The plant produces tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, which it then digests for nutrients. Its leaves are typically thin and delicate, adapted for underwater environments.

Alloplectus rubidus is notable for its vibrant red stems and its unique method of obtaining nutrients through predation.
