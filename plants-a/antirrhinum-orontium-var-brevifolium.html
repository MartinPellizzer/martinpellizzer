Antirrhinum orontium var. brevifolium

Antirrhinum orontium var. brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
