Acer cordatum var. microcordatum

Acer cordatum var. microcordatum, commonly known as the dwarf paper birch, is a small deciduous tree native to the Appalachian Mountains in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a serrated margin and a bright green upper surface that turns yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable addition to native plant collections.
