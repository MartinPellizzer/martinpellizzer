Asperula cynanchica subsp. neglecta

Asperula cynanchica subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the creeping azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States. It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It is often used in rock gardens or as ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both landscape design and horticultural study.
