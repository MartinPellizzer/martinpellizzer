Aquilegia ottonis subsp. taygetea

Aquilegia ottonis subsp.

taygetea, commonly known as the Taygetean columbine, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five sepals that are often reflexed.

The plant has compound leaves with palmate lobes, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
