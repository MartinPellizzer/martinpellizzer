Andropogon exilis var. petitianus

Andropogon exilis var. petitianus, commonly known as the slender bluestem grass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in full sun to partial shade and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, low-maintenance turf.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for erosion control and landscape design.
