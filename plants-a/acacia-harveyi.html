Acacia harveyi

Acacia harveyi, commonly known as the Harvey's wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, phyllodes that are typically green and linear. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Acacia harveyi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
