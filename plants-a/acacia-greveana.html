Acacia greveana

Acacia greveana, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and bipinnate leaves that are feathery in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in summer. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.

Acacia greveana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
