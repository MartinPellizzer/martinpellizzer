Aristolochia oblongata subsp. maestrensis

Aristolochia oblongata subsp.

maestrensis, commonly known as the Maestrense Dutchman's Pipe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, pipe-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its unique floral morphology.

The subspecies is valued in traditional medicine but contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
