Alchemilla vulgaris var. paniculata

Alchemilla vulgaris var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled lady's mantle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, serrated leaves. The plant produces upright, branched inflorescences of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
