Aulomyrcia pyrifolia var. robusta

Aulomyrcia pyrifolia var. robusta, commonly known as the robust appleberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, apple-like fruits that are typically orange to red in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
