Aspalathus quadrata

Aspalathus quadrata, commonly known as the African green tea or rooibos, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, bushy growth habit that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are used to produce a popular herbal tea, which is rich in antioxidants and caffeine-free.

Aspalathus quadrata plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
