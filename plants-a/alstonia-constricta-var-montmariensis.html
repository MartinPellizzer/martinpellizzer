Alstonia constricta var. montmariensis

Alstonia constricta var. montmariensis, commonly known as the Montmariensis alstonia, is a variant of the alstonia tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and long, slender leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions for horticultural purposes.
