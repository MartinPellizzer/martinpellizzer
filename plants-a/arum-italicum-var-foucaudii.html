Arum italicum var. foucaudii

Arum italicum var. foucaudii, commonly known as the Sicilian arrowhead, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of striking red spathes that enclose small, white spadices, creating a dramatic visual effect. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated in water gardens or boggy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture but requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive in natural habitats.
