Apargia haenkei

Apargia haenkei, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces striking violet to purple flower heads that resemble small coneflowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Apargia haenkei is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
