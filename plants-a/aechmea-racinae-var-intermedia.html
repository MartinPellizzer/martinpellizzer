Aechmea racinae var. intermedia

Aechmea racinae var. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Aechmea, is a bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
