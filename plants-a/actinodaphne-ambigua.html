Actinodaphne ambigua

Actinodaphne ambigua, commonly known as the Malabar camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to produce camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and industry. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
