Argythamnia aphoroides

Argythamnia aphoroides, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and is adapted to poor soil conditions. Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional medicine.
