Acer negundo f. aureomarginatum

Acer negundo f. aureomarginatum, commonly known as the golden-marginied ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a serrated margin. One of its most notable features is the golden-yellow edge that borders the otherwise dark green leaf blade. This cultivar typically grows as a medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
