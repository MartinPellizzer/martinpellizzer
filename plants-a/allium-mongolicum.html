Allium mongolicum

Allium mongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian leek, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces upright flowering stems that bear small, white, bell-shaped flowers arranged in clusters. Its bulbs are often used in local cuisines and have a mild garlic-like flavor.

Allium mongolicum is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
