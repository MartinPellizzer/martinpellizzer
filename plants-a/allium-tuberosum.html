Allium tuberosum

Allium tuberosum, commonly known as Chinese leek or garlic chives, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs, which are edible, are used in various culinary traditions across Asia.

Botanically, it belongs to the Allium genus, which includes onions, garlic, and shallots.
