Amaranthus minor

Amaranthus minor, commonly known as dwarf amaranth, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can be green, red, or purple, adding visual interest.

Amaranthus minor is often cultivated as a ornamental plant and is also valued for its nutritional content in some regions.
