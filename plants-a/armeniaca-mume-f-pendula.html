Armeniaca mume f. pendula

Armeniaca mume f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese apricot, is a cultivar of the Japanese apricot tree characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to East Asia, particularly China. The plant produces fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits in late summer. Its ornamental value lies in its elegant, flowing form, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
