Asarum brevistylum

Asarum brevistylum, commonly known as the short-stemmed wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Asarum brevistylum is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
