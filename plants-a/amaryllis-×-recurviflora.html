Amaryllis × recurviflora

Amaryllis × recurviflora, commonly known as the hybrid amaryllis, is a striking hybrid plant resulting from crosses between Amaryllis belladonna and Amaryllis recurviflora.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and strap-shaped, often reaching several feet in length. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it a favorite among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts alike.
