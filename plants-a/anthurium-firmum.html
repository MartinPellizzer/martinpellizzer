Anthurium firmum

Anthurium firmum, commonly known as the velvet shield plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking spadices, which are often red or orange, surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

Anthurium firmum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
