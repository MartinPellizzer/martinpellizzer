Aucuba japonica var. variegata

Aucuba japonica var. variegata, commonly known as Japanese laurel or variegated aucuba, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation, creating a vibrant visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is highly tolerant of urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its dense foliage and year-round color make it an excellent ornamental plant for gardens and landscapes.
