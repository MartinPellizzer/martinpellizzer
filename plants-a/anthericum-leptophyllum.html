Anthericum leptophyllum

Anthericum leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender-leaved arum, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It features a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a showy, purple spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, and its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, swampy environments. It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small animals.

Anthericum leptophyllum is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
