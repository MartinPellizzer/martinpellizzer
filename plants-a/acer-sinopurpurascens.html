Acer sinopurpurascens

Acer sinopurpurascens, commonly known as the Chinese maple, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Acer genus within the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive foliage. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a reddish-purple hue during the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its propagation.
