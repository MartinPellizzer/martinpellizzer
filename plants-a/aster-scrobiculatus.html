Aster scrobiculatus

Aster scrobiculatus, commonly known as the rough aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink ray flowers and rough, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Aster scrobiculatus is often found in open areas with partial to full sunlight.
