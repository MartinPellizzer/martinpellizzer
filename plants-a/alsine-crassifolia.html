Alsine crassifolia

Alsine crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, broad leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on sandy or rocky soils. Its fleshy stems and leaves help it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Alsine crassifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
