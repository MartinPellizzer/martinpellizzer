Aspidium filix-mas var. chrysocoma

Aspidium filix-mas var. chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features fronds that are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside of the leaves. It produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are released during the late spring and summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
