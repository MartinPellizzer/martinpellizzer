Amomum globosum

Amomum globosum, commonly known as the globular cardamom, is a tropical plant belonging to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing dark brown seeds. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Amomum globosum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its essential oils and spice-like flavor.
