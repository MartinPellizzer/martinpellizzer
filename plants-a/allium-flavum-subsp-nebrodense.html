Allium flavum subsp. nebrodense

Allium flavum subsp.

nebrodense, commonly known as the Nebrodan garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous stem that is used in traditional culinary practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its historical use in local cuisines.
