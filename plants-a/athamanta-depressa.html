Athamanta depressa

Athamanta depressa, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Athamanta depressa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
