Alpinia stachyodes

Alpinia stachyodes, commonly known as the Chinese ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils.

Alpinia stachyodes is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
