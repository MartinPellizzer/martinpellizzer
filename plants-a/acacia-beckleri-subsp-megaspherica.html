Acacia beckleri subsp. megaspherica

Acacia beckleri subsp.

megaspherica, commonly known as the large globe acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive globular or spherical shape, formed by the dense clustering of its thorned branches.

The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are small, phyllodes, and it has a deep root system adapted to drought conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
