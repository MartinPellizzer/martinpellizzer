Anthemis jimenezii

Anthemis jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Anthemis jimenezii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
