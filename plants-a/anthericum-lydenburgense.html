Anthericum lydenburgense

Anthericum lydenburgense, commonly known as Lydenburg arum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and are adapted for pollination by insects.
