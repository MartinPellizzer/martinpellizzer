Acacia texensis

Acacia texensis, commonly known as the Texas acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves composed of small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellow, spherical flower heads that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Acacia texensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in desert and semi-desert regions.
