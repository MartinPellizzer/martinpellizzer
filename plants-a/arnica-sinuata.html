Arnica sinuata

Arnica sinuata, commonly known as the western sand arnica, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Arnica sinuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
