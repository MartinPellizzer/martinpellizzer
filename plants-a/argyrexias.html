Argyrexias

Argyrexias, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Argyrexias thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among gardeners.
