Anthurium acaule var. portoricensis

Anthurium acaule var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican anthurium, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in red or orange hues. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
