Aclisia × zollingeri

Aclisia × zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's moonflower, is a hybrid species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a glossy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Aclisia × zollingeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
