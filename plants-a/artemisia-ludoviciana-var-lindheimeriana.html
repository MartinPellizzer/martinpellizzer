Artemisia ludoviciana var. lindheimeriana

Artemisia ludoviciana var. lindheimeriana, commonly known as Lindheimer's sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the central and southern United States.

It features aromatic, silvery-gray foliage composed of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters in late spring to early summer, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and wildlife habitat in arid landscapes.
