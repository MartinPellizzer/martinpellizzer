Alsophila tahitensis

Alsophila tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its dense, fibrous roots, which help stabilize the soil.

Alsophila tahitensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and coastal areas.
