Areca whitfordii

Areca whitfordii, commonly known as the White Areca palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often a striking light green or white in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered fruit, which are typically round and fleshy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Areca whitfordii is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine.
