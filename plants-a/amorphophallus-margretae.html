Amorphophallus margretae

Amorphophallus margretae, commonly known as the Margaret's giant arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its spatheous inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful leaf-like structure called a spathe. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or green with a distinct, often fragrant center. Its underground tubers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential agricultural significance.
