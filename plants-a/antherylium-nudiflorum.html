Antherylium nudiflorum

Antherylium nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear before the leaves. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette at the base. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
