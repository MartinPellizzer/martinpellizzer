Acacia daemon

Acacia daemon, commonly known as the black wattle, is a species of acacia native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, woody pods and compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

Acacia daemon is often used in horticulture and is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
