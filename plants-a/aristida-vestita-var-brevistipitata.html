Aristida vestita var. brevistipitata

Aristida vestita var. brevistipitata, commonly known as the narrow-tipped feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This variety is noted for its delicate foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
