Arisaema cornutum

Arisaema cornutum, commonly known as the horned arisaema, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, horn-like structure at the base of its leaf stalk. The plant produces a single, showy, hooded flower that is often green with a yellowish tint. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along streambanks.

Arisaema cornutum is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique floral morphology and ornamental value.
