Ancistrocactus uncinatus var. wrightii

Ancistrocactus uncinatus var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's hooked cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, sprawling cactus with short, cylindrical stems that often branch at the base. The plant features small, spineless areoles that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to survive in dry environments.

This variety is notable for its distinctive hooked spines and is often found in rocky soils.
