Anisomeles salviifolia var. subtomentosa

Anisomeles salviifolia var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the silky-leaved anisomeles, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, silky grayish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Due to its unique texture and visual appeal, it is popular in horticulture for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
