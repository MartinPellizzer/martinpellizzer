Acacia pterophylla

Acacia pterophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and narrow, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact, spherical or cylindrical clusters, which are typically followed by flat, leathery pods. Acacia pterophylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native vegetation.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
