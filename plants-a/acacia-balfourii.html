Acacia balfourii

Acacia balfourii, commonly known as the white acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The seeds are encased in a hard pod and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
