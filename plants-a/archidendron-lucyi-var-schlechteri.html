Archidendron lucyi var. schlechteri

Archidendron lucyi var. schlechteri, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and light-colored, with a slightly rough texture when mature.
