Acrocomia ierensis

Acrocomia ierensis, commonly known as the Ierensis palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are similar to dates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
