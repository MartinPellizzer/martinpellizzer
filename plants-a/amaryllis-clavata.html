Amaryllis clavata

Amaryllis clavata, commonly known as the clustered amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces clusters of flowers on a single stem, which distinguishes it from other amaryllis species. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and grow from a bulbous base.

Amaryllis clavata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
