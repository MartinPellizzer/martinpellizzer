Abrus aureus subsp. littoralis

Abrus aureus subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal velvet tamarind, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and bean-like, toxic seeds enclosed in red pods.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces a strong, unpleasant odor, which may deter herbivores.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
