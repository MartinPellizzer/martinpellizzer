Anchusa officinalis

Anchusa officinalis, commonly known as common blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Anchusa officinalis has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
