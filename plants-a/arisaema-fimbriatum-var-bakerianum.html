Arisaema fimbriatum var. bakerianum

Arisaema fimbriatum var. bakerianum, commonly known as the Baker's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of spadices, which are typically green with a yellow or orange spathe. The plant is a member of the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
