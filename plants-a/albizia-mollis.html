Albizia mollis

Albizia mollis, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. Albizia mollis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
