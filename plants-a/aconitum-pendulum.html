Aconitum pendulum

Aconitum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging monk's hood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are borne in loose panicles at the top of the stems. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.

Aconitum pendulum is known for its toxic properties, containing potent alkaloids that make it dangerous to both humans and animals.
