Acaenops pilosus

Acaenops pilosus, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
