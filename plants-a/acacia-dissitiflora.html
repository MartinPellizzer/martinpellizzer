Acacia dissitiflora

Acacia dissitiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Acacia dissitiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
