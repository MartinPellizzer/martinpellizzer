Anacyclus mucronulatus

Anacyclus mucronulatus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, flattened disc, followed by small, hairy fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Anacyclus mucronulatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for small animals in its native habitat.
