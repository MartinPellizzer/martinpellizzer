Asperula euryphylla

Asperula euryphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Asperula euryphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
