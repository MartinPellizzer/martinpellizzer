Abolboda ptaritepuiana

Abolboda ptaritepuiana, commonly known as the Moonlight Fern, is a rare tropical plant native to the cloud forests of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-green fronds that shimmer under sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant has a unique root system that allows it to absorb moisture from the air, adapting to its high-altitude environment. Its flowers, which bloom only at night, are pale blue and emit a subtle fragrance.

Due to habitat loss, it is now classified as endangered and is protected in several conservation reserves.
