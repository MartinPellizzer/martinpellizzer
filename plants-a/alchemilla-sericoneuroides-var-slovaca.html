Alchemilla sericoneuroides var. slovaca

Alchemilla sericoneuroides var. slovaca, commonly known as the Slovak lady's mantle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its foliage is noted for its ornamental appeal and its ability to retain moisture, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
