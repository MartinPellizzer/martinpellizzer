Asplenium juglandifolium

Asplenium juglandifolium, commonly known as the walnut-leaf spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Aspleniaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are dark green and often have a leathery texture. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Asplenium juglandifolium is found in temperate regions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
