Achillea alpina subsp. japonica

Achillea alpina subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features wiry, upright stems and basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces flat, composite flower heads composed of small white to pinkish florets, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for garden use and traditional herbal medicine.
