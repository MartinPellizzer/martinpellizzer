Ampelamus ligulatus

Ampelamus ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Ampelamus ligulatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
