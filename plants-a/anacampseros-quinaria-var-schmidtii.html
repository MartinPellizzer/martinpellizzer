Anacampseros quinaria var. schmidtii

Anacampseros quinaria var. schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's ghostplant, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-forming growth habit with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
