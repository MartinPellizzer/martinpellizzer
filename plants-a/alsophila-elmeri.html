Alsophila elmeri

Alsophila elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in horticulture.
