Artabotrys tomentosus

Artabotrys tomentosus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, greyish leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its bark is thick and often covered in a white, waxy coating.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
