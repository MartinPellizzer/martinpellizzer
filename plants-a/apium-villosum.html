Apium villosum

Apium villosum, commonly known as clustered umbellifer or hairy parsley, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round fruits.

Apium villosum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
