Anathallis guimaraensii

Anathallis guimaraensii, commonly known as the Guimaraensii spider orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree branches or rock surfaces. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, narrow leaf and produces small, pale green or white flowers that resemble spiders, a characteristic feature of the Anathallis genus. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, highlighting its unique ecological relationship.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is a subject of conservation efforts.
