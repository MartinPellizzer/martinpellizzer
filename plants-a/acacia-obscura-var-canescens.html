Acacia obscura var. canescens

Acacia obscura var. canescens, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish-white, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is often flaky and may exude a milky sap when damaged.

This variety is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
