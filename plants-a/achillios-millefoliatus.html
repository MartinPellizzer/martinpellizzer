Achillios millefoliatus

Achillios millefoliatus, commonly known as the yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.

Yarrow is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory benefits.
