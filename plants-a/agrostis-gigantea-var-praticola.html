Agrostis gigantea var. praticola

Agrostis gigantea var. praticola, commonly known as roughstalk bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of open, spike-like clusters of small, blue-gray flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense turf in landscaping and restoration projects.
