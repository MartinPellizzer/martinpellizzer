Agrostis laxiflora var. tenuis

Agrostis laxiflora var. tenuis, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
