Acleia

Acleia, commonly known as blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant blue or purple flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. Acleia species are native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in dry, open habitats. The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the plants produce small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

They are often used in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
