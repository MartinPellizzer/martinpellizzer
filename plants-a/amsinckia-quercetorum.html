Amsinckia quercetorum

Amsinckia quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its distinctive, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed and gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Amsinckia quercetorum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
