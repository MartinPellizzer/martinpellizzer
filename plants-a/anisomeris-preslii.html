Anisomeris preslii

Anisomeris preslii, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, white spines. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
