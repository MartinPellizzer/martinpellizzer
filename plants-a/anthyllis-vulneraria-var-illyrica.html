Anthyllis vulneraria var. illyrica

Anthyllis vulneraria var. illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
