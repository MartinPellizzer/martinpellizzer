Amomum walang

Amomum walang, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes that contain essential oils responsible for its distinct flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are elongated and have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in spikes.

Amomum walang is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
