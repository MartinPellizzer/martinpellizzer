Astrantia maxima subsp. haradjianii

Astrantia maxima subsp.

haradjianii, commonly known as the Armenian aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia. It features showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often hairy as well. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
