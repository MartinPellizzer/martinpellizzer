Anthemis montana var. kabylica

Anthemis montana var. kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylica everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, tough, and long-lasting flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance.
