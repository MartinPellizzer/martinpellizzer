Abies excelsa

Abies excelsa, commonly known as the Colombian white pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and distinctive, elongated cones. The tree can reach heights of up to 50 meters, making it one of the tallest pine species in the world. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, offering shelter and food for various wildlife species.
