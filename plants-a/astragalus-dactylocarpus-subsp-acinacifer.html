Astragalus dactylocarpus subsp. acinacifer

Astragalus dactylocarpus subsp.

acinacifer, commonly known as the spiny milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, inflated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
