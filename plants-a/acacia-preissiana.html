Acacia preissiana

Acacia preissiana, commonly known as the white wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in globular clusters. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Acacia preissiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping.
