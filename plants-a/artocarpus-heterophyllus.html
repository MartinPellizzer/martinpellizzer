Artocarpus heterophyllus

Artocarpus heterophyllus, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, characterized by its broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces a fruit that is typically harvested when green and unripe, though it can be cooked or fermented. The fruit's structure includes a fibrous outer rind and a starchy, edible interior.

Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family and is notable for its unique, compound fruit structure.
