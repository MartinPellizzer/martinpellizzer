Acacia micradena

Acacia micradena, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. These flowers are followed by long, slender pods that contain seeds.

Acacia micradena is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
