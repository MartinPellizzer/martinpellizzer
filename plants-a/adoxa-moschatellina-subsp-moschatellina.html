Adoxa moschatellina subsp. moschatellina

Adoxa moschatellina subsp.

moschatellina, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant, white to greenish flowers arranged in a spike.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, often musky scent, which is most pronounced when the leaves are crushed. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The subspecies moschatellina is distinguished by its specific floral structure and habitat preferences compared to other subspecies of Adoxa.
