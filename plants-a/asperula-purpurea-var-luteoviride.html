Asperula purpurea var. luteoviride

Asperula purpurea var. luteoviride, commonly known as the yellow-green woodbine, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slight yellowish tinge on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to greenish-white, blooming in early spring. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing ornamental value in both winter and summer.
