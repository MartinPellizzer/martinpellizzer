Avena mandoniana

Avena mandoniana, commonly known as Mandon's oat, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Avena mandoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
