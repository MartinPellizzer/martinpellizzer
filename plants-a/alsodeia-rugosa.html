Alsodeia rugosa

Alsodeia rugosa, commonly known as the rough alsike, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in early summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Alsodeia rugosa is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
