Airochloa glauca

Airochloa glauca, commonly known as the bluegrass or bluegrass grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, blade-like leaves with a waxy coating that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Airochloa glauca is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
