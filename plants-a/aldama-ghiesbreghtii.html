Aldama ghiesbreghtii

Aldama ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
