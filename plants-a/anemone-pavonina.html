Anemone pavonina

Anemone pavonina, commonly known as the peacock anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, palmate flowers with vibrant pink to red hues that resemble a peacock's tail. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It blooms in early spring and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Anemone pavonina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
