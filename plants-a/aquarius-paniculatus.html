Aquarius paniculatus

Aquarius paniculatus, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a waterwheel, hence its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its submerged leaves are typically narrow and ribbon-like, while the floating leaves are broader and often have a waxy coating.

Aquarius paniculatus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic organisms.
