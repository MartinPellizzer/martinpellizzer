Aster borealis

Aster borealis, commonly known as the northern aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It features showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Aster borealis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
