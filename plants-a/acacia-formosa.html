Acacia formosa

Acacia formosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, feathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in winter, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Acacia formosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
