Acacia modesta var. laeta

Acacia modesta var. laeta, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow globular flower heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and used in reforestation projects.
