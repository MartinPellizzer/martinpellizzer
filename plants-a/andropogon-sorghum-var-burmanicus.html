Andropogon sorghum var. burmanicus

Andropogon sorghum var. burmanicus, commonly known as Burmese sorghum, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional landscaping and horticulture.
