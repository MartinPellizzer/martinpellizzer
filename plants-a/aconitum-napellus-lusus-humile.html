Aconitum napellus lusus humile

Aconitum napellus lusus humile, commonly known as the lesser monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmately compound leaves with a distinctive, spurred petal at the base of each flower. Native to central and eastern Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is important to handle the plant with care.
