Armeria chilensis subsp. andina

Armeria chilensis subsp.

andina, commonly known as the Andean thrift, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces dense clusters of white or pink flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
