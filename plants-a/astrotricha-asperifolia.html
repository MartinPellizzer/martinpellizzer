Astrotricha asperifolia

Astrotricha asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-hairy aster, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture, and its flowers are usually purple or blue in color.

Astrotricha asperifolia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
