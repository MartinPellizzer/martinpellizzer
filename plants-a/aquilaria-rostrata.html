Aquilaria rostrata

Aquilaria rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-beaked agarwood tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Thymelaeaceae and is characterized by its aromatic resinous heartwood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, winged fruits.

The plant's unique chemical composition, including sesquiterpenes and other volatile compounds, contributes to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
