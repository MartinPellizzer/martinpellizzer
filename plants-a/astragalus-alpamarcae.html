Astragalus alpamarcae

Astragalus alpamarcae, commonly known as the Andean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Astragalus alpamarcae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
