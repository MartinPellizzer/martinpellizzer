Anemia pohliana

Anemia pohliana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple petals and a distinctive conical seed head. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soil. It blooms in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
