Andropogon scoparius subvar. lolioides

Andropogon scoparius subvar. lolioides, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences that turn reddish-brown in the fall. This grass thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
