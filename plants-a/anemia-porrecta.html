Anemia porrecta

Anemia porrecta, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically broad, oval, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
