Anthora

Anthora, commonly known as the "scented orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula. They are characterized by their fragrant, often showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Anthora species typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with their succulent leaves and deep root systems.

Their unique floral structures and aromatic properties make them popular in horticulture and traditional medicine.
