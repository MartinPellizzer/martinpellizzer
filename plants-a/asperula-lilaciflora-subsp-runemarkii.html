Asperula lilaciflora subsp. runemarkii

Asperula lilaciflora subsp.

runemarkii, commonly known as the Runemark azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, lilac-pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
