Anchusa clavata

Anchusa clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Anchusa clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
