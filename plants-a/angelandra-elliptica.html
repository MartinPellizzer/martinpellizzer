Angelandra elliptica

Angelandra elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
