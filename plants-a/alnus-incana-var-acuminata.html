Alnus incana var. acuminata

Alnus incana var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender alder, is a variant of the gray alder native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in riparian zones and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting wildlife.
