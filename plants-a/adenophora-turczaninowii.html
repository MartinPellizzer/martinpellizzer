Adenophora turczaninowii

Adenophora turczaninowii, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.
