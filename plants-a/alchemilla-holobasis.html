Alchemilla holobasis

Alchemilla holobasis, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are arranged in a cup-like structure. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Alchemilla holobasis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
