Alchemilla fischeri subsp. fischeri

Alchemilla fischeri subsp.

fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly kidney-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
