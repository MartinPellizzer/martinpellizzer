Actinodaphne sasakii

Actinodaphne sasakii, commonly known as the Sasak nut or Sasak white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces drupes that are typically harvested for their edible seeds, which are rich in oil and used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
