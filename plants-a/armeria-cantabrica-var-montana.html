Armeria cantabrica var. montana

Armeria cantabrica var. montana, commonly known as the Cantabrian thrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of northern Spain.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with finely toothed leaves, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in rocky coastal areas and is adapted to harsh maritime climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
