Adesmia rigida

Adesmia rigida, commonly known as the rigid adesmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and woody stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Adesmia rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
