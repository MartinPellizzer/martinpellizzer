Aesculus tsiangii

Aesculus tsiangii, commonly known as the Yunnan horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its showy, umbellate flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces large, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds each. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it typically grows in mountainous regions.
