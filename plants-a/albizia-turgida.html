Albizia turgida

Albizia turgida, commonly known as the silky albizia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators.

Albizia turgida is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
