Artemisia ludoviciana var. albula

Artemisia ludoviciana var. albula, commonly known as white sage, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, aromatic leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in traditional herbal practices and as a companion plant in gardens.
