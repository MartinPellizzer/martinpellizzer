Ardisia ophirensis

Ardisia ophirensis, commonly known as the red-berried ardisia, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Ardisia ophirensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in shaded garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
