Adenophora remotiflora var. hirsuta

Adenophora remotiflora var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its hirsute (hairy) appearance is a distinguishing characteristic of this variant.
